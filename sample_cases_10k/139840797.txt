<p id="p_1">         (PER : HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE S.R.BRAHMBHATT)
</p><p id="p_2">1.     Present petition pertains to the implementation of the
amendment in the <a href="/doc/631210/" id="a_1">Wakf Act</a> brought about in the year 2013, as
on account of interpretation by the Board at the relevant time
irrespective       of       the    amendment,         applications         seeking
permission for transfer of wakf property were entertained on
the ground that pending applications would not be governed
by the amendment, under which prohibition is promulgated.
Shri Sunit Shah, learned counsel for respondent nos.8, 9 and
13 submitted that, in fact, his clients and some of the other

likely to be affected parties on account of the said amendment,
have moved this Court challenging the vires of the amendment
on various grounds and one such matter being Special Civil
Application No.13811 of 2017 is admitted, in which the Court
has passed following order:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">       "Heard Mr.Sunit S.Shah, learned advocate for the petitioner
       and Mr.Devang Vyas, learned Assistant Solicitor General for
       the respondent No.1.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">       Considering the submissions advanced and pleadings in the
       appeal, it deserves consideration. Hence, appeal is admitted,
       hence Rule."
</blockquote>

<p id="p_3">2.      In view thereof, it was urged that let all the matters be
heard together so that consistency of the view be maintained.
Learned counsel, Shri Hakim has no objection to this. In that
view of the matter, we direct the office to place these matters
before       Honourable         the   Acting         Chief   Justice    for     passing
appropriate order for placement thereof. Interim relief granted
earlier to continue till further order.
</p>
