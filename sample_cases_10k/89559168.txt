<pre id="pre_1">Order Sheet
                                CP No. 368 of 2008
                           IN THE HIGH COURT AT CALCUTTA

                                     ORIGINAL SIDE



       IN THE MATTER OF:
       M.G.COMMERCIAL PVT. LTD.
            AND
       IN THE MATTER OF:
       M/S. FERRAZZINI'S BAKERY
                                                                        Appearance:
                                                          Mr.Bidyut Dutta, Advocate

                                                      Mr.Promit Kumar Roy, Advocate

      BEFORE:
      The Hon'ble JUSTICE MAHARAJ SINHA</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Date: 25 March 2009.
</p>
<p id="p_2">

       Without prejudice to the rights and contentions of the parties

regarding the maintainability of the application, the company offers to

pay to the petitioning creditor a sum of Rs. 3,22,000/- (Rupees three

lakh Twenty two thousand only) in 10 monthly instalments.                  In addition

to    this,   the   company   is   directed   to   pay    interest   @7%    per   annum

aggregating to a sum of Rs. 18,000/- (Rupees eighteen thousand only).

Thus the company is to pay a total sum of Rs. 3,40,000/- in ten equal

monthly instalments.
</p>
<p id="p_3">       Accordingly, the company will pay a sum of Rs. 34,000/- (Rupees

thirty four thousand only) as the first of the ten instalments on 30

March 2009 and the remaining nine instalments of the above amount will

be paid on the last day of each succeeding month.                Upon payment of the

ten    instalments    as    above,    the   winding      up   petition   will     remain

permanently stayed.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                   2</span><p id="p_4">


</p><p id="p_5">           It is made clear that the petitioning creditor will not be entitled

to any further amount of occupation charges/licence fees for the period

of January 2007 to August 2008.                    However, this will not prevent the

petitioning creditor to realize the occupation charges/licence fees @

Rs. 17,715/- from the month of September 2008 if it is so entitled to in

accordance with law.
</p>
<p id="p_6">           In default of payment of any two monthly instalments as above, the

petitioning creditor shall have the liberty to mention the matter and

ask for appropriate orders.
</p>
<p id="p_7">           Since         no   affidavit   in   opposition   has   been   called   for,   the

allegations made in the winding up petition are deemed not to have been

taken to be admitted by the company.
</p>
<p id="p_8">           All concerned are to act on a xeroxed signed copy of this order to

be supplied to the parties on the usual undertakings.




</p><p id="p_9">                                                            (MAHARAJ SINHA, J.)
S. Kumar
Asst. Registrar (C.R.)
