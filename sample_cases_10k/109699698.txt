                  IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD
           CRIMINAL MISC.APPLICATION (FOR TEMPORARY BAIL) NO. 118 of
                                          2018
                      In CRIMINAL APPEAL NO. 2799 of 2008
         ==========================================================
           ANWAR SHAIKH AMIR SHAIKH BAIG THROUGH AFZAL SHAIKH AMIR
                            SHAIKH BAIG....Applicant(s)
                                    Versus
                      STATE OF GUJARAT &amp; 2....Respondent(s)
         ==========================================================
         Appearance:
         MR DIPAK H SINDHI, ADVOCATE for the Applicant(s) No. 1
         MR KSHITIJ M AMIN, CENTRAL GOVERNMENT STANDING COUNSEL for
         the Respondent(s) No. 3
         MR. PARTH H BHATT, ADVOCATE for the Respondent(s) No. 3
         MR HARDIK SONI, ADDITIONAL PUBLIC PROSECUTOR for the
         Respondent(s) No. 1
         ==========================================================
          CORAM: HONOURABLE SMT. JUSTICE ABHILASHA KUMARI
                 and
                 HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE B.N. KARIA
                            Date : 08/01/2018
                                    ORAL ORDER</pre>
<p id="p_1">               (PER : HONOURABLE SMT. JUSTICE ABHILASHA KUMARI)


</p><p id="p_2">         1.   This   application   has   been   preferred   by   the 

         brother of the convict with a prayer to release the 

         convict on temporary bail for a period of thirty days 

         in order to make financial arrangements and look after 

         the   medical  treatment   of   the   convict's  wife,  who   is 

         stated to be suffering from chest pain and has been 

         advised   to   undergo   angiography   and   possible 

         angioplasty on 09.01.2018.</p><pre id="pre_2">



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                       Page 1 of 4</span>

HC-NIC                              Page 1 of 4      Created On Mon Jan 08 23:03:36 IST 2018
                  R/CR.MA/118/2018                                           ORDER




</pre><p id="p_3">         2.   The   convict   has   been   sentenced   to   death   for 

         offences   punishable   under   the   Narcotic   Drugs   and 

         Psychotropic   Substances   Act,   1985   and   has   undergone 

         fifteen   years   and  seven  days   of   imprisonment  as  per 

         the Jail Report.
</p>
<p id="p_4">

</p><p id="p_5">         3.   Heard   Mr.Dipak   H.   Sindhi,   learned   advocate   for 

         the   applicant,   Mr.Hardik   Soni,   learned   Additional 

         Public   Prosecutor   for   respondent   No.1   -   State   of 

         Gujarat   and   Mr.Kshitij   M.   Amin,   learned   Central 

         Government   Standing   Counsel   for   respondent   No.3   and 

         perused the averments made in the application.



</p><p id="p_6">         4.   The   Jail   remarks   pertaining   to   the   convict 

         indicate that the conduct of the convict in Jail is 

         not good. The reason for this appears to be that at 

         one point of time, the convict had absconded for 878 

         days in the years 2001 to 2003 and had to be brought 

         back   to   the   Jail   by   the   Police.   Thereafter,   the 

         convict has been enlarged on temporary bail vide order 

         dated 29.12.2016, passed in Criminal Misc. Application 

         No.35079 of 2016 for a period of twenty­one days and 



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                       Page 2 of 4</span>

HC-NIC                              Page 2 of 4      Created On Mon Jan 08 23:03:36 IST 2018
                   R/CR.MA/118/2018                                           ORDER



         vide order dated 28.06.2017, passed in Criminal Misc. 

         Application   No.15750   of   2017   for   a  period   of   seven 

         days. On both occasions, the convict has surrendered 

         on time. 
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><p id="p_8">         5.   In   the   present   application,   the   reason   for 

         release   on   temporary   bail   is   stated   to   be   the 

         angiography of the wife of the convict, scheduled on 

         09.01.2018. A Certificate dated 29.12.2017, issued by 

         Dr.Piyush   Patel,   MD   (USA)   FACC   (USA),   has   been 

         produced on record. As per the said certificate, the 

         wife of the convict is advised to undergo angiography 

         and possible angioplasty on 09.01.2018.



</p><p id="p_9">         6.   Taking into consideration the Medical Certificate 

         issued by Dr.Piyush Patel, it appears that the reason 

         stated in the application is a genuine one.



</p><p id="p_10">         7.   Hence,   we   are   inclined   to   pass   the   following 

         order:
</p>
<p id="p_11">

</p><p id="p_12">              The convict shall be released on temporary bail 

         for a period of two days on furnishing a personal bond 



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                                        Page 3 of 4</span>

                     R/CR.MA/118/2018                                            ORDER



         of Rs.5,000/­ (Rupees Five Thousand Only) and upon the 

         usual terms and conditions, to the satisfaction of the 

         concerned Jail authority.
</p>
<p id="p_13">

</p><p id="p_14">                  The   convict   shall   mark   his   presence   before   the 

         nearest   Police  Station   on   each   day   of   the  temporary 

         bail. 
</p>
<p id="p_15">

</p><p id="p_16">                  Upon expiry of the aforesaid period, the convict 

         shall surrender before the concerned Jail authority, 

         forthwith. 
</p>
<p id="p_17">

</p><p id="p_18">         8.       The   application   is   partly­allowed,   in   the   above 

         terms. Rule is made absolute, to the aforesaid extent.



</p><p id="p_19">                  Direct   Service   of   this   order,   today,   is 

         permitted. 
</p>
