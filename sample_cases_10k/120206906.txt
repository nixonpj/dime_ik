<div class="doc_title">Upendra Yadav @ Gandhi vs The State Of Bihar on 21 October, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                              CRIMINAL MISCELLANEOUS No.66048 of 2019
                   Arising Out of PS. Case No.-157 Year-2019 Thana- MADANPUR District- Aurangabad
                 ======================================================
                 Upendra Yadav @ Gandhi, Aged about 41 years, Male, Son of Chitaman
                 Yadav, Resident of Village Laltenganj, P.S. Madanpur, District Aurangabad.

                                                                                ... ... Petitioner/s
                                                      Versus
                 The State of Bihar.

                                                        ... ... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :      Mr.Ashok Kumar Singh
                 For the Opposite Party/s :      Mr.Bishweshwar Ram
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE PRABHAT KUMAR JHA
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   21-10-2019                  Heard both sides.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                The petitioner apprehends his arrest in Madanpur P.S.

                   Case No.157 of 2019, registered under Section 30(a) of the

                   Bihar Prohibition and <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_1">Excise Act</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                The informant got information that the petitioner was

                   selling liquor and on such raid was conducted but the petitioner

                   and Mohan Choudhary alleged to have fled away after seeing

                   the police. 12 litres liquor was recovered from the house of

                   Mohan Choudhary.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that

                   nothing has been recovered from the house of the petitioner. The

                   recovery was made from the house of Mohan Choudhary.

</p><p id="p_5">                                Taking into consideration the fact that the recovery

                   was made from the house of Mohan Choudhary and nothing has
          Patna High Court CR. MISC. No.66048 of 2019(2) dt.21-10-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                     2/2</span>




                   been recovered from possession of the petitioner, let petitioner,

                   above named, in the event of his arrest or surrender before the

                   court below within a period of four weeks from the date of

                   receipt of the order, be enlarged on bail on furnishing bail bond

                   of Rs.10,000/- (ten thousand) with two sureties of the like

                   amount each to the satisfaction of the learned Additional

                   Sessions Judge-VII-cum-Special Judge (Excise), Aurangabad in

                   connection with Madanpur P.S. Case No.157 of 2019, subject to

                   the conditions laid down under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_2">Section 438(2)</a> of the Code of

                   Criminal Procedure.
</p>
<p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">                                           (Prabhat Kumar Jha, J)
S.KUMAR/-
</p>
