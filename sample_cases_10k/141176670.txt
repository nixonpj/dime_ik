<div class="doc_title">Smt.Sabitri Pandey vs State Of Jharkhand &amp; Ors. on 24 January, 2013</div><pre id="pre_1">IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
          WP(S) No. 2939 of 2006
Smt. Sabitri Pandey                              --- Petitioner
                      Versus
1. The State of Jharkhand through its Secretary,
   Human Resource Department, Govt. of Jharkhand
2. The District Superintendent of Education,
   East Singhbhum at Jamshedpur
3. The Deputy Commissioner, East Singhbhum
    at Jamshedpur                                --- Respondents

CORAM: -HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE SHREE CHANDRASHEKHAR
                      -------------
</pre><p id="p_1">For the Petitioner(s)       : Mr. M. Jagannath, Advocate.
</p><p id="p_2">For the Respondent(s)       : Mrs. J.C. to S.C.-III

05/Dated : 24th January, 2013

</p><p id="p_3">1. The petitioner is widow. the petitioner's husband was headmaster in the
  Primary School, Lodhabani, P.S. Bahragora, District-East Singhbhum.
  The petitioner's husband died at 8.10 a.m. in morning of 27.04.1998.
</p><p id="p_4">2. It appears from the record of the case a new Group Insurance Policy
  came into effect from 27.04.1998. The petitioner made representation to
  the authorities for payment under old scheme, however such benefit was
  denied by the respondents.
</p><p id="p_5">3. Aggrieved, the petitioner filed the present writ petition seeking direction
  to the respondents for the payment of enhanced amount at the rate as
  prescribed in the old scheme.
</p><p id="p_6">4. A counter affidavit has been filed on behalf of respondent no. 2 relying
  on the Government Notification dated 27.04.1998, whereby the State
  Government had taken a decision to implement the new Group
  Insurance Scheme, 1997 vide Government Notification No. A.F.C.
  (E)105/98/2757-Vi.(2) dated 27.04.1998.
</p><p id="p_7">5. It appears from the writ petition that in similar circumstances, the
  respondents had granted such benefits to one Smt. Rekha Kumari
  Shrivastava vide letter dated 01.07.1998 of the Finance Department. In
  the counter affidavit filed by the respondents, it has been plainly stated
  that the order as contained in Annexure No. 4 would not be applicable to
  the present petitioner and hence such benefits cannot be extended to the
  petitioner.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                              -2-</span>
<p id="p_8">        6. Heard learned counsel for the parties. I find no reasonable ground for
            denying the petitioner the benefit as extended to another persons vide
            letter dated 01.07.1998.
</p><p id="p_9">        7. In the facts and circumstance of this case the respondents are directed to
            consider the case of the petitioner in the light of Annexure No.4 and to
            communicate the reasoned decision to the petitioner within a period of
            eight weeks from today.
</p><p id="p_10">        8. The writ petition is disposed of with the aforesaid direction.
</p>



<p id="p_11">                                                 (SHREE CHANDRASHEKHAR ,J)



Sharma/ Tanuj
