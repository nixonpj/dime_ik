                            ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">     Heard.
</p>
<p id="p_2">     Miscellaneous     First   Appeal     No.4848/2007       was

disposed of on 30th August 2011 by enhancing the

compensation    to   Rs.2,31,000/-      per   acre   apart   from

granting statutory benefits.   However, the Division Bench

remitted the matter to the reference Court only for deciding

the question relating to damages suffered by the claimants

from the date of their dispossession till the date of

preliminary notification.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                               3</span><p id="p_3">



</p><p id="p_4">     2. This Review Petition is filed by the State praying to

modify the second part of the operative portion by clarifying

that the remand is made to the Land Acquisition Officer

instead of reference Court.
</p>
<p id="p_5">
</p><p id="p_6">     3. In the case of <a href="/doc/190336/" id="a_1">R.I. JAIN -vs- DDA</a> reported in

(2004)4 SCC 79 at paragraph-18, the Apex Court has

observed that it is for the Collector to decide the damages

and rent to be paid in favour of the land owner whose land

is taken possession of prior to the issuance of the

preliminary notification. Thus it is prima facie clear that the

matter relating to payment of damages and rent needs to

be decided by the concerned Land Acquisition Officer at the

first instance.   But this Court remitted the matter to the

reference Court for the purpose of assessing the damages.

Hence the Judgment of the Division Bench to that extent

needs to be modified by clarifying that the matter is

remitted to the Land Acquisition Officer for quantifying the

damages, if any on merits.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                   4</span><p id="p_7">




</p><p id="p_8">        Accordingly, the following order is made:


        The   operative   portion     of   the   order   in   MFA

No.4848/2007 at item No.(a) remains unaltered.           However,

in respect of operative portion of the order at item No.(b) is

concerned, we clarify that the matter is remitted to the

concerned Land Acquisition Officer instead of reference

Court for quantifying the damages, if any. It is open for the

parties to produce the records before the Land Acquisition

Officer justifying their claim.
</p>
<p id="p_9">

</p><p id="p_10">        Review Petition is allowed to the aforementioned

extent.
</p>
