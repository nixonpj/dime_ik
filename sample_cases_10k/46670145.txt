</pre><p id="p_1">            The appellant is aggrieved of the judgment and decree of the lower

Appellate Court whereby, the application filed under Order 41 Rule 27 of the

Code of Civil Procedure (hereinafter called 'CPC') has not been decided by the

lower Appellate Court.
</p>
<p id="p_2">            Mr. Rajesh Goyal, learned counsel appearing on behalf of the

appellants-defendants submits that during the pendency of the appeal filed

against the judgment and decree of the trial Court, whereby the suit of the

respondents was decreed, an application under order 41 Rule 27 of the Code of




                                      1 of 2


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                   ::: Downloaded on - 06-05-2016 00:00:30 :::</span>
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2"> RSA-2585-2012 (O&amp;M)                                                              2</span>



Civil Procedure, was moved, however, the application which was duly replied,

has not been decided either in the impugned judgment or by way of separate

order. In support of his contentions, he relies upon the judgment of Hon'ble

Supreme Court in "Malayalam Plantations ltd. V/s State of Kerala and

another, 2011 AIR SC 559.
</p>
<p id="p_3">             This fact is not disputed by the learned counsel for the

respondents.
</p>
<p id="p_4">             In view of the aforementioned facts and law laid down by the

Hon'ble Supreme Court in "Malayalam Plantations ltd.'s case (supra), the

impugned judgment and decree of the lower Appellate Court is hereby set aside

and the matter is remitted back to the lower Appellate Court to decide the

appeal along with the application filed under Order 41 Rule 27 CPC afresh in

accordance with law with a period of four months from the date of receipt of

the certified copy of this order.
</p>
<p id="p_5">             The said appeal is ordered to be restored to its original number.

             The parties are directed to appear before the lower Appellate Court

on 23.05.2016.
</p>
<p id="p_6">             With the aforesaid observations, the appeal stands disposed of.
