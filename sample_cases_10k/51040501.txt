<div class="doc_title">Karu Das vs State Of Bihar on 26 August, 2009</div><pre id="pre_1">                  IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                               Cr.Misc. No.20794 of 2009
                                         KARU DAS
                                            Versus
                                   STATE OF BIHAR
                                          -----------
</pre>

<p id="p_1">03   26.08.2009                Heard learned counsels for the petitioner, informant

                  and State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                               Sole petitioner has approached this court seeking

                  bail in a case instituted under <a href="/doc/695990/" id="a_1">Sections 364</a> and <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_2">120B</a> of the

                  Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                               As per allegation in the complaint, on 15.07.2006,

                  the petitioner took the son of the informant to some place in U.P.

                  for employment. The boy did not return. It is the case in the

                  complaint itself that the complainant had a talk with the boy on

                  few occasions. The complaint was lodged on 22.12.2008, which

                  was later treated as an F.I.R. on 08.02.2009. Allegation is that on

                  several occasions the informant tried to query the petitioner but

                  no satisfactory reply was coming with regard to whereabouts of

                  the son.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                               Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that there

                  is inordinate delay in lodging the case. Referring to the

                  statements of Mill owner and Manager contained in paragraph

                  nos. 15, 18 and 21 of the case diary, learned counsel for the

                  petitioner submits that they deposed to the effect that this boy

                  had taken certain advances (amount) from them and without

                  payment thereof he left the job.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                             2</span><p id="p_5">




                  Petitioner appears to be in custody since 09.02.2009.

                  Looking to the entire aspects of the matter, this court

       is persuaded to admit the petitioner to bail. Let the petitioner,

       namely, Karu Das, be released on bail on furnishing bail bond

       of Rs. 10,000/- ( ten thousand) with two sureties of the like

       amount each to the satisfaction of the      C.J.M. Nawadah in

       connection with Rajauli P.S.Case no. 19/09, subject to the

       condition that one of the bailors should be the own/close family

       member of the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_6">


</p><pre id="pre_2">Sym/                                   (Kishore K. Mandal, J.)
 </pre>
