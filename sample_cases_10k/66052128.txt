<div class="doc_title">Radhe Shyam Pathak vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 29 February, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                         MCRC-22197-2015
            (RADHE SHYAM PATHAK Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)


29-02-2016

Shri Ajay Kumar Jain, learned counsel for the applicant.
Shri Y. D. Yadav, learned Panel Lawyer, for the respondent-State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">At the outset of hearing of the case, the learned counsel for the
applicant prays for a direction from this court to the trial court to
expedite the disposal of the case.
</p><p id="p_2">Prayer is allowed.
</p><p id="p_3">The Additional Sessions Judge, Nagod, district Satna, is directed to
hold the trial of Sessions Trial No.47/2015, State of M.P. Vs. Radhe
alias Radheshyam Pathak and another, strictly following the
provisions of <a href="/doc/1645292/" id="a_1">Section 309</a> of the Cr.P.C. The same direction was
earlier issued by this court vide order dated 18.9.2015 passed in
M.Cr.C. No.15230/2015. However, upon perusal of all the order
sheets of hearings of the case between 2.2.2015 and 21.11.2015, it
appears that the learned trial Judge has not followed the directions
of this court in true spirit. He is directed to take coercive steps
against those prosecution witnesses who are not coming to give
evidence in the court despite they are properly served.
The Superintendent of Police, Satna, is also directed to issue
directions to the Station House Officer of Police Station Maihar for
proper and prompt service of summons upon the prosecution
witnesses
Copies of this order be sent to the aforesaid trial court and the
Superintendent of Police, Satna, within a week.
With the aforesaid observations, this second bail application under
<a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_2">Section 439</a> of the Cr.P.C. is dismissed.
</p><p id="p_4"> Certified copy as per rules.
</p>

<p id="p_5">                               (RAJENDRA MAHAJAN)
                                      JUDGE
