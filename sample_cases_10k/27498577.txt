<div class="doc_title">Mukesh vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 12 April, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                          1

             HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH

                        MCRC No.14868/2019

              (Mukesh &amp; Others Vs The State of M.P.)

Gwalior, Dated : 12.04.2019

      Shri G.S.Sharma, learned counsel for the applicant.

      Ms. Gayatri Surve, learned Public Prosecutor for the

respondent/State.</pre>

<p id="p_1">      The applicants have filed this first bail application u/S.439,

<a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Cr.P.C</a>. for grant of bail. The applicants have been arrested by Forest

Range Palpur, District Shivpuri in connection with Forest Crime

No.32716/2011 registered in relation to the offences punishable u/Ss.

27, 29, 30, 32, 50, 51(1) and 51 of <a href="/doc/1781078/" id="a_2">Wild Life (Protection) Act</a>, 1972

      Learned counsel for the applicant submits that as per

prosecution Forest Game Range Palpur filed a complaint under

<a href="/doc/463532/" id="a_3">Section 27</a>, <a href="/doc/942665/" id="a_4">29</a>, <a href="/doc/558666/" id="a_5">30</a>, <a href="/doc/50850/" id="a_6">32</a>, <a href="/doc/702677/" id="a_7">50</a>, <a href="/doc/793163/" id="a_8">51(1)</a> and <a href="/doc/672187/" id="a_9">51</a> of Wild Life (Protection) Act

in the Court of JMFC, Vijaypur, District Sheopur stating that on

18.03.2019 the Forest Guard Anand Gupta, Beat Incharge Pipal

Babadi East Harendra Jadon, Laborer Kailash Dhakad, Naresh Jatav

and Jitendra Dhanuk were on patrolling at that time at about 04.30 PM

three persons are found in the prohibited area of Kuno Century and

they are taping on the trees and they have arrest and Two Saluki and

one Ghanti (Bell), one matchbox and 700 Gram Cheed (Gum) have

been seized from their possession. It is also submitted that the

applicants have been falsely implicated. They are in custody since
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                            2</span>

               HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH

                         MCRC No.14868/2019

               (Mukesh &amp; Others Vs The State of M.P.)

18.03.2019. There is no possibility of their absconding or tampering

with the prosecution case. Under these circumstances, counsel for the

applicants prays for grant of bail.
</p>
<p id="p_2">      Learned Public Prosecutor for the respondent/State opposed the

prayer and prayed for dismissal of the application.

</p><p id="p_3">      Heard learned counsel for the parties and perused the case diary.

      Considering the pretrial detention, the facts and circumstances

of the case and without expressing any opinion on the merits of the

case, application is allowed.
</p>
<p id="p_4">      It is hereby directed that the applicants be released on bail on

their furnishing a personal bond of Rs.25,000/- (Rupees Twenty Five

Thousand Only) each with one solvent surety each to the

satisfaction of the Trial Court to appear before the Court on the dates

given by the concerned Court.
</p>
<p id="p_5">      This order will remain operative subject to compliance of the

following conditions by the applicants :-

</p><p id="p_6">1.    The applicants will comply with all the terms and conditions of

the bond executed by them;
</p>
<p id="p_7">2.    The applicants will cooperate in the investigation/trial, as the

case may be;
</p>
<p id="p_8">3.    The applicants will not indulge themselves in extending
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                   3</span>

                     HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH

                                MCRC No.14868/2019

                      (Mukesh &amp; Others Vs The State of M.P.)

       inducement, threat or promise to any person acquitted with the facts of

       the case so as to dissuade them from disclosing such facts to the Court

       or to the Police Officer, as the case may be;

</p><p id="p_9">       4.     The applicants will not seek unnecessary adjournments during

       the trial;
</p>
<p id="p_10">       5.     The applicants will not leave India without previous permission

       of the trial Court/Investigating Officer, as the case may be; and

</p><p id="p_11">       6.     The applicants shall not commit an offence similar to the

       offence of which they are accused.
</p>
<p id="p_12">              A copy of this order be sent to the Court concerned for

       compliance.
</p>
<p id="p_13">              Certified copy as per rules.
</p>
