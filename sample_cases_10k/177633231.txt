<div class="doc_title">Smt. Geeta Bais vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 13 July, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">       THE HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH
  W.A.No.928/2017 (Smt. Geeta Bais Vs. State of M.P. and
                        others)
                                                           1
Gwalior
13.7.2018
     Shri D.S. Raghuvanshi, learned counsel for the

appellant.

      Heard on admission.

      This appeal under Section 2(1) of Madhya Pradesh

Uchcha Nyayalaya (Khand Nyaypeeth Ko Appeal) Adhiniyam,

2005 is directed against the order dated 26.9.2017, whereby

the challenge    to an order dated 8.9.2017 has been

negatived.

      The grievance raised by the petitioner in the writ

petition was against the action of the respondents in</pre>

<p id="p_1">advertising the post of Warden in Government Girls Hostel

Badoda, District Sheopur on the anvil that the petitioner

was having an additional charge of the said post.   Learned

Single Judge found that since additional charge     does not

create any vested right in the incumbent declined to cause

any indulgence    with the action of the respondents       in

issuing the advertisement for appointment     of Warden in

Government Girls Hostel Badoda, District Sheopur.

</p><p id="p_2">      Though it is contended on behalf of the appellant that
                     THE HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH
              W.A.No.928/2017 (Smt. Geeta Bais Vs. State of M.P. and
                                    others)
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                                          2</span>
           the learned Single Judge grossly erred in construing        that

           additional charge does not create a right on the incumbent,

           however, the submission that additional charge creates

           vested right in the incumbent is not substantiated with any

           statutory provisions.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                   In view whereof, we do not perceive any illegality in

           the order passed by the learned Single Judge           as would

           warrant any indulgence.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                   The appeal fails and is dismissed. No costs.</p><pre id="pre_2">



                   (Sanjay Yadav)                (Ashok Kumar Joshi)
                        Judge                         Judge
