<div class="doc_title">Dr.(Mrs.) Mamta Jhala (Bhawsar) vs Higher Education Department on 11 May, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                                1

              Writ Petition No.3420/2016
11.05.2016
     Shri L.C. Patne, learned counsel for the petitioner.
     Shri C.S. Ujjainiya, learned Panel Lawyer for the
respondent / State, on advance notice.
     With the consent of the learned counsel for the
parties, this writ petition is heard finally.
                          ORDER
</pre><p id="p_1">     By this writ petition under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the
Constitution of India, the petitioner is praying for issuance
of writ of mandamus, directing the respondents to grant
benefit of Senior Grade Pay Scale of Rs.10,000-15,200/-;
benefit of Selection Grade of Rs.12,000-18,300/- and
benefit of Pay Band - IV of Pay Scale of Rs.37,400-
67,000/- + AGP of Rs.9,000/- from the date of initial
appointment on emergency basis and to re-fix the pay of
the petitioner and release arrears thereof with interest at
the rate of 12% per annum.
</p><p id="p_2">2.   It is submitted that in the case of <a href="/doc/158148328/" id="a_2">Dr. Ramesh
Chandra Dixit v. State of MP &amp; others</a> reported in
2012 (3) MPHT 86 and also in the case of <a href="/doc/168292273/" id="a_3">Smt. Richa
Pandviya (Mehta) v. State of MP &amp; others</a>, whereby
this Court had held thus: -
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">        "5. Not only this, the aforesaid judgment has
        been affirmed by the Hon'ble Supreme Court
        and the SLP preferred by the State of MP i.e.
        No.6269/2012 (State of MP &amp; others v. Bangali
        Babu) has been dismissed. Meaning thereby,
        the order passed by this Court has been
        affirmed.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">        6.    In the light of the aforesaid, this Court is
        of the considered opinion that the petitioner
        who is an identically placed person, is entitled
        for the same relief as extended to Dr. Ramesh
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                  2</span>

          Chandra Dixit and other identically placed
          person.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">          7.    This writ petition stands allowed and the
          judgment delivered in the case of Dr. Ramesh
          Chandra Dixit shall be applicable mutatis
          mutandis in the present case also. The exercise
          of granting all consequential benefits to the
          petitioner be concluded within a period of three
          months from the date of receipt of certified
          copy of this order.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_4">          8.    With the aforesaid observation, the writ
          petition stands allowed."
</blockquote>
<p id="p_3">3.      Learned Panel Lawyer for the respondent / State has
submitted that the controversy involved in the present case
is covered by the order passed in the case of <a href="/doc/158148328/" id="a_4">Dr. Ramesh
Chandra Dixit v. State of MP &amp; others</a> (supra) and he
has no objection to the reasonable prayer made by the
learned counsel for the petitioner.
</p><p id="p_4">4.      It is also pointed out by the learned counsel for the
petitioner that the decision in the case of <a href="/doc/158148328/" id="a_5">Dr. Ramesh
Chandra Dixit v. State of MP &amp; others</a> (supra) has
been confirmed by a Division Bench of this Court in the
case of <a href="/doc/28596677/" id="a_6">State of MP &amp; others v. Dr. Ramesh Chandra
Dixit</a> reported in 2013 (4) MPJR 123 and the same has
been subsequently affirmed by the Apex Court in the case
of SLP (Civil) No.30492/2013 decided on 18.02.2015.
It is also pointed out that the review petition filed by the
State     of   Madhya    Pradesh     vide   Review      Petition
No.1348/2015 has also been dismissed on 13.05.2015.
</p><p id="p_5">5.      On due consideration of the above submissions, the
present writ petition is also disposed of with a direction to
the respondent - Competent Authority to extend the same
benefit to the present petitioner, if found to be eligible,
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                              3</span>

             which has been extended to the petitioner in the case of Dr.
             Ramesh Chandra Dixit (supra). The directions issued in
             the matter of Dr. Ramesh Chandra Dixit (supra) will apply
             mutatis mutandis to the case of the present petitioner also.
</p><p id="p_6">             6.   With the aforesaid observations and directions, this
             writ petition is disposed of.
</p><p id="p_7">                  No order as to costs.
</p><p id="p_8">                  C. c. as per rules.
</p>

<p id="p_9">                                                   (P.K. Jaiswal)
                                                       Judge
Pithawe RC
