<div class="doc_title">Mahendra Pandit vs The State Of Bihar &amp; Ors on 1 March, 2011</div><pre id="pre_1">                       IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                     CWJC No.4540 of 2006

                        Mahendra Pandit, Son of Late Sipahi Pandit, resident of village-
                        Amarpur, P.S. Bihpur, District-Bhagalpur ----   Petitioner
                                           Versus

                     1. THE STATE OF BIHAR
                     2. The District Superintendent of Education, Bhagalpur,
                     3. The District Provident Fund Officer, Bhagalppur
                                                        --------------------- Respondents.
                                                 -----------
</pre>
<p id="p_1">02      01-03-2011                   Heard Sri Mahendra Pathak, learned counsel for the

                         petitioner and Sri Subesh Sharma, learned A.C. to Govt. Pleader

                         no.16

                                     The present petition was filed with a prayer to direct

                         the Respondents to make payment of G.P.F. amount, which was

                         deducted up to December, 1976.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                     In this case, counter affidavit was filed on behalf of

                         Respondent no.2, wherein it was indicated that Respondent no.2,

                         i.e. the District Superintendent of Education, Bhagalpur has

                         already sent communication to the District Provident Fund

                         Officer, Bhagalpur for making payment of the due amount with

                         up-to-date interest . Today, it was informed by the learned

                         counsel for the petitioner that finally a cheque has already been

                         handed over to the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                     In view of the facts and circumstances of the case,

                         there is no need to keep the matter pending further, Accordingly,

                         the writ petition stands disposed of since the grievances of the

                         petitioner have already been redressed.
</p>
<p id="p_4">
</p><pre id="pre_2">NKS/-                                                     ( Rakesh Kumar, J.)
