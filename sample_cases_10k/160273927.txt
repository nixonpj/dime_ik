                    Arising Out of PS. Case No.-20 Year-2007 Thana- KASIMBAZAR District- Munger
                 ======================================================
                 Kanhai Sao @ Kanhai Sah, son of Kokai Sah, resident of village/Muhalla-
                 Betwan Bazar Argara Road, P.S.- Kasim Bazar, District- Munger.

                                                                                   ... ... Appellant
                                                     Versus
                 The State of Bihar

                                                            ... ... Respondent
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Appellant/s     :      Mr. Sanjiv Kumar Singh, Adv.
                 For the Respondent/s    :      Mr. Satya Narayan Prasad, A.P.P.

                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE RAKESH KUMAR
                         and

                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">                 (Per: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE RAKESH KUMAR)

5   19-11-2018                 I.A. No. 2669 of 2018

                              After some argument, Sri Sanjiv Kumar Singh,

                  learned counsel for the appellant, in presence of Sri Satya

                  Narayan Prasad, learned Addl. Public Prosecutor, submits that

                  on instruction, he is not pressing the Interlocutory Application

                  i.e. I.A. No. 2669 of 2018 filed under <a href="/doc/1116025/" id="a_1">Section 389(1)</a> of the

                  Code of Criminal Procedure for suspending sentence and

                  granting bail to the appellant during pendency of the appeal.

</p><p id="p_2">                              Sri Satya Narayan Prasad, learned Addl. Public

                  Prosecutor informs that in terms of earlier order, he has already

                  filed written objection against the prayer for bail, which is on

                  record.
</p><p id="p_3">              Patna High Court CR. APP (DB) No.771 of 2018(5) dt.19-11-2018
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                          2/2</span>




                                   Since, learned counsel for the appellant is not pressing

                      the Interlocutory Application for bail, it is dismissed as not

                      pressed.
</p>
<p id="p_4">

</p><p id="p_5">                                                  (Rakesh Kumar, J.)


                                                ( Arvind Srivastava, J)
