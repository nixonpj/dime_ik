<div class="doc_title">Jawahir Singh &amp; Ors. vs The State Of Bihar on 20 April, 2011</div><pre id="pre_1">                       IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                    Cr.Misc. No.10050 of 2011
                 1.   JAWAHIR SINGH SON OF MAHESH SINGH.
                 2.   JYOTI SINGH @ JYOTI KUMAR SON OF JAWAHIR SINGH.
                 3.   SANJAY SINGH SON OF BISHWANATH SINGH.
                 4.   GUPUT SINGH SON OF GHUGHULI SINGH.
                 5.   RAHUL SINGH @ RAHUL KUMAR SON OF GUPT SINGH.
                 6.   KASHI NATH SINGH SON OF NIRMAL SINGH.
                                                 Versus
                                      THE STATE OF BIHAR
                                             -----------
</pre>


<p id="p_1">2   20.04.2011                  Heard learned counsels for the petitioners

                      and the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                The petitioners are apprehending their arrest

                      in a case registered under <a href="/doc/1258372/" id="a_1">Sections 147</a>, <a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_2">149</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_3">323</a> and

                      <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_4">307</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                The    accusation     against   seven   accused

                      persons is to have made assault to the informant.

</p><p id="p_4">                                The injury report has been brought on record,

                      which reflects six injuries out of which one injury has

                      been found to be grievous on the hand.

</p><p id="p_5">                                It is submitted that there is general

                      accusation against seven accused persons and it is

                      difficult to figure out as to who caused the grievous

                      injury.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                It is further submitted that there is serious
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                               -2-</span>




             land dispute between the parties and a suit was also

             going on between the parties since, 1999 in which the

             petitioners have got the decree. The informant has

             himself admitted the land dispute.

</p><p id="p_7">                       Considering the aforesaid facts, let the above

             named petitioners, be released on anticipatory bail, in

             the event of their arrest or surrender before the learned

             Court below within a period of 12 weeks from today,

             on furnishing bail bond of Rs. 10,000/- (ten thousand)

             with two sureties of the like amount each to the

             satisfaction of the learned Sub-divisional Judicial

             Magistrate, Bikramganj in connection with Narwar P.S.

             Case No. 02/2011, subject to the conditions as laid

             down under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_5">Section 438(2)</a> Cr.P.C.


</p><p id="p_8">                                          (Dinesh Kumar Singh, J.)
Amrendra/-
</p>
