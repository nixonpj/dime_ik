<div class="doc_title">Vinod @ Kalu vs State Of Haryana on 13 December, 2012</div><pre id="pre_1">CRR No. 3564 of 2012 (O&amp;M)                                     [1]




          IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA AT
                         CHANDIGARH.


                                 CRR No. 3564 of 2012 (O&amp;M)

                                 Date of Decision: December 13, 2012

Vinod @ Kalu

                                        .....Petitioner

               Vs.

State of Haryana

                                        .....Respondent


CORAM:         HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE M.M.S. BEDI.

                           -.-

Present:-      Mr.N.D. Achint, Advocate
               for the petitioner.


                     -.-

M.M.S. BEDI, J. (ORAL)
</pre>
<p id="p_1">               Petitioner has been convicted under <a href="/doc/409589/" id="a_1">Section 304</a> Part II IPC for

having stabbed Naresh Kumar in his abdomen with scissor. He alongwith

his co-accused Balbir and Sanjeev was tried by Juvenile Justice Board

whereas the 4th accused Birbal @ Bablu was tried by the Court of Sessions

Judge.      Balbir Singh and Sanjeev have been convicted for offence under

<a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_2">Section 323</a> IPC whereas the petitioner has been convicted under <a href="/doc/409589/" id="a_3">Section

304</a> Part II IPC. Petitioner has been detained in Special Home for about 11

months.
</p><p id="p_2"> CRR No. 3564 of 2012 (O&amp;M)                                       [2]




                Counsel for the petitioner submits that since the petitioner,

during the pendency of the trial, has attained majority, he can not be kept in

Special Home as such his sentence be suspended.

</p><p id="p_3">                I do not find any force in the contention of learned counsel for

the petitioner for suspension of sentence on the ground that the petitioner

has attached majority.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                Counsel for the petitioner has also raised argument regarding

the validity of the order of conviction contending that the weapon of offence

i.e. scissor having not been recovered, the petitioner should be granted the

benefit of doubt.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                A perusal of the judgment passed by the Courts below indicates

that the conviction order has been passed on the basis of ocular version of

the eye-witnesses Birpal, PW1, Sushma, PW3, Ranbir PW4 and Beeran

Devi PW5. The said ocular evidence has been corroborated by the medical

evidence. The recovery of weapon in view of the overwhelming evidence

against the petitioner, looses any significance for the purpose of

appreciation of material against the petitioner. No ground is made out for

interference.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                Dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_7">
</p><pre id="pre_2">December 13 , 2012                                    (M.M.S.BEDI)
 sanjay                                                 JUDGE
