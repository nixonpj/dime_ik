<div class="doc_title">Magma Fincorp Limited vs Jagdish &amp; Anr on 24 September, 2008</div><div class="doc_author">Author: Indira Banerjee</div>

<pre id="pre_1">                              AP No. 499 of 2008
                        IN THE HIGH COURT AT CALCUTTA
                    Ordinary Original Civil Jurisdiction



                             MAGMA FINCORP LIMITED
                                     Versus
                                 JAGDISH &amp; ANR.


BEFORE:
The Hon'ble JUSTICE INDIRA BANERJEE
Date : 24th September, 2008.


        The Court : Counsel appearing on behalf of the petitioner prays for

leave under Clause 12 of the Letters Patent.

        This Court is, however, of the view that no leave under Clause 12 of

the Letters Patent is required since an application under <a href="/doc/1120409/" id="a_1">Section 9</a> of the

Arbitration and <a href="/doc/1306164/" id="a_2">Conciliation Act</a>, 1996, hereinafter referred to as the 1996

Act, is not a suit.

        Applications under <a href="/doc/1120409/" id="a_3">Section 9</a> of the 1996 Act cannot be equated with

applications   under   <a href="/doc/811701/" id="a_4">Section   20</a>   of    the   Arbitration   Act,   1940   which   were

categorised as special suits.

        However, since it has been the practice so long of the Court to

grant leave even in applications under <a href="/doc/1120409/" id="a_5">Section 9</a> of the 1996 Act such leave

is granted with a view to avoid disputes on the issue of jurisdiction.

        The    petitioner   and/or    its    predecessor-in-interest      financed    the

purchase of a vehicle particulars whereof have been given in Schedule C of

the petition, by the Respondent No.1.</pre>

<p id="p_1">        An agreement was duly executed in terms whereof the respondent No.1

was required to make repayment in 59 instalments.

</p><p id="p_2">        It is submitted that the petitioner paid the first 21 instalments

and part of the 22nd        instalment and thereafter committed default.              The
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                2</span>


   petitioner    has     become   entitled   to      terminate    the   agreement   and     take

   possession of the vehicle in question.
</p>
<p id="p_3">             The petitioner has made out a strong prima facie case for interim

   relief.     If the vehicle is transferred and/or removed the petitioner will

   seriously be prejudiced.
</p>
<p id="p_4">             There will, therefore, be an order of injunction in terms of prayer

</p><p id="p_5">   (b) of the petition.
</p>
<p id="p_6">             Mr. Sougata Bhattacharya Advocate (Bar Association) is appointed

   Receiver over the vehicle in question.             The Receiver shall take symbolical

   possession of the vehicle, make an inventory thereof and submit a report to

   this Court within two weeks from the date of reopening after the puja

   vacation.    The Learned Receiver shall be entitled to police assistance if

   necessary.      The    respondent   shall       inform   the   Learned   Receiver   of    the

   whereabouts of the vehicle.
</p>
<p id="p_7">             The Learned Receiver shall be entitled to remuneration of 500 GMs.

   to be paid by the petitioner at the first instance.                  The petitioner shall

   make necessary arrangements for travel and accommodation of the Receiver in

   a manner commensurate with his status as a practising advocate of this

   court.
</p>
<p id="p_8">             Let the matter appear in the list three weeks after the vacation.

             Receiver, concerned authorities and all parties concerned are to act

   on a xerox signed copy of this order on the usual undertakings.




</p><p id="p_9">                                                                  (INDIRA BANERJEE, J.)




Kc.
</p><p id="p_10">R.O.(ct.)
