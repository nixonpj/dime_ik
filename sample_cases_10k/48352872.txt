<div class="doc_title">Balubhai Bhagabhai Patel vs State Of on 6 February, 2013</div><pre id="pre_1">  
	 
	 BALUBHAI BHAGABHAI PATEL....Applicant(s)V/SSTATE OF GUJARAT....Respondent(s)
	 
	 
	 
	 
	 
	 
	 
	 
	

 
 


	 


	R/CR.MA/1857/2013
	                                                                    
	                           ORDER

 
	  
	  
		 
			 

IN
			THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD
		
	

 


CRIMINAL MISC.APPLICATION (FOR REGULAR BAIL) NO. 1857 of 2013
 

===========================================================
 


BALUBHAI BHAGABHAI
PATEL....Applicant(s)
 


Versus
 


STATE OF
GUJARAT....Respondent(s)
 

================================================================
 

Appearance:
 

MR
PRANAV S DAVE, ADVOCATE for the Applicant(s) No. 1
 

MR
SHIVANG M SHAH, ADVOCATE for the Applicant(s) No. 1
 

Mr.Maulik
G. Nanavati, ADDL PUBLIC PROSECUTOR for the Respondent(s) No. 1
 

================================================================
 


	 
		  
		 
		  
			 
				 

CORAM:
				
				
			
			 
				 

HONOURABLE
				MR.JUSTICE ANANT S. DAVE
			
		
	

 


 

 


Date : 06/02/2013
 


 

 


ORAL ORDER</pre>
 


 


 


<p id="p_1">1.	Rule.
</p><p id="p_2">Learned Additional Public Prosecutor waives service of Rule on behalf
of respondent-State.
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_3">2.	Learned
advocate for the applicant has submitted an undertaking dated
2.2.2013 on behalf of the applicant, who is in jail since 10.7.2012
at Navsari, which is ordered to be taken on record.
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_4">3.	This
application is filed under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of the Code of Criminal
Procedure, 1973 for regular bail in connection with first information
report registered at M
Case No.2
of 1999 with Bilimora Police Station, Dist.Navsari for
the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_2">Sections 406</a>, <a href="/doc/1131784/" id="a_3">408</a>, <a href="/doc/1317063/" id="a_4">465</a>, <a href="/doc/1985627/" id="a_5">467</a>, <a href="/doc/556166/" id="a_6">468</a> and
<a href="/doc/722115/" id="a_7">477</a> (C) of Indian Penal Code. 
</p>
 


 


 


<p id="p_5">4.	Learned
advocate for the applicant submits that this application is filed
against the order passed by the learned Additional Chief Judicial
Magistrate, by which, a request for recalling the order of issuance
of arrest of warrant came to be dismissed on 11.7.2012 and against
which, an application for bail filed before the learned Sessions
Judge, also came to be rejected on 16.7.2012. The above orders were
passed, as the applicant failed to remain present before the
concerned Court when his presence was necessary. It is further
submitted that the applicant has explained the circumstances and in
addition to above, the applicant has filed an undertaking dated
2.2.2013 to abide by any orders or directions of the concerned Court
and to remain present unless so exempted by the Court. Considering
the above aspects, the applicant may be enlarged on bail, by imposing
suitable conditions. 
</p>
 


 


 


<p id="p_6">5.	Heard
learned APP for the respondent-State, who opposed grant of bail
looking to the nature and gravity of offence.
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_7">6.	Having
heard the learned advocates for the parties and considering the
overall facts and circumstances of the case and nature of
allegations, role attributed to the applicant and punishment
prescribed for the alleged offences, by imposing suitable conditions
so as to secure his presence at the stage of trial, the applicant is
ordered to be enlarged on bail.
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_8">7.	In
the facts and circumstances of the case, the application is allowed
and the applicant is ordered to be released on bail in connection
with first information report registered at M Case No.2
of 1999 with Bilimora Police Station, Dist.Navsari,
on executing a bond of Rs.10,000/- (Rupees Ten Thousand
only) with one surety of the like amount to the satisfaction of the
trial court and subject to the conditions that he shall;
</p> 


<p id="p_9">(a)
not take undue advantage of liberty or misuse liberty;
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_10">(b)
not act in a manner injurious to the interest of the prosecution; 
</p>
 


 


 
	  


<p id="p_11">	surrender
	passport, if any, to the lower Court within a week;
</p>	 


	

 
	  


<p id="p_12">	not
	leave the State of Gujarat without prior permission of the Sessions
	Judge concerned;
</p>
 


<p id="p_13">(e)
mark presence at the concerned police station on the first Sunday of
every month between 10.00 a.m. And 3.00 p.m. for three months only; 
</p>
 


 


 


<p id="p_14">(f)
furnish the present address of residence to the I.O. and also to the
Court at the time of execution of the bond and shall not change the
resident without prior permission of this Court;
</p> 


 


 
	 


<p id="p_15">	8.	The
	Authorities will release the applicant only if not required in
	connection with any other offence for the time being.
</p>	 


	

 
	 


<p id="p_16">	9.	If
	breach of any of the above conditions is committed, the Sessions
	Judge concerned will be free to issue warrant or take appropriate
	action in the matter.
</p>
 
	 


	
	 


<p id="p_17">	10.	Bail
	bond to be executed before the lower Court having jurisdiction to
	try the case.
</p>	 


	

 
	 


<p id="p_18">	11.	For
	modification and/or deletion of any of the conditions hereinabove,
	the applicant/s will be at liberty to approach the concerned Court
	and such Court shall decide the application for modification and/or
	deletion of any of the conditions of this order in accordance with
	law.
</p>	 


	

 
	 


<p id="p_19">	12.	At
	the trial, the trial Court shall not be influenced by the
	observations of preliminary nature, qua the evidence at this stage,
	made by this Court while enlarging the applicant on bail.
</p>	 


	

 


<p id="p_20">13.	Rule
is made absolute to the aforesaid extent. Direct Service is
permitted. 
</p>
 


 


 


<p id="p_21">(ANANT S.DAVE, J.) 

 

chandresh
