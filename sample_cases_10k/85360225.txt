<div class="doc_title">Radharani Biswas vs The State Of West Bengal &amp; Ors on 18 June, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                 1



                       IN THE HIGH COURT AT CALCUTTA
18-06-2018                   Constitutional Writ Jurisdiction
                                      Appellate Side
 Subrata
.
</pre><p id="p_1">                                 W.P.No.1353(W) of 2018
                                     Radharani Biswas
</p><p id="p_2">                                            -vs-
</p><p id="p_3">                              The State of West Bengal &amp; Ors.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                      Mr. Biswaroop Biswas             ...for the petitioner

                      Mr. Chandi Charan De
                      Mr. Anirban Sarkar                  ...for the State


                      The writ petitioner is aggrieved with the statutory
             arbitrator not having communicated the award in respect
             of the arbitration sought to be made under <a href="/doc/26881941/" id="a_1">section 3G(5)</a> of
             the National Highways Act, 1956.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                      Mr De, learned Additional Government Pleader,
             appears along with Mr Anirban Sarkar and submits that
             the award has already been passed; a copy whereof is
             furnished before the court which is taken on record.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                      Under the circumstances, the writ petition has
             become infructuous and is disposed of by the following
             order.   Since the writ petitioner has come to know only
             today that such an award has been passed but has not
             been served, the District Magistrate and Arbitrator, Nadia
             is hereby directed to serve a copy of the award on the
             petitioner in accordance with law, within ten days from the
             date of communication of this order.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                      I have decided nothing in this matter on merits.
             The writ petitioner shall have her statutory remedy, if she
             is dissatisfied with the award.      In case she accepts the
             award, let the amount of compensation awarded by the
             arbitrator be paid to her within three months from the date
             of communication of this order from acceptance of the
             award. No costs.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                      Certified website copy of this order, if applied for,
             shall be given to the parties.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> 2</span>

<p id="p_9">    [Protik Prakash Banerjee, J]
