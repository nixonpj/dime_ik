<div class="doc_title">Hardeep Kaur vs Surjit Singh &amp; Ors on 22 October, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">      IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA AT
                     CHANDIGARH
                                 FAO No.253 of 2017 (O&amp;M)
                                  Date of decision: 22.10.2018
Hardeep Kaur                         ... Appellant

                                      Versus

Surjit Singh and others                                     ... Respondents

CORAM: HON'BLE MRS. JUSTICE REKHA MITTAL

Present :       None for the claimant.

                Mr. Gopal Mittal, Advocate for the insurance company.

        ****
REKHA MITTAL, J. (Oral)
</pre><p id="p_1">                The     claimant     is   in    appeal    seeking    enhancement    of
compensation on account of death of Sukha Singh in a motor vehicular
accident that took place on 22.07.2015 when the deceased along with other
passengers had gone for paying obeisance at Gurudwara Shri Manikaran
Sahib.
</p><p id="p_2">                The Tribunal has awarded compensation of Rs.3,50,000/-,
detailed hereunder:-
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">                1. Annual income of the deceased                    Rs.15,000/-
</blockquote><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                2. Multiplier                                       15</span>
<blockquote id="blockquote_2">                3. Loss of dependency                               Rs.2,25,000/-
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">                4. Loss of love and affection                       Rs.1,00,000/-
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_4">                5. Funeral expenses                                 Rs.25,000/-</blockquote>
<p id="p_3">                When the case is examined in the light of judgment of this
Court Baljit Singh and another Vs. Devi Lal and others, FAO No.391 of
2003, decided on 17.01.2018, there is no justification for enhancement of
compensation. Accordingly, compensation assessed by the Tribunal is
affirmed.
</p><p id="p_4">                The appeal stands disposed of.
</p>

ashok                                                           JUDGE
