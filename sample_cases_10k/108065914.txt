<div class="doc_title">Jamil Khan vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 17 January, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                       M.Cr.C.No.224/2017
17.01.2017
             Shri A.K.Saraswat, learned Counsel for the applicant

Jamil Khan.

             Shri Milind Phadke, learned Govt. Advocate for the

non-applicant/State.

             Heard with the aid of the case-diary.

                             ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">             This is a First bail application of the applicant Jamil

Khan under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of the Cr.P.C for grant of bail in

connection with Crime No.733/2015 registered at Police Station

City Kotwali, District Mandsaur for the offence punishable

under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_2">Sections 302</a>, <a href="/doc/1128948/" id="a_3">120(B)</a> &amp; <a href="/doc/513074/" id="a_4">109</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_5">34</a> of the IPC and <a href="/doc/73862/" id="a_6">Sections

25</a>, <a href="/doc/244673/" id="a_7">27</a> of the Arms Act.
</p>
<p id="p_2">             Learned Counsel for the applicant submits that the

applicant has been implicated in this case on the basis of

memorandum of co-accused Ajamlala, however, no recovery

has been made from the possession of the applicant. Even as per

the prosecution case, at the place of occurrence applicant was

not present. The applicant has been falsely implicated in this

case. He is detained since 30.12.2016; whereas the trial will take

considerable time to conclude. In such circumstances, he be
 released on bail.
</p>
<p id="p_3">            On the other hand, learned Govt. Advocate opposes

the prayer and submits that there is evidence that the applicant

has hatched conspiracy with other co-accused persons, hence he

is equally liable for the offence. Hence, he prays for dismissal of

application.
</p>
<p id="p_4">            Considering overall facts and circumstances of the

case and the role attributed to the present applicant, I am of the

considered view that the applicant has made out a case for

granting bail. Thus, the application is allowed and it is directed

that applicant Jamil Khan be released on bail upon his furnishing

personal bond in the sum of Rs.50,000/- (Rs. Fifty Thousand)

with two local solvent sureties in the like amount to the

satisfaction of the Trial Court for his appearance as and when

directed.
</p>
<p id="p_5">            The applicant Jamil Khan is directed to attend each

hearing of his trial before the Trial Court out of which this bail

arises. Any default in attendance in Court would result in

cancellation of the bail granted by this Court.

</p><p id="p_6">            Certified copy as per rules.</p><pre id="pre_2">


                                    (JARAT KUMAR JAIN)
ns                                         JUDGE
