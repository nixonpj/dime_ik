             SPECIAL CIVIL APPLICATION NO. 844 of 2015
                                     With
              SPECIAL CIVIL APPLICATION NO. 845 of 2015
                                       TO
            SPECIAL CIVIL APPLICATION NO. 1065 of 2015
================================================================
           BALWANTSINH HARISINH GOHIL - 199....Petitioner(s)
                             Versus
               STATE OF GUJARAT &amp; 2....Respondent(s)
================================================================
Appearance:
MR KAUSHAL D PANDYA, ADVOCATE for the Petitioner(s) No. 1
Ms.Shruti Pathak, learned ASST.GOVERNMENT PLEADER for the
Respondent(s) No. 1
MR MAULIK G NANAVATI, ADVOCATE for the Respondent(s) No.2- 3
================================================================

         CORAM: HONOURABLE SMT. JUSTICE ABHILASHA
                KUMARI

                                Date : 03/03/2015


                           COMMON ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">1.   These are one-page petitions,                       preferred by the

petitioners pursuant to the order dated 24-11-2014

passed   by     this      Court     in    Special     Civil     Application

No.17025     of    2014     (herein-after           referred     to     as   the

composite petition). The petitioners in the present

petitions      were       all    petitioners        in    the      composite

petition. However, pursuant to the above-stated order,

they have filed separate, one page petitions, that


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                    Page 1 of 3</span>
           C/SCA/844/2015                                     ORDER



have come on board after the composite petition has

been finally disposed of                   by judgment dated 2-12-

2014,as they were under office objections.



</p><p id="p_2">2.    The    petitioners      of     the        present   petitions   have

stated that the challenge in these petitions and the

prayers made herein, are the same as those made in the

composite petition; hence the petitioners accept and

adopt the averments made             in the composite petition.



</p><p id="p_3">3.    Heard Mr.Kaushal D.Pandya, learned advocate for

the   petitioners,         Ms.Shruti       Pathak,learned       Assistant

Government Pleader for respondent No.1 and Mr.Maulik

G.Nanavati, learned advocate for respondents Nos.2 and

</p><p id="p_4">3.



</p><p id="p_5">4.    It is submitted by the learned advocate for the

petitioners that the composite petition, being Special

Civil Application No.17025 of 2014, has been finally

decided       by       judgment     dated         2-12-2014.     As   the

petitioners were also parties to the said petition,

the order disposing of the composite petition would

apply in the present petitions, as well.




<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                  Page 2 of 3</span>
</p><pre id="pre_2">           C/SCA/844/2015                                         ORDER



5.    Ms.Shruti            Pathak,   learned         Assistant    Government

</pre><p id="p_6">Pleader for respondent No.1 and Mr.Maulik G.Nanavati,

learned advocate for respondents Nos.2 and 3 have also

reiterated the same position.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><p id="p_8">6.    In view of the fact that composite petition to

which the petitioners were parties has already been

decided     by        judgment       dated         2-12-2014,    the     present

petitions are disposed of,                   for reasons stated in the

judgment      dated         2-12-2014       passed      in   Special      Civil

Application No.17025 of 2014 and connected matters,

which would cover them in all respects.



</p><p id="p_9">      In view of the above, no separate orders are

required to be passed in the petitions.



</p><p id="p_10">                                                   (SMT. ABHILASHA KUMARI, J.)
