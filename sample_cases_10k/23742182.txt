THIS WRIT PETITION (CIVIL) HAVING COME UP FOR ADMISSION ON
07.01.2019, THE COURT ON THE SAME DAY DELIVERED THE FOLLOWING:
 WP(C).No. 42649 of 2018               3

                                   JUDGMENT
</pre><p id="p_1">                  Petitioner       is          the   Chairman          of   a     trust

        functioning in the name and style of Payyakkottu

        Trust,    a     registered     trust.             For    the    purpose       of

        construction       of   mini       auditorium           adjacent        to    the

        temple, ie, Payyakkottu family temple, a portion of

        the property has to be levelled by removing surface

        red earth and thereupon, secured Ext.P2 building

        permit and Ext.P3 development permit from the 6th

        respondent.             According            to     the         petitioner,

        petitioner submitted an application before the 7th

        respondent along with all requisite documents for

        getting transit pass, but the 7th respondent is not

        considering the application.                       That apart, it is

        submitted that, as per Rule 14(2) of Kerala Minor

        Mineral       Concessions         Rules,       2015,       no       quarrying

        permit is required for extraction of ordinary earth

        for   the       construction           of    residential            buildings

        including flats and commercial building below 300

        square    meters.        It       is    also      submitted         that      the

        petitioner       has    submitted            an    application,              even

        though, the same is not produced along with the

        writ petition.
</p>
<p id="p_2">        2.          I    have   heard          learned      counsel         for       the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> WP(C).No. 42649 of 2018                  4</span>

        petitioner          and   learned           Government            Pleader        and

        perused the pleadings and the documents on record.

</p><p id="p_3">        3.         Learned counsel for the petitioner reiterated

        the    contentions         raised        in       the       writ        petition.

        Learned      Government        Pleader        pointed         out       that     the

        construction         sought      to     be     carried            out     by     the

        petitioner is an auditorium and the same will not

        come under Rule 14(2) of the Rules specified above

        and a proper application will have to be submitted

        under Rule 106 of the Rules.
</p>
<p id="p_4">        4.           Taking into account respective submissions

        made across the Bar, there will be a direction to

        the    7th       respondent      to    consider             the    application

        submitted by the petitioner in accordance with law

        at the earliest, and at any rate, within one month

        from       the    date    of     receipt       of       a    copy        of    this

        judgment.
</p>
<p id="p_5">        5.         I make it clear that, if there is any defect

        in    the    application         submitted         by       the    petitioner,

        appropriate notice shall be sent to the petitioner

        so    as     to    cure   the        defect       and       the    time        limit

        prescribed above will depend upon the circumstances

        under which the application is submitted by the

        petitioner.           I   also       make    it    clear          that    the    7th
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2"> WP(C).No. 42649 of 2018       5</span>

        respondent shall make a visit to the property and

        then consider the application in accordance with

        the provisions of the law.
</p>
<p id="p_6">              The writ petition is disposed of, accordingly.




</p><p id="p_7">                                                      Sd/-
