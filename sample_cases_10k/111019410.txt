<div class="doc_title">Amar Brahma vs The Bodoland Territorial Council ... on 23 December, 2016</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: Ajit Singh, Manojit Bhuyan</div>

<pre id="pre_1">                                                     PIL 78/2016

                      BEFORE
      HON'BLE THE CHIEF JUSTICE MR. AJIT SINGH
        HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE MANOJIT BHUYAN

23.12.2016
(Ajit Singh, CJ)

       Mr. M.K. Choudhury, learned senior counsel assisted by
Mr. S. Baruah, learned counsel for the petitioner.

       Ms. B. Bhuyan, learned Standing Counsel, Bodoland
Territorial Council and Mr. H Baruah, learned Standing Counsel,
Pollution Control Board for the respondents.</pre>

<p id="p_1">       Respondent No.3 purchased the land in question at
village Kukurmari in an auction sale held as far back as on
26.2.2008 under Debts Recovery Tribunal, Assam. The sale
certificate was then issued by the Recovery Officer of Debts
Recovery Tribunal on 24.3.2008. Thereafter, vide order dated
3.1.2009 passed by the Circle Officer, Sidli Revenue Circle,
mutation was also done in favour of respondent No.3. Not only
this, after obtaining required statutory permissions from
different departments, respondent No.3 has set up an industry
on the said land. And in the present Public Interest Litigation
the petitioner has challenged the very sale of land in favour of
respondent No.3.
</p>
<p id="p_2">       After hearing the learned counsel for the petitioner, we
are convinced that this is not a genuine Public Interest
Litigation. As mentioned above, the land was purchased by
respondent No.3 in an auction sale in the year 2008.
</p>
<p id="p_3">       The petition is accordingly dismissed summarily.
</p>


<pre id="pre_2">       JUDGE                         CHIEF JUSTICE
