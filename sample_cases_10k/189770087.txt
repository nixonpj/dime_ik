<div class="doc_title">Virendra Singh vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 30 September, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                                  1
                                            M.Cr.C.No.10017/2015

       (<a href="/doc/119329886/" id="a_1">Virendra Singh &amp; Others v. State of MP</a>)
30.09.2015
         Shri Raja Girraj Sharma, Advocate for the applicants.
         Shri R.P.Gupta, Public Prosecutor for the respondent/</pre>
<p id="p_1">State.
</p><p id="p_2">         Case Diary is perused.
</p><p id="p_3">         Learned counsel for the rival parties are heard.
         Applicants apprehend arrest in connection with offences
punishable u/Ss. 294, 323, 452, 506, 427, 34, 326 of <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_2">IPC</a>
registered     as    Crime   No.172/2015      at   Police   Station
Shamshabad, District Vidisha.
</p><p id="p_4">         Learned Public Prosecutor for the State opposed the
application and prayed for its rejection by contending that on
the basis of the allegations and the material available on
record, no case for grant of anticipatory bail is made out.
</p><p id="p_5">         Applicants apprehend arrest in respect of aforesaid
offences. The allegation against the applicants No.1 Virendra
Singh and No.3 Bhaiya Maina, respectively, is of presence and
wielding Lohangi and causing assault which has led to minor
injury. The allegation against the applicant No.2 Golu is of
wielding Pharsa which has led to fracture of frontal bone
which has given rise to offence punishable u/S.326 of <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_3">IPC</a>. It
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                  2</span>
                                              M.Cr.C.No.10017/2015

appears that the applicant No.2 Golu is the main accused
therefore his anticipatory bail application is rejected.
</p><p id="p_6">      In view of the above, this Court is though inclined to
extend the benefit of bail to the applicants No.1 Virendra
Singh and No.3 Bhaiya Maina but with certain stringent
conditions in view of nature of offence and pending
investigation.
</p><p id="p_7">      Accordingly, without expressing opinion on merits of the
case, I deem it appropriate to allow this application u/S 438
<a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_4">Cr.P.C</a> in the following terms.
</p><p id="p_8">      It is hereby directed that in the event of arrest,          the
applicants No.1 Virendra Singh and No.3 Bhaiya Maina shall be
released   on    bail   on   furnishing   a    personal    bond    of
Rs.1,00,000/-(Rupees One Lac only) each with two
solvent sureties each of Rs.50,000/- in case of each of the
applicants to the satisfaction of Arresting Authority.
</p><p id="p_9">      This order will remain operative subject to compliance of
the following conditions by the applicants :-
</p><p id="p_10">   1. The applicants will comply with all the terms and
      conditions of the bond executed by them;
</p><p id="p_11">   2. The applicants will cooperate in the investigation/trial,
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                            3</span>
                                                     M.Cr.C.No.10017/2015

                  as the case may be;
</p><p id="p_12">               3. The applicants will not indulge themselves in extending
                  inducement, threat or promise to any person acquainted
                  with the facts of the case so as to dissuade them from
                  disclosing such facts to the Court or to the Police
                  Officer, as the case may be;
</p><p id="p_13">               4. The applicants shall not commit an offence similar to the
                  offence of which they are accused;
</p><p id="p_14">               5. The applicants will not seek unnecessary adjournments
                  during the trial; and
</p><p id="p_15">               6. The applicants will not leave India without previous
                  permission of the trial Court/Investigating Officer, as the
                  case may be.
</p><p id="p_16">               7. Till conclusion of investigation, the applicants will mark
                  their attendance at the concerned Police Station once
                  every week.
</p><p id="p_17">                  A copy of this order be sent to the Court concerned for
             compliance.
</p><p id="p_18">                  C.c. as per rules.
</p>
<p id="p_19">                                                         (Sheel Nagu)
                                                           Judge
ar/   AK/-
</p>
