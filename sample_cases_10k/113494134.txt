<div class="doc_title">Subhash Chandra Majumdar vs Unknown on 12 April, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                                              1


    59
12.04.2017</pre>
<p id="p_1">    rrc
 (Rejected)

                                          C.R.M. 2620 of 2017
              In Re:- An application for anticipatory bail under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> of the
              Code of Criminal Procedure affirmed on 29.03.2017.

              In the matter of : Subhash Chandra Majumdar
                                                       ...petitioner

                       Mr. Atis Kumar Biswas
                                                         ...For the petitioner
                       Mr. Aniket Mitra
                                                        .....For the State

                       Apprehending arrest in course of investigation of Tehatta Police

              Station F.I.R. No. 26 of 2017 dated 21st January, 2017 under <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_2">Sections

              406</a>/<a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_3">420</a>/<a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_4">323</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_5">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code, the petitioner presented

              this application on 30th March, 2017 for a direction that in the event of

              his arrest, he may be released on bail.

</p><p id="p_2">                       We have heard learned advocates for the parties and perused the

              materials in the case diary, more particularly, the statements of the

              available witnesses recorded under <a href="/doc/447673/" id="a_6">Section 161</a> of the Code of Criminal

              Procedure, at pages - 114 and 116 thereof.

</p><p id="p_3">                       On such perusal, we find that the complicity of the petitioner

              cannot be totally ruled out and that his custodial interrogation is

              necessary for effective and meaningful progress of investigation of the

              F.I.R.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                       C.R.M. 2620 of 2017 stands rejected.


</p><p id="p_5">                                                           (Dipankar Datta, J.)

                                                           (Debi Prosad Dey, J.)
