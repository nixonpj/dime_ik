           IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

                  F/FIRST APPEAL NO.  15019 of 2018
                                With 
                   CIVIL APPLICATION NO. 2 of 2018
==========================================================
</pre><p id="p_1">              RELIANCE GERERAL INSURANCE CO. LTD.
</p><p id="p_2">                             Versus
          KARSHANBHAI VASHRAMBHAI DHADHODARA (FATHER)
==========================================================
Appearance:
</p><p id="p_3">MR RATHIN P RAVAL(5013) for the PETITIONER(s) No. 1
 for the RESPONDENT(s) No. 3
MR RAJESH O GIDIYA(5222) for the RESPONDENT(s) No. 1,2
==========================================================

    CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE R.M.CHHAYA
 
                            Date : 10/07/2018
 
                               ORAL ORDER


</p><p id="p_4">     1. The   present   appeal   is   preferred   by   the 
       appellant­Insurance  Company   challenging   interim 
       award   dated   01.02.2016   passed   below   Exh.5,   in 
       Motor Accident Claim Petition No.15/2015, by the 
       Motor Accident Claims Tribunal (Main), Bhavnagar 
       granting   interim   compensation   of   Rs.25,000/­ 
       under   Section   140   of   the   Motor   Vehicles   Act, 
       1988 (for short 'the Act').
</p>
<p id="p_5">     2. Heard   Mr.   Rathin   P.   Raval,   learned   counsel   for 
       the   appellant-Insurance   Company,   Mr.Rajesh   O. 
       Gidiya,   learned   advocate   for   respondents   no.1 
       and   2   and   Mr.   Nirav   Sanghvi,   learned   advocate 
       for respondent no.3.
</p>
<p id="p_6">     3. Learned   counsel   for   the   appellant   has,  inter 
       alia,   contended   that   the   appellant   insurance 
       company   is   not   at   all   liable   to   satisfy   any 


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                 Page 1 of 3</span>
      C/FA/15019/2018                           ORDER




  award   as   the   insurance   policy   was   an   "Act 
  only/Liability only Policy and that the claimant 
  was   travelling   as   an   unauthorised   passenger. 
  It   was   therefore   contended   that   the   learned 
  Tribunal   could   not   have   passed   the   impugned 
  award fastening the liability upon the Insurance 
  Company. 
</p>
<p id="p_7">4. Taking   into   consideration   the   above   contention 
  raised by learned counsel for the appellant, it 
  deserves   to   be   appreciated   that   after   evidence 
  on record, however, since this is interim award 
  passed   under   Section   140   of   the   Act, 
  consequently  therefore,   the   rival   parties   have 
  not   got   any   liberty   of   adducing  and   producing 
  evidence.   Therefore,   this   issue   raised   by   the 
  learned   counsel   for   the   appellant   can   only   be 
  adjudicated at the stage of full fledged trial. 
  This   issue   cannot   be   decided   at   the   interim 
  stage   and   taking   into   consideration   the 
  intention of legislature for interim award, the 
  appellant   is   at   liberty   to   raise   such   issue 
  while defending the main claim petition and the 
  Tribunal shall decide the same on its own merits 
  without   being   influenced   by   the   decision 
  rendered   by   the   Tribunal   itself   while 
  considering the application under Section 140 of 
  the   MV   Act   and   without   being   treating   as  res 
  judicata.
</p>
<p id="p_8">5. Since   this   is   an   appeal   against   the   interim 
  award and main claim petition is pending before 


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                         Page 2 of 3</span>
          C/FA/15019/2018                             ORDER




       the learned Tribunal, it appears just and proper 
       to dispose of this appeal at the admission stage 
       with   a   direction   to   the   learned   Tribunal   to 
       decide the main claim petition, as expeditiously 
       as   possible,   preferably   within   a   period   of  six 
       months from the date of receipt of this order.
</p>
<p id="p_9">   6. While   considering   the   main   claim   petition,   the 
       learned   Tribunal   shall   afford   reasonable 
       opportunity   to   the   rival   parties   to   proceed   so 
       far as the controversy raised in this matter is 
       concerned and the learned Tribunal shall decide 
       the   said   issue   without   being   influenced   by   any 
       of the observations made by this Court at this 
       stage.
</p>
<p id="p_10">   7. With   these   directions,   present   appeal   stands 
       disposed of. As the main appeal is disposed of, 
       no   order   is   required   to   be   passed   in   Civil 
       Application   and   consequently,   the   same   stands 
       disposed of.
</p>
<p id="p_11">   8. Registry   may   permit   Mr.   Nirav   Sanghvi   to   file 
       vakalatnama for respondent no.3


                                                (R.M.CHHAYA, J)
