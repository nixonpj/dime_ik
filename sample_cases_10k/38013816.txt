<div class="doc_title">V.Mehalingam vs The Commissioner Of Police on 11 December, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">        

 

BEFORE THE MADURAI BENCH OF MADRAS HIGH COURT               

DATED: 11.12.2017  

CORAM   
THE HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE R.MAHADEVAN             

W.P(MD)No.19157 of 2017   
and 
W.M.P(MD)No.15550 of 2017   

V.Mehalingam                                    ..  Petitioner

Vs.

1.The Commissioner of Police, 
   Tirunelveli City, Tirunelveli.

2.The Assistant Commissioner of Police,
   (Law and Order), Palayamkottai,
   Tirunelveli Town.

3.The Assistant Commissioner of Police,
   Intelligence Section, Tirunelveli.                   ..  Respondents

PRAYER: Writ Petition is filed under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the Constitution of India
praying for the issuance of a Writ of Mandamus directing the first respondent
to renew the petitioner's crackers license for storing and selling crackers
at No.77A, Swamy Sannathi Street,  Tirunelveli Town.

!For Petitioner                 : Mr.R.Subramanian 

For Respondents         : Mr.T.S.Mohammed Mohideen,    
                                  Additional Government Pleader.


:ORDER  
</pre><p id="p_1">        This writ petition is filed seeking a writ of Mandamus to direct the
first respondent to renew the petitioner's crackers shop license for storing
and selling crackers at No.77A, Swamy Sannathi Street,  Tirunelveli Town.
</p>
<p id="p_2">        2.Heard both sides.
</p>
<p id="p_3">        3.According to the petitioner, he has been given licence for possessing
and selling fireworks at his shop by the authorities in the previous years
and on its expiry, the petitioner has submitted the application seeking
renewal of licence, however, the same has not been considered so far.
Therefore, the petitioner is before this Court.
</p>
<p id="p_4">        4.The learned Additional Government Pleader appearing for the
respondents would submit that subsequent to the filing of the writ petition,
the renewal application came to be rejected on the ground that there are no
separate exist for the shop.
</p>
<p id="p_5">        5.The learned counsel appearing for the petitioner, on the other hand,
submitted that the deficiencies had been rectified and now there is a
provision for entry and therefore his case may be considered, but, the
authorities have not inspected the shop so far, despite repeated
representations.
</p>

<p id="p_6">        6.Under such circumstances, the petitioner is directed to resubmit his
renewal application to the first respondent, within a period of one week from
the date of receipt of a copy of this order and on such submission, the first
respondent is directed to consider the same and pass appropriate orders on
its own merit and in accordance with law, after affording due opportunity of
hearing to the petitioner so as to produce the required certificates obtained
from the authorities concerned, on or before 20.01.2018.  Inspection of the
shop also be completed in the meanwhile.  It is directed that the interim
order, dated 12.10.2017, with regard to temporary licence, is extended, till
then.
</p>
<p id="p_7">        7.The writ petition is disposed of as above. No costs.  Consequently,
connected Miscellaneous Petition is closed.     
</p>                                                                        

<p id="p_8">To

</p><p id="p_9">1.The Commissioner of Police, 
   Tirunelveli City, Tirunelveli.
</p>
<p id="p_10">2.The Assistant Commissioner of Police,
   (Law and Order), Palayamkottai,
   Tirunelveli Town.
</p>
<p id="p_11">3.The Assistant Commissioner of Police,
   Intelligence Section, Tirunelveli.
