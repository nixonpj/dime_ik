<pre id="pre_1">       R/CR.MA/17087/2017                            ORDER




         IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

         R/CRIMINAL MISC.APPLICATION NO. 17087 of 2017

==========================================================
               MOGAL MUSTAQ MAHAMMAD HAYATKHAN
                             Versus
                       STATE OF GUJARAT
==========================================================
Appearance:
HARSH K RAVAL(9068) for the PETITIONER(s) No. 1
MR HS SONI, ADDL.PUBLIC PROSECUTOR(2) for the RESPONDENT(s)
No. 1
==========================================================

 CORAM: HONOURABLE MS JUSTICE SONIA GOKANI

                            Date : 23/04/2018

                             ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">  1. This   is   an   application   seeking   leave   to   appeal 

     under   section   378(4)   of   the   Code   of   Criminal 

     Procedure   against   the   judgment   and   order   dated 

     19.07.2014   passed   by   the   learned   2nd  Additional 

     Chief   Judicial   Magistrate,   Mehsana   in   Criminal 

     Case No.4678 of 2013. 
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">  2. It   is   the   case   of   the   complainant   before   the 

     learned 2nd Additional Chief Judicial Magistrate, 

     Mehsana   that   the   applicant   preferred   Criminal 

     Case No.2131 of 2012 for dishonour of a cheque. 

     where   settlement   had   been   arrived   at   and   the 


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                Page 1 of 3</span>
     R/CR.MA/17087/2017                           ORDER



  cheque  of Rs.  1 lakhs  bearing  No.173933  of the 

  Ranuj   Nagarik   Sahakari   Bank   Limited   of   Mal 

  Godown   Road,   Mehsana   dated   21.03.2013.     After 

  the   settlement   since   again   the   said   cheque   was 

  dishonoured fresh Criminal Case No.4678 of 2013 

  came to be preferred. 
</p>
<p id="p_4">
</p><p id="p_5">3. It is lamented by learned advocate appearing for 

  the   applicant   that   the   Court   has   committed 

  serious   error   in   overlooking   strong   factual 

  matrix,   which   had   emerged   in   favour   of   the 

  applicant.   It   is   further   urged   that   legal 

  presumption also has been overlooked of sections 

  118 and 139 of the N.I. Act. 
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><p id="p_7">4. Learned   Additional   Public   Prosecutor   for 

  respondent   No.1   has   supported   the   case   of   the 

  applicant. 
</p>
<p id="p_8">

</p><p id="p_9">5. Having   thus   heard   learned   advocate   for   the 

  applicant   and   learned   Additional   Public 

  Prosecutor   for   the   State,   the   Court   finds 

  sufficient   grounds   to   permit   this   application 


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                         Page 2 of 3</span>
           R/CR.MA/17087/2017                           ORDER



         for leave to prefer appeal. 
</p>
<p id="p_10">
</p><p id="p_11">   6. Accordingly, the application is allowed.




</p><p id="p_12">                                             MS. SONIA GOKANI, J. )
SUDHIR
