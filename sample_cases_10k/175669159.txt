<div class="doc_title">Narayan Prasad Sharma vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 20 June, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">                   W.P. No.8791/2014
  (<a href="/doc/1192622/" id="a_1">Narayan Prasad Sharma v. The State of M.P.
                           &amp; Ors</a>.)
20.06.2014
     Shri Mohan Lal Sharma, Advocate for the
petitioner.
     Shri S.P. Rai, Panel Lawyer for respondents-</pre>
<p id="p_1">State on advance copy.
</p><p id="p_2">     Heard on the question of admission.
     The petitioner has filed this petition being
aggrieved by the fact that the petitioner has not
been paid arrears of subsistence allowance and
regular pay during suspension in spite of the fact
that he has been acquitted by the competent
Criminal Court.
</p><p id="p_3">     It       is     submitted        that      petitioner's
representation dated 03.12.2013 has also not
yielded any result, hence this petition.
</p><p id="p_4">     Counsel       for    the   petitioner   submits     that
respondents-authorities be directed to consider
and decide the representation of the petitioner.
</p><p id="p_5">     Counsel for the respondents submits that the
representation       of     the    petitioner    shall    be
considered         and     decided     expeditiously       in
accordance with law.
</p><p id="p_6">             In view of the limited prayer made by the
     Counsel for petitioner, the petition filed by the
     petitioner is disposed of with a direction to the
     respondents-authorities to consider and decide the
     representation of the petitioner in accordance with
     law.
</p><p id="p_7">            However, it is made clear that this Court has
     not expressed any opinion on the merits of the
     case and therefore the authority would be at
     liberty to examine the matter keeping all facts and
     facets into consideration and thereafter either
     accept or reject the representation by passing a
     reasoned order.
</p><p id="p_8">            With the aforesaid direction, the petition filed
     by the petitioner stands disposed of.
</p><p id="p_9">            Certified copy as per rules.
</p>
<p id="p_10">                                               (R.S.Jha)
                                                Judge
SS
