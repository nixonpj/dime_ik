                                IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT MADRAS

                                                   DATED : 05.12.2018

                                                        CORAM

                               THE HONOURABLE Mr. JUSTICE S.M.SUBRAMANIAM

                                                  WMP.No.17106 of 2018


                     Mr.Thirupathy                                                 Petitioner

                                                                Vs

                     1. The District Collector,
                        Dharmapuri,
                        Dharmapuri District.

                     2. The Tashildar,
                        Karimangalam Taluk,
                        Dharmapuri District.

                     3. Power Grid Corporation of India Ltd,
                        Tharamangalam Road,
                        Rep by Assistant General Manager,
                        K.R.Thoppur,

                     PRAYER: Writ Petition filed under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the Constitution of India, to
                     issue a Writ of Certiorarified Mandamus, calling for the records of the third
                     respondent in communication/Reply Notice dated 14.12.2017 vide reference
                     No.SR-II/HVDC/TPR/TLC/2017 and quash the same and consequently
                     direct the third respondent to consider the petitioner representation dated
                     21.11.2017 in accordance with law.

                                      For Petitioner   : Mr.R.Krishnan
                                      For Respondents : M/s.Janaki,

                                                        Mr.R.Thiyagarajan, Senior Counsel
                                                            for M/s.Aiyar and Dolia for R3

                                                     ORDER
</pre><p id="p_1">                             The order of rejection dated 14.12.2017 issued by the Assistant

                     General Manager, Power Grid Corporation of India Ltd / 3rd respondent is

                     under challenge in this present writ petition.



</p><p id="p_2">                            2. The learned counsel for the petitioner states that the petitioner is

                     residing at Uchampatti Village, Thindal Post, Karimangalam Taluk,

                     Dharmapuri District. The petitioner is the absolute owner of the said

                     property, wherein he resides. The officials of the respondents informed the

                     writ petitioner that they are going to commence Central Power Project for the

                     purpose of laying transmission lines from Raigarh(Chattisgarh) to Pugalur

                     (State of Tamil Nadu),



</p><p id="p_3">                            3. The grievances of the writ petitioner is that laying of transmission

                     line falls above his building which causes hindrance for the development of

                     his own property. Thus, the writ petitioner is constrained move the present

                     writ petition.
</p>
<p id="p_4">

http://www.judis.nic.in
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                              3</span>

</p><p id="p_5">                     4. The learned counsel for the petitioner further states that in the event of

                     laying such high voltage transmission lines over and above the Pakka

                     building constructed by the writ petitioner, the rights of the writ petitioner will

                     be affected. This apart, the writ petitioner has not given any consent for

                     laying any such Transmission line above his building.



</p><p id="p_6">                           5. The learned senior counsel appearing on behalf of the 3rd

                     respondent disputed the contentions raised on behalf of the writ petitioner by

                     stating that the transmission lines are proposed to be laid for the length of

                     about 1843 Kilometres from Raigarh(Chattisgarh) to Pugalur (State of Tamil

                     Nadu), out of which, 345 Kilometres are falling within the territorial

                     Jurisdiction of the State of Tamil Nadu. The learned Senior Counsel further

                     informed that the major beneficiaries are the people of the State of Tamil

                     Nadu and therefore, maximum care has been taken to give protection to all

                     concerned and to utilise the power supply for the benefit and the Welfare of

                     the State at large. Further, it is urged that the petitioners building is not at all

                     falling in the line and the building belongs to the petitioner situates adjacent

                     to the proposed laying of transmission lines. This apart, adequate safety

                     measures are taken to protect the interest of all the people, who all are

                     residing in that locality. The experts, while submitting proposals had taken

                     care of the inconvenience, if any caused to the local people and avoided all


http://www.judis.nic.in
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                              4</span>

                     such inconvenience to the maximum possible. The transmission line is

                     proposed by adopting the techno economically feasible considerations. The

                     project is being implemented only on the expert opinion and by the persons,

                     who all are well versed in this field.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><p id="p_8">                           6. Thus, an assurance is made before this Court that adequate

                     measures are already taken to protect the interest of the people, who all are

                     reside in the particular locality, wherein the transmission lines are laid and

                     therefore the order of rejection is in accordance with the procedures as

                     contemplated and the rules in force.
</p>
<p id="p_9">

</p><p id="p_10">                           7. This Court is of an opinion that undoubtedly, it is a Welfare project

                     implemented for the benefit of the people at large. Public interest is of

                     paramount importance and a policy decisions taking, if it is otherwise

                     unconstitutional, can never be interfered with by the Courts. When the

                     learned Senior Counsel appearing on behalf of the 3rd respondent informed

                     this Court that all latest technological aspects are being followed by the

                     experts for the purpose of laying transmission lines in that particular area,

                     there cannot be any inconvenience to the people of that locality. Maximum

                     care has been taken to protect the interest of all concerned. This apart, the

                     policy decision is taken in order to provide Electricity power supply to the


http://www.judis.nic.in
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                                                            5</span>

                     larger extent for the benefit of the State of Tamil Nadu.



</p><p id="p_11">                           8. Under these circumstances, this Court is of an opinion that the

                     decision taken in this regard is to be construed as a policy decision and the

                     Court cannot interfere unless the petitioner is able to establish that such a

                     decision is perverse or unconstitutional.



</p><p id="p_12">                           9. The map submitted by the respondents also establishes that the

                     building belongs to the writ petitioner is not falling under the transmission

                     lines laid by the Grid Corporation. This being the factum of the case, the writ

                     petitioner cannot be construed as an aggrieved person. If at all, the

                     petitioner is aggrieved and the authorities is of an opinion that if any

                     damages caused to the property of the writ petitioner, then suitable

                     compensation can be claimed as per the rules in force.



</p><p id="p_13">                           10. Thus, the writ petitioner has not established any acceptable

                     ground for the purpose of considering the relief as such sought for in the

                     present writ petition and the order impugned is not in firm.




http://www.judis.nic.in
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                                                           6</span>

</p><p id="p_14">                           11. Accordingly, the writ petition stands dismissed. However, there

                     shall be no order as to costs. Consequently, connected miscellaneous

                     petition is also closed.
</p>
