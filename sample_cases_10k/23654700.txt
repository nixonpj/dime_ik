<p id="p_1">             This criminal revision petition has been filed under <a href="/doc/1571667/" id="a_1">Section 401</a>

Cr.P.C. challenging the impugned judgment dated 12.4.2016 passed by

learned Additional Sessions Judge, Sri Muktsar Sahib, whereby the appeal

filed against the judgment of conviction and order of sentence dated

22.9.2015 passed by learned Judicial Magistrate Ist Class, Malout,

convicting and sentencing the petitioners for the offences under <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_2">Sections

323</a>, <a href="/doc/838469/" id="a_3">452</a>, <a href="/doc/763672/" id="a_4">148</a> and <a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_5">149</a> IPC and sentencing them to undergo rigorous

imprisonment for one year each and to pay a fine of `500/- each and in

default of payment of fine to further undergo rigorous imprisonment for one

month each for the offence under 452 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_6">IPC</a> and also sentencing them under

other Sections of <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_7">IPC</a>, has been dismissed.

</p><p id="p_2">             At the time of preliminary hearing, learned counsel for the

petitioner did not dispute the concurrent findings of the Courts below

regarding conviction and only contended on the point of reduction of

sentence.
</p>
<p id="p_3">             Therefore, notice of motion was issued qua quantum of

sentence only.
</p>
<p id="p_4">             Ms. Shivali, learned Assistant Advocate General, Punjab has

put in appearance on behalf of the respondent-State and contested this

criminal revision petition.
</p>

Judicial Magistrate Ist Class, Malout in his judgment dated 22.9.2015 are as

under:-

</p><p id="p_7">      Learned Judicial Magistrate Ist Class, Malout, after appreciating the

evidence, convicted and sentenced the petitioners as stated above. An

appeal was filed by the petitioners and the same was dismissed by learned

Additional Sessions Judge, Sri Muktsar Sahib vide judgment dated

12.4.2016.
</p>
<p id="p_8">             Aggrieved from the above-said judgments, present revision

petition has been filed.
</p>
<p id="p_9">             Learned counsel for the petitioners argued that the petitioners

were young persons at the time of commission of the offences. Petitioners

Tikka Singh, Pala Singh and Bhola Singh were young persons between the

age of 25 to 28 years age and petitioner Lal Singh was aged about 48 years.

They are suffering from the long protracted criminal proceedings for the last

more than four years. He also argued that only simple injuries are attributed

to the petitioners. They are poor persons and only bread earners of their

families. They have already undergone imprisonment of one month and 13

days each out of the total sentence.
</p>
<p id="p_10">             I have heard learned counsel for the petitioners as well as

learned State counsel and have gone through the record.



</p><p id="p_11">                                   4 of 5


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                ::: Downloaded on - 03-06-2016 23:58:48 :::</span>
                                          Cr. Revision No.1744 of 2016 (O&amp;M)
                                        [5]

             Keeping in view the facts and circumstances of the present case

and in view of the fact that petitioners are only bread earner of their families

and are suffering from long protracted criminal proceedings for the last

more than four years and in view of the fact the petitioners have already

undergone imprisonment of 1 month and 13 days each out of the total

sentence, the sentence imposed upon the petitioners is reduced and they are

directed to undergo rigorous imprisonment for three months each instead of

one year/six months each for the offences under <a href="/doc/838469/" id="a_8">Sections 452</a>, <a href="/doc/763672/" id="a_9">148</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_10">323</a> and

<a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_11">149</a> IPC. However, the sentence of fine and in default thereof, will remain

the same. All these sentences shall run concurrently.

</p><p id="p_12">             With the above modification in the sentence of imprisonment,

the present revision petition is dismissed.


May 28, 2016.                                         (Inderjit Singh)
                                                           Judge
