<p id="p_1">3   14-12-2016               Heard the counsel for the petitioners and Mr. Nawal

                 Kishore Prasad, APP for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                             All the petitioners herein are cited as accuseds of

                 Forbesganj P.S. Case No. 210 of 2016 arising out of complaint

                 case no. 1488C of 2015, registered under sections 406 and 420

                 and other allied sections of <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_1">IPC</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                             Initially, a complaint was lodged which was treated as

                 an FIR on 05.07.2016. The complainant/informant alleged that,

                 on negotiation with the petitioners who belong to one family, the

                 land was agreed to be purchased for which Rs. 1, 93,000/- was

                 paid whereafter on a non-judicial stamp paper some recitals were

                 made, but thereafter the accused persons(petitioners) did not
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">        Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.47731 of 2016 (3) dt.14-12-2016</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                  2/2</span>

                    execute the sale-deed and left the place.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                 The contention of the petitioners       is that there is

                    inordinate delay in lodging the complaint which was later treated

                    as FIR. It is, in fact, a civil dispute. It is not acceptable that for a

                    consideration amount of Rs. 1, 93,000/-, the sale document would

                    be prepared on the stamp paper of Rs. 2000/-. The petitioners have

                    no criminal antecedents.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                 Considering the above, let the petitioners named above,

                    in the event of arrest/surrender within four weeks from today, be

                    released on bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs. 10,000/- each, with

                    two sureties of the like amount each, to the satisfaction of Chief

                    Judicial Magistrate, Araria in Forbesganj P.S. Case No.210 of

                    2016 arising out of complaint case no. 1488C of 2015S                on

                    condition that one of the bailors of each of the petitioner shall be

                    their own/close family members. In the event of framing of

                    charge, the petitioners shall appear in person on each date fixed at

                    the trial. In case of default in doing so on two consecutive dates,

                    the trial court shall have liberty to cancel their bail bonds and

                    secure their arrest in accordance with law.



</p><p id="p_6">                                            (Kishore Kumar Mandal, J)
HR/-
</p>
