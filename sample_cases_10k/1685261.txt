<div class="doc_title">Union Of India vs Joga Singh And Others on 16 December, 2009</div><pre id="pre_1">              In the High Court of Punjab &amp; Haryana at Chandigarh

                                          FAO No. 314 of 2002 (O&amp;M)


Union of India                                                ... Appellant
                                                   vs
Joga Singh and others                                         .... Respondents</pre>


<pre id="pre_2">Coram:        Hon'ble Mr. Justice Rajesh Bindal


Present:      Mr. S. K. Sharma, Advocate, for Union of India.

              None for the respondents.

Rajesh Bindal J.
</pre><p id="p_1">              This appeal has been filed by the Union of India seeking reduction
of compensation for the acquired land.
</p><p id="p_2">              Briefly, the facts are that land of the respondents situated in village
Gill, Tehsil and District Ludhiana was acquired by the Government for defence
purpose. The landowners feeling dissatisfied filed petitions under the
Requisitioning and Acq
                             uisition of Immovable Property Act, 1952, for
determination of compensation. The learned court below was appointed as
Arbitrator, who determined the compensation payable to the landowners @ Rs.
200/- per square yard. The Union of India is in appeal against the award of the
Arbitrator.
</p><p id="p_3">              Learned counsel for the appellant fairly submitted that the issue
involved in the present appeal is squarely covered by the judgment of this court in
Dr. Jagdish Parkash vs The Competent Authority Powers of Special Land
Acquisition Collector, Jalandhar 2006 (4) RCR (Civil) 711, whereby award of the
Arbitrator was upheld in the appeal by the Union of India whereas some relief was
granted to the landowners.
</p><p id="p_4">              For the reasons recorded in Dr. Jagdish Parkash's case (supra), the
present appeal is disposed of in the same terms.
</p>
