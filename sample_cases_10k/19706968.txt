            Sukhman Preet Kaur and another                            .....Petitioners

                                                Versus

            State of Punjab and others                               ....Respondents

            CORAM:- HON'BLE MR.JUSTICE SURINDER GUPTA

            Present:           Mr. P.S. Ghuman, Advocate
                               for the petitioners.

                               Mr. Varun Gupta, AAG, Punjab.

<p id="p_1">                               Heard.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                               Reply filed on behalf of respondents No. 1 to 3 is taken

            on record.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                               Petitioner No. 1 is present in the Court. On the asking of

            the Court and in the presence of the learned counsel, petitioner No. 1

            has stated that she is presently residing with her parents and she

            does not want to accompany petitioner No. 2. She also does not

            want to pursue this petition.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                               It has been submitted that the FIR has been registered

            against petitioner No. 2/Jag Preet Singh and others bearing No. 233

            dated 22.09.2013 registered at Police Station Sultanwind, Amritsar.

</p><p id="p_5">                               Learned State counsel has submitted that the FIR is still

            under investigation.
</p><p id="p_6">Sharma Ritu
2014.02.26 11:37
I attest to the accuracy and
integrity of this document
Chandigarh
             CRM No. M- 32013 of 2013 (O&amp;M)                             -2-
</p>
<p id="p_7">
</p><p id="p_8">                               Learned counsel for the petitioners submits that now

            petitioner No. 1 is under influence of the family and has levelled

            allegations against petitioner No. 2. Counsel wants to supply certain

            documents to the police, which as per the learned counsel for the

            petitioners, will assist in investigation of the case. The petitioners

            may supply the documents in their possession to the investigating

            officer, who is investigating the case.

</p><p id="p_9">                               Learned counsel for respondents No. 4 and 5 has

            submitted that respondents No. 4 and 5 undertake and assure not to

            cause any type of threat to petitioner No. 2 or any of his family

            member. In view of the undertaking given by learned counsel for

            respondents No. 4 and 5 and the statements made by the petitioners,

            this petition has become infructuous. However, in the event of any

            threat being faced by petitioner No. 2 from respondents No. 4 and 5,

            he may approach the police with representation, which will be looked

            into and action in accordance with law shall be taken on that

            representation.
</p>
<p id="p_10">                               The petition is dismissed as infructuous.</p><pre id="pre_2">
