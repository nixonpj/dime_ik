<p id="p_4">


                         The previous order on this contempt petition records

              that CSTC claimed that the dues on account of the petitioner's

              brother had been made over to his widow.        The petitioner claims

              that the brother was unmarried and, in any event, an order was

              passed in the petitioner's favour on WP 10568(W) of 2015 in the

              presence of Advocate representing the State and CSTC.



</p><p id="p_5">                       If the petitioner perceives that the payment has been

              wrongly made by the employer to any person not authorised to

              receive the same, it will be open to the petitioner to take appropriate
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                            2</span>


fresh action. As far as the contempt petition is concerned, once the

alleged contemnors claim and demonstrate that the payment has

been made, there can be no contempt.

</p><p id="p_6">         CPAN 1136 of 2016 is dropped.
</p>
<p id="p_7">         There will be no order as to costs.



</p><p id="p_8">                                               (Sanjib Banerjee, J.)
