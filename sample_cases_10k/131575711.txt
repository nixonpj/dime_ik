<div class="doc_title">Sunil Jain vs State Of Bihar &amp; Anr on 7 November, 2012</div><pre id="pre_1">      Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.7857 of 2010 (6) dt.07-11-2012




                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                   Criminal Miscellaneous No.7857 of 2010
                   ======================================================
                   Sunil Jain son of Yaswant Roy Jain, resident of Village/Mohalla-Rambagh
                   Road, Azad Market Delhi-6, Director North Eastern Corporation Ltd.
                   Corporate Office 9062/1/2 47, Ram Bagh Road Market, Delhi-6.
                                                                            .... .... Petitioner
                                                    Versus
                   1.      The State Of Bihar.
                   2.      Pankaj Kumar Jain, Manager son of Sri Parasmal Sethia,
                           Proprietor Raju Devi Sethis, M/S Jai Hanuman Handloom Cloth
                           Merchant Khetan Market, Saharsa, P.S. and District-Saharsa.
                                                                     .... .... Opposite Parties
                   ======================================================

                   CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE RAJENDRA KUMAR
                   MISHRA
                   ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">6   07-11-2012                      Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned

                   Additional Public Prosecutor for the State.

</p><p id="p_2">                                    This application under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> of the Code of

                   Criminal Procedure is to quash the order dated 16.05.2006 passed

                   in Complaint Case No. 996-C of 2005 by the Court of Sri Indrajit

                   Singh, Judicial Magistrate, 1st Class, Saharsa, whereby learned

                   Magistrate, on enquiry, summoned the accused-petitioner finding

                   prima facie case under <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_2">Sections 406</a>, <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_3">420</a> and <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_4">504</a> of the Indian

                   Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                    Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that the

                   complainant, Pankaj Kumar Jain and accused-petitioner has

                   compromised the case outside the Court and at the time of hearing

                   anticipatory bail petition No.63 of 2010 of the accused-petitioner

                   in the Court of Sessions Judge, Saharsa, Pankaj Kumar Jain also
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">           Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.7857 of 2010 (6) dt.07-11-2012</span>




                       filed the application in this regard.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                        Learned counsel for opposite party no. 2 also

                       admitted that the dispute has been settled in between the parties.

</p><p id="p_5">                                        On perusal of the impugned order, it appears that the

                       summoning order was passed for the offence under <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_5">Sections 406</a>,

                       <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_6">420</a> and <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_7">504</a> of the Indian Penal Code, out of which the offence

                       under <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_8">Section 504</a> of the Indian Penal Code is compoundable

                       whereas offence under <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_9">Sections 406</a> and <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_10">420</a> of the Indian Penal

                       Code are also compoundable with permission of the Court.

</p><p id="p_6">                                        In view of the aforesaid submissions as advanced on

                       behalf of both the parties, this application is disposed of with

                       direction to both the parties to file a compromise petition in the

                       trial court and the trial court will dispose of the same without any

                       delay in accordance with law.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><p id="p_8">                                                      (Rajendra Kumar Mishra, J)


Safik/-
</p>
