<div class="doc_title">Prabhat Kumar vs The State Of Bihar on 31 October, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                   Criminal Miscellaneous No.31658 of 2018
                          Arising Out of PS. Case No.-47 Year-2017 Thana- BARH District- Patna
                 ======================================================
                 Prabhat Kumar S/o Krishna Gopal @ Krishna Gopal Prasad, R/o Vill.-
                 Mirdhaha Chak, Majhaulli, P.S.- Salimpur, District- Patna.
                                                                               ... ... Petitioner/s
                                                    Versus
                 1. The State of Bihar
                 2. Mukesh Kumar, S/O Late Rajan Yadav, resident of village - Gulab Bag,
                 P.S. - Barh, District - Patna
                                                                       ... ... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner     : Ms. Sangha Mitra Ghosh, Advocate
                 For the Opposite Party : Ms. Meena Singh, APP.
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE ASHUTOSH KUMAR
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">5   31-10-2018                 Heard the learned counsel for the petitioner and
                  the State.
</p><p id="p_2">                               The petitioner seeks bail in anticipation of his
                  arrest in connection with Barh P.S. Case No. 47 of 2017
                  dated 20.02.2017 instituted for the offences under
                  <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_1">Sections 420</a> and <a href="/doc/1985627/" id="a_2">467</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p><p id="p_3">                               The petitioner is alleged to have purchased a
                  tractor from the opposite party no. 2 for a total agreed
                  consideration amount of Rs. 6.20 lakhs, but only Rs. 4
                  lakhs was paid by the petitioner and the balance amount
                  of Rs. 2,20,000/- was promised to be paid within a
                  particular time. The further allegation in the FIR is that a
                  cheque        of     Rs.      2,20,000/-          was       given       to     the
                  informant/opposite party no. 2, but the aforesaid cheque
                  could not be en-cashed as it bounced because of
                  insufficiency of funds in the bank account of the
                  petitioner.
</p><p id="p_4">             Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.31658 of 2018(5) dt.31-10-2018
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                         2/2</span>




                                  Learned counsel for the petitioner however has
                     submitted that the contention of the informant/opposite
                     party no. 2 that the agreed consideration amount was
                     Rs. 6.20 lakhs is incorrect. In fact, it was agreed
                     between the parties to purchase the tractor for an
                     amount of Rs. 5.80 lakhs and out of the aforesaid
                     amount Rs. 4.10 lakhs has already been given to the
                     opposite party no. 2.
</p><p id="p_5">                                  The petitioner is a farmer and is physically
                     challenged person and submits that he is ready to pay
                     the balance amount of Rs. 1.79 lakhs within a period of
                     three months from today.
</p><p id="p_6">                                  In order to instill confidence in opposite party
                     no. 2 that the petitioner intends to make payment of the
                     tractor which he has purchased from him, the petitioner
                     is ready to pay, upfront, an amount of Rs. 50,000/-
                     within a period of one month. The balance amount shall
                     be paid by the petitioner within three months from
                     today.
</p><p id="p_7">                                  List this case after four months i.e. on
                     27.02.2019.
</p><p id="p_8">                                  Interim protection in the meanwhile shall
                     continue.
</p>


<pre id="pre_2">krishna/-                                        (Ashutosh Kumar, J)

U       T
