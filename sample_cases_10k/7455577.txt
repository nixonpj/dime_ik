<pre id="pre_1">        C/SCA/2225/2017                              ORDER




         IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

            SPECIAL CIVIL APPLICATION NO. 2225 of 2017

==========================================================
                            KRISHNA TEXTILES
                                  Versus
                          PUNJAB NATIONAL BANK,
==========================================================
Appearance:
MR DHARMESH C GURJAR for the PETITIONER(s) No. 1
NATASHA SUTARIA for the PETITIONER(s) No. 1
REKHABEN C GURJAR for the PETITIONER(s) No. 1
DS AFF.NOT FILED (N) for the RESPONDENT(s) No. 1,2
MR MANISH M KAUSHIK for the RESPONDENT(s) No. 1
==========================================================

 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE R.M.CHHAYA

                             Date : 07/03/2018

                              ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">1.   Heard   Ms.   Natasha   Sutaria,   learned   advocate 
     for   the   petitioner   and   Mr.   Manish   Kaushik, 
     learned advocate for the respondents.
</p>

<p id="p_2">2.   By way of this petition under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of 
     Constitution   of   India,   the   petitioner   has 
     prayed for the following reliefs:­


            "(A) Your   Lordships   be   pleased   to 
            issue   a   writ   of   mandamus   or   any 
            other   appropriate   writ   order   or 
            direction   by   quashing   and   setting 
            aside   the   action   of   the   respondent 
            no.1 for public auction of the said 
            property   i.e.   Power   Looms   (60 
            Looms)   at   Plot   No.25­26,   Block 


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                 Page 1 of 5</span>
        C/SCA/2225/2017                          ORDER



           No.130,   Gokul   Industrial   Estate, 
           Village   Tanthithaiya,   Taluka 
           Palsana,   Dist.   Surat   and   also 
           directed   to   open   the   seal   of   the 
           said   property   and   given   the 
           possession   to   the   petitioner   on 
           suitable   terms   and   condition 
           decided   by   this   Hon'ble   High   Court 
           in the interest of justice; 
</p>
<p id="p_3">           (B) Your   Lordships   may   be   pleased 
           to   direct   the   respondents   to 
           consider   the   application   of   the 
           petitioner   dated   20/10/2016 
           (Annexure­K)   in   the   interest   of 
           justice;
</p>
<p id="p_4">           (C) Pending   admission   and   final 
           disposal   of   this   petition,   be 
           pleased to stay the further process 
           of   auction/tender   sale   of   the   said 
           property   i.e.   Power   Looms   (60 
           Looms)   at   Plot   No.25­26,   Block 
           No.130,   Gokul   Industrial   Estate, 
           Village­Tanthithaiya,              Taluka­
           Palsana,   Dist.   Surat   (Owned   by   the 
           petitioner   Mr.   Jignesh   Pravinbhai 
           Patel) in the interest of justice;
</p>
<p id="p_5">           (D) ... ... ..."
</p>
<p id="p_6">3.   Having   heard   the   learned   counsel   for   the 
     parties   and   on   perusal   of   the   record   and 
     proceedings,   it   appears   that   the   petitioner 
     has taken loan from the respondent No.1 and 
     the petitioner has an outstanding dues which 
     were not paid to the tune of Rs.67,47,459/­ 
     (as   on   27/7/2015).   The   respondent­Bank 
     initiated   recovery   proceedings.   The   record 

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                           Page 2 of 5</span>
        C/SCA/2225/2017                          ORDER



     indicates that the petitioner  filed  Original 
     Application   No.89/2016   before   the   Tribunal, 
     which   according   to   the   learned   counsel   is 
     disposed of.
</p>

<p id="p_7">4.   It further appears from the record that the 
     respondents   undertook   the   proceedings   under 
     the   provisions   of   the   Securitisation   and 
     Reconstruction   of   Financial   Assets   and 
     Enforcement   of   Security   Interest   Act,   2002 
     (hereinafter   referred   to   as   "the   Act)   and 
     ultimately,   the   notice   as   provided   under 
     Section 13(4) of the said Act is issued and 
     symbolic possession of 72 machines were taken 
     over.
</p>

<p id="p_8">5.   Learned   counsel   for   the   petitioner   pointed 
     out that the actions taken under the Act by 
     the respondent No.1 are challenged by way of 
     filing   S.A.   No.185/2016   before   the   Debt 
     Recovery   Tribunal,   which   is   pending.   The 
     present   petition   as   is   indicative   from   the 
     prayers   itself   is   filed   for   restraining   the 
     respondent   authorities   from   undertaking 
     public   auction   of   the   machines   in  question. 
     Mr. Manish Kaushik, learned advocate for the 
     respondents   states   that   the   secured   assets 
     have   been   sold   to   one   M/s.   D.H.   Patel 
     Infrastructure Private Limited on 17/10/2016. 
</p>
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                          Page 3 of 5</span>
<p id="p_9">        C/SCA/2225/2017                          ORDER



     As   such   therefore,   the   prayers   which   are 
     prayed   for   have   become   academic.   The 
     aforesaid   fact   is   not   denied   by   the 
     petitioner   by   filing   any   rejoinder.  Even 
     otherwise, the Securitisation Application is 
     pending   before   Tribunal   and   therefore,   the 
     petitioner can redress the grievance, if any, 
     before the Tribunal.
</p>

<p id="p_10">6.   Ms.   Sutaria,   learned   counsel   appearing   for 
     the   petitioner   further   submitted   that   the 
     petitioner   is   ready   and   willing   to   purchase 
     the   same.   The   said   contention   also   deserves 
     to   be   negatived   as   the   assets   are   already 
     sold. Ms. Sutaria further submitted that the 
     petitioner is ready and willing to even pay 
     the   dues.   The   said   aspect   can   be   agitated 
     before the Bank or before the Tribunal.
</p>

<p id="p_11">7.   Even   otherwise,   considering   the   ratio   laid 
     down by the Hon'ble Apex Court in the case of 
     Authorized Officer, State Bank of Travancore 
     and another Vs. Mathew K.C., reported in 2018 
     (1) Scale 618, the petition is otherwise not 
     maintainable   in   view   of   the   fact   that   the 
     petitioner has alternative efficacious remedy 
     which   in   fact   in   case   on   hand   is   already 
     resorted to by the petitioner.
</p>


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                          Page 4 of 5</span>
<p id="p_12">              C/SCA/2225/2017                     ORDER



</p><p id="p_13">8.    As   such   the   petitioner   has   already   availed 
      the   remedy   even   as   per   the   judgment   before 
      the Hon'ble Apex Court of Authorized Officer, 
      State   Bank   of   Travancore   (supra).   The 
      petition is therefore liable to be dismissed 
      and is hereby dismissed. 
</p>

<p id="p_14">                                             (R. M. CHHAYA, J.)
N.V.MEWADA
