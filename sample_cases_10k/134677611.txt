       IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

CRIMINAL MISC.APPLICATION (FOR CONDONATION OF DELAY)
                   NO. 9393 of 2014

                  In CRIMINAL APPEAL NO. 815 of 2014

=============================================
==
              VITHHALBHAI CHHITABHAI BARIA....Applicant(s)
                                Versus
                   STATE OF GUJARAT....Respondent(s)
=============================================
==
Appearance:
HCLS COMMITTEE, ADVOCATE for the Applicant(s) No. 1
MRS REKHA H KAPADIA, ADVOCATE for the Applicant(s) No. 1
MR JK SHAH ADDL. PUBLIC PROSECUTOR for the Respondent(s) No. 1
=============================================
==

           CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE ANANT S. DAVE
                  and
                  HONOURABLE MS JUSTICE SONIA GOKANI

                                 Date : 30/07/2014

                      ORAL ORDER</pre>
<p id="p_1">       (PER : HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE ANANT S. DAVE)


</p><p id="p_2">1.    Heard learned advocate for the applicant and learned APP for
the respondent.
</p>

<p id="p_3">2.    That, the applicant/appellant/original accused has preferred
Criminal Appeal against judgement and order dated 16.11.2013 in
Sessions Case No.1 of 2013 rendered by learned 7 th Additional
Sessions       Judge,          Panchmahal     at    Godhara   whereby   the
applicant/appellant is sentenced with life with fine of Rs.1,000/- and
in default thereof to undergo one year of rigorous imprisonment in
the offence under Sections 302 read with 114<a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_1"> of Indian Penal Code</a>. It
is submitted that the accused-applicant has approached Gujarat High
Court Legal Services Committee and after availing the certified copy


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                      Page 1 of 2</span>
           R/CR.MA/9393/2014                               ORDER




of the judgement and relevant record, the appeal was filed and there
is a delay of about 160 days in filing the above appeal and, therefore,
grounds stated in memo of appeal are also pressed into service to
point out prima facie good case in favour of the appellant as the trial
Court has not considered the defence of the accused in the context of
evidence appeared. However, the reason for delay in filing the
Criminal appeal is two fold namely, ignorance on the part of the
accused and seeking legal advice initially and arranging for funds etc.


</p><p id="p_4">3.      Considering overall facts and circumstances and in the interest
of justice sufficient cause is shown for condoning delay, we are
inclined to exercise powers under <a href="/doc/100581/" id="a_2">Section 5</a> of Limitation Act and
considering the decisions of the Apex Court in the case of <a href="/doc/1117226/" id="a_3">Collector,
Land Acquisition v. Katiji</a> reported in AIR 1987 SC 1353.
</p>

<p id="p_5">4.      Application is allowed. Rule is made absolute accordingly.
</p>


<p id="p_6">                                                  (ANANT S.DAVE, J.)



                                               (MS SONIA GOKANI, J.)
SMITA
