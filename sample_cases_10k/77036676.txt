<div class="doc_title">Sri Indrajit Hait vs The State Of West Bengal &amp; Ors on 26 April, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                                               1


32   26.4.16

                                        W.P.24089 (W) of 2015


                                        Sri Indrajit Hait
                                               Vs.
                                      The State of West Bengal &amp; Ors.


               Mr. Debojyoti Basu
               Mr. S. Bhattacharjee      ... . For the Petitioner.

               Mr. N.C. Behani      ... For the H.M.C.

               Mr. S. Betal    ...    For the State.




                              The    writ   petitioner      is   a   retired   medical

               officer/Homeo of the Howrah Municipal Corporation. He

               retired on 28th February, 2011. According to <a href="/doc/1934248/" id="a_1">Section 4</a> of

               the Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972, the gratuity became

               payable on his retirement. However, under <a href="/doc/1974722/" id="a_2">Section 7</a> of

               the said Act the employer had one month's time to make

               such payment. Such time expired on 30th March, 2011.

                          Therefore, from 31st March, 2011 under <a href="/doc/1376810/" id="a_3">Section

               7(3A)</a> of the said Act the employer/Howrah Municipal

               Corporation was obliged to pay interest on the said

     S.D.
               gratuity amount. A part of the said gratuity was paid on

               6th February, 2012. Therefore, from 31st March, 2012 till

               5th February, 2013 the said Corporation will pay interest

               on the whole of the gratuity amount at the rate of 9% p.a
                            2


simple interest.

          The rest of the amount was paid on 12th April,

2013. Therefore, from 6th February, 2012 till 11th April,

2013 the said Corporation will pay interest at the said

rate on the balance sum of gratuity to the petitioner.

          Let us assume that the interest on the first

installment was payable on 6th February, 2012 and

interest on the balance sum was payable on 6th April,

2016.

          Therefore, the petitioner will be entitled to

further interest at the said rate on the interest amount

unpaid from the date it accrued i.e 6th February, 2012

and 12th April, 2013 respectively till the date of payment.

          The entire interest amount should be made

within eight weeks of communication of this order. The

respondent/Corporation will also hand over a calculation</pre>

<p id="p_1">sheet.
</p>
<p id="p_2">              All   the   papers   are   before   this   Court.

Affidavits were not invited. The allegations contained in

the writ petition are deemed not to have been admitted.

</p><p id="p_3">          The writ application is thus disposed of.

          Urgent certified photocopy of this order, if

applied for, be supplied to the parties on priority basis.
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> 3</span>




    ( I.P. Mukerji, J.)
