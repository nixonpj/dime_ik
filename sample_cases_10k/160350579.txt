22.06.2018</pre><p id="p_1">.
</p><p id="p_2">59.
as
(Rejected)                           C.R.M. 4083 of 2018

              In Re:- An application for anticipatory bail under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> of
              the Code of Criminal Procedure filed on 21.06.2018 in connection
              with English Bazar P.S. Case No.243 of 2017 dated 06.12.2017
              under <a href="/doc/105503/" id="a_2">Sections 417</a>/<a href="/doc/1279834/" id="a_3">376</a>/<a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_4">506</a> of the Indian Penal Code.

              In the matter of : Samir Ghosh.
</p><p id="p_3">                                                             ... Petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_4">              Mr. S. Bhattacharyya.
</p><p id="p_5">                                                         ....for the Petitioner.
</p><p id="p_6">              Mr. Arijit Ganguly,
              Mr. Avik Ghatak.
</p><p id="p_7">                                                             ...for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_8">
                   Leave is granted to the learned Advocate of the petitioner to

              correct the cause title of the petition.

</p><p id="p_9">                   Heard the learned Counsels appearing on behalf of the

              parties.
</p>
<p id="p_10">                   Having considered the materials in the Case Diary prima

              facie disclosing involvement of the petitioner in ravishing a deaf

              and dumb girl on the false promise of marriage, we are of the

              opinion that this is not a fit case to grant anticipatory bail to the

              petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_11">                    Accordingly, the prayer for anticipatory bail of the petitioner

              stands rejected.
</p>
<p id="p_12">
              (Ravi Krishan Kapur, J.)                   (Joymalya Bagchi, J.)
