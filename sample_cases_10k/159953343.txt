<div class="doc_title">Lakhan vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 17 April, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                 Criminal Appeal No.1278/2012
17.04.2015
     Shri Ajay          Bagadiya,       advocate     for    appellant
Lakhan s/o Ramesh Jat.
     Shri       Devendra       Singh,   Panel      Lawyer    for    the
respondent / State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">     Heard      on     IA     No.1595/2015,      second     /    repeat
application for suspension of jail sentence of the
appellant.
</p><p id="p_2">     Learned counsel for the appellant submits that
earlier his first application for grant of bail was
allowed.     Thereafter, he committed another offence on
03.06.2014 vide Crime No.376/2014 registered against
him under <a href="/doc/838469/" id="a_1">Sections 452</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_2">323</a> and <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_3">506</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_4">34</a> of the Indian
Penal Code.       He submitted that after cencellation of
the bail by order dated 31.10.2014, the appellant
came to know that in respect of Crime No.376/2014 on
the basis of compromise, he has been acquitted by the
learned Magistrate on 27.06.2014 and this fact could
not be brought          to the knowledge by the                 learned
counsel for the respondent or by the Officer-in-
charge     of    the        case,   otherwise,      the     order   of
       cancellation of suspension of jail sentence of the
      appellant could not have been passed.            It is also
      submitted that against the order dated 31.10.2014, a
      Petition   for   Special   Leave   to   Appeal   (Criminal)
      No.1350/2015 / CRLMP No.44/2015 was filed before the
      Apex Court.      By order dated 06.02.2015 the SLP was
      dismissed as withdrawn with liberty to approach the
      High Court.
</p><p id="p_3">           Considering the aforesaid, we direct the learned
      counsel for the appellant to file a complete set of
      the SLP within two weeks from today.
</p><p id="p_4">           List thereafter.
</p>


<pre id="pre_2">          (P.K. Jaiswal)                      (D.K. Paliwal)
               Judge                               Judge
