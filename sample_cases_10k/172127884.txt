<div class="doc_title">Mukesh Kumar Alias M Kumar vs The State Of Jharkhand on 2 September, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">            IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                         B. A. No. 6590 of 2015
                                       ---
            Mukesh Kumar @ M. Kumar, son of Kapildeo Choudhary, residing at
            Premchand Path, Baridih Basti P.O. &amp; P.S. - Sidgora, Town -
            Jamshedpur, District - East Singhbhum (Jharkhand)
                                                        ...     ...     Petitioner
                                      Versus
            The State of Jharkhand                      ...     ...     Opp. Party
                                       ---
      CORAM       : HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE RONGON MUKHOPADHYAY
                                      ---
            For the Petitioner        : Mr. Chandrajit Mukherjee, Advocate
            For the Opp. Party        : M/s. Vandana Bharti, A.P.P.
                                      ---
02/02.09.2015</pre><p id="p_1">     Heard Mr. Chandrajit Mukherjee, learned counsel for the
           petitioner and M/s. Vandana Bharti, learned A.P.P. for the State.
</p><p id="p_2">                 Petitioner has been made accused in connection with S. T. No.
           498 of 2012 arising out of Telco P.S. Case No. 53 of 2007 corresponding
           to G. R. No. 387 of 2007 registered for the offences punishable under
           <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_1">Sections 307</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_2">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code and 27 of the <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_3">Arms Act</a>.
</p><p id="p_3">                 It has been submitted by the learned counsel for the petitioner
           that the petitioner has been falsely implicated in the present case and
           infact the bullet had merely grazed the head of the informant. He
           further adds that the petitioner is in custody since 26.02.2015.
</p><p id="p_4">                 Learned A.P.P., on the other hand, has opposed the prayer for
           bail and has submitted that there is direct allegation against the
           petitioner having shot on the head of the injured resulting in the
           informant suffering grievous injuries.
</p><p id="p_5">                 Regard being had to the direct nature of allegation, I am not
           inclined to grant bail to the petitioner. Accordingly, the prayer for bail
           of the petitioner is rejected.
</p>

<p id="p_6">                                                        (R. Mukhopadhyay, J.)
Umesh/-
</p>
