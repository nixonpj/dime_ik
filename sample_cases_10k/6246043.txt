<div class="doc_title">Paramhans Singh @ Parbhansh Singh vs The State Of Bihar &amp; Ors on 14 July, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA

                                      Criminal Revision No.781 of 2011
                 ======================================================
                 1. Paramhans Singh @ Parbhansh Singh, son of Late Ram Bhajan Singh
                 2. Ashok Kumar Singh, son of Tej Narain Singh
                 Both resident of village Chatar, P.O.-Babhangawan, Anchal Barahara,
                 Police Station Barahara, District Bhojpur
                                                                        .... .... Petitioners
                                                     Versus
                 1. The State of Bihar
                 2. Sheo Komal Singh, son of Late Rameshwar Singh
                 3. Om Prakash @ Phudan Singh
                 2 and 3 are resident of village Chatar, p.O. Babhawangaon, Anchal Barahra,
                 Police Station Barahara, District Bhojpur
                                                                       .... .... Respondents
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s      :   Mr. Anil Kumar Mishra
                                                Mr. Om Prakash Pandey
                 For the State              :   Mr. B.Lal(App)
                 For the O. P. No. 2        :   Mr. Bharat Bhushan
                                                Mr. Nitesh Kumar
                                                Mr. Anand Varshan
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE CHAKRADHARI
                 SHARAN SINGH
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">5   14-07-2017                   Heard     learned     Counsel      for   the    petitioner,

                  learned Counsel appearing on behalf of Opposite Party Nos.

                  2 &amp; 3 and learned Additional Public Prosecutor representing

                  the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                 The petitioners are aggrieved by the judgment

                  and order, dated 19.05.2010, passed by learned Sessions

                  Judge, Bhojpur, at Ara, in Criminal Revision No. 51 of 2010,

                  whereby he has revised an order, dated 02.02.2010,

                  passed by the Sub Divisional Magistrate, Sadar, Ara, in

                  Case No. 240 of 2007, under Section 144 of the Code of
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Patna High Court CR. REV. No.781 of 2011 (5) dt.14-07-2017</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                          2/3</span>




               Criminal Procedure, 1973 (hereinafter referred to as 'the

               Code'), which was attached under Section 146 (1) of the

               Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                              I have perused the impugned judgment and

               order.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                              This is not in dispute that the parties are

               claiming their rights over the disputed land, as co-sharer.

               The Court of learned Sub Divisional Magistrate, Sadar, Ara,

               by order, dated 02.02.2010, passed in Case No. 240 of

               2007, had attached the disputed land under Section 146

               (1) of the Code.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                              I do not find any flaw in the reasoning assigned

               by the learned Court below, while allowing the criminal

               revision application filed on behalf of the Opposite Party

               Nos. 2 and 3 on the ground that attachment of a disputed

               land under Section 146 (1) of the Code was not justified

               because parties were claiming their rights over the disputed

               land as co-sharer.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                              Learned Court below has rightly recorded that

               when the Civil Court is not in a position to grant relief or

               declare possession of any of the parties, then the learned

               Sub Divisional Magistrate has no authority to pass any

               prohibitory order.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                              I do not find any illegality in the impugned
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">         Patna High Court CR. REV. No.781 of 2011 (5) dt.14-07-2017</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                                                  3/3</span>




                       order,     requiring      this    Court's     interference   in   criminal

                       revisional jurisdiction.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                                      This application is accordingly dismissed.



</p><p id="p_9">                                                        (Chakradhari Sharan Singh, J.)
Prabhakar Anand/-
</p>
