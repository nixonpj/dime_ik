<div class="doc_title">Smt. Vimla Singh vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 18 October, 2012</div><pre id="pre_1">              Writ Appeal No. 1205/2012
    (Smt. Vimla Singh ..Vs. ..State of M.P. and others)

18-10-2012</pre>

<p id="p_1">     Heard Shri V.D.S. Chouhan, learned counsel for the
appellant/petitioner on the question of admission.
</p><p id="p_2">     Though the present writ appeal has been filed by the
appellant/petitioner assailing the order passed by this court
dated 21-9-2012 in W.P.No. 15830/2012 (s) raising several
grounds, the learned counsel for the appellant, after
arguing at length, submits that the liberty granted by the
learned single Judge to the petitioner to approach the
Collector may be clarified by stating that the Collector may
consider the representation of the petitioner by taking into
consideration the order dated 31-8-2012 passed by the
S.D.O., Katangi modifying the petitioner's order of transfer
and the appeal be disposed of accordingly.
</p><p id="p_3">     In the circumstances, in view of the limited prayer
made by the appellant, while the order passed by the
learned single Judge in W.P.No. 15830/2012 (s) dated
21-9-2012 is upheld, it is observed that the observation
made by the earned single Judge regarding consideration
of the petitioner's representation are clarified to the extent
that in case the petitioner raises the aforesaid issues
regarding non-consideration of the order of modification of
his previous order of transfer by order dated 31-8-2012 in
the representation, the concerned Collector shall look into
the same and take a decision expeditiously in accordance
       with law.
</p>
<p id="p_4">            On the request of the learned counsel for the
      appellant it is further clarified that in case a representation
      is filed by the petitioner alongwith a copy of order passed
      today and a copy of the petition within fifteen days of
      obtaining the same before the Collector, Balaghat, the
      concerned authority shall consider and decide the same in
      accordance with law expeditiously preferably within a
      period of one month thereafter.
</p>
<p id="p_5">            With the aforesaid clarification/directions the appeal
      filed by the appellant/petitioner stands disposed of.
</p><p id="p_6">            C.C. as per rules.
</p>


<pre id="pre_2">            (R.S.Jha)                           (Smt. Vimla Jain)
