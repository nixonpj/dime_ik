<div class="doc_title">Lokesh Soni vs State Of Rajasthan on 28 April, 2011</div><pre id="pre_1">                                        S.B.CIVIL WRIT PETITION NO. 2202//2001
                                                      Lokesh Soni vs. State &amp; Ors.
                                                              Order dt:28/4/2011


                                 1/2

            S.B.CIVIL WRIT PETITION NO. 2202/2001
                  (<a href="/doc/1731697/" id="a_1">Lokesh Soni vs. State &amp; Ors</a>.)
                  DATE OF ORDER : 28/4/2011

           HON'BLE DR.JUSTICE VINEET KOTHARI


Mr.Khet Singh for
Mr.J.P.Joshi, for the petitioner.
Mr.Ankur Mathur for
Mr.Vinit Mathur, for the respondents.



            Learned counsel for the parties submit that the present

writ petition is covered by a Division Bench decision of this Court in

the case of Bhupendra Singh vs. State &amp; Ors. - D.B.Civil Writ

Petition No. 2259/2001 - decided on 3/7/2003. The operative portion

of the said judgment is quoted below for ready reference:


           "We are of the opinion firstly that the filling of
     vacancies existing in the Commercial Taxes Department
     was statutory obligation of the Commercial Taxes
     Department and merely because the contractor has been
     utilized for the purpose of securing the employees, it
     cannot be said that such appointment does not create
     relationship of master and servant directly between the
     petitioner and the department. We are of the opinion that
     the case of the petitioner is governed by the decision of
                                          S.B.CIVIL WRIT PETITION NO. 2202//2001
                                                       Lokesh Soni vs. State &amp; Ors.
                                                               Order dt:28/4/2011


                                   2/2

     Suresh Chandra's case decided on 7/4/2003. Therefore,
     the termination order of the petitioner deserves to be
     quashed following the aforesaid decision and the same is
     accordingly quashed. There is no dispute about the fact
     that the petitioner is in continuous service as LDC with
     the     Commercial      Tax    Department         since       1991
     uninterruptedly until the filing of writ petition on
     7/6/2001. In view of this it is a prima facie case which is
     required to be considered for regularization by the
     employer and in accordance with principles enunciated
     by this Court in D.B.Civil Writ Petition No. 1/<a href="/doc/1038746/" id="a_2">2002
     Bhawani Singh vs. State of Rajasthan and ors</a>. decided
     on 23/7/2002, since reported in (2202) 3 WLC 728.The
     respondents are directed to reinstate the petitioner and
     consider his case for regularization in accordance with
     the principles enunciated in Bhawani Singh's case.
             Accordingly, the petition is allowed. There shall be
     no order as to costs.


     (O.P.Bishnoi), J.                      (Rajesh Balia), J."



      The present writ petition is also disposed of in aforesaid terms.


                                         (DR.VINEET KOTHARI), J.
