                         J U D G M E N T

<p id="p_1">           The petitioner admittedly did not comply with the

directions contained in Ext.P1 judgment disposing of W.P.(C)

No.21436/2004. The warrant of arrest was in force when the

second writ petition - W.P.(C) No.24462/2012 - was filed by the

petitioner. The same is evident from Ext.P3 judgment disposing

of W.P.(C) No.24462/2012.        The petitioner gave up all his

contentions therein and was rest contended with a facility to

repay the amount in 3 equal monthly instalments.

</p><p id="p_2">           2.   The first respondent has by Ext.P7 proceedings

granted time to the petitioner to pay the sales tax dues on

furnishing a bond with two solvent sureties. The present writ

petition challenging the warrant of arrest is plainly barred by res

judicata   in view of Ext.P3 judgment disposing of W.P.(C)

No.24462 of 2012. The coercive steps under Section 65 of the

Kerala Revenue Recovery Act have been resorted to only after

due enquiry as evident     by    Ext.P5.   I do not find any valid

W.P(C) No.29514/2013

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                  2</span>



ground to quash the proceedings initiated under Section 65 of the

Kerala Revenue Recovery Act for realisation of the sales tax dues

from the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_3">           3. I however grant the petitioner easy instalments to

pay the sales tax dues to avert the warrant of arrest being

executed.    The entire dues shall be paid in 4 equal monthly

instalments starting from 1.1.2014 onwards.        The coercive

proceedings indicated above will continue if the petitioner omits

to comply with any of the conditions which can even lead to his

detention in civil prison.
</p>
<p id="p_4">           4.    The coercive steps shall be put on hold if the

petitioner deposits the instalments in time.

</p><p id="p_5">           The Writ Petition is disposed of.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><p id="p_7">                                           V.CHITAMBARESH,
                                                Judge.
