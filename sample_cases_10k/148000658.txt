 Ct. 09
28.08.17                    Bishal Mishra
                                    Vs.

            Mr. Mir Anowar          ... for the Petitioner.

            Mr. Chandi Charan De, Addl. Govt. Pleader ... for the
                                        State/respondents.</pre>


<p id="p_1">                   It is contended on behalf of the petitioner that

           the petitioner entered into a lease agreement with the

           State of West Bengal on July 12, 2011 for extracting

           sand from the riverbed of Damodar for the period of five

           years. The further contention made on behalf of the

           petitioner is that the petitioner was unable to extract

           sand from the riverbed for the entire period of five years

           for promulgation of prohibitory order under Section

           144 (2) of the Code of Criminal Procedure and as such

           the petitioner submitted an application before the

           respondent no. 2 on February 22, 2016 along with

           requisite fees, but the said application has not been

           considered by the concerned respondent till date.

</p><p id="p_2">                   None appears on behalf of the State. I have

           requested Mr. De, learned counsel in the State panel to

           represent    the    State      respondents       in    this   writ
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                        2</span>


application. Mr. De has referred to the provisions of

Rule 21 of the West Bengal Minor and Minerals

Concessions Rules, 2016 and submitted that the

application of the petitioner may be considered by the

respondent no. 2 in accordance with law.

</p><p id="p_3">          In view of the fact that the petitioner was not in

a position to utilize the entire period of lease for

extracting     sand    from    the   riverbed,    I   direct   the

respondent no. 2 to consider the representation

submitted by the petitioner on February 22, 2016

(Annexure 'P-3' of the writ application) in accordance

with law after giving the petitioner an opportunity of

hearing within a period of eight weeks from the date of

communication of the order and the result of the same

must be communicated to the petitioner within a

period of two weeks thereafter.
</p>
<p id="p_4">          With the above direction, the writ application is

disposed of.
</p>
<p id="p_5">          Urgent photostat certified copy of this order, if

applied    for,   be   given    to   the    parties   on     usual

undertakings.
</p>
<p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">                                           (R. K. Bag, J.)
