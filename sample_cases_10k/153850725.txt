<div class="doc_title">Sanjeet Kumar Pandey vs State Of Bihar &amp; Anr on 11 August, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                 Criminal Miscellaneous No.33066 of 2015
                       Arising Out of PS.Case No. -90 Year- 2015 Thana -DUMRA District- SITAMARHI
                 ======================================================
                 1. Sanjeet Kumar Pandey son of Gopal Pandey resident of Village- Puran
                 chapra (Kalyanpur), Hindu Chakia, Police Station- Bara Chakia, District -
                 East champaran.

                                                                                .... ....   Petitioner/s
                                                   Versus
                 1. The State of Bihar
                 2. The District Manager, Bihar State Food Corporation ltd. , Samastipur

                                                               .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :  Mr. Anil Kumar
                 For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr. Madhuranand Jha (App)
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE DINESH KUMAR
                 SINGH
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   11-08-2015               Heard learned counsels for the petitioner and the State.

</p><p id="p_2">                             The petitioner being a rice miller is apprehending arrest

                 in a case registered for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_1">Sections

                 406</a>,<a href="/doc/1326844/" id="a_2">409</a> and <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_3">420</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_4">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                             The prosecution case is that Sitamarhi unit of Bihar

                 State Food and Civil Supplies Corporation Ltd. supplied 1742.20

                 quintals of paddy to the petitioner being rice miller under an

                 agreement during the agricultural years 2012-13 in lieu thereof the

                 petitioner was supposed to supply 1170.60 quintals of custom

                 milled rice but the petitioner failed to supply processed rice

                 worth Rs.25,35,047.85/-.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                             It is submitted by the learned counsel for the petitioner
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.33066 of 2015 (2) dt.11-08-2015</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                           2/3</span>




             that the rice could not be supplied due to supply of poor quality of

             paddy and the apathetic attitude of the officials of The Bihar State

             Food and Civil Supplies Corporation Ltd. The calculation of the

             rice supplied was not properly made and due to supply of poor

             quality of paddy the petitioner could only get 30% to 40% rice

             prepared.       Of the paddy supplied. It is further submitted that

             during enquiry it was found that the poor quality of paddy was

             supplied. Moreover,             under agreement the due amount was

             supposed to be realized by initiating certificate proceeding under

             the provision of Bihar and Orissa Public Demands Recovery Act,

             1914 or through arbitration.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                           It is submitted by Mr. A.N. Rai, learned counsel for the

             BSFC that the petitioner failed to make supply within time frame

             i.e. 31.12.2013 and the BSFC has already initiated legal

             proceeding being certificate case no. 5 of 2014-15. However, it is

             submitted by learned counsel for the petitioner that the petitioner

             will deposit 20% of the alleged due amount through bank draft in

             favour of Bihar State Food and Civil Supplies Corporation Ltd.,

             Patna within five months.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                           Considering the present stand of the petitioner, let the

             above named petitioner be released on provisional anticipatory

             bail for six months in the event of arrest or surrender before the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">          Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.33066 of 2015 (2) dt.11-08-2015</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                                                    3/3</span>




                      learned Court below within a period of six weeks from today on

                      furnishing bail bond of Rs. 10,000/- (ten thousand) with two

                      sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of learned CJM,

                      Sitamarhi in connection with Dumara P.S. Case No. 90 of 2015

                      subject to the conditions as laid down under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_5">Section 438(2)</a>

                      Cr.P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                                    The aforesaid deposit will not be treated to be an

                      admission of the petitioner with regard to the claim of the

                      informant but the same will be subject to the certificate proceeding

                      or any legal proceeding. It is made clear that this court has not

                      expressed any opinion with regard to the merits of claim and

                      counter claim of the parties.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                                        The provisional bail of the petitioner will be confirmed

                      by the learned court below on submission of 20% of the due

                      amount through bank draft within five months in favour of Bihar

                      State Food and Civil Supplies, Corporation Ltd., Patna.



</p><p id="p_9">                                                 (Dinesh Kumar Singh, J)
Anil/-
</p>
