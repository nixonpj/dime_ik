<div class="doc_title">Nagar Palika Nigam Thr. vs Ajay Kandu on 27 March, 2018</div><p id="p_1">                HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                             1                    M.C.C.No. 786/2018</span>
               (Nagar Palika Nigam &amp; Anr. Vs. Ajay Kandu)

Gwalior Bench:
</p><p id="p_2">Dated:27/03/2018
      Shri Devendra Sharma, learned counsel for the applicants.
      This     is   an   application   for    restoration      of   W.P.no.
3093/2015, which stood dismissed for non-compliance of court order
dated 22/2/2017 by the order of OSD/Registrar dated 3/3/2017.
</p><p id="p_3">      At the outset, learned counsel for the applicants submits that
the order under challenge is assailable on the ground that due to
non-compliance of Section 17-B of the Industrial Dispute Act, 1947,
petition cannot be dismissed. He relied upon the Division Bench
decisions of this Court in the matter of Krishi Upaj Mandi Samiti,
Bada Malhara Vs. Yashwant Singh Bundela &amp; Anr., 2008 (2)
MPLJ 282 and Punj Lloyd Limited Vs. Rakesh Shrivastava
(W.A.No. 399/2017 decided on 10/11/2017).
</p><p id="p_4">      Heard.
</p><p id="p_5">      The Division Bench of this Court in the matter of Krishi Upaj
Mandi Samiti (supra) has observed as under:-
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">        "19. The next aspect that is required to be
        adverted is whether the direction of the learned
        single Judge that if there would be non-
        compliance of section 17-B of the Act, the writ
        petition shall automatically dismissed. Section 17-
        B of the Act does not so stipulate. In this regard,
        we may usefully record a two judge bench
        decision in <a href="/doc/942392/" id="a_1">Hindustan Zinc Ltd. vs. Industrial
        Tribunal and another</a>, (2001) 10 SCC 211 wherein
        Their Lordships were dealing with a matter where
        the High Court declined to address itself on merits
        as the order passed under section 17-B of the Act
        had not been complied with. In this context, Their
        Lordships stated thus :
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">                "The High Court noticed that the order
                made     under   <a href="/doc/770112/" id="a_2">section     17-B</a>   of   the
                Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, had not
                                         HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                     2                    M.C.C.No. 786/2018</span>
                                       (Nagar Palika Nigam &amp; Anr. Vs. Ajay Kandu)

                                           been complied with and therefore there
                                           was no necessity to go into the merits of
                                           the case. This conclusion is rather
                                           surprising. The High Court ought to have
                                           dealt with the merits of the case and
                                           decided the case but it went at a tangent
                                           and proceeded to dispose of the matter
                                           for non-compliance with the interim
                                           order made by the High Court."
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">                                 20.       In view of the aforesaid the order of the
                                 learned Single Judge directing automatic dismissal
                                 of the writ petition because of non-compliance of
                                 <a href="/doc/770112/" id="a_3">section 17-B</a> of the Act is not sustainable.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_4">                                 21.       In the ultimate result the direction to comply
                                 with <a href="/doc/770112/" id="a_4">section 17-B</a> is upheld and the direction for
                                 automatic dismissal for non-compliance of the
                                 order as regards <a href="/doc/770112/" id="a_5">section 17-B</a> is set aside. Thus,
                                 the writ appeal is allowed in part without any order
                                 as to costs."
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_5">                              In view of said legal position, the order dated 3/3/2018 passed
                       by the OSD/Registrar cannot be allowed to stand.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_6">                              According, the MCC is allowed. W.P.No. 3093/2015 is
                       restored to file.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_7">                              A copy of this order be kept in the file of W.P.No. 3093/2015.
                              The MCC stands disposed of.</blockquote>

<blockquote id="blockquote_8">                                                                   (Anand Pathak)
                                                                       Judge
jps/-
</blockquote>
