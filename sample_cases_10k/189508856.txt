<pre id="pre_1">      IN THE COURT OF SHRI SANATAN PRASAD:
     METROPOLITAN MAGISTRATE: BEGGAR'S COURT:
                    DELHI

                                         Case No. 603/14.
                                         U/S 5(5) BPB Act, 1959.
                                         State Vs Sh. Om Prakash.



a.    Sr. No. of the case                603/14.


b.    Date of commission of offence      3.11.2014.


c.    Name of the complainant            Mr. P.K. Shukla.


d.    Name of the inmate &amp; his           Sh. Om Prakash, S/o Sh. Kamalshah,

      parentage &amp; address                R/o Footpath, Delhi.

e.    The offence complained of

      or proved                          5(5)BPB Act, 1959.

f.    The date of hearing of arguments   11.11.2014


g.    The date of such order             11.11.2014


h.    Final order                        Declared beggar.


Present:


Ld. APP for the state.


Accused produced from R.C.C.
 JUDGMENT.


A brief reason for statement of decision:-



1.     By this Judgment I shall decide the present case of the prosecution. The case of

the prosecution is this that on 3.11.2014 at about 10.48 A.M at in front of Hanuman

Mandir, Yamuna Bazar, Delhi accused was found begging and he was arrested and

was proceeded U/S 4(1) of Bombay Prevention of Begging Act, 1959 and a kalandra

was filed before the court.

2.     Notice U/S 25l <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_1">CrPC</a> for the offence of begging defined 2(1)(1) of the Bombay

Prevention of Begging Act, 1959 read with 4(1) of Bombay Prevention of Begging Act,

1959, by explaining to the accused in vernacular has been served and he has been

cautioned that he is not required to plead guilty and further he has been told about the

consequences thereof also and despite the consequences being told accused pleads

guilty to the offence/ accusation explained to him, as above.

3.     After conducting summary inquiry in the matter I am satisfied that accused has

voluntarily pleaded guilty and therefore, accused is found begging and he is, therefore,

declared a beggar and his case, therefore, falls U/S 5(5)of The Bombay Prevention of

Begging Act, 1959.

ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">4.     I have heard Ld. APP for the state and also the inmate as well and after

conducting a summary inquiry, by explaining accusation to the convicted person in

vernacular and also by questioning the circumstances of his arrest I find that convict is a

beggar and he is leprosy affected. He is unable to do any work. He desires to live in an
 institution. He has no visible source of income. The convict is leper having no visible

source of income who also wants to live in a certified institution and hence convict is

ordered to be detained in certified institution for a period of three years. A copy of this

order be given to the accused free of costs. File be consigned to Record Room.




</p><pre id="pre_2">ANNOUNCED IN THE OPEN                            (SANATAN PRASAD)
COURT ON 11.11.2014                          METROPOLITAN MAGISTRATE
                                               BEGGAR'S COURT : DELHI.
 Case No. 603/14                               State Vs Shri Om Prakash.




                     NOTICE OF ACCUSATION U/S 25l <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_2">CrPC</a>

</pre><p id="p_2">       It is alleged against you that on 3.11.2014 at about 10.48 A.M at in front of

Hanuman Mandir, Yamuna Bazar, Delhi you accused Om Prakash was found begging

from the passersby by raising your hands and thereby you committed an offence of

begging defined U/S2 (1) (i) of The Bombay Prevention of Begging Act, 1959 and you

are required to be dealt in accordance with Section 5(5) of the Bombay Prevention of

Begging Act, 1959 and within my cognizance.
</p>
<p id="p_3">


</p><p id="p_4">                                              MM Delhi/11.11.2014


       Notice U/S 5(5)BPB Act,59 has been read over and explained to the accused in

vernacular and he is questioned as under:-
</p>
<p id="p_5">
</p><pre id="pre_3">Q.     Do you plead guilty or claim trial?


Ans. I plead guilty and do not claim trial.




RO&amp;AC                                         MM Delhi/11.11.2014
