<div class="doc_title">Sri Ram Kumar Paswan vs The State Of Bihar on 12 January, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.61961 of 2017
                         Arising Out of PS.Case No. -24 Year- 2017 Thana -RAJAUN District- BANKA
                 ======================================================
                 Sri Ram Kumar Paswan, Son of Sakhi Chand Paswan @ Sahhichan Paswan,
                 resident of Village- Mohna, P.S. Rajoun, District- Banka.

                                                                                  .... ....   Petitioner
                                                        Versus
                 The State of Bihar.

                                                                .... .... Opposite Party
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     : Mr. Sanjay Kumar Jha
                 For the Opposite Party/s  : Smt. Sucheta Yadav
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE VINOD KUMAR SINHA
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">3   12-01-2018              Heard learned counsel for the petitioner.

</p><p id="p_2">                            This is an application for bail in connection with Rajoun

                 P.S. Case No. 24 of 2017 registered for the offences punishable

                 under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_1">Sections 302</a>, <a href="/doc/386021/" id="a_2">201</a> and <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_3">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code.

</p><p id="p_3">                            Petitioner is not named in the FIR and it appears that two

                 witnesses have named this petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                            Submission of learned counsel for the petitioner is that

                 though the case has been lodged on 20.1.2017 but these two

                 witnesses after nine months of the occurrence have named this

                 petitioner and that too on suspicion and it is alleged that petitioner

                 was seen along with the deceased. Further submission is that

                 though the impugned order shows that third witness in paragraph-

                 93 of the case diary has also named this petitioner but it is a
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">          Patna High Court Cr.M isc. No.61961 of 2017 (3) dt.12-01-2018</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                     2/2</span>




                      mistake of fact as the witness has not named him. Further

                      submission is that petitioner is in custody for about five months,

                      having no criminal antecedent.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                   Heard learned APP also.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                   Having heard both sides and in the facts and

                      circumstances of the case, let petitioner, above named, be released

                      on bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs.25,000/- (Rupees twenty five

                      thousand) with two sureties of the like amount each to the

                      satisfaction of the Chief Judicial Magistrate, Banka, in connection

                      with Rajoun P.S.Case No. 24 of 2017, subject to the conditions

                      that (1) one of the bailors shall be a local person having sufficient

                      immoveable properties within the jurisdiction of court concerned

                      and (2) petitioner will co-operate in trial and appear in court as

                      and when required, except on genuine ground, failing which his

                      bail bond shall be cancelled.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><p id="p_8">                                                  (Vinod Kumar Sinha, J)
spal/-
</p>
