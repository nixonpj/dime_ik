<div class="doc_title">Sadeeq Mohd. vs State Th.P.H.E. Deptt.And Ors. on 8 December, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">            HIGH COURT OF JAMMU AND KASHMIR
                       AT JAMMU


SWP No. 1199/2014

                                                 Date of Order: 08.12.2016



Sadeeq Mohd.                               Vs.   State of J&amp;K &amp; ors.
Coram:


Appearing Counsel:
For the Petitioner(s):   Mr. Rahil Raja, Advocate.
For the Respondent(s): None.</pre>
<pre id="pre_2">i/    Whether to be reported in            :            Yes/No
      Press/Media

ii/   Whether to be reported in            :            Yes/No
      Digest/Journal-Net


</pre><p id="p_1">1.    The writ petition has been filed in the year 2014.
</p><p id="p_2">2.    It is a case relating to the transfer. The writ petition has
been filed for the following reasons:
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">      "i)   writ, order or direction in the nature of the writ of
      certiorari quashing Government order NO. 531-
      PW(Hyd.) of 2012 dated 19th December, 2012
      (Annexure-A) insofar as it pertains to the transfer and
      attachment of the petitioner in the office of respondent
      No.2.;
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">      ii)   writ, order or direction in the nature of the writ of
      mandamus commanding the respondents particularly
      the respondent No.3 not to relieve the petitioner form
      Mahore Irrigation Division Dharmari pursuant to
      Government order No. 531-PW(Hyd.) of 2012 dated
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                    2</span>




      19th December, 2912 and allow the petitioner to
      continue in Mahore Irrigation Division, Dharmari;
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">      iii)   Any other writ, order or direction which this
      Hon'ble Court may deem fit and proper in the facts and
      circumstances of the case, may also be granted in
      favour of the petitioner and the respondents along with
      the cost of the petition."
</blockquote><p id="p_3">3.    By virtue of the interim order of this Court dated
16.05.2014, the petitioner continues on the same post.
</p><p id="p_4">4.    Learned counsel for the respondents states that the
petitioner was transferred on March 04, 2014. The cause of action
for filing of this writ petition accrued to the petitioner when he
was to be relief in the year 2014. By virtue of the interim order
passed by this Court on 16.05.2014, the petitioner is continuing in
the said place for more than two years.
</p><p id="p_5">5.    The Full Bench of this Court in case Syed Hilal Ahmad and
ors v. State of J&amp;K and ors, SLJ 2015 (1) 01, has held that
Government servant has no enforceable right to insist that he/she
shall be permitted to serve for definite/specific period in a
reticular station or a post.
</p><p id="p_6">6.    In view of the Full Bench judgment passed by this Court in
case Syed Hilal's case (supra) it will not be appropriate for this
Court to allow the petitioner to continue on interim order. The
respondents' authority has to consider the case of the petitioner
for appropriate posting and that will meet the ends of justice.
</p><p id="p_7">7.    In this view of the matter, the petitioner is at liberty to make
a representation to respondent Nos.1 and 2 within a period of two
weeks from the date of receipt of copy of this order for posting
appropriately. The authority shall accord consideration within a
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                   3</span>




period of two weeks thereafter. Till such time a consideration
order is passed, present position of the petitioner need not to be
disturbed, if the petitioner is still continuing as per interim order
of Court.
</p><p id="p_8">7.   The writ petition is, accordingly, disposed of as above.
</p>


<p id="p_9">                                        ( Ramalingam Sudhakar)
                                             Judge
Jammu:
</p>
