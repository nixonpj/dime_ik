<p id="p_1">3   04-03-2015               Both these applications arise out of Jhajha P. S. Case

                 No. 105 of 2014 for the offence punishable under <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_1">Sections 341</a>,

                 <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_2">324</a>, <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_3">307</a>, <a href="/doc/1540253/" id="a_4">326</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_5">504</a>, <a href="/doc/1128948/" id="a_6">120(B)</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_7">34</a> IPC and 27 of the <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_8">Arms Act</a>.

</p><p id="p_2">                             Heard learned counsel for the petitioners and learned

                 A.P.P. for the State and learned counsel for the informant.
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">         Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.4814 of 2015 (3) dt.04-03-2015</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                   2/2</span>




</p><p id="p_3">                                     Considering the allegation and injury report submitted

                     by the doctor, the above named petitioners are directed to be

                     released on bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs.10,000/- (ten

                     thousand only) each with two sureties of the like amount each to

                     the satisfaction of Sri Shyamal Kumar, Judicial Magistrate, 1st

                     Class, Jamui /court concerned in Jhajha P. S. Case No. 105 of

                     2014 with following conditions:-
</p>
<p id="p_4">                        1.    The petitioners will not indulge themselves in similar or any

                              other offence.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                        2.    One of the bailors must be the close relative of the

                              petitioners.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                        3.    The petitioners will be well represented in the Court on

                              each and every date and in case of absence for two

                              consecutive dates or in case of violation of terms of bail,

                              their bail bonds would be liable to be cancelled by the

                              learned Court concerned.
</p>
<p id="p_7">


</p><p id="p_8">                                                (Amaresh Kumar Lal, J)
Kanchan/-
</p>
