<div class="doc_title">Shivam Singh vs State Of U.P. &amp; Anr. on 13 December, 2019</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: Anant Kumar</div>

<pre id="pre_1">




HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT ALLAHABAD, LUCKNOW BENCH
 
 

?Court No. - 15
 

 
Case :- CRIMINAL APPEAL No. - 1874 of 2019
 
Appellant :- Shivam Singh
 
Respondent :- State Of U.P. &amp; Anr.
 
Counsel for Appellant :- Shiv Shankar Singh
 
Counsel for Respondent :- Govt. Advocate,Dileep Kumar Gautam
 

 
Hon'ble Anant Kumar,J.
</pre> 
<p id="p_1">Counter affidavit filed on behalf of private respondent no.2 filed today is taken on record. Learned counsel for the appellant does not want to file rejoinder affidavit.
</p> 
<p id="p_2">Heard learned counsel for the appellant, the learned A.G.A. for the State as well as learned counsel for private respondent no.2 and perused the record.
</p> 
<p id="p_3">This criminal appeal has been filed by the appellant against impugned order dated 16.09.2019, passed by the Special Judge, SC/ST (P.A.) Act, Sultanpur in Bail Application No.2207 of 2019, (Shivam Singh &amp; another Versus State of U.P.), arising out of Case Crime No.328 of 2019, under <a href="/doc/838469/" id="a_1">Sections 452</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_2">323</a>, <a href="/doc/329571/" id="a_3">392</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_4">504</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_5">506</a>, <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_6">34</a> IPC and <a href="/doc/1507082/" id="a_7">Section 3</a> (1) (Dha) of SC/ST (P.A.) Act, Police Station Kadipur, District Sultanpur. Further a prayer has been made to enlarge the appellant/applicant on bail in the said case crime.
</p> 
<p id="p_4">As per version of the F.I.R. on 01.07.2019, in the morning at about 9.00 A.M. some dispute had taken place between the complainant and named accused persons whereupon the accused Shivam Singh made a phone call to some unknown persons whereupon six unknown persons had come on fourwheeler and had beaten the family members of the complainant and during this scuffle accused persons snatched chain of the complainant. While going from the scene of occurrence, they gave filthy abuses by caste and also threatened that in case they would do any proceedings, they would be killed. It is submitted by learned counsel for the appellant that neither any chain has been recovered, nor any specific name has been mentioned as to who snatched the chain. Injuries of the injured persons are simple in nature. 
</p> 
<p id="p_5">Learned A.G.A. as well as learned counsel for the complainant while opposing the bail have submitted that appellant Shivam Singh is involved in another case Crime No.329 of 2019, under <a href="/doc/1258372/" id="a_8">Sections 147</a>, <a href="/doc/763672/" id="a_9">148</a>, <a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_10">149</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_11">323</a>, <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_12">302</a>, <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_13">307</a> IPC and <a href="/doc/1507082/" id="a_14">Section 3</a> (2) (V) of SC/<a href="/doc/53524/" id="a_15">ST Act</a>, P.S. Kadipur, District Sultanpur. In case he is released on bail he will commit further offence. It is also stated that the said occurrence has also been committed in the same sequence. Learned counsel for the appellant has stated that appellant Shivam has already been granted bail by this Court in Bail Application No.1705 of 2019, so as on today there is no other case pending against him.  
</p> 
<p id="p_6">Accordingly, the appeal is allowed and impugned order dated 16.09.2019, passed by the Special Judge, SC/ST (P.A.) Act, Sultanpur in Bail Application No.2207 of 2019, (Shivam Singh &amp; another Versus State of U.P.), arising out of Case Crime No.328 of 2019, under <a href="/doc/838469/" id="a_16">Sections 452</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_17">323</a>, <a href="/doc/329571/" id="a_18">392</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_19">504</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_20">506</a>, <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_21">34</a> IPC and <a href="/doc/1724307/" id="a_22">Section 3</a> (1) (Dha) of SC/ST (P.A.) Act, Police Station Kadipur, District Sultanpur is set aside.
</p> 
<p id="p_7">Let appellant/applicant (Shivam Singh) be released on bail in the aforesaid case crime number on his furnishing a personal bond and two reliable sureties of the like amount to the satisfaction of the court concerned subject to following additional conditions :-
</p> 
<p id="p_8">(i) The appellant/applicant shall file an undertaking to the effect that he shall not seek any adjournment on the dates fixed for evidence when the witnesses are present in court. In case of default of this condition, it shall be open for the trial court to treat it as abuse of liberty of bail and pass orders in accordance with law.
</p> 
<p id="p_9">(ii) The appellant/applicant shall remain present before the trial court on each date fixed, either personally or through his counsel. In case of his absence, without sufficient cause, the trial court may proceed against him under <a href="/doc/77781517/" id="a_23">Section 229-A</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p> 
<p id="p_10">(iii) In case, the appellant/applicant misuses the liberty of bail during trial and in order to secure his presence, proclamation under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_24">Section 82</a> Cr.P.C. is issued and the applicant fails to appear before the court on the date fixed in such proclamation, then, the trial court shall initiate proceedings against him, in accordance with law, under <a href="/doc/119436650/" id="a_25">Section 174-A</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p> 
<p id="p_11">(iv) The appellant/applicant shall remain present, in person, before the trial court on the dates fixed for (i) opening of the case, (ii) framing of charge and (iii) recording of statement under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_26">Section 313</a> Cr.P.C. If in the opinion of the trial court absence of the applicant is deliberate or without sufficient cause, then it shall be open for the trial court to treat such default as abuse of liberty of bail and proceed against him in accordance with law.
</p> 
<p id="p_12">Order Date :- 13.12.2019
 
ML/-
</p>
