<p id="p_1">      Petitioner is the accused, in Crime No.1074 of 2013 of the

Kareelakulangara Police Station registered for the offences

punishable under <a href="/doc/556166/" id="a_1">Sections 468</a>, <a href="/doc/1466184/" id="a_2">471</a>, <a href="/doc/1326844/" id="a_3">409</a> and <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_4">420</a> of the Indian

Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_2">      2.    The allegation is that the petitioner was working as

General Manager of the Co-operative Spinning Mills from

January 2010 onwards by producing forged qualification

certificates.
</p>
<p id="p_3">      3.    Public Prosecutor opposed the anticipatory bail

application. C.D perused.
</p>
<p id="p_4">      4.    Learned Senior counsel for the petitioner submitted

that in 1985 the petitioner obtained Diploma in Textile

Technology with First class awarded by Kerala State Board of

Technical Education, on successful completion of three year

course from Central Polytechnic, Trivandrum.

</p><p id="p_5">      5.    Learned Senior counsel for the petitioner submitted

that on 26.09.1990 after working in private textile mills in

Tamil Nadu, petitioner applied and obtained selection and was

appointed as Supervisor at Trichur Co-operative Spinning Mills,

B. A.No. 978 of 2014
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                2</span>

one among the 14 co-operative spinning mills established in

the state of Kerala in the Public Sector and which is working

under the Industries Department, State of Kerala. Invoking

clause 6 of the order of appointment petitioner was transferred

to various other Co-operative Societies in the State during the

course of his service.
</p>
<p id="p_6">      6.    Learned Senior counsel for the petitioner submitted

that on 26.11.2009 the Government of Kerala was pleased to

accord sanction to depute the petitioner, who was then working

as Maintenance Supervisor at Trichur Co-operative Spinning

Mills as General Manager of Aleppey Co-operative Spinning

Mills for a period of one year.
</p>
<p id="p_7">      7.    Considering the facts and circumstance of the case I

am of the view that arrest and detention of the petitioner is

not required for the purpose of investigation. Hence this

petition is allowed.
</p>
<p id="p_8">      It is hereby directed that in the event of arrest of the

petitioner in connection with the above case, he shall be

released on bail on executing a bond for `25,000/- with two

sureties each for the like sum to the satisfaction of the officer

effecting arrest.    The petitioner is directed to appear before

the Investigating Officer as and when required. It is made clear

B. A.No. 978 of 2014
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                 3</span>

that if the petitioner surrenders before the Judicial First Class

Magistrate having jurisdiction, within 30 days from today, the

petitioner shall be released on bail on executing a bond for

`25,000/- with two sureties each for the like sum to the

satisfaction of the Judicial First Class Magistrate. In that case

also,  the petitioner is     directed to      appear before the

Investigating Officer as and when required.



</p><p id="p_9">                                        Sd/-</p><pre id="pre_2">
                            M.L.JOSEPH FRANCIS, JUDGE

AS                                      /True Copy/

                                        P.A. to Judge
