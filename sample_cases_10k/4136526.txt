       Shri   Neelesh Singh Tomar,        Advocate, for the
petitioner.
       Shri    N.S.   Kirar,     Panel   Lawyer,      for     the

<p id="p_1">       With the consent of the parties, the matter is
heard finally.
</p><p id="p_2">       In this writ petition, the petitioner inter-alia
seeks a direction to the respondents to calculate his
earlier services and accordingly make payment of
arrears and grant him consequential benefits.
</p><p id="p_3">       When the matter is taken up today, learned
counsel for the petitioner submitted that with regard
to his grievance, the petitioner has already filed
representations, Annexures P-5 (A) (B) &amp; (C) to the
respondent No.2 and the instant petition may be
disposed of with a direction to the said authority to
consider and decide the same.
</p><p id="p_4">       On     the   other   hand,      learned   Government
Advocate for the respsondent/State fairly submitted
that representation filed by the petitioner shall be
dealt with in accordance with law.
</p><p id="p_5">       Taking into account the aforesaid submissions
made by the learned counsel for the parties and as
agreed to by them, the writ petition is disposed of
with    a     direction     to   the     respondent         No.2-
Commissioner Tribal Welfare, Bhopal to consider and
decide the representation Annexures P-5 (A) (B) &amp; Â©
preferred by the petitioner in accordance with law by
                                                  2 W.P.3166/2017

       a speaking order expeditiously preferably within a
       period of two months from the date of production of
       certified copy of this order. It is made clear that this
       Court has not expressed any opinion on the merits of
       the case.
</p><p id="p_6">            Certified copy as per rules.</p><pre id="pre_2">
