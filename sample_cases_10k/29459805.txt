                   Arising Out of PS.Case No. -41 Year- 1990 Thana -NAWADAH COMPLAINT CASE District-
                                                           NAWADA
                 ======================================================
                 Md. Nasim, son of Abdul Rahman, resident of Village Narhat, P.S. Narhat
                 in the district of Nawada
                                                                       .... .... Appellant/s
                                                   Versus
                 1. The State of Bihar
                 2. Abbas Mian, son of Abdul Subhan Mian
                 3. Shyamuddin Mian, son of Abdul Subhan Mian
                 4. Arjani Khatoon, Wife of Abbas Mian
                 5. Ashraf Mian, son of Nawi Mian
                     All are resident of Village Radhwa, P.S. Kauakol, in the district of
                     Nawada
                 6. Ajim Mian, son of Idu Mian
                 7. Bano Khatoon, wife of Azim Mian
                     Both are resident of Village Sarauni, P.S. Kauakol, in the district of
                     Nawada
                                                                      .... .... Respondent/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Appellant         :  Mr. Sunil Kumar, Advocate
                                              Mr. Ranjeet Kumar, Advocate
                 For the State             :  Mr. Ajay Mishra, A.P.P.
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE V.N. SINHA
                             and
                             HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE PRABHAT KUMAR JHA
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>
<p id="p_1">                 (Per: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE V.N. SINHA)

3   07-11-2014                   Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and the State.

</p><p id="p_2">                                 2. Complainant/ informant of Kauakol P.S. Case No.

                 41/90 has filed this appeal assailing the judgment dated

                 24.05.2014, passed by Ad hoc Additional District and Sessions

                 Judge-III, Nawada, in Sessions Trial No. 229 of 2008/ 29 of 2013

                 whereunder charges under <a href="/doc/653797/" id="a_1">Sections 304-B</a>, <a href="/doc/386021/" id="a_2">201</a> of the Penal Code

                 levelled against respondent nos. 2 to 7 has failed and they have

                 been acquitted.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Patna High Court CR. APP (DB) No.845 of 2014 (3) dt.07-11-2014</span><p id="p_3">

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                          2/3</span>




</p><p id="p_4">                              3. It appears, initially Complaint Case No. 41/90 was

             filed for the offences under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_3">Sections 302</a>, <a href="/doc/386021/" id="a_4">201</a> of the Penal Code

             alleging that the sister of the complainant was done to death by her

             husband and the in-laws. Aforesaid complaint was sent to the

             concerned police station for registration of First Information

             Report and investigation. After investigation police submitted

             final form whereafter protest petition was filed, which was

             entertained, cognizance taken for the offences under <a href="/doc/1439698/" id="a_5">Sections 304-</a>

             B/ 201<a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_6"> of the Penal Code</a> and the case committed to the Court of

             Sessions for trial. During trial the complainant and other witnesses

             asserted that the respondents deliberately killed his sister for her

             failure to bring dowry.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                              4. Aforesaid prosecution case has not been believed

             by the trial Court, as it has found that the deceased was pregnant

             and she died in the night of occurrence on account of premature

             abortion and excessive bleeding. From the evidence of the

             complainant/ informant itself, it is evident that he candidly

             admitted before the Court that he had not filed any petition for

             post mortem of the dead body and he cannot say the name of the

             person who informed him about the incident. Prosecution case has

             further been disbelieved as there is contradiction in the evidence

             of the prosecution witnesses i.e. P.W. 4 and others about demand
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">            Patna High Court CR. APP (DB) No.845 of 2014 (3) dt.07-11-2014</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                                                     3/3</span>




                        of dowry and the death caused for failure to bring dowry. The

                        view taken by the trial Court appears to be plausible. In the

                        circumstances, we are not inclined to interfere with the impugned

                        judgment. Appeal is dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">                                                           (V.N. Sinha, J)


                                                  (Prabhat Kumar Jha, J)
Rajesh/-
</p>
