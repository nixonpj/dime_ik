<div class="doc_title">Kesa vs State on 21 April, 2017</div><p id="p_1">     HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR RAJASTHAN AT
                      JODHPUR
               S.B. Criminal Misc. Bail No. 3204 / 2017
Kesa S/o Naya Meena, R/o Jodhsagar Ki Bhagal, Police Thana
Salumbar, Distt. Udaipur.
</p><p id="p_2">(At Lodged in Sub Jail Salumbar)
</p><p id="p_3">                                                           ----Petitioner
                                  Versus
The State of Rajasthan
</p><p id="p_4">                                                         ----Respondent
_____________________________________________________
For Petitioner(s)   : Mr. Jitendra Ojha.
For Respondent(s) : Mr. R.K. Bohra, P.P. for the State.
_____________________________________________________
                HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE P.K. LOHRA
                         Judgment / Order
21/04/2017

     By the instant bail application, accused-petitioner has craved

for grant of bail in connection with FIR No.41/2016-17, registered

at Excise Police Station Salumber, District Udaipur.        In the FIR,

petitioner is charged for offence punishable under Section 16/54

of the Rajasthan Excise Act.       At the behest of petitioner, earlier

endeavor was made for grant of bail by laying S.B. Criminal Bail

Application No.782/2017, but the same was dismissed as not

pressed.
</p>
<p id="p_5">     It is submitted by learned counsel for the petitioner that

after dismissal of first bail application, there is change is

circumstances inasmuch as investigation has been completed and

charge-sheet has been filed against the petitioner.             Learned

counsel for the petitioner would contend that alongwith the

charge-sheet    a   letter   of   Excise   Department,    Salumber,    is
                                 (2 of 2)
                                                      [CRLMB-3204/2017]

submitted containing recitals that no other case against petitioner

for offence under the Rajasthan Excise Act is registered.     Lastly,

learned counsel has submitted that the petitioner is in custody

since 05.01.2017, therefore, favourable consideration of his

second bail application is desirable.

</p><p id="p_6">      Learned Public Prosecutor has opposed the bail application of

petitioner in general.
</p>
<p id="p_7">      Having heard learned counsel for the petitioner and upon

perusal of the challan papers and taking into account the

aforementioned changed circumstances, without expressing any

opinion on merits of the case, I feel persuaded to accede to the

prayer of petitioner to grant him bail on his second bail

application.
</p>
<p id="p_8">      Accordingly, the bail application under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> Cr.P.C. is

allowed and it is ordered that the accused-petitioner, Kesa S/o

Naya Meena, arrested in connection with FIR No.41/2016-17,

Excise Police Station Salumber, District Udaipur, may be released

on bail; provided he furnishes a personal bond of Rs.25,000/- with

two surety bonds of like amount to the satisfaction of learned trial

Court with the stipulation to appear before that Court on all dates

of hearing and as and when called upon to do so.




</p><p id="p_9">                                                   (P.K. LOHRA)J.
</p>
