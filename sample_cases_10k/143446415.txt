<p id="p_1">Shaffique, J.
</p>
<p id="p_2">      The petitioner has approached this Court seeking police

protection.   It is inter alia submitted that the petitioner has

married one Sathya, the daughter of the 4th respondent. They are

living at their native place.      But, there is threat from the 4th

respondent and his men.
</p>
<p id="p_3">      2.   The petitioner had already approached this Court for

anticipatory bail.  This Court, recording the submission of the

learned Public Prosecutor that the police will not arrest the

petitioner, dismissed the said bail application.

</p><p id="p_4">      3.   Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that a

Habeas Corpus petition is pending before the High Court of Tamil

Nadu in regard to missing of his wife, Sathya. If that be the case

and if there is an order from the High Court or from the

Magistrate Court to produce the petitioner or his wife, the police

will have to comply with the same.

</p><p id="p_5">      4.   We only observe that if the 4th respondent or his men

commit any offence or attempt to commit offence, on the request

W.P.(C) No.16124 of 2015

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                -: 2 :-</span>

of the petitioner, the police shall look into the matter and do the

needful.
</p>
<p id="p_6">           With the above observation, this writ petition is closed.

</p><p id="p_8">                                              ASHOK BHUSHAN
