<div class="doc_title">Smt. Vinita Bai vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 4 November, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                                  1
 Smt. Vinita Bai Vs. State of M.P.          M.Cr.C.No.11954/2015

 04/11/2015
       Mr. Prasoon Maheshwari Advocate for the applicant.
       Ms. (Dr.) Anajali Gyanani, Govt. Advocate for the
respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">       Case Diary is perused.
</p><p id="p_2">       Learned counsel for the rival parties are heard.
       The applicant has filed this 3rd application u/S 439,
<a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_1">Cr.P.C</a>. for grant of bail. The applicant has been arrested by
Police Station Pathari, District Vidisha in connection with Crime
No.33/2015 registered in relation to the offences punishable
u/Ss. 302 and 34 of <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_2">IPC</a>.
</p><p id="p_3">       Learned Public Prosecutor for the State opposed the
application and prayed for its rejection by contending that on
the basis of the allegations and the material available on
record, no case for grant of bail is made out.
</p><p id="p_4">       The applicant is in custody since 28.03.2015. This repeat
bail application has been filed after rejection of the earlier one
on 15.6.2015 in shape of M.Cr.C. No. 9931/2015. The trial
was fixed on 29.9. 2015 , 26.10.2015 and 27.10.2015 where
no prosecution witness came forward and the matter is now
listed for 23rd and 24th of November, 2015.
</p><p id="p_5">      Though very little time has elapsed since rejection of
the last bail application but considering the nature of allegation
and the fact that the applicant is a woman aged about 31
years and early conclusion of the trial is a bleak possibility and
prolonged pre-trial detention is anathema to the concept of
liberty and the material placed on record does not discloses
the possibility of the applicant fleeing from justice, this Court is
inclined to extend the benefit of bail to the applicant but with
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                                       2</span>
      Smt. Vinita Bai Vs. State of M.P.         M.Cr.C.No.11954/2015

     certain stringent conditions, in view of nature of offence.
</p><p id="p_6">            Accordingly, without expressing any opinion on merits of
     the case, this application is allowed and it is directed that the
     applicant be released on bail on furnishing a personal bond in
     the sum of Rs.1,00,000/- (Rs. One Lac only) with two solvent
     sureties of the like amount to the satisfaction of the
     concerned Trial Court.
</p><p id="p_7">            This order will remain operative subject to compliance of
     the following conditions by the applicant :-
</p><p id="p_8">        1. The applicant will comply with all the terms and
            conditions of the bond executed by her;
</p><p id="p_9">        2. The applicant will cooperate in the investigation/trial, as
            the case may be;
</p><p id="p_10">        3. The applicant will not indulge herself in extending
            inducement, threat or promise to any person acquainted
            with the facts of the case so as to dissuade him/her from
            disclosing such facts to the Court or to the Police Officer,
            as the case may be;
</p><p id="p_11">        4. The applicant shall not commit an offence similar to the
            offence of which she is accused;
</p><p id="p_12">        5. The applicant will not seek unnecessary adjournments
            during the trial; and
</p><p id="p_13">        6. The applicant will not leave India without previous
            permission of the trial Court/Investigating Officer, as the
            case may be.
</p><p id="p_14">        7. The applicant will mark er attendance before the
            concerning trial court once every fortnight
                   A copy of this order be sent to the Court
     concerned for compliance.
</p><p id="p_15">            C.c. as per rules.
</p><p id="p_16">                                                    (Sheel Nagu)
                                                       Judge
ar
