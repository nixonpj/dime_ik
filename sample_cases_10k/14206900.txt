<div class="doc_title">Pawan Singh &amp; Anr vs State on 3 May, 2018</div><p id="p_1">     HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR RAJASTHAN AT
                      JODHPUR
      S.B. Suspension Of Sentence(Appeal) No. 1264 / 2017
</p><p id="p_2">1. Pawan Singh S/o Pabu Singh, Aged About 25 Years, By Caste
Rajput, R/o Mandarpura, P.S. Nohar, District Hanumangarh
(Rajasthan)

</p><p id="p_3">2. Surendra Singh S/o Jattan Singh, By Caste Rajput, R/o
Mandarpura, P.S. Nohar, District Hanumangarh (Rajasthan) (At
Present Lodged in District Jail, Hanumangarh)
</p><p id="p_4">                                                          ----Appellants
                                Versus
The State of Rajasthan
</p><p id="p_5">                                                        ----Respondent
_____________________________________________________
For Appellant(s)    : Mr. Avinash Bhati
For Respondent(s) : Mr. V.N. Kalla
                       Mr. O.P. Rathi, P.P.
</p><p id="p_6">_____________________________________________________
    HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE RAMCHANDRA SINGH JHALA
                                Order
03/05/2018

            Heard   learned   counsel    for   the   parties   on   the

application for suspension of sentence.


</p><p id="p_7">            Learned counsel for the accused-applicants relied

upon the judgment rendered by Hon'ble Supreme Court in the

case of Babu Singh &amp; Ors. Vs. State of U.P. reported in 1978 AIR

527, 1978 SCR(2) 777.
</p>
<p id="p_8">
</p><p id="p_9">            Learned counsel for the complainant has submitted

that the applicants were not on bail during the trial, therefore,

the instant bail application filed by the applicants may kindly be

rejected.   He relied upon the judgment rendered by Calcutta
                                  (2 of 3)
                                                             [SOSA-1264/2017]

High Court in the case of Anil Ari Vs. State of West Bengal

reported in 2009 AIR SCW 1090.
</p>
<p id="p_10">
</p><p id="p_11">              In my considered view, it is not a ground to reject

the application under <a href="/doc/985477/" id="a_1">Section 389</a> Cr.P.C. that the accused-

applicants were not on bail during the trial.          At the time of

disposal of application under <a href="/doc/985477/" id="a_2">Section 389</a> Cr.P.C., the Court is to

see whether the accused-applicants have arguable case or not.

If the accused-applicants have strong arguable case, then

rejection of application under <a href="/doc/985477/" id="a_3">Section 389</a> Cr.P.C. is not justified.


</p><p id="p_12">              Looking to the document Ex.P/11, the delay in filing

the FIR, statement of prosecution witnesses, this Court is

satisfied that the accused-applicants have strong arguable case,

disposal of appeal will take time and no previous conviction is on

record against the accused-applicants and in view of the law laid

down by Honb'ble Supreme Court in the case of Babu Singh &amp;

Ors. (supra), it is a fit case to suspend the sentence awarded by

the trial court.
</p>
<p id="p_13">
</p><p id="p_14">              Having   considered    the    totality   of   facts    and

circumstances of the case, I consider it just and proper to

suspend the substantive sentence awarded to the accused-

applicants.
</p>
<p id="p_15">
</p><p id="p_16">              Accordingly, the bail application filed under Sec.389

<a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_4">Cr.P.C</a>. is allowed and it is ordered that the substantive sentence

passed by the trial court vide judgment dated 7.12.2017 in

Sessions Case No.11/2016 against applicants No.1 Pawan Singh

S/o Pabu Singh and No.2 Surendra Singh S/o Jattan Singh shall
                                (3 of 3)
                                                       [SOSA-1264/2017]

remain suspended till final disposal of the aforesaid appeal,

provided each of them executes a personal bond in the sum of

Rs.50,000/- with two sureties of Rs.25,000/- each to the

satisfaction of the learned trial Judge for his appearance before

this court on 7.6.2018 and whenever ordered to do so with the

incorporation in the bond that as and when he will shift his place

of residence, he will intimate to this Court and his lawyer about

his new place of residence.
</p>
<p id="p_17">


</p><p id="p_18">                                  (RAMCHANDRA SINGH JHALA)J.
</p>
<p id="p_19">Babulal/47
