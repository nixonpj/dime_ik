<div class="doc_title">Ram Kumar Ganjhu vs The State Of Jharkhand on 20 June, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                           B.A. No. 4803 of 2018
                                 .....
</pre><pre id="pre_2">             Ram Kumar Ganjhu                             ..... Petitioner
                                      -V e r s u s-
             The State of Jharkhand                 ..... Opposite Party
                                 .....
</pre><p id="p_1">             CORAM: HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE ANANDA SEN
                                 .....</p><pre id="pre_3">
              For the Petitioner      : Mr. P. S.Dayal, Advocate
              For the State           : A.P.P
2/20.06.2018        Heard learned counsel for the parties.
</pre><p id="p_2">                    The petitioner is an accused for allegedly committing
         offence punishable under <a href="/doc/695990/" id="a_1">Sections 364</a>, <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_2">302</a>, <a href="/doc/386021/" id="a_3">201</a> and <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_4">34</a> of the
         Indian Penal Code.
</p><p id="p_3">                    Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that the
         petitioner is not named in the F.I.R. and the name of the petitioner
         transpired from the confessional statement of the co-accused
         persons. He further submits that the similarly situated co-accused
         persons have been granted bail by coordinate Benches of this Court
         in B.A. Nos. 120 of 2017, B.A. No. 2381 of 2018, B.A. No. 3752 of
         2018 and B.A. No. 1546 of 2016, respectively and the case of the
         petitioner stands on similar footing.
</p><p id="p_4">                    Learned Addl. P.P. opposes the prayer for bail, but cannot
         deny the aforesaid fact.
</p><p id="p_5">                    Taking into consideration the period of custody, I am
         inclined to allow this application. Accordingly, the petitioner, namely
         Ram Kumar Ganjhu, is directed to be released on bail on furnishing
         bail bonds of Rs. 10,000/- (Rupees Ten Thousand) with two sureties
         of the like amount each to the satisfaction of the learned Additional
         Chief Judicial Magistrate, Ramgarh, in connection with Mandu,
         Police Station Case No. 438 of 2015, corresponding to G.R. No. 4302
         of 2015.
</p>


<p id="p_6">                                                             (Ananda Sen, J.)
      Amar/cp3
