<div class="doc_title">Ajay Kumar Singh vs The State Of Bihar on 14 October, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                 Criminal Miscellaneous No.42832 of 2013

                          Arising Out of PS.Case No. -172 Year- 2011 Thana -THAKRAHA District-
                                             WESTCHAMPARAN(BETTIAH)
                 ======================================================
                 1. Ajay Kumar Singh S/O Sri Prahlad Singh Resident Of 37, Mohaddipur,
                 Gorakhpur, P.S.- Gorakhpur, District- Gorakhpur (UP)
                                                                                .... ....   Petitioner/s
                                                       Versus

                 1. The State Of Bihar
                                                            .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s : Mr. Subhash Kumar Jha, Advocate
                 For the Opposite Party/s      :    Mr. Chandra Bhushan Prasad (APP)
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE VIKASH JAIN
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">5   14-10-2014                  Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and

                  learned APP for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                2. The petitioner apprehends his arrest for the

                  offences alleged under <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_1">Sections 420</a>, <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_2">406</a> of the Indian Penal

                  Code registered in connection with Thakraha P.S. Case No.

                  172 of 2011.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                3. It is submitted that the petitioner being the

                  contractor in question has been falsely implicated in a

                  criminal case in a transaction of pure contractual nature for

                  which the relevant agreement itself provided for adequate

                  remedy in case of disputes or dissatisfaction of the authority

                  with regard to work done. In any event, there was no question

                  of any misappropriation of Government fund by the petitioner
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">          Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.42832 of 2013 (5) dt.14-10-2014                  2</span>




                        in view of the report of the Chief Engineer addressed to the

                        Superintendent of Police, Economic Offence Unit, Patna, to the

                        effect that the value of the work done was not short of the

                        payment made on the basis of N.I.T. report. Other co-accused

                        Vijay Kumar Singh, Masood Alam etc. have been granted

                        anticipatory bail by this Court.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                       4. Having regard to the entirety of the facts and

                      circumstances of the case, in the event of the petitioner's arrest

                      or surrender before the court below within six weeks from the

                      date of communication of this order, let the above named

                      petitioner be released on bail on furnishing bail bond of

                      Rs.10,000/- (ten thousand) with two sureties of like amount

                      each to the satisfaction of learned Additional Chief Judicial

                      Magistrate, Bagha, West Champaran, in connection with

                      Thakraha P.S. Case No. 172 of 2011, G.R. No. 1529 of 2011,

                      subject to the conditions as laid down under <a href="/doc/1144664/" id="a_3">Section 438</a> (2)

                      <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_4">Cr.P.C</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_5">


</p><p id="p_6">                                                                        (Vikash Jain, J)
P.K./-
</p>
