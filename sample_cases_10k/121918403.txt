IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR RAJASTHAN
                 AT JODHPUR

                       O R D E R
</pre>
<p id="p_1">    D.B. CIVIL WRIT PETITION NO.1645/2012

               DR. ANSHU SHEKHAWAT
                       Vs.
</p><p id="p_2">             STATE OF RAJASTHAN &amp; ORS.
</p>
<p id="p_3">DATE:11.12.2012

   HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE NARENDRA KUMAR JAIN
       HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE R.S. CHAUHAN


Mr. Ravindra Singh, for the petitioner.
Mr. Khet Singh Rajpurohit, for respondents.
Mr. Girish Joshi, for respondent UGC.
</p><p id="p_4">                   ****

           Learned     counsel        for       the       parties

agreed     that    point   involved          in      this       writ

petition    has    already         been   decided         by      the

Division     Bench    of   this       Court,         at     Jaipur

Bench,     which     has   been       followed            at      the

Principal Seat of this Court also in D.B.

Civil    Writ     Petition     No.7642/2011-Ripudaman

Singh Ujjwal Vs. State(Education) &amp; Ors., and

other      connected         writ         petitions,               on

01.10.2012.        Order      dated        01.10.2012              is

reproduced as under:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">                "It is frankly submitted that
           with the order dated 13.09.2012
           as passed by the Division Bench
           of this Court at Jaipur Bench in
           the batch of petitions led by
           D.B.     Civil    Writ    Petition
           No.8582/2011:    Ravindra    Singh
           Shekhawat Vs. Union of India &amp;
           Ors., the issue involved in these
           writ petitions stands squarely
           covered.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">                                   {2}     DB CIVIL WRIT PETITION 1645/2012




                In view of the above, these
            writ petitions stand dismissed
            following the aforesaid order
            dated   13.09.2012 in  Ravindra
            Singh Shekhawat and in the same
            terms.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_3">                   No costs."</blockquote><p id="p_5">


</p><p id="p_6">             As agreed by the parties, the present

writ petition stands dismissed, in view of

order     dated      01.10.2012         passed       in    Ripudaman

Singh      Ujjwal       Vs.     State(Education)               &amp;     Ors.

(supra) and other connected writ petitions,

as reproduced above.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

(R.S. CHAUHAN), J.                (NARENDRA KUMAR JAIN),J.
