<div class="doc_title">Date Of Decision: 03.07.2012 vs State Of Punjab &amp; Ors on 3 July, 2012</div><pre id="pre_1">  HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA AT CHANDIGARH
                               ****
                    CWP No.5889 of 2008 (O&amp;M)
                    Date of Decision: 03.07.2012
                               ****
Kulwant Kaur &amp; Ors.                             . . . . Petitioners
                             VS.

State of Punjab &amp; Ors.                                               . . . . Respondents
                                               ****
CORAM : HON'BLE MR.JUSTICE SURYA KANT
        HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE R.P. NAGRATH
                                               ****
1. Whether Reporters of local papers may be allowed to see the judgment?
2. To be referred to the Reporters or not?
3. Whether the judgment should be reported in the Digest?
                                     ****
Present:       Mr. BR Mahajan, Advocate for the petitioners
               Ms. Monica Chhibber Sharma, DAG Punjab
               Mr. Gurminder Singh, Advocate for respondents No.2&amp;3
                                     ****
SURYA KANT, J. (ORAL)
</pre>
<p id="p_1">(1).             The petitioners seek quashing of the order dated 28.05.2002

                 (Annexure P2) passed by the Estate Officer, PUDA, SAS Nagar,

                 Mohali which has been further upheld in appeal and revision

                 whereby site of SCO No.511, Sector 70, Mohali allotted to the

                 petitioner stands resumed and the appeal etc. against the

                 resumption order have been turned down.
</p>
<p id="p_2">(2).             The above-stated resumption order was passed on account of

                 non-payment of the due instalments.
</p>
<p id="p_3">(3).             It was stated on March 9, 2009 that more than Rs.93 lacs have

                 already been deposited by the petitioners and they are ready

                 and willing to clear all the dues including penal and compound

                 interest as well as penalty, if any, imposed by the respondents.

                 Learned counsel for the respondents was directed to have

                 instructions in this regard.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> CWP No.5889 of 2008.doc                                             -2-</span><p id="p_4">




(4).         On April 20, 2009, it was stated that the offer made by the

             petitioners for withdrawal of the resumption order was not

             acceptable though fresh allotment in their favour could be made

             "on the market price". The matter was consequently adjourned

             to enable the respondents to inform the petitioners the market

             price for re-allotment of the subject site.
</p>
<p id="p_5">(5).         It is stated by learned counsel for the petitioners that the

             respondents have informed that the current market price of the

             subject site is Rs.3,80,76,197/-.     The petitioners have been

             further informed that after deducting the amount of Rs.93 lacs

             already deposited by them, the balance amount comes out to be

             Rs.2,87,58,197/-.
</p>
<p id="p_6">(6).         Learned counsel for the petitioners submits that given some

             reasonable time, the petitioners are ready and willing to deposit

             the above-stated amount of Rs.2,87,58,197/- along with future

             interest.
</p>
<p id="p_7">(7).         In view of the above-mentioned categoric stand taken by the

             parties before us, we deem it appropriate to dispose of this writ

             petition with a direction that if the petitioners deposit the

             above-stated balance amount along with future interest at the

             rate prescribed by the respondents under the Rules/Policy in

             four quarterly instalments, the subject site shall be re-allotted

             to them. However, in the event of failure to deposit the above-

             stated amount, the resumption order already passed against the

             petitioners shall stand revived.
</p>
<p id="p_8">(8).         The first instalment shall be deposited by the petitioners before

             31st July, 2012.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_2"> CWP No.5889 of 2008.doc                                   -3-</span><p id="p_9">




</p><pre id="pre_2">(9).             Ordered accordingly. Dasti.

                                               (SURYA KANT)
                                                   Judge


03.07.2012
vishal shonkar
                                               (R.P. NAGRATH)
                                                     Judge
