<div class="doc_title">Balak Ram vs State Of U.P. on 2 March, 2012</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: S.C. Agarwal</div>

<pre id="pre_1">




HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT ALLAHABAD, LUCKNOW BENCH
 
 

?Court No. - 11
 

 
Case :- BAIL No. - 1709 of 2012
 

 
Petitioner :- Balak Ram
 
Respondent :- State Of U.P.
 
Petitioner Counsel :- Vinod Kumar Misra,Mukesh Kumar Tewari
 
Respondent Counsel :- Govt.Advocate
 

 
Hon'ble S.C. Agarwal,J.
</pre> 
<p id="p_1">Heard learned counsel for the applicant, learned A.G.A. and perused the record.
</p> 

 
<p id="p_2">Learned counsel for the applicant submitted that in  the alleged occurrence, the applicant is a marginal witness of the forged sale deed. It is further submitted that the applicant has been falsely implicated in this case due to enmity. The applicant is not beneficiary in the alleged sale-deed. The dispute is purely of civil nature. The application for lodging of the F.I.R. under Section 156 (3) <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_1">Cr.P.C</a>. has been moved after a long gap of about two and half years without any plausible explanation regarding delay in lodging of the F.I.R. The applicant has no previous criminal history and is languishing in jail since 08.08.2011. The offence so levelled against the applicant is triable by the court of Magistrate. 
</p> 
<p id="p_3">Learned A.G.A. opposed the prayer for bail.
</p> 
<p id="p_4">Co-accused Keshan @ Sri Keshan having identical role, has been granted bail by another Bench of this Court vide order dated 29.2.2012 passed in Bail Application No. 1554 of 2012.
</p> 
<p id="p_5">In view of the above and without expressing any opinion on the merits of the case, I find it to be a fit case for bail, let the applicant Balak Ram involved in Case Crime No. 292 of 2011, under <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_2">Section 420</a> IPC, P.S. Laharpur, District-Sitapur be released on bail on his furnishing a personal bond and two sureties each in the like amount to the satisfaction of the Court concerned with the following conditions:- 
</p> 
<p id="p_6">(a) The applicant shall attend the court according to the conditions of the bond executed by him;
</p> 

 
<p id="p_7">(b) The applicant shall not directly or indirectly make any inducement, threat or promise to any person acquainted with the facts of the case so as to dissuade him from disclosing such facts to the Court or to any police officer or tamper with the evidence. 
</p> 
<p id="p_8">Order Date :- 2.3.2012
 
Ashish Nayan
