<div class="doc_title">Nathi Yadav @ Jagannath Yadav vs The State Of Bihar on 18 November, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                              CRIMINAL MISCELLANEOUS No.72288 of 2019
                      Arising Out of PS. Case No.-124 Year-2019 Thana- CHANDAN District- Banka
                 ======================================================
                 Nathi Yadav @ Jagannath Yadav, Son of Late Harkhu Yadav Resident of
                 Village- Gondara, P.S.- Chandan (Anandpur O.P.), District- Banka.
                                                                              ... ... Petitioner/s
                                                   Versus
                 The State of Bihar
                                                                       ... ... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :      Mr.Ajay Mukherjee
                 For the Opposite Party/s :      Mr.Bhanu Pratap Singh
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE RAJENDRA KUMAR
                 MISHRA
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   18-11-2019                     Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and the

                   learned A.P.P. for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                   The petitioner apprehends his arrest in connection

                   with Chandan (Anandpur) P.S. Case no. 124 of 2019, registered

                   under <a href="/doc/1535430/" id="a_1">Sections 328</a> and <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_2">302</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_3">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code.

</p><p id="p_3">                                   The accusation is that in the matrimonial house of

                   Bindu Devi, sister of the informant Kamal Yadav, dispute

                   occurred in which Naren Yadav and Balkishore Yadav, both

                   sons of Nathi Yadav (petitioner) and Ranju Devi killer her

                   sister by administering poison.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                   Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that,

                   admittedly, the petitioner is the father-in-law of the deceased

                   Bindu Devi, wife of Bal Kishore Yadav and due to quarrel in

                   between Bal Kishore Yadav, husband of deceased, and Naren
            Patna High Court CR. MISC. No.72288 of 2019(2) dt.18-11-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                       2/2</span>




                     Yadav, another son of petitioner, petitioner Nathi Yadav

                     decided partition of land between them and in that course the

                     paper which was taken by the husband of the deceased vacant

                     land was shown in her favour, whereas, residential house and

                     some other land was come in the share of Naren Yadav, another

                     son of petitioner, due to that reasons, Bindu Devi, wife of

                     Balkishore Yadav committed suicide by consuming poison, but

                     the petitioner, who is father-in-law of the deceased has falsely

                     been implicated in this case. Moreover, Bal Kishore Yadav,

                     husband of deceased is in custody. The petitioner has no

                     criminal antecedent.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                     Having regard to the facts and circumstances of the

                     case, let the above named petitioner, be released on bail, in the

                     event of his arrest or surrender before the learned Court below

                     within a period of four weeks from today, on furnishing bail

                     bond of Rs. 10,000/- (Ten thousand) with two sureties of the like

                     amount each to the satisfaction of the learned Chief Judicial

                     Magistrate, Banka in connection with Chandan (Anandpur) P.S.

                     Case No. 124 of 2019, subject to the condition as laid down

                     under Section 438 (2) of the Cr.P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><p id="p_7">                                         (Rajendra Kumar Mishra, J)
manish/-
</p>
