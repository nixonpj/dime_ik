                           EC No. 143 of 2009
                      IN THE HIGH COURT AT CALCUTTA
                  Ordinary Original Civil Jurisdiction


                             GITA DEY &amp; ORS.
                                  Versus
                          NALINIBALA DEY &amp; ORS.


    BEFORE:
    The Hon'ble JUSTICE SOUMEN SEN</pre>
<p id="p_1">    Date: 20th November 2014.
</p><p id="p_2">                                                               Appearance:
</p><p id="p_3">                                                    Mr. Arif Ali, Advocate
                                                Mr. R. B. Prasad, Advocate
                                                    ...for the plaintiffs.
</p><p id="p_4">                                                   Mr. Sakya Sen, Advocate
                                         Mr. P.C. Paul Chowdhury, Advocate
                                                Mr. D. Mukherjee, Advocate
                                                    ...for the defendants.
</p>
<p id="p_5">
    COURT:       The plaintiffs want to circumvent the orders passed

by this court and has sought to create third party interest in the

properties during the pendency of the execution application, when

admittedly the Special Officer was in possession of the properties

and in the process of implementing the final decree.

    The Special Officer submits that demarcation has been made of

Schedule 'P' property but the actual handing over of demarcated

portion to the respective co-sharers could not be effected since

some persons alleged that they had acquired some interest in the

property upon payment of consideration to Mr. Susanta Dey, one of

the plaintiffs.
</p>
<p id="p_6">    Since the Special Officer is in charge of the properties, the

Special     Officer   shall   ignore   such   resistance,   if   any,   and

implement    the order, if required,      with the help of       the local
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                               2</span>

police authorities since any such transfer would be considered

void ab initio.          Insofar as the building at Nayachak is concerned,

the Special Officer is empowered to break open the padlock and

give       possession    to    the    respective   parties    according   to   their

declared shares since the plaintiffs did not come forward to open

the padlock and the other co-sharers would contend that they do

not have the keys.            The parties are directed to cooperate with the

Special Officer.          The Plaintiffs shall cooperate with the Special

Officer in handing over the respective demarcated portions to the

parties in terms of the final decree.
</p>
<p id="p_7">       The report filed by the Special Officer is kept with the

record.       The Special Officer shall file a further report on 18th

December 2014.          The Special Officer shall be entitled to a further

remuneration of 1000 GM to be shared by the plaintiffs and the

defendants in equal share.
</p>
<p id="p_8">       The matter will appear on 18th December 2014.

       Learned counsel appearing on behalf of the plaintiffs submits

that an application for review of the order passed by the Hon'ble

Division       Bench    accepting       the   report   of    the   Commissioner   of

Partition is pending.                But, as I find out from the submission,

there is no dispute with regard to the seven properties and this

direction is passed for the purpose of effecting partition of

these seven properties.
</p>
<p id="p_9">

</p><p id="p_10">                                                            ( SOUMEN SEN, J. )

S. Kumar
A.R.(CR)
