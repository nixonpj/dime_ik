<div class="doc_title">Parimal Chandra Ghosh vs State Of West Bengal &amp; Ors on 10 April, 2019</div><p id="p_1"><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                             1</span>


 10.04.2019
  rpan/274
  ct. no.17
                              W. P. No.1573 (W) of 2019
                                  Parimal Chandra Ghosh
</p><p id="p_2">                                           -vs-
</p><p id="p_3">                               State of West Bengal &amp; Ors.
</p>
<p id="p_4">        Mr. Ajay Barman Ray
                  ... for the petitioner.
</p><p id="p_5">        Mr. Ibrahim Shaikh
                       ... for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_6">       Affidavit of service filed in Court today is kept with the record.

       The material facts of the case are admitted and hence I have not called for

affidavits.
</p>
<p id="p_7">       The petitioner was appointed as an Assistant Teacher of a High School who

retired from service on 31st January, 2010. The first pension payment order was issued

on 19th March, 2010. Under the ROPA Rules, 2009 the pension payment order towards

revised benefits was issued on 15th March, 2013 and the benefits of revised arrear

pension amount was disbursed on 24th June, 2013. The petitioner claims interest on

delayed payment of the revised arrear pension amount.

</p><p id="p_8">       There is a considerable delay in filing of the writ petition, which the petitioner

seeks to justify by stating that there is no statutory period of limitation and neither

parties have suffered due to this delay. It is the submission of the petitioner that

accordingly the petition should be allowed. The petitioner relies upon an order in W.P.

No.17557 (W) of 2017 (Narayan Chandra Saha Vs. State of West Bengal &amp; ors.) wherein a

co-ordinate Bench had relied upon the Supreme Court judgement in the case of Union

of India Vs. Tarsem Singh, reported in (2008) 8 S.C.C. 648 on the issue of limitation

relating to payment or re-fixation of pay or pension wherein the Apex Court had held
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                               2</span>

that relief may be granted in spite of delay as it does not affect the rights of the third

party.
</p>
<p id="p_9">         In view of the aforesaid, I direct the Director of Pension, Provident Fund and

Group Insurance, Government of West Bengal as also the concerned Treasury Officer to

pay interest to the writ petitioner         @ 8% per annum on the revised arrear pension

amount calculated on and from 1st June, 2009 till actual date of payment.

</p><p id="p_10">         Such payment is to be made within eight weeks from the date of communication

of this order to the concerned authorities.

</p><p id="p_11">         This writ petition, being W. P. No.1573 (W) of 2019, is, thus, disposed of.

         Urgent Photostat certified copy of this order, if applied for, be handed over to

the parties on compliance of necessary formalities.


</p><p id="p_12">                                (Moushumi Bhattacharya, J.)
