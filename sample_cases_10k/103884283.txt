<div class="doc_title">Vidya Nand Kumar vs State Of Bihar on 18 April, 2009</div><pre id="pre_1">            IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                         Cr.Misc. No.5226 of 2009
                VIDYA NAND KUMAR, S/o. Jagdish Prasad,
                         R/o. Village- Hasan Chak,
                      P.S. Harnaut, District- Nalanda.
                                   Versus
                         THE STATE OF BIHAR
                                  -----------
</pre><p id="p_1">02   18.04.2009              Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and the

                   State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                             The informant is the wife of the petitioner making

                   allegations under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_1">Sections 498A</a> and <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_2">307</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_3">34</a> of the I.P.C. and

                   Section Â¾ <a href="/doc/1763444/" id="a_4">Dowry Prohibition Act</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                             The allegation is of hanging the informant in a well

                   for the purpose of extracting dowry. He is in custody since

                   21.10.2008.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                             This Court is not persuaded to grant him bail at this

                   stage in view of the seriousness of the allegation. If the

                   petitioner renews his prayer for bail before the court-below

                   after completing one year in custody. This Court expects that

                   his application for regular bail shall then be considered in

                   light of the guidelines laid down by this Court for grant of

                   bail in matrimonial matters without requiring him to move an

                   application for regular bail before this Court again.


</p><p id="p_5">                                                   (Navin Sinha, J.)
        Mkr.
</p>
