<p id="p_1">        Respondent   No.1    filed   Civil.Misc.No.18/2001

against the other respondents in the Court of Principal

Civil    Judge,   (Junior    Division),   and   JMFC    at

Chamarajnagar, to direct the respondents therein to

hand over the vacant possession of the property

described in the schedule therein. In the said petition,

the petitioner herein filed I.A.No.7, to permit her to get

impleaded as respondent No.8 for effective adjudication

of the case. The said application having been rejected

and delivery warrant having been ordered to be issued,

this writ petition was filed on 02.06.2011.


</p><p id="p_2">        2.   Sri. R.S. Ravi, learned counsel for the

petitioner submitted that the writ petition may be
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                               4</span>



closed, by reserving liberty to the petitioner to prosecute

O.S.No.24/2007 filed against respondent No.1 herein.


</p><p id="p_3">       3.    Sri. R.C.Nagaraj, learned counsel appearing

for respondent No.1 submitted that O.S.No.24/2007

has stood dismissed for non-prosecution on 02.06.2012.

He further submitted that pursuant to the impugned

order, the possession of the property shown in the

schedule of Civil.Mis.No.18/2001 was delivered and

hence, the writ petition has become infructuous.


</p><p id="p_4">       In   view   of   the   submission   made   by   Sri.

R.C.Nagaraj, the writ petition is dismissed as not

surviving for consideration. However, it is open to the

petitioner to take lawful steps, if any in the matter, in

accordance with law.
</p>
