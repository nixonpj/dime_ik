<div class="doc_title">Aditya Bhatt vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 11 July, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                                          1                               CRA-728-2016
The High Court Of Madhya Pradesh
                                       CRA-728-2016
                          (ADITYA BHATT Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)




         Indore, Dated : 11-07-2018
               Shri S.K. Vyas, learned senior counsel assisted by Shri R.R.
         Bhatnagar, learned counsel for the appellant.
              Shri Hemant Sharma, learned Government Advocate for the
         respondent/State.</pre>

<p id="p_1">             Heard on IA No.20983/2017 , an application for suspension of jail
         sentence of appellant â€" Aditya Bhatt under Section 389 (1) of <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_1">Cr.P.C</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_2">              This is second repeat application for suspension of jail sentence of
         appellant â€" Aditya Bhatt. Earlier application was dismissed vide order
         dated 28/06/2017 on merits.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                This time the appellant has advanced two fold arguments; one is
         that <a href="/doc/764237/" id="a_2">Section 394</a> of IPC is not made out and second is the period of
         custody.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                It is submitted that in case <a href="/doc/764237/" id="a_3">Section 394</a> of IPC is not made out,
         the maximum sentence awarded to the appellant for the offence under
         <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_4">Section 307</a> of IPC is 5 years and more than half of the sentence has
         already been passed in jail, therefore, he may be granted suspension.
</p>
<p id="p_5">             Learned Public Prosecutor has opposed the prayer submitting that all
         the contentions of the appellant has already been considered at earlier
         occasion and nothing substantial is changed at present, therefore,
         application cannot be allowed.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                 Learned counsel for the appellant invited my attention towards
         Dehati-Nalisi x.P/2 and act of the victim Kalpana and submitted that at
         the first instance no charges were levelled regarding robbery, therefore,
         <a href="/doc/764237/" id="a_5">Section 394</a> of IPC cannot charged and the learned trial Court has
         committed error in convicting the appellant for that offence.
</p>
<p id="p_7">               I have gone through the statement of prosecutrix, statement of
         other witnesses, evidence produced by the prosecution during trial and
         considering the earlier order passed by this Court.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                Considering the aforesaid, I am not inclined to grant suspension
         to the appellant on any of the grounds raised by the appellant,
         therefore, suspension is declined. Application (IA No.20983/2017) is
         dismissed .</p><pre id="pre_2">

                                                                (VIRENDER SINGH)
                                                                     JUDGE
                                 2             CRA-728-2016
