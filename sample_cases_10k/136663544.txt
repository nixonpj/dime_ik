<div class="doc_title">Meghsingh Pal vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 28 October, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                                1
                                          M.Cr.C.No.10220/2015

              (<a href="/doc/43700993/" id="a_1">Megh Singh Pal v. State of MP</a>)
28.10.2015
      Shri K.K. Dubey Advocate for the applicant.
      Shri B.K. Sharma GA for the respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Shri Arun Pateria, Advocate for the victim.
      Case Diary is perused.
</p><p id="p_2">      Learned counsel for the rival parties are heard.
      Applicant apprehends arrest in connection with offences
punishable u/Ss.302, 394, 397, 201, 120 B of <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_2">IPC</a> and 11/13
of MPDVPK Adhiniyam registered as Crime No.348/2015 at
Police Station Bahodapur, District Gwalior.
</p><p id="p_3">      Learned Public Prosecutor for the State opposed the
application and prayed for its rejection by contending that on
the basis of the allegations and the material available on
record, no case for grant of anticipatory bail is made out.
</p><p id="p_4">       Petitioner apprehends arrest in respect of aforesaid
offences where the allegation against the petitioner is of
murder and robbery.
</p><p id="p_5">      Though the case on merits of the petitioner appears to
be somewhat similar to the case of Kamlesh who has been
granted bail u/S. 439 of <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_3">Cr.P.C</a> but the present application is
u/S. 438 <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_4">Cr.P.C</a>. The allegation is serious in nature which is of
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                     2</span>
                                              M.Cr.C.No.10220/2015

     murder as well as dacoity. Recovery is yet to be made from the
     petitioner. Custodial interrogation assumes relevance in the
     matter and therefore no case for anticipatory bail is made out.
</p><p id="p_6">           Learned counsel for the petitioner further submits that
     statement of Prosecution witness Brijesh u/S. 161 has been
     recorded late ie. 30 days after the incident and since      the
     petitioner was not named in the FIR his source of implication
     is his own confessional statement.
</p><p id="p_7">           Looking to the gravity of offence and the fact that there
     is prima facie some evidence available against the petitioner
     which does not indicate that no offence is made out and
     therefore statutory bar contained in Sec. 5 (2) of MPDVPK Act
     comes in way of the petitioner to succeed in this anticipatory
     bail application.
</p><p id="p_8">           Accordingly, bail petition stands dismissed as not
     maintainable.
</p>

<p id="p_9">                                                 (Sheel Nagu)
                                                   Judge
ar
