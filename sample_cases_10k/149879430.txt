<p id="p_1">                                  The petitioner prays for an opportunity to participate

                     in the interview so as to gain access to employment in the

                     selection process which already stands concluded. The petitioner

                     has taken a plea that he did not have the access to the internet

                     which prevented him from responding promptly to the interview.

                     This itself may not be a justifiable explanation considering the

                     fact that the whole selection process was online commencing

                     from the application onwards. If that be so, the petitioner cannot

                     plead the fact that he had no access to the internet. Be that as it

                     may, it is also stated that vacancies are still existing and that the

                     petitioner is higher up in the merit and in the eventuality of

                     getting employment he would not disturb any of the incumbent

                     who already stands selected.
</p><p id="p_2">DALJIT SINGH
2014.11.14 15:17
I attest to the accuracy and
integrity of this document
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                      CWP No.23260 of 2014               -2-</span>

                                Considering     the limited prayer of the petitioner, I

                     deem it appropriate to dispose of the instant petition with a

                     direction to the respondents to consider the plea raised by the

                     petitioner in regard to the availability of vacancies and he being

                     higher in merit and take a decision thereon as expeditiously as

                     possible preferably within a period of four weeks from the date of

                     receipt of a certified copy of this order.
</p>
