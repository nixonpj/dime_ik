<p id="p_1">BRIEF FACTS OF THE CASE

</p><p id="p_2">1.                          The case of the prosecution as mentioned in the charge­sheet
is as under:­
</p><p id="p_3">                     (i).  That on 18.06.2016, complainant Babloo (PW2) visited PP
                     New Subzi Mandi of PS Mahendra Park and his statement (Ex.
                     PW2/A)   was   recorded   by   HC   Parveen   Kumar,   wherein
                     complainant   claimed   that   he   had   been   robbed   of   his   mobile
                     phone make Samsung at about 1.00 am at the point of knife on
                     17.06.2016   while   he   was   sleeping   inside   truck   no.   GJ­31T­
                     3233.   On the basis of said statement, FIR in question was got
                     registered U/s 392/397 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_3">IPC</a> and investigation was entrusted to SI
                     Jaibir (PW­4);
</p>

</pre><p id="p_4">                     (ii). It is further the case of prosecution that IO prepared rough
                     site plan of the place of occurrence and apprehended accused
                     herein on the identification of complainant.  The robbed mobile

                     of   offence,   same   were   seized   in   this   case.     IO   also   recorded
                     statements U/s 161 <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_5">Cr.PC</a> of the relevant witnesses;
</p><p id="p_5">                     (iii).  After completion of investigation, charge­sheet was filed
                     before the Court of Ld. M.M.  
</p><p id="p_6">2.                         After   compliance   of   section   207   <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_6">Cr.P.C</a>.,   the   case   was
committed to the Court of Sessions and was assigned to this Court.
</p>

<p id="p_7">                      CHARGES FRAMED AGAINST THE ACCUSED

</p><p id="p_8">3.                         After hearing arguments on the point of charge, Court framed
the   charge   for   the   offences   punishable   U/s   392/397/411   <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_7">IPC</a>   against
accused Man Singh vide order dated 31.08.2016, to which he pleaded not
guilty and claimed trial.
</p><p id="p_9">4.                          In   support   of   its   case,   the   prosecution   has   examined   five
witnesses     i.e   PW1   PSI   Pardeep   Singh,   PW2   Sh.   Babloo,   PW3   Sh.
Vikram, PW4 SI Jaivir Singh and PW5 Ct. Rajesh during trial. 
</p><p id="p_10">5.                          Thereafter,   statement   U/s   313   <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_8">Cr.P.C</a>.   of   accused   was
recorded,   during   which   all   the   incriminating   evidence   which   came   on
record,  were   put   to   him.     He   denied   the   same   and  claimed   that   he  is
innocent and has been falsely implicated in this case.  However, he opted
not to lead any evidence towards his defence.
</p>

<pre id="pre_3">State Vs. Man Singh ("Acquitted")                                                                               Page  2  of 8

</pre><p id="p_11">6.                          I have heard Sh. Pankaj Bhatia, Ld. Additional PP on behalf
of State and Ld counsel Sh. R.K Verma, Adv. on behalf of   accused.   I
have also gone through the material available on record.
</p><p id="p_12">7.                          Before discussing the rival submissions made on behalf of
both the sides, it would be appropriate to discuss, in brief, the testimonies
of   prosecution   witnesses   which   have   come   on   record.     The   said
testimonies are detailed as under:­
PUBLIC WITNESS:­

</p><p id="p_13">8.           PW2 Sh. Babloo  :­  He is the complainant/victim in this case. He
supported the prosecution story to the extent of incident of robbery of his
mobile phone, committed during the intervening night of 17/18­06­2016
while he was sleeping inside truck no. GJ­31T­ 3233. However, he turned
hostile   on   other   material   aspects   including   on   the   point   of   identity   of
accused herein to be the offender.   Rather, he testified that the offender
had covered his face with helmet, due to which he could not see his face.
The said unknown offender had committed robbery of his mobile phone
make Samsung at the point of knife and had fled away from the spot.  He
had narrated the occurrence to his cousin Vikram, who took him to PP
New Subzi Mandi, where police official obtained his signatures on some
blank documents on the assurance that they would register FIR in this
case.  He identified his mobile phone as (Ex. P1) during trial but could not
identify dagger and accused.
</p><p id="p_14">                            He was subjected to detailed cross examination on behalf of
State, during which all the relevant suggestions were put to him on the
lines of prosecution story but same were denied by him. Although, he
admitted   his   signatures   appearing   on   police   statement   Ex.   PW2/A   but

denied the contents thereof and reiterated that he did not make any such
statement   before   the   police.     His   attention   was   also   drawn   towards
accused but still he did not identify him.   He also denied to have joined
investigation   of   this   case   on   18.06.16   at   the   time   when   accused   was
apprehended in this case.   He also denied to have made supplementary
statement   (Mark   PW2/A).     He   categorically   denied   that   accused   was
arrested in his presence or  that robbed mobile phone and dagger were
recovered from the possession of accused in his presence. He has not been
cross examined by accused despite grant of opportunity.
</p><p id="p_15">9.           PW3 Sh. Vikram:­ He is the cousin brother of victim/complainant.
He supported the prosecution story only to the extent that complainant
Babloo had narrated the entire occurrence with regard to robbery of his
mobile phone by assailant to him during the intervening night of 17/18­
06­2016 and both of them having visited PP New Subzi Mandi for lodging
the complaint with the police. He deposed on identical lines of PW2 by
testifying that police officials had obtained signatures of Babloo on some
blank documents on the assurance that they would register the FIR.   He
did   not   support   the   prosecution   story   even   during   cross   examination
conducted on behalf of State and denied to have made statement U/s 161
<a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_11">Cr.PC</a> (Mark PW3/A) before the police. He has not been cross examined
on behalf of accused. 
</p><p id="p_16">POLICE WITNESS:­

</p><p id="p_17">10.          PW­1 PSI Pardeep Singh:­    This witness was working as Duty
Officer   in PS  Mahendera  Park  on 18.06.2016.   He  has  proved factum
regarding recording of FIR No. 312/16 in PS Mahendra Park.  He proved
copy of said FIR as Ex.PW1/A and his endorsement as Ex.PW1/B made

Rajesh had left PS alongwith rukka and copy of FIR at about 11.50 a.m.
He   denied  the  suggestion  that  FIR   in  question  was   ante  dated  or   ante
timed at the instance of IO of the case.
</p><p id="p_19">11.          PW­4 SI Jaivir Singh &amp; PW­5 Ct. Rajesh:­ Both these witnesses
remained   associated   during   investigation   of   the   case.   Both   of   them
deposed   on   similar   lines   to   the   effect   that   on   18.06.2016,   PW4   had
prepared   rough   site   plan   (Ex.   PW4/A)   at   the   instance   of   complainant
Babloo,   whereafter, they had apprehended accused on the identification
of complainant and robbed mobile phone as well as weapon of offence i.e.
dagger were recovered from the possession of accused.   After preparing
rough sketch (Ex. PW4/B) of said dagger, its pullanda was prepared which
was seized vide memo (Ex. PW4/C).  Mobile phone was also seized vide
seizure memo Ex. PW4/D. Thereafter, accused was arrested vide memo
(Ex.   PW4/E).     Site   plan   (Ex.   PW4/J)   of   the   place   of   apprehension   of
accused was also prepared.
</p><p id="p_20">                            PW4   also   deposed   that   he   had   collected   CAF,   CDRs   and
other related documents of robbed SIM connection number 9549636396.
Both of them identified the mobile phone as Ex. P1 and dagger as Ex. P2
during trial.
</p><p id="p_21">                            In   their   respective   cross   examination,   they   denied   the
suggestion that complainant did not join investigation with them at any
point   of   time   or   that   no   such   recovery   was   effected   either   from   the

possession or at the instance of accused or that IO had obtained signatures
of complainant Babloo on various memos at PS itself.
</p><p id="p_22">                            ARGUMENTS ADVANCED &amp; CASE LAW CITED
</p><p id="p_23">12.                         After   referring   to   the   testimonies   of   prosecution   witnesses
examined   during   trial,   Ld   Additional   PP   candidly   conceded   that   since
PW2 Babloo (victim/complainant) could not identify accused herein to be
assailant having robbed his mobile phone at the point of knife and also
could not identify the dagger ( Ex. P2) during trial, offences punishable
U/s 392/397 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_15">IPC</a> could not be proved against accused but he vehemently
argued that there has been recovery of robbed mobile phone ( Ex. P1)
from the possession of accused.   For the said purpose, he heavily relied
upon   the   testimonies   of   PW4   SI   Jaibir   and   PW5   Ct.   Rajesh   who   are
claimed   to   be   alleged   recovery   witnesses   of   said   mobile   phone.   He,
therefore,   urged   that   accused   is   liable   to   be   convicted   for   offence
punishable U/s 411 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_16">IPC</a>.
</p><p id="p_24">13.                         On the other hand, Ld. Defence counsel vehemently argued
that   the   prosecution   has   miserably   failed   to   prove   the   charge   levelled
against accused for offence punishable U/s 411 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_17">IPC</a> beyond shadow of
doubt.  For   the  said  purpose,   he  referred   to  the   relevant  portion  of  the
testimony of PW2 Babloo who denied the factum of recovery of robbed
mobile   phone   from   the   possession   of   accused   in   his   presence.     He
therefore, urged that the accused is entitled to be acquitted. 
</p><p id="p_25">14.                         As already noted above, complainant/victim namely Babloo
(PW2) was the sole eye witness of the incident in question. Thus, he alone
could have proved the case of prosecution by deposing on the lines of
prosecution story as propounded in the charge sheet.  No doubt, he partly


State Vs. Man Singh ("Acquitted")                                                                               Page  6  of 8 
 FIR No. 312/16 U/s 392/397/411 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_18">IPC</a>; PS Mahendra Park                                                        D.O.D.: 30.11.2016



supported the prosecution case to the extent of incident of robbery of his
mobile phone committed by assailant at the point of knife. However, he
could not identify accused herein to be the robber.  In other words, there is
no   iota   of   evidence   available   on   record   to   connect   accused   with   the
commission of offence of robbery at the point of knife. Rather, he deposed
that assailant was wearing helmet and thus, he was not able to see his face.
He also testified that he was not in a position to identify the dagger even if
shown to him as neither the said dagger was recovered in his presence nor
he was able to see the said dagger which was used by unknown offender
during the commission of offence.   For all these reasons, Court has no
hesitation to hold that prosecution has miserably failed to prove the charge
in respect of offences punishable U/s 392/397 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_19">IPC</a> against accused beyond
pales of reasonable doubt.
</p><p id="p_26">15.                         This brings me down to the offence punishable U/s 411 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_20">IPC</a>
charged against accused. In order to prove the said charge, the prosecution
has examined three witnesses i.e. PW2 Sh. Babloo, PW4 SI Jaivir Singh
and PW5 Ct. Rajesh.  According to the case of prosecution, accused was
arrested   by   PW4   and   PW5   on   the   identification   of   PW2   who   is   the
complainant/victim of this case.  No doubt, both the police witnesses have
deposed on identical  lines  regarding arrest of  accused  and recovery of
robbed mobile phone and dagger from the possession of accused but the
third alleged recovery witness i.e.  PW2 Sh. Babloo demolished the case
of   prosecution   as   he   categorically   deposed   that   he     did   not   join
investigation at any point of time and neither accused was apprehended in
his presence nor robbed mobile phone and dagger were recovered from
the possession of accused in his presence.   Thus, the testimony of PW2
has put serious dent on the case of prosecution and creates reasonable

State Vs. Man Singh ("Acquitted")                                                                               Page  7  of 8 
 FIR No. 312/16 U/s 392/397/411 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_21">IPC</a>; PS Mahendra Park                                                        D.O.D.: 30.11.2016



doubt in the prosecution story regarding recovery of robbed mobile phone
from the possession of accused.  It is well settled law that whenever two
views are possible on the basis of material available on record, the view
which goes in favour of accused, must be accepted by the Court.  Hence,
Court is of the view that prosecution could not establish charge for the
offence punishable U/s 411 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_22">IPC</a> against accused beyond shadow of doubt.
</p><p id="p_27">16.                         Resultantly,   it   is   hereby   held   that   prosecution   has   been
unsuccessful   in   bringing   home   the   guilt   of   accused   in   respect   of   any
offence, beyond shadow of doubt.   Consequently, accused namely Man
Singh @ Kana S/o Sh  Bhikam Singh is hereby acquitted of the charges
levelled against him, by giving him benefit of doubt.   File be consigned to
Record Room after compliance of Section 437­A <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_23">Cr.PC</a>.
</p>
