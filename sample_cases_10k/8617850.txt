                                  Cr.Misc. No.43567 of 2009
                1. BINOD YADAV S/O RAMESHWAR YADAV
                2. CHANDAR YADAV @ CHANDRA NARAYAN YADAV
                 S/O RAMESHWAR YADAV
                3. RAMESHWAR YADAV S/O RAM JI YADAV
                 4. SAKINDAR YADAV S/O RAMESHWAR YADAV
                5. KAMESHWAR YADAV @ KAMO YADAV
                S/O RAMESHWAR YADAV
                ALL R/O VILL- BELA GANAURNI, P.S. JAMALPUR,
                DISTT. DARBHANGA
                6. RAMDEO YADAV S/O RAMJI YADAV R/O VILL- PALWA,
                 P.S. JAMALPUR, DISTT. DARBHANGA
                                                               Petitioners
                                     Versus
                1. THE STATE OF BIHAR
                2. Lakshami Yadav son of Shobhit Yadav
                R/O VILL- PALWA, P.S. JAMALPUR, DISTT. DARBHANGA
                                                         Opposite parties
                                        --------
</pre><p id="p_1">2-   21.12.2009          Heard learned counsel for the parties

                           This application has been filed against the order dated

                  7.1.2008 passed by the Judicial Magistrate, Ist Class, Biraul at

                  Benipur, Darbhanga in Protest-cum- Complaint Case no. 291 of 2007/

                  Tr. no. 2102 of 2008 by which cognizance has been taken against the

                  petitioners under <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_1">sections 323</a> and <a href="/doc/1101188/" id="a_2">379</a> of the Indian Penal Code.

</p><p id="p_2">                          The facts stated by learned counsel for the petitioners are that

                  there is proceeding under <a href="/doc/930621/" id="a_3">section 144</a> of the Code of Criminal

                  Procedure between the petitioners and the informant and due to this

                  fact a false case was instituted. The police investigated the case and

                  found that it is a false case and recommended for proceeding under

                  <a href="/doc/534484/" id="a_4">section 182</a> and <a href="/doc/172919/" id="a_5">211</a> of the Indian Penal Code. In the meantime, the

                  informant filed protest petition and the court has proceeded on protest

                  petition and taken cognizance in this case for the offence under

                  <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_6">sections 323</a> and <a href="/doc/1101188/" id="a_7">379</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                               -2-</span>




                 I find that there is no illegality in the order of cognizance

         when the question that land dispute between the parties will be

         considered at a later stage. In the circumstances, this application is

         dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                              (Sheema Ali Khan,J)

Shahid
