<div class="doc_title">State vs . Bhagat Singh on 12 February, 2007</div><div class="doc_author">Author: Sh. Ajay Gupta</div>

<pre id="pre_1">               IN THE COURT OF SH. AJAY GUPTA, M.M.
                       KARKARDOOMA, DELHI

                                                             FIR No. 459/04
                                                    State vs. Bhagat Singh
                                                        U/s 25 of <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_1">Arms Act</a>
                                                             PS Seelampur


1. Sl. no. of the case                               459/04

2. Date of commission of offence                     11.08.04

3. Name of complainant                               Ct. Rajesh Kumar.

4. Name of accused                        Bhagat Singh,
                                                    s/o         Mahender
                         Singh.

           r/o H-1/203, PS Seelampur,

           New Seelampur,
                                                                Delhi.

5. Offence complained or proved                      U/s 25 of <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_2">Arms Act</a>

6. Plea of the accused                                          Pleaded
not guilty

7. Final order
            Convicted

8. Date of such order                                12.02.07




JUDGMENT
</pre>



<p id="p_1">1. Briefly the facts of the case are that allegedly on 11.08.04 at 5.30 pm

  at/near Y Point Dharampura, New Seelampur, Delhi, the accused was
   found in possession of one buttondar knife. Thereupon the accused was

  apprehended and charge sheeted in this case.



</p><p id="p_2">2. The   Ld. Predecessor of this court vide his order dt.   15.09.04 was

  pleased to frame a charge u/s 25 of <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_3">Arms Act</a> against the accused to

  which he pleaded not guilty and claimed trial.



</p><p id="p_3">3. In order to prove its case against the accused the prosecution

  examined two witnesses ie PW 1 ct. Rajesh Kumar the complainant and

  PW2 ASI Satender Prasad IO of the case.
</p>
<p id="p_4">

</p><p id="p_5">4. The prosecution examined the complainant ct. Rajesh as PW1.      This

  witness deposed that on 11.08.04 he was on patrolling duty alongwith

  Ct. Dharmender and were present at GT Road, Seelampur. At about

  5.30 pm they reached at Y-point Dharampura, GT Road meanwhile

  they saw that a person was running towards the side of ISBT and public

  persons were also chasing him raising the alarm Pakro-Pakro. Seeing

  the police party he turned towards the jhuggi Seelampur and seeing his

  suspicious activity and alarm of purblic persons both the constables

  apprehended him who disclosed his name as Bhagat Singh who is a BC

  of the area.     Thereafter from formal search of the accused one

  buttondar knife was recovered. In the mean time ASI Satender Prasad

  reached the spot. After that he handed over accused along with
   alleged buttondar knife to ASI Satender Prasad. After that ASI Satender

  Prasad is stated to have recorded the statement ex PW-1/A, prepared

  the sketch of knife ex. PW1/B, sealed it, seized it vide seizure memo ex.

  PW1/C and prepared rukka and got the case registered. After that

  accused was arrested and his personal search was conducted vide

  memo ex. PW1/D and ex. PW1/E. This witness has proved the alleged

  knife as ex. P1 and has correctly identified the accused correctly.



</p><p id="p_6">5. The prosecution has examined the IO ASI Satender Prasad as PW2. This

  witness stated that on 11.08.04 he was on patrolling duty at about

  5.40p.m. He reached at Dharampura Y-Point where Ct. Rajesh Kumar

  and Ct Dharmender met him. They produced the accused along with

  knife stated to be recovered from the possession of the accused, to

  him. After that this witness has deposed more or less on the similar terms

  as deposed by PW1. After that he completed the formalities.



</p><p id="p_7">6. After examination of the aforesaid witnesses prosecution evidence was

  closed and case was fixed for statement of accused.



</p><p id="p_8">7. Statement of accused u/s 281 Cr. P.C is recorded to which he denied

  the allegations and submitted that he has been falsely implicated in this

  case. He did not opt to lead any defence evidence. After that case

  was listed for final arguments.
</p><p id="p_9"> 8. Arguments are heard.       Case file and statement of witnesses are

  perused carefully.
</p>
<p id="p_10">9. During the course of arguments Ld. APP submitted that the prosecution

  has been able to prove its case beyond reasonable doubt through the

  testimonies of PW1 and PW2.
</p>
<p id="p_11">

</p><p id="p_12">10.PW1   and   PW2     in   thier   examination   correctly   disclosed   the

  measurement of the knife, sketch of which is ex.PW1/A and they have

  also got exhibited the knife ex. P1 allegedly recovered from the

  possession of accused. Both these witnesses PW1 and PW2 have

  identified the accused correctly.
</p>
<p id="p_13">

</p><p id="p_14">11.This court after perusal of the material available on record, is of the

  considered opinion that the statements of both the witnesses are in

  harmony with one another and prosecution has been able to prove

  that on 11.08.04 at 5.30 pm at/near Y Point Dharampura, New

  Seelampur Delhi, the accused Bharat Singh was found in possession of

  button actuated knife in contravention of law to that effect. Accused is

  accordingly held guilty and convicted accordingly.



</p><p id="p_15">                                                  ( Ajay Gupta )
                                       M. M., Karkardooma Courts, Delhi
           	  
          
  
                            
                                                                     FIR no. 459/04
                                                                    PS Seelampur

ORDER ON SENTENCE

12.02.07

Present.    APP for the state.
</p>
<p id="p_16">                         Convict Bharat Singh in person.
</p>
<p id="p_17">                         He is heard on point of sentence.
</p>
<p id="p_18">                         During the course of arguments accused submitted that

he has already remained in JC about two months. Accused further submits that

he is the only bread earner of his family and prayed for a lenient view.

</p><p id="p_19">            Accused is of young age. Keeping in view the submission of accused

and period of detention, this court is of the considered opinion that the ends of

justice will be met if the accused is sentenced to undergo simple imprisonment

for the period which he has already undergone during the course of trial in judicial

custody. Orders accordingly. Benefit u/s 428 <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_4">Cr.P.C</a> given to the accused. He

be released immediately if not wanted otherwise.

</p><p id="p_20">            A copy of the judgment and order on sentence be given to the

accused free of costs.
</p>
<p id="p_21">                                                             ( AJAY GUPTA )
                                                  METROPOLITAN MAGISTRATE
                                                    KKD COURTS SHAHDHARA
Announced in open
court on 12.02.07
