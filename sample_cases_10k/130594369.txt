ORAL JUDGMENT</pre>
<p id="p_1">       The petitioner being aggrieved by order passed

by the appellate authority dated 03-11/12/2003                      is
 SCA/9181/2004                          2/4                          JUDGMENT



before this Court praying that orders at Annexure

'D', 'E' and 'I'              be quashed and set aside and it be

declared        that    the        petitioner     does    not   hold    any

surplus land within the meaning of the terms of the

<a href="/doc/1005850/" id="a_1">ULC Act</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_2">

</p><p id="p_3">2.     The      matter    was        heard   at    length.        Learned

advocate Mr.Sharma could not successfully assail the

order impugned so far as it relates to the lands

other      than      Rajkot    lands.        However,     the   order    is

required to be modified to the extent that the land

held by the petitioner in Rajkot admeasuring 2848 sq.

meters,         which         is     declared      surplus,      without

considering that the petitioner is entitled to get

one unit.            Rajkot being in Category 'C', one unit

consists        of     1500    sq.    meters.       The    entire      land

admeasuring 2848 sq. meters which is declared excess

is required to be modified and the land admeasuring

1348 sq. meters only is required to be taken.



At     this       juncture,          learned      advocate      Mr.Sharma

requested that under the law the authorities are duty

bound to give the petitioner an option while taking
 SCA/9181/2004                       3/4                                    JUDGMENT



possession         of    excess    land        of        1348   sq.    meters.

Mr.Nanavati,        learned       Assistant          Government        Pleader

submitted that the authorities will abide by law and

will allow the petitioner to exercise an option and

the petitioner will be able to surrender the surplus

land admeasuring 1348 sq. meters of his choice.



Mr.Sharma,         learned    advocate          submitted          that       the

petitioner,         at    present,        is        80     years      of    age,

therefore, the authorities be directed to act utmost

expeditiously, preferably within two weeks from the

date of receipt of this order.
</p>
<p id="p_4">

The authorities are directed to act expeditiously in

the matter, preferably within two weeks from the date

of receipt of this order.                      The possession of the

excess      land    admeasuring      1348       sq.       meters      be   taken

after giving an option to the petitioner. With the

aforesaid direction the petition is partly allowed.

Rule is made absolute to the aforesaid extent. No

order as to costs.
</p>
<p id="p_5">


</p><p id="p_6">                                           (RAVI R. TRIPATHI, J.)
 SCA/9181/2004   4/4   JUDGMENT


karim
