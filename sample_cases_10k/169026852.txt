<div class="doc_title">Sunil Mandal vs The State Of Bihar on 26 October, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                   Criminal Miscellaneous No.63300 of 2018
                     Arising Out of PS. Case No.-320 Year-2017 Thana- PIRPAINTI District- Bhagalpur
                 ======================================================
                 Sunil Mandal, son of Late Jagarnath Mandal @ Late Jagarnathi Mandal,
                 Resident of Village- Bakharpur, West Tola, P.S. Bakharpur (Pirpainti),
                 District- Bhagalpur.

                                                                                     ... ... Petitioner
                                                        Versus
                 The State of Bihar

                                                         ... ... Opposite Party
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :       Mr. Ranjan Kumar Jha, Advocate
                 For the Opposite Party/s :       Mr. Md. Sufiyan (APP)
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE S. KUMAR
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   26-10-2018                    Heard learned counsel for petitioner and learned

                  counsel for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                  Petitioner, who is in custody, seeks bail in

                  connection with Pirpainty P.S. Case No. 320 of 2017 registered

                  for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_1">Sections 302</a>, <a href="/doc/386021/" id="a_2">201</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_3">34</a> of the

                  Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                   Informant who is the father of the deceased has

                  alleged that petitioner and other co-accused named in F.I.R.

                  along with 2-3 unknown persons                     had called his son on

                  06.11.2017 at 6:00 P.M. for strolling in village and thereafter on

                  09.11.2017 his dead body was found in decomposed state.

</p><p id="p_4">                                  It has been submitted on behalf of the petitioner

                  that he is innocent and has been falsely implicated in this case
            Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.63300 of 2018(2) dt.26-10-2018
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                        2/2</span>




                    on the basis of suspicion and dirty village politics. Except his

                    own confession there is no incriminating material against him

                    Deceased was traceless from 06.11.2017 but F.I.R. was

                    instituted on 10.11.2017.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                     Considering the aforesaid fact and circumstances of

                    the case, let the petitioner named above be released on bail upon

                    furnishing bail bond of Rs. 10,000/- (ten thousand) with two

                    sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of learned 2 nd

                    Additional District &amp; Sessions Judge, Bhagalpur, in connection

                    with S.T. No. 485 of 2018 arising out of Pirpainty P.S. Case No.

                    320 of 2017 (G.R. No. 6037 of 2017).</p><pre id="pre_2">



                                                     (S. Kumar, J)
Rajiv/Manoj

U      T
