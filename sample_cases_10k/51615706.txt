                          IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                             Criminal Miscellaneous No. 41762 of 2008
                     ===================================================
                     1. Raj Kishore Yadav @ Raj Kishore Ray, Son of Chandrika
                        Ray.
                     2. Braj Nandan Prasad, Son of Akhileshwar Prasad.
                        Both are resident of Village-Laguniya, P.S.-Maker, District-
                        Saran.
                                                               .... .... Petitioner/s
                                                  Versus
                     The State of Bihar.
                                                          .... .... Opposite Party/s
                     ===================================================

             CORAM: HONOURABLE JUSTICE SMT. ANJANA PRAKASH
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">05.    05.07.2013                      The Petitioners seek quashing of the order dated

                          07.06.2008 passed by the Additional Sessions Judge,

                          F.T.C.-II, Saran, Chapra in Cr. Rev. No. 311/06 by which

                          he has affirmed the order dated 08.12.2006 passed by the

                          Judicial Magistrate, 1st Class, Saran, Chapra in Tr. No.

                          1384/06 arising out of Chapra Town P.S. Case No.

                          366/87 (G.R. No. 3562/87).
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                       Since this is a second revision in the garb of an

                          application filed under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C. I am not

                          inclined to interfere in the order. The application is

                          disposed off with the direction to the Trial Court to

                          conclude the trial within a period of three months if he

                          has not done so already.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                       It is made clear that this Court has not

                          expressed its opinion on the merits of the case.




</p><pre id="pre_2">      Vikash/-                                                  (Anjana Prakash, J.)
