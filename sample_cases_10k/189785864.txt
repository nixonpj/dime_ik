<div class="doc_title">Annu vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 1 August, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                        1                           MCRC-28687-2018
                               The High Court Of Madhya Pradesh
                                         MCRC-28687-2018
                                            (ANNU Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)


                      Jabalpur, Dated : 01-08-2018
                              Shri Ashish Sinha, counsel for the applicant.
                            Shri Vivek Lakhera, Govt. Advocate for the respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">                            Heard with the aid of case diary.
</p><p id="p_2">                            This is First application of the applicant Annu filed under <a href="/doc/1329373/" id="a_1">section
                     4</a> <a href="/doc/47577/" id="a_2">3</a> <a href="/doc/811293/" id="a_3">9</a> Cr.P.C. for grant of bail in connection with Crime No.392/2018
                     registered at Police Station Kotwali, District Sehore for the offence
                     punishable under <a href="/doc/1983271/" id="a_4">Sections 302</a>, <a href="/doc/1086076/" id="a_5">294</a>, <a href="/doc/770961/" id="a_6">323</a>, <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_7">506</a> r/w 34 of <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_8">IPC</a>.
</p><p id="p_3">                           A s per the prosecution case, on 24.05.2018 at 07:00 PM when
                     applicant sat at grocery shop situated at Indra Nagar, Tappar Road,
                     complainant Sandeep Gautam, his father Shivnarayan Gautam, brother
                     Dilip Gautam and his friend Sonu Maheshwari demanded the fare of
                     tanga which he was not giving to the complainant father, he abused them
                     and threatened to kill them. At that time co-accused Jitendra Yadav also
                     came there and both of them pelted stones on them. At that time, when
                     deceased Shivnarayan Gautam came to rescue, co-accused Jitendra
                     assaulted his father by stone due to which he sustained injury and died.
</p><p id="p_4">                           Learned counsel for the applicant submits that applicant has not
                     committed any offence and has been falsely implicated in the offence. In
                     the postmortem report, it is mentioned that deceased Shivnarayan Gautam
                     sustained two injuries, one is on his head and one abrasion on his right
                     leg and deceased died due to head injury. Applicant did not cause that
                     injury. Charge-sheet has been filed. The applicant is in custody since
                     26.05.2018 and the conclusion of trial is likely to take long time, hence
                     prayed for release of the applicant on bail.
</p><p id="p_5">                            Learned counsel for the State opposed the prayer and submitted
                     that sufficient evidence is available on record to connect the applicant
                     with the offence in question so he may not be released on bail.
</p><p id="p_6">                            Looking to fact and circumstances of the case and as to the fact that
                     the deceased Shivraj Gautam died due to head injury and according to
                     FIR that injury was caused by co-accused Jitendra and the fact that
                     charge sheet has been filed, applicant is in custody since 26.05.2018 and
                     conclusion of trial will take time, without commenting on the merits of
                     the case, the application is allowed and the applicant is directed to be
                     released on bail upon his furnishing personal bond in the sum of

Digitally signed by RANJEET
AHIRWAL

                     Rs.50,000/- (Rs. Fifty Thousand Only) with one surety in the like
                     amount to the satisfaction of the concerned C.J.M/trial Court for his
                     appearance before the trial Court on all such dates as may be fixed in this
                     behalf by the trial Court during the pendency of trial.
</p><p id="p_7">                           This order will remain operative subject to compliance of the
                     following conditions by the applicant :-
</p><p id="p_8">                           1. The applicant will comply with all the terms and conditions of
                     the bond executed by him;
</p><p id="p_9">                            2. The applicant will cooperate in the investigation/trial, as the
                     case may be;
</p><p id="p_10">                            3. The applicant will not indulge himself in extending inducement,
                     threat or promise to any person acquainted with the fact of the case so as
                     to dissuade him from disclosing such facts to the Court or to the Police
                     Officer, as the case may be;
</p><p id="p_11">                            4. The applicant shall not commit an offence similar to the offence
                     of which he is accused;
</p><p id="p_12">                            5. The applicant will not seek unnecessary adjournments during
                     the trial; and
</p><p id="p_13">                            6. The applicant will not leave India without previous permission
                     of the trial Court.
</p><p id="p_14">                            C.C. on payment of usual charges.
