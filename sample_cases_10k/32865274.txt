<div class="doc_title">Chitranjan Kumar @ Chit Ranjan ... vs The State Of Bihar on 20 December, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                     IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                              Civil Writ Jurisdiction Case No.23412 of 2019
                 ======================================================
                 Chitranjan Kumar @ Chit Ranjan Yadav, Male, aged about 29 years, S/o
                 Awadhesh Yadav, R/o Village- Chandauli (Kharkani), P.S.- Mufassil, District-
                 Aurangabad (Bihar).

                                                                    ... ... Petitioner/s
                                                  Versus
           1.    The State of Bihar through Principal Secretary, Excise Department,
                 Government of Bihar, Old Secretariat, Patna.
           2.    The District Magistrate, Aurangabad (Bihar).
           3.    The Superintendent of Police, Aurangabad (Bihar).
           4.    The District Supply Officer (Excise), Aurangabad (Bihar).
           5.    The S.H.O. Mufassil P.S. (Town P.S.), District- Aurangabad (Bihar).

                                                           ... ... Respondent/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s   :      Mr.Lal Bahadur Singh
                 For the Respondent/s   :      Mr.Vikash Kumar (SC-11)
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE DINESH KUMAR SINGH
                         and
                         HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE ANIL KUMAR SINHA
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">                 (Per: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE DINESH KUMAR SINGH)

3   20-12-2019                 The present writ application has been filed for release

                   of Bajaj Discover 125 motorcycle bearing Registration No. BR-

                   26F-7752 seized in connection with Aurangabad (Bihar) Town

                   P.S. Case No. 390 of 2019, registered for the offences

                   punishable under Section 30(a) of the Bihar Prohibition and

                   <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_1">Excise Act</a>, 2016, as amended by the <a href="/doc/1210757/" id="a_2">Amendment Act</a> 8 of 2018.

</p><p id="p_2">                               The prayer made in the writ application has been

                   stipulated in paragraph no. 1 of the writ petition, which reads as

                   follows:
</p><p id="p_3"> Patna High Court CWJC No.23412 of 2019(3) dt.20-12-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                           2/4</span>




                                         "That the present Civil Writ
                           application is being filed for issuance of
                           writ     of   mandamus           and    any        other
                           appropriate writ / writs, order / orders
                           and     direction      /       directions     to     the
                           respondents authority to release the
                           vehicle (Motorcycle) bearing Registration
                           no.       BR-26F-7752,             CHASIS           No.
                           MD2A15BZ2CRJ03487,                   Engine         No.
                           JZZRCJ03640, which was seized by the
                           Mufassil       Police,     under       District       -
</p><p id="p_4">                           Aurangabad ( Bihar ) in connection with
                           Town P.S. Case No. 390 of 2019 dated 5-
</p><p id="p_5">                           10-2019 u/s 30(a) of Bihar Prohibition
                           and Excise (Amendment ) Act, 2018 and
                           the vehicle in question is lying in the open
                           sky without any care in the open campus
                           of Town Police Station, Aurangabad
                           (Bihar)"
</p>
<p id="p_6">
                      The prosecution case got initiated on the basis of

          written report of Assistant Sub Inspector of Police namely,

          Sanjay Kumar - III of Town P.S. Aurangabad (Bihar) submitted

          to the Inspector -cum- Officer-in-charge, Town P.S. Aurangabad

          (Bihar) to the effect in order to maintain the law and order

          checking of vehicles was conducted when search was made of

          one Bajaj Discover 125 motorcycle bearing Registration No.
 Patna High Court CWJC No.23412 of 2019(3) dt.20-12-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                           3/4</span>




          BR-26F- 7752 and from the same, seven sachets of 200 ml each

          of country made liquor was recovered.

</p><p id="p_7">                      Learned counsel for the respondent has filed a counter

          affidavit on behalf of the respondent nos. 2 and 3 and specific

          averment has been made in paragraph no. 8 of the said counter

          affidavit that confiscation proceeding has already been initiated

          vide Confiscation Case No. 670 of 2019 and notice has been

          issued to the petitioner on 16.12.2019 which has duly been

          served upon the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                      In the circumstances, though the petitioner claims to

          be the registered owner of the vehicle in question and has

          brought on record certificate of registration of the vehicle in

          question, learned counsel for the petitioner prays for a direction

          to the respondent authorities i.e. respondent no. 2 - District

          Magistrate, Aurangabad ( Bihar) to conclude the confiscation

          case within a time frame.
</p>
<p id="p_9">                      In response to the contention raised by learned

          counsel for the petitioner, learned counsel for the respondent-

          State submits that the confiscation proceeding will be concluded

          within a fixed time frame.
</p>
<p id="p_10">                       As a result, we direct the District Magistrate-cum-

          Collector, Aurangabd (Bihar) to conclude the confiscation
            Patna High Court CWJC No.23412 of 2019(3) dt.20-12-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                                                      4/4</span>




                     proceeding being Confiscation Case No. 670 of 2019 within a

                     period of forty five days from the date of receipt / production

                     copy of this order. On non conclusion of the same within the

                     time fixed by this court, without any laches on the part of the

                     petitioner, who undertakes to appear regularly in the

                     confiscation proceeding, the petitioner will be at liberty to

                     renew his prayer for release of the vehicle in question.

</p><p id="p_11">                                 The writ petition stands disposed of.



</p><p id="p_12">                                           (Dinesh Kumar Singh, J)


                                              ( Anil Kumar Sinha, J)
praful/-
</p>
