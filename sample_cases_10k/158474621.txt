<div class="doc_title">Mohanlal Mukati vs M.P.State Warehousing Corp. &amp; Ors on 7 May, 2011</div><pre id="pre_1">                       W.P.(S) No.74/2003
07.05.2011
       The Industrial Tribunal in its impugned award, by which
it   has   refused   reinstatement   and   granted   lump    sum
compensation instead of reinstatement, has mentioned as one
of the factors for refusing reinstatement that the workman has
not been able to prove the take-over of the respondent No.1 by</pre>
<p id="p_1">the respondent No.2.
</p>

<p id="p_2">       The take-over is by a Gazette Notification which has
been published in the Extraordinary Gazette of Madhya
Pradesh dated 6th July, 2000. A copy of the same has been
enclosed as Annexure R-2/A. This Gazette Notification has
been issued by the State Government in exercise of the powers
under Section 76(1) of the Madhya Pradesh Nagar Tatha
Gram Nivesh Adhiniyam, 1973.
</p><p id="p_3">       Once there is a statutory notification, published in the
official gazette, every Court or Tribunal is bound to take
judicial notice of the same, and it does not require to be proved.
Therefore, one of the major factors because of which
reinstatement has been refused, cannot stand.
</p><p id="p_4">       In these circumstances, we allow this writ petition of the
workman, set aside the order of the Industrial Tribunal and
remand the matter back to the Industrial Tribunal for
examining the matter afresh.
</p><p id="p_5">             We make it clear that in view of what has been
      mentioned above we have not examined the other findings or
      the other questions, which will remain open before the tribunal.
</p><p id="p_6">            The   Industrial    Tribunal   will   decide   the   matter
      expeditiously.
</p>

<pre id="pre_2">            (Sushil Harkauli)                       (S.C. Sinho)
               Judge                                  Judge
