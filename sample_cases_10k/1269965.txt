  IN THE HIGH COURT OF KERALA AT ERNAKULAM

Crl.MC.No. 1997 of 2008()


1. SUNIL KUMAR K.G., S/O.GANGADHARAN
                      ...  Petitioner
2. GANGADHARAN

1. STATE OF KERALA REPRESENTED BY
                       ...       Respondent

2. STATION HOUSE OFFICER

3. VINITA MANAMMAL, D/O.VELAYUDHAN

                For Petitioner  :SRI.DILIP MOHAN

                For Respondent  :SRI.ALEXANDER GEORGE

The Hon'ble MR. Justice R.BASANT

 Dated :23/05/2008

 O R D E R
                              R.BASANT, J
                      ------------------------------------
                     Crl.M.C. No.1997 of 2008
                      -------------------------------------
               Dated this the 23rd day of May, 2008

                                  ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">     Petitioners face indictment in a prosecution for offences

punishable under Section 498 A r/w 34 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_1">I.P.C</a>.                  They are the

husband of the 3rd respondent and the relatives of the husband.

They are allegedly guilty of matrimonial cruelty of the culpable

variety. The crime has been registered on the basis of a private

complaint filed by the 3rd respondent which was referred to the

police under <a href="/doc/99487/" id="a_2">Section 156(3)</a> Cr.P.C. Investigation is complete.

Final report has already been filed. Cognizance has been taken

by the learned Magistrate. A Calendar Case is pending before

the learned Magistrate now.
</p>
<p id="p_2">     2.    At this stage, the parties have settled their disputes, it

is reported. The petitioner along with the 3rd respondent have

approached this Court and it is reported that all their outstanding

disputes have been settled and an agreement to effect divorce

has been entered into. The same is produced as Annexure-III.

In the said agreement, both the 1st petitioner and the 3rd

respondent have signed. All the outstanding disputes have been

settled. Both the parties are before this Court through counsel

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">Crl.M.C. No.1997 of 2008        2</span>

and the counsel confirm that such disputes have been settled

genuinely and amicably.
</p>
<p id="p_3">     3.   In these circumstances, the petitioners and the 3rd

respondent make a joint request before this Court that the

powers under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_3">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C may be invoked and the

proceedings against the petitioners may be quashed.

</p><p id="p_4">     4.   The offence under <a href="/doc/1868826/" id="a_4">Section 498</a> A I.P.C is not

compoundable.     But the decision in <a href="/doc/469138/" id="a_5">B.S.Joshy v. State of

Haryana</a> [A.I.R (2003) SC 1386] is authority for the proposition

that in the peculiar facts and circumstances of the case, the

powers under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_6">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C can be invoked to bring to

premature termination a prosecution under <a href="/doc/1868826/" id="a_7">Section 498</a> A I.P.C.

The parties having settled their disputes and the disputes being

purely a personal one between the parties, I am satisfied that the

prayer to quash the proceedings can be accepted.

</p><p id="p_5">     5.   In the result:
</p>
<p id="p_6">     i)   This Crl.M.C is allowed;
</p>
<p id="p_7">     ii)  C.C.464    of  2006    pending   before   the   Judicial

Magistrate of the First Class against the petitioners is hereby

quashed.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                                          (R.BASANT, JUDGE)
rtr/-
