       IN THE HIGH COURT OF KARNATAKA AT BENGALURU

            DATED THIS THE 12th DAY OF JULY 2017

                         BEFORE

            THE HON'BLE MRS.JUSTICE RATHNAKALA

           CRIMINAL PETITION NO.4828/2017

BETWEEN:

1.     SMT.KAMALAMMA
       W/O MURTHAPPA
       AGED 42 YEARS

2.     SRI MURTHAPPA S/O LATE RAMANNA
       AGED ABOUT 45 YEARS

3.     SRI MANJUNATH S/O MURTHAPPA
       AGED ABOUT 21 YEARS

       ALL ARE RESIDENTS OF
       BELAGURU VILLAGE
       SRIRAMPURA HOBLI
       HOSADURGA TALUK
       CHITRADURGA DISTRICT - 577 501.    ...PETITIONERS

(BY SRI N.PRAVEEN KUMAR, ADV.)

AND:

STATE OF KARNATAKA
STATION HOUSE OFFICER
PANCHANAHALLI POLICE STATION
CHIKKAMANGALURU DISTRICT - 577 182
REPRESENTED BY
SPECIAL PUBLIC PROSECUTOR
HIGH COURT OF KARNATAKA
BANGALORE - 560 001.                     ...RESPONDENT

(BY SRI CHETAN DESAI, HCGP.)
                                   -2-



      THIS CRIMINAL PETITION IS FILED UNDER SECTION 439
OF CR.P.C., PRAYING TO ENLARGE THE PETITIONERS ON BAIL
IN CR.NO.23/2017 (C.C.NO.844/2013) OF PANCHANAHALLI
POLICE STATION, CHIKKAMAGALURU, FOR THE OFFENCES P/U/S
324, 504, 306, 506 R/W 34 OF <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_1">IPC</a>.

      THIS CRIMINAL PETITION COMING ON FOR ORDERS THIS
DAY, THE COURT MADE THE FOLLOWING:

                           ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Heard   the     learned      counsel      appearing    for    the

petitioners/accused and the learned High Court Government

Pleader appearing for the respondent-State.


</p><p id="p_2">      2.    The     petitioners    are    in   judicial   custody    in

Cr.No.23/2017     registered      by    the    respondent-police     in

respect of the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_2">sections 324</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_3">504</a>,

<a href="/doc/92983/" id="a_4">306</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_5">506</a> r/w <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_6">section 34</a> of IPC.
</p>
<p id="p_3">
</p><p id="p_4">      3.    The complaint allegation is, on 30.4.2017 the

accused persons demanded share in the family property,

assaulted the husband of the complainant with a wooden

log and vowed to finish him off and his wife and left the

place. The complainant/wife of the deceased called for 108

vehicle.   Since it was felt that 108 vehicle cannot be

procured, the deceased told the complainant to go over to
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                -3-</span>



the house of one Lakkajji with children and sleep there and

he will go to the hospital in the vehicle of some other

person. Accordingly, she left the place. On 1.5.2017 when

she came to the house, she saw the door open and her

husband hanging to the roof.          The complaint is lodged

stating that unable to bear the torture of the accused, he

has committed suicide.
</p>
<p id="p_5">
</p><p id="p_6">       4.   Perused      the   post   mortem   report.   There

appears to be injury marks on the neck of the deceased

apart from injury on the body suffered by him in the

incident of previous evening. In that view of the matter, it

is felt that it is not the appropriate stage to enlarge the

petitioners on bail.
</p>
<p id="p_7">
</p><p id="p_8">       Hence, the petition is rejected.    However, liberty is

reserved to the petitioners to file fresh bail petition after

charge sheet is filed.
</p>
<p id="p_9">

</p><p id="p_10">                                            Sd/-
</p><p id="p_11">                                           JUDGE
