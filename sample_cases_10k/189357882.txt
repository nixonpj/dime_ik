                  IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

                      SPECIAL CIVIL APPLICATION NO. 9795 of 2016

         ==========================================================
                   AGRICULTURAL PRODUCE MARKET COMMITTEE,
                            VADODARA....Petitioner(s)
                                    Versus
                 VADODARA MUNICIPAL CORPORATION....Respondent(s)
         ==========================================================
         Appearance:
         MR DILIP B RANA, ADVOCATE for the Petitioner(s) No. 1
         ==========================================================

         CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE S.H.VORA

                                      Date : 23/06/2016


                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">         1.    Leave to amend is granted. Amendment be carried out
         forthwith.
</p>
<p id="p_2">         2.    By way of the present petition under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the
         Constitution of India, the petitioner challenges order dated
         30.05.2016          passed      by           the        respondent-Municipal
         Commissioner, Vadodara Municipal Corporation on the ground
         that possession of the premises, as shown in Kabja Pavti
         (possession receipt at Annexure-G) was forcibly taken by the
         respondent - Corporation though, period of lease agreement
         executed for 99 years in the year 1975, has not yet completed.
</p>
<p id="p_3">         3.    Before the submissions made at bar are considered, it is
         relevant to note here that the petitioner - Market Committee is
         allotted another piece of land in Sayajipura Market, Vadodara
         and, at the relevant time, administrator of the Market

         Committee expressed readiness and willingness to shift from
         the disputed premises vide communication dated 07.12.2015.
         Accordingly, the respondent took possession of the premises in
         question. It is not the case of the petitioner that said
         communication              dated    07.12.2015             addressed              by          the
         Administrator of the Market Committee was obtained or invited
         either by force or by misrepresentation and, therefore, now,
         the petitioner cannot be permitted to challenge the act which
         is done by the petitioner voluntarily and, therefore, this Court
         do not find any substance in the present petition and hence,
         the same is hereby rejected at the admission stage. However,
         the petitioner is at liberty to make appropriate representation
         with regard to the construction or its removal, as the case may
         be, before the respondent. As and when such representation is
         made, the respondent - Corporation shall consider the same
         and pass appropriate order in accordance with law.
</p>
