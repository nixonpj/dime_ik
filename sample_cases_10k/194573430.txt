<div class="doc_title">Sudhir Kumar @ Dhiraj Raj vs The State Of Bihar on 6 August, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                              CRIMINAL MISCELLANEOUS No.15718 of 2019
                  Arising Out of PS. Case No.-251 Year-2018 Thana- PATNA COMPLAINT CASE District-
                                                          Patna
                 ======================================================
                 Sudhir Kumar @ Dhiraj Raj, Son of Pramod Kumar Resident of Village -
                 Mahmudabad, P.s.- Bind, Distt.- Nalanda.

                                                                                ... ... Petitioner/s
                                                      Versus
           1.    The State of Bihar
           2.    Shashikala Kumari D/o late Jagdish Prasad, Resident of Village - Shahpur,
                 P.s.- Shahpur, Distt.- Patna.

                                                        ... ... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :      Mr.Anil Kumar No.1
                 For the Opposite Party/s :      Mr.Chandra Sen Prasad Singh
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE ANIL KUMAR SINHA
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">6   06-08-2019                     Heard Mr. Anil Kumar No.1 for the petitioner as

                   well as Mr. Ajit Kumar, learned counsel appearing for

                   complainant, i.e., Opposite Party No.2.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                The petitioner is husband of Opposite Party No.2. The

                   matter was referred in Mediation but it appears that the

                   Mediation between the parties has failed. During the course of

                   hearing, learned counsel for the petitioner has offered that the

                   petitioner is ready to pay sum of Rs.10,000/- per month in

                   favour of complaint-opposite party No.2 in order to meet day to

                   day expenses of Opposite party No.2. The offer made by the

                   petitioner has been accepted by complainant-Opposite Party

                   No.2 However, learned counsel for the Opposite Party No.2 on
 Patna High Court CR. MISC. No.15718 of 2019(6) dt.06-08-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                            2/4</span>




          instruction submits that petitioner has kept all the valuable

          documents, testimonials of Opposite Party No.2 in his house

          and the same is not being returned to opposite Party No.2.

          Upon query, learned counsel for the petitioner on instruction

          submits that all the documents and other valuable are kept in the

          trunk, the key of which is with the Opposite Party No.2 and

          petitioner is ready to return the entire trunk in favour of

          Opposite Party No.2 on date and time convenient to Opposite

          Party No.2

                       After having heard learned counsel for the petitioner

          as well as learned counsel appearing on behalf of Opposite Party

          No.2 and taking into consideration the fact that offer made by

          petitioner has been accepted by opposite party No.2 and the

          petitioner is ready to return all the documents, testimonials

          along with trunk in favour of opposite party No.2 at convenient

          date and time fixed by Opposite party No.2, let the petitioner,

          above named, be directed to surrender before the learned Court

          below within a period of four weeks from today and in the event

          of surrender by him, he shall be released on anticipatory bail by

          the Court below upon furnishing bail bond of Rs.10,000/- (Ten

          thousand) with two sureties of the like amount each to the

          satisfaction of learned Additional Chief Judicial Magistrate,
 Patna High Court CR. MISC. No.15718 of 2019(6) dt.06-08-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                            3/4</span>




          Danapur, Patna, subject to the condition that the petitioner shall

          be paying Rs.10,000/- per month regularly to the complainant-

          Opposite Party No.2, subject to any order passed in

          Maintenance case or similar proceeding between the parties.

</p><p id="p_3">                       In so far as the return of valuable, testimonials and

          other documents kept in the trunk along with trunk by the

          petitioner in favour of Opposite Party No.2 is concerned, both

          the parties have agreed that on 16.08.2019, the Opposite Party

          No.2 will visit the house of the petitioner along with her relative

          and Advocate Commissioner appointed by this court, Mr.

          Mritunjay Kumar, in order to receive the trunk from petitioner.

</p><p id="p_4">                       It is made clear that expenses incurred in visit of the

          Advocate Commissioner, Mr. Mritunjay Kumar, shall be

          incurred by Opposite Party No.2 and Opposite Party No.2 shall

          pay a sum of Rs. 2000/- to learned Advocate Commissioner as

          his remuneration.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                       The S.P. (Rural) Patna is directed to take necessary steps

          through local police station for providing protection to learned

          Advocate Commissioner while his visit to the house of the petitioner.

</p><p id="p_6">                       The amount of Rs.10,000/- to Opposite Party No.2 shall

          be made by the petitioner per month starting from 15 th August,

          2019 onwards. It is made clear that at the time of surrender, the
             Patna High Court CR. MISC. No.15718 of 2019(6) dt.06-08-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                                                        4/4</span>




                      petitioner shall attach duly acknowledged receipt of payment of

                      Rs.10,000/- made to the opposite party No.2 along with the

                      surrender application.
</p>
<p id="p_7">


</p><p id="p_8">                                                              (Anil Kumar Sinha, J)
sanjeev/-
