<div class="doc_title">Sandip Roy @ Sunny vs Unknown on 2 December, 2010</div><div class="doc_author">Author: Ashim Kumar Roy</div>

<p id="p_1">2.2010
                                       CRM No. 16297 of 2010

         In Re:- An application under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of the Code of Criminal
         Procedure filed on 11th October, 2010 in connection with Barasat
         P.S. Case No. 312 dated 09.02.2010 under <a href="/doc/1119707/" id="a_2">Sections 395</a>/<a href="/doc/844968/" id="a_3">412</a> of the
         I.P.C.
</p><p id="p_2">                               And
         In the matter of:-Sandip Roy @ Sunny
                                                Petitioner

         Mr. Chinmoy Pal,
         Ms. Anee Ray                              for the petitioner

         Sk. Abdus Salam                           for the State


               Heard the learned Counsel appearing on behalf of the parties.

         Perused the case diary.
</p>
<p id="p_3">               This is an application for bail relating to the offences

         punishable under <a href="/doc/1119707/" id="a_4">Sections 395</a>/<a href="/doc/844968/" id="a_5">412</a> of the I.P.C.

</p><p id="p_4">               It is contended by the learned Counsel appearing on behalf of

         the petitioner that petitioner is in custody for about 234 days

         and neither he was identified in the T.I. Parade nor any stolen

         articles was recovered from his possession and case is entirely

         based on the statement of the co-accused.

</p><p id="p_5">               Such   contention       of    the    petitioner     could      not   have   been

         disputed by the learned Counsel of the State.

</p><p id="p_6">               We have also carefully gone through the case diary and found

         the   submissions   of    the      learned   Counsel      of   the   petitioner    has

         substance neither there was any T.I. Parade held so far as the

         present   petitioner     is     concerned     nor   any    stolen     articles    were

         recovered from his possession.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                 2</span><p id="p_7">




    In view of above, we are of the opinion that this is a fit

case where the petitioner may be favoured with an order of bail.

The prayer for bail stands allowed.

</p><p id="p_8">     Let the petitioner be released on bail upon furnishing P.R.

Bond of Rs. 20,000/- with one local surety of the like amount and

shall not commit any offence while on bail or tamper with the

prosecution case. He shall attend the concerned P.S. thrice a week

between 5 p.m. to 7 p.m. until further order.

</p><p id="p_9">     The application is, thus, disposed of.



</p><p id="p_10">                                                (Ashim Kumar Roy, J.)



                                                 (Asim Kumar Ray, J.)
