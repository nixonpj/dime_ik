<div class="doc_title">The Bengal Rowing Club vs The State Of West Bengal &amp; Ors on 6 April, 2015</div><div class="doc_author">Author: Tapabrata Chakraborty</div>

<pre id="pre_1">                                  1



06.04.2015
S.L.-17(KB)


                                  W.P. 5911 (W) of 2015


                              The Bengal Rowing Club
                                     versus
                            The State of West Bengal &amp; Ors


                        Mr. Atish Dipankar Ray
                        Mr. Sougata Chatterjee
                                              ... For the petitioner.


                         Records reveal that an interim order of status

              quo in respect of the order impugned in the writ

              application was passed on 9th December, 2004. By the

              said order, this Court also directed the parties to exchange

              their affidavits.

                         Mr. Ray, learned advocate appearing for the

              petitioner submits that no affidavit-in-opposition has been

              filed by the petitioner till date.

                         Records further reveal that by an order dated 5th

              February, 2015, this Court granted leave to the petitioner

              to convert the writ petition to the Appellate Side and

              accordingly, such conversion has been effected and the

              matter has appeared today before this Court.

                         Mr. Ray, learned advocate appearing for the

              petitioner submits that by the orders of the Controlling
                   2


Authority and the Appellate Authority under the <a href="/doc/553799/" id="a_1">Payment

of Gratuity Act</a>, 1972, the petitioner was directed to pay

gratuity to the respondent no.4 without considering the</pre>

<p id="p_1">fact that a criminal proceeding was initiated against the

respondent    no.4    on   an   allegation    that   he   had

misappropriated an amount of Rs.20,000/-.

</p><p id="p_2">          Mr. Ray is, however, not in a position to furnish

instruction as regards the stage of the criminal proceeding

and accordingly he has prayed for an adjournment to avail

necessary instruction to that effect.

</p><p id="p_3">          Drawing the attention of this Court to page 67 of

the writ application, Mr. Ray submits that prior to

preference of the statutory appeal, a cheque dated 11th

July, 2002 of an amount of Rs.47,595/- has been

deposited before the Controlling Authority.

</p><p id="p_4">          The instant writ application is of the year 2004

and is relating to disbursement of gratuity and as such

the matter needs to be decided expeditiously.

</p><p id="p_5">          Accordingly, list this matter under the same

heading in the Daily Supplementary List of this Court on

14th May, 2015 as 'For Orders'.
</p>
<p id="p_6">          In the meantime, the respondent no.4 would be

at liberty to use an affidavit-in-opposition to the main writ
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                  3</span>


application within a period of two weeks from the date of

communication of this order. Reply thereto, if any, be filed

within a week thereafter.
</p>
<p id="p_7">          The petitioner is directed to intimate this order

to the respondents and to affirm an affidavit of service on

the returnable date.
</p>
<p id="p_8">

</p><p id="p_9">                              (Tapabrata Chakraborty,J.)
