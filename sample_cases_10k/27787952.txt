<div class="doc_title">Majdari vs State Of Rajasthan on 18 December, 2018</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: Sandeep Mehta, Vinit Kumar Mathur</div>

<p id="p_1">     HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR RAJASTHAN AT
                      JODHPUR
                    D.B. Criminal Writs No. 409/2018

Majdari W/o Sh. Hazari, Aged About 60 Years, B/c Kanjar ,
Deawli P.s. Pur Distt. Bhilwara (Convict/prisoner Kailash @
Pehlash S/o Sh. Hazari At Present Lodged In Central Jail Ajmer)
</p><p id="p_2">                                                        ----Petitioner
                                 Versus
</p><p id="p_3">1.     State Of Rajasthan, Through Secretary , Department Of
       Home Secretariat , Raj. Jaipur
</p><p id="p_4">2.     The Distt. Collector, Bhilwara
</p><p id="p_5">3.     The Superintendent, Central Jail , Ajmer
</p><p id="p_6">                                                     ----Respondents


For Petitioner(s)         :   Mr. Kaluram Bhati
For Respondent(s)         :   Mr. H.S. Gaur, associate to Mr. S.K.
</p><p id="p_7">                              Vyas, GA



           HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE SANDEEP MEHTA
        HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE VINIT KUMAR MATHUR

                                 Order

18/12/2018

          This parole writ petition is preferred by the wife of

convict prisoner Kailash @ Pehlash with the prayer for issuance of

a direction to release him on first parole for 20 days. The District

Parole Committee after considering his prayer for grant of first

parole rejected the same by citing the reason that there is

reasonable apprehension of his absconding and furthermore his

criminal background disentitles him to parole.

</p><p id="p_8">          We have perused the minutes of the meeting of the

District Parole Committee and also examined the reasons, which

are forthcoming for rejection of first parole to the convict. After

examining the minutes of the meeting of the District Parole
                                                                      (2 of 2)              [CRLW-409/2018]



                                   Committee, we are constrained to observe that the reasons spelt

                                   out for declining parole to the convict are absolutely jejune and

                                   therefore, this sort of order passed by the District Parole

                                   Committee cannot be sustained. It is also brought to our notice

                                   that co-convicts Shiv Lal @ Shiva, Shambhulal @ Shambhudiya

                                   and Rameshwar @ Sogosiya have already granted the benefit of

                                   parole by this court while accepting their writ petitions.

</p><p id="p_9">                                              In view thereof, this parole writ petition is allowed. The

                                   respondents are directed to release the convict-petitioner Kailash

                                   @ Pehlash S/o Shri Hazari to avail 20 days' first parole as per the

                                   provisions of the Rajasthan Prisoners Release on Parole Rules,

                                   1958, subject to furnishing a personal bond in a sum of

                                   Rs.50,000/- alongwith two surties of Rs.25,000/- (out of which

                                   one of the surety will be of close family member) to the

                                   satisfaction   of   Superintendent,   Central   Jai,   Ajmer.      The

                                   Superintendent, Central Jail, Ajmer shall be at liberty to impose

                                   other adequate and reasonable conditions to ensure return of the

                                   convict-prisoner to the State custody after availing the parole. His

                                   term of parole shall be computed from the date of his actual

                                   release.
</p>
<p id="p_10">

                                   (VINIT KUMAR MATHUR),J                       (SANDEEP MEHTA),J


                                    21-Pramod/-
</p>
