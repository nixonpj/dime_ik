<div class="doc_title">B.Manohar Reddy, vs A.P.State Road Transport ... on 26 September, 2018</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: Abhinand Kumar Shavili</div>

<pre id="pre_1">        HON'BLE SRI JUSTICE ABHINAND KUMAR SHAVILI

                            W.P.No.16443 OF 2002
ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">1.       This writ petition is filed for the following relief:

</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">               "...to call for the records from the respondents and issue
            an appropriate writ, order or direction, particularly one in
            the nature of Writ of Mandamus, declaring that the action of
            the respondents in preventing the petitioner from discharge
            of his duties w.e.f 05.07.2002 without giving any order in
            writing and without disclosing any reasons as illegal, unjust,
            contrary to law, arbitrary, in violation of principles of natural
            justice, in violation of Sec.25-N and Sec.25-F of the
            Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, amounts to an act of
            victimization, unfair labour practice and violative of Articles
            14, 16 &amp; 21 of the Constitution of India and grant all
            consequential benefits."
</blockquote>

<p id="p_2">2.       When the matter is taken up for hearing, learned counsel

on either side, submit in pursuance of the interim order

granted by this Court on 02-09-2002 in WPMP No.20623 of

2002, the petitioner has been continued in service and

subsequently, his services were regularized.


</p><p id="p_3">3.       In view of the said submission, the Writ Petition is

closed. No costs.
</p>
<p id="p_4">
</p><p id="p_5">         Miscellaneous petitions, if any, pending shall stand

closed.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><p id="p_7">                                       ________________________________
                                       JUSTICE ABHINAND KUMAR SHAVILI
26th   September, 2018
