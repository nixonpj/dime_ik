                   PRAYER: This Writ Petition filed under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the Constitution
                   of India praying for issuance of a Writ of Mandamus, to directing the
                   respondents    1   and      2    to    consider   and   dispose      of    petitioner's
                   representation dated 21.03.2018 on the file of the respondents 1 and 2
                   in accordance with law.


                                      For Petitioner           :     Mr.S.Sairaman

                                      For Respondents :              Mr.E.Balamurugan
                                                                     Special Government Pleader
http://www.judis.nic.in
                                                       2

                                                  ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">                      The petitioner claims to be a Doctor, who is running a hospital in

              the name and style of “Dayasankar Clinic” at Kaveripakkam.



</p><p id="p_2">                      2. It is stated that the second respondent had lodged a complaint

              before the third respondent stating that some unqualified persons were

              giving treatment to the public in the said Clinic. Therefore, the second

              respondent conducted enquiry and found that two nurses in the clinic

              were not qualified. A Criminal complaint was lodged against the

              petitioner and he had taken anticipatory bail in Crl.O.P.No.3596 of

              2018.
</p>
<p id="p_3">

</p><p id="p_4">                      3. According to the second respondent, he has given a report

              alleging that the treatment is given by unqualified persons. Therefore,

              the premises was sealed. The petitioner has sent a representation on

              21.03.2018 to the first and second respondents to de-seal the premises

              and the said petition has not been considered till today. Therefore, the

              above writ petition has been filed seeking a direction to the respondents

              1 and 2 to consider the said representation and pass appropriate orders

              on merits.
</p>
<p id="p_5">

</p><p id="p_6">                      4. Considering the facts and circumstances of the case and the

                   submissions
http://www.judis.nic.in          of the learned counsel on either side, this Court, without
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                        3</span>

                   expressing any opinion on the merits of representation, directs the

                   second respondent to conduct enquiry after issuing notice to all the

                   persons interested in this regard and affording them an opportunity of

                   hearing and pass appropriate orders within a period of four weeks from

                   the date of receipt of a copy of this order.



</p><p id="p_7">                          5. With the above direction, the writ petition is disposed of. No

                   Costs.
</p>
