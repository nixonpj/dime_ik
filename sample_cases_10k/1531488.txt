<div class="doc_title">Sajan Ram vs The State Of Bihar &amp;Amp; Ors on 28 February, 2011</div><pre id="pre_1">                  IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                               CWJC No.18861 of 2010
                                     SAJAN RAM .
                                        Versus
                            THE STATE OF BIHAR &amp; ORS .
                                      -----------
</pre>
<p id="p_1">2.   28.02.2011                 Heard learned counsel for the petitioner

                  and the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                The petitioner is aggrieved by the orders

                  dated 5.11.2009 and 29.1.2010 cancelling the Public

                  Distribution     System    Licence    dealership     of    the

                  petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                Learned     counsel    for    the    petitioner

                  submits that his case is fully covered by a Bench

                  decision in C.W.J.C. No. 11095 of 2008 and

                  analogous cases (including C.W.J.C. No. 5448 of

                  2010) disposed on 26.08.2010. It is submitted that

                  the petitioner proposes to approach the authorities

                  seeking similar treatment.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                Counsel for the State submits that it

                  remains a matter for examination by the authorities

                  whether the petitioner is similarly situated on facts

                  and law or not only in which event can he be granted

                  similar   treatment.    The   stand    of    the   State    is

                  unexceptionable.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                The writ application is disposed off with a

                  direction to the respondents to examine the claims of
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                              2</span>




           the petitioner in light of the orders of this Court in

           C.W.J.C. No. 11095 of 2008 and analogous cases

           (including C.W.J.C. No. 5448 of 2010).           If the

           authorities are satisfied that he is similarly situated,

           they shall accord him similar treatment.         If the

           authorities opined otherwise, they shall be at liberty

           to dispose off his representation by a reasoned and

           speaking order.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                      The writ application stands disposed.


</p><pre id="pre_2">P. Kumar                                      ( Navin Sinha, J.)
 </pre>
