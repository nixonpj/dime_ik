and

V.R.Vijayaraman                                         ..  Petitioner

Vs.

1.The Director,
   Central Bureau of Investigation,
   Office of the Central Bureau of Investigation,
   Plot No.5B, C.G.O. Complex, 
   10th Floor, B Wing, Lodhi Road,
   New Delhi ? 110 003.

2.The Deputy Secretary (WD), 
   Government of India,
   Department of Women and Child Welfare, 
   Ministry of Human Resources Development, 
   Shastri Bhavan, New Delhi ? 110 001.

3.The Secretary to the Government of Tamil Nadu,
   Department of Social Welfare and Nutritious Meals Programme,
   Fort St. George, Chennai ? 600 009.          ..  Respondents

PRAYER: Writ Petition is filed under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the Constitution of India
praying for the issuance of a Writ of Mandamus directing the respondents
herein to consider the petitioner's representation dated 01.12.2016 and take
necessary action within the time stipulated by this Court.

!For Petitioner                 : Mr.V.R.Vijayaraman 
                                  (Party-in-person)
For Respondent No.2     : Mr.R.Murugappan, 
                                  Senior Panel Counsel for
                                  Central Government.
        For Respondent No.3             : Mr.M.Govindan, 
                                  Special Government Pleader.
        

:ORDER  
</pre>
<p id="p_1">[Order of the Court was made by A.SELVAM, J.]  
        This writ petition has been filed under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_2">Article 226</a> of the Constitution
of India praying to direct the respondents to consider the representation
alleged to have been given by the petitioner on 01.12.2016, by way of issuing
a writ of mandamus. 
</p>
<p id="p_2">        2.The petitioner is present.
</p>
<p id="p_3">        3.Mr.R.Murugappan, learned Senior Panel Counsel for Central Government, 
has taken notice for the second respondent and Mr.M.Govindan, learned Special
Government Pleader, has taken notice for the third respondent.  Considering
the nature of the relief sought in the writ petition, notice need not be sent
to the first respondent.
</p>
<p id="p_4">        4.It is seen from the records that the petitioner has given a
representation on 01.12.2016.  The main grievance expressed on the side of
the petitioner is that the said representation has not been considered by the
concerned authority.  Under the said circumstances, the following order can
be passed. 
</p>
<p id="p_5">        5.In fine, this writ petition is allowed without costs and the third
respondent is directed to consider the representation dated 01.12.2016 and
dispose of the same within a period of one month.
</p>

</p>
