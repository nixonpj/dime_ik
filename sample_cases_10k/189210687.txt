         IN THE HIGH COURT OF KARNATAKA,
                  GULBARGA BENCH

       DATED THIS THE 17TH DAY OF JUNE, 2014

THE HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE ANAND BYRAREDDY

 MISCELLANEOUS FIRST APPEAL NO.31513/2010 (MV)

BETWEEN:

The Depot Manager, KSRTC,
Sindhanur,
Through the Divisional Controller,
NEKRTC Raichur, Raichur,
Represented by its
Chief Law Officer,
Central Office, Sarige Sadana,
Gulbarga.                                  ... APPELLANT.

(Shri Sudhir Singh R. Vijapur, Advocate)

AND:

Basavaraj,
S/o. Karilingappa @ Kariyappa,
Aged 18 years,
Occupation: Nil,
Resident of Mad-Sirwar,
Taluk: Sindhanur,
Now resident of Ruma Battery,
Arab Mohalla Circle,
Raichur &amp; also Azad Nagar,
                               2




Raichur,
District: Raichur.                      ... RESPONDENT


       This Miscellaneous First Appeal is filed under Section
173 (1) of <a href="/doc/785258/" id="a_1">Motor Vehicles Act</a>, 1988 against the Judgement and
Award dated 16.06.2010 passed in MVC No.8/2009 on the file
of the MACT &amp; Fast Track Court-I, Raichur, partly allowing
the claim petition and awarding compensation of Rs.1,72,600/-
with interest at 6% per annum.

      This appeal coming on for further orders, this day, the
Court delivered the following:

                        JUDGMENT
</pre>
<p id="p_1">      There was a peremptory order dated 3.8.2011, whereby

the appellant was required to furnish correct address, cover,

acknowledgement, etc., to issue notice to the respondent.

However, the condition imposed by the peremptory order was

that, if needful was not done within two weeks, the appeal

would stand dismissed. There was default and the appeal is

deemed to have been dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_2">      2. By virtue of a circular dated 26.08.2011 in

M.F.A.3616/2007, whereby the Registry was directed that in all

such cases where by virtue of peremptory orders appeals stand
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                 3</span>




dismissed or are deemed to be dismissed, the same should be

placed before the Court for further orders and the Registry

ought not to consign the appeal straightaway to the Decree

Branch, the present appeal is listed before this Court.


</p><p id="p_3">      This Court having chosen to impose the condition that

the appeal itself would stand dismissed if needful was not done

in respect of issue of notice to the respondent, that order cannot

be amended at this point of time. Therefore, the appeal stands

dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_4">      The amount in deposit shall be transferred to the

Tribunal, for the benefit of the claimant.




</p><p id="p_5">                                              Sd/-
</p><p id="p_6">                                             JUDGE


KS
