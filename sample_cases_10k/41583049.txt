<div class="doc_title">Dev Singh vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 5 May, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                      1
                M.Cr.C.No.4536/2017

            (<a href="/doc/414120/" id="a_1">Dev Singh v. State of M.P</a>.)
05/05/2017
      Shri J.S. Kushwah, Counsel for the applicant.
      Mohd.     Irshad,    Panel     Lawyer   for    the
respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Case diary is available.
</p><p id="p_2">      By order dated 28.04.2017, this Court had
directed the State to produce the case diary of
Crime No.138/2017 but the said case diary has not
been produced.
</p><p id="p_3">      Under these circumstances, this case is finally
heard on the strength of the case diary of this case.
</p><p id="p_4">      This is an application filed under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_2">Section 439</a>
of Cr.P.C. for grant of bail.
</p><p id="p_5">      The     applicant    has     been   arrested   on
27.03.2017 in connection with Crime No.137/2017
registered by Police Station Mungawali, District
Ashok Nagar for offence punishable under <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_3">Sections
307</a>, <a href="/doc/594493/" id="a_4">294</a>, <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_5">341</a>, <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_6">34</a> of IPC.
</p><p id="p_6">      It is submitted by the counsel for the applicant
that according to the prosecution case, the applicant
had assaulted the injured Dev Singh on his head by
means of an axe, whereas, according to the MLC
report of Govind Singh, three injuries were found,
out of which, two were lacerated wounds and the
doctor had specifically opined that although injuries
were caused by a hard and blunt object and the
nature of the injuries are simple. It is submitted
that in fact on the report of the accused party, a
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                                 2</span>
                         M.Cr.C.No.4536/2017

       cross-case      has   been     registered   against    the
       complainant party in Crime No.138/2017 registered
       by Police Station Mungawali, District Ashok Nagar. It
       is further submitted that in fact the applicant had
       also sustained injuries in the incident. The applicant
       is aged about 40 years and he is an agriculturist
       and has no criminal history and he is a reputed
       resident of the locality and there is no possibility of
       his absconding or tampering with the prosecution
       case.
</p><p id="p_7">               Per contra, the counsel for the respondent
       vehemently opposed the application.
</p><p id="p_8">               Considering the facts and circumstances of the
       case and without commenting on the merits of the
       case, the application is allowed. It is directed that
       the applicant be released on bail on furnishing a
       personal bond in the sum of Rs.40,000/-(Rupees
       forty thousand only) with a surety bond of the
       same amount to the satisfaction of the Trial Court to
       appear before the Trial Court on the dates given by
       the concerned Court.
</p><p id="p_9">               This order shall be effective till the end of trial
       but in case of bail jump, it shall become ineffective.
</p><p id="p_10">               Certified copy as per rules.</p><pre id="pre_2">


                                              (G.S.Ahluwalia)
(ra)                                               Judge
 </pre>
