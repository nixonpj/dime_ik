       Daughter of Huseinsab,
       Advocate, Bijapur,

                                            ...RESPONDENTS
                               2




(By Shri. B.J. Eswarappa, Government Pleader for Respondent
No.1
Shri. Umesh V Mandapur, Advocate for Respondent No.2
                             *****
       This Criminal Petition filed under Section 482 code of
Criminal Procedure, 1973, praying to quash the entire
proceedings commencing from the complaint lodged by Meera
Nadaf, so being one, as abuse of process of court and direct an
enquiry on the points germane like as to why the court and
presiding officer has not noted the receipt of the order dated
9.7.2003 in Criminal Petition 129/2000.

      This Criminal Petition coming on for Hearing this day,
the court made the following:

                              ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">      The    petitioner appearing in person and the learned

Counsel for respondent no.2 would submit that the complaint is

of the year 2000 and having regard to the lapse of time and on

the intervention of well-wishers, the matter stands compounded

as it is a compoundable offence that is alleged. The petitioner

also agrees to pay a sum of Rs.25,000/- ex-gratia as a token of

goodwill which the learned Counsel for the respondent shall

receive on behalf of respondent no.2 and further the petitioner

also undertakes to withdraw the case in CC No.20903/2013
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                 3</span>




pending before the CMM VIII Court, and the proceedings

initiated by the petitioner before the Bar Council is already said

to have been withdrawn.
</p>
<p id="p_2">      Recording the joint memo, as regards the payment and

receipt of Rs.25,000/-, the matter stands closed. The

compounding of the complaint would result in the complaint as

against accused no.1 also being quashed.



</p><p id="p_3">                                          Sd/-
</p><p id="p_4">                                         JUDGE
