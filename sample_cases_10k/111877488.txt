<div class="doc_title">Bharat Bhushan Manocha vs Unknown on 28 January, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">          IN THE COURT OF MS. POOJA TALWAR:
      JSCC-CUM-ASCJ-CUM-GUARDIAN JUDGE (SOUTH):
               SAKET COURTS, NEW DELHI



RCA No. 5751/2016
CNR No. DLST03­001924­2016


1.     Bharat Bhushan Manocha
       S/o Late Wazir Chand Manocha
       R/o E­2, Preeet Vihar,
       Delhi.                  
2.     Raj Kumar Manocha
       S/o Late Wazir Chand Manocha
       R/o D­91, Preet Vihar, 
       Delhi.                                              .....Appellants


                                        versus

                                           
Gaon Sabha Neb Sarai
Through BDO (South)
M.B. Road, Saket, 
New Delhi.                                                         ....Respondent</pre>




<pre id="pre_2">       Date of filing of the appeal               :       24.10.2016
       Date of reserving order                    :       24.01.2017
       Date of pronouncement                      :       28.01.2017




<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">RCA No.5751/2016 Bharat Bhushan Manocha &amp; Anr. Vs. Gaon Sabha Neb Sarai Page 1 of 10</span>
                              J U D G E M E N T


1.            The   present   appeal   has   been   filed   assailing   the
</pre><p id="p_1">judgment and decree dated 28.09.2016 by virtue of which, suit
of the plaintiff was dismissed. The present appeal has been filed
by  the   plaintiff.  Parties   are   referred   to   hereunder  according  to
their litigative status before the learned Trial Court.
</p>

<p id="p_2">Plaintiff's version:
</p><p id="p_3">2.            It is averred by plaintiffs that plot of land measuring
1 bigha (1008 sq. yards) falling in Khasra No. 421 min situated
in Village Neb Sarai, Mehrauli, New Delhi (hereinafter referred
to as suit property) was purchased by their father by virtue of
Sale Deed dated 21.01.1992. The land was  got demarcated by
father of plaintiffs and a boundary wall was raised surrounding
the   same.   Two   rooms   were   constructed   and   an   iron   gate   was
installed. From the date of purchase, father of plaintiffs was in
uninterrupted peaceful possession of land. After the death of his
father, plaintiffs are in actual physical possession of the property
and are duly assessed by the Property Tax by MCD. In August,
2012,   when   the   plaintiffs   visited   the   property   they   found   a
portion of the boundary wall was broken and a board of  "Gaon
Sabha   Land"  was   installed.   Therefore,   plaintiffs   sent   an
application   to   the   Deputy   Commissioner   (South)   dated

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">RCA No.5751/2016 Bharat Bhushan Manocha &amp; Anr. Vs. Gaon Sabha Neb Sarai Page 2 of 10</span>
 02.09.2012 for demarcation of their land falling in Khasra No.
421 min, Village Neb Sarai, New Delhi. The said letter was not
replied   by   the   Deputy   Commissioner.   On   07.02.2013,   plaintiff
no. 1 visited the suit property and found some labourers cutting
down the plants grown in the suit property. The said labourers
informed the plaintiffs that they have been engaged by defendant
for construction of boundary wall surrounding Khasra No. 420,
Village, Neb Sarai, New Delhi. Plaintiff no. 1 immediately stopped
them from carrying out any work and reported the matter to the
police  and  the  work  could  be  stopped  with   the   intervention  of
police. Plaintiffs have been enjoying actual physical possession of
the   property   since   1992.   On   11.03.2013,   plaintiff   no.   2   was
threatened   from   carrying   out   any   construction   on   the   suit
property by a group of men comprising of an official of defendant
and Contractor. There is constant threat of dispossession to the
plaintiffs. Hence, the suit was filed. 
</p>

<p id="p_4">Defendant's version:
</p><p id="p_5">3.            Defendant entered contest by filing written statement.
As per the defendant, plaintiffs have encroached upon the Gram
Sabha land and hence, no relief can be granted to plaintiffs. As
per the revenue record, the suit land is not recorded in the name
of   plaintiffs.   The   boundary   wall   raised   by   plaintiffs   was
demolished after proper demarcation by the defendant. The land
in   question   has   been   allotted   to   the   Health   Department   for


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">RCA No.5751/2016 Bharat Bhushan Manocha &amp; Anr. Vs. Gaon Sabha Neb Sarai Page 3 of 10</span>
 construction  of dispensary by the Gram  Sabha. Plaintiffs have
been informed not to encroach upon the Gram Sabha land. The
land in question falls in Khasra No. 420 which belongs to Gram
Sabha. The suit deserves dismissal. 
</p>

<p id="p_6">Issues :
</p><p id="p_7">4.            On the pleadings of the parties, following issues were
framed   by   Ld.   Trial   Court   on   29.10.2015.   The   issues   are   as
under :
</p><p id="p_8">1.     Whether   the   plaintiffs   are   entitled   to   decree   of
permanent injunction against the defendant as prayed for?
OPP
</p><p id="p_9">2.     Relief.
</p>

<p id="p_10">Evidence of plaintiffs:
</p><p id="p_11">5.            Plaintiffs   in   order   to   prove   their   case   examined
plaintiff no. 1 Sh. Bharat Bhushan Manocha as PW1. He relied
upon documents Ex.PW1/1 to Ex.PW12.
</p><p id="p_12">              One   Sh.   Pawan   Kumar,   Patwari   from   SDM   Office,
M.B.   Road   was   examined   as   PW2.   He   produced   the   original
record of Khatoni and the same is exhibited as Ex.PW2/A. 
</p><p id="p_13">              Sh. Baljit Singh Dhankar deposed as PW3. He relied
upon document Mark A. 
</p><p id="p_14">              Sh.   Jagbir   Singh   who   carried   out   the
demarcation   was   examined   as   PW4.   He   proved   the   document

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">RCA No.5751/2016 Bharat Bhushan Manocha &amp; Anr. Vs. Gaon Sabha Neb Sarai Page 4 of 10</span>
 Ex.PW1/12.
</p>

<p id="p_15">Defendant's Evidence :
</p><p id="p_16">6.            Sh.   S.D.   Sharma,   BDO   (South)   was   examined   as
DW1. He also relied upon documents Mark A to Mark D.
</p><p id="p_17">              Sh.   N.D.   Vasist,   Horticulture   Assistant,   BDO   Office
was examined as DW2. 
</p>

<p id="p_18">Findings of learned Trial Court :
</p><p id="p_19">7.            The   learned   Trial   Court   vide   its   judgment   dated
28.09.2016 dismissed the suit of plaintiffs holding that plaintiffs
have not approached the Court with clean hands hence, are not
entitled to an equitable relief in the form of injunction. Plaintiffs
have   failed   to   prove   that   the   defendant   is   raising   or   trying   to
raise construction in the property which belongs to the plaintiffs.
With these observations, the suit of plaintiffs was dismissed.
</p>

<p id="p_20">Grounds of Appeal :
</p><p id="p_21">8.            Plaintiff has challenged the judgment on the following
grounds:­
              The impugned order has been passed against law and
facts   of   the   case.   The   learned   Trial   Court   has   erred   in   not
appreciating that defendant has demolished the wall of plaintiffs
without proper demarcation and without following due process of
law. The learned Trial Court has overlooked the fact that the suit


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_5">RCA No.5751/2016 Bharat Bhushan Manocha &amp; Anr. Vs. Gaon Sabha Neb Sarai Page 5 of 10</span>
 land was bounded by plaintiffs who are in physical possession
since 1992. The testimony of PW3 has been ignored. Evidence of
PW2 has not been relied upon by the learned Trial Court Judge.
The learned Trial Court erred in not considering the fact that all
the demarcation reports have been challenged by defendant and
in absence of any demarcation report, defendant could not have
demolished the boundary wall.  The learned Trial Court has not
placed   reliance   on   Special   Provisions   Act   of   2007   by   virtue   of
which, defendant was directed to maintain the status quo with
regard to any encroachment of land including government land if
the same forms part of an unauthorized colony till 31.12.2017.
The learned Trial Court has ignored the observation of learned
Appellate Court vide order dated 02.07.2014. 
</p>

<p id="p_22">Findings of the Court :
</p><p id="p_23">9.            I   have   heard   the   arguments   advanced   by   rival
counsels  and   have   scrutinized   the  entire  material  available   on
record   and   specifically   gone   through   the   order   passed   by   the
learned   Predecessor   of   this   Court   while   deciding   the   appeal
against the Interim Order dated 02.07.2014.
</p>

<p id="p_24">10.           Learned  Trial   Court   has   decided   all   the   issues.
However, I would decide all the issues once again. 
</p><p id="p_25">              Issue no. 1
              Whether   the   plaintiffs   are   entitled   to   decree   of


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_6">RCA No.5751/2016 Bharat Bhushan Manocha &amp; Anr. Vs. Gaon Sabha Neb Sarai Page 6 of 10</span>
 permanent injunction against the defendant as prayed for? OPP
              The onus to prove this issue was on the plaintiffs. It
was for the plaintiffs to prove that they are entitled to relief of
permanent injunction.  
</p><p id="p_26">              The learned Trial Court has dismissed the suit of the
plaintiffs on returning a finding that the plaintiffs have failed to
establish   that   the   defendant   is   raising   or   trying   to   raise   any
construction in the property of plaintiffs and also on the ground
that the plaintiffs have not got the demarcation of the property
done   properly   and   have   not   approached   the   Court   with   clean
hands. 
</p><p id="p_27">              As   far   as   proper   demarcation   of   the   property   is
concerned,   as   per   the   learned   Predecessor   of   this   Court   the
demarcation   was   carried   out   and   a   report   was   filed   on
30.06.2013.   The   said   demarcation   was   carried   out   for   both
Khasra No. 420 and 421, Village Neb Sarai, Mehrauli, New Delhi.
As per the said demarcation report, an area of 29.61 sq. mtrs in
possession  of  plaintiffs  was  over  and  above  their  ownership  of
the   area   falling   in   Khasra   No.   421.   It   is   mentioned   in   the
aforesaid   order   of   the   Appellate   Court   that   the   physical
demarcation was done in presence of representatives of both the
parties.   It   is   also   stated   in   the   said   order   that   counsel   for
defendant   did   not   dispute   the   demarcation   report   which   was
submitted on 30.10.2013.
</p><p id="p_28">              The   report  dated  30.06.2013   has  not  been   reversed


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_7">RCA No.5751/2016 Bharat Bhushan Manocha &amp; Anr. Vs. Gaon Sabha Neb Sarai Page 7 of 10</span>
 till date by any competent authority.  The said report is the last
demarcation   got   done   in   presence   of   both   the   parties   by   the
order of the Court. Both the parties at the time of appeal had
accepted its propriety. Hence, the said report can be said to be
an authentic demarcation got done after following due process of
law. As per the said report an area of 29.61 sq. mtrs is more in
possession of plaintiffs. Plaintiffs had consented to forgo the said
chunk   of   land   in   order   to   avoid   further   dispute   between   the
parties. 
</p><p id="p_29">              In  my  considered  view  there   was  no  requirement of
leading any evidence once this report had come on record. The
learned Trial Court erred in returning a finding that the plaintiffs
have   failed   to   establish   any   construction   being   carried   out   by
defendant   on   the   land   of   plaintiffs.   Admittedly,   the   boundary
wall   constructed   by   plaintiffs   was   demolished   by   defendant,
though   the   entire   land   on   which   the   boundary   wall   was
constructed   does   not   belong   to   them   as   per   the   demarcation
report. Prior to the demarcation report dated 30.06.2013, both
the   parties   had   got   their   respective   demarcations   done.   The
defendant has even challenged the demarcation which was done
by defendant itself. 
</p><p id="p_30">              Be   that   as   it   may,   the   report   dated   30.06.2013   is
before the Court which is prepared by following due process of
law and in presence of both the parties is a reliable document.
Since even after getting demarcation done, plaintiffs were unsure


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_8">RCA No.5751/2016 Bharat Bhushan Manocha &amp; Anr. Vs. Gaon Sabha Neb Sarai Page 8 of 10</span>
 about the area which is owned by them and for this reason they
came before the Court. In case, plaintiffs had malafide intention,
they would not have approached the Court. Moreover, after filing
of the demarcation report they have been even consented to forgo
an   area   of   29.61   sq.   mtrs   of   land   which   as   per   the   report   is
excess in their possession. So by saying that plaintiffs have not
approached the Court with clean hands would be incorrect. 
</p><p id="p_31">              Even DW2 who is a witness of defendant has stated
that the area delivered to the Health Department had a pakka
road on its two sides and on the other two sides of the said land,
there was a private property. It can be safely discerned that the
private property is that of plaintiffs which further substantiates
the claim of plaintiffs that the suit property belongs to them. 
</p><p id="p_32">              In   my   considered   view   plaintiffs   have   succeeded   in
proving that defendant has tried to carry out construction on the
area which is owned by them. 
</p><p id="p_33">              As it has come on record and conceded by plaintiffs
that they will forgo an area of 29.61 sq. mtrs which exceeds the
ownership   of   land   of   plaintiffs   and   the   defendant   shall   not
interfere   in   the   use   and   enjoyment   of   area   as   per   the
demarcation   report   which   falls   in   Khasra   No.   421,Village   Neb
Sarai,   Mehrauli,   New   Delhi   under   the   ownership   of   plaintiffs.
This issue is decided in favour of plaintiffs. 
</p>

<p id="p_34">              With these observations, the judgment of the learned


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_9">RCA No.5751/2016 Bharat Bhushan Manocha &amp; Anr. Vs. Gaon Sabha Neb Sarai Page 9 of 10</span>
 Trial Court  is  hereby set aside.  Defendant and  its officials are
restrained from causing any interference or disturbance in the
peaceful occupation and enjoyment of plaintiffs and further from
forcibly eviction or dispossession of plaintiffs from the property
of   the   plaintiffs   as   shown   in   demarcation   report   dated
30.06.2013.
</p>

<p id="p_35">11.           TCR be sent back with the copy of this Judgment. 
</p>

<p id="p_36">12.           Appeal file be consigned to Record Room.
</p>
