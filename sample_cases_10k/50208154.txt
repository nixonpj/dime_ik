                                            (PRASHIK Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)


                      Jabalpur, Dated : 02-12-2019
                              Shri Chiman Lal Sethi, learned counsel for the appellant.

                              Shri Shivam Hazari, learned Panel Lawyer for the respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">                              No ne for the respondent No.2/complainant despite compliance of
                     provision of Section 15(A)(III) of SC/ST (Prevention of Atrocities) Act by
                     the respondent No.1.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                              Case diary perused and arguments heard.
</p><p id="p_3">                              This criminal appeal has been filed under Section 14 (A) of SC/ST
                     (Prevention of Atrocities) Act 1989 against the order dated 18.10.2019
                     passed by Special Judge, SC/ST (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, Burhanpur,
                     in SC ATR No.21/2019, whereby learned Special Judge rejected the bail
                     application filed by appellant Prashik under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of Cr.P.C. to get
                     bail in Crime No.390/2019 registered at Police Station Nepa Nagar, District
                     Burhanpur for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/203036/" id="a_2">Sections 354</a>, <a href="/doc/886598/" id="a_3">365</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_4">506</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_5">34</a> of
                     IPC, Section 7/8 of POCSO Act, <a href="/doc/1507082/" id="a_6">Section 3</a> (1)(w), 3 (2)(v), 3(2)(va) of

                     SC/ST (Prevention of Atrocities) Act.
</p><p id="p_4">                              A s per the prosecution case, on 16.08.2019 at 10:00 AM, when the
                     prosecutrix was going to Government Higher Secondary School, Nepa
                     Nagar, on the way, near Ekta Nagar, appellant Prashik and co-accused Rajesh
                     and Prafulla came there on bike and abducted the prosecutrix and took her in
                     a forest where co-accused Prafulla committed rape with the prosecutrix. On
                     that, police registered Crime No.390/2019 for the offences punishable under
                     <a href="/doc/203036/" id="a_7">Sections 354</a>, <a href="/doc/886598/" id="a_8">365</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_9">506</a>, <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_10">34</a> of IPC Section 7/8 of POCSO Act, <a href="/doc/1507082/" id="a_11">Section 3</a> (1)
                     (w), 3 (2)(v), 3(2)(va) of SC/ST (Prevention of Atrocities) Act and arrested
                     the appellant on 20.08.2019. On that, appellant filed an application under
                     <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_12">Section 439</a> of Cr.P.C. for releasing him on bail, which was rejected by the
                     learned Special Judge, SC/ST (Prevention of Atrocities) Act vide order dated
                     18.10.2019. Being aggrieved by the impugned order, appellant filed this

                     Criminal Appeal.
</p><p id="p_5">                              Learned counsel for the appellant submits that appellant is innocent and
                     has falsely been implicated in the offence. It is further submitted that in the
                     FIR and in the statement of the prosecutrix recorded under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_13">Section 164</a> of
                     CrPC by JMFC, the prosecutrix did not depose that co-accused Prafulla

                     committed rape with her. Even otherwise, there is no allegation against the
                     appellant that he committed rape with the prosecutrix. Charge sheet has been
                     filed. The appellant is in custody since 20.08.2019 and the conclusion of trial
                     will take time, hence prayed for release of the appellant on bail.
</p><p id="p_6">                              Learned counsel for the State opposed the prayer and submitted that
                     appellant Prashik and co-accused Rajesh and Prafulla abducted the
                     prosecutrix and co-accused Prafulla committed rape with the prosecutrix. So,
                     he should not be released on bail.
</p><p id="p_7">                              Looking to the facts and circumstances of the case and the fact that
                     there is no allegation against the appellant that he committed rape with the
                     prosecutrix, appellant has no criminal past, he is in custody since 20.08.2019,
                     charge sheet has been filed and conclusion of trial will take time, without
                     commenting on merit, the appeal is allowed and it is directed that the
                     appellant Prashik b e released on bail upon his furnishing personal bond in
                     the sum of Rs.50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand only) with one surety in the
                     like amount to the satisfaction of the concerned C.J.M/trial Court for his
                     appearance before the concerned Court on all such dates as may be fixed in
                     this behalf by the trial Court during the pendency of trial.
</p><p id="p_8">                              This order will remain operative subject to compliance of the following
                     conditions by the appellant :
</p><p id="p_9">                              1.The appellant will comply with all the terms and conditions of the
                     bond executed by him;
</p><p id="p_10">                              2.The appellant will cooperate in the trial;
</p><p id="p_11">                              3.The appellant will not indulge himself in extending inducement, threat
                     o r promise to any person acquainted with the fact of the case so as to
