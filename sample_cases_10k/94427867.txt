<div class="doc_title">M/S Sreegopal Automobiles vs State Of Jharkhand &amp; Ors on 23 August, 2013</div><pre id="pre_1">            IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                    Cont. Case (Civil) No.427 of 2012

            M/s Sreegopal Automobiles.                  ...... Petitioner
                                  -Versus-
            The State of Jharkhand &amp; Ors.              ....... Opposite Parties.
                                   ------

          CORAM : HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE NARENDRA NATH TIWARI
                                  ------
            For the Petitioner   :       Mr. V. Shivnath, Sr. Advocate
            For the Opp. Parties :       J.C. to S.C. (Mines)
                                 ------

6/23.08.2013</pre><p id="p_1">:     When this case is taken up, learned counsel appearing on
            behalf of the opposite parties submitted that a reasoned order has
            been passed in compliance with the direction of this Court and it
            has been found that the claim of the petitioner is not admissible.
            Learned counsel submitted that the order of this Court is fully
            complied with.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                  Mr. V. Shivnath, learned senior counsel appearing on behalf
            of the petitioner submitted that the order is wholly erroneous,
            arbitrary and perfunctory. There is no compliance of the order as
            the opposite parties have refused the claim on frivolous ground.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                  Having heard learned counsel for the parties and perused
            the show-cause reply, I find that the reasoned order has been
            passed by the opposite parties. They have not admitted the claim
            and the reasons have been assigned for not admitting the same.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                  In view thereof, I find that the order of this Court has been
            complied with. No further proceeding is warranted in this case.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                  Rule is, accordingly, discharged.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                  Proceeding is dropped.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                  However, if the petitioner is aggrieved by the order, it has
            liberty to challenge the same and seek appropriate relief before the
            proper forum.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                  This contempt case stands disposed of.
</p>


<p id="p_9">                                                 (Narendra Nath Tiwari, J.)

Shamim/
 </p>
