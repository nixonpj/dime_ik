<div class="doc_title">Amrit Sao vs The State Of Bihar on 2 June, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.16642 of 2014
                 ======================================================
                 Amrit Sao, son of Ganauri Sao, resident of village- Mahuayet, Police
                 Station- Wazirganj, District- Gaya
                                                                        .... .... Petitioner
                                                     Versus
                 The State of Bihar
                                                                   .... .... Opposite Party
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :    Mr.
                 For the Opposite Party/s    : Mr.
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE PRABHAT KUMARJHA
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>
<p id="p_1">                                              ------------------
</p>
<p id="p_2">2   02-06-2014             Heard learned counsel for the petitioner, Addl. Public

                 Prosecutor and learned counsel for the informant.

</p><p id="p_3">                           The petitioner, Amrit Sao, seeks regular bail in Mufassil

                 (Gaya) P.S. Case No.121 of 2013 registered for the offence under

                 <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_1">Sections 302</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_2">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code and 27 of the <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_3">Arms Act</a>.

</p><p id="p_4">                           Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that the

                 informant Nilu Devi named Sanjay Singh, Amrit Sao( petitioner),

                 Mithu Sao,      Santosh Singh, his mother Prabha Devi, Dhanbir

                 Singh and others and alleged that when she opened the door of her

                 house, all the seven persons were standing near the door of her

                 house, but in the meantime two unknown persons came there and

                 fired at her husband. The informant did not name the two

                 unknown person. No allegation of firing was made against the

                 petitioner. Learned counsel for the petitioner has further submitted
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> 2   Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.16642 of 2014 (2) dt.02-06-2014</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                             2/3</span>




             that Mithu Sao, Dhanveer Singh and Prabha Devi have been

             granted bail by this Court vide Cr.Misc.No.36977 of 2013,

             Cr.Misc.No. 23097 of 2013 and Cr.Misc.No.22865 of 2013. Co-

             accused Sanjay Singh @ Sanjay Kumar has been granted bail by

             the      learned        Addl.         Sessions        Judge-III,   Gaya   vide

             B.P.No.766/14/128/14. The case of the petitioner stands on similar

             footing. Hence, the petitioner be enlarged on bail.

</p><p id="p_5">                         Learned Addl. Public Prosecutor has opposed the prayer

             for bail of the petitioner and submitted that accused persons

             were indulged in black marketing and that is why the murder of

             the husband of the informant was committed.

</p><p id="p_6">                         Perused the record. It appears that the informant, Nilu

             Devi, wife of the deceased named petitioner along with six other

             persons and made very specific allegation that when she opened

             the door, the petitioner and others were standing there, but she

             alleged that two unknown persons came there and made firing

             causing death of her husband. There is no allegation of firing

             against the petitioner. Similarly situated co-accused persons have

             been granted bail.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                          Considering the facts and circumstances, the petitioner,

             namely, Amrit Sao, is directed to be enlarged on bail on furnishing

             bail of Rs.10,000/-( ten thousand) with two sureties of the like
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3"> 3       Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.16642 of 2014 (2) dt.02-06-2014</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                                                 3/3</span>




                 amount each to the satisfaction of learned Chief Judicial

                 Magistrate, Gaya in connection with Mufassil ( Gaya) P.S. Case

                 No.121 of 2013.
</p>
<p id="p_8">

</p><p id="p_9">                                            (Prabhat KumarJha, J)
NKS/-
</p>
