0       C.R.M. 215 OF 2014

    In Re: Mosslama Begam &amp; Anr. . . . Petitioners.
    Re: An application under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure filed on
    06-01-2014 in connection with Chinsura Police Station Case No.40/2013 dated 23-
    12-2013 under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_2">Sections 498A</a>/<a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_3">406</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_2">    Mr. Keshab Chandra Das. . . .For the petitioner.
    Mr. Pabitra Talukdar. . . . .For the State.
</p>

<p id="p_3">             We   have   heard   the   submissions     of   the     learned   Advocate
    appearing for the petitioners and the learned Advocate for the
    State.
</p>

<p id="p_4">         Considering the materials in the case diary, produced before
    us, we do not find that custodial interrogation of the petitioner
    no.1 is necessary. Nor is there apprehension of his absconding.
    We are inclined to allow the prayer of the petitioner no.1 under
    <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_4">Section 438</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure.
</p>

<p id="p_5">         Accordingly,      we    direct   that   in   the   event    of   arrest,   the
    petitioner no.1 shall be released on bail upon furnishing bond of
    Rs.5,000/- with one surety of like amount to the satisfaction of
    the Arresting Authority and subject to the conditions as laid down
    under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_5">Section 438(2)</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973.
</p>

<p id="p_6">         So far as prayer for the petitioner no.2 is concerned such
    prayer is considered and rejected.
</p>

<p id="p_7">         This application for anticipatory bail is, thus disposed of.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                                 ( INDIRA BANERJEE,J.)
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">            2</span>

(   ASIM KUMAR MONDAL,J)
 </p>
