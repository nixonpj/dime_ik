<p id="p_1">(Per
: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE BHAGWATI PRASAD)
 

 


 

	Heard
learned counsel for the parties.   Having considered the submissions
made by the learned counsel for the appellant and the learned counsel
for the intervening applicant   M/s Best Foods International, this
Court is of the opinion that the trial Court has only issued notice
in relation to an injunction application made before it and therefore
it would not be appropriate to consider the question of granting of
injunction by this Court.  Therefore, the present proceedings are
considered to be unnecessary because, unless the reasons assigned by
the trial court are available before the appellate court, it is not
proper to adjudicate a lis only on allegations and affidavits
without there being any sufficient material in the shape of
documentary evidence and affidavits which are supposed to be filed
before the Court, which is stage of Section 9 application.   
</p>
 

 


 

<p id="p_2">	The
counsel for the appellant   Navbharat International Limited submit
that they will join Best Food International (P) Limited as party
respondent to the application under <a href="/doc/1120409/" id="a_1">Section 9</a> of the Arbitration and
<a href="/doc/1306164/" id="a_2">Conciliation Act</a>, 1996 and would delete cargo as respondent.   In
that view of the matter, learned counsel for Best Food International
(P) Limited submits that they would appear before the trial court on
22nd March 2010 and before that counsel for Navbharat
International Limited supplies all the relevant documents to them,
which are required for a party respondent.  
</p>
 

 


 

<p id="p_3">	Interim
order dated 16th March 2010 passed by this Court is
vacated without prejudice to the rights and contentions which parties
can avail as a party before the Court concerned.    The trial Court
will expeditiously entertain the application as far as possible
within a period of two weeks.
</p> 

 


 

<p id="p_4">	With
the aforesaid observations and directions, First Appeal No.575 of
2010 along with other connected applications is disposed of. Direct
Service is permitted today.
</p>
