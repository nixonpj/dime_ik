<div class="doc_title">Suresh Prasad Yadav vs The State Of Jharkhand on 12 February, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                     IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                                A.B.A. No.159 of 2015

            Suresh Prasad Yadav                              .....   Petitioner
                                        with
                                  A.B.A. No.195 of 2015
            Munni Devi                                       ....      Petitioner
                                         Versus
            The State of Jharkhand                           ....      Opposite Party

            CORAM:          HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE H.C. MISHRA

            For the Petitioners          :       Mr. Raj Mangal Singh
                                                 Mr. Sidhartha Roy
            For the State                :       A. P.P.
                                         -----

   2/12.2.2015</pre><p id="p_1">       Both these applications arise out of the same case, as such, they are being
            disposed of by this common order.
</p><p id="p_2">                     Heard the learned counsel for the petitioners and learned counsel for the
            State.
</p><p id="p_3">                     The petitioners are apprehending their arrests in connection with Ramgarh
            P.S. Case No.408 of 2014, corresponding to G.R. No.4203 of 2014, for the alleged
            offences under <a href="/doc/94452/" id="a_1">Sections 414</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_2">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code and <a href="/doc/1914327/" id="a_3">Section 30(ii)</a> of the
            Coal Mines Act.
</p><p id="p_4">                     One truck, loaded with allegedly illegal coal, was apprehended by the
            police. Petitioners are the owner and driver of the truck.
</p><p id="p_5">                     It has been submitted by the learned counsel for the petitioners that the
            documents of coal produced by the petitioners were verified by the police and the
            same have been found to be genuine.            Learned counsel for the petitioners
            accordingly, prayed for anticipatory bail.
</p><p id="p_6">                     Learned counsel for the State though has opposed the prayer for
            anticipatory bai, but upon going through the case diary, has admitted that upon
            verification of the documents, the documents were found to be genuine.
</p><p id="p_7">                     In the facts and circumstances of the case, I am inclined to grant
            anticipatory bail to the petitioners. Accordingly, it is directed that in the event of
            surrender / arrest, the petitioners Suresh Prasad Yadav and Munni Devi shall be
            released on bail on furnishing bail bonds of Rs.10,000/- (Ten thousand) each with
            two sureties of the like amount of each to the satisfaction of learned SDJM,
            Hazaribagh, in connection with Ramgarh               P.S. Case No.408 of 2014,
            corresponding to G.R. No.4203 of 2014, subject to the conditions as laid down
            under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_4">Section 438(2)</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure.
</p>

<p id="p_8">                                                                                  (H. C. Mishra, J)
R.Kumar
