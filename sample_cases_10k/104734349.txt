<div class="doc_title">Lokesha R vs State By Magadi Ps on 13 June, 2019</div><div class="doc_author">Author: S.Sunil Dutt Yadav</div>

<pre id="pre_1">IN THE HIGH COURT OF KARNATAKA AT BENGALURU

       DATED THIS THE 13TH DAY OF JUNE, 2019

                       BEFORE

     THE HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE S. SUNIL DUTT YADAV

        CRIMINAL PETITION No.1696/2019
BETWEEN:

1.     Lokesha. R,
       S/o Ranganna,
       Aged about 42 years,
       Kottanagere Beedi,
       Magadi Town,
       Ramanagara District-562120.

2.     L. Sharathkumar @ Sharath,
       S/o Lakshman,
       Aged about 25 years,
       No.164, 3rd Cross,
       Papareddypalya,
       Nagarabhavi 2nd Stage,
       Bengaluru-560072.              ... Petitioners

(By Sri Manjunatha.G, Advocate)
AND:
State by Magadi P S.,
Magadi Town,
Ramanagara District-562120,
Represented by Learned SPP,
High Court of Karnataka,
Bengaluru-560001.                    ... Respondent

(By Sri K.P.Yoganna, HCGP)
                             2


      This Criminal Petition is filed under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> of
Criminal Procedure Code praying to enlarge the
petitioners on bail in the event of their arrest in Crime
No.34/2019 of Magadi Police Station, Ramanagara for
the offence punishable under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_2">Sections 323</a>,<a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_3">324</a>,<a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_4">307</a> and
<a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_5">504</a> read with <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_6">Section 34</a> of IPC.

      This Criminal Petition coming on for Orders, this
day, the Court made the following:

                         ORDER
</pre><p id="p_1">     Petitioner Nos.1 and 2 are seeking to be enlarged

on bail in connection with their detention pursuant to

the proceedings in Crime No.34/2019 with respect to

the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_7">Sections 323</a>, <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_8">324</a>, <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_9">307</a>

and <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_10">504</a> read with <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_11">Section 34</a> of IPC.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">     2.    The complainant makes out the case that on

01.02.2019 at about 12.15 p.m. the complainant's son

was proceeding in his motor cycle. The petitioners who

have been arraigned accused Nos.1 and 2 had stopped

the complainant's son, abused him and assaulted him

causing injuries. It is further stated that petitioner No.2

is stated to have injured the complainant's son with a
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                             3</span>


stone. It is further stated that complainant has also

been assaulted. The complaint came to be lodged, FIR

has been registered and investigation is in progress. It is

stated that the injured have been discharged from the

hospital.
</p>
<p id="p_4">
</p><p id="p_5">       3.   Learned counsel for the petitioners states

that there is a history to the altercation and that the

injured is stated to have developed intimacy with first

petitioner's wife and despite having been advised to

keep away, he has continued with such behavior. The

copy of wound certificate is stated to have revealed that

injuries Nos.1 to 4 are simple, whereas injury No.5 is

grievous. However, taking note of the fact that injured

has been discharged and also that injuries Nos.1 to 4

are simple, there is sufficient force in the submission of

learned counsel for the petitioners that there was no

intention to commit the offence under <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_12">Section 307</a> of

IPC.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                4</span><p id="p_6">


</p><p id="p_7">       4.   It is also to be noticed that III Additional

District and Sessions Judge, Ramanagara in Criminal

Miscellaneous      No.64/2019,     by   its    order      dated

25.02.2019 had rejected the petition while observing

that the act of petitioners is heinous in nature.


</p><p id="p_8">       5.   It is further to be noticed in light of the case

that   is   made    out   by   petitioners    including     the

background of the incident there is force in the

submission of the counsel for the petitioner that

custodial interrogation may not be required.


</p><p id="p_9">       6.   Accordingly, the petitioners are directed to

be enlarged on bail in Crime No.34/2019 with respect to

the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_13">Sections 323</a>, <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_14">324</a>, <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_15">307</a>

and <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_16">504</a> read with <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_17">Section 34</a> of IPC in the event of their

arrest.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                              5</span><p id="p_10">


</p><p id="p_11">     7.      Accordingly,   bail   petition   filed   by   the

petitioners under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_18">Section 438</a> of Cr.P.C. is allowed,

subject to the following conditions:

</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">     (i)     Each of the petitioners shall appear in
             person before the Investigating Officer
             in connection with Crime No.34/2019
             within 15 days from today and shall
             execute a personal bond for a sum of
             Rs.50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand
             only) each, with a surety for the
             likesum to the satisfaction of the
             Investigating Officer.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_2">     (ii)    The petitioners shall fully co-operate
             with the Investigating Officer and shall
             not indulge in any criminal activities
             henceforth.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_3">     (iii)   The petitioners shall not tamper with
             evidence, influence in any way, any
             witness.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_4">     (iv)    The petitioners shall physically present
             themselves and mark their attendance
             before the concerned Station House
             Officer once a week between 10.00 a.m.
             and 5.00 p.m. till filing of the final
             report.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_5">     (v)     In the event of change of address, the
             petitioners to inform the same to the
             concerned SHO.
</blockquote><span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                            6</span>


<blockquote id="blockquote_6">      (vi)   Any violation of the aforementioned
             conditions by the petitioners, shall
             result in cancellation of bail.</blockquote><p id="p_12">

</p><p id="p_13">      Any observation made herein shall not be taken as

an expression of opinion on the merits of the case.




</p><p id="p_14">                                     Sd/-
</p><p id="p_15">                                    JUDGE
ssb
