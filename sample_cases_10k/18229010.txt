<div class="doc_title">Amit Kumar Singh @ Amit Singh vs State Of Bihar &amp; Anr on 17 April, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                     IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                    Criminal Miscellaneous No.45050 of 2014
                     Arising Out of PS.Case No. -82 Year- 2014 Thana -BUXAR INDUSTRIAL District- BUXAR
                   ======================================================
                   Amit Kumar Singh @ Amit Singh Son of Hari Shankar Singh, resident of
                   village- Gopthhula, P.S.- Mofassil, District- Bhojpur

                                                                                 .... ....   Petitioner/s
                                                         Versus
                   1. The State of Bihar
                   2. Nitu Devi, D/o Kedar Singh, P.O. Kothiya, P.S.- Industrial Area Buxar,
                      District- Buxar

                                                                  .... .... Opposite Party/s
                   ======================================================
                   Appearance :
                   For the Petitioner/s     :  Mr. Ashok Kumar Sinha
                   For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr. Arun Kumar Pandey (App)
                   ======================================================
                   CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE DINESH KUMAR
                   SINGH
                   ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">03/   17-04-2015                              Heard learned counsels for the petitioner and

                   the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                              The petitioner being the husband of the

                   informant is apprehending his arrest in a case registered for the

                   offences punishable under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_1">Section 498A</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_2">34</a> the Indian Penal Code

                   and 3/4 of <a href="/doc/1763444/" id="a_3">Dowry Prohibition Act</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                              The basic accusation is of torture for non-

                   fulfillment of the dowry demand.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                              Notices were issued to the informant-
</p>
<p id="p_5">                   opposite party no. 2 vide order dated 18.12.2014, but it appears

                   that in spite of valid service of notice, none is appearing on behalf

                   of opposite party no. 2.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.45050 of 2014 (03) dt.17-04-2015</span><p id="p_6">

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                           2/3</span>




</p><p id="p_7">                                         It is submitted by learned counsel for the

             petitioner that petitioner filed Matrimonial Suit No. 224 of 2014

             for divorce on 03.07.2014 and thereafter the present FIR was

             lodged on 08.07.2014. However, the petitioner is ready to pay

             Rs.1,500/- per month from May, 2015 to the informant by

             depositing the same in her account by second week of every

             succeeding month.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                                         Considering the fact that the matrimonial suit

             was filed by the petitioner at earlier point of time and the present

             stand of the petitioner, let the above named petitioner be released

             on anticipatory bail in the event of his arrest or surrender before

             the learned court below within a period of twelve weeks from

             today, on furnishing bail bond of Rs.10,000/- (Ten thousand) with

             two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of learned

             Chief Judicial Magistrate, Buxar in connection with Industrial

             Area Buxar P.S. Case No. 82 of 2014, subject to the conditions as

             laid down under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_4">Section 438(2)</a> of the Cr.P.C.

</p><p id="p_9">                                         The petitioner undertakes to supply a copy of

             this order to the informant within a period of three weeks from

             today when it is expected that the informant will supply bank

             account number by submitting the same on affidavit before the

             learned court below.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">       Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.45050 of 2014 (03) dt.17-04-2015</span><p id="p_10">

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                                                 3/3</span>




</p><p id="p_11">                                               The aforesaid payment will be subject to any

                   order being passed in matrimonial, maintenance or other

                   proceeding arising out of matrimonial dispute.

</p><p id="p_12">                                               Three      consecutive   defaults   in   making

                   payment will give liberty to the informant to file application for

                   cancellation of bail bond of the petitioner.



</p><p id="p_13">                                             (Dinesh Kumar Singh, J)
    DKS/-
</p>
