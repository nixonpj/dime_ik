<div class="doc_title">Civil Revision No.6563 Of 2011 ... vs Surinder Pal Singh on 31 October, 2011</div><pre id="pre_1">      IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA AT
                      CHANDIGARH.


                             Civil Revision No.6563 of 2011 (O&amp;M)
                                        Date of decision: 31.10.2011
Mohan Singh
                                                          -----Petitioner
                                  Vs.
Surinder Pal Singh
                                                       -----Respondent

CORAM:- HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE RAKESH KUMAR GARG

1.    Whether reporters of local newspapers may be allowed to
      see judgment?
2.    To be referred to reporters or not?
3.    Whether the judgment should be reported in the Digest?

Present:-    Mr. Arun Kumar Kaundal, Advocate
             for the petitioner.
               ---


RAKESH KUMAR GARG, J.
</pre>
<p id="p_1">             This is landlord's revision petition challenging, order of

the   Rent   Controller,   Ludhiana     dated    12.3.2011,    whereby

provisional rent has been assessed.
</p>
<p id="p_2">             It is useful to refer to judgment of the Hon'ble

Supreme Court in the case of <a href="/doc/67460548/" id="a_1">Harjit Singh Uppal v. Anup

Bansal, JT</a> 2011(6) SC 236, wherein it has been observed as

under:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">             "25. Section 15(1) (b) of the 1949 <a href="/doc/679372/" id="a_2">Rent Act</a> provides, to a
             person aggrieved by an order passed by the Rent
             Controller, a remedy of appeal. The Section provides for
             limitation for filing an appeal from that order and also the
             forum to which such appeal would lie. The provision, for
             maintaining the appeal, does not make any difference
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> C.R. No.6563 of 2011                                              2</span>




           between the final order and interlocutory order passed by
           the Rent Controller in the proceedings under the 1949 <a href="/doc/679372/" id="a_3">Rent
           Act</a>. There is no specific provision in the Section that if a
           party aggrieved by an interlocutory order passed by the
           Rent Controller does not challenge that order in appeal
           immediately, though provided, and waits for the final
           outcome, whether in the appeal challenging the final order
           of the Rent Controller, the correctness of the interlocutory
           order from which an appeal lay could or could not be
           challenged in the appeal from the final order."</blockquote><p id="p_3">


</p><p id="p_4">           In view of the aforesaid law laid down by the Hon'ble

Supreme Court, the impugned order is appealable.

</p><p id="p_5">           Faced with this situation, learned counsel for the

petitioner wishes to withdraw the instant revision petition with

liberty to challenge the impugned order before the appropriate

authority in accordance with law.
</p>
<p id="p_6">           With the aforesaid liberty, the petition is ordered to be

dismissed as withdrawn.
</p>
<p id="p_7">
</p><pre id="pre_2">October 31, 2011                      ( RAKESH KUMAR GARG )
ak                                           JUDGE
