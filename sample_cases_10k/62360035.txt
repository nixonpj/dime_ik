<div class="doc_title">Sanjeet Kumar @ Sanjeev Kumar vs The State Of Bihar on 20 August, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                    Criminal Miscellaneous No.49486 of 2018
                       Arising Out of PS. Case No.-290 Year-2018 Thana- MASAUDHI District- Patna
                 ======================================================
                 Sanjeet Kumar @ Sanjeev Kumar S/o Rama Shankar Paswan @ Ram
                 Shankar Paswan, Resident of Village - Kharant, P.S. - Masaurhi, District -
                 Patna.

                                                                                    ... ... Petitioner
                                                       Versus
                 The State of Bihar.

                                                         ... ... Opposite Party
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :       Mr. Bachan Ji Ojha, Adv.
                                                  Mr. Sheo Nandan Pandit, Adv
                 For the Opposite Party/s :       Mr. None.
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE RAKESH KUMAR
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   20-08-2018                  Heard Sri Bachan Ji Ojha, learned counsel, assisted by

                  Sri Sheo Nandan Pandit, learned counsel for the petitioner.

                  None appeared on behalf of the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                The sole petitioner, who is in custody since

                  `04.05.2018 in Masaurhi P.S. Case No.290 of 2018 registered

                  for the offence under Sections 25(1-b)a/26/35 of the <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_1">Arms Act</a>,

                  has prayed for grant of bail.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that it is

                  true that the petitioner was arrested along with another accused,

                  from whose possession, a loaded country-made pistol was

                  recovered, fact remains that nothing was recovered from the

                  possession of the petitioner. Learned counsel for the petitioner

                  further submits that the petitioner was also made accused in
              Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.49486 of 2018(2) dt.20-08-2018
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                          2/2</span>




                      Masaurhi P.S. Case No.289 of 2018 registered for the offence

                      under <a href="/doc/1258372/" id="a_2">Section 147</a>, <a href="/doc/763672/" id="a_3">148</a>, <a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_4">149</a>, <a href="/doc/838469/" id="a_5">452</a>, <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_6">341</a>, <a href="/doc/1243353/" id="a_7">342</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_8">504</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_9">506</a>, <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_10">307</a>, <a href="/doc/695990/" id="a_11">364</a>

                      of the Indian Penal Code and <a href="/doc/244673/" id="a_12">Section 27</a> of the Arms Act, but in

                      the said case, the petitioner has already been granted bail by the

                      learned Sessions Court. On the aforesaid ground, a prayer has

                      been made for granting bail.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                   However, on perusal of the F.I.R., it is evident that

                      from a room, where a raid was being conducted by the police,

                      one person was apprehended and on his disclosure, the victim

                      boy, who was kidnapped, was recovered and this petitioner

                      along with another accused started fleeing after seeing the

                      police, however both were apprehended and one country-made

                      pistol with two live cartridges was recovered .

</p><p id="p_5">                                   Considering the nature of accusation, there is no

                      reason to pass favourable order. The prayer for bail stands

                      rejected.
</p>
<p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">                                                   (Rakesh Kumar, J.)
nawalkrs/-
</p>
