<div class="doc_title">Dheeraj Singh vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 30 April, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                         M.Cr.C.No.5710/2015




                              M.Cr.C.No.5710/2015
30.4.2015         Shri     Sukhendra       Singh,     counsel      for     the
            applicants.
                  Shri Akhilendra Singh, G.A. for the State/
            respondent.</pre>
<p id="p_1">                  Heard the learned counsel for the parties.
                  The applicants have an apprehension of their
            arrest relating to crime No.23/2015 registered at
            Police Station Ayodhya Nagar, District Bhopal for
            offence punishable under <a href="/doc/594493/" id="a_1">Sections 294</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_2">323</a>, <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_3">324</a>,
            <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_4">506</a> of IPC and <a href="/doc/1507082/" id="a_5">Section 3</a> (1) (x) of SC/ST (Prevention
            of Atrocities) Act, 1989.
</p><p id="p_2">                  Learned counsel for the applicants submits
            that the applicants are reputed citizens of the
            locality. They do not have any criminal past alleged
            against them. Except of offence punishable under
            <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_6">Section 324</a> of IPC and <a href="/doc/1507082/" id="a_7">Section 3</a> (1) (x) of the
            <a href="/doc/701797/" id="a_8">Special Act</a>, remaining offences are bailable. In first
            FIR lodged by the complainant, he did not mention
            that the quarrel took place on the basis of the caste
            of   the     complainant.          Actually     a   contract    of
            construction was given to the complainant but, he
            did not fulfil the same and demanded for extra
            money and therefore, the quarrel took place. After
            sometime a complaint was sent to S.P., in which it
            is   alleged     that    the       applicants       abused     the
            M.Cr.C.No.5710/2015




complainant with the words relating to his caste.
However,       such     allegations     are    afterthought,
otherwise, it could be mentioned in the first FIR
itself. No offence under <a href="/doc/1249080/" id="a_9">Section 3</a> (1) (x) of SC/ST
(Prevention of Atrocities) Act is made out against the
applicants and therefore, prohibition under <a href="/doc/701797/" id="a_10">Section
18</a> of that Act is not applicable in the present case.
Offence under <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_11">Section 324</a> of IPC is not so grave, so
that the applicants        may not be enlarged on
anticipatory bail.      Under such circumstances, the
applicants pray for bail of anticipatory nature.
</p><p id="p_3">     Learned     G.A.    for     the   State   opposes   the
application.
</p><p id="p_4">     After considering the submissions made by the
learned counsel for the parties and looking to the
facts and circumstances of the case, without
expressing any view on the merits of the case, I am
of the view that the applicants may be enlarged on
anticipatory bail.      Consequently application under
<a href="/doc/701797/" id="a_12">Section 438</a>, <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_13">Cr.P.C</a>. filed by the applicants              is
hereby allowed.
</p><p id="p_5">     It is directed that in the event of arrest, present
applicants Dheeraj Singh and Virendra Singh shall
be released on bail on each furnishing a personal
bond in the sum of Rs.25,000/- (Rupees twenty five
thousand) with a solvent surety in the like amount
                         M.Cr.C.No.5710/2015




             to   the   satisfaction    of    the   Arresting   Authority
             (Investigation officer).
</p><p id="p_6">                  The applicants shall make themselves available
             for interrogation by a police officer as and when
             required. They shall further abide by the other
             conditions enumerated in sub-Section (2) of <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_14">Section
             438</a> of Cr.P.C.
</p><p id="p_7">                  This order shall remain in force for a period of
             60 days and in the meanwhile, if the applicant so
             desires, may move an application for regular bail
             before the competent Court.
</p><p id="p_8">                  Bail under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_15">Section 438</a> of the Cr.P.C. is given
             for a limited period, so that the evidence received
             against the applicant during further investigation
             may be considered by the concerned Court, who
             shall consider his application under <a href="/doc/701797/" id="a_16">Section 437</a> or
             439 of the <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_17">Cr.P.C</a>.
</p><p id="p_9">                  Certified copy as per rules.
</p><p id="p_10">                                                       (N.K.GUPTA)
                                                          JUDGE
Pushpendra
 </p>
