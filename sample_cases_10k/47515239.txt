<div class="doc_title">Sanikommu Krishna Reddy vs Kummari Jhansi Rani on 12 October, 2018</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: M.Satyanarayana Murthy</div>

<pre id="pre_1">   HON'BLE SRI JUSTICE M. SATYANARAYANA MURTHY

                   I.A. Nos.2 AND 3 OF 2018
                            IN/AND
             CRIMINAL PETITION No.11021 OF 2018
COMMON ORDER:

      The main Criminal Petition, under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> of the Code of

Criminal Procedure, 1973 (for short 'Code'), is filed by the petitioner -

accused viz., Sanikommu Krishna Reddy, to quash the proceedings

against him in Crime/FIR No.85 of 2018, dated 29.06.2018, on the file

of Narsaraopet I Town Police Station, Guntur District, Andhra Pradesh

State, registered for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_2">Sections 323</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_3">506</a>,

<a href="/doc/105503/" id="a_4">417</a> and <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_5">420</a> of the Indian Penal Code, 1860 (for short '<a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_6">IPC</a>') and

<a href="/doc/1507082/" id="a_7">Section 3</a> (1) (r) and 3 (2) (va) of the Scheduled Castes and the

<a href="/doc/25085007/" id="a_8">Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Amendment Act</a>, 2015 (for

short 'Act, 2015').


      2. The petitioner herein is accused in the aforesaid Crime, and

respondent No.1 is the de facto complainant. Both parties entered into</pre>

<p id="p_1">compromise and filed I.A. No.2 of 2018 to permit them to enter into

compromise and I.A. No.3 of 2018 to compound the offences by

recording the compromise.
</p>
<p id="p_2">

</p><p id="p_3">      3.   The petitioner - accused and respondent No.1 - de facto

complainant are present and they are identified by their respective

counsel, and produced Photostat copies of Aadhar Cards in proof of

their identity.   When the terms and conditions of compromise are

explained in vernacular language, they admitted to be true and correct.
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                       2</span>
</p><p id="p_4">                                                                          MSM,J
                                                            I.A. Nos.2 &amp; 3 OF 2018
                                                                           IN/AND
                                                           Crl.P. No.11021 OF 2018


</p><p id="p_5">          4.     On enquiry, the parties stated that they entered into

compromise with the intervention of elders and well-wishers to lead

peaceful life, and that the compromise is voluntary.



</p><p id="p_6">          5. Except the offence punishable under Act, 2015, other offences

are bailable offences, and the bar under <a href="/doc/197893640/" id="a_9">Section 18</a> of the Act, 2015 will

not come in the way when the parties entered into compromise.


</p><p id="p_7">          6. As seen from the allegations made in the complaint, though

they constitute an offence punishable under Act, 2015, as the parties

entered into compromise voluntarily, even if the investigation is taken

up, chance of conviction is weak and, therefore, to avoid unnecessary

waste of time to the Courts and parties, besides expenditure to the

parties, I deem it appropriate to permit the parties to enter into

compromise and to compound the offences by exercising power under

<a href="/doc/903398/" id="a_10">Section 482</a> of the Code and also by applying principles laid down in

<a href="/doc/69949024/" id="a_11">Gian Singh v. State of Punjab1</a>.                   Hence, compromise is

recorded in terms of joint memo filed along with the petition.



</p><p id="p_8">          7.     Accordingly, I.A. Nos.2 and 3 of 2018 are allowed.

Consequently, the present Criminal Petition is also allowed quashing the

proceedings against accused in Crime/FIR No.85 of 2018, dated

29.06.2018, on the file of Narsaraopet I Town Police Station, Guntur

District, Andhra Pradesh State.
</p>
<p id="p_9">

</p><p id="p_10">       8. Registry is directed to annex a copy of the joint memo filed by

both parties, to this order.
</p>
<p id="p_11">

</p><p id="p_12">       Consequently, Miscellaneous Petitions, if any, pending in the

present Criminal Petition, stand closed.
