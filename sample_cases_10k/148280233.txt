<div class="doc_title">Rajesh Kumar vs State Of Bihar &amp; Anr on 18 February, 2009</div><pre id="pre_1">                IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                              Cr.Misc. No.52664 of 2008
            RAJESH KUMAR, son of Umesh Prasad Singh, resident of village
               Mokamah, P.S. Mokamah and District Patna ....Petitioner
                                         Versus
            1. STATE OF BIHAR
            2. Nisha Devi, daughter of Suresh Prasad Singh, resident of village Aunta, P.S.
               Hathidah and District Patna              ..... Opposite parties
                                      -----------
</pre>
<p id="p_1">03/   18.2.2009                    Counsel for the petitioner states that in compliance of

                         the orders of this Court dated 19.11.2008, passed in Cr. Misc.

                         No. 44896 of 2008, petitioner could not appear along with his

                         wife as she was unwell, although a petition duly signed by him

                         and his father-in-law was filed on 24.11.2008, the date fixed for

                         appearance of the petitioner and his wife.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                   Without going into the correctness or otherwise of the

                         contention raised in the modification application, as it is a

                         matrimonial matter, I grant one more indulgence to the petitioner

                         to appear with his wife in the court below on 28.2.2009 and

                         satisfy the court below that matrimonial peace prevails between

                         him and his wife and if that be so then petitioner, Rajesh Kumar

                         be admitted to the privilege of bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs.

                         5,000/- (Rs. Five thousand only) with two sureties of the like

                         amount each to the satisfaction of the S.D.J.M., Barh in

                         connection with Complaint Case No. 462(C ) of 2004, otherwise

                         the court below shall pass appropriate orders in accordance with

                         law.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                 This modification application is, accordingly, disposed

                         of.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                             2</span><p id="p_4">




                   Let this order be communicated to the court below

         through fax on payment of usual charges by the petitioner.




</p><pre id="pre_2">Arjun/                          (V.N. Sinha, J.)
