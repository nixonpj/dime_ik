<div class="doc_title">Aparna Ghosh vs State Of West Bengal &amp; Others on 11 June, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                    1

                11.06.18
                 Item No.07
                 Court No.15
                     rpan
                                      W.P. No. 7125 (W) of 2018
                                         Aparna Ghosh
                                          - Versus -
                               State of West Bengal &amp; Others
    Ms. Deblina Lahiri,
    Mr. Amit Dey
                    ... for the petitioner.

    Mr. T. M. Siddiqui,
    Mr. Nilotpal Chatterjee
                     ... for the State respondents.</pre>


<p id="p_1">       Affidavit of service filed by the petitioner be kept on record.

       The grievance of the petitioner is that the respondent no.7 has not taken steps

to treat the petitioner's complaint dated 7th May, 2018 as FIR.

</p><p id="p_2">       Learned advocate appearing for the petitioner submits that the petitioner is

being constantly threatened of dire consequences by one Shri Sadhan Biswas and one

Shri Dilip Sarkar and their associates. Such fact was brought to the notice of the

respondent no.7 by a representation dated 7th May, 2018.                 In spite of the said

representation, the police authorities have not taken any steps and aggrieved

thereby, the petitioner has approached this Court.
</p>
<p id="p_3">       Mr. Siddiqui, learned advocate appearing for the State respondents denies such

contention of the petitioner and submits that upon receipt of telephonic information,

the Assistant Sub-inspector from the Chakdaha Police Station visited the premises of

the petitioner but the petitioner could not identify any person who had allegedly

threatened her of dire consequences.                    However, the police authorities have

maintained a vigil round the clock.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                           2</span><p id="p_4">

</p><p id="p_5">      The grievance of the petitioner is that the police authorities have not taken

appropriate steps on the basis of her complaint and has not treated the same as FIR

and has not conducted proper investigation. For redressal of such grievance, the

petitioner ought to have availed the remedies as provided under the Code of Criminal

Procedure instead of approaching this Court.
</p>
<p id="p_6">      Accordingly, the writ petition is disposed of with liberty to the petitioner to

assert her right before the appropriate forum.
</p>
<p id="p_7">      It is, however, made clear that the police authorities shall ensure that no

physical harm is caused to the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_8">      With the observations and directions the writ petition is disposed of.

      There shall, however, be no order as to costs.
</p>
<p id="p_9">      Urgent photostat certified copy of this order, if applied for, be supplied to the

parties, upon compliance of all requisite formalities.



</p><p id="p_10">                                              (Tapabrata Chakraborty, J.)
