<div class="doc_title">Sanjay Gautel vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 15 February, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                        1                               MCRC-86-2019
                             The High Court Of Madhya Pradesh
                                        MCRC-86-2019
                                       (SANJAY GAUTEL Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)

                     6
                     Jabalpur, Dated : 15-02-2019
                           Shri Gyan Prakash Tripathi, counsel for the applicant.
                           Shri S.Awasthy, Government Advocate for the respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">                           Arguments heard upon the application filed on 1.01.2019 under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section
                    439</a> of Cr.P.C.on behalf of applicant Sanjay Goutel.
</p><p id="p_2">                           Case diary of Crime No.139/2018 registered at Police Station Civil

                    Line, District Jabalpur under <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_2">Sections 324</a>, <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_3">307</a>, <a href="/doc/1540253/" id="a_4">326</a>, <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_5">120-B</a>, <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_6">34</a> of IPC, also
                    perused.
</p><p id="p_3">                           It is submitted by the counsel for the applicant that challan has been
                    filed. The applicant is in custody since 18.06.2018. No test identification
                    parade was conducted by the Investigating Officer. This is first bail
                    application. The applicant having no criminal background. Therefore, he
                    should be enlarged on bail.
</p><p id="p_4">                           On the other side learned counsel for the State has opposed the
                    application and submitted that the applicant is the middle man who hired the

                    other three accused for committing the crime. Therefore, the applicant is not
                    entitled to get the bail.
</p><p id="p_5">                           As per prosecution case, prosecutrix Priya Gupta is the sister of the
                    wife of accused Ved Prakash Gupta. On the date of incident i.e .13.06.2017
                    at about 15:15 pm when complainant Priya Gupta was going towards her girl
                    hostel at that time three accused persons came on white coloured Activa and
                    when they were passing nearby the complainant the boy who was sitting as
                    pillion rider in the Activa assaulted by knife upon hips of the complainant.
                    The complainant was examined under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_7">Section 164</a> of Cr.P.C. During
                    investigation the query also obtained from Doctor who reported that the
                    injuries caused to the complainant was sufficient to cause her death, if the
                    treatment was not provided. During investigation, it is found that the master

                    mind of the conspiracy Ved Prakash contacted to Sanjay, who engaged three
                    accused persons namely Vivek, Nikhil and Rohit. Sanjay Gotel promised to
                    all three persons that he will give Rs.5,000/- after the assault by Knife. All
                    three accused persons have observed the movement of complainant,
                    thereafter they committed crime on the date of incident. After committing
                    crime they met with Sanjay Gotel and received Rs.5,000/-.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                           Therefore looking to the role of the applicant in the crime, he is not
                    entitled to get the bail.
</p><p id="p_7">                           Hence, the application is dismissed.
</p>
