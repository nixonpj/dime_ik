<div class="doc_title">Niraj Kumar Singh @ Niraj Singh vs State Of Bihar on 7 January, 2010</div><pre id="pre_1">                IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                       Cr.Misc. No.33252 of 2009
                NIRAJ KUMAR SINGH @ NIRAJ SINGH SON OF SHYAM KISHORE SINGH
                RESIDENT OF VILLAGE DENA,P.S.DUMRA,DISTT.SITAMARHI
                                 Versus
                             STATE OF BIHAR
                For the petitioner : Mr.Arjun Prasad
                For the State      : Mr.Addl.P.P.
                              -----------
</pre>
<p id="p_1">5/   7.1.2010                 Heard       learned     counsel         for     the

                   parties.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                              The petitioner seeks bail in a case

                   which has been registered for offence under

                   <a href="/doc/895891/" id="a_1">Section 320</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_2">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code.

</p><p id="p_3">                              Informant's      daughter         Nandan      Kumari

                   was married with the petitioner in the year

                   2005 and the allegation is that her in-laws

                   were demanding various type of dowry articles

                   and when the same were not given, she was

                   killed     by   all     her-laws        by     administering

                   poison.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                              Submission     is     that         prior   to   the

                   occurrence, the victim fell down as                   she was

                   suffering       from      epilepsy           and   sustained

                   injuries. The victim was taken to a doctor

                   and when her condition deteriorated, she was

                   referred to S.K.M.C.H. Muzaffapur but in the

                   way she succumbed to the injuries. Further

                   submission is that         the allegation is general

                   in nature and other similarly situated co-
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                           2</span>




          accused namely, Pawan Devi and Shyam Kishore

          have been granted bail by different orders

          Vide    Cr.Misc.Nos.       19455    and    29501    of     2009

          respectively.
</p>
<pre id="pre_2">                    Considering             the       facts           and

          circumstances       of    the     case,    let    the    above

          named    petitioner        be    released    on     bail     on

          furnishing    bail         bond     of     Rs.10,000/-(ten

          thousand)    with        two    sureties    of     the     like

          amount each to the satisfaction of                      learned

</pre><p id="p_5">          Chief Judicial Magistrate, Sitamarhi in Dumra

          P.S.Case No. 281 of 2008.
</p>
<p id="p_6">


Tahir/-                       ( Shyam Kishore Sharma, J.)
 </p>
