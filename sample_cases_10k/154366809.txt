                                 RUPAK GOSWAMI

  BEFORE:
  The Hon'ble JUSTICE SANJIB BANERJEE</pre>
<p id="p_1">  Date : 4th December, 2015.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">                                                                       Appearance:
</p><p id="p_4">                                                  Mr. Narendra Prasad Gupta, Adv.
</p><p id="p_5">                                                             ...for Subrata Ghosal.
</p><p id="p_6">                                                      Mr. Ranajit Chowdhury, Adv.
</p><p id="p_7">                                                       ...for the Official Liquidator.
</p><p id="p_8">                                                         Mr. Dipanjan Ghosh, Adv.
</p><p id="p_9">                                                   Mr. Debrup Bhattacharjee, Adv.
</p><p id="p_10">                                                           ...for the S.S. Syndicate.
</p>
<p id="p_11">            The Court : The application is for recalling an order passed for the

sale of the meagre movable assets of the company in liquidation on the ground

that the applicant has a better offer for the assets.

</p><p id="p_12">            Ordinarily, orders confirming sales made by the company Court are

not interfered with lightly; but when a company goes into liquidation and it is the

duty of the Court to ensure that the assets of such company fetch the best price,
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                           2</span>


if a subsequent bidder makes a better offer prior to the sale being completed, the

company Court may entertain the same.
</p>
<p id="p_13">             The sale of the movable assets of the company in liquidation was

confirmed in favour of S.S. Syndicate at Rs.2 lakh.         The Official Liquidator

acknowledges that the entire consideration has been made over to the Official

Liquidator. However, the Official Liquidator has not made over the assets sold to

the purchaser identified by the order of October 9, 2015.

</p><p id="p_14">             The present application was made with an initial bid of Rs.2.20 lakh.

The purchaser identified by the order of October 9, 2015 and the applicant have

bid in Court today and S.S. Syndicate has made the higher final bid of Rs.3.15

lakh for the goods.
</p>
<p id="p_15">             Accordingly, the sale of the advertised assets of the company in

liquidation is now confirmed in favour of S.S. Syndicate at Rs.3.15 lakh, subject

to the balance consideration being made over to the Official Liquidator within a

fortnight from date. If the balance consideration is not tendered to the Official

Liquidator by the time permitted, 20 per cent of the money deposited till then will

stand forfeited.
</p>
<p id="p_16">             The Official Liquidator will make over the assets to the purchaser

within 48 hours of the final payment being received.

</p><p id="p_17">             In the event of any default on the part of S.S. Syndicate, the sale will

stand confirmed at Rs.3.10 lakh in favour of the applicant, Subrata Ghosal,

subject to such person making the balance payment (in addition to the sum of
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                           3</span>


Rs.50,000/- deposited by way of earnest) within ten days of the receipt of the

Official Liquidator's demand. In default, the earnest deposit will stand forfeited.

</p><p id="p_18">             CA No. 671 of 2015 is disposed of as above. The order dated October

9, 2015 passed on CA No.527 of 2015 stands modified.

</p><p id="p_19">            There will be no order as to costs.
</p>
<p id="p_20">            Urgent certified website copies of this order, if applied for, be

supplied to the parties subject to compliance with all requisite formalities.




</p><p id="p_21">                                                   (SANJIB BANERJEE, J.)
