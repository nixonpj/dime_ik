<div class="doc_title">Gulshan Kumar vs The State Of Bihar on 11 December, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.56321 of 2015
                       Arising Out of PS.Case No. -168 Year- 2014 Thana -PANDARAK District- PATNA
                 ======================================================
                 Gulshan Kumar, S/o Ajay Singh, resident of village- Raily, P.S.- Pandarak,
                 Distict- Patna
                                                                       .... .... Petitioner/s
                                                  Versus
                 The State of Bihar
                                                                  .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :  Mr. Arjun Prasad, Advocate
                 For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr. Indu Bala Panday(App)
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE DINESH KUMAR
                 SINGH
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   11-12-2015                   Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and the

                     State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                 The petitioner is apprehending his arrest in a

                     case registered for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_1">Sections

                     302</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_2">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code, 27 of the <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_3">Arms Act</a> and

                     3(2) (v) of the S.C./S.T.(Prevention of Atrocities) Act.

</p><p id="p_3">                                 The prosecution case is that the informant and

                     his   younger        brother       Sanjay       Choudhary          @     Ghuro

                     Chaudhary were returning home, when petitioner and

                     Ramjeevan Kumar started abusing the informant and his

                     younger brother. When the brother of the informant made

                     protest, then co-accused Ramjeevan Kumar caused injury

                     to the brother of the informant, who died on the spot.

</p><p id="p_4">                                 It is submitted by the learned counsel for the

                 petitioner that no overt act has been attributed against the

                 petitioner. The petitioner was not sent up for trial and
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">          Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.56321 of 2015 (2) dt.11-12-2015                 2</span>




                      cognizance has been taken. Statement has been made in para-

                      3 of the petition that petitioner has no criminal antecedent.

</p><p id="p_5">                                        Considering the aforesaid facts, let the above

                      named petitioner be released on bail in the event of his arrest

                      or surrender before the learned court below within a period of

                      twelve weeks from today, on furnishing bail bonds of Rs.

                      10,000/- (Ten Thousand) with two sureties of the like amount

                      each to the satisfaction of learned Additional Chief Judicial

                      Magistrate, Barh, Patna in connection with Pandarak P.S. Case

                      No. 168 of 2014, subject to conditions as laid down under

                      <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_4">Section 438(2)</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure.




</p><p id="p_6">                                                 (Dinesh Kumar Singh, J)
P.K./-
</p>
