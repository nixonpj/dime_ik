<div class="doc_title">Yogesh Kumar Chaturvedi vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 11 December, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                                 1




                     W. P. No. 14644/2017
11/12/2017
      Shri V. C. Rai, learned counsel for the petitioners.
      Shri Divesh Jain, learned Government Advocate for the
respondents/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Heard.
</p><p id="p_2">      In this writ petition, the petitioners who are Meter Readers
in the Office of Executive Engineer, M.P. Poorva Kshetra Vidyut
Vitaran Co. Ltd., Satna (M.P.)/respondent No. 4, is aggrieved by
the order issued by respondent No.4, whereby the respondent No.4
by considering the waiting list has appointed fresh Meter Readers
in place of presently working meter readers.
</p><p id="p_3">      Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that the
controversy involved in the present petition is squarely covered by
a decision of this Court passed in W.P. No.1963/2016 (Avineesh
Kumar Garg &amp; Ors. Vs. State of M.P. &amp; Ors.), decided on
11.07.2016. This Court has held as under:
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">         â
          Considering the fact that the petitioners' are
         continuing to work though on yearly contract for
         number of years, the respondents shall be well
         advised     to    consider     their   prayer       for
         continuing/extending the contract for work as
         Meter Readers with compassion and bearing in
         mind the fact that the petitioners have dedicated
         their services, though on contract basis, for more
         than a decade and in-fact are sub-serving the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                  2</span>




         people at large and are also generating revenue for
         the establishment. Aforesaid direction is issued in
         view of the Division Bench's decision in the case
         of Madhya Pradesh Vidyut Meter Vachak Singh
         (supra), the same is binding upon this Court.
         Respondents are directed to bear in mind the
         directions contained in the order passed by the
         Division Bench in writ petition No.1110/2009
         (Madhya Pradesh Vidyut Meter Vachak Sangh Vs.
         State of Madhya Pradesh), unless the petitioners
         have made themselves liable for discontinuation
         under the law.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">         Petitioners are directed to institute the proceedings
         before the Labour Court for the relief claimed in
         the writ petitions as early as possible, not later
         than 3 months from today. If the said claims are
         made within the aforesaid period, the Labour
         Court shall be well advised to decide the said
         claims as early as possible preferably within a
         period of one year.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">         Accordingly, the writ petitions stand disposed of
         with the aforesaid observations and directions.</blockquote>
<p id="p_4">      Therefore, prays that the petition may be disposed of in
terms of the said order.
</p><p id="p_5">      In the aforesaid facts and circumstances of the case,
petitioners are directed to institute the proceedings before the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                             3</span>




Labour Court for the relief claimed in the writ petition as early as
possible, not later than 3 months from today. If the said claims are
made within the aforesaid period, the Labour Court shall be well
advised to decide the said claims as early as possible preferably
within a period of one year.
</p><p id="p_6">       Accordingly, the writ petition stands disposed of with the
aforesaid observations and directions.
</p><p id="p_7">       
       C.C. as per rules.
</p>
