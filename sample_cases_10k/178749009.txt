                                                  Date of Decision: 30.01.2014

           Harbans Singh
                                                                                  ..... Petitioner

                                           Versus

           Jagtar Singh and others
                                                                               ..... Respondents

           CORAM:-              HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE RAJIV NARAIN RAINA

           Present:            Mr.Sham Lal Bhalla, Advocate
                               for the petitioner.

           RAJIV NARAIN RAINA, J. (Oral)
</pre>
<p id="p_1">                               The only arguement raised before this Court is that from

           reading para. 2 of the trial court order, it appears that the words "as per him"

           refers to the plaintiff, the petitioner before this Court, and this statement

           imputed to him is not supported by the judicial record. A reading of para. 2

           brings out that when the trial court used the words "as per him", it was

           referring to the defendant and not the plaintiff.

</p><p id="p_2">                               The only grievance expressed before this Court is that the trial

           court may later read the order dated 23rd December, 2013 as an admission of

           the plaintiff that the documents are on record, i.e., the record of allotment of

           plot. The affidavit Ex.D1 filed by the defendant by way of his examination-

           in-chief will be subjected to cross-examination by the plaintiff. Needless to

           say, during the cross-examination, it would remain the duty of the trial court

           to confine the defendant to his pleadings in the written statement with

           reference to the issues framed and onus placed.

</p><p id="p_3">                               With these observations, the petition stands disposed of.
