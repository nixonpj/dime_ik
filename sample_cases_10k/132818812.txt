                     KIRITBHAI MOTILAL SOLANKI
                                Versus
                          STATE OF GUJARAT

Appearance:
MR JIGAR D DAVE(6528) for the Applicant(s) No. 1

 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE VIPUL M. PANCHOLI

<p id="p_1">1.   Rule.      Learned APP Mr.Ronak Raval waives service
     of Rule on behalf of the respondent State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">2.   The     present       bail    application              is     filed        under
     <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure,
     1973, for regular bail in connection with FIR
     being       I足C.R.No.02         of         2019        registered            with
     Mehsana ACB Police Station, Mehsana for offence
     punishable under <a href="/doc/324254/" id="a_2">Sections 7</a>, <a href="/doc/178303/" id="a_3">13</a>/<a href="/doc/1500729/" id="a_4">1</a>, <a href="/doc/178303/" id="a_5">13</a>/<a href="/doc/62140/" id="a_6">2</a> of the
     Prevention of Corruption Act.
</p>
<p id="p_3">3.   Learned       advocate       appearing            on    behalf         of      the
     applicant submits that considering the nature of
     the offence, the applicant may be enlarged on
     regular bail by imposing suitable conditions.</p><pre id="pre_2">


4.   Learned         APP    appearing            on         behalf         of       the
     respondent足State         has       opposed         grant        of     regular

</pre><p id="p_4">     bail looking to the nature and gravity of the
     offence.
</p>
<p id="p_5">5.   Learned          advocates         appearing        on   behalf         of     the
     respective               parties      do     not   press       for     further
     reasoned order.
</p>
<p id="p_6">6.   Having          heard       the      learned       advocates         for       the
     parties          and       perusing        the     material       placed         on
     record and taking into consideration the facts
     of the case, nature of allegations, gravity of
     offences,                role    attributed         to     the       accused,
     without discussing the evidence in detail, this
     Court is of the opinion that this is a fit case
     to      exercise           the     discretion        and       enlarge         the
     applicant on regular bail.
</p>
<p id="p_7">7.   I      have        also         taken      into     consideration              the
     following aspects :
</p><p id="p_8">     a.       applicant is in jail since 12.07.2019;
     b.       remand period is over and investigation is
     almost concluded qua applicant;
     c.       I have perused the role attributed to the
     applicant and the material produced on record;
     d.       I     have        also       considered         the     punishment
     prescribed for the alleged offences punishable
     under the <a href="/doc/1331755/" id="a_7">Prevention of Corruption Act</a>;
</p>
<p id="p_9">     Looking to the overall facts and circumstances
     of the present case, I am inclined to consider
     the case of the applicant.
</p>

</p><p id="p_11">8.   This Court has also taken into consideration the
     law laid down by the Hon'ble Apex Court in the
     case of Sanjay Chandra Vs. Central Bureau of
     Investigation, reported in (2012) 1 SCC 40.
</p>
<p id="p_12">9.   Hence, the present application is allowed. The
     applicant is ordered to be released on regular
     bail in connection with FIR being I足C.R.No.02 of
     2019 registered with Mehsana ACB Police Station,
     Mehsana        on       executing              a    personal          bond         of
     Rs.10,000/足 (Rupees Ten Thousand only) with one
     surety of the like amount to the satisfaction of
     the trial Court and subject to the conditions
     that he shall;
</p>
<p id="p_13">     [a] not take undue advantage of liberty or
           misuse liberty;
</p><p id="p_14">     [b]   not      act      in   a     manner          injurious        to     the
           interest of the prosecution;
</p><p id="p_15">     [c] surrender passport, if any, to the lower
           court within a week;
</p><p id="p_16">     [d] not leave India without prior permission
           of the concerned Trial Court;
</p><p id="p_17">     [e]   mark        presence            before         the       concerned
           Police Station between 1st to 10th day of
           every           English        calendar         month         for        a
           period of six months between 11:00 a.m.
           and 2:00 p.m.;
</p><p id="p_18">     [f]   furnish the present address of residence
           to the Investigating Officer and also to
           the Court at the time of execution of
