<div class="doc_title">Smt.Lalta vs Suresh Lodhi on 20 August, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                            1               CRA 1220/14
   Smt. Lalita Singh Lodhi Vs. Suresh Lodhi &amp; another

20/8/15
       None for the appellant even on calling the case in
second round.
       Shri Praveen Newaskar, learned Govt. Advocate for
respondent No.2/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">       Having perused the appeal-memo, we have found
that this appeal is preferred by the victim under <a href="/doc/81939/" id="a_1">Section
372</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure being aggrieved by
the judgment dated 31/10/2014 passed by the Additional
Sessions    Judge,   Seondha,     district   Datia    (M.P.)     in
S.T.No.10/12 whereby the respondent No.1-Suresh has
been    acquitted    from   the   charge     of   <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_2">Sections     323</a>,
<a href="/doc/1133601/" id="a_3">325</a>,<a href="/doc/1540253/" id="a_4">326</a>,<a href="/doc/594493/" id="a_5">294</a> and <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_6">506-B</a> of I.P.C.
</p><p id="p_2">       This case is listed today alongwith Misc. Cri. Case
No.2216/15 filed at the instance of the State of Madhya
Pradesh against respondent No.1-accused Suresh under
<a href="/doc/613293/" id="a_7">Section 378(3)</a> of Cr.P.C. for grant of leave to appeal
challenging the acquittal of respondents No.1-Suresh
awarded by the impugned judgment.
</p><p id="p_3">       We are of the considered view that when State has
already approached this court with the aforesaid Misc.
Criminal Case against the impugned judgment of acquittal
of respondent No.1-accused Suresh then there is no
necessity to keep this appeal pending separately because
                                    2               CRA 1220/14
          Smt. Lalita Singh Lodhi Vs. Suresh Lodhi &amp; another

       subject to leave, if aforesaid Misc. Cri. Case is admitted
       then the appellant/victim of present case shall be at liberty
       to assist the State counsel in such appeal. Even at the
       stage of argument for grant of leave, she may assist to the
       State counsel in such Misc. Cri. Case. So in such premises,
       without expressing any opinion on the impugned judgment
       of the acquittal of respondent No.1-Suresh, this appeal is
       hereby disposed of with a direction to keep the record of
       the same with the Misc.Cri.C.No.2216/15 with further
       direction that the appellant herein shall be entitled to
       assist the State counsel through her engaged private
       counsel to prosecute aforesaid appeal.
</p><p id="p_4">            The appeal is disposed of, as indicated above.
</p>



<pre id="pre_2">            (U.C.Maheshwari)                    (B.D. Rathi)
                Judge                              Judge
