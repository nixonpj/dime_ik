<div class="doc_title">Sharvan Kumar Budrak vs State Of Rajasthan on 8 April, 2010</div><pre id="pre_1">    

 
 
 

 IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR RAJASTHAN  JAIPUR BENCH, JAIPUR
ORDER 

S.B. Cr. Misc Bail Application No. 3361/2010
Sharvan Kumar Budrak Vs State of Rajasthan through PP
8.4.2010
HON'BLE MR JUSTICE MN BHANDARI
Mr Shailender Balwada  - for the petitioner
Mr Sanjeev Mehla   PP 
BY THE COURT:</pre>

<p id="p_1">		Heard learned counsel for the parties and perused the record of the case.
</p>
<p id="p_2">		Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that petitioner is pursuing his studies for MA (Previous) and examination has already started  from 3.4.2010. Taking note of aforesaid, petitioner has been granted interim bail by the Division Bench in a case wherein he was convicted and matter is pending in appeal. 
</p>
<p id="p_3">		On the other hand, learned PP has opposed the application for interim bail. 
</p>
<p id="p_4">			After considering rival submissions of parties and without expressing any opinion on merits of the case, which may otherwise affect  outcome of trial,  looking to facts of this case, I am of the view that  petitioner deserves to be enlarged on interim bail till 24.4.2010. 
</p>
<p id="p_5">		Accordingly, the bail application is accepted and  it is directed that petitioner namely, Sharvan Kumar Budrak  may be released on interim bail till 24.4.2010 in FIR No. 70/2009,  registered at Police Station  Vishwakarma , District  Jaipur   for offence under Section/s  379 and 414  <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_1">IPC</a> provided, he  furnishes a personal bond in the sum of Rs.25,000/- together with one surety in the like amount to the satisfaction of the learned trial Court. Petitioner will surrender to the Superintendent Jail, Ajmer on 24.4.2010 itself. In case petitioner fails to  surrender on 24.4.2010  then  court below is directed to  proceed against him in accordance with law. 
</p>
<p id="p_6">(MN BHANDARI), J.
</p>
