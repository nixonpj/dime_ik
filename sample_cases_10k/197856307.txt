S.J. VAZIFDAR, C.J. (ORAL)

CWP-19-2017
          The petitioner had been given three opportunities to
inform the Court as to whether it would match the initial
reserve price at which the work has been awarded originally. It
states that it is not willing to. This is a strong indication
at least that the price that the official respondents are
likely to realize would be less than the reserve price.
</p><p id="p_8">2.        The official respondents state that the work has been
advertised three times already without an adequate response.
</p><p id="p_9">                             1 of 2
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">          ::: Downloaded on - 18-02-2017 19:02:19 :::</span>
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2"> CWP-19-2017 (O&amp;M)                                                                          2</span>




They further state that in the event of the fresh tenders being
invited they are likely to suffer a huge loss.
</p><p id="p_10">3.             Prima-facie at least, therefore, the petitioner seeks
to take advantage on account of the prices having fallen in the
meantime.       Thus,      without              going   into        the   merits   of    the
contentions         raised     by       the       petitioner         in   the    facts   and
circumstances of this case, we see no reason to invoke our
extra-ordinary          jurisdiction               under      <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article      226</a>     of    the
Constitution of India.
</p><p id="p_11">4.             The petition is dismissed.
CM-2059-2017
               The petition itself has been dismissed.
</p><p id="p_12">2.             It is, therefore, not necessary to pass any orders in
the application for impleadment.
