<p id="p_1">      The petitioners in these batch of writ petitions have called

in   question     the     orders      passed   by   the   Karnataka   State

Administrative Tribunal.           Though the Tribunal was impressed

with the contentions of the petitioners that the applicants who

belong to Davanagere District and are working as Assistant

Teachers, have been transferred to Harapanahalli Taluk which is

now in Bellary District, was found to the contrary to provisions of

law, the petitioners are not satisfied with the order. However,

keeping the interest of the students in mind, the Tribunal

directed    the         petitioners     herein      to    make   individual

representations to the respondents and the respondents were

directed to consider the same in terms of Rule 4(8) of the

Teachers Transfer Rules, 2017 and the letter of Commissioner of
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                   4</span>




Public Instructions dated 18th July, 2019 within a period of three

months from the date of receipt of such representation.


</p><p id="p_2">        2. The learned counsel for the petitioners submit that with

respect to some of the petitioners, relief has already been

granted by the State Government, while the same relief has not

been extended to the other petitioners.


</p><p id="p_3">        3.   After hearing the learned counsel for the petitioners

and the learned Additional Government Advocate, we deem it fit

to modify the order passed by the Tribunal, while directing the

respondents to consider the representations within a period of

eight    weeks    from   today   and   pass   orders   taking   into

consideration the benefit given to the other petitioners/teachers

similarly situated.
</p>
<p id="p_4">
</p><p id="p_5">        With these observations, petitions stand disposed of.
