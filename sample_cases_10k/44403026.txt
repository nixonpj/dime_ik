                          JUDGMENT
</pre>
<p id="p_1">       The petitioner, who was running a curio stall

in the Thiruvalla Railway Station on licence, seeks

extension of his licence. Admittedly the shop has

been    closed    down   for   reason    of    expiry    of   the

licence granted to the petitioner. The petitioner

prays that there is no point in keeping the stall

closed and the Railways if intending to tender, may

tender    it     expeditiously      or   the    petitioner    be

granted    the   permission    to    continue    the    business

till a tender is notified and awarded.

</p><p id="p_2">       2. The learned Standing Counsel appearing for

the Railways submits that a tender proceeding is
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">W.P(C). No.35945 of 2017 [P]            2</span>




contemplated             and      it    would       be     expeditiously

notified.
</p>
<p id="p_3">       3.    In     the        circumstances      of     the   proceedings

having not been issued as of now, the petitioner

shall be permitted to continue the business and he

shall be granted resumption of possession, which

shall be on the orders of this Court and shall be

continued only till the fresh tender is awarded to

a third party.
</p>
<p id="p_4">       4. The petitioner shall also be liable to pay

the     enhanced         licence       fee.   The      petitioner   shall

execute an agreement to that effect showing the

enhanced licence fee as also the terms of surrender

on a new tenderer being awarded with the Railways.

The petitioner shall be allowed resumption of the

proceedings            immediately          but     on     condition   of

executing of such agreement within a period of two

weeks from the date of receipt of a certified copy
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">W.P(C). No.35945 of 2017 [P]   3</span>




of this judgment.
</p>
<p id="p_5">       The writ petition is disposed of with the above

directions.
</p>

</p>

                                         JUDGE.
