<div class="doc_title">Subhash Sah vs The State Of Bihar on 19 July, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                 Criminal Miscellaneous No.18111 of 2017
                             Arising Out of PS.Case No. -18 Year- 2015 Thana -PIPRA District-
                                            EASTCHAMPARAN(MOTIHARI)
                 ======================================================
                 Subhash Sah, son of Late Raghu Nath Sah, resident of village Tajeyapur,
                 P.S. Pipra, District East Champaran
                                                                         .... .... Petitioner
                                                   Versus
                 The State of Bihar
                                                                    .... .... Opposite Party
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s        :   Mr. Sunil Kumar Verma
                 For the Opposite Party/s      : Mr. Ram Sevak Choudhary
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE VINOD KUMAR SINHA
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">4   19-07-2017                 Heard learned counsel for the petitioner.

</p><p id="p_2">                               This is an application for bail in connection with Pipra

                 P.S.Case No. 18 of 2015, corresponding to G.R.No. 349 of 2015,

                 registered for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/653797/" id="a_1">Sections 304B</a>, <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_2">302</a>,

                 <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_3">120B</a> and <a href="/doc/386021/" id="a_4">201</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                               Petitioner is husband of the deceased.

                               It has been submitted on behalf of the petitioner that

                 there is delay of 11 days in lodging FIR, which creates suspicion

                 about the prosecution case. Further submission is that earlier

                 compromise was made between the parties and the deceased was

                 sent to her matrimonial house and she died in natural death and he

                 is in custody since 3.8.2016.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                               Heard learned APP also, who has opposed the prayer

                 for bail stating that petitioner is husband and deceased died due to
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">          Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.18111 of 2017 (4) dt.19-07-2017</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                    2/2</span>




                      unnatural circumstance.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                      Having heard both sides and considering the aforesaid

                      facts and circumstances, I am not inclined to grant bail to the

                      petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                      As the petitioner is in custody for 11 months, learned

                      trial court is directed to expedite the trial of the petitioner and try

                      to conclude it within nine months and at the same time

                      Superintendent of Police, East Champaran is directed to ensure the

                      presence of witnesses in court on the date fixed, so that trial be

                      concluded within the said period.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                                      With the above observation, this application is

                      dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_8">


</p><p id="p_9">                                                 (Vinod Kumar Sinha, J)
spal/-
</p>
