<p id="p_1">31.10.2018 nobody appeared.
</p><p id="p_2">      At this stage,       Shri Akash Chaturvedi, learned counsel for the

respondents No.3 and 4 appears and submits that he is coming from Court
Room No.12 and, therefore, his brief is not available and prays for time to
bring on record the compliance which was made by respondents No.3 and 4,
whom he is representing before this Court.
</p><p id="p_3">      On his request, seven days time is granted to bring on record
documentary evidence to show that what compliance was made by
respondents No.3 and 4, failing which they shall remain personally present on
the next date.
</p><p id="p_4">      Shri N. K. Gupta submits that one H.B. Sharma, S.D.M., Morar,

Gwalior, who is not a party in this case had filed an affidavit to the effect that
S.L.P. is pending. Shri Gupta has filed a copy of the order dated 20.07.2018
passed in Special Leave Petition (Civil) Diary No(s).19108/2018, whereby it is
apparent that SLP which was filed challenging the order dated 14.02.2017 in
Writ Appeal No.69/17 has been dismissed on the ground of delay.
</p><p id="p_5">      This affidavit has been filed on behalf of Dr. Sanjay Goyal and Others
by one Shri H.B. Sharma, S.D.M., Morar, district Gwalior. It is apparent that
S.D.M., Morar, District Gwalior, was not a party to the Writ Petition or Writ
Appeal.
</p><p id="p_6">      Shri Akash Chaturvedi submits that he is In-charge S.D.M. at this point
of time in Gwalior city.
</p><p id="p_7">      On the next date, let respondents No.3 and 4 shall also clarify that how

     record by a person who is not a party in the contempt petition and he was
