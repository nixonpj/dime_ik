<div class="doc_title">Kishanbhai Raghunath Bhoglele vs State Of Gujarat on 16 June, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">        R/CR.MA/8208/2015                              ORDER




         IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

CRIMINAL MISC.APPLICATION (FOR REGULAR BAIL) NO. 8208 of 2015

==========================================================
          KISHANBHAI RAGHUNATH BHOGLELE....Applicant(s)
                            Versus
                STATE OF GUJARAT....Respondent(s)
==========================================================
Appearance:
MR P P MAJMUDAR, ADVOCATE for the Applicant(s) No. 1
MR RC KODEKAR, APP for the Respondent(s) No. 1
==========================================================

        CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE Z.K.SAIYED

                            Date : 16/06/2015


                             ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">1.   Rule.   Mr.R.C.Kodekar,   learned   APP   waives 
service of Rule on behalf of the respondent.
</p>

<p id="p_2">2.   This   Application   has   been   preferred   under 
Section   439   of   the   Code   of   Criminal   Procedure, 
1973   in   connection   with   the   offence   being   CR 
No.I­101   of   2014   registered   with   Umargam   Police 
Station,   Valsad   for   the   offences   u/s.   379,   413 
and 114 of Indian Penal Code. 
</p>
<p id="p_3">3.   Heard   Mr.Majmudar,   learned   advocate   for   the 
applicant.   He   has   contended   that   present 
applicant is receiver of the muddamal motorcycle. 
The   present   applicant   is   bonafide   purchaser   of 



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                Page 1 of 4</span>
        R/CR.MA/8208/2015                         ORDER



the property and he is from Maharashtra. He was 
not knowing that motorcycle are stolen property. 
He   has   prayed   to   grant   bail   to   the   present 
applicant on any stringent conditions. 
</p>
<p id="p_4">4.     Heard   Mr.R.C.Kodekar,   learned   APP   for   the 
respondent   State.   He   has   read   case   papers   and 
contended   that   as   per   the   address   given   by   the 
applicant   in   the   cause   title   he   is   resident   of 
Maharashtra.   He   has   opposed   present   application 
strongly and prayed not to grant bail to present 
applicant. 
</p>

<p id="p_5">5.   Having heard the learned Counsel for both the 
sides and looking to the facts and circumstances 
of the case, statement of the witnesses, gravity 
of the offence and quantum of punishment and the 
fact there is no definite allegation made against 
the applicant, I am inclined to grant bail to the 
applicant without going into merits of the case. 
</p>

<p id="p_6">6.   Considering   the   above,   this   Application   is 
allowed. The applicant is ordered to be released 
on   bail   in   connection   with   CR   No.I­101   of   2014 
registered   with   Umargam   Police   Station,   Valsad 
for   the   offence   alleged   against   him   in   this 
Application   on   his   executing   a   Bond   of 
Rs.50,000/­ (Rupees Fifty thousand only) with one 
solvent   surety   of   the   like   amount   to   the 


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                           Page 2 of 4</span>
        R/CR.MA/8208/2015                         ORDER



satisfaction   of   the   trial   Court   and   subject   to 
the conditions that he shall­


</p><p id="p_7">a)   not   take   undue   advantage   of   his   liberty   or 
abuse his liberty;
</p>

<p id="p_8">b)   not   to   try   to   tamper   or   pressurise   the 
prosecution   witnesses   or   complainant   in   any 
manner; 
</p>

<p id="p_9">c)   maintain   law  and  order  and  should  cooperate 
the Investigating Officer;
</p>

<p id="p_10">d)   not act in a manner injurious to the interest 
of the prosecution;
</p>

<p id="p_11">e)   not   leave   the   country   without   the   prior 
permission of the concerned Sessions Judge;
</p>

<p id="p_12">f)   mark presence before Umargam Police Station, 
Valsad   on   every   15th  of   the   month   between   11:00 
a.m. to 5:00 p.m.  
</p>
<p id="p_13">g)   furnish the address of his residence to the 
I.O.   and   also   to   the   Court   at   the   time   of 
execution   of   the   bond   and   shall   not   change   the 
residence without prior permission of this Court;
</p>

<p id="p_14">h)   surrender his passport, if any, to the lower 


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                           Page 3 of 4</span>
         R/CR.MA/8208/2015                      ORDER



Court within a week.
</p>

<p id="p_15">7.    If the breach of any of the above conditions 
is   committed,   the   concerned   Sessions   Judge   will 
be   free   to   issue   warrant   or   take   appropriate 
action in the matter.
</p>

<p id="p_16">8.    Bail   before   the   lower   Court   having 
jurisdiction to try the case. It would be open to 
the trial Court concerned to give time to furnish 
the solvency certificate if prayed for.
</p>

<p id="p_17">9.    Rule is made absolute. Direct service is permitted. 
</p>
<p id="p_18">                                             (Z.K.SAIYED, J.)
KKS
