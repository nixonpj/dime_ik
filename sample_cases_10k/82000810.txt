<div class="doc_title">Ayushi Sharma And Anr vs State Of Haryana And Ors on 10 June, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">     IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA AT
           CHANDIGARH
137                        C.W.P. No.16433 of 2019
                           Date of Decision:- 10.06.2019
Ayushi Sharma and another                    ....Petitioners

                   vs.

State of Haryana and others           ....Respondents
                  ***
CORAM:- HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE SUDHIR MITTAL

                  ***
Present:-    Ms. Ekta Saini, Advocate, for the petitioners.
                  ***

SUDHIR MITTAL, J. (Oral)
</pre><p id="p_1">             The present petition has been filed under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the

Constitution of India invoking the right to life guaranteed under <a href="/doc/1199182/" id="a_2">Article 21</a> thereof.

</p><p id="p_2">2.           Learned counsel for the petitioners submits that the petitioners are

above the age prescribed for solemnizing marriage and have got married out of

their own free will. This act has enraged the private respondents, who are now

threatening to cause them physical harm. A detailed representation dated 6.6.2019

(Annexure P-5) has been submitted before respondent No.2, but he has failed to

take any action, thereupon.
</p>
<p id="p_3">3.           Notice of motion.
</p>
<p id="p_4">4.           Mr. D.K. Mittal, DAG, Haryana, accepts notice on behalf of

respondent Nos.1 to 3. Let four copies of the petition be supplied to him during

the course of the day.
</p>
<p id="p_5">5.           Since directions are sought to be issued to respondents No. 1 to 3

only, there is no necessity to issue notice to private respondents.

</p><p id="p_6">6.           The petition is disposed of with direction to respondent No.2 to take

into consideration representation dated 6.6.2019 (Annexure P-5) as well as any
