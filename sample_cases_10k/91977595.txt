<div class="doc_title">In Re : Sk. Rahamatulla vs The State Of West Bengal &amp; Ors on 16 January, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                                               1

       16.01.2019
                .
</pre><p id="p_1">         Item No 26
              Court No.15
               Avijit Mitra
W.P. No. 25981 (W) of 2018
In re : Sk. Rahamatulla
</p><p id="p_2">              - Versus -
</p><p id="p_3">        The State of West Bengal &amp; Ors.
</p>
<p id="p_4">Mr. Moloy Roy,
Mr. Koustav Manna,
Ms. Sutapa Upadhyay
              For the Petitioner
Mrs. Subhra Nag
              For the State Respondents
Mr. Kazi Safiullah
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">              For the Respondent Nos. 4 to 10</span>

         Affidavit of service filed by the petitioner be kept on record.

         Mr. Roy, learned advocate appearing for the petitioner submits that the

petitioner is the owner of the land detailed in paragraph 2 of the writ petition. On 27th

November, 2018, when the petitioner was cultivating the said land, the private

respondents attempted to forcibly dispossess the petitioner and also threatened the

petitioner of dire consequences. Such fact was reported to the police authorities.

Thereafter, an application under <a href="/doc/80360/" id="a_1">Section 144(2)</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure was

also filed. Subsequent thereto, on 6th December, 2018 the private respondents again

forcibly entered into the petitioner's property with deadly weapons and assaulted the

petitioner. Such fact was again reported to the police authorities but no steps were

taken.
</p>
<p id="p_5">         Mr. Safiullah, learned advocate appearing for the private respondent nos. 4 to 10

submits that the said respondents are co-sharers of the property detailed in paragraph 2

of the writ petition. The dispute among the parties is civil in nature and as such no

interference is called for.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                             2</span><p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">      Mrs. Nag, learned advocate appearing for the State respondents submits that the

petitioner's complaint was treated as FIR and Chanditala P.S. Case No.6 of 2019 dated

4th January, 2019 under <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_2">Sections 341</a>/<a href="/doc/162506/" id="a_3">447</a>/<a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_4">323</a>/<a href="/doc/1133601/" id="a_5">325</a>/ <a href="/doc/1540253/" id="a_6">326</a>/<a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_7">307</a>/<a href="/doc/203036/" id="a_8">354</a>/<a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_9">506</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_10">34</a> of the Indian

Penal Code has been registered and investigation is in progress. On the basis of the prior

complaint of the petitioner, a proceeding under <a href="/doc/1914745/" id="a_11">Section 107</a> of the Code of Criminal

Procedure has also been drawn up to maintain peace and tranquility.

</p><p id="p_8">      Upon hearing the learned advocates appearing for the respective parties and

considering the materials on record it appears that the dispute between the petitioner

and the private respondents pertains to an immovable property. It involves disputed

questions of fact.
</p>
<p id="p_9">      For redressal of such grievance, the petitioner would be at liberty to approach

the competent civil forum. Since a criminal case has already been registered on the

basis of the complaint lodged by the petitioner and as investigation is in progress, the

investigating agency is directed to conduct such investigation in the aforesaid case in a

fair and impartial manner and conclude the same as expeditiously as possible.

</p><p id="p_10">      The respondent no.3 is also directed to ensure that no physical harm is caused to

the either set of parties due to the dispute between them.

</p><p id="p_11">      The writ petition is, accordingly, disposed of.

      There shall however be no order as to costs.
</p>
<p id="p_12">      Urgent photostat certified copy of this order, if applied for, be given to the

learned advocates for the parties.
</p>
<p id="p_13">

</p><p id="p_14">                                                             (Tapabrata Chakraborty, J.)
