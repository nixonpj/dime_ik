<div class="doc_title">Dr Uddipan Mukherjee vs Debasree Sanyal Nee Mukherjee on 23 February, 2017</div><p id="p_1"><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                        1</span>


    Serial 63.
</p><p id="p_2">February 23, 2017
     SG
                                CO 4050 of 2016


                           Dr Uddipan Mukherjee
                                     versus
                           Debasree Sanyal nee Mukherjee


             Mr Mayukh Maitra
                                ... for the petitioner.
</p>


<p id="p_3">        The petitioner complains of an order by which the alimony pendente lite
has been enhanced despite there being no application in such regard from the
wife.
</p>

<p id="p_4">        It appears that the wife applied both under <a href="/doc/1449825/" id="a_1">Section 24</a> of the Hindu
Marriage Act and under <a href="/doc/1056396/" id="a_2">Section 125</a> of the Criminal Procedure Code and in both
cases the orders provided for a sum of Rs.5,000/- per month to be paid to the
wife. The petitioner prayed before the matrimonial court that the two orders did
not imply that the petitioner would have to pay a sum of Rs.10,000/- per month,
but the petitioner would have to pay one consolidated sum. In undertaking such
exercise, the court below enhanced the alimony pendente lite from Rs.5,000/-
per month to Rs.7,000/- per month.      In view of the nominal increase and the
fact that Rs.5,000/- per month would not be enough for any person to survive on
in these days, the order impugned does not call for any interference.
</p>

<p id="p_5">        However, the court below is requested to ensure that Mat Suit No.13 of
2012 is taken up and disposed of as expeditiously as possible and, preferably,
within eight months of the receipt of a copy of this order, without granting
unnecessary adjournments to either party.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                         2</span>


<p id="p_6">      Since this order does not prejudice the wife, no service has been directed to
be effected on the wife.
</p>

<p id="p_7">      CO 4050 of 2016 is disposed of without any order as to costs.
</p>



<p id="p_8">                                 (Sanjib Banerjee, J.)
