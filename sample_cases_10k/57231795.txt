<div class="doc_title">Ramesh Swami vs State Of Rajasthan Through Pp on 8 September, 2016</div><p id="p_1"> IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR RAJASTHAN AT JAIPUR
                       BENCH JAIPUR
                         ORDER

            S.B.Criminal Misc. Bail Application No.9430/2016

Ramesh Swami, son of Late Shri Satya Narayan Swami, aged about 30
years, by caste Swami, resident of Ward No.14, behalf Goshala,
Salasar Road, Swamiyon ka Mohalla, near Nehru Park, Sikar (Raj.)
(At present in District Jail, Sikar)
                                              ACCUSED-PETITIONER
                                  VERSUS
State of Rajasthan, through Public Prosecutor
                                              ........NON-PETITIONER

Date of Order                          :::::                          08.09.2016

           HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE PRASHANT KUMAR AGARWAL

Mr. Ripu Daman Singh Naruka, for the petitioner.
Mr. Anil Yadav, Public Prosecutor for State.

</p><p id="p_2">             Heard learned counsel for the parties.

             On consideration of submissions made on behalf of the respective

parties and the material made available on record as well as the evidence

collected during investigation which has been placed on record by way of copy of

the charge-sheet and also statement of the victim recorded during the course of

trial, copy of which has been made available on record and more particularly

looking to the role attributed to the present petitioner who is husband of the

victim and also gravity of the offence, but without expressing any final opinion

on the merit and demerit of the case, I am not inclined to grant benefit of bail to

the petitioner under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> Cr.P.C.

</p><p id="p_3">             Consequently, the application for grant of bail under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_2">Section 439</a>

Cr.P.C. is, hereby, dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_4">             Looking to the statement of the victim recorded during the course

of trial, it is expected from the pro secution to move an appropriate application to

take cognizance against the persons who have not been charge-sheeted.




</p><p id="p_5">                                    (PRASHANT KUMAR AGARWAL), J
Jyoti/-
</p><p id="p_6">S.No.121
