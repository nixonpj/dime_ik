<p id="p_1">Siri Jagan, J.
</p>
<p id="p_2">      The appellants are the claimants in L.A.R.No.124/2003

before the Sub Court, Palakkad. By a notification dated

22.4.1999 issued under <a href="/doc/169774/" id="a_1">Section 4(1)</a> of the Land Acquisition Act,

certain lands belonging to the appellants were acquired. The

land acquisition officer fixed land value at the rate of ` 1203/-

per cent. Dissatisfied with the land value fixed by the land

acquisition officer, the appellants sought reference and

consequently, the L.A.R. came to be referred to the Sub Court.

The said L.A.R. was considered along with three other L.A.Rs

and the Sub Court enhanced the land value to ` 1,584/- per cent.

Still dissatisfied with the land value fixed by the Sub Court also,

the appellants have filed this appeal seeking enhanced land

value.
</p>
<p id="p_3">      2.    We have heard the parties.
</p>
<p id="p_4">      3.    In respect of the same notification for comparable

land, for which, the land acquisition officer had fixed land value

L.A.A.103/3                     - : 2 :-
</p>
<p id="p_5">at ` 1203/- per cent, which was enhanced to ` 1,584/- per cent by

the reference court, in L.A.R.No.99/2003, this Court has in

L.A.A.No.581/2012 refixed land value at the rate of ` 2,500/- per

cent. Since the land involved in this appeal is comparable to the

land involved in L.A.A.No.581/2012, we are satisfied that the

appellants are also entitled to the same relief. Accordingly, this

L.A.A. is disposed of refixing the land value at the rate of

` 2,500/- per cent. Needless to say, the appellants would be

entitled to all statutory benefits based on that land value.

</p><p id="p_6">      The L.A.Appeal is disposed of as above.
</p>
