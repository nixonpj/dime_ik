            main case is taken up today for hearing itself.

            Khewat/Khatauni No.218 min/238 min. bearing Khasra No.726/604 situated

            at village Fatehpur Diwanwala HB 181 Tehsil Kalka, District Panchkula,

</p><p id="p_3">                                The petitioner has laid challenge to the order dated 27.01.2004

            passed by Commissioner and Secretary to Government Haryana,

            Rehabilitation Department, exercising suo motto powers of Central

            Government under <a href="/doc/1493413/" id="a_1">Section 33</a> of the Displaced Persons (Compensation and

            <a href="/doc/223852/" id="a_2">Rehabilitation) Act</a>, 1954 whereby the orders passed by the Chief

            Settlement Commissioner restoring the land after a delay of more than 20

            years of cancellation of the original allotment in favour of said Surat Lal,

            were quashed and declared null and void.               As a result apart from the

            cancellation of the entire allotments, the alleged many bonafide purchasers

            including the petitioner was effected. The plea of bona fide purchasers was

            negated since apart from the cancellation of the allotment of the original

            allottee, the possession of the alleged land concededly was never taken by

            the original allottee.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                At the time of hearing today, learned Counsel for the petitioner

            as well as the respondent State are agreed that the issue stands decided in

            favour of the State Government in cases of identical situated other bonafide

            purchasers of different parcels of the same allotted land to the original

            allottee Surat Lal. It is stated that the present writ petition is squarely

            covered by a decision of this Court rendered in Letters Patent Appeal

            NO.184 of 2004 titled Subash Chand and others Vs. The Financial

            Commissioner Revenue and Ors decided on 24.12.2008 as well as CWP

            No.9443 of 2004 titled as Onkar Mal &amp; Ors. Vs. The State of Haryana &amp;

            Ors. Decided on 24.12.2008. Accordingly, they concede that the present
