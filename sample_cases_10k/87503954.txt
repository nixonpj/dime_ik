<p id="p_1">2   16-05-2016                   Heard learned counsel for the petitioner, learned

                 counsel for the State and learned counsel for the Bank.

</p><p id="p_2">                                 The claim of the petitioner is pertaining to the

                 Chattisgarh State from where the petitioner retired. The

                 petitioner was appointed as Guard in Central Jail, Raipur, in the

                 year 1956 and superannuated from the service in the year 1986

                 from Raipur.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                 After superannuation, the petitioner has been

                 getting the pension through one P.P.O. No. When the State of
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">           Patna High Court CWJC No.14992 of 2015 (2) dt.16-05-2016</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                    2/2</span>




                       Madhya Pradesh was bifurcated and at present Raipur is the

                       capital of Chattisgarh, the petitioner was given another P.P.O. by

                       the State of Chattisgarh and in the State of Bihar the Accountant

                       General has also given separate P.P.O.

</p><p id="p_4">                                       The statements made in the counter affidavit of the

                       Punjab National Bank and the documents attached therein shows

                       that the petitioner is getting proper pension in terms of 6th pay

                       Revision Commission report.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                       In such view of the matter, the present writ petition

                       has no substance to sustain in the facts and circumstances of the

                       case. Accordingly, this petition is dismissed.


</p><p id="p_6">                                                                     (Shivaji Pandey, J)
pawan/-
</p>
