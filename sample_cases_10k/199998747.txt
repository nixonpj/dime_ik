                               JUDGMENT
</pre>
<p id="p_1">     Petitioner had obtained Ext.P2 permit for construction of a

residential building in an extent of 9.57 Ares of land situate in

Re-sy.Nos.211/8-1 and 211/8-2 of Vijayapuram Village in

Kottayam Taluk. Petitioner has also secured Ext.P3 series of

permit/certificates since the land is abutting National Highway.

According to the petitioner, the construction requires excavation

of ordinary earth and certain quantity need to be transported

outside the subject premises. Therefore, petitioner approached

the 3rd respondent, the Geologist for issuance of mineral transit

passes as provided under rule 14(2) of the Kerala Minor Mineral

Concession Rules, 2015 and the appended other Rules. The said

application is numbered as 3083/2016. It is contended that, in

spite of the earnest efforts of the petitioner, the application is

not considered by the 3rd respondent. It is in this background

seeking appropriate direction this writ petition is filed.

</p><p id="p_2">     2. Having heard learned counsel for petitioner, learned

Government Pleader and perusing the pleadings and documents

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">W.P.(C). No.14017 of 2017        2</span>




on record, there will be a direction to the 3rd respondent to

consider the application specified above at the earliest possible

time and at any rate within three weeks from the date of receipt

of a copy of this judgment. I make it clear that, if the petitioner

produces all the necessary applications to establish his claim on

the basis of Ext.P2 building permit and there are no legal

hurdles standing in the way, the same shall be considered

positively.
</p>
<p id="p_3">     Writ petition is disposed of accordingly.
