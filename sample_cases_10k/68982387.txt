<p id="p_1"> S.B.CR. MISC. BAIL APPLICATION NO.7624/2008
NASIR ALI VS. STATE OF RAJ.
</p>
<p id="p_2">15.10.2008

HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE MAHESH BHAGWATI


Mr. Rajesh Mootha, for the petitioner.
</p><p id="p_3">Mr. R.P.Kuldeep, Public Prosecutor for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_4">		This order governs the disposal of  bail application filed by Shri Rajesh Mootha, Advocate on behalf of the applicant Nasir Ali pertaining to Criminal Case No. 324/1997 pending before the Judicial Magistrate, Tonk in the offence under Section 19/54 of the Rajasthan Excise Act.
</p><p id="p_5">		Heard the learned counsel for the petitioner as also the learned Public Prosecutor for the State and perused the material on record.
</p><p id="p_6">		The learned counsel for the petitioner has contended that the alleged offence under Section 19/54 of the Rajasthan Excise Act is triable by the Court of Judicial Magistrate-Ist Class, he has further contended that the accused on account of illness could not attend the Court on 23.7.2008 as a result of which his bail bonds were forfeited, the accused petitioner has been in custody for the last one month, the trial is likely to take time, hence, he may be given one more opportunity of remaining on bail.
</p><p id="p_7">		The learned Public Prosecutor has opposed the bail petition on the ground that the trial of the case has been pending in the Court since 1998.  The petitioner evaded trial on 8.9.1998, 29.5.2001, 21.10.2003 and 26.5.2007.  On account of his having remained absent continuously, the trial has been hampered, as such his bail petition should be dismissed.
</p><p id="p_8">		Having considered the submissions made at the bar and perused the bail order dated 23.9.2008 of Sessions Judge, Tonk, it is found that the petitioner has jumped bail bonds many a times but keeping in view the fact that the petitioner has been in custody for the last one month, I, looking to the nature of the offence, do feel inclined to accord one more opportunity to the petitioner to remain on bail.
</p><p id="p_9">		In the result, the bail petition is allowed and it is ordered that the accused petitioner Nasir Ali S/o Shri Saadat Ali shall be released on bail on furnishing a personal bond in the sum of Rs.50,000/- (Rs. Fifty Thousand )  and three surety bonds each of Rs.20,000/- to the satisfaction of the learned trial Court with the stipulation that he shall appear before that Court on all dates of hearing and as and when called upon to do so till the trial is concluded.
</p>				
<p id="p_10">(MAHESH BHAGWATI)J.
</p><p id="p_11">PC
</p>
