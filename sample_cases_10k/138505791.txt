<div class="doc_title">Sanat Kumar Patel vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 30 November, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                         CRA-3204-2016
          (SANAT KUMAR PATEL Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)


30-11-2016

Shri S.K.Dubey, learned counsel for the appellant.
      Shri Amod Gupta, P.L. for the respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Heard. Case diary perused.
</p><p id="p_2">        This is an appeal filed under <a href="/doc/50707649/" id="a_1">Section 14-A</a> of the
Scheduled Castes and <a href="/doc/25085007/" id="a_2">Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of
Atrocities) Act</a> against the impugned order dated 18.10.2016
passed by the Special Court under S.C./S.T (Prevention of
Atrocities) Act in B.A.No.2001280/16 whereby the court
below has dismissed the application filed by the appellant
under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_3">Section 439</a> Cr.P.C. The appellant is in custody since
14/09/2016 for the offence under <a href="/doc/838469/" id="a_4">Sections 452</a>, <a href="/doc/203036/" id="a_5">354</a>, <a href="/doc/1279834/" id="a_6">376</a>/<a href="/doc/1185693/" id="a_7">511</a>
of the IPC, and section of the 3(1)(xi) and 3(2)(v) of the SC/ST
(Prevention of Atrocities) Act in Crime                No.237/2016
registered at Police Station Semariya, District Rewa (M.P.).
</p><p id="p_3">       According to the prosecution story, the allegation
against the appellant is that he entered into the house of
prosecutrix and made attempt to commit rape with her. She
belongs to the Scheduled Castes community.
</p><p id="p_4">       Learned counsel for the applicant/accused submitted
that he is innocent and has been falsely implicated in this
case. The applicant/accused is in custody since 14.09.2016.
In this case, charge-sheet has been filed and trial will take
time to conclude. In view of the aforesaid circumstances,
prayer is made to enlarge the applicant/accused on bail.
</p><p id="p_5">      Learned P.L. for the respondent/State has opposed the
 bail and prayed for rejection for the same.
</p><p id="p_6">      Looking to the facts and circumstances of the case and
perusal of impugned order, this Court is of the view that this
is not a case in which the appellant is required to be kept in
custody during the whole trial, but without commenting
anything on the merits of the case, impugned order is set
aside and this appeal is allowed. It is ordered that the
appellant/accused Sanat Kumar Patel be released on bail
on his furnishing a personal bond in the sum of Rs.40,000/-
(Rs. Forty Thousand only) with one solvent surety in the
like amount to the satisfaction of the trial court for securing
his presence before the said Court on all the dates of hearing
fixed in this regard during trial and for complying with the
conditions enumerated in sub-section (3) of <a href="/doc/848468/" id="a_8">Section 437</a> of
Cr.P.C.
</p><p id="p_7">      CC as per rules.
</p>


<p id="p_8">                                               (J. P. GUPTA)
                                                   JUDGE




HS
