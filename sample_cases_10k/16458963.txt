                                The petitioner was convicted under <a href="/doc/1823824/" id="a_1">Section 138</a> of

            instituted on 24.3.2008/16.4.2009                   and sentenced to undergo

            imprisonment for a period of one year and to pay fine to the tune of

            Rs.1000/- and in default of payment of fine to further undergo

            imprisonment for one month. His appeal was thereafter dismissed by

                                 With the intervention of the respectables, parties have

            entered into a compromise to pay the amount against cheque No.

            205777 dated 1.2.2008 and the affidavit was executed by the

            complainant dated 8.5.2013 (Annexure P-1).

</p><p id="p_8">                                 Petitioner sought compounding of the offence in view of

            the affidavit (Annexure P-1) as the matter stood compromised.

            Notice of motion was issued on 17.5.2013 and the petitioner was

            directed to deposit 15% of the cheque amount with Haryana State

            Legal Services Authority. Vide order dated 6.8.2013, parties were

            directed            to   get   the   statements   recorded   before   the       Illaqa

            Magistrate/Chief Judicial Magistrate, Ambala.

</p><p id="p_9">                                 In compliance of order of this Court dated 6.8.2013,

            status report dated 13.9.2013 has been received to the effect that

            both the parties have entered into compromise with their free consent

            without any pressure and undue influence. As per the status report

            in pursuance of compromise between the parties, the accused has

            already made the payment of Rs.1,20,000/- to the complainant which

            has been accepted by him. Further the accused has also deposited

            the compounding charges of Rs.22,500/- in District Legal Aid Fund

            vide receipt No. 087782 dated 6.8.2013 and the complainant has

            stated that nothing is left due against the accused and he has no

            objection if the offence is compounded. Statement of petitioner

            Niranjan Singh and complainant Rakesh Kumar have been recorded

            to the same effect on 12.9.2014. The statements of both parties with

            regard to the compromise and receipt of compounding charges in

            judgment passed by the Supreme Court in <a href="/doc/1594211/" id="a_2">Damodar S. Prabhu vs.

            Act is being compounded and the petitioner is acquitted of the

            charges framed against him.
