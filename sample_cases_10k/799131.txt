  IN THE HIGH COURT OF KERALA AT ERNAKULAM

WP(C).No. 24727 of 2008(R)


1. K.P.RAJENDRAN
                      ...  Petitioner

                        Vs



1. THE STATE OF KERALA, REPRESENTED BY THE
                       ...       Respondent

2. THE DIRECTOR

3. THE DISTRICT MEDICAL OFFICER

                For Petitioner  :SRI.SOJAN MICHEAL

                For Respondent  : No Appearance

The Hon'ble MR. Justice P.N.RAVINDRAN

 Dated :18/08/2008

 O R D E R
                             P.N.Ravindran, J.
                       =====================
                         W.P(C).No.24727 of 2008
                       =====================

                Dated this the 18th day of August, 2008.

                                JUDGMENT
</pre>

<p id="p_1">      The petitioner challenges Ext.P2 order of transfer whereby he was

transferred to Government Ayurveda Hospital, Mannarkkad.              The

petitioner challenges Ext.P2 on various grounds. The petitioner has in

Ext.P3 representation addressed to the second respondent sought a

reconsideration of his transfer.
</p>
<p id="p_2">      2. The petitioner has in Ext.P3 brought to the notice of the second

respondent that his wife is employed at Government Commercial

Institute at Mala and therefore he has to look after his aged father who is

an epilepsy patient. The petitioner also relies on other circumstances in

support of his request to retain him at Palakkad.        He has in Ext.P3

pointed out that a vacancy of Upper Division Clerk exists in the District

Ayureveda Hospital, Palakkad when the incumbent Sri.M.G.Mohanadasan,

was transferred and posted as per Ext.P2 itself in the District Medical

Office (ISM), Palakkad.
</p>
<p id="p_3">      3. Having heard Sri.Sojan Micheal, the learned counsel appearing

for the petitioner and Smt.M.R.Sreelatha, the learned Senior Government

Pleader appearing for the respondents, I am of the opinion that the

request made by the petitioner in Ext.P3 requires to be considered by the

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">WP(C) 25727/08                    -: 2 :-</span>

second respondent. The second respondent is directed to consider and

pass orders on Ext.P3, after notice to and affording the petitioner an

opportunity of being heard within one month from the date on which the

petitioner produces a certified copy of this judgment.        Since the

petitioner has already been relieved from Palakkad, I am not inclined to

pass any interim order permitting him to continue at Palakkad as

requested by the learned counsel for the petitioner.

</p><p id="p_4">      The Writ Petition is disposed of as above.
</p>
<p id="p_5">


</p><p id="p_6">                                                P.N.Ravindran,
                                                Judge.
