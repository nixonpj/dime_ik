<div class="doc_title">Chandra Shekhar Kushwaha vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 21 December, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                             WP-8464-2016
          (CHANDRA SHEKHAR KUSHWAHA Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)


21-12-2016

      Shri Prashant K. Badarya, learned counsel for the petitioner.
      Shri Santosh Yadav, learned counsel for the respondents/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Heard.
</p><p id="p_2">      Shri Badarya, learned counsel for the petitioner submits that
the petitioner has challenged the Charge-sheet dated 30-12-2014 to
the extent it relates to Charge No.2. The singular contention
advanced by Shri Badarya is that for the same set of allegations, the
petitioner is subjected to disciplinary proceeding by issuance of a
Charge-sheet dated 30-12-2014. Parallely, the petitioner is subjected
to a criminal case wherein the charge No.5 is founded upon the same
facts. By placing reliance on AIR 1960 SC 806 (<a href="/doc/1301089/" id="a_1">Delhi Cloth &amp;
General Mills Ltd. vs. Kushal Bhan</a>) and AIR 1999 SC 1416
(Capt. <a href="/doc/888207/" id="a_2">M. Paul Anthony vs. Bharat Gold Mines Ltd. &amp; Anr</a>.), it is
submitted that when criminal case and departmental enquiry is based
on same set of facts, the departmental enquiry should be stayed.
</p><p id="p_3">      Prayer is opposed by Shri Santosh Yadav, learned Panel
Lawyer.
</p><p id="p_4">      No other point is pressed by the parties.
</p><p id="p_5">      I have heard the parties on this aspect and perused the record.
      Charge No.2 shows that allegation against the petitioner is that
on 14-11-2014 at around 03:30 am, he visited the house of his fellow
female employee and misbehaved with her which violates Rule 3 of
M.P. Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1965. For this misconduct, it is
alleged that the petitioner has also violated the Clause 64 of the
Police Regulation.
</p><p id="p_6">      In the criminal case, the allegation against the petitioner is
 relating to the offence punishable under <a href="/doc/68146/" id="a_3">Section 509</a> of the IPC. It is
alleged that the petitioner, in order to outrage the modesty of the
complainant removed the blanket from her body. Thus, the
allegations in both the matters are different. In the departmental
enquiry, the allegation is regarding violation of conduct rules
whereas in the criminal case the allegation is arising out of offence
under <a href="/doc/68146/" id="a_4">Section 509</a> of the IPC.
</p><p id="p_7">      The judgment of Supreme Court in Delhi Cloth &amp; General
Mills Ltd. (supra) was considered in the subsequent case of Capt.
M. Paul Anthony (supra).
</p><p id="p_8">      A plain reading of the said judgments shows that merely
because the departmental enquiry and criminal case are founded
upon the same facts, as a thumb rule, departmental enquiry cannot
be stayed. It can be done when there are complicated questions of
facts and law are involved.
</p><p id="p_9">      In the present case, the allegations are based on a simple facts.
The petitioner is unable to show that any complicated question of fact
and law are involved in the present case. Hence, the judgments relied
by the petitioner are of no assistance to the petitioner. I find no
reason to interfere in this matter.
</p><p id="p_10">      The petition fails and is hereby dismissed.
</p>

<p id="p_11">                                                      (SUJOY PAUL)

mohsin
