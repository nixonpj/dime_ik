<div class="doc_title">Amit Yadav @ Chinu vs State Of U.P. on 14 November, 2019</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: Aniruddha Singh</div>

<pre id="pre_1">




HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT ALLAHABAD, LUCKNOW BENCH
 
 

?Court No. - 12
 

 
Case :- BAIL No. - 5945 of 2019
 

 
Applicant :- Amit Yadav @ Chinu
 
Opposite Party :- State of U.P.
 
Counsel for Applicant :- Satyendra Kumar Maurya,Brij Kishore
 
Counsel for Opposite Party :- Govt. Advocate,Sunil Kumar Yadav
 

 
Hon'ble Aniruddha Singh,J.
</pre> 
<p id="p_1">Heard learned counsel for the applicant as well as Sri Arun Kumar Pandey, learned AGA for the State and perused the record.
</p> 
<p id="p_2">According to prosecution case, FIR was lodged on 22.3.2019 at 19:37 against Manish Yadav and Amit Yadav @ Chinu alleging that on 16.3.2019 at 10:45 P.M.(night) they assaulted Raju Yadav(brother of complainant) with stick(danda). According to postmortem report, he received three ante-mortem injuries(one on neck), cause of death was found cardio respiration failure due to ante-mortem spinal injury and hyoid bone was found 'NAD'.
</p> 
<p id="p_3">It is submitted by learned counsel for the applicant that the applicant is languishing in jail since 23.3.2019(about eight months) having no criminal history. He has been falsely implicated. FIR was lodged after thought with legal consultation. Even though statement of Rajesh Yadav and Pawan (so-called eye-witness) stated that deceased with others had consumed wine and chicken. Later on, dispute arose all of a sudden and the incident happened. The incident is of night. Nobody has seen the incident. According to postmortem report, deceased received three injuries(only one injury was found on the neck of the deceased). General allegations are made against two persons but it is not clear as to who was author of causing said injury. There was no intention or knowledge of death of the deceased. There is no independent witness or eye-witness account. In case applicant is released on bail, he will not misuse the liberty of bail and will cooperate in the trial.
</p> 
<p id="p_4">On the other hand, learned A.G.A. opposed the prayer for bail but could not dispute the aforesaid fact as argued by learned counsel for the applicant. He admitted that the applicant has no criminal history.
</p> 
<p id="p_5">Considering the submission of learned counsel for the parties, facts of the case, nature of allegation, period of custody, gravity of offence and without expressing any opinion on the merits of the case, the Court is of the view that the applicant has made out a case for bail. The bail application is allowed.
</p> 
<p id="p_6">Let applicant Amit Yadav @ Chinu involved in Case Crime No.137 of 2019, under <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_1">Section 323</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_2">504</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_3">506</a>, <a href="/doc/409589/" id="a_4">304</a> IPC, Police Station B.K.T., District Lucknow be released on bail on furnishing a personal bond and two sureties each in the like amount to the satisfaction of the court concerned subject to following conditions:
</p> 
<p id="p_7">1. The applicant will not tamper with the evidence during the trial. 
</p> 
<p id="p_8">2. The applicant will not pressurize/ intimidate the prosecution witness.
</p> 
<p id="p_9">3. The applicant will appear before the trial court on the date fixed, unless personal presence is exempted.
</p> 
<p id="p_10">4. The applicant shall not commit an offence similar to the offence of which he is accused, or suspected, of the commission of which he is suspected.
</p> 
<p id="p_11">5. The applicant shall not directly or indirectly make any inducement, threat or promise to any person acquainted with the facts of the case so as to dissuade him from disclosing such facts to the Court or to any police officer or tamper with the evidence.
</p> 
<p id="p_12">In case of breach of any of the above conditions, the court below shall be at liberty to cancel the bail.
</p> 
<p id="p_13">Order Date :- 14.11.2019
 
P.P.
</p>
