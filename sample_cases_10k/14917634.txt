BRIEF FACTS AND REASONS FOR DECISION.


1.</pre><p id="p_1"> Brief facts of this case are that on 30.10.09 at 10.30 PM at on the road, Gali
     No.24, Jafrabad, Delhi falling within the jurisdiction of PS Zafrabad, Delhi
     accused persons were committed an offence U/s 160 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_3">IPC</a> by scuffling with each
     other at public place and also violated the peace. Accused persons were arrested
     and released on bail and on completion of investigation challan was put in the
     court.
</p>



<p id="p_2">2.       After completion of investigation, chargesheet was filed by the IO in the
     court against the accused persons to which they pleaded their guilt. Heard. I am
     satisfied that accused persons have voluntarily confessed their guilty. In these
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                           2</span>

  circumstances, I am of the considered opinion that since accused persons have
  pleaded guilty and voluntarily accepted accusation that they were not only
  scuffling at the public place but violated the peace also, no purpose will be
  served by pursuing the trial against the accused persons.           In these
  circumstances, I convict the aforesaid accused persons for the offence under
  <a href="/doc/518086/" id="a_4">Section 160</a> IPC. Let they be heard on the point of sentence. Copy of judgment
  be given to the convict free of cost.
</p>



<p id="p_3">Announced in open court
on 15.12.09.
</p><p id="p_4">                                              (Sanjay Khanagwal)
                                              Metropolitan Magistrate (NE)-05
                                              Karkardooma courts, Delhi.

ORDER ON SENTENCE


Present:       APP for the State.
               Accsued/Convict in person.
</pre><p id="p_7">               I have heard the Ld. APP and convict on the point of sentence and carefully
perused the record.
</p><p id="p_8">               It has been submitted by the convict that lenient view be taken.
               On the other hand Ld. APP for State submits that convicts do not deserve
any leniency as accused are threat to society and public at large.
</p><p id="p_9">               Considering the submissions made by both the sides and keeping in view of
facts and circumstances of the case and that accsued persons have voluntarily confessed
their guilt. I am of the view that it will be in the interest of justice if accused are
sentenced to pay fine of Rs.100/-each (Rs. One Hundred Only) in default of payment of
fine to undergo S.I for one day. Fine paid. Bail bond and surety bond cancelled.
Documents if any be returned.
</p><p id="p_10">               Copy of order be given to the convicts free of cost. File be consigned to
record room.
</p>

<p id="p_11"> Announced in open court
on 15.12.09
                                                 (Sanjay Khanagwal)
