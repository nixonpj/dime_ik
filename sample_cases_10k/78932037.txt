                                            Date of Decision : 25.10.2018

Sunil Sharma and another
                                                               ...... Petitioners
                                 Versus

State of Haryana and others
                                                               ...... Respondents

CORAM : HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE RAJ SHEKHAR ATTRI

                        ***
Present : Mr. Hitesh Kumar Sammi, Advocate
          for the petitioners.
                        ***
RAJ SHEKHAR ATTRI, J. (Oral)
</pre><p id="p_1">          The petitioners have preferred the instant petition under

<a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C. for issuance of direction to respondent Nos.1 to 3 to

expedite the trial in case FIR No.69 dated 08.02.2015, registered under

<a href="/doc/180291/" id="a_2">Sections 287</a>, <a href="/doc/1402213/" id="a_3">337</a>, <a href="/doc/1721129/" id="a_4">338</a>, <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_5">34</a> IPC, at Police Station Kundli, District Sonepat

and conclude it as early as possible within a stipulated period.

</p><p id="p_2">             Notice of motion.
</p>
<p id="p_3">             Mr. Raj Kumar Makkad, DAG, Haryana, who is present in

Court, has accepted notice on behalf of respondents-State. Complete copy

of the paper book has been supplied to him.
</p>
<p id="p_4">             Service is complete.
</p>
<p id="p_5">             This is a case where trial has been kept pending and delay has

been caused unnecessarily. The record of the trial court transpires that the

witnesses did not turn up before the trial court.

</p><p id="p_6">             In view of above, the instant petition stands disposed of with a

direction to the concerned Investigating Officer to produce the entire

prosecution witnesses for examinatiion on the date fixed before the trial

court.   In failure to do so, then the Senior Superintendent of Police,
