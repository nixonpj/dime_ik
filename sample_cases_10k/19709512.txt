<div class="doc_title">An Application For Bail Under ... vs In Re: Ujjal Bapari @ Lala &amp; Anr on 23 May, 2018</div><p id="p_1"><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                          1</span>

  8    23.05.2018
AGM/   Court No.8
                                   CRM 3119 of 2018
 SK

                    In the matter of an application for bail under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of the
                    Code of Criminal Procedure filed on 21.05.2018 in connection
                    with NDPS Case No. 46/2017 arising out of Rishra Police Station
                    Case No. 111/2017 dated 29.08.2017 under <a href="/doc/330464/" id="a_2">Section 20(b)(ii)(c)</a> of
                    the NDPS Act.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">                                              And


                    In Re: Ujjal Bapari @ Lala &amp; Anr.             Petitioners.


                     Mr. Sudip Ghosh Chowdhury              ......For the Petitioners.


                     Mr. Sanjay Bardhan,
                     Mr. Pratick Bose                             ... For the State.



</p><p id="p_4">                           Learned advocate for the petitioners submit that the

                    petitioners are in custody for nine months in a proceeding under

                    the <a href="/doc/1727139/" id="a_3">N.D.P.S. Act</a>. He submits that after the framing of charges,

                    the trial has not commenced. He seeks bail.

</p><p id="p_5">                           Learned advocate for the State submits that the next date

                    fixed in the proceedings from June 14, 2018 for the purpose of

                    recording evidence.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                           Since commercial quantity is involved, we are not inclined

                    to grant bail. However we request the court below to expedite the

                    trial. The court below is requested not to grant any unnecessary

                    adjournment to the parties.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">               2</span><p id="p_7">


C.R.M. 3119 of 2018 is rejected.


</p><p id="p_8">                          (Debangsu Basak, J.)



                        (Rajarshi Bharadwaj, J.)
