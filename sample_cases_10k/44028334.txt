                     CHANDIGARH


                                        CRM-M-775-2018
                                        Date of decision: 27.07.2018

Nirmal Singh
                                                                  ...Petitioner

                                    Versus

State of Punjab
                                                                 ...Respondent

CORAM: HON'BLE MS. JUSTICE JAISHREE THAKUR

Present:     Mr. B.S. Sidhu, Advocate
             for the petitioner.

             Ms. Seena Mand, D.A.G., Punjab.

                   ****

JAISHREE THAKUR, J. (ORAL)
</pre>
<p id="p_1">             This is a petition that has been filed under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> Cr.P.C.

for grant of anticipatory bail to the petitioner in case FIR No. 23 dated

24.03.2015 under <a href="/doc/1279834/" id="a_2">Sections 376</a>, <a href="/doc/619940/" id="a_3">363</a>, <a href="/doc/796352/" id="a_4">366</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_5">506</a>, <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_6">34</a> IPC and Sections 3 &amp; 4

of POCSO       Act, registered at Police Station City Malout, District Sri

Muktsar sahib.
</p>
<p id="p_2">             Learned counsel for the petitioner would contend that the

petitioner has been summoned under <a href="/doc/435819/" id="a_7">Section 319</a> Cr.P.C. It is argued that

name of the petitioner was not reflected either in the statement under

<a href="/doc/447673/" id="a_8">Section 161</a> Cr.P.C., 164 <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_9">Cr.P.C</a>. or FIR itself as well as in the inquiry. It is

contended that pursuant to the order dated 11.01.2018, the petitioner herein

has put in appearance before the trial Court and has been admitted to bail by

order dated 11.06.2018 and that he is facing trial therein.




</p><p id="p_3">                                      1 of 2

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2"> CRM-M-775-2018                                                          -2-</span>


             In view of the above, the petition is allowed and interim order

dated 11.01.2018 is hereby made absolute subject to the condition that the

petitioner will not tamper with evidence or hamper the investigation; will

not leave India without permission of the Court and will comply with the

conditions contained in <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_10">Section 438(2)</a> Cr.P.C.
</p>
