<div class="doc_title">Pradip Hait vs The State Of West Bengal And Others on 21 November, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                           1

S/L. 98.
November 21, 2016.
MNS.

                                                W. P. No. 34389(w) of 2014
                                                         Pradip Hait
                                                            Vs.
                                            The State of West Bengal and others

  Mr. S. P. Lahiri

                     ...for the petitioner.

          In spite of service, nobody responded for the State-respondents either in the first</pre>
<p id="p_1">  round or in the second round.
</p><p id="p_2">          Heard the learned counsel for the petitioner and perused the record.
          The instant writ petition has been filed by the petitioner praying for direction upon the respondent nos. 2
  and 3 to approve the post-graduate scale in favour of the petitioner on and from April 28, 2005 to November 5,
  2007.
</p><p id="p_3">          Accordingly, without going into the merits of the instant writ petition, this Court directs
  the concerned respondents, particularly, the respondent nos. 2 and 3 to consider the case of
  the petitioner, if the same has not been considered as yet and if he is entitled for the same
  and if there is no legal impediment, within a period of three months from the date of
  furnishing certified copy of this order after verification of the relevant documents.
</p><p id="p_4">          With the aforesaid observations, the writ petition is disposed of.
          I make no order as to costs.
</p><p id="p_5">          The Registry is directed to supply photostat certified copy of this order to the applicant, if applied for, on
  urgent basis.
</p>

<p id="p_6">                                      (Rajiv Sharma, J.)
