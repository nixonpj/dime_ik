<p id="p_1"> IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR RAJASTHAN AT JAIPUR BENCH, JAIPUR


ORDER


S.B. Criminal Misc.5th Bail Application No.10550/2009
Smt. Parveen vs. State of Rajasthan


Dated :	08.01.2010


HON'BLE MR. MAHESH BHAGWATI,J.
</p>

<p id="p_2">Mr. R.S. Rathore, for the petitioner.
</p><p id="p_3">Mr. Amit Punia, Public Prosecutor for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_4">		This order governs the disposal of  fith bail application filed under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of Cr.P.C. by Mr. R.S. Rathore Advocate on behalf of the applicant Smt. Parveen pertaining to F.I.R. No. 291/2008 of Police Station Khanpur, District Jhalawar, in the offences under <a href="/doc/92983/" id="a_2">Sections 306</a>, <a href="/doc/68146/" id="a_3">509</a>, <a href="/doc/1279834/" id="a_4">376</a>, <a href="/doc/513074/" id="a_5">109</a> and <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_6">120-B</a> of IPC.
</p><p id="p_5">2.		Heard the learned counsel for the petitioner as also the learned Public Prosecutor for the State and perused the relevant material available on record.	
</p><p id="p_6">3.		Learned counsel for the petitioner has reiterated those very arguments which were advanced at the time of arguing the first bail petition and the same were taken into consideration by this Court.
</p><p id="p_7">4.		Learned Public Prosecutor has opposed the bail application. 
</p><p id="p_8">5.		Having reflected over the submissions made at the bar and scanned the relevant material available on record, it is noticed that the first bail petition was dismissed on 18.03.2009 by detailed order. I do not find any good ground to change my earlier view, hence, the fifth bail petition filed on behalf of the petitioner also stands dismissed for those very reasons which were enumerated in the first bail order.
</p>		
<p id="p_9">						(MAHESH BHAGWATI),J.
</p><p id="p_10">Mak/-
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">29</span>
