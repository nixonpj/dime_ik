<p id="p_1">                     Mr. Manabendra Thakur.
</p><p id="p_2">                               ...for the petitioner.
</p>



<p id="p_3">                      The Central Administrative Tribunal, by the impugned
              judgment and order, rejected an original application holding that
              the petitioner is not entitled to appointment on compassionate
              ground.
</p>

<p id="p_4">                      The father of the petitioner was an employee of the Calcutta
              Telephones. He died on August 6, 2002 leaving behind his widow,
              three sons and one married daughter.
</p>

<p id="p_5">                      The mother applied for compassionate appointment of his
              son.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                2</span>


<p id="p_6">        The respondent no.3 on August 29, 2003 informed the widow
that her prayer seeking employment on compassionate ground for
her son could not be recommended by the committee.
</p>

<p id="p_7">        Thereafter, the widow and one of the sons, namely, Vijay
Nath,    moved     this     application   for   a   direction   for   giving
compassionate appointment.
</p>

<p id="p_8">        The tribunal, in our view, rightly held that the prayers were
barred by limitation and there was no reason to condone the delay.
</p>

<p id="p_9">        The compassionate appointment is not a regular source of
induction in Government service. Compassionate appointment is
for the survival of the family. If the family could survive for more
than last ten years, there is no justification to offer an employment
on compassionate ground to this petitioner.
</p>

<p id="p_10">        The writ petition is, therefore, dismissed.
</p>

<p id="p_11">        We make no order as to costs.
</p>

<p id="p_12">                      (Subhro Kamal Mukherjee, J.)


                          (Murari Prasad Shrivastava, J.)
