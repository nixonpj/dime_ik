<div class="doc_title">(Double Ppo - Disposed Of) vs The State Of West Bengal &amp; Ors on 19 July, 2016</div><p id="p_1"><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">1</span>

    19.07.2016                                         WP 12764 (W) of 2016
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">    Court No. 03</span>
    Item No. SL-142
    snandy
                                                            Mirarani Das
    (DOUBLE PPO - DISPOSED OF)
                                                                 Vs.
</p><p id="p_2">                                                   The State of West Bengal &amp; Ors.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                 Mr. Subhajit Panja, Advocate
                                                               ...For the Petitioners
                                 Mr. Syed Mosihar Rahaman, Advocate
                                                                    ...For the State

                                        The affidavit of service filed in court today is kept
                                 on record.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                        The writ-petitioner claims interest on the delayed
                                 payment of retiral benefits and approached this court by
                                 filing this writ-petition.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                        The learned advocate for the writ petitioner has
                                 relied on an order passed by the Director of Pension,
                                 Provident Fund and Group Insurance, West Bengal,
                                 under Memo No. 48/L/1(3)/DPPG dated January 15,
                                 2015, which has allowed interest at the rate of nine (9)
                                 per centum per annum on the gratuity amount from the
                                 date when it became due and payable till the date of its
                                 actual payment.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                        By a circular bearing no. 1259-SE dated October
                                 21, 2013, the Deputy Secretary to the Government of
                                 West Bengal has requested all concerned to follow the
                                 guidelines of the directions contained in the judgment of
                                 a division bench of this court rendered in MAT 1118 of
                                 2013, MAT 1119 of 2013 and MAT 1120 of 2013 for
                                 payment of interest.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                                        The writ petitioner is similarly situated in so far as
                                 the delay in receiving the pensionary benefit is
                                 concerned, which is legitimately due to the writ petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                                        In such circumstances, the order passed by the
                                 Director     of     Pension,   Provident   Fund   and   Group
                                 Insurance, West Bengal, dated June 23, 2015, which is
                                 impugned in the present writ petition, cannot be
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3"> 2</span>


    sustained and the same is, therefore, set aside.
</p>
<p id="p_9">          The Director of Pension, Provident Fund and
    Group Insurance, West Bengal, and the concerned
    Treasury Officer are directed to pay interest at the rate of
    nine (9) per centum per annum for the delayed period of
    issuance of revised gratuity, that is, on and from May 19,
    2009, the date on which the Revision of Pay and
    Allowances Rules, 2009 comes into effect till the date of
    disbursal of the revised gratuity amount, within a period
    of twelve (12) weeks from the date of communication of
    an authenticated copy of this order.
</p>
<p id="p_10">          The writ-petition is disposed of. There will be no
    order as to costs.
</p>
<p id="p_11">          Photostat certified copy of this order, if applied for,
    will be made available to the applicant within a week from
    the date of putting in the requisites.
</p>

<p id="p_12">                                             (C.S. Karnan, J.)
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4"> 3</span>
