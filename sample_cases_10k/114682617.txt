<div class="doc_title">Manjas Minj vs The State Of Jharkhand on 20 November, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">         IN       THE     HIGH    COURT    OF   JHARKHAND                   AT       RANCHI
                            A.B. A. No. 3016 of 2014

         Manjas Minj                                      .....   ...     Petitioner
                                       Versus
         The State of Jharkhand                              ..... ...      Opposite Party
                                    --------
                  CORAM       :   HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE H. C. MISHRA
                                    ------
         For the Petitioner                  : Mr. Nilesh Kumar, Advocate
         For the State                       : A.P.P.
                                    --------

05/ 20.11.2014</pre><p id="p_1">          Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned A.P.P. for the
         State.
</p><p id="p_2">                        The petitioner is apprehending his arrest in connection with Senha
         P.S. Case No. 26 of 2014 corresponding to G.R. Case No. 116 of 2014, for the
         alleged offences under <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_1">Sections 341</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_2">323</a>, <a href="/doc/838469/" id="a_3">452</a> / <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_4">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code and
         Sections 3/4 of the Prevention of Witchcraft (Daain) Practices Act.
</p><p id="p_3">                        Though there is allegation against the petitioner and the other
         co-accused to have assaulted and harassed the mother and wife of the complainant
         on the pretext of practicing witchcraft, it is submitted by learned counsel for the
         petitioner that in the similar circumstance, the other co-accused has been granted
         anticipatory bail by order dated 13.11.2014 in A.B.A. No. 1692 of 2014. Learned
         counsel has accordingly, prayed for anticipatory bail.
</p><p id="p_4">                        In the facts of this case, since the other co-accused has already been
         granted anticipatory bail, I am inclined to grant anticipatory bail to the petitioner,
         Manjas Minj. Accordingly, it is directed that in the event of his surrender / arrest,
         the petitioner, named above, shall be released on bail, on furnishing bail bond of
         Rs. 10,000/- (ten thousand), with two sureties of the like amount each, to the
         satisfaction of learned Sub-Divisional Judicial Magistrate, Lohardaga, in
         connection with Senha P.S. Case No. 26 of 2014 corresponding to G.R. Case
         No. 116 of 2014, subject to conditions as laid down under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_5">Section 438(2)</a> of the
         Code of Criminal Procedure.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                                                          ( H. C. Mishra, J.)
  Amitesh/-
</p>
