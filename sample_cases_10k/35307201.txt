          IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT BOMBAY
                  CIVIL APPELLATE JURISDICTION

                        WRIT PETITION NO.9596 OF 2019

 Nanabhau Barku Ahire                     ... Petitioner
       Vs.
 Balu Laxman Satalkar &amp; Anr.        ... Respondents
                                .....
 Ms. Divya Parab I/b. Mr. R.N. Gite for the Petitioner.
 Mr. Sachin S. Padaye I/b. Mr. G.R. Agarwal for the Respondents.
                                .....

                               CORAM      : M. S. KARNIK, J.
</pre><pre id="pre_2">                               DATE       : 4th SEPTEMBER, 2019.

 P. C.:

</pre><p id="p_1"> 1.                Heard learned counsel for the petitioner.


</p><p id="p_2"> 2.                The petitioner is the original plaintiff. The petitioner

 challenges an order dated 10.7.2018 passed by the Trial Court

 below Exhibit 59 rejecting the said application. The application

 Exhibit 59 was filed by the plaintiff for issuance of summons for

 examining the witnesses which is mentioned in the application.

 The said application is rejected by the Trial Court on the ground

 that the plaintiff has filed a vague application. The Trial Court

 observed that no materials have been pleaded in the application as

 to on what point the plaintiff wants to examine the witnesses.</p><pre id="pre_3">


                                                                               1 of 2




<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">::: Uploaded on - 05/09/2019                    ::: Downloaded on - 06/09/2019 00:02:55 :::</span>
  Pradnya Bhogale                          2                     31 wp 9596-19.doc


</pre><p id="p_3"> 3.                Learned counsel for the respondents opposed the

 petition. He would submit that the application as filed by the

 petitioner is vague. According to him, the said application is made

 only with a view to protect the litigation. He pointed out that

 plaintiff has already been examined.
</p>
<p id="p_4">

</p><p id="p_5"> 4.                Be that as it may, I find that the Trial Court rejected

 the application on the ground that the same has been filed in a

 casual manner and no specific details are mentioned in the

 application. In the interest of justice I am inclined to observe that

 in the event the plaintiff files a proper application for examining

 the witnesses, the Trial Court to consider the same on its own

 merits and without being influenced by any of the observations

 made in the impugned order dated 10.7.2018 passed below

 Exhibit 59.
</p>
<p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7"> 5.                With these observations, the petition stands rejected.



</p><p id="p_8">                                                 ( M. S. KARNIK, J. )
