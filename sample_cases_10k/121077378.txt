                             The High Court Of Madhya Pradesh
                                       MCRC-32205-2019
                                         (ANSU KOL Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)


                     Jabalpur, Dated : 16-08-2019
                            Shri Surdeep Khampariya, learned counsel for the applicant.

                            Shri Mohd. Siddique, learned P. L. for respondent-State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">                            Heard.
</p><p id="p_2">                            This is first bail application filed by the applicant under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of
                    the Code of Criminal Procedure. The applicant is in custody since

                    14.07.2019, in connection with Crime No. 217/2019 registered at Police
                    Station- Dhanpuri, District- Shahdol (M.P.) for the offence punishable under
                    <a href="/doc/578299/" id="a_2">Sections 454</a>, <a href="/doc/556739/" id="a_3">382</a> and <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_4">506</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_5">34</a> of IPC.
</p><p id="p_3">                            The prosecution case in brief is that the applicant and other co-accused
                    persons theft the old iron box from UG Mines campus which is govt.
                    property, therefore, the police registered the case against the applicant and
                    other co-accused persons and arrested the present applicant and sent to
                    judicial custody.
</p><p id="p_4">                            Learned counsel for the applicant submits that the applicant has not

                    committed any offence, he is innocent and he has been falsely implicated in
                    this case. He is in custody since 14.07.2019. The applicant did not inflict any
                    injury to the complainant. There is no criminal antecedent against the
                    applicant. There is no likelihood of his absconding or tampering with the
                    prosecution evidence. The trial will take time for its final disposal. He is ready
                    to furnish bail as per the order, abiding with all conditions imposed
                    by the Court. On these grounds, learned counsel for the applicant prays for
                    grant of bail to the applicant.
</p><p id="p_5">                            Per-contra, learned P.L. opposes the bail application.
                            Considering the contention of the both the parties and looking to the
                    facts and circumstances of the case and the fact that the applicant did not
                    inflict any injury to the complainant and the applicant is in custody since
                    14.07.2019. There is no likelihood of his absconding or tampering with the

                    appropriate to release the applicant on bail, therefore without commenting on
                    merits of the case, application of the applicant under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_6">Section 439</a> of the
                    Cr.P.C. seems to be acceptable. Consequently, it is hereby allowed.
</p><p id="p_6">                            It is directed the the applicant- Ansu Kol be released on bail on his

                    furnishing a bail bond in the sum of Rs.50,000/- (Rupees fifty Thousand
                    Only) with one surety in the like amount to the satisfaction of the trial Court
                    for his appearance as and when directed. The applicant will attend each
                    hearing of his trial before the trial Court out of which this bail arises and shall
                    also comply with the provisions of Section 437 (3) Cr.P.C.
