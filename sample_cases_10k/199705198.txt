<div class="doc_title">Vijay Kewat And Anr vs The State Of Jharkhand on 20 September, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                 IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                          B. A. No. 6086 of 2017

       1. Vijay Kewat
       2. Kunal Kewat                                          ...... Petitioners
                                          Versus
       The State of Jharkhand                                   ...... Opposite Party
                                   --------
                 CORAM       :   THE HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE H. C. MISHRA
                                   ------
       For the Petitioners         :        Mr. Prem Pujari Roy, Advocate.
       For the State               :        A.P.P.
                                    ------

4/20.09.2017</pre><p id="p_1">          Heard learned counsel for the petitioners and learned counsel for
        the State.
</p><p id="p_2">                      The petitioners have been made accused in connection with
        Barhi P.S case No. 150 of 2017 corresponding to G.R No.1657 of 2017, for
        the offence under <a href="/doc/471092/" id="a_1">Sections 272</a>, <a href="/doc/285163/" id="a_2">273</a>, <a href="/doc/1368265/" id="a_3">278</a> / <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_4">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code and
        <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_5">Section 47A</a> of the Excise Act.
</p><p id="p_3">                      There is recovery of 2000 pouches of country-made liquor from
        the vehicle of the petitioners.
</p><p id="p_4">                      In the facts and circumstances of the case, I am inclined to
        enlarge the petitioners, Vijay Kewat and Kunal Kewat, on bail. Accordingly,
        the petitioners, named above, are directed to be released on bail, on furnishing
        bail bonds of Rs. 10,000/- (ten thousand) each with two sureties of the like
        amount each, to the satisfaction of Sri Mayank Maliyaz, learned Judicial
        Magistrate, Hazaribag, or his successor, in connection with Barhi P.S case
        No. 150 of 2017 corresponding to G.R No.1657 of 2017.
</p>

<p id="p_5">                                                                   ( H. C. Mishra, J.)
        DS-BS/
