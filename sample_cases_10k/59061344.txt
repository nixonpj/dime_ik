<div class="doc_title">Chandrabhan Singh vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 21 November, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">                       M.Cr.C. No.17350/2014
21.11.2014

         Shri G.S. Baghel, learned counsel for the applicant.

         Shri A. Singh, learned Panel Lawyer for respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">         Panel Lawyer submits that he is under receipt of the case
diary.
</p><p id="p_2">         Heard.
</p><p id="p_3">         On behalf of the applicant, this petition is preferred under
<a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of Cr.P.C. for grant of bail to him as he is in custody
since 25.10.2014 in connection with Crime No.319/2014 registered
at Police Station Barhi, District Katni for the offence under
<a href="/doc/1258372/" id="a_2">Sections 147</a>, <a href="/doc/763672/" id="a_3">148</a>, <a href="/doc/1517318/" id="a_4">436</a>, <a href="/doc/838469/" id="a_5">452</a>, <a href="/doc/222396/" id="a_6">427</a>/<a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_7">149</a> of IPC and <a href="/doc/162932376/" id="a_8">section 3</a>/<a href="/doc/161022203/" id="a_9">4</a> of
Protection from <a href="/doc/177673971/" id="a_10">Damage of Public Property Act</a>.
</p><p id="p_4">         Applicant's counsel after taking me through the rejection
order argued that the alleged incident is relating to some public
agitation in which by fabricating the false story, the applicant has
been falsely implicated alongwith some other persons of the
village and the locality while he has not committed any alleged
offence. In continuation, he said that the charge-sheet has already
been filed in the matter and the trial may take years together for
adjudication and prayed for extending the benefit of bail by
allowing this petition.
</p><p id="p_5">         On the other hand with the assistance of the case diary, the
Panel Lawyer opposes aforesaid prayer saying that looking to the
nature of the offence and the public property which was damaged
by so many accused including the present applicant for which
sufficient evidence is available in the case diary, the applicant
 does not deserve for extending the benefit of bail and prayed for
dismissal of the petition.
</p><p id="p_6">      Having heard the counsel, keeping in view the arguments
advanced, taking into consideration the nature of the evidence
collected by the prosecution agency and filed with the charge-
sheet and especially in respect of the present applicant, without
expressing any opinion on merits of the matter, this petition is
allowed.
</p><p id="p_7">      It is directed that on furnishing a personal bond of
Rs.30,000/- (Rs.Thirty thousand) along with one surety of the like
amount     to   the   satisfaction   of   the   trial   Court,   applicant
Chandrabhan Singh shall be released on bail with a direction to
appear on each and every date of hearing before the trial Court.
</p><p id="p_8">      His single non-appearance before the trial Court shall lead
to automatic dismissal of this bail order.
</p><p id="p_9">      C.C. as per rules.
</p>

<p id="p_10">                                          (U. C. Maheshwari)
                                                 Judge
