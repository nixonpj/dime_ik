<div class="doc_title">Ganesh Kumar Yadav vs The State Of Jharkhand on 10 May, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">          IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                        B. A. No.2716 of 2017
                                  -----
          Ganesh Kumar Yadav.                .......... Petitioner.
                               -Versus-
          The State of Jharkhand.            .......... Opposite Party.
                                 ------
          CORAM : HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE RAJESH SHANKAR
                                 ------
          For the Petitioner :   Mr. Vishal Kumar Trivedi, Advocate
          For the State      :   A.P.P.
          For the Informant:     Mr. Tarun Kumar, Advocate
                                 ------
03/10.05.2017</pre><p id="p_1">: Heard learned counsel for the parties.

</p><p id="p_2">                 The petitioner is an accused in a case registered under

           <a href="/doc/1559723/" id="a_1">Sections 366A</a> and <a href="/doc/1279834/" id="a_2">376</a> of the Indian Penal Code and Section

           4/8 of POCSO Act.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                 Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that the

           petitioner has been falsely implicated in the present case and

           has not committed any offence as alleged in the first

           information report. It is alleged that the petitioner kidnapped

           the victim girl, namely, Rubi Kumari and, thereafter, she was

           subjected to rape by the petitioner, co-accused Amit Kumar

           Tanti and Subhash Kumar. It is further submitted that, in fact,

           the victim girl has performed marriage with co-accused Amit

           Kumar Tanti. During the investigation, some of the witnesses

           have also stated that she was found moving freely in Deoghar

           along with the petitioner and co-accused-Amit Kumar Tanti. In

           the medical examination of the victim girl, the doctor has not

           found any sign of rape. The petitioner is in judicial custody

           since 20th December, 2016 and, therefore, he may be given

           the privilege of regular bail.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                 Learned APP and learned counsel for the informant,

           while opposing the petitioner's prayer for bail, submit that the

           age of the girl is about 17 years. Her statement under <a href="/doc/497457/" id="a_3">Section

           164</a> Cr.P.C. was also recorded in which she has clearly stated
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                  -2-</span>

          that she was subjected to rape by the petitioner, co-accused-

          Amit Kumar Tanti and Subhash Kumar. In that view of the

          matter, the petitioner may not be given the privilege of

          regular bail.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                Considering the facts and circumstances of the case, I

          am not inclined to enlarge the petitioner, above named, on

          bail, in connection with Jamua P.S. Case No.252 of 2016,

          corresponding to G.R. no.3001 of 2016.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                Accordingly, the petitioner's prayer for bail stands

          rejected.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><p id="p_8">                                            (Rajesh Shankar, J.)
Sanjay/
 </p>
