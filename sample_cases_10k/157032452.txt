<pre id="pre_1">         IN THE HIGH COURT OF KARNATAKA
            CIRCUIT BENCH AT DHARWAD

     DATED THIS THE 08TH DAY OF MARCH 2012

                         BEFORE

         THE HON'BLE MR.JUSTICE B.S.PATIL

            W.P.No. 62938/2011 (GM-CPC)

BETWEEN:

1.     Srnt. Yarnanawwa,
       W/o Beerappa Pujari,
       Age: 75 years, 0cc: Household,
       R/o Mamadapur village,
       Tq. Gokak, Dist: Belgaum.

2.     Shri Shiddabeerappa @ Siddappa
       Beerappa Pujari,
       Age: 54 years, 0cc: Agriculture,
       R/o Mamadapur village,
       Tq. Gokak, Dist: Belgaum.
                                             Petitioners
(By Sri Vitthal. S. Teli, Adv.)

AND:

1.     Jayashree Laxman Hoolikatti,
       Age: 24 years, 0cc: Household,
       R/o Panehanaikanhatti,
       Tq. Gokak, Dist: Belgaum.

2.     Nagappa Shiddabeerappa Balobal     @ Pujari,
       Age: 24 years, 0cc: Agriculture,
       R/o Mamadapur village,
       Tq. Gokak, Dist: Belgaum.

3.     Laxmibai, W/o Shiddabeerappa Balobal
       @ Pujari, Age: 60 years, 0cc: Agriculture,
                             2

     R/o Mamadapur village,
     Tq. Gokak, Dist: Belgaum.

4.   Tippawwa, W/o Halappa Girdi,
     Age: 51 years, 0cc: Agriculture,
     R/o Mamadapur village,
     Tq. Gokak, Dist: Belgaum.
                                          Respondents

      This writ petition is filed under Articles 226 &amp;
227 of the Constitution of India, praying to quash the
final decree dated 02.08.2008 passed in 0.S.No.244/
2006 by the Prl. Civil Judge (Sr.Dn.), Gokak, on the
basis of the compromise recorded by the Lok Adalat,
Gokak, produced at Annexure-C.

      This writ petition coming on for preliminary
hearing this day, the Court made the following:-

                          ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">     Learned counsel for the petitioners        is not

present.     Also   the    learned   counsel   for   the

respondents is not present.       It is stated that the

advocates are on strike, protesting against some

incident that has taken place in the recent past and

hence they are abstaining from Court proceedings.

This is consecutively the fourth day the advocates are

abstaining from Court work.       Court time cannot be

wasted by merely adjourning the cases. In these
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                       3</span>


circumstances, I have carefully perused the materials

on record.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">2.     In   this   writ   petition,   petitioners   are challenging

the compromise decree dated 02.08.2008 in O.S.No.

244/2006 passed by the Fri. Civil Judge (Sr.Dn.),

Gokak, based on the compromise recorded by the Lok

Adalath, Gokak.
</p>
<p id="p_4">
</p><p id="p_5">3.    This writ petition is filed in the year 2011, after

a lapse of nearly three years from the date of passing

of the compromise decree.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><p id="p_7">4.     Petitioners have alleged in para-7 of the writ

petition that respondents 1 to 3 herein induced/

forced them to enter into compromise suppressing

the fact regarding pendency of O.S.No.273/2001 on

the file of the Fri. Civil Judge (Jr.Dn.), Gokak.


</p><p id="p_8">5.    Such grievance made by the petitioners cannot

be gone into in exercise of writ jurisdiction. Whether

there was any inducement or force exerted by the

respondents herein on the petitioners to enter into

 jj/
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                              4</span>


compromise and whether there was any suppression

of material facts, are the matters that cannot be gone

into in writ jurisdiction.       The disputed question of

fact raised requires proof by leading evidence. Hence,

writ petition cannot be entertained.


</p><p id="p_9">6.    In view of the aforementioned facts, writ petition

is dismissed reserving liberty to the petitioners to

avail any other remedy available in law.
