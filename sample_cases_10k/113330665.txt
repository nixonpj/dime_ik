<div class="doc_title">S.Sivakumar vs C.Uma on 4 August, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">        

 

IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT MADRAS

DATED :04.08.2015

CORAM

	  
THE HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE D.HARIPARANTHAMAN
				
C.R.P.(PD)No.2980 of 2015 &amp;
M.P.No.1 of 2015 

S.Sivakumar	                                      	                 ... Petitioner 
  

v.



1.C.Uma
2.Minor Rasika S.Kumar
3.Minor S. Raja Vivek

   Minor respondents 2 and 3 are
   represented by their mother 1st 
   respondent C.Uma		  	                                 ... Respondents 

 
 

	 Civil Revision Petition filed under <a href="/doc/1331149/" id="a_1">Article 227</a> of the Constitution of India, to set aside the fair and decreetal order dated 06.03.2015 made in I.A.No.45 of 2015  in O.S.No.7 of 2013  on the file of Subordinate Judge, Perundurai. 

			For Petitioner       :  Mr.M.V.Seshachari


 
O R D E R  
</pre>
<p id="p_1">	The first respondent is the wife of the revision petitioner.  The 1st  respondent filed the Original Petition in H.M.O.P.No.293 of 2012  on the file of Subordinate Court, Poonamallee, for divorce.
</p>
<p id="p_2">	2.  The 1st  respondent has two minor children born through the revision petitioner, who are respondents 2 and 3 in this revision petition. 
</p>
<p id="p_3">	3.  During the pendency of the  Original Petition in H.M.O.P.No.293 of 2012, the respondents 1 to 3, filed an application in I.A.No.407 of 2013   in H.M.O.P.No.293 of 2012, seeking interim maintenance.
</p>
<p id="p_4">	4.  The Trial court, passed an order dated 23.09.2014 in I.A.No.407 o 2013 in H.M.O.P.No.293 of 2012  granting  a sum of Rs.20,000/- (Rupees twenty thousand only) as interim maintenance. 
</p>
<p id="p_5">	5.  It is not in dispute that the revision petitioner's monthly salary is Rs.53,920/-(Rupees fifty three thousand nine hundred and twenty only).
</p>
<p id="p_6">	6.  Since the petitioner's monthly salary is Rs.53,920/-, I am of the view that the amount of Rs.20,000/-  for the wife and two minor children is not excessive. 
</p>
<p id="p_7">	7.  Hence, I am not inclined to interfere with the order passed by the Trial Court.  
</p>	
<p id="p_8">	8.   In these circumstances,   the Civil Revision  Petition  fails and the same is dismissed.  No costs.  Consequently, connected miscellaneous petition is closed.  
</p>
