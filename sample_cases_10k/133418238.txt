<div class="doc_title">Kanuji Sambhuji Thakor vs State Of Gujarat &amp; 2 on 3 March, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">         C/SCA/3115/2015                                        ORDER




         IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

            SPECIAL CIVIL APPLICATION NO. 3115 of 2015

================================================================
                 KANUJI SAMBHUJI THAKOR....Petitioner(s)
                               Versus
                 STATE OF GUJARAT &amp; 2....Respondent(s)
================================================================
Appearance:
MR. YOGENDRA THAKORE, ADVOCATE for the Petitioner(s) No. 1
MS. AMITA SHAH AGP for the Respondent(s) No. 1
================================================================

        CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE K.M.THAKER

                                Date : 03/03/2015


                                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">      Heard Mr. Thakore, learned advocate for the

petitioner, and Ms. Amita Shah, learned AGP for

the respondent State, who has appeared on advance

service of copy of the petition.
</p>

<p id="p_2">1.1   In     view          of   the     facts       of   the     case      and

submissions by learned counsel for the contesting

parties.
</p>
<p id="p_3">      Rule.
</p>
<p id="p_4">      Ms.     Amita         Shah,     learned        AGP,      has      waived

service of Rule for the respondent State.</p><pre id="pre_2">



                                      Page 1
             C/SCA/3115/2015                                             ORDER




</pre><p id="p_5">1.2 With consent of learned AGP, the petition is

taken up for hearing and appropriate order today.
</p>

<p id="p_6">2.     In      present           petition,              the    petitioner         has

prayed, inter alia, that:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">       8(B) That this Honourable Court may be pleased to
       issue writ of mandamus or any other appropriate
       writ,        order        or     direction         by     directing       the
       respondents to give the benefits of the Government
       Resolution             dated     17.10.1988        and     pay     all    the
       consequential            benefits          including     salary,    arrears
       and      regularize            the     service     of    the     petitioner
       pursuance to the aforesaid resolution along with
       interest of 18%.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_2">       (C)     Pending         hearing        and     final    disposal    of    the
       present        petition         this       Honourable     Court     may    be
       pleased        to      direct        the   respondents     to     forthwith
       start making payment of adhoc salary of permanent
       employee to the petitioner."
</blockquote>

<p id="p_7">3. Learned advocate for the petitioner submitted

that    the        petitioner               has       joined    the     respondent

department               as       'Driver'              on      28.9.2004          at

Gandhinagar             Vanya          Jiv        Talim       Vibhag.     He     also

claimed that the petitioner has been working with

the respondent on daily wage basis continuously

and uninterruptedly since 2004.</p><pre id="pre_3">


                                             Page 2
          C/SCA/3115/2015                                    ORDER




</pre><p id="p_8">3.1 Learned advocate for the petitioner relied on

the judgment in the case of <a href="/doc/161584890/" id="a_1">State of Gujarat and

&amp; Ors. v. PWD Employees Union</a> [(2013) 8 Scale

579]. He also submitted that the said decision

was carried before Hon'ble Apex Court and the

said judgment is upheld and Hon'ble Apex Court

has    directed            the   State       Government      to     grant

benefits of the scheme as contained in GR dated

17.10.1988 to all daily wage workers of Forest

and Environment Department who have been working

for more than 5 years.
</p>

<p id="p_9">3.2 Learned advocate for the petitioner submitted

that   by     present        petition,        the   petitioner       also

claims      the      benefits      under      the    said    GR     dated

17.10.1988.
</p>

<p id="p_10">3.3    Learned         advocate      for     the    petitioner       also

submitted that having regard to the judgment in

case       of         PWD        Employees          Union     (supra),

subsequently, in various proceedings orders have

been passed and the benefits under the GR dated



                                    Page 3
             C/SCA/3115/2015                              ORDER



17.10.1988 are extended to the employees of the

said        department        on   their     completing       minimum

service as provided for under the said GR dated

17.10.1988. He also submitted that even Forest

Department is covered under said G.R.
</p>

<p id="p_11">3.4 Learned advocate for the petitioner relied on

the decision dated 16.12.2014 in Special Civil

Application             No.17938   of    2014,   which   is      passed

after taking into account the said decision, and

prayed that similar order may be passed in this

case     also.          The   decision      dated   16.12.2014       in

Special Civil Application No.17938 of 2014 reads

thus:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_3">       1.  Rule. Learned A.G.P. Mr.Swapneshwar Goutam
       waives   service   of  Rule   on   behalf  of   the
       respondents. On the facts and in the circumstances
       of the case and with the consent of learned counsel
       for the respective parties, the petition is being
       heard and decided finally.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_4">       2. By preferring this petition under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_2">Article 226</a> of the
       Constitution of India, the petitioner No.1 Union, on
       behalf of petitioner Nos.2 and 3, has, inter alia,
       prayed to grant benefits flowing from the Government
       Resolution dated 17.10.1988 to the petitioner Nos.2 and
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_5">       3.</blockquote>

<p id="p_12">       3. It is submitted that petitioner Nos.2 and 3 were
       working in the Forest Department of the State of Gujarat
       since the past several years. The individual details of
       the services rendered by the petitioner Nos.2 and 3 are
       given in the document at Annexure-A (Colly.) at running


                                   Page 4
   C/SCA/3115/2015                                   ORDER



page No.30 to the petition. They have put in more than 5
years of service in the Forest Department as daily
wagers and have rendered continuous and uninterrupted
service.
</p>
<p id="p_13">4. The petitioner No.1, on behalf of petitioner Nos.2
and 3, assert that similarly situated daily wagers have
been granted the benefits of the Government Resolution
dated 17.10.1988. In State of Gujarat and others V/s.
PWD Employees Union, reported in 2013(8) SCALE 579, the
Supreme Court has directed that the benefits of
Government Resolution dated 17.10.1988 be conferred upon
the daily wagers working in the Forest and Environment
Department, who have been working as such for more than
five years. It is the case of petitioner Nos.2 and 3
that they are squarely covered by the abovementioned
judgment of the Supreme Court, therefore, the prayers
made in the petition be granted.
</p>
<p id="p_14">5. Learned advocate Ms.Vidhi Bhatt for petitioner Nos.2
and 3 submitted that even though the Supreme Court has
now ruled in favour of similarly situated persons, the
State Government has not extended the benefits of the
Government Resolution dated 17.10.1988 to the petitioner
Nos.2 and 3 so far. The review petition filed by the
State Government in the Supreme Court against the
abovementioned judgment has now been dismissed by an
order dated 29.01.2014 passed in Review Petition (C)
Nos.2826 and 2827 of 2013 in Civil Appeal Nos.5321-5322
of 2013.
</p>
<p id="p_15">6. Learned A.G.P. Mr.Swapneshwar Goutam submits that in
view of the judgment of the Supreme Court, the
respondents may be directed to examine the cases of
petitioner Nos.2 and 3 individually so as to ascertain
their entitlement to the benefits flowing from the
Government Resolution dated 17.10.1988.
</p>
<p id="p_16">7. This Court has heard learned counsel appearing for
the respective parties and considered the material on
record. The petition is squarely covered by the judgment
of the Supreme Court in State of Gujarat and others Vs.
PWD Employees Union (supra). The relevant paragraphs of
the judgment are quoted hereinbelow:
</p>
<p id="p_17">25. As per scheme contained in Resolution dated 17th
October, 1988 all the daily wage workers were not
entitled for regularization or permanency in the
services. As per the said Resolution the daily wagers
are entitled to the following benefits:</p><pre id="pre_4">


(i)   They    are   entitled   to   daily   wages   as   per   the



                           Page 5
   C/SCA/3115/2015                           ORDER



</pre><p id="p_18">prevailing Daily Wages. If there is presence of more
than 240 days in first year, daily wagers are eligible
for paid Sunday, medical allowance and national festival
holidays.
</p>
<p id="p_19">(ii) Daily wagers and semi skilled workers who has
service of more than five years and less than 10 years
are entitled for fixed monthly salary along with
dearness allowance as per prevailing standard, for his
working days. Such daily wagers will get two optional
leave in addition to 14 misc. leave, Sunday leave and
national festival holidays. Such daily wagers will also
be eligible for getting medical allowance and deduction
of provident fund.
</p>

<p id="p_20">(iii) Daily wagers and semi skilled workers who has
service of more than ten years but less than 15 years
are entitled to get minimum pay scale at par with
skilled worker along with dearness allowance as per
prevailing standard, for his working days. Moreover,
such daily wagers will get two optional leave in
addition to 14 misc. leave, Sunday leave and national
festival holidays. He/she will be eligible for getting
medical allowance and deduction of provident fund.
</p>
<p id="p_21">(iv) Daily wagers and semi skilled workers who has
service of more than 15 years will be considered as
permanent worker and such semi skilled workers will get
current pay scale of skilled worker along with dearness
allowance,   local  city   allowance  and   house  rent
allowance. They will get benefit as per the prevailing
rules of gratuity, retired salary, general provident
fund. Moreover, they will get two optional leave in
addition to 14 misc. leave, 30 days earned leave, 20
days half pay leave, Sunday leave and national festival
holidays. The daily wage workers and semi skilled who
have completed more than 15 years of their service will
get one increment, two increments for 20 years service
and three increments for 25 years in the current pay
scale of skilled workers and their salary will be fixed
accordingly.
</p>
<p id="p_22">26. Considering, the facts and circumstances of the
case, the finding of Gujarat High Court dated 29th
October, 2010 in SCA No.8647/2008 and connected matters
and the fact that the said judgment is binding between
the parties, we are of the view that the appellants
should be directed to grant the benefit of the scheme as
contained in the Resolution dated 17th October, 1988 to
all the daily wage workers of the Forest and Environment
Department working for more than five years, providing
them the benefits as per our finding at Paragraph 25
above. The appellants are directed accordingly. The


                       Page 6
       C/SCA/3115/2015                           ORDER



    judgment and order passed by the learned Single Judge
    dated 29th October, 2010 as affirmed by the Division
    Bench by its order dated 28th February, 2012 stands
    modified to the extent above. The benefit should be
    granted to the eligible daily wage workers of the Forest
    and Environment Department working for more than five
    years including those who are performing work other than
    building maintenance and repairing but they will be
    entitled for the consequential benefit w.e.f. 29th
    October, 2010 or subsequent date from which they are so
    eligible   within   four    months  from   the date   of
    receipt/production of the copy of this order. The
    appeals stand disposed of with the aforesaid observation
    and   directions   to    the   appellant-State and   its
    authorities. There shall be no separate orders as to
    costs.
</p>
<p id="p_23">    8. The Review Petitions filed by the State Government
    against this judgment have now been dismissed by the
    Supreme Court vide order dated 29.01.2014, passed in
    Review Petition (C) Nos.2826 and 2827 of 2013 in Civil
    Appeal Nos.5321-5322 of 2013. In the view of this, there
    remains no impediment at all on the part of the State
    Government in extending the benefits of the Government
    Resolution dated 17.10.1988 to the petitioner Nos.2 and
    3 subject to fulfilling the requisite conditions.
</p>
<p id="p_24">    9. In view of the above, the following order is passed:
</p>
<p id="p_25">    The respondents shall examine the cases of the
    petitioner Nos.2 and 3 individually and, if found
    eligible, shall extend the benefits of the Government
    Resolution dated 17.10.1988 to them as per the
    directions of the Supreme Court. The needful be done
    within a period of four months from the date of receipt
    of copy of this order.
</p>
<p id="p_26">    10. The petition is allowed to the above extent. Rule is
    made absolute accordingly. Direct service is permitted.
</p>

<p id="p_27">4. Ms. Amita Shah, learned AGP for the respondent

State, did not dispute the submission by learned

advocate for the petitioner.
</p>

<p id="p_28">5. Having regard to the above mentioned facts,

more particularly the fact that in Special Civil

                           Page 7
          C/SCA/3115/2015                                         ORDER



Application No.17938 of 2014 and Special Civil

Application No.1504 of 2014 certain orders have

been passed by the Court and present petitioner

prays for similar order and while learned AGP for

the respondent has not opposed the said request,

therefore,           it      appears          appropriate         to     pass

following order:-
</p>

<p id="p_29">5.1    The    competent         authority           of    the    respondent

shall    take       up the      case          of   the    petitioner     and

consider and examine the same in light of the

terms and conditions of the GR dated 17.10.1988

and if the petitioner is found to be eligible for

the benefits flowing from the said GR in light of

the     applicable           terms     and         if    the     petitioner

fulfills       the         requirement         prescribed        under    the

said     GR      dated        17.10.1988,               then,    the     said

authority       will        extend    applicable           and    available

benefits to the petitioner as per the direction

by Hon'ble Apex Court.
</p>

<p id="p_30">5.2 The respondent will complete the procedure as

expeditiously as possible and preferably within

                                     Page 8
             C/SCA/3115/2015                        ORDER



period of four months from the date of receipt of

certified copy of this order.
</p>

<p id="p_31">          With    aforesaid   observations   and   direction,

present petition stands disposed of. Rule is made

absolute to the aforesaid extent.
</p>
<p id="p_32">          Direct service is permitted.
</p>


<p id="p_33">                                               (K.M.THAKER, J.)
Suresh*




                                Page 9
