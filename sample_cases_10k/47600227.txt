<div class="doc_title">Sh. Surender Pal Singh vs Shri Pausha Lal on 9 January, 2012</div><div class="doc_author">Author: Sh. Gagandeep Jindal</div>

<pre id="pre_1">         IN THE COURT OF SH. GAGANDEEP JINDAL, CIVIL JUDGE­05 
                                      CENTRAL, DELHI 
                                           Suit No. 224/2011


IN THE MATTER OF:­ 


Sh. Surender Pal Singh 
S/o Late Sh. Ch. Harpal Singh 
R/o 7055, Gali  Tanki Wali
Pahari Dhiraj, Delhi­110006.                                                  ...........Plaintiff


                                                 VERSUS

Shri Pausha Lal 
son of Shri Panchu
11137, Dhobi Katra,
Idgah Road, Motia Khan, Delhi­110055          ...........Defendant

Date of Institution                                        : 26.02.2007
Date of reserved for judgment                              : 20.12.2011
Date of decision                                           : 09.01.2012


         SUIT FOR POSSESSION AND RECOVERY OF RS. 3600/­ AS
              DAMAGES/MESNE PROFIT WITH FUTURE INTEREST 


JUDGMENT:­( Ex­parte)

1.</pre><p id="p_1"> This  is a suit  for possession  of the land with regard  to  property

    bearing No. 11137, Dhobi Katra, Idgah Road, Motia Khan, Delhi

    ( hereinafter referred as suit property) and more specifically shown

    in red colour in the site plan and for mesne profits/damages at the

Suit No.   224/2011                                                                          Page no.    1   of  6
    rate of Rs. 100/­ per month for the last immediately three years i.e.

   from   June,   2004  to   May  ,   2007   and  future   mesne   profits   at   the

   same rate with pendente­lite and future interest, till its realization. 

</p><p id="p_2">2.  The brief facts of the case are as follows:­  That the ancestors of

   the plaintiff namely Ch. Harpal Singh and before  him Ch. Nahar

   Singh was the original owner of land measuring 30593 Sq. Yds.

   comprising   and forming   part   of  land  of  Khasra   No.  169/5/3,  min

   Mauza   Qasam   Sharif,   Motia     Khan   Tehsil   and   District   ,   Delhi

   ­110055.     Ch.   Nahar   Singh   died   in   the   year   1929   and   was

   succeeded by his widow Smt. Janki Devi, who also died in the year

   1929. After the death of Smt. Janki Devi, Sh. Kehar Singh and Ch.

   Umrao Singh became the owner of the land in equal shares being

   the nearest reversioner of Sh. Nahar Singh, a part of which was

   acquired  by the government for public purpose and the remaining

   30593   Sq.   Yds.   was   given   on   rent   to   different   person   by   the

   erstwhile owners; that in about 1956. DDA and Custodian Evacuee

   property laid claim to the said land on which Ch. Kehar Singh and

   Ch. Umaro Singh, one of the then joint owners Ch. Kehar Singh

   filed a suit having No. 280/1967 for declaration of his ownership

   against Union of India and DDA, which was finally decreed by the

   Hon'ble   High Court.   It is further submitted that the defendant in

Suit No.   224/2011                                                                          Page no.    2   of  6
    the said suit tried their luck  in regular first appeal having No. ( OS)

   60/69 which was also dismissed by the Hon'ble High Court .  It is

   further submitted that DDA approached Supreme Court by way of

   SLP No. 717/85 which was also dismissed . It is further submitted

   that by virtue of the aforesaid judgment, the father of the plaintiff

   become   paramount   title   holder   of   the   said   remaining   land.   It   is

   further   submitted   that   as   Ch.   Kehar   Singh   died   on   16­12­1962

   during   the   court   of   suit   proceedings   and   Ch.   Harpal   Singh   was

   substituted in his place as his legal representative, thus the decree

   was   in   the   name   of   Ch.   Harpal   Singh   declaring   his   ownership

   jointly with Ch. Umrao Singh in equal share. It is further submitted

   that after the death of Ch. Umrao Singh on 04­03­1975, his share

   was inherited under his Will by his maternal grand daughter /Smt.

   Surya   Kumari.   It   is   further   submitted   that   on   oral   and   mutual

   partition   dt.   26­06­1977   between   the   co­owners,   land   in

   possession   of   the   defendant   fallen   to   the   share   of   Ch.   Harpal

   Singh. It is further submitted that Ch. Harpal Singh died on 01­06­

   2004 leaving behind the plaintiff as on of his legal heirs. It is further

   submitted that consequently, the plaintiff has become co­owner of

   the   suit   property   ,   on   which   the   defendant   raised   construction

   without the consent of the plaintiff or his predecessor. It is further

Suit No.   224/2011                                                                          Page no.    3   of  6
    submitted that the suit property is in unauthorized possession of

   the defendant, for 8 years which is about 10 Sq. yds. It is further

   submitted that the plaintiff as co­owner requested the defendant  to

   surrender the possession of the suit property but the defendant did

   not agree. It is further submitted that the plaintiff sent a notice dt.

   23­04­2006   through   registered   AD   as   well   as   UPC   to   the

   defendant. But the defendant despite receipt of the notice did not

   comply  with the  same,  gave  a cause  of action  to the plaintiff  to

   institute the present suit. 
</p>
<p id="p_3">3. Defendant was duly served   but none has appeared on behalf of

   the defendant.  Hence, he  was proceeded ex­parte vide order dt.

   10­05­2007.
</p>
<p id="p_4">4. To   prove   his   case   the   plaintiff   has   examined   himself   as   PW1.

   PW1 has filed his evidence by way of affidavit which is Ex. PW1/A

   and   reiterated   the   facts   mentioned   in   the   plaint   .   PW1   has   put

   reliance on the following document:­

</p><p id="p_5">   i)  Certified copy of the judgment of Hon'ble High Court in the suit

   bearing No. 28020/67 which is Ex. PW1/1. 
</p>
<p id="p_6">   ii) Copy of order dt. 07­08­1984 passed by the Hon'ble High Court

   of Delhi in appeal is Ex. PW1/2

</p><p id="p_7">   iii)   Certified   copy   of   the   order   dt.   29­10­1990   passed   by   the

Suit No.   224/2011                                                                          Page no.    4   of  6
    Hon'ble Supreme Court is Ex. PW1/3

</p><p id="p_8">   v)  Site   plan   of  the  entire  land  is  Ex.  PW1/4  bearing   Khasra   no.

   169/5/3

</p><p id="p_9">   v) Site  plan  in  which  suit  property  is shown  in red colour  is Ex.

   PW1/5 

</p><p id="p_10">   v) Copy of legal notice dt. 23­05­2006 is Ex. PW1/.6 postal receipt

   and PPC receipt &amp; AD Card for sending the same is Ex. PW1/7 to

   PW1/9.
</p>
<p id="p_11">   5.  I have heard the counsel for the plaintiff and have gone through

   the record carefully.
</p>
<p id="p_12">   6.  There   is   no  reason   to   disbelieve   the   deposition   made   by   the

   witness   PW1   as   his   testimony   is   remained   uncontested   and

   unrebutted . 
</p>
<p id="p_13">   7. The plaintiff has duly proved his case from testimony of PW1

   and documents Ex. PW1/1 to Ex. PW1/9. Therefore, the defendant

   is directed to hand over the peaceful and vacant possession of the

   property bearing No. 11137, Dhobi Katra, Idgah Road, Motia Khan,

   Delhi as shown in red colour in the site plan.
</p>
<p id="p_14">                 The plaintiff has claimed the mesne profit /damage from

   Jan.   2004   to   December   2006   @   Rs.   100/­   per   month.   But   the

   present suit has been filed only on 27­02­2007. The plaintiff is not

Suit No.   224/2011                                                                          Page no.    5   of  6
    entitled for damages for the month January 2004 as the same is

   barred   by   limitation   .   Therefore,   the   plaintiff   is   entitled   to   the

   damage from Feb 2004 to Feb. 2007 @  Rs. 100 per month.  The

   plaintiff is also entitled to future damages @ Rs. 100/­ per month

   till actual realization of the possession of the suit property . Cost of

   the suit is also awarded to the plaintiff.  
</p>
<p id="p_15">   Decree   sheet   be   prepared   accordingly.     File   be   consigned   to

   Record Room after due compliance.  </p><pre id="pre_2">



Announced &amp; signed in the                                                      (Gagandeep Jindal)
open court  on 09.01.2012                                 Civil Judge/Central­05
                                                                                          Delhi




Suit No.   224/2011                                                                          Page no.    6   of  6
