<p id="p_1">Harsimran Singh Sethi, J.(Oral)

              The present writ petition has been filed by the petitioner

praying that the service rendered by him with respondent No. 2 should be

taken into consideration while calculating the pensionary benefits for which

he is entitled for.
</p>
<p id="p_2">              In support of his claim, the petitioner has placed reliance upon

a judgment of this Court passed in CWP No. 22868 of 2014, decided on

26.02.2016 (Annexure P-7).
</p>
<p id="p_3">               The petitioner is seeking a writ of mandamus. There is no

demand which has been made to the respondents in respect of the claim

made in the present writ petition. The petitioner has directly approached

this Court. On being confronted with this, learned counsel for the petitioner

states that he be allowed to file a representation with the respondents in

respect of the claim, which has been made in the present writ petition.

</p><p id="p_4">              In case, the petitioner files a representation in respect of the

claim, which has been made in the present writ petition for counting the
