                                   ORDER
</pre><p id="p_1">Date : 11-06-2019

             Heard Mr. A.M. Bora, learned senior counsel for the petitioners.
           Also heard Mr. R.J. Baruah, learned Additional Public Prosecutor for the state as
      well as Mr. J. Patowary, learned counsel appearing for the respondent No. 2.
</p><p id="p_2">           This criminal petition has been filed under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> of the Cr.PC, praying for
      quashing of the FIR dated 10.4.2018, based of which Pragjyotishpur PS Case No.
      78/2018, corresponding to GR Case No. 4664/2018 has been registered.
</p><p id="p_3">           Mr. A.M. Bora, learned senior counsel for the petitioners submits that the matter
      has since been compromised by the parties, and therefore, further continuance with
      the investigation in the connected GR Case would lead to miscarriage of justice. Under
      the circumstances, a prayer has been made to quash the FIR dated 10.04.2018.
      Supporting the arguments made by the petitioners' counsel, Mr. J. Patowary, learned
      counsel for the respondent No. 2 submits that the FIR was lodged out of a
      misunderstanding but subsequently, the matter has been settled by and between the
      parties, and therefore, his client is no longer keen to proceed with the matter.
</p><p id="p_4">           It appears from the record that based on the FIR dated 10.4.2018, Pragjyotishpur
      PS Case No. 78/2018 was registered against the petitioners under <a href="/doc/1133601/" id="a_2">Sections 325</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_3">34</a> IPC
      read with <a href="/doc/148942/" id="a_4">Sections 75</a>/<a href="/doc/148942/" id="a_5">78</a>/<a href="/doc/148942/" id="a_6">82</a> of the Juvenile Justice (Care &amp; <a href="/doc/1973522/" id="a_7">Protection of Children) Act</a>,
      2015 (for short, Act of 2015).
</p><p id="p_5">           Mr. Bora, submits that the offence under <a href="/doc/1133601/" id="a_8">Section 325</a> of the IPC is compoundable
      in nature and although the Act of 2015 is silent in this regard, the offences under the
      aforesaid provisions also would be compoundable. The said submission of Mr. Bora
      has not been opposed either by the learned Additional Public Prosecutor appearing for
      the state or by Mr. Patowary learned counsel appearing for the respondent No. 2.
</p><p id="p_6">           A plain reading of <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_9">Section 320</a> of the Cr.PC makes it clear that an offence under
      <a href="/doc/1133601/" id="a_10">Section 325</a> IPC is compoundable by the person to whom hurt is caused. As per
      <a href="/doc/148942/" id="a_11">Section 78</a> of the Act of 2015, the maximum punishment is of 3 years with the fine of
      Rs. 1,00,000/- or both. Having regard to the language of the FIR, this court is of the
      prima-facie opinion that <a href="/doc/148942/" id="a_12">Section 78</a> of the Act of 2015 would not be attracted in this
      case. Insofar as <a href="/doc/148942/" id="a_13">Section 82</a> of the Act of 2015 is concerned, the first conviction under
      the said provisions would lead to a fine of Rs. 10,000/- only.
</p><p id="p_7">           In view of the above and considering the compromise arrived at between the
      parties, I am of the view that for ends of justice, present is a fit case where the FIR
      dated 10.4.2018 deserves to be quashed.
</p><p id="p_8">           Ordered accordingly.
</p><p id="p_9">           The criminal petition stands allowed.
</p>
