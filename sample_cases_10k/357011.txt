<div class="doc_title">Rakesh Kumar Varma &amp; Others vs State Of U.P. &amp; Others on 23 July, 2010</div><pre id="pre_1">Court No. - 33

Case :- WRIT - C No. - 34815 of 2009

Petitioner :- Rakesh Kumar Varma &amp; Others
Respondent :- State Of U.P. &amp; Others
Petitioner Counsel :- B.N. Singh Rathore
Respondent Counsel :- C.S.C.

Hon'ble Pankaj Mithal,J.
</pre>
<p id="p_1">Heard learned counsel for the petitioners and the learned Standing counsel.
</p>
<p id="p_2">Counter and rejoinder affidavits exchanged between the parties have also
been perused.
</p>
<p id="p_3">The sale deed dated 8.10.2003 after registration was referred under <a href="/doc/74910796/" id="a_1">Section
47A</a> of the Indian Stamp Act for determination of the market value for the
purposes of levying stamp duty. The Additional Collection(City) Ghaziabad
vide order dated 30.11.2007 determined the market value by applying the
circle rate notified for the residential use. The aforesaid order has been upheld
in revision vide order dated 31.3.2009.
</p>
<p id="p_4">Both the above orders have been assailed by the petitioners in the writ petition
contending that the land in dispute is recorded as agricultural land. It has not
been declared to be of non agricultural purposes. The impugned orders have
been passed solely on the premise that the land is likely to be used for non
agricultural purposes as cultivation is not possible on it. In determining the
market value no exemplar has been relied upon and the petitioners evidence
showing the determination of market value of a piece of land acquired in the
vicinity for payment of compensation at the rate of Rs. 300/- per sq.yard was
not considered.
</p>
<p id="p_5">The nature of the land has been treated to be residential for the reason that on
the land in dispute cultivation is not possible. The nature of the land has no
relevance once the sale deed has been registered in as much as the market
value after registration of sale deed is required to be determined by applying
the general principles which are applicable for determining the market value
for payment of compensation under the provision of the <a href="/doc/7832/" id="a_2">Land Acquisition Act</a>
vide Ram Khilawan Vs. State of U.P. and others 2005 (2) AWC 1987.
</p>
<p id="p_6">The authorities below have not applied the general principles and have not
placed reliance upon any exemplar. Above all the orders of the courts below
stand vitiated for non consideration of the evidence of the petitioners which
was in the form of an exemplar pertaining to the acquisition of a piece of land
in the vicinity wherein compensation at the rate of market value of Rs. 300/-
per sq. meter was awarded.
</p>
<p id="p_7">In view of the above defects, I am of the opinion that the impugned orders can
not be sustained in law.
</p>
<p id="p_8">Accordingly, the writ petition is allowed and the impugned orders dated
8.10.2003 and 31.3.2009 are quashed and the matter is remanded to the
 Additional Collector for redetermination of the market value in accordance
with law.
</p>
<p id="p_9">Any amount paid/deposited by the petitioners pursuant to the impugned
orders shall be refunded forthwith.
</p>
<p id="p_10">Order Date :- 23.7.2010
SKS
