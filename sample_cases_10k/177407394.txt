Prayer: Writ Petition is filed under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the Constitution of India
praying for the issuance of a  Writ of Mandamus forbearing the respondents
from demanding the arrears of subscription fee by giving retrospective effect
from May 2012 from the petitioner having Enrollment No: LOC No:15324 with the
respondents 2 &amp; 3.


!For Petitioner         : Mr. J. Anandkumar

                                                  Central Govt. St. Counsel

                                for RR-2 and 3

:ORDER  
</pre><p id="p_1">        The petitioner has filed this Writ Petition seeking a direction,
forbearing the respondents from demanding the arrears of subscription fee by
giving retrospective effect from May 2012 from the petitioner having
Enrollment No: LOC No:15324 with the respondents 2 &amp; 3. 
</p><p id="p_2">        2.The grievance of the petitioner is that without considering the
actual date of giving signals to Local Cable TV Operators (LCOs), the
respondents calculated the arrears and at any time, they would disconnect the
petitioner's cable TV network for non payment of the arrears.  Hence, he has
filed the present writ petition.
</p><p id="p_3">        3. Mr.Abdul Saleem, learned Standing counsel appearing for the
respondents/Arasu Cable TV Corporation fairly submitted that the petitioner
can approach the third respondent/Special Tahsildar,  Tamil Nadu Arasu Cable
TV Corporation Limited and produce the relevant records to establish the
correct date of receipt of  signals from the respondent and also submit all
other relevant records before the third respondent, enabling him to consider
the objections of the petitioner.
</p><p id="p_4">        4.In view of the above submission made by the learned Standing Counsel
for the respondent, the petitioner is directed to approach the third
respondent/Special Tahsildar,  Tamil Nadu Arasu Cable TV Corporation Limited
and submit relevant records, along with his objection.  On receipt of such
objections and relevant records, the third respondent/Special Tahsildar,
Tamil Nadu Arasu Cable TV Corporation Limited is directed to conduct an
enquiry and consider all the materials filed by the petitioner and pass
suitable orders on merits and in accordance with law within a period of six
weeks from the date of receipt of a copy of this order.  Till then, the
interim order, if any already granted is extended.
</p>
<p id="p_5">        5.Accordingly, this writ petition is disposed of.  Consequently,
connected miscellaneous petitions are closed.  No costs.
</p>
