<div class="doc_title">Amar Singh Gurjar S/O Dhanna Ram ... vs State Of Rajasthan Through Pp on 3 January, 2019</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: Kanwaljit Singh Ahluwalia</div>

<pre id="pre_1">            HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR RAJASTHAN
                        BENCH AT JAIPUR

            S.B. Criminal Miscellaneous (Petition) No. 7561/2018

Amar Singh Gurjar S/o Dhanna Ram Gurjar, Aged About 45 Years, R/o
Ward No.15, Gurjar Colony, Neem Ka Thana, Chhawni.

                                                              ----Petitioner

                                    Versus

1.         State Of Rajasthan Through Pp, Rajasthan.

2.         Madu Ram S/o Buddh Ram, R/o Dhani Guman Singh, Neem Ka
           Thana, Sadar Neem Ka Thana, District Sikar, Raj.

                                                            ----Respondents</pre>


<pre id="pre_2">For Petitioner(s)            :   Mr. Vikram Yadav
For Respondent(s)            :   Mr. Rakesh Kumar Paliwal
For the State                :   Mr. N.S. Dhakad, P.P.



</pre><p id="p_1">     HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE KANWALJIT SINGH AHLUWALIA

                                    / Order

03/01/2019

              Today, an application, bearing Inward No.96441/2018 for

vacation of the stay has been listed before this Court.

</p><p id="p_2">              With the consent of ld. counsel appearing for the petitioner,

ld. Public Prosecutor appearing for the State and ld. counsel appearing

for the complainant/respondent No.2, the hearing of the main case i.e.

S.B. Criminal Miscellaneous Petition No.7561/2018 is preponed from

29.01.2019      to   today   and   S.B.   Criminal   Miscellaneous   Petition

No.7561/2018 is taken on the Board.
</p>
<p id="p_3">              This Court in the main case i.e. S.B. Criminal Miscellaneous

Petition No.7561/2018, on 30.11.2018 had passed the following

order :-
</p>
<p id="p_4">
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">      "The learned counsel appearing for the petitioner contends that
      on the same set of allegations, the complainant has lodged
      earlier two F.I.Rs., at the same Police Station.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">                                        (2 of 3)             [CRLMP-7561/2018]

       Issue notice to the respondents, for 29.01.2019.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_3">       The learned Public Prosecutor appearing for the State is
       directed to call concerned Investigating Officer along with the
       records of present F.I.R. and earlier F.I.R. lodged by the
       complainant.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_4">       List this case on 29.01.2019.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_5">       Meanwhile, the further proceedings arising out of impugned
       F.I.R. shall remain stayed.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_6">       Let a copy of this order, under the seal and signatures of the
       Court Master, be handed over to ld. Public Prosecutor
       appearing for the State, for onward transmission and
       necessary compliance."
</blockquote>

             Mr. Prabhati Lal, Deputy Superintendent of Police, Neem-ka-<p id="p_5">

Thana, District Sikar is present in person before this Court. He has

submitted that the F.I.R. earlier lodged and the present F.I.R. are not

similar and in both the F.I.Rs., different allegations have been levelled.

He has further submitted that the investigation is complete and he has

already prepared the challan. He has stated that tomorrow Mr. Amar

Singh son of Jai Narayan, an adopted son of Sunda Ram shall complete

ninety days in the custody and if the charge-sheet is not filed, he shall

be released on bail under Section 167 (2) <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_1">Cr.P.C</a>. for default on the part

of the prosecution.
</p>
<p id="p_6">             After hearing ld. counsel appearing for the parties, S.B.

Criminal Miscellaneous Petition No.7561/2018 is disposed of, by issuing

the following direction :-
</p>
<p id="p_7">
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_7">       "A.   That Mr. Prabhati Lal, Deputy Superintendent of Police,
       Neem-ka-thana, District Sikar, being Investigating Officer of
       the case, present in person, in consonance with the statement
       made by him shall file charge-sheet in the concerned Court by
       tomorrow.
</blockquote>

<blockquote id="blockquote_8">       B.    That the petitioner, in terms of the judgment rendered
       by the Supreme Court in the case of Amitbhai Anilchandra
       Shah Vs. The Central Bureau of Investigation and
       another, reported as 2013 (1) W.L.C. (S.C.) Criminal 753,
       shall file an application before the trial Court stating therein
                                                                           (3 of 3)              [CRLMP-7561/2018]

                                           that the proceedings arising out of the second F.I.R. on the
                                           same allegations being impermissible are liable to be dropped.
</blockquote>

<blockquote id="blockquote_9">                                           C.    That upon filing of the application by the petitioner, the
                                           trial Court, after hearing all concerned, shall pass a detailed
                                           speaking order within one month from the date of filing of the
                                           application."
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_10">                                                In view of the directions issued herein above, the present

                                   petition is disposed of, with liberty to the petitioner to take recourse to

                                   the lawful remedy available to him, in case the trial Court declines the

                                   application filed by the petitioner.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_11">                                                Let a copy of this order be placed in S.B. Criminal

                                   Miscellaneous     Petition   Nos.4148/2017,       561/2017   and    7777/2018

                                   respectively.</blockquote><p id="p_8">

</p><p id="p_9">                                                                      (KANWALJIT SINGH AHLUWALIA),J




                                   ashok
