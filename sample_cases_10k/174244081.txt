<div class="doc_title">Dr. Anju Sharma vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 1 August, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">                      WP 12595/08
1.8.2014
        Heard Shri Gaurav Tiwari, learned counsel for
the petitioner and Shri S.M.Lal, learned Government
Advocate for the respondent/State on the question of</pre>
<p id="p_1">admission as well as on I.A. No. 4993/2014.
</p><p id="p_2">        The learned counsel appearing for the petitioner
submits that      a similar petition         i.e. W.P. No.
13834/2007 (S) disposed of by this Court vide order
dated     11.3.2011 directing the respondents to decide
the representation and therefore,          the petitioner be
also given liberty to approach the respondents/
authorities by filing a representation which may be
directed to be considered and decided in accordance
with law expeditiously.
</p>
<p id="p_3">        In view of the limited prayer made by the
learned counsel for the petitioner, which has not
been opposed by the learned counsel appearing for
the State, the petitioner is permitted to with draw the
petition with liberty aforesaid.
</p>
<p id="p_4">         It goes without saying that in case the
petitioner   does approach         respondents/authorities,
alongwith a certified copy of the order passed today
and a copy of the petition          within one week from
today, the competent authority shall consider and
decide the representation of the petitioner in
 accordance with law as expeditiously as possible
preferably within six weeks thereafter.
</p>
<p id="p_5">      However, it is made clear that this court has not
expressed any opinion on the merits of the case and
therefore the authority would be at liberty to examine
the   matter   keeping     all   facts   and     facets      into
consideration and thereafter either accept or reject the
representation by passing a reasoned order.
</p>
<p id="p_6">      With the aforesaid liberty/direction, the petition
filed by the petitioner stands disposed of.
</p>
<p id="p_7">      C.C. as per rules.
</p>



<p id="p_8">                                               ( R.S.Jha )
                                                  Judge
