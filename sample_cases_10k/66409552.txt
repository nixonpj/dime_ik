<p id="p_1">      Heard the learned counsel for the petitioner and the learned Public

Prosecutor.
</p>
<p id="p_2">      2. The petitioner is the registered owner of a tipper lorry involved in

Crime No.429 of 2013 of Enathu Police Station. The offence against the

accused in the aforesaid crime is under Section 4 read with Section 21 of the

Miners and Minerals Development and <a href="/doc/1489134/" id="a_1">Regulation Act</a>. The vehicle has been

seized in the crime by the 2nd respondent and is kept under custody. The

petitioner prays that if he files an application for composition of the offence

in terms of the provisions of the Act, the same be considered by the

concerned authority and appropriate orders be passed in accordance with

law. I see no harm in allowing the said prayer.
</p>
<p id="p_3">      3. Crl.M.C is disposed of directing the 2nd respondent, S.I. of Police,

the authorised officer under the provisions of the Miners and Minerals

Development and <a href="/doc/1489134/" id="a_2">Regulation Act</a> to pass appropriate orders on the

application for composition of the offence under the Act, if any, to be

presented by the petitioner.
</p>

</p><p id="p_5">      4.    It is hereby made clear that this Court has not expressed

anything on the merits of the case.</p><pre id="pre_2">
