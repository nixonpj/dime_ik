<div class="doc_title">Dharmendra Dhakad vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 6 October, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">(Dharmendra Dhakad Vs. State)           1 M.Cr.C. No. 10318/2015

06/10/2015
        Shri Narendra Dubey, Advocate for the applicant.
        Dr. (Smt.) Anjali Gyanani, Public Prosecutor for the
respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">        Heard.
</p><p id="p_2">        This is the first application for regular bail under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section
439</a> of the Cr.P.C.
</p><p id="p_3">        The applicant is in custody since 06.09.2015 in
connection with Crime No. 355/2015 registered at Police Station
Bairad District Shivpuri (MP) for the offence punishable under
Section 34(2) of M.P. Excise Act.
</p><p id="p_4">        It is alleged that 54 bulk litres of illicit liquor was seized
from the possession of the applicant.
</p><p id="p_5">        On behalf of the applicant, it is submitted that the
applicant is innocent. He has been falsely implicated in the
matter. He is the only bread earner in his family. There is no
criminal antecedent. Therefore, applicant be enlarged on bail.
</p><p id="p_6">        Learned Public Prosecutor opposed the application for
bail.
</p><p id="p_7">        Keeping in view the facts that applicant is in custody since
10.08.2015 and disposal of case will take considerable time,
without commenting anything on the merits of the case, I deem
it proper to extend the benefit of bail to the present applicant.
Accordingly, this bail application is allowed.
</p><p id="p_8">           (Dharmendra Dhakad Vs. State)         2 M.Cr.C. No. 10318/2015

                It is directed that the applicant shall be released on bail
          on his furnishing personal bond in a sum of Rs.50,000/-
          (Rupees Fifty Thousand only) with one solvent surety in the
          like amount to the satisfaction of the Trial Court for securing his
          presence before the concerned Court on all the dates of hearing
          fixed in this regard during trial.
</p><p id="p_9">                This order will remain operative subject to compliance of
          the following conditions by the applicant:-
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">                 1.   The applicant will not interfere or influence the
                      prosecution witnesses;
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">                 2.   The applicant will make himself available or
                      represent through his counsel on early date of
                      proceedings
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">                 3.   The applicant will not indulge in any similar offences
                      during the pendency of the trial.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_4">                 4.   If the applicant found breach of any of the conditions
                      above, the learned Trial Court would be at liberty to
                      reconsider on the question of bail.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_5">                Certified copy as per rules.</blockquote>
<p id="p_10">                                                         (S.K. Palo)
                                                           Judge
Sateesh
