<div class="doc_title">Keval Kishore Sharma &amp; Another vs State Of Uttarakhand &amp; Others on 10 October, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">WPPIL No. 141 of 2013


Hon'ble Sudhanshu Dhulia, J.
</pre><p id="p_1">Hon'ble U.C. Dhyani, J.
</p><p id="p_2">       Mr. P.C. Petshali, Advocate present for the
writ petitioners.
</p><p id="p_3">       Mr. Pradeep Joshi, Standing Counsel
present for the State / respondent no, 1, 2, 4, 5, 6
and 7.
</p><p id="p_4">       Mr. Arvind Vashisth, Sr. Advocate assisted
by Mr. Hemant Singh, Advocate for respondent
nos. 8 to 12.
</p><p id="p_5">       By an order of the Court, a joint inspection
was conducted. Consequently, a Joint Inspection
Report has been filed before this Court.
</p><p id="p_6">       Learned Senior counsel appearing for
respondent nos. 8 to 12, has filed his objections on
Joint Inspection Report. The same be taken on
record.
</p><p id="p_7">       Perused the Joint Inspection Report. Not
only does the Report says that many villagers who
reside nearby had strong objections to the Stone
Crusher being constructed in their vicinity, as it is
causing noise pollution as well as air pollution and
is harmful, particularly, to their children, who
cannot sleep in the night because of the noise
caused during construction of the Stone Crushing
Unit. There are more than 10 houses, which are in
the vicinity between 184 mts. to 372 mts. of the
said construction, whereas the permissible limit is
beyond 500 mts. of a residential house, as this
Court has been informed.
</p><p id="p_8">       Whereas learned Senior counsel appearing
for the respondent Stone Crushers has filed his
objections, learned counsel for the petitioner is
given three weeks' time to file reply thereto.
</p><p id="p_9">       List after three weeks.</p><pre id="pre_2">



 (U.C. Dhyani, J.)       (Sudhanshu Dhulia, J.)
     10.10.2014              10.10.2014
HN
