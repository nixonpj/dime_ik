<div class="doc_title">Satyendra Kumar vs The State Of Bihar on 4 May, 2012</div><pre id="pre_1">    Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.7711 of 2012 (2) dt.04-05-2012




                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.7711 of 2012
                 ======================================================
                 Satyendra Kumar
                                                                         .... .... Petitioner/s
                                                   Versus
                 The State Of Bihar
                                                                    .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance:
                 For the Petitioner     : Mr. Ajay Kumar, Advocate
                 For the State           : Mr. Yogendra Kumar Singh, A.P.P
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE HEMANT KUMAR
                 SRIVASTAVA
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>
<p id="p_1">                                                 ---------
</p><p id="p_2">2 04.05.2012                   Heard learned counsel for the petitioner as well as learned

                      Addl. Public Prosecutor for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                               Petitioner was working as accounts clerk at Ratni block at the

                      relevant time and allegedly, he as well as one co-accused Sunil Kumar

                      defalcated government money.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                               The contention on behalf of the petitioner is that enquiry was

                      conducted by the department of the petitioner and in the aforesaid

                      enquiry, petitioner was found innocent which is evident from perusal

                      of the enquiry report dated 29.5.2007(Annexure 3). It is further

                      contended by him that after due investigation, police submitted final

                      form showing the case as "mistake of fact" but the learned Chief

                      Judicial Magistrate, without giving         any reason, differed with the

                      findings of the police and took cognizance of the offence under

                      <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_1">sections 406</a> and <a href="/doc/1326844/" id="a_2">409</a> of the IPC against the petitioner.

</p><p id="p_5">                               Call for the carbon copy of the case diary of Sakurabad P.S.

                      Case no. 74/2006 from the court of Smt Rachana, Raj, Judicial

                      Magistrate, Ist Class, Jehanabad and list this matter after receipt of the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">          Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.7711 of 2012 (2) dt.04-05-2012</span>




                           case diary.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                     In the meantime, further proceedings of the above stated case

                           shall remain stayed till further order of this court in respect of the

                           petitioner only.
</p>
<p id="p_7">
</p><pre id="pre_2">Shahid                                              ( Hemant Kumar Srivastava,J)
