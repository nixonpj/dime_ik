<div class="doc_title">S.Sarath Kakumanu vs State Rep. By on 26 February, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">        

 

IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT MADRAS

DATED: 26.02.2016
CORAM
THE HONOURABLE MS.JUSTICE R. MALA

CRL.O.P.No.4259 of 2016


S.Sarath Kakumanu			.. Petitioner

.. Vs ..

State rep. by,
The Inspector of Police,
Central Crime Branch,
Team XVIII,
Chennai.
(Cr.No.766/2006)				  .. Respondent

		
Prayer:- Criminal Original Petition filed under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure, praying to direct the respondent to file final report in Crime No.766/2006 on the file of the respondent police.


		For Petitioner	: 	Mr.S.P.Meenakshi Sundaram
		For Respondent	:	Mr.C.Emalias,
						Additional Public Prosecutor
- - - - -


ORDER
</pre>
	
<p id="p_1">		The petitioner has come forward with this petition under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_2">Section 482</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure, seeking for a direction directing the respondent police to file a final report in Crime No.766/2006 on the file of the respondent police.
</p>
<p id="p_2">		2. Heard the learned counsel appearing for the petitioner and the learned Additional Public Prosecutor appearing for the respondent.
</p>
<p id="p_3">		3. The learned counsel for the petitioner would submit that on the basis of the complaint given by the petitioner, a case has been registered in Crime No.766 of 2006 on 28.09.2006 for the  offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1128948/" id="a_3">Sections 120(b)</a>, <a href="/doc/1667388/" id="a_4">419</a>, <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_5">420</a>, <a href="/doc/1317063/" id="a_6">465</a>, <a href="/doc/1985627/" id="a_7">467</a>, <a href="/doc/556166/" id="a_8">468</a> and <a href="/doc/1466184/" id="a_9">471</a> of IPC. However, the respondent police has not shown any interest to investigate the matter and lay a final report. Hence, the petitioner has come up with the present petition for the relief as stated above.
</p><p id="p_4">		4. Resisting the same, the learned Additional Public Prosecutor would submit that investigation is going on and hence, he prays for six months time to complete the investigation and file a final report.
</p>
<p id="p_5">		5. Considering the submissions made on both sides and also considering the fact that on the basis of the complaint given by the petitioner, after due enquiry, the case in Crime No.766 of 2006 has been registered as early as on 28.09.2006 for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1128948/" id="a_10">Sections 120(b)</a>, <a href="/doc/1667388/" id="a_11">419</a>, <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_12">420</a>, <a href="/doc/1317063/" id="a_13">465</a>, <a href="/doc/1985627/" id="a_14">467</a>, <a href="/doc/556166/" id="a_15">468</a> and <a href="/doc/1466184/" id="a_16">471</a> of IPC, the respondent police is directed to complete the investigation in Crime No.766 of 2006 and file a final report, within a period of six months from the date of receipt of copy of this order.
</p>
<p id="p_6">		6. The Criminal Original Petition is disposed of with the above direction.
</p><p id="p_7">									26.02.2016
Index		: No
Internet	: Yes
Jrl




R.MALA, J.
</p>
