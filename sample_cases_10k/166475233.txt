<p id="p_1">KRISHNA MURARI, C.J. (Oral)

                When the matter was heard on 03.07.2018, we passed the
following order:
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">                       "Without prejudice to the rights of the respective parties,
               learned counsel for the respondent(s) is granted two weeks time
               to seek instructions if out of the total amount of the earnest
               money deposited (EMD) forfeited by the respondents some
               reasonable amount could still be re-imbursed to the petitioners
               to resolve the dispute finally.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">                       Adjourned to 19.07.2018.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">                       A photocopy of this order be placed on the file of
               connected case."</blockquote>


<p id="p_2">                Today when the matter has been called out, learned counsel for the
petitioner has produced before us an order dated 2nd August, 2018, said to have
been passed by Director, Health &amp; Family Welfare, Chandigarh Administration,
whereby considering the entire facts and circumstances, the petitioner has been
allowed a refund of sum of Rs.5,29,194/- being 50% of the total amount of EMD,
which was forfeited.
</p>

<p id="p_3">                                  1 of 2
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">               ::: Downloaded on - 20-01-2019 00:08:33 :::</span>
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2"> CWP No.3714 of 2018                                                     2</span>


                Learned counsel further submits that terms and conditions and
refund of 50% of the EMD is acceptable to him, but since the same is on account
of a settlement in view of the above quoted order passed by us, the retention of the
remaining 50% of the EMD mentioned as penalty is objectionable inasmuch as the
imposition of penalty may have some consequence in future.
</p><p id="p_4">                Considering the facts, we hereby dispose of these writ petitions in
terms of the order dated 2nd August, 2018, produced before us, which is taken on
record, with the modification that 50% amount of the EMD, which is being
retained by the respondent(s), shall not be treated as a penalty, but on account of
settlement between the parties.
</p><p id="p_5">                With the aforesaid observation, the writ petitions stand disposed of.
</p>
