         IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT BOMBAY
                 CRIMINAL APPELLATE JURISDICTION


                 BAIL APPLICATION NO. 1003 OF 2018


1. Mohd. Arif S/o Mustafa Sayyed
2. Mohd. Sheif S/o Mustafa Sayyed                 ... Applicants
     Vs.
The State of Maharashtra &amp; Anr.                   ... Respondents
                                   ...
Mr. Aniket Gawand I/by R.P. Javanjal for the applicants.
Ms. A.A. Takalkar, APP for the Respondent-State.
Mr. Gujar, P.I. Traffic Department Kurla is present in person.
                                   ...
                                   CORAM  : PRAKASH D. NAIK, J.
                                   DATE      :  27th AUGUST, 2018.
                              
P.C.

1.      This is an application for bail in connection with CR No. 84

of 2017 registered with Shahu Nagar Police Station for an offence

under Section 302, 325 read with 34<a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_1"> of Indian Penal Code</a>. 

2.      The   case   of   the   prosecution   is   that   the   applicant   and   the

other accused had allegedly assaulted Mohd. Sarif (deceased) with

fist and kick blows as he had caused damage to taxi of one of the

accused.  The applicants were arrested on 19th March, 2017.

4.      Learned counsel for the applicants submitted that taking the

case of the prosecution as it is, <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_2">Section 302</a> of Indian Penal Code is

not made out.   It is submitted that the co-accused who has been



                                                                              1 of  4
      Sknair                                                 15-ba-1003-18.odt

attributed   similar   role   has   been   granted   bail   by   this   Court.

Applicant is in custody for about one and half year.

5.    Learned  APP  submitted that  all  accused had assaulted the

deceased by fist and kick blows which has resulted in his death.

The deceased was a drug addict who was assaulted by the accused

for no reason and on account of the assault, deceased had died.  It

is submitted that whether the case would fall under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_3">Section 302</a> of

Indian Penal Code or not can be considered at the time of trial.

Co-accused has been granted bail because she was lady.  Learned

APP also submitted that applicant is permanent resident of U.P.

and they have no permanent address at Mumbai.   On the other

hand learned advocate for applicants submitted that applicants are

residing   at   the   address   mentioned   in   the   cause   title   of   the

application.  Applicants are willing to report the concerned police

station during the pendency of trial. 

6.    On   going   through   the   documents,   it   is   apparent   that   the

deceased was assaulted by the accused as he had caused scratch to

taxi   of  one   of  the   accused. All  of  them had  given   fist  and kick

blows to the deceased.  Investigation is completed and chargesheet

has  been   filed.     The  applicants are  in  custody since  long.     On

perusal of the postmortem report it can be seen that the deceased

                                                                        2 of  4
        Sknair                                                15-ba-1003-18.odt

had sustained the following injuries.

        1) Contusion, 2 cm x 2 cm over middle of chest on right side.

        2) Two abrasions with reddish scab, 1.5 cm x 1 cm and 2 cm
             x 1.5 cm over lateral aspect of right knee joint.

        3) Abrasion with reddish scab, 4 cm x 0.8 cm over medial  
             aspect of middle part of right leg.

        4) Two abrasions with reddish scab, 2 cm x 2 cm and 1 cm x 
             0.3 cm over right scapular area.

        5) Abrasion with dark brownish scab 1.5 cm x 1 cm over 
             lateral aspect of left knee joint.

Admittedly, no weapon was used in the commission of the offence.

The   other   accused   has   been   granted   bail.     Both   of   them   were

attributed     same   identical   role.     In   the   circumstances,   further

custody   is   not   required.  Taking  into  consideration   the  aforesaid

aspects, the case for grant of bail is made out.  

                                   ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">i)      Bail Application No. 1003 of 2018 is allowed;

</p><p id="p_2">ii)     The   applicants   are   directed   to   be   released   on   bail   in

connection with C.R No. 84 of 2017 registered with Shahu Nagar

Police Station which is subject matter of Sessions Case No. 490 of

2017   on   furnishing   P.R.   Bond   of   Rs.25,000/-   each   with   one   or

more sureties in the like amount;</p><pre id="pre_2">

iii)    Applicants are permitted to furnish cash security in the sum

                                                                         3 of  4
           Sknair                                                     15-ba-1003-18.odt

</pre><p id="p_3">   of Rs.25,000/- (Rs. Twenty Five Thousand only) for a period of

   four weeks

</p><p id="p_4">   iv)        Applicants shall report at Shahu Nagar Police Station once in

   a   month   on   first   Saturday   between   10   a.m.   to   12   noon   till

   conclusion of the trial;
</p>
<p id="p_5">   v)         Applicants shall co-operate with the conduct of the trial;

</p><p id="p_6">   vi)        Applicants   shall   inform   their   latest   place   of   residence   and

   contact number or change of residence or contact number, if any,

   to the Investigating Officer;

</p><p id="p_7">   vii)       The   applicant   shall   not   tamper   with   the   evidence   and/or

   influence the prosecution witnesses;

</p><p id="p_8">   viii) The application stands disposed off.
