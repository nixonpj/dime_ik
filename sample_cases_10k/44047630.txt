<div class="doc_title">Ashish Kumar @ Ashish Sahu vs State Of Jharkhand on 29 October, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                                        B.A. No. 6370 of 2018
            Ashish Kumar @ Ashish Sahu.....................  Petitioner
                                   Versus
            State of Jharkhand. ............                       Opp. Party
                                   ......
</pre><p id="p_1">            Coram: Hon'ble Mr. Justice Ananda Sen
                                   ......</p><pre id="pre_2">
            For the Petitioner           : Mr. D.K.Prasad, Advocate
            For the State                : A.P.P.
                                   ......

5/29.10.2018         Heard learned counsel appearing for the petitioner and the
</pre><p id="p_2">            learned counsel for the State, who opposes the prayer for bail.
</p><p id="p_3">                     The petitioner is an accused for allegedly committing offence
            punishable under <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_1">Sections 323</a>, <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_2">307</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_3">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code, Section,
            27 of the <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_4">Arms Act</a> and <a href="/doc/1845798/" id="a_5">Sections 17</a> (i) (ii) of C.L.A Act.
</p><p id="p_4">                     The impugned order clearly suggests that one Md. Sahbaj was
            arrested from the spot, who disclosed the name of this petitioner to be his
            associates. Admittedly, this petitioner was not arrested from the spot and
            he was not put on TIP. The petitioner is in custody since 08.05.2018 and
            charge sheet has already been submitted in this case.
</p><p id="p_5">                     Taking into consideration the aforesaid fact, I am inclined to
            enlarge the petitioner on bail. Accordingly, petitioner namely, Ashish
            Kumar @ Ashish Sahu is directed to be released on bail on furnishing bail
            bond of Rs. 10,000/- (ten thousand) with two sureties of the like amount
            each to the satisfaction of Additional Chief Judicial Magistrate, Chatra in
            connection with Simaria P.S. Case No. 19 of 2017 [G.R. No. 161/2017],
            subject to the condition that the petitioner shall appear before the Officer-
            in-Charge, Simaria Police Station, Chatra once in a month and shall mark
            his attendance. If he fails to appear in any particular month, his bail
            bonds shall stand cancelled and the Officer-in-charge would be at liberty
            to arrest the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                                                        (Ananda Sen, J)
Mukund/-cp. 3
 </p>
