IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA AT CHANDIGARH

                                              CRM-M-36292-2018 (O&amp;M)
                                              Date of Decision:-15.10.2018.


Manjinder Singh and others

                                                               .....Petitioners
                          Versus


State of Punjab and another

                                                             ......Respondents

CORAM:       HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE P.B. BAJANTHRI
                          ****

Present:    Mr. Rakesh Kumar, Advocate for the petitioners.

            Mr. Sukhbeer Singh, AAG, Punjab.

            Ms. Monika, Mehta, Advocate for respondent No.2.

                          ****

P.B. BAJANTHRI, J. (Oral)
</pre>
<p id="p_1">            Petitioners are seeking quashing of FIR No.28 dated

21.04.2017 under <a href="/doc/222396/" id="a_1">Sections 427</a>/<a href="/doc/162506/" id="a_2">447</a>/<a href="/doc/1185693/" id="a_3">511</a>/<a href="/doc/763672/" id="a_4">148</a>/<a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_5">149</a> IPC, registered at Police

Station, Fattu Dhinga, District Kapurthala and all other consequential

proceedings arising therefrom on the basis of compromise (Annexure P-2)

arrived at between the parties.
</p>
<p id="p_2">2.)         Learned counsel for the parties submitted that FIR is the

outcome of sudden quarrel and in fit of anger. Now with the intervention of

relatives and friends, parties have amicably settled their dispute and entered

into compromise.
</p>
<p id="p_3">3.)         This Court vide order dated 23.08.2018 had directed the parties

to appear before the Trial Court on 10.09.2018 for recording their

effected without any fear or undue influence.

<p id="p_5">4.)          In view of the law laid down by Hon'ble the Supreme Court in

the case of "Anita Maria Dias and another Vs. State of Maharashtra and

another" reported in (2018) 3 SCC 290, no useful purpose would be served

by continuing the criminal proceedings. Therefore, petition is allowed and

FIR No.28 dated 21.04.2017 under <a href="/doc/222396/" id="a_6">Sections 427</a>/<a href="/doc/162506/" id="a_7">447</a>/<a href="/doc/1185693/" id="a_8">511</a>/<a href="/doc/763672/" id="a_9">148</a>/<a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_10">149</a> IPC,

registered at Police Station, Fattu Dhinga, District Kapurthala and all other

consequential proceedings arising therefrom are hereby quashed qua the

petitioners on the basis of compromise (Annexure P-2), subject to payment

of litigation cost to the State of Punjab a sum of ` 3,000/- by each of the

petitioner. Cost be remitted in the District Treasury, Kapurthala, State of

Punjab within a period of 3 months from today.
</p>
