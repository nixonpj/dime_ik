<div class="doc_title">Rajeev Kumar Rai @ Rajeev Kumar vs The State Of Bihar on 25 April, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.16987 of 2016
                        Arising Out of PS.Case No. -247 Year- 2015 Thana -PUPRI District- SITAMARHI
                 ======================================================
                 1. Rajeev Kumar Rai @ Rajeev Kumar Son of Ram Sharan Rai, R/o
                 Village- Harsaha, P.s.- Pupri, District- Sitamarhi
                                                                         .... .... Petitioner/s
                                                     Versus
                 1. The State of Bihar
                                                                    .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :    Mr. Ashok Kumar Jha
                 For the Opposite Party/s    : Mr. M. Dayal(App)
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE KISHORE KUMAR MANDAL
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   25-04-2016                    Heard Mr. Jha for the petitioner and Mr. M. Dayal,

                 APP for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                  Petitioner is accused of Pupri P.S. Case No. 247 of

                 2015 registered under <a href="/doc/619940/" id="a_1">Sections 363</a> and <a href="/doc/796352/" id="a_2">366</a> of the Indian Penal

                 Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                  In relation to the missing of the 20 years old daughter

                 of the informant, the F.I.R. was lodged on 22.09.2015. Four

                 persons excluding the petitioner are named therein. On retrieval,

                 the girl was produced for her statement under <a href="/doc/497457/" id="a_3">Section 164</a> Cr.P.C..

                 In her such statement (Annexure-2), she has stated that on her

                 own volition, she got married with the petitioner. Mr. Jha states

                 that the girl is living in the house of the petitioner as his wife as

                 the Magistrate released her in favour of the mother of the

                 petitioner.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">            Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.16987 of 2016 (2) dt.25-04-2016</span><p id="p_4">

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                      2/2</span>




</p><p id="p_5">                                      Considering the above, in the event of arrest or

                        surrender in the Court below within four weeks, the petitioner

                        abovenamed is directed to be released on anticipatory bail on

                        furnishing bail bond of Rs. 10,000/- (ten thousand) with two

                        sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of learned Chief

                        Judicial Magistrate, Sitamarhi in Pupri P.S. Case No. 247 of 2015

                        subject to the condition as laid down under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_4">Section 438(2)</a> of the

                        Cr.P.C. with a further following condition that one of the bailers

                        shall be the own/close family members of the petitioner.




</p><p id="p_6">                                                (Kishore Kumar Mandal, J)
Pankaj/-
</p>
