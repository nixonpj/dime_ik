                           JUDGMENT

<p id="p_1">     The petitioner has preferred Ext.P1 representation

alleging irregularities in a loan account. It is contended

that the petitioner, his wife and son had availed loans

from the second respondent bank and that there were

irregularities in the loan accounts. It is stated that the

documents sought for under the <a href="/doc/671631/" id="a_2">RTI Act</a> were also not

provided to the petitioner. Pointing out these aspects, the

petitioner        has   approached        the    first    respondent        by

preferring Ext.P1 complaint.                  The petitioner seeks a

<a href="/doc/671631/" id="a_3">Section 65</a> enquiry into the affairs of the second

respondent on the basis of the complaint preferred by

him. It is contended that the first respondent has ample

power to take note of Ext.P1 complaint to initiate an

enquiry under Section 65 of the Kerala Co-operative

Societies Act, 1969 ('the Act' for short).

</p><p id="p_2">     2.      It    is   submitted        by     the      learned        counsel
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">W.P.(C).No.6168 of 2018        : 2   :</span>


appearing for the second respondent bank that the

complaint of the petitioner is a monetary dispute. It is

contended that such disputes are to be considered in

accordance with the provisions of <a href="/doc/671631/" id="a_4">Section 69</a> of the Act.

</p><p id="p_3">      3.     I am of the opinion that it is for the petitioner to

raise a dispute before the competent authority, in which

case, it is to be proceeded with in accordance with law.

In case, the petitioner raises a dispute with regard to

irregularities      in    loan accounts,     the   same         shall be

considered in accordance with the provisions of <a href="/doc/671631/" id="a_5">Section

69</a> of the Act.
</p>
<p id="p_4">      With the above observation, the writ petition is

disposed of.
</p>
