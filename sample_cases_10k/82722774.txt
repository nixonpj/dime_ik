                        O R D E R

<p id="p_1">     In all these cases, appeal filed by the revenue

was dismissed on merits.           It is to review the said

order, these review petitions are filed.


</p><p id="p_2">2.   It is not in dispute that the tax liability in all

these appeals/review petitions is less than Rs.4

lakhs.    By   virtue   of    Circular    No.2/2005,       the

Revenue   could   not    have      preferred   the    appeals

against the order passed by the tribunal where the

amount involved was less than Rs.4 lakhs.
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                 -4-</span>


</p><p id="p_3">3.    In that view of the matter, whatever may be

the merit of the decision, the appeal itself was not

maintainable       and     therefore,       all   these     review

petitions are dismissed on the ground that the

subject matter of the proceedings is less than Rs.4

lakhs.
</p>
<p id="p_4">
</p><p id="p_5">4.    In fact, this Court in the case of M/s Ranka

and      Ranka    has     held,      even    though       Circular

No.3/2011 in particular clause 11 makes the said

circular    prospective,        it    is    retrospective     and

therefore the assessee are entitled to the benefit. It

is submitted that the revenue has preferred an

appeal against the said order before the Apex Court.

As the subject matter of these review petitions is

less than Rs.4 lakhs and as this Court has already

taken     the    view    that   notwithstanding       the     said

circular being prospective in nature, it applies to

pending proceedings also, the question of recalling

the order passed on merits even if it is erroneous

would not arise.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                          -5-</span><p id="p_6">



</p><p id="p_7">5.    However, in the event the Apex Court were to

set-aside the order passed by this Court making the

said circular applicable to pending proceedings and

holds it is only prospective in operation, liberty is

reserved to the income tax to seek review of these

review   petitions.   With   this   observation,   these

review petitions are disposed of.



</p><p id="p_8">                                       SD/-
