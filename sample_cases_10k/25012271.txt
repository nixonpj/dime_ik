  IN THE HIGH COURT OF KERALA AT ERNAKULAM

WP(C).No. 34561 of 2010(U)


1. P.M.ALI AKBAR,
                      ...  Petitioner

                        Vs



1. COMMERCIAL TAX OFFICER,
                       ...       Respondent

2. DEPUTY COMMISSIONER (APPEALS),

3. INSPECTING ASSISTANT COMMISSIONER,

                For Petitioner  :SRI.N.MURALEEDHARAN NAIR

                For Respondent  : No Appearance

The Hon'ble MR. Justice C.K.ABDUL REHIM

 Dated :19/11/2010

 O R D E R
                     C.K.ABDUL REHIM, J
                ---------------------------------------
                  W.P(C) No.34561 of 2010-U
                ----------------------------------------
        Dated this the 19th day of November, 2010.

                         J U D G M E N T
</pre>

<p id="p_1">     Aggrieved     by    Ext.P1     order      of   assessment, the

petitioner had preferred Ext.P2 appeal before the second

respondent. Along with the appeal, Ext.P3 stay petition was

also filed. The petitioner had also filed an early hearing

petition as evidenced from Ext.P4.                 Grievance of the

petitioner is that without considering pendency of the

appeal and the accompanying applications, steps for

recovery of the amounts has now been initiated as per

Ext.P5 demand notice.             Hence the petitioner seeks

interference of this Court to restrain the recovery, till the

disposal of the appeal.
</p>
<p id="p_2">     2.   Heard;     learned Government Pleader appearing

for the respondents. Considering pendnecy of the statutory

appeal before the second respondent, I am of the view that

the writ petition can be disposed of directing an expeditious

disposal of the matter by that authority.

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">W.P(C) No.34561 of 2010-U     2</span>

</p><p id="p_3">     3.     In the result, the writ petition is disposed of

directing the second respondent to consider and pass

orders    on   Ext.P3    stay petition, after affording   an

opportunity of hearing to the petitioner, as early as

possible, at any rate within one month from the date of

receipt of a copy of this judgment.
</p>
<p id="p_4">     4. Till such time orders are passed on the stay petition

as directed above, further steps of recovery pursuant to

Ext.P5 shall be kept in abeyance.
</p>
<p id="p_5">     5.    The petitioner will produce a copy of this

judgment before the second respondent.




</p><p id="p_6">                              C.K.ABDUL REHIM
                                    JUDGE

ab

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">W.P(C) No.34561 of 2010-U    3</span>

</p>
