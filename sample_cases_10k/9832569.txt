COMMON ORDER: (Per Hon'ble Sri Justice Sanjay Kumar)

        The grievance of the petitioners in this batch of cases was that

their candidature was not being considered for admission into

MBBS/BDS courses during the academic year 2019-20. Some of the

petitioners aspired to seats under Category 'A' while others sought

admission to seats under Categories 'B' and 'C'. As the petitioners had

failed to upload their applications, Kaloji Narayana Rao University of

Health Sciences, Warangal, did not permit them to participate in the

counselling for admission. At that stage, these cases came to be filed

and interim orders were passed in all the matters directing the

University to consider the candidature of the petitioners and allow

them to participate in the counselling, in the event they presented

themselves before the Registrar of the University along with their

original certificates within the time stipulated.
</p>

<p id="p_2">2.      We are now informed that these interim orders were given

effect to and some of the petitioners have secured admission while

others failed.
</p>

<p id="p_3">3.      In any event, these writ petitions no longer survive for

consideration on merits inasmuch as the last date for admission into

MBBS/BDS courses expired on 31.08.2019 for the academic year

2019-20, in terms of the law laid down by the Supreme Court.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                      3</span>



<p id="p_4">         The writ petitions are accordingly closed. Pending miscellaneous

petitions, if any, shall also stand closed. There shall be no order as to

costs.
</p>
