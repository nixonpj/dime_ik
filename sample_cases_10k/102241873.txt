<div class="doc_title">P.Venkatapathy vs State Rep By Its Inspector Of ... on 25 August, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">       

  

  

 
 
 IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT MADRAS
						
Dated : 25.08.2014

Coram :

THE HONOURABLE Ms. JUSTICE K.B.K.VASUKI

Crl.O.P.No.31284 of 2013
and MP.Nos.1 &amp; 2 of 2013
1.P.Venkatapathy
2.Kumari
3.Aruna Devi					.. Petitioners
vs.
1.State rep by its Inspector of Police,
Pallikaranai Police Station,
Chennai.

2.Nesamoni 		.. Respondents
	Criminal Original Petition filed under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure, praying to call for the records in CC.No.974 of 2012 on the file of the Judicial Magistrate, Alandur and to quash the same.
	For Petitioner   	:  Mr.Chitiraputhiran
	For Respondents	:  Mr.C.Emalias, APP for R1.
				   Mr.K.R.Rameshkumar for R2.
O R D E R
</pre><p id="p_1">	The learned counsel for the petitioners and the second respondent/defacto complainant along with his counsel on record are present. The second respondent has also filed an affidavit dated 22.08.2014 stating no objection for quashing the proceedings in CC.No.974 of 2012 on the file of the Judicial Magistrate, Alandur against the petitioners herein, on the basis of the compromise entered into between the parties.
</p>
<p id="p_2">	2.Admittedly, the parties entered into compromise between them.  When this Court enquired the second respondent/defacto complainant about the factum of the compromise and the terms of the compromise, he admitted the same and he has also categorically submitted that he is not interested in continuing the criminal prosecution against them, due to compromise arrived at between them.
</p>
<p id="p_3">3. It is now well settled that the proceedings can be quashed in appropriate cases on the basis of the settlement arrived at between the parties.  Here is the case wherein the defacto complainant in his own interest and on his own free will thought fit to compromise the matter with the petitioners/accused. That being so, the de-facto complainant is, in my considered view, not likely to support the prosecution case at later point of time during trial in respect of the petitioners/accused, as such, no useful purpose will be served by keeping the case on file and it would be only a futile exercise and it would be sheer waste of the time of the Court.  Hence, in order to maintain the present state of affairs, which is according to the parties more beneficial to them, this Court is of the view that the criminal proceedings initiated against the petitioner herein is liable to be quashed.
</p>
<p id="p_4">	4. In the result, this petition is allowed by quashing the proceedings in CC.No.974 of 2012 on the file of the Judicial Magistrate, Alandur.  Consequently, connected miscellaneous petition is closed.
</p>
