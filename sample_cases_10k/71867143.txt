<div class="doc_title">Sheo Kumar Yadav &amp; Ors vs The State Of Bihar on 8 June, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">    Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.17148 of 2015 (2) dt.08-06-2015




                IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                               Criminal Miscellaneous No.17148 of 2015
                       Arising Out of PS.Case No. -9 Year- 2013 Thana -SC/ST District- SAHARSA
         ======================================================
         1.  Sheo Kumar Yadav, son of Anandi Yadav, resident of village -
             Menha, P.S. - Bihra, District - Saharsa.
         2. Pravin Yadav @ Pavin Kumar Yadav, son of Jagarnath Yadav
         3. Robin Yadav, son of Jagarnath Yadav
             Both resident of village - Dumma, P.S. - Bihra, District -
             Saharsa.
                                                          .... .... Petitioners
                                        Versus
         The State of Bihar
                                                      .... .... Opposite Party
         ======================================================
         Appearance :
         For the Petitioner/s     :   Mr. Bhaskar Shankar
         For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr. Ambika Bhagat(Spl. P.P.)
         ======================================================
          CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE NAVANITI PRASAD SINGH
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2     08-06-2015                          The petitioners apprehend their arrest in

                       relation to Saharsa (SC/ST) P.S. Case No. 9/2013

                       registered under <a href="/doc/1101188/" id="a_1">Sections 379</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_2">504</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_3">506</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_4">323</a>, <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_5">341</a> of the

                       Indian Penal Code and Section 3(1)(X) of SC/ST

                       (Prevention of Atrocities) Act.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                          Learned counsel for the petitioners submits

                       that initially the case was lodged as a complaint case two

                       months after the alleged occurrence. There is no

                       explanation for the delay. It was then sent to be registered

                       as a police case and thereafter the petitioners having

                       come to know about the case and then move for bail in

                       anticipation of arrest which are rejected by the learned
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">      Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.17148 of 2015 (2) dt.08-06-2015</span>




                        Sessions Court.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                          Learned Court for the petitioners points out

                        that there is no explanation for the delay and in fact the

                        case has been lodged at the instance of father-in-law of

                        petitioner no. 1. The provision of SC/ST have been

                        introduced only to ensure that petitioners are physically

                        arrested and humiliated. No villager would keep up the

                        sale proceeds of sale of his cow worth Rs. 15,000/- was

                        taken away.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                          Be that as it may, in the event of their

                        arrest/surrender before the court below within four

                        weeks, let the petitioner, namely, Sheo Kumar Yadav,

                        Pravin Yadar @ Pavin Kumar Yadav and Robin Yadav,

                        be enlarged on bail on furnishing bail bonds of Rs.

                        10,000/- (ten thousand only) each with two sureties of the

                        like amount each to the satisfaction of Chief Judicial

                        Magistrate, Saharsa, in connection with Saharsa (SC/ST)

                        P.S. Case No. 9/2013, subject to condition laid down

                        under <a href="/doc/1144664/" id="a_6">Section 438</a> (2)<a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_7"> of the Code</a> of Criminal Procedure.



</p><p id="p_5">                                                            (Navaniti Prasad Singh, J.)
Rajeev/-
</p>
