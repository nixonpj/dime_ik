<div class="doc_title">Barkat Khan vs State (Home Guards) &amp; Ors on 19 May, 2011</div><pre id="pre_1">                               !! 1 !!

     IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR RAJASTHAN
                      AT JODHPUR


                           :ORDER:
</pre>

<p id="p_1">           S.B. CIVIL WRIT PETITION NO.4422/2011
              (Barkat Khan Vs. The State of Raj. &amp; Ors.)



                  Date of Order    ::     19.05.2011


                          PRESENT


     HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE GOPAL KRISHAN VYAS


Mr. R.K. Bohra, for the petitioner.
</p><p id="p_2">                               --------
</p>
<p id="p_3">             Heard learned counsel for the petitioner.



             Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that the

petitioner was selected as Home Guard according to the

Rajasthan Home Guards Rules, 1962 (for short, "the Rules of

1962). Although, there is tenure provided under Rule 9 of the

Rules of 1962 of five years but petitioner's engagement was

extended from time to time and he has completed 15 years of

service.    Now, vide order dated 29.4.2011 (Annexure-1),

petitioner's engagement has been discontinued while rejecting

his claim for renewal.
</p><p id="p_4">                                !! 2 !!

            It is conteded by learned counsel for the petitioner

that as many as 81 persons were granted benefit of extension,

who have completed more than 15 years of service as Home

Guard. The case of petitioner is required to be considered at par

with these persons, whose names are appearing in the list

annexed with the Annexure-3 in the writ petition.



</p><p id="p_5">            After considering the arguments of learned counsel

for the petitioner, I am of the opinion that petitioner may file a

representation to the respondents pointing out the fact that

services of 81 Home Guards were extended beyond 15 years.

Upon filing the representation, the respondents are directed to

consider the case of petitioner for extension of his services as

Home Guard while considering the fact that services of 81

persons were extended beyond 15 years. The representation of

the petitioner shall be decided within one month from the date of

filing the certified copy of this order.



</p><p id="p_6">            The writ petition is disposed of in above terms.



</p><p id="p_7">                                   (GOPAL KRISHAN VYAS), J.
