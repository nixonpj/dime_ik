                          BEFORE THE MADURAI BENCH OF MADRAS HIGH COURT

                                              DATED : 13.02.2019

                                                      CORAM

                              THE HONOURABLE MRS. JUSTICE T.KRISHNAVALLI

                                              Crl. A.(MD)No.22 of 2019


                   Sabareeswaran                                       .. Appellant

                                                        Vs.


                   1.The State represented by its
                      The Deputy Superintendent of Police,
                      Paramakudi,
                      Ramanatharpuam District.           ... 1st Respondent

                   2.The State represented by its
                     The Inspector of Police,
                     Paramakudi Town Police Station,
                      Ramanathapuram District.
                     Crime No.21 of 2019                      ... 2nd Respondent


                   3.Murali                                   ... 3rd Respondent/
                                                                       Defacto Complainant


                   Prayer : This appeal is filed under <a href="/doc/50707649/" id="a_1">Section 14-A(2)</a> of the Scheduled
                   Castes and the <a href="/doc/25085007/" id="a_2">Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act</a>, 1989 as
                   amended by Act 1/2016, against the order dated 28.01.2019 made in
                   Cr.M.P.No.158 of 2019 on the file of the learned Principal District and
                   Sessions Judge, Ramanathapuram.


                              For Appellant          : Mr.M.S.Jeyakarthik

                              For R-1 &amp; R-2          : Mr.A.Robinson
                                                       Government Advocate (Crl.Side)
http://www.judis.nic.in
                                                           2


                                                JUDGMENT
</pre>
<p id="p_1">                            Heard Mr.M.S.Jeyakarthik, learned counsel appearing for the

                   appellant and Mr.A.Robinson, learned Government Advocate (Criminal

                   Side) appearing for the first and second respondents.



</p><p id="p_2">                            2.This Criminal Appeal has been filed to set aside the order,

                   dated 28.01.2019 made in Crl.M.P.No.158 of 2019 on the file of the

                   learned Principal District and Sessions Judge, Ramanathapuram and

                   enlarge the appellant on bail, in connection with Crime No.21 of 2019,

                   on the file of the respondent Police.
</p>
<p id="p_3">

</p><p id="p_4">                            3.The Appellant, who is arrested and remanded to judicial

                   custody on 19.01.2019, for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1258372/" id="a_3">Sections 147</a>,

                   <a href="/doc/1198799/" id="a_4">294(b)</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_5">323</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_6">506(i)</a> of IPC., and <a href="/doc/42436223/" id="a_7">Section 3(1)</a> (r), 3(1)(s)         of the

                   Schedule Castes and <a href="/doc/25085007/" id="a_8">Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities)

                   Amendment Act</a>, 2015, in Crime No.21 of 2019,            on the file of the

                   respondent police, seeks appeal bail.
</p>
<p id="p_5">

</p><p id="p_6">                            4.The learned counsel appearing for the appellant would

                   submit that the appellant has been falsely implicated in this case and

                   the injured have been discharged from the hospital and the appellant is

                   in custody from 19.01.2019 onwards. He would further submit that the
http://www.judis.nic.in
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                 3</span>

            defacto complainant also assaulted the appellant's party and due to that

            counter case has been filed as against the defacto complainant's party in

            Crime No.22 of 2019 and the appellant is undertake to abide the

            condition imposed by this Court.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><p id="p_8">                   5.The learned Government Advocate (criminal side) appearing for

            the respondent police, on instructions, would submit that totally six

            accused in this case and the appellant       herein is arrayed as A3. He

            would also submit that on 11.02.2019, the defacto complainant/Murali

            was appeared in person before the first respondent police and objected

            to grant bail to the appellant, he has also written a letter to that effect.

            He would further submit that the injured was discharged from the

            hospital and the investigation is still pending, if the appellant/A3 is

            released on bail, he will tamper with the prosecution witnesses and

            hamper the investigation and therefore, he objected to grant bail to the

            appellant.
</p>
<p id="p_9">

</p><p id="p_10">                       7.Considering the above facts and circumstances of the case

            and also considering the fact that the injured have been discharged from

            the hospital,     this Court is inclined to allow the Criminal Appeal by

            setting aside the order, dated 28.01.2019 made in Crl.M.P.No.158 of

            2019 on the file of the learned Principal District and Sessions Judge,

                   Ramanathapuram.
</p><p id="p_11">http://www.judis.nic.in
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                   4</span>

</p><p id="p_12">                       8.Accordingly, the Criminal Appeal is allowed and the order,

           dated 28.01.2019 made in Crl.M.P.No.158 of 2019 on the file of the

           learned Principal District and Sessions Judge, Ramanathapuram, is set

           aside. The appellant is ordered to be released on bail on his executing a

           bond for a sum of      Rs.10,000/- (Rupees Ten Thousand Only) with two

           sureties, each for a like sum to the satisfaction of the learned Principal

           District and Sessions Judge, Ramanathapuram, and on further condition

           that:
</p>
<p id="p_13">                       [a] the appellant shall appear before the respondent police,
           daily twice i.e. at 10:30 a.m., in the morning and 05:30 p.m., in the
           evening until further orders.
</p>
<p id="p_14">
</p><p id="p_15">                       [b] the appellant shall not tamper with evidence or witness
           either during investigation or trial.
</p>
<p id="p_16">
</p><p id="p_17">                       [c] the appellant shall not abscond either during investigation
           or trial.
</p>
<p id="p_18">
</p><p id="p_19">                       [d] On breach of any of the aforesaid conditions, the Trial
           Court is entitled to take appropriate action against the appellant in
           accordance with law, as if the conditions have been imposed and the
           appellants released on bail by the Trial Court itelf as laid down by the
           Hon'ble Supreme Court in <a href="/doc/440533/" id="a_9">P.K.Shaji vs. State of Kerala</a> [(2005)AIR
           SCW 5560].
</p>
