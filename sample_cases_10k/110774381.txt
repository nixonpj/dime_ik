<div class="doc_title">Gulli Kol vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 10 October, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">     THE HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH
               WP-15597-2017
           (GULLI KOL Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)


Jabalpur, Dated : 10-10-2017
     Shri Vikas Mishra, learned counsel for the
petitioner.
     Shri G.S. Thakur, learned Government Advocate for
the respondents/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">     Heard.
</p><p id="p_2">     The petitioner before this Court is presently
working as Head Master at Primary School Chandoora,
District Panna (M.P.) and he is aggrieved by the order of
transfer dated 14.09.2017 (Annexure P/1) passed by the
District Education Officer, District Panna
(M.P.)/respondent No.2. Against the aforesaid order, the
petitioner has already submitted a detailed
representation (Annexure P/4) on 20.09.2017, but the
same is still not decided.
</p><p id="p_3">     In the circumstances, the petition is disposed of
with a direction to the respondents/concerned authority
to decide the petitioner's representation (Annexure P/4),
in accordance with law, within a period four weeks from
the date of receipt of certified copy of this order.
</p><p id="p_4">     The petitioner is also free to file an application for
stay before the respondents/concerned authority and if
such an application for stay files by the petitioner within
a period of one week from today, the same shall be
decided by the respondents/concerned authority within a
 period of one week from the date of submission of its
application for stay along with certified copy of this
order, by speaking and reasoned order.
</p><p id="p_5">     Till the application for stay is decided, the
petitioner shall be allowed to continue at the present
place of posting.
</p><p id="p_6">     It is made clear that this court has not expressed
any opinion on the merits of the case.
</p><p id="p_7">     Accordingly, this petition stands disposed of.
     Certified copy as per rules.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                               (SUBODH ABHYANKAR)
                                      JUDGE
