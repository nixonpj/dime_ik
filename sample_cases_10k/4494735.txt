<div class="doc_title">Mantu Kumar @ Hagna vs The State Of Bihar on 23 July, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                 Criminal Miscellaneous No.10320 of 2013
                            Arising Out of PS.Case No. -74 Year- 2008 Thana -BIHPUR District- -
                 ======================================================
                 Mantu Kumar @ Hagna son of Byash Kumar, resident of village- Sonbarsa
                 P.S. Bihpur, District Bhagalpur under the guardianship of Byas Kumar the
                 father.
                                                                        .... .... Petitioner/s
                                                   Versus
                 The State of Bihar
                                                                   .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s        :   None.
                 For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr. Shyam Kr. Singh, APP
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE BIRENDRA PRASAD VERMA
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">7   23-07-2015               Despite repeated calls, none appears on behalf of the
                 petitioner in support of the present application.
</p><p id="p_2">                             Learned Additional Public Prosecutor appearing on
                 behalf of the State, by referring to the averments made in the
                 counter-affidavit filed on behalf of the Superintendent of Police,
                 Naugachia, in compliance of the order dated 30.08.2012 passed by
                 this Court, submits that the petitioner was produced before the
                 Medical Board and his age was assessed to be 40-45 years on
                 15.06.2012 i.e. the date of examination of the petitioner by the
                 Medical Board. Hence, according to him, on the date of
                 occurrence the petitioner was aged about 36 years. Therefore, his
                 claim to be juvenile on the date of occurrence cannot be
                 entertained and he has to face regular trial of the criminal offence
                 registered for sections 302, 364 and some other allied offences<a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_1"> of
                 the Indian Penal Code</a>.
</p><p id="p_3">                             Learned Additional Public Prosecutor appears to be
                 correct, as no rejoinder affidavit has been filed on behalf of the
                 petitioner to the aforesaid counter-affidavit filed way back in the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">         Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.10320 of 2013 (7) dt.23-07-2015</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                   2/2</span>




                     year 2012.
</p><p id="p_4">                                  In the aforesaid facts and circumstances, this Court is of
                     the opinion that the present matter has become infructuous and it
                     is, accordingly, dismissed.
</p>


<p id="p_5">                                             (Birendra Prasad Verma, J)
BTiwary/-
</p>
