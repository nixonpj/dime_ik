<div class="doc_title">Bilua Kol vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 3 October, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">         THE HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH

                       M.Cr.C. No.38144/2018
                    (Bilua &amp; others Vs. State of M.P.)
                                        1

Jabalpur, Dated: 03.10.2018
     Shri Vivek Shukla, learned counsel for the applicants.
     Shri B.S. Kushwaha, learned Government Advocate for
the respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">     Heard on this first bail application under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of
Cr.P.C. filed on behalf of the applicants in connection with
Crime    No.648/2017       registered       by   Police   Station   Van
Parikshetra, Churhat, Tehsil Rampur Naikin, District Sidhi for
offence punishable under <a href="/doc/1783355/" id="a_2">Sections 9</a>, <a href="/doc/463532/" id="a_3">27</a>, <a href="/doc/942665/" id="a_4">29</a>, <a href="/doc/912273/" id="a_5">39</a>, <a href="/doc/672187/" id="a_6">51</a> of Wild
Life (Protection) Act, 1972.
</p><p id="p_2">     As per the case of the prosecution, on 30.06.2018 at about
10:00 am the Forest Officer of Forest Circle, Churhat
intercepted the applicants along with other co-accused persons,
who have found in possession of dead-body of spotted deer
(Cheetal) in the village Kankati, near Kathehi Nala. Skin, head
and legs etc. of the said spotted deer have been seized from the
possession of the applicants and other co-accused persons.
</p><p id="p_3">     Learned counsel for the applicants submitted that the
applicants are innocent and have falsely been implicated in the
crime. It is further submitted that the applicants are alleged to
have been found in the possession of the various parts of the
killed deer but there is no direct evidence regarding hunting of
the dear. It is also submitted that the applicants are in judicial
custody since 24.08.2018 and 28.08.2018 respectively. In view
of the aforesaid, it has been prayed that the applicants be
released on bail.
</p><p id="p_4">     Learned Government Advocate for the respondent/State
on the other hand has opposed the application.
</p><p id="p_5">     Keeping in view the facts and circumstances of the case,
particularly, the fact as pointed out by the learned counsel for
the applicant and the fact that there is no direct evidence
                                                THE HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH

                                                          M.Cr.C. No.38144/2018
                                                      (Bilua &amp; others Vs. State of M.P.)
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                                      2</span>
                                    regarding hunting of the spotted deer (Cheetal), in the opinion
                                    of this Court, it is a fit case to enlarge the applicants on bail.
</p><p id="p_6">                                          Consequently, this application for bail under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_7">Section 439</a>
                                    of the Code of Criminal Procedure filed on behalf of applicants
                                    Bilua Kol, Ram Piyare Kewat and Bajrang Kol, stand
                                    allowed.
</p><p id="p_7">                                          It is directed that the applicants shall be released on bail
                                    on furnishing a personal bond in the sum of Rs.30,000/-
                                    (Rupees Thirty Thousand Only) with one solvent surety each
                                    in the like amount to the satisfaction of the trial Court for their
                                    appearance before that Court on all dates fixed in the case and
                                    for complying with the conditions enumerated under <a href="/doc/1781078/" id="a_8">Section
                                    437</a> (3)<a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_9"> of the Code</a> of Criminal Procedure.
</p><p id="p_8">                                          Certified copy as per rules.
</p>
