		For Petitioner     :   Mr.R.Parthiban

					O R D E R
</pre>
<p id="p_1">	This writ petition has been filed praying for a Writ of Certiorari, to call for and quash the order of the second respondent, dated 19.11.2004, attaching the properties of the petitioner for non-payment of the Employees State Insurance contributions.
</p>
<p id="p_2">	2. The only contention raised by the learned counsel for the petitioner is that the petitioner has been declared to be a sick company, as per the provisions of <a href="/doc/438563/" id="a_2">The Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act</a>, 1985, and therefore, the petitioner is not liable to pay the Employees State Insurance contributions.
</p>
<p id="p_3">	3. At this stage of the hearing of the writ petition the learned counsel appearing on behalf of the respondents had placed before this Court a decision of a Full Bench of this Court, reported in <a href="/doc/707716/" id="a_3">Gowri Spinning Mills (P) Ltd. V. Assistant Provident Commissioner</a> (2006(5) CTC 1), wherein it has been held that even if a company is declared to be sick, as per the BIFR proceedings, it is liable to pay the Employees State Insurance contributions.
</p>
<p id="p_4">	4. The learned counsel appearing on behalf of the petitioner had not refuted the submissions made by the learned counsel for the respondents.
</p>
<p id="p_5">	5. In view of the decision cited supra, the present writ petition stands dismissed. No costs. Consequently, connected writ petition miscellaneous petition is closed.	
</p>
