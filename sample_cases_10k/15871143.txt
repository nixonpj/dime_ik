Anand                IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT BOMBAY
                         ORDINARY ORIGINAL CIVIL JURISDICTION

                            ARBITRATION PETITION NO. 1185 OF 2018

         Indusind Bank Ltd.                                                  .Petitioner

                      Vs.

         Rahi Electronics Pvt. Ltd. &amp; ors.                                   .Respondents

         Mr. Sachin Masurkar a/w Ms Aarti Kulkarni &amp; Mr. Nilesh Parab,
         Advocate, for the Petitioner

                            CORAM       :      G. S. Kulkarni, J.
</pre>
<pre id="pre_2">                            DATE        :      16.01.2019
         P.C.

         .                  Heard learned counsel for the Petitioner.



</pre><p id="p_1">         2.                 This Petition is filed under <a href="/doc/1120409/" id="a_1">Section 9</a> of the Arbitration and

         <a href="/doc/1306164/" id="a_2">Conciliation Act</a>, 1996. The case of the Petitioner is that a Loan

         Agreement dated 31.08.2016 was executed between the Petitioner and

         the Respondent No. 1 whereby financial facilities for Rs. 35,00,000/-

         would be extended to the Respondent No. 1. It is the case of the

         Petitioner that the Respondents have defaulted in making repayment of

         the said loan. It is stated that the Petitioner has by its notice dated

         07.08.2018 already re-called the loan and that as on date, the

         Respondents are liable to pay an amount of about Rs.38,05,131/-.


</p><p id="p_2">                                                                                            1 of 3


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">        ::: Uploaded on - 17/01/2019                       ::: Downloaded on - 18/01/2019 05:01:19 :::</span>
                                                                         ARBP 1185-18.doc


 Learned counsel for the Petitioner states that private service of this

 Petition is already effected and Affidavit of service to that effect dated

 15.12.2018 is already placed on record.
</p>
<p id="p_3">

</p><p id="p_4"> 3.                In the above circumstances, learned counsel for the

 Petitioner has prayed for ad-interim relief in terms of prayer clause (b).



</p><p id="p_5"> 4.                Having heard learned counsel for the Petitioner and

 having perused the documents as placed on record, in my prima facie

 opinion, it appears that the financial facilities were extended to the

 Respondents under the said Loan Agreement and there appears to be

 only default on the part of the Respondents in paying the instalments

 of the said loan. There is an arbitration agreement between the parties.



</p><p id="p_6"> 5.                In the above circumstances, at this stage of the

 proceedings, it is in the interest of justice, an ad-interim relief in terms

 of part of prayer clause (b) is granted, to the following extent :-

</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">                   "(b) Pending the hearing and final disposal of
                   the arbitration proceedings or at any time after
                   the making of the Arbitral Award but before it
                   is enforced in accordance with <a href="/doc/1286256/" id="a_3">Section 36</a> of
                   the Arbitration and <a href="/doc/1306164/" id="a_4">Conciliation Act</a>, 1996, this
                   Hon'ble Court be pleased to direct the
                   Respondents to disclose the location of the
                   other assets belong to and owned by the
                   Respondents and each of them."
</blockquote>

<p id="p_7"> In addition to the Court notice, learned counsel for the Petitioner is

 permitted to serve the Respondents by private service. This is for the

 reason that all the Respondents, as seen from the title of the Petition

 are having their addresses at Mumbai. Affidavit of service to that effect

 be placed on record before the adjourned date of hearing.
