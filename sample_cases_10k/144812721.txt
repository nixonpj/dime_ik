<div class="doc_title">Rikta Biswas vs The State Of West Bengal &amp; Ors on 28 November, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                  1

28.11.2018
      110
   PK

                  W.P. 20356(W) of 2018
                     Rikta Biswas
                         -vs-
             The State of West Bengal &amp; Ors.

         Mr. Asraf Mandal
                       ... for the Petitioner

         Mr. Safik Dewan
                        ... for the State


          The affidavit of service filed by the learned Advocate for the petitioner in Court today be

  kept on record.

         The learned Advocate for the petitioner submits that before attaining the age of

  superannuation, the husband of the petitioner was an employee of the concerned School and he

  died-in-harness on 3.2.2014. Pension Payment Order was issued in favour of the petitioner on

  16.10.2014 and the gratuity amount of the husband of the petitioner was released on

  14.10.2015.

         Although the petitioner was entitled to payment of gratuity amount from the date death of

  the husband of the petitioner, the same was not paid.       By presenting this writ petition, the

  petitioner prays for an order on the respondents to award interest on delayed payment of gratuity</pre>

<p id="p_1">  amount.
</p>
<p id="p_2">         I have heard the learned Advocates for the parties and have also considered the orders

  passed by different Hon'ble Judges on the writ petitions involving similar issues. It is reported

  that the State has accepted all such orders.
</p>
<p id="p_3">         In view thereof, this writ petition stands disposed of with a direction upon the respondents

  and particularly the concerned Treasury Officer to pay to the petitioner interest on gratuity

  amount @ 8% per annum from the date of death of the husband of the petitioner till the date of

  actual payment within 16 weeks from the date of service of an authenticated copy of this order

  failing which, additional interest @ 2% per annum shall be payable to the petitioner.

</p><p id="p_4">         There will be no order as to costs.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                   2</span><p id="p_5">

       Urgent certified photostat copy of this order, if applied for, shall be given to the parties as

expeditiously as possible on compliance of all necessary formalities.



</p><p id="p_6">                          ( Rajasekhar Mantha, J.)
