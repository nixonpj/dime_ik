<div class="doc_title">Ramlakhan vs State Of Rajasthan on 10 July, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">    

 
 
 

 IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR RAJASTHAN 
 JAIPUR BENCH, JAIPUR
ORDER 
SB Cr Misc Bail Application No.6939/2015
Ramlakhan versus State of Rajasthan
10.7.2015
HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE MN BHANDARI
Mr Anil Jain - for the petitioner
Mr Sudesh Saini,   PP  for the State
BY THE COURT:</pre>

<p id="p_1">		Learned counsel  submits that other than the present case, no other case is pending against the petitioner, who has been implicated for offence under <a href="/doc/1101188/" id="a_1">sections 379</a> and <a href="/doc/812083/" id="a_2">411</a> IPC. He undertakes not to commit any offence during the period of bail, if granted. 
</p>
<p id="p_2">		On the other hand, learned PP has opposed the bail application. 
</p>
<p id="p_3">		After considering rival submissions of the parties and without expressing any opinion on merits of the case, which may otherwise affect  outcome of the trial,  looking to the facts of this case, I am of the view that the petitioner deserves to be enlarged on bail. Accordingly, bail application under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_3">section 439</a> CrPC  is allowed. It is ordered that petitioner  Ramlakhan may be released on bail in FIR No.265/2015,  registered with  Police Station  - Mania, Dholpur,   provided he/she  furnishes a personal bond in the sum of Rs.50,000/- together with one surety  in the like amount to the satisfaction of the learned trial court for his/her appearance before that Court on all subsequent dates of hearing and as and when called upon to do so. The  petitioner shall not commit any offence during the period of bail, otherwise, prosecution would be at liberty to file application for cancellation of his bail.		
</p><p id="p_4">		(MN BHANDARI), J.
</p><p id="p_5">bnsharma

All corrections made in the judgment/ order have been incorporated in the judgment/ order being emailed.
</p>
<p id="p_6"> (BN Sharma)
PS-cum-JW
