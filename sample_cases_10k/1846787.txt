<p id="p_1">Heard learned Sr. Counsel, Mr.B.B.
	Naik appearing with learned counsel, Mr.N.D. Gohil and learned
	counsel, Mr.P.J. Kanabar for the applicant-accused of Special
	Criminal Application No.2010 of 2011 and Criminal
	Misc. Application Nos.11440 to 11441 of 2011 respectively,
	learned A.P.P., Mr.K.P. Raval appearing for the respondent
	no.1-State and learned counsel, Mr.H.K. Prachchhak for the
	respondent no.2-original complainant in all matters.
</p>	  

<p id="p_2">An affidavit of the respondent
	no.2-original complainant has been tendered by the learned counsel,
	Mr.Prachchhak for the respondent no.2-original complainant stating
	that the parties have amicable settled the dispute with regard to
	land in question. It is also specifically stated that there is no
	grievance remain and, therefore, FIR may be quashed and set aside as
	prayed for.
</p>	  

<p id="p_3">Therefore, having regard to the
	facts and circumstances and also the observations made by the
	Hon'ble Apex
	Court in a judgment reported in case
	of  <a href="/doc/1936939/" id="a_1">Jagdish
	Chanana And Others v. State of Haryana and Another</a>,
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">	(2008)</span>
	15 SCC 704, wherein
	it has been observed that when the dispute is of purely personal
	nature and if the parties have settled, FIR may be quashed, all
	these petitions deserve to be allowed.
</p>	  

<p id="p_4">Similarly,
	it has been observed laying down the guidelines by the Hon'ble Apex
	Court in case of <a href="/doc/1442974/" id="a_2">Madan
	Mohan Abbot v. State of Punjab</a>, (2008) 4 SCC 582
	that the approach should be pragmatic based on the ground of
	realities. Therefore, considering the fact that the parties have
	amicable settled the dispute, which has been reduced to writing, all
	these petitions deserve to be allowed.
</p>	  


<p id="p_5">	In
	the circumstances, all these petitions are allowed. The impugned FIR
	being C.R.No.I-52/2011 registered with Savarkundla Taluka Police
	Station dated 24.07.2011 is hereby quashed and set aside. Rule is
	made absolute. Direct service is permitted.
</p>	  


<p id="p_6">	Registry
	to keep a copy of this judgment in each matter.
</p>
