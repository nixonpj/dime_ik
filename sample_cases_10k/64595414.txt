<div class="doc_title">Udesingbhai Mansingbhai Dhamot vs State Of Gujarat on 11 February, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">     R/CR.MA/2828/2015                                       ORDER




          IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

  CRIMINAL MISC.APPLICATION (FOR REGULAR BAIL) NO. 2828 of 2015

================================================================
             UDESINGBHAI MANSINGBHAI DHAMOT....Applicant(s)
                               Versus
                   STATE OF GUJARAT....Respondent(s)
================================================================
Appearance:
HL PATEL ADVOCATES, ADVOCATE for the Applicant(s) No. 1
MR KL PANDYA, APP for the RESPONDENT(s) No. 1
================================================================

         CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE J.B.PARDIWALA

                            Date : 11/02/2015


                             ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">       Rule returnable forthwith. Mr.Pandya, the learned APP
waives service of notice of rule for and on behalf of the
respondent-State.
</p>

<p id="p_2">       The present application is filed under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of the
Code    of     Criminal   Procedure,     1973,   for   regular   bail   in
connection with I-C.R.No.129 of 2014 registered with the
Santrampur Police Station, District Mahisagar, of the offence
punishable under <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_2">Sections 323</a>, <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_3">498A</a> and <a href="/doc/92983/" id="a_4">306</a> of the Indian
Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">       The learned advocate appearing on behalf of the
applicant submits that considering the nature of the offence,
the applicant may be enlarged on regular bail by imposing
suitable conditions.
</p>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                Page 1 of 4</span>
<p id="p_4">         R/CR.MA/2828/2015                                     ORDER




        The learned APP appearing on behalf of the respondent-
State has opposed grant of regular bail looking to the nature
and gravity of the offence.
</p>
<p id="p_5">        The learned advocates appearing on behalf of the
respective parties do not press for further reasoned order.
</p>
<p id="p_6">        I have heard the learned advocates appearing on behalf
of the respective parties and perused the papers of the
investigation and considered the allegations levelled against
the applicant and the role played by the applicant. I have also
considered the fact that charge-sheet is filed.
</p>
<p id="p_7">        This Court has also taken into consideration the law laid
down by the Hon'ble Apex Court in the case of <a href="/doc/1563495/" id="a_5">Sanjay Chandra
v. Central Bureau of Investigation</a>, reported in [2012]1 SCC 40.
</p>
<p id="p_8">        In    the    facts   and   circumstances   of   the   case    and
considering the nature of the allegations made against the
applicant in the FIR, without discussing the evidence in detail,
prima facie, this Court is of the opinion that this is a fit case to
exercise the discretion and enlarge the applicant on regular
bail.
</p>
<p id="p_9">        Hence, the present application is allowed and the
applicant is ordered to be released on regular bail in
connection with the I-CR.No.129 of 2014 registered with the
Santrampur Police Station, District Mahisagar, on executing a
personal bond of Rs.10,000/- (Rupees Ten Thousand only) with
one surety of the like amount to the satisfaction of the trial
Court and subject to the conditions that he shall;
</p>


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                   Page 2 of 4</span>
<p id="p_10">     R/CR.MA/2828/2015                                  ORDER




     [a] not take undue advantage of liberty or misuse
     liberty;
</p>
<p id="p_11">     [b]    not act in a manner injuries to the interest of the
            prosecution;</p><pre id="pre_2">

     [c]    surrender passport, if any, to the lower court within
     a      week;

     [d]    not leave the State of Gujarat without prior
</pre><p id="p_12">            permission of the Sessions Judge concerned;
</p>
<p id="p_13">     [e]    mark presence before the concerned Police Station
     on     alternate Monday of every English calendar month
     for    a period of six months between 11:00 a.m. and 2:00
            p.m.;
</p>
<p id="p_14">     [f]  furnish the present address of residence to the
          Investigating Officer and also to the Court at the
     time of execution of the bond and shall not change the
          residence without prior permission of this Court;
</p>
<p id="p_15">     [g]    not enter the revenue limits of Santrampur till the
            conclusion of the trial, except for the purpose of
            marking presence at the concerned Police Station.
</p>
<p id="p_16">     The authorities will release the applicant only if he is not
required in connection with any other offence for the time
being. If breach of any of the above conditions is committed,
the Sessions Judge concerned will be free to issue warrant or
take appropriate action in the matter.
</p>
<p id="p_17">     Bail bond to be executed before the lower Court having
jurisdiction to try the case. It will be open for the concerned
Court to delete, modify and/or relax any of the above
conditions, in accordance with law.
</p>
<p id="p_18">     At the trial, the trial Court shall not be influenced by the
observations of preliminary nature qua the evidence at this


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                             Page 3 of 4</span>
        R/CR.MA/2828/2015                              ORDER


stage made by this Court while enlarging the applicant on bail.
Rule is made absolute to the aforesaid extent. Direct service is
permitted.
</p>

<p id="p_19">                                             (J.B.PARDIWALA, J.)
MOIN




<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                            Page 4 of 4</span>
