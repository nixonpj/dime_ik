<div class="doc_title">Ramswroop Pandit &amp; Anr. vs The State Of Bihar on 5 March, 2012</div><pre id="pre_1">                   IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.39974 of 2011
                 ======================================================
                 1. Ramswroop Pandit S/o Late Ghanshayam Pandit R/o Village-
                   Gangter,PS-Rajauli,District-Nawada.
                 2. Akash Devi Wife Of Ramswroop Pandit R/o Village-Gangter,PS-
                 Rajauli,District-Nawada.                         .... .... Petitioners
                                                   Versus
                  The State Of Bihar                            .... .... Opposite Party
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     : Mr.
                 For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr.
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE AKHILESH CHANDRA
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">3   05-03-2012                    Heard learned counsel for the petitioners and

                 learned Additional Public Prosecutor for the State.

</p><p id="p_2">                                   Both the petitioners are named accused in this

                 case being parents in-law of deceased daughter of the informant

                 who died within five years of her marriage under unnatural

                 circumstances though intimation was given about her illness but

                 without waiting for arrival of her parents and other family

                 members, body was disposed of. She was suffering from some

                 demand of dowry.         Submission is of general and omnibus

                 allegation.   Death of the deceased was some sort of illness.

                 Informant has entered into compromise and petition is on record

                 of the court below.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                  If it is so, in the event of informant and her

                 husband appearing before the court below supports contents and
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                     2</span>




            factum of compromise with their free will and consent without any

            coercion and husband of the informant also stood as one of the

            bailers,   the petitioners, in the event of surrender or arrest within

            four weeks, are directed to be enlarged on bail on furnishing bonds

            of Rs.10,000/- (Rupees ten thousand) with two sureties of the like

            amount each to the satisfaction of       Chief Judicial Magistrate,

            Nawada, in Rajauli P.S. Case no. 74 of 2010, subject to the

            conditions laid down in <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_1">Section 438(2)</a> of the Code of Criminal

            Procedure with additional condition that the petitioners         shall

            remain present before the Court below till disposal of the case.

            In the event of failure to attend the Court on two consecutive dates

            without any reasonable explanation, the privilege granted shall be

            deemed to be cancelled.
</p>
<p id="p_4">

</p><p id="p_5">                                  (Akhilesh Chandra, J)
A.Ahmad/-
</p>
