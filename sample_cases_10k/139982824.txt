                            JUDGMENT

<p id="p_1">      Aggrieved by the Judgment and award passed by the

Tribunal,   the   Insurer    has       filed   the    present   appeal

questioning its liability to satisfy the award.


</p><p id="p_2">      2.    The Tribunal while considering the claim of the

claimant awarded a compensation of Rs.40,000/- with

interest at the rate of 6% per annum. The amount

awarded is a paltry sum. However, the learned counsel for
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                3</span>




the appellant contends that it is a question of liability and

hence the quantum awarded should be overlooked. A limit

of Rs.10,000/- has been postulated in terms of <a href="/doc/51393310/" id="a_1">Section

173(2)</a> of the Motor Vehicle Act.          No appeal would lie

where     the   subject   matter   in   dispute   is   less   than

Rs.10,000/-. <a href="/doc/785258/" id="a_2">The Act</a> was promulgated in the year 1988.

We are presently in the year 2013. If the value of money is

to be applied, then it is just and proper that the Court

should refrain from interfering purely on the question of

quantum. In terms of the Act, the minimum compensation

to be granted would be Rs.50,000/- even in cases of a

dispute on liability. Hence, without going into the question

of liability, in view of a paltry sum being awarded, the

appeal requires to be rejected.
</p>
<p id="p_3">        Ordered accordingly.
</p>
