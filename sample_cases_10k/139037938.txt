<pre id="pre_1">




HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT ALLAHABAD
 
 

Court No. - 51
 

 
Case :- APPLICATION U/S 482 No. - 29425 of 2019
 

 
Applicant :- Sanjay Kumar
 
Opposite Party :- State Of U.P. And 2 Others
 
Counsel for Applicant :- Jay Babu Kesharwani
 
Counsel for Opposite Party :- G.A.
 

 
Hon'ble Vipin Sinha,J.
</pre> 

 
<p id="p_1">	Heard learned counsel for the applicant and learned A.G.A. for the State.
</p> 
<p id="p_2">	The present application has been filed for quashing the order dated 16.5.2019 passed by Addl. District Judge/Fast Track Court No.1,  Aligarh in ST No.473/<a href="/doc/120603172/" id="a_1">2015 State vs. Sanjay Kumar</a> under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_2">Sections 498A</a>, <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_3">302</a> IPC and ¾ DP Act PS Gabhana District Aligarh arising out of case crime no.139/2015.
</p> 
<p id="p_3">	Contention of the learned counsel for the applicant is to the effect that the applicant had filed an application on 4.4.2019 before the  learned trial court to adjourn the cross examination of PW-1 and PW-2 namely Raj Kumar Sharma and Dharmendra. However, this application has been rejected vide order dated 16.5.2019 which is under challenge in the present application.
</p> 
<p id="p_4">	Perusal of the impugned order dated 16.5.2019 shows that the repeated opportunity was given by the court concerned to PW-1 Raj Kumar Sharma and PW-2 Dharmendra for their cross examination but in spite of the same, the said witnesses had not appeared before the court concerned and their cross examination could not be done and thus, the court concerned had closed the opportunity for cross examination of PW-1 and PW-2 as far back in the year 2017 and 2018 respectively. The court concerned while passing the impugned order has clearly observed that the application to adjourn the cross examination of PW-1 and PW-2 has been filed in the year 2019 just to linger on the proceedings. The relevant extract of the findings recorded by the court concerned is being quoted herein as under:-
</p>

	The court concerned has also observed the fact that on the one hand, the Hon'ble High Court has passed the order for expeditious disposal of the matter and on the other hand, every effort is being taken by the applicant in the trial court to delay the proceedings.
</p> 
<p id="p_6">	After hearing the learned counsel for the applicant, learned A.G.A. and after perusing the order impugned as well averments made in the present application, this Court is of the opinion that learned counsel for the applicant could not point out any illegality or perversity in the order impugned which may warrant any interference by this Court. 
</p> 
<p id="p_7">	Accordingly, the application u/s 482 <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_4">CrPC</a> lacks merit and is dismissed.
