<div class="doc_title">Sunil Kumar vs The State Of Bihar And Ors on 5 March, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">         IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                   Civil Writ Jurisdiction Case No.3703 of 2019
     ======================================================
</pre><p id="p_1">     Sunil Kumar, aged about 26 years (male), S/o Paspat Sah, R/o Gosaipur, P.O.
     Madhwapur, P.S. Bairgania,Dist.-Sitamarhi

                                                                ... ... Petitioner/s
                                       Versus
</p><p id="p_2">1.   The State of Bihar through the Principal Secretary, Registration, Excise and
     Prohibition Department, Govt. of Bihar, Patna
</p><p id="p_3">2.   The Collector-cum-District Magistrate,Sitamarhi
</p><p id="p_4">3.   The Superintendent of Excise, Sitamarhi
</p><p id="p_5">4.   The Sub-Inspector, Excise,Sitamarhi

                                               ... ... Respondent/s
     ======================================================
     Appearance :
</p><p id="p_6">     For the Petitioner/s   :      Mr.Ashok Kumar Jha, Advocate
     For the Respondent/s   :      Mr.Anil Kr. Sinha (GA1)
     ======================================================
     CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE JYOTI SARAN
             and
             HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE ARVIND SRIVASTAVA
                         ORAL JUDGMENT
     (Per: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE JYOTI SARAN)

      Date : 05-03-2019

                     Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned

      counsel appearing on behalf of the state.

</p><p id="p_7">                     The petitioner prays for provisional release of the

      Motorcycle bearing Chasis No. ME4JC652HG7195701, Engine

      No. JC65E-070631526, which has been seized in connection

      with Excise Case No. C2- 580 of 2018 for the offences

      punishable under Section 30(a) of the Bihar Prohibition and

      <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_1">Excise Act</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                      It is stated by learned counsel for the petitioner that

      confiscation proceeding is pending and the vehicle is lying
 Patna High Court CWJC No.3703 of 2019 dt.05-03-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                           2/3</span>




         under the open sky in the police station. The seizure list reflects

         the seizure of 15 liters Nepali Saufi wine.

</p><p id="p_9">                         Having heard learned counsel for the parties and

         taking note of the nature of seizure made as well as the fact that

         the confiscation proceeding is pending, we direct that the

         vehicle in question be released provisionally in favour of the

         petitioner on production of ownership and registration with

         respect to vehicle in question in his name before the Collector-

         cum-District Magistrte, Sitamarhi with two sureties to the extent

         of the value of the vehicle as indicated in the insurance

         document. The petitioner while submitting the sureties shall also

         furnish the following affidavits/undertakings:

</p><p id="p_10">                         (i) That the vehicle in question has never been

         involved in any offence of similar nature in past and shall not

         indulge in similar offence in future:
</p>
<p id="p_11">                         (ii) That the petitioner shall not indulge in creating

         any third party right or interest in respect of the vehicle during

         the pendency of the confiscation proceeding and shall not

         alienate the vehicle during this period.

</p><p id="p_12">                         (iii) The petitioner shall furnish an undertaking to

         produce the vehicle before the confiscating authority as and

         when required.
</p><p id="p_13">              Patna High Court CWJC No.3703 of 2019 dt.05-03-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                        3/3</span>




</p><p id="p_14">                                      (iv) Prior to release of the vehicle, a Panchanama

                      would be got prepared by the Collector-cum-District Magistrate,

                      Sitamarhi, wherein the photograph of the vehicle shall be taken

                      and will be certified by the petitioner and the same shall be kept

                      on record so that in future, if so required, it may be used as a

                      secondary evidence. The petitioner shall furnish an undertaking

                      not to challenge the said Panchanama in course of trial.

</p><p id="p_15">                                      The release shall be allowed within a period of 14

                      days from the date of submission of the sureties and the

                      undertakings as stated above. This release would, however, be

                      subject to finalization of the confiscation proceeding.

</p><p id="p_16">                                      With the observations/directions above, this writ

                      petition is allowed.</p><pre id="pre_2">




                                                     (Jyoti Saran, J)


                                                 ( Arvind Srivastava, J)
