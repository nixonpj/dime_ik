                                                        ORDER
</pre>

<p id="p_1">                               The revision petitioner filed a suit for partition in the year 1994.

                          It was decreed on 30.03.2011, against which, the respondents in the

                          above Civil Revision Petitions     preferred appeal viz., A.S.No.16 of

                          2011, A.S.No.37 of 2013 and A.S.No.7 of 2012 respectively.          Even

                          though the records were received by the lower appellate Court, the

                          appeals were not    disposed of till date.



</p><p id="p_2">                               2. Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that the petitioner

                          had waited for   a long period of       17 years to get the   preliminary



http://www.judis.nic.in
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                              3</span>

                          decree in the partition suit. After that, it has been prolonged for the

                          past six years by way of filing    appeals.    The appeals filed by the

                          respondents viz., A.S.No.16 of 2011, A.S.No.37 of 2013 and A.S.No.7

                          of 2012 are yet to be disposed of.      She would further submit that the

                          petitioner is a senior citizen and therefore she seeks earlier disposal

                          of the appeal.
</p>
<p id="p_3">

</p><p id="p_4">                                3. It is seen that obviously 25 years         have passed     after

                          institution of the suit for partition and now the petitioner is holding a

                          preliminary decree without any benefits.



</p><p id="p_5">                                4. Considering the long pendency of the litigation, this Court is

                          inclined to give a direction to the Additional District Judge, Fast Track

                          Court, Arani to dispose of A.S.No.16 of 2011, A.S.No.37 of 2013 and

                          A.S.No.7 of 2012 within a period of two months from the date of

                          receipt of a copy of this order.
</p>
<p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">                                5. The Civil Revision Petitions are disposed of with the above

                          direction. No costs.
</p>
