<div class="doc_title">Smt. Jalisa @ Jarina vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh Thr on 23 June, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">            1                               M.Cr.C. No.6157/2017


23.06.2017
     Shri A.K. Jaiswal, Advocate for the applicant.
     Shri R.D. Agarwal, Panel Lawyer for the respondent/</pre>
<p id="p_1">State.
</p><p id="p_2">     Learned counsel for the rival parties are heard and
case diary is perused.
</p><p id="p_3">     This is first bail application under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> Cr.P.C.
filed by the applicant for grant of bail.
</p><p id="p_4">     The applicant has been arrested on 06.05.2017 by
Police   Station   Madhoganj,    District    Gwalior,   M.P.   in
connection with Crime No.38/2017 registered in relation
to the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/9545/" id="a_2">Sections 376-D</a>/<a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_3">120-B</a>,
<a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_4">506</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_5">34</a> of IPC.
</p><p id="p_5">     As per prosecution case, Raja and Imran who are
brother-in-law of the prosecutrix have committed rape
upon her and during this act the present applicant closed
the door of the room from outside.
</p><p id="p_6">     It is submitted by the counsel for applicant that the
applicant is mother-in-law of the prosecutrix and it is not
possible that any mother allow her sons to commit rape
with their sister-in-law (bhabi). Due to the family dispute
the applicant has falsely been implicated. It is also
submitted that the applicant being a lady cannot be
prescribed for the offence punishable under <a href="/doc/1279834/" id="a_6">Section 376</a>
of IPC and in view of the judgment of the Hon'ble Apex
Court in the case of Priya Patel Vs. State of M.P. &amp;
another reported in (2006)3 SCC (criminal) 96. The
applicant is in custody since 06.05.2017 and she is not
required for further investigation. In these circumstances,
learned counsel prays for grant of bail to the applicant.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                    2                           M.Cr.C. No.6157/2017</span>


<p id="p_7">             Learned Panel Lawyer for the State opposed the
        application and prayed for its rejection by contending that
        on the basis of the allegations and the material available
        on record no case for grant of bail is made out.
</p><p id="p_8">             After considering the submissions made by the
        learned counsel for the parties and looking to the facts
        and circumstances of the case, but without commenting
        on the merits of the case, the application filed by the
        applicant is allowed. The applicant-Jalisa @ Jarina is
        directed to be released on bail on her furnishing a
        personal   bond in the sum of Rs.45,000/- (Rupees
        Forty Five Thousand only) with one solvent surety of
        the like amount to the satisfaction of the trial Court for
        her regular appearance before the trial court during trial
        with a condition that she shall remain present before the
        court concerned during trial and shall also abide by the
        conditions enumerated under <a href="/doc/259682/" id="a_7">Section 437(3)</a> Cr.P.C.
</p><p id="p_9">             Certified copy as per rules.
</p>


<p id="p_10">                                            (S.K. Awasthi)
                                                 Judge
neetu
