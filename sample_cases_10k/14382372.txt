<div class="doc_title">Abutaleb vs Unknown on 11 September, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                   1


11.09.2018
Ct.28
RP 28


                                WP 3328 (W) of 2018

                   In the matter of : Abutaleb
                                                       .... Petitioner
                                         -
</pre>
<p id="p_1">                   Md. Younush Mondal, Adv.
</p><p id="p_2">                                                       .... For the Petitioner


                 Despite service nobody appears on behalf of the State.

                  Ms.   Susmita      Biswas      Chodhury,    learned   Advocate     who

             ordinarily appears for the State is directed to appear in this matter.

             A copy of the writ petition is handed over to her in Court today.

</p><p id="p_3">                  The   petitioner    complains        that   his   representation    for

             compensation in respect of acquisition of his land has been kept

             pending by the respondent authorities.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                  Learned Counsel for the State submits that the concerned

             respondent being the respondent no.4 be directed to consider the

             petitioner's representation.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                  In view of such submission made on behalf of the State, the

             writ petition being WP 3328 (W) of 2018 is disposed off by directing

             the concerned District Magistrate, being the respondent no.4, to

             consider the petitioner's representation (a copy whereof is Annexed

             at page 21 being Annexure P-3 to the petition) in accordance with
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                    2</span>


law and communicate a reasoned decision to the petitioner within

eight weeks of service of this order.
</p>
<p id="p_6">      Nothing in this order shall be construed as an adjudication

on the merits of the instant proceeding which the respondent no.2

shall decide independently without being influenced by any

observation made in this order.
</p>
<p id="p_7">      There shall be no order as to costs.
</p>
<p id="p_8">      Since affidavits are not called for, the allegations contained

in the petition are deemed not to have been admitted by the

respondents.
</p>
<p id="p_9">      Urgent Photostat certified copy of this order, if applied for, be

delivered to the learned Advocates for the parties, upon compliance

of all formalities.
</p>
<p id="p_10">


</p><p id="p_11">                                   (Ravi Krishan Kapur, J.)
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2"> 3</span>
