<div class="doc_title">State Of Gujarat vs Khima Arjan Khandhar &amp; 2 on 29 January, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">        R/CR.MA/15478/2013                                  ORDER




         IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

CRIMINAL MISC.APPLICATION (FOR LEAVE TO APPEAL) NO. 15478 of
                                     2013

                  In CRIMINAL APPEAL NO. 1379 of 2013

================================================================
                  STATE OF GUJARAT....Applicant(s)
                             Versus
             KHIMA ARJAN KHANDHAR &amp; 2....Respondent(s)
================================================================
Appearance:
PUBLIC PROSECUTOR for the Applicant(s) No. 1
MR NITIN M AMIN, ADVOCATE for the Respondent(s) No. 1 - 3
================================================================

        CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE RAJESH H.SHUKLA

                             Date : 29/01/2014


                              ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">   1. The present Criminal Miscellaneous Application has been filed

      by the Applicant State under <a href="/doc/1796168/" id="a_1">Section 378(1)(3)</a> of the Code of

      Criminal Procedure seeking leave to appeal challenging the

      impugned judgment and order rendered in Sessions Case No.

      86 of 2003 by the learned 5th Additional Sessions Judge, Jam

      Khambhaliya, Jamnagar dated 26.12.2012 on the grounds

      stated in the application.
</p>
<p id="p_2">   2. Heard learned APP Ms. Monali Bhatt for the Applicant State and

      learned Advocate Shri Nitin N. Amin for the Respondents /

      Original Accused.
</p>
<p id="p_3">

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                   Page 1 of 2</span>
</p><pre id="pre_2">            R/CR.MA/15478/2013                               ORDER



      3. Having perused the impugned judgment and order             and the

</pre><p id="p_4">        deposition, the reasoning given for the findings and the

        conclusion arrived at cannot be said to be perverse or contrary

        to the material and evidence on record, which would call for

        any interference in the present acquittal appeal.

</p><p id="p_5">      4. Therefore, as the court is in broad agreement with the findings

        and the conclusion arrived at and also considering the

        guidelines with regard to the scope of acquittal appeals under

        <a href="/doc/487026/" id="a_2">Section 378</a> of Cr.PC, including the observations made by the

        Hon'ble Apex Court in a judgment reported in (2007) 4 SCC

        415 - <a href="/doc/761643/" id="a_3">Chandrappa and Ors. v. State of Karnataka</a>, the

        present Criminal Misc. Application seeking leave to appeal

        cannot be entertained and deserves to be dismissed. The

        present      Criminal Misc. Application seeking leave to appeal

        therefore stands dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_6">


</p><p id="p_7">                                                (RAJESH H.SHUKLA, J.)


JNW
