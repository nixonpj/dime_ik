<div class="doc_title">An Application For Bail Under ... vs In Re:- Asgar Ansari on 2 May, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                   1

   27
02.05.2017</pre>
<p id="p_1">   sm
Allowed
                             CRM No.3730 of 2017


              In the matter of an application for bail under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of the
              Code of Criminal Procedure filed on 28.04.2017 in connection with
              Bhagabangola Police Station Case No.102 of 2017 under <a href="/doc/399026/" id="a_2">sections
              399</a>/<a href="/doc/1784492/" id="a_3">402</a> of the Indian Penal Code and <a href="/doc/73862/" id="a_4">sections 25</a>/<a href="/doc/244673/" id="a_5">27</a> of the Arms
              Act.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">                                       And

             In Re:- Asgar Ansari .. Petitioner.</p><pre id="pre_2">


             Md.Manwar Ali    .... for the petitioner

             Mr.Bidyut Roy
             Ms.Rita Dutta    ... for the State.


</pre><p id="p_4">                    Heard the learned advocates appearing on behalf of the

             parties. Perused the case diary.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                    The petitioner is in custody for about 56 days.

                    The learned advocate for the petitioner submits that one of the

             co-accuseds from whose possession fire-arms was recovered, has

             been granted bail by the trial court and the present petitioner is

             standing on same footing with him. Such fact has not been disputed

             by the learned advocate for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                    Having regard to above and considering the length of

             detention of the present petitioner and when no case is made out

             from the side of the State that if the petitioner is released on bail, he
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                  2</span>


is likely to abscond, the prayer for bail of the petitioner stands

allowed on parity.
</p>
<p id="p_7">       Let the petitioner be released on bail to the satisfaction of the

learned Additional. Chief Judicial Magistrate, Murshidabad at Lalbag

upon furnishing a Bond of Rs.40,000/-, with two sureties of

Rs.20,000/- each, one of whom must be local and on further

condition that after release, he shall not leave the jurisdiction of

Bhagabangola Police Station except for the purpose of attending the

court proceeding and shall report to the Officer-in-Charge of that

police station on every alternate day, until further orders.

</p><p id="p_8">       Accordingly, this application for bail is disposed of.




</p><p id="p_9">                                               (Ashim Kumar Roy, J.)



                                             (Amitabha Chatterjee, J.)
