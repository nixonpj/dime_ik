<div class="doc_title">The State Of Madhya Pradesh vs Rakesh Sahu on 5 February, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                         MCRC-5797-2008
              (THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH Vs RAKESH SAHU)


05-02-2016

Shri Shiv Kumar Shrivastava, learned P.L. for the
applicant/State.
Shri Shivendra Pandey, learned counsel for the respondent.</pre>
<p id="p_1">Learned counsel for the respondent submits that against the
very same judgment of acquittal passed in favour of the
respondent, complainant Mamta Sahu had filed Criminal
Revision No.739/08, which has been dismissed by this Court
vide order dated 30/03/12 after considering the statements of
complainant Mamta Sahu (PW/5), her brother Mahesh (PW/1)
and mother of the complainant Ramkali PW/8.
Having regard to the aforesaid and having gone through the
impugned judgment apart from the above, I find that two
prosecution witnesses namely Rakesh (PW/6) and Rafiq (PW/7)
did not support the prosecution case and have been declared
hostile. The trial Court after going through the evidence of
Mamta Sahu (PW/5), found her statement to be not reliable.
The trial Court has relied the statement of the defence
witnesses who are neighbours of the complainant and the
accused and has recorded a finding that the complainant
failed to prove that the respondent herein illtreated the
complainant for non-payment of dowry.
</p><p id="p_2">Having gone through the evidence available on record and the
impugned judgment and taking note of the fact that the
complainant's Cr.R.No.739/08 has already been dismissed by
this Court on 30/03/12, I am of the view that no case for grant
of leave to appeal is made out.
</p><p id="p_3"> Accordingly, M.Cr.C. deserves to be and is hereby dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                       (SHANTANU KEMKAR)
                                             JUDGE
