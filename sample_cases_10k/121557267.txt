                                JUDGMENT
</pre>
<p id="p_1">       The petitioner, who stood guarantor to a loan availed by the

3rd   respondent    from    the    1st   respondent     bank,    defaulted   in

repayment of the same.             Consequently, the respondent bank

initiated proceedings under the Securitisation and Reconstructions

of Financial Assets and <a href="/doc/198257891/" id="a_1">Enforcement of Security Interest Act</a>, 2002,

hereinafter referred to as the '<a href="/doc/52229129/" id="a_2">SARFAESI Act</a>', to recover the loan

amounts. Ext.P1 is the possession notice issued to the petitioner

under the <a href="/doc/52229129/" id="a_3">SARFAESI Act</a>.             In the writ petition, the petitioner

impugns the steps initiated by the respondent bank for recovery of

the loan amounts.
</p>
<p id="p_2">

</p><p id="p_3">       2.   I have heard the learned counsel appearing on behalf of

the petitioner as also the learned Standing Counsel appearing on

behalf of the respondents.
</p>
<p id="p_4">

</p><p id="p_5">       3.   On a consideration of the facts and circumstances of the

case as also the submissions made across the bar, I note that the

sole prayer of the petitioner is to permit her to remit the balance

amounts outstanding to the bank in easy installments. Taking into

account the plea of financial hardship raised by the petitioner, I

dispose the writ petition with the following directions:-
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                           -2-</span>
W.P.(C). No. 29207 of 2017




</p><p id="p_6">                 (i) The total amount outstanding, in respect of the
           loan availed by the petitioner,              is stated to be
           Rs.51,55,000/-.      Accordingly, if the petitioner remits
           the aforesaid amount of Rs.51,55,000/- together with
           accrued interest and other charges, in eleven equal
           and successive monthly installments commencing
           from 20.11.2017, then the recovery steps initiated
           against her by the respondent bank shall be kept in
           abeyance.
</p>
<p id="p_7">
</p><p id="p_8">            (ii) It is made clear that if the petitioner commits a
           default in respect of any of the installments, she will
           lose the benefit of this judgment and the respondent
           bank         will   be   free   to    continue    the   recovery
           proceedings against her from the stage at which they
           presently stand.
</p>
<p id="p_9">
</p><p id="p_10">           (iii)The respondent bank shall furnish the petitioner
           with an up-to-date statement, of the dues position
           together with interest, within 10 days from today so
           as to enable the petitioner to discharge her liability in
           accordance with the directions in this judgment.



</p><p id="p_11">                                                      Sd/-
</p>
