          HIGH COURT OF CHHATTISGARH, BILASPUR

                                 M.CR.C. No. 6690 of 2016

Savitri Bai Dewar, aged about 35 years, D/o. Mahipal Dewar, R/o.
Mathpuraina Near Water Tank, Tikrapara, Raipur, Tahsil and District - Raipur
(C.G.)
                                                                                    ---- Applicant

                                              Versus

The State of Chhattisgarh, Through : Station House Officer, Police Station -
Tikrapara, Raipur, Tahsil and District - Raipur (C.G.)

                                                                                ---- Respondent

For Applicant              :       Mr. L.C. Dash, Advocate
For Respondent             :       Mr. Ashutosh Pandey, Panel Lawyer
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
</pre>
<p id="p_1">                         Hon'ble Shri Justice Goutam Bhaduri
                                   Order On Board
02/11/2016


</p><p id="p_2">1.       This is the first bail application filed under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of the Code of

         Criminal Procedure for grant of regular bail to the applicant who has

         been arrested in connection with Crime No. 303/2016, registered at

         Police Station- Tikrapara, Raipur District - Raipur (C.G.) for the

         offence punishable under <a href="/doc/387648/" id="a_2">Section 34</a> (2) of the Chhattisgarh Excise

         Act.
</p>
<p id="p_3">2.       The prosecution alleges that the applicant was found to be in

         possession of illicit liquor measuring about 5.760 bulk liters and she

         was arrested on 01.08.2016.
</p>
<p id="p_4">3.       Learned counsel for the applicant submits that the applicant has falsely

         been implicated in this case and she is in jail since 01.08.2016;

         therefore, she may be released on bail.

</p><p id="p_5">4.       Learned State counsel opposes the prayer for grant of bail, however,

         he would submit that as per the information received from the
                                                                                Page No.2

              concerned SHO, the applicant has no previous antecedents of similar

              offence.
</p>
<p id="p_6">         5.   Taking into consideration the totality of the circumstances and the fact

              that the quantity of seized liquor is 5.760 bulk liters; offence is triable

              by the JMFC and the applicant is in jail since 01.08.2016, this Court is

              inclined to release the applicant on bail.

</p><p id="p_7">         6.   Accordingly, the bail application filed under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_3">Section 439</a> of Cr.P.C. is

              allowed.
</p>
<p id="p_8">         7.   It is directed that the applicant shall be released on bail on furnishing a

              personal bond for a sum of Rs.25,000/- with one surety in the like sum

              to the satisfaction of the concerned trial Court, for her appearance as

              and when directed.
</p>
<p id="p_9">         8.   Certified copy as per rules.
</p>
<p id="p_10">                                                                    Sd/-
</p><p id="p_11">                                                               (Goutam Bhaduri)
                                                                    Judge
Balram
