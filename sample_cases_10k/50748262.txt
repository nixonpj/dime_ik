                            ORDER

<p id="p_1">     This revision petition is preferred by the accused

against the    judgment     in   Crl.Appeal   No.225/2001 of

Additional Sessions Judge, Fast Track Court-I, Palakkad.

They were charge sheeted in C.C.No.498/1998 before

Judicial First Class Magistrate-III, Palakkad for having

committed an offence punishable u/s.341, 324, 427 and 326

r/w.34 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_1">IPC</a>. The learned Magistrate acquitted A3 for the

offence u/ss.341, 324, 427 and 326 r/w.34 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_2">IPC</a>, but

convicted the accused 1 and 2 u/s.341, 324 and 326 r/w.34

<a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_3">IPC</a>. Against that, the accused 1 and 2 preferred Crl.Appeal

No.225/2001 before Additional Sessions Judge, Fast Track

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">Crl.R.P. No.3461/2005          2</span>

Court-I, Palakkad, where learned Sessions Judge set

aside the conviction u/s.326 r/w.34 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_4">IPC</a>, but confirmed

the conviction u/ss.324 and 341      r/w.34 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_5">IPC</a>.   Being

aggrieved by that, the accused preferred this revision

petition.
</p>
<p id="p_2">      2.      The charge against the accused is that on

23.5.1998 at 1.30 p.m., they wrongfully restrained PW5

and voluntarily caused hurt with deadly weapons like

sword and knife, as a result, PW5 sustained serious

injuries.     They also destroyed the front glass of the

autorickshaw and sustained a loss of 850/-. For this,

Malampuzha Police registered a crime and after

completing investigation, Asst. Sub Inspector of Police,

Malampuzha laid charge in the trial Court. During trial,

prosecution examined PW1 to PW8 and marked Exts.P1 to

P10. MO1 and MO2 were admitted in evidence. The

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">Crl.R.P. No.3461/2005          3</span>

incriminating circumstances brought out in evidence were

denied by the accused while questioning him. Exts.D1 to

D1 (g) were marked by the accused.
</p>
<p id="p_3">      3.      When the revision petition came up for

hearing, the learned counsel appearing for the revision

petitioners submitted that both parties have settled the

matter out of Court on condition that they will pay

compensation of       60,000/-   (Rupees sixty thousand

only) to PW5, the injured (impleaded as 2nd respondent in

this revision petition) and they have filed a compromise

petition.      In Crl.M.A.3865/2016, revision petitioners

admitted that an amount of 50,000/- will be paid on

28.7.2016 and the balance amount of 10,000/- will be

paid on 26.08.2016. Both parties are present in Court

and both counsel signed in the compromise petition. The

learned Public Prosecutor also identified the injured.

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">Crl.R.P. No.3461/2005          4</span>

When both parties settled the dispute out of court,

there is no objection in allowing        the compromise

petition.
</p>
<p id="p_4">      4.      The offences compoundable under the sections<a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_6">

of the Indian Penal Code</a> are specifically mentioned in the

table shown in <a href="/doc/91933/" id="a_7">Section 320</a> of Cr.P.C. The offence u/s.324

<a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_8">IPC</a> is compoundable on 23.5.1998. According to <a href="/doc/895891/" id="a_9">Section 320</a>

(6) <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_10">Cr.P.C</a>., a High Court or Court of Session while

exercising its powers of revision under <a href="/doc/986100/" id="a_11">Section 401</a> may

allow any person to compound any offence which such

person is competent to compound under this section.

When composition is made, it shall have the effect of an

acquittal of the accused with whom the offence has been

compounded u/s.320(8) <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_12">Cr.P.C</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_5">      In the result, the conviction and sentence u/s.324

&amp; 341 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_13">IPC</a> against the revision petitioners are set aside

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">Crl.R.P. No.3461/2005            5</span>

and they are acquitted u/s.320(8) <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_14">Cr.P.C</a>, provided they

shall pay 10,000/- to the 2nd respondent on 26/8/2016.

</p><p id="p_6">      This Crl.R.P. is disposed of as above.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><p id="p_8">                                     P.D. RAJAN, JUDGE.
</p>
