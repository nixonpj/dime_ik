<div class="doc_title">Chuka vs State &amp; Ors on 8 November, 2012</div><pre id="pre_1">                              SB Criminal Misc. Petition No.1281/2011
                                  Chuka. Vs. State of Rajasthan &amp; Ors.


                              1


 IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR RAJASTHAN AT

                         JODHPUR.

                             :::
                         JUDGMENT
                             :::

      S.B. Criminal Misc. Petition No.1281/2011

           Chuka. vs. State of Rajasthan &amp; Ors.


Date of Judgment ::   8.11.2012.



        HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE SANDEEP MEHTA

Mr.BS Bhalasria for Mr.MS Purohit, for the petitioner.

Mr.AR Nikub, for the respondent State.

None present for the respondents no.2 to 4 despite service.

                             ...

BY THE COURT :</pre>

<p id="p_1">     Heard.
</p>
<p id="p_2">     The instant misc. petition has been preferred by the

petitioner/complainant against the order dated 15.7.2011

passed by Additional Sessions Judge, Phalodi rejecting the

application filed by the petitioner to be represented by a

counsel.
</p>
<p id="p_3">     After hearing the learned counsel for the petitioner &amp;

learned PP and after considering the amendment made in

<a href="/doc/1271595/" id="a_1">Section 24</a> Cr.P.C., this Court is of the opinion that now the
                                  SB Criminal Misc. Petition No.1281/2011
                                     Chuka. Vs. State of Rajasthan &amp; Ors.
</p>
<p id="p_4">
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                 2</span>


Criminal<a href="/doc/1645922/" id="a_2"> Procedure Code</a> itself provides for a victim to

engage a counsel of his/her choice for assisting the

prosecution. Therefore, the order passed by the trial court

refusing permission to the petitioner's advocate to plead the

petitioner's case is absolutely illegal and amounts to an

abuse of process of Court.
</p>
<p id="p_5">     Resultantly, the present misc. petition succeeds and

the order dated 15.7.2011 passed by Additional Sessions

Judge,   Phalodi   is   hereby    quashed.      The     petitioner     is

permitted to appoint an advocate of her choice for pleading

her case in order to assist the prosecution.

</p><p id="p_6">     Stay petition also stands disposed of.



</p><p id="p_7">                                         (SANDEEP MEHTA), J.
</p>
<p id="p_8">
S.Phophaliya
