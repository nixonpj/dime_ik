<div class="doc_title">Anand Swarup Gilra vs Jharkhand State Housing Board ... on 4 February, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                                         1

           IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                      W.P.(C) No. 1888 of 2014

      Anand Swarup Gilra, son of late Jyoti Swarup Gilra, resident of 
      MH1/110, MIG, Adityapur­2, P.O. &amp; P.S. Adityapur, Jamshedpur
                                                   ...   ...    Petitioner
                                       Versus
      1.   Jharkhand   State   Housing   Board   through   Managing   Director 
      near Harmu Housing Colony, P.O. &amp; P.S. Harmu, Ranchi
      2. Revenue Officer, Jharkhand State Housing Board, near Harmu 
      Housing Colony, P.O. &amp; P.S. Harmu, Ranchi
      3. Executive Engineer, Jharkhand State Housing Board, Adityapur, 
      P.O. &amp; P.S. Adityapur, Jamshedpur ...        ...   ...    Respondents
CORAM: HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE SHREE CHANDRASHEKHAR
                                        ­­­­­
      For the Petitioner                : Mr. Siddharth Ranjan, Advocate   
      For the Housing Board             : Mr. Sachin Kumar, Advocate
                                        ­­­­­
      Order No. 03                                       Dated: 04.02.2016

                   At   the   outset,   raising   an   objection   to   the 
      maintainability of the writ petition, Mr. Sachin Kumar, the learned 
      counsel for the respondent­Housing Board submits that the writ 
      petition   is   premature.   The   petitioner   without   approaching   the 
      respondent no. 1 has rushed to this Court. 

      2.           Aggrieved   by   demand   notice   dated   21.05.2008 
      requiring the petitioner to deposit Rs. 3,82,235/­ calculated till 
      31.05.2008</pre><p id="p_1">, the present writ petition has been filed. 
</p>
<p id="p_2">      3.           The learned counsel for the petitioner submits that the 
      calculation   appended   to   demand   notice   dated   21.05.2008   is 
      apparently incorrect inasmuch as, compound interest @ 8.5% has 
      been   charged   on   the   price   of   the   house   though,   there   is   no 
      provision in the agreement for the same. It is further contended 
      that   the   calculation   provided   by   the  respondent­Housing   Board 
      discloses different rate of interest on various components which 
      cannot  be  justified in law. The learned counsel referring to the 
      decision   of   the   Housing   Board   in   its   40th   meeting   dated 
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                             2</span>

      07.04.2015   submits   that   the   respondent   may   be   directed   to 
      provide a fresh calculation to the petitioner. 
</p>
<p id="p_3">      4.            The learned counsel for the respondent­Housing Board 
      does   not   dispute   that   in   its   40th  meeting   the   Jharkhand   State 
      Housing   Board   has   taken   a   decision   not   to   charge   compound 
      interest. A copy of the minutes of meeting dated 07.04.2015 of 
      the Housing Board has been tendered by the learned counsel for 
      the   respondent­Jharkhand   State   Housing   Board.   It   is   taken   on 
      record. 
</p>
<p id="p_4">      5.            From   a   perusal   of   the   minutes   of   meeting   dated 
      07.04.2015,   it   appears  that   certain  decision  has  been   taken, in 
      view of which, the demand notice dated 21.05.2008 is required to 
      be modified. Considering the fact that the respondent­Jharkhand 
      State Housing Board itself has taken a decision which is reflected 
      in   its   meeting   held   on   07.04.2015,   the   writ   petition   stands 
      disposed of with direction to the respondent no. 2 to recalculate 
      the demand for the house allotted to the petitioner. Finally, if it is 
      found   that   the   petitioner   is   liable   to   pay   and,   if   the   petitioner 
      makes   such   payment,   the   respondent­Housing   Board   shall   take 
      necessary steps for registration of the house in the name of the 
      allottee,  in  accordance with the terms under the Hire Purchase 
      agreement and its Regulations.
</p>
  
<p id="p_5">                                                   (Shree Chandrashekhar, J.)
Manish
