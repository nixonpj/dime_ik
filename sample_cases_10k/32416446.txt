FOLLOWING:
                            JUDGMENT
</pre>
<p id="p_1">      The lands of the writ petitioner, who claims himself to be

the kartha of a Hindu Undivided Family, were acquired for the

purpose of construction of direct approach road to a proposed

Truck Terminal.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">      2.    The   learned    advocates    appearing    for   the

respondents inform us that, already, an award has been

passed on December 13, 2012, and that possession of the

lands has been taken.
</p>
<p id="p_4">
</p><p id="p_5">      3.    The Hon'ble Single Judge recorded in the order

that the writ petitioner is the sole objector to such acquisition

proceedings.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                 3</span><p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">      4.    Mr.M.S.Rajendra Prasad, learned senior advocate

appearing in support of the writ appeal, vehemently argues

that there were alternative lands, which could have been

utilized for construction of the proposed approach road.


</p><p id="p_8">      5.    Whether     a   particular   land   is   suitable   for

construction of road or not is a matter for the executive

domain and it is not a matter for the Court to decide.


</p><p id="p_9">      6.    Moreover, award has been passed long back. No

request was made for reference under <a href="/doc/1517117/" id="a_1">Section 18</a> of the Land

Acquisition Act, 1894. The Hon'ble Single Judge was right in

dismissing the writ petition.
</p>
<p id="p_10">
</p><p id="p_11">      7.    We do not find any merit in the writ appeal.


</p><p id="p_12">      8.    The writ appeal is dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_13">
</p><p id="p_14">      9.    We make no order as to costs.
</p>
