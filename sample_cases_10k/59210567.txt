                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                   Criminal Miscellaneous No.48608 of 2012
                   ======================================================
                   Meraj Ali S/O Ali Emam Shah R/O Vill-Hardeo Tola, Banorpur, P.S.-
                   Pachrukhi, Distt-Siwan

                                                                      .... ....   Petitioner/s
                                                          Versus
                   1. The State of Bihar
                   2. Uday Shankar Singh @ Puttus Singh S/O Sri Dinesh Nr. Singh R/O Vill-
                   Hardin Badhe, P.S.-Barahiya, Distt-Lakhisarai

                                                        .... .... Opposite Party/s
                   ======================================================
                   Appearance :
                   For the Petitioner/s       :  None
                   For the Opposite Party/s   : A.P.P.
                   ======================================================
                   CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE VIKASH JAIN
                                            ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   23-04-2015                       The present petition has been filed for quashing

                   the order dated 25.05.2012 passed by Sri Subhash Chandra,

                   learned Judicial Magistrate, Ist Class, Begusarai, in Complaint

                   Case No. 1933 of 2008 rejecting the petition for discharge

                   under <a href="/doc/1568411/" id="a_1">Section 245(i)</a> of Cr. P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                     2.     None appears on behalf of the petitioner

                   when the matter is called out.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                     3. According to para-8 of the petition, a number

                   of reasons have been enumerated by the petitioner to submit

                   that he ought to have been discharged.

</p><p id="p_4">                                     4.     A perusal of the impugned order however

                   discloses that the discharge petition has been dismissed as

                   not maintainable, as the matter related to an offence under

                   <a href="/doc/1823824/" id="a_2">Section 138</a> N.I. Act which is summons triable. This Court
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">           Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.48608 of 2012 (2) dt.23-04-2015</span>




                       finds no infirmity in the order passed by the learned court

                       below.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                         5. The petition stands dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><p id="p_7">                                                                         (Vikash Jain, J)
Md. Ibrarul/-
</p>
