                                     2.     Jagan Pal Singh 
                                            S/o Lt. Sh. Chiranji Lal

                                      JUDGMENT
</pre>
<p id="p_1">1.                    Briefly,   the   case   of   prosecution   is   that   on   24.03.2014
   upon   receiving   DD   NO.43B,   ASI   Dharampal   along   with   Ct.   Naveen
   reached at the spot where they came to know that injured was already
   taken   to   GTB   hospital.   Thereupon,   ASI   Dharampal   reached   at   GTB
   hospital   and   collected   the   MLC   of   injured   Amit   S/o   Rampalat   and
   recorded  his   statement.   The  gist   of  statement   is  that  on  24.03.2014  at
   about 9.00 pm, complainant was talking with his friend Arun Kumar in
   front of his house and during that period his neighbour Ravinder Kumar
   and his father Jagan Pal were abusing each other. When he was passing in
   front of their house, Ravinder Kumar called and slapped him without any
   reason   and   on   this   account   a   quarrel   took   place   between   them.   After
   seeing this Jagan Pal caught hold him and Ravinder Kumar hit on his head
   with an iron rod (saria) due to which he fell down on the ground. His
   friend   informed   his   brother,   who   took   him   to   GTB   hospital.   After
   completing   other   necessary   formalities,   charge­sheet   against   accused
   persons was filed  u/s 308/34 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_2">IPC</a>.
</p>

<p id="p_2">2.                    On appearance, in compliance of section 207 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_3">IPC</a>, copies
   were supplied to accused persons and as offence punishable u/s. 308 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_4">IPC</a>
   is   triable   by   the   Court   of   Sessions,   present   case   was   committed   to
   Sessions Court. During proceedings accused Ravinder Kumar expired and
________________________________________________________________________
FIR No.123/2014 ,PS.Harsh Vihar            Page 2 of 4       St. Vs. Ravinder Kumar &amp; Anr.
.
</p><p id="p_3">      proceedings against him were abated vide order dated 11.10.2017. 
</p>

<p id="p_4">3.                      Charge u/s 308/34 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_5">IPC</a> was framed against accused Jagan
     Pal Singh, to which he pleaded not guilty and claimed trial. 
</p>                     
<p id="p_5">4.                      In support of its case, prosecution examined PW­1 HC
     Pawan   Kumar,  who   was   working   as   duty   officer   on   24.03.2014   from
     12.00 midnight to 8.00 am. PW­1 proved the copy of FIR Ex.PW1/B and
     his endorsement on the rukka as Ex.PW1/A. PW­2 is complainant Amit,
     who has deposed that the quarrel had taken place with accused Ravinder
     only (since expired) and accused Jagan Pal is not involved in the incident. 
</p>               
<p id="p_6">5.                      Arguments   have   been   advanced   by   Sh.   Ashok   Kumar,
     Ld. Addl. PP for State as also by Sh. S.P. Singh, Ld. Defence Counsel for
     accused. 
</p><p id="p_7">6.                      PW­2   Sh.   Amit   is   the   star   witness   of   the   prosecution
     being   complainant/injured.     PW­2   has   deposed   that   on   23.03.2014   a
     scuffle took place between him and Ravinder. He has specifically deposed
     that he was assaulted by accused Ravinder (since expired) and accused
     Jagan Pal is not involved in the incident.  He was cross examined by Ld.
     Addl. PP for State but he denied the suggestion that he gave statement to
     police that during the scuffle between him and Ravinder, accused Jagan
     Pal   had   caught   hold   him.   He   was   confronted   with   his   statement
________________________________________________________________________
FIR No.123/2014 ,PS.Harsh Vihar            Page 3 of 4       St. Vs. Ravinder Kumar &amp; Anr.
.
</p><p id="p_8">    Ex.PW2/A, but he denied having made such statement to police. 
</p><p id="p_9">7.                    In   the   instant   case,   complainant   Amit   has   exonerated
   accused Jagan Pal Singh while deposing that he was not involved in the
   incident   and   thus   has   not   supported   the   case   of   prosecution   against
   accused Jagan Pal. As complainant Amit has not attributed any role to the
   accused Jagan Pal Singh, in the alleged incident, examination of other
   witnesses,   who   remained   to   be   examined   are   not   going   to   help   the
   prosecution  to prove the facts of  the case,  continuation of prosecution
   evidence   would   be   a   futile   exercise,   particularly   when
   complainant/injured deposed that accused Jagan Pal is not involved in the
   present case. As such, prosecution evidence is closed and statement of
   accused u/s. 313 <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_6">Cr.P.C</a> is also dispensed with. Accused Jagan Pal Singh
   is   acquitted   of   the   charge   framed   against   him.   His   bail   bond   stands
   cancelled.  However, in view of provisions of section 437­A of the Code,
   accused is directed to furnish personal bond in the sum of Rs.10,000/­with
   one surety in the like amount within a week. File be consigned to record
