<div class="doc_title">Chanchal Kumar vs The State Of Bihar on 6 August, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.47785 of 2018
                      Arising Out of PS.Case No. -177 Year- 2018 Thana -TAJPUR District- SAMASTIPUR
                 ======================================================
                 Chanchal Kumar, S/o Sunil Pouddar, Resident of Village-Riwari Dala, P.S.
                 Angarghat, District-Samastipur
                                                                        .... .... Petitioner
                                                Versus
                 The State of Bihar
                                                                   .... .... Opposite Party
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s       : Mr. Bijay Bhushan Prasad
                 For the Opposite Party/s   : Smt Sangeeta Sharma
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE SUDHIR SINGH
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   06-08-2018              Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned APP

                 for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                             The petitioner is apprehending his arrest in a case

                 registered under Sections-272 &amp; 273<a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_1"> of the Indian Penal Code</a> and

                 Section-30(a) of the Bihar Prohibition and <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_2">Excise Act</a>, 2016.

</p><p id="p_3">                             The prosecution case, in short, is that 22.140 liters wine

                 is recovered.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                             It has been submitted on behalf of the petitioner that

                 there is no allegation of tampering of witnesses alleged against the

                 petitioner. The name of the petitioner has transpired on the basis

                 of disclosure made by the co-accused namely, Rajan Kumar.

                 Except for this, there is no other substantive evidence to suggest

                 the implication of the petitioner in this case. It is alleged that

                 22.140 liters wine is recovered from an open field situated by the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">            Patna High Court Cr.M isc. No.47785 of 2018 (2) dt.06-08-2018</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                   Page 2 of 2</span>




                        side of the house of one Amarjeet Poddar. Nothing incriminating

                        has been recovered from the conscious possession of the

                        petitioner. The petitioner had no knowledge regarding the alleged

                        incident. There is no compliance of <a href="/doc/1137396/" id="a_3">Section 100</a> Cr.P.C.

</p><p id="p_5">                                     On behalf of the State, it is submitted that the petitioner

                        is named in the F.I.R.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                           Considering the aforesaid facts and circumstances,

                        let the petitioner, above named, in the event of arrest/surrender

                        before the learned court below within a period of six weeks from

                        today, be released on anticipatory bail on furnishing bail bonds of

                        Rs.10,000/- (Ten thousand) with two sureties of the like amount

                        each to the satisfaction of learned 6th Additional Sessions Judge-

                        cum-Special Judge, Samastipur in connection with Tajpur P.S.

                        Case No. 177 of 2018, subject to the conditions as laid down

                        under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_4">Section 438(2)</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure.




</p><p id="p_7">                                                         (Sudhir Singh, J)
A.K.V./-
</p><pre id="pre_2">  U           T
