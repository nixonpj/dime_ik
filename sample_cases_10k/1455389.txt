<div class="doc_title">Kunjumuth vs State Of Kerla Represnted By The ... on 2 July, 2008</div><pre id="pre_1">       

  

  

 
 
  IN THE HIGH COURT OF KERALA AT ERNAKULAM

Crl.Rev.Pet.No. 2179 of 2008()


1. KUNJUMUTH, REEJA MANZIL, PUTHUKKAD MURI,
                      ...  Petitioner

                        Vs



1. STATE OF KERLA REPRESNTED BY THE PUBLIC
                       ...       Respondent

2. INDIRAMMA, KALLOOR THEKKATHIL,PUTHUKKAD

                For Petitioner  :SRI.K.SUBASH CHANDRA BOSE

                For Respondent  : No Appearance

The Hon'ble MR. Justice V.RAMKUMAR

 Dated :02/07/2008

 O R D E R
                        V.RAMKUMAR, J.
              =======================
                   CRL.R.P. NO.2179 OF 2008
              ========================
              Dated this the 2nd day of July 2008

                            ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Petitioner was the complainant in a private complaint filed

as CMP 3730/2007 before the JFCM, Karunagappally alleging

offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1280620/" id="a_1">Sections 378</a>, <a href="/doc/1101188/" id="a_2">379</a>, <a href="/doc/839778/" id="a_3">380</a>, <a href="/doc/1328629/" id="a_4">463</a>, <a href="/doc/1745798/" id="a_5">464</a>, <a href="/doc/1317063/" id="a_6">465</a>

and <a href="/doc/556166/" id="a_7">468</a> IPC. He challenges the order dated 11.4.2008 passed

by the learned Magistrate dismissing the complaint under <a href="/doc/443138/" id="a_8">Section

203</a> Cr.P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_2">     2. The learned Magistrate considered the sworn statement

of the complainant and two witnesses and also the documentary

evidence produced by the revision petitioner to hold that the

case that the accused, who is a neighbour of the complainant,

had committed theft of her cheque book containing 8 leaves, at

the time of the shifting of the complainant's residence on

29.12.2003 is false and presumably put forward in anticipation

of a defence to a prosecution under <a href="/doc/1823824/" id="a_9">Section 138</a> of the Negotiable

Instruments Act launched against the revision petitioner two

years prior to the filing of the complaint. The said finding

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">CRRP 2179/2008                        2</span>

recorded by the court below is after a careful evaluation of the

averments in the complaint as well as the sworn statement and

documentary evidence. This court sitting in revision will be loathe

to interfere with the said finding. I, therefore, do not find any

good reason to interfere with the order passed by the Magistrate.

</p><p id="p_3">       This revision is accordingly dismissed.




Dated this the 2nd day of July, 2008


                                          V.RAMKUMAR,
                                            JUDGE
