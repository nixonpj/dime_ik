           In the High Court At Calcutta
                             Constitutional Writ Jurisdiction
                                    Appellate Side.

18.02.15</pre>

<p id="p_1">       WP No.24711 (W) of 2005
              Sri Bishnupada Sarkara
                  v.
</p><p id="p_2">          The State of West Bengal &amp; Ors.
</p>
<p id="p_3">Mr. Gouri Sankar De
Mr. Rudranil De ... for the petitioner.
</p>

<p id="p_4">     The writ petition is pending before this court from January
2007. The petitioner claimed quashing of the Memo dated June
4,1998 issued by the Secretary to the Government of West Bengal.
The petitioner claims that he is entitled to pensionary benefits by
virtue of Notification dated March 7,1990 which has been rejected
by the Memo dated June 4, 1998. It appears that during the
pendency of the writ petition, the writ petitioner has retired from
service after attaining the age of sixty years.
</p>
<p id="p_5">     In these circumstances, the petitioner is directed to file a
supplementary affidavit disclosing the facts that even after
retirement he is still entitled to obtain reliefs on this application.
The petitioner shall also serve a copy of the supplementary
affidavit along with the copy of the writ petition upon the office of
the Government Pleader within one week. The petitioner shall also
inform the Government Pleader that this matter will be taken up
for hearing on February 27,2015.
</p>


<pre id="pre_2">sh                                     ( Ashis Kumar Chakraborty, J)
