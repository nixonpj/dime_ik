<div class="doc_title">Kojraj Singh vs State &amp; Anr on 25 November, 2016</div><p id="p_1">       IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR
              RAJASTHAN AT JODHPUR
     S.B.CRIMINAL MISC(PET.) NO. 3330 / 2016
        Kojraj Singh S/o Shri Jethmal Singh, Address:
        Thaiyat,   Jaisalmer     Sadar Police Station,
        Jaisalmer, Distt. Jaisalmer.
</p><p id="p_2">                                                  ----Petitioner
                             Versus
</p><p id="p_3">1.      The State of Rajasthan.
</p><p id="p_4">2.      Bhura Ram S/o Tara Ram, Address: Thaiyat,
        Jaisalmer Sadar Police Station, Jaisalmer, Distt.
        Jaisalmer.
</p><p id="p_5">                                            ----Respondents
__________________________________________
Mr. Sumer Singh Rathore, for the petitioner.
Mr. M.S.Panwar, P.P. for the State.

__________________________________________
          HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE P.K. LOHRA
                             Order
25/11/2016

        By the instant misc. petition, petitioner has

challenged the FIR No.91/2016 of Police Station Sadar,

Jaisalmer.       In    the   impugned      FIR,    petitioner   is

attributed offence under <a href="/doc/162506/" id="a_1">Section 447</a> IPC and under

<a href="/doc/42436223/" id="a_2">Section      3(1)(f)</a>    of   the    Scheduled      Castes   and

Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of atrocities), Act 1989

(for short, 'Act of 1989').
</p>
<p id="p_6">        It is pleaded by the petitioner that FIR is based

on false and concocted facts and therefore, no

cognizable offence as such, against petitioner is made

out.      Learned      counsel     has   also   submitted   that

complainant is well within his right to avail appropriate
                           (2 of 2)
                                              [CRLMP-3330/2016]
remedy before the revenue Court and therefore

launching criminal prosecution against the petitioner is

not sustainable.
</p>
<p id="p_7">     Per contra, learned Public Prosecutor submits

that a bare reading of FIR makes it crystal clear that

the same discloses commission of cognizable offence

by the petitioner.   Learned Public Prosecutor further

submits that if anybody tries to grab land of the

Scheduled Caste/Scheduled Tribe person, he can be

criminally prosecuted for the offence under <a href="/doc/42436223/" id="a_3">Section

3(1)(v)</a> of the Act of 1989. Learned Public Prosecutor

has also placed heavy reliance on factual report dated

24.11.2016 addressed to him by SHO Police Station,

Sadar, Jaisalmer.
</p>
<p id="p_8">     As per factual report, investigation in the matter

has been completed and prima facie, Investigating

Agency has collected incriminating evidence against

the petitioner for the aforesaid offence.

</p><p id="p_9">     In this view of the matter, after consideration of

the arguments advanced at Bar and upon perusal of

the impugned FIR, I feel dissuaded to interfere in the

matter.
</p>
<p id="p_10">     Consequently, the petition fails and the same is

hereby dismissed summarily.
</p>
<p id="p_11">                                            (P.K. LOHRA)J.
</p>
<p id="p_12">Bharti/27
