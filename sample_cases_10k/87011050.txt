<div class="doc_title">Manjit Kaur Alias Parminder Kaur vs State Of Punjab on 16 September, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">           CRM No.M-30375 of 2014                                                       -1-



                        IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA AT
                                        CHANDIGARH


                                               CRM No.M-30375 of 2014
                                               Date of Decision: 16.09.2014


           Manjit Kaur @ Parminder Kaur
                                                                   .....Petitioner

                                                    Versus
           State of Punjab
                                                                   .....Respondent


           CORAM: HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE JITENDRA CHAUHAN


           Present:             Mr. Manish Dadwal, Advocate for the petitioner.

                                Mr. Luvinder Sofat, AAG, Punjab
                                assisted by ASI Major Singh.


           JITENDRA CHAUHAN, J. (Oral)
</pre>
<p id="p_1">                                The instant petition has been preferred under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a>

           Cr.P.C. seeking regular bail/interim bail in case FIR No.93 dated

           24.08.2012, registered under <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_2">Sections 304-B</a> read with <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_3">Section 34</a> of

           IPC as well as for the alternative charge framed under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_4">Section 302</a> IPC

           on 14.02.2013 by the learned Addl. Sessions Judge, Gurdaspur,

           registered at Police Station Rangar Nangal, District Batala on the

           compassionate grounds to see the ailing husband and the grand child

           of the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                Learned   counsel   for   the   petitioner   contends    that

           complainant-daughter-in-law of the petitioner did not come forward to

           claim the custody of the child who is being looked after by the grand-

           father.          The husband of the petitioner has undergone surgery for

           laprotomy. Vide order dated 12.08.2014, passed by the learned Addl.



MANOJ KUMAR
2014.09.17 10:16
I attest to the accuracy and
authenticity of this document
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">            CRM No.M-30375 of 2014                                                 -2-</span>



           Sessions Judge, Gurdaspur, the petitioner was granted liberty to meet

           her ailing husband for a day.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                On the other hand, the learned State counsel submits

           that the husband of the petitioner has now been discharged after the

           successful surgery and he is hale and hearty.

</p><p id="p_4">                                I have heard learned counsel for the parties and perused

           the record.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                The surgery carried out by the husband of the petitioner

           is not in dispute.             The son of the petitioner, husband of the

           complainant is also in custody in the same FIR. The petitioner did

           not misuse the concession of bail earlier granted to her.                The

           petitioner and her husband both being senior citizens, this Court

           feels that ailing husband of the petitioner requires post operation

           care by the family member.              The petitioner being the wife would

           serve as a best nurse for the ailing husband and look after her grand

           children.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                In the circumstances, without adverting to the merits of

           the case, the prayer of the petitioner is allowed, on compassionate

           ground and the petitioner is granted two week's interim bail to look

           after the ailing husband and care of grand-child on her furnishing

           adequate surety bonds, to the satisfaction of the trial Court. The date

           of release to be indicated by the releasing Court.              In case the

           petitioner holds the passport the same be deposited with the trial

           Court.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><pre id="pre_2">           September 16, 2014                           (JITENDRA CHAUHAN)
           Manoj Bhutani                                       JUDGE


MANOJ KUMAR
2014.09.17 10:16
