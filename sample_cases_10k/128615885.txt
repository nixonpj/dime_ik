<div class="doc_title">Raju @ Raj Kumar vs State Of Punjab And Another on 24 September, 2012</div><pre id="pre_1">CRM No. M-25657 of 2012 (O&amp;M)                                                -1-


IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA AT CHANDIGARH

                                          CRM No. M-25657 of 2012 (O&amp;M)
                                          Date of Decision:-24.09.2012


Raju @ Raj Kumar                                                       ...Petitioner

                                     Versus

State of Punjab and another                                       ....Respondents</pre>


<p id="p_1">CORAM: HON'BLE MR.JUSTICE MEHINDER SINGH SULLAR


Present:-      Mr. Amit Dhawan, Advocate
               for the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_2">               Mr. C.S. Brar, D.A.G., Punjab
               for the respondent.
</p>
<p id="p_3">               Nemo for respondent No.2.
</p>
<p id="p_4">
Mehinder Singh Sullar , J . (Oral)

               Petitioner Raju @ Raj Kumar son of Roshan Lal, has preferred the

instant petition for anticipatory bail in a case instituted on private complaint, in

which, he was summoned to face the trial, for the commission of offences

punishable under <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_1">Sections 406</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_2">504</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_3">506</a> IPC and <a href="/doc/42436223/" id="a_4">Section 3</a> of the Scheduled

Castes and <a href="/doc/25085007/" id="a_5">Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities Act</a>), 1989 by the trial

Court, invoking the provisions of <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_6">Section 438</a> Cr.P.C.

</p><p id="p_5">2.             Notice of the petition was issued to the respondents.

</p><p id="p_6">3.             After hearing the learned counsel for the parties, going through the

record with their valuable assistance and after deep consideration of the entire

matter, to my mind, the present petition deserves to be accepted in this context.

</p><p id="p_7">4.             During the course of preliminary hearing a Co-ordinate Bench of this

Court (G.S. Sandhawalia, J.), passed the following order on August 24, 2012:-

</p><p id="p_8">                      "Contention of the counsel for the petitioner is that the
              petitioner has been summoned in a complaint case under
 CRM No. M-25657 of 2012 (O&amp;M)                                                -2-
</p>
<p id="p_9">
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">             <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_7">Section 406</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_8">504</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_9">506</a> IPC and under <a href="/doc/42436223/" id="a_10">Section 3</a> of Scheduled
             Caste and <a href="/doc/25085007/" id="a_11">Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act</a>, 1989
             (for short, the 'Act') pending in the Court of Smt.Jasbir Kaur,
             Judicial Magistrate, Ist Class, Jalandhar for 25.08.2012.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">                    It is contended that the complainant owes some money to
             the petitioner who is running a jewellery shop and the daughter
             of the complainant was working with the wife of the petitioner
             who also owes some money and it is in such circumstances, the
             complaint has been lodged to avoid the payment and to put
             pressure. The Addl.Sessions Judge, Jalandhar has declined to
             grant bail on the ground that there is a bar under <a href="/doc/82911775/" id="a_12">Section 18</a> of
             the Act.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">                    Counsel for the petitioner states that since it is a
             complaint case and custodial interrogation is not required, the
             petitioner shall appear on the date fixed by this Court and
             directions be issued that the bail should be decided on the same
             date.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_4">                    At this stage, Mr.Raman Sharma, Advocate has put in
             appearance in the Court to oppose the bail application.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_5">                    Keeping in view the fact that custodial interrogation is
             not required and it is a complaint case, the petitioner is
             directed to appear on 25.08.2012 before the trial Court and file
             a bail application who shall decide the regular bail application
             on the same day.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_6">                    Notice of motion for 24.09.2012."
</blockquote>
<p id="p_10">5.           At the very outset, learned counsel for the petitioner has placed on

record the certified copy of order dated 28.08.2012 and affidavit, which would

reveal that the bail bonds and surety bonds, submitted by the petitioner have

already been accepted and attested by the Magistrate, in pursuance of order of this

Court .
</p>
<p id="p_11">6.           In this view of the matter, the instant petition for anticipatory bail is

accepted. The interim bail already granted to the petitioner, by this Court, vide

order dated August 24, 2012 is hereby made absolute.</p><pre id="pre_2">


September 24, 2012                                     (Mehinder Singh Sullar)
naresh.k                                                      Judge
 </pre>
