<div class="doc_title">An Application For Anticipatory ... vs In Re: Bhim Mondal &amp; Ors on 25 May, 2018</div><p id="p_1"><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                           1</span>

 37    25.05.2018
AGM/   Court No.08
                                     CRM 3180 of 2018
 SK


                     In the matter of an application for anticipatory bail under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_1">Section
                     438</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure affirmed on 21.05.2018 in
                     connection with Karimpur Police Station Case No. 84 of 2018
                     dated 17.05.2018 under <a href="/doc/1208971/" id="a_2">Sections 353</a>/<a href="/doc/1440821/" id="a_3">332</a>/<a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_4">307</a>/<a href="/doc/1552733/" id="a_5">435</a>/<a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_6">506</a> of the
                     Indian Penal Code and Sections 3/4 of the Explosive Substance
                     Act and under <a href="/doc/73862/" id="a_7">Sections 25</a>/<a href="/doc/244673/" id="a_8">27</a> of the Arms Act.

</p><p id="p_2">                                                And

                     In Re: Bhim Mondal &amp; Ors.                      Petitioners.

                      Mr. Atis Kumar Biswas           ......For the Petitioners.

                      Mr. Sudip Ghosh,
                      Mr. Manoranjan Mahata                   ... For the State.



</p><p id="p_3">                            It is submitted on behalf of the petitioners that they have

                     been falsely implicated in the instant case out of political rivalry.

</p><p id="p_4">                            Learned counsel for the State opposes the prayer for

                     anticipatory bail and submits that there was extensive loss of

                     public property on the date of counting of votes in the ensuing

                     Panchayat Election.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                            We have considered the materials on record. No doubt the

                     allegations are serious. However, we do not find any specific

                     overt act attributed to the petitioners in the alleged crime.

</p><p id="p_6">                            Under such circumstances, we are of the opinion though

                     custodial interrogation of the petitioners may not be necessary,

                     petitioners require to cooperate with the investigation in
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                        2</span>


accordance with law.
</p>
<p id="p_7">       Accordingly, we direct that in the event of arrest the

petitioners shall be released on bail upon furnishing a bond of

Rs. 10,000/- each with two sureties of like amount, to the

satisfaction of the arresting officer and also subject to the

conditions as laid down under <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_9">Section 438</a> (2)<a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_10"> of the Code</a> of

Criminal Procedure, 1973 and on further condition that the

petitioners shall meet the Investigating Officer of Karimpur Police

Station once in a week until further orders.

</p><p id="p_8">       The application for anticipatory bail is, thus, disposed of.



  (Amitabha Chatterjee, J.)              (Joymalya Bagchi, J,)
 </p>
