<p id="p_2">

</p><p id="p_3">            HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE PANKAJ BHANDARI

                          Judgment / Order

03/10/2018

</p><p id="p_4">1.      Petitioner has preferred this revision petition aggrieved by

order    date   15.09.2017    passed   by   Additional    Chief   Judicial

Magistrate No.1, Kota whereby the Court has awarded interim

maintenance of Rs.3,000/- to respondent-non-petitioner and

against the judgment and order dated 24.04.2018 passed by

Additional Sessions Judge (Women Atrocities Cases) No.2, Kota,

whereby appeal preferred by the petitioner was rejected on the

ground of being barred of limitation.

</p><p id="p_5">2.      This Court in S.B. Criminal Revision No.483/<a href="/doc/194053878/" id="a_1">2018 "Anshul

Kulshreshtha vs. Smt. Swarnima Kulshreshtha and Anr</a>."

decided on 31.08.2018 has held that a revision petition does not

lie against an order on ground of interim maintenance by the

Court below. The revision petition is thus not maintainable and is,

accordingly, dismissed.
</p><pre id="pre_2">                                                               (2 of 2)                             [CRLR-992/2018]



                                   3.        However,   petitioner       would   be   free   to   approach    the

</pre><p id="p_6">                                   appropriate forum, if so advised.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                                   4.        The trial Court is directed to decide the main application

                                   expeditiously.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                                   5.        Stay application also stands disposed of.




</p><p id="p_9">                                                                                        (PANKAJ BHANDARI),J
