<div class="doc_title">Date Of Decision: 11.09.2018 vs Unknown on 11 September, 2018</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: Honourable Mr. Sharma</div>

<p id="p_1">                IN THE HIGH COURT OF HIMACHAL PRADESH, 
                              SHIMLA

                                                              Cr. MMO No.409 of 2018




                                                                                     .
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                 Date of Decision:  11.09.2018
    __________________________________________________________
    Rockey Singh                                    ........ Petitioner





                                                   Versus 
    State of Himachal Pradesh &amp; Others                                        .....Respondents





    Coram:
</p><p id="p_3">    Hon'ble Mr. Justice Sandeep Sharma, Judge.
    Whether approved for reporting? 1  

    For the Petitioner   :   Mr. Anuj Gupta, Advocate. 
</p>
<p id="p_4">       For the Respondents: Mr.   S.C.Sharma   &amp;   Mr.   Dinesh 
                            Thakur, AAG, with Mr. Amit Kumar 
                            Dhumal, Deputy Advocate General for 



                            respondent No.1.
</p><p id="p_5">                           Mr.   Ajay   Kashyap,   Advocate   vice 
                           Mr.V.S.Chauhan,   Advocate,   for 




                           respondent No.2.
</p>




<p id="p_6">    __________________________________________________________
    Sandeep Sharma, Judge (oral):
</p>




<p id="p_7">                        Being   aggrieved   and   dissatisfied   with   the 

    impugned  order  dated 25.7.2018,  passed by  learned Judicial 

    Magistrate,   1st  Class,   Manali,   District   Kullu,   H.P,   whereby 

    complaint   having   been   filed   by   the   petitioner   came   to   be 

    dismissed in default for non presence of complainant and for 
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">    1</span>
        Whether the reporters of the local papers may be allowed to see the judgment?
</p>



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                                  ::: Downloaded on - 13/09/2018 22:58:35 :::HCHP</span>
<p id="p_8">     non prosecution, petitioner has approached this Court in the 

    instant   proceedings,   praying   therein   for   grant   of   one   more 




                                                                     .
</p><p id="p_9">    opportunity   to   lead   preliminary   evidence   in   support   of   the 





    complaint filed by him against the respondents.
</p>




<p id="p_10">    2.           Having   heard   learned   counsel   representing   the 

    parties   and   perused   the   material   available   on   record, 





    especially order dated 3rd  July, 2018, passed by this Court in 

    Cr.MMO   No.143   of   2018,   this   Court   sees   no   illegality   and 


    infirmity in the impugned order passed by the learned court 

    below. This Court vide order dated 3 rd  July, 2018, passed in 


    Cr.MMO No.143 of 2018, had fixed the matter before the Court 

    below  for recording preliminary evidence  on 6 th August, 2018. 
</p>



<p id="p_11">    It   was   categorically   observed   in   the   order   that     in   case 





    preliminary evidence is not produced  on the given date by the 





    petitioner,  his right  to do the same shall be deemed to have 

    been closed.   This Court while fixing the date  i.e. 6 th August, 

    2018 for recording   preliminary evidence,   had also observed 

    that in case court below has already granted or fixed the date 

    for leading preliminary evidence on or before 6th August, 2018, 




<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                                                  ::: Downloaded on - 13/09/2018 22:58:35 :::HCHP</span>
     petitioner shall be allowed to lead preliminary evidence on the 

    given date,but in no situation, petitioner would be allowed to 




                                                                       .
</p><p id="p_12">    lead   preliminary   evidence   in   support   of   his   case   beyond   6 th 





    August, 2018.
</p>




<p id="p_13">    3.            Material available on record, clearly suggests that 

    learned   court   below   had   already   directed   the   present 





    petitioner   to   lead   preliminary   evidence   in   support   of   his 

    allegations against the respondent­accused and in this regard, 


    complainant's witnesses were summoned for 25th July, 2018. It 

    is not in dispute that on 25 th  July, 2018 despite there being 


    specific direction issued by this Court vide order dated 3 rd July, 

    2018,   passed     in   Cr.MMO   No.143   of   2018   and   order   dated 




    28.5.2018, passed by the learned court below, petitioner failed 





    to present himself for getting preliminary evidence recorded in 





    support   of   his   complaint/case.   Order   dated   25 th  July,   2018 

    reveals that complainant despite repeated pass overs failed to 

    put in appearance and as such, complaint having been filed by 

    him came to be dismissed in default for non prosecution.
</p>



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                                                    ::: Downloaded on - 13/09/2018 22:58:35 :::HCHP</span>
<p id="p_14">     4.            Consequently, in view of the  aforesaid, this Court 

    sees   no   illegality   and   infirmity   in   the   order   passed   by   the 




                                                                        .
</p><p id="p_15">    learned trial Court because admittedly this Court vide order 





    dated 3rd  July, 2018   passed in Cr.MMO No.143 of 2018, had 





    specifically ordered that only one opportunity shall be afforded 

    to the petitioner to lead preliminary evidence i.e. either on the 





    date already fixed by the learned court below or on  6 th August, 

    2018 i.e. date fixed by this Court and as such, present petition 


    is dismissed being devoid of merit. The  impugned order dated 

    25.7.2018 is upheld. Pending application(s), if any also stands 


    disposed of.
</p>



<p id="p_16">                                                       (Sandeep Sharma),
                                                           Judge





    11th September,2018
          (shankar)
