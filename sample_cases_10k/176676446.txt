<div class="doc_title">N.Latha, vs The State Of Andhra Pradesh, on 29 November, 2018</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: Kongara Vijaya Lakshmi</div>

<pre id="pre_1">      HON'BLE SMT. JUSTICE KONGARA VIJAYA LAKSHMI

                    WRIT PETITION NO.13785 of 2015

ORDER:
</pre>
<p id="p_1">         This writ petition is filed to declare the action of the 3rd respondent

in allotting the shop No.2, New Choultry at Tirumala vide proceedings

Roc.No.Rev.2/168/AEO(Rev)/Tml/2008 dt.10.9.2008 in favour of the 4th

respondent on the ground of purchase of shop which was originally in

favour      of     the     petitioner's   mother     vide     proceedings     in

Roc.No.PO4/546/AEO (REV)/TML/89 dated 9.4.1992 of the 2nd

respondent as illegal and arbitrary and a consequential direction is sought

to direct the respondents to re-allot and handover Shop No.2, New

Choultry, Tirumala, to the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_2">         The case of the petitioner is that her father's land was acquired for

widening of the then by-pass road in Tirupati by the 2nd respondent; her

father expired at the time of acquisition of the site; the 2nd respondent

allotted shop to her mother as compensation; after the death of her

mother, the same was run by the petitioner; her brother filed WP.No.1923

of 2015 and the said writ petition was disposed of directing the 1st

respondent therein to consider the objections of the writ petitioner and

the 5th respondent and to pass appropriate orders.

</p><p id="p_3">         When the matter came up for hearing, learned standing counsel for

the         2nd          respondent       passed         on        proceedings
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                     2</span>




Roc.No.Rev.2/168/AEO(P&amp;R)/TTD/TML/2008                  dated    26.11.2015,

which reads as under:
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">             "Perused the connected files and records available.
      It is evident that Smt.J.Devi has been running the shop
      from 16.1.1998. the license of the shop was transferred in
      the name of Smt.J.Devi in this office orders dt.10.8.2008.
      Sri N.Suresh, N.Ramesh andN.Latha, legal heirs of
      N.Vasanthamma the original allottee of Shop did not
      raise any objection till 2015. Further, Smt. J.Devi has
      produced an affidavit of Sri N.Suresh attested by Notary
      stating that he has no objection for transfer of license for
      the said shop in favour of Smt.J.Devi. Even after
      transferring the license in the name of Smt.J.Devi the
      above said individuals did not file any objection
      regarding running of shop or transfer of license in
      favournof Smt.J.Devi for more than 6 years.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">             In view of the above balance of convenience is in
      favour of Smt.J.Devi and it is hereby ordered to maintain
      status-quo as on 13.8.2015 and Smt.J.Devi is allowed to
      run the shop for the time being. However, it will not
      preclude other three individuals i.e. Sri N.Suresh,
      N.RAmesh &amp; Smt.N.Latha to approach the competent
      Civil Court on the validity of theun-registered sale letter
      purported to be executed by late N.Vasanthamma
      dt.16.1.1998 and sale letter executed by Sri N.Suresh
      dt.10.07.2008."</blockquote><p id="p_4">


</p><p id="p_5">      In view of the above proceedings, the Writ Petition is disposed of

giving liberty to the petitioner to work out her remedies, in accordance

with law. No order as to costs.
</p>
<p id="p_6">      Miscellaneous petitions pending, if any, shall stand closed.




</p><p id="p_7">                                        ____________________________
                                        KONGARA VIJAYA LAKSHMI, J

Date: 29/11/2018
