<div class="doc_title">An Application For Bail Under ... vs In Re: Madhabendu Ganguly on 25 November, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                                          1

10   25.11.2016</pre>
<p id="p_1">SK   Court No.26
                                    CRM 8146 of 2016

                   In the matter of an application for bail under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of the
                   Code of Criminal Procedure filed on 28.09.2016 in connection
                   with Suri Women Police Station Case No. 21 of 2014 dated
                   14.11.2014 under <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_2">Section 506</a> of the Indian Penal Code and
                   <a href="/doc/1832444/" id="a_3">Section 6</a> of the Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act,
                   2012 and <a href="/doc/112223967/" id="a_4">Section 66E</a> of the Information Technology Act, 2000.

</p><p id="p_2">                                              And


                   In Re: Madhabendu Ganguly                                 Petitioner.


                    Mr. Debasish Roy,
                    Mr. Biswajit Manna                        ......For the Petitioner.


</p><p id="p_3">                    None                                             ... For the State.
</p>
<p id="p_4">
</p><p id="p_5">                           By our order passed on November 3, 2016, we granted

                   bail to one Madhabendu Ganguly in connection with CRM No.

                   8146 of 2016, arising out of Suri Women Police Station Case No.

                   21 of 2014 dated 14.11.2014 under <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_5">Section 506</a> of the Indian

                   Penal Code and <a href="/doc/1832444/" id="a_6">Section 6</a> of the Protection of Children from

                   Sexual Offences Act, 2012 and <a href="/doc/112223967/" id="a_7">Section 66E</a> of the Information

                   Technology Act, 2000.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                           Today,   the   learned   counsel    for    the   petitioner   Mr.

                   Debasish Roy, points out that in our order, in the second

                   paragraph it was recorded that no charge has yet been framed,

                   but that is not correct. The charge has been framed, but
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                       2</span>


commencement of evidence has not been started.

</p><p id="p_7">       Accordingly, the sentence 'charge has not yet been

framed', be read as 'charge has been framed, but recording of

evidence has not been commenced'.
</p>
<p id="p_8">       Office is directed to supply Photostat certified copy of

both the orders, if applied for, in course of today at once.




</p><p id="p_9">                                   (Ashim Kumar Roy, J.)



                                 (Malay Marut Banerjee, J.)
 </p>
