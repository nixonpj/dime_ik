<p id="p_1">CS No.: 18/13 Sh. Iqbal Ahmed Siddiqui Vs. Masood Siddiqui Page No. 1 of 4
</p><p id="p_2"> 1.     The   present   suit   has   been   filed   by   the   plaintiff   Sh.   Iqbal   Ahmed 

Siddiqui against defendant Sh. Masood Siddiqui for a decree for recovery of 

Rs. 2.5 lacs along with interest till realization of the amount. It is submitted 

by the plaintiff that the defendant approached him to provide a friendly loan 

and he considering their relations extended a loan of Rs. 5,50,000/­ to the 

defendant on 10.04.06 by way of two cheque bearing no. 0325104 of Rs. 

10,000/­ and cheque no. 0325103 of Rs. 4.50 lacs both dated 10.04.06.  It is 

further submitted that the plaintiff also gave Rs. 90,000/­ in cash to the 

defendant on the very same date.  It is stated that in order to discharge the 

liability, the defendant issued a cheque bearing no. 351296 dated 18.03.10 

for Rs. 2.5 lac drawn on M/s Corporation Bank and that the cheque was 

returned unpaid by the banker with the remarks "insufficient funds" vide 

memo dated 20.03.10 when was presented to the bank.  It is submitted that 

the   plaintiff   immediately   contacted   the   defendant   about   dishonour   of 

cheuqe but he did not pay any heed to the request of the plaintiff nor made 

any payment and that the plaintiff left with no other alternative got issued a 

legal notice dated 07.04.10 which was sent through registered post and UPC 

and that the defendant did not reply the notice nor complied the same.  It is 

submitted that the plaintiff filed a criminal complaint against the defendant 

for the offence punishable U/s 138 <a href="/doc/1132672/" id="a_1">Negotiable Instrument Act</a>, 1881 which 

is pending trial.  It is claimed that the plaint has been valued properly for 

the purpose of jurisdiction as well as court fees and that the requisite court 


CS No.: 18/13 Sh. Iqbal Ahmed Siddiqui Vs. Masood Siddiqui Page No. 2 of 4
 fees has been affixed on the plaint and that this court has  jurisdiction to try 

the case as both the parties to the suit voluntarily resides and work for gain 

at District Shahdara and entire cause of action has arisen here.

</p><p id="p_3">2.     The summons for appearance of the suit were issued to the defendant 

and on the date of hearing before the court one lady counsel appeared for 

defendant   and   at   her   request   matter   was   passed   over   awaiting   the 

appearance   and   Vakalatnama   but   no   appearance   was   filed   on   behalf   of 

defendant, the court considering the pendency of application of plaintiff 

seeking amendment in the plaint adjourned the matter.  The defendant filed 

the appearance on 15.04.13 and he was again ordered to be served with the 

notice of the amendment application but defendant did not appear to contest 

the application as such the application was allowed and upon filing of the 

amended plaint as per prayer of the plaintiff the suit was considered as an 

ordinary recovery suit. 
</p>
<p id="p_4">3.     The plaintiff was  asked to prove his case by leading evidence and 

accordingly he filed his affidavit in support of his case.

</p><p id="p_5">4.     The plaintiff has deposed in line of his contentions and everments 

made in  the plaint and has also placed on file the certified copy of the 

cheque   issued   in   favour   of   his   firm   M/s   Mark   International   and   its 

dishonour   upon   presentation.     The   court   has   no   grounds   to   have   any 

suspicious on the testimony of the plaintiff as well as the documents filed 

by him.   In the facts where case of the plaintiff remains  un­rebutted and 


CS No.: 18/13 Sh. Iqbal Ahmed Siddiqui Vs. Masood Siddiqui Page No. 3 of 4
 unchallenged, the suit of the plaintiff is decreed for recovery of Rs. 2.5 lacs. 

The plaintiff is also entitled for the interest @ 18% pa as per the provisions 

of <a href="/doc/1132672/" id="a_2">Negotiable Instrument Act</a>, 1881 from the date of cheque till realization. 

Decree sheet be prepared.  
</p>
