<div class="doc_title">Uday Singh @ Rana Uday Prakash ... vs The State Of Bihar on 14 March, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.10787 of 2016
                        Arising Out of PS.Case No. -163 Year- 2015 Thana -SILAO District- NALANDA
                                                     (BIHARSHARIFF)
                 ======================================================
                 Uday Singh @ Rana Uday Prakash Singh S/o Late Narayan Singh resident
                 of Village- Bhavani Bigha, P.S. Silao District Nalanda.

                                                                                .... ....   Petitioner/s
                                                        Versus
                 The State of Bihar

                                                                   .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :  Mr. Rana Baljit Singh
                 For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr. Veena Kri.Jaiswal(App)
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE DINESH KUMAR
                 SINGH
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">02/ 14-03-2016                             Heard learned counsels for the petitioner

                 and the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                            The petitioner being the father of the

                 husband of the victim is apprehending his arrest in a case

                 registered for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/653797/" id="a_1">Sections 304B</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_2">34</a> of

                 the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                            The prosecution case is of killing the

                 daughter of the informant after two years of marriage for non-

                 fulfillment of the dowry demand by administering poison.

</p><p id="p_4">                                            It is submitted by learned counsel for the

                 petitioner that the accusation is omnibus and general against

                 entire in-laws family and the victim died natural death when the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">       Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.10787 of 2016 (2) dt.14-03-2016</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                 2/2</span>




                   information was given to her parental family. It is further

                   submitted that though some of the injuries were found on the

                   body of the victim but the cause of death has not been

                   ascertained, hence, the viscera has been preserved and others

                   have been granted anticipatory bail.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                               Considering the aforesaid facts, let the

                   above named petitioner be released on anticipatory bail, in the

                   event of arrest or surrender before the learned court below within

                   a period of twelve weeks from today, on furnishing bail bonds of

                   Rs.10,000/- (Ten thousand) with two sureties of the like amount

                   each to the satisfaction of learned Chief Judicial Magistrate,

                   Nalanda at Biharsharif in connection with Silao P.S. Case No.

                   163 of 2015, subject to the conditions as laid down under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_3">Section

                   438(2)</a> of the Cr.P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_6">


</p><p id="p_7">                                              (Dinesh Kumar Singh, J)
    DKS/-
</p>
