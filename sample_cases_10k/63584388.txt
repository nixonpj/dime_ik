<div class="doc_title">Rahul Jaswal vs State Of Punjab on 21 October, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">CRM-M-28551-2016 (O&amp;M)                                             -1-


      IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA AT
                     CHANDIGARH

                                                   CRM-M-28551-2016 (O&amp;M)
                                                   Date of decision : 21.10.2016

Rahul Jaswal
                                                              ...Petitioner

                                        Versus
State of Punjab
                                                              ...Respondent

CORAM: HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE JITENDRA CHAUHAN

Present:     Mr. Veneet Sharma, Advocate for the petitioner.

             Mr. Luvinder Sofat, AAG, Punjab,
             assisted by ASI Kuldeep Singh.


JITENDRA CHAUHAN, J. (Oral)
</pre>

<p id="p_1">            The petitioner seeks pre-arrest bail in case FIR No.141 dated

02.05.2015, registered under <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_1">Sections 420</a>, <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_2">406</a>, <a href="/doc/1985627/" id="a_3">467</a>, <a href="/doc/556166/" id="a_4">468</a>, <a href="/doc/1466184/" id="a_5">471</a> and <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_6">120-B</a> of

the Indian Penal code, at P.S. Civil Lines, Amritsar City.

</p><p id="p_2">            Heard.
</p>
<p id="p_3">            On 22.08.2016, the following order was passed:-

</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">                          "Contends        that   only    negligence     has   been

                   attributed to the petitioner. The petitioner did not act in

                   a mala fide or dishonest manner to defraud the

                   complainant. The learned counsel refers to Annexures P-

                   3 to P-5 to contend that the matter stands amicably

                   settled.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_2">                          Notice of motion for 21.10.2016.</blockquote><p id="p_4">

</p><p id="p_5">                          Meanwhile, in the event of arrest of the petitioner

                   by the Arresting Officer, he shall be released on interim

                               1 of 2

 CRM-M-28551-2016 (O&amp;M)                                             -2-
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_3">                      bail subject to the following conditions:-

</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_4">                     1.That he shall make himself available for interrogation
                     by a police officer as and when required;
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_5">                     2.That he shall not directly or indirectly make any
                     inducement, threat or promise to any person acquainted
                     with the facts of the case so as to dissuade him from
                     disclosing such facts to the Court or to any police officer
                     and;
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_6">                     3.That he shall not leave India without prior permission
                     of the Court."</blockquote><p id="p_7">
</p><p id="p_8">               Learned State counsel, on instructions, submits that in

pursuance of the order passed by this Court, the petitioner has joined the

investigation and he is not required for custodial interrogation. He further

acknowledges the factum of the compromise having been arrived at between

the parties.
</p>
<p id="p_9">               In view of the above, without expressing any opinion on the

merits of the case, the interim bail granted by this Court vide order dated

22.08.2016, is made absolute, subject to furnishing bail bonds/surety bonds

to the satisfaction of the Chief Judicial Magistrate/Duty Magistrate

concerned.
</p>
<p id="p_10">               The petition stands allowed.
</p>
