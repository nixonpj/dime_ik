                          JUDGMENT

<p id="p_1">     As to how the discretion should be exercised by the Claims

Tribunal to exempt a party from the payment of fee under Rule

397 of the Kerala Motor Vehicles Rules has been delineated in

the following decisions.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                (i)   Raman Nair Vs. Mukudan

                      Nair [2005(3) KLT SN 83].
</p>
<p id="p_3">                (ii)  Gopi Vs. Biju [2010(4) KHC

                      96].
</p>
<p id="p_4">                (iii) Suma Vs. Rajesh P.A. [2011

                      (4) KLT 109].
</p>
<p id="p_5">     2.    The exercise of discretion by Claims Tribunal should

be reflected in the order which is wholly absent in the instant

case.    The impugned order reads "No ground to exempt.

Rejected."
</p>
<p id="p_6">     3.    There is no necessity to issue notice to the respondent

in the instant case as the claim petition itself is yet to be

numbered.    The Motor Accident Claims Tribunal, Palakkad is

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                  2</span>

OP(MAC) No. 128/2015




directed to re-entertain the claim petition and pass fresh orders

in terms of Rule 397 of the Kerala Motor Vehicles Rules. The

needful shall be done within a period of one month from the date

of re-presentation of the claim petition.

</p><p id="p_7">      The Original Petition is disposed of.




</p><p id="p_8">                                       V. CHITAMBARESH
                                             JUDGE
