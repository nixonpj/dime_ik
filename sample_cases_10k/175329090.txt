                                                        Versus
                 1. The State of Bihar.
                 2. Prabhakar Singh S/o Rup Narayan Singh, R/o Village- Sareya Ratnakar,
                    P.S.- Tarayan Distt.- Saran at Chapra.

                                                               .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     : Mr. Vijay Kumar
                 For the Opposite Party/s   : Smt. Sahin Begam
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE MOHIT KUMAR SHAH
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">3   26-02-2018                    Heard the learned counsel for the petitioner and the

                 learned A.P.P. for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                  This is an application for grant of anticipatory bail

                 in connection with Tarayan P.S. Case No.241 of 2017 registered

                 for the offence punishable under <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_1">Sections 406</a>, <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_2">420</a>, <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_3">34</a> of the

                 Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                  The allegation in the present case is that the

                 petitioner had taken a sum of Rs.10 lacs from the informant of

                 the present case and thereafter, had executed a bond to return the

                 same but has failed to return the said amount.

</p><p id="p_4">                                  The learned counsel for the petitioner submits that

                 the present dispute can at best be said to be a case of civil dispute
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">          Patna High Court Cr.M isc. No.7980 of 2018 (3) dt.26-02-2018</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                     2/2</span>




                      and the proper remedy for the informant shall be to approach a

                      court of competent civil jurisdiction.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                         I find that the instant case is purely a case of civil

                      dispute and the petitioner is at liberty to approach a court of

                      competent civil jurisdiction for recovery of the said amount and

                      for enforcement of the executed bond between the accused

                      persons and the informant of the present case.

</p><p id="p_6">                                        Having regard to the facts and circumstances of the

                      case, I deem it fit and proper to admit the petitioner herein to the

                      privilege of anticipatory bail, in the even of his arrest or surrender

                      before the court below within a period of six weeks from the date

                      of receipt/production of a copy of this order on furnishing bail

                      bond of Rs.10,000/- (rupees ten thousand) with two sureties of the

                      like amount each to the satisfaction of C.J.M., Saran at Chapra in

                      connection with Taraiyan P.S. Case No.241 of 2017, subject to the

                      conditions as laid down under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_4">Section 438(2)</a> of the Code of

                      Criminal Procedure.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><p id="p_8">                                                  (Mohit Kumar Shah, J)
N.H./-
</p>
