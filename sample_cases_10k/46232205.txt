<div class="doc_title">Bhola Mansuri @ Md. Ibrahim vs The State Of Bihar on 1 May, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                              CRIMINAL MISCELLANEOUS No.28115 of 2019
                    Arising Out of PS. Case No.-50 Year-2018 Thana- LAKHNAUR District- Madhubani
                 ======================================================
           1.     BHOLA MANSURI @ MD. IBRAHIM Son of Late Jhameli Mansuri @
                  Late Md. Gafur @ Uchit Ram Resident of Village - Nandenagar, P.S.-
                  Lakhnour, District - Madhubani.
           2.    Digamber Chaupal @ Bishambhar Chaupal Son of Late Pritam Chaupal
                 Resident of Village - Tamuria, P.S.- Lakhnaour, District- Madhubani.

                                                                                ... ... Petitioner/s
                                                      Versus
                 The State of Bihar

                                                        ... ... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :      Mr.Md. Nazir Ansari
                 For the Opposite Party/s :      Mr.Shyam Kumar Singh
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE PRAKASH CHANDRA
                 JAISWAL
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   01-05-2019                    Heard learned counsel for the petitioners and learned

                   A.P.P. for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                The petitioners apprehend their arrest in connection

                   with Lakhnour P.S. Case No. 50 of 2018, registered for the

                   offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1258372/" id="a_1">Sections 147</a>, <a href="/doc/763672/" id="a_2">148</a>, <a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_3">149</a>, <a href="/doc/828685/" id="a_4">151</a>, <a href="/doc/1432790/" id="a_5">188</a>,

                   <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_6">341</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_7">323</a>, <a href="/doc/1243353/" id="a_8">342</a>, <a href="/doc/1183069/" id="a_9">386</a>, <a href="/doc/1101188/" id="a_10">379</a>, <a href="/doc/222396/" id="a_11">427</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_12">504</a> and <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_13">506</a> of the Indian Penal

                   Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                Petitioners along with 1400-1500 unknown men and

                   women armed with various weapons started constructing hut on

                   the land of the informant. On protest they assaulted the

                   informant and his family members.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                  It is submitted by the learned counsel for the
               Patna High Court CR. MISC. No.28115 of 2019(2) dt.01-05-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                          2/2</span>




                        petitioners that no such occurrence as alleged ever took place.

                        Petitioners happens to be member of mob. They have no

                        concern with alleged occurrence. They have no criminal

                        antecedent. Similarly situated other co-accused have been

                        enlarged on bail by different co-ordinate Bench of this Court in

                        Cr. Misc. No. 48411 of 2018, Cr. Misc. No. 70213 of 2018 and

                        Cr. Mics. No. 70980 of 2018 vide order dated 24.08.2018,

                        07.12.2018 and 19.12.2019 respectively.

</p><p id="p_5">                                     Having regard to the facts and circumstances of the

                        case, let the above named petitioners, be released on bail, in the

                        event of their arrest or surrender before the learned Court below

                        within a period of four weeks from today, on furnishing bail

                        bond of Rs.10,000/- (Rupees Ten Thousand) each with two

                        sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of the learned

                        Additional Chief Judicial Magistrate, 1st Jhanjharpur in

                        connection with Lakhnour P.S. Case No. 50 of 2018, subject to

                        the condition as laid down under Section 438 (2) of the Cr.P.C.



</p><p id="p_6">                                            (Prakash Chandra Jaiswal, J)
T.Kr./-
</p>
