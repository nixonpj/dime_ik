<p id="p_1">     The    petitioner        has     challenged     the

judgment    and   order       dated    12.11.2008     in

Criminal Appeal No.213/2007 dismissing the

appeal and confirming the order of the learned

Magistrate passed under <a href="/doc/1823824/" id="a_1">Section 138</a> of the

N.I.Act. There is a delay of 1551 days in filing

the revision petition.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">     2.    In   para-4    of    the     affidavit,   the

petitioner says that as he suffered heavy loss

in the business and went to Rajastan for work

and his mother is suffering from diabetes

because of her frequent ill health his family

was shifted to Poona.     So he was not in touch

with his advocate at Belgaum.           In the month
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                             3</span>


of January 2013 he said to have come to

Belgaum and in the month of February 2013

he   enquired      with   the   counsel    about     the

disposal of the appeal.
</p>
<p id="p_4">
</p><p id="p_5">     3.     The    explanation       offered    in   the

affidavit reveals that the appellant was not

diligent in prosecuting the appeal and he did

not take care of the case or talk with his

counsel for a period of four years from the

date of disposal of the appeal. The appeal was

filed in the year 2007 and it came to be

dismissed     on    12.11.2008.      As   the   ground

pleaded is insufficient, the delay cannot be

condoned.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><p id="p_7">     4.     In the result, the interim application

I.A. No.1/2013 is dismissed.


</p><p id="p_8">     5.     Criminal      Revision   petition   is   not

maintainable and it is rejected.
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                         4</span>


</p><p id="p_9">     I.A.   No.2/2013   does   not   survive   for

consideration and is accordingly disposed of.
