<div class="doc_title">Sonu Kumar Shah &amp; Anr vs State Of Chhattisgarh on 8 March, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                                       Page No.1


                                                                      NAFR
               HIGH COURT OF CHHATTISGARH, BILASPUR
                              M.CR.C. No. 1252 of 2017
     1. Sonu Kumar Shah, S/o. Ravinder Shah, Aged About 21 Years, R/o.
        Village-Jamuniya, Post-Office &amp; Police Station - Parbatta, District -
        Bhagalpur (Bihar)
     2. Bhavesh Kumar Gupta, S/o. Parmanand, Aged About 28 Years, R/o.
        Village -Govindpur, Post Office &amp; Police Station- Mahesh Khunt,
        District- Khagadiya (Bihar)
                                                                 ----Applicants
                                    Versus
     1. State Of Chhattisgarh, Through : The Station House Officer, Police
        Station - Devbhog, District - Gariyaband, Chhattisgarh.
                                                              ---- Respondent</pre>


<p id="p_1">For Applicants              : Mr. Shivendu Pandya, Advocate
For Respondent/State        : Mr. Neeraj Jain, Govt. Advocate



                    Hon'ble Shri Justice Goutam Bhaduri

                               Order On Board

08/03/2017

</p><p id="p_2">1.      This is the second bail application filed under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of the Code

        of Criminal Procedure, 1973 for grant of regular bail to the applicants

        who have been arrested in connection with Crime No.199/2016,

        registered at Police Station - Devbhog, District - Gariyaband (C.G.) for

        the offence punishable under <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_2">Section 420</a>, <a href="/doc/386021/" id="a_3">201</a>, <a href="/doc/812083/" id="a_4">411</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_5">34</a> of the Indian

        Penal Code. The first bail application was dismissed as withdrawn with

        liberty to repeat the same after examination of the victim vide order

        dated 03.01.2017 in M.Cr.C. No.8340/2016.


</p><p id="p_3">2.      Case of the prosecution in brief is that a report was made that on

        02.09.2016 the applicants came to the house of Vishwabihari Awasthi

        and projected themselves to be the sellers of some detergent powder,

        thereafter, they cleaned one copper utensils. Subsequently, daughter-
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">                                                                              Page No.2

              in-law of the complainant Manisha Awasthi gave Mangalsutra worth

              Rs.1,05,000/- and two ear top worth Rs.15,000/-, which was closed in

              a tiffin box with the powder in order to clean them and subsequently,

              the applicants got it heated. Subsequently, the ornaments were

              missing. Thereby the offence has been committed.


</blockquote><p id="p_4">         3.   Learned counsel for the applicants would submit that this is second

              bail application. The first bail application was dismissed as withdrawn

              with liberty to repeat the same after examination of the complainant

              vide order dated 03.01.2017. It is stated that the complainant has been

              examined and the compromise has been affected between the parties,

              therefore, the applicants may be enlarged on bail.


</p><p id="p_5">         4.   On the other hand, learned counsel for the State opposes the bail

              application.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><p id="p_7">         5.   I have heard the learned counsel appearing on behalf of the parties.


</p><p id="p_8">         6.   Perused the statement of the victim. The statement was recorded on

              23.01.2017, wherein positive allegations have been attributed. Though

              the application under <a href="/doc/895891/" id="a_6">Section 320</a> (2) of <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_7">Cr.P.C</a>. has been filed for

              compromise. Taking into such fact at this stage the disputed facts have

              come to fore, therefore, I am not inclined to release the applicant on

              bail.
</p>
<p id="p_9">
</p><p id="p_10">         7.   Accordingly, the bail application filed under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_8">Section 439</a> of the Cr.P.C.

</p><p id="p_11">              is dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_12">
</p><p id="p_13">                                               8.                     Sd/-
</p><p id="p_14">                                                                (Goutam Bhaduri)
                                                                     Judge



Balram
