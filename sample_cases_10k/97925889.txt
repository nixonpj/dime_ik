                                      1




    IN THE COURT OF MS JYOTI KLER: CIVIL JUDGE: DELHI

Suit No. 109/05

Sarda Solvent Extractions ( P) Ltd.
at C-200, Industrial Area,
Phase-I, Naraina, New Delhi-28
Through its Director
Sh. Kailash Newar

                                                              ...... Plaintiff
     VERSUS

Jagdish Diwedi
Jai Durga Enterprises
175/6, Pul Pehladpur,
Near Badarpur, Delhi.
                                                             .... Defendant

(1)Date of conclusion of final argument: 08.07.2008
(2)Date of Judgment: 15.07.2008
(3)

                        SUIT FOR RECOVERY



Ex-parte Judgment

           Vide this ex-parte Judgment, I shall decide the present suit

for recovery of RS.42,504/- filed by Sarda Solvent Extraction (P) Ltd.,

                                                                         1 of 7
                                                    Sarda Solvent Vs Jagdish Diwedi
                                     2




through its Director Sh. Kailash Newar, (hereinafter referred as the

plaintiff) against Sh. Jagdish Diwedi (hereinafter referred as the

'defendant').

2.          The facts averred in the plaint are as follows:-

            Plaintiff company is dealing in business of printing and

dyeing of fabrics at C-200, Industrial Area Phase-I, Naraina, New Delhi.

Sh. Kailash Newar is Director of the plaintiff company.            He is fully

conversant with the facts and circumstance of the case. He has also

been duly authorised by the company to sign, verify, file and pursue the

present suit. Defendant is a Proprietor of Jai Durga Enterprises, 175/6,

Pul Pehladpur near Badarpur, Delhi. Defendant is dealing in sale of

printed and dyed fabrics and, therefore, plaintiff and defendant had

business dealings since long. Defendant used to purchase printed and

dyed fabrics from the plaintiff. The defendant purchased these printed

and dyed fabric from the plaintiff vide Bills No. 815,819,821,015,026

dated 25.3.2002, 27.3.2002, 28.3.2002, 11.4.2002 and 15.4.2002


                                                                            2 of 7
                                                  Sarda Solvent Vs Jagdish Diwedi
                                   3




respectively. These bills are amounting to Rs.1,08,512.50. Out of this

total sum the defendant paid a total sum of Rs.80,912/- through cheque

and cash.    Balance of Rs.27,600/- remains, which the defendant did

not pay despite many requests and despite telegraphic notice sent by

the plaintiff to the defendant on 11.4.2005. Hence, the present suit for

recovery of Rs.42,504.50 which includes principal amount as well as

interest in the sum of Rs. 14,904/- calculated @ 18% per annum.

Plaintiff has further claimed pendente-lite and future interest @ 24% per

annum.

3.          Defendant did not appear despite service and, accordingly,

he was proceeded ex-parte on 06.2.2006.

4,          Ex-parte evidence of the plaintiff was recorded. In his ex-

parte evidence the plaintiff has examined one witness namely Sh.

Sushil Verma, its Liaison Officer as PW1.      PW1 has reiterated the

contents of the plaint in his ex-parte deposition. He has also proved

certain documents which are Ex. PW1/1 to Ex. PW1/10. Ex. PW1/1 is


                                                                          3 of 7
                                                  Sarda Solvent Vs Jagdish Diwedi
                                   4




Board resolution authorizing PW1 to file and swear affidavit on behalf of

the plaintiff company. PW1/2 is bill no. 815 dated 25.3.2002 amounting

to Rs.3247.50/-. Ex. PW1/3 is bill no. 819 dated 27.3.2002 amounting

to Rs.35,690/-. Ex. PW1/4 is bill no. 821 dated 28.3.2002 amounting to

Rs.17,975/-. Ex. PW1/5 is bill no. 015 dated 11.4.2002 amounting to

Rs.48,150/-. Ex. PW1/6 is bill no. 026 dated 15.4.2002 amounting to

Rs.3,450/-. Statement of account is Ex. PW1/7 which reflects that all

the aforesaid bills collectively amounted to Rs.1,27,662.50. Out of this,

Rs.90,062/- were paid by the defendant. The balance that remains to be

paid as is Rs.27,600/-. Interest up to 31.3.2005 has been calculated as

Rs.14,904/-.    Therefore, the total outstanding towards defendant

amounts to Rs. 42,504.50.     Ex. PW1/8 is a copy of the telegraphic

notice sent by the plaintiff to the defendant wherein the defendant has

been requested to pay Rs.42,504.50 within 24 hours. Ex. PW1/9 are

the challans attached with various bills detailed above which are the

proof of delivery of printed &amp; dyed fabrics to the defendants.             Ex.


                                                                          4 of 7
                                                  Sarda Solvent Vs Jagdish Diwedi
                                    5




PW1/10 is postal receipts through which legal notice was sent to the

defendant.

5.           PW1 has specifically deposed about the aforementioned

documents as well as about the facts of the case. The testimony of

PW1 has gone un-rebutted and unchallenged.           Further there is no

reason as to why his testimony should not be accepted.               He has

proved all the documents on record. These documents reveal that the

material was delivered to the defendant towards which the defendant

failed to pay some of the amount. The plaintiff was maintaining the

running account of the defendant and the last bill was raised on

15.4.2002</pre><p id="p_1">. The suit has been filed on 15.4.2005 i.e. within a period of

three years and hence, within a period of limitation.           Further the

defendant is residing and carrying on business in Delhi as per address

given in the plaint.   Therefore, this Court has jurisdiction to try and

entertain the present suit.   In these circumstances, the suit of the

plaintiff is decreed for the sum of Rs.42,504/- in favour of the plaintiff


                                                                          5 of 7
                                                  Sarda Solvent Vs Jagdish Diwedi
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                    6</span>




and against the defendant. The plaintiff is also entitled to interest @

18% per annum as the present transaction is a business transaction.

Plaintiff is also entitled to pendente-lite and future interest @ 18% per

annum. Costs of the suit is also awarded in favour of the plaintiff.

Decree Sheet be prepared accordingly. File be consigned to Record

Room.
</p>
