<div class="doc_title">Prehlad Singh vs State Of M.P on 28 February, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                                             1                              MCRC-6756-2013
                    The High Court Of Madhya Pradesh
                              MCRC-6756-2013
                                   (PREHLAD SINGH Vs STATE OF M.P)

5
Gwalior, Dated : 28-02-2019

             Shri Rajeev Shrivastava, learned counsel for the petitioner.

             Shri Sushant Tiwari, learned PP for the respondent No. 1/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">             Shri Neeraj Kumar Bhargava, learned counsel for respondent No. 3.
             Learned counsel for the petitioner informs this Court that respondent No. 2 is
his wife.
</p><p id="p_2">             The present petition under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> of Cr.P.C. has been filed by the

petitioner seeking quashment of FIR which has been registered against the petitioner
at Police Station Narwar, District Shivpuri vide crime No. 29/2012 for alleged
offence under <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_2">Section 420</a> of IPC.
</p><p id="p_3">             After arguing for a while, learned counsel for the petitioner fairly submits that
he intends to submit complete statement of account of present petitioner alongwith
the interest he paid to the account holder Geeta Bai, whose money has been
erroneously utilized by the petitioner because the said money has been transferred by
bank in petitioner's account under mistaken belief.
</p><p id="p_4">             Learned counsel for respondent No. 3-Bank's representative/officer fairly
submits that if detailed statement of account is submitted by the petitioner then

respondent No. 3 shall take into account exact nature of controversy by computing
the loss incurred by the victim Geeta Bai and thereafter if possible then appropriate
steps for settlement of the matter shall be ensured.
</p><p id="p_5">             Considering the submissions, two weeks time is granted to petitioner to move
appropriate application with the statement of account in which petitioner is at liberty
to refer the amount of interest already paid by the petitioner to the victim Geeta Bai
for the money he utilized.
</p><p id="p_6">             List the matter in the week commencing 15th April, 2019 for further
orders.
</p><p id="p_7">             IR, if any, to continue till next date of hearing.
</p>

<p id="p_8">                                                                     (ANAND PATHAK)
                                                                            JUDGE


JPS/-
</p><p id="p_9"> JAI PRAKASH
 SOLANKI
