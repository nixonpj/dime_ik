                 For the Petitioner/s    : Mr. Dinnu Kumar, Advocate.

                  CORAM: HONOURABLE JUSTICE SMT. ANJANA PRAKASH
                                            ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">4   15-07-2013              The Petitioner seeks quashing of the entire proceeding

                 including the order of cognizance dated 03.07.2009 passed in

                 Complaint Case No. 1782 of 1998 by Shri D.R. Upadhyay,

                 Judicial Magistrate, 1st Class, Saran at Chapra under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_1">Section 302</a>

                 of Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                            Since the Petitioner seeks quashing on the disputed

                 question of facts which cannot be gone into at the stage of 482

                 <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_2">Cr.P.C</a>. I am not inclined to interfere with the impugned order.

</p><p id="p_3">                             Hence, the petition is disposed of and the Magistrate is

                 directed to immediately commit the case to the Court of Sessions

                 thereafter the trial-court shall take up the case on priority basis and

                 dispose of the same within a period of nine months from the date
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">  Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.31161 of 2009 (4) dt.15-07-2013</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                            2/2</span>




              of receipt of the case in his file, if it has not done so already. For

              this reason he will send a list of the witnesses fixing specific dates

              for production of the witnesses along with a copy of this order to

              the Superintendent of Police concerned, who is directed to ensure

              production of the witnesses on the date so fixed by the Trial Court

              so that there is no further delay in trial.
</p>
<p id="p_4">


</p><p id="p_5">                                            (Anjana Prakash, J)
