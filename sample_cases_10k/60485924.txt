<p id="p_1">                                  CO 1589 of 2017
    gd
                  Sri Ashok Kumar Jaiswal @ Asok Kumar Jaiswal
                                        Vs.
</p><p id="p_2">                             Sri Mulchand Yadav &amp; Anr.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                       Mr. Sambhunath De
                                 ..for the Petitioner


                        The grievance of the petitioner is that the trial

                court has condoned the delay of more than four years in

                filing the written statement despite no apparent cause

                being shown by the opposite party no.1 herein.

</p><p id="p_4">                        A copy of the application for condonation of delay

                in filing the written statement has been appended to the

                petition.   There are some grounds made out and it is

                evident that the trial court has found sufficiency in the

                grounds urged on behalf of the opposite party no.1. Since

                there is an element of discretion available with the trial

                court and the exercise of such discretion does not appear

                to be palpably perverse, the order impugned cannot be

                interfered with. However, in view of the extremely

                recalcitrant conduct of the opposite party no.1, the trial

                court is requested to take up the suit expeditiously and

                dispose of the same without granting any adjournment to

                the defendant till the conclusion of the suit.

</p><p id="p_5">                        CO 1589 of 2017 is disposed of.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                        There will be no order as to costs.

                        Urgent certified website copies of this order, if
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                     2</span>

applied for, be made available to the parties upon

compliance with the requisite formalities.



</p><p id="p_7">                                   (Sanjib Banerjee, J.)
