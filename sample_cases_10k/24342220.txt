<pre id="pre_1">#33
         IN THE HIGH COURT OF DELHI AT NEW DELHI

                                                  Date of decision: 17.02.2016
W.P.(CRL) 519/2016
ISRAR @ SONU &amp; ORS                             ..... Petitioners
                           Through:     Mr. Zakir Raza, Advocate

                           versus
STATE &amp; ANR                                   ..... Respondents</pre>
<p id="p_1">                           Through:     Mr. Sanjay Lao, ASC (Criminal) with
                                        Mr. Siddharth Sindhu, Advocate and
                                        ASI Krishan Pal, PS- Jafrabad, Delhi
                                        for R-1
                                        Respondent No.2 in person
CORAM:
</p><p id="p_2">HON'BLE MR JUSTICE SIDDHARTH MRIDUL

SIDDHARTH MRIDUL, J (ORAL)

CRL.M.A.2852/2016 (Exemption)

        Exemption granted subject to all just exceptions.

        The application is disposed of accordingly.

W.P.(CRL) 519/2016

</p><p id="p_3">1.      The present is a petition under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the Constitution of India

read with <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_2">Section 482</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (<a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_3">Cr.P.C</a>.)

seeking quashing of FIR No.145/2015, under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_4">Section 498A</a>/<a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_5">406</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_6">34</a> IPC,




<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">W.P. (CRL) 519/2016                                                Page 1 of 3</span>
 registered at Police Station- Jafrabad, Delhi and the proceedings arising

therefrom.
</p>
<p id="p_4">2.      It is nothing but good news as appositely stated by Ms. Sanjay Lao,

learned Additional Standing Counsel (Criminal) appearing on behalf of the

police. It is further stated that the petitioner-husband as well as the

complainant-wife on account of whose matrimonial discord the subject FIR

came to be registered, have reconciled their differences and have been living

together happily, once again since 06.10.2015.

</p><p id="p_5">3.      It would only be fair and appropriate to enable the couple to renew

their vows and attempt to live happily ever after. I am also informed at the

Bar that the petitions under <a href="/doc/1056396/" id="a_7">Section 125</a> of Cr.P.C. and <a href="/doc/1933289/" id="a_8">Section 12</a> of

Protection of Women from <a href="/doc/542601/" id="a_9">Domestic Violence Act</a>, 2005 instituted by the

complainant-wife shall be withdrawn unconditionally as per the terms and

conditions of the settlement agreement dated 06.10.2015.

</p><p id="p_6">4.      Consequently, while wishing the reunited couple a long and happy

life, it would be just expedient and necessary to remove the only road-block

that may become an obstacle in their path of happiness.




<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">W.P. (CRL) 519/2016                                           Page 2 of 3</span>
</p><p id="p_7"> 5.      Resultantly, FIR No.145/2015, under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_10">Section 498A</a>/<a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_11">406</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_12">34</a> IPC,

registered at Police Station- Jafrabad, Delhi and the proceedings arising

therefrom are hereby set aside and quashed qua the petitioner.

</p><p id="p_8">6.      The writ petition is allowed and disposed of accordingly.




</p><p id="p_9">                                              SIDDHARTH MRIDUL, J
        FEBRUARY 17, 2016
        dn




<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">W.P. (CRL) 519/2016                                              Page 3 of 3</span>
