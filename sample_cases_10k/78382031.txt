<div class="doc_title">Pranab Kumar Mondal vs Unknown on 11 January, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                        1

72   11.01.2018</pre><p id="p_1">                                     C.R.M. 333 of 2018
sg   Ct. No.28
                  In Re: - An application for anticipatory bail under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> of the
     (Allowed)
                  Code of Criminal Procedure filed on 09/1/2018 in connection with
                  Rampurhat P.S. Case No. 388/2017 dated 14.12.2017 under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_2">Sections
                  498A</a>/<a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_3">302</a>/<a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_4">120B</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">                                                              And


                  In the matter of: Pranab Kumar Mondal

                                                                                                 ....petitioner
                  Mr. Bitasok Banerjee, Adv.
</p><p id="p_4">                  Mr. R. Mondal, Adv.
</p><p id="p_5">                                                                                        ...for the petitioner

                  Mr. Madhusudan Sur, APP
                  Mr. D. Paramanick, Adv.
</p><p id="p_6">                                                                                              ...for the State


                            It is submitted on behalf of the petitioner that he has been suffering from kidney

                  ailment and was admitted in the hospital at the time of occurrence.

</p><p id="p_7">                           Learned lawyer for the State opposes the prayer for bail and submits that he

                  demanded money from the victim for his treatment.

</p><p id="p_8">                           We have considered the materials on record and we find that the petitioner was

                  admitted in hospital at Bangalore for treatment at the time when the incident occurred.

</p><p id="p_9">                           In view of the aforesaid facts and bearing in mind the precarious health

                  condition of the petitioner, we are inclined in granting anticipatory bail to the petitioner.

</p><p id="p_10">                           In the event of arrest, the petitioner shall be released on bail upon furnishing

                  bond of Rs.10,000/- (Rupees Ten Thousand only) with two sureties of like amount each

                  to the satisfaction of the arresting officer subject to the conditions as laid down under

                  <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_5">Section 438(2)</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973.

</p><p id="p_11">                           The application for anticipatory bail is, accordingly, allowed.

                           Urgent photostat certified copy of this order, if applied for, be supplied

                  expeditiously after complying with all necessary legal formalities.




                  (Rajarshi Bharadwaj, J.)                                     (Joymalya Bagchi, J.)
