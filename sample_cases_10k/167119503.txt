                                   3


                            ORDER

<p id="p_1">       Petitioners          who        are      accused        in

C.C.No.20546/2014 which proceedings are pending on

the   file   of   VI   Addl.Chief     Metropolitan    Magistrate,

Bengaluru are seeking for quashing of said proceedings

and to acquit the petitioners of the offences punishable

under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_2">Sections 498-A</a> and <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_3">506</a> of IPC and <a href="/doc/751411/" id="a_4">Sections 3</a> &amp;

<a href="/doc/1023340/" id="a_5">4</a> of Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">      2.      Today, second respondent - complainant and

her husband - fourth petitioner herein are present

before Court and they have filed a joint memo which is

duly signed by both parties. In the said joint memo, it is

stated that parties have settled their disputes in

M.C.No.1119/2016 before Family Court, Bengaluru,

which agreement is also enclosed to joint-memo. They

also submit that in view of the settlement arrived at

between parties in the mediation held on 09.10.2017,

pursuant to which Family Court has accepted the

compromise and dissolved the marriage solemnised on

12.06.2013        between   fourth     petitioner    and   second
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                4</span>


respondent -complainant, present proceedings pending

against petitioners may be quashed.



</p><p id="p_4">     3.    Second respondent - complainant submits

that she has no objection for proceedings being quashed

against petitioner. Both parties submit that out of their

own free will and volition and without any force, threat

or coercion, they have affixed their signatures to the

joint memo.    Second respondent - complainant also

acknowledges receipt of Demand Draft for a sum of

` 6.5 lakhs from petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_5">
</p><p id="p_6">     4.    Learned     Advocate    appearing    for   second

respondent has filed a photo copy of the identity card

issued by the statutory authority and also copy of

memorandum        of   settlement     entered     into    in

M.C.No.1119/2016 and order sheet of the Family Court.

Same is placed on record.
</p>
<p id="p_7">
</p><p id="p_8">     5.    In the light of joint memo filed by parties and

keeping in mind law laid down by Hon'ble Apex Court in

GIAN SINGH VS. STATE OF PUNJAB AND ANOTHER
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                    5</span>


reported in (2012) 10 SCC 303, this Court is of the

considered    view      that      continuation        of    proceedings

against petitioners would be an abuse of process of law.

</p><p id="p_9">      Hence, I proceed to pass the following:

</p><p id="p_10">                               ORDER


      (1) Criminal petition is hereby allowed.


      (2) Proceedings pending in C.C.No.20546/2014

         on   the    file    of    VI   Addl.Chief         Metropolitan

         Magistrate, Bengaluru            against petitioners is

         hereby quashed.
</p>
<p id="p_11">      (3) Petitioners       are    acquitted     of        the   offences

         punishable under <a href="/doc/1763444/" id="a_6">Sections 498A</a>, <a href="/doc/1763444/" id="a_7">504</a> read with

         <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_8">Section 34</a> IPC &amp; <a href="/doc/751411/" id="a_9">Sections 3</a> &amp; <a href="/doc/1023340/" id="a_10">4</a> of Dowry

         Prohibition Act, 1961.
</p>

</p><p id="p_13">                                                SD/-
</p><p id="p_14">                                               JUDGE
