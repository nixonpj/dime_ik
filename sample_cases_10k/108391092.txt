<div class="doc_title">Manoj Paswan vs State Of Bihar on 26 May, 2010</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                   Cr.Misc. No.18423 of 2010
                                        MANOJ PASWAN
                                               Versus
                                        STATE OF BIHAR
                                             -----------
</pre>
<p id="p_1">2         26.5.2010               Heard the learned counsel for the petitioner, Manoj

                      Paswan, who is in custody since 23.11.2009 in Rajoun P.S. Case

                      No.120/09 corresponding to S.Tr. No.94/10 under sections 25(1-

                      B)a and 35 of the <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_1">Arms Act</a> and 4/5 Explosiv Substance Act.

                      Heard the learned A.P.P. also.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                  According to the allegation the house of co-accused

                      Mangal Harijan was raided and from his house firearms and bombs

                      were recovered. The said Mangal Harijan informed the raiding party

                      that the firearms belonged to this petitioner.

</p><p id="p_3">                                  The learned counsel for the petitioner submitted that the

                      prayer for bail of the co-accused from whose house firearms and

                      bombs were recovered has already been rejected by this Court.

                      Nothing has been recovered from the possession of this petitioner

                      and he is in custody since 23.11.2009 only on the basis of the

                      statement made by the co-accused Mangal Harijan.

</p><p id="p_4">                                  Considering the nature of the allegation and the period of

                      detention    I direct that the petitioner be released     on bail on

                      furnishing bail bond of Rs.5,000/- with two sureties of the like

                      amount each to the satisfaction of the Addl. Sessions Judge, F.T.C.

                      1, Banka.
</p>
<p id="p_5">
</p><pre id="pre_2">    AnilKr.Sinha                              ( Mungeshwar Sahoo, J. )
 </pre>
