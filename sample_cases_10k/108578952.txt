<div class="doc_title">Raees vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 21 August, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                      1                           MCRC-28945-2018
                              The High Court Of Madhya Pradesh
                                        MCRC-28945-2018
                                         (RAEES Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)


                     Jabalpur, Dated : 21-08-2018
                            Shri Durgesh Pandey, learned counsel for the applicant.
                           Shri V.S. Mishra, learned Dy. G.A. for the respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">                           Heard with the aid of case diary.
</p><p id="p_2">                           This is first application filed under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of the Cr.P.C. for
                     grant of bail in connection with Crime No.302/2018 registered at Police
                     Station Gautam Nagar, Bhopal for the offence punishable under <a href="/doc/73862/" id="a_2">Section
                     25</a> of the Arms Act.
</p><p id="p_3">                           As per prosecution case on 01/07/2018 on the information of the
                     informant Mohd. Irshad, Inspector, Police Station Gautam Nagar, Bhopal
                     went to D.I.G. Bungalow Square and apprehended the applicant and
                     seized one gupti from his possession.
</p><p id="p_4">                           Learned counsel for the applicant submits that the applicant is
                     innocent and has falsely been implicated in the offence. The applicant is
                     in custody since 01/07/2018. The charge-sheet has been filed and
                     conclusion of trial is likely to take long time, hence prayed for release of
                     the applicant on bail.
</p><p id="p_5">                           On the other hand learned counsel for the respondent/State opposed
                     the prayer and submitted that other offences are also registered against
                     the applicant, so he should not be released on bail.
</p><p id="p_6">                            After hearing learned counsel for the parties, considering the facts
                     and circumstances of the case and as to the fact that the applicant is in
                     custody since 01/07/2018 and charge-sheet has been filed and conclusion
                     of trial is likely to take long time, without commenting on the merits of
                     the case the application is allowed and the applicant is directed to be
                     released on bail upon furnishing personal bond in the sum of Rs.50,000/-
                     (Rs. Fifty Thousand Only) with one surety in the like amount to the
                     satisfaction of the concerned C.J.M/trial Court for his appearance before
                     the trial Court on all such dates as may be fixed in this behalf by the trial
                     Court during the pendency of trial.
</p><p id="p_7">                            This order will remain operative subject to compliance of the
                     following conditions by the applicant :
</p><p id="p_8">                            1. The applicant will comply with all the terms and conditions of
                     the bond executed by him ;
</p>                            2. The applicant will cooperate in the trial;

<p id="p_9">Digitally signed by ANURAG SONI
Date: 23/08/2018 10:11:42
                                                     2                         MCRC-28945-2018
</p><p id="p_10">                            3. The applicant will not indulge himself in extending inducement,
                     threat or promise to any person acquainted with the fact of the case so as
                     to dissuade them from disclosing such facts to the Court or to the Police
                     Officer, as the case may be;
</p><p id="p_11">                            4. The applicant shall not commit an offence similar to the offence
                     of which he is accused;
</p><p id="p_12">                            5. The applicant will not seek unnecessary adjournments during the
                     trial; and
</p><p id="p_13">                             6. The applicant will not leave India without previous permission
                     of the trial Court.
</p><p id="p_14">                             Certified copy as per rules.
