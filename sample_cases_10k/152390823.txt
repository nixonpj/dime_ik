<p id="p_1">AJAY TEWARI J. (ORAL)

            The controversy in this bunch of petitions relates to fixation of

seniority of JE/SDO/X.EN in the Development and Panchayat Department.

It is not disputed that the matter initially came to the Court in CWP

No.15928 of 1999 titled as <a href="/doc/1312998/" id="a_1">Mast Ram and others v. State of Haryana and

others</a> decided on 7.3.2001 and a Division Bench of this Court held as

follows:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">                  "We are in full agreement with the aforesaid view point,

                  it must, therefore, follow and we have no hesitation in

                  holding that before January, 1996 the posts that fell

                  vacant to the category of Diploma Holder Junior

                  Engineer must be given to them. Thereafter, it the posts

                  fell vacant, the same be given as per the new Rules. The

                  exercise in this regard should be done within four

                  months".
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_2">                         Subject to aforesaid, the writ petition is disposed

                  of."</blockquote><p id="p_2">

</p><p id="p_3">            It is stated that thereafter in another case bearing CWP

No.17681 of 2004 <a href="/doc/1787277/" id="a_2">Zile Singh V. State of Haryana and others</a> this

direction was reiterated. Today, learned Addl. Advocate General has stated
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> CWP No.4115 of 2002                                 -4-</span>


that yet in another case in CWP No.17621 of 2007 titled as <a href="/doc/348666/" id="a_3">Sohan Singh v.

State of Haryana</a> decided on 16.12.2008 another Division Bench of this

Court had directed the respondent to reframe the seniority list. Mr. H. S.

Sra, Addl. A. G., Haryana has fairly stated that State will now reframe the

entire seniority list after inviting objections from all concerned officials and

by following the principle of old vacancies old rules, new vacancies new

rules.
</p>
<p id="p_4">             Learned counsel for the petitioners have no objection to this

course of action. The only caveat made by learned counsel for the

petitioners is that the whole exercise should be completed within a

reasonable time and all objections of all concerned persons should be

considered. Mr. H. S. Sra, Addl. A. G., Haryana states that the whole

exercise will be completed within six months from the date of receipt of a

certified copy of this order.
</p>
<p id="p_5">             These petitions are disposed of with the direction to the

respondents to finalize the seniority of JE/SDO/X.EN within a period of six

months from the date of receipt of a certified copy of this order after

inviting and considering all objections of all concerned persons.

</p><p id="p_6">             It is further stated that no reversion shall be made till the final

order is passed.
</p>
