<div class="doc_title">Sanjit Pandit vs The State Of Bihar on 9 February, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                   Criminal Miscellaneous No.40731 of 2014
                        Arising Out of PS.Case No. -7 Year- 2011 Thana -PARWATTA District- KHAGARIA
                   ======================================================
                   Sanjit Pandit son of Umesh Pandit resident of village- Temtha, P.S.
                   Parbatta, District- Khagaria

                                                                                 .... ....   Petitioner/s
                                                         Versus
                   The State of Bihar

                                                                  .... .... Opposite Party/s
                   ======================================================
                   Appearance :
                   For the Petitioner/s     :  Mr. Bishweshwar Ram
                   For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr. Rajendra Pd.Nat(App)
                   ======================================================
                   CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE DINESH KUMAR
                   SINGH
                   ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">03/   09-02-2015                              Heard learned counsels for the petitioner and

                   the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                              The petitioner is languishing in custody since

                   16.10.2013 in a case registered for the offences punishable under

                   <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_1">Sections 498A</a> and <a href="/doc/653797/" id="a_2">304B</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_3">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code.

</p><p id="p_3">                                              The accusation is of killing the daughter of

                   the informant within three years of the marriage for non-

                   fulfillment of the dowry demand by administering poison.

</p><p id="p_4">                                              It is submitted by learned counsel for that the

                   petitioner that the victim died due to the snack bite.

</p><p id="p_5">                                              The report of the FSL reflects that the

                   victim consumed Aluminum Phosphide commercially known as

                   Celphos.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">        Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.40731 of 2014 (03) dt.09-02-2015</span><p id="p_6">

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                  2/2</span>




</p><p id="p_7">                                               Considering the nature of accusation, this

                    Court is not inclined to grant bail to the petitioner in connection

                    with Sessions Case No. 143 of 2014 arising out of Parbatta P.S.

                    Case No. 07 of 2011 pending in the court of learned III Ad hoc

                    Additional Sessions Judge, Khagaria.

</p><p id="p_8">                                               Accordingly, the prayer for bail of the

                    petitioner is rejected.
</p>
<p id="p_9">                                               Let the trial be expedited.
</p>
<p id="p_10">


                                              (Dinesh Kumar Singh, J)
    DKS/-
</p>
