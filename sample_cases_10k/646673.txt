<div class="doc_title">Yaswant Kumar vs State Of Bihar on 26 March, 2011</div><pre id="pre_1">             IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                       Cr.Misc. No.42077 of 2010
                          YASWANT KUMAR .
                                 Versus
                          STATE OF BIHAR .
                                -----------
</pre>

<p id="p_1">03.   26.03.2011                Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and for

                   the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                The submission on behalf of the petitioner in a

                   Police Case under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_1">Sections 302</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_2">34</a> of the Indian Penal

                   Code is of grant of anticipatory bail to similarly situated co-

                   accused with common allegations in Cr. Misc. No. 21016 of

                   2010 noticing that contrary to the allegations for pressing

                   of the neck, the post-mortem report showed no injury on

                   the neck to submit that the petitioner should also be

                   granted anticipatory bail as he remains the only 5th

                   accused, the other four having been granted anticipatory

                   bail.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                The allegations are that the petitioner and his

                   accomplices pushed down the mother of the informant and

                   pressed her neck, when she died. The informant is stated

                   to be an eye witness to the occurrence. The post-mortem

                   report at paragraph-34 of the case diary placed by the

                   counsel for the petitioner notices that the deceased was 56

                   years of age. Injuries were found on the right occipital

                   region of the head. This was not brought to the attention of

                   the Court in Cr. Misc. No. 21016 of 2010.
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                         2</span>




                The   Court   finds   it     difficult   to   grant   him

      anticipatory bail on the principle of consisting alone.

</p><p id="p_4">                The application is rejected.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                Any application for regular bail on his behalf is

      required to be considered on its own merits without being

      prejudiced by the present order the facts remaining that

      co-accused had been granted bail.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><pre id="pre_2">P.K                                        ( Navin Sinha, J.)
