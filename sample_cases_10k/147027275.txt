<pre id="pre_1">         HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR RAJASTHAN
                     BENCH AT JAIPUR

         S.B. Criminal Miscellaneous (Petition) No. 5410/2018

1.      Sumant Meena S/o Devchand Meena, Aged About 33 Years,
        R/o Village Ranglal Ka Pura, Ps Todabhim, District Karauli.

2.      Shiv Singh Meena S/o Gordhan Meena, R/o Village Ranglal Ka
        Pura, Ps Todabhim, District Karauli.

3.      Yogendra Meena S/o Shri Hariprasad, Aged About 20 Years,
        R/o B-58, Bal Nagar, Police Station Mahesh Nagar, Jaipur.

                                                            ----Petitioners

                                 Versus

1.      State Of Rajasthan Through Pp, Rajasthan.

2.      Hanuman Sahay S/o Sabri Lal, R/o Village Hudla, Tehsil
        Mahua, District Dausa.

3.      Bhagwan Sahay S/o Sabri Lal, R/o Village Hudla, Tehsil Mahua,
        District Dausa.

                                                        ----Respondents</pre>


<pre id="pre_2">For Petitioner(s)         :   Mr. Anurag Shukla
For Respondent(s)         :   Mr. Amit Mundia
For the State             :   Mr. Prakash Thakuriya, P.P.



</pre><p id="p_1">     HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE KANWALJIT SINGH AHLUWALIA

                                 / Order

06/09/2018

           Instant petition has been preferred under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a>

Cr.P.C. seeking quashing of impugned F.I.R. No.218/2007 registered at

Police Station Mahesh Nagar, Jaipur for offences punishable under

<a href="/doc/838469/" id="a_2">Sections 452</a>, <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_3">341</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_4">323</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_5">504</a>, <a href="/doc/222396/" id="a_6">427</a> and <a href="/doc/1096965/" id="a_7">143</a> I.P.C.

</p><p id="p_2">           The learned counsel appearing for the petitioners has

submitted that subsequently, offence punishable under <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_8">Section 324</a>

I.P.C. was added.
</p>
<p id="p_3">           In the alleged occurrence, Bhagwan Sahay had received

injuries. An F.I.R. was lodged by his brother - Hanuman Sahay.
</p><p id="p_4">                                                   (2 of 3)                           [CRLMP-5410/2018]



                  In the present case, quashing of impugned F.I.R., along with

all subsequent proceedings has been sought on the basis of the

compromise, as it is submitted that the dispute has been amicably

resolved and the parties have decided to promote everlasting peace,

amity and harmony.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                  Complainant - Hanuman Sahay is present in person before

this Court, along with his brother - injured Bhagwan Sahay. Both,

complainant - Hanuman Sahay and Bhagwan Sahay have been

identified by their Counsel - Mr. Amit Mundia.

</p><p id="p_6">                  Counsel appearing for the respondents has vouchsafed the

factum of compromise affected between the parties.

</p><p id="p_7">                  Counsel appearing for the petitioners has submitted that

compromise was presented before the trial Court. The trial Court, vide

order dated 14.08.2018 accepted compromise, qua offences punishable

under <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_9">Sections 323</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_10">34</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_11">504</a>, <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_12">341</a> and <a href="/doc/222396/" id="a_13">427</a> I.P.C., as said offences are

compoundable. However, said compromise was rejected, qua offences

punishable under<a href="/doc/838469/" id="a_14">Sections 452</a> and <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_15">324</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_16">34</a> I.P.C. on the ground that the

said offences are non-compoundable.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                  The order dated 14.08.2018 passed by the trial Court reads

as under :-

</p><p id="p_10">                  Injured - Bhagwan Sahay and Complainant - Hanuman

Sahay, respondents Nos.2 and 3, present in person have stated that in
                                                                              (3 of 3)              [CRLMP-5410/2018]



                                           terms of compromise arrived at between the parties, they no longer

                                           intend to pursue the impugned F.I.R.
</p>
<p id="p_11">                                                       Counsel appearing for the petitioners has submitted that

                                           since the parties have decided to promote everlasting peace, amity and

                                           harmony, therefore, dispute which is essentially private in nature stands

                                           resolved.
</p><p id="p_12">                                                       Counsel appearing for the petitioners has prayed that since

                                           the dispute has been amicably resolved, the criminal case pending

                                           between the parties as well as impugned F.I.R. be quashed.

</p><p id="p_13">                                                       It has been often held by the Courts that hour of the

                                           compromise is the finest hour between the parties and the Court while

                                           exercising its inherent jurisdiction under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_17">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C. can quash

                                           the proceedings even qua non-compoundable offences.

</p><p id="p_14">                                                       Relying upon the case of Gian Singh Vs. State of Punjab

                                           &amp; Another [(2012) 10 S.C.C. 303], learned counsel for the

                                           petitioners has pleaded that this Court while exercising its inherent

                                           jurisdiction under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_18">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C. ought to quash the criminal

                                           proceedings, which have been amicably resolved.

</p><p id="p_15">                                                       Considering the fact that parties have amicably resolved

                                           their dispute, the principle established by the Apex Court in the case of

                                           Gian Singh [supra] and in the interest of justice, this Court, hereby,

                                           quashes impugned F.I.R. No.218/2007 registered at Police Station

                                           Mahesh Nagar, Jaipur for offences punishable under <a href="/doc/838469/" id="a_19">Sections 452</a>, <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_20">341</a>,

                                           <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_21">323</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_22">504</a>, <a href="/doc/222396/" id="a_23">427</a> and <a href="/doc/1096965/" id="a_24">143</a> I.P.C., along with all subsequent proceedings.

</p><p id="p_16">                                                       Resultantly, the petition is, hereby, allowed.


</p><p id="p_17">                                                                           (KANWALJIT SINGH AHLUWALIA),J
