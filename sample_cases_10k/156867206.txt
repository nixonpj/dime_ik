           IN THE HIGH COURT OF KARNATAKA
              CIRCUIT BENCH AT GULBARGA

       DATED THIS THE 20TH DAY OF MARCH, 2013

                           BEFORE

   THE HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE HULUVADI G. RAMESH

 MISCELLANEOUS FIRST APPEAL NO.31063/2012 (MV)

Between:
SHIVASHANKAR S/O BHIMAPPA AMBALI
AGE: 26 YEARS, OCC: AGRIL.
R/O NEHARU NAGAR, BIJAPUR-586101.

                                               ....APPELLANT
(BY SRI SANGANABASAVA B. PATIL, ADVOCATE)

And:
1. GOVIND S/O GYANAPPA BUDDAPPANNAVAR
   AGE: 43 YEARS, OCC: BUSINESS,
   R/O H.NO.1942 OM NAGAR,
   BEHIND GOVERNMENT HOSPITAL,
   B.BAGEWADI, BIJAPUR-586101.

2. THE BRANCH MANAGER,
   NEW INDIA INSURANCE CO. LTD.,
   BIJAPUR-586101.


                                            .... RESPONDENTS
(SRI. S.S.ASPALLI, ADVOCATE FOR R-2)

     THE MFA FILED U/S. 173(1) OF MV ACT AGAINST THE
JUDGMENT AND AWARD DATED 12.12.2011 PASSED IN MVC
NO.416/2009 ON THE FILE OF THE MOTOR ACCIDENT CLAIMS
TRIBUNAL NO. VII AT BIJAPUR, PARTLY ALLOWING THE CLAIM
                                   2




PETITION AND AWARDING COMPENSATION OF RS. 1,06,920/- WITH
INTEREST AT 6% P.A.

      The Misc. First Appeal coming on for orders this day, the
court delivered the following:-


                            JUDGMENT
</pre>
<p id="p_1">      The delay of 92 days in preferring the appeal is

condoned subject to the condition that claimant is not

entitled for interest on the enhanced amount, if any for the

delay period.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">      2.    On 14.08.2008 around 12.40 p.m., when the

claimant was travelling on the motorcycle bearing NO. KA-

28/J-5770 towards Muttagi village from Basavana Bagewadi,

at that time, an auto-rickshaw bearing No.KA-28/A-2776

came from opposite direction and dashed to the motorcycle.

Due to the impact, claimant sustained grievous injuries on

left hand and it was operated and steel rod is inserted. There

were injuries on the right knee, head, right ankle, nose and

other parts of the body. He was shifted to BLDE hospital, and

thereafter for higher treatment, he was shifted to S.P.
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                 3</span>




Institute of Neuro Sciences at Solapur. On the claim petition

filed, matter was contested.        The Tribunal after inquiry,

having held that the accident was due to negligence on the

part of the driver of the Auto-Rickshaw, awarded total

compensation of Rs.1,06,920/- in MVC No.416/2009. Being

not satisfied, this appeal is by the claimant.

</p><p id="p_4">     3. Heard.
</p>
<p id="p_5">     4.   Claimant is said to have sustained fracture of left

ulna, and was said to be doing agriculture earning Rs.7,000/-

per month. The disability is assessed at 25% to 30%. Having

regard to the nature of injury, fracture and disability suffered,

the claimant has been awarded compensation on the

following heads;
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">     i. Loss of income and disability : Rs. 32,400/-
</blockquote><pre id="pre_2">     ii. Pain and agony                   : Rs. 12,000/-
     iii. Medical expenses.               : Rs. 35, 520/-
     iv. Loss of earning during laid up
         period.                        : Rs. 09,000/-
     v. Loss of amenities.                : Rs. 10,000/-
     vi. Attending charges                : Rs. 08,000/-
           Total                          : Rs. 1,06,920/-
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                4</span>




</pre><p id="p_6">      5.   Claimant would be awarded another Rs.15,000/-

towards pain and suffering, Rs. 3,000/- towards loss of

earning during laid up period, Rs.10,000/- towards loss of

amenities, Rs.20,000/- towards loss of future earning, taking

the disability and income on the higher side, and Rs. 3,000/-

towards incidental charges. Thus, the claimant is entitled for

Rs.51,000/- over and above what has been awarded by the

Tribunal with interest @ 6% p.a. from the date of petition till

deposit.   The Insurer to deposit the amount within three

months. Appeal is allowed in part.
</p>
