<div class="doc_title">Gyaneshwar vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 1 September, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                   M.Cr.C. No.8717/2017
                (<a href="/doc/190261224/" id="a_1">Gyaneshwar vs. State of M.P</a>.)

01.09.2017
     Shri Aayush Pandey, learned counsel for the applicant.
     Shri Vibhore Khandelwal, learned Public Prosecutor for
the respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">     This is first bail application under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_2">Section 439</a> of Cr.P.C.
The applicant has been arrested in Crime No.356/2016
registered at Police Station-Betma, District - Indore under
Sections 376(2) N, 366, 363 of <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_3">IPC</a> and 5L/6 of Protection of
Children from Sexual Offence Act 2012.
</p><p id="p_2">     As per the prosecution case, the applicant manipulating
the prosecutrix while she was 17 years and 10 months old and
fled away with the her, thereafter upon her recovery the case is
registered.
</p><p id="p_3">     It is contended by learned counsel for the applicant that
as a matter of fact the applicant and the prosecutrix has
solemnized the marriage and she is living with the family of the
applicant. Affidavit on her own has also been filed retaliating
the same submission of facts. Therefore, under such
circumstances the applicant deserves to be enlarged on bail.
</p><p id="p_4">     Learned Public Prosecutor for the respondent/State
opposes the bail application and prays for its rejection.
</p><p id="p_5">     Looking to the totality of facts and circumstances of the
case coupled with the fact that the prosecutrix is living with the
family of the applicant, the applicant deserves to be enlarged on
bail. Accordingly, without expressing any opinion on the merits
of the case, this application is allowed and it is directed that
applicant be enlarged on bail subject to his furnishing personal
bond in the sum of Rs.50,000/- (Rs. Fifty Thousand Only)
                            M.Cr.C. No.8717/2017
                        (<a href="/doc/190261224/" id="a_4">Gyaneshwar vs. State of M.P</a>.)

        with one solvent surety in the like amount to the satisfaction of
        the Trial Court for his regular appearance in the trial Court
        during trial with a condition that he shall remain present before
        the Court concerned during trial and also comply with the
        conditions enumerated under <a href="/doc/259682/" id="a_5">Section 437(3)</a> of Cr.P.C.
</p><p id="p_6">             Let a copy of this order be sent to the concerned trial
        court for necessary compliance.
</p><p id="p_7">             Certified copy as per rules.
</p>

<p id="p_8">                                                 (Rohit Arya)
                                                     Judge

JYOTI
