<div class="doc_title">Ramchandra vs Union Of India on 14 November, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">M.Cr.C. No.21444/2017




                    M.Cr.C. No.21444/2017
                RamchandraVs. Union of India
Indore, dated 14.11.2017
          Shri A.K.Saraswat, learned counsel for the
applicant.
          Shri Manoj Soni, learned counsel for the
respondent.</pre>
<p id="p_1">          This application under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C. is
filed     for   modification   of   the   order   passed    in
M.Cr.C.No.10324/2017 dated 13.10.2017 where the
following conditions were imposed:-
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">                   "By way of abundant caution, it is
          further directed that after being released on
          bail he will not indulge in any kind of
          criminal activities whatsoever and he shall
          also mark his presence in the concerned
          police station on second Sunday of every
          month at 11:00 am during the pendency of
          the trial. Any default in attendance in Court
          and marking their presence in the concerned
          police station, would result in cancellation of
          bail granted by this Court thereby entitling
          the police to take the applicant in custody
          immediately."</blockquote>

<p id="p_2">          Learned counsel for the applicant submits
that on Sunday the office of the respondent normally
remain closed and if the applicant goes to the office
for marking his presence, nobody is available in the
office.
</p><p id="p_3">          After due consideration the prayer is
allowed. It is directed that in place of marking his
presence in the concerning police station on second
 M.Cr.C. No.21444/2017




Sunday of every month at 11:00 am he should mark
his presence on second Wednesday of every month
at 11:00 am in the office of the respondent at Indore.
</p><p id="p_4">          With     the   aforesaid   modification,   the
application stands disposed of. Both the order passed
today and passed in M.Cr.C.No.10324/2017 dated
13.10.2017 shall be read conjunctively.
</p><p id="p_5">         Certified copy as per rules.
</p><p id="p_6">                                          (Alok Verma)
                                              Judge
RJ




Reena Joseph
