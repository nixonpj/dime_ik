<p id="p_1">                Goods being transported by the petitioners have been

             seized under Section 76 of the West Bengal Value Added Tax

             Act, 2003. The petitioners also complain that the goods have

             been exorbitantly valued.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                In keeping with the orders passed in similar matters which

             have been entertained for a limited purpose as the West Bengal

             Taxation Tribunal is not functioning at the moment, the

             petitioners are permitted to deposit half of the amount

             demanded as penalty as condition precedent for obtaining

             release of the seized goods.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                Since the merits of the petitioners' claim have not been

             examined, it is only observed that the petitionera will be

             entitled to obtain release of the goods within 24 hours of the

             deposit made in terms of this order.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                       2</span><p id="p_4">


    In the event the petitioners fail to file an appropriate

petition before the tribunal challenging the impugned penalty

within four weeks from date, the petitioners will be required to

pay the entire amount demanded by the impugned notice.

    The writ petition stands disposed of, leaving the tribunal

free to decide all questions upon the appropriate petition being

carried to it.
</p>
<p id="p_5">    Urgent photostat certified copy of this order, if applied for,

be given to the parties as expeditiously as possible.



</p><p id="p_6">                                          ( Dipankar Datta, J. )
