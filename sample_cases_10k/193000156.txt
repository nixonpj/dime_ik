ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">	Mr.A.Periyasamy has filed this writ petition seeking a direction to the respondents to settle the petitioner's retirement benefits including Gratuity, Provident Fund, Labour Welfare Fund, Earned Leave Salary, ML Credit, ERBS Pension arrears, Pension DA arrears, IRT &amp; Medical College Fund and Interest for belated settlement of Provident Fund, Gratuity, Commutation and Labour Welfare Fund and other benefits etc., with interest at the rate of not less than 18% per annum payable from the date of retirement i.e., 31.01.2017 to till date of actual payment to the petitioner, within the time that may be stipulated by this Hon'ble Court.
</p>
<p id="p_2">	2. Mr.V.Jayaprakash Narayanan, learned Special Government Pleader takes notice on behalf of the first respondent.  Mr.R.K.Gandhi, learned standing counsel takes notice on behalf of the second and third respondents.
</p>
<p id="p_3">	3. The petitioner retired from the services of the respondent Corporation as Foreman, on reaching the age of superannuation, on 31.1.2017. However, since his retirement benefits as mentioned above have not been till date settled to him, he has been advised to come to this Court.
</p>	
<p id="p_4">	4. Since the issue is squarely covered by the various orders passed by this Court, in the light of the Hon'ble Division Bench judgment in W.A.(MD) Nos.383 to 457 of 2015 dated 12.6.2015 (<a href="/doc/174794209/" id="a_2">K.Rajendran and others v. The Tamil Nadu State Transport Corporation (Madurai) Limited</a> represented by its Managing Director, Madurai and others), this writ petition is disposed of with a direction to the respondents to settle the entire retirement benefits due and payable to the petitioner through twelve equal monthly instalments carrying interest of 6% p.a.  The instalments should commence from June, 2017 and each of the instalments should be paid on or before 7th of each month.  In case of any delay in the payment of the instalments, the interest payable would become 18% p.a. for the delayed period apart from any other remedy which may be available to the petitioner for such non-payment of the instalments.  No costs.
</p>
