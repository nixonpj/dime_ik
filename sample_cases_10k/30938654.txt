<pre id="pre_1">"Bhagwan Vs M/s. Sanjay Automotive"                                           'ID No. 52-10'

 IN THE COURT OF SH. PRATAP S. MALIK THE PRESIDING OFFICER

         IN LABOUR COURT XI, KARKARDOOMA COURTS, DELHI



              Type of Case                                    REFERENCE CASE


                                                      F.24 (434)/WD/LABOUR/7994 DATED
              Reference No
                                                                 22.10.2009


          Date of Institution                                    10.02.2010


             Date of Award                                       31.07.2010

            WORKMAN                                               MANAGEMENT

SH. BHAGWAN S/O SH. MANGAL                                 M/S SANJAY AUTOMOTIVE (A UNIT
SINGH C/O RASHTRIYA RAJDHANI                     VS        OF     M/S       INDERPRASHTH
SHETRA ENGG. AND GENERAL                                   AUTOMOBILES PVT. LTD.), 33,
MAZDOOR UNION REGD., C-139,                                RAMA ROAD, NEW DELHI-110015
KARAMPURA, NEW DELHI-110015

PRESENT:

        NONE FOR THE W ORKMAN

      MANAGEMENT IS EX-PARTE

AWARD:


1.</pre><p id="p_1">        Secretary (Labour) Government of National Capital Territory of Delhi, Delhi has

          referred this dispute arising between the parties named above for adjudication to

          this Labour Court vide notification No. F. 24 (434)/WD/LABOUR/7994

          DATED 22.10.2009 with the following term of the reference:-

</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">                  "Whether the services of SH. BHAGWAN S /O SH. MANGAL SINGH have been
                  terminated illegally and/or unjustifiably by the management and if yes, to
                  what relief is he entitled?"
</blockquote>

<p id="p_2">2.        The claim of the workman was that he had been working with the Management

          since 01.07.2005 as 'DRIVER' on a monthly salary of Rs. 5000/-P.M. It was

          further stated that he had been performing his duty sincerely,

          honestly and never gave any chance of complaint during his



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">AWARD                                                                          Page 1 of 3</span>
 "Bhagwan Vs M/s. Sanjay Automotive"                                        'ID No. 52-10'

        tenure of service with the management. It was further stated that services of the

        workman were illegally terminated on 21.10.2008 without assigning any reason

        or giving any notice. It was further stated that a notice through Regd. A.D/Speed

        post was served upon the management on 22.10.2008, but the management did

        not give any reply to the same. It was stated that dismissal of the workman was

        fully illegal, arbitrary, mala fide and an act of unfair labour practices. It was

        prayed that the management would be directed to reinstate him with full back

        wages and continuity in service with all other consequential benefits.

</p><p id="p_3">3.      Notice of the claim was issued to the management. But none had appeared on

        behalf of the Management despite service and the management was proceeded

        ex-parte on 05.04.2010. The matter was adjourned for ex-parte workman

        evidence.
</p>
<p id="p_4">4.      The workman got himself examined as WW1 by tendering his affidavit and

        closed his evidence.
</p>
<p id="p_5">5.      Arguments heard. The record was perused carefully.

</p><p id="p_6">6.      In order to establish its case, besides his testimony, workman has also proved on

        record Ex.WW-1/1 to Ex. WW-1/5 (i.e. the original copy of Demand Notice dt.

        22.10.2008 exhibited as Ex. WW-1/1. The original postal receipt and

        acknowledgement card are exhibited as Ex.WW-1/2 &amp; exw-1/3. The copy of

        'Statement of claim' filed before Conciliation Officer exhibited as Ex. WW-1/4.

        The original Delivery Challan No.1888 dt. 18.08.2008 exhibited as Ex. WW-1/5.

        The photocopy of form-17 (Form of Trade Certificate) marked as Mark-A.

</p><p id="p_7">7.      On the basis of uncontroverted testimony of the workman, it stands proved on

        record that he was working with the Management as 'DRIVER' and his services

        were terminated on 21.10.2008 without assigning any reason and in violation of

        provisions of <a href="/doc/500379/" id="a_1">Industrial Disputes Act</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_8">8.      In these circumstances, on the basis of uncontroverted evidence, it is held that

        the service of the workman was terminated illegally and

        unjustifiably by the management.
</p>
<p id="p_9">

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">AWARD                                                                        Page 2 of 3</span>
</p><p id="p_10"> "Bhagwan Vs M/s. Sanjay Automotive"                                         'ID No. 52-10'

</p><p id="p_11">9.      Keeping in view the above facts and circumstances, it is held that the services of

        workman was terminated illegally as it has been done without the compliance of

        the provisions of SECTION 25-F OF INDUSTRIAL DISPUTES ACT. The only question

        that now remains to be determined is the relief to be granted to the workman.

        Workman has also stated in his claim that since the date of his termination, he is

        unemployed.
</p>
<p id="p_12">10.     It is directed that the workman namely 'BHAGWAN' be reinstated back in

        place of his post as he was on the date of aforesaid termination.

</p><p id="p_13">11.     It is directed that along with his reinstatement the workman be also given 40% of

        his back wages be calculated on the basis of his last drawn wages along with all

        proportionate and consequential relief.
</p>
<p id="p_14">12.     It is ordered accordingly a copy of the Award be sent to an appropriate

        Government for its publication. File be consigned to Record Room after

        completing due formalities.
</p>
<p id="p_15">

ANNOUNCED IN THE OPEN C OURT ON 31.07.2010




PRATAP S. MALIK
POLC XI, Karkardooma Courts, Delhi
