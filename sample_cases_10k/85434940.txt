<div class="doc_title">Bablu Sah vs State Of Bihar &amp; Anr on 24 June, 2013</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                Criminal Miscellaneous No.3552 of 2013
                 ======================================================
                 Bablu Sah son of late Maheshwar Sah, resident of village- Mohiuddinnagar
                 Bazar, PS-Mohiuddinnagar, District-Samastipur.

                                                                       .... ....   Petitioner/s
                                                   Versus
                 The State of Bihar &amp; Anr.
                                                                  .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     : Mr. J. N. Sinha, Adv.
                 For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr. Ansuya Jaiswal, APP
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE SHIVAJI PANDEY
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   24-06-2013               Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned

                 counsel for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                              In this case, the petitioner is challenging the order

                 dated 17th December 2012 passed in Criminal Revision No. 481 of

                 2012 whereby and whereunder the court of Sessions refused to

                 interfere with the order of the Judicial Magistrate 1 st class,

                 Samastipur dated 8th June 2012 passed in G.R. No. 467 of 1999

                 (T.R. No. 804 of 2012) allowing the prosecution to examine

                 Makeshwar Sah, Rinku Kumari and Manju Devi as they were busy

                 for earning their livelihood.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                              The grievance of the petitioner is that after lapse of five

                 years when it was at the stage of 313<a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_1"> of the Criminal Procedure

                 Code</a>, the informant side engaged a lawyer who filed a petition for

                 allowing certain witnesses to be examined in the case. The reason
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">            Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.3552 of 2013 (2) dt.24-06-2013</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                      2/2</span>




                        assigned is private witnesses remained busy for earning his

                        livelihood cannot be termed as wrong reason for interference so

                        much so, the rest witnesses are official witnesses.

</p><p id="p_4">                                         Aforesaid witnesses are essential along with other

                        official witnesses namely, Dr. Shashi Bhusan Singh (Doctor) and

                        Md. Anwarul Haque, Police official who are also essential for the

                        ends of justice.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                         The reason has been affirmed by the Sessions court

                        which cannot be faulted in any way.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                         This Court does not feel inclined to interfere with the

                        order impugned. Accordingly, this petition is dismissed. However,

                        the petitioner is directed to complete evidence of five witnesses

                        within a period of two months from the date of receipt/production

                        of a copy of this order.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><p id="p_8">                                                       (Shivaji Pandey, J)


Mahesh/-
</p>
