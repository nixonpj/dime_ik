                                                                                                   1



                                      <a href="/doc/761026/" id="a_1">Ram Dayal and others vs State of M.P</a>.

09 / 0 9 / 2 0 1 4
                             Shri Narendra Nikhare, Advocate for the applicants.
                             Shri     Ramesh          Kushwah,     Panel     Lawyer      for    the
                     respondent /State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">                             Heard finally. Case diary is perused
                             This is a repeat bail application on behalf of the
                     applicants       under     <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_2">Section 438</a> of Cr.P.C. Their earlier
                     application has been dismissed on merits vide order dated
                     12.06.2014 passed in M.Cr.C.No.8085 / 2014.
</p><p id="p_2">                             The applicants have          apprehension of their arrest in
                     connection with Crime No.48 / 2014 registered at Police
                     Station        Singhpur      District     Satna    (M.P.)     for     offence
                     punishable under <a href="/doc/1439698/" id="a_3">Sections 294</a>, <a href="/doc/1439698/" id="a_4">308</a>, <a href="/doc/1439698/" id="a_5">323</a> and <a href="/doc/1439698/" id="a_6">506-</a> B /34 of
                     <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_7">IPC</a>.
</p><p id="p_3">                             Learned counsel for the applicants                  submits        that
                     applicants have falsely been implicated in this case. It is
                     further submitted that no grievous injury has been caused
                     to the complainant nor there is any fracture and a counter
                     case has also been registered against the complainant
                     party. The applicants are reputed citizen of the locality,
                     having no past criminal antecedents, in the event of arrest
                     their   reputation        will    be tarnished,       therefore,    they    be
                     released on anticipatory bail.
</p><p id="p_4">                             Learned     Panel        Lawyer      vehemently     opposed        the
                     application for anticipatory bail on the ground that injuries
                     caused to complainant Ramsakha are grievous in nature.
</p><p id="p_5">                             Detailed    examination         of    evidence     and      elaborate
                     documentation of merits is not desirable and should be
                          M.Cr.C.No.9815 of 2014




<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                                         2</span>



        avoided while passing orders on bail applications. What is
        necessary is the satisfaction about prima facie case and not
        an exhaustive exploration of merits in the order itself. It is
        the duty of the parties as well as the Court to see that at
        the stage of deciding bail application, the Court is not
        dragged   into   minutest    details   and   in   an    arena   of
        conjectures of hypotheses.
</p><p id="p_6">             This a repeat      bail of the applicant,         his earlier
        application was dismissed on merits. Ever since then, there
        is no change in the circumstances to take a different view.
        Still with a view to satisfy the judicial mind, once again I
        have gone through all the documents filed by the applicants
        and also the case diary. After considering the same, I am of
        the view that no case is made out for grant of bail to the
        applicants at this stage.    Hence, this repeat anticipatory
        bail application being devoid of merits is hereby dismissed.</p><pre id="pre_2">


                                               (SUBHASH KAKADE)
SJ/ -                                                JUDGE
