            Nityanand Pandey vs. State of M.P. &amp; ors.
Indore</a>
17.10.2019

         Ms. Raina Bairagee, learned counsel for the petitioner.
         Shri Arvind Sharma, learned GA for the respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">         The petitioner has filed the present petition being aggrieved by order dt.
28.9.2019 whereby he has been transferred from District Ratlam to District Rewa on
administrative grounds.
</p><p id="p_2">         The petitioner is assailing the transfer order on the ground that vide order dt.
12.9.2017 he was transferred to Ratlam in the Office of Superintendent Land Record.
Thereafter vide order dt. 28.6.2019 he was posted as Tehsildar Tehsil Jaora. Now he
has been transferred to Rewa which is more than 850 kms away. According to the
petitioner, his wife is pursuing studies of Ph.D. and same is about to complete. The
petitioner has already submitted a representation on 3.10.2019 to the Secretary
Revenue Department. Under Clause-17 of the transfer policy the Head of the
Department is competent to consider the representation.
</p><p id="p_3">         Clause-17 is reproduced below:-

         Since the petitioner is assailing the impugned order on the ground of personal
inconvenience and alleging violation of transfer policy, therefore, this Court cannot
interfere. It is settled law that High Court can only interfere on the ground of
malafide or violation of any rules and regulations. In absence of these grounds there
is no exception circumstances in this case for High Court to interfere.
</p><p id="p_5">         Let the representation be considered and decided as early as possible.
