<pre id="pre_1">                                       1


Sl. 19
02.03.2015.
S.d.
                                  W. P 19197(W) of 2014

                              Basuli Goswami
                                    -versus-
         The Chairman, Damodar Valley Corporation and others.



                   Mr. Hiranmoy Bhattacharyya
                   Mr. Sounak Bhattacharyya
                                        ..for the petitioner.

                   Mr. Pradip Tarafdar
                   Mr. Subir Pal
                                           ...for the DVC.

                   Ms. Sipra Majumdar
                   Ms. Srikekha Bhattacharyya
                                       .....for the State.



                   The grievance of the petitioner is that though her

           candidature for employment in the Damodar Valley Corporation

           was sponsored by her father who lost the requisite area of land in

           course of the Mejia Thermal Power Station being set up, her

           candidature has not been considered on the ground that all 520

           posts reserved in Damodar Valley Corporation for such purpose

           have been filled up.

                The petitioner refers to the responses received following

           queries raised under the Right to <a href="/doc/1965344/" id="a_1">Information Act</a>, 2005.         The

           petitioner says that the responses to the queries reveal that not all

           520 posts have been filled up.
                              2




        Though the respondents say that all 520 posts earmarked</pre>

<p id="p_1">for members of the land-losers' families have been filled up, since

the responses to the RTI queries show otherwise, the respondents

should consider the petitioner's candidature.

</p><p id="p_2">     WP 19197(W) of 2014 is disposed of by directing the

respondent no. 5 to consider the petitioner's candidature in

accordance with law and the eligibility criteria applicable and

communicate a reasoned decision to the petitioner within a period

of eight weeks from date. It will not be obligatory on the part of the

said respondent to give the petitioner a hearing, but it will be open

to such official to call the petitioner for a hearing, if necessary.

</p><p id="p_3">       There will be no order as to costs.
</p>
<p id="p_4">       Certified website copies of this order, if applied for, be

urgently made available to the parties, subject to compliance with

all requisite formalities.
</p>
<p id="p_5">


</p><p id="p_6">                                 (Sanjib Banerjee, J.)
