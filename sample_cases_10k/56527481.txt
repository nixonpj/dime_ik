<div class="doc_title">Ramveer vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 9 March, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                 1    M.Cr.C.No. 2815/2016 and M.Cr.C.2869/2016

(Ballu @ Balveer Vs. State of M.P.)
(Ramveer Vs. State of M.P.)
09.03.2016
         Shri Pratip Visoriya, counsel for the applicant Ballu
@ Balveer.
         Shri M.S. Rawat, counsel for the applicant Ramveer.
         Shri B.P.S. Chauhan, Public Prosecutor, for the
respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">         Heard the learned counsel for the parties.
         Since both the applications are connected with the
same crime, therefore, decided by the present common
order.
</p><p id="p_2">         The applicant Ballu @ Balveer is in custody since
11.01.2016 and applicant Ramveer is in custody since
19.01.2016 respectively relating to Crime No.27/2015
registered at Police Station Aswar, district Bhind, for the
offence punishable under <a href="/doc/1258372/" id="a_1">Sections 147</a>, <a href="/doc/763672/" id="a_2">148</a>, <a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_3">149</a>, <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_4">302</a>,
<a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_5">307</a>, <a href="/doc/958439/" id="a_6">396</a>, <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_7">120-B</a> of IPC read with Section 11/13 of
MPDVPK Act, 25, 27 and 30 of <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_8">Arms Act</a> and 39/192,
146/196 <a href="/doc/785258/" id="a_9">M.V. Act</a>.
</p><p id="p_3">         Learned counsel for the applicants submit that
applicants are reputed citizen of the locality, who have no
criminal past alleged against them.          Few cases were
registered against the applicants in the past but they were
not convicted in any of them. Though a named F.I.R. was
lodged against the actual culprits but name of the
applicants were not mentioned in the F.I.R. It was alleged
that they involved in the criminal conspiracy whereas
there is no direct evidence to that application.           The
evidence against the applicants depends upon the memo
under <a href="/doc/1312051/" id="a_10">Section 27</a> of the Evidence Act given by either the
applicants or by other co-accused persons.        Nothing has
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                          2      M.Cr.C.No. 2815/2016 and M.Cr.C.2869/2016</span>

       been recovered from the applicant Ballu @ Balveer
       whereas a mouser pistol has been recovered from the
       applicant Ramveer. It is not established that the applicant
       Ramveer was present at the spot or he used any gun. He
       may be guilty of offence under <a href="/doc/812083/" id="a_11">Section 411</a> of the IPC
       which is not so grave. Both the applicants are in custody
       without     any         substantial      reason.      Under          these
       circumstances, applicants pray for grant of bail.
</p><p id="p_4">             Learned Public Prosecutor opposes the application.
             Considering        the   submissions      made        by   learned
       counsel    for    the    parties,     looking   to    the    facts    and
       circumstances of the case including the gravity of offence,
       without expressing any view on the merits of the case, I
       am of the view that application under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_12">Section 439</a> of
       Cr.P.C.   filed   by     the   applicants       may    be    accepted.
       Consequently, it is hereby allowed.
</p><p id="p_5">             It is directed that the applicant namely Ballu @
       Balveer and           Ramveer be released on bail on their
       furnishing a personal bond in the sum of Rs.50,000/-
       (Rupees Fifty thousand) each with a surety bond each
       of the same amount to the satisfaction of the trial Court
       Lahar to appear before the trial Court on the dates given
       by the concerned Court.
</p><p id="p_6">             This order shall be effective till the end of trial but in
       case of bail jump, it shall become ineffective.
</p><p id="p_7">             Certified copy as per rules.</p><pre id="pre_2">
                                                             (N.K. Gupta)
mani                                                               Judge
 </pre>
