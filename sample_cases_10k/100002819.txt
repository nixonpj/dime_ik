<div class="doc_title">M/S Supreme Brahmaputra (Jv) vs The State Of Bihar &amp; Ors on 22 December, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                   IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                              Civil Writ Jurisdiction Case No.10232 of 2015
                 ======================================================
                 M/s Supreme Brahmaputra (JV), Supreme House, Plot No. 94C,
                 Pratapgarh, Opp-IIT Main GAte, Powai, Mumbai through its authorized
                 Signatory Mr. Pankaj Sharma, Resident Director of the petitioner Company.

                                                                      .... ....   Petitioner/s
                                                    Versus
                 1. The State of Bihar through the Principal Secretary, Water Resources
                 Department, Government of Bihar, Patna.
                 2. The Engineer-in-Chief, Water Resources Department (Central), Patna.
                 3. The Chef Engineer, Water Resources Department, Siwan.
                 4. The Executive Engineer, Irrigation Department, Siwan.

                                                                 .... .... Respondent/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s : Mr. Y.V. Giri, Sr. Adv.
                                        Mr. Ashish Giri, Adv.
                                        Mr. Vikas Ratan Bharti, Adv
                 For the Respondent/s : Mr. Raj Nandan Prasad, SC 9
                                        Mr. Rakesh Prabhat, AC to SC 9
                                        Mr. Rakesh Kumar, AC to SC 9.
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE VIKASH JAIN
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   22-12-2015                 1. Interlocutory Application No. 9871 of 2015 has

                 been filed for amendment in the relief portion of the writ petition

                 with following terms :
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">                                1(v) To issue an appropriate writ/order/direction in
                              the nature of Certorari for quashing the decision taken
                              in the meeting dated 05.12.2015 by the Principal
                              Secretary, Water Recourses Department, Bihar, Patna
                              by which the petitioner has been held guilty for delay
                              in completing the project and the Chief Engineer,
                              Water Resources Department, Siwan has been directed
                              to take necessary step for terminating the contract and
                              has also recommended for blacklisting the registration
                              of the petitioner as forwarded by letter dated
                              14.12.2015 bearing no. 1620.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">                                (vii) To prevent the authorities from taking any
                              coercive action against the petitioner such as
                              blacklisting, termination of the contract, forfeiture of
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Patna High Court CWJC No.10232 of 2015 (2) dt.22-12-2015</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                          2/3</span>




                           various Bank guarantees etc. unless an adjudication in
                           relation to the default in delay is made by an
                           independent Arbitrator.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">                             (viii) To treat the pleadings and the submissions
                           along with the Annexures in the present interlocutory
                           application as part of the writ petition.</blockquote><p id="p_2">

</p><p id="p_3">                        2. Having regard to the nature of the prayer, the

             petitioner is permitted to make necessary amendment in para 1 of

             the writ petition. Interlocutory Application No. 9871 of 2015

             stands allowed.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                        3. Interlocutory Application No. 9936 of 2015 has been

             filed for interim relief, directing the respondents authorities not to

             encash the bank guarantee of the petitioner.

</p><p id="p_5">                        4. Learned Senior counsel Mr. Y.V. Giri submits that the

             impugned order dated 08.12.2014 vide memo no. 6196 as well as

             minutes of the meeting dated 05.12.2015 whereby the Chief

             Engineer has been directed by the Principal Secretary, Water

             Resources Department to initiate steps for termination of the

             agreement and for black-listing of the petitioner, are unsustainable

             in view of the judgment of a co-ordinate Bench of this Hon'ble

             Court passed in <a href="/doc/152515230/" id="a_1">M/s NCC Ltd &amp; Anr. vs. The State of Bihar

             and Ors</a>. [2013 (1) PLJR 952].
</p>
<p id="p_6">                        5. It is submitted that the issue relating to the correctness

             of the said judgment is subject matter of LPA No. 2190 of 2015
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">             Patna High Court CWJC No.10232 of 2015 (2) dt.22-12-2015</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                                                      3/3</span>




                         pending before the Division of this Court.

</p><p id="p_7">                                    6. Let this matter be listed after disposal of LPA No.

                         2190 of 2015.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                                    7. In view of the statement made by learned counsel for

                         the respondents that they do not propose to invoke bank guarantee

                         of the petitioner at this stage, the petitioner is directed to extend

                         the period of bank guarantee for further period of six months.

</p><p id="p_9">                                    8. It is expected that the petitioner shall continue

                         discharging his duty under the terms of the agreement without any

                         delay.
</p>
<p id="p_10">
</p><p id="p_11">                                                                       (Vikash Jain, J)
Prakash/-
</p><p id="p_12">  U
