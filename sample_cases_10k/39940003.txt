<div class="doc_title">Md. Abdul Majid vs Kolkata Municipal Corporation &amp; ... on 7 November, 2013</div><div class="doc_author">Author: Soumen Sen</div>

<pre id="pre_1">74.   November
       7, 2013              High Court at Calcutta
                         Constitutional Writ Jurisdiction
                              Civil Appellate Side

                                   W.P. 26555 (W) of 2013

                                       Md. Abdul Majid

                                             Versus

                          Kolkata Municipal Corporation &amp; ors.

                          No one appears in support of the writ petition. Mr.
                 Biswajit Mukherjee, learned advocate, appears along with his
                 junior, Mr. Sanat Chowdhury, learned advocate, on behalf of the
                 respondents no. 1 to 5, when the matter is called.</pre>
<p id="p_1">                          Mr. Mukherjee submits that he has received instruction
                 from the Assistant Engineer (Civil), Borough-VII, Kolkata
                 Municipal Corporation, that as per the order of Justice Soumitra
                 Pal, the special officer (building) passed an order on October 12,
                 2012 directing demolition of the entire structure of the premises
                 and, accordingly, the demolition proceeding was fixed on
                 September 3, 2013, when the corporation men with the assistance
                 of local police station went to the premises to carry out the
                 demolition work, but as the building was fully occupied by several
                 occupiers, the demolition work could not be performed except two
                 (2) numbers of columns and with regard to the rest portion of the
                 building, steps are being taken. The written instruction dated
                 September 4, 2013 is taken on record.
</p><p id="p_2">                          I, however, dispose of this writ petition by observing that
                 the entire demolition process pursuant to the order dated October
                 12, 2013, as aforesaid, shall be completed, notwithstanding any
                 resistance that may be put, with the help of police authorities, if
       required, within a period of twelve (12) weeks from date, unless
      there is any previous order from any court restraining the
      demolition work.
</p><p id="p_3">               I record the submission of Mr. Mukherjee, learned
      advocate appearing on behalf of the corporation that the
dns   corporation has not been served with any court's order restraining
      them from carrying out the demolition.
</p><p id="p_4">               Since the corporation is representing through its lawyer,
      there is no necessity to communicate this order to the corporation.
</p><p id="p_5">               There will be no order as to costs.
</p>


<p id="p_6">                                                          ( Soumen Sen, J. )
