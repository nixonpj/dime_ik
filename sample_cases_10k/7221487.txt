<p id="p_1"> CORAM:         HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE APARESH KUMAR SINGH
                HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE B. B. MANGALMURTI

 For the Petitioner       : M/s Ritu Kumar, S.B.Deo
 For the Opposite Parties : Mr. Vikash Kumar, J.C to A.G.
</p>
<p id="p_2">03/16.07.2018         Heard learned counsel for the parties. Petitioner has prayed for
       restoration of W.P.(PIL) No. 1463 of 2015, which was dismissed for non-
       compliance of the peremptory order dated 24.07.2017.
</p><p id="p_3">                Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that the order passed on
       24.07.2017 could not be properly heard, as a result of which, defects could not
       be removed within the peremptory time. Petitioner may suffer irreparably, if
       the P.I.L is not restored.
</p><p id="p_4">                We, however, find from the connected record that the P.I.L was filed on
       09.04.2015 and defects were not removed even at the Lawazima stage.
       Thereafter, matter was taken twice earlier before the Bench when sufficient
       time was granted, before the order to remove the defects was made peremptory
       on the third date. It therefore appears that the explanation furnished by the
       petitioner is not satisfactory. In that view of the matter, we are not inclined to
       allow the instant restoration application.
</p><p id="p_5">                Instant CMP is accordingly dismissed.
</p>

<p id="p_6">                                                        (Aparesh Kumar Singh, J.)
