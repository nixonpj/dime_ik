<p id="p_1">      The writ petition was filed challenging Ext.P4 notice issued by

the 3rd respondent Corporation imposing a penalty on the petitioner

alleging unauthorised usage of additional load of 5KW. It is submitted

that, during the pendency of the writ petition, the matter has been

settled between the petitioner and the 2nd and 3rd respondents through

mediation. The mediation settlement agreement records the terms of

settlement as follows:
</p>



<blockquote id="blockquote_1">        1. The petitioner and respondents 2 &amp; 3 have settled the

        entire disputes between the parties and also agreeing that

        no further relief is required from the respondent 1 &amp; 4 in

        view of this settlement.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_2">
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">        2. The original Writ petition is filed challenging Ext.P4

        notice dated 23.03.2010 issued by the 3rd respondent

        corporation, imposing penalty of Rs.1,08,134/- (Rupees

        One Lakh Eight Thousand One Hundred and Thirty Four

        only) alleging unauthorised additional load of 5KW against

        the petitioner (consumer No.14249.B). The respondent

        corporation made a proposal to settle the entire liability of

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">W.P.(C).No.25955/2010                   2</span>




         the petitioner by paying an amount of Rs.43,254/- (Rupees

         Forty Three Thousand Two Hundred and Fifty Four only),

         which the petitioner has already paid an amount of

         Rs.36,045/- (Rupees Thirty six thousand and Forty Five

         only) by Demand Draft No.542075 dated 31.08.2010 of

         SBT, A/c.    The balance due as on today is Rs.7,209/-

         (Rupees Seven Thousand Two Hundred and Nine only) .


</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_4">         3.    The petitioner agrees that the said amount of

         Rs.7,209/- (Rupees Seven Thousand Two Hundred and

         Nine only) in full will be paid on or before 30.11.2015 as

         full and final settlement of the entire claim against the

         petitioner company.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_5">
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_6">         4. In case any default is committed by the petitioner, the

         second and third respondents will be at liberty to recover

         the same together with interest in accordance with law

         and subject to the provision of <a href="/doc/132967048/" id="a_1">Electricity Act</a>.


</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_7">         5. In view of the above settlement, the petitioner has no

         claim against the respondents 1 &amp; 4 and hence no further

         relief is required by the petitioner.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_8">
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_9">         6.   The parties agree to request this Hon'ble Court to

         record the above Memorandum of Agreement/Settlement

         and dispose of the case above.</blockquote>



<p id="p_2">       Resultantly, the writ petition is closed, by recording the terms of

settlement arrived at between the petitioner and respondents 2 and 3.
</p>
