<div class="doc_title">Takdeer vs Dilbagh Singh on 25 October, 2013</div><pre id="pre_1">                               IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA
                                           AT CHANDIGARH

                                                  RSA No.3352 of 2012 (O&amp;M)
                                                  Date of decision:25.10.2013

            Takdeer                                                 ....Appellant
                                                  Versus

            Dilbagh Singh                                           ....Respondent</pre>

<p id="p_1">            CORAM:- HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE RAKESH KUMAR GARG

</p><p id="p_2">            1.         Whether reporters of local newspapers may be allowed to
                       see judgment?
</p><p id="p_3">            2.         To be referred to reporters or not?
</p><p id="p_4">            3.         Whether the judgment should be reported in the Digest?

            Present:- Mr. Sanjay Mittal, Advocate, for the appellant.

</p><p id="p_5">                               Mr. Sunil Chadha, Advocate, for caveator/ respondent.

            RAKESH KUMAR GARG, J (ORAL)

                               This   is   plaintiff's   second   appeal   challenging   the

            judgments and decrees of the Courts below whereby, his suit for

            declaration to the effect that consent decree suffered by him being

            illegal was liable to be set aside.

</p><p id="p_6">                               Before this Court, it cannot be disputed that no

            independent suit can be filed on the plea of fraud. Not only this,

            counsel for the appellant could not point out any evidence on record

            to establish that any fraud was committed upon him at the time of

            suffering the consent decree.

</p><p id="p_7">                               In view thereof, I find no merit in the present appeal.

                               No substantial question of law, as raised, arises in this

            appeal.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                               Dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_9">            October 25, 2013                                  (RAKESH KUMAR GARG)
            savita
Kadian Savita
