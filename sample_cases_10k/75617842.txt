<div class="doc_title">Smt. Surjeet Kaur vs Ramesh Solanki on 25 September, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">          IN THE COURT OF  Dr. VIJAY KUMAR DAHIYA
                      SPECIAL JUDGE :  CBI [PC ACT]: 
                       DWARKA COURTS :  NEW DELHI.



In the matter of :­

                                                 CR No.386/17

Smt. Surjeet Kaur                      
W/o Late Sh. Mohan Singh
R/o B­49, Avantika, Sector­1, 
Rohini, Delhi - 110 084.                             ...... Petitioner/
                                                            Revisionist
Versus

Ramesh Solanki
S/o Late Sh. Sadh Ram
R/o WZ­621, Village Palam,
New Delhi - 110 045.                                 ....... Respondent</pre>


<pre id="pre_2">Date of Institution                      :  24.08.2017
Date of conclusion of arguments          :  21.09.2017
Date of Order                           :  25.09.2017



J U D G E M E N T



</pre><p id="p_1">1.           Vide this order I shall dispose of the present revision
petition preferred against the impugned orders dated 16.08.2017

CR No.386/17                                                        1/10
 passed by Sh. Abhishek Kumar, Ld. MM, SW, Dwarka whereby
the   application   of   the   applicant   for   leading   further   defence
evidence and summoning of witnesses was dismissed. 
</p>

<p id="p_2">2.            Brief facts relevant for disposal of the present revision
petition are like this. The complainant/respondent had filed a case
against the revisionist/petitioner under Section 138 of Negotiable
Instruments   Act     on   the   basis   of   cheque   for   an   amount   of
Rs.4,00,000/­  issued  by the petitioner.   The petitioner had taken
a   friendly   loan   of   Rs.4,00,000/­   in   the   month   of   January,   2009
from the complainant and the alleged cheque was issued by the
petitioner   in   the   month   of   December,   2009.     The   cheque   in
question   was   returned   uncleared   with   cheque   returning   memo
dated   01.05.2010   with   the   reason   of   "Insufficient   Funds".     The
complainant filed a case under section 138 of N. I. Act against the
accused.     The   complainant   was   examined   and   cross   examined.
Statement of the petitioner was also recorded in which she stated
that she don't even know the complainant  and she had not taken
any loan from the complainant. The cheque in question alongwith
some   other   cheques   were   given   in   blank   to   the   sister   of   the
complainant   namely,   Smt.   Suresh,   who   was   engaged   in   the
business of chit­fund (committee) and the petitioner was one of
the member of the said chit­fund (committee).  On 17.10.2012 the
petitioner examined herself as DW­1 and moved an application for

CR No.386/17                                                                2/10
 summoning   of   DW­2   Smt.   Suresh,   who   is   the   sister   of   the
complainant   and   whose   name   was   mentioned   as   complainant
witness   in the list of witnesses filed by the complainant, which
was allowed and DW­2 Smt. Suresh   was summoned, examined
and   cross   examined.     The   said   witness   DW­2   had   deposed   in
favour of the complainant and against the petitioner despite being
summoned by petitioner.  On 26.07.2013 counsel for the petitioner
moved an application u/s 311 <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_1">Cr.P.C</a>. for recalling of DW­2 for re­
examination   and   the   said   application   was     allowed   vide   order
dated 26.07.2013 and DW­2 was summoned  and re­examined on
two dates.  Thereafter the petitioner moved an application u/s 65
of Indian Evidence Act for leading secondary evidence as well as
for   taking   the   opinion   of   any   expert   but   said   application   was
dismissed   vide   order   dated   11.12.2015.     The   revision   preferred
against the above said order was dismissed by the revisional court
through   order   dated   13.01.2017.     Thereafter     the   petitioner
approached DLSA for appointment of a counsel from DLSA and
the said counsel came to know about the irregularities committed
by the previous counsel for the petitioner.  Thereafter the counsel
for the petitioner moved an application u/s 311 <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_2">CrPC</a> for recalling
of   CW­1   and   DW­1   which   was   dismissed   by   ld.   Trial   court
therefore, vide order dated 07.07.2011.
</p>

<p id="p_3">4.            Feeling   aggrieved   by   the   said   order,   the   present

CR No.386/17                                                               3/10
 revision petition has been filed.
</p>

<p id="p_4">5.            It   has   been   contended   by   counsel   for   the   petitioner
that petitioner should not suffer on account of mistake committed
by   the   previous   counsel   in   as   much   as   DW­2   sister   of   the
complainant was allowed to be called as a witness, despite the fact
that other members of the committee were there, who should have
been   examined.   In   addition   to   it,   previous   counsel   for   the
petitioner   has   not   given   any   suggestion   to   the   witness   during
their cross examination and to remove these defects the present
application   has   been   moved   which   was   dismissed   by   the   trial
court.     Ld.     Trial   court   has   failed   to   appreciate   that   proposed
witness namely, Smt. Rekha was one of the member of the Chit
Fund   Committee   run   by   DW­2   Smt.   Suresh,   sister   of   the
complainant and the credibility of the testimony of DW­2 can be
demolished by the said witness Smt. Rekha.   Ld. Trial court has
considered the one aspect of the application u/s 311 <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_3">Cr.P.C</a>. and
dismissed the same on the ground of delay in flagrant violation of
the settled principle of law. 
</p>

<p id="p_5">6.            Per contra ld. Counsel for the respondent contended
that the petitioner is bent upon to delay the trial on one pretext or
the   other   in   as   much   as   the   petitioner   has   taken   more   than   5
years   to   conclude   DE   and   even   thereafter   she   has   moved   this

CR No.386/17                                                                  4/10
 application for recalling  witnesses.   This is the delaying tactics
adopted   by   the   petitioner   to   delay   the   payment   of   the   amount
involved   in   the   present   cheque   in   question.     This   application
devoid of merits, deserves to be dismissed.
</p>

<p id="p_6">7.            I   have   heard   ld.   counsel   for   the   parties   and   have
perused the material available on record.
</p>

<p id="p_7">8.            Law with regard to the recalling of the witness is well
settled, as has been held in  "<a href="/doc/69347951/" id="a_4">State of Haryana vs. Ram Mehar

&amp; Ors</a>." (2016) 8 SCC762 wherein all the case law titled Natasha

Singh , Rajender Prasad   were discussed in detail and sum and
substance of the ratio of law laid down in the above said judgment
is laid down in para no.35 which is as under :
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">          The Court referred to the earlier decisions and culled
          out certain principles which are to be kept in mind
          while exercising power under Section 311 <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_5">Cr.P.C</a>. We
          think it seemly to reproduce some of them : (Rajaram
          Prasad case (2013) 145 SCC 461).
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">              "   17.2.    The   exercise   of   the   widest   discretionary   power
          under   Section   311   <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_6">CrPC</a>   should   ensure   that   the   judgment
          should   not   be   rendered   on   inchoate,   in   conclusive   and
          speculative presentation of facts, as thereby the ends of justice
          would be defeated.</blockquote>
<p id="p_8">              17.3.    If evidence of any witness appears to the court to be
          essential to the just decision of the case, it is the power of the
          court to summon and examine or recall and re­examine any

CR No.386/17                                                                            5/10
          such person.
</p><p id="p_9">              17.4.       The exercise of power under Section 311 <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_7">CrPC</a>
         should be resorted to only with the object of finding out the
         truth or obtaining proper proof for such facts, which will lead
         to a just and correct decision of the case.
</p><p id="p_10">              17.5.    The exercise of the said power cannot be dubbed as
         filing in a lacuna in a prosecution case, unless and facts and
         circumstances of the case make it apparent that the exercise of
         power by the court would result in causing serious prejudice to
         the accused, resulting in miscarriage of justice.
</p><p id="p_11">              17.6.                  The   wide   discretionary   power   should   be
         exercised judiciously and not arbitrarily.
</p><p id="p_12">              17.7                The court must satisfy itself that it was in
         every respect essential to examine such a witness or to recall
         him for further examination in order to arrive at a just decision
         of the case.
</p><p id="p_13">                       *                   *               *
                17.10.        Exigency of the situation, fair play and good
         sense should be the safeguard, while exercising the discretion.
         The court should bear in mind that no party in a trial can be
         foreclosed from correcting errors and that if proper evidence
         was  not   adduced  or  a   relevant  material  was   not  brought  on
         record   due   to   any   inadvertence,   the   court   should   be
         magnanimous in permitting such mistakes to be rectified.
</p><p id="p_14">                17.11.            The   Court   should   be   conscious   of   the
         position that after all the trial is basically for the prisoners and
         the court should afford an opportunity to them in the fairest
         manner possible.  In that parity of reasoning, it would be safe
         to err in favour of the accused getting an opportunity rather
         than protecting the prosecution against possible prejudice at the
         cost   of   the   accused.     The   court   should   bear   in   mind   that
         improper or capricious exercise of such a discretionary power ,
         may lead to undesirable results.
</p><p id="p_15">                         *                 *               *
         17.14.        The power under Section 311 <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_8">CrPC</a> must therefore,
         be   invoked   by   the   court   only   in   order   to   meet   the   ends   of
         justice   for   strong   and   valid   reasons   and   the   same   must   be
         exercised  with  care,  caution  and  circumspection.     The  court
         should bear in mind that fair trial entails the interest of the
         accused, the victim and the society and, therefore, the grant of
         fair and proper opportunities to the persons concerned, must be

CR No.386/17                                                                                 6/10
           ensured being a constitutional goal, as well as a human right."
</p>

<p id="p_16">             It was further observed in para 35 xxxxxxxxxx : 
             "15.  ...  While advancement of justice remains the prime
             object of law, it cannot be understood that recall can be
             allowed   for   the   asking   or   reasons   related   to   mere
             convenience.     It   has   normally   to   be   presumed   that   the
             counsel conducting a case is competent particularly when a
             counsel is appointed by choice of a litigant.   Taken to its
             logical end, the principle that a retrial must follow on every
             changes   of a  counsel,  can  have  serious  consequences  on
             conduct   of   trials   and   the   criminal   justice   system.     The
             witnesses   cannot   be   expected   to   face   the   hardship   of
             appearing in court repeatedly, particularly in sensitive cases
             such as the present one.  It can result in undue hardship for
             the victims, especially so, of heinous crimes, if result in
             undue hardship for the victims, especially so, of heinous
             crimes, if they are required to repeatedly appear in court to
             face cross­examination."
</p>
<p id="p_17">             In   Best   Bakery   case,   it   was   observed   that   the
provision of Section 311 <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_9">Cr.P.C</a>. is in two parts.   The first part
gives the discretion to the court to examine the witness at any
stage and the second part which is the mandatory portion compels
the   court   to   examine   the   witness   in   evidence   appears   to   be
essential for the just  decision of the case.  Though the discretion
given to the court is very wide, therefore, the very width requires
the corresponding caution.   The object of the section is to enable
the court to arrive at the truth irrespective of the fact that neither
prosecution nor defendant had produced some evidence which is
necessary for just and proper disposal of the case.
</p>


<p id="p_18">CR No.386/17                                                                            7/10
</p><p id="p_19"> 9.            With the above said  principle of law with regard  to
recalling/summoning of the witness u/s 311 <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_10">CrPC</a>, I would like to
advert to the facts of the present case.  The petitioner has already
led DE and DW­2 who happens to be sister of the complainant
was summoned as a witness but she denied having handed over
the cheque in question by the petitioner to the respondent as a
security in the Chit Fund allegedly run by DW­2.  Otherwise DW­
2   has   testified   that   petitioner   has   taken   Rs.4   lacs   from   her
brother Ramesh Solanki and she has never run any Chit Fund
Committee.     In   order   to   controvert   the   testimony   of   DW­2
petitioner   want   to   summon   the   proposed   witness   Smt.   Rekha,
who allegedly, participating in the Chit Fund Committee run by
DW­2.  Apart from the oral averments made in the application, no
documentary   evidence   has   been   placed   on   record   even   to   infer
that DW­2 has ever run any Chit Fund Committee in which the
petitioner as well as Smt. Rekha was member.
</p>

<p id="p_20">10.           It may be noted here that in this case DE was led by
the   petitioner   w.e.f.   08.06.2012   and   thereafter   the   petitioner
moved   an   application   u/s   315   <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_11">CrPC</a>   which   was   allowed   and
further   the   petitioner   also   moved   another   application   u/s   311
<a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_12">Cr.P.C</a>.  to examine DW which was allowed.  Thereafter petitioner



CR No.386/17                                                              8/10
 moved another application u/s 311 <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_13">Cr.P.C</a>. for recalling of DW­2
for   further   examination   of   DW­2   which   was   allowed.   The
petitioner   further   moved   application   for   leading   secondary
evidence   as   well   as   getting   expert   opinion   which   was   allowed.
But   the   petitioner   did   not   examine   any   expert   witness   but
petitioner moved an application u/s 243 <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_14">Cr.P.C</a>. recalling of DW­2
for   production   of   documents   which   was   dismissed   by   ld.   Trial
court.   The   petitioner   is   moving   one   application   after   another
either to recall any witness or to further cross examine witness or
to lead expert evidence but petitioner failed to conclude her DE
since   2012.     The   name   of   the   alleged   defence   witness   who   is
sought to be provided as DW­3 has never been surfaced by the
petitioner at any stage of the proceedings.  Apart from that quick
and prompt trial of criminal offence is the mandate of law as laid
down  by   the  Supreme Court   of  India.    Therefore,  no  ground   is
made by the petitioner during the course of arguments, either to
recall CW­1 Ramesh Solanki or summoning of Smt. Rekha as DW­
</p><p id="p_21">3.


</p><p id="p_22">11.           From   the   above   discussions,   I   find   no   ostensible
reasons to  take a different view which has already been taken by
ld. MM.  I find no illegality in the  order  passed by the ld. MM.
The revision petition  is devoid of merits and is hereby dismissed.
</p><p id="p_23">               Copy of this order be sent to the ld. Trial court for the

CR No.386/17                                                              9/10
 purposes of record and this revision petition file be consigned to
record room.
</p>
