<div class="doc_title">Ashok Rai vs The State Of Bihar on 20 December, 2011</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                            Criminal Miscellaneous No.43710 of 2011
                      Ashok Rai son of late Dharamdeo Rai R/o village + P.O.
                      Mukhraon, P.S. Kuchhila, Dist. Kaimur at Bhabhua
                                                             ..........petitioner
                                               Versus
                      The State of Bihar    ......................opposite party
                                          ------------------
</pre><p id="p_1">2     20.12.2011              Heard learned counsel for the petitioner, State and the

                   informant.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                The petitioner has been described the shooter of the

                   informant's son. The case has been registered under section 307,

                   387 and some other sections<a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_1"> of the Indian Penal Code</a> and 27 of

                   the <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_2">Arms Act</a>. From annexure-2 it appears that the allegation of

                   <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_3">section 387</a> has not been found to be correct and so charge sheet

                   has not been submitted under the aforesaid section.

</p><p id="p_3">                                Prayer for bail has been opposed by the informant who

                   has submitted that the injury caused to the petitioner was severe.

</p><p id="p_4">                                It has been mentioned in paragraph 10 of the petition

                   that the petitioner is a main of clean antecedent.

</p><p id="p_5">                                Considering the facts and circumstances of the case,

                   the above named petitioner is directed to be enlarged on bail on

                   furnishing bail bond of Rs. 10,000/- (ten thousand) with two

                   sureties of like amount each to the satisfaction of Chief Judicial

                   Magistrate, Kaimur at Bhabhua in Kuchhila P.S. Case No. 11 of

                   2011 (G.R. No. 901/11).
</p>
<p id="p_6">


</p><p id="p_7">                                           (Shyam Kishore Sharma, J)

M.Rahman
 </p>
