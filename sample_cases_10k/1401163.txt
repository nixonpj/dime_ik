ORAL

<p id="p_1">Heard
	learned counsels for the parties. Ms. Mandavia, learned counsel for
	the petitioner Corporation vehemently contended that the Labour
	Court ought not to have awarded continuity of service, though she
	could not dispute that the findings with regard to breach of Section
	25-F was recorded. She also candidly submitted that staying the
	impugned award would leave liberty to the Corporation to assign work
	as and when the same is available.
</p>	 


	  

<p id="p_2">Shri
	Mukesh Rathod, learned counsel for the workman submitted that the
	stay order if granted would amount to denying the fruits of the
	litigation which the workman could reap only after waiting for so
	many years and, therefore, if this Court is inclined to admit the
	matter interim relief may not be granted.  However, even in case the
	interim relief is granted, there shall be compliance with <a href="/doc/678364/" id="a_1">section
	17(B)</a> of the I.D. Act.
</p>	 


	  

<p id="p_3">In
	view of this, the matter deserves to be admitted. Hence RULE. By way
	of interim relief the award impugned is stayed, subject to
	compliance with provision of <a href="/doc/678364/" id="a_2">Section 17(B)</a> of I.D. Act. It goes
	without saying that, in the interregnum period it would be
	absolutely open to the petitioner Corporation to utilize the
	services of the workman instead of paying him idle wages and
	reinstate him in service, which shall ofcourse be subject to result
	of this petition.
</p>
 

 


 

      
<p id="p_4">                                                            [
S.R. BRAHMBHATT, J ]
