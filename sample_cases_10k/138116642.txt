<div class="doc_title">Nirbhay Singh Through vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 24 January, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                       1                                           WP. No. 386/2017
                                                 (Nirbhay Singh Vs. State of M.P.)


      24/01/2017
             Shri Abhishek Parashar, learned counsel for the petitioner.
             Ms.       Nidhi   Patankar,   learned   Govt.      Advocate   for   the
      respondents/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">             With the consent of learned counsel for the parties, the petition is
      heard finally.
</p><p id="p_2">             Learned counsel for the petitioner at the outset submits that
      grievances of the petitioner are covered by the judgment of this Court
      passed in W.P. No. 1320/2007(s) (Veerpal Singh Chauhan Vs. State of
      M.P. and others) decided on 20.01.2009. Learned counsel submits that
      along with said judgment, he intends to prefer a representation before
      respondent No. 2 and 3 which may be directed to be decided within a
      stipulated time.
</p><p id="p_3">             On the other hand learned counsel for the State has no objection
      to this innocuous prayer. Accordingly, petition is disposed of without
      expressing any opinion on the merits of the case with the following
      directions:-
</p><p id="p_4">         1. Petitioner shall re-submit representation alongwith copy of
             judgment passed in W.P. No. 1320/2007(s) before respondents
             No. 2 and 3.
</p><p id="p_5">         2. In turn, the said respondents shall decide it within ninety days by
             a     reasoned    and    speaking   order.   The    outcome    of   the
             representation shall be communicated to the petitioner within the
             same time.
</p><p id="p_6">         3. If the said authority comes to the conclusion that any dues are
             payable to the petitioner, the same be paid to the petitioner within
             the same time.
</p><p id="p_7">             With the aforesaid, the petition stands disposed of.
             Certified copy as per rules.</p><pre id="pre_2">
                                                     (S.A. Dharmadhikari)
LJ*                                                          JUDGE
 </pre>
