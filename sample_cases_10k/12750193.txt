<div class="doc_title">Md. Mozahid &amp; Ors. vs The State Of Bihar on 14 March, 2011</div><pre id="pre_1">               IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                 Cr.Misc. No.3569 of 2011
                        1. MD. MOZAHID , S/o Md. Habib Mian.
                              2. Md. Qasim, S/o Md. Shahid.
                               3. Md. Zakir, S/o Md. Shahid.
                            4. Md. Tahir, S/o Md. Habib Mian.
     All resident of village - Bhelawa Tola, P.S. - Jainagar, District - Madhubani.
                                          Versus
                                  THE STATE OF BIHAR
                                         -----------
</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   14.03.2011                     The     petitioners      are   apprehending            their

                     arrest in a case registered under <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_1">Sections 341</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_2">323</a>,

                     <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_3">324</a>, <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_4">307</a>, <a href="/doc/1101188/" id="a_5">379</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_6">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code and 27 of

                     the <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_7">Arms Act</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                   The petitioner nos. 1 &amp; 2 alleged to have

                     assaulted on the head with iron rod, Gandasa and

                     sharp cutting weapon.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                   I am not inclined to grant anticipatory bail

                     to petitioner no. 1 and 2.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                   Let the learned court below consider the

                     regular bail application of these two petitioners without

                     being prejudiced by the order of this court.

</p><p id="p_5">                                   So    far   as   petitioner    nos.    3    &amp;      4    are

                     concerned, it is contended by learned counsel for the

                     petitioners that there is no specific                accusation of

                     assault. Moreover, the other injuries are simple in

                     nature which has not been controverted by learned

                     counsel for the informant.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                   Considering        the     aforesaid       facts       and
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                             2</span>




           circumstances, let the petitioner nos. 3 &amp; 4 namely, Md.

           Zakir and Md. Tahir be released on anticipatory bail, in

           the event of their arrest or surrender before the learned

           Court below within a period of twelve       weeks from

           today, on furnishing bail bond of Rs. 10,000/- (ten

           thousand) each with two sureties of the like amount

           each to the satisfaction of the learned Chief Judicial

           Magistrate, Madhubani in connection with Jainagar

           P.S. Case No. 219 of 2010.
</p>
<p id="p_7">


</p><p id="p_8">                                ( Dinesh Kumar Singh,J.)

Praful/-
</p>
