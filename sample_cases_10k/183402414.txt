<div class="doc_title">Kamlesh Kumar vs State Of U.P. on 3 December, 2019</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: Bachchoo Lal</div>

<pre id="pre_1">




HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT ALLAHABAD
 
 

?Court No. - 68
 

 
Case :- CRIMINAL MISC. BAIL APPLICATION No. - 53413 of 2019
 

 
Applicant :- Kamlesh Kumar
 
Opposite Party :- State of U.P.
 
Counsel for Applicant :- Narendra Kumar
 
Counsel for Opposite Party :- G.A.
 

 
Hon'ble Bachchoo Lal,J.
</pre> 
<p id="p_1">Learned counsel for the applicant is permitted to make necessary correction in the array of the parties in bail application. 
</p> 
<p id="p_2">Heard learned counsel for the applicant, learned AGA and perused the record.
</p> 
<p id="p_3">Learned counsel for the applicant submits that the applicant is husband of the deceased. He is in jail since 28.11.2017. There was no dispute of demand of dowry. The applicant has not harassed or tortured the deceased. In the postmortem report, the cause of death of the deceased has been shown asphyxia due to ante mortem hanging. The deceased has committed suicide herself. The applicant has not compelled the deceased to commit suicide. There is no cogent evidence with regard to abetment. During trial the statement of Ram Sajivan ( father of the deceased) has been recorded as p.w.-1. In his cross examination he has stated that there was no dispute of demand of dowry. The applicant has not harassed or tortured the deceased. The deceased has committed suicide herself. P.-W.-2 Raj Bahadur, in his statement he has stated that the deceased has committed suicide. This witness has not made any allegation against the applicant. P.W.-3 Rajendra Prasad is neighbour of the informant. This witness has also not supported the prosecution version and has been declared hostile. P.W.-4 Umesh Chandra is mediator of the marriage who did also not support the prosecution version and has been declared hostile. The applicant has no concern with the alleged incident and has falsely been implicated in the present case. There is no criminal history of the applicant.
</p> 
<p id="p_4">Per contra, learned AGA opposed the prayer for bail.
</p> 
<p id="p_5">Having given my thoughtful consideration to the submissions of the learned counsel for the parties, without expressing any opinion on the merits of the case, I am of the opinion that it is a fit case for bail.
</p> 
<p id="p_6">Let the applicant Kamlesh Kumar involved in Case Crime No. 609 of 2017, under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_1">Section 498-A</a>, <a href="/doc/653797/" id="a_2">304-B</a> IPC and 3/4 D.P.Act, Police Station Karhal District Mainpuri be released on bail on his furnishing a personal bond and two sureties each in the like amount to the satisfaction of the court concerned with the following conditions;
</p> 
<p id="p_7">1. The applicant will not tamper with the evidences.
</p> 
<p id="p_8">2. The applicant will not pressurize/intimidate the prosecution witnesses and he will cooperate with the trial.
</p> 
<p id="p_9">3. The applicant will appear on each and every date fixed by the trial court unless personal appearance is exempted by the court concerned.
</p> 
<p id="p_10">In case of breach of any conditions mentioned above, the trial court shall be at liberty to cancel the bail of the applicant.
</p> 
<p id="p_11">Order Date :- 3.12.2019
 
Gss
