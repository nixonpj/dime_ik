<div class="doc_title">Dhupendra Singh vs The Jharkhand Mineral Area ... on 18 September, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">           IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                               W.P.(S) No. 4212 of 2019
        Dhupendra Singh                                     ...           ....     Petitioner
                                               Vs.
        1. The Jharkhand Mineral Area Development Authority through its Managing
           Director, Dhanbad
        2. The Managing Director, Jharkhand Mineral Area Development Authority,
           Dhanbad
                                                     ...            .....   Respondents
                                      ------
</pre><pre id="pre_2">     CORAM:       HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE DR. S. N. PATHAK
                                      ------
     For Petitioner            : Mr. Niranjan Singh, Advocate
     For Respondents           : Dr. A.K. Singh, Advocate
                                      ------
03/ 18.09.2019    The petitioner, who was an employee of Jharkhand Mineral Area
</pre><p id="p_1">     Development Authority (hereinafter to be referred as MADA) has superannuated on
     31.10.2018. The petitioner has approached this Court claiming his post retiral
     benefits, as mentioned in prayer portion of the writ petition, which the petitioner is
     entitled to receive from MADA.
</p><p id="p_2">                  Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that suffice it would be if a
     direction is given to the Managing Director, MADA to decide the claim of the
     petitioner and to pass an appropriate order on the representation of the petitioner
     and further respondent No.2, the Managing Director, MADA be directed to pay the
     admitted dues of the petitioner as per the scheme.
</p><p id="p_3">                  On the other hand, learned counsel appearing on behalf of the MADA
     has no objection, if a direction is given to the Managing Director, MADA to dispose
     of the representation of the petitioner and to pay the admitted dues to the petitioner
     on account of his post retiral benefits as per the scheme.
</p><p id="p_4">                  Be that as it may, having gone through the submissions of the parties
     and without going into the merits of this case, I hereby direct the petitioner to file a
     detailed representation claiming his post retiral benefits under different heads
     before respondent No.2- Managing Director, MADA along with a copy of this order
     within a period of six weeks from the date of receipt of a copy of this order. On
     receipt of such representation, the Managing Director, MADA shall consider the
     claim of the petitioner in terms of the scheme formulated by the MADA for payment
     of post retiral benefits to the employees and will pass a reasoned order, within a
     further period of six weeks'. If any amount is found payable, the said amount would
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                   2</span>




         be paid to the petitioner, within a period of two weeks' thereafter as per the scheme.
</p><p id="p_5">                        If any portion of the claim of the petitioner is denied or disputed by the
         respondents, the reasons for such denial, should be communicated to the petitioner
         within the said period.
</p><p id="p_6">                        With the aforesaid observation and direction, writ petition stands
         disposed of.
</p>

<p id="p_7">                                                                      (Dr. S.N. Pathak, J.)
kunal/
 </p>
