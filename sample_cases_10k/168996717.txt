<div class="doc_title">Vinod Kumar Dubey vs State Of M.P on 17 December, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                                      1

Vinod Kumar Dubey Vs. State of M.P. &amp; Ors.
                                                    W.P. No. 6670/2012

17.12.2015
         Mr. Prakash Braru, learned counsel for the petitioner.
         Shri S.K. Jain, Government Advocate for respondents-</pre>
<p id="p_1">State.
</p><p id="p_2">         With the consent of parties, this petition is heard finally.
         In this petition under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of Constitution of India,
the petitioner who is daily wager employee has assailed the
validity of order dated 18/03/2011, by which, claim of the
petitioner for regularization has been rejected.
</p><p id="p_3">         When the matter was taken up today, learned counsel for
the petitioner submits that subsequently the State Government
has issued another circular dated 29/09/2014 with regard to
regularization of the services of daily wager employee and the
petitioner may be granted liberty to submit representation to the
respondent No. 2- Director, Horticulture, Bhopal and the
aforesaid authority be directed to decide the representation
submitted by the petitioner in accordance with law within fixed
time limit.
</p><p id="p_4">         On the other hand, learned Government Advocate for the
State submits that in case such representation is submitted, the
same shall be dealt with in accordance with law.
</p><p id="p_5">         In view of aforesaid submission made by learned counsel
for the parties and as agreed to by them, this writ petition is
disposed      of   with   direction   to   the   petitioner   to   submit
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                              2</span>

            Vinod Kumar Dubey Vs. State of M.P. &amp; Ors.
</p><p id="p_6">                                                             W.P. No. 6670/2012

            representation to the respondent No. 2 within a period of two
            weeks along with certified copy of this order passed today and
            the   aforesaid   authority   shall   consider    and   decide   the
            representation submitted by petitioner within a period of two
            months by speaking order in the light of circular dated
            29/09/2014 issued by the State Government from the date of
            receipt of certified copy of this order passed today.
</p><p id="p_7">                  It is made clear that this court has not expressed any
            opinion with regard to entitlement of the petitioner.
</p><p id="p_8">                  With the aforesaid direction, this writ petition stands
            disposed of.
</p><p id="p_9">                  C.C. as per rules.
</p><p id="p_10">                                                             (Alok Aradhe)
                                                                Judge
Durgekar*
