                                            ORAL ORDER

33   29-01-2018                   Learned counsel for the petitioners; State and the

                    Universities have assisted the Court.
</p>
<p id="p_39">                                  2. In view of the queries which have cropped up
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_16"> Patna High Court CWJC No.5192 of 2015 (33) dt.29-01-2018</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_17">                                         9/10</span>




              during arguments relating to the formula which is required to be

              adopted in terms of the circulars of the State Government itself,

              the person who was present in Court being the Senior Auditor

              in the Education Department, though on deputation from the

              Finance Department, was unable to assist the Court or to

              explain the formula, either to the learned counsel for the State

              or to the Court directly.
</p>
<p id="p_40">                            3. Faced with the situation, learned counsel for the

              State submitted that the person is unable to explain the issues

              and he himself feels that such issues need to be clarified by a

              person who is more qualified and in a better position to do so.
</p>
<p id="p_41">              He has assured the Court that he would talk directly to the

              Principal Secretaries of both the Education Department and the

              Finance Department and would take the assistance of a senior

              experienced person of the Finance Department who is

              unconnected with the present case, who shall be given an

              opportunity to go through the issues raised in the writ petitions

              and explain the correct import and requirement relating to the

              methodology of computation of pension/family pension of

              persons who have superannuated prior to 01.01.2006 and whose

              pensionary benefits have to be revised with effect from

              01.01.2006. The Court would make it clear that the person or
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_18">            Patna High Court CWJC No.5192 of 2015 (33) dt.29-01-2018</span>

                                                    10/ 10




                         persons so deputed should not feel encumbered by the fact that

                         they are employees of the Department and have to toe the

                         departmental line, for they are required to assist the Court as

                         officers of the Court giving an independent and fair assistance,

                         both to the learned counsel and the Court for correctly

                         interpreting the circulars of the State Government and the

                         formula which would be required to be adopted in terms of such

                         circulars, keeping in mind the policy of the Central Government

                         and the various decisions of the Hon'ble Courts including the

                         Hon'ble Supreme Court of India.
</p>
<p id="p_42">                                       4. Accordingly, as prayed for by learned counsel for

                         the State, the matters be listed on 9th February, 2018 at 11.00

                         A.M.
</p>

<p id="p_43">                                                                      (Ahsanuddin Amanullah, J.)
