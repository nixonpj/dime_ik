<div class="doc_title">Tota Singh vs State Of Punjab on 11 May, 2012</div><pre id="pre_1">Crl. Misc. No. M-13684 of 2012 (O&amp;M)                            -: 1 :-


IN THE HIGH COURT FOR THE STATES OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA
                    AT CHANDIGARH


                                Crl. Misc. No. M-13684 of 2012 (O&amp;M)
                                      Date of decision: May 11, 2012.

Tota Singh
                                                          ... Petitioner(s)

             v.

State of Punjab
                                                          ... Respondent(s)</pre>

<p id="p_1">CORAM: HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE PARAMJEET SINGH

Present:     Shri A.S. Cheema, Advocate, for the petitioner.


Paramjeet Singh, J. (Oral):
</p>
<p id="p_2">             The prayer in this petition filed under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C. is for

the issuance of a direction to the respondents for the premature release of

the petitioner on humanitarian grounds in view of the precarious health

condition of the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_3">             Learned Counsel for the petitioner submits that the petitioner,

along with his co-accused, was tried and convicted in FIR No.09 dated

11.1.1991, registered at Police Station Dialpura, District Bathinda, and

sentenced to undergo RI for a period of two years and to pay a fine of

Rs.500/- for committing substantive offence under <a href="/doc/1540253/" id="a_2">Section 326</a> IPC by the

Court of Judicial Magistrate 1st Class, Phul, District Bathinda. However, on

an appeal, the Court of learned Additional Sessions Judge, Bathinda reduced

the sentence of the petitioner and his co-accused to one and a half years.

Learned counsel further submits that out of the total sentence of one and a

half years, the petitioner has already undergone actual sentence of eight
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Crl. Misc. No. M-13684 of 2012 (O&amp;M)                           -: 2 :-</span>


months.
</p>
<p id="p_4">            Notice of motion.
</p>
<p id="p_5">            On the asking of the Court, Shri J.S, Bhullar, Assistant

Advocate General, Punjab accepts notice.
</p>
<p id="p_6">            Having heard learned counsel for the parties, this petition is

disposed of with a direction to the respondents to sympathetically consider

and decide the case of the petitioner for premature release, after duly

verifying the factum of his serious illness, within a period of fifteen days

from the date of receipt of a certified copy of this order, if no decision has

been taken so far.
</p>
<p id="p_7">
</p><p id="p_8">                                                 [ Paramjeet Singh ]
May 11, 2012.                                          Judge
kadyan
