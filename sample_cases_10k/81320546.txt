<div class="doc_title">Crl.O.P.No.17709 Of 2017 vs The Assistant Commissioner on 29 August, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">        

 
IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT MADRAS

DATED: 29.08.2017

CORAM:

THE HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE M.S.RAMESH

Crl.O.P.No.17709 of 2017
Gopinath							..Petitioner

         Vs.


1. The Assistant Commissioner,
     Vepery Range,
     Vepery, Chennai.

2. The Inspector of Police,
    Periamet Police Station,
    Chennai.						..Respondents

     									
PRAYER: Criminal Original Petition filed under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C. praying to direct the FIR on the complaint dated 22.06.2017.

		For Petitioner  	: 	Mr. M. Soundar Vijay Arulram

	    	For Respondents	: 	Mr.P.Govindarajan
						Additional Public Prosecutor.

*****




 

ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">	This petition is filed seeking a direction to the 2nd respondent to register a case based on the complaint lodged by the petitioner dated 22.06.2017 and investigate the same according to law.
</p>
<p id="p_2">	2.By consent of both sides, this Criminal Original Petition is taken up for final disposal.
</p>
<p id="p_3">	3.The grievance of the petitioner is that inspite of a complaint given by him on 22.06.2017 to the 1st respondent , the same has been kept in abeyance without any action. It is well settled in the judgment of the Hon'ble Supreme Court in Lalita Kumari Vs. Government of Uttar Pradesh and others [2013 (6) CTC 353], that registration of an FIR  is mandatory under <a href="/doc/1980578/" id="a_2">Section 154</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure if an information furnished to the police officer disclose commission of a cognizable offence and in cases where the information does not disclose a cognizable offence, a preliminary enquiry has to be conducted.
</p>

<p id="p_4">	4. The 2nd  respondent is not justified in having received the complaint and keeping it unattended without any further action.  In view of the same, the petitioner has made out a case for this Court to invoke its inherent powers under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_3">Section 482</a> of the Criminal Procedure Code.  Hence, the 2nd respondent is directed as follows:
</p><p id="p_5">	1)If the information received by the 2nd  respondent  discloses  commission of a cognizable offence, then, the same shall be forthwith registered.
</p><p id="p_6">	2)If an information received does not disclose a cognizable offence, the 2nd  respondent shall conduct a preliminary inquiry to ascertain whether cognizable offence is disclosed therein or not and such inquiry shall be completed within a period of seven days from the date of information.
</p><p id="p_7">	3) If the inquiry discloses the commission of a cognizable offence, the FIR must be registered, if not already registered or closed.
</p><p id="p_8">	4) If the preliminary inquiry ends in closing the complaint, the disclosure report must be recorded along with the reasonings and a copy of the same shall be furnished to the complainant within one week.
M.S.RAMESH.J,

bri
</p><p id="p_9">		5)All information relating to cognizable offences 	whether resulting in registration of FIR or leading an 	inquiry must be reflected in the  general diary/station 	diary/daily diary of the 2nd respondent's police station.
</p>
<p id="p_10">	5.In the result, the Criminal Original Petition is allowed with the above directions.
</p>
<p id="p_11">									   29.08.2017
Index:Yes/No
bri

To

</p><p id="p_12">1. The Assistant Commissioner,
     Vepery Range,
     Vepery, Chennai.
</p>

    Chennai.		
</p>
