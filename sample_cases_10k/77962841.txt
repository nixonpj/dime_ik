<div class="doc_title">Abdul Kayam Sarkar &amp; Anr vs The State Of West Bengal &amp; Ors on 22 June, 2010</div><div class="doc_author">Author: Jayanta Kumar Biswas</div>

<pre id="pre_1">                                         1




                          In the High Court at Calcutta
                         Constitutional Writ Jurisdiction
                                  Appellate Side
Present:
The Hon'ble Mr. Justice Jayanta Kumar Biswas.

                             W.P. No. 7054 (W) of 2010
                           Abdul Kayam Sarkar &amp; Anr.
                                        v.
                          The State of West Bengal &amp; Ors.

Mr   Subir K. Bhattacharyya, advocate, for the petitioners. Mr              Gobinda
Bhattacharjee, advocate,for the state.

Heard on: June 22, 2010.

Judgment on: June 22, 2010.

         The Court: - Counsel submits that though the police were under an
obligation to take cognizance under s.156(3) of the Code of Criminal Procedure,
1973, the police did not take cognizance inspite of receipt of complaint from the
petitioners that the private respondents, who have no right, title or interest in the</pre>
<p id="p_1">properties in question, committed various offences.
</p>
<p id="p_2">         I am unable to appreciate the argument. The provisions of s.156 do not
empower the police to take cognizance. An information about commission of
some cognizable offence cannot be treated by the police as an application under
s.156(3) of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973. The petitioners have
approached the wrong forum. They were free to approach the magistrate with
their complaint and they could invite the magistrate to make an order under
s.156(3), or to take cognizance and issue process. They are not entitled to any
relief from the writ court.
</p>
<p id="p_3">         For these reasons, the <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">art.226</a> petition is dismissed. No costs. Certified
xerox.
</p>

<p id="p_4">                                                        (Jayanta Kumar Biswas, J.)
