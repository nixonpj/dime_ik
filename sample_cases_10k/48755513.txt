<div class="doc_title">Mukesh Jain vs The State Of Jharkhand on 11 April, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                     A.B.A. No.329 of 2019
                              ------
</pre><pre id="pre_2">    Mukesh Jain                      .... .... .... Petitioner
                              Versus
    1. The State of Jharkhand
    2. Arun Kumar Pandey             .... .... .... Opposite Parties
                              ------
CORAM       : HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE ANIL KUMAR CHOUDHARY
                                        ------
     For the Petitioner          : Mr. J. N. Upadhyay, Advocate
     For the State               : Addl.P.P.
     For the O.P. No.2           : None
                                        ------
     Order No.04 Dated- 11.04.2019
</pre><p id="p_1">            Apprehending his arrest in connection with Adityapur (R.I.T.) P.S. Case
     No.10 of 2008 corresponding to G.R. No.36 of 2008 instituted under <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_1">Sections 406</a>,
     <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_2">420</a> of the Indian Penal Code, the petitioner has moved this Court for grant of
     privileges of anticipatory bail.
</p><p id="p_2">            Though notice was issued to the opposite party No.2 in the address of the
     opposite party No.2 mentioned in the F.I.R. but the same has returned with the
     endorsement that he is not known in the locality.
</p><p id="p_3">            Heard learned counsel appearing for the petitioner and learned Addl.P.P.
     appearing for the State.
</p><p id="p_4">            Learned counsel appearing for the petitioner submits that the petitioner is
     not named in the F.I.R. It is submitted that the allegations against the petitioner
     are all false and general and omnibus in nature. It is next submitted that though
     non-bailable warrant of arrest has been issued against the petitioner yet C.J.M.,
     Seraikella has not mentioned any reason as to how the petitioner was involved
     in the case. It is lastly submitted that the petitioner is ready and willing to co-
     operate with the investigation of the case. Hence, it is submitted that the
     petitioner be given the privileges of anticipatory bail.
</p><p id="p_5">            Learned Addl.P.P appearing for the State opposes the prayer for
     anticipatory bail of the petitioner.
</p><p id="p_6">            Considering the submissions of learned counsels and the facts and
     circumstances stated above, I am inclined to grant privileges of anticipatory bail
     to the petitioner. Accordingly, the petitioner is directed to surrender in the Court
     of learned C.J.M. at Seraikella within four weeks from today and in the event of
     his arrest or surrendering, he will be enlarged on bail on furnishing bail bond of
     Rs.25,000/- (Twenty five thousand) with two sureties of the like amount each to
        the satisfaction of learned C.J.M. at Seraikella in connection with Adityapur
       (R.I.T.) P.S. Case No.10 of 2008 corresponding to G.R. No.36 of 2008 with the
       condition that he will co-operate with the investigation of the case and appear
       before the investigating officer as and when noticed by him and furnish his
       mobile number and photocopy of the Aadhar Card with an undertaking that
       he will not change his mobile number during the pendency of the case and
       subject to the conditions as laid down under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_3">Section 438(2)</a> of the Code of
       Criminal Procedure.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                                            (Anil Kumar Choudhary, J.)
Animesh/
