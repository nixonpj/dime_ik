<div class="doc_title">Hajibhai Musabhai Bhadula vs Bhavnagar Municipal Corporation ... on 12 February, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                  C/SCA/1387/2003                                                ORDER




                  IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

                     SPECIAL CIVIL APPLICATION NO. 1387 of 2003

         ==========================================================
                    HAJIBHAI MUSABHAI BHADULA....Petitioner(s)
                                   Versus
              BHAVNAGAR MUNICIPAL CORPORATION &amp; 1....Respondent(s)
         ==========================================================
         Appearance:
         NOTICE SERVED for the Petitioner(s) No. 1
         MR MANAN MEHTA, AGP for the Respondent(s) No. 2
         MR HS MUNSHAW, ADVOCATE for the Respondent(s) No. 1
         ==========================================================

                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE R.M.CHHAYA

                                       Date : 12/02/2016


                                        ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">         1.   Though notice is served upon the petitioner, no one
         appears for the petitioner.
</p>


<p id="p_2">         2.   Heard       Mr.       H. S. Munshaw,             learned advocate                     for
         respondent no.1 and Mr. Manan Mehta, learned Assistant
         Government Pleader for respondent no.2.
</p>


<p id="p_3">         3.   By way of this petition under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the
         Constitution of India, the petitioner has prayed for the
         following reliefs:
</p>


<p id="p_4">              "20(A)      Your Lordships may be pleased to admit and
              allow this petition;</p><pre id="pre_2">


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                            Page 1 of 4</span>

HC-NIC                                   Page 1 of 4      Created On Sat Feb 13 03:21:24 IST 2016
                  C/SCA/1387/2003                                               ORDER




              20(B)      Your Lordships may be pleased to issue a writ
</pre><p id="p_5">              of mandamus or a writ in the nature of mandamus or any
              other appropriate writ, order or direction by restraining
              the present respondents from disturbing the business
              carried out by the present petitioner o selling Mutton at
              Vertejia Fali, Vadva area of Bhavnagar City.
</p>

<p id="p_6">              20(C)      Pending admission, hearing and final disposal
              of this petition, Your Lordships may be pleased to
              restrain the present respondents from disturbing the
              business carried on by the present petitioner by selling
              Mutton at Vertejia Fali, Vadva area of Bhavnagar City.
</p>

<p id="p_7">              20(D)      To pass such other and                           further        orders
              necessary in the interest of justice."
</p>

<p id="p_8">         4.   This Court, by an order of admission, has granted
         interim relief in favour of the petitioner and the said relief has
         continued.
</p>


<p id="p_9">         5.   This Court is, therefore, left with no option but to hear
         the advocates for the respondents on merits and pass
         appropriate order.
</p>


<p id="p_10">         6.   It deserves to be noted that earlier similarly situated
         person had approached this Court by way of writ petition
         being Special Civil Application No.7596 of 1998 wherein this
         Court was pleased to pass necessary directions as indicated in
         para-5 and 6 of the order dated 16/10/2000 passed in Special
         Civil Application No.7596 of 1998 which are as under:
</p>

<p id="p_11">                      "5.    In view    of such          submissions             by issuing


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                          Page 2 of 4</span>

HC-NIC                                 Page 2 of 4      Created On Sat Feb 13 03:21:24 IST 2016
                    C/SCA/1387/2003                                            ORDER



                necessary directions, the petition can be disposed of.
</p>
<p id="p_12">                      6.   The Standing Committee of the respondent
                No.1 Bhavnagar Municipal Corporation is hereby
                directed to treat the present petition as a
                representation, and hearing the petitioners as well as
                considering the law on the point as well as the
                decisions rendered by the Supreme Court, it shall pass
                appropriate order as early as possible but not later
                than 31st December, 2000. Till then the respondent -
                Bhavnagar Municipal Corporation shall not disturb the
                petitioners in carrying on their business    and   the
                petitioners would also see that least disturbance or
                harassment, if any is caused to the public. Whatever
                decision is taken by the Standing Committee of the
                Corporation - respondent No.1, the same shall be
                implemented 15 days after the date thereof. With such
                directions, the   petition is    disposed   of. Notice
                discharged."
</p>

<p id="p_13">         7.     Mr. H. S. Munshaw, learned advocate for respondent
         no.1-Corporation has stated that as the interim relief is
         granted, the petitioner is not disturbed by the respondent-
         Corporation.         However, Mr. Munshaw, learned advocate
         submitted that the issue raised in the petition is of the year
         2002-03 and thereafter the city of Bhavnagar has undergone
         development to a very great extent.                 Mr.Munshaw, learned
         advocate further submitted that even during the pendency of
         this petition, revised final development plan has also come
         into   force.        Mr.Munshaw,       learned         advocate,           however,
         submitted that till date the respondent-Corporation has not
         taken any action against the petitioner and present petition is
         filed merely on an apprehension as no notice was issued by
         the Respondent-Corporation.
</p>


<p id="p_14">         8.     In light of the aforesaid and considering the fact that the


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                                         Page 3 of 4</span>

HC-NIC                                Page 3 of 4      Created On Sat Feb 13 03:21:24 IST 2016
                   C/SCA/1387/2003                                                ORDER



         petitioner is protected since 2003, interest of justice would be
         served if respondent no.1 is directed to hear the petitioner
         qua the grievance of the petitioner and decide the issue raised
         in this petition.
</p>


<p id="p_15">         9.    Accordingly,         respondent         no.1    shall       call      upon           the
         petitioner by a notice and after hearing the petitioner, pass
         appropriate order upon the grievance raised by the petitioner.
         Such exercise shall be carried out by respondent no.1 within a
         period of six months from the date of receipt of this order. Till
         then, the petitioner shall not be disturbed.
</p>


<p id="p_16">         10.   With this observation, present petition is disposed of.
         Rule is discharged. Interim relief stands vacated. No order
         as to costs.
</p>
