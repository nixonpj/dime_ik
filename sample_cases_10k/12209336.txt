                For the Petitioner         : Mr. Niranjan Kumar Sinha, Adv

</p>
<p id="p_8">05/09.10.2018         Heard learned counsel for the petitioner, State of Bihar and
        Jharkhand.
</p><p id="p_9">                      Petitioner has retired on 31.03.2017 as a Toll Peon (Class-IV
        employee) from the office of the Executive Engineer, N.H. Division,
        Lakhisarai under Bihar. According to the petitioner, his G.P.F. dues for the
        period 1987 to 1999 i.e. the period during which he was posted at Dhanbad
        Division remained due for absence of deduction statement from the concerned
        Executive Engineer, N.H. Division, Dhanbad. Annexure-1 being memo
        no.810 dated 11th November 2016 is a correspondence on the subject between
        the Executive Engineer, N.H. Division, Lakhisarai, Munger and the Executive
        Engineer, N.H. Division, Dhanbad (respondent no.6 and 5 respectively).
        Therefore, petitioner had to approach this Court for relief after his
        representation (Annexure-2) remained unredressed.
</p><p id="p_10">                      Counsel for the State of Bihar and Jharkhand submit that
        instructions have not been received as yet, as the case has been taken up for
        the first time. However, writ petition can be disposed of with liberty to the
        petitioner to approach the respondent no.5 with a representation for
        consideration of his grievances.
</p><p id="p_11">                       Having regard to the relevant material facts noted above and the
        submissions of learned counsel for the parties, the writ petition is disposed of
        with liberty to the petitioner to pursue his grievance before the respondent
        no.5 duly supported with all necessary facts and documents. Needless to say,
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                            -2-</span>
          the respondent no.5 would examine the contention of the petitioner in the light
          of the relevant records in accordance with law and if the contention of the
          petitioner is found tenable, necessary statement of deduction, etc. be
          transmitted to his counterpart in Bihar so that claim for balance G.P.F dues of
          the petitioner can be redressed by the competent authority under Bihar.
</p>



<p id="p_12">                                                      (Aparesh Kumar Singh, J.)
Shamim/
