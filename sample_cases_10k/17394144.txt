<div class="doc_title">Bachubhai Khodabhai Rabari &amp; vs This Petition In The Nature Of ... on 25 November, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">         C/WPPIL/94/2012                                       ORDER




         IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

                   WRIT PETITION (PIL) NO. 94 of 2012

================================================================
         BACHUBHAI KHODABHAI RABARI &amp; 1....Applicant(s)
                           Versus
      STATE OF GUJARAT THRO SECRETARY &amp; 4....Opponent(s)
================================================================
Appearance:
MR VIJAY H NANGESH, ADVOCATE for the Applicant(s) No. 1 - 2
MR UTKARSH SHARMA, AGP for the Opponent(s) No. 1
MR SP HASURKAR, ADVOCATE for the Opponent(s) No. 5
NOTICE SERVED BY DS for the Opponent(s) No. 1 - 4
================================================================

         CORAM: HONOURABLE THE ACTING CHIEF JUSTICE MR.
                VIJAY MANOHAR SAHAI
                and
                HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE R.P.DHOLARIA

                                Date : 25/11/2014


                                 ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">          (PER : HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE R.P.DHOLARIA)


</p><p id="p_2">1.          This petition in the nature of Public

Interest Litigation is filed by the petitioners

ventilating          their      grievance         that   the    Collector

has granted 60000 square meters of land of Survey

No.1421      paiki         1,   which       was     earmarked     for   the

purpose of gauchar land, in favour of the Gujarat

Energy       Transmission               Corporation        Limited        -



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                    Page 1 of 4</span>
</p><pre id="pre_2">          C/WPPIL/94/2012                                   ORDER



respondent          No.5    -    a    Government       company      for

</pre><p id="p_3">construction of Power House to supply electricity

to the villagers as well as their agricultural

fields.       Mr.Nangesh,         learned        counsel      for   the

petitioners has urged that the land being gauchar

land, the same could not have been allotted to

respondent No.5 company for installation of Power

House.
</p>
<p id="p_4">
</p><p id="p_5">2.          We have heard Mr.Vijay Nangesh, learned

counsel for the petitioners, Mr.Utkarsh Sharma,

learned          AGP       for        respondent         No.1       and

Mr.S.P.Hasurkar, learned counsel for respondent

No.5.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><p id="p_7">3.          On going through the material available

on record, more particularly, the affidavit-in-

reply filed by the Mamlatdar, Bhachau, District

Kutch    at      page      73   of    the      paper   book    wherein

paragraph 6 reads as under.
</p>
<p id="p_8">
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">            "It is respectfully submitted that, by
            way   of  the  present  petition,  the
            petitioner has prayed for removal of


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                 Page 2 of 4</span>
        C/WPPIL/94/2012                                         ORDER



           construction made by the Respondent No.5
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">           -     Gujarat      Energy     Transmission
           Corporation    Ltd.    from   the   Survey
           No.1421,   which   is   identified  as   a
           Traverse land. The main grievance which
           is raised by the petitioner is that the
           said construction is being made on a
           Gauchar land.    I am filing the present
           affidavit   pursuant     to  the   Hon'ble
           Court's order dated 27.08.2014, so as to
           place on record the details of Gauchar
           land i.e. 3200 Acres and the details of
           Cattles i.e. 4050 in number and the
           relevant documents."
</blockquote>
<p id="p_9">4.         It is, therefore, clearly reveals that

the land in question was identified as traverse

land   and      in       the    said        village,     as    stated    in

further affidavit at page 103, there are only

1965 useful animals and the total land available

as gauchar as on today is 3185 acres which is

more enough as per the criteria laid down by the

Government by circular dated 14.11.1950.


</p><p id="p_10">5.         In        view       of        the      aforesaid       factual

position,        when          the       gauchar        land    is     more

sufficient for useful animals and the land in

question     is      being       allotted          to   the    Government

company for the purpose of supplying electricity

to the villagers and their fields, in our view,


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                                     Page 3 of 4</span>
          C/WPPIL/94/2012                     ORDER



there appears no necessity to interfere with the

order of allotment passed by the Collector after

following due process of law.
</p>
<p id="p_11">
</p><p id="p_12">6.          In the result, this writ petition being

devoid of any merit deserves to be dismissed and

is accordingly dismissed. Notice is discharged.

There shall be no order as to costs.




</p><p id="p_13">                                         (V.M.SAHAI, ACJ.)




                                         (R.P.DHOLARIA,J.)
pathan




<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                           Page 4 of 4</span>
