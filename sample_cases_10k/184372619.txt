<div class="doc_title">Ritesh Kumar vs . The State Of Rajasthan &amp; Anr. on 10 December, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                                    1

   IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR RAJASTHAN AT
                       JODHPUR
                           ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">          S.B.CRIMINAL MISC.PETITION NO.903/2015
        Ritesh Kumar Vs. The State of Rajasthan &amp; Anr.

</p><p id="p_2">     Date of order             :            10.12.2015

            HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE SANDEEP MEHTA

None present, for the petitioner.
</p><p id="p_3">Mr.Pankaj Awasthi, P.P.
</p><p id="p_4">Mr.Pankaj Gupta, for the respondent No.2.
</p><p id="p_5">                            &lt;&gt;&lt;&gt;&lt;&gt;


     The instant misc. petition has been preferred by the

petitioner seeking quashing of the proceedings of Cr.Case

No.170/2013    pending    in       the   court   of   learned   Judicial

Magistrate First Class, Padampur for the offences under

<a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_1">Sections 498A</a> and <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_2">406</a> IPC on the ground of the compromise

entered into between the parties.
</p>
<p id="p_6">     Learned counsel for the complainant submits that the

matter, which arose because of matrimonial discord between

the husband and wife, has been mutually settled between the

spouses, who are living together happily. A compromise

application was also filed before the learned trial Court.

However, the learned trial Court accepted the same only to the

extent of the offence under <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_3">Section 406</a> IPC vide order dated

9.10.2015. He thus submits that the entire proceedings of the

case pending before the trial Court should be quashed on the

strength of the compromise arrived at between the parties.

</p><p id="p_7">     In this view of the matter and looking to the guidelines
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                      2</span>

    issued by the Hon'ble Apex Court in the case of Gian Singh Vs.

    State of Punjab &amp; Anr. reported in JT 2012(9) SC-426, it is

    apparent that further continuance of the proceedings going on

    against the petitioner in the learned trial Court is not expedient

    in the interest of justice. If the proceedings are allowed to

    continue, it may result into the compromise being unsettled.

</p><p id="p_8">          Accordingly, the misc. petition is allowed and the

    proceedings of the Cr.Case No.170/2013 pending in the court

    of learned Judicial Magistrate First Class, Padampur are hereby

    quashed.
</p>
<p id="p_9">          Stay petition is also disposed of.
</p>
<p id="p_10">                                                (SANDEEP MEHTA), J.
</p><p id="p_11">/tarun/
