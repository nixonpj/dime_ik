      This M.F.A. is filed under Section 37(1) (b) of
Arbitration and <a href="/doc/1306164/" id="a_1">Conciliation Act</a>, against the judgment and
decree dated 06/01/2009 passed in Arbitration Suit
No.2/2003 on the file of the Principal District Judge,
Hassan, dismissing the suit filed U/s. 34 of Arbitration and
<a href="/doc/1306164/" id="a_2">Conciliation Act</a> R/w. rule IV of 14 High Court of Karnataka
Arbitration Proceedings Rules of 2001.

      This M.F.A. coming on for           Orders   this   day,
N.K. PATIL J., delivered the following:

                        :JUDGMENT:
</pre>
<p id="p_1">      The appellant, assailing the correctness of the

impugned     judgment and award dated 06/01/2009

passed in Arbitration Suit No.2/2003 by the Principal

District Judge, Hassan, has presented this appeal.

</p><p id="p_2">      2.    Learned Additional Government Advocate

appearing for appellant, at the outset, submitted that,

this appeal may be disposed of,     following the judgment

and award passed by this Court dated 3rd April 2012

in M.F.A.No.8264/2009 (The State of Karnataka Vs. H.V.

Krishna and another) and connected matters, which are

arising out of the same contract and for the reasons stated

therein.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                             3</span><p id="p_3">




</p><p id="p_4">       3. Placing the above submissions made by learned

Additional    Government        Advocate    appearing   for

appellant on record and following the judgment dated

3rd April 2012 in M.F.A.No.8264/2009 (The State of

Karnataka Vs. H.V. Krishna and another) and connected

matters, and for the reasons stated therein, this appeal

filed by the appellant is dismissed.

</p><p id="p_5">       In view of the dismissal of the main matter, the

relief sought by the appellant in I.A.No.II/2010 does not

survive for consideration. Hence, it is disposed of as

having become infructuous. Ordered accordingly.
