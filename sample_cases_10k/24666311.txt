<p id="p_1">1.     Vide this order I shall decide the present revision filed against the
 order dated 14.10.2014 passed by learned ACMM in the case titled
 Sanjay   Gandhi   vs.   Piyush   Infrastructure   Private   Limited   and
 others.
</p>

<p id="p_2">2.     Brief submissions of the petitioner are:
</p>

<p id="p_3">CR No. 204283/16   Sanjay Gandhi v. Piyush Infrastructure Pvt. Ltd. and Ors.             Page 2 of 15
</p><p id="p_4"> a)             The   petitioner/complainant   had   booked   a   shop/office   in   the
     commercial property namely Piyush Mahindra Metropolitan Mall, NIT
</p><p id="p_5">     - 3, opposite Dassehra Ground, Faridabad, Haryana and had made a
     payment of ₹3,90,000/­ as booking amount. 
</p><p id="p_6">b)             On   04.08.2009,   petitioner/complainant   was   informed   by   the
     respondents that letter of intent has been obtained for the said project
     and the petitioner/complainant was asked to make up for the payment
     of ₹3,88,800/­ as second installment.
</p><p id="p_7">c)             The   respondents   also   confirmed   that   the
     petitioner/complainant was allotted an office/shop measuring 400 sq.
     feet   at   the   1st  floor   in   the   said   project.   As   he   accordingly   made   a
     payment of ₹3,88,800/­ through 2 cheques dated 19.08.2009.
</p><p id="p_8">d)             The petitioner/complainant was again asked on 13.09.2010 to
     make   over   the   payment   of   ₹2,66,284/­   towards   the   work   of
     excavation.
</p><p id="p_9">e)             On   07.03.2011,   the   respondents   issued   a   letter   regarding
     wrong payment for shop/office measuring 579.98 sq. feet instead of
     400   sq.   feet   whereby   demand   of   ₹10,22,255/­   was   made   by   the
     respondents.   On   receiving   the   said   notice/letter,   the
     petitioner/complainant   contacted   the   respondents/accused   persons
     and he told that he was allotted a shop/office measuring 400 sq. feet
     and   ₹10,22,555/­   was   wrongly   demanded   from   him.   But   the
     respondents   did   not   do   anything   and   issued   another   letter   dated
     14.06.2011 in the form of reminder demanding ₹10,73, 558.97 paise.
</p><p id="p_10">f)             Finally in the meeting the petitioner/complainant was informed
     that at the shop/office measuring 400 sq. feet would be allotted to him


CR No. 204283/16       Sanjay Gandhi v. Piyush Infrastructure Pvt. Ltd. and Ors.             Page 3 of 15
      in case he deposits an amount of ₹15,00,000/­.
</p><p id="p_11">g)            The respondents/accused person did not a lot shop/office to
     the   petitioner/complainant   and   played   a   fraud   upon   him.   The
     respondents/accused   persons   even   threatened   the   petitioner/
     complainant.
</p><p id="p_12">h)            The   petitioner/complainant   was   made   a   complaint   to   the
     police   but   no   action   was   taken   and   consequently   the   application
     under section 156 (3) <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_1">Cr.P.C</a>. was filed by the petitioner/complainant
     praying for directing SHO PS Badarpur to register the case against
     the   respondents/accused   persons   and   investigate   the   matter.   An
     Action Taken Report was called from SHO PS Badarpur where it is
     stated that the petitioner/complainant had invested for the allotment of
     shop number 131 in the project Piyush Mahindra Metropolitan Mall
     but   the   petitioner/complainant   failed   to   make   the   payment   as
     demanded by the respondents/accused persons and as a result of
     which allotment of shop was cancelled video letter dated 27.07.2011
     and   petitioner/complainant   was   informed   that   money   would   be
     refunded to him. It was further stated that the matter is of civil nature
     and no offence has been made out.
</p><p id="p_13">i)            Learned trial court after hearing the arguments of the parties
     passed   the   impugned   order   dismissing   the   application   of   the
     complainant/petitioner filed under section 156 (3) <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_2">Cr.P.C</a>.
</p><p id="p_14">j)            In the impugned order, learned trial court has observed that
     the petitioner/complainant has booked an office/shop in the project of
     respondents/accused   persons   at   Faridabad   and   had   also   paid
     booking amount of  ₹3,90,000/­ and thereafter, second installment of


CR No. 204283/16     Sanjay Gandhi v. Piyush Infrastructure Pvt. Ltd. and Ors.             Page 4 of 15
  ₹3,88,000/­. According to the petitioner/complainant, the office/shop
 measuring   400   sq.   feet   was   allotted   to   him   but   later   on   another
 shop/office   measuring   579.98   sq.   feet   was   allotted   and   the
 respondents/accused   persons   have   demanded   more   money   which
 was   not   paid   by   the   petitioner/complainant.   It   has   been   further
 observed that petitioner/complainant himself is in a position to pursue
 the present complaint and no specific investigation is required to be
 done by investigating agency. The respondents/accused persons are
 also   known   to   the   petitioner/complainant.   Learned   trial   court   has
 observed that it is well­settled law that it is not necessary that in each
 and  every  case  a magistrate  should  give  direction  to the  police  to
 register the FIR and investigate the matter. Learned trial court has
 observed   that   considering   all   these   facts,   the   case   of   the
 petitioner/complainant is not a fit case to give direction to the police
 under section 156 (3) <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_3">Cr.P.C</a>. to register a case.
</p><p id="p_15">k)         The petitioner has submitted that the respondents had the ill
 intention to grab the hard earned money of the petitioner to cause
 wrongful loss to him and that amounts invested by the petitioner have
 been utilized for the development/construction of the said project by
 the respondents. The petitioner has submitted that the respondents
 misrepresented that they had obtained the requisite sanction from the
 requisite authorities whereas it was later revealed that the sanction for
 the development of the site in question was never provided by the
 requisite   authorities   and   on   account   of   false   and   fabricated
 documents,   the   respondents   had   malafidely,   dishonestly   and
 fraudulently induced the petitioner/complainant to invest his hardened


CR No. 204283/16   Sanjay Gandhi v. Piyush Infrastructure Pvt. Ltd. and Ors.             Page 5 of 15
      money into an unapproved project by making false representations.
</p><p id="p_16">l)            Petitioner   has   also   stated   that   the   respondent   persons
     dishonestly/fraudulently and malafidely played a trick under the entity
     of company and have looted huge money from various other innocent
     investors including the petitioner and taken booking amount as well
     as   further   installment   amounts   for   respective   units   and   booked
     number   of   units   which  is   more   than   the   units   available   with   them.
     Petitioner has also stated that the respondent persons adopt all the
     illegal means to achieve their early. Designed motives and that there
     are various criminal complaints already registered into FIRs against
     the respondent persons in various cities.
</p><p id="p_17">m)            The   respondents   dishonestly,   malafidely   and   fraudulently
     misused the hard earned money of the petitioner and played a fraud
     upon   the   petitioner   and   public   at   large.   The   respondents   with   the
     common intention to cheat misappropriated the funds of petitioner to
     use the same for their personal interest in furtherance of their ulterior
     designed motives and common intention made false representation.
</p><p id="p_18">n)            The petitioner has also submitted that on one occasion in the
     month of November, 2013, on request of one of the representatives of
     the respondent persons namely Mr. Amit to meet with the respondent
     persons and to settle the dispute amicably, the petitioner visited the
     respondent   persons   on   8th  November,   2013   but   the   respondent
     persons along with their associated persons namely Pundhir instead
     of   settling   the   issue   threatened   the   petitioner   to   face   the   dire
     consequences in case he would not  take back his complaint.
</p>

</p><p id="p_20"> 3.      The   grounds   of   the   petitioner   for   filing   the   present   revision
 petition are:
</p>

<blockquote id="blockquote_1">     a) The learned MM misdirected himself in forming an opinion that
        the   dispute   is   in   respect   of   demand   of   more   money   by   the
        respondent   despite   the   fact   that   the   petitioner   had   booked   a
        commercial unit of 400 sq. feet (+ / ­ 10%) and after receiving
        installment as well as allotment of the said unit in the name of the
        petitioner, the respondents unilaterally changed units space from
        400 square feet to 579 square feet. 
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_2">     b) Learned   trial   court   ignored   that   vide   letter   dated   07.03.2011,
        approximately 6 months after affirming the allocation to be 400
        sq. feet at 1st floor of the said project and providing of statement
        of   account,   the   respondents/   accused   persons   deliberately   by
        forging   statement   of   account   issued   letter   dated   07.03.2011
        representing the allocation of commercial space of 579.980 sq.
        feet   for   consideration   of   ₹41,46,285   to   the   complainant   and
        raised a demand of  ₹9,71,494/­ and in the forced statement of
        account,   the   respondents/   accused   persons   deliberately
        manipulated   the   amounts   already   procured   from   the
        petitioner/complainant   and   despite   petitioner/complainant
        requesting   the   respondents   to   rectify   the   deed   through   letter
        dated   04.04.2011   and   03.06.2011,   the   respondents/accused
        persons continue to demand  ₹9,71,494/­ along with interest and
        penalties. 
</blockquote>
<p id="p_21">CR No. 204283/16   Sanjay Gandhi v. Piyush Infrastructure Pvt. Ltd. and Ors.             Page 7 of 15
</p><p id="p_22">    c) The learned trial court ignored the fact that the respondents have
       cheated   him   by   representing   that   this   approved   project   as   an
       approved one.
</p>
<p id="p_23">   d) The   learned   ACMM   failed   to   consider   the   fact   that   the
       respondent   persons   have   also   shown   certain   documents   in
       respect   of   the   said   project   is   approved   by   the   statutory
       authorities   and   the   same   is   still   in   the   possession   of   the
       respondents which were later revealed to be fabricated by the
       respondents with sole intention to allure the petitioner to book the
       said   unit   and   that   the   said   documents   cannot   be   called   out
       without the interrogation conducted by the police.
</p>
<p id="p_24">   e) The learned ACMM failed to consider that the petitioner, with his

       feet and the respondents also promised or represented the safe

       illegitimate earning.
</p>
<p id="p_25">   f) The learned magistrate failed to consider the status report filed

<p id="p_28">4.      The   petitioner   has   prayed   for   setting   aside   the   order   of   the

<p id="p_29">5.      On   receipt   of   the   notice   of   the   petitioner,   the   respondents

<p id="p_30">6.     The   respondents   had   filed   detailed   written   submissions   and

<p id="p_31">7.     Arguments heard. Record perused carefully.
</p>

<p id="p_32">8.     Learned counsel for petitioner has known attention to copy of the
 receipt/acknowledgement issued by the respondent of an amount of
 ₹3,90,000/­ dated 09.01.08 which reveals that the said amount was
 taken/accepted   by   the   respondent   as   advance   for   professional

 the shop showroom is subject to variation of 10%. Learned counsel
 for   petitioner   has   drawn   attention   to   letter   dated   4 th  August,   2009
 written   by   the   respondent   to   the   petitioner   in   reference   to   the
 registration of office space of 400 sq. feet by the petitioner informing
 the petitioner that the respondents had obtained the LOI for the said
 project.   Through   the   said   letter,   the   respondents   requested   the

  petitioner to make for the payment of ₹3,88,800/­ within 15 days from

 allotment.   The   said   payment   was   accordingly   made   and   the
 respondents had issued a receipt dated 10.09.2009 in respect of the
 said payment.
