<p id="p_1">            By filing the present petition under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> of the Code

of Criminal Procedure, the petitioner has sought anticipatory bail in case

FIR No.328, dated 27.04.2016, registered under <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_2">Sections 420</a>, <a href="/doc/1985627/" id="a_3">467</a>, <a href="/doc/556166/" id="a_4">468</a>,

<a href="/doc/1466184/" id="a_5">471</a> and <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_6">120-B</a> IPC, at Police Station Civil Lines, Karnal.

</p><p id="p_2">            It is contended that petitioner has been falsely roped in the

instant case by the complainant. The alleged fake and fabricated transfer

certificate is a valid document as the same was issued after proper

verification and attestation by the Principal of the school. The Central

Board of Secondary Education also verified the said certificate. The co-

accused Pankaj Bhalla has already been allowed interim bail by this

Court, vide order dated 21.07.2016. In pursuance of order dated



           For Subsequent orders see CRM-M-21257-2016

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2"> CRM-M-19942-2016                                                            -2-</span>

02.06.2016, the petitioner has joined the investigation.

</p><p id="p_3">             On the other hand, the learned State counsel submits that

though the petitioner has joined investigation but he along with her co-

accused/wife and one Pankaj Bhalla, obtained a fake and forged transfer

certificate in the name of his wife to meet the eligibility condition for

contesting the election.
</p>
<p id="p_4">             Heard.
</p>
<p id="p_5">             As per the record, the petitioner in connivance with co-

accused has obtained the transfer certificate on the basis of forged and

fabricated documents. The petitioner had appended his signature against

column for parents in the admission form of Kalawati, co-accused/wife of

the petitioner. In fact, the wife of the petitioner/co-accused was never

admitted to Guru Harkrishan School. The certificate held by the petitioner

is not as per record and is a fabricated document. The petitioner is the

beneficiary of the certificate in question. Keeping in view the nature of

offence, this Court is not inclined to grant anticipatory bail at this stage.

</p><p id="p_6">             Dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_7">             Anything said herein above shall not be construed as an

expression of opinion on the merits of the case.
