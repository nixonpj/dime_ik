<div class="doc_title">Shailendra Kumar Chaudhary vs The State Of Bihar on 2 April, 2012</div><pre id="pre_1">      Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.12159 of 2011 (8) dt.02-04-2012




                  IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                   Criminal Miscellaneous No.12159 of 2011
                   ======================================================
                   Shailendra Kumar Chaudhary @ Shailendra Chaudhary, son of Sri
                   Nagendra Chaudhary, resident of Village- Fatehpur Parai, P.S. Mahua,
                   District- Vaishali.
                                                                           .... .... Petitioner.
                                                    Versus
                   1. The State of Bihar.
                   2. Ashok Sharma S/o Shiv Naresh Sharma, Vishunpur, Vaishali.
                   3. Rubi Kumari, W/o Shailendra Chaudhary, R/o Village- Vishunpur,
                      P.O. &amp; P.S. Sarai, Vaishali.
                                                                    .... .... Opposite Parties.

                   CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE GOPAL PRASAD
                   ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">8   02-04-2012                   Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned

                   counsel for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                 The petitioner is apprehending his arrest in connection

                   with Complaint Case No. C 1 1480/03 (Trial No. 2173/07),

                   registered under <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_1">Sections 323</a>, <a href="/doc/1101188/" id="a_2">379</a>, <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_3">498A</a> of the Indian Penal Code

                   and <a href="/doc/751411/" id="a_4">Section 3</a>/<a href="/doc/1023340/" id="a_5">4</a> of Dowry Prohibition Act.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                 There is allegation of demand and subjecting cruelty

                   against the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                  Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that

                   petitioner has filed a divorce case on the ground that the

                   complainant is not ready to live with him.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                 Learned counsel for the complainant contends that she

                   has no knowledge of divorce case filed by the petitioner up till

                   date.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                Under the facts and circumstances of the case, the above
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">        Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.12159 of 2011 (8) dt.02-04-2012</span>




                    named petitioner in the event of his arrest/surrender within a

                    period of four weeks from today, shall be released on bail on

                    furnishing bail bond of Rs. 10,000/- (Ten thousand) with two

                    sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of the Sub-

                    divisional Judicial Magistrate, Vaishali, Hajipur in connection

                    with Complaint Case No. C1 1480/03, subject to condition as laid

                    down under <a href="/doc/1763444/" id="a_6">Section 438</a> (2) of <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_7">Cr.P.C</a>. with a further condition

                    that petitioner shall pay maintenance to the tune of Rs. 1,000/- per

                    month to the complainant-wife till higher maintenance shall be

                    fixed by the competent court and shall appear on each and every

                    date fixed in this case and in any absence shall be subject to

                    discretion of the court below on reasonable ground. However, any

                    violation of condition may be ground for proceeding for

                    cancellation of the bail bond of the petitioner and court below

                    shall proceed for expeditious disposal of the case.



</p><pre id="pre_2">m.p.                                                (Gopal Prasad, J)
 </pre>
