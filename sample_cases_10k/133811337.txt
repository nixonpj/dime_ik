<p id="p_2">         ORAL JUDGMENT (PER B.R. GAVAI, J.)





         1]               Rule.  Rule made returnable forthwith.  Heard the learned

         Counsel for the parties finally by consent.</p><pre id="pre_2">



         2]               The petitioner has approached this Court being aggrieved

         by the rejection of his application for furlough.     The perusal of the


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">    ::: Uploaded on - 17/02/2017                                            ::: Downloaded on - 19/02/2017 00:49:13 :::</span>
                                                                    2                                         CWP.941.16.odt

</pre><p id="p_3">         affidavit in reply would reveal that the petitioner was arrested in the




                                                                                                          
         year 2011 when he did not surrender for a period of 126 days from




                                                                            
         the due date.   However, the perusal of the reply itself would reveal

         that the petitioner has been thereafter released on four occasions and




                                                                           
         on all these occasions he has surrendered himself though belatedly.
</p>



                                                         
<p id="p_4">         3]               For   the   delayed   surrender,   the   petitioner   must   have
                                
         already   been   penalized   in   accordance   with   the   Rules.     When   the

         respondent   authorities   have   themselves   released   the   petitioner   on
                               
         parole/furlough after he was required to be arrested, we find that on

         the same ground the application cannot be now rejected.
</p>      
   



<p id="p_5">         4]               The   Division   Bench   of   this   Court   in   the   case   of

         <a href="/doc/38873429/" id="a_1">Jaggusingh  Ramsingh Mungona .vs. The D.I.G. Prisons (E)(R),





         Nagpur &amp; another</a> in Criminal Writ Petition No. 470/14 at Nagpur

         decided   on   18.7.2014  has   held   that   merely   because   the   prisoner





         was required to be arrested cannot be a ground for denying the right

         to furlough.  It has further been held that each application has to be

         considered in the light of the facts and circumstances as appearing in

         the matter.</p><pre id="pre_3">



         5]               In   that   view   of   the   matter,  we   are   inclined   to   allow   the

                                                                    3                                         CWP.941.16.odt

         petition.     The petitioner is directed to be released on furlough after




                                                                                                          
</pre><p id="p_6">         following the procedure prescribed in law.
</p>



                                                                            
<p id="p_7">         6]               Rule is made absolute in the above terms.    Fees payable




                                                                           
         to the learned Counsel appointed for the petitioner are quantified at

         Rs.1500/-.
</p>
