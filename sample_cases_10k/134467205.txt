<div class="doc_title">V.R.Rogner vs The State Of Kerala on 22 February, 2011</div><pre id="pre_1">       

  

  

 
 
  IN THE HIGH COURT OF KERALA AT ERNAKULAM

Bail Appl..No. 988 of 2011()


1. V.R.ROGNER,
                      ...  Petitioner

                        Vs



1. THE STATE OF KERALA,
                       ...       Respondent

                For Petitioner  :SRI.JIJO PAUL

                For Respondent  :PUBLIC PROSECUTOR

The Hon'ble MR. Justice V.RAMKUMAR

 Dated :22/02/2011

 O R D E R
                       V. RAMKUMAR, J.
                 ===================
                     B.A. No. 988 of 2011
                ====================
         Dated this the 22nd day of February, 2011.

                             ORDER
</pre>


<p id="p_1">     Petitioner who is the sole accused in Crime No. 617 of 2010

of Vellikulangara Police Station, Thrissur for offences punishable

under <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_1">Sections 341</a>, <a href="/doc/1198799/" id="a_2">294(b)</a>, <a href="/doc/1208971/" id="a_3">353</a> and <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_4">323</a> I.P.C., seeks

anticipatory bail.
</p>
<p id="p_2">     2.    The    learned   Public    Prosecutor    opposed     the

application.
</p>
<p id="p_3">     3.    After evaluating the factors and parameters which

are to be taken into consideration in the light of paragraph 122

of the verdict     dated   2-12-2010     of the Apex Court       in

<a href="/doc/1108032/" id="a_5">Siddharam Satlingappa Mhetre v. State of Maharashtra

and Others</a> (2010 (4) KLT 930),            I am of the view that

anticipatory bail cannot be granted in a case of this nature, since

the investigating officer     has not had      the advantage of

interrogating the petitioner.     But at the same time,       I am

inclined to permit the petitioner to surrender before the

Investigating Officer for the purpose of interrogation and then to

B.A. No. 988/2011

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                2</span>

have his application for bail allowed by the Magistrate or the

Court      having jurisdiction. Accordingly, the petitioner shall

surrender before the investigating officer on 03.03.2011 or on

04.03.2011 for the purpose of interrogation and recovery of

incriminating material, if any. In case the investigating officer

is of the view that having regard to the facts of the case arrest

of the petitioner is imperative he shall record his reasons for

the arrest in the case diary as insisted in paragraph 129       of

Siddharam Satlingappa Mhetre's case (supra).                 The

petitioner shall thereafter be produced before the Magistrate or

the Court concerned and permitted to file an application for

regular bail.     In case the interrogation of the petitioner is

without arresting him, the petitioner shall thereafter appear

before the Magistrate or the Court concerned        and apply for

regular bail. The Magistrate or the Court on being satisfied that

the petitioner has been interrogated by the police shall, after

hearing the prosecution as well, release the petitioner on bail.

</p><p id="p_4">      4.   In case the accused    while surrendering before the

Investigating Officer has deprived      the investigating officer

sufficient time for interrogation, the officer shall complete the

B.A. No. 988/2011

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                     3</span>

interrogation even if it is beyond the time limit fixed as above

and submit a report to that effect to the Magistrate or the Court

concerned. Likewise, the Magistrate or the Court also will not

be bound by the time limit fixed as above if sufficient time

was not available after the production or appearance of the

accused .
</p>
<p id="p_5">      5.     The release of the        petitioner shall be on the

petitioner executing a bond for `15,000/- (Rupees              fifteen

thousand only) with two solvent sureties each for the like amount

to the satisfaction of the Court concerned and subject to the

following conditions:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">             1.    The   petitioner      shall  report  before  the

             Investigating Officer between 9 a.m. and 11 a.m. on all

             Wednesdays.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_2">
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">             2.  The petitioner shall make himself    available for

             interrogation including custodial interrogation as and

             when required by the Investigating Officer.


</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_4">             3.    Petitioner shall not influence or intimidate the

             prosecution witnesses nor shall he attempt to tamper

             with the evidence for the prosecution.

</blockquote><p id="p_6">B.A. No. 988/2011

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                                     4</span>



</p><p id="p_7">             4.    Petitioner shall not commit any offence while on

             bail.
</p>
<p id="p_8">
</p><p id="p_9">              5. If the petitioner commits breach of any of the above

             conditions, the bail granted to him shall be liable to be

             cancelled.
</p>
<p id="p_10">      This petition is disposed of as above.
</p>
<p id="p_11">      Dated this the 22nd day of February, 2011.




</p><p id="p_12">                                                 V.RAMKUMAR, JUDGE.
</p>
