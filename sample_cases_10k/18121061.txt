<div class="doc_title">Mintu Kumar Yadav vs The State Of Bihar on 16 December, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                     IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA

                                Civil Writ Jurisdiction Case No.16131 of 2019
                 ======================================================
                 Mintu Kumar Yadav, S/o Jang Bahadur Yadav, Resident of Village- Hetampur,
                 P.S.- Badhara (Krishnagarh), Distt.- Bhojpur

                                                                          ... ... Petitioner/s
                                                  Versus
           1.    The State of Bihar through the Principal Secretary, Department of Excise,
                 Bihar, Patna
           2.    The District Magistrate, Bhojpur
           3.    The Superintendent of Police, Ara, Distt.- Bhojpur
           4.    The Officer-in-Charge of Badhara (Krishnagarh) Police Station P.S.-
                 Badhara (Krishnagarh), Distt.- Bhojpur

                                                           ... ... Respondent/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s   :      Mr.Arun Kumar Singh, Advocate
                 For the Respondent/s   :      Mr.Kumar Manish (SC-5)
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE THE CHIEF JUSTICE
                         and
                         HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE ANIL KUMAR UPADHYAY
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">                 (Per: HONOURABLE THE CHIEF JUSTICE)

2   16-12-2019                 Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned

                   counsel appearing on behalf of the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                               The petitioner prays for provisional release of Hero

                   Glamour Motorcycle bearing Registration No. BR03V-2779,

                   which     has    been    seized    in   connection      with    Badhara

                   (Krishnagarh) P.S. Case No. 517 of 2018, for the offences

                   punishable under Section 30(a) of the Bihar Prohibition and

                   <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_1">Excise Act</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                               It is continued practice of this Court that in cases of
 Patna High Court CWJC No.16131 of 2019(2) dt.16-12-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                           2/9</span>




          drunken driving; no recovery from the vehicle; recovery of less

          than commercial quantity; where ex-facie, vehicle is not liable

          to be confiscated; where there is inordinate delay in initiating

          proceedings for confiscation of the vehicle etc., this Court has

          been      directing      the      State     to   provisionally   release

          vehicle/property, subject to initiation/conclusion/finalisation of

          the confiscatory proceedings, as the case may be. Reference can

          be made to the judgments/ orders passed by different co-
</p>
<p id="p_4">          ordinate Benches of this Court, viz:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">                          (i)    Judgement dated 22.03.2018 passed in CWJC
                                  No.5049 of 2018, titled as Diwakar Kumar
                                  Singh versus The State of Bihar &amp; Ors.;
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">                          (ii)    order dated 31.07.2018 passed in CWJC
                                  No.13162 of 2018 titled as Rajesh Kumar
                                  Pandit @ Rajesh Pandit Vs. The State of
                                  Bihar &amp; Ors.;
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">                          (iii) order dated 31.07.2018 passed in CWJC
                                  No.14242 of 2018 titled as Amar Kumar Vs.
                                  The State of Bihar &amp; Ors.;
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_4">                          (iv) order dated 12.02.2018 passed in CWJC
                                  No.2437 of 2018 titled as Mahendra Manjhi
                                  Vs. The State of Bihar &amp; Ors.;
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_5">                          (v)    judgement dated 12.02.2018 passed in CWJC
                                  No.2470 of 2018 titled as Laxman Das @
                                  Lakshman Ravidas Vs. The State of Bihar &amp;
                                  Ors.;</blockquote>
<p id="p_5">                          (vi) order dated 11.09.2017 passed in CWJC
 Patna High Court CWJC No.16131 of 2019(2) dt.16-12-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                           3/9</span>




                                  No.13158 of 2017 titled as Sanjay Kumar
                                  Versus The State of Bihar &amp; Ors.;
</p><p id="p_6">                          (vii) order dated 27.03.2018 passed in CWJC
                                  No.5528 of 2018 titled as Bikash Kumar Vs.
                                  The State of Bihar &amp; Ors.;
</p><p id="p_7">                          (viii) order dated 27.03.2018 passed in CWJC
                                  No.5528 of 2018 titled as Bikash Kumar
                                  Versus The State of Bihar &amp; Ors.;
</p><p id="p_8">                          (ix) order dated 01.05.2018 passed in CWJC
                                  No.7755 of 2018 titled as Anandi Prasad
                                  Versus The State of Bihar &amp; Ors.;
</p><p id="p_9">                          (x)    order dated 01.05.2018 passed in CWJC
                                  No.7644 of 2018 titled as Suraj Ram Versus
                                  The State of Bihar &amp; Ors.;</p><pre id="pre_2">
                          (xi)    order dated 07.08.2018 passed in CWJC
                                  No.15435        of      2018   titled   as   Kalesar
</pre><p id="p_10">                                  Chaudhari Versus the State of Bihar &amp; Ors.;
</p><p id="p_11">                          (x)    judgement dated 18.01.2019 passed in CWJC
                                  No.1215 of 2019 titled as Raushan Kumar @
                                  Raushan Kumar Singh Versus The State of
                                  Bihar &amp; Ors.;
</p><p id="p_12">                          (xi)   judgement dated 29.01.2019 passed in CWJC
                                  No.1620 of 2019 titled as Asharfi Kumar @
                                  Rakesh Kumar Versus the State of Bihar &amp;
                                  Ors.;
</p><p id="p_13">                          (xii) judgement dated 08.02.2019 passed in CWJC
                                  No.2380 of 2019 titled as Avinash Kumar
                                  Versus the State of Bihar &amp; Ors.;
</p><p id="p_14">                          (xiii) judgement dated 29.01.2019 passed in CWJC
                                  No.1648 of 2019 titled as Roshan Kumar
 Patna High Court CWJC No.16131 of 2019(2) dt.16-12-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                                           4/9</span>




                                  Versus The State of Bihar &amp; Ors.; and
</p><p id="p_15">                          (xiv) judgement dated 22.01.2019 passed in CWJC
                                  No.1314 of 2019 titled as Shanti Devi Versus
                                  The State of Bihar &amp; Ors..
</p>
<p id="p_16">                       In fact, in CWJC No.5049 of 2018 titled as Diwakar

          Kumar Singh Versus the State of Bihar &amp; Ors. the Court issued

          the following directions:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_6">                                "That apart, in the confiscation proceedings, the
                          confiscating authority shall take note of the provisions
                          of Section 56 of the Bihar Prohibition and <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_2">Excise Act</a>,
                          2016 and record a positive finding after hearing the
                          petitioner as to whether when the petitioner is found or
                          the vehicle is found to be used by a person in drunken
                          condition and no liquor is seized from the vehicle or
                          when the vehicle is not used for transportation of
                          liquor, whether the provision of <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_3">Section 56</a> of the Act
                          will apply. It shall be mandatory for the confiscating
                          authority to decide this issue before passing any order
                          on the confiscation proceedings. The confiscating
                          authority shall consider the provision of <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_4">Section 56</a> of
                          the Act, apply his mind and pass a speaking order with
                          regard to confiscation initiated. Without deciding the
                          aforesaid issue as a preliminary issue, further
                          proceedings in the confiscation proceedings shall be
                          prohibited.</blockquote>
<p id="p_17">                                We further request the office of the Advocate
                          General to communicate this order to all the District
                          Magistrates in the State of Bihar, who would be
                          mandated to pass an appropriate order in such cases
                          where the vehicle has been confiscated under <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_5">Section
                          56</a> of the Act only on the allegation that the vehicle was
                          being driven in a drunken condition and no liquor was
                          seized from the vehicle nor the vehicle used for
                          transportation or carriage of liquor. The issue shall be
                          decided by each and every District Magistrate before
                          proceeding in the confiscation proceedings where the
                          allegation is about the vehicle being driven in a
 Patna High Court CWJC No.16131 of 2019(2) dt.16-12-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                                           5/9</span>




                          drunken condition and no liquor was found from the
                          possession of the vehicle.
</p><p id="p_18">                               It shall be the duty of the Advocate General to
                          communicate this order to each and every District
                          Magistrate and inform the Registrar General of this
                          Court. In spite thereof, if we find that the District
                          Magistrates are passing confiscation order without
                          addressing this issue first, we may consider initiating
                          contempt proceedings against the concerned District
                          Magistrate."
</p>
<p id="p_19">                      It is further seen that in CWJC No.15003 of 2019

          titled as Shobha Devi Versus the State of Bihar &amp; Ors. the Court

          observed as under:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_7">                                       "6. On examination of aforesaid fact,
                          particularly allegation of the petitioner that in a
                          court proceeding before the learned Special Judge,
                          Excise, a false information was given, we are of the
                          opinion that the court of learned Special Judge,
                          Excise would be competent court to pass an
                          appropriate order, in view of provisions contained
                          in <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_6">Section 340</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure,
                          1973.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_8">                                       7. Accordingly, the petitioner is granted
                          liberty to file appropriate petition before the learned
                          Special Judge, Excise for prosecuting the concerned
                          police official.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_9">                                       8. So far as claim of compensation is
                          concerned, obviously on going through the material
                          on record, since there was no recovery of liquor
                          from the vehicle and it was a case, in which, the
                          occupants of the vehicle were alleged to be in
                          drunken condition and were creating nuisance,
                          though were liable to be arrested. In any event, the
                          vehicle was not required to be seized, since it was
                          not liable to be confiscated.</blockquote>
<p id="p_20">                                       9. In such situation, we are of the
                          opinion that it is a fit case, in which, we may direct
                          to pay adequate compensation to the petitioner,
 Patna High Court CWJC No.16131 of 2019(2) dt.16-12-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_5">                                           6/9</span>




                          being owner of the vehicle, to the tune of
                          Rs.75,000/- (seventy five thousand), however, Sri
                          Kumar Manish, learned Standing Counsel - 5
                          requests for granting an opportunity for obtaining
                          detailed instruction and filing counter affidavit in
                          the matter. The request of Sri Kumar Manish, S.C.-5
                          is allowed for filing counter affidavit so that final
                          order may be passed.
</p><p id="p_21">                                      10. It goes without saying that before
                          filing counter affidavit, the respondent no.
                          4/Superintendent of Police, Darbhanga may conduct
                          a preliminary inquiry regarding the conduct of the
                          police officer, who had seized the vehicle of the
                          petitioner and state all those facts in its counter
                          affidavit, which must be filed by 29 th of November,
                          2019. The affidavit must be sworn by the
                          Superintendent of Police himself.
</p><p id="p_22">                                      11. It further goes without saying that if
                          after considering all the facts, including counter
                          affidavit, which is proposed to be filed, the Court
                          comes to the conclusion that the petitioner is
                          entitled for claim of amount of compensation,
                          which has been referred hereinabove, the said
                          compensation amount must be recovered from the
                          pocket of the police officer, who was responsible
                          for such illegal seizure."
</p>
<p id="p_23">                      Despite the same, only before this Court, when

          matters of similar nature came up for hearing today, the learned

          Advocate General assisted by Shri Vikash Kumar, learned

          Standing      Counsel-11,       and     Shri    Vivek   Prasad,   learned

          Government Pleader-7, vehemently opposed the petition for

          release of vehicle.
</p>
<p id="p_24">                        Without adjudicating the petitioner's petition on

          merits, we are of the considered view that interest of justice
 Patna High Court CWJC No.16131 of 2019(2) dt.16-12-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_6">                                            7/9</span>




          would be best met, if the petition is disposed of in the following

          terms:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_10">                          (a)    Since the vehicle in question stands seized in
                                  relation to the FIR which stood registered
                                  long ago, in case confiscation proceeding has
                                  not been initiated, it must be initiated within
                                  a period of 15 days from today and that
                                  confiscation proceeding stands initiated, we
                                  direct the appropriate authority under the Act
                                  to forthwith ensure that such proceedings be
                                  concluded not later than 30 days.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_11">                          (b)     The petitioner undertakes to make himself
                                  available in the office of the appropriate
                                  authority empowered under Section 58 of the
                                  Act i.e. District Collector, Bhojpur in his
                                  office on 31.12.2019 at 10:30 A.M.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_12">                          (c)    We further direct the appropriate authority to
                                  positively        conclude      the    confiscation
                                  proceeding within next thirty days on
                                  appearance of the petitioner. If for whatever
                                  reason,         such     proceeding    cannot    be
                                  concluded, in that event it shall be open for
                                  the authority to take such measures, as are
                                  permissible in law, for release of the vehicle
                                  in question by way of interim measure, on
                                  such terms as may be deemed appropriate,
                                  considering        the     attending   facts    and
                                  circumstances of the case.</blockquote>
<p id="p_25">                          (d)    If eventually, the appropriate authority arrives
 Patna High Court CWJC No.16131 of 2019(2) dt.16-12-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_7">                                           8/9</span>




                                  at a conclusion that the property is not liable
                                  to be confiscated, it shall be open for the
                                  petitioner to seek damages in accordance
                                  with law and have appropriate proceedings
                                  initiated against the erring officials/officers.
</p>

<p id="p_26">                      Learned counsel for the petitioner states that the

          certified copy of the order shall be made available to the

          concerned District Collector on the date so fixed.
</p>
<p id="p_27">                      The writ petition stands disposed of.
</p>
<p id="p_28">                      For future guidance, where parties have not

          approached this Court, we issue the following direction:-
</p>
<p id="p_29">                      The expression "reasonable delay" used in Section 58

          of Chapter VI of the Act, in our considered view, necessarily has

          to be within a reasonable time and with despatch, which period,

          in our considered view, three months time is sufficient enough

          for any authority to adjudicate any issue, more so, when we are

          dealing with confiscatory proceedings.
</p>
<p id="p_30">                      We clarify that we have not adjudicated the writ

          petition on merits and it shall be open for the parties to take all

          stand in the adjudicatory proceedings and wherever parties are

          aggrieved, it shall be open to them to initiate appropriate

          proceeding before the appellate authority.
</p>
<p id="p_31">                      Learned counsel for the State also undertakes to
          Patna High Court CWJC No.16131 of 2019(2) dt.16-12-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_8">                                                    9/9</span>




                   communicate the order to the concerned appropriate authority

                   i.e. District Magistrate, empowered under <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_7">Section 58</a> of the Act.
</p>


<p id="p_32">                                             (Sanjay Karol, CJ)


                                         ( Anil Kumar Upadhyay, J)
uday/-
</p>
