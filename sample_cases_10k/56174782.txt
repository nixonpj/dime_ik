                     AT CHANDIGARH

                                   CRM M-23421 of 2018
                                   Date of Decision: January 10, 2019

Saurabh Khanna                                                  ......Petitioner
                                              Versus
State of Punjab                                                 .... Respondent


CORAM :      HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE FATEH DEEP SINGH


Present :    Mr. R.K. Trikha, Advocate
             for the petitioner.

             Mr. Avtar Singh Sandhu, Addl. A.G., Punjab.

             Ms. Ishma Randhawa, Advocate
             for the complainant.

FATEH DEEP SINGH, J. (Oral)
</pre>
<p id="p_1">             Learned State counsel on instructions from ASI Harinder

Singh Police Station Airport District Police Commissionerate,

Amritsar submits that in consequence of interim order dated

29.05.2018, the petitioner has since joined the investigation and

nothing is to be recovered from him and does not oppose the grant of

bail together with the fact as has been brought to the notice of the

Court that the parties have already effected a settlement/agreement

before the mediation and Conciliation Centre of this Court, copy of

which has been placed on record as Annexure P-2.

</p><p id="p_2">             Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that since the

petitioner has business interest abroad, he be granted liberty to go

abroad, if so necessitated.
</p>
<p id="p_3">             Heard.
</p>
<p id="p_4">             Liberty is granted to the petitioner to go abroad if so

necessitated upon necessary intimation to the trial Court by giving his

</p>
<p id="p_5">             In the light of the statement made by the learned State

counsel, the interim bail granted to the petitioner vide order dated

29.05.2018 is made absolute till submission of report under <a href="/doc/1412034/" id="a_1">Section

173</a> Cr.P.C. (challan). Thereafter, petitioner will be permitted to

furnish regular bail bonds to the satisfaction of the trial Court. The

petitioner shall abide by the conditions laid down in Section 438(2)

Cr.P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_6">             With these observations, the present petition stands

disposed off.
