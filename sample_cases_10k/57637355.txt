<div class="doc_title">Rajesh Kumar vs The State Of Bihar on 6 October, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA

                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.42377 of 2016
                     Arising Out of PS.Case No. -61 Year- 2016 Thana -BHAGWANPUR District- VAISHALI
                                                           (HAJIPUR)
                 ======================================================
                 1. Rajesh Kumar Son of Satrughan Rai Resident of Village- Bakhari, P.S.-
                 Minapur, District- Muzaffarpur.
                                                                      .... .... Petitioner/s
                                                 Versus
                 1. The State of Bihar
                                                                 .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s      :   Mr. Nachiketa Jha
                 For the Opposite Party/s    : Mr. Anzarul Haque Sahara
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE CHAKRADHARI
                 SHARAN SINGH
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">2   06-10-2016                    Heard learned Counsel for the petitioner and

                  learned Additional Public Prosecutor for the State.

</p><p id="p_2">                                  This application, for grant of anticipatory bail,

                  arises out of Bhagwanpur Police Station Case No. 61 of

                  2016, disclosing offences under <a href="/doc/3563/" id="a_1">Sections 429</a>/<a href="/doc/1985627/" id="a_2">467</a>/<a href="/doc/556166/" id="a_3">468</a>/

                  <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_4">120B</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_5">34</a> of the Indian Penal.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                  The petitioner has been named in the First

                  Information Report itself on the basis of statement of co-

                  accused Ramesh Kumar, who was apprehended from A.T.M.

                  booth. From the possession of Ramesh Kumar, several

                  A.T.M. cards were recovered. He described the present

                  petitioner as his accomplice, who, according to him, had

                  sneaked away from the place on seeing the Police.

</p><p id="p_4">                                  Learned         Counsel        for     the      petitioner          has
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">         Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.42377 of 2016 (2) dt.06-10-2016</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                   2/2</span>




                       submitted that since the petitioner's implication is based

                       merely on the statement of co-accused before the Police,

                       he should be allowed anticipatory bail. He has further

                       submitted that said Ramesh Kumar has been granted

                       regular bail by this Court vide order, dated 19.09.2016,

                       passed in Criminal Misc. No. 39391 of 2016.

</p><p id="p_5">                                       Considering the above submission and the facts

                       and circumstances in entirety I dispose of this application

                       with a liberty to the Police to ask the petitioner to appear

                       before it as and when required, by issuing notice, under

                       <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_6">Section 41-A</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973. If

                       the petitioner co-operates with the Police and appears

                       before it in compliance of the said notice, he shall not be

                       arrested.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                       This application stands disposed of accordingly.

                                       Let a copy of this order be communicated to the

                       Superintendent of Police, Vaishali.</p><pre id="pre_2">



                                                           (Chakradhari Sharan Singh, J.)

Prabhakar Anand/-c
