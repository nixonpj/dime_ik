<div class="doc_title">Naresh vs State Of Haryana on 13 March, 2013</div><pre id="pre_1">CRM NO. M-4324 OF 2013 (O&amp;M)




     IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA AT
                     CHANDIGARH


                         CRM NO. M-4324 OF 2013 (O&amp;M)
                         DECIDED ON : 13.03.2013


Naresh
                                           ...Petitioner

          versus


State of Haryana
                                           ...Respondent


CORAM : HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE K. C. PURI



Present : Mr. Raj Mohan Singh, Advocate,
          for the petitioner.

          Mr. Amandeep Singh, AAG, Haryana.


K. C. PURI, J. (ORAL)
</pre>

<p id="p_1">          The law was set in motion by recording the statement

of Mohan Lal, father of the prosecutrix, who has stated that

prosecutrix Puja aged about 13 years has gone outside yesterday

evening and she seems to have been abducted by Naresh Kumar,

who is also missing.
</p>
<p id="p_2">          Learned counsel for the petitioner has submitted that

prosecutrix was examined under <a href="/doc/497457/" id="a_1">Sections 164</a> and <a href="/doc/447673/" id="a_2">161</a> Cr.P.C

and she was also examined in Court but she has not levelled any

allegation of kidnapping and rape against the petitioner. It is
 CRM NO. M-4324 OF 2013 (O&amp;M)




further contended that Mohan Lal-father of the prosecutrix has

also been examined and he has also not supported the case of

prosecution. The petitioner is in custody since 04.10.2012.

</p><p id="p_3">           The said factual position has not been disputed by

learned State counsel.
</p>
<p id="p_4">           Since the material witnesses have not supported the

case of prosecution, I deem it a fit case to grant the concession

of regular bail. Consequently, the petition stands allowed.

Petitioner Naresh is ordered to be released on bail on his

furnishing bail bonds to the satisfaction of trial Court.



</p><pre id="pre_2">MARCH 13, 2013                                (K. C. PURI)
shalini                                         JUDGE
