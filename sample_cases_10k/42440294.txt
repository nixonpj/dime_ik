J U D G M E N T
</pre><p id="p_1">(Judgment of the Court was delivered by The Hon'ble Chief Justice)
	
	It really cannot be seriously disputed before us that in view of the Judgment of the Hon'ble Supreme Court in <a href="/doc/706635/" id="a_1">State of Punjab  vs.  Amar Nath Goyal and Others</a>, (2005) 6 SCC 754 as also the judgment in <a href="/doc/415684/" id="a_2">Government of A.P.  vs.  N.Subbarayudu and Others</a>, (2008) 14 SCC 702 with the latter referring to the earlier judgment, the issue raised in these matters is no more res integra.
</p>
<p id="p_2">	2.Learned counsel appearing for the respondent in W.A.No.1570 of 2007 states that before the Hon'ble Supreme Court, the State Government had taken a stand that they would follow the Central Government.  However, nothing can preclude the State Government from specifying a different date, even if it be so.
</p>
<p id="p_3">	3.As a consequence of the aforesaid, Writ Appeal No.1570 of 2007 has to be allowed and the impugned order of the learned Single Judge dated 08.02.2006 has to be set aside, while the writ petitions have to be dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_4">	4.Accordingly, the writ appeal is allowed and the writ petitions are dismissed. The parties will have to bear their own costs.  Consequently, M.P.No.2 of 2007 in W.A.No.1570 of 2007 stands closed.	
</p>
