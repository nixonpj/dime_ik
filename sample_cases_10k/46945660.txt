<pre id="pre_1">     IN THE COURT OF SHRI SUKHDEV SINGH: ADDITIONAL
     DISTRICT JUDGE: SHAHDARA DISTRICT: KARKARDOOMA
     COURTS: DELHI.

     H.M.A NO.60/13
     Unique I.D No. 02402C0083282013


     Shri Pankaj Girdhar
     S/o Shri Subhash Chand Girdhar,
     R/o C-136, Jhilmil Colony,
     Delhi                                      .......Petitioner No.1

     And

     Smt. Jyoti Mahendru
     W/o Shri Pankaj Girdhar,
     D/o Shri Ramesh Mahendru
     R/o 1/785-786, Rehman Building,
     Shahdara, Delhi-110032                     ..... Petitioner No.2

                  Date of Institution: 18.03.2013
                  Order reserved for:17.04.2013
                  Order passed on :17.04.2013

JUDGMENT
</pre><p id="p_1">           Petitioner No.1 Shri Pankaj Girdhar and Smt. Jyoti Mahendru

 Petitioner No.2      have filed the second motion     petition under

 Section 13-B-(2) of the <a href="/doc/590166/" id="a_1">Hindu Marriage Act</a>, 1955 ( hereinafter

 referred to as 'the Act') for dissolution of their marriage through a

 decree of divorce by mutual consent.
</p>
<p id="p_2"> 2         First motion petition under <a href="/doc/1439698/" id="a_2">Section 13</a> B (1) of the Act has

 already been disposed of vide order of dated 05.09.2012 passed

 by Shri Anurag Sain, Ld. ADJ, Karkardooma Courts, Delhi.

 3         Separate statements of the parties under <a href="/doc/1439698/" id="a_3">Section 13</a> B (2)

 of the Act has been recorded.
</p>
<p id="p_3"> 4         Petitioners have reiterated in their statements that their
 marriage was solemnised on 20.06.2010 at Delhi according to

Hindu rites and ceremonies; no child is born out of the wedlock;

Due to temperamental differences and attitudes, they have been

living separately since 20.06.2011. They have further stated in

their respective statements that they have not been able to live

together as husband and wife since the day of separation and

even during the statutory period of six months.

5     Petitioners have amicably settled their claims and disputes

regarding Istridhan, dowry, maintenance present, past and future

and permanent alimony before Delhi Mediation Centre, KKD,

Courts vide Ex. C-3. They have reiterated that there has been no

cohabitation between them since the day of separation and there

are no chances of their living together as husband and wife.

6     I have heard Ld. Counsels for the petitioners and have also

perused the material placed on record.
</p>
<p id="p_4">7    From the statements of the petitioners, it is evident that the

marriage of the petitioners has broken down irretrievably; they have

reached to a point of no return as they have been living separately

due to temperamental differences since 20.06.2011; and they have

mutually agreed to get their marriage dissolved through a decree of

divorce by mutual consent.
</p>
<p id="p_5">8      In view of the above, I am satisfied that the requirements of

petition under <a href="/doc/1439698/" id="a_4">Section 13</a> B (2) of the Act have been fulfilled.

Therefore, I allow the petition and dissolve the marriage between
 the   petitioners   viz;   Shri   Pankaj   Girdhar   and   Smt.   Jyoti

Mahendru under <a href="/doc/1439698/" id="a_5">Section 13</a> B (2) of the Act through a decree of

divorce by mutual consent. Decree sheet be prepared accordingly.

File be consigned to Record Room.
</p>
