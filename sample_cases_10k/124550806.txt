<div class="doc_title">Gaurishankar Pal vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 6 August, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                                           HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH
                                                     M.Cr.C.No.29931/2018
                         JABALPUR : 06/08/2018
                                   Shri S.K..Tiwari, Advocate for the applicant.
                                   Shri Subhod Kathal, G.A. for the respondent / State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">                                   Arguments heard. Case diary perused.
</p><p id="p_2">                                   This is second bail application under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of the Cr.P.C. filed
                         on behalf of applicant Gaurishankar Pal, who is in custody since 15.5.2017
                         in relation to Crime No.318/2016 registered at Police Station Amarpatan,
                         District Satna (MP) for the offence punishable under <a href="/doc/619940/" id="a_2">Sections 363</a>, <a href="/doc/796352/" id="a_3">366</a>,
                         <a href="/doc/1279834/" id="a_4">376</a> of the I.P.C. and section 3/4 of the POCSO Act. First application was
                         dismissed as withdrawn with liberty to file afresh after recording of the
                         statement of the prosecutrix.
</p><p id="p_3">                                   This application has been filed on the ground that now statement of
                         the prosecutrix has been recorded and she has not stated anything against
                         the applicant. There is no other evidence to connect him with the crime.
                         In such circumstances, prayer is made to enlarge the applicant on bail.
</p><p id="p_4">                                   On the other hand, learned Public Prosecutor appearing for the
                         State opposed the application and prayed for rejection of the same.
</p><p id="p_5">                                   Having considered the contentions of learned counsel for the
                         parties and on perusal of record, in view of this Court applicant's further
                         custody is not required during the whole trial; hence, this application is
                         allowed.      It is ordered that applicant - accused Gaurishankar Pal be
                         released on bail on his furnishing a personal bond for the sum of
                         Rs.50,000/- (Rs. Fifty Thousand only) with a solvent surety in the like
                         amount to the satisfaction of the trial court for securing his presence
                         before the said Court on all the dates of hearing fixed in this regard during
                         trial and for complying with the conditions enumerated in sub-section (3)
                         of <a href="/doc/848468/" id="a_5">Section 437</a> of Cr.P.C.
</p><p id="p_6">                                   Certified copy as per rules.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                                                                         (J.P.Gupta)
                                                                           Judge
                         HS
