<div class="doc_title">Sunil Ram vs State Of Bihar &amp; Anr on 23 May, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.23069 of 2016
                  Arising Out of PS.Case No. -317 Year- 2015 Thana -SITAMARHI COMPLAINT CASE District-
                                                          SITAMARHI
                 ======================================================
                 Sunil Ram son of Rameshwar Ram.
                                                                      .... .... Petitioner/s
                                                  Versus
                 State of Bihar &amp; Anr
                                                                 .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :  Mr. Ashok Kumar Jha
                 For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr. Binod Kr. 3(App)
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE DINESH KUMAR SINGH
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   23-05-2016                Heard learned counsels for the petitioner and the State.

</p><p id="p_2">                              The petitioner being the husband of the complainant is

                 apprehending arrest in a complaint case wherein process has been

                 directed to be issued after cognizance being taken for the offences

                 punishable under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_1">Section 498A</a> of the Indian Penal Code and

                 <a href="/doc/751411/" id="a_2">Section 3</a>/<a href="/doc/1023340/" id="a_3">4</a> of the Dowry Prohibition Act.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                              The basic accusation is of torture for non-fulfilment of

                 the dowry demand.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                              Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that the

                 petitioner admits his marriage with the complainant and is ready to

                 keep the complainant as wife with full dignity and honour. A

                 statement to that effect has been made in para 6 of the petition

                 which reads as follows:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">                                    "That the petitioner though the husband of the
                                     complainant and still he is ready to keep her with
                                     full dignity and respect."</blockquote><p id="p_5">
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.23069 of 2016 (2) dt.23-05-2016</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                           2/3</span>




</p><p id="p_6">                           It is further submitted that similar was the stand of the

             petitioner before the learned court below but it appears from

             paragraph no.6 of the impugned order that the petitioner is not

             ready to keep the complainant as wife which reads as follows:-

</p><blockquote id="blockquote_2">                            "The petitioner has stated that he is ready to keep his
                             wife, but from his conduct, it appears that he is not
                             serious to keep his wife."</blockquote><p id="p_7">


</p><p id="p_8">                           Considering the present stand of the petitioner, let the

             above named petitioner be released on provisional anticipatory

             bail for six months in the event of arrest or surrender before the

             learned Court below within a period of twelve weeks from today,

             on furnishing bail bond of `10,000/- (ten thousand) with two

             sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of learned

             SDJM, Pupari, Sitamarhi in connection with Complaint Case No.

             C/1 317 of 2015, subject to the conditions as laid down under

             <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_4">Section 438(2)</a> Cr.P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_9">                           Let the learned court below issue notice to the

             complainant for her appearance. On her appearance, the petitioner

             will take the complainant to keep her as wife with full dignity and

             honour.
</p>
<p id="p_10">                            The provisional bail of the petitioner will be confirmed

             by learned court below in three eventualities (i) if the matrimonial
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">         Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.23069 of 2016 (2) dt.23-05-2016</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                                                   3/3</span>




                     harmony is substantially restored (ii) if the complainant fails to

                     appear before the learned court below or (iii) if the complainant

                     gets reluctant to reconcile the issue.
</p>
<p id="p_11">
</p><p id="p_12">                                                                       (Dinesh Kumar Singh, J)
Amrendra/-
