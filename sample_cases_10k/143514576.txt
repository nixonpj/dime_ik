<div class="doc_title">Vijay Singh @ Vijay Yadav @ Usman vs The State Of Bihar on 15 February, 2010</div><pre id="pre_1">               IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                          Cr. Misc. No.5560 of 2010
      VIJAY SINGH @ VIJAY YADAV @ USMAN, S/O LATE MOHAN SINGH
                                    Versus
                           THE STATE OF BIHAR
                                     ****
</pre>
<p id="p_1">/2/    15.02.2010                    Heard both the sides.
</p><p id="p_2">                                     2. The petitioner seeks bail in a case instituted
                    under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_1">Sections 302</a> and <a href="/doc/386021/" id="a_2">201</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_3">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code and 27
                    of the <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_4">Arms Act</a>.
</p><p id="p_3">                                     3. It is only recently, on 17.08.2009, the
                    petitioner has been refused bail on merits. Unfortunately the
                    petitioner has renewed his prayer for bail even though there is
                    absolutely no merit in the case. Undoubtedly, the case is of the
                    year 1997 wherein the petitioner was named in the first
                    information report and he has been remanded to custody after
                    eleven years of the occurrence in November, 2008, this is itself
                    enough to refuse bail to the petitioner.
</p><p id="p_4">                                     4. The prayer for bail is rejected.
</p><p id="p_5">                                     5. The trial Court is directed to expedite the trial
                    in view of the fact that the case is of the year 1997 and the
                    petitioner is in custody. It is made clear that delay in trial is not
                    going to affect any further application for bail.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                          (Anjana Prakash, J.)
        S.A.
</p>
