                         JUDGMENT

<p id="p_1">    The petitioner had availed of two housing

loans; one in 2003 and the other in 2004. The

petitioner prays for some time to settle the entire

loan on the ground that she is retiring next month.

However, the learned counsel for the respondent

Bank stoutly opposes the prayer contending that the

petitioner while in service, had not paid any

amounts towards the instalments from 2009 to 2014.

Though the petitioner has a contention about other

expenditure,    that         cannot          absolve        her from

satisfying the loan account; at least, making some

payments during the said period.




</p><p id="p_2">    2. However, considering the confined prayer

made for instalments, it is directed that the

coercive proceedings shall be kept in abeyance on

condition of the petitioner settling the total dues

WP(C).21641/14
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                            2</span>

in eight equal monthly instalments along with the

regular payment of EMIs, on the respective due

dates. The respondent-Bank shall quantify the dues

as on 20.09.2014 and issue a statement of accounts

including charges, in accordance with which the

instalments shall be paid. The 1st instalment shall

be paid on or before 27.09.2014 and thereafter; the

due date of instalments falling on the 27th     of each

succeeding month. If default is committed in two

consecutive       instalments,    then     the recovery

proceedings shall be revived and continued from the

stage at which it has been kept in abeyance. On the

satisfaction of the dues as per the statement, the

Bank shall give a statement of the future interest

from    20.09.2014 and the    same shall be settled as

the 9th     instalment.
</p>
<p id="p_3">      The writ petition stands disposed of as above.

</p><p id="p_4">                                     Sd/-
