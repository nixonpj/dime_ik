<div class="doc_title">Manjit Singh vs State Of Punjab &amp; Others on 5 September, 2012</div><pre id="pre_1">            IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB &amp; HARYANA AT
                          CHANDIGARH

                                            CWP No.17332 of 2012 (O&amp;M)
                                            Date of Decision: 05.9.2012.

Manjit Singh                                             --Petitioner

                           Versus

State of Punjab &amp; others                                 --Respondents

CORAM:- HON'BLE MR.JUSTICE TEJINDER SINGH DHINDSA.

Present:-   Mr. S.P.S. Tinna, Advocate for the petitioner.

            ***
</pre>
<p id="p_1">TEJINDER SINGH DHINDSA.J

            The Department of School Education, State of Punjab had

issued an advertisement (Annexure P-1) inviting applications for

recruitment to the post of Education Service Providers. Out of the total

4000   posts    advertised,   650   posts   were    earmarked     for   Hindi

Masters/Mistresses.    The petitioner, who claims to be possessing the

qualification of B.A, M.A &amp; B.Ed applied in response to the advertisement

for the post of Hindi Master being eligible in all respects. The petitioner

secured 65.094 marks in the merit list and was accordingly, selected under

the general category. In pursuance to such selection even an appointment

letter dated 27.9.2008 (Annexure P-2) was issued in his favour.          It is

pleaded that on account of the serious illness of the father of the petitioner

which ultimately led to his unfortunate demise, the petitioner could not join

in pursuance to the appointment letter (Annexure P-2).

</p><p id="p_2">            The grievance of the petitioner is that inspite of the post of

Hindi Masters still being available, the respondent-authorities are not

permitting him to join on the post in question. Learned counsel for the

petitioner submits that even a representation dated 10.2.2012 (Annexure P-
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> CWP No.17332 of 2012 (O&amp;M)                           -2-</span>

</p><p id="p_3">4) already stands submitted to the respondent-authorities but the same has

not evoked any response.
</p>
<p id="p_4">             Without making any observations as regards the merits of the

claim of the petitioner, I deem it appropriate to dispose of the present writ

petition with a direction to the respondent-authorities to decide the

representation dated 10.2.2012 (Annexure P-4) strictly in accordance with

law and by passing a speaking order within a period of four months from the

date of receipt of a certified copy of this order.

</p><p id="p_5">             Petition disposed of.
</p>
<p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">                                          (TEJINDER SINGH DHINDSA)
                                                    JUDGE

05.9.2012.
