<div class="doc_title">High Court Of Uttarakhand At ... vs State Of Uttarakhand &amp; Others on 27 May, 2013</div><pre id="pre_1">     HIGH COURT OF UTTARAKHAND AT NAINITAL
              Writ Petition No.1140 of 2011 (S/S)
      Rajesh Kumar Yadav
      S/o Sri Master Singh                        ... Petitioner
                                 Vs.
      State of Uttarakhand &amp; others               ... Respondents

Hon'ble Sudhanshu Dhulia, J.
</pre><p id="p_1">1.       Heard Mr. Rajendra Dobhal, Senior Advocate assisted
by Mr. Vipul Sharma, Advocate for the petitioner, Mr. Subhash
Upadhyaya, Standing Counsel for the respondents and Mr.
Bhagwat Mehra, Advocate for the caveator.
</p><p id="p_2">2.       The petitioner is an Assistant Teacher in L.T. Grade in
Government      Inter-College,    Chandpur,     Kotabagh,       District
Nainital. His services has been terminated vide order dated
02.07.2011. By means of this writ petition, the petitioner has
challenged his termination.            The impugned order dated
02.07.2011 states that there are some discrepancies in the
testimonials submitted by the petitioner. It is alleged that the
petitioner has filed a forged testimonial on which basis he got the
appointment on the post of Assistant Teacher.
</p><p id="p_3">3.       The petitioner denies the said charges and contends that
the impugned order has been passed without giving an
opportunity of hearing to the petitioner, and since it was passed
without giving an opportunity of hearing to the petitioner, it is in
violation of the principles of natural justice and fair play.
</p>
<p id="p_4">4.       The impugned order has been passed in violation of the
provisions of the Uttarakhand Government Servant (Discipline
and Appeal) Rules, 2003 (hereinafter referred to as the Rules of
2003) as no disciplinary proceedings were initiated against the
petitioner and no chargesheet was served upon him. In fact,
there is a communication dated 04.03.2013 written by the
Director, Secondary Education, Dehradun to the Secretary,
School Education, Dehradun (Annexure-6 to the Supplementary
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                    -2-</span>




Affidavit filed by the petitioner), in which it was stated that the
impugned order is in violation of the Rules of 2003 as before
passing the impugned order the petitioner was not given an
opportunity of hearing.
</p><p id="p_5">5.         On there pointed assertions, there is no plausible
explanation from the State Government, in fact, Mr. Subhash
Upadhyaya, Standing Counsel has candidly expressed that no
opportunity of hearing appears to have been given to the
petitioner, prior to the passing of the impugned order dated
02.07.2011.
</p><p id="p_6">6.         The writ petition succeeds and is hereby allowed. The
impugned order dated 02.07.2011 passed by respondent no.2 is
set aside.
</p><p id="p_7">7.         Having said this, since there is a serious charge against
the petitioner that he has submitted forged testimonial in order
to procure the employment, the respondent authority is directed
to proceed against the petitioner in accordance with law.
Meaning thereby, the respondent authority shall conduct a
proper enquiry and the charges must be served upon the
petitioner in order to defend himself and he must be given an
opportunity of hearing. Till then, the petitioner is liable to be
treated under suspension w.e.f. 20.11.2010 and will be paid the
subsistence allowance in accordance with rules. In case, the
previous subsistence allowance has not been paid so far, the
arrears of the same shall be paid to the petitioner within one
month from the date of production of a certified copy of this
order.     However, it is made clear that the petitioner shall
cooperate in the proceedings.
</p>
<p id="p_8">8.         No order as to costs.</p><pre id="pre_2">

                                         (Sudhanshu Dhulia, J.)
Dated    27th   May, 2013
Rawat
