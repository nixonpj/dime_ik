                        ORDER

<p id="p_1">      Petitioner, a conductor in the respondent-Road

Transport Corporation when dismissed from service by

order dt. 30.8.2008 for proved act of misconduct,

sought conciliation under the <a href="/doc/500379/" id="a_1">Industrial Disputes Act</a>,

1947, for short '<a href="/doc/500379/" id="a_2">ID Act</a>', resulting in an order of the

State Government referring the dispute for adjudication

before the Presiding Officer, Labour Court, Hubli

registered as Ref.No.1/2010.   Reference when rejected

by award dt. 11.7.2011 Annexure-F has resulted in this

petition.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">     2. There is no dispute that I.D. No. 148/2005

pending before the Industrial Tribunal at Bangalore, is

an industrial dispute over charter of demands, raised by

the KSRTC staff and Workers' Federation, in which the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                             3</span>




petitioner is a concerned workman.      The Corporation

having not sought approval of the termination from

service of the said petitioner, by filing an Application

under <a href="/doc/1774629/" id="a_3">Section 33</a> (2) (b) of the <a href="/doc/500379/" id="a_4">I.D. Act</a>, it cannot but be

said that the order of termination from service is void

and inoperative, in the light of the authoritative

pronouncement of a Five Judge Bench decision in

<a href="/doc/34445/" id="a_5">Jaipur Zila Sahakari Bhoomi Vikas Bank Limited

vs. Ram Gopal Sharma1</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_4">
</p><p id="p_5">         3. Learned counsel for petitioner - workman

submits that, the petitioner would give up full back

wages, if reinstated into service with consequential

benefits and continuity of service within a time frame.


</p><p id="p_6">          In that view of the matter, the petition is

accordingly allowed.    The award is modified directing

the respondent - Corporation to reinstate the petitioner-

workman with continuity of service and consequential

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">1</span>
    AIR 2002 SC 643.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                         4</span><p id="p_7">




benefits, however without back wages on or before

25.7.2012.
</p>
<p id="p_8">

</p><p id="p_9">                              SD/
