<div class="doc_title">Sharda Devi &amp; Ors vs The State Of Bihar on 23 September, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.41812 of 2016
                       Arising Out of PS.Case No. -89 Year- 2016 Thana -ARIYARI District- SEKHPURA
                 ======================================================
                 1. Sharda Devi W/O. Sitaram Yadav
                 2. Tushan Yadav Son of Sitaram Yadav , both resident of village May
                    Amarpur, P.S. Ariayari, District Sheikhpura.
                 3. Leela Devi wife of Adhik Yadav
                 4. Adhik Yadav son of Upendra Yadav both resident of village Dalhar,
                    P.S. Ariyari, District Sheikhpura.
                                                                      .... .... Petitioner/s
                                                   Versus
                 The State of Bihar
                                                                 .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s        :   Mr. Ajit Kumar
                 For the Opposite Party/s    : Mr. Satyavarat Verma, A.P.P.
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE NAVANITI PRASAD
                 SINGH
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   23-09-2016                        Heard learned counsel for the petitioners and

                 learned A.P.P. for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                      The petitioners            apprehend arrest in a case

                 registered for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/653797/" id="a_1">sections 304-B</a> &amp;

                 <a href="/doc/386021/" id="a_2">201</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_3">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                      The allegation is that the petitioners had been

                 instrumental in killing the daughter-in-law for non fulfillment of

                 the demand of dowry. How death took place has not been stated

                 in the bail petition. The death has taken place within seven years

                 of the marriage in the house of the petitioners. Therefore, it is for

                 the petitioners to explain the cause of death.

</p><p id="p_4">                                      In the facts and circumstances of the case, I am
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">          Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.41812 of 2016 (2) dt.23-09-2016</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                    2/2</span>




                      not inclined to grant the privilege of anticipatory bail to the

                      petitioners in connection with Ariyari P.S. Case No. 89 of 2016

                      pending in the court of the learned Sub-divisional Judicial

                      Magistrate, Sheikhpura. This application for anticipatory bail is,

                      accordingly, dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                      However, the petitioners may surrender in the court

                      below within four weeks and pray for regular bail which will be

                      considered and disposed of on its own merit in accordance with

                      law without being prejudiced by this order. The court below may

                      take into account the fact that petitioner no. 1 Sharda Devi is an

                      old lady.
</p>
<p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">                                                (Navaniti Prasad Singh, J)
Amin/-
</p>
