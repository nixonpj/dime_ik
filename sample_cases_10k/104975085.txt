<div class="doc_title">Mohanlal vs Dulichand on 9 September, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                                   -1-

                      Writ Petition No. 4970/14
9/9/2015
      Shri J.B. Dave learned counsel for petitioners.
      Shri S. Bhargava, learned counsel for respondent.</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Heard finally with consent.
</p><p id="p_2">      This writ petition under <a href="/doc/1331149/" id="a_1">Article 227</a> of the Constitution of India
has been filed by the plaintiffs challenging the order of trial court
dated 4/4/14 rejecting the petitioner's application under Order 14
Rule 5 CPC for framing the additional issue.
</p><p id="p_3">      Having heard the learned counsel for parties and on perusal of
the record, it is noticed that the petitioners have filed the suit for
declaration of easement as also the injunction on the plea that the
petitioners are owner of the suit land and respondent has no right of
passage from the suit land but he has initiated proceedings before
the Revenue court for opening of the passage from the suit land.
Trial court on 26/6/13 has framed four issues and thereafter the
petitioners had filed application for framing additional four issues
which has been rejected by the trial court. Trial court while rejecting
the application has noted that the additional issues can be framed in
future if need so arises but while rejecting the application the trial
court has failed to appreciate that in the suit the petitioners had
prayed for relief of injunction therefore, the issue was required to be
framed in respect of entitlement of petitioners for the relief of
injunction but no such issue has been framed. Hence the trial court is
directed to frame the additional issue in respect of entitlement of the
petitioners to get the relief of injunction.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                        -2-</span>

<p id="p_4">      The writ petition is accordingly disposed of.
      C.C. as per rules.
</p><p id="p_5">                               (PRAKASH SHRIVASTAVA)
                                       Judge
BDJ
