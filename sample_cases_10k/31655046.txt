<p id="p_2">
                                      Re : C.A.N. 3132 of 2015

                                          This application has been filed for condoning the
                                delay in preferring the appeal.
</p><p id="p_3">                                          Having heard the learned Advocate representing the
                                respective parties and considering the averments made in
                                this application, we are satisfied that sufficient reasons
                                have been furnished and adequate grounds have been
                                mentioned for condoning the delay in preferring the appeal.
</p><p id="p_4">                                          Therefore, delay in preferring the appeal is condoned.
                                          Let the appeal be registered, if the same is otherwise
                                in form.
</p><p id="p_5">                                          This application, therefore, stands allowed.
                    There will be no order as to costs.
</p>
<p id="p_6">


</p><p id="p_7">                                      Re : C.A.N. 12165 of 2014

                    Having heard the learned Advocate representing the respective parties and also

           considering the facts and circumstances of this case, we are of the opinion that the learned

           single Judge upon considering the submissions of the learned Advocate representing the

           respective parties and also going through the relevant records refused to grant any relief to the

           appellant/petitioner herein since the penalty ultimately imposed on the appellant/petitioner is

           stoppage of one increment which cannot be said to be disproportionate to the charges levelled

           and established against the appellant/petitioner herein.

</p><p id="p_8">                    We do not find any error and/or infirmity in the aforesaid decision of the learned

           single Judge. Therefore, we refuse to interfere with the impugned order under appeal passed

           by the learned single Judge.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                  2</span><p id="p_9">


         Accordingly, we dismiss both the application as well as appeal upon treating the said

appeal as on day's list.
</p>
<p id="p_10">         In the facts of the present case, there will be no order as to costs.



</p><p id="p_11">                                               ( Pranab Kumar Chattopadhyay, J.)


                                                         (Ishan Chandra Das, J. )
