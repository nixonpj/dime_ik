<div class="doc_title">Nathuni Prasad vs The State Of Bihar on 12 August, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                   IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                 Criminal Miscellaneous No.31189 of 2015
                         Arising Out of PS.Case No. -47 Year- 2014 Thana -MURAR District- BUXAR
                 ======================================================
                 Nathuni Prasad, S/o Chotak Prasad @ Sri Chotak Prasad, Resident of
                 village - Nagwa, P.S.- Simri, District - Buxar

                                                                                .... ....   Petitioner/s
                                                       Versus
                 The State of Bihar

                                                              .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     : Mr. Sumeet Kumar Singh, Advocate
                 For the Opposite Party/s : Mr. Nawal Kishore Pd.(APP)
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE JUSTICE SMT. ANJANA MISHRA
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   12-08-2015              A supplementary affidavit has been filed today,

                 indicating the criminal antecedents of the petitioner.

</p><p id="p_2">                            Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and the

                 learned counsel for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                            The petitioner is apprehending his arrest in

                 connection with Murar P.S. Case No.47 of 2014 for allegedly

                 having committed the offence under <a href="/doc/1131784/" id="a_1">Sections 408</a>, <a href="/doc/1326844/" id="a_2">409</a>, <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_3">420</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_4">34</a>

                 of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                            Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that only

                 allegation against this petitioner is that the purchases of solar

                 lights were made not from Breda/Beltron; rather it was

                 purchased from outside the agency. Learned counsel submits

                 that at the relevant point of time, there was no such embargo
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">     Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.31189 of 2015 (2) dt.12-08-2015</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                               2/2</span>




                 and the direction was issued only later.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                               Considering the aforementioned submissions and

                 that, in fact, the letter for purchase of solar lights from

                 Breda/Beltron was issued much after the said purchases are

                 already made, let the petitioner, above named, in the event of

                 his arrest or surrender before the court below within a period of

                 three weeks from the date of receipt/production of a copy of

                 this order, be released on bail on furnishing bail bond of

                 Rs.10,000/- (Ten thousand) with two sureties of the like

                 amount each to the satisfaction of the learned Chief Judicial

                 Magistrate, Buxar, in connection with Murar P.S. Case No.47

                 of 2014, subject to the conditions as laid down under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_5">Section

                 438(2)</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure.</p><pre id="pre_2">




                                                (Anjana Mishra, J)
    PNM

U      T
