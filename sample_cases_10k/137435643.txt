                 1. The State Of Bihar
                 2. Navin Kumar Dhandhania M/S Meghna Handlooms At Tansi Kunj ,
                    Dr. R.P. Road , P.S Kotwali, Dist-Bhagalpur

                                                                 .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :   Mr. Anil Chandra, Adv.
                 For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr. Anil Kumar Singh No.1, APP
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE BIRENDRA PRASAD VERMA
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">4   24-01-2013             By the impugned order dated 08.03.2010 passed in
                 Complaint Case No. 1519 of 2008 by the learned Judicial
                 Magistrate, 1st Class, Bhagalpur, cognizance has been taken for
                 offence under <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_1">Section 406</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p><p id="p_2">                           By taking the plea of defence, the petitioners have
                 approached this Court under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_2">Section 482</a> Cr. P. C. with a prayer
                 for quashing the order taking cognizance.
</p><p id="p_3">                           It is well settled that at the stage of taking cognizance,
                 defence of accused persons is not required to be looked into. At
                 that stage, the learned Magistrate is required to find out only a
                 prima facie case on the basis of the materials produced by the
                 prosecution. In the present case the petition of complaint was filed
                 on behalf of the opposite party no. 2, in which as many as three
                 witnesses were examined during the course of enquiry under
                 <a href="/doc/1149595/" id="a_3">Section 202</a> Cr. P.C., whereafter the impugned order dated
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> 2           Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.28794 of 2011 (4) dt.24-01-2013</span>


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                   2/2</span>




                     08.03.2010 was passed taking cognizance of the offence.
</p><p id="p_4">                                   This Court does not find any legal infirmity warranting
                     any interference by this Court at this stage. In the result, the
                     application has to fail and is dismissed. However, the petitioners
                     shall be at liberty to take all the pleas, which have been taken in
                     the present proceeding, at an appropriate stage of trial.
</p>


<p id="p_5">                                             (Birendra Prasad Verma, J)


BTiwary/-
</p>
