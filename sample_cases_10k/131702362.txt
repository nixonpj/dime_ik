<p id="p_1">                The amendments consequential to the Order dated 4th April, 2012 be carried out within a week.

                The records reveal that the Special Messenger cost of Rs.800/- for bringing the Lower Court Records have

       been put in on 18th June, 2012 under Serial No. D-213. Although over a year and two months have elapsed, the Lower

       Court Records have not yet been transmitted to this Court. The order sheet reveals that the Lower Court Records were

       called for by Special Messenger vide Memo. No. 1957-F dated 7th July, 2012.

</p><p id="p_2">                The learned Registrar General is directed to ensure that the Lower Court Records are transmitted to this Court

       by special messenger forthwith and in any case within two weeks from date.

</p><p id="p_3">                Immediately on arrival of the Lower Court Records, notice of arrival of Lower Court Records shall be issued

       to the appellant to enable the appellant to prepare and file requisite number of informal paper books as directed by the

       earlier Division Bench of this Court by its Order dated 4th April, 2012.

</p><p id="p_4">                The informal paper books shall be prepared within four weeks from the date of service of notice of arrival of

       Lower Court Records as directed by the Order dated 4th April, 2012.
</p>

</p><p id="p_6">                                         ( Indira Banerjee, J. )
