<div class="doc_title">Nawab vs State Of U.P. on 26 September, 2019</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: Siddharth</div>

<pre id="pre_1">




HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT ALLAHABAD
 
 

?Court No. - 78
 

 
Case :- CRIMINAL MISC. BAIL APPLICATION No. - 36747 of 2019
 

 
Applicant :- Nawab
 
Opposite Party :- State Of U.P.
 
Counsel for Applicant :- Dhiraj Kumar Pandey
 
Counsel for Opposite Party :- G.A.
 

 
Hon'ble Siddharth,J.
</pre> 
<p id="p_1">Heard learned counsel for the applicant and learned A.G.A. for the State. 
</p> 
<p id="p_2">Submission is that the victim has not supported the allegation made in the first information report in her statement under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section 164</a> Cr.P.C. She has admitted marrying co-accused, Naushad on 17.04.2019 and has claimed herself to be major. No medical examination has taken place. She is above 17 years. Given margin of two years on higher side she can be presumed to be major. The applicant is in jail since 29.05.2019. 
</p> 
<p id="p_3">On the other hand learned AGA has opposed the prayer for bail. 
</p> 
<p id="p_4">Keeping in view the nature of the offence, evidence on record regarding complicity of the accused, submissions of the learned counsel for the parties noted herein above, larger mandate of the <a href="/doc/1199182/" id="a_2">Article 21</a> of the Constitution of India and the dictum of Apex Court in the case of Dataram Singh Vs. State of U.P. and another reported in (2018)3 SCC 22 and without expressing any opinion on the merits of the case, the Court is of the view that the applicant has made out a case for bail. The bail application is allowed. 
</p> 
<p id="p_5">Let the applicant, Nawab, involved in Case Crime No.94 of 2019, under <a href="/doc/619940/" id="a_3">Sections 363</a>, <a href="/doc/796352/" id="a_4">366</a> IPC, Police Station Nagal, District- Saharanpur be released on bail on his furnishing a personal bond and two sureties each in the like amount to the satisfaction of the court concerned subject to following conditions. Further, before issuing the release order, the sureties be verified. 
</p> 
<p id="p_6">1. The applicant will not tamper with the evidence during the trial. 
</p> 
<p id="p_7">2. The applicant will not pressurize/ intimidate the prosecution witness. 
</p> 
<p id="p_8">3. The applicant will appear before the trial court on the date fixed, unless personal presence is exempted. 
</p> 
<p id="p_9">4. The applicant shall not commit an offence similar to the offence of which they are accused, or suspected of the commission of which they are suspected. 
</p> 
<p id="p_10">5. The applicant shall not directly or indirectly make any inducement, threat or promise to any person acquainted with the facts of the case so as to dissuade them from disclosing such facts to the Court or to any police officer or tamper with the evidence. 
</p> 
<p id="p_11">In case of breach of any of the above conditions, the complainant is free to move an application for cancellation of bail before this court.
</p> 
<p id="p_12">Order Date :- 26.9.2019
 
SS
