<div class="doc_title">Anokhi Lal vs The Land Acuquisition And ... on 23 March, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">               WRIT PETITION No.8207/2014                       1




23/3/2015

      Shri Shashank Upadhyay, learned Counsel for the
petitioner.
      Heard on the question of admission.
      Issue    notices     of   this   writ   petition   to   the
respondents.</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Shri Vaibhav Tiwari, learned Panel Lawyer appeared
and waives notice on behalf of the respondents-State.
</p><p id="p_2">      Since the issue is covered by the decision rendered
by the Apex Court in the case of Khazan Singh (dead)
by L.R.s vs. Union of India, [(2002) 2 SCC 242] , the
matter is heard finally.
</p><p id="p_3">      The reference was made under <a href="/doc/1517117/" id="a_1">Section 18</a> of the
Land Acquisition Act on an application made by the
petitioner before the Collector, Land Acquisition, to the
District Court,   where the same was registered               as
Reference Case No.306/2009. Since nobody could appear
to adduce evidence, without recording any evidence, the
reference was rejected by the Court below affirming the
award passed by the Land Acquisition Officer.
</p><p id="p_4">      After examining the order passed by learned
Reference Court, it seems that the Reference Court was
required to answer the reference and not to dismiss the
same mechanically, as has been done vide order
impugned.
</p><p id="p_5">      In view of the aforesaid and in view of the law laid-
down by the Apex Court in case of Khazan Singh
(supra), wherein it has categorically been held that the
reference is required to be answered and not to be
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                     WRIT PETITION No.8207/2014               2</span>




     dismissed in default, the writ petition is allowed.   The
     order dated 5/10/2010 is hereby set aside. The reference
     case is restored to its original number and remitted back
     to the Reference Court to decide the same in accordance
     to law. Needless to say, in case the petitioner makes an
     appropriate application for grant of permission to adduce
     the evidence, the Reference Court will decide the same in
     accordance to law.
</p><p id="p_6">          With the aforesaid, the writ petition stands finally
     disposed of.
</p><p id="p_7">          Certified copy as per rules.
</p>

<p id="p_8">                                          (K.K. Trivedi)
                                              Judge
