<div class="doc_title">Pramod Kumar Ojha &amp; Ors vs State Of Bihar on 19 January, 2010</div><pre id="pre_1">          IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                      Cr.Misc. No.751 of 2010
          1. PRAMOD KUMAR OJHA, S/o Laxman Ojha.
          2. Manoj Sharma, S/o Ram Keshwar Sharma.
          3. Rama Shankar Dubey, S/o Ramashish Dubey.
                               Versus
                       THE STATE OF BIHAR
                             -----------
</pre>
<p id="p_1">02.   19.01.2010           Heard learned counsel for the petitioners and

                   the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                           The petitioners seek bail in a case instituted

                   for the offences under <a href="/doc/94452/" id="a_1">Sections 414</a>, <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_2">420</a>, <a href="/doc/1985627/" id="a_3">467</a>, <a href="/doc/556166/" id="a_4">468</a>,

                   <a href="/doc/756306/" id="a_5">474</a>, <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_6">120B</a> and <a href="/doc/386021/" id="a_7">201</a> of the Indian Penal Code and

                   <a href="/doc/1961417/" id="a_8">Sections 25</a> (1-B) A, 26 and 35 of the <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_9">Arms Act</a>.

</p><p id="p_3">                           Taking into consideration the fact that the

                   petitioners are in custody since 29.09.2009 and the

                   entire case is based on documents and the charge

                   sheet has already been submitted and they are not

                   accused in any case of similar nature, let the

                   petitioners above named be released on bail on

                   furnishing bail bonds of Rs. 5,000/- (Five Thousand)

                   each with two sureties of the like amount each to the

                   satisfaction of Chief Judicial Magistrate, Rohtas in

                   connection with Dehri P.S. Case No. 466 of 2009

                   subject to the following conditions:- (i) That one of the

                   bailors will be a close relative of the petitioners who
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                            -2-</span>




           will give an affidavit giving genealogy as to how he is

           related with the petitioners. (ii) That the bailor shall

           also state on affidavit that he will inform the court

           concerned if the petitioners are implicated in any

           other case of similar nature after their release in the

           present case and thereafter the court below will be at

           liberty to initiate the proceeding for cancellation of

           bail on the ground of misuse. (iii) That the petitioners

           will give an undertaking that they will receive the

           police papers on the given date and be present on

           date fixed for charge and if they fail to do so on two

           given dates and delays the trial in any manner, their

           bail will be liable to be cancelled for reasons of

           misuse.
</p>
<p id="p_4">


</p><p id="p_5">                                      (Anjana Prakash, J.)
Vikash/-
</p>
