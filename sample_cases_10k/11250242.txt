<div class="doc_title">Dharamnath Singh vs The State Of West Bengal And Others on 19 March, 2014</div><div class="doc_author">Author: Sanjib Banerjee</div>

<pre id="pre_1">                                        1


Serial No. 68
March 19, 2014.
     md                  W.P. No. 8269 (W) of 2014

                            Dharamnath Singh
                               -versus-
                       The State of West Bengal and others

                     Mr. Kallol Basu
                     Mr. Arnab Sinha
                                   .. for the petitioner

                     Mr. Rajarshi Dutta
                     Mr. Sayantan Bose
                     Mr. P.P. Naskar

                           .. for the respondent nos. 2 to 4</pre>
<p id="p_1">                              (power not supplied)

                     Mr. Hiranmay Bhattacharya
                     Mr. Sounak Bhattacharya
                                 .. for the respondent no. 5
                                   (power not supplied)

                     Mr. Mintu Kr. Goswami
                     Mr. Tapas Kr. Mandal

                              .. for the State

                     Both the Asansol Durgapur Development Authority and

             the Asansol Municipal Corporation are represented.

</p><p id="p_2">                     The grievance of the petitioner is that a part of the

             petitioner's land being R.S. plot No. 496 recorded in R.S. Khatian

             No. 82 of mouza Kalipahari in the Asansol area has been sought to

             be taken over in course of widening or construction of a road

             without such land being acquired by any procedure established by

             law.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                            2</span><p id="p_3">


         The grievance was noticed in the order dated March 12,

2014 when the municipal corporation was not represented.                The

municipal corporation says that it has not made any attempt to

either acquire the land or otherwise use the land for the purpose of

widening or construction of any road or for any other purpose

whatsoever. It is submitted on behalf of the ADDA that upon the

petitioner complaining to the relevant Executive Engineer, ADDA,

which is only the implementing agency under the municipal

corporation, reported the matter to the municipal corporation and

has not undertaken any work on the petitioner's land.

</p><p id="p_4">         W.P. No.8269 (W) of 2014 is disposed of by restraining

both the Asansol Durgapur Development Authority and the Asansol

Municipal   Corporation   from      interfering   with   the   petitioner's

possession or use of the land at R.S. plot No. 496 recorded in R.S.

Khatian No. 82 of mouza Kalipahari in the Asansol area in any

manner whatsoever except by procedure established by law and

upon affording the petitioner due opportunity to contest the same.

</p><p id="p_5">         In the event there is any further encroachment or

attempted   encroachment       by   the   respondent     authorities,   the

petitioner will be entitled to cite this order before the District

Magistrate, Burdwan for the District Magistrate to take appropriate

steps in accordance with law.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                              3</span><p id="p_6">


</p><p id="p_7">          In the event any building or construction material may

have been accidentally or otherwise stored at the petitioner's land,

ADDA should remove the same within a week from date.

</p><p id="p_8">          There will be no order as to costs.
</p>
<p id="p_9">          Certified website copies of this order, if applied for, be

urgently made available to the parties, subject to compliance with

all requisite formalities.
</p>
<p id="p_10">


</p><p id="p_11">                                 (Sanjib Banerjee, J.)
