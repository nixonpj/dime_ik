<div class="doc_title">Tarani Kanta Barman vs The State Of West Bengal &amp; Ors on 11 April, 2019</div><p id="p_1">    11.4.2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">Court No. 19</span>
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2"> Item No. 47</span>
     Ashoke
W.P. 7477(W) of 2018


Tarani Kanta Barman
</p><p id="p_2">-versus-
</p><p id="p_3">The State of West Bengal &amp; Ors.
</p>

<p id="p_4">Mr. Debabrata Mondal,
Ms. Sreetama Neogi,
Ms. Nibedita Barui.
</p><p id="p_5">            ... For the Petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_6">Mr. Subroto Ghosh,
Mr. Arun Saha.
</p><p id="p_7">            ... For the State.
</p>
<p id="p_8">The petitioner is aggrieved by an order dated June 5, 2017 by which the
petitioner's prayer for higher scale of pay on enhancement of his qualification
from B.Sc. to M.Sc. degree in Physics was rejected due to violation of G. O. No.
593-SE(B)/ES/ BIM-98/2007 dated November 11, 2007.
</p>
<p id="p_9">I find that the order of the District Inspector of Schools (S.E.), Cooch Behar,
was passed without any reason, although the said authority was directed by a
learned Single Judge of this Court to take a decision with respect to the
eligibility of the petitioner to get higher scale of pay in terms of the ROPA
Rules. He was also directed that a reasoned order should be passed and
communicated to the petitioner. Despite such direction and ignoring the true
purport and meaning of the order passed by another learned Single Judge of this
Court dated March 21, 2017; the District Inspector of Schools (S.E.), Cooch
Behar, mechanically rejected the claim of the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_10">The said order of the District Inspector of Schools (S.E.), Cooch Behar, cannot
be sustained.
</p>
<p id="p_11">Hence, the order dated June 5, 2017 passed by the District Inspector of Schools
(S.E.), Cooch Behar, being annexure 'P-7' to this writ petition at page 55 is
quashed and set aside.
</p>
<p id="p_12">The District Inspector of Schools (S.E.), Cooch Behar, is directed to reconsider
the prayer of the petitioner for higher scale of pay on obtaining the degree of
M.Sc. in Physics in accordance with law upon hearing the petitioner and all
concerned parties, including the school authorities. The order should be a
reasoned one and should be communicated to all the parties. The entire exercise
should be completed within a period of six weeks from the date of communication
of this order.
</p>
<p id="p_13">The writ petition is disposed of.
</p>
<p id="p_14">There will be no order as to costs.
</p>
<p id="p_15">Urgent photostat certified copy of this order, if applied for, be given to the
parties as expeditiously as possible.
</p><p id="p_16">     (Shampa Sarkar, J.)
