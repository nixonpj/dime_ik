<p id="p_1">4.   17.01.2011              1.      Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and

                   learned counsel for the respondents Life Insurance Corporation of

                   India and its authorities.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                             2.      This writ petition has been filed by the petitioner

                   to settle and pay insurance claim with interest at the rate of 18% per

                   annum to the petitioner, who is nominee with respect to Life

                   Insurance Policy No. 512094342 issued by the Branch Manager,

                   Life Insurance Corporation of India, Branch No. 3, Patna

                   (Respondent no. 3) commencing from 28.11.2001 (Annexure-1) in

                   the name of the petitioner's husband Mahendra Prasad, who

                   subsequently died on 19.02.2002 due to heart failure.

</p><p id="p_3">                             3.      Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that

                   although she had approached the authority in the year 2002 and the

                   claim of the petitioner with regard to the other three Life Insurance
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                              2</span>




        Policies of Late Mahendra Prasad had been settled, but nothing has

        been done with regard to the instant policy.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                  4.     Learned counsel for the respondents vehemently

        opposes the contentions of learned counsel for the petitioner and

        submits that the petitioner has neither given the details of ailments

        nor the petitioner or the hospital concerned had provided the details

        as required, hence, the matter could not be decided by the

        authorities.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                  5.     Considering the aforesaid facts and circumstances

        of the case as well as the materials on record, this writ petition is

        disposed of with a liberty to the petitioner to approach to the

        Manager (Claims) Life Insurance Corporation, Divisional Office,

        Patna. However, if the petitioner files such an application before the

        said authority within 15 days from today along with the documents

        available with the petitioner and the facts of the deceased in detail

        which are available to the petitioner, the said authority shall decide

        the same by a speaking order within three months thereafter holding

        an appropriate enquiry in that regard. If the petitioner is aggrieved

        by any order of the authority, she will be at liberty to approach the

        appropriate Forum for redressal of her grievances.


</p><p id="p_6">                                                       (S.N.Hussain,J.)
Sujit
