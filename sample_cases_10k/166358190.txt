                              CRIMINAL MISCELLANEOUS No.20236 of 2019
                  Arising Out of PS. Case No.-1408 Year-2014 Thana- COMPLAINT CASE District- Araria
                 ======================================================
                 Rajesh Mukhiya Son of Sukdeo Mukhiya Resident of Village - Khojri, Ward
                 No. 06 Milik, P.S.- Bhargama,Dist.- Araria

                                                                                 ... ... Petitioner/s
                                                      Versus
           1.    The State Of Bihar
           2.    Jagmaya Devi Wife of Rajesh Mukhiya Resident of Village - Khojri, Ward
                 No. 06 Milik, P.S.- Bhargama,Dist.- Araria

                                                        ... ... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :      Mr.Ramesh Kumar Singh
                 For the Opposite Party/s :      Mr.Rana Randhir Singh
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE SUDHIR SINGH
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   02-04-2019                    Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned

                   APP for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                         The petitioner is apprehending his arrest in a case

                   registered under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_1">Section 498A</a> of the Indian Penal Code.

</p><p id="p_3">                         Allegation against the petitioner is of committing torture

                   upon the victim due to non-fulfilment of demand of dowry.

</p><p id="p_4">                         It has been submitted on behalf of the petitioner that there

                   is no allegation of tampering of witnesses alleged against the

                   petitioner. The petitioner has falsely been implicated in the

                   present case due to petty family dispute. On earlier occasion

                   also, the victim had instituted a case for similar offence against

                   the petitioner and his family members. The case is triable by the
              Patna High Court CR. MISC. No.20236 of 2019(2) dt.02-04-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                         2/2</span>




                       Magistrate. The petitioner has relied upon the judgment of this

                       Court in the case of Md. Naimul Haque Ansari @ Naimul

                       Haque Ansari &amp; Ors. Vs. The State of Bihar, reported in 2006(3)

                       PLJR 182.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                             On behalf of the State, it is submitted that the petitioner is

                       named in the complaint case.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                    Considering the aforesaid facts and circumstances, let

                       the petitioner, above named, in the event of arrest/surrender

                       before the learned court below within a period of six weeks

                       from today, be released on anticipatory bail on furnishing bail

                       bonds of Rs.10,000/- (Ten thousand) with two sureties of the

                       like amount each to the satisfaction of learned Sub Divisional

                       Judicial Magistrate, Araria in connection with Complaint case

                       No.1408C of 2014, subject to the conditions as laid down under

                       <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_2">Section 438(2)</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure.



</p><p id="p_7">                                                    (Sudhir Singh, J)
Narendra/-
</p>
