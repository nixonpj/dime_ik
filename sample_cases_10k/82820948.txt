<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">2014</span>
                                           CRM No.16366 of 2013
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">5</span>
                                         In the High Court at Calcutta
       .                             Criminal Miscellaneous Jurisdiction
                                              Appellate Side


                         In Re: Samirul Sk. @ Islam. ...petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                         Re An application under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> of the Code of Criminal
           Procedure filed on December 17, 2013 in connection with Raghunathganj
           Police Station    Case No.45 of 2007 dated 12.7.2007 under <a href="/doc/325366/" id="a_2">Section 18</a>
           Narcotic Drugs and <a href="/doc/1727139/" id="a_3">Psychotropic Substances Act</a>, 1985.


</p><p id="p_3">                  Mr. Sabir Ahmed ....for the petitioner.
</p><p id="p_4">                  Mr. Amanul Islam         .. for the State

                        We have heard the submissions of the learned Advocate for the

           petitioners and the learned Advocate for the State.

</p><p id="p_5">                        It prima facie appears from the materials produced before us by

           Counsel appearing on behalf of the petitioner as also materials in the Case

           Diary that the petitioner is not the owner of the land in which cultivation of

           Poppy seed is going on. The charge-sheet has been submitted.

</p><p id="p_6">                        Considering the materials produced before us in the Case Diary,

           we do not find that custodial interrogation of the petitioner is necessary or

           there is apprehension of his absconding. We are, therefore, inclined to allow

           the prayer of the petitioner under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_4">Section 438</a> of the Code of Criminal

           Procedure.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                        Accordingly, we direct that in the event of arrest the petitioner

           shall be released on bail, upon furnishing bond of Rs.5,000/-(Rupees Five

           thousand) with one surety of like amount to the satisfaction of the arresting

           authority and subject to the conditions as laid down under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_5">Section 438(2)</a> of

           the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 and on the further condition that the

           petitioner shall report to the Investigating Officer whenever necessary but at

           least once a week. The petitioner shall also appear in the Court below within

           three weeks from the date of receipt of the certified copy of this order and
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                                                                                    2</span>




shall not, without the leave of the Court below, leave its jurisdiction.

</p><p id="p_8">                    This application for anticipatory bail is, thus disposed of.



</p><p id="p_9">                                                                         (Indira Banerjee, J.)


                                                                       (Indrajit Chatterjee,J.)
