<p id="p_1">     Petitioner challenges Ext.P1 order dated 26/12/2008

passed by the District Collector under Section 27(3) of the

Kerala Protection of River Banks and Regulation of Removal

of Sand Rules 2002. It is contended by the petitioner that he

was not given proper opportunity for hearing while passing

Ext. P1 order that it is not a speaking order. It is further

contended that the sand transported in the vehicle is very

old sand which was kept in a work site and he was

transporting the same from a work site to another work site.

In Ext. P1, District Collector had referred to seizure mahazar

of a special squad.         It is indicated that at the time of

transportation, the petitioner was not in possession of

necessary pass as provided under Rule 29(8) of the above

rules.
</p>
<p id="p_2"><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">W.P.C.No.8527 OF 2009           -2-</span>

</p><p id="p_3">     2.   The stipulation for transportation of sand from one

site to another or river bank to a site is the possession of a

valid pass as provided under rule 29(8). The only defence

available to a transporter is the possession of a valid pass. If

the petitioner could not produce the pass, necessarily the

District Collector will have to pass orders under Rule 27(3).

</p><p id="p_4">     3.   Under these circumstances, it cannot be said that

there was any illegality in the District Collector passing the

impugned order. The petitioner was definitely heard in the

matter and therefore, the allegation regarding violation of

natural justice also does not arise.
</p>
<p id="p_5">     In the above circumstances, there is no merit in the

above writ petition and the same is dismissed.


</p><p id="p_6">     Post as to be spoken to on 12/01/2012.
</p>
