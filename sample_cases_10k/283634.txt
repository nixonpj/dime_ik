<div class="doc_title">Sirajudeen Thangal vs State Of Kerala on 17 June, 2009</div><pre id="pre_1">       

  

  

 
 
  IN THE HIGH COURT OF KERALA AT ERNAKULAM

Bail Appl..No. 3256 of 2009()



1. SIRAJUDEEN THANGAL
                      ...  Petitioner

                        Vs

1. STATE OF KERALA
                       ...       Respondent

                For Petitioner  :SRI.MANSOOR.B.H.

                For Respondent  :PUBLIC PROSECUTOR

The Hon'ble MR. Justice K.T.SANKARAN

 Dated :17/06/2009

 O R D E R
                      K.T. SANKARAN, J.
                   ---------------------------
                    B.A. No.3256 of 2009
                ------------------------------------
             Dated this the 17th day of June, 2009

                           O R D E R
</pre>
<p id="p_1">      This is an application for anticipatory bail under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_1">section

438</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure. The petitioner is the

first accused   in Crime No.78/2009 of Malampuzha          Police

Station.
</p>
<p id="p_2">      2.   The offence alleged against the petitioner is under

<a href="/doc/1868826/" id="a_2">Section 498</a> A of the Indian Penal Code.

</p><p id="p_3">      3.   The petitioner and other accused filed applications

for anticipatory bail before the Court of Sessions, Palakkad

Division. The learned Sessions Judge granted anticipatory bail

in favour of the accused Nos. 2 to 4, but rejected the prayer

made by the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_4">      4.   I have gone through the case diary. Taking into

account the facts and circumstances of the case, the nature of

the offence and other circumstances, I am of the view that

anticipatory bail can be granted to the petitioner. There will

be a direction that in the event of the arrest of the petitioner,

the officer in charge of the police station shall release him on

B.A. No.3256/2009
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                     2</span>

bail on his executing bond for Rs.25,000/- with two solvent

sureties for the like amount to the satisfaction of the officer

concerned, subject to the following conditions:

</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">         A) The petitioner shall         report   before      the
             investigating officer between 9 A.M and 11 A.M.
             on all alternate Mondays, till the final report is
             filed or until further orders;
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_2">         B) The     petitioner    shall   appear   before     the
             investigating officer for interrogation as and
             when required;
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_3">         C) The petitioner shall not try to influence the
             prosecution     witnesses   or tamper     with   the
             evidence.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_4">         D) The petitioner shall not commit any offence or
             indulge in any prejudicial activity while on bail.
</blockquote>

<blockquote id="blockquote_5">         E) In case of breach of any of the conditions
             mentioned above, the bail shall be liable to be
             cancelled.</blockquote><p id="p_5">

</p><p id="p_6">       The Bail Application is allowed to the extent indicated

 above.
</p>
<p id="p_7">


</p><p id="p_8">                                      K.T. SANKARAN, JUDGE

scm

</p>
