 O R D E R

                 Dated this the 12th October, 2006

                            J U D G M E N T
</pre>

<p id="p_1">      The petitioner is the widow of late Shri. M.V. Raghavan


who   was  working  as  L/NK  in  102  BN.BSF.    The  relief  prayed


for   by   the   petitioner   is   for   a   direction   to   the   1st


respondent-Director General, Border Security Force to grant


her   pensionary   benefits   such   as   extra   ordinary   family


pension,   DCRG,   family   pension,   GPF   and   other   benefits   and


to quash Ext. P2 order of the 1st respondent.


</p><p id="p_2">      2. Upon request made by the petitioner for the grant of


the   aforesaid   benefits,   the   1st  respondent   passed   Ext.   P2


order   stating   that   the   petitioner   cannot   be   granted   the


terminal   benefits   since   her   husband   was   dismissed   from


service.  In the statement filed by the respondents through


their   counsel   it   is   stated   that   Rule   49   of   the   Central


Civil   Services   Pension   Rules   does   not   confer   the   right   of


pension on a person who stands dismissed from service.   As


per Rule 48 of the aforesaid Rules as also Rule 56 of the


Fundamental Rules and Rule 48A of the Pension Rules, even a


person   who   voluntarily   retires   on   completion   of   certain


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">WPC No. 18577/06                 -2-</span>


period of service is entitled for terminal benefits. But a


person who stands dismissed from service will not have the


right   to   claim     terminal   benefits.     That   being   the


position,   the   dependents   of   the   employee   who   stands


dismissed and later on died will not have the right to get


terminal benefits.
</p>
<p id="p_3">
</p><p id="p_4">     There   is   no   merit   in   this   writ   petition.     Hence,


dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_5">



</p><p id="p_6">                                                   K.K. DENESAN
                                                       JUDGE
jan/-
</p>
