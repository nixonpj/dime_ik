<p id="p_1">         1.     Rule. Learned advocate Ms.Archana Amin is directed to
         appear and she waives service of Rule for the opponent.
</p>

<p id="p_2">         2.     The present application under <a href="/doc/100581/" id="a_1">Section 5</a> of the Limitation
         Act, 1963 ("the Act" for brevity) has been preferred by the
         applicants for condonation of delay of 441 days caused in filing
         the First Appeal.
</p>

<p id="p_3">         3.     Learned advocate Ms.Archna Amin opposes the present
         application for condonation of delay on the ground that no
         sufficient explanation is given by the applicants to condone the
         delay and, therefore, present application may be dismissed.
</p>

<p id="p_4">         4.     Reasons for delay caused in preferring the appeal are

<p id="p_5">         5.       The words, "Sufficient cause for not making application
         within the period of limitation" should be applied in a
         reasonable and liberal manner depending upon the facts and
         circumstances of the case and the type of the case. The
         expression "sufficient cause" in <a href="/doc/100581/" id="a_2">section 5</a> of the Act needs
         liberal construction so as to advance substantial justice when
         the delay is not on account of any negligence or inaction or
         want of bona fide or dilatory tactics on the part of the
         applicants. In nutshell, the decisive factor for condonation of
         delay is not length of delay but sufficiency and satisfactory
         explanation. Since there is nothing on record so as to infer
         inaction or want of bona fide that can be attributed to the
         applicants, the applicants cannot be non-suited at threshold
         and thus, cannot be deprived of substantial justice which has
         been made available to the applicants by way of statutory
         appeal. In order to advance                substantial justice                to the
         applicants and as the explanation for delay does not smack
         mala fide, the delay caused in preferring the appeal is hereby
         condoned. The application stands disposed of. Rule is made
         absolute to the aforesaid extent.
</p>
