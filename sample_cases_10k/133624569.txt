</p><p id="p_4">1.       Appeal is admitted for consideration of                 following

substantial questions of law:-

</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">         "i. Whether the Tribunal erred in holding
         that the Appellant is not entitled to
         depreciation of Rs.93,75,63,113/- on leased
         assets?
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">         ii.  Whether the Tribunal ought to have
         allowed the Appellant's claim for deduction
         of Rs.27,52,444/- as payment towards
         scientific research assistance?
         iii. Whether the Tribunal erred in upholding
         the reduction of exemption claimed by the
         Appellant under section 10 of the Act and in
         this regard relying upon the provisions of
         section 14A of the Act?"
</blockquote>

</p><p id="p_6">2.       We notice that the appellant-assessee has                 raised

following questions:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_3">         "a. Whether the Tribunal erred in setting
         aside the additional grounds raised by it
         relating to grant of deduction in respect of
         write off of bad debts under section 36(1)(vii),
         exclusion of the amount received as recovery
         of bad debts written off not liable to tax under
         section 41(4) and assessment of income
         earned by it from foreign branches to the
         Respondent No.1 for adjudication?
         b.    Whether the Tribunal ought to have
         allowed the Appellant's claim for deduction of
         the amount of Rs.1302,24,75,974/- as write
         off of bad debts under section 36(1)(vii) of the
         Act in view of the decision of the Hon'ble
         Supreme Court in the case of <a href="/doc/1863413/" id="a_1">Vijaya Bank v.
         CIT</a> 323 ITR 166?
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_4">         c.    Whether the Tribunal ought to have held
         that the recovery of bad debts earlier written
         off could not be assessed to tax by invoking
         the provisions of section 41(4) of the Act as in
         the earlier years deduction had not been
         allowed in respect of write off of such bad
         debts under section 36(1)(vii)?
         d.    Whether the Tribunal ought to have held
         that income earned by the Appellant from a
         foreign branch could not be assessed in India
         in view of the tax treaty between India and
         the respective Country?
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_5">         e.    Whether    the Tribunal ought to have
         allowed the Appellant's claim for deduction of
         Rs.1215,90,793/- in respect of depreciation on
         matured investments?"
</blockquote>
