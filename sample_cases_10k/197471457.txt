<pre id="pre_1">                             1


           IN THE HIGH COURT OF KARNATAKA
                  KALABURAGI BENCH


       DATED THIS THE 8TH DAY OF DECEMBER-2017


                     BEFORE
       THE HON'BLE MRS. JUSTICE RATHNAKALA


             M.F.A.No.201331/2017 (MV)


BETWEEN:

Baburao @ Babu Bhimappa Bellubbi,
Age: 28 years, Occ: Teacher,
R/o Kolhar, Tgal.: B.Bagewadi,
Now residing at Honaganahalli,
Tal. &amp; Dist: Vijayapura-586101.

                                              ... Appellant

(By Sri Sanganagouda. V. Biradar, Advocate)


AND:

The Divisional Controller,
K.S.R.T.C., Vijayapura Division,
Vijayapura-586101.

                                         ... Respondent


       This Miscellaneous First appeal is filed under
<a href="/doc/93665491/" id="a_1">Section 173(1)</a> of M.V. Act, praying to allow this appeal
and enhance the compensation as claimed in the claim
petition by modifying the judgment and award dated
29.09.2016 passed by the Court of III Addl. Senior Civil
                               2


Judge and Member, MACT No.XII, at Vijayapura, in
M.V.C.No.1123/2014.

      This appeal coming on for Orders this day, the Court
delivered the following:

                      JUDGMENT
</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Heard Sri Sanganagouda V.Biradar, learned counsel

for the appellant.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">      2.    The appellant is aggrieved by the quantum of

compensation awarded in his favour by the III Addl. Senior

Civil Judge and Member MACT, Vijayapura (hereinafter

referred to as 'the Tribunal) in a claim petition filed by him

under <a href="/doc/136948773/" id="a_2">Section 166</a> of Motor Vehicles Act, 1988.


</p><p id="p_4">      3.    Briefly stated, the appellant filed the claim

petition before the Tribunal alleging that on 25.02.2014,

he sustained injuries in a vehicular accident involving the

KSRTC bus belonging to the respondent. Because of the

accident he suffered fracture of Condylar fracture of left

humorous bones and other injuries.
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                 3</span>


</p><p id="p_5">     4.     The claim was contested by the respondent-

Corporation.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><p id="p_7">     5.     The Tribunal on over all consideration material

placed before the Court, allowed the petition with interest

at the rate of 9% per annum by awarding compensation on

the following heads ;-</p><pre id="pre_2">


  Pain and sufferings.                           Rs.15,000-00
  Medical expenses incurred and Future           Rs.75,000-00
  medical      expenses,     Attendant,
</pre><p id="p_8">  Conveyance, Nourishing food, and
  other incidental expenses.
</p><p id="p_9">  Loss of income during laid-up period.          Rs.18,000-00
  Loss of future income on account of            Rs.97,000-00
  permanent disability
  Loss of Amenities, Life comforts and           Rs.10,000-00
  expectancy of life.
</p><pre id="pre_3">                  Total                      Rs.2,15,000-00




     6.     As   such     the   appellant   is   a   Teacher   by

</pre><p id="p_10">profession and it is not his case that the injuries suffered

during the accident will have it's bearing on his career /

profession. The amount awarded under the head of loss of

future income is baseless and the prayer of the claimant in
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                             4</span>


this appeal for enhanced compensation is without merit.

Hence, the appeal so also I.A.No.1/2017 is rejected

without notice to the respondent.




</p><p id="p_11">                                      Sd/-
</p><p id="p_12">                                     JUDGE
sn/SMP
