<div class="doc_title">Ramsukh vs State Of Uttarakhand And Another on 19 April, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1"> IN THE HIGH COURT OF UTTARAKHAND AT NAINITAL
              Criminal Misc. Application No.390 of 2016
                       ( Under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> of Cr.P.C.)

Ramsukh                                              .......Applicant

                             Versus

State of Uttarakhand and another                     ... Respondents

Hon'ble Sudhanshu Dhulia, J. (Oral)
</pre><p id="p_1">       Heard Mr. Karan Anand, Advocate for the applicant and
Mr. Hari Om Bhakuni, A.G.A. for the State of Uttarakhand.
</p><p id="p_2">       A first information report has been lodged by respondent
No.2 against the present applicant which has been registered
as Case Crime No.94 of 1992 under <a href="/doc/1517318/" id="a_2">Sections 436</a>/<a href="/doc/1185693/" id="a_3">511</a>/<a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_4">506</a> of
IPC,   at     Police   Station   Patel    Nagar,    Dehradun.   After
investigation, police submitted charge sheet. Thereafter, the
learned Magistrate took cognizance in the matter and summon
has been issued to the present applicant. But the applicant
did not appear before the court below. Now Non-bailable
warrant has been issued against the present applicant by the
court below. Hence, the applicant has filed present application
under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_5">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C. invoking the inherent jurisdiction of
this Court.
</p><p id="p_3">       At this stage, no interference is being called for by this
Court in the matter. However, considering the fact that the
applicant is 72 years of age, in case the present applicant
appears before the trial court within a week from today, the
non-bailable warrant issued against him shall be kept in
abeyance.
</p><p id="p_4">       However, the applicant would be at liberty to move an
application for his bail before the court concerned, which shall
be taken as expeditiously as possible If for any reason, the bail
is deferred or rejected, the learned Magistrate shall forward the
papers to the Sessions court on the same day itself which shall
be considered in accordance with law.
</p><p id="p_5">       The courts below shall also consider granting an interim
bail to the applicant considering the age of the applicant which
is 72 years, and the fact that it is an extremely old matter.
</p><p id="p_6">      With the aforesaid observation, the application filed
under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_6">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C. stands disposed.</p><pre id="pre_2">


                                   (Sudhanshu Dhulia, J.)
JKJ                                          19.04.2016
 </pre>
