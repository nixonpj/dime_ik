	 Writ Petition filed under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the Constitution of India praying for the issuance of a Writ of Certiorarified Mandamus calling for the records issued by the 2nd respondent in Na.Ka.No.7028/2009/A5, dated 08.03.2010 and order issued by the 3rd respondent Na.Ka.63/2010, dated 23.07.2010 and quash the same as illegal and directing the 3rd respondent to approve the petitioner planning permission dated 29.01.2010.

	 		For Petitioner         :  M/s.A.Sumathy
			For Respondents     :  Mr.N.Inbanathan, AGP, for RR1&amp; 2
						   Mr.J.Raja Kalifulllah, for R3
						   Mr.T.Sathyamoorthi, for R4


ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">	Heard the learned counsel for the petitioner, learned Additional Government Pleader for the respondents 1 and 2 and learned counsels for the respondents 3 &amp; 4.
</p>
<p id="p_2">	2. According to the petitioner, she is the absolute owner of the property measuring an extent of 3190 sq.ft. in Walajabad, Kancheepuram Taluk in Survey No.221/1, vide registered sale deed, dated 19.09.2007.
</p>
<p id="p_3">	3. The petitioner has filed this writ petition, challenging the assignment of land in favour of the fourth respondent by the second respondent in Na.Ka.No.7028/2009/A5, dated 08.03.2010 and rejection of the building plan application of the petitioner, dated 23.07.2010.
</p>
<p id="p_4">	4. It is now represented that in respect of the same property a Civil Suit in O.S.No.85 of 2010 is pending before the District Munsif Court, Kancheepuram.
</p>
<p id="p_5">	5. The learned counsel for the petitioner seeks permission of this Court to withdraw this writ petition and to agitate her right before the competent Civil Court.
</p>
<p id="p_6">	6. In the light of the above submission, this writ petition is dismissed as withdrawn with liberty. No costs. 
</p>
