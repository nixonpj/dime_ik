<div class="doc_title">Vinit Goyal vs Rashmi Goyal on 20 August, 2013</div><pre id="pre_1">                      Crl. Misc. No. M-34247 of 2012 (O&amp;M)                                  1

                                 IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA
                                               AT CHANDIGARH


                                                    Crl. Misc. No. M-34247 of 2012 (O&amp;M)
                                                    Date of decision: 20.08.2013


                      Vinit Goyal

                                                                                 ...Petitioner

                                                    Versus


                      Rashmi Goyal

                                                                                 ...Respondent


                      CORAM : HON'BLE MS JUSTICE RITU BAHRI


                      Present:      Mr. Parveen Rai, Advocate,
                                    for Mr. Sumeet Goel, Advocate,
                                    for the petitioner.



                      RITU BAHRI, J.
</pre>

<p id="p_1">                                    Challenge is to the order dated 10.07.2012 (Annexure P-4)

                      passed by the Additional Sessions Judge, Gurgaon, whereby revision against

                      the order dated 12.03.2012 (Annexure P-2) passed by the Judicial Magistrate,

                      Ist Class, Gurgaon, has been dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                    The petitioner filed a complaint under <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_1">Sections 420</a>, <a href="/doc/1985627/" id="a_2">467</a>, <a href="/doc/556166/" id="a_3">468</a>,

                      <a href="/doc/1466184/" id="a_4">471</a> IPC against his wife-Rashmi Goyal (respondent), alleging therein that

                      their marriage was solemnized on 11.10.1980 and out of the said wedlock,

                      three children were born. The complainant-petitioner was settled in U.S.A.,

                      but the accused-respondent decided to stay in India along with her children.

Prasher Ajay
2013.09.17 18:24
I attest to the accuracy and
integrity of this document
High Court Chandigarh
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                       Crl. Misc. No. M-34247 of 2012 (O&amp;M)                                    2</span>

                      It has been further alleged that the respondent has presented a divorce petition

                      in the Los Angeles Superior Court, while she was living at Gurgaon at that

                      time and therefore, she should be proceeded under <a href="/doc/308396/" id="a_5">Section 193</a> of the IPC.

                      Both the Courts below have dismissed the complaint on the ground that there

                      was no evidence that Rashmi Goyal-respondent had ever forged and

                      fabricated any document.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                   It is not a case of the complainant-petitioner that the signatures

                      on the divorce petition was not of Rashmi Goyal-respndent or of somebody

                      else and she has forged the signatures of someone while presenting her

                      petition before the Superior Court of Los Angeles. She got recorded her

                      statement before the police on 21.07.2011 to the effect that she had filed a

                      petition in the Court at Los Angeles through her counsel Mical Naathan by

                      sending the same by fax.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                   After going through the impugned orders, this Court is of the

                      opinion that in this case, offence under <a href="/doc/1145599/" id="a_6">Section 193</a> Cr.P.C. is not made out as

                      there was no false document, which was presented in a judicial proceedings

                      pending before the trial Court. Further, it is for the Superior Court at Los

                      Angeles, before which the aforesaid documents have been produced, to see

                      whether the said Court can entertain the divorce petition on original

                      signatures or even through facsimile. Since there was no evidence that she

                      has given false evidence in the Court, as she had sent her petition by fax, no

                      ground is made out to interfere in the impugned orders.

</p><p id="p_5">                                   Dismissed.</p><pre id="pre_2">


                                                                                   (RITU BAHRI)
                      August 20, 2013                                                 JUDGE
