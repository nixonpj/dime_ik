<pre id="pre_1">                                         1




      THE HON'BLE SRI JUSTICE A. RAJASEKHAR REDDY

                WRIT PETITION No.40808 of 2018

ORDER:
</pre>
<p id="p_1">       This Writ Petition, under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the Constitution of India,

is filed for the following relief :

</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">       "To declare the Proceeding dt. 10.10.2018 vide ROC
       No.684/2018       (B)    and   consequent           proceedings       dt.
       5.11.2018      under     ROC      No.684/2018(B)          issued       by
       respondent      No.4     are      illegal     and    arbitrary        and
       unconstitutional and violative of principles of natural
       justice and the provisions of Land Encroachment Act,
       1905."</blockquote><p id="p_2">

</p><p id="p_3">       Learned    Assistant     Government          submits     that   alternative

remedy of appeal is available to the petitioner.


</p><p id="p_4">       Learned counsel for the petitioner says that in the Order

dt.05.11.2018 itself, the Executing Officer was asked to be ready

along with machinery for demolition of the building.


</p><p id="p_5">       Since the petitioner is having the alternative remedy of

appeal, this Court is not inclined to entertain the present Writ

Petition.   However,    there    shall     be      suspension    of    the    Order

dt.05.11.2018 for a period of two weeks to enable the petitioner to

file appeal before competent authority.


</p><p id="p_6">       With the above direction, this Writ Petition is disposed of.


       The miscellaneous Petitions, if any, pending in this case shall
stand closed.
</p>

                                                     A. RAJASHEKER REDDY, J
Dt: 13-11-2018
