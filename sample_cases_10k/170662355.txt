<div class="doc_title">Kiran Devi vs The State Of Bihar on 15 July, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.26081 of 2016
                       Arising Out of PS.Case No. -487 Year- 2015 Thana -DHANARUA District- PATNA
                 ======================================================
                 Kiran Devi W/o Arvind Kumar
                                                         .... .... Petitioner/s
                                             Versus
                 1. The State of Bihar

                                                                  .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :  Mr. Satish Chandra, Advocate
                 For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr. Sri Matloob Rab, APP
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE DINESH KUMAR SINGH
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">3   15-07-2016                 Heard learned counsels for the petitioner and the

                  State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                               The petitioner being the sister-in-law (gotani) of the

                  husband of the victim is apprehending arrest in a case registered

                  for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_1">Sections 498A</a>, <a href="/doc/653797/" id="a_2">304B</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_3">34</a> of

                  the Indian Penal Code and 3/4 of the <a href="/doc/1763444/" id="a_4">Dowry Prohibition Act</a>.

</p><p id="p_3">                               Basic accusation is of killing the sister of the

                  informant namely Rekha Devi, after six years of marriage for

                  non-fulfillment of dowry demand. The dead body of the victim

                  was found hanging on a tree. It is alleged that the sister of the

                  informant was being tortured by her husband, husband's brother

                  and brother's wife for non-fulfillment of dowry demand of

                  Rs.50,000/- and a motorcycle.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                               It is submitted by learned counsel for the petitioner
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">             Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.26081 of 2016 (3) dt.15-07-2016</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                       2/2</span>




                           that the petitioner claims to be separate from the husband of the

                           victim. The thrust of accusation is against the husband of the

                           victim, who is in custody. Though, the petitioner is accused in

                           Dhanarua P.S. Case No.14/2016, levelling accusation under

                           <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_5">section 302</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_6">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code, but she is on bail.

</p><p id="p_5">                                        Considering the nature of accusation, this Court is not

                           inclined to grant anticipatory bail to the petitioner.

</p><p id="p_6">                                        Let the learned Court below consider the prayer for

                           regular bail of the petitioner, if the petitioner surrenders within a

                           period of six weeks in connection with Dhanarua P.S. Case

                           No.487/2015, pending before the learned Judicial Magistrate, 1st

                           Class, Masaurhi at Patna.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                                        With the above observation, this application is,

                           accordingly, disposed off.
</p>
<p id="p_8">


</p><p id="p_9">                                                    (Dinesh Kumar Singh, J)
Ashwini/-
</p>
