       DATED THIS THE 24TH DAY OF OCTOBER, 2016

         THE HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE A S BOPANNA

BETWEEN

DEEP FRIENDS ASSOCIATION
DOOR NO.3-276/4,
MAHARAJA COMPLEX,
MAIDEN WEST CROSS ROAD,
MANGALORE-575 001.

PRAKASH CHANDRA RAI,
AGED ABOTU 57 YEARS,
S/O RAMANNA RAI
                                             ... PETITIONER
(BY SRI NISHIT KUMAR SHETTY, ADV.)

AND

      BY ITS SECRETARY,
      HOME DEPARTMENT,
      VIDHANA SOUDHA,
      BANGALORE-560 001.

      D.K. DISTRICT-575 001.

3.    THE DEPUTY COMMISSIONER OF POLICE
      MANGALORE DIVISION (LAW N&amp; ORDER),
      MAIDAN ROAD,
      MANGALORE-575 001.
      DAKSHINA KANNADA DISTRICT.

4.    THE ASSISTANT COMMISSIONER OF POLICE
      PANDESHWARA SUB-DIVISION,
      MANGALORE-575 001.
                                 2




     DAKSHINA KANNADA.

5.   THE INSPECTOR OF POLICE
     MANGALORE SOUTH POLICE STATION,
     PANDESHWARA,
     MANGALORE-575 003,
     D.K.

6.   THE INSPECTOR OF POLICE
     CENTRAL CRIME BRANCH, (CCB)
     MANGALORE-575005.
                                                 ... RESPONDENTS

(BY SMT PRATHIMA HONNAPURA, HCGP)

       THIS PETITION IS FILED UNDER ARTICLES 226 &amp; 227 OF
THE CONSTITUTION OF INDIA, PRAYING TO DECLARE THAT THE
PETITIONER IS NOT REQUIRED TO OBTAIN ANY PERMISSION OR
LICENSE UNDER THE PROVISIONS OF LICENSING AND
CONTROLLING OF PLACES OF THE PUBLIC AMUSEMENENT
ORDER; DIRECT THE RESPONDENTS NOT TO INSIST UPON THE
PETITIONER TO OBTAIN LICENSE from the provisions of LICENSING
AND CONTROLLING OF PLACES OF THE PUBLIC AMUSEMENENT
ORDER to the recreational activities to be carried on by members of
the petitioner's association situated at DEEP FRIENDS ASSOCIATION
DOOR NO.3-276/4, MAHARAJA COMPLEX, MAIDEN WEST CROSS
ROAD, MANGALORE-575 001.

      THIS PETITION COMING ON FOR PRELIMANARY HEARING,
THIS DAY, THE COURT MADE THE FOLLOWING:

                           ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Learned Government Advocate to accept notice for

respondents No.1 to 6 and file memo of appearance in four

weeks.
</p>

<p id="p_2">      2.    The petitioner is before this Court seeking for issue

of mandamus or any other appropriate writ in the manner as

has been sought in the petition.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                    3</span>




<p id="p_3">      3.      The issue which has been raised in the instant

petition has already       been    considered by          this Court    in

W.P.No.30071/2014         dated        15-10-2014    (<a href="/doc/87944862/" id="a_1">The       Media   N

Members Club vs. State of Karnataka and Others</a>) wherein

the petition was disposed of with the following directions:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">      "(i) The petitioner shall install within a period of six
              weeks, CC TV cameras, at all the places of
              access to its members and also at all the
              places, wherein games(s) is / are played by
              the members.    The CC TV footage of atleast
              prior 15 days' period shall be made available
              by the petitioner, to the police, as and when
              called upon to do so.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_2">      (ii)    The petitioner shall issue identity card(s) to
              all its member(s), which shall be produced by
              the member(s), when called upon by the
              police, during the raid(s) and surveillance etc.

</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">      (iii)   The    petitioner    shall       not   allow      any
              non-member(s)       or     the   guest(s)    of   the
              member(s), to make use of its premises for
              the purpose of playing any kind of game(s) or
              recreational activities.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_4">      (iv)    The petitioner shall not permit any activity by
              any of its member(s), by indulging in acts of
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                4</span>




        amusement, falling within the definition of
        Ss.2(14) &amp; 2(15) of the Act and shall not
        permit     any     game(s)    of   chance    as   per
        Explanation (II) of Sub-section (7) of Section
        2 of Karnataka Police Act, 1963.                  The
        member(s) shall not be allowed to play any
        kind of game(s) with stakes or make any
        profit or gain out of the game(s) played.
</blockquote>
<p id="p_4">(v)     The petitioner shall put proper mechanism in
        place and shall ensure that no game(s) is
        played in any unlawful manner by the
        member(s).        If the police find that game(s)
        played is contrary to any law and in violation
        of the settled practice, it is open to them to
        take     action    against    petitioner    and   the
        offenders, in accordance with law.
</p>
<p id="p_5">(vi)    The jurisdictional police shall have liberty to
        visit premises periodically and/or on receipt
        of   any    information      about   any    unlawful
        activity being carried on in the petitioner's
        premises.
</p>
<p id="p_6">(vii)   The respondents are directed not to interfere
        with the lawful recreational activities carried
        on by the members of the petitioner - Club /
        Association.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                                       5</span>




<p id="p_7">      (viii) It is made clear that this order would not
            come in the way of the jurisdictional police
            invoking the provisions of the Act and taking
            action   in     accordance        with   law,    if   the
            member(s) of the petitioner are found to have
            indulged       in   any       unlawful   or     immoral
            activities."
</p>
<p id="p_8">This writ petition is also disposed of in the aforesaid terms.
</p>


<p id="p_9">                                                        Sd/-
</p><p id="p_10">                                                      JUDGE

VP
