<div class="doc_title">Jagdish Gurjar @ Jaggu vs State Of Rajasthan Through Pp on 8 March, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1"> HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR RAJASTHAN BENCH AT
                      JAIPUR
        S.B.Criminal Miscellaneous Bail No. 3564 / 2017
Jagdish Gurjar @ Jaggu S/o Sh. Lal Chand B/c Gurjar, R/o Village
Ghugra, PS Civil Lines, Ajmer. (At Present Confined in Central Jail,
Ajmer).
                                                         ----Petitioner
                                Versus
State of Rajasthan Through P.P.
                                                       ----Respondent</pre>
<p id="p_1">_____________________________________________________
For Petitioner(s)   : Mr. Neeraj K. Tiwari
For Respondent(s) : Mr. Rishi Raj Singh, PP
_____________________________________________________
            HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE PANKAJ BHANDARI
                         Judgment / Order
08/03/2017

</p><p id="p_2">1.     Petitioner has filed this bail application under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> Cr.

P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_3">2.     F.I.R. No. 116/2017 is registered at Police Station Civil Lines,

Ajmer, for offence under <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_2">Sections 341</a>,<a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_3">323</a>,<a href="/doc/1208971/" id="a_4">353</a> I.P.C. &amp; Section 3

PDPP Act.
</p>
<p id="p_4">3.     It is contended by the counsel for the petitioner that no

major damage to public party has been caused. The petitioner is

behind the bars and is not required for further investigation.

</p><p id="p_5">4.     Learned Public Prosecutor has opposed the bail application.

</p><p id="p_6">5.     Taking note of the fact that the petitioner is not required for

further investigation, the present bail application deserves to be

allowed.
</p>
<p id="p_7">6.     This bail application is accordingly allowed and it is directed

that accused petitioner Jagdish Gurjar @ Jaggu S/o Sh. Lal Chand
                                 (2 of 2)
                                                        [CRLMB-3564/2017]

be released on bail provided he furnishes a personal bond in the

sum of Rs.20,000/- (Rupees twenty thousand) together with two

sureties in the sum of Rs.10,000/- (Rupees ten thousand) each to

the satisfaction of the trial court with the stipulation that he shall

appear before that Court and any court to which the matter is

transferred, on all subsequent dates of hearing and as and when

called upon to do so.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                                            (PANKAJ BHANDARI)J.
</p>
<p id="p_9">Arti/72
