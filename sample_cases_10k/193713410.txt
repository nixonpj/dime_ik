             SPECIAL CIVIL APPLICATION  NO. 19404 of 2015
         ===================================================
</pre><p id="p_1">              NATVARSINH AMARSINH BHATI....Petitioner(s)
                                Versus
                STATE OF GUJARAT  &amp;  1....Respondent(s)
         ===================================================
         Appearance:
</p><p id="p_2">         MR KAIVAN K PATEL, ADVOCATE for Petitioner(s) No. 1
         MR SWAPNESHWAR GOUTAM,AGP ON ADVANCE COPY for Res. authorities
         ===================================================
               CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE R.M.CHHAYA
                           Date : 19/01/2016
                               ORAL ORDER
         (1) Heard learned counsel for the parties. 
</p>
<p id="p_3">         (2)   By way of this petition under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the 
               Constitution   of   India              the        petitioner                has 
               challenged order dated 12.10.2015 passed by the 
               State   Government   in   Appeal   Weapon   Dispute 
               Application  No.778/13   whereby   the   appellate 
               authority,   in   exercise   of   powers   under   Section 
               18   of   the   Arms   Act,   1959,   confirmed   the   order 
               dated   30.09.2013   passed   by   District   Magistrate, 
               Sabarkantha, Himatnagar. 
</p>
<p id="p_4">         (3)   On   perusal   of   the   aforesaid   order   dated 
               12.10.2015   it   appears   that   the   sole   reason   for 
               which the appeal came to be rejected was that a 
               criminal   case   was   pending   against   the 
               petitioner.   From   the   fact   it   appears   that 
               technically   the   appellate   authority   has   not 
               committed any error as on the date on which the 
               appeal   came   to   be   decided,   Sessions   Case   No.13 
               of   2015   was   pending   before   learned   Additional

                        C/SCA/19404/2015                                           ORDER



                   Sessions Judge, Sabarkantha at Idar wherein the 
                   petitioner was accused No.4. 
</p>
<p id="p_5">         (4)       Learned advocate for the petitioner has invited 
                   attention   of   this   Court   to   the   judgment   of 
                   Sessions Court dated 16.10.2015 in Sessions Case 
                   No.13 of 2015 whereby the petitioner was accused 
                   No.4 has been acquitted for the alleged offences 
                   under Sections 323, 506(2), 427 and 114  of the 
                   Indian Penal Code, 1860.
</p>
<p id="p_6">         (5)       In light of the aforesaid development, which has 
                   taken   place   incidentally   four   days   after   the 
                   impugned   order   was   passed   by   the   appellate 
                   authority, this Court is of the opinion that the 
                   aforesaid   order   dated   12.10.2015   passed   by   the 
                   appellate   authority   deserves   to   be   quashed   and 
                   set aside and the proceedings of the said appeal 
                   deserve to be remanded for re­hearing. Petitioner 
                   shall produce certified copy of the judgment and 
                   order  passed   in   Sessions   Case   No.13   of   2015 
                   within   a   week   from   today.   Appellate   authority 
                   shall   re­consider   the   appeal   by   giving   an 
                   opportunity of being heard to the petitioner and 
                   decide the appeal de novo. 
</p>
<p id="p_7">         (6)       With   these   observations   and   directions,   the 
                   petition stands allowed to the aforesaid extent. 
                   D.S. permitted. 
</p><p id="p_8">                                                                            Sd/­      
                                                                      [R.M.CHHAYA, J ]
