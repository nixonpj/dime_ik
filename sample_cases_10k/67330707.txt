            1:THE STATE OF ASSAM
            REPRESENTED BY THE PUBLIC PROSECUTOR, ASSAM

Advocate for the Petitioner   : MR. A KADDUS

Advocate for the Respondent : PP, ASSAM
                                                                                             Page No.# 2/3

                                      BEFORE
                        HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE AJIT BORTHAKUR

                                                ORDER
</pre><p id="p_1">Date : 04-12-2019

       Heard Mr. A. Mannaf, learned counsel for the accused petitioners and Mr. K. Konwar,
learned Addl. Public Prosecutor, Assam appearing on behalf of the State.
</p><p id="p_2">       By this application under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> Cr.P.C., the accused petitioners, namely, 1. Md.
Isub Ali and 2. Md. Amir Ali, have prayed for granting bail in connection with Sualkuchi
Rivier PS Case No. 33/2019 under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_2">Sections 302</a>/<a href="/doc/386021/" id="a_3">201</a> of the IPC.
</p>
<p id="p_3">       The case diary, as called for, is placed before the Court.
</p>
<p id="p_4">       Mr. A. Mannaf, learned counsel for the accused petitioners, submits that although the
learned Courts below rejected the accused petitioners' bail prayer, the case against them is
based on mere suspicion and they are not named in the F.I.R. Therefore, Mr. Mannaf submits,
considering the length of detention, the accused petitioners may be released on bail.
</p>
<p id="p_5">       Mr. K. Konwar, learned Addl. Public Prosecutor, Assam, submits that the post-mortem
report shows that the cause of death of the deceased was asphyxia resulting from ante-
mortem ligature strangulation hanging, 2/3 days before. Mr. Konwar further submits that the
case against the accused petitioners, is based on last seen Together Theory.
</p>
<p id="p_6">       On scrutiny of the case diary, it appears that the deadbody of the deceased was
recovered after three days of his missing bearing injuries. There is no direct prima facie
incriminating evidence against the accused petitioners' involvement in the aforesaid crime,
subject of course to further investigation. The accused petitioners have been in custody since
31.10.2019. The investigation has been progressing satisfactorily. Therefore, this Court finds
that if the liberty of bail is granted to the accused petitioners, further investigation is unlikely
to be adversely affected.
</p>
<p id="p_7">Accordingly, it is provided that each of the accused-petitioners, named above, shall be released on bail
of Rs. 30,000/- (Rupees Thirty Thousand) only with one surety of the like amount to the

satisfaction of the learned Chief Judicial Magistrate, 1 st Class, Hajo, Kamrup, subject, of course, to the
following conditions:
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">       1.   That the accused shall co-operate in the investigation as and when required;
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">                                                                                            Page No.# 3/3

</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">      2.    That the accused shall not, directly or indirectly, make any inducement, threat or promise
           to any person acquainted with the facts of the case so as to dissuade him from disclosing
           such facts either to the Court or to any police officer; and

</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_4">      3.    That the accused shall not leave the jurisdiction of Sualkuchi River P.S. without prior
           written permission of the I.O.
</blockquote>


<blockquote id="blockquote_5">      With the above directions, the bail application stands disposed of.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_6">      Return the case diary.</blockquote>




<p id="p_8">                                                                            JUDGE
