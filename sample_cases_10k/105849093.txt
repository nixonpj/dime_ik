<p id="p_1">      In   these   appeals,   the   insurance   company   has

challenged the judgment and award dated 06.11.2009

made by the Addl. MACT, Hirekerur in MVC Nos.558 &amp;

559/2006 whereby it has awarded a compensation of

Rs.62,400/- and Rs.84,000/- respectively with interest at

6% p.a.
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                4</span>




</p><p id="p_2">      2.    The short point addressed by the learned

counsel for the insurance company is that the driver in

question did not have an endorsement from the R.T.O. on

his driving licence for driving the transport vehicle.


</p><p id="p_3">      3.    Learned counsel for the respondents-claimants

submits that this issue is no longer res integra and that the

Apex Court has already decided the same in the case of

Mukund     Devangan    Vs.   Oriental   Insurance   Co.   Ltd.,

reported in AIR 2017 SC 3668, answering the same issue

against the insurer.
</p>
<p id="p_4">
</p><p id="p_5">      4.    In view of the above, both the appeals fail and

accordingly dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><p id="p_7">      5.    The amount in deposit be transmitted to the

jurisdictional M.A.C.T. forthwith.
