<div class="doc_title">Bhim Sao &amp; Ors vs State Of Bihar on 25 May, 2010</div><pre id="pre_1">           IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                           Cr Misc No 4536 of 2010
         1 Bhim Sao, son of late Vishwanath Sao
         2 Lalan @ Lallan Kumar, son of Bhim Sao (Halwai), residents of village -
            Hakikatpur, P S - Bakhtiyarpur, District - Patna
         3 Raju Kumar, son of Sri Anil Prasad, resident of Karamganj, P O -
            Dhoorgaon, P S - Ekangarsarai, District - Nalanda -       Petitioners
                                   Versus
            State of Bihar                                     -      Opposite Party
                                  -----------
</pre>
<p id="p_1">3   25.05.2010                 The petitioners apprehend arrest in Bakhtiyarpur Police

                 Station Case No 1139 of 2009 instituted under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_1">Section 302</a> of Indian

                 Penal Code and other Sections as well. It is alleged, in the present case,

                 that there being a dispute of drain between the two parties, the accused

                 persons came and started a quarrel. They assaulted one Manoj Kumar and

                 took him to their house and got him assaulted by the police as well.

                 Having been taken in an injured condition, Manoj Kumar succumbed to

                 his injuries. On behalf of petitioners, it is submitted that the fact is totally

                 otherwise.   It is submitted that Manoj Kumar and others started the

                 quarrel in a drunken condition at the house of the accused persons. Manoj

                 Kumar was caught and beaten up. The police was also called because Raj

                 Kumar, in whose house this was happening, informed the police. Police

                 also beat up Manoj Kumar who died. Raj Kumar had earlier instituted the

                 case in respect of the said incident which had taken place on 09.06.2009

                 being Bakhtiyarur Police Station Case No 111 of 2009. The present case

                 with regard to the same occurrence is being instituted on 11.06.2009 only

                 to cover up. It is also urged that petitioners No 2 and 3 are minors.

</p><p id="p_2">                               Considering the aforesaid, in the facts and circumstances of

                 the case, the petitioners abovenamed are directed to surrender before the

                 Court below within four weeks from today and if they do so, they shall be
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                   2</span>
          released on bail on each of them furnishing bonds of Rs 10,000/- (Rupees

          Ten Thousand) with two sureties of the like amount each to the

          satisfaction of Additional Chief Judicial Magistrate, Barh in Bakhtiyarpur

          Police Station Case No 113 of 2009 subject to all conditions as laid down

          in <a href="/doc/1144664/" id="a_2">Section 438</a> (2)<a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_3"> of Criminal Procedure Code</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_3">

</p><pre id="pre_2">M.E.H./                                     (Navaniti Prasad Singh)
