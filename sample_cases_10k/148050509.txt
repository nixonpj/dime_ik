<div class="doc_title">Akalu Sah vs The State Of Bihar &amp; Ors on 9 December, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                Civil Writ Jurisdiction Case No.177 of 2016
                 ======================================================
                 Akalu Sah son of Teju Sah, resident of Village- Beruar, Police Station
                 Kudra, District- Kaimur, Bhabua.

                                                                        .... ....    Petitioner/s
                                                     Versus

                 1. The State of Bihar through the Principal Secretary, Department of Forest
                    and Environment, Bihar, Patna.
                 2. The Divisional Forest Officer-cum-Authorised Officer, Rohtas, Forest
                    Division, Rohtas.
                 3. The Forester Tilauthu Range, Rohtas (Sasaram)

                                                              .... .... Respondent/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance:
                 For the Petitioner/s : Mr. Sanjay Kumar Tiwary
                 For the Respondent/s : Mr. M.K. Sinha, SC-1
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE JYOTI SARAN
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">3   09-12-2016                  Heard Mr. Sanjay Kumar Tiwary, learned counsel

                 appearing for the petitioner and learned counsel for the State.

</p><p id="p_2">                                The petitioner questions the initiation of proceedings

                 in Forest Case No. 49 of 2015 giving rise to Confiscation Case

                 No. 102 of 2015 registered against the petitioner for alleged

                 violation of the provisions of the <a href="/doc/654536/" id="a_1">Indian Forest Act</a>, 1927

                 (hereinafter referred to as 'the Act'). According to the petitioner,

                 he has not violated any provisions of the Act.

</p><p id="p_3">                                Considering that the matter is yet pending for

                 consideration      before     the    Divisional     Forest         Officer-cum-

                 Authorized      Officer,    Rohtas,     Forest    Division,        Rohtas     for

                 adjudication on the merits of the contest, I am not persuaded to
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">          Patna High Court CWJC No.177 of 2016 (3) dt.09-12-2016                                 2</span>




                      interfere with the matter at the present stage and the writ petition

                      is accordingly disposed of.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                      The      Divisional         Forest   Officer-cum-Authorized

                      Officer, Rohtas, Forest Division, Rohtas would be well advised to

                      dispose of the confiscation proceedings in accordance with law

                      and with opportunity of hearing to the petitioner expeditiously and

                      preferably within six months from the date of receipt / production

                      of a copy of this order.
</p>
<p id="p_5">

</p><p id="p_6">                                                      (Jyoti Saran, J)
S.Sb/-
</p>
