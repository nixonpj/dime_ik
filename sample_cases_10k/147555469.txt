         IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD
                            SPECIAL CIVIL APPLICATION  NO. 11273 of 2012

 
FOR APPROVAL AND SIGNATURE: 
  
HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE A.J.DESAI                                                                    ­ Sd­

</p>
<p id="p_2">     5      Whether it is to be circulated to the civil judge ?                                                                                        No



=============================================
                                    RABARI JIVANBHAI UKABHAI....Petitioner(s)
                                                   Versus
                                     STATE OF GUJARAT  &amp;  4....Respondent(s)
=============================================
Appearance:
</p><p id="p_3">MR PRADEEP PATEL, ADVOCATE for the Petitioner(s) No. 1
MR RAKESH PATEL, AGP for the Respondent(s) No. 1
NOTICE NOT RECD BACK for the Respondent(s) No. 2
NOTICE SERVED for the Respondent(s) No. 3 ­ 5
=============================================

                       CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE A.J.DESAI
 
                                                              Date : 24/03/2014
 
                                                               ORAL JUDGMENT


</p><p id="p_4">1.             By way of the present petition  filed under Articles 14, 226 and 
               227   of   the   Constitution   of   India,   the   petitioner   has   prayed   as 
               under:
</p>

<p id="p_5">               "[A] May be pleased to admit and allow this petition.
</p>

<p id="p_6">                                                                          Page  1 of  5
  C/SCA/11273/2012                                                                                                                                     JUDGMENT




              [B] Be   pleased   to   issue   a   writ   of   certiorari   or   any   other  
                         appropriate writ, order, direction quashing and setting  
                         aside the impugned orders dated 27.02.12, 21.10.05 and  
                         30.03.05 passed by the respondent No. 2 to 4 respectively  
                         (Annexure - A colly) in Revisional Application No. 6/06,  
                         Appeal   No.104/04­05   and   Breach   of   Condition   Case  
                         No.84/04­05   respectively,   after   holding   the   same   as  
                         arbitrary illegal and contrary to evidence on record and  
                         further   be   pleased   to   hold   the   sale   qua   land   bearing  
                         Survey   No.   175,   village   -   Nana   Ankevadia,   Taluka   -  
                         Lakhtar,   District   -   Surendranagar,   in   favour   of   the  
                         petitioner   vide   Registered   Sale   Deed   dated   10.5.94   as  
                         legal and absolute;
</p>

<p id="p_7">              [C] Pending   admission   hearing   and   final   disposal   of   the  
                         petition be pleased to stay the execution, implementation,  
                         and   operation   of   the   impugned   orders   dated   27.2.12,  
                         21.10.05 and 30.3.05 (Annexure ­ A colly) passed by the  
                         respondent Nos. 2 to 4 respectively and further be pleased  
                         to restrain the respondents from disturbing the possession  
                         of the petitioner qua the land bearing Survey No. 175,  
                         village   Nana   Ankevadia,   Taluka-Lakhtar,   District-
                         Surendranagar, in manner whatsoever.
</p>

<p id="p_8">              [D] Be pleased to pass such other/further orders deemed fit."
</p>

<p id="p_9">2.          Mr.   Pradeep   Patel,   learned   advocate   for   the   petitioner   would 
            submit   that   under   the   provisions   of  'The   Saurashtra   Bhoodan 
            Yagna Act, 1953',  the  land in dispute was originally granted to 
            Alambhai Rajabhai in the year 1958 and after death of the original 


                                                                       Page  2 of  5
  C/SCA/11273/2012                                                                                                                                     JUDGMENT



            grantee of the land namely Alambhai Rajabhai, the legal heir of 
            original grantee sold the land to the present petitioner by way of 
            registered   sale   deed   dated   10.05.1994.   Pursuant   to   the   said 
            registered   sale   deed   dated   10.05.1994,   an   entry   No.   2247   was 
            mutated on 14.07.1994. Deputy Collector, Dhangadhara, District 
            Surendranagar ­ Respondent No. 4 initiated suo motu proceedings 
            against   the   land   bearing   Survey   No.175   purchased   by   the 
            petitioner and the said proceedings were numbered as Breach of 
            Condition Case No. 84/2004­05 and passed order on 30.03.2005 
            to vest the land in the name of the Government. Being aggrieved 
            by the order, he preferred Land Appeal Case No. 104/04­05 before 
            the   Collector,   Surendranagar   -   Respondent   No.   3,   who   has 
            confirmed   the   order   of   Deputy   Collector,   Dhangadhara   - 
            respondent   No.4.   Being   aggrieved   by   the   order   of   Collector, 
            Surendranagar   ­   respondent   No.   3,   the   petitioner   preferred 
            Revision  Application  No. 6/06 before the Additional  Secretary ­ 
            respondent   No.2,   who   has   also   confirmed   the   order   passed   by 
            Deputy Collector, Dhangadhara - respondent No.4. Hence, by way 
            of this petition, he has challenged all orders. 
</p>
<p id="p_10">3.          Mr.   Pradeep   Patel,   learned   advocate   would   further   submit   that 
            though   he   has   challenged   the   orders,   he   would   request   the 
            concerned   authority   to   consider   the   case   of   the   petitioner   for 
            regularizing   the   land   in   view   of   oral   order   dated   10.07.2009 
            passed   in   Letters   Patent   Appeal   No.   1477   of   2008   (Coram   : 
            Hon'ble The Chief Justice Mr. K.S.Radhakrishnan and Hon'ble Mr. 
            Justice Akil Kureshi). He would further submit that the petitioner 
            shall   make   an   application   for   post   facto   sanctioned   to   the 
            Collector,   Surendranagar   and   shall   pay   premium,   if   so   order 
            passed   by   the   Collector,  Surendranagar   considering   the   date   of 


                                                                       Page  3 of  5
  C/SCA/11273/2012                                                                                                                                     JUDGMENT



            sale deed. He would further submit by relying upon Rule 14(2) of 
            'The Saurashtra Bhoodan Yagna Rules, 1964'  that the petitioner, 
            who is purchaser of the land in dispute, had never used the said 
            land except for the agricultural purpose.
</p>

<p id="p_11">4.          Mr.Rakesh Patel, learned AGP for the respondent would submit 
            that if such application shall be made by the petitioner, the same 
            shall be dealt with in accordance with law. 
</p>

<p id="p_12">5.          I have also considered the observation of the Division  Bench of 
            this Court, which reads as under:
</p>

<blockquote id="blockquote_1">                          "Considering   the   fact   that   the   appellant   is   the   third  
                           purchaser   and   considering   the   fact   that   he   is   in  
                           possession of the land from 1983 onwards, we are of  
                           the view that without disturbing the orders passed by  
                           the Revenue Authorities and the learned Single Judge,  
                           it would be appropriate that the petitioners make an  
                           application to the Government for regularization of the  
                           possession and the same be considered sympathetically.  
                           Learned   counsel   for   the   appellant   submitted   that   he  
                           spent   lot   of   money   for   improving   the   land   and   also  
                           developed the land and had grown various trees. Since  
                           the   land   has   been   improved   and   the   appellant   is   in  
                           possession for the last 26 years, it will be appropriate  
                           for the Government to consider the request subject to  
                           payment of premium, without disturbing the orders of  
                           the Revenue Authorities and the learned single Judge.
</blockquote>

<p id="p_13">6.          Considering the peculiar facts and circumstances of the case and 
            in view of above observations made by the Division Bench of this 


                                                                       Page  4 of  5
   C/SCA/11273/2012                                                                                                                                     JUDGMENT



             Court, I pass following order.
</p>

<p id="p_14">             [a]           The petitioner  shall make an application  to the Collector, 
                           Surendranagar  for regularization of land within a period of 
                           four   weeks   from   today   and   the   Collector,   Surendranagar 
                           shall   decide   such   application   in   view   of   the   observation 
                           made by the Division Bench of this Court in Letters Patent 
                           Appeal No. 1477 of 2008 within a period of four months 
                           from the date of receipt of such application. 
</p>

<p id="p_15">             [b]           Parties are directed to maintain status quo with regard to 
                           the land in dispute.
</p>

<p id="p_16">7.           It is hereby made  clear that  if  such application  is made  by the 
             petitioner, the authority shall deal with the same without being 
             influenced by the observation made in the orders impugned.
</p>

<p id="p_17">8.           It would be open for the petitioner that if any adverse order is 
             passed   by   the   concerned   authority,   he   can   challenge   the   same 
             before appropriate authority.
</p>
<p id="p_18">9.           In   view   of   the   above,   Mr.   Pradeep   Patel,   learned   advocate   for   the 
             petitioner does not press this petition and seeks permission to withdraw. 
             Permission, as prayed for, is granted. Notice is discharged. Direct service 
             is permitted.
</p>

<p id="p_19">                                                                                                                                     ­Sd­           
                                                                                                                                (A.J.DESAI, J.) 
*Kazi...
</p>
