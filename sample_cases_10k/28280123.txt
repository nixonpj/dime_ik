<div class="doc_title">Mehar Chand &amp; Anr vs Jitender Kumar on 17 December, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">             IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA
                       AT CHANDIGARH

                                               COCP No.1780 of 2018 (O&amp;M)
                                               Date of Decision: 17.12.2018

Mehar Chand and another                             ....Petitioners

                          Versus

Jitender Kumar                                      ....Respondent


CORAM: HON'BLE MS. JUSTICE NIRMALJIT KAUR

Present:     Mr. Sanjay Mittal, Advocate, for the petitioners.

             Mr. Ashish Yadav, Additional Advocate General, Haryana.

NIRMALJIT KAUR, J. (ORAL)
</pre>
<p id="p_1">             The present contempt petition has been filed for non-compliance

of the order dated 12.3.2018, vide which direction was issued to respondent

No.5 to decide the representation dated 11.9.2017 of the petitioners within a

period of four weeks. Further direction was given that in case the petitioners

are found entitled for their necessary claim, the payment shall be disbursed to

them forthwith.
</p>
<p id="p_2">             Reply has been filed. As per the said reply, the petitioners were

found entitled for royalty/annuity, which has been paid up to the year 2017

and the said decision has also been placed on record as Annexure R-5.

</p><p id="p_3">             Learned counsel for the petitioners, however, submits that rate

of royalty/annuity should have been ` 21,000/- per acre per annum instead of

` 15,000/- per acre per annum.
</p>
<p id="p_4">             There was no such rate mentioned in the legal notice of the

petitioners or the order passed by this Court.

</p><p id="p_5">             In view of above, no contempt is made out. Accordingly, the

contempt petition is dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
