           SUIT FOR RECOVERY OF Rs. 2,08975.04 p
 JUDGMENT:
</pre>
<p id="p_1">             This judgment shall dispose off suit for recovery of a sum of Rs.

2,08,975.04p.
</p>
<p id="p_2">1.           Present suit is filed under Order 37 CPC.
</p>
<p id="p_3">2.           Facts in brief as averred in the plaint are that the plaintiff is a

     banking company and the present suit has been filed by Sh. Mr.

     Muneesh Dhawan, duly authorised representative of the bank.

     Defendant approached the plaintiff bank for personal loan under Loan

     Scheme and accordingly plaintiff sanctioned a loan of Rs1,91,000/- in

     favor of the defendant. Defendant also executed loan agreement,

     Demand Promisory note,        loan agreement no. 9324229 and other

     documents all dated 15.07.06. Defendant agreed to repay the loan

     amount in 36 equal monthly installments of Rs.6,905/- each.            It is

     further contended that thereafter several cheques issued by the

     defendant were dishonored on presentation and defendant failed to

     adhere to the terms of the loan agreement.          As per the accounts

     maintained by the plaintiff bank, the defendant is liable to pay a sum

     of Rs.2,08975.04p. Plaintiff bank has sent a demand notice dated

     10.09.07 to the defendant but defendant did not gave any reply to that

     notice also. Therefore, plaintiff was constrained to file the present suit.

</p><p id="p_4">3.           Defendant was duly served by way of publication on 18.05.08.
</p><p id="p_5">      But no appearance was filed by the defendant within statutory time

     period.    As per order XXXVII Rule2 Sub Rule 3 CPC if defendant has

     not filed his appearance within statutory time period the allegations in

     the plaint shall be deemed to be admitted and the plaintiff is entitled

     to judgment forthwith.        Hence, the suit is decreed for a sum of

     Rs.2,08,975.04p.
</p>
<p id="p_6">4.             Plaintiff has prayed for pendente lite and future interest at the

     rate of 18% per annum. As per order 37 Rule 2(3) CPC interest can be

     awarded only up to the date of decree hence, pendente lite interest at

     the rate of 9% per annum is also awarded without any future interest.

</p><p id="p_7">5.             Hence the suit is decreed for the sum of Rs.2,08,975.04p along

     with pendente lite interest at the rate of 9% p.a. Cost of litigation is also

     awarded. Decree sheet be prepared accordingly.
</p>
<p id="p_8">               File be consigned to record room.</p><pre id="pre_2">



ANNOUNCED IN THE OPEN COURT
Today i.e. On 18.10.08                          (DHARMENDER RANA)
                                                CIVIL JUDGE : DELHI
