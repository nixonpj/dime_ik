<div class="doc_title">Amit vs State Of Haryana on 16 May, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">           IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA AT
                          CHANDIGARH
                              *****
</pre><p id="p_1">                                    CRM-M No.16450 of 2017
                                    Date of decision : 16.05.2017
Amit                                                                 ...Petitioner
                                              Versus
State of Haryana                                                    ...Respondent

CORAM: HON'BLE MR. AMOL RATTAN SINGH

Present:        Ms. Anita Balyan, Advocate, for the petitioner.
</p><p id="p_2">                Mr. Vikas Chopra, Deputy Advocate General, Haryana.
</p>
<p id="p_3">AMOL RATTAN SINGH J. (ORAL)
                This petition has been filed under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> Cr.P.C., seeking
'regular bail' to the petitioner in case FIR No.42 dated 20.02.2017, registered
at Police Station Beri, District Jhajjar, for the alleged commission of
offences punishable under <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_2">Sections 307</a>, <a href="/doc/1183069/" id="a_3">386</a> and <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_4">34</a> of the Indian Penal
Code, 1860 and <a href="/doc/73862/" id="a_5">Sections 25</a> of the Arms Act, 1959.
</p><p id="p_4">                Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that the petitioner
never having been named in the FIR, there is nothing actually to connect
him to the occurrence.
</p><p id="p_5">                Learned State counsel, upon seeing the police file brought by
HC Narender, submits that, as a matter of fact, the complainant himself on
the next day made a statement to the effect that though he could not name
the petitioner and his co-accused on the previous day on account of the fact
that he had received a fire arm injury and was not in a fit state of mind, he
specifically, thereafter, named him.
</p><p id="p_6">                Consequently, in view of what has been stated herein-above,
but without        making      any       comment upon the actual merits of the
contentions raised on either side, which would obviously be gone into by
the learned trial Court, I do not consider it appropriate to admit the
petitioner to bail, at this stage.
</p><p id="p_7">                Dismissed, at this stage.</p><pre id="pre_2">

                                                       (AMOL RATTAN SINGH)
16.05.2017                                                     JUDGE
adhikari
