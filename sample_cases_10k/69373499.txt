<div class="doc_title">Pratap Munda Alias Motka vs The State Of Jharkhand on 29 April, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">        IN   THE       HIGH     COURT         OF    JHARKHAND       AT    RANCHI
                           B. A. No. 9459 of 2014
        Pratap Munda @ Motka                           ..... ... Petitioner
                                     Versus
        The State of Jharkhand                           ..... ...    Opposite Party
                                  --------
               CORAM : HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE H. C. MISHRA
                                  ------
        For the Petitioner        :        M/s Mahesh Tewari &amp; Binod Kumar, Advs.
        For the State             :        A.P.P.
                                  --------

 6/ 29.04.2015</pre><p id="p_1">         Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned A.P.P. for the
        Prosecution.
</p><p id="p_2">                       The petitioner has been made accused for the offences under
        <a href="/doc/1119707/" id="a_1">Sections 395</a> and <a href="/doc/844968/" id="a_2">412</a> of the Indian Penal Code, in connection with Gua P.S.
        Case No. 22 of 2013 corresponding to G.R. No. 349 of 2013, S.T. No. 283
        of 2013.
</p><p id="p_3">                       The case relates to dacoity and the case was instituted against
        unknown.
</p><p id="p_4">                       Learned counsel for the petitioner has submitted that the petitioner
        has been falsely implicated in this case and has prayed for bail.
</p><p id="p_5">                       Learned counsel for the State has opposed the prayer for bail and
        has pointed out from the case diary that the petitioner has been identified in test
        identification parade.
</p><p id="p_6">                       In the facts of this case, I am not inclined to enlarge the petitioner,
        Pratap Munda @ Motka, on bail. Accordingly, his prayer for bail is rejected.
</p>



<p id="p_7">                                                                        ( H. C. Mishra, J.)
R.Kr.
</p>
