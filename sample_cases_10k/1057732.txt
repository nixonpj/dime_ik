<div class="doc_title">K.M.Chacko @ Saji Aged 28 Years vs State Of Kerala on 2 March, 2010</div><pre id="pre_1">       

  

  

 
 
  IN THE HIGH COURT OF KERALA AT ERNAKULAM

Crl.Rev.Pet.No. 686 of 2010()


1. K.M.CHACKO @ SAJI AGED 28 YEARS,
                      ...  Petitioner

                        Vs



1. STATE OF KERALA, REPRESENTED BY
                       ...       Respondent

                For Petitioner  :SRI.JIMMY GEORGE

                For Respondent  : No Appearance

The Hon'ble MR. Justice V.RAMKUMAR

 Dated :02/03/2010

 O R D E R
                      V. RAMKUMAR, J.
               ===================
                   Crl.R.P. No. 686 of 2010
               ===================
          Dated this the 2nd day of March, 2010.

                             O R D E R
</pre>
<p id="p_1">     In this revision filed under Section 397 read with Sec. 401

<a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_1">Cr.P.C</a>., the revision petitioner who was the sole accused in C.C.

No. 246 of 2001 on the file of the Judicial First Class Magistrate-I,

Hosdurg for offences punishable under <a href="/doc/267625/" id="a_2">Sections 324</a> and <a href="/doc/661069/" id="a_3">341</a>

challenges the conviction entered and the sentence passed

against him.
</p>
<p id="p_2">     2.    The case of the prosecution can be summarised as

follows:
</p>
<p id="p_3">     At about 11 a.m., on 21.01.2001, out of previous enemity

towards PW1, the accused wrongfully restrained PW1 at

Edathode-Mukkuzhi      Panchayat    mud     road    at  Koliyar   in

Thayyannur village and during the course of the same

transaction, the accused voluntarily caused hurt to PW1 on his

forehead, hand, legs and other parts of the body by using a

stone.    The accused have thereby committed the aforesaid

offences.
</p>
<p id="p_4"><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">Crl.R.P. No. 686/2010            -:2:-</span>




</p><p id="p_5">      3.      On the accused pleading not guilty to the charge

framed against him by the trial       court for the aforementioned

offences, the prosecution was permitted to adduce evidence in

support of its case.      The prosecution altogether examined 7

witnesses as P.Ws 1 to 7 and got marked 4 documents as Exts.

P1 to P4 and a material object as MO1.
</p>
<p id="p_6">      4.      After the close of the prosecution evidence, the

accused was questioned under Sec. 313 (1)(b) <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_4">Cr.P.C</a>. with

regard to the incriminating circumstances appearing against him

in the evidence for the prosecution.              He denied those

circumstances and maintained his innocence. He did not adduce

any defence evidence when called upon to do so.

</p><p id="p_7">      5.        The learned Magistrate, after trial, as per judgment

dated 22.08.2005 found the revision petitioner guilty of the

offences      and sentenced him to simple imprisonment for six

months under <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_5">Section 324</a> IPC and simple imprisonment for 15

days under <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_6">Section       341</a> IPC.     On appeal preferred by the

revision petitioner as Crl. Appeal No. 246 of 2005 before the

Sessions Court, Kasaragod, the learned Additional Sessions Judge

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">Crl.R.P. No. 686/2010            -:3:-</span>




(Adhoc-I) as per judgment dated 21.08.2009           confirmed the

conviction, but altered the sentence under <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_7">Section 324</a> IPC to

simple imprisonment for three months instead of six months.

The sentence imposed by the trial court for the offence under

<a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_8">Section 341</a> IPC was not interfered with. Hence, this Revision.

</p><p id="p_8">      6.      Even though the learned counsel appearing for the

revision petitioner   assailed on various grounds the conviction

entered against the revision petitioner, in as much as the

conviction has been recorded by the courts below concurrently

after a careful evaluation of the oral and documentary evidence

in the case, this Court      sitting in revision will be loathe to

interfere with the said conviction which is accordingly confirmed.

</p><p id="p_9">      7.      What now survives for consideration is the question

regarding the adequacy or otherwise of the sentence imposed on

the revision petitioner. Having regard to the facts and

circumstances of the case, I do not think that the revision

petitioner deserves penal servitude by way of incarceration for

the said conviction. I am of the view that      interests of justice

will be adequately met by imposing a sentence to be passed

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">Crl.R.P. No. 686/2010             -:4:-</span>




hereinafter. Accordingly, for his conviction under <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_9">Section 341</a>

IPC, the revision petitioner is sentenced to pay a fine of

Rs.1000/- (Rupees one thousand only) and on default to pay the

fine, to suffer simple imprisonment for 15 days.          For his

conviction under <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_10">Section          324</a> IPC, he is sentenced to

imprisonment till the rising of the court and to pay a

compensation of Rs.8000/- (Rupees eight thousand only) to PW1,

the injured. On default to pay the compensation, he shall suffer

simple imprisonment for three months.         The petitioner shall

deposit the fine amount as well as the compensation amount

within one month from today.
</p>
<p id="p_10">      In the result, this Revision is disposed of confirming the

conviction entered but modifying the sentence imposed as above.

</p><p id="p_11">      Dated this the 2nd day of March, 2010.
</p>
<p id="p_12">


</p><p id="p_13">                                         V.RAMKUMAR, JUDGE.
</p>
