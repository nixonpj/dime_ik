<div class="doc_title">Ravichandran vs The Revenue Divisional Officer on 21 June, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                        1

                          BEFORE THE MADURAI BENCH OF MADRAS HIGH COURT

                                              DATED: 21.06.2019

                                                      CORAM

                           THE HONOURABLE DR.JUSTICE ANITA SUMANTH

                                          W.P(MD)No.13989 of 2019

                 Ravichandran                                          ... Petitioner

                                                       Vs.

                 1.The Revenue Divisional Officer,
                   Aruppukottai Taluk,
                   Virudhunagar District.

                 2.The Tahsildar,
                   Taluk Office, Kariyapatti Taluk,
                   Virudhunagar District.

                 3.The Inspector of Police,
                   Mallankinaru Police Station,
                   Virudhunagar District.

                 4.The Assistant Director,
                   Geology and Mines and Minerals Department,
                   Virudhunagar District.                                    ... Respondents


                 PRYAER: Writ Petition filed under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the Constitution of India
                 praying for the issuance of a writ of Mandamus, directing the respondents
                 to release the petitioner's two wheeler Regn.No.TN 58 AX 4591 TVS XL
                 Super Heavy Duty which was seized on 15.05.2019 on the basis of the
                 representation given by the petitioner dated 13.06.2019 submitted to the
                 first respondent.
                             For Petitioner     :     Mr.G.Mariappan
                             For Respondents :        Mrs.J.Padmavathy
                                                      Special Government Pleader


http://www.judis.nic.in
                                                            2

                                                         ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">                            The petitioner is the owner of a TVS XL Super Heavy Duty (In

                 short 'vehicle') bearing Registration No. TN 58 AX 4591. On 15.05.2019,

                 the vehicle was seized by the third respondent on the ground that it was

                 transporting sand without a valid license. The vehicle is presently in the

                 custody of the respondent police.
</p>
<p id="p_2">

</p><p id="p_3">                            2. According to the petitioner, the above allegation is incorrect and

                 his stand will be vindicated in the course of enquiry, that is, according to

                 the learned Special Government Pleader, who appears for all the

                 respondents, presently on-going.
</p>
<p id="p_4">

</p><p id="p_5">                            3. It is represented that a case in Crime No.88 of 2019 has been

                 registered by the Sub-Inspector of Police, Mallankinaru Police Station,

                 Virudhunagar District.
</p>
<p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">                            4. Pending enquiry, I am of the view that the vehicle should be

                 handed back to the possession of the petitioner for the reason that the

                 respondents are not in a position to protect the same against the

                 onslaught of the elements.
</p>
<p id="p_8">

</p><p id="p_9">                            5. The third respondent is directed to release the vehicle forthwith

                 and in any event within a period of one week from today strictly subject to

                 the
http://www.judis.nic.in   satisfaction of the following conditions:-
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                            3</span>

</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">                      i. The petitioner is directed to remit a sum of Rs.5,000/- (Rupees
                          Five thousand only) to the fourth respondent for deposit into the
                          District Mineral Foundation Trust that has been constituted under
                          the aegis of the fourth respondent.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">                      ii. The petitioner is directed to file an affidavit of undertaking within
                          three days from today to the effect that he is the owner of the
                          vehicle, he will cause production of the vehicle in question before
                          the competent/concerned respondent (as the case may be) as and
                          when called for and that he will not alienate the vehicle in question
                          till the enquiry initiated is completed;
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">                      iii. The petitioner is directed to co-operate in the enquiry to the fullest.
                      iv. The fourth respondent/Assistant Director and Revenue Divisional
                          Officer are directed to pass final orders in the adjudication
                          proceedings, if any, within a period of forty five (45) days from
                          today.
</blockquote>


<blockquote id="blockquote_4">                          6. A Division Bench of this Court has, on 29.10.2018 issued a series

                 of detailed directions to the concerned authorities targeted at containing

                 illegal sand mining as well as measures to address and prevent such acts.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_5">                 The same are extracted hereunder:-
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_6">                                 “(i)The District Level Task Forces and Taluk Level Task
                          Forces, constituted pursuant to the order passed in WP(MD)No.
                          9806 of 2018 should follow the G.O.(Ms)No.135 Industries (MMA.1)
                          Department, dated 13.11.2009 in letter and spirit.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_7">                                 (ii)As stated in the above said Government Order, periodical
                          meetings will have to be held which is inclusive of action taken/to be
                          taken for the illicit mining.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_8">                                 (iii)Steps will have to be taken for dereliction of the duty by
                          the concerned officials.</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_9">                                 (iv)Taluk Level Task Forces shall also comply with the
http://www.judis.nic.in
                          directions issued in the Government Order by making frequent
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                             4</span>

                          surprise checks and submit their report to the District Level Task
                          Forces.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_10">                                 (v)The Taluk Level Task Forces shall meet every fortnight as
                          mandated in the Government Order.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_11">                                 (vi)The responsibility fixed in the Government Order will have
                          to be strictly construed and action will have to be taken against the
                          erring Village Administrative Officer, Tahsildar, Officer in-charge of
                          Department of Geology and Mining at District Level.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_12">                                 (vii)Action taken report will have to be sent by the District
                          Collector concerned for the purpose of taking necessary action. The
                          District Collector concerned shall take appropriate departmental
                          action by himself as per the Rules provided so.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_13">                                 (viii)Separate records will have to be maintained by the
                          Village Administrative Officer, Tahsildar and Officer in-charge of the
                          Department of Geology and Mining with respect to the cases
                          involving illicit mining.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_14">                                 (ix)As and when illicit mining is reported, the same will be
                          recorded in the records.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_15">                                 (x)The respective District Collectors will have to ensure by
                          making vide publicity of phone particulars assigned to the District
                          Level Task Forces and the Talk Level Task Forces, so that, the
                          general public can give their complaints. There should be affixture or
                          display of the phone particulars in the Collectorate, Taluk office,
                          Office of Deputy Director and Assistant Director of Geology and
                          Mining and that of the Village Administrative Officer.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_16">                                 (xi)Complainant will have to be intimated on the action taken
                          within a period of one week from the date of receipt of the
                          complaint. A complaint shall also be received even when made
                          through phone calls.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_17">                                 (xii)Complaints by an authorised person under <a href="/doc/112147/" id="a_2">Section 21</a> of
                          the Mines and Minerals (Development and <a href="/doc/1489134/" id="a_3">Regulation) Act</a>, 1957 are
                          to be made immediately and not later than one week from the date
                          of seizure.
</blockquote><p id="p_10">http://www.judis.nic.in
</p><p id="p_11">                                 (xiii)Whenever, a final report is filed for the offence under
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                                                             5</span>

                          <a href="/doc/1101188/" id="a_4">Section 379</a> IPC by the jurisdictional police before the jurisdictional
                          Magistrate, the same shall also be committed to the Special Court.
                          This is for the reason that it would be appropriate to deal with both
                          the police case and the private complaint by the same Court and in
                          order to avoid any possible conflict.
</p><p id="p_12">                                (xiv)The revenue officials at the time of seizure can issue a
                          memo to the person in-charge of the vehicle, mineral among other
                          things, indicating the seizure made, along with the date and time.
</p><p id="p_13">                                (xv)In so far as the seized vehicles are concerned, they shall
                          be produced before the concerned Magistrate Court by the revenue
                          authorities at the time of filing their respective complaints.
</p><p id="p_14">                                (xvi)Any application for release of vehicle etc., can only be
                          filed before the Special Court alone.
</p><p id="p_15">                                (xvii)Any violation of the above would constitute a contempt
                          of the order passed by this Court, for which, appropriate application
                          can either be filed before the First Bench of this Court or any other
                          Bench as per the direction of the Hon'ble Chief Justice.”



</p><p id="p_16">                          7. Directions No:(xiii) &amp; (xvi) above provide for action to be taken

                 before / by the Special Court, that is, as confined by the same Division

                 Bench on 11.01.2019 in a batch of writ petitions (W.P.(MD)No.22023 of

                 2018 (batch)), yet to be constituted. Till such time the Special Courts are

                 constituted, release of the seized vehicles may be sought before this

                 Court in terms of <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_5">Article 226</a> of the Constitution of India.

</p><p id="p_17">                          8. This Writ Petition is disposed in the above terms. No costs.</p><pre id="pre_2">
