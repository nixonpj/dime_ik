<div class="doc_title">Sunil @ Sonu Pandey vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 23 December, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                             CRA-3479-2015
             (SUNIL @ SONU PANDEY Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)


23-12-2015

Shri Anurag Prajapati, counsel for the appellants.
Heard on admission.
Admit. Shri Pradeep Singh, Government Advocate accepts
notice on behalf of respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">Let record of the trial Court be requisitioned.
Heard on I.A. No.25085/2015, an application under <a href="/doc/1116025/" id="a_1">Section
389(1)</a> of the Cr.P.C. filed on behalf of appellants Sunil @
Sonu Pandey, Chandrika Prasad Pandey and Rajiv @
Rajju Pandey for suspension of sentence and grant of bail.
On perusal of impugned judgment dated 5.12.2015 passed by
the Court of Second Additional Sessions Judge, Anuppur in
S.T. No. 49/2013, it reveals that appellant No. 1 has been
convicted under <a href="/doc/1947545/" id="a_2">Sections 448</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_3">323</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_4">34</a> of the IPC and
sentenced to R.I.for 2 months each with fine of Rs.500/- each,
in default of payment of fine, R.I. for 7 days. Appellant No. 2
has been convicted under <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_5">Section 323</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_6">34</a> of the IPC and
sentenced to R.I. for 2 months with fine of Rs.500/-. In default
of payment of fine R.I. for 7 days. Appellant No. 3 has been
convicted under <a href="/doc/513777/" id="a_7">Sections 451</a>, <a href="/doc/203036/" id="a_8">354</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_9">323</a> of the IPC and
sentenced to R.I. for 1 year with fine of Rs.500/-, R.I. for 2
years with fine of Rs.1000/- and R.I. for 2 months with fine of
Rs.500/-. The sentences are directed to run concurrently.
Learned counsel for the appellants submits that the
appellants have been released on bail by learned trial Court
under <a href="/doc/579488/" id="a_10">Section 389(3)</a> of the Cr.P.C. till 4.1.2016, therefore, it
has been prayed that substantive jail sentence of the
 appellants be suspended.
</p><p id="p_2">Learned Government Advocate for the respondent/State has
opposed the application.
</p><p id="p_3">However, Keeping in view the facts and circumstances of the
case, the quantum of sentence imposed upon the appellants
and the period already undergone by them, in the opinion of
this Court, it is a fit case for suspension of sentence and grant
of bail to the appellants. Consequently, I.A. No. 25085/2015 is
allowed.
</p><p id="p_4">It is directed that on depositing the fine amount and
furnishing a personal bond in the sum of Rs.40,000/- each
with one solvent surety in the same amount to the satisfaction
of the trial Court for their appearance before the registry of
this Court on 4.4.2016 and all other subsequent dates fixed
in this regard, the remaining part of the substantive jail
sentence imposed upon appellants Sunil @ Sonu Pandey,
Chandrika Prasad Pandey and Rajiv @ Rajju Pandey shall
stand suspended and they shall be released on bail.
Certified copy as per rules.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                              (C V SIRPURKAR)
                                                    JUDGE
