<div class="doc_title">Ramdev Kumar vs The State Of Jharkhand And Ors on 13 October, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">       IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
               Cr. Appeal (S.J.). No. 1241 of 2017

        Ramdev Kumar @ Ramdeo Kumar                            ...... Appellant
                                 Versus
        1.The State of Jharkhand
        2. Deodas Marandi
        3. Meree Murmu                                         ......     Respondents
                      ---------
        CORAM: HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE ANANT BIJAY SINGH
                      ---------
        For the Appellant                 :      Mr. N.P. Choudhary, Advocate
        For the State                     :      A.P.P.
        For the Respondent No.2           :      Mr. P.K. Paul, Advocate
                          ---------
        04/Dated: 13/10/2017
              This appeal has been filed under Section 14-A(2) of the SC/ST (Prevention
        of Atrocities) Act, 2015, being aggrieved and dissatisfied with the order dated
        28.06.2017</pre><p id="p_1"> passed by learned Special Judge, Jamtara, whereby bail petition of
        the petitioner was rejected.
</p><p id="p_2">              Under order dated 12.09.2017, appellant was directed to surrender before
        the court below on or before 09.10.2017 and a report was called for regarding
        the surrender of the appellant.
</p><p id="p_3">              Perused the report of District &amp; Additional Sessions Judge-1, Jamtara,
        which reveals that appellant has been surrendered before the court below on
        09.10.2017.
</p><p id="p_4">              Further, on 12.09.2017, learned counsel for the appellant was directed to
        take instruction from the appellant as to whether he is ready to pay Rs.
        1,00,000/- to respondent No.2.
</p><p id="p_5">              Learned counsel for the appellant has submitted that appellant is very
        poor person and he is not in position to pay Rs. 1,00,000/- to the respondent
        No.3, rather he is ready to pay Rs. 25,000/- to the respondent No.3.
</p><p id="p_6">              In the facts and circumstances of the case, the appellant above named, is
        directed to be released on bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs. 10,000/- (Rupees
        ten thousand) with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of
        learned Special Judge, Jamtara in connection with Bagdahari P.S. Case No. 14 of
        2016 corresponding to G.R. No. 884 of 2016 (S.C/S.T. No. 64 of 2017), subject to
        the condition that, and subject to the further condition that the appellant shall
        deposit Rs. 25,000/- (twenty five thousand) in the trial Court on the date of
        furnishing bail bond thereafter his bail bond shall be accepted.
</p><p id="p_7">              Thereafter, the trial Court will issue notice to the Respondent Nos.2 and 3
        and after their appearance the trial Court after proper verification shall release the
        aforesaid amount in favour of Respondent No.3- Meree Murmu.
</p><p id="p_8">              However, the aforesaid deposition will not prejudice of this case.
              Further, petitioner is directed to appear before the trial Court till charges has
        been framed as and when required, if he fails to appear without valid explanation it
        shall be open to the trial Court he shall cancell the bail of the appellant.
</p><p id="p_9">              Accordingly, this appeal stands allowed.
</p>


<p id="p_10">                                                               (Anant Bijay Singh, J.)
Satayendra/
 </p>
