<div class="doc_title">Hasibur Rahaman Khan vs Union Of India &amp; Anr on 23 October, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                                     ORDER SHEET

                                    AP 839 OF 2017
                          IN THE HIGH COURT AT CALCUTTA
                           Ordinary Original Civil Jurisdiction
                                    ORIGINAL SIDE


                               HASIBUR RAHAMAN KHAN
                                       Versus
                                UNION OF INDIA &amp; ANR.
                                       .........
</pre>

<p id="p_1">   BEFORE:
</p><p id="p_2">   The Hon'ble JUSTICE I. P. MUKERJI
   Date : 23rd October, 2017.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                                 Mr. P. Mukherjee, Mr. Mastafizur Rahaman,
                                                       Mr. S.K. Sariful Haque...for petitioner.

                                                      Mr. Maloy Kumar Das...for respondents.
</p>
<p id="p_4">


             The Court : An affidavit of service is on record. None appears for the

       respondents.
</p>
<p id="p_5">             Mr. Maloy Kumar Das, who is a regular counsel for the Eastern Railways,

       is directed by this court to represent this case on behalf of the Railway

       administration. The Railways are directed to regularise his appointment.

</p><p id="p_6">             This is a section 11 application.
</p>
<p id="p_7">             The petitioner is agreeable to an arbitrator being appointed in terms of the

       arbitration clause, which is clause 64[3] of the General Conditions of Contract of

       the Railways. The value of the claim is stated to be more than Rs.50 lakhs.

       Therefore, let a panel of arbitrators be appointed as provided by the above clause.

       The Railways are to nominate a sufficient number of persons, who may act as the

       petitioner's nominee arbitrator, within two weeks of communication of this order,

       out of this the petitioner, will make his choice within two weeks thereafter.

       Thereupon, the Railways will constitute the arbitral tribunal in accordance with

       the above clause so that it is positively constituted within eight weeks of

       communication of this order to the Railways.

</p><p id="p_8">             This application is, accordingly, disposed of.




</p><p id="p_9">                                                              (I.   P. MUKERJI, J.)

Pkd.
</p>
