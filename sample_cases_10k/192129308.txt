<div class="doc_title">Ram Pukar Paswan And Anr vs The State Of Bihar on 25 January, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                    Criminal Miscellaneous No.3621 of 2019
                     Arising Out of PS. Case No.-848 Year-2018 Thana- FORBESGANJ District- Araria
              ======================================================
           1. Ram Pukar Paswan s/o.Chandra Lal Paswan r/o. Village - Gandhi Tola,
              Manihari, P.S. Manihari, Dist. Katihar
           2. Munni Devi, wife of Ram Pukar Paswan
              r/o. Village - Gandhi Tola, Manihari, P.S. Manihari, Dist. Katihar
                                                                   ... ... Petitioner/s
                                                  Versus
              The State of Bihar

                                                        ... ... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :       Mr.Ramesh Kumar Singh
                 For the Opposite Party/s :       Mr.Shailendra Kumar
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE VIKASH JAIN
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   25-01-2019              Heard learned counsel for the petitioners and learned APP for
                   the State.
</p><p id="p_2">                         2. The petitioners apprehend their arrest for the offences alleged
                   under <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_1">Sections 406</a>/<a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_2">420</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_3">34</a> IPC registered in connection with
                   Forbesganj P.S. Case No. 848 of 2018.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                           3. It is submitted that the petitioners have received money from
                   the informant on the promise made by co-accuse Raju Baitha for
                   arranging employment as Railway T.T.E. to the informant. Out of the
                   total amount of Rs. 1,90,000/- only a sum of Rs. 45,000/- has been
                   refunded to the informant. It is submitted that the petitioners are in
                   no way connected with the Railway and in any event it is improbable
                   that the informant would have advanced money to the petitioners for
                   illegal object. There is considerable delay in institution of the FIR on
                   03.11.2018 for alleged transaction of some time in the year 2015.
                   The petitioners claim clean antecedents.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                         4. Be that as it may, having regard to the entirety of the facts and
                   circumstances, in the event of the petitioners arrest or surrender
                   before the court below within six weeks from the date of
                   communication of this order, let the above named petitioners be
                   released on bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs.10,000/- (ten thousand)
              Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.3621 of 2019(2) dt.25-01-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                          2/2</span>




                       each with two sureties of like amount each to the satisfaction of
                       learned CJM, Araria in connection with Forbesganj P.S. Case No. 848 of
                       2018. subject to the conditions as laid down under <a href="/doc/1144664/" id="a_4">Section 438</a> (2)
                       <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_5">Cr.P.C</a>. and also subject to the following further conditions:
</p>
<p id="p_5">                           i. That one of the bailors shall be a close relative of the petitioners.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                           ii. That the petitioners shall not indulge in any similar offence till
                       conclusion of the trial.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                          iii. That the petitioners shall co-operate with the investigation, if not
                       already concluded, and make themselves available as and when so
                       required and in case of failure, the State shall be at liberty to move for
                       cancellation of bail.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                                    iv. The petitioners shall remain physically present in Court
                       on each and every date during trial and in the event of failure on two
                       consecutive dates without sufficient reason,their bail bonds shall be
                       liable to be cancelled by the learned Court concerned.
</p>



<p id="p_9">                                                      (Vikash Jain, J)
Chandran/-
</p>
