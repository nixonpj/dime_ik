<div class="doc_title">Belal Sheikh And Ors vs The State Of Jharkhand on 25 November, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">            IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI

                       Cr. Revision No.1591 of 2017
                                    ----
              1. Belal Sheikh.
              2. Ajfarul Sheikh.
              3. Mojibur Rahman.                    ...Petitioners
                                    Versus
            The State of Jharkhand.                 .....Opposite Party
                                    ----
            Coram:     HON'BLE MR JUSTICE RONGON MUKHOPADHYAY
                                  ----------
           For the Petitioners            : Mr. Arun Kumar Sinha, Advocate
           For the State                  : APP
                                  -----
02/25.11.2017</pre><p id="p_1">    Heard the parties.
</p><p id="p_2">                      It has been submitted by the learned counsel for the petitioners
            that none of the learned courts below have properly appreciated the
            materials available on record before convicting the petitioners for the
            offence under <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_1">sections 341</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_2">323</a>, <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_3">324</a>, <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_4">307</a> and <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_5">34</a> of the Indian Penal
            Code. It has further been submitted that maximum sentence imposed
            upon the petitioners is of three years S.I. for the offence under sec-
            tion 307<a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_6"> of the Indian Penal Code</a>. It has further been submitted that
            petitioners are in custody since 13.11.2017.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                      Call for the Lower Court Records.
</p><p id="p_4">                      List this case under the heading "For Admission" after six
            months.
</p><p id="p_5">                      During pendency of the application, the petitioners, named
            above, are directed to be released on bail, on furnishing bail bond of
            Rs.10,000/- (Ten Thousand) only each, with two sureties of the like
            amount each to the satisfaction of learned Assistant Sessions Judge,
            1st, Pakur in connection with Sessions Case No. 148 of 2006, arising
            out of Pakur (Mufassil) P.S. Case No. 223 of 2005, G.R. Case No. 588
            of 2005.
</p><p id="p_6">                      Let the order be sent through Fax at the cost to be deposited by
            the petitioners.
</p><p id="p_7">                                                     (Rongon Mukhopadhyay,J)
            Rakesh/
 </p>
