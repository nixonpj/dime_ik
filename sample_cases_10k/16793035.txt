<p id="p_1">2   07-08-2013                 Heard learned counsel for the petitioners as well as

                   learned Additional Public Prosecutor for the State.

</p><p id="p_2">                               At the very outset, it is submitted by learned counsel for

                   the petitioners that petitioner No. 2 namely, Bhardul Ram wants to

                   withdraw his prayer and accordingly, the prayer for anticipatory

                   bail of petitioner No. 2 namely, Bhardul Ram stands dismissed as

                   withdrawn.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                               So far as petitioner Nos. 1, 3 &amp; 4 are concerned, they are

                   in-laws of the complainant and it appears that the dispute arose

                   between the complainant and her husband due to so-called illicit
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">            Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.23803 of 2013 (2) dt.07-08-2013</span>




                        relationship of her husband with petitioner No. 4.

</p><p id="p_4">                                    Considering the aforesaid facts and circumstances as well

                        as submission of the parties, let the petitioner Nos. 1, 3 &amp; 4,

                        named above, in the event of their arrest or surrender within four

                        weeks from today in the court below, be released on bail on

                        furnishing bail bonds of Rs 10,000/- (ten thousand) each with two

                        sureties of the like amount each in connection with Tandwa P.S.

                        Case No. 26 of 2012                 to the satisfaction of Chief Judicial

                        Magistrate, Aurangabad subject to conditions as laid down u/S

                        438(2) of the <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_1">Cr.P.C</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                              (Hemant Kumar Srivastava, J)
A.K.V./-
</p>
