<div class="doc_title">Matoo vs The State Of M.P. on 15 June, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                       CRA-1803-2003
                    (MATOO Vs THE STATE OF M.P.)


15-06-2015

Smt. Smita Arora, counsel for the appellant.
Shri Pradeep Singh, Government Advocate for the
respondent-State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">Learned Govt. Advocate for the respondent State filed written
objection to this second application (IA No. 5463/2015) for
suspension of sentence and grant of bail under Section 389
(1) of the Code of Criminal Procedure filed on behalf of the
appellant Matoo.
</p><p id="p_2">Copy of written objection is served upon learned counsel for
the appellant and it is taken on record.
</p><p id="p_3">The application is heard by the consent of the parties.
Appellant Matoo has been convicted under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_1">Section 302</a> of the
IPC and has been sentenced to undergo life imprisonment
and fine in the sum of Rs. 500/- together with sentence of
imprisonment of two months in default of fine.
His first application for suspension of sentence was dismissed
on merits on 28.6.2004.
</p><p id="p_4">It has been submitted by learned counsel for the appellant
that eye witnesses have stated that the appellant had dealt
three blows with Axe to the head and neck of the deceased;
however, in postmortem report only two blows were
confirmed. He has also laid emphasis on the fact that the
appellant has been in custody since 15.6.2002. As such, he
has already undergone 13 years of imprisonment and there is
no likelihood of this criminal appeal of the year, 2003 to be
heard finally in near future.
</p><p id="p_5"> Learned Government Advocate on the other hand has
opposed the bail application mainly on the ground that the
appellant had dealt two blows of Axe to the neck of the
deceased with brute force resulting in his death.
Considering the facts and circumstances of the case,
particularly the fact that the appellant has already undergone
13 years of imprisonment, in the opinion of this Court, he
deserves to be enlarged on bail.
</p><p id="p_6">Consequently, this second application for suspension of
sentence under <a href="/doc/970675/" id="a_2">Section 389</a> (1)<a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_3"> of the Code</a> of Criminal
Procedure is allowed.
</p><p id="p_7">It is directed that on depositing the fine amount and
furnishing a personal bond in the sum of Rs.50,000/- with
one solvent surety in the same amount to the satisfaction of
the trial Court for his appearance before the Registry of this
Court on 23.09.2015 and all other subsequent dates fixed by
the Registry in this regard, the remaining part of the
substantive jail sentence imposed upon him shall stand
suspended and he shall be released on bail.
Certified copy as per rules.
</p>
<pre id="pre_2">(SHANTANU KEMKAR)                              (C V SIRPURKAR)
      JUDGE                                         JUDGE
