<div class="doc_title">Md. Sagir vs The State Of Bihar on 26 September, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                   Criminal Miscellaneous No.12052 of 2014
                        Arising Out of PS.Case No. -172 Year- 2013 Thana -KOTWALI District- MUNGER

                  ======================================================
                  Md. Sagir Son Late Md. Aiyub Resident Of Churamba, P.S- Kotwali
                  (Basudebpur O.P), District- Munger.

                                                                                .... ....   Petitioner/s
                                                        Versus
                  The State Of Bihar

                                                       .... .... Opposite Party/s
                  ======================================================
                  Appearance :
                  For the Petitioner/s     : Mr.
                  For the Opposite Party/s : Mr.
                  =====================================================

                  CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE AHSANUDDIN
                         AMANULLAH

                                                  ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">5.   26.09.2014                       Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and

                  learned A.P.P. for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                      The petitioner seeks bail in Kotwali P.S. Case

                  No. 172 of 2013 dated 13.06.2013 instituted under Sections

                  25(1-A)/ 25(1-AA)/25(1-B) (a)/26((1)(2)(3) of <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_1">The Arms Act</a>,

                  1959.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                      Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that

                  though it is alleged that from his house in a plastic sack

                  certain furnished and semi-furnished parts of firearms have

                  been recovered but the house is not his own and he does not

                  reside in it and he has not been caught at the spot. It is

                  submitted that as per the F.I.R. the door of the room was open

                  and from under the cot, in a plastic sack, many incriminating
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.12052 of 2014 (5) dt.26-09-2014</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                           2/3</span>




             articles were recovered and thus if at all the recovery is

             genuine, the articles could not have been kept in an open room

             and the seizure itself is not in accordance with law as there is

             no independent witness even though the house is located in

             the village. It is submitted that the petitioner has clean

             antecedent and has surrendered before the Court himself on

             26.08.2013. It is submitted that even among the so called

             articles seized, except for a hand grill, no arms-manufacturing

             material has been recovered.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                  Learned A.P.P., upon going through the case

             diary, does not dispute the aforesaid fact that there is no

             independent witness in the seizure list and that no arms

             manufacturing material has been recovered.

</p><p id="p_5">                                  Considering the facts and circumstances of the

             case and submissions of learned counsel for the parties, let the

             petitioner be released on bail on furnishing bail bonds of Rs.

             10,000/- (ten thousand) with two sureties of the like amount

             each to the satisfaction of the learned Adhoc Additional District

             and Sessions Judge, IIIrd, Munger in Kotwali P.S. Case No. 172

             of 2013. The petitioner and the bailors shall execute bond with

             regard to good behaviour of the petitioner. The petitioner shall

             also give an undertaking to the Court that he shall not indulge

             in any criminal activity. Any violation of the terms and

             conditions of the bonds or the undertaking shall lead to

             cancellation of his bail bonds. The petitioner shall cooperate in
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">       Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.12052 of 2014 (5) dt.26-09-2014</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                                                 3/3</span>




                   the trial and be present before the Court on each and every

                   date. Failure to do so on two consecutive dates, without

                   sufficient cause, shall also lead to cancellation of his bail

                   bonds.</p><pre id="pre_2">


                                                                (Ahsanuddin Amanullah, J.)

P. Kumar
