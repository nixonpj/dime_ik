                   BENCH AT JAIPUR

       S.B. Criminal Miscellaneous (Petition) No. 6114/2016

Hasan @ Kala Son Of Shri Salemi By Caste Mev, R/o Dholete
Tehsil And Police Station Pahari District Bharatpur.
                                                                   ----Petitioner
                                   Versus
1.      State Of Rajasthan Through P.p.
2.      The Director General Of Police Rajasthan, Jaipur.
3.      The Inspector General Of Police Bharatpur Range.
4.      The Superintendent Of Police Bharatpur.
5.      The S.h.o. Police Station Pahari District Bharatpur.
                                                                ----Respondents</pre>


<p id="p_1">For Petitioner(s)        :     Mr. Ajay Tanwar for
                               Mr. Ashvin Garg
For Respondent(s)        :     Mr. Ganesh Saini, P.P.



</p><p id="p_2">          HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE PANKAJ BHANDARI

                                    Order

11/07/2019

</p><p id="p_3">1.   Petitioner has preferred this Criminal Miscellaneous Petition

seeking fair investigation in FIR No.89/2015 registered at Police

Station Pahari, District Bharatpur.

</p><p id="p_4">2.     Earlier also petitioner has preferred S.B. Criminal Misc.

Petition No.2014/2015, which was dismissed by the Court vide

order dated 23.09.2015. Charge-sheet has been filed against five

accused and the matter has been kept pending under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section

173(8)</a> Cr.P.C. against sixteen other persons.

</p><p id="p_5">3.   It is contended by counsel for the petitioner that police is not

proceeding with the case.
</p>
<p id="p_6">


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                    (Downloaded on 30/08/2019 at 10:23:41 PM)</span>
</p><p id="p_7">                                                                          (2 of 2)                   [CRLMP-6114/2016]



</p><p id="p_8">                                   4.    Apex Court in "<a href="/doc/1836621/" id="a_2">Sakiri Vasu vs. State of U.P. &amp; Ors</a>. AIR

                                   2008 SC Page 907" has held that the Judicial Magistrate has

                                   very wide powers to enquire about the investigation and for

                                   monitoring the investigation to ensure that the investigation is

                                   done properly. Apex Court has further observed that the High

                                   Court should discourage the practice of filing Writ Petitions or

                                   petitions under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_3">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C. simply because a person has a

                                   grievance that investigation has not been done properly by the

                                   Police. For this grievance, the remedy lies before the Magistrate

                                   concerned.
</p>
<p id="p_9">                                   5.    In view of the judgment of Apex Court, I am not inclined to

                                   entertain the misc. petition, the same is dismissed.

</p><p id="p_10">                                   6.    However, petitioner would be free to approach the concerned

                                   Magistrate to inquire about the progress of the investigation.



</p><p id="p_11">                                                                                            (PANKAJ BHANDARI),J

                                   Simple Kumawat /18
