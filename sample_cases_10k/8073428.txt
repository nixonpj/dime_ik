<div class="doc_title">Rajesh Chopra vs Anil Kumar And Another on 24 May, 2011</div><pre id="pre_1">CR No. 3452 of 2011 (O&amp;M)                                          -1-

        IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA
                     AT CHANDIGARH.


                            Civil Revision No. 3452 of 2011 (O&amp;M)
                            Date of Decision: 24.5.2011.


Rajesh Chopra                                       .......Petitioner


                                Vs.

Anil Kumar and another                            ......Respondents



CORAM:        HON'BLE MRS. JUSTICE SABINA


Present:      Mr. Manish Prabhaker, Advocate
              for the petitioner.
                          .....

SABINA, J.
</pre>

<p id="p_1">              Plaintiff has filed a suit for mandatory injunction

directing the defendant No. 1 to comply with the terms of the

settlement orally arrived at between the parties on 3.6.2008.

Now the suit is fixed for final arguments.       At this stage, an

application was filed by the plaintiff under Order 18 Rule 17 of

the Code of Civil Procedure for allowing the plaintiff to recall

Naresh Kumar as a witness to prove the original agreement to

sell dated 20.4.2008.
</p>
<p id="p_2">              Learned counsel for the petitioner has submitted that

at the time when the plaintiff had led his evidence, the original

agreement to sell in question was not in his possession. Now the

plaintiff was in possession of the agreement to sell dated

20.4.2008 and in order to prove the due execution of the same, it

was very essential for the plaintiff to examine Naresh Kumar,

attesting witness of the agreement to sell dated 20.4.2008.
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> CR No. 3452 of 2011 (O&amp;M)                                       -2-</span>

</p><p id="p_3">              After hearing learned counsel for the petitioner,

I am of the opinion that the instant petition deserves dismissal.

</p><p id="p_4">              Plaintiff closed his evidence on 6.1.2011.       The

witness, now sought to be examined by the plaintiff, is defendant

No.2. The plaintiff cannot force the defendant to appear in the

witness box to prove his case. Now the case is listed for

arguments and it appears that the application had been merely

filed to delay the proceedings.
</p>
<p id="p_5">              No ground for interference is made out.

              Dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><p id="p_7">                                              (SABINA)
                                              JUDGE
May 24, 2011
Gurpreet
