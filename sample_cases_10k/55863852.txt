<div class="doc_title">Bharatbhai Rambhai Vaghela vs State Of Gujarat on 25 September, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                 R/CR.MA/22887/2017                                                 ORDER




                IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

         CRIMINAL MISC.APPLICATION (FOR REGULAR BAIL) NO. 22887 of 2017

         ===============================================
                       BHARATBHAI RAMBHAI VAGHELA....Applicant(s)
                                          Versus
                             STATE OF GUJARAT....Respondent(s)
         ===============================================
         Appearance:
         MR RUTURAJ NANAVATI, ADVOCATE for the Applicant(s) No. 1
         MR.H.K.PATEL, APP for the Respondent(s) No. 1
         ===============================================

          CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE A.Y. KOGJE

                                       Date : 25/09/2017

                                          ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">         [1]          This application is filed by the applicant under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section
         439</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 for regular bail in
         connection with FIR registered as C.R.No.I-61 of 2008 with Gir-
         Gadhadha Police Station, Junagadh for the offence punishable
         under <a href="/doc/619940/" id="a_2">Sections 363</a>, <a href="/doc/796352/" id="a_3">366</a> and <a href="/doc/1279834/" id="a_4">376</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>

<p id="p_2">         [2]          Learned advocate appearing on behalf of the applicant
         submits that considering the nature of offence, the applicant may
         be enlarged on regular bail by imposing suitable conditions.
</p>

<p id="p_3">         [3]          On the other hand, the learned Additional Public
         Prosecutor appearing for the respondent-State has opposed grant
         of regular bail looking to the nature and gravity of the offence.
</p>

<p id="p_4">         [4]          Learned         advocates      appearing         on      behalf       of        the
         respective parties do not press for a further reasoned order.
</p>

<p id="p_5">         [5]          I have heard the learned advocates appearing on
         behalf of the respective parties and perused the papers. Following

         aspects are considered :-
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">                          I)      The prosecutrix and the applicant have already
                          married long back and are having a seven years old
                          daughter;
</blockquote><pre id="pre_2">                          II)     The offences of 2008;
                          III)    The affidavit of the prosecutrix is annexed at
                          page No.24;

</pre><blockquote id="blockquote_2">                          This Court has taken into consideration the law laid
         down by the Hon'ble Apex Court in the case of <a href="/doc/1563495/" id="a_5">Sanjay Chandra
         v. Central Bureau of Investigation</a> reported in [2012] 1 SCC
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">         40.

</blockquote><p id="p_6">         [6]              In the facts and circumstances of the case and
         considering the nature of the allegations made against the
         applicant in the First Information Report, without discussing the
         evidence in detail, prima facie, this Court is of the opinion that this
         is a fit case to exercise the discretion and enlarge the applicant on
         regular bail.
</p>
<p id="p_7">         [7]              Hence, the present application is allowed.                               The
         applicant is ordered to be released on regular bail in connection
         with C.R.No.I-61 of 2008 with Gir-Gadhadha Police Station,
         Junagadh on executing a personal bond of Rs.10,000/- (Rupees
         Ten Thousand Only) with one surety of the like amount to the
         satisfaction of the trial Court and subject to the conditions that he
         shall;
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_4">               [a]       not take undue advantage of liberty or misuse liberty;
               [b]       not act in a manner injurious to the interest of the
                         prosecution;
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_5">               [c]       surrender passport, if any, to the lower court within a
                         week;
</blockquote>

                [d]       not leave the State of Gujarat without prior permission of
                          the Sessions Judge concerned;
</p><p id="p_9">                [e]       mark presence before the concerned Police Station on
                          alternate Monday of every English calendar month for a
                          period of six months between 11:00 a.m. and 2:00 p.m.;
                [f]       furnish latest and permanent address of residence to the
                          Investigating Officer and also to the Court at the time of
                          execution of the bond and shall not change the residence
                          without prior permission of this Court;
</p>

<p id="p_10">         [8]               The authorities will release the applicant only if he is
         not required in connection with any other offence for the time
         being. If breach of any of the above conditions is committed, the
         Sessions Judge concerned will be free to issue warrant or take
         appropriate action in the matter.
</p>
<p id="p_11">         [9]               Bail bond to be executed before the lower Court having
         jurisdiction to try the case. It will be open for the concerned Court
         to delete, modify and/or relax any of the above conditions, in
         accordance with law.
</p>
<p id="p_12">         [10]              At the trial, the trial Court shall not be influenced by
         the observations of preliminary nature qua the evidence at this
         stage made by this Court while enlarging the applicant on bail.
</p>
<p id="p_13">         [11]              The application is allowed in the aforesaid terms. Rule
         is made absolute to the aforesaid extent.                          Direct Service is
         permitted.
</p>
