<div class="doc_title">B Saraswathi, Khammam Dist vs B Nageshwar Rao, Nalgonda Dist on 12 October, 2018</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: T.Sunil Chowdary</div>

<pre id="pre_1">       THE HON'BLE SRI JUSTICE T.SUNIL CHOWDARY

                    Tr.C.M.P.No.107 of 2017

ORDER:
</pre>
<p id="p_1">      This petition is filed under Section 24 of C.P.C. seeking to

withdraw O.P.No.78 of 2016 from the file of the Court of the Senior

Civil Judge, Huzurnagar, Nalgonda District, and transfer the same

to the file of the Court of the Senior Civil Judge, Kothagudem,

Khammam District.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">2.    In spite of service of notice, the respondent did not choose to

appear and oppose the petition. Hence, this Court is inclined to

pass orders on merits.
</p>
<p id="p_4">3.    Heard the learned counsel for the petitioner.


</p><p id="p_5">4.    A perusal of the record reveals that the marriage of the

petitioner was performed with the respondent on 08.03.2000 at

Chinthakuntla Village of Nereducherla Mandal, Nalgonda District,

as per Hindu Rites and Caste Customs.         Immediately after the

marriage, the petitioner joined the respondent to lead marital life.

Out of lawful wedlock, the petitioner and respondent were blessed

with two daughters.      For one reason or other, disputes arose

between the petitioner and respondent; therefore, the petitioner

has been residing at her parents' house in Somulagudem Village,

Palvancha Mandal, Khammam District. Basing on the complaint

lodged by the petitioner, the Station House Officer, Palvancha

Police Station, registered a case in Crime No.227 of 2014 against

the respondent and others.     The petitioner filed M.C.No.2115 of

2015, under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section 125</a> Cr.P.C., on the file of the Court of the III

Additional Judicial Magistrate of First Class at Kothagudem
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                      2</span>


against the respondent seeking maintenance. While things stood

thus, the respondent filed O.P.No.78 of 2016, under <a href="/doc/932494/" id="a_2">Section

13(1)(ia)</a> of Hindu Marriage Act, on the file of the Court of the

Senior Civil Judge at Huzurnagar against the petitioner for

dissolution of marriage between them.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><p id="p_7">5.      It is the case of the petitioner that she is facing much

difficulty to travel from Palvancha to Huzurnagar in order to

prosecute O.P.No.78 of 2016.         Invariably, the respondent has to

attend the Court of III Additional Judicial Magistrate of First Class,

Kothagudem, in view of pendency of M.C.No.2115 of 2015.


</p><p id="p_8">6.      While deciding the petitions of this nature, the Court has to

take into consideration the inconvenience likely to be caused to the

parties to the proceedings, more particularly, to the wife and

children.     As per the principle enunciated in <a href="/doc/128520049/" id="a_3">T.Gayatri Devi v.

Dr. Tallepaneni Sreekanth1</a>, Rachna Kanodia v. Anuk Kanodia2

and     <a href="/doc/1858688/" id="a_4">Sumita       Singh   v.   Kumar   Sanjay</a>   and   another3,   the

paramount consideration, in transfer of matrimonial cases, is the

convenience of the wife.
</p>
<p id="p_9">
</p><p id="p_10">7.      Having regard to the facts and circumstances of the case and

also the principle enunciated in the cases cited supra, I am of the

considered view that the relief sought by the petitioner deserves to

be granted.
</p>
<p id="p_11">
</p><p id="p_12">8.      In the result, the Transfer Civil Miscellaneous Petition is

allowed. O.P.No.78 of 2016 is withdrawn from the file of the Court


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">1</span>
  2013 (6) ALT 42 (SC)
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">2</span>
  2001 (7) Supreme 96
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">3</span>
  AIR 2002 SC 396
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_5">                                   3</span>


of the Senior Civil Judge, Huzurnagar, Nalgonda District, and

transferred to the file of the Court of the Senior Civil Judge,

Kothagudem, Khammam District, for disposal in accordance with

law. There shall be no order as to costs.
</p>
<p id="p_13">
</p><p id="p_14">9.    Consequently, Miscellaneous Petitions, if any, pending in

this Transfer Civil Miscellaneous Petition shall stand closed.


</p><p id="p_15">                                            _________________________
                                             T.SUNIL CHOWDARY, J
Date: 12.10.2018
