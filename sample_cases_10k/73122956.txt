                         Cr.M.P. No. 2972 of 2017
                                   -----
</pre><p id="p_1">            Kanthi Lal Mandal                   .... Petitioner
</p><p id="p_2">                                       -Versus-
</p>
<p id="p_3">           1. The State of Jharkhand
</p><p id="p_4">           2. Ashok Kumar Rajjak                 ......Opposite Parties
</p><p id="p_5">                                 ------
</p><p id="p_6">   CORAM:       HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE SHREE CHANDRASHEKHAR
</p><p id="p_7">                                  -----
</p>
<p id="p_8">                For the Petitioner      : Mr. Mukesh Kumar Sinha, Adv.
                For the State           : Mr. P.K. Appu, Adv.
</p><p id="p_9">                                        -----
</p>
<p id="p_10">06/09.01.2019          The petitioner is aggrieved of order dated 16.05.2017
                passed in Jharia P.S Case No.442 of 2015, corresponding to
                G.R. No.6306 of 2015 by which the court has taken
                cognizance of the offences puhishable under sections 420,
                406 and 506 I.P.C.
</p><p id="p_11">                      Plea raised by the petitioner is that accepting money
                for arranging employment would not constitute offences
                punishable under sections 420, 406 and 506 I.P.C.
</p><p id="p_12">                      The learned counsel for the petitioner has relied on a
                decision in "Vijay Sharma &amp; Anr. Vs. State of Bihar"
                reported in 2011 (1) PLJR 780 to fortify his aforesaid
                contention.
</p><p id="p_13">                      Mr. Praveen Kumar Appu, the learned A.P.P appears
                for the State.
</p><p id="p_14">                      After arguing for some time, the learned counsel for
                the petitioner seeks permission to withdraw this petition
                with liberty to the petitioner to raise the aforesaid ground
                and any other ground which may be available to him at the
                time of framing of the charge.
</p><p id="p_15">                      In view of the limited prayer made on behalf of the
                petitioner, Cr. M.P. No.2972 of 2017 stands dismissed as
                withdrawn with liberty to the petitioner, as prayed for.
</p>

<p id="p_16">                                             (Shree Chandrashekhar, J.)
         sudhir
