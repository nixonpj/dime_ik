<div class="doc_title">Smt. Bharti Ved vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh ... on 13 May, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">                     M.Cr.C. No.6797/2014
13.5.2014
      Shri K.S. Rajput, Advocate for the applicant.
      Shri Akhilesh Singh, P.L. for the respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">      This is the first bail application filed by the applicant
under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> of the Cr.P.C. for grant of anticipatory bail.
</p><p id="p_2">      The applicant apprehends her arrest in connection with
Crime No.216/2014 registered at P.S. Harda, District Harda
for the offence punishable under <a href="/doc/980159/" id="a_2">Sections 286</a>, <a href="/doc/1402213/" id="a_3">337</a>, <a href="/doc/386021/" id="a_4">201</a>, <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_5">34</a>
of I.P.C. read with Section 3/5 of Explosive Substance Act.
</p><p id="p_3">      As per prosecution, it is alleged against this applicant
that the applicant was having in possession of explosive
substance to make the countrymade fire crackers and rope
bomb. It is also alleged that applicant used to make the fire
crackers in the factory of co-accused. It is further alleged that
instead of using the fire crackers in the factory she brought
the material of fire crackers in her house where the material
got exploded.
</p><p id="p_4">      Learned counsel for the applicant has submitted that
the applicant is a poor worker of the factory. She was not
aware that such material could not be brought in her house.
She is only earning member of the family and if she is
arrested then her children will become orphans. It is further
submitted that she is ready to cooperate in the investigation.
She has no criminal past, therefore, she be released on
anticipatory bail.
</p><p id="p_5">      Learned counsel for State has opposed the application.
      On due consideration of the contention raised by the
learned counsel for the parties and overall facts and
circumstances of the case, I am of the considered view that it
       is a fit case to release the applicant on anticipatory bail.
      Therefore, without commenting on the merits of the case, this
      application is allowed and it is directed that in the event of
      arrest, the applicant shall be released on bail on her
      furnishing a personal bond in a sum of Rs. 25,000/-
      (Rs. Twenty Five Thousand only) with one surety in the
      like amount to the satisfaction of arresting officer.
</p><p id="p_6">            The applicant is directed to join the investigation
      immediately and fully co-operate with the investigation. She
      shall further abide by the other conditions enumerated in
      sub-section (2) of <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_6">Section 438</a> of Cr.P.C.
</p><p id="p_7">            In view of the ratio laid down by Hon'ble Apex Court in
      Siddharam        Satlingappa       Mhetre          Vs.   State   of
      Maharashtra and others JT 2010 (13) SC 247, this order
      shall remain in force till the end of the trial.
</p><p id="p_8">            Certified copy as per rules.
</p>
<p id="p_9">                                                    (G.S.Solanki)
                                                       Judge
psm
