<p id="p_1">     We have heard learned counsel for the parties.



     These appeals by special leave are directed against the final judgment

     and order dated 18.09.2007 passed by the High Court of Judicature

     for Rajasthan, Jaipur Bench, Jaipur in D.B. Civil Miscellaneous

     Appeal Nos. 1923 and 2207 of 2006 wherein the High Court, while

     allowing the appeals in part, did not interfere with the decree of

     divorce passed by the trial court.



     We have perused the judgment passed by the High Court as well as by

     the trial court. The High Court, while disagreeing with some of the

     findings of the trial court, has taken into consideration the

     hence ruled that the marriage stands dissolved. Further, the High
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                   2</span>

Court’s judgment also secures the future of the appellant and her

children, having directed the husband to pay an amount of Rs.12

lakhs towards alimony and maintenance.



In light of the peculiar facts and circumstances of this case, we see no

reason to interfere with the impugned judgment. The appeals are,

accordingly, dismissed.
