<p id="p_1">           S.B.CIVIL WRIT PETITION NO. 4550/2002
                            Shanker
                              Vs.
</p><p id="p_2">                   State of Rajasthan &amp; Ors.
</p>
<p id="p_3">
</p><p id="p_4">            Date of order             :            1.4.2015

           HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE SANDEEP MEHTA

Mr. Sanjay Mathur &amp; Mr. Manoj Pareek, for the petitioner.
Mr. S.R.Paliwal, A.G.C. for the respondents.

</p><p id="p_5">                            &lt;&gt;&lt;&gt;&lt;&gt;


     By way of the instant writ petition, the petitioner seeks to

assail the legality and validity of the award Annexure-4 dated

5.9.2001 whereby, the industrial dispute instituted at the

petitioner's instance was partly accepted and while holding the

petitioner's removal as amounting to illegal retrenchment

carried out in violation of the           mandatory procedure

prescribed under <a href="/doc/607947/" id="a_1">Section 25</a> of the Industrial Disputes Act, the

Tribunal instead of directing the petitioner's reinstatement in

service, ordered that he shall be entitled to compensation of

Rs. 15,000/- in lieu of reinstatement.

</p><p id="p_6">     Counsel for the petitioner submits that the impugned

order is absolutely illegal and unjustified. The petitioner's

services were utilized on daily wage basis for continuous

period of 4 years. He was retrenched from service without
                                    S.B.CIVIL WRIT PETITION NO. 4550/2002
                                                                   Shanker
                                                                         Vs.
</p><p id="p_7">                                                        State of Raj. &amp; Ors.
</p>
<p id="p_8"><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                   2</span>
following the mandatory procedure prescribed under the

<a href="/doc/500379/" id="a_2">Industrial Disputes Act</a>, and therefore, the Tribunal ought to

have directed the petitioner's reinstatement in service with all

consequential benefits rather than directing payment of

compensation in lieu of reinstatement.

</p><p id="p_9">      Per contra, learned counsel for the respondents opposed

the submissions advanced by the counsel for the petitioner.

</p><p id="p_10">      Heard and considered the arguments advanced by the

learned counsel for the parties. Since the petitioner was

retrenched from service way back in the year 1990, the

question of his reinstatement has virtually been rendered

academic. However, looking to the fact that the petitioner was

retrenched illegally after having served the department for

four years, this Court is of the opinion that the compensation

awarded by the Tribunal in lieu of retrenchment is on the

lower side and deserves to be enhanced. Thus, keeping in view

the guidelines laid down by Hon'ble Supreme court in B.S.N.L.

Vs.   Bhurumal    reported    in        2014    AIR    SCW-528,         the

compensation     awarded     to        the   petitioner    in    lieu    of

reinstatement deserves to be enhanced to Rs. 50,000/- from

the amount of Rs. 15,000/- as awarded by the Tribunal.

</p><p id="p_11">      The writ petition is partly allowed in the above terms.

The Tribunal's award/order dated 5.9.2001 is modified. The

amount of compensation of Rs. 15,000/- awarded to the
                                 S.B.CIVIL WRIT PETITION NO. 4550/2002
                                                                Shanker
                                                                      Vs.
</p><p id="p_12">                                                     State of Raj. &amp; Ors.
</p>
<p id="p_13"><span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                               3</span>
petitioner by the Tribunal in lieu of his reinstatement is

enhanced to Rs. 50,000/-. The amount as directed above shall

be paid to the petitioner within a period of two months with

interest @ 6% per annum on the unpaid amount from the date

of award till the date of actual payment. No order as to costs.

</p><p id="p_14">                                             (SANDEEP MEHTA), J.
</p>
