<div class="doc_title">Surendra Uikey vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 9 January, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                        1                           MCRC-52744-2018
                               The High Court Of Madhya Pradesh
                                         MCRC-52744-2018
                                       (SURENDRA UIKEY Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)


                      Jabalpur, Dated : 09-01-2019
                              Shri A.K. Chakarvarti, learned counsel for the applicant.
                          Ms. Shobna Sharma, Panel Lawyer for the respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">                          Heard with the aid of case diary.
</p><p id="p_2">                          This i s first bail application filed by applicant Surendra Uikey
                     under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of the Cr.P.C. for grant of bail in connection Crime
                     No.78/2018 registered at Police Station Chandameta, District-
                     Chhindwara (M.P) for the offence punishable under <a href="/doc/619940/" id="a_2">Sections 363</a>, <a href="/doc/796352/" id="a_3">366</a>,
                     <a href="/doc/1279834/" id="a_4">376</a> of IPC and Section 3/4, 5/6 of POCSO Act and <a href="/doc/120962339/" id="a_5">Section 5</a> of Immoral
                     Traffic (Prevention) Act.
</p><p id="p_3">                           As per the prosecution case, co-accused Ranjeeta and Shyamwati
                     introduced the co-accused Sangit to prosecutrix who was minor at the
                     time of incident. Co-accused Ranjeeta and Shyamwati made her talk to
                     co-accused Sangit on mobile. On 17/04/2018 co-accused Sangit and
                     Vishal came to co-accused Ranjeet'Ã‚Â™s house. She sent prosecutrix
                     with them. Co-accused Sangit and Vishal took the prosecutrix to
                     Chandameta on bike, where they kept her in the house of co-accused
                     Sangit's relative i.e., applicant Surendra Uikey. Where Sangit committed
                     rape with the prosecutrix. On the next day co-accused Sangit and Vishal
                     took her to Nasrullaganj, where they kept her in the house of Sangit's
                     relative, where also co-accused Sangit committed rape with her.
                     Thereafter Sangit took the prosecutrix to his aunt's, house and where he
                     kept her for one month and committed rape with her. Thereafter Sangit
                     took her to Hoshangabad, from where co-accused Lavkush and co-
                     accused Seema Kahar, Raju and Sangit took her to Kota, Rajasthan at co-
                     accused Chetram's house and they sold the prosecutrix to Chetram in
                     Rs.2.50 Lakhs. Thereafter, co-accused Chetram committed rape with the
                     prosecutrix.
</p><p id="p_4">                            Learned counsel for the applicant submits that the applicant has not
                     committed any offence and have falsely been implicated in the offence.
                     There is no evidence on record to connect the applicant with the crime.
                     Police only on the basis of memorandum of co-accused implicated the
                     applicant in the crime. Prosecutrix in her statement recorded by the
                     police under <a href="/doc/447673/" id="a_6">Section 161</a> of CrPC as well as by the Judicial Magistrate
                     First Class under <a href="/doc/497457/" id="a_7">Section 164</a> of CrPC did not state that applicant is also

                     involved in the crime. The applicant is in custody since 29/07/2018. The
                     charge-sheet has been filed and conclusion of trial will take time, so it is
                     prayed that the applicant be released on bail.
</p><p id="p_5">                           Learned counsel for the State opposed the prayer and submitted
                     that the applicant is also involved in the crime. So he should not be
                     released on bail.
</p><p id="p_6">                           After hearing learned counsel for the parties, considering the facts
                     and circumstances of the case and as to the fact that in the statement of the
                     prosecutrix recorded by the police under <a href="/doc/447673/" id="a_8">Section 161</a> of CrPC as well as
                     JMFC under <a href="/doc/497457/" id="a_9">Section 164</a> of CrPC it is not mentioned that applicant is
                     also involved in the crime and applicant is in custody since 29/07/2018
                     and charge-sheet has been filed and conclusion of trial will take time,
                     without commenting on the merits of the case the application is allowed
                     and the applicant is directed to be released on bail upon furnishing
                     personal bond in the sum of Rs.50,000/- (Rs. Fifty Thousand Only) with
                     one surety in the like amount to the satisfaction of the concerned
                     C.J.M/trial Court for his appearance before the trial Court on all such
                     dates as may be fixed in this behalf by the trial Court during the pendency
                     of trial.
</p><p id="p_7">                             This order will remain operative subject to compliance of the
                     following conditions by the applicant :
</p><p id="p_8">                             1. The applicant will comply with all the terms and conditions of
                     the bond executed by him;
</p><p id="p_9">                             2. The applicant will cooperate in the trial;
</p><p id="p_10">                             3. The applicant will not indulge himself in extending inducement,
                     threat or promise to any person acquainted with the fact of the case so as
                     to dissuade them from disclosing such facts to the Court or to the Police
                     Officer, as the case may be;
</p><p id="p_11">                             4. The applicant shall not commit an offence similar to the offence
                     of which he is accused;
</p><p id="p_12">                             5. The applicant will not seek unnecessary adjournments during the
                     trial; and
</p><p id="p_13">                             6. The applicant will not leave India without previous permission
                     of the trial Court.
