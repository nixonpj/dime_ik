<div class="doc_title">Ran Vijay Singh vs The State Of Bihar on 11 June, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.20136 of 2014
                        Arising Out of PS.Case No. -22 Year- 2014 Thana -DIGHWARA District- SARAN
                 ======================================================
                 Ran Vijay Singh Son of Late Kamleshwar Singh @ Kamleshwari Prasad
                 Singh Resident of Village - Raghopur Dumri Buzurg, P.S. - Nayagaon,
                 District - Saran at Chapra (Bihar).

                                                                                .... ....   Petitioner/s
                                                        Versus
                 The State of Bihar.

                                                                .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :   Mr. Rakesh Kumar, Adv.
                 For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr. Jitendra Kumar Ray-I, APP
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE SHIVAJI PANDEY
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   11-06-2014                   Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned

                 counsel for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                 In this case, the petitioner is seeking regular bail in

                 connection with Dighwara P.S. Case No. 22 of 2014 registered for

                 offence under <a href="/doc/796352/" id="a_1">section 366(A)</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_2">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code.

</p><p id="p_3">                                 Allegation has been made that the victim girl, Mamta

                 Kumari has been kidnapped for the purpose of marriage.

</p><p id="p_4">                                 The counsel for the petitioner submits that Mintu

                 Kumar Singh and the victim girl are still traceless. He further

                 submits that the petitioner is the father of Mintu Kumar Singh and

                 he is not involved in the incident and whatever has been done by

                 his son. He further submits that the responsibility cannot be

                 attached to the father of the son.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">            Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.20136 of 2014 (2) dt.11-06-2014</span><p id="p_5">

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                      2/2</span>




</p><p id="p_6">                                         Looking to the facts and circumstances of the case,

                        let the petitioner, above named, be released on bail on his

                        furnishing bail bond of Rs. 10,000/- (ten thousand) with two

                        sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of learned Chief

                        Judicial Magistrate, Saran at Chapra in connection with Dighwara

                        P.S. Case No. 22 of 2014.
</p>
<p id="p_7">


</p><p id="p_8">                                                       (Shivaji Pandey, J)
Mahesh/-
</p>
