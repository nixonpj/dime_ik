                 Having heard learned Additional Advocate General No.6 and

        all the counsels representing the respondents, the whole issue can now

        be wrapped up keeping in mind the decision, which was rendered in

        the case of Akhauri Bijay Prakash Sinha Vs. State of Bihar, reported

        in 2014 (2) PLJR 798 as well as the order of the Hon'ble Supreme

        Court passed in Civil Appeal No (s).6178-6181 of 2015, dated

        27.02.2017 and the consequential notification, which the State of

        Bihar itself has issued, vide letter no.1301, dated 06.07.2017, under

        the signature of Additional Secretary, Department of Education,

        Government of Bihar, a copy of which has been produced before this

        Court.
</p>
<p id="p_921">                 The latest notification of the Department of Education, which is

        letter no.1301, will now govern the issue because the State itself has

        decided to act upon the protection, which has been extended by the

        Hon'ble Supreme Court while disposing of the Civil Appeal No(s)

        6178-6181 in paragraphs 2 and 3, which read as under:
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">                         " These appeals arise out of challenge to the Bihar
                 State Universities (Amendment and <a href="/doc/187873126/" id="a_1">Validation) Act</a>, 2012
                 <a href="/doc/1054471/" id="a_2">(Bihar Act</a> 22 of 2012) which has been upheld by the High
                 Court by the impugned order. We do not find any ground
                 to interfere with the view taken by the High Court in
                 upholding the said Act.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">                         We however make it clear that the present status,
                 rank and pay of the appellants will not be disturbed. If
                 any lab assistant has been given designation of
            Patna High Court LPA No.317 of 2015 dt.01-08-2017

                                                   47/ 47




                           demonstrator, which he continues to hold till date, it will
                           not be withdrawn. They will not be entitled to any further
                           benefits in conflict with the impugned Act."


                           All these appeals stand disposed of now with a direction upon

                   the State authorities that they will abide by the decision dated

                   06.07.2017 contained in letter no.1301.

</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">                           Before parting, some of the counsels pointed out that quite a

                   University across the State have not yet implemented or carried out

                   the order of the Hon'ble Supreme Court and even the replacement

                   scales etc. have not been extended till date.

</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_4">                           Now that the clarity has been given by this Court in the order

                   passed today, it goes without saying that the State and the University

                   will take a follow up action which they are bound to do.

</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_5">                           All the Letters Patent Appeals stand disposed of in view of the

                   above.</blockquote>
