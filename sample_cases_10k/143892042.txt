              IN THE COURT OF SH. VIJAY KUMAR DAHIYA
       ADDITIONAL DISTRICT JUDGE­II: ROHINI COURTS: DELHI

PC­38/09

Raju Sethi
S/o Late Sh. Gopal Krishan Sethi
R/o 432, Kohat Enclave, Pitam Pura,
Delhi­110034.
                                             ..... Petitioner.

Vs.

1. State 
2. Smt. Shobha Sethi W/o Lt. Sh. Gopal Krishan Sethi
3. Ms. Manju Bakshi W/o Sh. Prem Bakshi
D/o Lt. Sh. Gopal Krishan Sethi
4. Ms. Sonia Sethi D/o Lt. Sh. Gopal Krishan Sethi
All R/o 432, Kohat Enclave, Pitam Pura,
Delhi­110034.                            .......   Respondents 

                                             Date of Institution:  17.08.09
                                             Date on which the
                                             case was reserved
                                             for order           :  15.06.10
                                             Date of Decision :  15.06.10
ORDER 
</pre>

<p id="p_1">1.           Present petition has been filed by the petitioner under <a href="/doc/1032616/" id="a_1">section 

     276</a> of Indian Succession Act for grant of probate.  It is disclosed in the 

PC­38/09                                                              Page 1/4
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                             2</span>




   petition   that     Lt.   Sh.   Gopal   Krishan   Sethi   R/o   432,   Kohat   Enclave, 

   Pitam Pura,Delhi­110034 was father of the petitioner.   That during his 

   lifetime,   Lt.   Sh.   Gopal   Krishan   Sethi     had   executed   a     Will   dated 

   29.03.08   vide   which   he   had   bequeathed     his   entire   movable   and 

   immovable   properties   includines   shares,   car,   etc,   in   favour   of   the 

   petitioner as per Schedule A annexed with the petition. 

</p><p id="p_2">2.          Notice   of   this   application   was   issued   to   the   State   through 

   Collector  and a copy was got affixed on the notice board of the Court 

   House on 16.03.10. Citation was also published in the newspaper "The 

   Statesman" dated 26.02.10 for information of general public but none 

   came forward to file any objection.  
</p>
<p id="p_3">3.          On   21.10.09,   statements   of  respondents   no.2,   3   and   4   was 

   recorded   separately   whereby   they   have   given   their   no  objection   for 

   grant of probate in favour of the petitioner in respect of the Will dated 

   29.03.08 executed by Late Sh. Gopal Krishan Sethi. 

</p><p id="p_4">4.          On  06.05.10 petitioner appeared in witness box as PW­1 and 

PC­38/09                                                                      Page 2/4
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                               3</span>




   filed   his   evidence   by   way   of   affidavit.   He   tendered   his   affidavit   as 

   Ex.PW­1/A wherein he reiterated all the facts as stated in the petition. 

   He also proved the original Will as Ex.PW­1/1, death certificate of Lt. 

   Sh. Gopal Krishan Sethi  as Ex.Pw­1/1A, share certificates as Ex.Pw­

   1/2 (coly) and RC of the maruti car as Ex.Pw­1/3. 

</p><p id="p_5">5.          Statement   of   PW­2   Sh.   Subhash   Chander   Kalia   was   also 

   recorded   who   was   attesting   witnesse   to   the   Will   dated   29.03.08 

   executed   by     Lt.   Sh.Gopal   Krishan   Sethi.           Thereafter,   petitioner 

   evidence was closed.
</p>
<p id="p_6">6.            Report   received   from   the   office   of   Collector     wherein   it   is 

   stated that  no immovable property of the deceased is subject matter 

   of the Will in question.
</p>
<p id="p_7">7.          I have heard the arguments and gone through the documents 

   placed on record.
</p>
<p id="p_8">8.          Having regard to the averments made in the petition and the 

   testimonies of the  witnesses, which has gone unrebutted,  I allow the 

PC­38/09                                                                        Page 3/4
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                                         4</span>




   present petition and it is ordered that  probate in respect of Will dated 

   29.03.08 be issued in favour of the petitioner as per schedule A filed 

   alongwith  the petition    subject  to their furnishing  requisite  court  fee, 

   identity proof and administration bond cum surety bond.  It is clarified 

   that in this petition question of title and ownership of the property in 

   question is not decided.  An inventory of the property of the deceased 

   shall be exhibited in the court within six months from the date of grant 

   of certificate and a statement of account shall be filed within one year 

   thereafter.
</p>
<p id="p_9">9.         File be consigned to record Room.</p><pre id="pre_2">



Announced in the Open Court                        (VIJAY KUMAR DAHIYA)
On this 15.06.10                                   ADJ­II : ROHINI : DELHI  




PC­38/09                                                               Page 4/4
