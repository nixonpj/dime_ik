<div class="doc_title">Sunil Rai &amp; Ors vs The State Of Bihar on 18 May, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                   Criminal Miscellaneous No.30724 of 2018
                           Arising Out of PS.Case No. -71 Year- 2017 Thana -SAHPUR District- PATNA
                   ======================================================
                   1. Sunil Rai Son of Late Kailka Rai
                   2. Vishok Rai
                   3. Ashok Rai Both are Sons of Ram Rup Rai
                      All are R/o Hawaspura, P.S. - Shahpur, District - Patna.

                                                                                  .... ....   Petitioner/s
                                                          Versus
                   The State of Bihar.

                                                                  .... .... Opposite Party/s
                   ======================================================
                   Appearance :
                   For the Petitioner/s     : Mr. Ghanshyam Tiwary
                   For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr. Sri Lalan Kumar
                   ======================================================
                   CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE DINESH KUMAR
                   SINGH
                   ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">02/   18-05-2018                            Heard learned counsel for the petitioners and

                   learned APP for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                            The petitioners are apprehending their arrest

                   in a case registered for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1258372/" id="a_1">Sections

                   147</a>, <a href="/doc/763672/" id="a_2">148</a>, <a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_3">149</a>, <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_4">341</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_5">323</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_6">504</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_7">506</a>, <a href="/doc/1101188/" id="a_8">379</a>, <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_9">307</a>, <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_10">120B</a> of the Indian

                   Penal Code and <a href="/doc/244673/" id="a_11">Section 27</a> of the Arms Act.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                            The prosecution case as per the fardbeyan of

                   Gopal Rai recorded by A.S.I. of Shahpur P.S. on 13.04.2017 at

                   6.00 A.M. at PMCH Surgical Ward is to the effect that on

                   12.04.2017 at 6.00 P.M. the informant's son Ratnesh Kumar was

                   gone to bring milk but on the way, Bega Rai, Subodh Rai, Bittu

                   Kumar, Golu Kumar and 5-6 unknown persons snatched the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Patna High Court Cr.M isc. No.30724 of 2018 (2) dt.18-05-2018                        2</span>




             motorcycle and Rs.350/- of the son of the informant. The

             informant's son came home and subsequently the passersby

             informed that the motorcycle is lying near a tree then the

             informant along with his son went there but one of the accused

             Govind Rai exhorted others to kill and then one of the accused

             started firing in the air. The informant and his son started fleeing

             away and one of them fired at the informant causing injury on

             the right knee. Thereafter the informant was taken to hospital.

             The petitioners' name sprang up during investigation.

</p><p id="p_4">                                        It is submitted by learned counsel for the

             petitioners that the petitioners are not named in the FIR. The

             name of the petitioners sprang up during investigation and there

             is no recovery from the petitioners. Though, the petitioners are

             accused in one another case in which they are on bail. It is

             further submitted that FIR named accused persons have been

             granted anticipatory bail by co-ordinate Benches of this Court

             vide Cr. Misc. Nos. 38927 of 2017 and 42950 of 2017.

</p><p id="p_5">                                        Learned APP submits that the name of the

             petitioners sprang up during investigation.

</p><p id="p_6">                                        Considering the fact that the FIR named

             accused persons have been granted anticipatory bail, let the above

             named petitioners be released on anticipatory bail in the event of
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">       Patna High Court Cr.M isc. No.30724 of 2018 (2) dt.18-05-2018                 3</span>




                   arrest or surrender before the learned Court below within a period

                   of twelve weeks from today, on furnishing bail bonds of

                   Rs.10,000/- (Ten thousand) each with two sureties of the like

                   amount each to the satisfaction of learned Additional Chief

                   Judicial Magistrate, Danapur in connection with Shahpur P.S.

                   Case No. 71 of 2017, subject to the conditions as laid down under

                   <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_12">Section 438(2)</a> of the Cr.P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><p id="p_8">                                              (Dinesh Kumar Singh, J)
    DKS/-
</p>
