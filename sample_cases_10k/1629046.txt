  IN THE HIGH COURT OF KERALA AT ERNAKULAM

WP(C).No. 30940 of 2010(N)


1. MEZ IMPEX,XI/66 A.B.NORTH EDATHALA PO,
                      ...  Petitioner

                        Vs



1. THE ASSISTANT LABOUR OFFICER, ALUVA
                       ...       Respondent

                For Petitioner  :SRI.P.RAMAKRISHNAN

                For Respondent  : No Appearance

The Hon'ble MR. Justice C.T.RAVIKUMAR

 Dated :08/10/2010

 O R D E R
                          C.T.RAVIKUMAR, J.
                        ----------------------------
                       W.P.(C)No.30940 of 2010
                        ----------------------------
                         Dated 8th October, 2010

                               JUDGMENT
</pre>
<p id="p_1">            The petitioner is a proprietory concern.        The workers

attached to it have submitted Exts.P1 to P4 applications for the purpose

of getting the registration as headload workers under Rule 26A(1) of the

Headload Workers Rules.      Thereafter, the petitioner was called upon to

file objections, if any, or to attend meeting on 8.9.2010 to raise his

objections. In response to the same the petitioner has submitted Ext.P6

that carried request for registration of the said four workers at the

earliest. The grievance of the petitioner is that despite the receipt of

Ext.P6 no action has been taken by the respondent for the purpose of

registering the said workers under Rule 26A. Evidently, the respondent

has received applications from four workers engaged by the petitioner.

Obvious, it was pursuant to the receipt of their applications that the

respondent has issued Ext.P5 to the petitioner.        The petitioner has

readily responded to the same through Ext.P6.               In the said

circumstances, the respondent is bound to take a decision on the

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">WP(C).No.30940/2010                2</span>


applications submitted by the workers attached to the petitioner.

Therefore, this writ petition is disposed of with a direction to the

respondent to consider and pass orders on Exts.P1 to P4 applications

after complying with the formalities required therefor expeditiously, at

any rate, within a period of four weeks from the date of receipt of a

copy of this judgment.
</p>
