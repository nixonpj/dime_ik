<div class="doc_title">Anvarkhan Rasidkhan Pathan vs The State Of Gujarat on 19 March, 2019</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: Umesh A. Trivedi</div>

<pre id="pre_1">            C/SCA/4701/2019                                               ORDER




            IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

              R/SPECIAL CIVIL APPLICATION NO. 4701 of 2019

==========================================================
                        ANVARKHAN RASIDKHAN PATHAN
                                   Versus
                           THE STATE OF GUJARAT
==========================================================
Appearance:
MR Y J PATEL(3985) for the Petitioner(s) No. 1
for the Respondent(s) No. 2,3
MR RASHESH RINDANI AGP (99) for the Respondent(s) No. 1
==========================================================

 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE UMESH A. TRIVEDI

                                  Date : 19/03/2019

                                     ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">       Since         this      application            is      preferred       by        the
petitioner even without there being any proper pleading
in it contending that there is any proposal for even
detaining the petitioner under PASA on the basis of the
FIR which is annexed by the petitioner in this petition,
which has been registered on 18.2.2019.
</p>

<p id="p_2">       It has been sought to be argued that this is the
single      solitary          offence    registered           in    the   year      2019.
However, earlier in the year 2015, present petitioner has
already been detained under the PASA as a Bootlegger. The
very     conduct         of    the   petitioner            dis-entitles           him    to
exercise the extra ordinary powers vested under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article
226</a>    of     the      Constitution         of        India    to    entertain          the
petition not only at the pre-execution stage but even at
the pre-proposal stage.
</p>



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                        Page 1 of 2</span>
<pre id="pre_2">            C/SCA/4701/2019                                         ORDER



       Mr.Y.J.Patel,          learned    advocate      for   the    petitioner
submits       that      co-accused      of    this    very    FIR    has        been
</pre><p id="p_3">preventively detained by an order where past incident in
his    case     has    been    considered.         Drawing   analogy       of    the
same, he apprehends that such order will be passed. In
absence of any basic fundamental facts with regard to the
same, even his apprehension appears to be not genuine.
Hence, this petition is rejected.
</p>

<p id="p_4">                                                        (UMESH A. TRIVEDI, J)
ASHISH M. GADHIYA
