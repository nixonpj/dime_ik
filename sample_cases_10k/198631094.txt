<div class="doc_title">Sanjay Kumar Mallik vs The State Of Bihar &amp; Ors on 6 May, 2011</div><pre id="pre_1">                  IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                              CWJC No.8080 of 2011
                 SANJAY KUMAR MALLIK, son of late Baleshwar Mallik,
                 resident of village- Madanpur, Police Station- Araria, District-
                 Araria.                                      .....Petitioner.
                                               Versus
                 1. THE STATE OF BIHAR.
                 2. The Principal Secretary, Welfare Department, Bihar, Patna.
                 3. The Principal Secretary, Maha Dalit Commission, Bihar, Patna.
                 4. The District Magistrate, Araria.
                 5. The District Welfare Officer, Araria.
                 6. The Sub-divisional Officer, Araria.
                 7. The Block Development Officer, Araria Block, District- Araria.
                                                          ..........Respondents.
                                            -----------
</pre>
<p id="p_1">02-   6.5.2011             Heard learned counsel for the parties. The petitioner

                 claims payment of arrears of honorarium while functioning as

                 Vikash Mitra in Madanpur Panchayat under Araria Block.

</p><p id="p_2">                 2.        We have perused the materials on record and

                 considered the submissions of learned counsel for the parties. In

                 view of the provisions of Section 11(3) of the Gram Panchayat

                 Act 2006, every Gram Panchayat is a body corporate with

                 perpetual succession and common seal and can sue and be sued

                 on its own name. As the Gram Panchayat has not been made a

                 party-respondent, the writ petition cannot proceed in its

                 absence. Secondly, the petitioner approached this Court without

                 exhausting the alternative remedy available to him in law.

</p><p id="p_3">                 3.        The writ petition is dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_4">

</p><p id="p_5">                                                 (S K Katriar, J.)
S.K.Pathak/
