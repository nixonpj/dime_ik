                                 versus

                               JUDGMENT
</pre>

<p id="p_1">BADAR DURREZ AHMED, J (ORAL)


</p><p id="p_2">1.      The counter affidavit handed over by Mr Yeeshu Jain, Advocate on

behalf of respondent No.2, 3 and 4 is taken on record. The learned

counsel for the petitioners does not wish to file any rejoinder affidavit

inasmuch as he would be relying on the averments made in the writ

petition.
</p>
<p id="p_3">


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">W.P.(C) No. 5001/2015                                               Page 1 of 3</span>
</p><p id="p_4"> 2.      The petitioners seek the benefit of <a href="/doc/39713/" id="a_1">Section 24(2)</a> of the Right to

Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation

and Resettlement Act, 2013 (hereinafter referred to as 'the 2013 Act')

which came into effect on 01.01.2014. A declaration is sought to the

effect that the acquisition proceeding initiated under the <a href="/doc/7832/" id="a_2">Land Acquisition

Act</a>, 1894 (hereinafter referred to as 'the 1894 Act') in respect of which

Award No. 04/2002-03 dated 03.04.2002 was made, inter alia, in respect

of the petitioners' land comprised in Khasra No. 23/3/2 measuring 18

biswas in all in village Pansali, Delhi shall be deemed to have lapsed.


</p><p id="p_5">3.      Though the respondents claimed that possession of the said land

was taken on 12.05.2000, the petitioners dispute this and maintain that

physical possession has not been taken. However, insofar as the issue of

compensation is concerned, it is an admitted position that it has not been

paid.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><p id="p_7">4.      Without going into the controversy of physical possession, this

much is clear that the Award was made more than five years prior to the

commencement of the 2013 Act and the compensation has also not been

paid. The necessary ingredients for the application of Section 24(2) of




<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">W.P.(C) No. 5001/2015                                            Page 2 of 3</span>
 the 2013 Act as interpreted by the Supreme Court and this Court in the

following cases stand satisfied:-
</p>
<p id="p_8">
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">        (1)      <a href="/doc/185065833/" id="a_3">Pune Municipal Corporation and Anr v. Harakchand
                 Misirimal Solanki and Ors</a>: (2014) 3 SCC 183;
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_2">        (2)      <a href="/doc/90662043/" id="a_4">Union of India and Ors v. Shiv Raj and Ors</a>: (2014) 6
                 SCC 564;
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_3">        (3)      Sree Balaji Nagar Residential Association v. State of
                 Tamil Nadu and Ors: Civil Appeal No. 8700/2013
                 decided on 10.09.2014;
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_4">        (4)      <a href="/doc/489216/" id="a_5">Surender Singh v. Union of India &amp; Others</a>: WP(C)
                 2294/2014 decided on 12.09.2014 by this Court; and

        (5)      Girish Chhabra v. Lt. Governor of Delhi and Ors:
</blockquote>                 WP(C) 2759/2014 decided on 12.09.2014 by this Court.<p id="p_9">


</p><p id="p_10">5.      As a result, the petitioners are entitled to a declaration that the said

acquisition proceedings initiated under the 1894 Act in respect of the

subject land are deemed to have lapsed. It is so declared.


</p><p id="p_11">6.      The writ petition is allowed to the aforesaid extent. There shall be

no order as to costs.</p><pre id="pre_2">


                                          BADAR DURREZ AHMED, J



SEPTEMBER 15, 2015                               SANJEEV SACHDEVA, J
