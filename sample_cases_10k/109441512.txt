<div class="doc_title">Amlesh Jha vs The State Of Bihar on 18 April, 2011</div><pre id="pre_1">                  IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                          Cr.Misc. No.1023 of 2011
                                 AMLESH JHA
                                   Versus
                             THE STATE OF BIHAR
                             -----------------
</pre><p id="p_1">4.   18.04.2011               Heard learned counsels for the petitioner,

                   informant and the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                              The petitioner is apprehending his arrest in a case

                   registered under <a href="/doc/1258372/" id="a_1">Sections 147</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_2">323</a>, <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_3">341</a>, <a href="/doc/1243353/" id="a_4">342</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_5">504</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_6">506</a>, <a href="/doc/1101188/" id="a_7">379</a>,

                   <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_8">498A</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                              The petitioner is ready to keep the informant as

                   wife with full dignity and honour.

</p><p id="p_4">                              It is further submitted that the petitioner works at

                   Delhi whereas, the wife is employed on the post of teacher

                   in her native village.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                              Learned counsel for the informant submits that

                   informant is reluctant to leave her job.

</p><p id="p_6">                              Let the petitioner and O.P.no.2 appear before this

                   court on 18th of May, 2011 at 2.15 P.M. in chambers.

</p><p id="p_7">                              Let no coercive steps be taken against the

                   petitioner in Bhairav Asthan P.S.Case No.04/10 pending in

                   the court of learned Additional Chief Judicial Magistrate,

                   Jhanjharpur.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                                                 (Dinesh Kumar Singh, J.)
        Nitesh
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> 2</span>
