                               The High Court Of Madhya Pradesh
                                         MCRC-35079-2019
                                           (JAIMANGAL SINGH Vs CHHOTELAL SARAOGI)

                      1
                      Jabalpur, Dated : 26-08-2019
                             Shri H.S. Ruprah, Advocate for the petitioner.
                             This petition has been filed by the petitioner under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section 407</a> of
                     Cr.P.C. for transfer of criminal revision no. 631/18 from Special Court,
                     Bhopal to the court of its original jurisdiction, Budhar, Distt.-Shahdol.
                             Issue notice to the respondent on payment of P.F. by both modes</pre>

<p id="p_1">                     within a period of seven working days.
</p><p id="p_2">                             Notice be made returnable in three weeks.
</p><p id="p_3">                             Heard on I.A. No. 15710/2019, which is an application for staying the
                     further proceedings in criminal revision No. 631/18 pending in the court of
                     Sessions Judge, Bhopal.
</p><p id="p_4">                             Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that learned lower court has
                     erred to transfer the case No. 631/2018 from special court Bhopal to the
                     court of its original jurisdiction, Budhar, Shahdol.
</p><p id="p_5">                             Respondent no.1 has preferred a private complaint before JMFC,

                     Budhar against the petitioner for the offence punishable under <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_2">section 420</a>,
                     <a href="/doc/1985627/" id="a_3">467</a>, <a href="/doc/556166/" id="a_4">468</a>, <a href="/doc/1466184/" id="a_5">471</a>, <a href="/doc/756306/" id="a_6">474</a> &amp; <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_7">120-B</a> of IPC. Learned trial court took cognizance
                     against the petitioner under the abovesaid sections. The petitioner filed a
                     revision before the court of Addl. Sessions Judge, Budhar challenging the
                     order dated 01.10.2015 in Cr.Case No. 1580/2015, which was registered as
                     Cr.R. No. 11/2016 and was pending before learned revisional court, Budhar.
                     During its pendency, the Hon'ble Supreme Court of India passed order dated
                     14.12.2017 in W.P. (Civil) No. 699/16, Ashwini Kumar Upadhyay Vs. Union
                     of India and another whereby learned Apex Court has directed all the states
                     of India to establish Special Courts to expedite the criminal cases pending
                     against the MPs and MLAs of the country. In pursuant to that order, the State
                     of Madhya Pradesh also established Special Court at Bhopal. When this


Digitally signed by PALLAVI SINHA
Date: 29/08/2019 10:30:31
                                                          2                     MCRC-35079-2019
                     court was established, the High Court of Madhya Pradesh issued a
                     memorandum No. 1A/3428/III-6-3/2018 dated 27.09.2018 to transfer all the
                     criminal cases pending against the MPs and MLAs to the Special Court. In
                     pursuant to this notification, the Sessions Court at Budhar transferred the
                     Cr.R. No. 11/2016 to the Special Court at Bhopal and the case was registered
                     as Criminal Revision No. 631/2018.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                               After considering the contention of the learned counsel for the
                     petitioner, it appears that this is not a case against any MLA. According to
                     abovesaid memorandum of High Court, cases pending against MPs and
                     MLAs has to be transferred but this case is neither against any MPs nor
                     MLAs. So, in these circumstances, the proceedings of Cr.R. No. 631/2018
                     shall remain stayed till next date of hearing.
</p><p id="p_7">                               List the matter after three weeks.
</p>
