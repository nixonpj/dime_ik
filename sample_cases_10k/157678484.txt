<div class="doc_title">Rahul Kumar @ Vikash Kumar vs The State Of Bihar on 16 November, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                              CRIMINAL MISCELLANEOUS No.45589 of 2019
                   Arising Out of PS. Case No.-118 Year-2018 Thana- BHAGWAN BAZAR District- Saran
                 ======================================================
                 RAHUL KUMAR @ VIKASH KUMAR Son of Mahendra Prasad Resident
                 of Mohalla Bartand, P.S.- Dhanbad, District- Dhanbad.

                                                                                ... ... Petitioner/s
                                                      Versus
                 The State of Bihar Bihar

                                                        ... ... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :     Mr.Mukesh Kumar Singh
                 For the Opposite Party/s :     Mr.Kanhaiya Kishroe (App 100)
                 For the Informant              Mr. Sachida Nand Rai
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE CHAKRADHARI SHARAN
                 SINGH
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">4   16-11-2019               Heard learned counsel for the parties.

</p><p id="p_2">                             This application for anticipatory bail arises out of

                   Bhagwan Bazar P.S. Case N0. 118 of 2018, disclosing

                   offences under <a href="/doc/619940/" id="a_1">Sections 363</a>,<a href="/doc/1629021/" id="a_2">368</a> and <a href="/doc/1153041/" id="a_3">370</a> of the Indian

                   Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                             It is the informant's case that he had allowed her

                   son to work as domestic help with co-accused Rajiv Prasad

                   on payment of a sum of Rs. 1,000/-. Subsequently, co-

                   accused Rajiv Prasad informed the informant that her son

                   had been sent to his brother-in-law, who is the petitioner in

                   the present application. At the instance of Rajiv Prasad, the

                   informant had talked on phone to her son, who is missing

                   and has yet not been recovered. It is not there in the First

                   Information Report that the informant's son had told her

                   that he was working with the petitioner. Over and above
          Patna High Court CR. MISC. No.45589 of 2019(4) dt.16-11-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                     2/2</span>




                   the allegation that co-accused Rajiv Prasad had told that

                   the informant's son was working with this petitioner, there

                   is no material to connect this petitioner with the alleged

                   offence.       Further, except that the informant's son is

                   missing, there is no allegation against the petitioner of

                   commission of any offence.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                             Considering         the       nature       of   accusation,   this

                   application is allowed.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                             Let the petitioner above named in the event of his

                   arrest/surrender within six weeks from today in the Court

                   below, be released on bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs.

                   10,000/-(ten thousand) with two sureties of the like amount

                   each to the satisfaction of the learned Chief Judicial

                   Magistrate, Saran at Chapra in Bhagwan Bazar P.S. Case

                   No. 118 of 2018, subject to the conditions as laid down

                   under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_4">Section 438(2)</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure.

</p><p id="p_6">                             This is subject to the condition that the petitioner

                    shall present himself before the Police/Court, as the case

                    may be, as and when required and in the event of failure

                    on his part to appear before the Court on two consecutive

                    occasions, his bail bonds shall be liable to be cancelled.




</p><p id="p_7">                                      (Chakradhari Sharan Singh, J)
arun/-
</p>
