                     dated July 13, 2016 passed by the learned 6th 
                     Additional   Sessions   Judge,   Mehsana   at 
                     Visnagar   in   Sessions   Case   No.10   of   2015,

              the Forensic Science Laboratory. The request 
              qua the same made by the petitioner to the 
              concerned Court, has been denied.

              2016.

              behalf of the respondent No.1­State. 
              Direct   Service   is   permitted  TODAY,   qua   the 
              respondent   No.2.   To   be   served   through   the  
              concerned Police Station."

<p id="p_8">              2.   It   appears   that   application   had   been 
              moved on account of complaint under Section  
              173   (8)   of   the   Code   of   Criminal   Procedure  
              urging   the   Court   concerned   to   further 
              investigation   in   respect   of   the   suicidal 
              note as it is his case that at no stage, the 
              Investigating   Officer   had   asked   him   to 
              produce   any   original   /   natural   writing   of  
              the   deceased.   It   is   allegation   that   the 
              writings are not of deceased and it has been  
              concocted by the accused. </p><pre id="pre_2">

              application imposing cost on the complainant 
              vide   order   dated   13.07.2016   and   therefore 
              this petition.  
</p>
<p id="p_10">              4.   On   verifying   from   the   Investigating 
              Officer, who is present, he confirms that at  
              no stage suicidal note has been sent to the 
              Forensic   Science   Laboratory.   It   is   as 
              admitted   that   natural   writings   of   the 
              deceased   had   not   been   called   from   the 
              complainant   and   therefore   there   was   no 
              occasion   for   sending   the   document   to   the  
              Forensic Science Laboratory. 
</p>
<p id="p_11">              5.   Learned   advocate   appearing   for   the 
              complainant submits that it is the family of  
              the deceased who is ready to produce natural  
              writings of his sister since deceased.  

</p><p id="p_12">              6.   Let   the   officer   concerned   collect   such  
              writings from the complainant following the 
              criminal   manual   and   send   the   same   to   the 
              Forensic   Science   Laboratory   which   shall 
              expeditiously   examine   the   same   and   shall 
              opine. The criminal proceedings in the form  
              of   sessions   case   will   continue   in   the 
              meantime. However, it shall not be concluded 
              without   opinion   of   the   Forensic   Science 
              Laboratory on this and additionally get the  
              report and without examining the handwriting

                  and is quashed.  
</p>
<p id="p_14">                  8.   The   petitioner   possesses   diary   of   her  
                  sister   which   will   be   produced   if   necessary 
                  and the Investigating Officer can also make  
                  a   request   to   the   University   where   she   had  
                  studied   and   obtained   degree   of   Bachelor   of 
                  Arts.  
</p>
<p id="p_15">                  9.   The   copy   shall   be   given   to   learned 
                  Additional   Public   Prosecutor   for   onward 
                  communication and for necessary execution on 
                  the   part   of   the   concerned   police   officer.  
                  The   entire   exercise   shall   be   completed   as  
                  soon   as   possible   but   latest   by   two   weeks 
                  from the receipt of copy of this order.
                          Direct service is permitted."
</p>
<p id="p_16">         2. This application has been moved by the applicant­

           original petitioner in view of the fact that an 

           application   was   moved   by   the   Investigating 

           Officer to part with the original suicidal note 

           of   the   deceased   to   enable   the   Investigating 

           Officer to send the same to the Forensic Science 

           Laboratory   for   comparing   with   the   material 

           collected.   The   trial   Court   concerned   by   passing

           such application :
</p>
<p id="p_17">

                  per the direction given by the Hon'ble High 
                  Court   to   I.O.   in                 Special   Criminal 
                  Application  No.6735   of   2016.   I.O.   have   to

                  complainant   or   where   deceased   was   studying 
                  and   send   to   F.S.L.   While   I.O.   seeking 
                  original Suicide Note of deceased which are  
                  already exhibited and part of record of case  
                  so prayer of I.O. is hereby rejected."
</blockquote>
<p id="p_18">         3. The   applicant­original   complainant   is   aggrieved 

           by the above quoted order and, therefore, he is 

           before this Court.

           counsel   appearing   for   the  applicant­original 

           complainant;   Shri   N.J.   Shah,   learned   Additional 

           Public   Prosecutor   on   behalf   of   the   respondent­

           State   and   Shri   Tejas   Satta,  learned   counsel 

           appearing   for   the                respondent   No.2­original 

           accused.
</p>
<p id="p_21">
</p><p id="p_22">         5. According   to   Shri   Darshan   Dave,  learned   counsel 

           appearing   for   the  applicant   and   even   Shri   N.J. </p><pre id="pre_3">

</pre><p id="p_23">           Shah,   learned   Additional   Public   Prosecutor,   the 

           impugned order amounts to nullifying the earlier 

           order. The trial Court itself would have sent the 

           suicidal note to the Forensic Science Laboratory 

           if it had not trusted the Investigating Officer.


</p><p id="p_24">         6. Shri   Tejas   Satta,  learned   counsel   appearing   for 

           the respondent No.2, urges that it is nothing but 

           a   harassment   to   the   respondent­accused   and   the 

           applicant has no locus standi. He has also urged 

           that   the   suicidal   note   is   admitted   by   the 

           respondent   No.2   and,   therefore,   there   is   no 

           requirement of sending it to the Forensic Science 

           Laboratory.
</p>
<p id="p_25">
</p><p id="p_26">         7. The   challenge   by   the   complainant   and   the 

           prosecution is to the suicidal note on the ground 

           that   it   is   a   doctored   piece   of   document.   This 

           Court   will   not   enter   into   that   controversy   at 

           this stage. The Court below ought to have known 

           that the whole exercise undertaken by the trial 

           Court   and   the   directions   issued   to   the 

           Investigating   Officer   were   for   the   purpose   of 

           examining the genuineness of the rival claims by

           sending the suicidal note to the Forensic Science 

           Laboratory   with   admitted   handwritings   of   the 

           deceased.   It   is   not   in   dispute   that   the 

           Investigating Officer would succeed in collecting 

           handwritings of the deceased after following the 

           due   procedure.   His   request   to   part   with   the 

           suicidal note in the wake of exhibited document, 

           as rightly pointed out by the learned Additional 

           Public Prosecutor, could have been directly sent 

           in   a   sealed   cover   either   through   a   trusted 

           employee or by any other acceptable mode to the 

           Forensic Science Laboratory. Instead of doing so, 

           the   trial   Court   has   refused   to   part   with   the 

           same. The impugned order surely would amount to 

           not obeying the dictum of this Court rendered on 

           September   21,   2016,   while   dealing   with  Special 

           Criminal Application No.6735 of 2016.


</p><p id="p_27">         8. Assuming that it was the prosecuting agency which 

           could have challenged such an order, if is does 

           not   so   do   it,   it   would   not   preclude   the 

           complainant   to   approach   this   Court,   especially 

           when  ex   facie  the   impugned   order   would   have 

           effect of nullifying the earlier order. </p><pre id="pre_4">

                 application is allowed. The impugned order dated 

                 October   07,   2016,   is   quashed   and   set   aside.   If 

                 the   trial   Court   has   not   sent   the   said   suicidal 

                 note to the Forensic Science Laboratory, the same 

                 shall   be   sent   in   a   sealed   cover   forthwith, 

                 without   further   loss   of   time,   through   an 

                 authorised person.
</p>
<p id="p_29">                                  Once   received,   the   Forensic   Science 

                 Laboratory,   Gandhinagar,   shall   ensure   that   by 

                 undertaking   the   exercise   as   provided   under   the 

                 law   and   rules   thereof,   the   said   suicidal   note 

                 shall be examined by comparing the same with the 

                 relevant   documents.   Such   exercise   shall   be 

                 completed   within   a   period   of   six   weeks 

                 thereafter. D.S. is permitted.
