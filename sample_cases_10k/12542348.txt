<div class="doc_title">Lallan Prasad vs State Of U.P. Thru Prin.Secy. Home ... on 3 September, 2019</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: Abdul Moin</div>

<pre id="pre_1">




HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT ALLAHABAD, LUCKNOW BENCH
 
 

?Court No. - 20
 

 
Case :- SERVICE SINGLE No. - 23907 of 2019
 

 
Petitioner :- Lallan Prasad
 
Respondent :- State Of U.P. Thru Prin.Secy. Home Lucknow And Ors.
 
Counsel for Petitioner :- Akhilesh Kumar Kalra,Rahul Kapoor,Shyam Mohan Pradhan
 
Counsel for Respondent :- C.S.C.
 

 
Hon'ble Abdul Moin,J.
</pre> 
<p id="p_1">Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned Standing Counsel appearing for respondents.
</p> 
<p id="p_2">The petitioner being aggrieved with the dismissal order dated 29.01.2019, a copy of which is Annexure-1 to the petition, is before this Court.
</p> 
<p id="p_3">Admittedly the petitioner has got an alternative remedy of preferring an appeal under the provisions of Rule 20 of the Uttar Pradesh Police Officers of the Subordinate Ranks (Punishment and Appeal) Rules, 1991.
</p> 
<p id="p_4">Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that he did not prefer an appeal and approached this Court directly inasmuch as the misconduct on the basis of which the petitioner is sought to dismissed cannot be considered to be misconduct taking into consideration the law laid down by Hon'ble Apex Court in the case of <a href="/doc/91938676/" id="a_1">K.S. Puttaswamy and another vs. Union of India and others</a> - (2017) 10 SCC 1.
</p> 
<p id="p_5">Having heard learned counsel for the contesting parties, this Court is of the view that once the petitioner has got a statutory remedy of preferring an appeal under the provisions of 1991 Rules consequently he would have to avail the same. This Court is not inclined to entertain the petition without the petitioner having exhausted the said remedy. In this view of the matter, the present petition is disposed of giving liberty to the petitioner to file an appeal before the appellate authority against the impugned order of dismissal dated 29.01.2019 within a period of one week from today. In case said appeal is filed the appellate authority shall proceed to decide the same in accordance with law and on merits within a period of six weeks from the date of filing of the appeal along with certified copy of this order.
</p> 
<p id="p_6">Order Date :- 3.9.2019
 
A. Katiyar
