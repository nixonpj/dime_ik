<div class="doc_title">498A/304B/34 Of The Indian Penal ... vs Re: Hrishikesh Samanta on 9 August, 2018</div><p id="p_1"><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                          1</span>


August 9, 2018
30 ARDR
(Allowed)                         CRM No. 5374 of 2018

                 In Re: An application for anticipatory bail under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_1">section 438</a> of the
                 Code of Criminal Procedure filed on 26/07/2018 in connection with
                 Mahishadal Police Station Case No. 152/2018 dated 11/06/2018
                 registered for investigation into offences punishable under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_2">Sections
                 498A</a>/<a href="/doc/653797/" id="a_3">304B</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_4">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code, 1960.

</p><p id="p_2">                         And

                 Re: Hrishikesh Samanta,
                                                                                      ... Petitioner
                 Mr.   Siladitya Sanyal,
                 Mr.   Brajesh Jha,
                 Mr.   Sujan Chatterjee,
                 Mr.   Soumik Pramanick,
                                                                              ... for the petitioner
                 Mr. Rana Mukherjee,
                 Mrs. Manashi Roy,
                                                                                    ..for the State


                       The   petitioner    seeks   anticipatory   bail   in     connection      with

                 Mahishadal Police Station Case No. 152/2018 dated 11/06/2018

                 registered for investigation into offences punishable under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_5">Sections

                 498A</a>/<a href="/doc/653797/" id="a_6">304B</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_7">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code, 1960.

</p><p id="p_3">                       The petitioner is the father-in-law of the victim.

                       The State produces the Case Diary and the general nature of

                 allegations levelled by the neighbours.

</p><p id="p_4">                       Considering   the    extent   of   petitioner's   involvement       in    the

                 commission of the alleged crime, there is no need to detain him in

                 custody.
</p>
<p id="p_5">


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                          1</span>
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                                      2</span>


    Accordingly, in the event of arrest, the petitioner will be granted

bail upon furnishing security of Rs.10,000/- (Rupees ten thousand

only) with two sureties of Rs.5,000/- (Rupees five thousand only)

each, one of whom must be local, to the satisfaction of the Arresting

Officer, subject to the conditions as laid down in <a href="/doc/1144664/" id="a_8">Section 438</a> (2)<a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_9"> of

the Code</a> of Criminal Procedure, 1973. In addition, the petitioner will

also report to the Investigating Officer at such time and place as may

be specified by the concerned police officer.

    The petition for anticipatory bail is allowed subject to the

conditions as indicated above.
</p>
<p id="p_6">    A certified copy of this order be immediately made available to the

petitioner subject to compliance with all requisite formalities.


</p><p id="p_7">     (Abhijit Gangopadhyay, J.)                 (Sanjib Banerjee, J.)




<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                                     2</span>
