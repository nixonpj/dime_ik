<p id="p_3">                   For the Appellant            : Mr. Kumar Vimal,Advocate
                   For the Respondents          : JC to Advocate General

                                              ....
</p><p id="p_4">                                                           Dated 12th May,2011


</p><p id="p_5">           1.      Heard learned counsel for the parties.

</p><p id="p_6">           2.      It appears from the facts of the case that the learned Single Judge has

           carefully examined not only the factual aspects of the matter but also the

           legal proposition and thereafter rightly held that the petition under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article

           226</a> is not the appropriate remedy for the petitioner.

</p><p id="p_7">           3.      In view of the involvement of civil dispute as well as in view of the

           nature of reliefs claimed, we are of the considered view that in the facts and

           circumstances of the present case, where the matter relates to contract and

           question of civil dispute is involved, the proper remedy is by way of filing a

           civil suit.
</p>
<p id="p_8">           4.      There being no merit, the appeal is dismissed.



</p><p id="p_9">                                                           ( Prakash Tatia, J.)




                                                              ( H.C.Mishra, J.)




G.Jha/
