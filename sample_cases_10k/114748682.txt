<div class="doc_title">Aditya Gaur And Others vs State Of Haryana And Another on 12 December, 2011</div><pre id="pre_1">Criminal Revision No.908 of 2011              1

  IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA AT
                  CHANDIGARH


                           Criminal Revision No.908 of 2011
                           Date of Decision : December 12, 2011


Aditya Gaur and others
                                                               ...Petitioners

                                    Versus

State of Haryana and another                                  ...Respondents


CORAM;       HON'BLE MR.JUSTICE ALOK SINGH

             1. Whether Reporters of local news papers may be allowed to see
                judgment?
             2. To be referred to the Reporters or not?
             3. Whether the judgment should be reported in the Digest?


Present:     Mr. H.S.Gill, Sr. Advocate
             with Mr.A.S.Gill, Advocate
             for the petitioners.

             Mr.Gourav Dhir, D.A.G.,Haryana.

             Mr.Arjun Lakhanpal, Advocate,
             for respondent No.2.

Alok Singh, J.
</pre>
<p id="p_1">             Present petition is filed assailing order dated 2.4.2011

passed by the Additional Sessions Judge, Kurukshetra, whereby, learned

Additional Sessions Judge while exercising the powers of revisional

Court was pleased to summon the accused-petitioners to face trial for the

offences punishable under <a href="/doc/653797/" id="a_1">Sections 304-B</a> IPC, 406, 498-A read with

<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_2">Section 34</a> IPC.
</p>
<p id="p_2">             The brief facts of the present case are that a private
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Criminal Revision No.908 of 2011         2</span>

complaint was filed by respondent no. 2 in the Court of learned

Additional Chief Judicial Magistrate, Kurukshetra. The Learned

Additional Chief Judicial Magistrate has summoned the accused-

revisionists to face the trial for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_3">Sections

406</a>, <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_4">498-A</a> read with <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_5">Section 34</a> IPC. However, learned trial Magistrate

did not summon the accused for an offence punishable under <a href="/doc/653797/" id="a_6">Section

304-B</a> IPC. Feeling aggrieved complainant had preferred a revision

before learned Sessions Judge, which was allowed vide impugned

judgment dated 2.4.2011 summoning the accused to face trial for an

offence punishable under <a href="/doc/653797/" id="a_7">Section 304-B</a> IPC as well.

</p><p id="p_3">               Mr. H.S.Gill, Sr. Advocate assisted by Mr. A.S.Gill,

Advocate has vehemently argued that before the Revisional Court

petitioners herein were neither impleaded as respondents nor were heard

by the Revisional Court before issuing the summoning order under

<a href="/doc/653797/" id="a_8">Section 304-B</a> IPC. Further argued that only Course open before the

Revisional Court was to remand the case for further enquiry to the

Magistrate as contemplated under <a href="/doc/534676/" id="a_9">Section 398</a> of the Cr.P.C. to find out

as to whether accused should be summoned to face trial under <a href="/doc/653797/" id="a_10">Section

304-B</a> IPC as well.
</p>
<p id="p_4">             Mr. Arjun Lakhanpal, Advocate appearing for respondent

no. 2. has fairly contended that if Magistrate has refused to summon the

accused or has discharged the accused, Revisional Court cannot take

cognizance against the accused or cannot issue the summoning order.

However, the only Course open to the revisional Court was to remand
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2"> Criminal Revision No.908 of 2011           3</span>

the matter to the Magistrate to pass fresh orders after holding fresh

enquiry as contemplated under <a href="/doc/534676/" id="a_11">Section 398</a> of the Cr.P.C.

</p><p id="p_5">             <a href="/doc/534676/" id="a_12">Section 398</a> Cr.P.C. reads as under:-
</p>
<p id="p_6">                    "On examining any record under <a href="/doc/1865117/" id="a_13">Section 397</a> or
             otherwise, the High Court or the Sessions Judge may direct
             the Chief Judicial Magistrate by himself or by any of the
             Magistrates subordinate to him to make,       and the Chief
             Judicial Magistrate may himself make or direct any
             subordinate Magistrate to make, further enquiry into any
             complaint which has been dismissed under <a href="/doc/1096902/" id="a_14">Section 203</a> or
             sub-section (4) of <a href="/doc/1905618/" id="a_15">Section 204</a> or into the case of any person
             accused of an offence who has been discharged:
</p><p id="p_7">                    Provided that no court shall make any direction under
             this section for inquiry into the case of any person who has
             been discharged unless such person has had an opportunity
             of showing cause why such direction should not be made."

             After the perusal of <a href="/doc/534676/" id="a_16">Section 398</a> Cr.P.C., this Court is of the

opinion that if learned Magistrate dismisses the complaint or refuses to

take cognizance     or to issue summoning order      or    discharges the

accused the Revisional Court for the reasons recorded in writing, can

remand the matter to the Magistrate to to find out as to whether accused

should be summoned or charged as the case may be to face trial for the

offences alleged to have been committed by him.
</p>
<p id="p_8">             If accused is not summoned by the trial Magistrate,

Revisional Court should dispose off the revision only after hearing the

accused sought to be summoned.
</p>
<p id="p_9">             Present petition is allowed. Impugned order is set aside.
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3"> Criminal Revision No.908 of 2011           4</span>

             Learned counsel for the parties have stated that now the trial

has been transferred to Patiala from Kurukshetra, therefore, matter

should be remanded to the Sessions Judge, Patiala to decide the revision

in the light of the observations made herein above.

</p><p id="p_10">             Parties are directed to appear before the Sessions Judge,

Patiala on 22.12.2011. Record of the revision shall be transmitted to the

Sessions Judge, Patiala by the Kurukshetra Court. Sessions Judge,

Patiala shall either himself hear the revision or shall transfer the same to

any other Additional Sessions Judge, who shall dispose off the revision

in accordance with law within 90 days from the date of first appearance

of the parties before the learned Sessions Judge, Patiala.</p><pre id="pre_2">



                                                      (Alok Singh)
December 12, 2011                                        Judge
BB
