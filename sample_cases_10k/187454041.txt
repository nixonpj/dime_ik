<div class="doc_title">Moti Lal Singh &amp; Ors vs The State Of Bihar on 4 May, 2010</div><pre id="pre_1">                   IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                Cr. Misc. No.11431 of 2010
             1. MOTI LAL SINGH SON OF LATE LAL BABU SINGH
             2. MUNCHUN KUMAR SON OF SRI HARI NARAIN SINGH
             3. LAFU KUMAR SON OF SRI PACHHU KAHAR
              ALL ARE RESIDENTS OF VILLAGE- PARTHU, P.S.                                PIPRA
             KALYANCHAK, DISTRICT-PATNA
                                             Versus
                                  THE STATE OF BIHAR
                                           -----------
</pre>
<p id="p_1"> 2     4-5-2010             Heard learned counsel for the petitioners and learned

                  counsel for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                            The prayer in this application is for grant of anticipatory bail

                  in Jehanabad Rail PS case no. 221/09 u/s 174(B), 153 and 147 of the

                  <a href="/doc/1022405/" id="a_1">Railway Act</a>. During the investigation the names of petitioners have

                  also transpired among members of the mob which committed mischief

                  and obstructed the railway track disturbing the running of the train not

                  on account of any happening with the railways but due to an accident

                  on the road of a TATA 407 vehicle near Potahi railway station.

</p><p id="p_3">                            It has been submitted that there is no legal evidence against

                  the petitioners except confessional statement of co-accused Tinku

                  Singh and Pintu Singh who have already been released on bail by the

                  Sessions Court.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                            It is not a fit case for grant of privilege of anticipatory bail.

                  The prayer is rejected. However, if the petitioners surrender within six

                  weeks and pray for regular bail then the same should be considered

                  expeditiously, preferably on the date of surrender itself, considering

                  that evidence is only of co-accused who have been allowed bail.


</p><pre id="pre_2">BKS/                                           (Shiva Kirti Singh, J.)
 </pre>
