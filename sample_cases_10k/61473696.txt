GAHC010047802019




                              THE GAUHATI HIGH COURT
   (HIGH COURT OF ASSAM, NAGALAND, MIZORAM AND ARUNACHAL PRADESH)

                                     Case No. : AB 674/2019

            1:MOHAMMAD ALI LASKAR
            S/O MIZAZUL HOQUE LASKAR, VILL-SAYEDBOND PART-III, P.O.-
            KALIBARI BAZAR, P.S. AND DIST-HAILAKANDI (ASSAM), PIN-788150

            VERSUS

            1:THE STATE OF ASSAM
            REPRESENTED BY THE PUBLIC PROSECUTOR, ASSAM

Advocate for the Petitioner   : MR. S B LASKAR

Advocate for the Respondent : PP, ASSAM




                                   BEFORE
                     HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE AJIT BORTHAKUR

                                            ORDER
</pre><p id="p_1">Date : 24-04-2019

      Heard Mr. H.A. Laskar, learned counsel for the petitioner as well as Mr. T.K. Mishra,
learned Additional Public Prosecutor, Assam for the State respondent.
</p>
<p id="p_2">       By this petition under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">section 438</a> of the Cr.P.C., the petitioner namely, Mohammad
Ali Laskar has prayed for grant of pre-arrest bail apprehending his arrest in connection with
Hailakandi P.S Case No. 70/2019 under <a href="/doc/1075683/" id="a_2">Section 115</a>/<a href="/doc/96849/" id="a_3">117</a> of the IPC.
</p>
<p id="p_3">       The case diary, as called for, is placed before the Court.
</p>
<p id="p_4">       Mr. H.A. Laskar, learned counsel for the petitioner submits that the petitioner alleged
                                                                                       Page No.# 2/2

by uploaded Facebook Posts out of frustration when the dead body of one Jubair Ahmed, who
was a deaf and dumb boy was recovered by police but failed to arrest the culprit, who
committed the crime.
</p>
<p id="p_5">       Mr. T.K. Mishra, learned Additional Public Prosecutor, Assam for the State respondent
fairly submits that the case diary shows that there is no justification for custodial
interrogation of the petitioner as the alleged Facebook Post was uploaded by the petitioner
out of frustration.
</p>
<p id="p_6">       Having considered the materials on the case diary and hearing the learned counsel for
both the sides, this Court finds that the custodial interrogation of the petitioner is not
necessary in the interest of further investigation in the case.
</p>
<p id="p_7">       Accordingly, it is provided that in the event of arrest, the petitioner, namely,
Mohammad Ali Laskar shall be released on interim pre-arrest bail of Rs. 20,000/- with one
suitable surety of the like amount to the satisfaction of the arresting authority, on the
following conditions:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">                      1.    That the petitioner shall appear before the investigating officer within
                           10(ten) days from today and co-operate in investigation;
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_2">                      2.     That the petitioner shall not, directly or indirectly, make any
                           inducement, threat or promise to any person acquainted with the facts
                           of the case so as to dissuade him from disclosing such facts to the
                           Court or to any police officer; and

</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">                      3.    That the petitioner shall not hamper in the investigation, or tamper
                           with the evidence of the case.
</blockquote>


<blockquote id="blockquote_4">       Return the case diary.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_5">       The anticipatory bail application stands disposed of.</blockquote>

<p id="p_8">                                                                               JUDGE
