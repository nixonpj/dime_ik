<div class="doc_title">Sintu Kumar @ Pintu Kumar vs The State Of Bihar on 23 May, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                   Criminal Miscellaneous No.27113 of 2018
                         Arising Out of PS. Case No. -170 Year- 2016 Thana -AIRPORT District- PATNA
                  ======================================================
                  1. Sintu Kumar @ Pintu Kumar, S/o Late Ranjan Mandal @ late Rajjan
                     Mandal, R/o Vill.- Shyam Tola Balupar, P.S.- Lakhisarai, District-
                     Lakhisarai.
                                                                     .... .... Petitioner/s
                                               Versus
                  1. The State of Bihar
                                                                .... .... Opposite Party/s
                  ======================================================
                  Appearance :
                  For the Petitioner/s     : Mr. Sanjeev Kumar, Advocate
                  For the Opposite Party/s : Mr. Suresh Prasad Singh, A.P.P.
                  ======================================================
                  CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE PRAKASH CHANDRA
                  JAISWAL
                  ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">03   23-05-2018                   Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned

                  APP for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                  The petitioner seeks bail in Patna Airport P.S. Case

                  No. 170 of 2016 registered under <a href="/doc/695990/" id="a_1">Section 364</a>, <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_2">120-B</a> of the Indian

                  Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                  Two Delhi based businessmen are said to have been

                  kidnapped by some unknown miscreants from the Jai Prakash

                  Narayan International Airport, Patna and during the course of

                  combing operation one Ranjit Mandal alias Ranjit Don was

                  apprehended who disclosed the name of the petitioner as his

                  accomplice in the occurrence.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                  It is submitted by learned counsel for the petitioner

                  that the petitioner is quite innocent. He has no concern with the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">        Patna High Court Cr.M isc. No.27113 of 2018 (03) dt.23-05-2018</span>




<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                  -2-</span>



                    aforesaid occurrence. He is neither named in the F.I.R. nor he was

                    apprehended on the spot. There is nothing in the record indicating

                    the complicity of the petitioner in the occurrence barring the

                    confessional statement of Ranjit Mandal alias Ranjit Don which

                    has no evidentiary value in the eye of law. Though two criminal

                    cases have been lodged against the petitioner but he is on bail in

                    the aforesaid cases. The petitioner has been languishing in custody

                    since 26.10.2016. The aforesaid Ranjit Mandal alias Ranjit Don

                    has been enlarged on bail by a coordinate bench of this court in

                    Cr. Misc. No.29115 of 2017 vide order dated 31.07.2017.

</p><p id="p_5">                             On the other hand, learned APP for the State opposed the

                    bail petition.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                              Considering the facts aforesaid, the above named petitioner

                    is directed to be enlarged on bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs.

                    10,000/- (Ten thousand) with two sureties of the like amount each

                    to the satisfaction of the learned Additional Chief Judicial

                    Magistrate-XV, Patna in connection with Patna Airport P.S. Case

                    No. 170 of 2016.
</p>
<p id="p_7">
</p><p id="p_8">                                            (Prakash Chandra Jaiswal, J)
    Trivedi/-
</p>
