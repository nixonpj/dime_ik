<div class="doc_title">Wpms No. 2046 Of 2012 vs Let Notices May To All The on 27 September, 2012</div><pre id="pre_1">WPMS No. 2046 of 2012
Hon'ble Sudhanshu Dhulia, J.
</pre><p id="p_1">      Mr.    Bhupesh      Kandpal,    Counsel,
present for the petitioners.
</p><p id="p_2">      Let notices may to all the respondents.
      Counter affidavit to be filed within
three weeks.
</p><p id="p_3">      List this matter after three weeks in the
daily cause list.
</p><p id="p_4">      The main contention of the petitioner is
that the impugned order dated 27.7.2012
which has been passed by Information
Commissioner is totally without jurisdiction
as single member of the Commission has no
power to pass such an order under the <a href="/doc/671631/" id="a_1">Right
To Information Act</a>, 2005.
</p><p id="p_5">      The second limb of argument of the
petitioner is that Right to <a href="/doc/1965344/" id="a_2">Information Act</a>,
2005 visualises that an order has to be
passed by a Commission and a single
member does not constitute a Commission.
He further submits that in view of the latest
decision of the Hon'ble Apex Court in <a href="/doc/19607639/" id="a_3">Namit
Sharma v. Union of India</a> decided on
13.9.2012 order can only be passed by a
bench which is consisted of a judicial
member and a non-judicial member.
</p><p id="p_6">      Prima facie there seems to be
substance in these arguments. Consequently
the operation and effect of order dated
27.7.2012 (Annexure No. 1 to the writ
petition) shall remain stayed till further
orders of this Court.
</p>

<p id="p_7">                  (Sudhanshu Dhulia,J)
                          27.9.2012
Kuldeep
