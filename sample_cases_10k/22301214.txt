<div class="doc_title">Ramendra Kumar Jha vs The State Of Bihar Thru. Cbi. on 19 March, 2010</div><pre id="pre_1">                   IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                    Cr.Misc. No.7329 of 2010
                   RAMENDRA KUMAR JHA son of late Yatindra Nath Jha
                   r/o Bardaha P.S. Ghailar Dist. Madhepura at present residing at
                   B-43, Police Colony,Anisabad, P.S.Gardanibagh, Dist. Patna
                                                                 ...Petitioner.
                                                  Versus
                             THE STATE OF BIHAR THRU. CBI.
                                               -----------
</pre><p id="p_1">2   19.3.2010                A supplementary affidavit has been filed on behalf of the

                petitioner. Let it be kept on record.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                              Heard Mr. D.K.Sinha, learned senior counsel appearing for the

                petitioner and Mr. Bipin Kumar Sinha, learned counsel appearing for the

                C.B.I.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                              Petitioner apprehends his arrest in a case instituted under

                <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_1">sections 420</a>, <a href="/doc/1985627/" id="a_2">467</a>, <a href="/doc/556166/" id="a_3">468</a>, <a href="/doc/1466184/" id="a_4">471</a>, <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_5">120B</a> of the Indian Penal Code as also under

                <a href="/doc/86648814/" id="a_6">section 13</a> (2) read with <a href="/doc/1101716/" id="a_7">section 13(1)(d)</a> of the Prevention of Corruption Act

                1988.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                              It is submitted by the learned counsel for the petitioner that

                according to the prosecution case, petitioner is said to have received an

                amount of Rs. 20,3000/ towards the fake claim of Insurance. He denied the

                allegation and claims that as a matter of fact he being a private person

                provisions under the <a href="/doc/1331755/" id="a_8">Prevention of Corruption Act</a> is not application against

                him. It is also stated that the petitioner is an old man aged about 70 years. He

                further submits that with almost similar allegation co-accused Braj Kishore

                Jha has been extended privilege of anticipatory bail by a Bench of this Court

                by order dated     2.7.2007, passed in Cr. Misc. No. 27803 of 2007 vide

                annexure-2. Learned counsel for the petitioner undertakes that he is prepared

                to deposit Rs. 20,300/-in cash subject to out come of the trial.

</p><p id="p_5">                              Learned counsel for the C.B.I. though opposed the prayer for
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                    -2-</span>




           anticipatory bail but has not disputed the aforesaid position.

</p><p id="p_6">                         Be that as it may, let the above named petitioner in the event of

           his arrest or surrender within four weeks from today , be enlarged on bail on

           furnishing bail bond of Rs. 10,000/- (ten thousand) with two sureties of like

           amount each to the satisfaction of Special Judge, C.B.I. , North Bihar, Patna

           in R.C. No. 5(A) of 2006/Spl. Case No. 7 of 2006, subject to the conditions

           laid down under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_9">section 438(2)</a> Cr.P.C. and further subject to the condition

           that petitioner shall deposit Rs. 20,000/- in cash in the court below subject to

           the out come of the case and the court below before accepting the bail bond

           will verify whether the amount has been deposited by the petitioner or not and

           only on being satisfied accept the bail bond of the petitioner.



</p><p id="p_7">                                           (Birendra Prasad Verma, J)




M.Rahman
 </p>
