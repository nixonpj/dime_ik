<div class="doc_title">Bhallu @ Ballu vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 19 October, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                           M.Cr.C.No.7621/2016
19.10.2016
               Shri P.Newalkar, learned Counsel for the applicant

Bhallu @ Ballu.

               Shri Rahul Vijaywargiya, learned Panel Lawyer for the

non-applicant/State.

               Heard with the aid of the case-diary.

                                  ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">               This is a First bail application under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of the

Cr.P.C for grant of bail in connection with Crime No.6/2016

registered at Police Station Tanda, District Dhar for the offence

punishable under <a href="/doc/619940/" id="a_2">Sections 363</a>, <a href="/doc/796352/" id="a_3">366</a> and <a href="/doc/1677485/" id="a_4">376(2)(N)</a> of the IPC and

POCSO Act.
</p>
<p id="p_2">               After arguing at length, learned Counsel for the

applicant seeks permission to withdraw this application with this

liberty that he will renew the prayer after recording of evidence of

prosecutrix.
</p>
<p id="p_3">               Prayer accepted.
</p>
<p id="p_4">               Thus, the application is hereby dismissed as withdrawn

with the aforesaid liberty.
</p>
<p id="p_5">               C.c. as per rules.</p><pre id="pre_2">


                                        (JARAT KUMAR JAIN)
ns                                             JUDGE
