ORAL
ORDER</pre> 

 

 


 

<p id="p_1">1.		 Rule.
</p><p id="p_2">Mr.R.C.Kodekar, learned APP waives service of rule on behalf of the
respondents. With the consent of the learned Advocates appearing on
behalf of the respective parties, present application is taken for
final hearing today.
</p> 

 


 

<p id="p_3">2.		By
way of this petition, under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the Constitution of India,
the petitioner has prayed for an appropriate Writ, order directing
the respondents to investigate the offence lodged vide
C.R.No.I-66 of 2008 registered with Diyodar Police Station through
high ranking officer of rank of Superintendent of Police. It is also
further prayed for an appropriate order to direct the Investigating
Officer of C.R.No.I-66 of 2008 not to arrest the petitioner. In the
alternative it is prayed to permit the petitioner to surrender before
the learned Sessions Judge, Banaskantha and his application may be
directed to be decided on the same day by the learned Sessions Judge
on merits.
</p> 

 


 

<p id="p_4">3.		Considering
the fact that offence alleged is under <a href="/doc/42436223/" id="a_2">Section 3</a> of the Scheduled
Castes and <a href="/doc/25085007/" id="a_3">Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act</a> also and
considering the provisions of the Act itself, investigation is to be
carried out by the Officer not below the rank of Dy.S.P., therefore,
further direction in terms of para -17(A) is not required to be
considered. In the facts and circumstance of the case, alternative
prayer of the petitioner to permit the petitioner to surrender before
the learned Sessions Judge, Banaskantha and his bail application may
be directed to be decided on the same day, can be considered.
</p> 

 


 

<p id="p_5">4.		Under
the circumstances and in the facts and circumstances of the case,
Mr.B.B.Naik, learned Senior Advocate appearing with Mr.Kogje, learned
Advocate for the petitioner under the instructions from his client
has submitted that the petitioner will surrender before the learned
Sessions Judge, Banaskantha on 19th September, 2008 at
11.00 a.m.  The learned Sessions Judge, Banaskantha may take
petitioner into custody and decide and dispose of the application to
be submitted by the petitioner for releasing him on bail on the same
day in accordance with law and on merits and after giving opportunity
to concerned Investigating Officer. With these, present Special
Criminal Application is disposed of. Direct service is permitted.
</p> 

 


 

 


 

<p id="p_6">[M.R.Shah,J.]
