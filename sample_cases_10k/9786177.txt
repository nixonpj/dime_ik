                                              CHANDIGARH

                                                      Date of decision: 16.7.2013

                     Yash Pal Singh
                                                                                     ...Petitioner

                                         Versus

                     State of Punjab
                                                                                    ...Respondent

                     CORAM: HON'BLE MR.JUSTICE JITENDRA CHAUHAN

                     Present:      Mr.KS Dadwal, Advocate for the petitioner

                                               ****

                     Jitendra Chauhan, J. (Oral)
</pre>
<p id="p_1">                                   By filing the present petition under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> of the Code

                     of Criminal Procedure, the petitioner has sought pre-arrest bail in case

                     FIR No.48 dated 13.6.2013, registered under <a href="/doc/1326844/" id="a_2">Sections 409</a>, <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_3">120-B</a> of the

                     Indian Penal Code at Police Station City Banga, District SBS Nagar.

</p><p id="p_2">                                   Learned counsel for the petitioner contends that the

                     petitioner was working as Inspector in the Food and Supply Department.

                     His duty is only to check up the godowns, which are being maintained by

                     various employees. He is not         the custodian, therefore, he is not

                     responsible for any shortage in the wheat stock.

</p><p id="p_3">                                   Heard.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                   There are specific finding in the inquiry report conducted in

                     the matter by Deputy Director Field, Roop Nagar that a huge shortage in

                     the wheat stock has been found and the responsibility of the same is of

                     Yashpal petitioner being custodian. It is further found that the aforesaid

                     Inspector, Commission Agents and unknown persons have caused a

                     financial loss of Rs.20,34,79,914/- to the Government. The custodial

                     interrogation of the petitioner is necessary in this case to unearth the

                     racket.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                   The Hon'ble Supreme Court in State rep. <a href="/doc/48179074/" id="a_4">By the CBI vs.

                     Anil Sharma</a>, reported in (1997) 7 Supreme Court Cases 187 has held

                     as under:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">                                          "The custodial interrogation is qualitatively more
                                          elicitation oriented than questioning a suspect who is
                                          well ensconced with a favourable order under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_5">Section
                                          438</a> of Cr.P.C."</blockquote><p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">                                   In the present case, the Government has suffered a huge loss

                     of Rs.20,34,79,914/-, therefore, the custodial interrogation of the

                     petitioner is required. Keeping in view the inquiry report and the loss

                     suffered by the State, this Court is not inclined to grant any discretionary

                     relief to the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                                   Dismissed.
</p>
