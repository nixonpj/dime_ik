              AT CHANDIGARH

                                                Date of decision:22.12.2017

Narinder Singh Sidhu                                       ... Petitioner

                                 Vs.

State of Punjab &amp; others                                   ... Respondents


CORAM: HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE TEJINDER SINGH DHINDSA.


Present:    Mr. G.S. Benipal , Advocate for the petitioner.

</pre>
<p id="p_1">TEJINDER SINGH DHINDSA, J. (ORAL).
</p>
<p id="p_2">            The respondent/Punjab State Cooperative Bank Limited is

stated to be a society registered under the <a href="/doc/108006076/" id="a_1">Cooperative Societies Act</a> and is

financed and controlled by the State of Punjab and as such is an

instrumentality of the State Government.
</p>
<p id="p_3">            It is pleaded that petitioner joined the Bank as a Clerk in the

year 1987. Thereafter, he earned promotions and is now serving on the post

of Assistant Manager (Legal). It has been averred that the petitioner is due

to retire on 31.12.2017 upon attaining the age of superannuation i.e. 58

years.
</p>
<p id="p_4">            Claim set up in the instant petition is for grant of extension in

service beyond the normal age of superannuation of 58 years and on the

strength of the provisions under the Punjab State Cooperative Financing

Institutions Service Rules, 1958 as also Circulars dated 08.10.2012 and

27.02.2013 issued by the State Government as Annexures P-2 and P-3

respectively.</p><pre id="pre_2">




                                       1 of 2
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                  ::: Downloaded on - 24-12-2017 04:17:24 :::</span>
 CWP-29767-2017 (O&amp;M)                                                 -2-

</pre><p id="p_5">               Counsel has adverted to the documents placed on record at

Annexures P-7 to P-9 to contend that other employees of the respondent/

Bank have been granted benefit of extension.
</p>
<p id="p_6">               Categoric contention raised is that work and conduct of the

petitioner has been without blemish and there is no inquiry/departmental

proceedings pending against the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_7">               Counsel states that an option/representation has already been

preferred by the petitioner seeking extension in service beyond the age of

superannuation but the same is still pending consideration and no final

decision thereupon has been taken.
</p>
<p id="p_8">               In view of the submissions advanced by counsel and without

going into the merits of the case, I deem it appropriate to dispose of the

instant writ petition with a direction to respondent No.4 i.e. Managing

Director, Punjab State Cooperative Bank Limited to consider the claim of

the petitioner for extension in service beyond age of superannuation strictly

in accordance with law and to take a final decision within a period of two

weeks from the date of receipt of a certified copy of this order.

</p><p id="p_9">               Disposed of.
</p>
