<div class="doc_title">Mohanlal vs Rajkumar on 11 December, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                           MA-525-2015
                        (MOHANLAL Vs RAJKUMAR)


11-12-2015

Counsel for the appellant and respondent/Insurance Company</pre>
<p id="p_1">submit that they have already negotiated and the matter can be
settled in the Lok Adalat but since the office is misconstruing the
previous order of this court, therefore, not listing the matter in the
Lok Adalat though there is no technical objection.
Counsel for the appellant has further pressed the I.A.
No.9029/2015, which is an application for condonation of delay in
filing the appeal.
</p><p id="p_2">There is a delay of 50 days in filing the appeal.
After hearing the learned counsel for parties and on the perusal of
the I.A, it is found that the appellant was prevented from filing the
appeal within time on account of the bona-fide reason. The delay is
unintentional and it has not taken place on account of any
deliberate lapse on the part of the appellant.
On due consideration, it is found that a good ground is made out
for condoning the delay.
</p><p id="p_3">Accordingly, IA No.9029/2015 is allowed subject to all just
exceptions and delay in filing appeal is condoned.
List before the National Lok Adalat tomorrow, if there is no other
technical objection.
</p>

<p id="p_4">                                          (PRAKASH SHRIVASTAVA)
                                                  JUDGE
