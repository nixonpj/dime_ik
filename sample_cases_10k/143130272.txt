<div class="doc_title">Girdaval @ Kallu vs State Of M.P. on 9 March, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                            1

            The High Court Of Madhya Pradesh
                    National Lok Adalat
                      CRR-490-2012
                (GIRDAVAL @ KALLU AND OTHERS Vs STATE OF M.P.)


Gwalior, Dated : 09.03.2019

      Shri Mayank Bajpai, learned counsel for petitioners.

      Shri R.K. Mishra, learned Public Prosecutor for respondent/State.</pre>

<p id="p_1">      Shri Ravindra Singh Rajpoot, learned counsel for respondents/

complainant.
</p>
<p id="p_2">      With concerned, heard finally.
</p>
<p id="p_3">      The present revision petition has been filed under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section 397</a> and

<a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_2">401</a> of Cr.P.C for setting aside and quashing the judgment and order

dated 06.02.2011 passed by Judicial Magistrate First Class, Bhitarwar,

District Gwalior in Criminal Case No. 563/2006 whereby petitioners

have been convicted under <a href="/doc/1133601/" id="a_3">Section 325</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_4">34</a>, <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_5">324</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_6">34</a> and <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_7">323</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_8">34</a> of IPC

and sentence accordingly. Appellate Court affirming the conviction

under <a href="/doc/1133601/" id="a_9">Section 325</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_10">34</a> and <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_11">323</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_12">34</a> of IPC whereby the petitioners have

been sentenced to undergo one year R.I with fine of Rs. 1000/- each and

fine of Rs. 500/- each respectively with default stipulation.

</p><p id="p_4">      Learned counsel for the petitioners submits that petitioners and

complainant-Amer Singh &amp; Naval Singh have moved the applications

for compromise under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_13">Section 320(2)</a> and <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_14">320</a> of Cr.P.C before this

Court. The applications are duly signed by the petitioners as well as

respondents/complainant and they submitted that they are ready to

compromise the matter. The parties have appeared before the Principal

Registrar of this Court for verification of the factum of compromise and
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                            2</span>

            The High Court Of Madhya Pradesh
                    National Lok Adalat
                      CRR-490-2012
               (GIRDAVAL @ KALLU AND OTHERS Vs STATE OF M.P.)


Principal Registrar has given report dated 06.03.2019 regarding

compromise, which is perused. Learned counsel for the parties submit

that parties want to live peacefully and with mutual existence.

</p><p id="p_5">      Learned counsel for respondents/complainant as well as

respondent/State have no objection, if the parties are at same page

regarding settlement.
</p>
<p id="p_6">      Counsel for the parties have relied upon the decision of Hon'ble

Supreme Court in the case of Gyan Singh Vs. State of Punjab, (2012)

10 SCC 303, Jagdish Chanana &amp; Ors Vs. State Of Haryana &amp; Anr

(2008) 4 SCC 511 and Nikhil Merchant Vs. Central Bureau of

Investigation &amp; Anr., 2008 (9) SCC 677 as well as of this Court in the

matter of Umaprasad Shrivas and Ors. Vs. State of M.P. &amp; Anr.,

2013 Legal Eagle 2352 and Ramvati and Anr. Vs. Smt. Sadhana and

Anr.,2016 (I) MPWN 159.
</p>
<p id="p_7">      Learned counsel for the parties also referred the judgment passed

by Hon'ble Apex Court in the case of Khursheed &amp; Anr. Vs. State of

U.P. &amp; Anr. reported in 2007 (4) RCR (Criminal) 495 wherein Hon'ble

Apex Court has allowed the compromise at appellate stage before the

Apex Court. They also relied the judgments rendered by this Court in

the case of Pawan Vs. State of M.P. passed in Criminal Revision No.

378/2015 vide judgment dated 10.03.2016, Gulab Singh Vs. State of

M.P. passed in Criminal Revision No.691/2014 vide order dated
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                 3</span>

                                  The High Court Of Madhya Pradesh
                                          National Lok Adalat
                                            CRR-490-2012
                                    (GIRDAVAL @ KALLU AND OTHERS Vs STATE OF M.P.)


                   16.11.2015 and judgment rendered by Punjab Haryana High Court in

                   the case of Hardeep Singh Vs. State of Punjab passed in Criminal

                   Revision No. 1149/2014 (O&amp;M) vide order dated 09.09.2014 and seeks

                   parity.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                             Offences under <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_15">Sections 323</a> and <a href="/doc/1133601/" id="a_16">325</a> of IPC are compoundable

                   with the permission of the Court and by the person to whom hurt is

                   caused. Here the victim wants to compromise the matter and the case

                   was precipitated spur of moment and no enmity or dispute was existing

                   between them. Therefore, considering the fact situation of the case, it is

                   better that the matter be compromised and parties may lead life

                   peacefully without fear of retaliation by either side. Therefore,

                   compromise is permitted while considering the fact situation as well as

                   judgments as referred above. The amount of fine, if deposited, shall be

                   refunded to petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_9">                             Consequently, revision is allowed. Both judgments dated

                   06/02/2011 and 29.06.2012 are hereby set aside. Bail bonds of the

                   petitioners stands discharged.
</p>
<p id="p_10">                             Accordingly, the petition (Criminal Revision No. 490/2012)

                   stands disposed of and allowed.
</p>
