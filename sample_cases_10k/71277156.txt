<div class="doc_title">Venkataiah Balimdi vs Union Of India on 28 November, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                                             W.P. No. 22268 of 2018

                             IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT MADRAS

                                                DATED : 28.11.2019

                                                      CORAM

                                THE HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE P.D. AUDIKESAVALU

                                             W.P. No. 22268 of 2018
                                                      and
                                       W.M.P. Nos. 26096 and 26097 of 2018

                      Venkataiah Balimdi                                           ... Petitioner

                                                           -vs-

                      1. Union of India,
                         Represented by its Ministry of Corporate Affairs,
                         Shastri Bhawan,
                         Dr. Rajendra Prasad Road,
                         New Delhi - 110 001.

                      2. The Registrar of Companies,
                         Tamil Nadu, Chennai,
                         Block No. 6, B Wing, 2nd Floor,
                         Shastri Bhawan,
                         No. 26, Haddows Road,
                         Chennai - 600 006.                                     ... Respondents

                      Prayer:    Writ Petition filed under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the Constitution of
                      India praying to issue a Writ of Certiorarified Mandamus, calling for
                      the records of the Second Respondent relating to the impugned order
                      dated 08.09.2017, read with the updated list dated 01.11.2017 uploaded
                      and hosted on the website of the First Respondent in so far as the
                      Petitioner herein is concerned, quash the same as illegal, arbitrary and
                      devoid of merit and consequentially direct the Respondents herein to


                      1/10
http://www.judis.nic.in
                                                                            W.P. No. 22268 of 2018

                      permit the Petitioner to get reappointed as Director of any Company or
                      appointed as Director in any Company without any hindrance.

                             For Petitioner   : Mr. C.V. Shailandhran

                             For Respondents : Mr. V. Venkatesan,
                                               Central Government Standing Counsel

                                                     ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">                             Heard Mr. C.V. Shailandhran, Learned Counsel appearing for the

                      Petitioner and Mr. V. Venkatesan, Learned Central Government

                      Standing Counsel appearing on behalf of the Respondents and perused

                      the materials placed on record, apart from the pleadings of the parties.



</p><p id="p_2">                      2.     In this Writ Petition, the list dated 01.11.2017 published by the

                      Registrar of Companies, Tamil Nadu, Chennai uploaded in the website

                      of the Ministry of Corporate Affairs, New Delhi disqualifying the

                      Petitioner to hold the Office of the Directorship of a Company under

                      Section 164 (2) (a) of the <a href="/doc/1353758/" id="a_2">Companies Act</a> 2013, which came into effect

                      from 01.04.2014, is challenged with the consequential direction to the

                      Respondents to permit the Petitioner to get re-appointed or appointed

                      as Director in any other Company without any hindrance.



</p><p id="p_3">                      3.     The lists dated 08.09.2017 and 01.11.2017 published by the

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                      2/10</span>
http://www.judis.nic.in
                                                                                W.P. No. 22268 of 2018

                      Registrar of Companies, Tamil Nadu, Chennai were the subject matter

                      of challenge before this Court in a batch of cases in W.P.

                      No. 25455 of 2017 etc. batch [(2018) 6 MLJ 704] and came to be

                      disposed by this Court by order dated 03.08.2018, the operative portion

                      of which is extracted below:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">                             "29. In fine,

</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">                             (a)   When the New Act 2013 came into effect from

                             1.4.2014, the second respondent herein has wrongly given

                             retrospective effect and erroneously disqualified the

                             petitioner-directors   from   1.11.2016   itself   before    the

                             deadline commenced wrongly fixing the first financial year

                             from 1.4.2013 to 31.3.2014.</blockquote><p id="p_4">

</p><p id="p_5">                             (b)   By virtue of the new <a href="/doc/310282/" id="a_3">Section 164(2)(a)</a> of the 2013 Act

                             using the expression “for any continuous period of three

                             financial years” and in the light of <a href="/doc/1063221/" id="a_4">Section 2(41)</a> defining

                             “financial year” as well as their own General Circular

                             No.08/14 dated 4.4.2014, the first financial year would be

                             from 1.4.2014 to 31.3.2015, the second financial year would

                             be from 1.4.2015 to 31.3.2016 and the third financial year

                             would be from 1.4.2016 to 31.3.2017, whereas the second


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                      3/10</span>
http://www.judis.nic.in
                                                                               W.P. No. 22268 of 2018

                             respondent clearly admitted in paras 15 and 22 of the

                             counter affidavit that the default of filing statutory returns

                             for the financial years commenced from 2013-14, 2014-15

                             and 2015-16 i.e., one year before the Act 2013 came into

                             force. This is the basic incurable legal infirmity that

                             vitiates the entire impugned proceedings.

</p><p id="p_6">                             (c)   By virtue of the first proviso to <a href="/doc/1780467/" id="a_5">Section 96(1)</a> of the

                             2013 Act, Annual General Meeting for the year ending on

                             31.3.2017 can be held within six months from the closing of

                             financial year i.e., 30.9.2017, additionally in the light of

                             <a href="/doc/310282/" id="a_6">Section   164(2)(a)</a>   referring   to   “annual   return”    and

                             “financial statement”, the time limit to file annual return

                             under <a href="/doc/729918/" id="a_7">Section 92(4)</a> of 2013 Act is sixty days from Annual

                             General Meeting or the last date on which Annual General

                             Meeting ought to have been held, hence, the time limit to

                             file balance sheet under <a href="/doc/1049113/" id="a_8">Section 137(1)</a> of the 2013 Act is

                             again thirty days from Annual General Meeting. Therefore,

                             in view of these legal position, the disqualification could get

                             triggered off only on or after 30.10.2017 only, if any

                             company fails to file annual forms for three financial years.


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                      4/10</span>
http://www.judis.nic.in
                                                                               W.P. No. 22268 of 2018

                             Importantly, it is to be borne in mind that even beyond that

                             time limit, additional time limit of 270 days was available

                             by virtue of the then first proviso to <a href="/doc/1540111/" id="a_9">Section 403</a>.

</p><p id="p_7">                             (d)   Although there is no statute or provision expressly

                             spelling out the observance of the principles of natural

                             justice against disqualification of directors, as the legal

                             right of the petitioners to continue as director in other

                             company or to be reappointed in any other company, which

                             are scrupulously following the provisions of the <a href="/doc/1353758/" id="a_10">Companies

                             Act</a>, have been deprived of, the principles of natural justice

                             should have been adhered to by issuing proper notice to all

                             the directors.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                             (e)   When the disqualification clause was not attracted to

                             the directors of private companies under the old Act of

                             1956, the same cannot be allowed to take a retrospective

                             effect under the new Act, when the provision of <a href="/doc/310282/" id="a_11">Section

                             164(2)(a)</a> came into force only from 1.4.2014. This is also for

                             one more reason that the failure to file the annual returns

                             has been adequately taken care of by the penal provision

                             under <a href="/doc/729918/" id="a_12">Section 92</a>, making it clear that every officer of the


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                      5/10</span>
http://www.judis.nic.in
                                                                              W.P. No. 22268 of 2018

                             company who is in default shall be punishable with

                             imprisonment for a term which may extend to six months

                             or with fine which shall not be less than fifty thousand

                             rupees but which may extend to five lakh rupees, or with

                             both.    Again under <a href="/doc/937488/" id="a_13">Section 137</a>, the failure to file the

                             financial statement visits punishment with imprisonment

                             for a term which may extend to six months or with fine

                             which shall not be less than one lakh rupees but which may

                             extend to five lakh rupees, or with both. Further, under

                             <a href="/doc/1948051/" id="a_14">Section 441(4)</a>, the default in filing returns or accounts

                             compoundable by Tribunal or Regional Director or by any

                             officer authorized by the Central Government.

</p><p id="p_9">                             (f)     In view of the above legal position, when the default

                             in filing the accounts or returns are made as compoundable

                             offence, <a href="/doc/310282/" id="a_15">Section 164(2)(a)</a> providing the disqualification of

                             director of private company not only in the defaulting

                             company, but also from other company in which the

                             petitioner is a director, diligently and meticulously

                             following     every   provision    of   law,   is   certainly

                             disproportionate to the lapse, as it is only regulatory in


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_5">                      6/10</span>
http://www.judis.nic.in
                                                                             W.P. No. 22268 of 2018

                             nature, because, notice to be sent under <a href="/doc/1353758/" id="a_16">Section 248(1)</a> of

                             the Companies Act, 2013 by the Registrar of Companies for

                             striking off the name of the company from the Registrar of

                             Companies on the premise that the company has not been

                             carrying on any business for a period of two financial years,

                             is different from the disqualification under <a href="/doc/310282/" id="a_17">Section

                             164(2)(a)</a>, inasmuch as a company can be struck off, if the

                             company has not been carrying on any business for a period

                             of two financial years, whereas for disqualification, the

                             criteria is three financial years.       Therefore, in my

                             considered opinion, although the petitioners have not

                             challenged the provision of <a href="/doc/310282/" id="a_18">Section 164(2)(a)</a>, as the

                             respondents have not followed the principles of natural

                             justice, extinguishing the corporate life of the directors to

                             the extent of disqualifying them to hold the directorship in

                             the other companies, the said provision is liable to be read

                             down, hence, <a href="/doc/310282/" id="a_19">Section 164(2)(a)</a> is read down to the extent it

                             disqualifies the directors in other companies which are

                             scrupulously following the requirements of law, making it

                             clear that no directors in other companies can be


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_6">                      7/10</span>
http://www.judis.nic.in
                                                                              W.P. No. 22268 of 2018

                             disqualified without prior notice.
</p>
<p id="p_10">                             (g)   However, it is made clear beyond any pale of doubt

                             that the mischief of removal of the names of the companies

                             by the Registrar of Companies and the disqualification of

                             the directors in the defaulting company will go together, as

                             it is inseparable, and the Registrar of Companies need not

                             give fresh notice to the directors for their disqualification

                             from the dormant company, if there is a failure to file the

                             financial statement or annual return for any continuous

                             period of three financial years as per <a href="/doc/310282/" id="a_20">Section 164(2)(a)</a>.

</p><p id="p_11">                             30.   For all the aforementioned reasons, the impugned

                             orders are set aside and the writ petitions shall stand

                             allowed.     Consequently,    all    the    connected       writ

                             miscellaneous petitions are closed. However, there shall be

                             no order as to costs.''



</p><p id="p_12">                      4.     As the Petitioner in this case is similarly placed to the Petitioners

                      in the aforesaid batch of cases relating to the same impugned lists

                      published in the website by the Respondents, he is entitled to identical

                      relief that has been granted to them. Accordingly, the Writ Petition



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_7">                      8/10</span>
http://www.judis.nic.in
                                                                          W.P. No. 22268 of 2018

                      stands allowed and the impugned order is set aside on the aforesaid

                      terms. Consequently, the connected Miscellaneous Petitions are closed.

                      No costs.
</p>
