<p id="p_1">Antony Dominic, J.
</p>
<p id="p_2">

</p><p id="p_3">      1. aThe petitioners are the residents of an apartment

complex by name "J.M.Paradise Apartments", Palarivattom.

Their main grievance is with respect to the indiscriminate

parking of vehicles, mainly of the customers of respondents

4 to 6, in the road which leads to their apartment. They

have also a complaint with respect to certain unauthorised

constructions obstructing the access.

</p><p id="p_4">      2. We heard the learned counsel appearing for the

petitioners and the learned Standing Counsel appearing for

the third respondent apart from the learned Government

Pleader.
</p>
<p id="p_5">      3. Insofar as the alleged indiscriminate parking of the

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">W. P. (C) No.4942 of 2017       -2-</span>




vehicles is concerned, to our mind, the proper remedy for

the petitioners is to seek appropriate reliefs from the civil

court and a petition under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the Constitution of

India cannot be maintained. However, that does not mean

anything that affects law and order in the area should go

unnoticed or unattended by the police.

</p><p id="p_6">         4. Insofar as the complaint of the petitioner regarding

the unauthorised construction mentioned by them in Ext.P4

petition which is made to the Corporation is concerned, the

learned Standing Counsel appearing for the third respondent

confirms that the said petition is pending and that the

allegations are being enquired into. We record the

submissions and direct that the enquiry should be

completed and needful shall be done in accordance with law,

at any rate, within eight weeks from the date of receipt of a

copy of this judgment.
</p>
<p id="p_7"><span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">W. P. (C) No.4942 of 2017            -3-</span>




</p><p id="p_8">        Writ petition is disposed of accordingly.
