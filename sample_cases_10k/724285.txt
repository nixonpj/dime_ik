  IN THE HIGH COURT OF KERALA AT ERNAKULAM

WP(C).No. 27704 of 2009(G)


1. PRADEEP KUMAR P.D, S/O.LATE P.T.DEV
                      ...  Petitioner

                        Vs



1. STATE OF KERALA
                       ...       Respondent

2. KERALA PUBLIC SERVICE COMMISSION

                For Petitioner  :SRI.S.SAJU

                For Respondent  :SRI.ALEXANDER THOMAS,SC,KPSC

The Hon'ble MR. Justice ANTONY DOMINIC

 Dated :19/10/2009

 O R D E R
                        ANTONY DOMINIC, J.
                  -------------------------
                     W.P.(C.) No.27704 of 2009
             ---------------------------------
           Dated, this the 19th day of October, 2009

                           J U D G M E N T
</pre>

<p id="p_1">     Ext.P2 is the notification inviting applications for the post of

Medical Photographer prescribing qualifications as SSLC and

proficiency in photography. The note added to the qualifications

reads as under:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">     "Note:- Proficiency of the candidates in Photography will be
     assessed by a practical test conducted by the Public Service

     Commission at the time of selection."
</blockquote>
<p id="p_2">Ext.P3 contains the topics for common objective type test for the

post notified by Ext.P2. Grievance of the petitioner is that Ext.P3

did not include any subject related to the Photography.              It is

complaining that the subjects incorporated for objective type test

are unrelated to the post notified, that this writ petition is filed.

</p><p id="p_3">     2.    The 2nd respondent has filed a counter affidavit, where

they have stated that proficiency in photography will be assessed in

a practical test to be conducted by them as provided in the

Recruitment Rule. It is also stated that the syllabus of the objective

WP(C) No.27704/2009
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                    -2-</span>

type test has been fixed with a view that apart from photographic

skills, candidates should have general awareness, knowledge in

English, mental ability and arithmetic, which is expected from

candidates with the academic qualification i.e. SSLC. It is also stated

that pursuant to the interim order passed by this Court on

05/10/2009, a representation was made by the petitioner, and that

after considering the same, Ext.R2(a) reply dated 13/10/2009 has

been given to the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_4">      3.   As already noticed, the grievance of the petitioner is that

the subjects prescribed for written test are totally unrelated to the

post that is to be filled up, namely, the Medical Photographer. It is

disclosed from the vacancy notification and the counter affidavit

filed by the 2nd respondent, that the proficiency in photography will

be assessed by the respondents by conducting a practical test. If

that be so, I do not find anything wrong with the steps taken by the

PSC.
</p>
<p id="p_5">      The writ petition fails, and is accordingly, dismissed.


</p><p id="p_6">                                    (ANTONY DOMINIC, JUDGE)

WP(C) No.27704/2009

</p>
