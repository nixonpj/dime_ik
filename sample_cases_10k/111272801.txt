<div class="doc_title">Gopinath Bairagya vs State Of West Bengal on 30 October, 2018</div><p id="p_1"><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                              1</span>

 Item      30.10.2018                                    C.R.M. 9568 of 2018
            Ct. No.8
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">No. 87</span>
                        In Re: - An application for bail under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure in
Sg/G.S.D                connection with Tehatta P.S. Case No. 48/2018 dated 25.01.2018 under Sections
   as
           (Rejected)   376(2)(i)/376(2)(l) <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_2">I.P.C</a>. and Section 6 of POCSO Act.

</p><p id="p_2">                                                            Gopinath Bairagya
                                                                  Versus
                                                           State of West Bengal

                        Mr. Arnab Saha, Adv.
</p><p id="p_3">                                                                                              ...for the petitioner.
</p><p id="p_4">                        Mr. Rana Mukherjee, Adv.
</p><p id="p_5">                        Ms. Sonali Das, Adv.
</p><p id="p_6">                                                                                                  ...for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

                                      In this application for grant of bail, the petitioner contends that he has

                        been falsely implicated.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                                 Learned Advocate appearing for the petitioner draws the attention of the

                        Court to the manner and mode for which the statement made under <a href="/doc/497457/" id="a_3">Section 164</a> of

                        the Criminal Procedure Code was recorded.

</p><p id="p_9">                                 The prayer for grant of bail is opposed by the State. Learned Advocate

                        appearing for the State draws the attention of the Court to the statement made under

                        <a href="/doc/497457/" id="a_4">Section 164</a> of the Criminal Procedure Code.

</p><p id="p_10">                                 Considered the case diary and the statement made under <a href="/doc/497457/" id="a_5">Section 164</a> of the

                        Criminal Procedure Code by the victim girl. There is prima facie evidence

                        implicating the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_11">                                 In such circumstances, we are not inclined to grant bail to the petitioner at

                        this stage.
</p>
<p id="p_12">                                 The application for grant of bail stands rejected.

                                 CRM 9568 of 2018 stands thus disposed of.



                        (Biswajit Basu, J.)                                       (Debangsu Basak, J.)
