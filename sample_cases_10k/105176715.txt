              TIS HAZARI COURTS : DELHI



J U D G M E N T
</pre>
<pre id="pre_2">1.FIR  No.                                        : 379/04

2.Unique Case ID No.                              :02401R0045192005

3.Name of accused                                 :Baldev S/o Phool Chand, R/o  
                                                  C­94,   Shyam   Nagar,   Subhash  
                                                  Nagar   marg   Jhuggi,
                                                  Tilak   Nagar,   Delhi.

4.Date of institution of challan                  :01.04.2005

5.Date of Reserving judgment                      :19.07.10

6.Date of pronouncement                           :19.07.10

7.Date of commission of offence                   :10.06.2004

8.Offence complained of                           :U/s  61/1/14 <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_1">Excise Act</a>

9.Offence charged with                            :U/s  61/1/14 <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_2">Excise Act</a>

10.Plea of the accused                            :Pleaded not guilty (Initially)

11.Final order                :Convicted U/s  61/1/14 Punjab 
                               Excise Act
</pre><p id="p_1">BRIEF REASONS FOR THE DECISION OF THE CASE:­

</p><p id="p_2">1.           The  case  of   the  prosecution  in brief  is  that  on   10.06.2004  at

about   7:50 PM   at T.C. Camp Road near Gauri Shankar Mandir, Shyam

Nagar, New Delhi, accused   was   found in possession of   one plastic cane

containing 12 bottles (750 ml each) of illicit liquor without any permit or

licence and thereby committed an offence punishable u/s 61.1.14 <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_3">Excise Act</a>.

</p><p id="p_3">FIR No. 379/04                                                               Page­1/4
</p><p id="p_4"> 2.                   Accordingly, charge sheet was filed, copies were supplied to

the accused in compliance of <a href="/doc/1613898/" id="a_4">section 207</a> Cr.PC and on the basis of record,

charge   u/s   61/1/14,   <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_5">Excise   Act</a>   was   framed   against   the   accused   on

03.04.2007 to which he pleaded not guilty and claimed trial.

</p><p id="p_5">3.                      As accused has voluntarily pleaded guilty vide his separate

statement dated 19.07.10, prosecution's evidence was dispensed with.

</p><p id="p_6">4.                  In   view   of   the   voluntarily   plea   of   guilt   of   accused   for

commission   of   offence   punishable   under   <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_6">Section   61</a>.1.14   <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_7">Excise   Act</a>,

accused stands convict for the offence   punishable under <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_8">Section 61</a>.1.14

<a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_9">Excise Act</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                      His surety is discharged. Bail bonds are   cancelled. Case

property be   forfeited to the state, to be destroyed after the expiry of the

period of appeal or revision, if any. 
</p>
<p id="p_8">                     Be heard separately on the  point of sentence.



Announced in the open                                 (MANISH YADUVANSHI )  
court on 19.07.10.                                    MM (West) ­05 . Delhi

           It is certified that this judgment contains two pages and each page
bears my signature.                    
</p>
<p id="p_9">                                                      (MANISH YADUVANSHI )  
                                                         MM (West) ­05 . Delhi
                                                                19.07.10.
</p>
<p id="p_10">


</p><pre id="pre_3">FIR No. 379/04                                                                      Page­2/4
                                                       STATE VS.  Baldev
                                                      PS Tilak Nagar
                                                      FIR NO        379/04
                                                      U/S              61­1­14 Ex. Act 
19.07.10.
</pre><p id="p_11">              File taken up today on an application of accused for cancellation
of NBWs.
</p>
<p id="p_12">Present:­ Ld. APP for the State.
</p><p id="p_13">            Accused along with counsel.
</p>
<p id="p_14">              Heard   on   the   application   of   accused.       Considering   the   grounds

mentioned in the application, same is allowed subject to fine of Rs. 500/­.   Fine

paid.
</p>
<p id="p_15">              At this stage, accused submits that he  wants to plead guilty in respect

of commission of offence punishable u/s 61/1/14 <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_10">Excise Act</a>.    The accused has

pleaded guilty.   His statement is recorded separately in this regard.   Accused is

warned that he is not bound to plead guilty and that his  plea of guilt may invite the

maximum sentence prescribed for the said offence.   The accused   has, however,

persisted in the plea of guilt.  The plea of guilt  appears to be voluntary.

</p><p id="p_16">              In view of his  plea of guilt, accused is convicted for the commission

of offences punishable under Sections 61/1/14 Punjab Excise Act.

</p><p id="p_17">              Arguments on sentence heard.
</p>
<p id="p_18">              Accused prays for a lenient sentence.
</p>
<p id="p_19">              Learned APP for the state requested for a strict view.  

              Keeping the facts and circumstances, the convict is sentenced to pay

a fine of Rs. 1000/­ in default of payment of fine, Simple Imprisonment for ten

days.  Fine paid.   
</p>
<p id="p_20">              His bail bonds stand cancelled. Surety is discharged. Endorsement, if



FIR No. 379/04                                                                      Page­3/4
 any,   be   cancelled.   Documents   of   surety,   if   any,   be   returned   against

acknowledgment.   Case property be     disposed of / confiscated to the state after

expiry   the period of appeal. 
</p>
<p id="p_21">            Earlier date fixed i.e 03.12.10 stands hereby cancelled.

            File be consigned to records after due compliance.



</p><p id="p_22">                                                  ( Manish Yaduvanshi )

                                            Metropolitan Magistrate­( West­05)
                                                                 19.07.10.
</p>
