<div class="doc_title">Rajveer vs State Of Up And Another on 19 August, 2019</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: Vivek Kumar Singh</div>

<pre id="pre_1">




HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT ALLAHABAD
 
 

?Court No. - 69
 
Case :- APPLICATION U/S 482 No. - 31614 of 2019
 
Applicant :- Rajveer
 
Opposite Party :- State Of Up And Another
 
Counsel for Applicant :- Arvind Kumar Srivastava,Akhilesh Kumar Yadav
 
Counsel for Opposite Party :- G.A.
 

 
Hon'ble Vivek Kumar Singh,J.
</pre> 
<p id="p_1">Heard Sri Arvind Kumar Srivastava, learned counsel for the applicant, Sri Abhinav Prasad, learned AGA for the State.
</p> 
<p id="p_2">This application has been filed under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">section 482</a> Cr.PC with a prayer seeking stay/quashing of the charge sheet dated 25.8.2018 cognizance order dated 23.1.2019, criminal case No. 337 of 2019 (<a href="/doc/60559467/" id="a_2">State v. Rajveer</a>) arising out of Case Crime No. 474 of 2018, under section 63 of the Uttar Pradesh Excise Act, Police Station- Syohara, District- Bijnor, pending in the court of Addl. CJM, Court No. 1, District Bijnor. 
</p> 
<p id="p_3">The contention of learned counsel for the applicant is that no offence against the applicant is disclosed and the present prosecution has been instituted with a malafide intention for the purpose of harassment. He pointed out certain documents and statements in support of his contention. 
</p> 
<p id="p_4">From the perusal of the material on record and looking into the facts of the case at this stage it cannot be said that no offence is made out against the applicant. All the submissions made at the Bar relates to the disputed questions of fact, which cannot be adjudicated upon by this Court under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_3">section 482</a> Cr.PC. At this stage only prime facie case is to be seen in the light of the law laid down by Supreme Court in cases of <a href="/doc/1033301/" id="a_4">R.P. Kapur v. State of Punjab</a>, AIR 1960 SC 866, <a href="/doc/1033637/" id="a_5">State of Haryana v. Bhajan Lal</a>, 1992 SCC (Cr.) 426, <a href="/doc/1287305/" id="a_6">State of Bihar v. P.P.Sharma</a>, 1992 SCC (Cr.) 192 and lastly <a href="/doc/279427/" id="a_7">Zandu Pharmaceutical Works Ltd. v. Mohd. Saraful Haq and another (Para-10</a>) 2005 SCC (Cr.)283.
</p> 
<p id="p_5">The prayer for quashing the proceedings of the aforesaid case pending before the court concerned is refused. 
</p> 
<p id="p_6">However, it is directed, that in case applicant appear and surrender before the court below within 30 days from today and apply for bail the court below shall consider and decide the bail prayer of applicant in view of the settled law laid by this Court in the case of <a href="/doc/385610/" id="a_8">Smt. Amarawati and another v. State of U.P</a>., reported in 2004 (57)ALR 290, as well as judgement passed by Hon'ble Apex Court in the case of <a href="/doc/674840/" id="a_9">Lal Kamlendra Pratap Singh v. State of Uttar Pradesh and others</a>, reported in (2009) 3 ADJ 322 (SC). For a period of 30 days from today or till the disposal of the application for grant of bail whichever is earlier, no coercive action shall be taken against the applicant. However, in case, the applicant does not appear before the court below within the aforesaid period, coercive action shall be taken against him. 
</p> 
<p id="p_7">With the above directions, present application is disposed of. 
</p> 
<p id="p_8">Order Date :- 19.8.2019
