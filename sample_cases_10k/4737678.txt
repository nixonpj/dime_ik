<div class="doc_title">Rakesh Son Of Shri Ramkaran @ Rana, ... vs State Of Rajasthan on 28 February, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">          HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR RAJASTHAN
                      BENCH AT JAIPUR

          S.B. Criminal Miscellaneous IIIrd Bail No. 2224/2019

Rakesh Son Of Shri Ramkaran @ Rana, By Caste Meena, R/o
Village Chainpura, P.s. Ayana, Distt. Kota. (At Present Accused
Petitioner Confined In Kota Jail, Distt. Kota)
                                                         ----Petitioner
                                  Versus
State Of Rajasthan, Through Pp
                                                      ----Respondent</pre>


<pre id="pre_2">For Petitioner(s)          :   Mr. Sunil Tyagi
For State                  :   Mr. Sudesh Saini, PP


             HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE PANKAJ BHANDARI
                                  Order
28/02/2019

</pre><p id="p_1">1.       Petitioner has filed this third bail application under Section

439 of Cr.P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">2.       F.I.R. No. 68/2013 was registered at Police Station Ayana,

District Kota for offence under Sections 302, 212 and 120-B of

I.P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_4">
</p><p id="p_5">3.       It is contended by counsel for the petitioner that co-accused

have been enlarged on bail. Statement of PW-1 has been recorded

who is the only eye witness to the incident, in his cross-

examination, this witness has stated that he on the basis of built

of the assailant thinks that the assailant was Rakesh. It is also

contended that second bail application was rejected by the Court

on 29.01.2018 and Trial Court was directed to expedite the

disposal of the case, as petitioner had remained in custody for a

period of four years.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><p id="p_7">4.       It is contended that only statement of one witness has been
                                                                    (2 of 2)             [CRLMB-2224/2019]


                                   recorded so far out of list of 23 witnesses.


</p><p id="p_8">                                   5.    Learned Public Prosecutor has opposed the bail application.


</p><p id="p_9">                                   6.    I have considered the contentions.
</p>
<p id="p_10">
</p><p id="p_11">                                   7.    Considering the contentions put forth by counsel for the

                                   petitioner as also the fact that petitioner has remained in custody

                                   for a period of four years &amp; eleven months and statement of one

                                   witness has been recorded inspite of directions of the Court for

                                   expeditious disposal of the case, I deem it proper to allow the

                                   third bail application.
</p>
<p id="p_12">
</p><p id="p_13">                                   8.    This third bail application is accordingly allowed and it is

                                   directed that accused petitioner shall be released on bail provided

                                   he furnishes a personal bond in the sum of Rs.50,000/- (Rupees

                                   Fifty Thousand only) together with two sureties in the sum of

                                   Rs.25,000/- (Rupees Twenty Five Thousand only) each           to the

                                   satisfaction of the learned trial court with the stipulation that he

                                   shall appear before that Court and any court to which the matter

                                   is transferred, on all subsequent dates of hearing and as and when

                                   called upon to do so.
</p>
<p id="p_14">
</p><p id="p_15">                                   9.    Copy of this order be sent to the concerned Court by

                                   fax/email today itself with a direction to serve a photostat copy of

                                   the same to the petitioner and an additional copy of this order be

                                   also sent to the concerned Court by ordinary process.


</p><p id="p_16">                                                                                  (PANKAJ BHANDARI),J

                                   Amit/31
