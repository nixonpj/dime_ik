<div class="doc_title">Ravi vs State Of Rajasthan Through Pp on 6 April, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">          HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR RAJASTHAN
                      BENCH AT JAIPUR
           S.B. Criminal Miscellaneous Bail No. 4231/2018

Ravi S/o Shri Krishan Kumar B/c Brahmin, Aged About 19 Years,
R/o Lambi Aheer, Police Station Pacheri Kalan, Distt. Jhunjhunu,
Rajasthan (At Present Confined At Distt. Jail Jhunjhunu)
                                                      ----Petitioner
                              Versus
State Of Rajasthan Through P.p.
                                                   ----Respondent</pre>


<p id="p_1">For Petitioner(s)       :   Mr.Anil Kumar Upman with
                            Mr.Mahendra Kumar Naharwal
For Respondent(s)       :   Mr.R.R.Singh Rathore,PP



            HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE PANKAJ BHANDARI

                        Judgment / Order

06/04/2018
</p><p id="p_2">1.   Petitioner has filed this bail application under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of

Cr.P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_3">2.   F.I.R. No.111/2017 was registered at Police Station Pacheri

Kalan, District Jhunjhunu for offence under <a href="/doc/1982060/" id="a_2">Sections 450</a>, <a href="/doc/1279834/" id="a_3">376</a>

I.P.C. and Section 3/4 of POCSO Act, 2012.

</p><p id="p_4">3.   It is contended by the counsel for the petitioner that there is

delay of six months in lodging of FIR. As per FIR and statement of

prosecutrix rape was committed on 15.2.2017. On that date

petitioner appeared in an examination at Delhi. The allegation that

rape was committed at Jhunjhunu is thus untrue. It is also

contended that charge-sheet has been filed and trial would

consume time.
</p>
<p id="p_5">4.   Learned Public Prosecutor has opposed the bail application.

</p><p id="p_6">5.   I have considered the contentions.
</p><p id="p_7">                                 (2 of 2)              [CRLMB-4231/2018]


</p><p id="p_8">6.    Considering the contentions put-forth by the counsel for the

petitioner, I deem it proper to allow the bail application.


</p><p id="p_9">7.    This bail application is accordingly allowed and it is ordered

that the accused-petitioner shall be released on bail provided he

furnishes a personal bond of Rs. 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty thousand

only) and two sureties in the sum of Rs.25,000/- (Rupees Twenty

Five thousand only) each to the satisfaction of the learned trial

court with the stipulation to appear before that Court and any

court to which the matter is transferred, on all dates of hearing

and as and when called upon to do so.
</p>
<p id="p_10">
</p><p id="p_11">                                              (PANKAJ BHANDARI),J

teekam/96
 </p>
