                 The Union Of India Through The N.D.P.S. Department
                                                                   .... .... Opposite Party/s

                                                  with
                               Criminal Miscellaneous No.13315 of 2013
                 ======================================================
                 Ram Balak Yadav son of Chinta Mani Yadav
                                                                       .... ....   Petitioner/s
                                                 Versus
                 The State Of Bihar Through N.D.P.S. Department.
                                                                   .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE DINESH KUMAR
                 SINGH
                                         ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">5   18-07-2013                      Petitioners being owner and truck driver of the

                 vehicle being Registration No. UP 14DT 2406 are languishing in

                 custody since 04.12.2012 in a case registered for the offences

                 punishable under <a href="/doc/1624837/" id="a_1">Sections 25A</a>, <a href="/doc/1252855/" id="a_2">29</a> of the N.D.P.S. Act.

</p><p id="p_2">                                    Recovery       is       of       3930          Kgs        of

                 Ephedrine/Pseudoephedrine.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                    This fact is admitted that petitioners are driver

                 and owner of the vehicle in question. It is claimed by the

                 petitioners that they were not aware about the contraband
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> 2   Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.14031 of 2013 (5) dt.18-07-2013</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                             2/3</span>




             being loaded in the truck when the truck was hired by one

             Naresh @ Ashok Sharma for transporting flour/suji.

</p><p id="p_4">                                    It is submitted by learned counsel for the

             petitioners that truck owner being petitioner Ram Balak Yadav

             was admittedly not present either at the place of loading or at

             the place of seizure nor he was aware about the transportation

             of any contraband. This argument of learned senior counsel for

             the petitioners has not been controverted by Mr. Dwedi

             Surender learned counsel, appearing on behalf of Narcotic

             Control Bureau.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                    A statement has been made in para 12 of the

             petition that petitioner Ram Balak Yadav has no criminal

             antecedent.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                    Considering the fact that vehicle was being

             driven by petitioner Jitendra Kumar @ Kauleshwar Prasad, this

             Court is not inclined to grant bail to the petitioner Jitendra

             Kumar @ Kauleshwar Prasad in connection with Special Case

             No. 41 of 2012, arising out of N.D.P.S. Case No. 41 of 2012,

             pending in the Court of learned ADJ, VII cum Special Judge,

             N.D.P.S., Patna.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                                    So far as truck owner Ram Balak Yadav is

             concerned, since he was neither aware about the contraband

             being loaded in the truck nor travelling in the truck, let the

             petitioner Ram Balak Yadav, be released on bail on furnishing

             bail bond of Rs. 10,000/-(ten thousand) with two sureties of
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3"> 3           Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.14031 of 2013 (5) dt.18-07-2013</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                                                     3/3</span>




                     the like amount each to the satisfaction of learned A.D.J., VII,

                     cum Special Judge, N.D.P.S., Patna in connection with Special

                     Case No. 41 of 2012 <a href="/doc/1727139/" id="a_3">(N.D.P.S. Act</a>), arising out of N.D.P.S. Case

                     No. 41 of 2012.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                                            The petitioner Jitendra Kumar @ Kauleshwar

                     Prasad will be at liberty to renew his prayer for bail, if the trial

                     is not concluded within a period of one year.




</p><p id="p_9">                                               (Dinesh Kumar Singh, J)
Shageer/-
</p>
