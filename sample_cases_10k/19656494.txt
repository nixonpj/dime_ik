<div class="doc_title">Krishnamoorthy vs The Inspector Of Police on 7 August, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">        

 
		IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT MADRAS
DATED 07.08.2018
CORAM
THE HONOURABLE Mr.JUSTICE P.N.PRAKASH
CRL.O.P.No.19638 of 2018


Krishnamoorthy 			                               .. Petitioner

 Vs 
The Inspector of Police,
Arni Taluk Police Station,
Thiruvannamalai District.
(Crime No.329/2018)                                                    	  .. Respondent

	Criminal Original Petition filed under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C., praying to direct the Principal District Sessions Judge, Thiruvannamalai to accept the surrender of the petitioner and consider the bail petition on the very same day in crime No.329 of 2018 on the file of the respondent Police.
	              For Petitioner     :   Mr.S.Ramesh
  	              For Respondent  :   Mr.C.Raghavan, Govt. Advocate 

O R D E R
</pre><p id="p_1">	The learned counsel for the petitioner submits that the petitioner has come forward with this petition seeking for a direction to the learned Principal District Sessions Judge, Thiruvannamalai to consider the bail application of the petitioner on the same day on his surrender in Crime No.329 of 2018 pending on the file of the respondent.
</p>
<p id="p_2">	2.The learned counsel for the petitioner also submits that the petitioner  has  been  implicated   in   this   case  for  the  alleged  offences under <a href="/doc/1198799/" id="a_2">Sections 294(b)</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_3">323</a> and <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_4">324</a> IPC r/w Sections 3(1)(r) and 3(1)(s) of  SC/ST [POA] <a href="/doc/1210757/" id="a_5">Amendment Act</a> 2015 and  that  in view of the specific bar under <a href="/doc/805985/" id="a_6">Section 18</a> of the said Act, the petitioner cannot move any anticipatory bail application and therefore, the petitioner has come forward with the said prayer.
P.N.PRAKASH, J.
</p><p id="p_3">gya
</p><p id="p_4">	3.The learned Government Advocate takes notice for the respondent.
</p>
<p id="p_5">	4.Considering the submissions of both sides and also considering the nature of the prayer in this case, in view of the specific bar under Section 18 of the SC/ST [POA] <a href="/doc/1210757/" id="a_7">Amendment Act</a> that the petitioner cannot move any anticipatory bail, the learned Principal District Sessions Judge, Thiruvannamalai is directed to consider the bail application, in the event of the petitioner filing such petition in Crime No.329 of 2018 on the file of the respondent police within a period of two weeks from the date of receipt of a copy of this order and dispose of the same on merits and in accordance with law on the same day. 
	With the above observation, this petition is disposed of accordingly.
</p>
<p id="p_6">07.08.2018
gya
Note : Issue order copy on 08.08.2018

To
</p><p id="p_7">1.Principal District Sessions Judge, 
Thiruvannamalai. 
</p>
<p id="p_8">2.The Inspector of Police,
Arni Taluk Police Station,
Thiruvannamalai District.
</p> 
<p id="p_9">3.The Public Prosecutor,                                                       
High Court, Madras.                                                
</p>
<p id="p_10">CRL.OP.No.19638 of 2018
