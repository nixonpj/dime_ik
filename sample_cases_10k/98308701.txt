CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE ASHWANI KUMAR SINGH
ORAL JUDGMENT
Date: 02-11-2017

                  The original writ petitioner Rangnath Choubey retired

   as a Reader on 28.02.2005. He died on 07.09.2015 and vide order

   dated 16.10.2017, his wife, three sons and a daughter have been
 Patna High Court CWJC No.22111 of 2014 dt.02-11-2017

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                         2/3</span>




        allowed to be substituted as petitioners in the present case.

</p><p id="p_15">        2.              In the writ petition, the prayer of the original writ

        petitioner was to direct the respondent authorities to pay the

        admitted arrears of salary payable since April 1980 to October,

        1992, admitted arrears of revised pay differences payable since

        01.01.1996 to 28.02.2005 and admitted arrears of pensionery

        benefits payable since March, 2005.
</p>
<p id="p_16">        3.              Having gone through the writ petition and the

        submissions made by the learned counsel for the petitioners, the

        Court is of the opinion that instead of passing any specific order,

        the purpose would be served by granting liberty to the petitioners

        to file a representation before respondent no. 3, the Vice

        Chancellor, J. P. University, Saran at Chapra raising all their

        claims. If such a representation is filed, the respondent no. 3 shall

        be required to examine the claims and pass a reasoned order within

        a period of four months from the date of filing of representation. In

        case, the claims of the petitioners are found to be admissible,

        payment shall be made to the petitioners in accordance with law

        within a period of one month from the date of passing of the order.

        In case, claim(s) of the petitioners is/are rejected, the order must be

        communicated to the widow of the deceased employee Smt. Rekha

        Choubey on her postal address.
</p><p id="p_17">          Patna High Court CWJC No.22111 of 2014 dt.02-11-2017

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                  3/3</span>




                 4               With the aforesaid observations and directions, the

                 writ petition is disposed of.
</p>
<p id="p_18">
</p><p id="p_19">                                             (Ashwani Kumar Singh, J.)
