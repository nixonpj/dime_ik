<div class="doc_title">Addepalli Vijaya Kumar Another vs State Of Ap.,Rep I Of P,Cid,Rep By ... on 30 October, 2018</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: T. Rajani</div>

<pre id="pre_1">                          SMT. JUSTICE T. RAJANI

                     CRIMINAL PETITION No.12622 of 2011

ORDER:
</pre>

<p id="p_1">          This Criminal Petition under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> of Cr.P.C., is filed

seeking to quash the proceedings in C.C.No.3 of 2002 on the file

of the Special Judge under E.C. Act-cum-Principal District and

Sessions Judge, Visakhapatnam, registered for the offences

punishable under <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_2">sections 120-B</a>, <a href="/doc/940928/" id="a_3">407</a>, <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_4">420</a>, <a href="/doc/1985627/" id="a_5">467</a>, <a href="/doc/556166/" id="a_6">468</a>, <a href="/doc/1466184/" id="a_7">471</a>, <a href="/doc/182233/" id="a_8">472</a>,

<a href="/doc/756306/" id="a_9">474</a> IPC and <a href="/doc/361626/" id="a_10">Section 7(i)</a> of the Essential Commodities Act, 1955,

r/w Sections 26 and 28 of the A.P. Petroleum Products (Licensing

and Regulation of Supplies) Order, 1980 and under <a href="/doc/812083/" id="a_11">Section 411</a>

IPC, against petitioners/A7 and A8.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">2.        Heard learned counsel for petitioners and learned Special

Public Prosecutor appearing for the respondent - State.


</p><p id="p_4">3.        Learned counsel for the petitioners submits that in similar

circumstances, this Court, by virtue of the order dated 07.09.2007

passed in Crl.P.No.4977 of 2007, observed as follows:


</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">          "Insofar as the offences under <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_12">Sections 120B</a>, <a href="/doc/940928/" id="a_13">407</a>, <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_14">420</a>, <a href="/doc/1985627/" id="a_15">467</a>,
          <a href="/doc/556166/" id="a_16">468</a>, <a href="/doc/1466184/" id="a_17">471</a>, <a href="/doc/182233/" id="a_18">472</a>, <a href="/doc/756306/" id="a_19">474</a> and <a href="/doc/812083/" id="a_20">411</a> IPC are concerned, the Division
          Bench in <a href="/doc/111558418/" id="a_21">Kasarbada Rajeswara Rao v. State of A.P</a>.1, held as
          follows:
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">                "For the reasons given hereinabove, we agree with the
            view of the Punjab and Haryana High Court and hold that
            the Special Judge cannot try other offences along with
            offences under <a href="/doc/774360/" id="a_22">Essential Commodities Act</a> which are not
            to be tried in a summary way. Since all the offences other
            than the offences under <a href="/doc/774360/" id="a_23">Essential Commodities Act</a> are
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">1</span>
    2004(1) ALT (CRL.) 443(DB)(AP)
                                           2                                           TR,J
                                                                    Crl.P.No.12622 of 2011



        not to be tried in a summary way, therefore, the Special
        Judge has no jurisdiction to take cognizance of the
        offences mentioned herein. The reference is answered
        accordingly. We do not think it will serve any purpose if
        we send the matter back to the learned Single Judge and
        therefore, we quash the proceeding as far as they relate to
        offences under <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_24">Sections 120B</a>,<a href="/doc/940928/" id="a_25">407</a>, <a href="/doc/812083/" id="a_26">411</a>, <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_27">420</a>, <a href="/doc/1985627/" id="a_28">467</a>, <a href="/doc/556166/" id="a_29">468</a>,
        <a href="/doc/1466184/" id="a_30">471</a>, <a href="/doc/182233/" id="a_31">472</a> and <a href="/doc/756306/" id="a_32">474</a> of the Indian Penal Code".
</blockquote>

<p id="p_5">This Court further observed as under:
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_3">             "Under        <a href="/doc/772278/" id="a_33">Section     7(1)(a)(i)</a>        of   the     Essential
        Commodities Act, 1955, if any person contravenes any
        order made under <a href="/doc/158335608/" id="a_34">Section 3</a>, he shall be punishable in
        case of an order made with reference to Clause (h) or
        Clause(i) of sub-section (2) of that Section, with
        imprisonment for a term which may extend to one year.
        Under   <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_35">Section       468</a>    Cr.P.C.,       no    Court     shall   take
        cognizance after one year, if the offence is punishable
        with imprisonment for a term not exceeding one year. As
        seen from the charge sheet, admittedly, the offence is
        alleged to have taken place in the year 1995 whereas the
        charge sheet was filed and cognizance was taken, in the
        year, 2002, for the offence under <a href="/doc/361626/" id="a_36">Section 7(i)</a> of the
        Essential Commodities Act, 1955, and hence, it is barred
        by   limitation.     So,     continuation        of   the    impugned
        proceedings against the petitioner is nothing but abuse of
        process of Court".
</blockquote>


<p id="p_6">4.     In the instant case, as seen from the charge sheet,

the offence is alleged to have taken place on 31.12.1996 and a

complaint was filed on the said date, whereas the charge sheet

was filed and cognizance was taken in the year 2001 for the

offence under <a href="/doc/361626/" id="a_37">Section 7(i)</a> of the Essential Commodities Act,

1955, which is beyond one year from the date of alleged offence,

and hence, it is barred by limitation.
</p><pre id="pre_2">                                  3                                  TR,J
                                                  Crl.P.No.12622 of 2011



</pre><p id="p_7">5.    In the circumstances, following the said judgment and for

the reasons mentioned therein, this Criminal Petition is allowed,

quashing the proceedings in C.C.No.3 of 2002 on the file of

Special Judge under E.C.Act-cum-Principal District and Sessions

Judge at Visakhapatnam, against the petitioners/A7 and A8.

Miscellaneous applications, if any, pending in this criminal petition

shall stand closed.
</p>
<p id="p_8">
</p><p id="p_9">                                                  ______________
                                                   T. RAJANI, J
30th October, 2018
