<div class="doc_title">Sk. Golam Ambia Mondal vs The State Of West Bengal &amp; Ors on 28 November, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                                          1

   S.D.




                                W.P. 6949 (W) of 2015

                             Sk. Golam Ambia Mondal
                                         Vs.
                           The State of West Bengal &amp; Ors.

                   Mr. Tapas Kumar Ghosh
                   Mr. Tanmoy Chowdhury
                                              ....For the Petitioner.

                   Mr. Gautam Kumar Roy
                            ...For the added Respondent No. 7.</pre>

<p id="p_1">                   Mr. Sourindra Mahapatra
                   Mr. Dibyendu Chatterjee
                   Mrs. Madhuparna Sarkar (Kanrar)
                           ....For the Minor Irrigation Corporation.



                   The petitioner claims that, the petitioner is not

             receiving   appropriate family pension in terms of the

             guidelines of the Government.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                   Learned Advocate appearing on behalf of the

             petitioner draws the attention of the Court to the pensionary

             benefits fixed under ROPA, 2009. He submits that, there

             should be a fitment of 40% with the minimum family
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                               2</span>


pension being Rs.3,300/‐. The petitioner is receiving much

less. The petitioner is governed by the ROPA, 2009.

</p><p id="p_3">       Learned Advocate appearing on behalf of the Minor

Irrigation Corporation submits that, the petitioner opted for

Contributory Provident Fund and, therefore, is not entitled

to the benefits under ROPA, 2009. Similar contentions are

raised on behalf of the added respondents.

</p><p id="p_4">       It appears from the records that, the petitioner applied

for and obtained pension under the Employees' Provident

Fund Scheme, 1995, i.e. to say Contributory Provident Fund.

The petitioner did not opt for Government Provident Fund.

The provisions of ROPA, 2009, relied upon by the petitioner,

gives benefits to persons who are covered under the

Government Provident Fund Scheme and not to persons who

are governed by the Contributory Provident Fund.

</p><p id="p_5">       In such circumstances, no relief can be granted to the

petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_6">       W.P. 6949 (W) of 2015 is dismissed without any order

as to costs.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                               3</span><p id="p_7">

for, be made available to the parties upon compliance of the

formalities.
</p>
