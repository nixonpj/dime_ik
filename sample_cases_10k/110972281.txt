<div class="doc_title">Mishrilal @ Pappulal vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 24 April, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                        1
  HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH, BENCH AT INDORE
            CRIMINAL APPEAL NO.1102 OF 2018
  Mishrilal @ Pappulal and Others vs. State of Madhya Pradesh

Indore, Dated 24.04.2018
     Mr. Abhay Saraswat, counsel for the appellants.
     Mr.    Mukesh    Parwal,   Public      Prosecutor      for   the
respondent-State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">     Heard on the question of admission.
</p><p id="p_2">     This appeal is already an admitted appeal.
     Record has been received.
</p><p id="p_3">     Heard on IA No.2767/2018 which is an application for
suspension of sentence and grant of bail filed on behalf of
appellant No.1-Mishrilal.
</p><p id="p_4">     The appellant No.1 has been convicted under <a href="/doc/409589/" id="a_1">Section
304(B)</a> of Indian Penal Code, 1860 and sentenced to undergo
ten years, vide judgment dated 31.01.2018 passed by
Sessions    Judge,   Neemuch     (MP)       in   Criminal    Appeal
No.23/2012.
</p><p id="p_5">     It is submitted that Court below has wrongly convicted the
appellant No.1. The Court below has not properly appreciated
the evidence. The appellant No.1 was on bail during trial and
he has not misused the liberty so granted to him. He submits
that other co-accused has been granted bail by this Court vide
order dated 04.04.2018. No over-tact has been attributed to the
present appellant and instead the omnibus allegations have
been made against the present appellant No.1. The disposal of
appeal will likely to take a long time. Hence, prayed for
suspension of execution of jail sentence.
</p><p id="p_6">     The prayer is opposed by Public Prosecutor for the
respondent-State.
</p><p id="p_7">     Taking into consideration the aforesaid facts and
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                       2</span>
                HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH, BENCH AT INDORE
                          CRIMINAL APPEAL NO.1102 OF 2018
                <a href="/doc/169003575/" id="a_2">Mishrilal @ Pappulal and Others vs. State of Madhya Pradesh</a>

             circumstances of the case, the disposal of appeal will likely to
             take the long time, but without expressing any opinion on
             merits of the case, the application is allowed. It is directed that
             the jail sentence of the appellant No.1-Mishrilal shall remain
             suspended and he be released on bail on his furnishing a
             personal bond in the sum of Rs.50,000/- (Rupees Fifty
             thousand only) with one solvent surety in the like amount to
             the satisfaction of the trial Court. The appellant No.1-Mishrilal is
             directed to appear before the Registry of this Court on
             18.05.2018 and on all other subsequent dates, as may be
             fixed, in this behalf.
</p><p id="p_8">                   Accordingly, the IA No.2767/2018 stands disposed of.
                   List this appeal for final hearing in due course.
                   Certified copy, as per Rules.
</p>
<p id="p_9">                                                              (ROHIT ARYA)
                                                                JUDGE
    Arun/-
</p>
