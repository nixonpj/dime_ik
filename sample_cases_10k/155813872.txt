<div class="doc_title">An Application Under Section 397 vs Abdul Mamud Khan on 3 September, 2015</div><p id="p_1">S/L. 12.                            C.R.R. 2210 of 2015
September
3, 2015
  Bpg.      In the matter of : An application under Section 397, 401, read with 482
            of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, filed on July 7, 2015;
</p><p id="p_2">                                     And
            In the matter of :       Abdul Mamud Khan.
</p><p id="p_3">                                                     ...petitioner.
</p><p id="p_4">                                        Versus
                                    The State.
</p><p id="p_5">                                                            ...opposite party.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                             Mr. Anil Kumar Chattopadhyay.
</p><p id="p_7">                                                ...for the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                                   Mr. Ayan Basu.
</p><p id="p_9">                                                ...for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_10">                             The    petitioner     has   preferred   this   revision
            challenging the order dated June 22, 2015 passed by learned
            Additional Chief Judicial Magistrate, Haldia, Purba Medipur, in G.R.
            Case no.972 of 2015 arising out of Bhabanipur Police Station Case
            no.213 of 2015 dated June 18, 2015 under <a href="/doc/1101188/" id="a_1">Section 379</a>/<a href="/doc/812083/" id="a_2">411</a>/<a href="/doc/1768645/" id="a_3">441</a> of
            the Indian Penal Code.
</p>

<p id="p_11">                       It appears from record that Bhabanipur Police Station
            Case no.213 dated June 18, 2015 was registered on the basis of the
            written complaint submitted by Sub-Inspector of Police, Amit Deb
            on the allegation that the driver and the registered owner of ten
            wheelers truck bearing no.WB-25 D/9537 was involved in the
            offence punishable under <a href="/doc/1101188/" id="a_4">Section 379</a>/<a href="/doc/812083/" id="a_5">411</a>/<a href="/doc/94452/" id="a_6">414</a> of the Indian Penal
            Code.
</p>

<p id="p_12">                       Mr.   Anil    Kumar       Chattopadhyay,   learned   counsel
            appearing on behalf of the petitioner submits that the petitioner
            being the registered owner of the seized vehicle no.WB-25 D/9537
            filed an application before the court of learned Magistrate praying
            for return of the seized vehicle on any condition pending final
            decision of the criminal case. Learned counsel further submits that
            learned Magistrate has not yet disposed of the application filed by
            the petitioner, because the Investigating Officer has not submitted
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                             2</span>




the report called for by the learned Magistrate.
</p>

<p id="p_13">            Mr. Ayan Basu, learned counsel appearing on behalf of
the State contends that learned Magistrate may be directed to
dispose   of   the    application   filed   by   the    petitioner   and   the
Investigating Officer may be directed to submit the report within a
specified period of time.
</p>

<p id="p_14">            In the facts and circumstances of the present case,
learned Additional Chief Judicial Magistrate, Haldia, is directed to
dispose of the application filed by the petitioner for return of the
seized vehicle in connection with G.R. Case no. 972 of 2015 within a
period of four weeks from the date of communication of the order.
The Investigating Officer of Bhabanipur Police Station Case no.213
of 2015 dated June 18, 2015 under <a href="/doc/1101188/" id="a_7">Section 379</a>/<a href="/doc/812083/" id="a_8">411</a>/<a href="/doc/1768645/" id="a_9">441</a> of the
Indian Penal Code is directed to submit the report called for by
learned Magistrate in connection with the said case within a period
of two weeks from the date of communication of the order.
</p>

<p id="p_15">            Leave is granted to learned counsel for the petitioner to
communicate the order to the Investigating Officer of Bhabanipur
Police Station Case no.213 of 2015 as early as possible.
</p>

<p id="p_16">            With the above direction, criminal revision is disposed of.
</p>

<p id="p_17">            Urgent photostat certified copies of this order, if applied
for, be given to the learned counsel for the parties, upon compliance
of all formalities.
</p>

<p id="p_18">                                                       (R.K. Bag, J.)
 </p>
