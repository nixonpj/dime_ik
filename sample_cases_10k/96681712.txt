<div class="doc_title">Dilip Yadav vs The State Of Jharkhand on 6 December, 2013</div><pre id="pre_1">                       IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                                  B. A. No. 8811 of 2013
       Dilip Yadav                                       ..... Petitioner
                                         Versus
       The State of Jharkhand                            ..... Opposite Party
                                          -----
                CORAM: HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE NARENDRA NATH TIWARI
                                          -----
       For the Petitioner        - Mr. R.L.Yadav
       For the State             - Mr. A.P.P
                                          -----

5/6.12.2013</pre><p id="p_1">   The petitioner is in custody in connection with the case registered under

       <a href="/doc/1258372/" id="a_1">Sections 147</a>/<a href="/doc/763672/" id="a_2">148</a>/<a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_3">149</a>/<a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_4">323</a>/<a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_5">307</a> IPC.
</p>
<p id="p_2">              Learned counsel for the petitioner submitted that earlier the petitioner was

       granted bail in this case by order dated 28.2.2011 of this Court; thereafter, he

       attended the Court for several months; however, on 16.12.2011, he could not appear

       in the Court due to some unavoidable circumstance; his bail-bond was cancelled and

       ultimately he was taken in custody on 11.6.2013 and since then he is in custody.

       Learned counsel further submitted that the petitioner is an old man of 62 years; he has

       undertaken to abide by the terms and conditions imposed by this Court; the petitioner

       is a permanent local resident and there is no chance of his absconding.

</p><p id="p_3">              Learned A.P.P opposed the petitioner's prayer for bail. However, he has not

       disputed the fact that the petitioner was earlier granted bail and he has remained in

       custody for about six months for misuse of privilege of bail.

</p><p id="p_4">              Considering the facts and circumstances of the case, the above named

       petitioner is directed to be enlarged on bail on furnishing bail-bond of Rs. 10,000/-

       (Ten thousand only) with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of

       learned Additional Sessions Judge-I, Koderma in connection with S. T. No.

       268A/2001, arising out of Koderma P. S. Case No. 200/1999 (G. R. No. 452/1999),

       subject to the condition that the petitioner shall physically appear in the Court below as

       and when his personal attendance is required in the case.
</p>
<p id="p_5">

</p><pre id="pre_2">    S.K                                                       (NARENDRA NATH TIWARI, J)
