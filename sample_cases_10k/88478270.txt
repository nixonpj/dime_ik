<div class="doc_title">Ananya Medical Hall, Represented ... vs The State Of Bihar &amp; Ors on 9 March, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">Patna High Court CWJC No.890 of 2018 dt.09-03-2018




               IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA

                      Civil Writ Jurisdiction Case No.890 of 2018
    ===========================================================
</pre><p id="p_1">    Ananya Medical Hall, Represented By Rakesh Kumar, S/o-Ramlakhan Singh,
    Resident of Road No.5, Mithila Colony, Phulwari, P.O. -Keshri Nagar, P.S.-
    Patliputra, District-Patna.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                                                .... .... Petitioner
                                       Versus
</p><p id="p_3">    1. The State of Bihar through the Principal Secretary, Health Department,
    Government of BIhar, New Secretariat, Patna.
</p><p id="p_4">    2. That State Drug Controller-Cum-Chief Licensing Authority, New Secretariat,
    Bihar, Patna
</p><p id="p_5">    3. The Assistant, Drug Controller, Drug Control Administration, Patna
    (Licensing Authority), NMCH Patna.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                                        .... .... Respondents
    ===========================================================
           Appearance :
</p><p id="p_7">           For the Petitioner  : Mr. Ram Shankar Das, Advocate.
           For the Respondents : Mr. Nagendra Kumar, AC to AAG-9
    ===========================================================
    CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE VIKASH JAIN
    ORAL JUDGMENT
    Date: 09-03-2018

                          The present writ petition has been filed for the following

        reliefs -
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">                     "i. To quash the letter no. 245 (15) dated 02.03.2017
                     issued by the Health Department Bihar, Patna whereby
                     and whereuder it has been decided by the Department
                     that no one application for New Drugs License may be
                     obtained and not issuing new licence for sale of Drugs
                     till online arrangement.         The petitioner has already
                     applied for the grant of license before the Resp. No. 3 but
                     not issuing the license.</blockquote>
<p id="p_8">                     ii. To direct the respondents to issue licence to the
                     needy people/organizations, for occupation trade or
                     business      by     directing   the   licensing   Authority
                     empowered to grant license to the petitioner with all
 Patna High Court CWJC No.890 of 2018 dt.09-03-2018




                     relevant papers &amp; documents in a prescribed Form
                     under Drugs and Cosmetics Rule 59(2) following the
                     <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_1">Central Act</a> &amp; Rules where such power vested to the
                     Central Government to make rules and such rules cannot
                     be restricted by the State Govt. in respect of granting or
                     renewal of licences.
</p><p id="p_9">                     iii. To take appropriate and suitable action forthwith by
                     lifting the ban of issuance of new Drug licence or
                     renewal which is against the fundamental right granted
                     under <a href="/doc/935769/" id="a_2">Article 19 (1) (g)</a> of the Constitution with respect
                     to any provision or to carry on any occupation, trade or
                     business in respect of existing law lifting such restriction
                     in the interest of the public. Whereby the State Govt. can
                     not deny statutory right of the public conferred on
                     persons or citizens and such right can not be taken away
                     by any legislation imposing such restriction. The action
                     under challenge by the State Govt. which is against
                     <a href="/doc/935769/" id="a_3">Article 19 (1) (g)21</a> of Constitution of India.
                     iv. To grant immediate retail license to the petitioner to
                     sell retail drugs specified in Schedule-C under the Rule
                     59(2) of the Drugs and <a href="/doc/1891720/" id="a_4">Cosmetics Act</a>, 1945 for which
                     the petitioner took the step and deposited the requisite
                     fee challan etc. on 21.11.2017 in the Govt. treasury for
                     the purpose of issuance of Drug license in Form 20 &amp; 21
                     to run the medicinal shop smoothly for the interest of
                     the patient."
</p><p id="p_10">                          2. At the very outset, learned counsel for the petitioner

        submits that it will suffice if the petitioner's representation dated

        27.11.2017         (Annexure-8)         filed   before   the   State    Drug

        Controller-cum- Chief Licensing Authority, New Secretariat, Patna
         Patna High Court CWJC No.890 of 2018 dt.09-03-2018




                 (Respondent No. 2) is directed to be disposed of, in which event, the

                 present writ petition need not be pressed on merits.
</p>
<p id="p_11">                                  3. Learned counsel for the respondents appears and has

                 been heard.
</p>
<p id="p_12">                                  4. Having regard to the nature of the grievances and the

                 stand of the petitioner, the present writ petition is disposed of with a

                 direction to the State Drug Controller-cum- Chief Licensing Authority,

                 New Secretariat, Patna (Respondent No. 2) to consider and dispose of

                 the petitioner's representation dated 27.11.2017 (Annexure-8), if the

                 same is pending before him, after grant of opportunity of hearing to the

                 petitioner in accordance with law expeditiously and preferably within

                 a period of eight weeks from the date of receipt/production of a copy

                 of this judgment.</p><pre id="pre_2">


                                                                         (Vikash Jain, J)

Md. Ibrarul/BT
