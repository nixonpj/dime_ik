 IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB &amp; HARYANA AT CHANDIGARH

                                        Date of decision: 08.08.2018

Baljinder Kaur

                                                     ..... Petitioner

                          Versus

State of Punjab and others

                                                     ..... Respondents

CORAM: HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE RAMENDRA JAIN

PRESENT: Mr. NS Sodhi, Advocate for the petitioner.

            Mr. AS Gill, DAG, Punjab.

RAMENDRA JAIN, J. (ORAL)
</pre>
<p id="p_1">            Through this petition under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C., prayer has

been made for issuance of a direction to respondents No. 1 to 3, to protect

the life and liberty of the petitioner from un-necessary harassment/sexually

torture, creating mental tension by mobile call or open threats by

respondents No. 4 and 5, in connivance with respondent No. 6, with ulterior

motive to plant false case upon the husband of petitioner under the <a href="/doc/1727139/" id="a_2">NDPS

Act</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_2">            Learned State counsel assisted by learned counsel for

respondents No. 4 to 6 submits that on the similar petitions of the petitioner

bearing CRM-M-30752-2015 and CRM-M-3423-2016, levelling same

allegations against the police, enquiry has been conducted twice i.e. one

enquiry was conducted by high ranked officer i.e. Deputy Inspector General
                               1 of 2
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">            ::: Downloaded on - 07-10-2018 04:06:56 :::</span>
 CRM-M-7433-2016 (O&amp;M)                                                -2-
</p>
<p id="p_3">of Police, Ferozepur Range, Ferozepur and another by the Deputy

Superintendent of Police. The petitioner is a habitual smuggler of narcotic

substances. Around 16 years back in the year 2002, the petitioner was

booked in a case of smuggling of narcotics vide FIR No. 264 dated

08.09.2002, under <a href="/doc/1046310/" id="a_3">Section 15</a> of the NDPS Act, Police Station City, Abohar.

Another FIR No. 200 dated 12.10.2000, was registered against her under

<a href="/doc/1046310/" id="a_4">Section 15</a> of the NDPS Act at Police Station Jagraon. During the pendency

of instant petition, husband of the petitioner, namely; Massa Singh, has been

convicted under <a href="/doc/1566465/" id="a_5">Section 21</a> of the NDPS Act and sentenced for 15 years

vide judgment of conviction dated 18.11.2017, rendered by the learned

Judge, Special Court, Moga, in case FIR No. 155 dated 18.09.2015, under

<a href="/doc/1566465/" id="a_6">Section 21</a> of the NDPS Act, Police Station Dharamkot, for carrying

contraband worth `3 crores.         The petitioner under the garb of filing

successive petitions with mala fide intentions wants to put undue pressure

upon the police not to arrest her in case, she is found or apprehended while

smuggling narcotic substance.
</p>
<p id="p_4">            In view of the above, this Court finds no merit in the instant

petition and the same is dismissed.
</p>
