<div class="doc_title">Jog Singh vs State &amp; Anr on 25 May, 2017</div><p id="p_1">     HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR RAJASTHAN AT
                      JODHPUR
              S.B. Criminal Misc(Pet.) No. 442 / 2017
Jog Singh S/o Shri Tag Singh, By Caste Rajput, Resident of
Dhaysar, Police Station Lathi, District Jaisalmer.
</p><p id="p_2">                                                          ----Petitioner
                                  Versus
</p><p id="p_3">1. State of Rajasthan

</p><p id="p_4">2. Ramesh Barupal S/o Bhanwar Lal Barupal, Thar, Tehsil Pokaran,
District Jaisalmer, Presently/the Then Posted As A.En (O&amp;M) of
JVVNL, Fategarh, Distt Jaisalmer.
</p><p id="p_5">                                                       ----Respondents
_____________________________________________________
For Petitioner(s)     :Mr. DL Rawla
For Respondent(s) :Mr. MS Panwar, PP
_____________________________________________________
     HON'BLE DR. JUSTICE PUSHPENDRA SINGH BHATI
                                  Order
25/05/2017

</p><p id="p_6">1.          Petitioner has preferred this misc. petition under

<a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> of Cr.P.C. for quashing the FIR No.245/2016, Police

Station Prevention of Electricity Theft, Jaisalmer for the offence

under <a href="/doc/261195/" id="a_2">Sections 135</a> of Indian Electricity Act, 2003.
</p>
<p id="p_7">2.          At the outset counsel for the petitioner submitted that

the offence under <a href="/doc/132967048/" id="a_3">Section 135</a> of the Electricity Act 2003 is non-
</p>
<p id="p_8">cognizable of compoundable (but only once for any person or

consumer).
</p>
<p id="p_9">3.          <a href="/doc/132967048/" id="a_4">Section 135</a> of the Electricity Act 2003 reads as

follows:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">        "135.       Theft   of   electricity.- (1)   Whoever,

        dishonestly,-
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">                         (2 of 5)
                                                 [CRLMP-442/2017]

</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">(a) taps, makes or causes to be made any
connection with overhead, underground or under
water lines or cables, or service wires, or service
facilities of a licensee or supplier, as the case may
be; or

</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_4">(b) tampers a meter, installs or uses a tampered
meter, current reversing transformer, loop
connection or any other device or method which
interferes with accurate or proper registration,
calibration or metering of electric current or
otherwise results in a manner whereby electricity
is stolen or wasted; or
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_5">(c) damages or destroys an electric meter,
apparatus, equipment, or wire or causes or allows
any of them to be so damaged or destroyed as to
interfere with the proper or accurate metering of
electricity; or

</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_6">(d) uses electricity through a tampered meter; or
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_7">(e) uses electricity for the purpose other than for
which the usage of electricity was authorised, so
as to abstract or consume or use electricity shall
be punishable with imprisonment for a term
which may extend to three years or with fine or
with both:
</blockquote><p id="p_10">Provided that in a case where the load
abstracted, consumed, or used or attempted
abstraction  or  attempted  consumption or
attempted use-
</p><p id="p_11">(i) does not exceed 10 kilowatt, the fine imposed
on first conviction shall not be less than three
times the financial gain on account of such theft
of electricity and in the event of second or
subsequent conviction the fine imposed shall not
be less than six times the financial gain on
account of such theft of electricity;
</p>
<p id="p_12">(ii) exceeds 10 kilowatt, the fine imposed on first
conviction shall not be less than three times the
financial gain on account of such theft of
electricity and in the event of second or
subsequent conviction, the sentence shall be
imprisonment for a term not less than six
months, but which may extend to five years and
                         (3 of 5)
                                                  [CRLMP-442/2017]

with fine not less than six times the financial gain
on account of such theft of electricity:
Provided further that in the event of second and
subsequent conviction of a person where the load
abstracted, consumed, or used or attempted
abstraction     or   attempted    consumption      or
attempted use exceeds 10 kilowatt, such person
shall also be debarred from getting any supply of
electricity for a period which shall not be less than
three months but may extend to two years and
shall also be debarred from getting supply of
electricity for that period from any other source
or generating station:
</p>
<p id="p_13">Provided also that if it is proved that any artificial
means or means not authorised by the Board or
licensee or supplier, as the case may be, exist for
the abstraction, consumption or use of electricity
by the consumer, it shall be presumed, until the
contrary is proved, that any abstraction,
consumption or use of electricity has been
dishonestly caused by such consumer.
</p>
<p id="p_14">(1A) Without prejudice to the provisions of this
Act, the licensee or supplier, as the case may be,
may, upon detection of such theft of electricity,
immediately disconnect the supply of electricity:
</p>
<p id="p_15">Provided that only such officer of the licensee or
supplier, as authorised for the purpose by the
Appropriate Commission or any other officer of
the licensee or supplier, as the case may be, of
the rank higher than the rank so authorised shall
disconnect the supply line of electricity:
</p>
<p id="p_16">Provided further that such officer of the licensee
or supplier, as the case may be, shall lodge a
complaint in writing relating to the commission of
such offence in police station having jurisdiction
within twenty-four hours from the time of such
disconnection:
</p>
<p id="p_17">Provided also that the licensee or supplier, as the
case may be, on deposit or payment of the
assessed amount or electricity charges in
accordance with the provisions of this Act, shall,
without prejudice to the obligation to lodge the
complaint as referred to in the second proviso to
                                (4 of 5)
                                                       [CRLMP-442/2017]

        this clause, restore the supply line of electricity
        within forty-eight hours of such deposit or
        payment.
</p>
<p id="p_18">        (2) Any officer of the licensee or supplier as the
        case may be, Authorized in this behalf by the
        State Government may--
</p><p id="p_19">        (a) enter, inspect, break open and search any
        place or premises in which he has reason to
        believe that electricity has been or is being, used
        unauthorisedly;
</p><p id="p_20">        (b) search, seize and remove all such devices,
        instruments, wires and any other facilitator or
        article which has been or is being, used for
        unauthorised use of electricity;
</p><p id="p_21">        (c) examine or seize any books of account or
        documents which in his opinion shall be useful for
        or relevant to, any proceedings in respect of the
        offence under sub-section (1) and allow the
        person from whose custody such books of
        account or documents are seized to make copies
        thereof or take extracts therefrom in his
        presence.
</p><p id="p_22">        (3) The occupant of the place of search or any
        person on his behalf shall remain present during
        the search and a list of all things seized in the
        course of such search shall be prepared and
        delivered to such occupant or person who shall
        sign the list:
</p><p id="p_23">        Provided that no inspection, search and seizure of
        any domestic places or domestic premises shall
        be carried out between sunset and sunrise except
        in the presence of an adult male member
        occupying such premises.
</p>
<p id="p_24">        (4) The provisions<a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_5"> of the Code</a> of Criminal
        Procedure, 1973 (2 of 1974) relating to search
        and seizure shall apply, as far as may be, to
        searches and seizure under this Act."
</p>

<p id="p_25">4.        In light of the language of <a href="/doc/132967048/" id="a_6">Section 135</a>, we deem it

appropriately to direct the investigating authority to complete the
                                    (5 of 5)
                                                                [CRLMP-442/2017]

investigation strictly in accordance with terms of <a href="/doc/132967048/" id="a_7">Section 135</a> of

Electricity Act 2003. The investigating authority shall consider the

terms of <a href="/doc/132967048/" id="a_8">Section 135</a> of the Electricity Act 2003 and also the fact

which     as    come    on   record     in    S.B.   Criminal    Misc.     Bail

No.11043/2016, whereby the record was placed before this Court

showing that the petitioner had already deposited the amount of

Rs.2,49,890/- in terms of the statement made in the VCR. Thus,

in the terms of <a href="/doc/132967048/" id="a_9">Section 135</a> of the Electricity Act, if the petitioner

has paid the amount and has not committed any offence before

then the investigation may be accordingly completed.
</p>
<p id="p_26">5.             Consequently, this misc. petition is disposed of with the

aforesaid directions.
</p>
<p id="p_27">                                 (DR. PUSHPENDRA SINGH BHATI)J.
</p>
<p id="p_28">sudheer
