                               Judgment


Koshy, Ag.C.J.

<p id="p_5">            This writ appeal is filed with permission by a person who is

not a party to the writ petition. The writ petitioner earlier filed W.P.(C)

No.13521 of 2008 wherein the appellant was respondent No.5 seeking

direction to the Taluk Surveyor to survey and demarcate her property.

That matter is under consideration in that case. Without disclosing the

pendency of that writ petition, the petitioner thereafter filed this writ

petition without making the aggrieved party as a respondent and

obtained an innocuous order. The relief prayed for in both petitions

were same. In effect, the prayer in both cases was to direct the Taluk

Surveyor to measure and demarcate her property. In fact, if there is

any property dispute, appropriate remedy is to file civil dispute. In any

event, filing of writ petition without disclosing the pendency of the

earlier petition and also without making the affected person in the

party array has to be discouraged.         Therefore, we set aside the

impugned judgment and proceedings pursuant thereto. By demanding

the property without deciding the civil dispute has no effect. If there is

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">W.A.No.350/2009                     2</span>

any civil dispute regarding the property, petitioner has to approach the

civil court and get the property demarcated by getting appropriate

directions from the court. In view of suppression of the material fact of

pendency of another writ petition by her on the same subject, we

award a cost of Rs.3,000/- to be paid by the petitioner who is

respondent No.1 in the appeal to the appellant. However, we make it

clear that any observation in this judgment shall not affect disposal of

the earlier writ petition. The question whether petitioner owns 17.5

cents or 20 cents of land in partition etc. can be decided only in a civil

suit.
</p>
<p id="p_6">            The writ appeal is allowed.
</p>
