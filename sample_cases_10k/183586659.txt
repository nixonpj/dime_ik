                                                         CORAM

                              THE HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE M.M.SUNDRESH
                                           AND
                     THE HONOURALE MR. JUSTICE K.KALYANASUNDARAM,J.

                                        Contempt Petition No.521 of 2019


                 Selvaraj                                                        .. Petitioner
                                                             Vs.

                 1.Subramanian, I.A.S.,
                  District Collector,
                  Villupuram District.

                 2.Lakshmi Priya,
                  Assistant Director,
                  The Department of Geology and Mining,
                  Collector Office Campus,
                  Villupuram District.                                          ... Respondents

                          Contempt petition is filed under <a href="/doc/974692/" id="a_1">Section 11</a> of the Contempt of
                 Courts Act, 1971, to punish the respondents 1 and 2 for their willful
                 disobedience of the order dated 31.10.2018 passed in W.P.No.26108
                 of 2018.
                          For petitioner        :      Mr.K.Balakrishnan
                          For respondent        :      Mr.M.Elumalai
                                                       Government Advocate


                                                        ORDER
</pre>

<p id="p_1">                          Alleging      that   the     order       dated   31.10.2018   passed    in

                 W.P.No.26108 of 2018 has not been complied with, the present

http://www.judis.nic.incontempt   petition has been filed.
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                           2</span>

</p><p id="p_2">                            2. The petitioner herein was arrayed as the seventh respondent

                      in the writ petition. The writ petition was disposed of by taking note

                      of the statement made by the writ petitioner.



</p><p id="p_3">                            3. The learned Government Advocate             appearing for the

                      respondents submits that now the petitioner has come forward to file

                      a petition inter alia alleging that      though the representation was

                      disposed of without any positive order, he was not permitted to do

                      quarrying operation coupled with a ground to initiate contempt.



</p><p id="p_4">                            4. Certainly it constitutes a separate cause of action. For this,

                      the petitioner has to work out the remedy by a separate proceedings

                      in the manner known to law.     Suffice    it is to state that there is no

                      willful disobedience of the order.    Accordingly, the contempt petition

                      stands closed.
</p>
