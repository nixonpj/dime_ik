<div class="doc_title">Amit vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 9 May, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">      Misc. Criminal Case No.4272/2016
09/05/2016

       Shri L.S. Chandaramani, learned counsel for the applicant.
       Shri Aniket Naik, learned Deputy Govt. Advocate               for the
respondent/State.
       Heard.
                             ORDER
</pre><p id="p_1">     This is repeat(second) application under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of Cr.P.C. The
applicant has been arrested in Crime No. 239/2015 registered at Police
Station Manpur District-Indore, for the offence punishable under <a href="/doc/92983/" id="a_2">Sections
306</a> and <a href="/doc/386021/" id="a_3">201</a> of the IPC. First application was dismissed as not pressed
vide order dated 07/10/2015 passed in M.Cr.C. No. 8285/2015 with a
liberty to file fresh application after recording the statement of the witness
Sangita.
</p><p id="p_2">       According to the prosecution case at about 8:30 a.m. Chowkidar of
Biccholi gave information that in the field of Shankarlal a dead body of a
girl was lying, then merg was recorded and dead body was identified as of
Heena. During investigation it was found that Sangita was residing as a
room partner of Heena. One Amit used to visit at her house to meet with
Heena and Amit wanted to marry with her. Thereafter, Amit started
suspecting on her character and on this issue quarrel took place between
them. Thereafter, Sangita started living separately, Heena talked with
Sangita on phone some time. Heena told her on phone that Amit used to
harass her and due to this harassment she would commit suicide.
Thereafter, she has committed suicide.
</p><p id="p_3">       Learned counsel for the applicant submits that the applicant has not
committed any offence. He has falsely been implicated. During trial
evidence of prosecutition witnessess were recorded. But they have not
supported the prosecution case. He is under custody since 11/07/2015.
There is no likelihood of his absconsion . The conclusion of trial likely to
take time. Hence, prayed for bail.
</p><p id="p_4">       The prayer is opposed by learned Deputy Govt. Advocate .
       Case diary perused.
</p><p id="p_5">       As per post-mortem report of Heena, death has been caused due
to Asphyxia as a result of hanging .
</p><p id="p_6">          Taking into consideration the allegation against the applicant and
statements of the witnessess coupled with the fact that applicant is under
custody since 07/10/2015 and charge sheet has been filed , but without
commenting anything on the merits of the case, the application is allowed.
It is directed that the applicant shall be released on bail on her furnishing
personal bond in the sum of Rs.50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand Only)
with one solvent surety in the like amount to the satisfaction of Trial
Court.
</p><p id="p_7">         This order will remain operative subject to compliance of the
following conditions by the applicant :-
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">      1. The applicant will comply with all the terms and conditions of the
         bond executed by her;
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">      2. The applicant will cooperate in the investigation/trial, as the case
         may be;
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">      3. The applicant will not indulge herself in extending inducement,
         threat or promise to any person acquainted with the facts of the
         case so as to dissuade him/her from disclosing such facts to the
         Court or to the Police Officer, as the case may be;
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_4">      4. The applicant shall not commit an offence similar to the offence of
         which she is accused;
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_5">      5. The applicant will not seek unnecessary adjournments during the
         trial; and
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_6">      6. The applicant will not leave India without previous permission of the
         trial Court/Investigating Officer, as the case may be.
         A copy of this order be sent to the Court concerned for compliance.
         C.c. as per rules.</blockquote>




<p id="p_8">                                                          (D.K.Paliwal)
                                                               Judge
skt
