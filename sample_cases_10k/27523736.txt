<div class="doc_title">Abul Kalam Azad Alias Kalu vs Unknown on 12 February, 2015</div><div class="doc_author">Author: Subhro Kamal Mukherjee</div>

<p id="p_1"><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                          1</span>



     S/L. 63
February 12, 2015.                                 C.R.M. 93 of 2015
         ss.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                     In re: An application for bail under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of the Code of
                     Criminal Procedure filed on January 5, 2015 in connection with
                     Harishchandrapur Police Station Case No. 614 of 2012 dated
                     December 3, 2012 under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_2">Sections 302</a>/<a href="/doc/386021/" id="a_3">201</a>/<a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_4">120B</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_5">34</a> of the Indian
                     Penal Code, 1860.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                                         And

                     In the matter of: Abul Kalam Azad alias Kalu
                                                                                  ...Petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                           Mr. Sekhar Basu,
                           Mr. Ranadeb Sengupta,
                           Mr. Pritam Roy                               ...for the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                           Mr. Arun Maity                                    ...for the State.
</p>

<p id="p_6">                              Heard the learned advocates appearing on behalf of the
                     respective parties.
</p>

<p id="p_7">                                 The accused/petitioner is seeking bail in connection with
                     a   case     relating    to     offences     punishable     under    <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_6">Sections
                     302</a>/<a href="/doc/386021/" id="a_7">201</a>/<a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_8">120B</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_9">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code, 1860.
</p>

<p id="p_8">                             After considering case diary and considering the materials
                     available in the record, we are not inclined to grant bail to the
                     accused/petitioner at this stage.
</p>

<p id="p_9">                                The application for bail is, thus, rejected at this stage.
</p>

<p id="p_10">                                However, the learned Judge is requested to conclude the
                     trial as expeditiously as possible, if necessary by conducting the trial
                     on day to day basis.
</p>


<p id="p_11">                                                          (Subhro Kamal Mukherjee, J.)



                                                              (Indrajit Chatterjee, J.)
