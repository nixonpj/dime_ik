<div class="doc_title">Manager Hdfc Ergo General ... vs Shri Rajendra Devda Since ... on 30 September, 2019</div><p id="p_1">      HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH, BENCH AT INDORE

                           Miscellaneous Appeal No.584/2019
          (Manager, HDFC Ergo Gen. Ins. Co. Ltd. Vs. Rajesh Devda and Others)
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                             -1-</span>

Indore, dated 30/09/2019
              Parties through their counsel.
              IA.No.5395/2019 is allowed.
              The claimants shall be free to withdraw the amount subject to
furnishing solvent surety. IA stands disposed of.
</p><p id="p_2">              The appeal is admitted on the following Substantial Questions of
Law:-
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">              "i)     Whether, the findings recorded by the learned Commissioner
                      holding death to have arisen out of and during the course of
                      employment are perverse and against the evidence on record?
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_2">              ii)     Whether, the findings recorded by the learned Commissioner
                      holding the death to have nexus with the alleged employment is
                      contrary to the evidence on record?
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_3">              iii)    Whether, the learned Commissioner erred in not considering the
                      fact that as per <a href="/doc/1850559/" id="a_1">Section 3</a> of the Workmen Compensation Act
                      there has to be an accident and admittedly there was no
                      accident in the instant case and moreover policy which has been
                      issued by the appellant as as per <a href="/doc/87183818/" id="a_2">Section 147</a> of the Motor
                      Vehicles Act and, therefore, there has to be an accident arising
                      out of and use of motor vehicle then only liability, if any, under
                      the policy would arise, in terms of the policy?
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_4">              iv)     Whether, the learned Commissioner further has erred in allowing
                      medical expenses without appreciating legal provision as
                      contained under <a href="/doc/176854/" id="a_3">Section 4(2)(2A)</a> of Workmen (Employees)
                      Compensation Act?
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_5">              v)      Whether, the learned Commissioner erred in holding the burden
                      to prove that the deceased driver did not possess a valid and
                      effective driving license upon appellant, when the claimants
                      came up with a specific case that the deceased was employed
                      as Driver on the vehicle, and therefore, the burden to prove that
                      the deceased had the license was upon the claimants /
                      employer?
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_6">              List the same for final hearing in due course.</blockquote>
