                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   18-07-2019                     The grievance of the petitioner in the present writ

                   petition is non-payment of salary for the period from March,

                   2018 onward.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                 Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that the

                   petitioner has been regularly working but she has not been paid

                   salary regularly.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                 Taking work and denying salary amounts to Beggari

                   which is violative of <a href="/doc/1071750/" id="a_1">Article 23</a> of the Constitution.

</p><p id="p_4">                                 Considering the claim of the petitioner that she has

                   been regularly working but not paid salary, the writ petition is
             Patna High Court CWJC No.13863 of 2018(2) dt.18-07-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                       2/2</span>




                      disposed of with a direction to the respondents to ascertain

                      whether the petitioner has worked for the period she is claiming

                      salary or not. In the event, after verification, the District

                      Programme Officer, comes to the conclusion that the petitioner

                      has actually worked, he shall ensure payment of salary to the

                      petitioner at the earliest preferably within a maximum period of

                      60 days from the date of receipt/production of a copy of this

                      order.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                  With the aforesaid, the writ petition stands disposed

                      of.
</p>
<p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">                                            (Anil Kumar Upadhyay, J)
spandey/-
</p>
