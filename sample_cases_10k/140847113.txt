<div class="doc_title">Jagdish Narayan Jha vs The Uttar Bihar Gramin Bank &amp; Anr on 16 September, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                Civil Writ Jurisdiction Case No.14778 of 2014
                 ======================================================
                 Jagdish Narayan Jha s/o Late Subodh Kant Jha, r/o Village &amp; Post Office
                 Bhikha Bhagwanpur via Madhepu, Police Station Jhanjharpur, District
                 Madhubani, presently residing at village-Behat, Police Station Jhanjharpur,
                 district Madhubani.
                                                                        .... .... Petitioner/s
                                                    Versus
                 1.The Uttar Bihar Gramin Bank through its Chairman Head Office,
                 Kalambagh Chowk, Muzaffarpur.
                 2.The Branch Manager, Uttar Bihar Gramin Bank Rambagh Chowk,
                 Rambagh Chowk, Madhepur, Post Office &amp; Police Station Madhepur,
                 District Madhubani.
                                                                       .... .... Respondent/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :   Mr. Prabhas Ranjan, Adv
                 For the Respondent/s       : Mr. Prabhakar Jha, Adv
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE MIHIR KUMAR JHA
                                                ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">3   16-09-2014             Heard learned counsel for the parties as with

                  regard to the following reliefs prayed in this writ

                  application:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">                               "A. A certiorari for setting aside the Notice
                           dated 17.06.2014 (Annexure-2) issued by the
                           Branch Manager, Utter Bihar Gramin Bank,
                           Branch Rambagh Chowk, Madhubani, whereby
                           notice under Section 13(4) of Surfesi Act has been
                           issued.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">                               (B)       A      Mandamus            commanding            the
                           respondents to settle the credit account 01/1999
                           (Loan Account) of the petitioner under one time
                           settlement scheme after adjusting his amount in
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Patna High Court CWJC No.14778 of 2014 (3) dt.16-09-2014                    2</span>




                       the loan account and thereafter no dues certificate
                       be issued to him."</blockquote><p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">                       Considering the aforesaid prayer this Court on

              02.09.2014 had passed an interim order relevant portion

              whereof reads as follows:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_3">                             While the petitioner is of the view that the
                       total dues against him for clearing the liability
                       would be in the vicinity of Rs. 3 lacs because of
                       his loan account of Rs. 2,54,336.81/- having
                       been declared as N.P.A., he will not be required
                       to pay any other interest beyond one fixed by
                       the R.B.I guidelines, learned counsel for the
                       Uttar Bihar Gramin Bank (hereinafter referred
                       to as the Bank) on instruction submits that the
                       total dues against the petitioner as on date is to
                       the tune of Rs. 11,81,855/-.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_4">                             This Court would accordingly direct the
                       Bank to file its counter affidavit not only with a
                       copy of the R.B.I policy but also with
                       calculation strictly made in terms of the R.B.I.,
                       policy as with regard to the payment of interest
                       of N.P.A account.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_5">                             It however goes without saying that the
                       pendency of this writ application will not stand
                       in the way of the petitioner in making adhoc
                       payment of Rs. 1.5 lacs and if the same is paid
                       by him to Bank by 8th of September, 2014, no
                       coercive step shall be taken against him till the
                       final disposal of this writ application.</blockquote><p id="p_4">

</p><p id="p_5">                       Pursuant to the aforesaid order the Bank has filed

              its counter affidavit and the issue at the end of the day
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2"> Patna High Court CWJC No.14778 of 2014 (3) dt.16-09-2014                3</span>




              gets settled by the counter affidavit filed by the Uttar

              Bihar Gramin Bank (hereinafter referred to as the Bank),

              wherein, it has been explained that the petitioner on

              account of laches on his part became liable to pay Rs.

              5,51,627/- out of which the petitioner has already paid

              Rs. 1.5 lacs under the order of this Court dated 2.9.2014

              and therefore, the liability of the petitioner now remains

              virtually of Rs. 4 lacs approximately..

</p><p id="p_6">                       Learned counsel for the petitioner has submitted

              that the petitioner is also prepared to pay the balance

              amount of Rs. 4 lacs approximately as per the

              calculation of the Bank in four equal monthly

              installments.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                       This Court would accordingly direct the petitioner

              to pay sum of Rs. 1 lacs on or before 15th of October,

              2014, whereafter the subsequent monthly installment of

              Rs. 1 lac will be paid by him on 15th of November, 2014,

              15th of December, 2014 and 15th of January, 2015. In the

              last installment payable in the month of January 2015,
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3"> Patna High Court CWJC No.14778 of 2014 (3) dt.16-09-2014                                    4</span>




              the petitioner would be liable to pay the up-to-date

              interest @ 9 per cent annum which would accrue on the

              balance sum of Rs. 5,51,627/ - as on 30.8.2014 vide

              Annexure-B to the counter affidavit which for sake

              clarity and convenience is also quoted herein below:-

</p><blockquote id="blockquote_6">                                  "UTTAR BIHAR GRAMIN BANK

                       REGIONAL OFFICE JHANJHAPUR

                       BRANCH:- RAMBAGH CHOWK

                       A/C NO.:- 1003551310001148{CC01}

                       A/C NAME :- M/S SHANKAR BRICK INDUSTRY

                                   SRI JAGDISH NR. JHA S/O LATE SHOBHA KANT JHA

                                   AT &amp; PO:- BHITH BHAGWANPUR,

                                   VIA- MADHEPUR, MADHUBANI 847408

                       LOAN LIMIT         3,00,000.00      AS PER HO LETTER NO. 73

                                                            DATED 14.01.1999

               TREATMENT MINIMUM AMOUNT TO BE RECOVERED
               A. RUNNING LEDGER BALANCE ON THE DATE OF Rs. 2,54,897.00
               NPA THAT IS 30.06.2003
               B. SIMPLE INTT. @ 9% {11 YEAR 2 MONTHS)  Rs. 2,59,230.00
               C. LEGAL DUES {i} Valuer Charge          Rs. 12,000.00
                               {ii} Paper Publication   Rs. 25,500.00
                                   Total                Rs. 5,51,627.00

               Less Yecovery                                                         0.00

               Net Minimum to                                             Rs. 5,51,627.00
               be Recovery



                           Sd/-
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_7">                       BRANCH MANAGER
                          09/09/14"
</blockquote><span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">       Patna High Court CWJC No.14778 of 2014 (3) dt.16-09-2014                            5</span>




<blockquote id="blockquote_8">                             It also goes without saying that if the petitioner

                    clears the Bank's liability to the full satisfaction of the

                    authorities of the Bank on or before 15th January 2015,

                    the petitioner would be entitled for issuance of no dues

                    certificate.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_9">                             At this stage learned counsel for the Bank has

                    pointed out that Miscellaneous Case No. 02 of 2002 has

                    also been instituted by the petitioner against the Bank

                    for settlement of account and refund of certain amount.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_10">                    Now, when this Court has passed this order after taking

                    into account the entire transaction between the petitioner

                    and the Bank, the aforementioned case has become

                    infructuous and would accordingly be disposed of by the

                    concerned court.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_11">                             With        the     aforementioned           observation   and

                    direction, this application is disposed of.</blockquote><p id="p_8">



</p><p id="p_9">                                                                 (Mihir Kumar Jha, J)
    Ranjan/-
</p><p id="p_10">U
