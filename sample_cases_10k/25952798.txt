<div class="doc_title">Richpal vs State Of U.P. on 3 December, 2019</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: Vipin Sinha</div>

<pre id="pre_1">




HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT ALLAHABAD
 
 

?Court No. - 51
 

 
Case :- CRIMINAL MISC. BAIL APPLICATION No. - 25886 of 2019
 

 
Applicant :- Richpal
 
Opposite Party :- State of U.P.
 
Counsel for Applicant :- Mohd. Afzal,Ardhendu Shekhar Sharma,Ram Babu Sharma
 
Counsel for Opposite Party :- G.A.,Anil Kumar Mishra
 

 
Hon'ble Vipin Sinha,J.
</pre> 
<p id="p_1">Heard learned counsel for the applicant, learned counsel for the complainant and Sri Mithilesh Kumar, learned A. G. A. for the State.
</p> 
<p id="p_2">Applicant has moved the present bail application seeking bail in Case Crime No. 81 of 2019, under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_1">Sections 498A</a>, <a href="/doc/653797/" id="a_2">304B</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_3">504</a> I.P.C. and <a href="/doc/751411/" id="a_4">Section 3</a>/<a href="/doc/1023340/" id="a_5">4</a> Dowry Prohibition Act, P.S. Mundali, District Meerut.
</p> 
<p id="p_3">I have perused the prosecution story as set up in the F.I.R. and also the bail rejection order.
</p> 
<p id="p_4">The contention as raised at the bar by learned counsel for the applicant is that applicant has been falsely implicated in the present case; that applicant happens to be father-in-law and husband has already been arrested and he is in jail; that daughter-in-law has committed suicide and as per post mortem report cause of death is hanging and there are no other injury on the body of the deceased; that applicant is a senior citizen aged about 62 years. It is lastly contended that the accused applicant is in jail since 30.05.2019 and in case he is released on bail, he will not misuse the said liberty. 
</p> 
<p id="p_5">The bail application has been vehemently opposed by learned A. G. A.
</p> 
<p id="p_6">Keeping in view the reasons as stated above, the facts and circumstances of the case as have been discussed at the Bar of this Court, without expressing any opinion on the merits of the case, at this stage, prima facie, a case for bail has been made out. However, the said prima facie view of this Court will not in any manner adversely affect the case of the prosecution.
</p> 
<p id="p_7">The prayer for bail is granted. The application is allowed. 
</p> 
<p id="p_8">Let the applicant Richpal involved in the aforesaid case crime number be released on bail on his executing a personal bond and furnishing two sureties each in the like amount to the satisfaction of the court concerned with the following conditions:
</p> 
<p id="p_9">i) The applicant shall not tamper with the prosecution evidence. 
</p> 
<p id="p_10">ii) The applicant shall not threaten or harass the prosecution witnesses. 
</p> 
<p id="p_11">iii) The applicant shall appear on the date fixed by the trial court. 
</p> 
<p id="p_12">iv) The applicant shall not commit an offence similar to the offence of which the applicant is accused, or suspected of the commission, of which applicant is suspected.
</p> 
<p id="p_13">v) The applicant shall not directly make any inducement, threat or promise to any person acquainted with the facts of the case so as to dissuade the applicant from disclosing such facts to the Court or to any police officer or tamper with the evidence. 
</p> 
<p id="p_14">In case of default of any of the conditions enumerated above, the learned counsel for the complainant is free to move an application for cancellation of bail before this Court. 
</p> 
<p id="p_15">Order Date :- 3.12.2019
 
Ujjawal
