<p id="p_1">[1]   Learned advocate Mr. Mahesh Poojara, states that he
has instructions to appear for respondent No.2 - complainant.
</p>

<p id="p_2">[2]   Learned advocate Mr. Mahesh Poojara confirms identity
of respondent No.2 - original complainant, who is present in
the Court and admits correctness and genuineness of the
affidavit filed by him through learned advocate Mr. Mahesh
Poojara, which is annexed at Annexure B.
</p>

<p id="p_3">[3]   Rule. Learned A.P.P. and learned advocate Mr. Mahesh
Poojara waive service of Rule for respondent Nos.1 and 2
respectively.       Learned APP objects quashment of present
proceedings on the premise of settlement.
</p>
<p id="p_4">[4]   With the consent of learned advocate for the applicants
and learned advocate for the respondents, present application

is taken up for final disposal today. The parties are present
before     the      Court       alongwith         their     respective         learned
advocates.
</p>
<p id="p_5">[5]   By way of the present application under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> of
the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (for short, the 'Code'),
the applicants pray for quashing and setting aside the F.I.R.
being C.R.No.I-76 of 2019 registered with Savar Kundla Town
Police Station for the offence punishable under <a href="/doc/1944660/" id="a_2">Sections 384</a>,
<a href="/doc/1128948/" id="a_3">120(B)</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_4">506(2)</a> of the Indian Penal Code and u/s 40, 40(a) and
40(b) of the Gujarat Money Lending Act.
</p>
<p id="p_6">[6]   Learned advocate for the applicants has taken this Court
through the factual matrix arising out of the present
application.
</p>
<p id="p_7">[7]      At the outset, it is submitted that the parties have
amicably resolved the dispute. In support of such submission
made at bar by the learned advocates appearing for the
respective parties, they have placed on record affidavit of
settlement of dispute duly signed by the respondent No.2 -
original complainant, who is present before the Court.
</p>
<p id="p_8">[8]   Since now, the dispute with reference to the impugned
F.I.R. is settled and resolved by and between parties which is
confirmed by the original complainant through his learned
advocate,       the     trial   would     be       futile    and      any       further
continuation of proceedings would amount to abuse of process
of law. Therefore, the impugned F.I.R. is required to be
quashed and set aside.
</p>

<p id="p_9">[9]   Resultantly, this application is allowed. Impugned F.I.R.
