<div class="doc_title">City Gold Multiplex Cinema vs Commissioner Of Entertainments ... on 19 April, 2018</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: Akil Kureshi, B.N. Karia</div>

<pre id="pre_1">        C/SCA/5309/2018                              ORDER



         IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

          R/SPECIAL CIVIL APPLICATION NO. 5309 of 2018

==========================================================
                CITY GOLD MULTIPLEX CINEMA
                           Versus
     COMMISSIONER OF ENTERTAINMENTS TAX, GUJARAT STATE
==========================================================
Appearance:
MR. AUM M KOTWAL(7320) for the PETITIONER(s) No. 1
MR.CHINTAN DAVE, AGP (1) for the RESPONDENT(s) No. 1,2,3
==========================================================

 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE AKIL KURESHI
        and
        HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE B.N. KARIA

                          Date : 19/04/2018

                       ORAL ORDER</pre>
<p id="p_1"> (PER : HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE B.N. KARIA)

</p><p id="p_2">1.    Being   aggrieved   by   the   order   passed   by   the 

learned   Commissioner   of   Entertainment   Tax,   Gujarat 

State­the respondent no.1, the petitioner has prayed 

to quash and set aside the order dated 05.03.2018 and 

condone   the   delay   in   preferring   the   Revision 

Application   preferred   by   the   petitioner.     Rule   was 

issued on 05.04.2018.   Mr.Chintan Dave, learned AGP 

appeared for the respondents.  
</p>
<p id="p_3">
</p><p id="p_4">2.    Heard   learned   counsel   Mr.Aum   Kotwal   for   the 

petitioner   and   learned   AGP   Mr.Chintan   Dave   for   the 

respondents.  It is submitted by the learned counsel 

for   the   petitioner   that   the   order   passed   by   the 

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                              Page 1 of 5</span>
         C/SCA/5309/2018                             ORDER



respondent no.1 dated 31.03.2017 was not known to the 

petitioner   as   the   acquainted   employee   of   the 

petitioner had left the job and therefore, record and 

documents were not available with the petitioner with 

respect   to   the   subject   matter   and   hence,   the 

petitioner   was   unable   to   file   Revision   Application 

within   90  days   as  provided   under   section   13  of   the 

Gujarat   Entertainment   Tax   Act,   1977.     That   the 

Revisional   Authority   was   required   to   consider   the 

prayer   of   the   petitioner,   without   considering   the 

real   dispute   between   the   petitioner   and   the 

respondent,   on   technical   ground,   the   prayer   to 

condone   the   delay   was   rejected   and   the   application 

was dismissed by the respondent authority.  Hence, it 

was requested by learned counsel for the petitioner 

to   quash   and   set   aside   the   order   dated   05.03.2018 

passed   by   the   Commissioner   of   Entertainment   Tax, 

Gujarat State, in the interest of justice.


</p><p id="p_5">3.   Learned AGP Mr.Chintan Dave for the respondents 

submitted that however, a copy of the order passed by 

the   respondent   authority   dated   31.03.2017   was 

conveyed   to   the   petitioner   in   time   and   the   grounds 

raised by the petitioner for not supplying the copy 


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                             Page 2 of 5</span>
         C/SCA/5309/2018                           ORDER



of   the   impugned   order   by   the   employee,   who   has 

hurriedly   left   the   employment   of   the   petitioner, 

cannot be a genuine or sufficient ground to condone 

the   delay.     It   is   further   submitted   that   the 

petitioner   must   be   vigilant   in   approaching   the 

concerned   authority   while   challenging   the   impugned 

order   passed   by   the   authority.     Hence,   it   was 

requested by him to retain the decision.  


</p><p id="p_6">4.   Having considered the facts of the case and the 

submissions   made   by   the   learned   advocate   for   the 

petitioner   as   well   as   learned   AGP   for   the 

respondents,   it   appears   that   the   Mamlatdar   of 

Entertainment Tax was pleased to pass an order dated 

14.10.2016 against the petitioner by imposing a short 

levied   tax   of   Rs.16,75,533/­   with   interest   at   the 

rate of 18%.  Being dissatisfied with the order dated 

14.10.2016,  the  petitioner   preferred   an  appeal   with 

the authority under section 12 of the Act.   Learned 

Collector   of   Entertainment   Tax,   Ahmedabad,   was 

pleased to reject the appeal and confirmed the order 

passed by the learned Mamlatdar of Entertainment Tax, 

Ahmedabad   dated   14.10.2016.   Being   aggrieved   and 

dissatisfied   with   the   order   passed   against   the 


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                           Page 3 of 5</span>
         C/SCA/5309/2018                             ORDER



petitioner,   the   petitioner   preferred   a   Revision 

Application   under   section   13   of   the   Act   along   with 

the   stay   application   and   an   application   for 

condonation   of   delay   raising   a   ground   that   the 

employee who was acquainted with the matter, had left 

the job and it was that employee who possibly had all 

the record and documents with regard to said matter 

and   because   of   the   said   difficulty,   the   petitioner 

was   not   in   a   position   to   file   the   Revision 

Application  before   the   competent   authority   in   time. 

It   appears   from   the   order   passed   by   the   respondent 

authority   dated   05.03.2018   observed   that   due   to 

mistake   on  the   part  of   the  management  as   no  appeal 

was preferred and certified copy was applied by the 

petitioner   on   29.12.2017   and   was   available   to   the 

petitioner   on   05.01.2018,   however,   the   objections 

were   removed   on   15.02.2018.     The   grounds   raised   by 

the petitioner were not found genuine and prayer to 

condone the delay was rejected.  Learned Commissioner 

of the Entertainment Tax has not given any reasons in 

dismissing the application for condonation of delay. 

We are of the view that no technical approach would 

be adopted by the concerned authority denying justice 



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                             Page 4 of 5</span>
              C/SCA/5309/2018                      ORDER



to the litigant and/or to the petitioner.  Hence, we 

are   of   the   view   that   the   order   passed   by   the 

respondent authority dated 05.03.2018 requires to be 

quashed and set aside and the petition is required to 

be allowed.   Hence, the petition is hereby allowed. 

Impugned   order   passed   by   the   respondent   authority 

dated 05.03.2018 is hereby quashed and set aside and 

the   delay   of   209   days   caused   in   preferring   the 

Revision Application is hereby condoned.  


</p><p id="p_7">5.     It is put to our notice that the petitioner has 

deposited 25% amount of the tax demand raised by the 

authority.     The   same   shall   be   adjusted   by   the 

Commissioner of Entertainment Tax, subject to outcome 

of   the   final   decision   in   the   Revision   Application 

preferred by the petitioner.   
</p>
<p id="p_8">
</p><p id="p_9">6.     Petition is disposed of accordingly.




</p><p id="p_10">                                              (AKIL KURESHI, J)




                                                 (B.N. KARIA, J)
ANKIT SHAH
