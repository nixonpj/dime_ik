JUDGMENT
</pre>
<p id="p_1">          Plaintiff filed a suit for recovery of Rs.231,672.09 and stated as under :-
</p>

<p id="p_2">1.        That Defendant applied for grant of loan of Rs.2,75,000/- against security
     of the vehicle i.e. 'INDIGO / LX' under the Loan cum Hypothecation Scheme
     of the plaintiff, which was sanctioned by the plaintiff bank. This amount was to
     be repaid in 48 equal monthly installments of Rs.7,550/- each. Defendant has
     executed documents in favour of plaintiff bank i.e. Credit facility application,
     Deed of Hypothecation, Irrevocable power of attorney authorising the plaintiff
     to take over the possession of the vehicle and sell the same to appropriate dues
     in the event of default, all dated 17.10.2006. Defendant failed to pay the loan
     amount and there was persistent default.
</p>
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">SUIT NO. 590/2008                                                               Page 1 of 3</span>
<p id="p_3"> 2.        Defendant did not adhere to the financial discipline of the repayment of the
     loan. However, several cheques/ECS instructions till date issued by the
     defendant against repayment of loan got dishonoured. Defendant further failed
     to make the said vehicle available for inspection to the officials of the Plaintiff
     in consonance with the agreement entered between the parties. Plaintiff sent
     demand notice dated 25.06.2008 to the defendant terminating the loan
     agreement and calling upon the defendant to repay the entire outstanding
     amount and return the vehicle which was hypothecated to the bank under the
     agreement, was sent by plaintiff bank but defendant failed to comply.
</p>

<p id="p_4">3.         Earlier plaintiff filed the present case under section 37 CPC but on the
     request of Plaintiff, present suit has been treated as ordinary vide order dated
     11.08.2008.
</p>

<p id="p_5">4.        Defendant could not be served through ordinary mode and was served by
     way of publication in the newspaper 'The Hindu' vide publication dated
     31.05.2010 but despite service defendant did not turn up subsequently
     thereafter till date and he was proceeded exparte on 10.08.2010.
</p>

<p id="p_6">5.        Plaintiff has led evidence by way of affidavit of Sh. Sameer Dhar, AR of
     plaintiff bank. He appeared to tender the affidavit on 09.05.2011 and
     documents were exhibited. Copy of power of attorney in favour of Sh. Sameer
     Dhar is Ex.PW1/1, Original Credit Facility application is Ex.PW1/2, Original
     deed of hypothecatiobn is Ex.PW1/3, Irrevocable power of attorney is
     Ex.PW1/4, Copy of notice is Ex.PW1/5. The Postal receipt is Ex.PW1/6,
     Statement of account dated 21.06.2008 duly certified under Bankers' book of
     <a href="/doc/1953529/" id="a_1">Evidence Act</a>, 1891 is Ex.PW1/7. Power of attorney in favour of Sh. Anil
     Panchal is marked as mark-X. Suit of the Plaintiff was filed on 08.08.2008 and
     the same was within time.
</p>
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">SUIT NO. 590/2008                                                               Page 2 of 3</span>
<p id="p_7"> 6.        Plaintiff proved its case by oral as well as documentary evidence. There is
     no reason not to rely upon unrebutted and uncontroverted oral and documentary
     testimony of the plaintiff. Suit of the plaintiff is decreed for sum of
     Rs.231,672.09 alongwith, pendente lite and future Interest @ 6% per annum
     with ex-parte costs of the suit. Decree-sheet be prepared accordingly. File be
     consigned to record room.
</p>
<p id="p_8">Announced in the open court
On this 12th day of March, 2013
                                              (Saurabh Pratap Singh Laler)
                                              ACJ/ARC(West)
                                              Tis Hazari, Delhi
