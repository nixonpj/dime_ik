<div class="doc_title">Navjot Kaur And Another vs State Of Punjab And Others on 20 April, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1"> IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB &amp; HARYANA AT CHANDIGARH

                                         CRM-M-13379 of 2017(O&amp;M)
                                         Date of decision : 20.04.2017

Navjot Kaur and another                                        ...... Petitioners

                          VERSUS

State of Punjab and others                                     ...... Respondents</pre>

<p id="p_1">CORAM: HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE SURINDER GUPTA

Present:    Petitioners in person with
            Mr. Sarbjit Singh, Advocate.
</p>
<p id="p_2">SURINDER GUPTA, J.(Oral)
            Learned counsel for petitioners submits that petitioners have
filed this petition seeking protection of their life and liberty alleging that
petitioner No.1-Navjot Kaur, who is major, has married petitioner No.2-
Ranjot Singh, who is below 21 years of age, against the wishes of
respondents No.4 to 6, who are parents and brother of petitioner No. 1.
Apprehending danger to their lives and liberty, they moved representation
dated 17.04.2017 (Annexure P-5) to Senior Superintendent of Police,
Gurdaspur. They fear their elimination on return to their home.
</p><p id="p_3">            In view of the submission of learned counsel for the
petitioners, but without expressing any opinion on the validity of marriage
of petitioners, petition is disposed of with direction to respondent No.2-
Senior Superintendent of Police, Gurdaspur to look into the representation
dated 17.04.2017 (Annexure P-5) and take appropriate action in accordance
with law, on receipt of copy of this order along with representation in his
office.
</p><p id="p_4">            However, it is made clear that this order will have no effect on
the validity of alleged marriage of the petitioners or on any other civil or
criminal proceedings instituted or intended against them under any law
including the provisions of <a href="/doc/790355/" id="a_1">Prohibition of Child Marriage Act</a>, 2006.</p><pre id="pre_2">

                                              (SURINDER GUPTA)
April 20, 2017                                     JUDGE
Sachin M.
