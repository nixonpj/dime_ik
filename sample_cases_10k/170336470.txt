                      JODHPUR
               S.B. Civil Writ Petition No. 217 / 2013
Jambu Chaplot S/o Shri Omkar Lal by caste Chaplot, aged 35
years resident of Village Mohi, Tehsil and District Rajsamand.
                                                         ----Petitioner
                               Versus
1. State of Rajasthan, through Secretary to the Government
Department of Mines and Geology Secretatiat, Jaipur.
2. The District Collector, Rajsamand
3. The Director (Mines) Mines and Geology Department Shastri
Circle, Udaipur
4. The Mining Engineer, Mines and Geology Department Division-
II, Infront of R.K. Hospital Rajsamand, Tehsil &amp; District
Rajsamand.
5. M/s Newlook Marbles Pvt. Ltd. through its Director-Shri Uma
Ram Makrana Road, N/B-Mohanpura, Halka-Tukda Kishangarh,
Distt. Ajmer.
6. M/s Deepak Marble through Shri Shiv Lal Choudhary resident of
Fiyavadi, Tehsil and District Rajsamand.
                                                    ----Respondents
_____________________________________________________
For Petitioner(s)   : Mr. C.S. Kotwani
For Respondent(s) : Mrs. Suman Porwal, for the State,
                      Mr. Deepak Nehra
_____________________________________________________
           HON'BLE MS. JUSTICE NIRMALJIT KAUR
                               Order
08/01/2018

     Learned counsel for the petitioner restricts his prayer only to

the extent that the private respondent herein may be restrained

from deposition of any waste material over the agricultural land of

the petitioner and that the respondent State should initiate action

against the respondents for depositing the waste material.

Learned counsel for the State submits that necessary instruction
                                 (2 of 2)
                                                          [CW-217/2013]

have been issued by the private respondents namely respondent

Nos. 5 and 6 not to dump the waste material on the land of the

petitioner and that the respondents are no more dumping the said

waste material. Learned counsel for the respondent Nos. 5 and 6

also states that any waste material lying on the land is of the

earlier lessee and not deposited by the present respondents.

       The present writ petition is accordingly disposed of in terms

of the statement made by learned counsel for the respondents.




                                             (NIRMALJIT KAUR),J.
