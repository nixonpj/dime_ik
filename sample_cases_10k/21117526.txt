<p id="p_1">      Learned      Counsel     for      the    appellant    submits

that in case if one year time is granted, he would

vacate     and     hand    over    the    possession       of   suit

property to the respondent.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">      2.     On his submission, learned Counsel for

the respondent submits that at the most, three

months time may be granted.
</p>
<p id="p_4">
</p><p id="p_5">      3.     Taking into consideration the fact that

the   findings       of    Courts       below    have     not   been

challenged before this Court except for praying

time to vacate and hand over the possession of

property     and     further      the    fact     that    the   suit

property     is     a     residential         accommodation,     the
                             3                 RSA 1038/14


interest of justice would be met in case, if six

months time is granted to the appellant to vacate

and hand over the suit property to the respondent.


</p><p id="p_6">     4.    Consequently, as there is no substantial

question of law for consideration in this appeal,

the appeal is dismissed.        However, the appellant

is granted six months time from today to vacate

and handover the possession of the suit property

subject to payment of all arrears of rent within

two months from today and go on paying the rent of

premises at the rate of Rs.2,000/- per month till

04.02.2015.
</p>
<p id="p_7">
</p><p id="p_8">     If the appellant fails to pay the arrears of

rent within two months or commits two defaults in

payment   of   rent,   respondent   is   at   liberty   to

execute the decree.
</p>
<p id="p_9">
</p><p id="p_10">     The appellant shall file an undertaking in

the aforesaid terms relating to payment of rent
                         4              RSA 1038/14


and to vacate from the suit property on or before

04.02.2015.
</p>
