<div class="doc_title">Arun Singh vs The State Of Bihar on 28 January, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                 Criminal Miscellaneous No.55478 of 2016
                        Arising Out of PS.Case No. -34 Year- 2013 Thana -AAYAR District- BHOJPUR
                 ======================================================
                 1. Arun Singh Son of Late Swaraj Singh, resident of village Baligaon, P.S.
                 Ayar, District-Bhojpur.

                                                                                .... ....   Petitioner/s
                                                       Versus
                 1. The State of Bihar

                                                                 .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :  Mr. Kumar Uday Singh
                 For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr. Sri Narsingh Tanti
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE ARVIND SRIVASTAVA
                                             ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">3   28-01-2017                    Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and

                  learned counsel for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                  Petitioner apprehends his arrest in connection with

                  Ayar P.S. Case No. 34 of 2013 registered under Sections-

                  304B/34<a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_1"> of the Indian Penal Code</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                  The allegation against the petitioner, who is

                  husband of the deceased, is of torture and assault due to non-

                  fulfillment of demand of dowry. Ultimately the deceased was

                  done to death by the husband and other in-laws of the deceased.

</p><p id="p_4">                                  Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that

                  deceased was having mental problem. On the date of alleged

                  occurrence petitioner was in Ara and when he came back he

                  learn about the incidence.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">            Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.55478 of 2016 (3) dt.28-01-2017</span><p id="p_5">

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                      2/2</span>




</p><p id="p_6">                                           Considering the gravity of allegations levelled

                          against the petitioner, who is husband of the deceased, I do not

                          feel inclined to grant the privilege of anticipatory bail to the

                          petitioner. His prayer for anticipatory bail is, accordingly,

                          rejected.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><p id="p_8">                                                     (Arvind Srivastava, J)
Manish/-
</p>
