                  Writ Petition No.14358/2016

24.10.2016

      Shri Ajay Tiwari, learned counsel for petitioner.

      Heard on admission.

      This writ petition is directed against the order dated

18.06.2016 passed in an Appeal under <a href="/doc/749287/" id="a_1">Section 9</a> of Public

Premises (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Act, 1971;

whereby, while affirming the order dated 09.12.2015

directing for eviction of the petitioner from public premises,

the Appeal has been dismissed.

      For establishing a Kiosk/Tea stall at Shastri Market, A

Sector, Piplani, BHEL Bhopal, the petitioner was given the

licence over 6x6=36 sq.ft. for a period from 01.04.1998 to

March 2003 vide licence agreement dated 29.07.1997. The

premises    belongs     to   BHEL,   a   Government    of   India

undertaking.

      Petitioner having been found in possession over 8.33 x

8.33 = 69.39 sq.ft. and having placed an iron shutter in

place of wooden panel was found in breach of the terms of

licence.   Vide     notice   dated   27.01.2014,    20.02.2014,

28.05.2014 and 05.06.2014, the petitioner was called upon

to   remove       the   encroachment.    The    non-compliance,

therefore, led the respondent Authorities cancel the licence</pre>

      Petitioner questioned the order in an Appeal under
<a href="/doc/749287/" id="a_2">Section 9</a> of 1971 Act on facts as well as the jurisdiction of
the Estate Officer in initiating the eviction proceedings.
</p><p id="p_3">      As to the jurisdiction of the Estate Officer, the
Appellate Court found the Estate Officers being authorized
by the Additional Manager (TAD) BHEL, Bhopal. This would
be borne out from the findings in paragraph 11 and 12 of
impugned order which being not shown to be perverse, no
indulgence is caused qua the jurisdiction of the Estate
Officer. Even the finding that the premises is a public
premises being a matter of fact cannot be interfered with in
absence of any cogent material evidence.
</p><p id="p_4">      As regard to findings of petitioner violating the terms
of license and not correcting the same despite that he was
given repeated opportunity is also a matter of fact which has
been elaborately dwelt upon by the Appellate Court. This will
be evident from the findings in paragraphs 17, 18 and 19
which is in the following terms :
