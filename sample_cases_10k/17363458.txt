</pre><p id="p_1">Date : 11-09-2019

     Heard Mr. M. Islam, learned counsel for the petitioner. Mr. D. Deka, learned counsel
appears for the respondent No. 8 and Ms. U. Das appears for the respondent No. 4.

</p><p id="p_2">      Office note dated 28.08.2019 states that there is no return of A/D card or unserved
notice from the respondent Nos. 9 &amp; 10. This Court vide order dated 25.04.2019 had granted
liberty to the petitioner to furnish necessary documents to the postal authorities to ascertain
whether notice had been served upon the respondent Nos. 9 &amp; 10.

</p><p id="p_3">      Today, the petitioner's counsel submitted the postal report, which is to the effect that
notice had been delivered to the respondent Nos. 9 &amp; 10.

</p><p id="p_4">     Accordingly, notice is deemed to be served upon the respondent Nos. 9 &amp; 10.

     Mr. A. Chetry, learned counsel submits that he will inform the respondent No. 9 that the
Panchayat &amp; Rural Development, Assam had submitted the pension paper to the Director of
Pension.
</p>
<p id="p_5">      Mr. D. Deka, learned counsel submits that he will inform the respondent No. 10
regarding the status of the present contempt petition.

</p><p id="p_6">     List the matter on 27.09.2019.
</p>
