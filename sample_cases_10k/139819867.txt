<p id="p_1">                                      DATE   :   1st FEBRUARY, 2019.
</p>
<p id="p_2">P.C.
</p>

<p id="p_3">1]       Heard learned counsel for respective parties.
</p>

<p id="p_4">2]       After hearing the matter at length, we find that the contentions raised
in the complaint lodged by the Petitioners were based on the Maharashtra
Ordinance No.XXV of 2018 dated 30th October, 2018 issued by the Co-
operation, Marketing and Textiles Department, in terms of which an
"associate member" could not contest the election in terms of Section 154-B
of the Maharashtra Co-operative Societies Act, 1960.
</p>

<p id="p_5">3]       This particular ordinance has in the meantime lapsed and learned
Government Pleader representing the State has produced before us a
communication received by her dated 1 st February, 2019, which indicates
that the Ordinance was placed before the Legislative Assembly and since the
assembly did not confirm the same the said ordinance has lapsed with effect
from 31st December, 2018. Communication is taken on record.
</p>

<p id="p_6">4]       Learned counsel for the Petitioners on instructions submits that he
seeks to withdraw the present Writ Petition keeping all the contentions
raised by him open for canvassing at an appropriate time and with liberty to
file Election Petition in the event the Petitioners seek to challenge election of
any person who may be elected in pursuance of the election scheduled to be
held on 3rd February, 2019.
</p>

5]     In view of the above, WRIT PETITION(L)NO.236 OF 2019, WRIT
PETITION(L)NO.237 OF 2019, WRIT PETITION(L)NO.238 OF 2019, WRIT
PETITION(L) NO.239 OF 2019 and WRIT PETITION(L)NO.242 OF 2019
stands dismissed as withdrawn, with liberty as prayed for.
</p>

<p id="p_8">6]     We make it clear that, if any such Election Petition is filed,              the
appropriate authority shall preferably decide the same within a period of six
months from the date of filing of the same, strictly in accordance with law.
</p>
