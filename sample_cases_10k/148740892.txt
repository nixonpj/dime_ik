<div class="doc_title">Rooplal vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 6 April, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                                MCRC-4077-2016
                       (ROOPLAL Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)


06-04-2016

Shri Manish Tiwari, counsel for the applicant.

Shri S.K.Shrivastava, PL for the respondent /State.</pre>

<p id="p_1">This is the first application filed by the applicant under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of Cr.P.C. for

grant of bail.
</p>
<p id="p_2">The applicant is in custody since 14.2.2016 in connection with Crime No. 65/2016

registered at P.S. Lakhnadon, District Seoni for offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1279834/" id="a_2">Sections

376</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_3">506</a> of the IPC.
</p>
<p id="p_3">Learned counsel for the applicant contends that the prosecutrix is a major though

unmarried lady, however, looking to the medical report, the allegation as alleged,

cannot be substantiated. It is a case of false implication of the applicant on account

of civil dispute. After completion of investigation challan has already been filed. In

view of the aforesaid, prayer is made to release the applicant on bail.

Learned counsel for the State opposes the application.

On due consideration of facts and circumstances of the case, this court deems it

proper to grant bail to the applicant.
</p>
<p id="p_4">Accordingly, the application is allowed. Applicant Rooplal is directed to be released

on bail on his furnishing a personal bond in a sum of Rs.50,000/- with a solvent

surety in the like amount to the satisfaction of JMFC concerned or CJM for his

appearance in the trial Court on the dates so fixed by that Court during the trial. It

is directed that the applicant shall comply the provisions of <a href="/doc/259682/" id="a_4">Section 437(3)</a> of Cr.P.C.

It is further directed that the applicant shall appear before the trial Court on each

and every date and in any case if he is not in a position to appear before the trial

Court, then he shall give prior intimation to the Court otherwise appropriate order

may be passed by the trial Court for cancellation of bail.

Certified copy as per rules.
</p><p id="p_5"> (J.K. MAHESHWARI)
       JUDGE
