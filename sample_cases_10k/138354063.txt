<pre id="pre_1">                                                      1

           IN THE COURT OF MS. VANDANA JAIN, MM­08
                   WEST DISTRICT, TIS HAZARI COURT


State Vs.  Johny
FIR No.374/02
PS: Kirti Nagar
U/S:  61/1/14 <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_1">Excise Act</a>


Sr. no. of the case                                        :         755/2
Date of commission of offence                              :         10.08.2002
Date of institution of the case                            :         04.03.2003
Name of the complainant                                    :         Ct. Vijay Singh
Name of accused and address                                :         Johny S/o Chuni Lal 
                                                                     R/o jhuggi no.B500, 
                                                                     Chunna Bhatti, Kirti 
                                                                     Nagar, New Delhi. 
Offence complained of or proved    :                                 U/S  61/1/14 <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_2">Excise Act</a>
Plea of  the accused                                       :         Pleaded not guilty
Final Order                                                :         Acquitted
Date of judgment                                           :         05.10.2010


J U D G M E N T:
</pre>
<p id="p_1">1.                 Brief facts of the prosecution story is that on 10.08.2002

     at about 7.00pm  at Chuna Bhatti  Chowk, Kirti  Nagar, Delhi  the

     accused   was   found   in   possession   of   plastic   cane   containing   11


FIR No.374/02 State Vs.Johny                                                                          Page 1/7
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                       2</span>

     bottles of liquor without any permit or license. 

</p><p id="p_2">2.                 Charge was framed against the accused on 27.02.2006 to

     which the accused pleaded not guilty and claimed trial.

</p><p id="p_3">3.                Prosecution has cited 7 witnesses in support of its case

     and out of  them 4  witnesses have been examined. 

</p><p id="p_4">4.                 PW1  Ct. Mukesh Kumar deposed that on 27.09.2002 he

     collected one bottle sealed with the seal of DS from MHC(M)  and

     deposited the same in excise lab. 
</p>
<p id="p_5">5.                 PW2   HC   Rameshwar   Dayal   is   the   MHC(M)   who   had

     received   the   case   property   and   entered   in   register   no.19.   He

     deposed that he sent Ct. Mukesh to excise office for depositing the

     sample. 
</p>
<p id="p_6">6.                 PW3  HC  Dalbir  Singh  deposed  that  on  10.08.2002  on

     receiving of DD no.13A, he reached at Chuna Bhatti chowk where

     one   Ct.   Vijay   Kumar   met   him   and   handed   over   accused   Johny

     along with one plastic cane containing illicit liquor and made his

     statement.  He deposed that the illicit liquor was measured with the

     help of bucket and same was found to contain 11 bottles of 750 ml

     out of which one bottle was  taken out for sample. He deposed that

     remaining illicit liquor was poured in the said plastic cane and both

     the case property were separately sealed with the seal of DS and



FIR No.374/02 State Vs.Johny                                                                          Page 2/7
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                       3</span>

   seal after use handed over to Ct. Vijay Kumar. He deposed that he

   prepared   form   M29   and   case   property   was   taken   into   police

   possession. He deposed that he prepared rukka and handed over to

   Ct.   Vijay   Kumar   for   registration   of  FIR  and   he   got   the  FIR

   registered and returned back at the spot along with carbon copy of

   FIR   and   rukka.   He   deposed   that   he   prepared   site   plan   and   the

   accused was formally arrested and the information regarding arrest

   was  given to the wife of accused. 
</p>
<p id="p_7">7.                 In his cross examination he stated that lid of the cane can

   be removed without disturbing the seal and he had not shown the

   source of light in the site plan prepared by him at the instance of

   Ct.   Vijay.   He   stated   that   the   proceedings   were   conducted   while

   sitting on a bench taken from a nearby shop/jhuggi.  He stated that

   Ct. Vijay  brought  a plastic bucket  and  a mug along  with empty

   bottle but  the person from whom the above things were taken has

   not been examined by him as witness nor he can give his name and

   address.   He   stated   that   he   reached   the   spot   at   about   7.15pm   or

   finally left the spot at about 9.45pm. He stated that there is neither

   any case diary nor memo regarding return of seal by the Constable

   to him  and he received the seal next day in the morning. He stated

   that that  the person  from whom empty bottle was taken has not



FIR No.374/02 State Vs.Johny                                                                          Page 3/7
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                                                       4</span>

   been paid anything. He stated that he was having black raxine bag

   with him.   He stated that the constable who took the rukka came

   back after registration of the case after 30 minutes. 

</p><p id="p_8">8.                 PW4  Ct.  Vijay  deposed  that  on 10.08.2002  he was on

   patrolling duty and reached at Chuna Bhatti Chowk where accused

   was  coming  and  was  carrying  one  plastic  cane  containing  illicit

   liquor and on suspicion he asked to stop him  and checked plastic

   cane which was containing illicit liquor on which he made a call to

   DO from where HC Dalbir Singh reached at the spot. He deposed

   that he handed over the accused Johny alongwith plastic cane to

   HC Dalbir and illicit liquor was measured with the help of empty

   bucket   and   same   was  found  to  be  11   bottles   of  750  ml.   out   of

   which  one was taken out  for sample and  remaining illicit  bottle

   poured   in   the   plastic   cane.   He   deposed   that   both   case   property

   sealed with the seal of DS and after using the same was handed

   over to  him.  He deposed  that  the case property  was   taken into

   possession     and   IO   recorded  his  statement.   He   deposed   that  IO

   prepared rukka and handed over to him for registration of the FIR

   and After  registration  of  FIR  he returned at the spot along with

   copy   of  FIR  and   rukka   and   same   was   handed   over   to   IO.   He

   deposed that IO prepared site plan  arrested the accused and later


FIR No.374/02 State Vs.Johny                                                                          Page 4/7
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                                                       5</span>

   on he was released on police bail.
</p>
<p id="p_9">9.                 In   his   cross   examination   he   stated   that   he   left   PS   for

   patrolling at 7.00pm and apprehended the accused at 7.00pm. He

   stated that he gave the call to PS through a PCO but he cannot give

   the name of the person who was manning the PCO. He stated that

   he  brought one bucket, one empty bottle and a mug from nearby

   jhuggi but cannot name the jhuggiwala or jhuggiwali and jhuggi

   number and nothing was paid to jhuggiwala for the empty bottle.

   He stated that IO was having  cloth of black colour and the bag of

   the   IO   contained   a   phatti   and   a   danda.   He   stated   that   the

   proceedings were conducted while sitting on a bench taken from a

   tea shop but he was not asked to join the proceedings. He stated

   that the proceedings were conducted under street light which was

   not   shown   in   site   plan   which   was   prepared   at   his   instance.   He

   deposed that he   returned the seal at the spot then and there.   He

   stated that they left the spot finally at 5pm  and  do not know how

   the surety happened to be the spot. 
</p>
<p id="p_10">10.                Statement of accused U/S 281 <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_3">Cr.P.C</a> has been recorded

   in which he has stated that he did not wish to lead any defence

   evidence.
</p>
<p id="p_11">11.                I   have   heard   arguments   advanced  by   Ld.  APP  for  the



FIR No.374/02 State Vs.Johny                                                                          Page 5/7
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_5">                                                       6</span>

   State and learned counsel for the accused. I have also perused the

   record carefully. 
</p>
<p id="p_12">12.                PW3  HC  Dalbir  Singh  in  his  cross  examination  stated

   that the lid of the cane can be removed without disturbing the seal.

   Therefore,   the   recovery   of   illicit   liquor   from   the   possession   of

   accused becomes doubtful.
</p>
<p id="p_13">13.                 He stated that Ct. Vijay brought a plastic bucket, a mug

   and empty bottle but he did not tell from whom he had brought

   these articles nor he was made a witness.
</p>
<p id="p_14">14.                 He further  stated that he received the seal next day on

   the other hand PW4 Ct. Vijay stated that he had returned the seal

   then and there. 
</p>
<p id="p_15">15.                PW3 HC Dalbir Singh deposed that they finally left the

   PS at about 9.45pm. PW4 Ct. Vijay stated that they finally left the

   spot at 5.00pm. PW4 Ct. Vijay deposed that he was on patrolling

   duty but no DD entry was proved in this regard.

</p><p id="p_16">16.                  He   also   deposed   that   site   plan   was   prepared   at   his

   instance but the same does not bear his signatures. PW4 Ct. Vijay

   stated that he do not know as to how the surety of accused had

   happened at the spot.
</p>
<p id="p_17">17.                From   the aforesaid  discussion  it  is  clear that  there  are



FIR No.374/02 State Vs.Johny                                                                          Page 6/7
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_6">                                                       7</span>

   several contradictions in the testimony of these two witnesses. The

   prosecution has failed to prove its case beyond reasonable doubt.

   Accordingly accused is acquitted for the charge framed U/s 61/1/14

   <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_4">Excise Act</a>.  
</p>
