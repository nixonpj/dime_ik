<div class="doc_title">Indradeep Sharma vs The State Of Bihar &amp; Ors on 8 April, 2010</div><pre id="pre_1">           IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                        LPA No.1398 of 2009
            INDRADEEP SHARMA S/O LATE CHETAN SHARMA
           VILL.-      NATHANPUR,             P.S.-  BIRUPUR,       BLOCK-
           BARAHIYA, DISTT.- LAKHISARAI
                                                ...      Petitioner/ Appellant
                                 Versus
           1. THE STATE OF BIHAR THROUGH THE SECRETARY,
           FOOD SUPPLY AND COMMERCE DEPARTMENT, OLD
           SECRETARIAT, PATNA
           2. THE COLLECTOR, LAKHISARAI
           3. THE S.D.O. LAKHISARAI, DISTT.- LAKHISARAI
                                              ...     Respondents/Respondents
                                -----------
</pre><p id="p_1">            For the appellant : Mr. Binod Kumar Singh, Adv.
            For the respondents: Mr. Lalit Kishore, AAG III with
                                   Mr. Pius Lal.
</p><p id="p_2">                                  -----------
</p><p id="p_3">2.   8.4.2010                 Heard Mr. Binod Kumar Singh, learned

                counsel for the appellant and Mr. Lalit Kishore, learned

                Addl. Advocate General No.III for the State.

</p><p id="p_4">                            Being dis-satisfied with the order dated

                5.10.2009 passed by the learned Single Judge in

                C.W.J.C.No. 12592/2009, whereby he has declined to

                interfere with the order passed by the appellate authority,

                namely, Collector, Lakhisarai, wherein the said appellate

                authority had declined to condone the delay, the present

                appeal has been filed.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                            On a perusal of the order passed by the

                learned Single Judge, it is manifest that he has declined

                to interfere as earlier in C.W.J.C.No. 4623/2007 liberty
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                   2</span>




was granted to prefer an appeal but the same was not

filed in quite promptitude.
</p>
<p id="p_6">             Having heard Mr. Binod Kumar Singh,

learned counsel for the appellant and Mr. Lalit Kishore,

learned Addl. Advocate General No.III, we are of the

considered opinion when the High Court had not fixed

any time limit to prefer the appeal and when the

appropriate explanation was given in the application for

condonation of delay before the appellate authority, the

appellate authority should have been well advised to

condone the delay and when the said authority had not

done so the learned Single Judge should have quashed

the order and directed the matter to be heard on merits by

the appellate authority.
</p>
<p id="p_7">             In view of aforesaid, we allow the appeal, set

aside the order passed by the learned Single Judge and

direct the appellate authority, namely, Collector,

Lakhisarai    to dispose of the appeal preferred by the

appellant on merits within a period of four months from

the date of receipt of the order passed today.

</p><p id="p_8">             A free copy of the order be handed over to

Mr. Pius Lal, learned counsel for the State, assisting Mr.
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                              3</span>




            Lalit Kishore, Addl. Advocate General No.III to do the

            needful in the matter.
</p>
<p id="p_9">

</p><p id="p_10">                                              (Dipak Misra,C.J.)



                                             (Mihir Kumar Jha,J.)

Surendra/
 </p>
