<div class="doc_title">Hari Tiwari vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 2 June, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                          MCRC-9298-2016
                (HARI TIWARI Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)


02-06-2016

      Shri Manish Datt, learned Senior Counsel assisted by
Shri Manish Kumar Tiwari, learned counsel for the applicant.
      Shri S.K. Shrivastava, P.L. for the respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">     This is first bail application under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">section 439</a> of Cr.P.C. for
offence under <a href="/doc/1667388/" id="a_2">Sections 419</a>, <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_3">420</a>, <a href="/doc/1985627/" id="a_4">467</a>, <a href="/doc/556166/" id="a_5">468</a>, <a href="/doc/1991013/" id="a_6">470</a>, <a href="/doc/1466184/" id="a_7">471</a> and <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_8">120-B</a> of
the IPC in connection with Crime No.157/2013 registered at
Police Station Sanchi District Raisen.
</p><p id="p_2">     It is stated that the total loan amount of Rs.2,60,000/- was
sanctioned by the applicant. Learned Senior Counsel appearing
on behalf of the applicant contends that the applicant shall
deposit the said amount if released on bail. The case is based on
documentary evidence and looking to the fact that co-accused
persons have already been enlarged on bail and subject to the
said condition, the applicant may also be enlarged on bail
imposing the aforesaid condition.
</p><p id="p_3">     Learned Panel Lawyer opposes the application.
     On due consideration of the facts and circumstances of the
case, this Court deems it proper to grant bail to the applicant Hari
Tiwari. He be released on bail on his furnishing a personal bond
in a sum of Rs.30,000/- with a solvent surety of like amount to
the satisfaction of JMFC concerned or CJM for their appearance
in trial Court on the dates so fixed by that Court during trial. It is
directed that applicant shall comply the provisions of <a href="/doc/259682/" id="a_9">Section
 437(3)</a> Cr.P.C.
</p><p id="p_4">       As offered, it is directed that within a period of one month
from the date of his release, the applicant shall deposit the
amount under protest in the trial Court, which shall be kept in the
fixed deposit and its disbursement shall be subject to final
outcome of the trial. It is made clear here that if the aforesaid
amount is not deposited within one month from the date of his
release by the applicant, then his order shall become ineffective
automatically without further reference to the Bench. It is also
observed that the deposit of the aforesaid amount will not
prejudice the defence of the applicant.
</p><p id="p_5">       Certified copy as per rules.
</p>

<p id="p_6">                                             (<a href="/doc/1315542/" id="a_10">J.K. MAHESHWARI)
                                                  V. JUDGE</a>
