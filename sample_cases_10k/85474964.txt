         IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

          R/SPECIAL CIVIL APPLICATION NO. 20836 of 2018

==========================================================
                      KAMLESH NARSINHBHAI TAILOR
                                Versus
                          STATE OF GUJARAT
==========================================================
Appearance:
MR MAULIN G PANDYA(3999) for the PETITIONER(s) No. 1
for the RESPONDENT(s) No. 2
MR ROHAN YAGNIK, AGP for the RESPONDENT(s) No. 1
==========================================================

 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE S.H.VORA

                            Date : 27/12/2018

                             ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">1.   Notice returnable on 01.02.2019. Learned A.G.P. waives
service of notice for the respondent State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">2.   By way of the present petition under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the
Constitution of India, the petitioner apprehends his detention
under the provisions of Gujarat Prevention of Anti Social
Activities Act, 1985 (for short, the PASA Act) on account of
registration of F.I.R/s. as described in para 3 of the petition.
</p>
<p id="p_3">3.   According to the petitioner, except registration of the
aforementioned FIR/s, no other material is available with the
competent authority to detain the petitioner under the
provisions of the PASA Act.
</p>
<p id="p_4">4.   Upon perusal of the F.I.R/s., it appears that petitioner is
not found at the scene of offence and, therefore, apprehension
of the petitioner is well-founded and liberty of the petitioner is

</p>
<p id="p_5">5.     It is made clear that the petitioner is protected only in
respect of the FIR/s as mentioned in the aforementioned para.
The competent authority is at liberty to take suitable action
against the petitioner in respect of offences, other than
mentioned in the said para, if any, registered and incriminating
materials found to detain the petitioner. The learned A.G.P. is
directed to place on record the detention order, if any, passed
against the petitioner for Court's perusal. Direct service is
permitted.
</p>
