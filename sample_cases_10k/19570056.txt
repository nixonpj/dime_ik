<div class="doc_title">Chiranji Lal vs Sh Randhir on 26 April, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1"> IN THE COURT OF SH AJAY PANDEY ADDL. DISTRICT JUDGE­
  02: SOUTH­WEST DISTRICT: DWARKA COURTS:NEW DELHI

RCA No. : 54697/16


Chiranji Lal, 
s/o Late Sh Tek Chand, 
r/o Village Paprawat,
New Delhi­110043                                      ....... Appellant 

                               Versus

Late Sh Duli Chand
s/o Late Sh Ram Dayal

Through its: 
Legal Representative 

Sh Randhir
s/o Sh Duli Chandigarh
r/o Village Paprawat, 
New Delhi­110043                                    .....Respondent</pre>


<p id="p_1">Date of institution of the suit :          05.12.2011
Date of arguments               :          26.04.2017
Date of pronouncement           :          26.04.2017


RCA No 54697/16            Chiranji Lal Vs. Duli Chand            Page no.1/9
 Civil   Appeal   under   sec.96   of   Civil   Procedure   code,
against   the   judgment   and   decree   dated   21.10.2011
passed by the court of Ms Swati Katiyar civil Judge­03,
West in Suit CS 216/07 titled :Duli Chand Vs. Chiranji Lal
&amp; Anr 


JUDGMENT 
</p><p id="p_2">   1. This   is   an   appeal   against   the   judgment   and   decree
       dated 21.10.2011 passed by Learned Civil Judge­03,
       West.
</p><p id="p_3">   2. The   appellant   was   defendant   before   Learned   trial
       court. Plaintiff had filed the suit for partition, permanent
       and  mandatory injunction for  a plot measuring about
       1400 Sq yards. The valuation clause in the plaint is as
       follows:­
                      That the requisite court fee of Rs 46/­ as required has
               been affixed on the plaint for   the relief of partition and  
               permanent/ mandatory injunction. 
</p>

<p id="p_4">   3. Plaintiff   was   claiming   the   partition   being   entitled   to
       inherit   the   property   from   the   common   ancestor   of

RCA No 54697/16               Chiranji Lal Vs. Duli Chand         Page no.2/9
        parties.
</p><p id="p_5">   4. Defendant/   appellant   defended   the   case   by   filing
       written statement taking various grounds  inter alia that
       the   oral  partition of  property took place between the
       parties/ their ancestor in the year 1970, and that the
       plaintiff   did   not   approach   the   court   with   clean   hand,
       and that suit was not properly valued .
</p><p id="p_6">   5. Following issues were framed by Learned Trial court
       on 28/5/2003:­
</p><p id="p_7">       i)   Whether   an   oral   partition   took   place   between   the
       parties/   their   ancestors   in   the   year   1970,   if   so,   its
       effect?                                               ...OPD
</p><p id="p_8">       ii)Whether   the  plaintiff  has  not  approached  the  court
       with clean hands, if so, its effect?                  ...OPD
</p><p id="p_9">       iii) Whether the suit has not been properly valued for
       the purpose of the court fee and jurisdiction? ..OPD
</p><p id="p_10">       iv) Whether the plaintiff is entitled for the partition as
       prayed for?                                                 ...OPP
</p><p id="p_11">       v)   Whether   the   plaintiff   is   entitled   for   the   relief   of
       mandatory/ permanent injunction as prayed for? ..OPP


RCA No 54697/16               Chiranji Lal Vs. Duli Chand          Page no.3/9
</p><p id="p_12">        vi) Relief 
</p><p id="p_13">   6. Court has heard arguments on appeal. 
</p><p id="p_14">   7. No   ground   as   mentioned   in   the   memorandum   of
       appeal  is made out for  interference in the impugned
       judgment. 
</p><p id="p_15">   8. It   is   however,   rightly   submitted   by   learned   Sh   Rajiv
       Yadav that in view of order XLI Rule 2 the appellate
       court   need   not   remain   confined   to   the   grounds   or
       objections set forth in the memorandum of appeal.
</p><p id="p_16">   9. On   perusal   of   record,   court   found   that   the   valuation
       clause of the plaint is prima facie defective. 
</p><p id="p_17">   10. Learned   counsel   for   respondent   was   given   an
      opportunity   to   satisfy   the   court     upon   findings     of
      learned   trial   court   on   issue   no.   3   regarding   the
      valuation   of   suit   for   the   purpose   of   court   fees   and
      jurisdiction. 
</p><p id="p_18">   11. The court on the last date of hearing, also directed the
      plaintiff to file affidavit about the market value of entire
      property, partition of which was sought by him. 
</p><p id="p_19">   12. Today the plaintiff has filed his affidavit stating that the


RCA No 54697/16              Chiranji Lal Vs. Duli Chand        Page no.4/9
       market value of land in question measuring 1400 Sq
      yards, was well below Rs 3 lacs. Alongwith his affidavit
      plaintiff has filed photocopy of certified copies of two
      sale deeds, in  first sale deed some property measuring
      2   bighas   2   biswa   appears   to   have   been   sold   for   a
      consideration of Rs 1 lac only. In second sale deed a
      property   measuring   230   sq   yards     appears   to   have
      been sold for a consideration of Rs 50,000/­ only. 
</p><p id="p_20">   13. Learned  Sh   Satish, counsel for plaintiff submits that
       onus to prove issue no. 3 was upon defendant and no
       evidence was led, no arguments were addressed by
       defendant,   therefore,   the     learned   trial   court   rightly
       decided the issue in favour of plaintiff.
</p><p id="p_21">   14. It is settled law that in a suit for partition , jurisdictional
      value has to be the total value of the land required to
      be partitioned . The trial court specifically framed the
      issues qua the value of suit for the purposes of court
      fees   and   jurisdiction.   There   is   a   finding     qua   the
      valuation  for  the   purpose   of   court   fee.   There   is
      however, no finding qua the jurisdictional value of the


RCA No 54697/16              Chiranji Lal Vs. Duli Chand       Page no.5/9
       suit. The trial court held that only fixed court fees was
      payable by the plaintiff . However, it did not deal with
      the submission of the defendant  that the value of the
      suit property is much more than Rs 5 lacs. 
</p><p id="p_22">   15. Hence, this court is inclined to give its findings qua the
      jurisdictional value of the suit. 
</p><p id="p_23">   16. In entire plaint no value was fixed by the plaintiff for
      the   purpose   of   pecuniary   jurisdiction   of   the   court.
      Hence , this court directed filing of affidavit of plaintiff
      qua the market value of land. 
</p><p id="p_24">   17. As already observed, today the plaintiff has filed his
      affidavit stating that the market value of the land is well
      below Rs 3 lacs i.e within the pecuniary jurisdiction of
      learned Civil Judge. 
</p><p id="p_25">   18. Learned   Sh   Rajiv   Yadav   has   produced   copy   of
      notification No. F.2(12)/Fin.(E.I)/Part File/Vol.1(ii)/3548
      dated   18.07.2007   issued  by   government   of   National
      Capital   territory   of   Delhi   prescribing   minimum   rates
      (Circle rates) for valuation of land and properties for the
      purpose of registration under the Registration Act.  The


RCA No 54697/16            Chiranji Lal Vs. Duli Chand      Page no.6/9
       minimum   land   rates   for   any   category   of   locality   are
      Rs6900/­ per sq meter. If   the value of land (1400 sq
      yards = 1170.40 sq meters) is calculated at this rate, it
      comes out at around Rs 81 lacs in the year 2007. 
</p><p id="p_26">   19. The     price   of   land,   taken   even   1/10th     of   the   said
      amount would render the   value of suit much beyond
      the pecuniary jurisdiction of Learned Civil Judge.
</p><p id="p_27">   20. The price of land taken @ 256.41 per sq meter would
      also   take   the   value   of   land   outside   the   pecuniary
      jurisdiction of Rs 3 lacs of Learned Civil  Judge. 
</p><p id="p_28">   21. The   court   cannot   be   oblivious   of   the   fact   that   no
      residential built up property in Delhi could have been
      purchased in the year 2002 at a rate below Rs 256.41
      per sq meter.
</p><p id="p_29">   22. In   the   facts   and   circumstances,   this   court   is   of   the
      opinion   that   the   valuation   clause   of   the   plaint   was
      defective.   This   court   is   of   the   further   opinion   that
      considering the market rate of rent, the value of the suit
      property   would   be   much   beyond   the   pecuniary
      jurisdiction of  the learned trial court. 
</p>

<p id="p_30">RCA No 54697/16               Chiranji Lal Vs. Duli Chand         Page no.7/9
</p><p id="p_31">    23. It is rightly submitted by learned Sh Satish Bhatti that
      at the time of filing of the suit there were no prescribed
      or   minimum   circle   rates   for   sale   purchase   of   land,
      however, it was obligatory upon the plaintiff   to state
      the market value of the land in his suit. The failure of
      defendant   to   lead   evidence   or   arguments   on   this
      aspect, would not benefit the plaintiff as the plaint has
      to   satisfy   the   requirement   of  law.   Though     the   circle
      rates were fixed by the Government of National Capital
      Territory   of   Delhi   in   the   year   2007   ,   however,   the
      judicial notice could have been taken by the court  of
      the   inherent   defect   in   valuation   clause   and  for
      considering   the   rates   prevailing  prior   to   the  issue   of
      notification of circle rates. 
</p><p id="p_32">   24. The issue of   pecuniary jurisdiction or the jurisdiction
      as   to   subject   matter   goes   to   the   root   of   the   case,
      divesting the vary authority of the court. 
</p><p id="p_33">   25. For   the   reasons   aforesaid   ,   this   court   is   inclined   to
      held   that   the   suit   was   not   valued   properly   for   the
      purpose of jurisdiction and the subject value of the suit


RCA No 54697/16               Chiranji Lal Vs. Duli Chand         Page no.8/9
       property, required to be partitioned was much beyond
      the   pecuniary  jurisdiction of learned civil judge.   The
      judgment   and   decree   passed   by   learned   Civil   Judge
      was,  therefore, without jurisdiction. Hence is liable to
      be set aside.
</p><p id="p_34">   26. Held   accordingly.   Impugned   Judgment   and   decree
      dated 21.10.2011  is therefore, set aside. The  suit filed
      by plaintiff is held to be beyond pecuniary jurisdiction of
      learned trial court. In exercise of its power u/o VII Rule
      10 CPC  this court hereby directs return of plaint by the
      learned trial court to the plaintiff with liberty to present
      the same in court of competent jurisdiction. 
</p><p id="p_35">   27. Trial court record alongwith copy of this order be sent
      back for completion of formalities. 
</p><p id="p_36">   28. Appeal file be consigned to record room. 
</p>


<p id="p_37">Pronounced in the open                          (Ajay Pandey)
court on 26.04.2017          Addl. District Judge­02/South­West,
                                    Dwarka Courts Complex,New  Delhi 




RCA No 54697/16            Chiranji Lal Vs. Duli Chand     Page no.9/9
