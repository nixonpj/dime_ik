        IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA
                     AT CHANDIGARH

                                 C.R.M-M No.16161 of 2019
                                 Date of Decision : 08.05.2019


Amrik Singh and another                                   ...... Petitioners

                          Versus


State of Punjab                                           ...... Respondent



CORAM : HON'BLE MR.JUSTICE AJAY TEWARI
                     ***

Present :    Mr. Rakesh Kumar, Advocate
             for the petitioners.

             Mr. Ramandeep Sandhu, Sr. D.A.G., Punjab.
                             ***

AJAY TEWARI, J. (Oral)
</pre>
<p id="p_1">             Prayer in the present petition filed under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> of the

Cr.P.C. is for grant of anticipatory bail to the petitioners in case FIR No.41

dated 13.09.2018 registered under <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_2">Sections 323</a>, <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_3">324</a>, <a href="/doc/838469/" id="a_4">452</a>, <a href="/doc/763672/" id="a_5">148</a>, <a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_6">149</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_7">506</a>

IPC and under <a href="/doc/1540253/" id="a_8">Section 326</a> IPC (added later on) at P.S. Kabirpur

(Ahalikalan), District Kapurthala.
</p>
<p id="p_2">             Counsel for the petitioners has argued that no overt action has

been attributed to the petitioners.
</p>
<p id="p_3">             Learned Sr. Deputy Advocate General on instructions from ASI

Rajinder Singh has accepted that no overt action has been attributed to the

petitioners but has argued that <a href="/doc/1540253/" id="a_9">Section 326</a> IPC is a serious offence .

</p><p id="p_4">             Counsel for the petitioners has countered this by arguing that

                               1 of 2
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">            ::: Downloaded on - 12-05-2019 00:30:05 :::</span>
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2"> C.R.M-M No.16161 of 2019                                         -2-</span>



the persons who allegedly caused injuries had been arrested and released on

bail.
</p>
<p id="p_5">             Learned Sr. Deputy Advocate General has accepted this fact.

             Keeping in view the entire conspectus of facts, without going

into the merits of the case, I do not deem it appropriate to deny the

concession of anticipatory bail to the petitioners.

</p><p id="p_6">             Resultantly, the petition is allowed. In the event of arrest of the

petitioners, they shall be released on bail by the Investigating Officer

subject to the conditions envisaged under Section 438(2) Cr.P.C.

</p><p id="p_7">             The petitioners are directed to appear before the investigating

officer on 20.05.2019 and on any other date as and when their presence are

required. In case the petitioners do not appear before the investigating

officer in the aforesaid manner the present petition would be deemed to

have been dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_8">             Since the main case has been decided, the pending Criminal

Misc. Application, if any, also stands disposed of.</p><pre id="pre_2">
