<p id="p_1">1.        By way of the present Judgment, I shall dispose off the

     present complaint filed by Sh. Akhlaq (hereinafter referred to as

     'complainant') against            Rungal (hereinafter referred to as

     'accused') u/s 138 of <a href="/doc/1132672/" id="a_1">Negotiable Instruments Act</a> (hereinafter

     referred to as '<a href="/doc/1132672/" id="a_2">N.I. Act</a>' in short).
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">2.        The factual matrix of the complaint in brief is that on

CC No.617050/2016   PS: Jamia Nagar
Akhlaq Vs. Rungal                                                    page 2 of 19
    22.01.2011, accused approached and requested the complainant

   for advancing a friendly loan of Rs.2,50,000/- for purchasing a

   property and personal needs and requirements. It is averred that

   complainant keeping in view the the genuine needs of accused

   and requirements, advanced a loan of Rs.2,50,000/- which was

   given to the accused in cash on 22.01.2011 and the accused

   assured to return the said loan amount within 3 months and given

   an undertaking by way of affidavit dated 22.01.2011. It is averred

   that the accused at the time of taking said loan in discharge of his

   liabilities towards the said loan had issued a post dated cheque

   bearing No.080539 dated 04.04.2011 for a sum of Rs.2,50,000/-

   drawn on Indian Bank, Jamia Millia Islamia, Jamia Nagar, New

   Delhi-25 in favour of the complainant.     It is averred that while

   issuing the above said cheque, the accused assured that the

   cheque is good for payment and would be honoured on its

   presentation, hence, the complainant accepted the said cheque.

   It is averred that as per the instructions of the accused,

   complainant deposited the said cheque with his bank, however,

CC No.617050/2016   PS: Jamia Nagar
Akhlaq Vs. Rungal                                            page 3 of 19
      the same was returned dishonoured with the remarks "Funds

     Insufficient" on returned memo dated 05.05.2011. It is averred

     that after dishonouring the said cheque, the complainant

     contacted the accused on mobile phone regarding the same, but

     the accused did not talk with the complainant and thereafter, the

     complainant sent a legal notice dated 10.05.2011 to the accused

     through his counsel demanding amount of dishonoured cheque

     within 15 days from the receipt of notice which was send through

     registered AD/courier.           However, accused failed to make the

     payment within stipulated period. Hence, the present case was

     filed.
</p>
<p id="p_4">
</p><p id="p_5">3.            In the pre summoning evidence, complainant examined

     himself as CW-1 by way of affidavit and relied upon the following

     documents:-
</p>
<p id="p_6">     (a) The undertaking given by the accused by way of affidavit

        Ex.CW-1/A.
</p>
<p id="p_7">     (b) The cheque bearing No.080539 dated 04.04.2011 Ex.CW-1/B


CC No.617050/2016   PS: Jamia Nagar
Akhlaq Vs. Rungal                                               page 4 of 19
        for the sum of Rs.2,50,000/-.
</p>
<p id="p_8">     (c) The cheque returning memo dated 05.05.2011 Ex. CW-1/C.

</p><p id="p_9">     (d) Legal notice dated 10.05.2011 Ex.CW-1/D and its courier and

       the postal receipt Ex.CW-1/E and Ex.CW-1/F.

</p><p id="p_10">     (e) The copy of AD Card bearing the signature of accused and

       registered A.D. Envelope returned back Ex.CW-1G and Ex.CW-

       1/H.
</p>
<p id="p_11">     (f) The complainat is Ex.CW-1/I.
</p>
<p id="p_12">
</p><p id="p_13">4.        Considering the material placed on record along with the

     complaint and the pre summoning evidence, the accused was

     summoned for the commission of offence u/s 138 <a href="/doc/1132672/" id="a_3">N.I. Act</a>.


</p><p id="p_14">5.        Notice u/s 251 <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_4">Cr.P.C</a>. was served to the accused for the

     offence u/s 138 N. I. Act to which he pleaded not guilty and

     claimed for trial and accused disclosed his plea of defence as "he

     does not plead guilty and claim for trial".   Accused also stated

     that complainant along with another person called Salim had

     shown him a property bearing No.18 admeasuring 50 sq. yards,

CC No.617050/2016   PS: Jamia Nagar
Akhlaq Vs. Rungal                                            page 5 of 19
    part of Khasra No.115, situated at Noor Nagar, Jamia Nagar,

   Okhla, New Delhi-110025.           Mr. Salim represented himself to be

   the owner of the said property and the sale consideration of the

   said property was fixed for Rs.11,50,000/-, out of which, Rs.9 lacs

   was paid by the accused in cash and the cheque in question was

   handed over as a blank signed cheque to the complainant and

   Salim as a security for the sale consideration and the same was

   handed over prior to the payment of Rs.9 lacs in cash. Accused

   also stated that after receipt of Rs.9 lacs, Mr. Salim executed a

   G.P.A, will and receipt in his favour all dated 22.01.2011 and later

   on, on inquiry it came to the knowledge of accused that no such

   property exists and they have forged and fabricated the above

   mentioned documents. Accused also stated that he did not

   arrange for the balance sale consideration and the complainant in

   collusion with said Salim had filed the blank cheque with the

   amount of balance sale consideration of Rs.2,50,000/- and had

   filed a complaint against him in PS: Jamia Nagar. Accused also

   stated that an application u/s 156 (3) <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_5">Cr.P.C</a>. has been filed by

CC No.617050/2016   PS: Jamia Nagar
Akhlaq Vs. Rungal                                               page 6 of 19
      him in the court of Ld. MM Ms. Soumya Chauhan, Saket Courts.

     Accused also stated that he had never            received any legal

     demand notice.          Accused also stated that this is a false and

     frivolous complaint filed against him by the complainant.


</p><p id="p_15">6.        After notice u/s 251 <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_6">Cr.P.C</a>. for offence punishable u/s 138

     <a href="/doc/1132672/" id="a_7">N.I. Act</a>, opportunity was given to the accused to cross examine

     the complainant witness/CW-1.          CW-1 was cross examined at

     length. In cross examination, complainant stated that he worked

     as a driver. He stated that he also worked as a property dealer.

     He stated that accused is known to him for the last 10-12 years.

     He stated that he cannot tell the details of the family members of

     the accused. He stated that he used to file Income Tax return in

     the year 2011. He stated that he did not give finance on credit to

     the public.     He stated that he knew the person namely Avdesh

     Kumar. He stated that he had filed a complaint case of cheque

     bounce against Mr. Avdesh Kumar.          He stated that the person

     namely Saleem is not known him. He denied the suggestion that


CC No.617050/2016   PS: Jamia Nagar
Akhlaq Vs. Rungal                                                page 7 of 19
    the accused had filed a complaint against him and he appeared in

   the court in that complaint case. He stated that he does not have

   any license of NBFC from RBI. He stated that he do not remember

   the telephone number from which the accused called to him. He

   stated that he made the complaint of misbehaviour by the

   accused to the police station. Witness denied the suggestion of

   defence counsel that witness and Saleem had proposed to sell the

   property No.18 measuring 50 sqyd Khasra No.115, Noor Nagar,

   Jamia Nagar, Okhla, New Delhi.     Witness denied that the said

   property was in the name of Saleem. Witness denied that cheque

   in question was given by the accused as sale consideration of the

   above said property. Witness expressed his inability to tell as to

   whether accused is an illiterate person.     Witness denied the

   suggestion that documents Ex.CW-1/A is a forged and fabricated

   document. Witness denied the suggestion that he had not served

   the statutory legal notice to the accused.    Witness denied the

   suggestion that he had cheated the accused in connivance with

   the Saleem for selling the property to the accused which was not

CC No.617050/2016   PS: Jamia Nagar
Akhlaq Vs. Rungal                                          page 8 of 19
      existing. Witness denied the suggestion that cheque was not

     given for legally recoverable debt. Witness denied the suggestion

     that cheque was given for sale consideration of the property in

     question. Witness stated that sum of Rs.2,50,000/- was arranged

     by him by selling his Maruti Van, however, witness has not

     disclosed the model number of the Maruti Van.       Witness stated

     that he sold the said vehicle 3-4 years back. Witness stated that

     the vehicle was bearing No.2903.
</p>
<p id="p_16">
</p><p id="p_17">7.                  After complainant evidence, statement of accused u/s

     313 <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_8">Cr.P.C</a>. was recorded in which all the incriminating evidence

     was put and explained to him to which he denied. Accused opted

     to lead defence evidence.
</p>
<p id="p_18">
</p><p id="p_19">8.                  In defence evidence accused examined DW-1 Kapil

     Rawat, Ahlmad with the record of CC No.622743/2016 titled as

     Rungal Vs. Akhlaq.
</p>
<p id="p_20">                    DW1 Kapil Rawat Ahlmad produced the record of

     the above mentioned file as per which the said complaint was at

CC No.617050/2016   PS: Jamia Nagar
Akhlaq Vs. Rungal                                              page 9 of 19
      the stage of arguments on the point of summoning and next date

     of hearing was 01.03.2018 and as per record of file, there were

     two respondents Akhlaq Ahmed and Saleem in the above

     mentioned case. In cross examination, DW1 stated that as per

     record, application of the complainant u/s 156 (3) <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_9">Cr.P.C</a>. was

     dismissed in the above mentioned case vide order dated

     20.07.2013.
</p>
<p id="p_21">
</p><p id="p_22">9.        After      defence          evidence,    matter   was   fixed    for    final

     arguments.
</p>
<p id="p_23">
</p><p id="p_24">10.       Final arguments heard.                  It is argued on behalf of the

     complainant that complaint was filed within limitation.                     It is

     further argued that the cheque in question was issued by the

     accused towards the liability of loan of Rs.2,50,000/-, however,

     the same was dishonoured on its presentation due to insufficient

     fund in the account of the accused.                It is further argued that

     despite legal notice, accused had not made the payment towards

     the liability of cheque amount. It is further argued that case of the

CC No.617050/2016   PS: Jamia Nagar
Akhlaq Vs. Rungal                                                         page 10 of 19
    complainant is proved beyond the reasonable doubt, hence,

   accused is liable to be convicted.
</p>
<p id="p_25">
</p><p id="p_26">11.       It is argued by Ld. Counsel for the defence from DLSA that

   case of complainant is not proved beyond reasonable doubts,

   hence, accused is liable to be acquitted. It is further argued that

   complainant has not supported his case by examining any

   independent witness qua the issuance of cheque by the accused

   or the advancement of the loan. It is further argued that cheque

   in question was given to the complainant and his associate

   Saleem towards the purchase of plot by the accused, however,

   complainant neither gave the plot, nor returned the money as

   advanced by the accused and filed the present complaint against

   the accused in order to harass the accused. It is further submitted

   that complainant has not disclosed his source of income regarding

   his capacity to advance the loan amount. It is further argued that

   case of complainant is not proved beyond the reasonable doubts.

   Hence, accused may be acquitted.</p>
<p id="p_27">

CC No.617050/2016   PS: Jamia Nagar
Akhlaq Vs. Rungal                                          page 11 of 19
</p><p id="p_28"> 12.       Heard the submissions of both the parties and perused the

   record.
</p>
<p id="p_29">
</p><p id="p_30">13.       As    per      case     of   complaint,   on   22.01.2011,     accused

   approached and requested the complainant for advancing a

   friendly loan of Rs.2,50,000/- for purchasing a property and for

   personal needs and requirements. It is averred that complainant

   advanced a loan of Rs.2,50,000/- to the accused in cash on

   22.01.2011 and the accused assured to return the said loan

   amount within 3 months and given an undertaking by way of

   affidavit dated 22.01.2011. It is averred that the accused at the

   time of taking said loan had issued a post dated cheque bearing

   No.080539 dated 04.04.2011 for a sum of Rs.2,50,000/- drawn on

   Indian Bank, Jamia Millia Islamia, Jamia Nagar, New Delhi-25 in

   favour of the complainant in discharge of his liabilities towards

   the said loan amount. It is averred that while issuing the above

   said cheque, the accused assured that the cheque is good for

   payment and would be honoured on its presentation, hence, the


CC No.617050/2016   PS: Jamia Nagar
Akhlaq Vs. Rungal                                                      page 12 of 19
    complainant accepted the said cheque.                     It is averred that as per

   the instructions of the accused, complainant deposited the said

   cheque       with       his   bank,       however,   the    same       was     returned

   dishonoured with the remarks "Funds Insufficient" on returned

   memo dated 05.05.2011. It is averred that after dishonour of the

   said cheque, the complainant contacted the accused on mobile

   phone regarding the same, but the accused did not talk with the

   complainant and thereafter, the complainant sent a legal notice

   dated 10.05.2011 to the accused through his counsel demanding

   amount of dishonoured cheque within 15 days from the receipt of

   notice which was send through registered AD/courier. However,

   accused failed to make the payment within stipulated period.

</p><p id="p_31">                    Complainant himself appeared as a witness CW-1 and

   tendered          his    evidence         by   way   of     affidavit    Ex.CW-1/X.

   Complainant             during      his   examination      by   way     of     affidavit

   reascertained the averments made in the complaint and relied

   upon the documents Ex.CW-1/A i.e. the undertaking given by the

   accused          regarding       the      advancement      of   loan    of     sum      of

CC No.617050/2016    PS: Jamia Nagar
Akhlaq Vs. Rungal                                                               page 13 of 19
    Rs.2,50,000/- by complainant to the accused and its repayment

   schedule.        Complainant has also relied and produced the cheque

   bearing No. 080539 dated 04.04.2011 Ex.CW-1/B for the sum of

   Rs.2,50,000/- drawn of Indian Bank, Jamia Milia Islamia, Jamia

   Nagar, New Delhi in favour of complainant. Complainant has also

   produced the cheque returned memo dated 05.05.2011 Ex.CW-

   1/C and legal notice dated 10.05.2011 Ex.CW-1/D and receipt of

   sending the legal notice through courier and postal receipt Ex.CW-

   1/E, Ex.CW-1/F, Ex.CW-1/G and Ex.CW-1/H.
</p>
<p id="p_32">                    During cross examination of CW-1, complainant stated

   that he worked as a driver. He stated that he also worked as a

   property dealer. He stated that accused is known to him for the

   last 10-12 years. He stated that he cannot tell the details of the

   family members of the accused. He stated that he used to file

   Income Tax return in the year 2011. He stated that he did not

   give finance on credit to the public. He stated that he knew the

   person namely Avdesh Kumar. He stated that he had filed a

   complaint case of cheque bounce against Mr. Avdesh Kumar. He

CC No.617050/2016   PS: Jamia Nagar
Akhlaq Vs. Rungal                                             page 14 of 19
    stated that the person namely Saleem is not known him.              He

   denied the suggestion that the accused had filed a complaint

   against him and he appeared in the court in that complaint case.

   He stated that he does not have any license of NBFC from RBI.

   He stated that he does not remember the telephone number from

   which the accused called to him. He stated that he made the

   complaint of misbehhaviour by the accused to the police station.

   Witness denied the suggestion of defence counsel that witness

   and Saleem had proposed to sell the property No.18 measuring

   50 sqyd Khasra No.115, Noor Nagar, Jamia Nagar, Okhla, New

   Delhi.     Witness denied the said property was in the name of

   Saleem.          Witness denied that cheque in question was given by

   the accused as sale consideration of the above said property.

   Witness expressed his inability to tell as to whether accused is an

   illiterate person. Witness denied the suggestion that documents

   Ex.CW-1/A is a forged and fabricated document. Witness denied

   the suggestion that he had not served the legal notice to the

   accused. Witness denied the suggestion that he had cheated the

CC No.617050/2016    PS: Jamia Nagar
Akhlaq Vs. Rungal                                            page 15 of 19
    accused in connivance with the Saleem for selling the property to

   the accused which was not existing. Witness denied the

   suggestion that cheque was not for legally recoverable debt.

   Witness denied the suggestion that cheque was given for sale

   consideration of the property in question.       Witness stated that

   sum of Rs.2,50,000/- was arranged by him by selling his Maruti

   Van, however, witness has not disclosed the model number of the

   Maruti Van. Witness stated that he sold the said vehicle 3-4 years

   back. Witness stated that the vehicle was bearing No.2903.

</p><p id="p_33">                    During cross examination of the complainant, accused

   took the defence that the cheque in question was handed over to

   the complainant by the accused as complainant worked as a

   property dealer and accused was willing to purchase a plot

   through the complainant from another person namely Saleem and

   the cheque in question was misused by the complainant.

   Accused also took the defence that he had already filed a

   complaint against the complainant and Saleem in that regard.

   Accused also took the defence that he had paid a sum of Rs.9 lacs

CC No.617050/2016   PS: Jamia Nagar
Akhlaq Vs. Rungal                                             page 16 of 19
    to the complainant qua the purchase of plot from Saleem through

   the complainant and the cheque in question was handed over in

   blanked condition which was filled up and misused by the

   complainant. Accused further took the defence that he was never

   served with legal notice.
</p>
<p id="p_34">                    Contrary to the defence taken by the accused,

   complainant produced the undertaking / affidavit Ex.CW-1/A

   regarding the advancement of loan by the complainant to the

   accused and regarding the handing over the cheque Ex.CW-1/B by

   the accused to the complainant.       Signature on the document

   Ex.CW-1/A is not disputed by the accused during the cross

   examination of CW-1 and accused only took the defence that the

   document Ex.CW-1/A is a fabricated document, however, no

   evidence was led by the accused to establish that document

   Ex.CW-1/A is a forged or fabricated document. In the document

   Ex.CW-1/A, the advancement of loan by the complainant to the

   accused, its payment schedule including the handing over of

   cheque in question with the cheque details is specifically

CC No.617050/2016   PS: Jamia Nagar
Akhlaq Vs. Rungal                                        page 17 of 19
    mentioned.           The execution of document Ex.CW-1/A by the

   accused is duly proved by the complainant and accused has failed

   to bring any material contradiction in the testimony of CW-1

   regarding the execution of document Ex.CW-1/A except the

   suggestion of the defence counsel regarding the fabrication of

   document Ex.CW-1/A which was denied by the complainant/CW-1.

   Accused has not disputed the fact regarding the handing over of

   the cheque to the complainant and accused only took the defence

   that the same was handed over in blank condition regarding the

   purchase of plot from one Saleem through the complainant,

   however, the accused failed to bring on record any material

   contradiction in the testimony of CW-1 to discard his testimony to

   rebut the presumption drawn in favour of the complainant

   regarding issuance of cheque against the legally recoverable

   liability. The cheque was presented by the complainant and the

   same was returned with the remark "Fund Insufficient".       Legal

   notice was issued to the accused by the complainant and was

   sent to his address which is mentioned in the complaint. Accused

CC No.617050/2016   PS: Jamia Nagar
Akhlaq Vs. Rungal                                         page 18 of 19
    failed to make the payment of cheque amount. Complaint of the

   complainant was filed within limitation.     The court is of the

   considered view that complainant has duly proved beyond

   reasonable doubts that cheque for the sum of Rs.2,50,000/- was

   issued by the accused to the complainant in discharge of his legal

   liability i.e. loan advanced by the complainant to him and the

   cheque was unpaid as accused failed to maintain the sufficient

   fund in his account for clearance of cheque. In view of the same,

   accused Rungal S/o Sh. Sumera is held guilty and convicted for

   the offence punishable u/s 138 <a href="/doc/1132672/" id="a_10">N.I. Act</a>.
</p>
