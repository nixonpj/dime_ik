<p id="p_1"> IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR RAJASTHAN AT JAIPUR BENCH, JAIPUR

ORDER

S.B. Criminal Misc.2nd Bail Application No.7146/2010
Virendra Singh vs. State of Rajasthan

Dated :	2nd November, 2010

HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE MAHESH BHAGWATI

Mr. Biri Singh Sinsinwar, for the petitioner.
</p><p id="p_2">Mr. G.S. Fauzdar, Public Prosecutor for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_3">		This order governs the disposal of  second bail application filed under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of Cr.P.C. by Mr. Biri Singh Sinsinwar Advocate on behalf of the applicant Virendra Singh pertaining to F.I.R. No. 438/2008 registered at Police Station Roopbas, District Bharatpur, for the offences under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_2">Section 302</a>, <a href="/doc/764237/" id="a_3">394</a> and <a href="/doc/996413/" id="a_4">347</a> of IPC. 
</p><p id="p_4">2.		Heard the learned counsel for the petitioner as also the learned Public Prosecutor for the State and perused the relevant material available on record.	
</p><p id="p_5">3.		Learned counsel for the petitioner canvassed that the co-accused Shankar lal @ Shankar of this case has already been enlarged on bail by the co-ordinate Bench and the case of the petitioner is akin to that of the co-accused person, hence, on the ground of parity, the petitioner may also be granted indulgence of bail.
</p><p id="p_6">4.		Learned Public Prosecutor appearing on behalf of the State has opposed the bail petition on the ground that the Registration Certificate, Driving License of the deceased as also the broken number plate of the said motor-cycle has been recovered from the possession or at the instance of the accused petitioner, hence, the bail should not be granted to the accused petitioner. 
</p><p id="p_7">5.		Having reflected over the submissions made at the bar and scanned the relevant material available on record, it is pertinent to reiterate that this case solely rests upon the circumstantial evidence and it is not required at this stage to appreciate the evidence collected by the Investigating Officer and emerged on recording during trial. I do not find any good ground to change my earlier view, hence, the second bail petition filed on behalf of the petitioner also stands dismissed for those very reasons as enumerated in the first bail order.
</p>		
<p id="p_8">					   (MAHESH BHAGWATI),J.
</p><p id="p_9">Mak/-
