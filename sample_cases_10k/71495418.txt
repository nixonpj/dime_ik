<p id="p_1">A.M. SHAFFIQUE, J.
</p>
<p id="p_2">      This appeal has been filed by a third party to the writ petition

after seeking leave of this Court. It is stated that, by virtue of the

directions issued by the learned Single Judge in judgment dated

21.12.2015 in W.P.(C).No.34361 of 2015, orders have been passed by

the respondent Board transferring him from Mannam Temple to

Thrikkakara temple. This order was passed when the writ petition was

filed by the 6th respondent in the appeal.

</p><p id="p_3">      2. The learned counsel for the 6th respondent has now filed an

affidavit before this Court expressing his readiness to join as Shanti at

Thrikkakara Mahadeva Temple. In that event there is no necessity to

transfer the appellant from Mannam Temple.

</p><p id="p_4">      3. We take on record paragraph '7' of the affidavit which reads

as follows:
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">               "7. Accordingly, this respondent is now making this

         request expressing his willingness to be shifted from

         Mannam Temple to Thrikkakara Mahadeva Temple and to

         provide  transfer sought    for by   the  appellant from

         Thrikkakara     Mahadeva       Temple     to     Mannam

         Subramanyaswami Temple."</blockquote><p id="p_5">

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">W.A. No. 86 of 2016                -2-</span>


</p><p id="p_6">       4. Having regard to the aforesaid submission made by the 6th

respondent, we are of the view that necessary orders in that regard is

to be passed by the 2nd respondent. Taking into account the aforesaid

affidavit filed by the 6th respondent, this writ appeal is closed and the

second respondent is directed to pass appropriate orders within ten

days from today. In all other respects, the judgment of the learned

Single Judge is sustained.
</p>
<p id="p_7">


</p><p id="p_8">                                           ASHOK BHUSHAN,
                                             CHIEF JUSTICE




                                            A.M. SHAFFIQUE,
                                                    JUDGE
