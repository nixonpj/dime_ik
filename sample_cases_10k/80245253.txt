<p id="p_1">      The petitioner has approached this Court aggrieved by the

inaction on the part of the respondents in permitting the petitioner to

revise the quarterly returns filed for the year 2015-16, in the KVATIS

system.
</p>
<p id="p_2">


</p><p id="p_3">      2. I have heard the learned counsel for the petitioner as also

the learned Government Pleader for the respondents.




</p><p id="p_4">      The learned Government Pleader would submit, on instructions,

that the request of the petitioner for revision of returns has been

forwarded to the Deputy Commissioner, for orders.                  On a

consideration of the facts and circumstances of the case as also the

submissions made across the bar, and finding that there has been no

proceedings initiated against the petitioner for the period in question,

for differential tax arrears etc., I am of the view that no prejudice will

be caused to the respondents by permitting the petitioner to revise

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">W.P.(C).No.34383/2017              2</span>




the returns for the quarter concerned. Accordingly, I dispose the writ

petition by directing the respondents to make arrangements to

facilitate a revision of returns by the petitioner for the period

aforementioned, in the KVATIS system, within a period of three weeks

from the date of receipt of a copy of this judgment, in the presence of

the Assessing Officer. The petitioner shall produce a copy of the writ

petition together with a copy of this judgment, before the

respondents, for further action.
</p>
