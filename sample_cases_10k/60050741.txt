<div class="doc_title">Manja Barman vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 13 August, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                          CRA-167-2015
             (MANJA BARMAN Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)


13-08-2015

Shri Manish Tiwari, learned counsel for the appellants.
Shri Akhilendra Singh, learned Government Advocate for
the respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">Heard on I.A.No.938/2015, an application for suspension
of sentence and grant of bail filed on behalf of the
appellant no.2 Savita.
</p><p id="p_2">Appellant no.2 has been convicted for offence under
<a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_1">Section 302</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_2">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code and has been
sentenced for life imprisonment with fine of Rs. 1000/-.
According to learned counsel for the appellants, as per
eye witness account of Devram (P.W.2) this appellant
had inflicted injuries on the deceased by 'Chappal' and
by lathi. Thereafter when the deceased fell down the co-
accused Manja caused injuries on the body of the
deceased causing his death.
</p><p id="p_3">In the circumstances, according to him the role ascribed
to this appellant is not in regard to causing injury on the
vital part of the deceased. He further submits that this
appellant is a female and was on bail during the trial.
On the other hand, Shri Akhilendra Singh, learned
Government Advocate for the respondent/State has
opposed the application. He has also filed written
objection.
</p><p id="p_4">Having considered the submissions made by learned
counsel for the parties and having gone through the
 evidence available on record, we are of the view the this
is a fit case for suspension of sentence and grant of bail
to the appellant no.2 Savita.
</p><p id="p_5">Accordingly, the application is allowed.
On appellant no.2's furnishing a personal bond in the
sum of Rs. 50,000/- with one surety in the like amount
to the satisfaction of the CJM, Damoh for her appearance
before the Registry of this Court on 16.11.2015 and on
such other subsequent dates, as may be fixed by Registry
in this regard, her jail sentence shall remain suspended
and she be released on bail.
</p><p id="p_6">C.c as per rules.</p><pre id="pre_2">

(SHANTANU KEMKAR)                 (KESHAV KUMAR TRIVEDI)
      JUDGE                                JUDGE
