<div class="doc_title">Amit Jindal vs Cesc Ltd. &amp; Anr on 9 August, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                                   ORDER SHEET

                                  WP 794 OF 2016

                       IN THE HIGH COURT AT CALCUTTA

                           Constitutional Writ Jurisdiction

                                  ORIGINAL SIDE



                                   AMIT JINDAL
                                     Versus
                                 CESC LTD. &amp; ANR.
                                     ............
</pre>

<p id="p_1">BEFORE:
</p>
<p id="p_2">The Hon'ble JUSTICE I. P. MUKERJI

Date : 9th August, 2016.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                                   Mr. K.C. Garg, Ms. S. Agarwal...appear.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                                     Dr. Madhusudan Saha Roy...appears.
</p>
<p id="p_5">
           The Court :-The finally assessed amount as per the assessment order

   dated 30th May, 2016 is Rs.1,60,039/-.
</p>
<p id="p_6">          Apart from making allegations of unauthorised use and/or theft of

   electricity, against the petitioner, Dr. Saha Roy submits that the petitioner

   appeared before the Assessing Officer. The assessment order was duly served

   upon him.
</p>
<p id="p_7">           Furthermore, the writ petitioner is a purchaser of the premises where the

   meter is situated. He has made no effort to get his name substituted as the

   consumer, it was submitted.
</p>
<p id="p_8">          There is some dispute between the parties with regard to the receipt of the

   assessment order. The petitioner denies receipt. Dr. Saha Roy denies this.

</p><p id="p_9">          This court is not going into this controversy. Let a copy of this assessment

   order be handed over by Dr. Saha Roy to Mr. Garg in Court. Today will be deemed

   to be date of service of the said assessment order. The petitioner will be entitled to

   challenge the said assessment order in appeal.

</p><p id="p_10">           His line will be reconnected and the appeal entertained only upon deposit

   of the entire assessed amount with the respondent licensee. The appeal has to be

   filed and the deposit made by 26th August, 2016. No further deposit will be

   required for filing and hearing of the appeal. Reconnection will be made within
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                                                              2</span>


         four working days of receipt of the assessed amount. The above deposit will be

         without prejudice to the rights and contentions of the parties and will abide by

         the order of the Appellate Authority. If no appeal is filed by 28th August, 2016,

         the respondent licensee may appropriate this deposit.

</p><p id="p_11">                The writ petitioner will be entitled to transfer of the connection in his

         name upon a proper application being made. The respondent licensee will effect

         such transfer within four weeks from the date of receipt of the application,

         payment of requisite fees and the deposit of the assessed sum.

</p><p id="p_12">               All the papers are before this court. The allegations contained in this

         petition are deemed not to have been admitted.

</p><p id="p_13">               This writ application is, accordingly, disposed of.

               Certified photocopy of this order, if applied for, be supplied to the parties

         subject to compliance with all requisite formalities.




</p><p id="p_14">                                                                     (I.   P. MUKERJI, J.)

nm
A.R.[C.R.]
 </p>
