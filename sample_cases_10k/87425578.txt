<div class="doc_title">Gd Sanjit Kumar Singha vs Prasanta Kumar Singha &amp; Ors on 11 July, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                                     1



478   11.7.2017</pre>
<p id="p_1">                                     CO 2825 of 2016

      gd                           Sanjit Kumar Singha
                                             Vs.
</p><p id="p_2">                               Prasanta Kumar Singha &amp; Ors.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                         Mr. P.P. Roy
                                   ..for the Petitioner


                        The petition is not entertained for the

                  inordinate     delay    and      there   being    no

                  justification for such delay.

</p><p id="p_4">                        A previous petition for the same purpose

                  was dismissed by an order dated August 11,

                  2015 for the petitioner not appending a copy

                  of the order passed on an application under

                  Order XLI Rule 27 of the Code to the petition.

                  It is evident that the merits of the matter were

                  not gone into.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                        The     petitioner    is   justified   in   the

                  petitioner's submission that the petitioner was

                  entitled to carry a second petition challenging

                  the same order after undoing the default for

                  which the previous petition stood dismissed.

                  The petitioner claims that it took some time

                  for the petitioner to apply for and obtain the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                 2</span>


certified copy of the relevant order passed on

the application under Order XLI Rule 27 of the

Code and the marginal delay was on such

account.
</p>
<p id="p_6">      The previous petition carried to this

court, CO 3261 of 2014, was dismissed by an

order of August 11, 2015. The petitioner ought

to have applied for a certified copy of the order

passed on the application under Order XLI

Rule 27 of the Code within reasonable time of

the order of dismissal of the previous petition.

However, such application appears to have

been made only on March 29, 2016 and a

certified copy of the relevant application

obtained sometime in May, 2016. The present

petition was filed on July 28, 2016, barely a

fortnight short of a year after the dismissal of

the previous petition on August 11, 2015.

</p><p id="p_7">      Though there is no period of limitation

prescribed for a petition to be filed under

<a href="/doc/1331149/" id="a_1">Article 227</a> of the Constitution, the court does

not come to the aid of a laggard or someone

who does not seek to protect his rights with a
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                3</span>


degree of alacrity. There is no explanation as

to why it took the petitioner more than six

months to apply for the certified copy of the

order passed on the application under Order

XLI Rule 27 of the Code and a further period

in excess of two months after obtaining the

certified copy to carry the petition to this

court.
</p>
<p id="p_8">     In view of the conduct of the petitioner

and the unexplained delay as referred to

above, CO 2825 of 2016 is dismissed.

</p><p id="p_9">     There will be no order as to costs.




</p><p id="p_10">                            (Sanjib Banerjee, J.)
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3"> 4</span>
