<div class="doc_title">Vimlesh Kumar Sharma vs State Of Rajasthan And Ors on 17 May, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                            1
     IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR RAJASTHAN
                  JAIPUR BENCH, JAIPUR

           S.B. Criminal Writ Petition No. 117/2016

Vimlesh Kumar Sharma S/o Madan Lal Sharma, by caste
Brahmin, resident of Near Moti Bagh Talab Ki Pal, P.S. Kotwali,
Tonk, Tehsil &amp; District Tonk (Raj.).

                                                ...Complainant-Petitioner

                                 Versus

1.     State of Rajasthan through Public Prosecutor.

                                                           ...Respondent.</pre>

<p id="p_1">2.     Superintendent of Police, Tonk.
</p>
<p id="p_2">3.     Station House Officer, Police Station Kotwali Tonk,
       District Tonk.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                                     .....Respondents.
</p>
<p id="p_4">
</p><p id="p_5">DATE OF ORDER              :::             17th May, 2017

          HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE BANWARI LAL SHARMA


Mr. Tara Chand Sharma for the Petitioner.
</p><p id="p_6">Mr. Jitendra Shrimali, PP.
</p>
<p id="p_7">            Petitioner complainant Vimlesh Kumar Sharma preferred

this misc. petition assailing the impugned order dated 05.11.2015

passed by learned Additional Sessions Judge, Tonk in Criminal

Revision Petition No. 106/2014 whereby learned lower Revisional

Court dismissed the revision petition of petitioner and affirmed the

order of learned Chief Judicial Magistrate, Tonk passed in case No.

84/2014 on 20.09.2014 by which learned Magistrate dismissed the

complaint of petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_8">            Learned    counsel     for    the     petitioner   submits   that

respondent No. 2 did not participate in District Level in Handball

Tournament and without participating in Tournament obtained false
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                      2</span>
certificate and on the basis of false certificate he appeared in State

Level Tournament, therefore, prima facie offence is clearly made out

against accused. Learned Magistrate without sending the complaint to

police under <a href="/doc/99487/" id="a_1">Section 156(3)</a> Cr.P.C. wrongly rejected the complaint,

vide order dated 20.09.2014 and lower Revisional Court also wrongly

dismissed the revision petition, therefore, this misc. petition may be

allowed and both the impugned orders may be quashed and set aside

and the complaint of petition may be sent under <a href="/doc/99487/" id="a_2">Section 156(3)</a>

Cr.P.C. to SHO Police Station Kotwali, Tonk for registering FIR.

</p><p id="p_9">             Heard.
</p>
<p id="p_10">             It is admitted position that in the list of selected

candidates for District Level Tournament of Handball competition

Bhanwar Lal found place at serial No. 1, therefore it is presumption

that when a candidate is selected in the list of selected candidates at

serial No. 1, he was participated in the competition. Further, earlier

petitioner preferred S.B. Criminal Misc. Petition No. 6747/2015 before

this Court for the same relief which has already been disposed of vide

order dated 07.01.2016, therefore, on the same ground this second

writ petition is not maintainable.
</p>
<p id="p_11">             In view of above, the writ petition is dismissed.




</p><p id="p_12">                                [BANWARI LAL SHARMA], J.
</p>
<p id="p_13">Komal/101.
</p>
