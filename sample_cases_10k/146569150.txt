<div class="doc_title">Veerammal vs The District Collector on 12 August, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">        

 

BEFORE THE MADURAI BENCH OF MADRAS HIGH COURT               

DATED: 12.08.2015  

CORAM   

THE HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE  T.RAJA         

Writ Petition(MD)No.14421 of 2015 
and 
M.P(MD)Nos.1 and 2 of 2015  

Veerammal                                                       .. Petitioner

Vs.

1.The District Collector,
   Pudukkottai District,
   Pudukkottai.

2.The Commissioner,  
    Pudukkottai Panchayath Union,
    Pudukkottai.

3.N.Tamilarasi                                                  ..Respondents 

Prayer
        Writ Petition filed under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the Constitution of
India for the issuance of a Writ of  Certiorarified Mandamus calling for the
records pursuant to the impugned order of the first respondent in
Na.Ka.NO.V5/817/2015 dated 17.7.2015 in appointing the third respondent as 
the Anganwadi Helper(Cooking Assistant) and to quash the same and further to
direct the first respondent to appoint the Petitioner for the post of
Anganwadi Helper(Cooking Assistant) in the noon-meal Centre at Panchayath  
Union Primary School, Royapatti.
                
!For Petitioner          : Porkodi Karnan
                           for M/s.Polax Legal Solution
^For Respondents         : Mr.R.Anandraj
                           Govt.Advocate
                           for Mr.S.Sathish Kumar               
                           Addl.Govt.Pleader
:ORDER  
</pre><p id="p_1">        This Writ Petition has been filed challenging the appontment of the
third respondent as Anganwadi Helper(Cooking Assistant) in the noon-meal
Centre at Panchayath Union Primary School, Royapatti. 
</p>
<p id="p_2">        2.Mr.R.Anandraj, learned Additional Government Advocate takes notice
for the respondents and by consent, the Writ Petition is taken up for final
disposal at the stage of admission itself.
</p>
<p id="p_3">        3.The Petitioner is not able to answer any one of the questions as to
whether the appointee third respondent is  having lesser qualification than
the Petitioner. Again, the learned counsel for the Petitioner is also not
able to answer in what way the Petitioner has got  superior merit except
repeatedly mentioning  before this Court that since the Petitioner is hailing
from the same village at a distance of  3 Kms, she should be given the
preference. This Court does not find merit in the Writ Petition since the
Petitioner has not made any acceptable challenge to the appointment made by 
the respondents in favour of third respondent. Therefore when the Petitioner
is not able to point out any infirmity in the impugned order, this Court is
not inclined to allow the Writ Petition. This Court has repeatedly held that
in the matter of appointment of Anganwadi Assistant, distance is not a matter
and the Petitioner  has no vested right to be considered on the ground that
she is  hailing from the same village. Therefore the Writ Petition fails and
the same is dismissed. Consequently, connected Miscellaneous Petitions are 
dismissed. No costs. 
</p>

</p><p id="p_5">1.The District Collector,
   Pudukkottai District,
   Pudukkottai.
</p>
