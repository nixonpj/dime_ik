<p id="p_1">     The petitioner herein was found guilty, and convicted

under <a href="/doc/1823824/" id="a_1">Section 138</a> of the Negotiable Instruments Act in C.C

289 of 2008 of the Judicial First Class Magistrate Court-I,

Kozhikode. He lost in appeal before the appellate court,

and in revision, this Court also confirmed the conviction.

However, the petitioner was given time by this Court till

13.8.2013 to surrender before the trial court to serve out

the sentence till rising of the court, and to make payment

of the compensation.       Now it appears that there is

execution proceeding against the petitioner. He failed to

appear in court as directed by this Court, and he also failed

to make payment of the compensation. When he tendered

the amount of compensation, the learned Magistrate

declined it, it is alleged.    The petitioner seeks orders

extending the time granted by the court to surrender before

the trial court to serve out the sentence and to make

payment of compensation.
</p>
<p id="p_2">     2.   On hearing the learned counsel and on a perusal

Crl.M.C No.1692 of 2016
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                    2</span>


of the materials, I find no reason or necessity to extend the

time granted by the court. It is submitted that the petitioner

is ready to make compensation and he is ready to deposit it

at any time. It is clarified that time was granted by the court

till 13.8.2013 only to avoid coercive steps.          Once the

sentence is confirmed in appeal and in revision also, the

accused can at any time surrender before the trial court to

serve out the sentence.            When the compensation is

tendered, the court is bound to accept it. If any Judicial

Magistrate says that the time has expired and so the

accused cannot be allowed to serve out the sentence, that

misapprehension will have to be corrected. If the petitioner

is ready to pay the amount, he will have to immediately

surrender before the trial court and make payment of the

compensation. Nothing will stand in the way of his serving

out the sentence or making payment of compensation.

</p><p id="p_3">      With the above clarification, this Crl.M.C is disposed of.
