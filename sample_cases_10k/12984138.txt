<div class="doc_title">Shyam Jee Chaudhary vs The State Of Bihar on 8 April, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                     IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                             CRIMINAL MISCELLANEOUS No.20429 of 2019
                   Arising Out of PS. Case No.-630 Year-2018 Thana- BAGHA District- West Champaran
                 ======================================================
                 Shyam Jee Chaudhary, aged about 39 Years, Male, Son of Bal Govind
                 Chaudhary, Resident of Village - Patkhauli, Ward No. 03, P.S.- Bagaha
                 (Patkhauli O.P.), District - West Champaran.

                                                                                    ... ... Petitioner
                                                      Versus
                 The State of Bihar

                                                          ... ... Opposite Party
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner     :        Mr.Brij Kishor Mishra, Advocate.
                 For the Opposite Party :        Mr.Akshay Lal Pandit, A.P.P.
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE SUDHIR SINGH
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   08-04-2019                  Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned

                   counsel for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                   The petitioner is languishing in custody since

                   31.12.2018 in a case for the offence registered under Section

                   30(a) of the Bihar Prohibition and <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_1">Excise Act</a>, 2016.

</p><p id="p_3">                               The prosecution story, in brief, is that total 20 liters

                   wine is said to have been recovered.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                               It has been submitted by learned counsel for the

                   petitioner that the petitioner has got no criminal antecedent.

                   Charge sheet/prosecution report has been submitted in the

                   present case. There is no allegation of tampering with the

                   witnesses alleged against the petitioner. The petitioner has

                   falsely been implicated in the present case. It is alleged that total
              Patna High Court CR. MISC. No.20429 of 2019(2) dt.08-04-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                         2/2</span>




                       20 liters wine is recovered from joint house of the petitioner.

                       The name of the petitioner has come in the present case on the

                       basis of alleged recovery made from joint house of the petitioner

                       where other family members also reside. There is no compliance

                       of Section 100 Cr. P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                     On behalf of the State, it is submitted that the

                       petitioner is named in the F.I.R/ Complaint Case.

</p><p id="p_6">                                    Considering the aforesaid facts and circumstances, let

                       the petitioner above named, be released on bail on furnishing

                       bail bonds of Rs.10,000/- (Ten Thousand) with two sureties of

                       the like amount each to the satisfaction of the learned Special

                       Judge, Excise, Bettiah, District-West Champaran, in connection

                       with Bagaha Patkhauli P.S. Case No. 630 of 2018.



</p><p id="p_7">                                                    (Sudhir Singh, J)
U.K./-
</p>
