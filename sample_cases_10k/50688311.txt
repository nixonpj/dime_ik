<div class="doc_title">Deyali Pandit @ Ram Dayal Pandit vs The State Of Bihar on 22 January, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.58427 of 2015
                      Arising Out of PS.Case No. -250 Year- 2014 Thana -PARIHAR District- SITAMARHI
                 ======================================================
                 1. Deyali Pandit @ Ram Dayal Pandit Son of Late Kishori Pandit R/o
                 Village Sahjauli, P.S. Parihar, District Sitamarhi
                                                                         .... .... Petitioner/s
                                                     Versus
                 1. The State of Bihar
                                                                    .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s       :    Mr. Raja Ram Rai
                 For the Opposite Party/s     : Mr. Sadanand Paswan (Spl. Pp)
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE JUSTICE SMT. ANJANA MISHRA
                                                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   22-01-2016          Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned

                 Additional Public Prosecutor for the State.

</p><p id="p_2">                        The petitioner apprehends his arrest in connection with a

                 case registered for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_1">Sections

                 341</a>/<a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_2">323</a>/<a href="/doc/1133601/" id="a_3">325</a>/<a href="/doc/203036/" id="a_4">354</a>/<a href="/doc/1101188/" id="a_5">379</a>/<a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_6">504</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_7">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code and <a href="/doc/42436223/" id="a_8">Section

                 3(i)(x)</a> of the Scheduled Castes and <a href="/doc/25085007/" id="a_9">Scheduled Tribes (Prevention

                 of Atrocities) Act</a>, 1989.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                        Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that there is case

                 and counter case between the parties with regard to the cutting of

                 earth by the informant's side in lands belonging to the petitioner.

                 It is submitted that the allegations with regard to the offence under

                 <a href="/doc/42436223/" id="a_10">Section 3(i)(x)</a> of the Scheduled Castes and <a href="/doc/25085007/" id="a_11">Scheduled Tribes

                 (Prevention of Atrocities) Act</a>, 1989 are being brought in only to

                 make the charge grievous.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">          Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.58427 of 2015 (2) dt.22-01-2016</span><p id="p_4">
</p><p id="p_5">                                                   P2/2




                               Considering the aforementioned facts and circumstances

                      and that there is land dispute between the parties and there is case

                      and counter case and also the fact that the petitioner has no

                      criminal antecedent, let the petitioner, in the event of his arrest /

                      surrender within a period of four weeks from the date of

                      receipt/production of a copy of this order, be enlarged on bail on

                      furnishing bail bonds of Rs. 10,000/- (ten thousand) with two

                      sureties of the each amount to the satisfaction of the learned Chief

                      Judicial Magistrate, Sitamarhi in connection with Parihar P.S.

                      Case No. 250/2014 subject to the conditions as laid down under

                      <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_12">Section 438(2)</a> of the Cr. P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                                                (Anjana Mishra, J)
Saif/-
</p><pre id="pre_2">  U         T
