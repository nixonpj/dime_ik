<div class="doc_title">Niraj Kumar vs The State Of Bihar on 30 May, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.24052 of 2016
                      Arising Out of PS.Case No. -198 Year- 2016 Thana -KADAMKUAN District- PATNA
                 ======================================================
                 Niraj Kumar Son of Kameshwar Kewat Resident of Village Biruachak , PS
                 Mehdiganj District Patna.

                                                                               .... ....   Petitioner/s
                                                       Versus
                 The State of Bihar

                                                              .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     : Mr. Kumar Rajeev, Adv.
                 For the Opposite Party/s : Mr. Mukesh Kumar Singh(App)
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE DINESH KUMAR SINGH
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   30-05-2016               Heard learned counsels for the petitioner and the State.

</p><p id="p_2">                             The petitioner is languishing in jail since 13.05.2016 in

                 a case registered for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1099867/" id="a_1">Section 290</a> of

                 the Indian Penal Code and 20 and 22 of the <a href="/doc/1727139/" id="a_2">NDPS Act</a>.

</p><p id="p_3">                             From the bag of the petitioner, 109 sachets each

                 containing 50 grams, total 545 grams ganja were recovered.

</p><p id="p_4">                             It is submitted by learned counsel for the petitioner that

                 the recovery is of small quantity. A statement has been made in

                 para 3 of the petition that the petitioner has no criminal

                 antecedent.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                             Learned APP does not dispute the quantum of recovery.

                               Considering the recovery of contraband being small

                 quantity, let the above named petitioner be released on bail, on
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">         Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.24052 of 2016 (2) dt.30-05-2016</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                   2/2</span>




                     furnishing bail bond of `10,000/- (ten thousand) with two sureties

                     of the like amount each to the satisfaction of learned District and

                     Sessions Judge, Patna in connection with Kadamkuan P.S. Case

                     No. 198 of 2016, Special Case No. 19 of 2016.

</p><p id="p_6">                                    The learned court below will be at liberty to cancel the

                     bail bonds of the petitioner if he defaults for three consecutive

                     occasions or gets involved in some similar nature of offence.


</p><p id="p_7">                                                                 (Dinesh Kumar Singh, J)
Amrendra/-
