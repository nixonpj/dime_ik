                           O R D E R

<p id="p_1">     These petitions are filed by the accused Nos.4, 1 to 3

respectively, in Crime No.221 of 2013 of Valappad Police Station for

offences punishable under Secs.467, 468, 471 and 474 r/w Sec.34<a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_1"> of

the Indian Penal Code</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_2">     2.    Dispute concerns right over 3.5 cents which according to

the de facto complainant is a pathway over which he has got a right

of way under the provisions of the Kerala Land Reforms Act on his

father getting kudikidappu from one Lakshmikutty as per order

passed by the Land Tribunal concerned. While so, petitioners in

B.A.No.5059 of 2013 (accused Nos.1 to 3) purchased 28 cents and

the above said 3.5 cents from one Mani as per document Nos.1718

and 1719 of 2007, the latter relating to the disputed 3.5 cents.

Those documents recited that Mani got the property from one

Mohandas who is the son of Lakshmikutty.

</p><p id="p_3">     3.    According to the de facto complainant, there is no

document showing that Mohandas assigned right over the 3.5 cents

which is in dispute.
</p>
<p id="p_4">B.A.Nos.4650 and 5059 of 2013
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                     2</span>


</p><p id="p_5">      4.    Petitioner in B.A.No.4650 of 2013 is the 4th accused

who is said to have prepared the documents in question. Hence

he is arrayed as accused in Crime No.221 of 2013.

</p><p id="p_6">      5.    Learned counsel for the petitioners requested for

anticipatory bail contending that what is involved is only a civil

dispute.
</p>
<p id="p_7">      6.    Learned counsel for the de facto complainant and the

learned Public Prosecutor opposed the applications.

</p><p id="p_8">      7.    Having regard to the nature of allegations and the

circumstances stated, I am inclined to think that petitioners could

be granted relief but subject to conditions.

</p><p id="p_9">      Resultantly these application are allowed as under:

</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">                   (i)    Petitioners shall surrender before

             the officer investigating Crime No.221 of 2013

             of Valappad Police Station on 17.10.2013 at

             10a.m.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_2">                   (ii)   After interrogation, the said Officer

             shall produce the petitioners before the

             jurisdictional magistrate the same day.

</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">                   (iii)  On    such   production,     learned

             magistrate shall release the petitioners on bail

             on their executing bond for `15,000/- (Rupees

             Fifteen Thousand Only) each with two sureties

             each for the like sum each to the satisfaction of

             the learned magistrate and subject to the

             following conditions:
</blockquote>
<p id="p_10">B.A.Nos.4650 and 5059 of 2013
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                     3</span>


</p><p id="p_11">                  (a)    Petitioners shall report to the

                  officer investigating Crime No.221 of

                  2013 on every Saturday between

                  10a.m and 12 p.m until filing of the

                  final report or for a period of two

                  months, whichever is earlier.
</p>
<p id="p_12">                  (b)    Petitioners shall report to the

                  Investigating Officer as and when

                  required for interrogation.
</p>
<p id="p_13">                  (c)    Petitioners shall co-operate with

                  investigation of the case.
</p>
<p id="p_14">                                                  Sd/-
</p>
