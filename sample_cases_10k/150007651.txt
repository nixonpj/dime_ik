<p id="p_1">            SANJAY KISHAN KAUL, C.J. (ORAL)

            CM-7512-2005

                                 Learned     counsel   for   the   petitioner   expresses   an
            apprehension arising from the fact that he is now the counsel for the
            Union of India. However, since the original petition was filed by him
            and the petitioner has made no other alternative arrangement, we see no
            impediment at least for this matter that we cannot hear the petitioner
            and decide the controversy.
</p><p id="p_2">                                 The petitioner before us and certain other parties had filed
            OAs before the Central Administrative Tribunal seeking to challenge
            the appointment of respondents No. 9, 17, 18 and 19 before us on
            various accounts. Part relief was granted vide order dated 12.10.2000 to
            the extent that the recruitment under the category of ex-servicemen in
            pursuance to the advertisement for recruitment of Mazdoor was set
Sharma Amodhaside on the ground that the advertisement did not contain the specific

            provision for such reservation. Qua the other aspects, there was no
            interference by the Central Administrative Tribunal.
</p><p id="p_3">                               In the application, all that has been stated is that the
            petitioner was under impression that the other persons similarly situated
            to the petitioner would file a petition, but he came to know
            subsequently that no such petition was filed and, therefore, the petition
            has been filed after almost five years of the order.
</p><p id="p_4">                               We find the aforesaid explanation to be completely
            inadmissible and without any basis. It is lacking in all material
            particulars. It is not a short period of delay, but of five years and it
            would not be appropriate to permit the chapter to be opened qua the
            issue which was closed by the Central Administrative Tribunal after
            five years on such a general allegation. Be that as it may, it is only the
            petitioner who has considered it appropriate to assail the findings of the
            Central Administrative Tribunal qua the other different categories.
</p><p id="p_5">                               We, thus, dismiss the present application.
            CWP-6596-CAT-2005
                               The petition being barred by time is accordingly dismissed.
</p>
