         IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

CRIMINAL MISC.APPLICATION (FOR CONDONATION OF DELAY) NO. 1
                            of 2018
      In R/CRIMINAL REVISION APPLICATION NO. 1311 of 2018
==========================================================
</pre><p id="p_1">   SITABEN W/O HARDAS DASA D/O MALDEBHAI BHURA BHUTIYA
                          Versus
                  HARDAS MALDEBHAI DASA
==========================================================
Appearance:
</p><p id="p_2">JAY R SHAH for the PETITIONER(s) No.
MR. K.L.PANDYA, APP for the RESPONDENT(s) No.
UNSERVED REFUSED (R) for the RESPONDENT(s) No.
==========================================================
 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE V.P. PATEL
                    Date : 26/03/2019
                       IA ORDER


</p><p id="p_3">  1. The present Application is filed by the Applicant under <a href="/doc/100581/" id="a_1">Section 5</a> of the
     Limitation Act for condonation of delay of 107 days occurred in
     preferring the Criminal Revision Application.
</p>

<p id="p_4">  2. Heard learned Advocate Mr. Jay R. Shah for the Applicant and learned
     APP Mr. K.L.Pandya for Opponent No.3 - State of Gujarat.
</p>

<p id="p_5">  3. The cause list reflects that Opponent Nos. 1 and 2 are unserved refused.
     It is submitted by learned Advocate for Applicant that DS was permitted
     by this court and the Opponent has refused to accept the service. The
     DS Affidavit is produced on record.
</p>

<p id="p_6">  4. The Applicant has stated the grounds in the Application more
     particularly in paragraphs 2 and 3 of the Application for delay caused in
     preferring the Criminal Revision Application.
</p>

<p id="p_7">  5. Learned Advocate for Opponent No.3 - State of Gujarat has fairly
     submitted that he has no objection if delay is condoned for cause of


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                 Page 1 of 3</span>
     R/CR.RA/1311/2018                                            IA ORDER



   justice.
</p>

<p id="p_8">6. The Opponent Nos. 1 and 2 have not filed any form of reply in affidavit
   against the contents of the application which is supported by affidavit.
</p>

<p id="p_9">7. The Hon'ble Apex Court has laid down the broad guidelines with regard
   to the approach in such matters of condonation of delay.
</p>

<p id="p_10">   6.1    The Hon'ble Apex Court in a judgment reported in (2010) 5 SCC
   459 in case of <a href="/doc/1264971/" id="a_2">Oriental Aroma Chemical Industries Ltd. v. Gujarat
   Industrial Development Corporation &amp; Anr</a>., wherein it has been
   observed:
</p>
<p id="p_11">   ".....the courts are bestowed with the power to condone the delay, if
   sufficient cause is shown. .......................... The expression 'sufficient
   cause' is elastic enough to enable the courts to apply the law in a
   meaningful manner which subserves the ends of justice. Although, no
   hard and fast rule can be laid down in dealing with the applications for
   condonation of delay, this Court has justifiably advocated adoption of a
   liberal approach in condoning the delay of short duration and a stricter
   approach where the delay is inordinate. ..."
</p>
<p id="p_12">   6.2    The Hon'ble Apex Court in a judgment reported in AIR 2010 SC
   3043 in case of <a href="/doc/473980/" id="a_3">Balvant Singh (Dead) v. Jagdish Singh &amp; Ors</a>., has
   also considered the sufficient cause with the observation that it should
   be elastic enough to allow the party to have the recourse before the
   higher forum.
</p>

<p id="p_13">8. Considering the facts and circumstances and the averments made in the
   Application and also the grounds mentioned in the Application, the
   applicant has satisfied to this Court that he has sufficient cause for not
   preferring the Appeal within prescribed time limit. Therefore, the
   present Application deserves to be allowed.
</p>



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                  Page 2 of 3</span>
<p id="p_14">           R/CR.RA/1311/2018                                     IA ORDER



</p><p id="p_15">    9. Hence, the present Application is allowed. Delay of 107 days caused in
         preferring the Criminal Revision Application is condoned.
         Rule is made absolute.
</p>

<p id="p_16">                                                               (V. P. PATEL,J)
J.N. W
