<div class="doc_title">Dhananjay Kumar Singh vs State Of Jharkhand on 9 March, 2011</div><pre id="pre_1">               IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                              B.A. No.8264 of 2010
            Dhananjay Kumar Singh.                  ......Petitioner. 
                                     ­Versus­
            The State of Jharkhand.                 .......Opposite Party.
                                      ­­­­­­
           CORAM : HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE NARENDRA NATH TIWARI
                                       ­­­­­­
            For the Petitioner :        Mr. P.K. Mukhopadhyay, Advocate.  
            For the State       :       A.P.P.
                                       ­­­­­­
03/09.03.2011</pre><p id="p_1">:     The petitioner is an accused in the case registered under 
            Sections 379 and 411 of the Indian Penal Code. 
</p><p id="p_2">                                  Learned   counsel   for   the   petitioner   submitted 
            that the petitioner has been falsely implicated in the case only 
            on the basis of his antecedent; the incriminating motorcycle has 
            not been recovered from the petitioner's possession, rather it was 
            recovered   from   the   house,   there   were  other   inmates   also;   the 
            petitioner   is   in   custody   since   August,   2010;   he   is   a   local 
            permanent resident; there is no chance of his absconding.  
</p><p id="p_3">                                  Learned A.P.P. opposed the petitioner's prayer 
            for bail and submitted that  there is similar antecedent against 
            the   petitioner.   However,   he   has   not   disputed   the   period   of 
            custody   and   said   contentions   of   learned   counsel   for   the 
            petitioner. 
</p><p id="p_4">                                  Regard   being   had   to   the   facts   and 
            circumstances   of   the   case,   the   petitioner,   above­named,   is 
            directed   to   be   released   on   bail   on   furnishing   bail   bond   of 
            Rs.10,000/­   (rupees   ten   thousand)   with   two   sureties   of   the   like 
            amount, each, to the satisfaction of learned Judicial Magistrate, 
            1st Class, Dhanbad in connection with Jharia P.S. Case no.240 of 
            2010, corresponding to G.R. no.2594 of 2010, with the condition 
            that   the   bailors   must   be   the   family   members   and   that   the 
            petitioner shall appear physically on all the dates in the case in 
            the court below. 
</p>             
<p id="p_5">                                                   (Narendra Nath Tiwari, J.)
Sanjay/
 </p>
