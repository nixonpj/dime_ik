<div class="doc_title">Ram Swarath Sah vs State Of Bihar &amp; Anr on 1 February, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                    Criminal Miscellaneous No.6265 of 2018
                  Arising Out of PS. Case No.-1571 Year-2012 Thana- DARBHANGA COMPLAINT CASE
                                                    District- Darbhanga
                 ======================================================
                 Ram Swarath Sah, son of Sri Parash Nath Sah, Resident of Village- Dashara,
                 P.S.- Mohadi Nagar District- Samastipur.
                                                                          ... ... Petitioner/s
                                                   Versus

            1. The State of Bihar.
            2. Shri Shailendra Kumar Pandey, Son of Sri Upendra Pandey, resident of
               Village- Maro, P.S.- Maro, District- Darbhanga.
                                                               ... ... Opposite Party/s
               ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :    Mr. Bamdeo Pandey, Adv.
                 For the Opposite Party/s :    Mr. J. N. Thakur, APP
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE DINESH KUMAR SINGH
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   01-02-2018            Heard learned Counsels for the petitioner and learned APP

                  for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                         The petitioner is apprehending arrest in a complaint case

                  wherein process has been directed to be issued after cognizance

                  being taken for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_1">Sections 406</a> and

                  <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_2">420</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                         The prosecution case as per the complainant Shailendra

                  Kumar Pandey, is to the effect that the petitioner being the

                  Junior Engineer of Rural Works Department, Laherisarai,

                  Darbhanga, came to brick-kiln of the complainant and asked

                  him to supply bricks and rabbish (crushed stone particles). The

                  complainant agreed to supply the bricks at the rate of Rs.2500/-

                  per trailor and rabbish @ Rs.900/- per trailor. The petitioner
 Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.6265 of 2018(2) dt.01-02-2018
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                              2/3</span>




         paid Rs.50,000/- as advance, but the complainant supplied the

         bricks and rabbish valuing Rs. 13,73,000/-, but the remaining

         amount was not paid to him.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                It is submitted by learned counsel for the petitioner that

         there is no document to suggest that any agreement was entered

         into between the petitioner and the complainant and the

         petitioner being the government servant cannot purchase

         anything without tender. Moreover, no tender was floated for

         any scheme. Hence, maliciously the present case has been

         lodged. Though the case was registered in 2012, but the order of

         cognizance was passed on 19.05.2014. A statement has been

         made in paragraph no.3 of the petition that the petitioner is not

         having any criminal antecedent.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                It is submitted by Mr. J. N. Thakur learned APP for the

         State that though there is no agreement on record to suggest the

         accusation levelled, but the accusation is specific against the

         petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                Considering the accusation arising out of contractual nature

         of dispute coupled with statement made in paragraph no.3 of the

         petition that the petitioner is not having any criminal antecedent,

         let the above named petitioner be released on anticipatory bail,

         in the event of arrest or surrender before the learned Court
             Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.6265 of 2018(2) dt.01-02-2018
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                          3/3</span>




                     below within a period of 12 weeks from today, on furnishing

                     bail bond of Rs.10,000/- (ten thousand) with two sureties of the

                     like amount each to the satisfaction of the learned JM, 1 st Class,

                     Darbhanga, in connection with C.R. Case No. 1571 of 2012,

                     subject to the conditions as laid down under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_3">Section 438(2)</a> of

                     the Cr.P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><p id="p_8">                                              (Dinesh Kumar Singh, J)
Ashwini/-
</p>
