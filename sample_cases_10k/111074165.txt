<div class="doc_title">Rohi Singh vs State Of Punjab on 30 April, 2013</div><pre id="pre_1">                         Crl. Misc. No. M-10962 of 2013                       1




IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA AT CHANDIGARH.


                         Case No. : Crl. Misc. No. M-10962 of 2013
                         Date of Decision : April 30, 2013



            Rohi Singh                  ....   Petitioner
                                  Vs.
            State of Punjab             ....   Respondent</pre>


<p id="p_1">CORAM : HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE L. N. MITTAL

                         *    *   *

Present :   Mr. Rajbir Singh, Advocate
            for the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_2">            Mr. Gaurav Garg Dhuriwala, DAG, Punjab.
</p>
<pre id="pre_2">                         *    *   *

L. N. MITTAL, J. (Oral) :

</pre><p id="p_3">            Accused Rohi Singh has filed this petition for anticipatory bail

in case FIR No.365 dated 04.12.2012 under <a href="/doc/1667388/" id="a_1">Sections 419</a>, <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_2">420</a>, <a href="/doc/1985627/" id="a_3">467</a>, <a href="/doc/556166/" id="a_4">468</a>,

<a href="/doc/1466184/" id="a_5">471</a> and <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_6">120-B</a> of the Indian Penal Code (in short - <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_7">IPC</a>), registered at

Police Station City Sangrur, District Sangrur.

</p><p id="p_4">            I have heard counsel for the parties and perused the case file.

            According to the prosecution version, the petitioner purchased

land of one Arvinder Kaur by way of sale deed executed by one Jaspreet

Kaur impersonating as Arvinder Kaur and identified inter alia by petitioner's
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                           Crl. Misc. No. M-10962 of 2013                     2</span>




co-accused Jaswinder Singh, Sukhwinder Singh and Puneet Garg etc.

             Counsel for the petitioner contended that the petitioner, as

purchaser of the land, is also victim of the fraud committed by co-accused

of the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_5">             On the other hand, learned State counsel, on instructions from

Inspector Kashmir Singh, stated that Jaswinder Singh, who wrongly

identified Jaspreet Kaur as vendor Arvinder Kaur, is none else but brother-

in-law of the petitioner and thus, the petitioner is not victim of the fraud and

is rather main Architect of the fraud.
</p>
<p id="p_6">             In response to the aforesaid contention, learned counsel for the

petitioner contended that Jaswinder Singh had identified the petitioner as

vendee and not Jaspreet Kaur as vendor Arvinder Kaur.

</p><p id="p_7">             To resolve the aforesaid conflicting contentions of counsel for

the parties, registration endorsement of the impugned sale deed has been

seen from the photostat copy of the sale deed, as shown by the counsel for

the petitioner himself. Perusal thereof reveals that before the Sub Registrar,

Jaspreet Kaur was wrongly identified as vendor Arvinder Kaur by

Sukhwinder Singh as well as by Jaswinder Singh. In fact, at the time of

registration, identification of vendor and not of the vendee is obtained.

Thus, it was completely misleading contention by counsel for the petitioner

that Jaswinder Singh had identified the petitioner as vendee and not Jaspreet
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                            Crl. Misc. No. M-10962 of 2013                 3</span>




Kaur as vendor Arvinder Kaur. The cat is out of the bag. The petitioner, as

purchaser of the land and beneficiary of the sale deed in question, thus

appears to be the main Architect, having obtained the impugned sale deed

by impersonation of the vendor.
</p>
<p id="p_8">               In the aforesaid circumstances, the question of extending the

extra-ordinary concession of anticipatory bail to the petitioner would not

arise.
</p>
<p id="p_9">               Dismissed, without meaning to express any opinion on merits

of the case.
</p>
<p id="p_10">

</p><pre id="pre_3">April 30, 2013                                ( L. N. MITTAL )
monika                                              JUDGE
