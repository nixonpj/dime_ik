<div class="doc_title">An Application For Anticipatory ... vs Ainul Mia &amp; Ors on 27 November, 2013</div><div class="doc_author">Author: Nishita Mhatre</div>

<p id="p_1"><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                             1</span>


27, 2013.
</p><p id="p_2">                    C.R.M. 13710 of 2013
            In the matter of : An application for anticipatory bail under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> of the
            Code of Criminal Procedure filed on 08.10.2013.
</p><p id="p_3">                                  And
            In the matter of : Ainul Mia &amp; Ors.
</p><p id="p_4">                                           Petitioners.
</p><p id="p_5">                               -versus-
</p><p id="p_6">                            The State of West Bengal
                                          Opposite party.
</p><p id="p_7">            Ms. Minoti Gomes,
            Md. Hafiz Ali
                                   For the petitioners.
</p><p id="p_8">            Mr. Sumnirmal Nag
                                   For the State.
</p>
<p id="p_9">                    The petitioners, apprehending arrest in connection with Tifanganj Police

            Station      Case     No.      157   of   2013       dated   24.07.2013   under   <a href="/doc/1258372/" id="a_2">Sections

            147</a>,<a href="/doc/763672/" id="a_3">148</a>,<a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_4">149</a>,<a href="/doc/1947545/" id="a_5">448</a>,<a href="/doc/1133601/" id="a_6">325</a>,<a href="/doc/1540253/" id="a_7">326</a>,<a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_8">307</a> of the Indian Penal Code, have come to this Court for

            anticipatory bail.
</p>
<p id="p_10">                    We have heard the learned Advocates for the parties and have seen the

            case diary and other relevant material on record. The petitioners are claiming

            parity with those who have been granted anticipatory bail on 1.10.2013 in C.R.M.

            12181 of 2013. We have verified from the material on record that the petitioners

            are similarly circumstanced as those who have already been granted anticipatory

            bail.    In our opinion, there is no reason for the custodial interrogation of the

            petitioners in this case.
</p>
<p id="p_11">                    Hence, in the event of arrest, the petitioners shall be released on bail upon

            furnishing a bond of Rs.5,000/-(Rupees five thousand) each with one surety each

            of like amount, to the satisfaction of the Court concerned subject to the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                    2</span>


conditions laid down under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_9">Section 438(2)</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure,

1973 with the further condition that the petitioners shall meet the Investigating

Officer once a week until further orders.



    The application for anticipatory bail is, thus, disposed of.


</p><p id="p_12">                           (Nishita Mhatre, J.)


                     ( Indrajit Chatterjee , J.)
