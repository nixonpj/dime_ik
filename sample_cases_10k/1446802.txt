<div class="doc_title">Lal Mohar Sah @ Basawan vs State Of Bihar on 9 August, 2010</div><pre id="pre_1">              IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                         Cr.Misc. No.26429 of 2010
            LAL MOHAR SAH @ BASAWAN S/O RAM GOVIND SAH
                                   Versus
                             STATE OF BIHAR
                                  -----------
</pre>
<p id="p_1">2.   09.08.2010              Heard learned Counsel for the petitioner and the

                  State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                             The petitioner seeks bail in a case instituted for the

                  offence under <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_1">Sections 323</a>, <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_2">324</a>, <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_3">307</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_4">504</a>, <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_5">307</a>, <a href="/doc/1101188/" id="a_6">379</a> of the

                  Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                             It has been submitted that even though in the First

                  Information Report, the allegation is that the petitioner who

                  was the brother of the informant had fired on account of which

                  the informant's son died but the post mortem examination

                  report reveals that the deceased had died on account of injury

                  cause by hard and blunt substance.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                             In view of such, let the petitioner above named,

                  be released on bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs.5,000/-

                  (Five thousand) with two sureties of the like amount each to

                  the satisfaction of learned 3rd Additional Sessions Judge,

                  Rohtas at Sasaram in Tr. No. 373 of 2009, in connection with

                  Kachchaw P.S. Case No. 32/2009, subject to the conditions,

</p><p id="p_5">                  (i) That one of the bailors will be a close relative of the

                  petitioner who will give an affidavit giving genealogy as to

                  how he is related with the petitioner. The bailor will undertake
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                -2-</span>




         to furnish information to the court about any change in the

         address of the petitioner, (ii) That the affidavit shall clearly

         state that the petitioner is not an accused in any other case

         and if he is, he shall not be released on bail, (iii) That the

         petitioner will give an undertaking that he will receive the

         police papers on the given date and be present on date fixed

         for charge and if he fails to do so on two given dates and

         delays the trial in any manner, his bail will be liable to be

         cancelled for reasons of misuse, (iv) That the petitioner will

         be well represented on each date and if he fails to do so on two

         consecutive dates, his bail will be liable to be cancelled.



</p><pre id="pre_2">Fahad.                                     ( Anjana Prakash, J. )
 </pre>
