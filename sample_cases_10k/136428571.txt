                               ORDER

<p id="p_1">            Petitioner is the owner of lorry bearing registration

No.KL-8-BE-902, which was intercepted by the second respondent

on 03.02.2017 alleging violation of the provisions of the Kerala

Minor Mineral Concessions Rules 2015 and Mines and Minerals

(Development and <a href="/doc/1489134/" id="a_1">Regulation) Act</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_2">      2.    The grievance of the petitioner herein at present is that

in spite of the seizure, the matter has not been reported by the

second respondent to the jurisdictional magistrate as mandatory

under <a href="/doc/648213/" id="a_2">section 102</a> Cr.P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_3">      3.    Having considered the limited prayer now sought in the

petition, I am inclined to dispose of the Crl.M.C with a direction that

the second respondent herein shall report the matter to the

jurisdictional magistrate within three working days from date of

receipt of order, if not reported already. The learned magistrate

shall thereafter consider the application for interim custody, if any

filed, as expeditiously as possible within seven days from the date of

filing. If the application is allowed, the court below shall impose

such conditions as it may deem fit including a condition regarding

Crl.M.C.2596/17
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                      2</span>


the deposit of the original records as laid down in Anfar v. State of

Kerala (2014(4) KHC 210) and regarding the amount to be

deposited, in accordance with the decision of this Court in Writ

Appeal No.1889 of 2015 with appropriate modifications. It was also

be intimated to the Geologist who shall take steps for

compounding the offence, if any. In the event of compounding, the

vehicle with sand shall be released forthwith to the competent

person.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                Crl.M.C. is disposed of as above.
</p>
