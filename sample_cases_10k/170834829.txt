<div class="doc_title">Gostopodo Ghosh And Another vs Maharaddi Mondal And Another on 15 March, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                                         1



15.03.2016</pre><p id="p_1">.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">Item No. 27</span>
<p id="p_2">                                       C.O. 1566 of 2015

                               Gostopodo Ghosh and another.
</p><p id="p_3">                                           Vs.
</p><p id="p_4">                               Maharaddi Mondal and another.
</p>

<p id="p_5">                              Mr. Tarak Nath Halder,
                              Mr. Kingsuk Mondal.
</p><p id="p_6">                                       ... for the petitioners.
</p>

<p id="p_7">                    The conduct of the petitioners in challenging the order dated
              1st April, 2014 itself disentitled the petitioners to have the benefit
              of extraordinary jurisdiction of this Court under <a href="/doc/1331149/" id="a_1">Article 227</a> of the
              Constitution of India.
</p><p id="p_8">                    On 1st April, 2014 the Court on the basis of the materials on
              record was satisfied that the signatures of Tafura Bibi in the two
              deeds are not same and identical and the Court was of the view
              that for fair adjudication of the dispute the specimen signatures
              are required to be examined with the admitted signatures and,
              accordingly, the signatures were sent to the Director, QDEB, DIG,
              CID, West Bengal, Bhabani Bhavan, Kolkata for examination. The
              order dated 1st April, 2014 was not contemporaneously challenged.
</p><p id="p_9">                    On 2nd February, 2015, although an opportunity was given
              to the plaintiffs/petitioners to place their defence but it appears
              that the plaintiffs were buying time before the Trial Court on a
              specious plea that the plaintiffs/petitioners would challenge the
              order dated 1st April, 2014 before this Court.
</p><p id="p_10">                     An innovative argument is made that since the order dated
              1st April, 2014 was not immediately implemented and it was by the
              subsequent order dated 2nd February, 2015 the signatures was
              sent to the Director for examination, the cause of action to
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                  2</span>


     challenge the order dated 1st April, 2014 arise only on 5th May,
     2015.
</p><p id="p_11">             This Court is unable to appreciate the said argument.
     Moreover, the Court is the best judge in this regard. No one can
     stifle the course of justice, as the Court is required to pronounce a
     judgment on production of all the evidence. The true picture must
     emerge before the Court so that the Court can pronounce a
     judgment on all issues fairly and effectively.
</p><p id="p_12">             Under such circumstances, this Court finds no reason to
     interfere with the impugned order.
</p><p id="p_13">             The revisional application is, accordingly, dismissed.
             However, there shall be no order as to costs.
</p>

<pre id="pre_2">ab                                        (Soumen Sen, J.)
