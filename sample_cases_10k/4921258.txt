<div class="doc_title">Sweety @ Savita Rana vs Anurag on 10 April, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                                       IN THE SUPREME COURT OF INDIA

                                         CIVIL ORIGINAL JURISDICTION

                             Transfer Petition(s)(Civil)          No(s).    509 of 2018

     SWEETY @ SAVITA RANA                                                        Petitioner(s)

                                                         VERSUS

     ANURAG                                                                      Respondent(s)

                                                   O R D E R
</pre>
<p id="p_1">                         We have heard learned counsel for the petitioner.
                         Without expressing any opinion on merits, we direct that
     proceedings in H.M.A. NO.1005 of 2013 titled “Anurag V. Sweety @
     Savita Rana” on the file of Principal Judge, Family Court at
     Fatehgarh, Farrukhabad, Uttar Pradesh, shall stand transferred to
     the Family Court at Jalandhar, Punjab, for hearing and disposal in
     accordance                 with   law.    Records    shall    be     sent   by   court   where
     proceedings are pending to the transferee court forthwith.                                  It
     will be open to the parties to seek clubbing of all the matters,
     if any, pending between them in accordance with law.
</p><p id="p_2">                         Learned counsel for the petitioner has made a statement that
     the petitioner will not object to video conferencing prayer of the
     respondent to the extent permissible under the law.
</p><p id="p_3">                         The Registry to transmit a copy of this order to the courts
     concerned.                Parties    to   appear    before     the    transferee    court   on
     Monday, the 14th May, 2018.
</p><p id="p_4">                         Since this order is being passed ex-parte, the respondent
     will be at liberty to move this Court, if aggrieved.
</p><p id="p_5">                         The transfer petition is, accordingly, allowed with the above
     direction.
</p>
