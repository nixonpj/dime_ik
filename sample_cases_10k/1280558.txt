             Dated this the 22nd day of May, 2008

                            JUDGMENT
</pre>

<p id="p_1">     The petitioner had filed a complaint before the Thiruvalla

Police alleging commission of offences punishable, inter alia,

under Sections 451 and 427 r/w. 149 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_1">I.P.C</a>. by a group of about

15 persons led by a named individual. That alleged incident took

place on 25.3.2008 and Ext.P1 complaint was lodged on the

same day. Ext.P2 F.I.R. was registered by the police on the same

day. Investigation is in progress.
</p>
<p id="p_2">     2. The petitioner is the Principal of the Mar Athanasious

College for Advanced Studies, Thiruvalla. He is not satisfied

with the investigation conducted by the police. According to

him, the offenders owe allegiance to SFI. They wield political

clout with the ruling political parties. Because of such clout

proper investigation is not conducted and the really guilty

persons are not brought on the array of accused.               In these

circumstances the petitioner filed a private complaint before the

W.P.C.No. 14097 of 2007
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                   2</span>

learned Magistrate while investigation into Ext.P2 crime was pending.

</p><p id="p_3">     3.    When the learned Magistrate received such a subsequent

complaint the learned Magistrate, rightly according to me, called for a

report from the S.H.O. as to the stage of investigation in Ext.P2 crime.

That report was received. Investigation, it was reported, was still in

progress. In these circumstances the learned Magistrate passed an

order of stay under <a href="/doc/788422/" id="a_2">Section 210</a> Cr.P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_4">     4. The petitioner claims to be aggrieved by the said order of stay

passed on 24.4.2008. What is the grievance? The learned Magistrate

appears to have followed the course which the law mandates that the

Magistrate should follow. I find no merit in the grievance raised

against the order dt. 24.4.08 staying further proceedings in the

complaint under <a href="/doc/788422/" id="a_3">Section 210</a> Cr.P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_5">     5. The learned counsel for the petitioner then submits that the

learned Magistrate must have issued appropriate further direction

under <a href="/doc/99487/" id="a_4">Section 156(3)</a> Cr.P.C. to the Investigating Officer as enjoined in

the decision in <a href="/doc/1836621/" id="a_5">Sakiri Vasu v. State of U.P</a>. (2008 (1) KLT 724)

(SC).
</p>
<p id="p_6">W.P.C.No. 14097 of 2007
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                    3</span>

</p><p id="p_7">      6. The petitioner had not requested the Magistrate for issue of

any direction to the Investigating Officer under <a href="/doc/99487/" id="a_6">Section 156(3)</a> Cr.P.C.

The petitioner instead had chosen to file a fresh complaint offering to

prove the case against the accused persons in respect of the incident

regarding which Ext.P2 crime was registered. Investigation was in

progress in that case.
</p>
<p id="p_8">      7. I am, in these circumstances, satisfied that the petitioner is not

entitled for any relief in this Writ Petition. If the petitioner has a

grievance that any proper steps are not being taken by the Investigating

Officer and direction under <a href="/doc/99487/" id="a_7">Section 156(3)</a> Cr.P.C. must be issued by

the learned Magistrate in accordance with the dictum in Sakiri Vasu,

it is certainly for the petitioner to approach the learned Magistrate

afresh with such a specific request and needless to say if such request/

application is received, the learned Magistrate shall consider the same

and pass appropriate orders thereon.
</p>
<p id="p_9">      8. With the above observations this Writ Petition is dismissed.



</p><p id="p_10">                                              (R. BASANT)
                                                   Judge
