<p id="p_1">(Per
: HONOURABLE THE ACTING CHIEF JUSTICE MR.BHASKAR BHATTACHARYA)
 


 


 


	When
this writ-application was taken up for hearing, Mr Sanjanwala,
learned advocate appearing on behalf of the writ-petitioners
submitted before us that pursuant to the order dated 6th
July 2011 his client has already made  representation before
respondent No.4, the Principal Secretary, Tourism Department for
taking into consideration his prayer. Mr Sanjanwala submits that till
today, the said representation has not been disposed of.
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_2">2.	Mr
Jani, learned Government Pleader appearing on behalf of the
State-respondents submits that if this Court passes direction for
disposal of the said representation by respondent No.4 and respondent
No.3, the Principal Secretary, Revenue Department, he has no
objection.
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_3">3.	In
view of such fact, we direct respondent Nos.3 and 4 to dispose of the
representation already given by the writ-petitioner before respondent
No.4 positively within four weeks from today by giving a reasoned
order and to communicate the decision to the petitioner within three
days thereafter.	If the petitioner is dissatisfied with such
decision, he would be free to challenge such decision in accordance
with law. 
</p>
 


 


 


<p id="p_4">4.	With
the above observations, this writ-application is disposed of. We make
it clear that we have otherwise not gone into the merits nor have we
considered the respective affidavit-in-reply and rejoinder.
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_5">	Direct
service is permitted.
</p> 


				
 

<p id="p_6">				(BHASKAR
BHATTACHARYA, ACTING CJ.)
 

 


 

 


 

				(J.B.
