                                            Date of Decision: August 13, 2018

Daler Singh
                                                                       ...Petitioner

State of Punjab

                                                                      ...Respondent

CORAM:- HON'BLE MS. JUSTICE JAISHREE THAKUR

Present:-     Mr. Satbir Gill, Advocate
              for the petitioner.

              Ms. Rajni Gupta, Senior DAG Punjab.

JAISHREE THAKUR, J. (Oral)
</pre>
<p id="p_1">              The instant second petition has been filed under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a>

Cr.P.C. for grant of regular bail to the petitioner in case FIR No.209 dated

12.10.2016, under <a href="/doc/92983/" id="a_2">Sections 306</a>, <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_3">406</a>, <a href="/doc/386021/" id="a_4">201</a>, <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_5">34</a> of Indian Penal Code,

registered at Police Station Patran, District Patiala.

</p><p id="p_2">              Learned counsel for the petitioner contends that the petitioner

herein was taken into custody in the aforesaid FIR on 16.10.2016. It is

submitted that the petitioner has been falsely implicated in the present case.

It is also contended that out of total 18 witnesses, material witness i.e. the

complainant has been examined and conclusion of trial will take sufficient

time, therefore, the petitioner is entitled to be enlarged on bail.

</p><p id="p_3">              Per contra, learned counsel appearing on behalf of respondent-

State, on instructions from the Investigating Officer, opposes the grant of

</p>
<p id="p_4">              I have heard learned counsel for the parties.
</p>
<p id="p_5">              Since, the trial is likely to take some time and in view of the

facts that the petitioner herein has been in custody since 16.10.2016 and that

the material witness i.e. the complainant has been examined, no useful

purpose would be served in keeping the petitioner behind bars. At this

stage, without commenting on the merits of the case, the instant petition is

allowed and the petitioner is directed to be released on regular bail on

execution of adequate personal bond and surety bond to the satisfaction of

concerned trial Court/Duty Magistrate.</p><pre id="pre_2">
