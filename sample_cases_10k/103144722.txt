<div class="doc_title">K.Andal vs The Revenue Divisional Officer on 4 October, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">        

 

BEFORE THE MADURAI BENCH OF MADRAS HIGH COURT               

DATED: 04.10.2017  

CORAM   

THE HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE R.MAHADEVAN             

W.P.(MD)No.6815 of 2017  

K.Andal                                                           ... Petitioner

Vs.

1.The Revenue Divisional Officer,
   The Revenue Divisional Office,
   Sivakasi,
   Virudhunagar District.

2.The Tahsildar,
   Sivakasi Taluk,
   Virudhunagar District.                                       ... Respondents

PRAYER:Writ Petition filed under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the Constitution of India to
issue a Writ of Mandamus directing the first respondent to consider the
petitioner's representation dated 23.01.2017, within the stipulated period.

!For Petitioner : Mr.K.Rajeshwaran 

For Respondents : Mr.T.S.Mohamed Mohideen,           
                                                  Additional Government Pleader



:ORDER  
</pre><p id="p_1">                The writ petition has been filed by the petitioner seeking a Writ
of Mandamus directing the first respondent to consider her representation
dated 23.01.2017.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                2. According to the petitioner, she had purchased the following
lands :
</p><p id="p_3">i)Survey No.83, Vadamalapuram Village, Sivakasi Taluk, Virudhunagar District,
measuring 44 cents, vide sale deed in Doc.No.3223/2011 
</p><p id="p_4">ii)Survey No.81/1A, Vadamalapuram Village, Sivakasi Taluk, Virudhunagar
District, measuring 605.76 sq.meter, each, vide sale deeds in Doc.Nos.3224 to
3227/2011 
</p><p id="p_5">iii)Survey No.81/4, Vadamalapuram Village, Sivakasi Taluk, Virudhunagar
District, measuring 6540 sq.feet, vide sale deed in Doc.No.4842/2011
        admeasuring to a total extent of 1 acre 21 cents and according to her
there is neither civil suit nor rival claim whatsoever pending in respect of
the properties in question and as such, she is enjoying possession over the
same. Therefore, she has submitted a representation dated 23.01.2017, to the
first respondent seeking patta in her name, in respect of the aforesaid
properties and as the same is kept pending, without any action, the
petitioner is before this Court.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                3. Heard the learned Counsel appearing for the petitioner as well
as the learned Additional Government Pleader appearing for the respondents.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                4. Considering the facts and circumstances of the case and also
taking into account the limited scope of the relief sought for by the
petitioner, this Court, without going into the merits of the petitioner's
claim, directs the first respondent to consider the representation of the
petitioner dated 23.01.2017 and pass appropriate orders, on its own merit and
in accordance with law, after affording due opportunity of hearing to the
petitioner as well as all the interested parties, if any, within a period of
six weeks from the date of receipt of a copy of this order.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                5. With the above direction, this Writ Petition is disposed of.
No costs.
</p>
                                                                
<p id="p_9">To

</p><p id="p_10">1.The Revenue Divisional Officer,
   The Revenue Divisional Office,
   Sivakasi,
   Virudhunagar District.
</p>
<p id="p_11">2.The Tahsildar,
   Sivakasi Taluk,
   Virudhunagar District.
</p>
