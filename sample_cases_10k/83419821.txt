<div class="doc_title">Sarita Batura vs Navin Shah on 12 January, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                                ORDER SHEET
                      IN THE HIGH COURT AT CALCUTTA
                       Ordinary Original Civil Jurisdiction
                                ORIGINAL SIDE


                              EC No.464 of 2014

                                In the matter of:
                                SARITA BATURA
                                     Versus
                                  NAVIN SHAH

                                      AND

                              EC No.465 of 2014

                             In the matter of:
                             SARITA BATURA
                                  Versus
                         DOLPHIN EDI-OIL PVT. LTD.



  BEFORE:
  The Hon'ble JUSTICE SOUMEN SEN</pre>
<p id="p_1">  Date : 12th January, 2018.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">                                                                  Appearance:
</p><p id="p_4">                                          Mr. Pratik Kumar Bhattacharyya, Adv.
</p>
<p id="p_5">
      The Court: Mr. Navin Shah is produced from custody. Mr. Saha has filed

an affidavit in which he has stated that he would ensure that a sum of Rs.10

lakhs will be paid to the decree holder by the adjourned date.    The judgment

debtor has also disclosed the names of the persons at paragraph 13 of the said

affidavit from whom the judgment debtor expects to realize amounts out of which

he proposes to pay the decretal dues. The matter is adjourned till 2nd February,

2018. The decree holder shall accept the said amount without prejudice to its
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                         2</span>


rights and contentions and adjust the said amount for the time being towards the

principal. The judgment debtor has assured this Court through his counsel that

he shall be present on the adjourned date for further examination.

</p><p id="p_6">      On the basis of the aforesaid the judgment debtor is released from the

custody of the Court. The judgment debtor shall deposit the passport in original

with the Registrar, High Court Original Side within a week from date.       The

judgment debtor volunteers to deposit the said passport in Court so as to dispel

any apprehension of the decree holder that the judgment debtor may flee the

country.
</p>
<p id="p_7">      The judgment debtor shall be present in Court on 2nd February, 2018 at 2

p.m. for further examination.
</p>
<p id="p_8">      A copy of the affidavit filed by Navin Shah in Court today is also served

upon the learned Counsel representing the decree holder in Court.




</p><p id="p_9">                                            (SOUMEN SEN, J.)


sp/
