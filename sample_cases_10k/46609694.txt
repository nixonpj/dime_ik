<div class="doc_title">Ram Sevak vs District Collector on 5 November, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                                WP-2590-2015
                        (RAM SEVAK Vs DISTRICT COLLECTOR)


05-11-2015

Shri Mahesh Goyal, Advocate for the petitioner.

Shri Amit Bansal, Dy. Govt. Advocate for the respondents/State.</pre>

<p id="p_1">Parties are heard on I.A.No.4729/2015.
</p>
<p id="p_2">By filing this application, it is urged that the respondents have requested the

State Government for releasing the fund. As soon as the funds are received,

they will pay it to the petitioner without any further delay.

In the opinion of this Court, the application is misconceived. If right and

liabilities of the parties are crystallized by a judicial order, the Collector is only

obliged to implement it. He cannot take different stand. He has ample power to

recover the said amount mentioned in the RRC. Thus, this I.A. is rejected. The

respondent No.1 is directed to implement the RRC and submit the compliance

report by the next date of hearing. If there exists any legal impediment in

implementing the RRC, a detailed return be filed by the next date of hearing.

</p><p id="p_3">                     th
List this matter on 9 December, 2015.
</p>
<p id="p_4">If the RRC is not implemented or reply is not filed, the Collector shall remain

present before this Court on the next date of hearing.

A typed copy of the order be given to AG Office.
</p>
<p id="p_5">
</p><p id="p_6">                                                                   (SUJOY PAUL)
                                                                       JUDGE
