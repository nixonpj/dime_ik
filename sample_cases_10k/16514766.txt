                                        IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT MADRAS

                                                        DATED : 24.04.2019

                                                              CORAM

                                          THE HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE R.SUBBIAH
                                                         and
                                     THE HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE KRISHNAN RAMASAMY

                                                 Writ Petition No. 11572 of 2019

                    Balamurugan                                                            ...Petitioner

                                                               Vs
                    1.The Revenue Divisional Officer
                      O/o.Revenue Divisional Office
                      Chidambaram, Cuddalore District.

                    2.The Tahsildar
                      O/o.Tahsildar Office
                      Kattumannarkoil Taluk
                      Cuddalore District.

                    3.The Inspector of Police
                      Kattumannarkoil Police Station
                      Kattumannarkoil, Cuddalore District.                              ... Respondents

                           Writ Petition filed under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of The Constitution of India praying
                    for the issuance of a Writ of Mandamus directing the first respondent to
                    release the vehicle namely TATA ACE bearing Registration No.TN 61 1850
                    seized by the 3rd respondent to the petitioner.
                                      For Petitioner      :     Mr.G.Pugazhenthi
                                      For Respondents     :     Mr.J.Pothiraj
                                                                Special Government Pleader

                                                          ORDER
</pre><p id="p_1">                                             [Order was delivered by R.SUBBIAH,J]

                               The petitioner has filed the above Writ Petition praying for issuance of a

http://www.judis.nic.inWrit   of Mandamus, directing the first respondent to release the vehicle namely
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                              2</span>

                      TATA ACE bearing Registration No.TN 61 1850 seized by the 3rd respondent to

                      the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_2">


</p><p id="p_3">                            2. Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned Special

                      Government Pleader for the respondents.




</p><p id="p_4">                            3. According to the petitioner, the respondents have seized the vehicle

                      in question on 21.12.2018 on the ground of illegal carrying of river sand and till

                      date, no order for release of the said vehicle had been passed by the

                      respondents. Hence, he has come forward with the present Writ Petition.




</p><p id="p_5">                            4. On the other hand, it is submitted by learned Special Government

                      Pleader for the respondents that the vehicle in question was used for illegal

                      transportation of mines and minerals like river sand and hence, the vehicle was

                      seized. He further submitted that the petitioner has no previous case.




</p><p id="p_6">                            5. In any event, as the vehicle is under the custody of the respondents

                      from the date of seizure and considering the fact that if the same is allowed to

                      be kept idle by exposing the same to rain and shine, it would certainly diminish

                      their value, this Court is of the view that the vehicle in question may be

                      released by imposing conditions on the petitioner.

http://www.judis.nic.in
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                               3</span>

</p><p id="p_7">                             6. Accordingly, the respondents are directed to release the vehicle in

                      question to the petitioner within a period of 7 days from the date of

                      compliance of the below mentioned condition Nos.(i) to (iii):

</p><p id="p_8">                      (i) The petitioner shall deposit a sum of Rs.10,000/- (Rupees Ten Thousand

                          only) before the jurisdictional Tahsildar concerned as non-refundable

                          deposit. After receipt of the above said amount, the same will have to be

                          deposited by the jurisdictional Tahsildar concerned, to the credit of the

                          District Mines and Minerals Foundation Trust as non-refundable deposit.

</p><p id="p_9">                      (ii) The petitioner shall execute a personal bond for a sum of Rs.10,000/-

</p><p id="p_10">                          (Rupees ten thousand only) with two sureties each for a like sum to the

                          satisfaction of the concerned jurisdictional Judicial Magistrate.

</p><p id="p_11">                      (iii)The petitioner shall give an undertaking before the respondents/authority

                          concerned stating that he will not use the vehicle in question for any illegal

                          activities in future and shall produce the same as and when required by the

                          respondents and also the trial Court, failing which the respondents/trial

                          Court is/are at liberty to confiscate the vehicle.

</p><p id="p_12">                      (iv)The petitioner shall not alienate the vehicle in question till the disposal of

                          the proceedings before the authority concerned.

</p><p id="p_13">                      (v) The petitioner is also directed to participate in the enquiry to be conducted

                          by the respondents.
</p>
<p id="p_14">

http://www.judis.nic.in
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                                                               4</span>

                                                                                    R.SUBBIAH, J
                                                                                            AND
                                                                           KRISHNAN RAMASAMY, J
                                                                                             maya



</p><p id="p_15">                               7.With the above observations and directions, this Writ Petition is

                      disposed of. No costs.
</p>
