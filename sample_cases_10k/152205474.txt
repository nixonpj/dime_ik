                        ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Submission of learned HCGP that an appeal lies as

against impugned order at Annexure J is sound. The

decision of respondent No.2 vide Annexure J can be

questioned in an appeal U/S. 49 of Karnataka Land

Revenue    Act    before   the   jurisdictional   Assistant

Commissioner. Unless statuary remedies are exhausted,

writ petition can not be entertained


      In the said view of the matter, writ petition is

disposed of, reserving liberty to the petitioner to question

Annexure J, by filing an appeal U/S. 49 of Karnataka
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                 3</span>




Land    Revenue    Act   before     Jurisdictional   Assistant

Commissioner.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">       If an appeal is preferred before 28.7.2012, time

spent in prosecuting this petition i.e., from 27.1.2010 till

today shall stand excluded upon an application seeking

condonation of delay is filed before the appellate authority.


</p><p id="p_4">       Interim order passed on 19.4.2010 will enure to the

benefit of petitioner till 28.7.2012 or the date of filing of

appeal, whichever is earlier.
</p>
<p id="p_5">       Contentions of both parties are kept open for

consideration by the appellate authority.
