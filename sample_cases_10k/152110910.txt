<div class="doc_title">Dr. Parsuram Singh vs Veer Kunwar Singh University &amp; Ors on 27 March, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                   IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                Civil Writ Jurisdiction Case No.19893 of 2016
                 ======================================================
                 Dr. Parsuram Singh Son of late Basudeo Singh Resident of Company Sarai,
                 Takiya Gumti, P.S. Sasaram Town, District Sasaram now retired from
                 service w.e.f. 31.7.2014 from Shankar College, Takiya, P.S. Sasaram,
                 District- Sasaram.
                                                                       .... .... Petitioner/s
                                                    Versus
                 1. Veer Kunwar Singh University, Ara (Bihar) through its Registrar.
                 2. Vice Chancellor, Veer Kunwar Singh University, Ara, (Bihar).
                 3. The Registrar, Veer Kunwar Singh University, Ara(Bihar).
                 4. The Finance Advisor, Veer Kunwar Singh University, Ara (Bihar).
                                                                      .... .... Respondent/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s      :
                 For the Respondent/s      :
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE AHSANUDDIN AMANULLAH
                                                ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">8   27-03-2018                 Learned counsel for the petitioner and Veer Kunwar

                 Singh University are present (hereinafter referred to as the

                 'University').
</p>
<p id="p_2">                               2. On 12.03.2018, the Court had adjourned the matter

                 on prayer made by learned counsel for the University for taking

                 instructions with regard to payment of arrears of Group Insurance

                 of the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                               3. Supplementary counter affidavit has been filed on

                 behalf of the University on 22.03.2018 which has been affirmed

                 by the Registrar of the University on 20.03.2018. In the said

                 affidavit, at paragraph no. 5, it has been stated that Group

                 Insurance amount of Rs. 96095/- has been found admissible to the

                 petitioner and letter dated 24.02.2018 has been issued by the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">            Patna High Court CWJC No.19893 of 2016 (8) dt.27-03-2018</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                     2/2</span>




                        University to the Finance Officer of the University.

</p><p id="p_4">                                        4. The Court finds such statement to be peculiar and

                        this is not the only case in which such totally vague statements

                        have been made, either inadvertently or may be deliberately to

                        mislead the Court. The University has to ensure that the actual

                        payment is made to the persons concerned. It cannot take a stand

                        that one officer has written a letter to another officer as it does not

                        satisfy the requirement of actual payment made to the party

                        concerned. Moreover, the letter is dated 24.02.2018, whereas the

                        affidavit has been affirmed on 20.03.2018, i.e., of after almost a

                        month of issuance of the said letter, but there being no statement

                        as to whether actual payment has been made, is unfortunate and

                        also unacceptable.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                        5. As this is not the first instance, the Court is

                        constrained to direct for the personal appearance of respondents

                        no. 2, 3 and 4 to explain such conduct as to why they have not

                        been properly sending instructions to assist the Court and also

                        ensuring actual payment to the petitioner and by stating such fact

                        on their affidavits, when the matter shall be listed under the

                        heading 'For Orders' at 10:30 A.M. on 11th April, 2018.


</p><p id="p_6">                                                           (Ahsanuddin Amanullah, J)
Anjani/-
</p><p id="p_7"> U
