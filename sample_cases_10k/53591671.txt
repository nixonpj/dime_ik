<div class="doc_title">Navdeep Gupta &amp; Anr. vs State Of J&amp;K; on 28 August, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">             HIGH COURT OF JAMMU AND KASHMIR
                        AT JAMMU

561-A <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_1">Cr.P.C</a> No. 394/2017
MP No. 01/2017
                                                  Date of order: 28.08.2017
Navdeep Gupta &amp; anr.              V.               State of J&amp;K
___________________________________________________________
Coram:
           Hon'ble Mr. Justice Janak Raj Kotwal, Judge.
Appearing Counsel:
For the petitioner(s)        : Mr. Jasbir Singh Jasrotia, Advocate.
For the respondent(s)        : Mr.S.S.Nanda, Sr. AAG.</pre>
<p id="p_1">       Mr. S.S.Nanda, learned Senior Additional Advocate General,
passes on the status report in regard to the investigation in the case, which
is taken on record. Learned counsel for the petitioners states that copy of
the same has already been provided to him.
</p>
<p id="p_2">       Heard.
</p>
<p id="p_3">       In seeking quashing of FIR No. 139/2017 of Police Station,
Satwari under Sections 420/34 RPC, learned counsel for the petitioners
submits that petitioners have been falsely implicated inasmuch as one of
the three accused, named in the FIR, namely, Rajesh Upal has died much
prior to the filing of the FIR. On reading the FIR, I am not persuaded to
take a view that the same does not prima facie disclose commission of
cognizable offence. The informant has given detailed account as to how
he was made to deliver material (cold drinks) to the accused persons and
bogus cheques were later on issued to him. Given the statement contained
in the FIR, it cannot be said that no case for investigation is made out.
The status report submitted on behalf of the respondents would show that
respondents ( accused), Navdeep Gupta and Rahul Thakur, are not



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">561-A <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_2"><a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_3">Cr.P.C</a></a>. No. 394/2017                                        Page 1 of 2</span>
 cooperating in the investigation after they were released on bail by the
court.
</p>
<p id="p_4">         For the aforementioned, no sufficient ground for showing
indulgence in terms of <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_4">Section 561-A</a> Cr.P.C. is made out. Whether one
of the accused persons had died prior to lodging of the FIR is a question
of fact which can be raised before the Investigating Officer and, if raised,
should be investigated.
</p><p id="p_5">         Viewed thus, this petition is dismissed.
</p>

<p id="p_6">                                                    (Janak Raj Kotwal)
                                                             Judge
Jammu
28.08.2017
Pawan Chopra
