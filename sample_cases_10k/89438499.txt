<div class="doc_title">Samir Kumar Banerjee vs The State Of West Bengal And Others on 20 December, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                         1

S/L. 20.
December 20, 2016.
MNS.

                                               W. P. No. 29409(w) of 2016

                                                Samir Kumar Banerjee
                                                          Vs.
                                          The State of West Bengal and others


  Mr. Chittapriya Ghosh

                 ...for the petitioner.

  Ms. Anjusri Mukherjee,
  Ms. Susmita Biswas Chowdhury

                 ...for the State-respondents.</pre>


<p id="p_1">         Affidavit-of-service filed in Court today is taken on record.
</p><p id="p_2">         Heard the learned counsel for the petitioner as well as the learned counsel for the State-respondents.
         The petitioner was appointed as Assistant Teacher in Garfa Dhirendranath Memorial High School(H.S.)
  and the appointment of the petitioner was duly approved by the respondent no. 5 with effect from March 17,
  1986 and he discharged his duties till August 2, 2013. Subsequently, as he was selected as Headmaster has
  immediately tendered his resignation and reported his duties as Headmaster on August 3, 2013 but the PF
  amount was not released in time which was only released on November 19, 2016 and as such the petitioner
  had prayed for payment of interest for the aforesaid delayed period by means of an application. The said
  application was rejected vide order dated September 20, 2016 whereby it was observed as follows:-
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">                 "Since the prevailing practice is to allow such disbursement including interest by calculating it
         upto the date of resignation only, not upto the month of actual disbursement is taking place, the present
         case as been processed accordingly.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">                 As per verbal discussion with authorities at Pay &amp; Accounts we cannot allow such interest. Now
         an advice from your end is urgently requested to deal such cases."</blockquote>
<p id="p_3">         In view of the liability of the PF department, this Court is of the opinion that the
  petitioner is entitled for payment of interest on delayed payment of PF amount.
</p><p id="p_4">         Therefore, the order dated September 20, 2016 is hereby quashed.
         Accordingly, this Court directs the concerned respondent no. 5 for payment of interest
  at the rate of 9% per annum from the date of accrual till the date of release within a period of
  three months from the date of furnishing certified copy of this order after verification of the
  relevant documents and the said interest shall be recovered by the respondent no. 2, that is,
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                         2</span>


the Director of Pension, Provident Fund and Group Insurance, West Bengal, after issuing
show cause notice to the officer concerned.
</p><p id="p_5">        With the aforesaid observations, the writ petition is allowed.
        I make no order as to costs.
</p><p id="p_6">        The Registry is directed to supply photostat certified copy of this order to the applicant, if applied for, on
urgent basis.
</p>



<p id="p_7">                                  (Rajiv Sharma, J.)
