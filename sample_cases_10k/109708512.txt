                 ======================================================
                 1. Allahabad Bank through its Zonal Manager, Muzaffarpur Zone, Om
                    Shanti Complex, Zila School, Main Road, Muzaffarpur
                 2. Zonal Manager, Allahabad Bank, Muzaffarpur Zone, Om Shanti
                    Complex, Zila School, Main Road, Muzaffarpur
                 3. Branch Manager, Allahabad Bank, Kanhauli Branch, Gaushala Road,
                    Muzaffarpur
                                                                    .... .... Petitioner/s
                                                 Versus

                 1. The State of Bihar through its Principal Secretary, Revenue Department
                 2. Commissioner, Tirhut Division, Muzaffarpur
                 3. Collector cum District Magistrate, Muzaffarpur
                 4. Sub Divisional Officer, East Muzaffarpur
                 5. Shyam Nandan Tiwary alias Munna Tiwary, son of Late Jagdish
                    Tiwary, resident of Mithanpura, P.S. Mithanpura, District-Muzaffarpur
                                                                        .... .... Respondent/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance:
                 For the Petitioner/s    :   Mr. Rajan Ghoshrave
                 For the Respondent/s      : Mr. Sajid Salim Khan,SC-25
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE JYOTI SARAN
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   30-09-2016                    Heard Mr. Rajan Ghoshrave, learned counsel for the

                 petitioner and Mr. Sajid Salim Khan, learned Standing Counsel-25

                 for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                  The Bank is before this Court being aggrieved by

                 fixation of fair rent. The claim of the private respondent raised for

                 fixation of fair rent under the provisions of Section 5 of the Bihar

                 Buildings (Lease, Rent and Eviction) Control Act, 1982 has been

                 upheld not only by the House Controller but the opinion has been

                 affirmed by the Collector in Appeal as well as by the revisional

                 authority.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">          Patna High Court CWJC No.16202 of 2016 (2) dt.30-09-2016                         2</span><p id="p_3">




</p><p id="p_4">                                      There being a concurrent finding of fact as regarding

                      the fixation of rent, I am not persuaded with the argument of Mr.

                      Ghoshrave to interfere with the same. The petitioner as a State

                      within the meaning of <a href="/doc/609139/" id="a_1">Article 12</a> should not be engaging itself in

                      frivolous litigation once there is a concurrent finding on facts and

                      the opinion stands confirmed by the statutory authorities at each

                      stage of the proceedings.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                      The writ petition is dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">                                                      (Jyoti Saran, J)
S.Sb/-
</p>
