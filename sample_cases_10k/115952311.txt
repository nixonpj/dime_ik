<div class="doc_title">Sarabjit Kaur And Another vs State Of Punjab And Others on 11 August, 2011</div><pre id="pre_1">IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA AT
              CHANDIGARH



                             Crl.Misc.No.M-24357 of 2011 (O&amp;M)

                              Date of decision : 11.8.2011


Sarabjit Kaur and another

                                                ....Petitioners
              Versus


State of Punjab and others

                                                ...Respondents


CORAM : HON'BLE MR.JUSTICE MAHESH GROVER
                  ....

Present : Mr.R.M.Sharma, Advocate
          for the petitioners.
                         .....

MAHESH GROVER, J.
</pre>
<p id="p_1">           Crl.Misc.No.42912 of 2011 is allowed.
</p>
<p id="p_2">           This is a petition under        <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section   482</a> of the Code

of Criminal Procedure praying for directions to respondents No.1 to 3

to protect their life and liberty which is alleged to be in danger at the

hands of respondent No.4 on account of their having got married

against their parental consent.
</p>
<p id="p_3">           Learned counsel for the petitioners contends that both the

petitioners are major.
</p>
<p id="p_4">           Even though this court is disinclined to entertain and to go

into such allegations, but at the same time it cannot be oblivious to

the fact that because of social friction and sectarian differences such

incidents are not entirely unheard of and prima facie the case also
 Crl.Misc.No.M-24357 of 2011 (O&amp;M)                 -2-
</p>
<p id="p_5">appears to be covered by the observations of Supreme Court in Fiaz

Ahmed Ahanger &amp; Ors. v. State of J &amp; K 2009(3) R.A.J.692, which

are as under :-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">                  "In such cases of intercaste or inter-religion marriage

           the Court has only to be satisfied about two things :

</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">                   (1) that the girl is above 18 years of age, in which

                   case, the law regards her as a major vide <a href="/doc/153138729/" id="a_2">Section 3</a>

                   of the Indian Majority Act, 1875. A major is

                   deemed by the law to know what is in his or her

                   welfare.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_3">                   (2) The wish of the girl.</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_4">

</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_5">                   In the circumstances, we direct that nobody will

           harass, threaten or commit any acts of violence or other

           unlawful act on the petitioner, Chanchali Devi/Mehvesh

           Anjum and the petitioner's family members and they shall

           not be arrested till further orders in connection with the

           case in question. If they feel insecure, they can apply to

           the police and, in such event, the police shall grant

           protection to them."</blockquote><p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">            In view of this, the petition is disposed of with a direction

to respondent No.2 to look into the allegations as contained in the

petition personally and take necessary steps in accordance with law if

the situation so warrants.
</p>
<p id="p_8">           This order shall not be construed to be conferring the

legitimacy or authenticity to the factum of marriage having been
 Crl.Misc.No.M-24357 of 2011 (O&amp;M)              -3-
</p>
<p id="p_9">performed as well as the age, as the Court is clearly deprived of any

means to determine the aforesaid facts.
</p>
<p id="p_10">        Copy of the petition along with a copy of this order be sent

to respondent No.2.
</p>

                                              JUDGE
