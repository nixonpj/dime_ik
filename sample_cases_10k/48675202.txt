<div class="doc_title">Bheema @ Yogendra Singh vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 13 August, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                                 1                              CRA-4648-2018
       The High Court Of Madhya Pradesh
                  CRA-4648-2018
           (BHEEMA @ YOGENDRA SINGH Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)


Gwalior, Dated : 13-08-2018
       Shri Kuldeep Thapak, leanred Counsel for the appellant.
       Shri BPS Chauhan, learned Public Prosecutor for the
respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">       None for the complainant.
</p><p id="p_2">       It is submitted by the Counsel for the State that the complainant
has been informed about the pendency of this appeal as required under
<a href="/doc/50707649/" id="a_1">Section 15-A</a> of Scheduled Caste and <a href="/doc/25085007/" id="a_2">Scheduled Tribes (Prevention
of Atrocities) Act</a>, 1989.
</p><p id="p_3">       Case Diary is available.
</p><p id="p_4">       This first Criminal Appeal for grant of bail has been filed under
<a href="/doc/50707649/" id="a_3">Section 14A(2)</a> of Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes
(Prevention of Atrocities), Act, 1989 (in short Act, 1989) against the
order dated 19.06.2018 passed by Special Judge, Atrocities, Bhind
wherein in Bail Application No. 1023/2018 by which the application
filed by the appellant for grant of bail has been rejected.
</p><p id="p_5">       The appellant has been arrested on 16.02.2018 in connection
with Crime No.10/2018 registered at Police Station Andori, District
Bhind for offence punishable under <a href="/doc/42436223/" id="a_4">Sections 3(1)(w)(i)</a>, <a href="/doc/42436223/" id="a_5">3(2)(v)</a>, <a href="/doc/42436223/" id="a_6">3(2)</a>
(va) of the SCST (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989.
</p><p id="p_6">       Learned counsel for the appellant points out that the prosecutrix
has turned hostile. She has not supported the case of the prosecution.
</p><p id="p_7">       Learned Public Prosecutor Shri Chauhan submits that
complainant had herself given the complaint in her own handwriting
and had given statement under <a href="/doc/447673/" id="a_7">Section 161</a> of Cr.P.C.
</p><p id="p_8">       In view of such facts by granting bail to the appellant as the
prosecutrix has turned hostile, the appeal is allowed. It is directed
that the appellant be released on bail on furnishing a personal bond in
the sum of Rs. 50,000/- (Rs. Fifty Thousand Only) with two
sureties in the like amount to the satisfaction of the Trial
Court/Committal Court to appear before the Court on the dates given
by the concerned Court.
</p><p id="p_9">       This order shall remain effective till the end of the trial but in
case of bail jump, it shall become ineffective.
</p><p id="p_10">                                                2                            CRA-4648-2018
                       It is directed that State shall be at liberty to recover any amount
                of compensation paid to the prosecutrix or to her guardian and also
                initiate action as may be prescribed under law for lodging a false
                report.
</p><p id="p_11">                       Let copy of action taken against the prosecutrix/guardian be
                filed before the Principal Registrar of this Court within 30 days by
                Shri BPS Chauhan, learned Public prosecutor, who shall place it for
                perusal of this Court in Chambers.
</p><p id="p_12">                      Certified copy as per rules.
</p>
<p id="p_13">                                                                   (VIVEK AGARWAL)
                                                                        JUDGE


                mani


SUBASRI MANI
