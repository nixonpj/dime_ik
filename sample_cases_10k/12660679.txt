Judgment on compromise
  1.</pre><p id="p_1"> The plaintiff has sought recovery of a sum of Rs.20,46,016/­ along

      with   interest   @   18%   p.a.   against   the   defendant   in   respect   of   the

      goods supplied by the plaintiff to the defendant. 

</p><p id="p_2">   2. The plaintiff is stated to be engaged in the business of Lumbricating

      oil   and   its   allied   products.   Defendant   was   alleged   to   be   having

      business   dealings   with   the   plaintiff,   and   used   to   place   order   for

      supply of lubricating oil and Hyd Oil Tellus, Shell Tellus, etc to the

      plaintiff,   and   the   same   used   to   be   dispatched   by   the   plaintiff

      company to the defendant at the site office of the defendant  at Sainj

      Hydro Electric Project, Central Stores Kartha, Village Kartha, Tehsil

      Sainj, District Kullu, Himachal Pradesh.

</p><p id="p_3">   3. As per ledger account maintained by the plaintiff in due course of


   CS No.2360/17                                                            page 2 of 5
    business, a sum of Rs.11,89,544.40/­ along with interest @ 24% pa,

   was stated to be due against the defendant. 

</p><p id="p_4">4. Plaintiff   stated   to   have   requested   the   defendant   to   clear   all   the

   outstanding dues, but the defendant failed.  As such, plaintiff issued

   legal notice dated 30.06.2017, but to no avail. 

</p><p id="p_5">5. Ultimately, the plaintiff has filed the present suit.

</p><p id="p_6">6. Defendant has filed Written Statement, wherein he has mainly raised

   the plea of lack of territorial jurisdiction. Defendant also disputed the

   amount claimed by the plaintiff, alleging that, there are certain failed

   invoices   filed   by   the   plaintiff.   Defendant   also   raised   the   plea   of

   inferior quality of goods. Defendant sought dismissal of the suit with

   exemplary costs.
</p>
<p id="p_7">7. Replication has been filed on behalf of the plaintiff to the Written

   Statement of the defendant, wherein it has denied the allegations of

   the defendant, and reiterated and reaffirmed the averments as made

   in the plaint.
</p>
<p id="p_8">CS No.2360/17                                                            page 3 of 5
</p><p id="p_9"> 8. Matter was listed for admission/denial of documents and framing of

   the issues. However, in between the parties have amicably settled the

   dispute,   and   prayed   for   passing   of   compromise   decree,   and

   accordingly,  statements of both the parties were recorded separately.

</p><p id="p_10">9. In view of the settlement arrived at between the parties and their

   statements,   the   suit   is   disposed   of   as   compromised   with   the

   following terms:­

   (1)The   defendant   shall   pay  a   total   sum   of   Rs.11,30,067/­   to   the

   plaintiff   towards  full  and   final  settlement   of  the  entire  dispute   in

   respect of the present suit, by way of four EMIs of Rs.2,82,517/­

   each,   payable   on   the   first   week   of   each   month,   starting   from

   February, 2018 and the last one being payable on the first week of

   May, 2018.
</p>
<p id="p_11">   (2)Defendant shall not commit any breach of his undertaking.

   (3)In case of breach of   undertaking by the defendant, he shall be

   liable     to   pay   penal   interest   @   18%   per   annum   on   the   entire

CS No.2360/17                                                         page 4 of 5
       outstanding   amount   to   the   plaintiff   with   effect   from   the   date   of

      breach,   and   the   decree   shall   be   executable   at   once   against   the

      defendant qua the entire outstanding as on the date of breach till

      realization of agreed amount.
</p>
<p id="p_12">      (4)Parties shall abide by their undertakings/statement given before

      this court today.
</p>
<p id="p_13">      Compromise decree be prepared accordingly.

      File be consigned to record room. 
</p>
