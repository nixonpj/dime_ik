<div class="doc_title">Mahesh Kumar Goyal vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 16 July, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                                    1                   WP 13325/18

                                               THE HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH
                                                     Writ Petition No. 13325/18
                                           Mahesh Kumar Goyal Vs. State of M.P. &amp; others
                                     Gwalior Dt. 16/7/2018
                                           Shri Estakar Qureshi, Advocate for the petitioner.
                                           Shri Yogesh Chaturvedi, Govt. Advocate for the State.
                                           Learned counsel for the petitioner in the present petition
                                     seeks direction for registration of cognizable offence which has not
                                     been done by the police despite being informed by the petitioner.
                                           The controversy involved in the present case stands settled
                                     by the decision of this court in the case of Sweta Bhadoriya Vs.
                                     State of M.P. &amp; others reported in 2017(1) MPLJ (Cri) 338
                                     which has recently been followed by the Full Bench of this court
                                     with approval.
                                           In view of the above and the foundational case rendered by
                                     the Apex Court in the case of Aleque Padamsee and others Vs.
                                     Union of India &amp; others (2007) 6 SCC 171 which has
                                     categorically laid down that no mandamus can be issued to the
                                     police authorities compelling them to register an offence especially
                                     in view of availability of statutory remedy u/Ss. 156(3), 190 and
                                     200 <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_1">Cr.P.C</a> this court declines interference and leaves the
                                     petitioner to avail such remedy u/Ss. 156(3), 190 or 200 <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_2">Cr.P.C</a>. ,
                                     as the case may be.
                                           No cost.
                                                                                (Sheel Nagu)
                                                                                   Judge
                              (Bu)

DHANANJAYA BUCHAKE
2018.07.16</pre><p id="p_1"> 17:56:39 +05'30'
