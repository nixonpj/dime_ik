<div class="doc_title">Santosh Kumar Singh @ Bablu &amp;Amp; ... vs The State Of Bihar on 20 August, 2010</div><pre id="pre_1">                  IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                 Cr.Misc. No.25805 of 2010
1.   SANTOSH KUMAR SINGH @ BABLU, son of Nageshwar Singh.
2.   Nageshwar Singh, son of late Sheo Prakash
     All resident of village - Sikhar, P.S. - Manpur, District - Gaya.
                                                                         ----------- Petitioners.
                                           Versus
THE STATE OF BIHAR                                                ------------ Opposite Party.
                                            -------
</pre>
<p id="p_1"> 02.     20.08.2010               Heard learned counsel for the two petitioners and

                        learned Additional Public Prosecutor for the State.

</p><p id="p_2">                                    Petitioners are named accused in this case carry

                        allegation that the marriage of petitioner no.1 was settled with

                        daughter of the complainant informant, but due to some

                        exorbitant demand negotiation failed in spite of part payment.

                        Police after investigation submitted final form but taking on the

                        protest complaint, cognizance was taken by the court below.

</p><p id="p_3">                                    Considering the facts and circumstances of the case,

                        in the event of their arrest/surrender before the court below

                        within four weeks of receipt of the copy of this order, petitioner,

                        namely, (1) Santosh Kumar Singh @ Bablu and (2) Nageshwar

                        Singh, are directed to be released on bail on furnishing bail

                        bond of Rs. 10,000/- (ten thousand only) each with two sureties

                        of the like amount each to the satisfaction of Sub-Divisional

                        Judicial Magistrate, Aurangabad, in connection with Complaint

                        Case No. 79 of 2005 and Tr. No. 476/2009, subject to condition

                        laid down under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_1">Section 438(2)</a> of the Criminal Procedure Code

                        with additional condition to remain physically present before the

                        court below on each and every date at least for one year or till
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                              -2-</span>




          disposal of the case whichever is earlier, in case of failure on

          two consecutive dates, the liberty shall be deemed to be

          cancelled.
</p>
<p id="p_4">


</p><pre id="pre_2">Rajeev/                        (Akhilesh Chandra, J.)
