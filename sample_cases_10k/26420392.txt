<div class="doc_title">Janardan Bhagat vs The State Of Bihar on 4 March, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.8910 of 2015
                       Arising Out of PS.Case No. -71 Year- 2014 Thana -BASNAHI District- SAHARSA
                 ======================================================
                 1. Janardan Bhagat Son of Late Lochan Bhagat, the then Chairman of PACS
                 or Gram Panchayat, Mahua Bazar, Uttarbari resident of village - Mahua
                 Bazar, P.S. Basanahi, District - Saharsa

                                                                                .... ....   Petitioner/s
                                                       Versus
                 1. The State of Bihar

                                                                 .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :  Mr. Anant Kumar-1, Advocate.
                 For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr. Chandra Bhushan Prasad(App)
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE PRABHAT KUMAR JHA
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   04-03-2015                Heard both sides.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                             The petitioner apprehends his arrest in Basanahi P.S.

                 Case No. 71 of 2014, registered for the offences punishable under

                 <a href="/doc/361626/" id="a_1">Section 7</a> of the Essential Commodities Act.

</p><p id="p_3">                             It is alleged that the petitioner being Chairman of the

                 PACS allowed Ashok Kumar Bhagat and Arvind Mandal to sale

                 the fertilizers in black market and fertilizers were recovered from

                 the possession of Ashok Kumar Bhagat and Arvind Mandal.

</p><p id="p_4">                             Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that nothing

                 has been recovered from the possession of the petitioner. Ashok

                 Kumar Bhagat from whose possession the fertilizers were

                 recovered has already been enlarged on anticipatory bail.

</p><p id="p_5">                             Considering the facts aforesaid, the petitioner in the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">        Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.8910 of 2015 (2) dt.04-03-2015</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                  2/2</span>




                    event of his arrest or surrender before the court below within a

                    period of four weeks from the date of receipt/production of a copy

                    of this order, is directed to be enlarged on bail on furnishing bail

                    bond of Rs. 10,000/- (Ten Thousand) with two sureties of the like

                    amount each to the satisfaction of the Learned Sub-Divisional

                    Judicial Magistrate, Saharsa in connection with Basanahi P.S.

                    Case No. 71 of 2014, subject to the conditions as laid down under

                    <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_2">Section 438(2)</a> of the Cr.P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><p id="p_7">                                               (Prabhat Kumar Jha, J.)
KKSINHA/-
</p>
