      IN THE HIGH COURT OF KARNATAKA
              DHARWAD BENCH

      DATED THIS THE 10TH DAY OF JULY 2014

                      BEFORE

  THE HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE H.BILLAPPA

        WRIT PETITION NO.105876/2014 (GM-CPC)

BETWEEN

VANAJAKSHI W/O. CHANNAYYA MATHAD
AGE: 60 YEARS, OCC: HOUSEHOLD WORK
R/O. GUTTIGERIGALLI, TQ: HALIYAL
                                       ... PETITIONER

(BY SRI.SURESH P.HUDEDAGADDI, ADVOCATE)

AND

MALLAYYA S/O. CHANDRAYYA ANAGOLMATH
AGE: 41 YEARS, OCC: BUSINESS
R/O. GUTTIGERIGALLI, TQ: HALIYAL
                                  ... RESPONDENT

     THIS WRIT PETITION IS FILED UNDER ARTICLES 226
AND 227 OF THE CONSTITUTION OF INDIA PRAYING TO
QUASH THE IMPUGNED ORDER DATED 18.03.2014,
DISMISSING THE SAID APPEAL FOR NON PROSECUTION,
VIDE ANNEXURE-C PASSED BY THE SENIOR CIVIL JUDGE,
YALLAPUR, SITTING AT HALIYAL, IN R.A.NO.21/2010.

    THIS PETITION COMING ON FOR PRELIMINARY
HEARING THIS DAY, THE COURT MADE THE FOLLOWING:
                                   :2:




                           O R D E R
</pre>
<p id="p_1">       The petitioner has called in question the order dated

18.03.2014      passed     by     the    appellate        Court   in

R.A.No.21/2010.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">       2.   By the impugned order, the appellate Court has

dismissed the appeal for non-prosecution.


</p><p id="p_4">       3.   The remedy for the petitioner is to approach the

appellate Court for restoration of the appeal. Instead of

that, the petitioner has rushed to this Court. Therefore, the

writ petition cannot be sustained in law.


</p><p id="p_5">       4.   Accordingly,    the   writ   petition    is   dismissed

reserving liberty to the petitioner to pursue the remedy

available in law.
</p>
