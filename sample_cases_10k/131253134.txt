<div class="doc_title">Naushad Alam vs State Of Bihar &amp; Anr on 12 January, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.1521 of 2016
                       Arising Out of PS.Case No. -188 Year- 2015 Thana -JOKIHAT District- ARRARIA
                 ======================================================
                 Naushad Alam
                                                                     .... .... Petitioner/s
                                                 Versus
                 State of Bihar &amp; Anr
                                                                .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     : Mr. Md. Naushad Uzzoha
                 For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr. Harendra Prasad(App)
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE DINESH KUMAR SINGH
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   12-01-2016               Heard learned counsels for the petitioner and the State.

</p><p id="p_2">                             The petitioner being the husband of the informant is

                 apprehending arrest in a complaint case wherein process has been

                 directed to be issued after cognizance being taken for the offences

                 punishable under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_1">Sections 498A</a>, <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_2">341</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_3">323</a> and <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_4">504</a> of the Indian

                 Penal Code and <a href="/doc/751411/" id="a_5">Section 3</a>/<a href="/doc/1023340/" id="a_6">4</a> of the Dowry Prohibition Act.

</p><p id="p_3">                             The basic accusation is of torture for non-fulfilment of

                 the dowry demand.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                             Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that the

                 petitioner admits his marriage with the informant and is ready to

                 keep the informant as wife with full dignity and honour. A

                 statement to that effect has been made in para 12 of the petition

                 which reads as follows:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">                                   "That the petitioner is always ready to keep his wife
                                    with full dignity and respect."
</blockquote><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">         Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.1521 of 2016 (2) dt.12-01-2016</span><blockquote id="blockquote_2">

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                   2/2</span>




                                   Considering the present stand of the petitioner, let the

                     above named petitioner be released on provisional anticipatory

                     bail for one year in the event of arrest or surrender before the

                     learned Court below within a period of twelve weeks from today,

                     on furnishing bail bond of `10,000/- (ten thousand) with two

                     sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of learned

                     SDJM, Araria in connection with Jokihat P.S. Case No. 188 of

                     2015, subject to the conditions as laid down under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_7">Section 438(2)</a>

                     Cr.P.C.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_3">                                   Let the learned court below issue notice to the informant

                     for her appearance. On her appearance, the petitioner will take the

                     informant to keep her as wife with full dignity and honour.

</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_4">                                    The provisional bail of the petitioner will be confirmed

                     by learned court below in three eventualities (i) if the matrimonial

                     harmony is substantially restored (ii) if the informant fails to

                     appear before the learned court below or (iii) if the informant gets

                     reluctant to reconcile the issue.</blockquote><p id="p_5">


</p><p id="p_6">                                                              (Dinesh Kumar Singh, J)
Amrendra/-
</p><pre id="pre_2"> U         T
