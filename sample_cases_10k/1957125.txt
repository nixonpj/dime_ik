<div class="doc_title">Smt. Abha Singh And Others vs State Of U.P. &amp; Another on 9 August, 2010</div><pre id="pre_1">Court No. - 45

Case :- APPLICATION U/S 482 No. - 5073 of 1994

Petitioner :- Smt. Abha Singh And Others
Respondent :- State Of U.P. &amp; Another
Petitioner Counsel :- Rajul Bhargava
Respondent Counsel :- A.G.A.

Hon'ble Rajesh Dayal Khare,J.
</pre>
<p id="p_1">Heard learned counsel for the applicants and learned AGA for the State-
respondent.
</p>
<p id="p_2">The Office report dated 21.02.1995 shows that notices issued to the opposite
party no. 2 have not been received after service.
</p>
<p id="p_3">Learned counsel for the applicants states that the applicant no. 1 is the wife of
the opposite party no. 2 and applicants no. 2 and 3 are sister and brother of the
applicant no. 1.
</p>
<p id="p_4">It is contended on behalf of the applicants that applicant no. 1 had initiated the
proceedings against her husband (opposite party no. 2) under <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_1">section 323</a>,
<a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_2">504</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_3">506</a> IPC and it is contended that as a counter blast the opposite party no.
2 has initiated the present proceedings being criminal case no. 531 of 1993
under <a href="/doc/1258372/" id="a_4">section 147</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_5">323</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_6">504</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_7">506</a> IPC pending before the 3rd Additional Chief
Judicial Magistrate, Agra.
</p>
<p id="p_5">It is contended that the criminal prosecution of the applicant is mala fide and
the applicants have been summoned for the offence which was never
committed by the applicants and therefore, the same is in contravention of the
principles of law as laid down by the Hon'ble Apex Court in the matter of
Pepsi Food Ltd. and another Vs. Special Chief Judicial Magistrate and
others reported in 1998 SCC (Cri) 1400.
</p>
<p id="p_6">It is next contended by Sri Bhargava that the present proceedings against the
applicants are nothing but a counter blast to the proceedings initiated by the
applicant against the opposite party no. 2 and therefore, the same is absolutely
illegal and against the principles of law laid down by Hon'ble Apex Court in
the judgement reported in 2009 (64) ACC 296 in the matter of M/s Eicher
Tractor Ltd. and others Vs. Harihar Singh and another.
 The present 482 Petition is finally disposed of with a direction that the
applicants may file an application for discharge under the provision<a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_8"> of the
Criminal Procedure Code</a> before the Court below within a period of one
month from today and if such application is filed, the same shall be
considered and decided by the court concerned as expeditiously as possible
after hearing the parties preferably within a period of four months.
</p>
<p id="p_7">For a period of four months or till the disposal of the application of the
applicant, whichever is earlier, no coercive action shall be taken against the
applicants pursuant to criminal case no. 531 of 1993 under <a href="/doc/1258372/" id="a_9">section 147</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_10">323</a>,
<a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_11">504</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_12">506</a> IPC pending before the 3rd Additional Chief Judicial Magistrate,
Agra.
</p>
<p id="p_8">The petition is accordingly disposed of. Interim order dated 01.12.1994 is
hereby vacated.
</p>
<p id="p_9">Order Date :- 9.8.2010
yachna
