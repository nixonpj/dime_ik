ORAL

<p id="p_1">(Per
: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE A.L.DAVE)
 

 


 
	  

Heard
	party-in-person. We have considered the submissions made by the
	party-in-person that in light of the amendment of <a href="/doc/1025846/" id="a_1">Section 28</a> of
	Hindu Marriage Act [ HM Act ], the period of limitation is now
	changed to 90 days from 30 days w.e.f. 23/12/2006.
</p>	 


	  

<p id="p_2">Ordinarily,
	this submission is to be accepted forthwith. But in the instant
	case, there is another provision of law which comes into operate,
	namely provisions of <a href="/doc/373687/" id="a_2">Family Courts Act</a>. <a href="/doc/1411868/" id="a_3">Section 19</a> of the said Act
	specifically provide that every appeal under the said section shall
	be filed before the period of 30 days from the date of judgment, or
	order of the Family Court. 
</p>	
	 


	  

<p id="p_3">The
	situation that  is arising before us therefore is that the
	substantive decree or order is passed by  the Family Court under the
	H.M. Act dismissing the petition of the petitioner. In light of
	Section 28 of H.M. Act, the appeal can be preferred without the
	period of 90 days from passing of the decree.
</p>	 


	 

<p id="p_4">3.1
	At the same time some decree is passed by the Family Court, and as
	per provisions contained in <a href="/doc/1093601/" id="a_4">Section 19(3)</a> of the Family Courts Act,
	appeal can be preferred against judgment or order of the  Family
	Court within the period of 30 days only. 
</p>	
	 


	  

<p id="p_5">The
	office has, therefore, raised an objection as to period of
	limitation, because the appeal is preferred by the appellant on the
	49th day and therefore, there is delay of 19 days, if
	period of limitation is considered to be 30 days.
</p>	 


	  

<p id="p_6">The
	party-in-person has taken a stand that he shall not file any
	application for condonation of delay. Matter is accordingly placed
	before us.
</p>	 


	  

<p id="p_7">Having
	regard to the provision, what emerges is that the decree is passed
	under a <a href="/doc/701797/" id="a_5">Special Act</a>, namely <a href="/doc/590166/" id="a_6">Hindu Marriage Act</a> by a Special Court
	constituted under special provision, namely under <a href="/doc/373687/" id="a_7">Family Courts Act</a>.
	Both these acts provide a specific period of limitation for
	preferring appeal, order or judgment passed under the said Acts and
	the period of limitation is in conflict.  <a href="/doc/590166/" id="a_8">Hindu Marriage Act</a>
	provides limitation of 90 days, while <a href="/doc/373687/" id="a_9">Family Courts Act</a> provides
	limitation of 30 days.
</p>	  

<p id="p_8">In
	our view when the decree is passed under a special substantive law,
	though by a Court constituted under <a href="/doc/701797/" id="a_10">Special Act</a>, the decree would
	still remain to be one under the provision of the said special
	substantial law, which in the instant case is a personal law. The
	party-in-person is pressing his cause under the said law and is
	justified in doing so. A pragmatic view has to be taken. The appeal
	is required to be treated as one preferred within the period of
	limitation of 90 days. The Registry is therefore directed to treat
	this appeal as an appeal preferred within the period of limitation,
	and register the same, subject to other verifications.
</p>

A.L. DAVE, J ]
 

 


 

      
                                                    [
S.R. BRAHMBHATT, J ]
