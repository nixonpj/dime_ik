<div class="doc_title">Sri Bhaktadas Kanrar vs State Of West Bengal &amp; Ors on 19 September, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                 1


17

     19.9.2019
        jb.




                                W.P. 9843(W) of 2008

                                    Sri Bhaktadas Kanrar
                                          vs.
                                 State of West Bengal &amp; Ors.



                  Mr. Anil Kr. Chatterjee
                             .... For the Petitioner




                    Since this writ petition can be disposed of without going into

              the merits of the case, I direct that the representation as in Annexure

              P/2 which has been made to the District Inspector of Schools and

              also the Director of Pension be considered and disposed of by the

              respondent No. 2. Since I am told that the writ petitioner has already</pre>

<p id="p_1">              retired from service and he is 82 years old and unless his

              representation is considered and disposed of in the twilight of life he

              shall be reduced to penury, the respondent No. 2 is directed to

              consider and dispose of the representation keeping in mind that the

              writ petitioner does not have long to live by giving adequate

              opportunity of being heard and to communicate the decision to the

              writ petitioner's representative after he/she produces due authority.

              The decision shall be a reasoned one which must be communicated
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                             2</span>


to the petitioner within a fortnight from the date of communication of

this order.
</p>
<p id="p_2">

</p><p id="p_3">      I make it clear that I have directed the respondent No. 2 to

consider and dispose of this matter since he is the auditing authority

and the respondent No. 4 being the District Inspector of Schools

though the approving authority may not be in a position to grant the

petitioner the relief he wants. In the event that the respondent No. 2

feels that this can be properly decided by the respondent No. 4 then

he can of course delegate this function to the respondent No. 4 and

in that case consequential relief be granted to the petitioner within a

further period of seven days.
</p>
<p id="p_4">

</p><p id="p_5">      There shall be no order as to costs.
</p>
<p id="p_6">


</p><p id="p_7">                            (Protik Prakash Banerjee, J.)
