<div class="doc_title">Pawan Kumar And Others vs State Of Punjab on 1 April, 2009</div><pre id="pre_1">Crl.Misc.No.M-5239 of 2009                                       1

      IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA AT
                    CHANDIGARH.



                                      Crl.Misc.No.M-5239 of 2009
                                      Date of Decision: 1.4.2009

Pawan Kumar and others                                    .....Petitioners

                               Vs.

State of Punjab                                           ....Respondent

                               ....
</pre>
<pre id="pre_2">CORAM :      HON'BLE MR.JUSTICE RAJIVE BHALLA

                               ****

</pre><p id="p_1">Present :   Mr. Rohan Chaudhary, Advocate for the petitioner.
</p><p id="p_2">            Mr. C.S. Brar, DAG, Punjab for the respondent.
</p><p id="p_3">                             ....
</p>
<p id="p_4">RAJIVE BHALLA, J (Oral)


            Prayer in this petition, filed under Section 438 of the code of

Criminal Procedure is for grant of pre-arrest bail, in case FIR No.7 dated

9.2.2009, registered under <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_1">Sections 324</a>,<a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_2">323</a>,<a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_3">506</a>,<a href="/doc/763672/" id="a_4">148</a>,<a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_5">149</a> IPC and <a href="/doc/1540253/" id="a_6">Section

326</a> IPC, at Police Station Rangar Nangal,, Distt. Batala.

</p><p id="p_5">            Interim pre-arrest bail was granted after the following facts

were brought to the notice of this court :-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">            "Counsel for the petitioners submits that petitioner no.1 is the

            Senior Chief Trustee of a registered trust that runs the affairs of

            Shri Achleshwar Mandir. Petitioner no.1 filed a civil suit to

            stop the misuse and interference etc. in the affairs of the temple.

            The civil Court has granted an injunction in favour of the

            society.   As a result, the petitioner ordered removal of all

            hoardings relating to political figures of the town. On 7.2.2009,
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Crl.Misc.No.M-5239 of 2009                                       2</span>

              petitioner no.1 was cornered by the local MLA's nephew, his

              the supporters and roughed up. The police arrived at the spot

              but on account of the pressure of the MLA they did not register

              the petitioner's complaint. On 7.2.2009 itself, petitioner no.1

              left for Agra to attend the marriage of his neice and remained at

              Agra upto 10.2.2009.       When he returned to Batala on

              11.2.2009, he was informed that the police has registered a case

              against him and <a href="/doc/1540253/" id="a_7">Section 326</a> IPC has been added.              It is

              submitted that the injury answering to the description of an

              offence punishable under <a href="/doc/1540253/" id="a_8">Section 326</a> of the Indian Penal Code,

              is on a non vital part. The genesis of the present dispute is the

              petitioners removal of political hoardings and the influence of

              the local MLA over the police."</blockquote><p id="p_6">



</p><p id="p_7">              Counsel for the petitioners submits that the petitioners have

joined investigation and their custodial interrogation is not required.

</p><p id="p_8">              Counsel for the State of Punjab submits that though the

petitioners have joined investigation, an Innova car, in which the petitioners

are alleged to have arrived at the place of the incident is to be recovered.

</p><p id="p_9">              I have heard learned counsel for the parties and am prima facie

of the opinion that the petitioners are being victimised by the police at the

behest of local politicians.    The facts narrated herein above speak for

themselves.    An accused cannot be compelled under pain or threat of

dismissal of his prayer for anticipatory bail to effect a recovery, where his

denial of the incident alleged appears to be prima facie correct.

</p><p id="p_10">              In view of what has been stated herein above, the order dated
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2"> Crl.Misc.No.M-5239 of 2009                                    3</span>

25.2.2009 is made absolute, upto the filing of the challan,whereafter the

petitioner would be required to seek regular bail.

</p><p id="p_11">            This petition stands disposed of accordingly.


</p><pre id="pre_3">1.4.2009                                             (RAJIVE BHALLA)
GS                                                        JUDGE
