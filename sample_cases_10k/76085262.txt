<div class="doc_title">Sakti Pada Rana vs The State Of West Bengal &amp; Ors on 22 June, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                       1

22.06.2016
(21)
DC.




                               W.P. 10607 (W) of 2016


                             Sakti Pada Rana.
                                     Vs
                      The State of West Bengal &amp; Ors.
                      Mr. Milan Bhattacharya
                      Ms. Sulagna Bhattacharya (Bagchi)... for petitioner

             Mr. P. C. Bhattacharya
                      Mr. Manas Ghosh
                      Mr. S. Panja.. for respondents</pre>

<p id="p_1">                      Mr. Pranab Dutta
                      Mr. Sudhakar Thakur..... for State


                      This writ challenges a departmental proceeding initiated after the retirement of the

              petitioner. His last date in service with the Cooperative Bank was 30th January, 2016.

</p><p id="p_2">                      The charge-sheet dated 25th May, 2016 accuses the writ petitioner of dereliction of duties

              and negligence in the advancement of loans.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                      Mr. Bhattacharya, learned senior Advocate appearing for the petitioner submits that this

              departmental proceeding could not have been initiated after the retirement of his client. There is no

              provision in the rule in that behalf. Even otherwise, the law does not permit such a departmental

              proceeding to be initiated.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                      By virtue of this except provident fund all other retiral benefits of the writ petitioner have

              been held up.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                      Learned counsel for the Cooperative Bank and the State submit that this proceeding is

              maintainable since monetary loss of the Bank is alleged. The maintainability of this writ is also

              questioned by the Cooperative Bank on the ground that it is purely a private dispute between the

              Bank and the petitioner.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                           2</span><p id="p_6">

        These questions have to be gone into. Nevertheless a substantial question has been raised in

this writ. It is formally admitted, subject to its maintainability.

</p><p id="p_7">        Affidavit-in-opposition by 14th July, 2016.
</p>
<p id="p_8">        List this application on 28th July, 2016.
</p>
<p id="p_9">        Affidavit-in-reply may be filed in the meantime.
</p>
<p id="p_10">        Considering the prima facie case of the parties, the gratuity dues of the petitioner may be

held up by the Bank pending further orders. The gratuity amount is to be invested by them in a term

deposit in a nationalised Bank earning the highest rate of interest.

</p><p id="p_11">        This is in my opinion, is adequate security for the Bank.

        The other retiral dues including leave encashment and pension be immediately released to

the petitioner. Departmental proceeding will not be continued without the leave of this Court.

</p><p id="p_12">                                                        (I.P.Mukerji,J.)
 </p>
