  IN THE HIGH COURT OF KERALA AT ERNAKULAM

Bail Appl No. 6433 of 2007()


1. RAJAPPAN, FROM PUTHUVAL HOUSE,
                      ...  Petitioner

                        Vs



1. STATE OF KERALA, REPRESENTED BY
                       ...       Respondent

2. PUBLIC PROSECUTOR, HIGH COURT OF

                For Petitioner  :SR<a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_1">I.P.C</a>.HARIDAS

                For Respondent  : No Appearance

The Hon'ble MR. Justice R.BASANT

 Dated :23/10/2007

 O R D E R
                                 R.BASANT, J
                         ------------------------------------
                          B.A.No.6433 of 2007
                         -------------------------------------
                Dated this the 23rd day of October, 2007

                                     ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">       Application for anticipatory bail.             The petitioner is the sole

accused in a crime registered under <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_2">Section 420</a> I.P.C. The crux of the

allegation is that the petitioner induced subscribers to part with weekly

deposits for a long period of time. But when time came for payment of

the money, he did not pay the amount. Thereby he is alleged to have

practised deception.      It is the case of the prosecution that the

petitioner had no intention at all to pay amounts even when he

accepted the amounts. Crime has been registered. Investigation is in

progress. The petitioner apprehends imminent arrest.

</p><p id="p_2">       2.    The learned counsel for the petitioner submits that

anticipatory bail may be granted to the petitioner. I find absolutely no

justification in the prayer for anticipatory bail. There are no features in

this case which can justify or warrant the invocation of the

extraordinary equitable discretion under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_3">Section 438</a> Cr.P.C. It is for

the petitioner to appear before the learned Magistrate and seek

regular bail in the ordinary course.
</p>
<p id="p_3">       3.    This application is, in these circumstances, dismissed, but I

may hasten to observe that if the petitioner surrenders before the

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">B.A.No.6433 of 2007               2</span>

learned Magistrate and applies for bail after giving sufficient prior

notice to the Prosecutor in charge of the case, the learned Magistrate

must proceed to pass appropriate orders on merits and expeditiously.




</p><p id="p_4">                                                (R.BASANT, JUDGE)
rtr/-
