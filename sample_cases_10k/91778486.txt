<div class="doc_title">Shishiliya Dikosta vs State Of West Bengal And Others on 17 August, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                                         1



17.08.2018.
Item No. 8
                                                  (W)
                                     W.P. 13216         of 2018

                                     Shishiliya Dikosta
                                             Vs.
                              State of West Bengal and others.



                    Mr. Partha Pratim Roy,
                    Mr. Dyutiman Banerjee.
                                              ... for the petitioner.

                    Mr. Susovan Sengupta,
                    Mr. Manas Kumar Sadhu.
                                                    ... for the State.

                    Ms. Manika Roy.
                          ... for the National Highway Authority.



                   A serious dispute is raised in the instant writ petition against
              the State respondents in not accepting an application under</pre>
<p id="p_1">              <a href="/doc/26881941/" id="a_1">Section 3G(5)</a> of the National Highways Act, 1956 where the
              petitioner intended to claim compensation on the structure
              standing upon the land.
</p><p id="p_2">                   The learned Advocate appearing on behalf of the State
              respondents submits that it is not evident from the writ petition
              that the petitioner has ever attempted to file the said application.
              The learned Advocate further submits that had it been a case that
              the authorities have refused to accept the application, the same
              could have been sent by post, which apparently has not been done
              in the instant case.
</p><p id="p_3">                   Without venturing into enter the disputed arena, more
              particularly, when there is no period of limitation prescribed for
              filing such application before the authority, this Court feels that
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                2</span>


     justice would be subserved if the petitioner would be permitted to
     file such application before the respondent no. 3, who is also the
     competent authority under the <a href="/doc/1222415/" id="a_2">National Highways Act</a>, 1956.
</p><p id="p_4">          Such application shall be filed by the petitioner within two
     weeks from date and if such application is tendered and/or
     submitted, the said respondent shall accept the same and
     endeavour shall be shown to dispose of the same within six weeks
     from the date of communication of this order after affording an
     opportunity of hearing to all interested persons and by recording
     proper reasons.
</p><p id="p_5">          With these observations, the writ petition is disposed of.
          There shall, however, be no order as to costs.
</p>



<pre id="pre_2">ab                                    (Harish Tandon, J.)
