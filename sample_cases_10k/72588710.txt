  IN THE HIGH COURT OF KARNATAKA AT BANGALORE

         DATED THIS THE 27th DAY OF MAY 2013

                            BEFORE

       THE HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE ANAND BYRAREDDY

         CRIMINAL PETITION NO.2519 OF 2013

BETWEEN:

Gopala,
s/o late Malegowda,
aged about 43 years,
r/at Kurubara Beedi Pete,
Arakalgud Town,
Kasaba hobli,
Hassan-573102.                              ...PETITIONER


(By Shri.Gnanesh H.Kempanna, Advocate)

AND:

State of Karnataka,
by ARakalgud police,
represented by
State Public Prosecutor
High Court Buildings,
Bangalore-560001.                          ...RESPONDENT


(By Shri.Satish R.Girji, HCGP)

                             *****

       This Criminal Petition is filed under Section 438 of the
code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, praying to enlarge the
petitioner on bail in the event of his arrest in Crime
                               2


No.91/2013 of Arakalgudu P.S.Hassan, which is registered
for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1408303/" id="a_1">Sections 171</a> (E) of Indian
Penal Code and Section 32 of Karnataka Excise Act.

     This petition coming on for orders this day, the court
made the following:

                         ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Heard the learned Counsel for the petitioner.

</p><p id="p_2">2.    The   petitioner   is   accused    of   the   offences

punishable under <a href="/doc/151968657/" id="a_2">Sections         32</a> of the Excise Act and

under <a href="/doc/1408303/" id="a_3">Section 171(E)</a> of Indian Penal Code in the

following circumstances:
</p>
<p id="p_3">      The complainant namely,        the Circle Inspector of

Police, Arakalagud has alleged that on 07.03.2013 in

view of the pattana panchayath election, when he and

his staff were patrolling on Arakalagud town,             it

transpires that he had received credible information

that some people were distributing liquor to the voters

and accordingly, the complainant had secured the

panchas and went towards the Electricity Board road

leading to Salugopalu road and he saw several people

standing and when they saw the complainant and his
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                              3</span>


staff approaching them, they ran away leaving behind

a bag containing ten liquor bottles of 180 ml each and

15 empty bottles with 20 plastic glasses which were all

seized under a mahazar and thereafter,      it transpires

on enquiry,    the complainant had learnt of petitioner

being one of the persons involved in distributing liquor

and accordingly, the complaint.
</p>
<p id="p_4">
</p><p id="p_5">3.     In view of there being no     witness who     has

spoken about the presence of the accused and on

suspicion the petitioner being     involved and the case

having been     initiated, the petitioner has made out a

case for enlargement on bail.         In the result, the

following order is passed:
</p>
<p id="p_6">                          ORDER

</p><p id="p_7">(i)    The petition is allowed.
</p>
<p id="p_8">(ii)   The petitioner shall be enlarged on bail in the

event of his arrest on he executing a self bond for a

sum of Rs.10,000/- (Rupees Ten       thousand) with one
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                               4</span>


solvent surety for a likesum to the satisfaction of the

concerned Court.
</p>
<p id="p_9">(iii)   The petitioner shall not directly or indirectly seek

to influence the prosecution witness.

</p><p id="p_10">(iv)    He shall appear before the Investigation officer as

and when required and shall co-operate with the

Investigating Officer.
</p>
<p id="p_11">(v)     The petitioner shall attend the Court regularly.

</p><p id="p_12">(vi)    In case of violation of any of these conditions, the

Court is at liberty to pass suitable orders.

</p><p id="p_13">        The Government Pleader is permitted to file his

memo of appearance within two weeks.



</p><p id="p_14">                                         SD/-
</p><p id="p_15">                                       JUDGE




sh
