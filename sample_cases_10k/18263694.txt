<div class="doc_title">Prem Bhoi vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 2 June, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">                           M.Cr.C. No.2986/2014


02.06.2014

       Shri Jitendra Sharma, learned counsel for the applicant. 
       Shri   Devendra   Singh,   learned   panel   lawyer   for   the 
respondent/State. </pre>
<p id="p_1">       Heard. 
</p><p id="p_2">       By filing this application under Section 439 of the Code of 
Criminal   Procedure,   1973,   the   applicant   is   seeking   bail   in 
connection   with   Crime   No.557/2013   registered   at   Police 
Station­Mahakal,   District­Ujjain   for   the   commission   of   the 
alleged offence under­Sections 376, 506/34 of the Indian Penal 
Code. 
</p><p id="p_3">       According to the learned counsel for the applicant, as per 
the   prosecution   case   itself,   the   alleged   offence   has   been 
committed   by   Raju   @   Riaz   and   the   applicant   was   simply 
standing   outside   and   near   to   the   bridge.   He   submits   that   no 
offence as has been alleged can be said to be made out against 
the applicant. He also submits that the applicant is in jail since 
31.12.2013.  
</p><p id="p_4">       Taking   into   consideration   the   over   all   facts   and 
circumstances of the case and the evidence collected in the case 
diary, I am of the view that the applicant has made out a case 
 for grant of bail. 
</p><p id="p_5">       Accordingly, the bail application is allowed. It is directed 
that   the   applicant   be   released   on   bail   upon   his   furnishing   a 
personal bond in the sum of Rs.30,000/­(Rs.Thirty Thousand) 
with   one   surety   in   the   like   amount   to   the   satisfaction   of   the 
concerned   JMFC/CJM   for   his   appearance   before   him   or   trial 
Court, as the case may be, on all dates of hearing as may be 
fixed in this behalf.  
</p><p id="p_6">       Cc by tomorrow.  
</p><p id="p_7">                                                     (Shantanu Kemkar)
                                                         Vacation Judge  
Arun
 </p>
