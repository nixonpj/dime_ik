<pre id="pre_1">                                                        1


22.07.2010</pre><p id="p_1">                        C.R.R. 1498 of 2008




                   This revisional application was filed by the petitioner Ashadulla Biswas, an
             accused in G.R. Case No. 613 of 2000 pending before the Additional Chief
             Judicial Magistrate, Malda, which arose out of Kaliachak Police Station Case No.
             99 of 2000 dated 19th April, 2000 under Section 302 read with <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_1">section 34</a> of the
             Indian Penal Code.
</p>

<p id="p_2">                   The petitioner has prayed for quashing the proceedings on the ground that
             he is completely innocent but has admitted that the police has filed a charge-
             sheet against him amongst others under the aforesaid sections. He further has
             admitted that he has been absconding. This petition was moved on 23rd June,
             2008 when directions were issued for service. Thereafter the matter was listed on
             8th July, 2010 when the learned advocate for the petitioner prayed for an
             adjournment of two weeks which was granted. Today when the matter was called
             on for hearing no one appeared for the petitioner.
</p>

<p id="p_3">                   Needless to mention that the petition is altogether without any merit.
             Merely by saying that the petitioner is innocent he cannot find fault with the
             criminal proceeding in which a charge sheet under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_2">Sections 302</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_3">34</a> has already
             been filed, which goes to show that prima facie materials against him have
             already been collected. This petition is nothing less than an abuse of the process
             of law and may be on this basis further proceedings have been kept in abeyance
             before the Learned A.C.J.M. Malda.
</p>

<p id="p_4">                   This petition is accordingly dismissed.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                              2</span>


<p id="p_5">           The Learned A.C.J.M. Malda, is directed to proceed with the matter if the
      matter has not already been committed. If the matter has already been
      committed this Court desires that the trial should be expedited as early as
      possible. In that event he shall communicate a copy of this order to the Learned
      Sessions Court.
</p>



<p id="p_6">                                                 (Girish Chandra Gupta, J.)


bd.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_2"> 3</span>
