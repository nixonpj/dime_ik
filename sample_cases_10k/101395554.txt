                                                       ORDER
</pre>

<p id="p_1">                                  This writ petition has been filed seeking a direction to the

                      respondents to consider the petitioner's representation dated

                      14.02.2019,     seeking   disbursement of her      husband's terminal

                      benefits.
</p>
<p id="p_2">

</p><p id="p_3">                                  2.Heard the learned counsel appearing for the petitioner

                      and the learned Additional Government Pleader appearing for the

                      respondents. By consent, the writ petition itself is taken up for final

                      disposal at the stage of admission.



</p><p id="p_4">                                  3.The case of the petitioner is that her husband was

                      working as a Village Administrative Officer at Virisoor (Group)

                      Devakottai and he retired from service on 30.04.2012. After

                      retirement, till his demise, he was not paid with any terminal

                      benefits. Therefore, the petitioner/wife of the deceased made a

                      representation dated 14.02.2019 to the respondents, seeking

                      disbursement of terminal benefits. But, no orders have been passed

                      on the same. Hence, this writ petition has been filed.




http://www.judis.nic.in
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                          3</span>

</p><p id="p_5">                                  4.The learned Additional Government Pleader appearing

                      for   the     respondents,   on   instructions,   submitted    that   the

                      representation of the petitioner would be considered on merits.



</p><p id="p_6">                                  5.In view of the above submission, this Court, without

                      going into the merits of the matter, directs the respondents to

                      consider the petitioner's representation dated 14.02.2019 with

                      regard to disbursement of terminal benefits and pass appropriate

                      orders      on merits and in accordance with law, within a period of

                      eight weeks from the date of receipt of a copy of this order, after

                      affording an opportunity of hearing to the petitioner.



</p><p id="p_7">                                  6.The writ petition is disposed of with the above direction.

                      No costs.
</p>
