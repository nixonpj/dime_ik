<div class="doc_title">Savitri Devi vs The State Of Bihar on 16 October, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                              CRIMINAL MISCELLANEOUS No.60577 of 2019
                      Arising Out of PS. Case No.-102 Year-2019 Thana- PAROO District- Muzaffarpur
                 ======================================================
                 Savitri Devi, (Female-38 years), Wife of Kabindra Singh, Resident of village
                 Singahi, P.S. Paroo, District Muzaffarpur.

                                                                                   ... ... Petitioner/s
                                                        Versus
                 The State of Bihar.

                                                        ... ... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :        Mr.Bhavesh Kumar
                 For the Opposite Party/s :        Mr.Md. Shakir Ahmad
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE PRABHAT KUMAR JHA
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   16-10-2019                  Heard both sides.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                The petitioner apprehends her arrest in Paroo P.S.

                   Case No.102 of 2019, registered under Sections 30 and 30(a) of

                   the Bihar Prohibition and <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_1">Excise Act</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                The petitioner and her husband are said to have

                   indulged in illegal trade of illicit liquor and on such a raid was

                   conducted. Recovery is said to have been made from a bamboo

                   bush situated behind the house of the petitioner.

</p><p id="p_4">                                Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that no

                   recovery was made from the conscious possession of the

                   petitioner. The bamboo bush is situated behind the house of the

                   petitioner and it is an open place. Anybody can keep anything in

                   the bamboo bush. On same facts, Kabindar Singh, the husband

                   of the petitioner has already been granted anticipatory bail by
          Patna High Court CR. MISC. No.60577 of 2019(2) dt.16-10-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                     2/2</span>




                   order dated 20.05.2019, passed in Cr. Misc. No.33235 of 2019.

                   The case of the petitioner stands exactly on the same footing.

</p><p id="p_5">                                Taking into consideration the facts and nature of

                   allegation made against the petitioner and the facts that the

                   petitioner is a lady, she has got no criminal antecedent and on

                   the same facts her husband has been enlarged on bail, let

                   petitioner, above named, in the event of her arrest or surrender

                   before the court below within a period of four weeks from the

                   date of receipt of the order, be enlarged on bail on furnishing

                   bail bond of Rs.10,000/- (ten thousand) with two sureties of the

                   like amount each to the satisfaction of the learned Special

                   Judge, Excise, Muzaffarpur in connection with Paroo P.S. Case

                   No.102 of 2019, subject to the conditions laid down under

                   <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_2">Section 438(2)</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure.



</p><p id="p_6">                                           (Prabhat Kumar Jha, J)
S.KUMAR/-
</p>
