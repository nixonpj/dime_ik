<p id="p_1">Harilal, J.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">           The petitioner is the landlord who filed RCP

No.8/2013 before the Rent Control Court, Attingal seeking

an order of eviction under Section 11(3) of the Kerala

Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1965 and the 1st

respondent is the tenant of the petition schedule building.

According to the petitioner, he is a B.Tech degree holder in

Information Technology and doing M.Tech in Computer

Science. He has no avocation for his livelihood. Therefore

he wants the petition schedule building for his own

occupation, at the earliest. The grievance of the petitioner

is that even though the Rent Control Petition was filed in

the year 2013, there is no progress in the proceedings and

the delay in disposal of the case would cause great

hardship to the petitioner.    The petitioner sought for a

direction to the court below to dispose the Rent Control

 OP(RC).137/17-O                 :2:
</p>
<p id="p_4">

Petition within a time frame.
</p>
<p id="p_5">      2.    On instructions, the Rent Control Court reported

that the petitioner has filed IA 2751/2014 seeking

amendment to the Rent Control Petition. Thereafter he

filed IA 1384/17 for appointing an advocate commissioner

and now the said IA is posted for objection and hearing on

18/9/2017. In view of the said report we do not find fault

with the court below in causing delay in the disposal of the

case and the disposal of the case is seen belated due to the

conduct from the part of the petitioner himself. However,

the Rent Control Court has requested to grant four months

time to dispose the case and the said request will stand

allowed.
</p>
<p id="p_6">      This OP(RC) is disposed of accordingly.
