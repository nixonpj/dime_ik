                        IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                 Civil Writ Jurisdiction Case No.6650 of 2014
                  ======================================================
                  Kadir Mian
                                                                       .... .... Petitioner/s
                                                    Versus
                  Narain Prasad &amp; Ors
                                                                      .... .... Respondent/s
                  ======================================================

                  For the Petitioner/s    :    Mr. Madhuri Lata

                  ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">3   19-02-2016                     Heard learned counsel, Ms. Madhuri Lata, appearing

                   for the petitioner and learned counsel, Mr. Ramchandra Singh,

                   appearing for the respondents.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                   2.              By the impugned order dated 13.12.2013, the learned

                   Sub-Judge-IV, Siwan in T.S. No. 5452 of 2010 directed that the

                   issue of res-judicata shall be decided as preliminary issue as

                   provided in Order 14 Rule 2 Sub-rule 2 C.P.C.

</p><p id="p_3">                   3.              Perused the order passed by the court below. In view

                   of the submission of the learned counsel for the petitioner and the

                   learned counsel for the respondents, it appears that in the present

                   case, the facts are admitted and question will be as to whether the

                   decision of previous suit i.e. T.S. No. 32 of 1983 will operate as

                   res-judicata in the present suit or not and for that, no evidence is

                   required. On the basis of the judgment of this Court and/or the trial

                   court or lower appellate court is to be gone into and, therefore, in
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">             Patna High Court CWJC No.6650 of 2014 (3) dt.19-02-2016</span>




                         my opinion, it is a pure question of law as facts have been

                         admitted.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                         4.              In such view of the matter, the learned court below

                         rightly held that the issue of res-judicata shall be decided as

                         preliminary issue. When the court below has exercised its

                         discretionary jurisdiction under Order 14 Rule 2 C.P.C., there is

                         no question of interfering the same in exercise of supervisory

                         jurisdiction arises. Thus, this writ application is dismissed.




</p><p id="p_5">                                                   (Mungeshwar Sahoo, J)
brajesh/-
