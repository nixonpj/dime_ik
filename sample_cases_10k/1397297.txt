<p id="p_1">2/   07/04/2011             Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and

                  learned counsel for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                            The petitioner is stated to be the defendant in

                  partition suit No.98/97. He is aggrieved by the order

                  dated 25.1.2010 closing his evidence.

</p><p id="p_3">                            If the fact that the suit has remained pending

                  since 1997 was not enough to persuade this Court not

                  to interfere with the impugned order dated 29.7.2010,
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                       2</span>




     the detailed recitals in the impugned order of the

     indulgence granted to him earlier on the same question,

     taken advantage of on more than one date to keep the

     suit pending, satisfies the Court that the present is not

     a fit case for exercising supervisory powers under

     <a href="/doc/1331149/" id="a_1">Article-227</a> of the Constitution of India to further

     prolong the suit on the submission made that in the

     interests of justice the petitioner be granted one

     opportunity.
</p>
<p id="p_4">              The interest of justice requires that this Court

     does not interfere with the impugned order.

</p><p id="p_5">              The writ application stands dismissed.


</p><pre id="pre_2">KC                        ( Navin Sinha, J.)
