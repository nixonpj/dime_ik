<div class="doc_title">Durjodhan Mandal vs The State Of Jharkhand on 30 August, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">           IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                   A. B. A. No. 174 of 2014

     Durjodhan Mandal                                     .....   ...      Petitioner
                                  Versus
     The State of Jharkhand                                 ..... ...        Opposite Party
                                      --------

               CORAM        :    HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE H. C. MISHRA
                                    ------

           For the Petitioner         :        Mr. K.K. Mishra, Advocate
           For the State              :        A.P.P.
                                      --------

3/30.08.2014</pre><p id="p_1">        Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned A.P.P. for the
           State.
</p><p id="p_2">           The petitioner is apprehending his arrest in connection with Narayanpur
           P.S. Case No. 124 of 2012 corresponding to G.R. No.829                           of
           2012, for the offence under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_1">Sections 498-A</a>, <a href="/doc/92983/" id="a_2">306</a> of the Indian Penal
           Code.
</p><p id="p_3">                    The petitioner is the husband of the deceased and though it is
           stated in the F.I.R. that the marriage has taken place about 15 years ago
           and they have two children, but there is allegation against the petitioner
           to have subjected the deceased to cruelty and torture for demand of
           dowry. The dead body of the deceased was found hanging in the house.
</p><p id="p_4">                    Learned counsel for the petitioner has submitted that the petitioner
           has been falsely implicated in this case and the deceased had committed
           suicide for some different reason. Learned counsel has, accordingly,
           prayed for anticipatory bail.
</p><p id="p_5">                    Learned counsel for the State, on the other hand, has opposed the
           prayer for anticipatory bail and has pointed out from the case diary that
           the processes under <a href="/doc/1598801/" id="a_3">Sections 82</a> and <a href="/doc/886456/" id="a_4">83</a> of the Cr.P.C., have already
           been issued against the petitioner which were also executed.
</p><p id="p_6">                    In that view of the matter, this application is not maintainable and
           is, accordingly, dismissed.
</p>

<p id="p_7">                                                                      ( H. C. Mishra, J.)
Shamim/
 </p>
