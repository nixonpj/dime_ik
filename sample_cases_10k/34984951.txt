<div class="doc_title">Tetari Devi &amp; Anr vs State Of Bihar on 9 June, 2010</div><pre id="pre_1">                     IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                  Cr.Misc. No.15400 of 2010
                      1. TETARI DEVI, WIFE OF RAMASHANKER SINGH.
            2. RAMASHANKER SINGH, SON OF LATE MAHADEO SINGH.
     BOTH RESIDENT OF VILLAGE- NAWKA TOLA BHAGWANPUR, P.S.- MIRGANJ,
                            DISTRICT- GOPALGANJ
                                              Versus
                                       STATE OF BIHAR
                                            -----------
</pre>
<p id="p_1">4.      09.06.2010.              Heard learned counsel for the petitioners and the State.

</p><p id="p_2">                                 Petitioners are the mother-in-law and father-

                      in-law     of     the    complainant     Bimla      Devi.    They     are

                      apprehending        their    arrest     in    connection       with    a

                      complaint       case     registered    for    the    offences       under

                      <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_1">Sections 498A</a> and <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_2">406</a> of the Penal Code and <a href="/doc/751411/" id="a_3">Sections

                      3</a> and <a href="/doc/1023340/" id="a_4">4</a> of the Dowry Prohibition Act. It is submitted

                      on their behalf that their son has gone to Middle

                      East     and after his departure to foreign country the

                      daughter-in-law went to her parental house and lodged

                      the present case. In this background it is submitted

                      that petitioners have no objection to continue the

                      matrimonial relationship with the complainant and to

                      that effect they shall file a written undertaking on

                      affidavit in the court within a period of four weeks

                      from the date of receipt/production of a copy of this

                      order in the court below. No sooner such undertaking

                      on affidavit is filed, petitioners, named above, be

                      admitted     to    the    privilege    of    provisional      bail    on

                      furnishing bail bonds of Rs. 5,000/- (Five thousand)

                      each with two sureties of the like amount each to the

                      satisfaction       of    S.D.J.M.,     Gopalganj      in    connection

                      with Complaint Case No. 95 of 2009 whereafter the

                      complainant should be noticed and when she appears in
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                  -2-</span>




         the     court    below,       in     presence      of     the    court,

         petitioners should persuade her to come back to the

         matrimonial fold not only by words of mouth but also

         by their suitable conduct and gesture and when the

         husband of the complainant returns to India efforts

         shall    be     made    to    persuade       him   to     resume      the

         matrimonial      ties     with     the   complainant.      The     court

         below should monitor the trial and ensure justice to

         the parties in accordance with law. In the event

         dispute is resolved or the complainant is back to her

         matrimonial home or she is found to be unreasonable,

         provisional bail granted to the petitioners shall be

         confirmed,      failing      which   court    below     shall    be    at

         liberty to pass appropriate orders in accordance with

         law considering the reasonableness of the stand of

         the parties.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                   This application is, accordingly, disposed of.

                   Let this order be communicated to the court

         below through fax on payment of usual charges by the

         petitioners.
</p>
<p id="p_4">


</p><pre id="pre_2">P.K.P.                                            (V.N.Sinha,J.)
