                     BEFORE THE MADURAI BENCH OF MADRAS HIGH COURT

                                            DATED : 16.04.2019

                                                    CORAM

                            THE HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE M.DHANDAPANI

                                       Crl OP(MD)No.5670 of 2019

            Vishnu                                               ... Petitioner

                                                     Vs.
            1.The Superintendent of Police,
              Office of the Superintendent of Police,
              Tuticorin District.

            2.State represented by
              The Inspector of Police,
              South Police Station,
              Tuticorin.

            3.Manicka Rajendran                               ...Respondents

            PRAYER:- Petition filed under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> of Criminal Procedure Code to
            direct the 2nd respondent to not to harass the petitioner and her family
            members.
                                   For Petitioner    : Mr.V.Malaiyendran
                                   For Respondent : Mr.K.K.Ramakrishnan,
                                         Nos.1 and2     Additional Public Prosecutor,


                                                    ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">                    This criminal original petition has been filed seeking to direct       the

            respondent Police not to harass the petitioner and her family members



</p><p id="p_2">                    2.Heard the learned Counsel appearing for the petitioners and the

                learned
http://www.judis.nic.in   Additional Public Prosecutor appearing the respondents Police.
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                          2</span>

</p><p id="p_3">                          3.The learned Additional Public Prosecutor would submit that on a

                complaint given against the petitioner, petition enquiry is pending against

                the petitioner, on the file of the second respondent police.



</p><p id="p_4">                          4.In view of the above, the petitioner is directed to cooperate with

                the police for enquiry and at the time of conducting of the enquiry, the

                petitioner shall not be harassed by the police. The police cannot call the

                petitioner on a regular basis in the guise of enquiry without even

                registering an FIR and        Supreme Court of India in Lalitha Kumari Vs.

                Government of Uttar Pradesh reported in 2013 (6) CTC 353 has

                enumerated the nature of cases where preliminary enquiry can be

                conducted and has also restricted the period, for which preliminary

                enquiry can be conducted. During the course of enquiry, if any cognizable

                offence is made out, respondent Police is free to register an FIR and this

                order shall not be a shield for the petitioner against the respondent Police

                proceeding to take action in accordance with law.



</p><p id="p_5">                          5.With the above directions, the Criminal Original Petition is closed.
