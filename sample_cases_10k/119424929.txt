                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                     Criminal Miscellaneous No.39493 of 2016
                       Arising Out of PS.Case No. -201 Year- 2014 Thana -PHULPARAS District- MADHUBANI
                   ======================================================
                   1. INDRAJIT RAI @ INDRAJIT @ AMARJIT RAI S/o Shyam Nandan
                   Rai Resident of Vill. - Mandaidih, P. S. - Patepur, District - Vaishali

                                                                                  .... ....   Petitioner/s
                                                          Versus
                   1. The State of Bihar

                                                                   .... .... Opposite Party/s
                   ======================================================
                   Appearance :
                   For the Petitioner/s     :  Mr. Anish Kumar
                   For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr. Sri Tarun Prasad Mandal
                   ======================================================
                   CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE VINOD KUMAR SINHA
                   ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">5   03-02-2017              Heard the parties.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                            This application has been filed in connection with Phulparas

                   P.S.Case No.201 of 2014 corresponding to the G.R.No.1055 of

                   2014 for the offence under <a href="/doc/1535430/" id="a_1">Sections 328</a>, <a href="/doc/1119707/" id="a_2">395</a> and <a href="/doc/1865117/" id="a_3">397</a> of the

                   Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                            It is submitted on behalf of the petitioner that he had earlier

                   moved before this Court for grant of regular bail but his prayer

                   was rejected. Further, it has been submitted that the other co-

                   accused, having similar allegation, have been granted bail by this

                   Court and the petitioner has remained in custody for more than

                   1 ½ years.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                            Heard learned A.P.P. also, who has opposed the prayer for

                   bail of the petitioner stating that there are some materials against
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">         Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.39493 of 2016 (5) dt.03-02-2017</span>




                     the petitioner and his case is not similar to the other co-accused

                     persons.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                              Having heard both sides. In view of the allegation against

                     the petitioner, I am not inclined to grant bail to the petitioner,

                     however, as the petitioner has remained in custody for about 1 ½

                     years, the learned court below is directed to expedite the trial

                     within a period of six months and if not concluded, the petitioner

                     is at liberty to renew his prayer for bail.




</p><p id="p_6">                                                (Vinod Kumar Sinha, J)
chn/-
</p>
