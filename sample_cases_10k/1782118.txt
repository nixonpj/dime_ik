<div class="doc_title">Ajay Pahan vs State Of Jharkhand on 28 June, 2011</div><pre id="pre_1">       IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                      Cr. Appeal (S.J.) No. 1092 of 2009
       Ajay Pahan                  ...      ...          Appellant
                                   Versus
       State of Jharkhand ...        ...      ...       ...    Opposite Party
                                   ------
       CORAM:        THE HON'BLE MRS. JUSTICE JAYA ROY
                                   ------
       For the Appellant           :Mr. P.C. Tripathy, Sr. Advocate
       For the State               : Mr. A.B. Mahto, A.P.P.
                                   ------
                     I.A. No. 443 of 2011

06/28.06.2011</pre><p id="p_1">         Heard the learned senior counsel Mr. P.C. Tripathy appearing for
             the appellant also Mr. A.B. Mahto learned counsel appearing for the
             State.
</p><p id="p_2">                      Learned counsel for the appellant submits that the prayer for bail
             of appellant was earlier rejected by another Bench of this Court vide
             order dated13.01.2010 which Bench is not available.
</p><p id="p_3">                      It is contended that the appellant is in custody since 18.07.2008
             i.e. more than two and half years.
</p><p id="p_4">                      He has further contended that even according to the judgment,
             from the possession of the appellant only one mobile phone has been
             recovered.
</p><p id="p_5">                      Mr. A. B. Mahto learned counsel for the State opposed but not
             disputed the period of custody of the appellant. It is also submitted that
             the another co-convict, from whose possession some materials were
             recovered convicted under <a href="/doc/844968/" id="a_1">Section 412</a> of the Indian Penal Code namely,
             Vijay Ram @ Bijay Ram, has been granted bail by this Court vide order
             dated 21.12.2009 in Cr. Appeal (S.J.) No. 1014 of 2009.
</p><p id="p_6">                      Considering the facts and circumstances of this case and
             considering the fact that the another co-convict has already been granted
             bail by this Court and considering the period of custody of the appellant
             i.e. more than two and half years, during the pendency of this appeal the
             appellant above named is directed to be released on bail on furnishing
             bail bond of Rs. 10,000/- (Rs. Ten thousand) with two sureties of the like
             amount each to the satisfaction of the Additional Judicial Commissioner,
             F.T.C.-VIII, Ranchi in connection with S.T. No. 98 of 2009, subject to the
             condition that one of the bailors will be his close relative and another will
             be of local resident having immovable property within the jurisdiction of
             the trial Court.
</p><p id="p_7">                      I.A. No. 443 of 2011 stands disposed of.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                                                                          ( Jaya Roy , J)
Satayendra
