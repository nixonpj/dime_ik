<div class="doc_title">Vikku Sonkar vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 29 June, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                           MCRC-10116-2017
                 (VIKKU SONKAR Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)


29-06-2017

Shri Anurag Sahu, counsel for the petitioner.
Shri K.S. Patel, panel lawyer for the respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">Heard on this second application for bail under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of the
Cr.P.C. filed on behalf of the petitioner Vikku Sonkar in crime
no.237/2017 registered by P.S.-Gorakhpur, Jabalpur under <a href="/doc/387648/" id="a_2">Section 34</a>
(2) of the <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_3">Excise Act</a>.
</p><p id="p_2">His first application for the same relief was dismissed by this Court by
order dated 03.05.2017 in M.Cr.C.No.6862/2017 as withdrawn.
By way of change in circumstances for this second bail application,
learned counsel for the petitioner has submitted that the seizure
witnesses have been examined and they have turned hostile.
Learned panel lawyer for the respondent State has opposed the bail
application.
</p><p id="p_3">However, keeping in view the fact that the statements of the
Investigating Officer is still to be recorded as also the fact that the
petitioner has a damning criminal record having as many as 21
criminal cases registered against him, which include six cases under
the <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_4">Excise Act</a>, in the opinion of this Court, petitioner does not
deserves to be released on bail.
</p><p id="p_4">Consequently, this second application for bail under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_5">Section 439</a> of
the Cr.P.C. filed on behalf of the petitioner Vikku Sonkar deserves to
be and is accordingly dismissed.
</p>

<p id="p_5">                                                        (C V SIRPURKAR)
                                                             JUDGE
