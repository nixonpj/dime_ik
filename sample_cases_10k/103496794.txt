<div class="doc_title">Girish Radhakishan Varde &amp; 2 vs State Of Gujarat &amp; on 13 October, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">        R/CR.MA/12403/2014                                  ORDER




         IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

     CRIMINAL MISC.APPLICATION (FOR QUASHING &amp; SET ASIDE
                        FIR/ORDER) NO. 12403 of 2014

================================================================
            GIRISH RADHAKISHAN VARDE &amp; 2....Applicant(s)
                             Versus
               STATE OF GUJARAT &amp; 1....Respondent(s)
================================================================
Appearance:
MR HARDIK H DAVE, ADVOCATE for the Applicant(s) No. 1 - 3
MR ALKESH N.SHAH, APP for the Respondent(s) No. 1
================================================================

        CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE J.B.PARDIWALA

                              Date : 13/10/2014


                               ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">      RULE returnable forthwith. Mr.Shah, the learned APP
waives service of notice of rule for and on behalf of the
respondent no.1 State of Gujarat. Mr.Hriday C.Buch, the
learned advocate has appeared and waives service of notice of
rule for and on behalf of the respondent no.2 original
complainant.       Registry    shall    accept    the   Vakalatnama   of
Mr.Hriday C.Buch appearing on behalf of the respondent no.2
original complainant.
</p>

<p id="p_2">      By this application, the petitioners seek to invoke the
inherent powers of this Court under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> of the Code of
Criminal Procedure, 1973, praying for quashing of the First
Information Report being CR-I No.59 of 2009 registered with
the Deesa City Police Station, for the offence punishable under


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                  Page 1 of 3</span>
           R/CR.MA/12403/2014                        ORDER



<a href="/doc/1430765/" id="a_2">Section 25(1)</a>(1-B)<a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_3">(A) of the Arms Act</a> and <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_4">Sections 365</a>, <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_5">387</a>,
<a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_6">511</a>, <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_7">386</a>, <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_8">120B</a> read with <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_9">Section 34</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>

<p id="p_3">     The investigation has resulted into filing of a charge-
sheet which has culminated into Criminal Case No.1754 of
2009 pending in the Court of learned Chief Judicial Magistrate,
Deesa.
</p>

<p id="p_4">     It appears that the parties have amicably resolved the
dispute with the intervention of the family members and well-
wishers. It also appears that some dispute relating to
commercial transactions between the parties led to the
incident in question. However, both the sides have decided to
bury their differences and the respondent no.2, viz. Dipak
Dhirajlal Thakkar, has filed an affidavit, inter alia, stating as
under :
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">     "I state and submit that the present applicants have also
     filed certain complaints against me and the same is
     pending for adjudication before the Ld.JMFC, Deesa. It is
     required to be noted that because of the intervention of
     common friends and considering the fact that applicants
     and myself are resident of Deesa and known to each
     other, we have decided to end all the disputes between
     us and therefore I have entered into compromise with
     the present petitioner.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_2">     I respectfully submit before this Hon'ble Court that I do
     not want that the applicant be prosecuted for as we have
     amicably settled the dispute. I respectfully submit before
     this Hon'ble Court that I do not have any objection if the
     FIR registered by me vide C.R.No.I-59/2009 for offences
     punishable U/Ss.25(1)(1-B)<a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_10">(A) of the Arms Act</a> and
     <a href="/doc/886598/" id="a_11">Sections 365</a>, <a href="/doc/1688284/" id="a_12">387</a>, <a href="/doc/1185693/" id="a_13">511</a> and <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_14">34</a> of the IPC registered with
     Deesa City Police Station, chargesheet and Criminal Case
     No.1754/2009 is quashed and set aside qua the present
     petitioner with my consent.
</blockquote>


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                               Page 2 of 3</span>
<blockquote id="blockquote_3">          R/CR.MA/12403/2014                           ORDER



       In this set of facts and factual background, I respectfully
       pray and urge to the Hon'ble Court that the First
       Information Report which I had lodged with C.R. No.I-
       59/2009 for offences punishable U/Ss.25(1)(1-B)<a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_15">(A) of the
       Arms Act</a> and <a href="/doc/886598/" id="a_16">Sections 365</a>, <a href="/doc/1688284/" id="a_17">387</a>, <a href="/doc/1185693/" id="a_18">511</a> and <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_19">34</a> of the IPC
       registered with Deesa City Police Station, chargesheet
       and Criminal Case No.1754/2009 qua the present
       applicants herein be quashed with my consent."</blockquote>


<p id="p_5">       The respondent no.2 Shri Dipak Dhirajlal Thakkar is
personally present in the court and is identified by Mr.Hriday
Buch appearing on his behalf.
</p>

<p id="p_6">       In view of the fact that the matter has been resolved
between the parties, no useful purpose would be served to
permit the Criminal Case No.1754 of 2009 pending in the Court
of learned Chief Judicial Magistrate, Deesa, to proceed further.
</p>

<p id="p_7">       In the result, this application is allowed. The further
proceedings of the Criminal Case No.1754 of 2009 pending in
the Court of learned Chief Judicial Magistrate, Deesa, are
hereby ordered to be quashed.
</p>

<p id="p_8">       Rule made absolute. Direct service is permitted.
</p>



<p id="p_9">                                              (J.B.PARDIWALA, J.)
MOIN




<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                              Page 3 of 3</span>
