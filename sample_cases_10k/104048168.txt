<div class="doc_title">Vinod Mahto @ Vinod Kumar Mahto &amp; ... vs The State Of Bihar on 17 December, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                   IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.55454 of 2015
                      Arising Out of PS.Case No. -95 Year- 2015 Thana -CHANAN District- LAKHISARAI
                 ======================================================
                 1. Vinod Mahto @ Vinod Kumar Mahto
                 2. Subodh Mahto @ Subodh Kumar Mahto
                     Both sons of Ajab Lal Mahto
                 3. Bablu Mahto, son of Krishna Mahto
                 4. Krishna Mahto son of Tulsi Mahto,
                                                         .... .... Petitioner/s
                                                 Versus
                 1. The State of Bihar

                                                                .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :   Mr. Jainendra Kumar, Advocate
                 For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr. Ashok Kr.Singh(App)
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE DINESH KUMAR SINGH
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   17-12-2015                 Heard learned counsels for the petitioners and the

                  State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                               The petitioners are apprehending arrest in a case

                  registered for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_1">sections 323</a>, <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_2">324</a>,

                  <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_3">307</a>, <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_4">341</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_5">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                               The accusation is of making assault to the informant,

                  his son and wife. Though one of the injury has been found to be

                  grievous whereas other injuries are simple in nature.

</p><p id="p_4">                               It is submitted by learned counsel for the petitioner

                  that in the background of land dispute the accusation has been

                  levelled. At earlier point of time, petitioner's side has lodged

                  Chanan P.S. Case No.96/2015 against the informant, levelling
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">             Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.55454 of 2015 (2) dt.17-12-2015</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                       2/2</span>




                           accusation under <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_6">sections 341</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_7">323</a>, <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_8">324</a>, <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_9">307</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_10">34</a> of the Indian

                           Penal Code. A statement has been made in paragraph 3 of the

                           petition that the petitioner has no criminal antecedent.

</p><p id="p_5">                                        The       aforesaid       facts    constitute   ground   for

                           consideration of prayer for regular bail. Let the learned Court

                           below consider the prayer for regular bail of the petitioners, if

                           the petitioners surrender within a period of six weeks in

                           connection with Chanan P.S. Case No.95/2015, pending before

                           the learned CJM, Lakhisarai.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                        With the above observation, this application is,

                           accordingly, disposed off.
</p>
<p id="p_7">


</p><p id="p_8">                                                    (Dinesh Kumar Singh, J)
Ashwini/-
</p>
