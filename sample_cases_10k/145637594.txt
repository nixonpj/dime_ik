<div class="doc_title">Sameer vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 7 June, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">Cr.A. No.603/2018                                                       1


        THE HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH

                             Cr.A. No.603/2018
                    (<a href="/doc/61332929/" id="a_1">Sameer vs. The State of Madhya Pradesh</a>)

Indore, Dated: 07/06/2018

            Shri Rakesh Sharma learned counsel for the appellant.
            Shri Vivek Patwa, learned Public Prosecutor for the
respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">            Appellant has preferred this appeal under Section 14-A(2) of the
SC/ST (PA) Act, 1989, feeling aggrieved with the order dated 19/01/2018,
rendered by Special Judge (SC/ST), Indore, District Indore in S.T.
No.69/2018, whereby the prayer for regular bail has been declined.
</p><p id="p_2">            Heard on I.A. Nos.4417/2018 and 4418/2018, applications for
urgent hearing and hearing during vacation.
</p><p id="p_3">            On due consideration, I.A. Nos. 4417/2018 and 4418/2018
stands allowed and disposed of.
</p><p id="p_4">            Appellant has been arrested on 24/09/2017 in connection with
crime No.755/2016, registered at Police-Station Lasudiya, District Indore,
in relation to offence punishable under <a href="/doc/619940/" id="a_2">Sections 363</a>, <a href="/doc/796352/" id="a_3">366</a>, <a href="/doc/1279834/" id="a_4">376</a> of IPC and
<a href="/doc/84602/" id="a_5">Section 5(l)</a>/<a href="/doc/1832444/" id="a_6">6</a> of Protection of Children from Sexual Offence, 2012 read
with <a href="/doc/111591460/" id="a_7">Section 3(2)(v)</a> of The Scheduled Castes And The Scheduled Tribes
(Prevention of Atrocities) 1989.
</p><p id="p_5">            Learned counsel for the appellant has submitted that in the
present case, prosecutrix and her mother have already been examined by
the trial Court and have not deposed anything against the present appellant
and have been declared hostile. Counsel has also placed on record, the
photocopy of their statements. Under these circumstances counsel prayed
for grant of regular bail of appellant.
</p><p id="p_6">            Learned counsel for the State submits that no sufficient ground
is made out for releasing the appellant on bail, hence the application filed
by the appellant be dismissed.
</p><p id="p_7">            Considering the facts and circumstance of the case and the
arguments advanced by learned counsel for the parties and on perusing the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">           Cr.A. No.603/2018                                                           2</span>


          statements, but without expressing any opinion on the merits of the case,
          this Court is of the view that since the prosecutrix has not supported the
          prosecution version, the appeal filed by the appellant deserves to be
          accepted. Consequently, setting aside the impugned order, the appeal is
          hereby allowed. It is directed that the appellant - Sameer shall be released
          on bail on execution of personal bond in the sum of Rs.50,000/- (Rupees
          fifty thousand only) with a solvent surety in the like amount to the
          satisfaction of the learned trial Court for his regular presence during trial
          and shall also abide by the conditions enumerated under <a href="/doc/1973522/" id="a_8">Section 437</a> (3) of
          <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_9">Cr.P.C</a>.
</p><p id="p_8">                      This order shall be effective till the end of the trial, however, in
          case of bail jump, it shall become ineffective.
</p><p id="p_9">                      Certified copy as per Rules.
</p>

<p id="p_10">                                                                  (Subodh Abhyankar)
                                                                       V. Judge
sumathi
