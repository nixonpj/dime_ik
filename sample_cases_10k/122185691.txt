                  IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

                  SPECIAL CRIMINAL APPLICATION NO. 769 of 2012

         ==========================================================
                  PATHAN ISMAILKHAN BAJITKHAN &amp; 6....Applicant(s)
                                      Versus
                 SURESHCHANDRA BAGMALBHAI DOSHI - ADVOCATE &amp;
                                1....Respondent(s)
         ==========================================================
         Appearance:
         (MR MUKUL SINHA), ADVOCATE for the Applicant(s) No. 2 - 3
         NOTICE SERVED for the Applicant(s) No. 2 - 3
         THAKKAR AND PAHWA, ADVOCATES, ADVOCATE for the Applicant(s) No. 1
         ,4-7
         MR JIGAR G GADHAVI, ADVOCATE for the Respondent(s) No. 1
         PUBLIC PROSECUTOR for the Respondent(s) No. 2
         ==========================================================

                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE J.B.PARDIWALA

                                      Date : 16/10/2015


                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>
<p id="p_1">         1.   Rule returnable forthwith. Mr. Pandya, the learned
         Additional Public Prosecutor, waives service of notice of rule for
         and on behalf of the respondent No.2-State of Gujarat. Mr. Jigar
         Gadhvi, the learned advocate, has entered appearance on
         behalf of the respondent No.1-original complainant and waives
         service of notice of rule.
</p>

<p id="p_2">         2.   By this application under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">section 482</a> of the Code of
         Criminal Procedure, 1973, the petitioners seek to invoke the
         inherent powers of this court, praying for quashing of the
         Criminal Case No.653 of 2012 pending in the court of the
         learned Chief Judicial Magistrate, First Class, Kalol of the

         offence punishable under <a href="/doc/1408202/" id="a_2">sections 500</a> and <a href="/doc/112749/" id="a_3">114</a> of the Indian
         Penal Code on the ground that there has been an amicable
         settlement between the parties and the respondent No.1-
         original complainant is no longer desirous of prosecuting the
         first   information          report       further.       The       respondent             No.1-
         Sureshchandra Bagmalbhai Doshi is personally present and he
         confirms about the settlement arrived at with the accused
         persons. The respondent No.1- Sureshchandra Bagmalbhai
         Doshi is identified by his learned advocate Mr. Jigar Gadhvi.
         Shri Sureshchandra Doshi has also filed an affidavit, inter alia,
         stating as under:
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">                 "I, Sureshchandra Bagmalbhai Doshi, respondent No.____
                 herein, aged : 75, residing at Kalol Dist. Gandhinagar, file
                 this affidavit / undertaking as hereunder:-
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_2">                 1.     I state and submit that I had filed criminal
                 proceedings being Criminal Case No.653 of 2012 which is
                 pending before the learned Chief Judicial Magistrate at
                 Kalol.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_3">                 2.    It is most humbly submitted that in the present
                 case, a full and final settlement has been taken place
                 and, therefore, I undertake as under.</blockquote>

<p id="p_3">                 3.     It is most humbly submitted that the deponent
                 herein undertake to give consent for quashment of the
                 criminal proceedings being Criminal Case No.653 of 2012
                 pending before the learned Chief Judicial Magistrate at
                 Kalol on compliance of the undertaking filed by the
                 petitioners herein whereby they have undertaken to
                 publish a story on first page of Deepmala weekly that the
                 allegations made against the deponent herein and the
                 story published in the weekly dated 27.01.2012 was false
                 and that they are apologetic about the same. The said
                 consent shall be given on the condition that all the
                 proceedings instituted against the deponent before the
                 Bar Council of Gujarat at Ahmedabad shall be withdrawn
                 unconditionally and on the condition that all other
                 proceedings (criminal / civil) if any pending before any
                 civil / criminal court or any competent authority shall be

</p>
<p id="p_4">              4.    It is most humbly submitted that the deponent on
              the aforesaid conditions give consent for quashment of
              the criminal proceedings being Criminal Case No.653 of
              2012 pending before the learned Chief Judicial Magistrate
              at Kalol.
</p>
<p id="p_5">              What is stated hereinabove is true to the best of my
              knowledge information and belief and I believe the same
              to be true and correct.
</p>
<p id="p_6">              Solemnly affirmed at Kalol on this 13th day of October,
              2015."
</p>
<p id="p_7">         3.   The petitioners herein also filed an affidavit/undertaking,
         inter alia, stating as under;
</p>

<blockquote id="blockquote_4">              "I, Pathan Ismailkhan Bajitkhan, Petitioner No.1 herein,
              Aged: 55 years, residing at B/h. St. Xavier High School,
              Sindbad Hotel, Kalol City, Taluka-Kalol, Distirct:
              Gandhinagar, file this affidavit / undertaking as
              hereunder:-
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_5">              1.    It is most humbly submitted that I have preferred
              the aforesaid petition, with a prayer for quashing and
              setting aside the proceedings of Criminal Case No.653 of
              2012 wherein the notice was issued and the respondents
              had appeared through their advocates.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_6">              2.    During the pendency of the aforesaid petition, the
              petitioners and the respondent No.2 have arrived at a full
              and final settlement. The said undertaking is for and on
              behalf of the petitioners and the same shall be binding
              to all the petitioners. The petitioners, therefore,
              undertake as under.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_7">              3.    The petitioners shall publish in weekly Saptahik
              namely Deepmala on its first page that the story
              published regarding the alleged defamatory contents
              against respondent No.2-advocate were false and for the
              same, apology will be recorded and published in the said
              publication.
</blockquote>

</p><p id="p_9">              4.    The petitioners shall withdraw the proceeding
              pending before the Bar Council of Gujarat at Ahmedabad
              instituted against respondent No.2-advocate.
</p>
<p id="p_10">              5.    The petitioners shall withdraw all the civil / criminal
              proceedings instituted against respondent No.2- original
              complainant if any pending before any Court (civil
              /criminal), competent authority or any other offices.
</p>
<p id="p_11">              6.   The petitioners shall abide by the aforesaid
              undertaking and shall follow the same and on compliance
              of the same, the Criminal Case No.653 of 2012 will be
              prayed to be quashed on the consent of respondent
              No.2-original complainant.
</p>
<p id="p_12">              What is stated hereinabove is true to the best of my
              knowledge information and belief and I believe the same
              to be true and correct.
</p>
<p id="p_13">              Solemnly affirmed at Ahmedabad on this 13th day of
              October, 2015."
</p>

<p id="p_14">         4.   Taking into consideration the nature of the dispute and
         the fact that the parties have now amicably decided to live
         peacefully, no useful purpose would be served to allow the
         police to continue with the investigation of the said first
         information report.
</p>

<p id="p_15">         5.   In the result, this application is allowed. The proceedings
         of the Criminal Case No.653 of 2012 pending in the court of
         the learned Chief Judicial Magistrate, First Class, Kalol are
         hereby ordered to be quashed. All consequential proceedings
         arising from the same also stand terminated. Rule is made
         absolute. The Registry shall accept the Vakalatnama of Mr.
         Jigar Gadhvi, the learned advocate appearing on behalf of the
         respondent No.1.
</p>
