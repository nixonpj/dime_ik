<div class="doc_title">K.Sarada vs The A.P.S.R.T.C. on 26 September, 2018</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: Abhinand Kumar Shavili</div>

<pre id="pre_1"> HONOURABLE SRI JUSTICE ABHINAND KUMAR SHAVILI

              WRIT PETITION No.17048 OF 2003
ORDER:
</pre>

<p id="p_1">1.   This writ petition is filed seeking to issue a writ of

Mandamus declaring the action of the respondents in not

considering the claim of the petitioner for appointment as

conductor/cleaner/shramik/clerk or in any other suitable post

under Bread Winner Scheme, and in rejecting her case vide

proceedings dated 26.11.2002, as illegal and arbitrary, and

consequently, to direct the respondents to appoint the petitioner

in any suitable post under the Bread Winner Scheme.


</p><p id="p_2">2.   Heard Sri M. Manohar Reddy, learned Counsel for the

petitioner and Sri A. Rama Rao, learned Standing Counsel for

the respondent-Corporation.
</p>
<p id="p_3">
</p><p id="p_4">3.   It has been contended by the petitioner that she is the

daughter of late K. Nagisetty, and while her father was working

as Painter, Zonal Work Shop, Cuddapah, died on 6.1.2002,

and then, she submitted a representation dated 9.11.2002 to

the respondents to consider her case on compassionate

grounds under Bread Winner Scheme, but the respondents

considered and rejected her case vide order dated 26.11.2002

impugned herein. Further, it has been contended that as per

the latest schemes introduced by the respondents during 2015
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                 2</span>




and 2017, she is eligible for appointment on compassionate

grounds and therefore, the respondents may be directed to

consider her case.
</p>
<p id="p_5">
</p><p id="p_6">4.   This Court having considered the submissions made by

the parties and the material available on record, is of the view

that the case of the petitioner deserves to be considered for

appointment.
</p>
<p id="p_7">
</p><p id="p_8">5.   Accordingly, the Writ Petition is disposed of directing the

respondents to consider the case of the petitioner for

appointment in any suitable post, as per her eligibility, on

compassionate grounds, in accordance with the schemes, and

pass appropriate orders, within a period of four weeks from the

date of receipt of a copy of this order. No costs. Consequently,

miscellaneous petitions pending, if any, shall stand closed.


</p><p id="p_9">                              ___________________________
                               ABHINAND KUMAR SHAVILI, J

Dated: 26.9.2018.
</p><p id="p_10">nn.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                         3</span><p id="p_11">




 HONOURABLE SRI JUSTICE ABHINAND KUMAR SHAVILI




          WRIT PETITION No.17048 OF 2003




                   26/09/2018
