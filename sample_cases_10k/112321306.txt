<div class="doc_title">Akhilesh Rai vs The State Of Bihar on 18 November, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                              CRIMINAL MISCELLANEOUS No.67937 of 2019
                     Arising Out of PS. Case No.-572 Year-2018 Thana- BIKRAMGANJ District- Rohtas
                 ======================================================
                 AKHILESH RAI Son of Late Shivaji Ray Resident of Village - Baruna, P.S.-
                 Bikramganj, District- Rohtas

                                                                                  ... ... Petitioner/s
                                                       Versus
                 The State of Bihar

                                                        ... ... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :       Mr.Brij Bihari Tiwary
                                                  Mr. Naresh Dixit
                 For the Opposite Party/s :       Ms. Gulnar Begum, APP
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE JUSTICE SMT. ANJANA MISHRA
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   18-11-2019                     Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned

                   Additional Public Prosecutor for the State.

</p><p id="p_2">                                    The petitioner is in custody since 01.04.2019 in

                   connection with Bikramganj P.S. Case No. 572 of 2018

                   registered       for   the    offence      punishable       under     <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_1">Sections

                   302</a>/<a href="/doc/1128948/" id="a_2">120(B)</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_3">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code and <a href="/doc/244673/" id="a_4">Section 27</a> of the

                   Arms Act.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                    Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that

                   though there had been indiscriminate firing, the narration in the

                   FIR which is of the eye-witness-informant (brother of the

                   deceased) who has seen the occurrence specifically assigns the

                   role of firing on the petitioner who is said to have fired while

                   running away and also from the front.
              Patna High Court CR. MISC. No.67937 of 2019(2) dt.18-11-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                         2/2</span>




                                       In view of the specific allegation against the

                       petitioner, no case for regular bail is made out.

</p><p id="p_4">                                       It is, accordingly, rejected.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                       Trial be expedited.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><p id="p_7">                                                  (Anjana Mishra, J)

Saif/-
</p>
