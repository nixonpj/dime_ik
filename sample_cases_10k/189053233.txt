AKJ &amp; J.S. SHETTY, MEMBER,
13/07/2019

                        CONCILIATION ORDER

      The     learned    counsel    for   the    appellant     and   the

representative of Respondent-Insurance Company along with</pre>

<p id="p_1">its counsel are present.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">      2.     After prolonged negotiations, the matter is settled.

The Appellant-owner has agreed to pay and the Respondents-

claimants have agreed to receive a global compensation of

Rs.3,60,000/- (Rupees three lakhs sixty thousand only),

inclusive of statutory deposit, since this is a owners appeal

and it is agreed between the parties that appellant - owner of

the offending vehicle would deposit the compensation amount

including the amount already paid within an outer limit of five

months      from   today,   which    would      be   payable    in   two

installments namely 50% shall be paid within three months

and balance 50% shall be paid within two months thereafter.

Thus, it is agreed between the parties that in all five months

time is granted to appellant to deposit aforesaid amount. A

joint Memo is filed on behalf of the parties to this effect.

Same is accepted.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                 -2-</span><p id="p_4">


</p><p id="p_5">       3.   On such deposit, amount shall be released in

favour of the claimant.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><p id="p_7">       4.   The Miscellaneous First Appeal stands disposed of

in terms of the Joint Memo. The award of the Tribunal shall

stand modified accordingly. Draw up the award accordingly.


</p><p id="p_8">       Amount in deposit is ordered to be transmitted to the

jurisdictional Tribunal.
</p>
