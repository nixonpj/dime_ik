<div class="doc_title">Masomat Geeta Devi vs The State Of Jharkhand on 21 September, 2013</div><pre id="pre_1">         IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                     B.A. No. 7539 of 2013
                                  -----
         Masomat Geeta Devi @ Masomat Geeta                   .... Petitioner
                            -Versus-
         The State of Jharkhand         .... .... .... .... Opposite Party
                                  -----
    CORAM : HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE P.P. BHATT
                                  -----
         For the Petitioner       : Mr. Yogesh Modi, Advocate
         For the State            : Mr. Moti Gope, A.P.P.
                                  -----
2/ 21.09.2013</pre><p id="p_1">        The present bail application is filed under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Sections 439</a> and

          <a href="/doc/60736/" id="a_2">440</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure, seeking bail in connection with

          Giridih (M) P.S.Case No. 143 of 2013 (G.R. No.975 of 2013) for the

          alleged offence punishable under <a href="/doc/1439698/" id="a_3">Sections 304</a> (B)/ 34 of the <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_4">I.P.C</a>. and

          under <a href="/doc/751411/" id="a_5">Section 3</a>/ <a href="/doc/1023340/" id="a_6">4</a> of the Dowry Prohibition Act, which is now pending in

          the Court of learned Chief Judicial Magistrate, Giridih.

</p><p id="p_2">                       Learned counsel for the petitioner has submitted that the

          petitioner is an old lady aged about 60 years as well as she is mother in

          law of the deceased lady. It is further submitted that she has been falsely

          implicated in the alleged crime. It is further submitted that the petitioner

          is in judicial custody since 19.4.2013 and she is willing to abide by the

          conditions that may be imposed by the Court. It is further submitted that

          the investigation is over and the charge sheet has been submitted,

          therefore, now there is no possibility of tempering with the evidence. It is

          also submitted that the petitioner is a resident of Giridih and having the

          responsibility towards family and therefore she is not likely to flee away

          from the trial and her presence can be secured at the time of trial by

          imposing appropriate conditions.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                       Learned A.P.P. while opposing the bail application has

          submitted that looking to the seriousness of the offence and the charges

          levelled against the present petitioner, she may not be enlarged on bail.
</p><p id="p_4">                Considering the aforesaid rival submissions, having regard

to the nature and gravity of the accusation in light of facts and

circumstances of the present case, the petitioner namely Masomat Geeta

Devi @ Masomat Geeta is directed to be enlarged on bail, on furnishing

bail bond of Rs.10,000/- (Rupees ten thousand) with two sureties of the

like amount each to the satisfaction of the learned Chief Judicial

Magistrate, Giridih, in connection with Giridih (M) P.S.Case No.143 of

2013, corresponding to G.R. No.975 of 2013, subject to the following

conditions ;
</p>
<p id="p_5">1.    that applicant shall abide by the conditions of the bond executed
under chapter XXXIII of the Code of Criminal Procedure,
</p><p id="p_6">2.    that the applicant shall make himself available for interrogation by a
police officer as and when required,
</p><p id="p_7">3.    that applicant shall not commit an offence similar to the offence of which
he is accused,
</p><p id="p_8">4.    that applicant shall not directly or indirectly make any inducement threat
or promise to any person acquainted with the facts of the case so as to
dissuade him from disclosing such facts to the court or to any police officer or
tamper with the evidence,
</p><p id="p_9">5.    that the applicant shall mark his presence before the concerned
police station in any day of the first week of every month till the
commencement of trial, and
</p><p id="p_10">6.     that the applicant shall not leave India without the previous
permission of the Court.
</p>
<p id="p_11">                                               (P.P. Bhatt, J.)


SI/
