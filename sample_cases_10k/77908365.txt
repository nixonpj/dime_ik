          IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

             OFFICIAL LIQUDATOR REPORT NO. 24 of 2014
                                        In
                    COMPANY PETITION NO. 138 of 2012



FOR APPROVAL AND SIGNATURE:



HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE R.M.CHHAYA

NOTICE UNSERVED for the Respondent(s) No. 1

        CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE R.M.CHHAYA

                          Date : 26/03/2014


                         ORAL JUDGMENT</pre>


<p id="p_1">1.   By   this   report,   the   Official   Liquidator   has 
     prayed for the following reliefs:­


            "(A) This Hon'ble Court may be pleased 
            to recall the winding up order dated 
            23.12.2013 passed in Company Petition 
            No.138 of 2012 and may be pleased to 
            discharge   the   liquidator   being 
            Official   Liquidator   of   M/s.   Rupani 
            Dyes Intermediates Pvt. Ltd. 
</p>
<p id="p_2">            (B) This Hon'ble Court may be pleased 
            to   direct   the   petitioning   creditor 
            i.e.   M/s.   Pokarmal   Gurdayal   Pvt. 
            Ltd.,   to   initiate   appropriate 
            proceeding   before   the   Hon'ble   High 
            Court   jurisdiction   at   Mumbai   as   the 
            Regd.  Office  of   the   Company  situated 
            at Mumbai. 
</p>
<p id="p_3">            (C) Such other and further orders and 
            directions   as   this   Hon'ble   may 
            consider  just  and   appropriate  in   the 
            matter may also be passed.
</p>
<p id="p_4">            (D) This Hon'ble Court may be pleased 
            to stay the order dt. 23/12/13 passed 
            in Co. Petition No.138/12."
</p>
<p id="p_5">2.   The relevant facts arising in this  report are 
     as under:­


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                              Page 2 of 8</span>
        O/OLR/24/2014                            JUDGMENT




</p><p id="p_6">3.   The   original   petitioner   -   Pokarmal   Gurdayal 
     Pvt. Ltd. filed Company Petition No.138 of 2012 
     against   the   respondent   Company   -   M/s.   Rupani 
     Dyes Intermediates Pvt. Ltd. The record reveals 
     that the matter was heard and by an order dated 
     27.7.2012,   this   Court   issued   notice   making   it 
     returnable   on   21.8.2012.   Thereafter,   by   a 
     further   order   dated   18.4.2013,   this   Court 
     admitted the matter and directed the petitioner 
     to advertise the notice of admission in "Indian 
     Express",   English   daily   and   "Divya   Bhaskar", 
     Gujarati daily, both Ahmedabad editions. It is 
     a matter of record that after having issued a 
     statutory notice as provided under Sections 433 
     and   434   of   the   Companies   Act,   1956,   the 
     petition   was   filed   as   the   respondent   Company 
     did   not   give   any   reply   to   such   a   statutory 
     notice. It is also a matter of record that the 
     respondent Company appeared through the learned 
     advocate and filed its reply and the petitioner 
     filed   a   rejoinder   affidavit.   Ultimately, 
     considering   the   submissions   made   by   both   the 
     sides, by judgment dated 23.12.2013, this Court 
     allowed the petition and ordered winding up of 
     the   respondent   Company,   namely,   M/s.   Rupani 
     Dyes Intermediates Pvt. Ltd. and appointed the 
     Official   Liquidator   attached   to   this   Court   as 
     Official   Liquidator   of   the   respondent   Company 
     and   directed   the   Official   Liquidator   to   take 

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                           Page 3 of 8</span>
        O/OLR/24/2014                             JUDGMENT



     possession   of   movable   and   immovable   assets   of 
     the Company in liquidation i.e. the respondent 
     Company.   The   Official   Liquidator   has, 
     therefore,   filed   this   report   for   recalling   of 
     the said judgment dated 23.12.2013. 
</p>

<p id="p_7">4.   Mr. Bhargav Hasurkar, learned advocate for the 
     Official Liquidator submitted that on inquiry, 
     it has been found that the registered office of 
     the   respondent   Company   is   situated   at   125, 
     Udhyog   Shetra   Ind.   Estate,   LBS   Marg,   Nahur 
     Mulund (W), Mumbai, Maharashtra­400080.
</p>

<p id="p_8">5.   Mr. Hasurkar, learned advocate for the Official 
     Liquidator   has   therefore   contended   that   in 
     light   of   the   provisions   of   Section   10   of   the 
     Act, this Court shall have no jurisdiction and 
     therefore,   the   judgment   dated   23.12.2013 
     deserves to be recalled. 
</p>

<p id="p_9">6.   It is indeed painful to note that even though 
     the   petitioner   and   the   respondent   have   filed 
     affidavits,   it   was   never   pointed   out   to   this 
     Court   that   the   registered   Company   of   the 
     respondent   Company   is   not   within   the 
     territorial   jurisdiction   of   this   Court.   The 
     learned   advocate   for   the   Official   Liquidator 
     has   rightly   pointed   out   that   in   light   of   the 
     fact   that   the   registered   office   of   the 
     respondent Company is situated in the State of 

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                            Page 4 of 8</span>
        O/OLR/24/2014                             JUDGMENT



     Maharashtra, this Court has no jurisdiction to 
     entertain a petition under Section  433 of the 
     Act.   Mr.   Jani,   learned   advocate   for   the 
     original petitioner submitted that the petition 
     was preferred as per the the address given in 
     the   letterhead   of   the   respondent   Company   and 
     the   petitioner   was   under   impression   that   the 
     address   mentioned   in   it   is   the   registered 
     office   of   the   respondent   Company.   Mr.   Jani 
     further   submitted   that   such   an   error   has 
     inadvertently crept in the winding up petition. 
     Mr.   Jani   contended   that   the   same   is   not   done 
     purposely. 
</p>

<p id="p_10">7.   Though   the   respondent   Company   is   not   made   a 
     party to this report, this Court thought it fit 
     to   call   the   respondent   Company   through   its 
     learned   advocate   Mr.   Pavan   S.   Godiawala.   Mr. 
     Godiawala candidly submitted that the Executive 
     Director   of   the   respondent   Company   who   was 
     looking after day­to­day affairs of the Company 
     has   expired   pending   the   petition   and   as   on 
     date,   there   is   no   staff   of   the   respondent 
     Company   available.   Mr.   Godiawala   pointed   out 
     that   because   of   this   position,   the   reply   was 
     filed long back and therefore, it could not be 
     pointed  out that  the registered  office of the 
     respondent Company is situated in the State of 
     Maharashtra.   Mr.   Godiawala   further   submitted 
     that it is the duty of the petitioner to verify 

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                            Page 5 of 8</span>
        O/OLR/24/2014                            JUDGMENT



     the   documents   filed   and   maintained   by   the 
     Registrar   of   Companies   and   the   petition   could 
     not   have   been   filed   without   proper 
     verification. Mr. Godiawala relying upon Rule 6 
     of   the   Company   (Court)   Rules,   1959   submitted 
     that   as   provided   under   the   said   Rule,   the 
     proceedings of the Company Court is governed by 
     the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, wherever the 
     Company   (Court)   Rules,   1959   are   silent.   Mr. 
     Godiawala   further   submitted   that   in   light   of 
     the   fact   that   the   registered   office   of   the 
     respondent   Company   is   not   situated   within   the 
     territorial   jurisdiction   of   this   Court   as 
     provided under Order 7 Rule 10 of the Code of 
     Civil   Procedure,   1908,   which   provides   for 
     return of plaint, the judgment deserves to be 
     recalled and the  petition  be  returned  back to 
     the   petitioner   to   secure   the   ends   of   justice 
     and/or to prevent the abuse of process of law. 
     Mr.   Godiawala   has   also   relied   upon   the   order 
     dated   21.10.2002   passed   by   this   Court   in 
     Company   Petition   No.224   of   1999   and   has 
     submitted   that   as   the   petition   is   not 
     maintainable,   the   petitioner   be   permitted   to 
     withdraw the said petition. 
</p>

<p id="p_11">8.   Considering the facts brought on record, it is 
     found   that   the   petitioner   had   not   remained 
     cautious   and   without   considering   the   vital 
     provisions   of   the   law,   filed   the   winding   up 

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_5">                           Page 6 of 8</span>
        O/OLR/24/2014                            JUDGMENT



     petition.   The   strict   adherence   to   the 
     prescribed   rules   for   filing   the   petition   was 
     not   followed,   had   it   been   followed,   the   said 
     serious mistake could not have happened. In the 
     present   case,   it   is   found   that   there   is   a 
     casual   approach   while   filing   the   petition   and 
     it was not bought to the notice of this Court, 
     otherwise, the order issuing the notice itself 
     could   not   have   been   passed   and   orders 
     thereafter. 
</p>

<p id="p_12">9.   Considering   the   submissions   and   vital 
     provisions   of   the   law,   this   Court   is   having 
     wide   powers   to   recall   its   own   judgment/order 
     which   was   passed   due   to   factual   incorrect 
     statements   in   the   petition   and   hence, 
     considering   the   vital   provisions,   this   Court 
     hereby recalls the judgment of winding up and 
     also   the   order   of   admission   and   return   the 
     petition   i.e.   Company   Petition   No.138   of   2012 
     to be filed before the appropriate Court having 
     jurisdiction   and   permit   the   petitioner   to 
     withdraw   the   present   petition   before   the 
     appropriate   Court   having   jurisdiction.   The 
     petition stands disposed of as withdrawn with a 
     liberty to file the same as per the procedure 
     and   rules   of   the   respective   Court   having 
     jurisdiction. The withdrawal is permitted with 
     a   view   to   present   the   petition   before   the 
     appropriate   Court   having   jurisdiction   after 

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_6">                           Page 7 of 8</span>
            O/OLR/24/2014                        JUDGMENT



     complying with the procedure  and Rules of the 
     respective Court. 
</p>

<p id="p_13">10. Accordingly,   this   report   is   allowed   and   the 
     judgment   dated   23.12.2013   passed   in   Company 
     Petition   No.138   of   2012   and   other   earlier 
     orders   passed   in   Company   Petition   No.138   of 
     2012   stands   recalled   consequently   and   the 
     petition   is   disposed   of   as   withdrawn   with   a 
     liberty   to   present   it   before   the   appropriate 
     Court   having   jurisdiction.   This   Court   hereby 
     orders cost of Rs.2,500/­ each to be borne by 
     the petitioner and the respondent. 
</p>

<p id="p_14">11. It is all the more necessary to bring this fact 
     to the notice of the Registrar General of this 
     Court   and   hence,   a   copy   of   this   judgment   be 
     sent   to   the   Registrar   General   of   this   Court. 
     This Court is of the opinion that in order to 
     eradicate any such mistake, the Registry should 
     insist   upon   the   latest   search   report   of   the 
     Company against whom the winding up petition is 
     being filed so that it can be verified at the 
     threshold   that   the   registered   office   of   the 
     Company   is   situated   within   the   State   of 
     Gujarat. 
</p>

<p id="p_15">                                               (R.M.CHHAYA, J.)
