<div class="doc_title">Sridam Chandra Biswas vs State Of West Bengal &amp; Ors on 29 July, 2015</div><div class="doc_author">Author: Samapti Chatterjee</div>

<pre id="pre_1">7.2015
75                 W.P. 17567(W) of 2015

                     Sridam Chandra Biswas
                              -vs-
                   State of West Bengal &amp; Ors.

                Mr. Sourav Mitra
                                 ... for the Petitioner

                Ms. Subhra Nag
                             ... for the State

                The affidavit of service filed by the learned Advocate for the petitioner in

         Court today be kept on record.

                The learned Advocate for the petitioner submits that having attained the age of

         superannuation, the petitioner retired from service as an employee of the school in question on

         29.2.2008. The Pension Payment Order was first issued in favour of the petitioner on 4.8.2008

         and after introduction of ROPA, 2009 Pension Payment Order was issued on 13.6.2013. The

         petitioner received the retirement benefits on 28.8.2013, on the basis of ROPA 2009.

                The learned Advocate for the respondents that having regard to the order passed by the</pre>

<p id="p_1">         Hon'ble Division Bench dated 14th August, 2013 in MAT 1118 of 2013 read with the Circular

         dated 25th June, 2002 issued by the Joint Director of Treasuries and Accounts, West Bengal,

         there cannot be any dispute that the petitioner is entitled to interest on the amount of revised

         gratuity based on ROPA, 2009, to be payable from 19th May, 2009.

</p><p id="p_2">                In view thereof, this writ petition stands disposed of with a direction upon the respondents

         and particularly the concerned Treasury Officer to pay to the petitioner interest on revised

         gratuity @ 10% per annum from 19th May, 2009 till the date of actual payment within 16 weeks

         from the date of service of an authenticated copy of this order failing which additional interest @

         2% per annum shall be payable to the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                       There will be no order as to costs.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                Urgent certified photostat copy of this order, if applied for, shall be given to the parties as

         expeditiously as possible on compliance of all necessary formalities.



</p><p id="p_5">                                 ( Samapti Chatterjee, J. )
 AKD
