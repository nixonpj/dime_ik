<div class="doc_title">Smt. Leelabai vs Smt. Kaveri Bai on 8 October, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">                                1

                      W.P. No.3865/2014
8.10.2014</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Ms. Manjulata Chouhan, learned counsel for the
petitioner.
</p><p id="p_2">      Heard on the question of admission.
</p><p id="p_3">      This writ petition is at the instance of the plaintiff in the
suit challenging the order of the trial Court dated 27.3.2014,
whereby the petitioner's application under Section 151 of the
CPC has been rejected.
</p><p id="p_4">      The record reveals that the trial Court by the order
dated 4.1.2011, had found the document in question to be
insufficiently stamped and had ordered for payment of stamp
duty and penalty.     The petitioner had filed an application
under Section 151 of the CPC for modification of the said
order.    The trial Court by the impugned order dated
27.3.2014 has rejected the petitioner's application for
modification of the order, on the ground that as per law the
said order cannot be modified.
</p><p id="p_5">      Learned counsel appearing for the petitioner has failed
to point out any illegality in the view which has been taken by
the trial Court. The petitioner has also not challenged the
order dated 4.1.2011 before this Court, nor a copy of the
order dated 4.1.2011 has been filed before this Court. Even
a prayer for filing a copy of the said order dated 4.1.2011 has
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                   2</span>

not been made by counsel for the petitioner, when the matter
was heard by this Court.
</p><p id="p_6">         The scope of interference in a writ petition under <a href="/doc/1331149/" id="a_1">Article
227</a> of the Constitution is limited. In the present matter, no
case for interference in the impugned order of the trial Court
is made out.
</p><p id="p_7">         The writ petition is accordingly rejected.
         C.C. as per rules.
</p>


<p id="p_8">                                        (Prakash Shrivastava)
                                               Judge
trilok
