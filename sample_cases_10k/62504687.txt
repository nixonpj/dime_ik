<div class="doc_title">Ramayan Singh vs State Of Bihar &amp; Anr on 2 December, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.54516 of 2015
                       Arising Out of PS.Case No. -132 Year- 2015 Thana -NAWANGAR District- BUXAR
                 ======================================================
                 Ramayan Singh S/o Late Maharaj Singh,
                                                                      .... .... Petitioner/s
                                                  Versus
                 1. The State of Bihar
                 2. Saurabh Kumar, Assistant Electricity Engineer, Electricity Supply Sub-
                 Division, Dumraon, District- Buxar

                                                                 .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :  Mr. Rakesh Singh, Advocate
                 For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr. Upendra Kumar(App)
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE DINESH KUMAR SINGH
                                                   ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">2   02-12-2015                Heard learned counsels for the petitioner and the South

                  Bihar Power Distribution Company Limited.

</p><p id="p_2">                              The petitioner is apprehending arrest in a case registered

                  for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/132967048/" id="a_1">Sections 135</a> and <a href="/doc/132967048/" id="a_2">136</a> of the

                  Electricity Act.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                              It is alleged that due to arrear of electricity charges to

                  the tune of Rs.2,39,110/- the electric connection of the petitioner

                  was disconnected. Subsequently, the petitioner consumed the

                  electricity energy unauthorisedly causing loss to the tune of

                  Rs.44,448/-.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                              It is submitted by learned counsel for the petitioner that

                  accusation of consuming electricity energy gets clouded with the

                  statement of the Junior Engineer before the learned Court below to
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">             Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.54516 of 2015 (2) dt.02-12-2015</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                       2/2</span>




                         the effect that after disconnection of electric connection of the

                         petitioner new connection was given to him.

</p><p id="p_5">                                       The aforesaid fact has not been controverted by the

                         learned counsel representing the South Bihar Power Distribution

                         Company.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                       Learned counsel for the petitioner further submits that

                         the petitioner is paying the arrears of energy and undertakes to pay

                         the arrears, if any, remains due. A statement has been made in

                         paragraph 3 of the petition that the petitioner has no criminal

                         antecedent.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                                       Considering the aforesaid facts, let the above named

                         petitioner be released on anticipatory bail, in the event of arrest or

                         surrender before the learned Court below within a period of 12

                         weeks from today, on furnishing bail bond of `10,000/- (ten

                         thousand) with two sureties of the like amount each to the

                         satisfaction of the learned CJM, Buxar in connection with

                         Nawanagar P.S. Case No.132 of 2015, subject to the conditions as

                         laid down under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_3">Section 438(2)</a> Cr.P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_8">


</p><p id="p_9">                                                    (Dinesh Kumar Singh, J)
Ashwini/-
</p>
