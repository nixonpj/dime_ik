<div class="doc_title">Vikram Nath vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 3 December, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">                                   M.Cr.C.No.18556 of 2014




                                                                                  1




03/12/2014
                      Shri Ashish Sinha, Advocate for the applicant.
                      Shri Y.D.Yadav, Panel Lawyer for the respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">                      Heard finally. Case diary is perused.
</p><p id="p_2">                      This is first bail application on behalf of the applicant
             under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of Cr.P.C.
</p><p id="p_3">                      The applicant is in custody since 28.08.2014 in connection
             with Crime No.212/2014 registered at Police Station Ichhawar,
             District Sehore (M.P.) for the offence punishable under <a href="/doc/1873755/" id="a_2">Sections
             457</a> and <a href="/doc/839778/" id="a_3">380</a> of IPC .
</p><p id="p_4">                      It is submitted by learned counsel for the applicant that the
             applicant is innocent and has been falsely implicated in the case.
             It is further submitted that just on the basis of suspicion and on
             the memorandum of other co-accused, false case has been
             registered against the applicant. The investigation is complete.
             He is in custody since 28.08.14 and conclusion of trial would
             take considerable time. On the aforesaid grounds, it is prayed that
             the applicant be released on bail.
</p><p id="p_5">                      Learned Panel Lawyer for the State vehemently opposed
             the application on the ground that applicant is involved in various
             cases of theft of Government property.
</p><p id="p_6">                      At the stage of considering bail matters it would not be
             proper for the Court to express any opinion on the merits or
             demerits of the prosecution case as well as the defence as any
             expression of opinion by the Court would undoubtedly affect the
             trial.
</p><p id="p_7">                           M.Cr.C.No.18556 of 2014




<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                                          2</span>


            Keeping in view the submissions made by learned counsel
      for the parties and the facts and circumstances of the case , the
      evidence collected by the prosecution , and looking to the nature
      and gravity of the accusation, I find that this is not a fit case for
      grant of bail to the applicant. Consequently, his application under
      <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_4">Section 439</a> of the Cr.P.C. is hereby rejected .</p><pre id="pre_2">

                                              (SUBHASH KAKADE)
Jk.                                                      JUDGE
