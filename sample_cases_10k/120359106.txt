<div class="doc_title">Manoj Prasad vs The State Of Bihar on 3 November, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                   IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                Criminal Miscellaneous No.45185 of 2015
                           Arising Out of PS.Case No. -246 Year- 2015 Thana -NAUTAN District-
                                             WESTCHAMPARAN(BETTIAH)
                 ======================================================
                 Manoj Prasad son of Sri Raghav Mahto
                                                         .... .... Petitioner/s
                                                 Versus
                 The State of Bihar

                                                                .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :  Mr. Umesh Chandra Verma, Advocate
                 For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr. Ashok Kr.Singh(App)
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE DINESH KUMAR SINGH
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   03-11-2015                 Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and the State.

</p><p id="p_2">                               The petitioner is apprehending his arrest in a case

                 registered for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1873755/" id="a_1">Sections 457</a> and <a href="/doc/839778/" id="a_2">380</a>

                 of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                               The prosecution case is that on 30.06.2015 jewellary

                 worth Rs. 1,00,000/- and cash Rs. 15,000/- were stolen from the

                 house of the informant. In the morning the police came and asked

                 the informant to locate the accused, thereafter the informant came

                 to know that the theft was committed by this petitioner and co-

                 accused Basant Prasad. It is further alleged that the petitioner and

                 co-accused Basant Prasad took dinner in the house of the

                 informant on the alleged night of occurrence.

</p><p id="p_4">                               It is submitted by the learned counsel for the

                 petitioner that for the occurrence of 30.06.2015 the First
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">             Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.45185 of 2015 (2) dt.03-11-2015</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                       2/2</span>




                         Information Report was registered on 10.07.2015, whereas in the

                         morning police came at the house of the informant, but no F.I.R.

                         was lodged, hence only on suspicion the accusation has been

                         levelled. There is no recovery from the possession of the

                         petitioner. Statement has been made in para-3 of the petition that

                         petitioner has no criminal antecedent.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                          Considering the aforesaid facts, let the above named

                         petitioner be released on bail in the event of his arrest or surrender

                         before the learned court below within a period of twelve weeks

                         from today, on furnishing bail bonds of Rs. 10,000/- (Ten

                         Thousand) with two sureties of the like amount each to the

                         satisfaction of learned Sri N.P. Singh, Judicial Magistrate, Ist

                         Class, Bettiah, West Champaran in connection with Nautan

                         (Jagdishpur) P.S. Case No. 246 of 2015, subject to conditions as

                         laid down under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_3">Section 438(2)</a> of the Code of Criminal

                         Procedure.
</p>
<p id="p_6">


</p><p id="p_7">                                                    (Dinesh Kumar Singh, J)
Ashwini/-
</p>
