<div class="doc_title">Maya Ram Baiga vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 2 September, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">                                     M.Cr.C.No.13386           of 2014




                                                                                    1



                                  <a href="/doc/140521593/" id="a_1">Maya Ram Baiga vs State of M.P</a>.


02 / 9 / 2 0 1 4
                         Shri Kaustubh Singh, Advocate for the applicant.
                         Shri R.P.Tiwari, Govt. Advocate for the respondent /</pre>
<p id="p_1">                   State.
</p><p id="p_2">                         Heard finally. Case diary is perused.
                         This is the first bail application on behalf of the
                   applicant under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_2">Section 439</a> of Cr.P.C. The applicant is in
                   custody since 10/7 / 2014 in connection with Crime No.
                   107/2014 registered at Police Station Singhpur            District
                   Shahdol (M.P.) for the offence punishable under <a href="/doc/962373/" id="a_3">Sections
                   363</a>, <a href="/doc/1146308/" id="a_4">366</a> (ka) and <a href="/doc/1279834/" id="a_5">Section 376</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_6">34</a> of IPC and Section 4 of
                   Protection of Children From Sexual Offence Act, 2012.
</p><p id="p_3">                         It is submitted by learned counsel for the applicant
                   that the applicant is innocent and has been falsely
                   implicated in the case. It is further submitted that there is
                   nothing   which   can establish    that     the applicant     had
                   committed rape with the prosecutrix and main accused is
                   Asan Baiga, who has been released on bail by the trial
                   Court. The applicant is in custody and conclusion of trial
                   would take considerable time. On the aforesaid grounds,
                   it is prayed that the applicant be released on bail.
</p><p id="p_4">                         Learned Govt. Advocate for the State vehemently
                   opposed the application.
</p><p id="p_5">                         Considering    the    totality   of     the     facts   and
                   circumstances of the case coupled with the material
                   available on record, without commenting upon the merits
                   of the case, the application under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_7">Section 439</a> of Cr.P.C.
</p><p id="p_6">                           M.Cr.C.No.13386        of 2014




<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                                  2</span>



       on behalf of applicant deserves to be and is hereby
       allowed.
</p><p id="p_7">              It is directed that applicant Maya Ram Baiga be
       released on bail on furnishing a personal bond in the sum
       of Rs.25,000 / - (Rupees Twenty Five Thousand Only)
       with   one solvent    surety in the like amount      to the
       satisfaction of the committal Court /trial Court securing
       applicant's presence before the trial Court on all the dates
       of hearing fixed in this regard during trial.
</p><p id="p_8">              Certified copy as per rules.
</p><p id="p_9">                                              (SUBHASH KAKADE)
                                                   JUDGE

SJ/-
</p>
