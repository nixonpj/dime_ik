<div class="doc_title">Ajim Khan vs The State Of Bihar on 19 September, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                   IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                 Criminal Miscellaneous No.46568 of 2018
                  Arising Out of PS.Case No. -668 Year- 2017 Thana -SA SARAM NAGAR District- SASARAM
                                                          (ROHTAS)
                 ======================================================
                 1. Ajim Khan S/o Late Farid Khan, R/o Vill.- Makaraine, P.S.-
                 Dalmiyanagar, District- Rohtas at Sasaram. .... .... Petitioner/s
                                                   Versus
                 1. The State of Bihar                      .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     : Mr. Md. Jubair Ansari
                 For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr. Sri Satyendra Narayan Singh
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE PRABHAT KUMAR
                 JHA
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   19-09-2018                    Heard both sides.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                  The petitioner apprehends his arrest in Sasaram

                 Model P.S. case No. 668 of 2017 for the offences punishable

                 under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_1">Section 302</a>, <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_2">120B</a> of the Indian Penal Code and under

                 <a href="/doc/244673/" id="a_3">Section 27</a> of the Arms Act.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                  The learned counsel for the petitioner submits that

                 although the prayer of petitioner for anticipatory bail was earlier

                 rejected vide order dated 21.02.2018 passed in Cr. Misc. No.

                 8535 of 2018 but from the allegations made it appears that the

                 entire prosecution case is concocted. The petitioner is a migratory

                 labour. It is beyond imagination that four persons were riding on a

                 motor cycle. On account of some enmity the petitioner has been

                 implicated in the case but no allegation of assault is made against

                 the petitioner. It is further submitted that Sifal Beg @ Seraj Beg @
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">         Patna High Court Cr.M isc. No.46568 of 2018 (2) dt.19-09-2018</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                    2/2</span>




                     Shimla Khan and Imroz Khan have already been granted

                     anticipatory bail by different benches of this court vide order dated

                     10.07.2018 passed in Cr. Misc. Nos. 32037 of 2018 and 31098 of

                     2018 respectively. Therefore, the matter requires rethinking and

                     the petitioner deserves anticipatory bail but I find that non else

                     than the informant, Javed Khan, himself alleged that while he was

                     returning along with Khurshid Alam @ Rinku and Tarakhatun on

                     auto rickshaw from Shivsagar block and Khurshid Alam was

                     driving the vehicle but in the meantime Kallu Khan @ Firoz Khan,

                     Shimla Khan, Ajim Khan, the petitioner, and Sonu Khan came on

                     a motor cycle and stopped the auto rickshaw. Kallu Khan fired at

                     his brother causing his death in the hospital. There appears that

                     petitioner along with other accused persons were accompanying

                     after having conspired among themselves and in pursuance of the

                     aforesaid conspiracy Kallu Khan made firing causing the death of

                     brother of informant.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                        I do not find any fresh ground to reconsider the

                     prayer of petitioner for anticipatory bail. Accordingly, the same is

                     once again rejected.
</p>
<p id="p_5">
</p><p id="p_6">                                                 (Prabhat Kumar Jha, J)
BKS/-
</p>
