<div class="doc_title">Mayandi Thevar vs The Revenue Divisional Officer on 7 December, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">        

 

BEFORE THE MADURAI BENCH OF MADRAS HIGH COURT               

DATED : 07.12.2015  

CORAM   
THE HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE K.RAVICHANDRABAABU               

W.P.(MD)No.21775 of 2015   

Mayandi Thevar                                          ... Petitioner          
        
Vs.

1.The Revenue Divisional Officer,
   Usilampatti,
   Madurai District.

2.The Tahsildar,
   Usilampatti,
   Madurai District.

3.Sudha 
4.Muthulakshmi                                          ...Respondents 

PRAYER: Writ Petition is filed under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the Constitution of India
for the issue of a Writ of Mandamus to direct the first respondent to pass
orders on the appeal preferred by the petitioner in Na.Ka.No.7934/2013/a2
dated 17.12.2013 within a time frame.

!For Petitioner         : Mr.R.J.Karthick
^For Respondents        : Mr.A.Muthukaruppan  
                        Additional Government Pleader 
                        for R.1 and R.2

:ORDER  
</pre>

<p id="p_1">         Mr.A.Muthukaruppan, learned Additional Government Pleader takes notice 
for the respondents 1 and 2.
</p>
<p id="p_2">        2. By consent, the writ petition is taken up for disposal at the
admission stage itself.
</p>
<p id="p_3">        3. Since the Writ Petition is being disposed of by passing an order
without affecting the interest of the private respondents 3 and 4, notice to
those respondents is dispensed with.
</p>
<p id="p_4">        4. The petitioner seeks for a Mandamus directing the first respondent
to dispose of the appeal preferred by him as against the order passed by the
second respondent-Tahsildar in respect of the patta proceedings.  It is
stated that such appeal was filed by the petitioner in the month of December
2013 itself.  It is further stated that even though subsequent hearings were
conducted by the first respondent, he has not passed any order so far.
Therefore, without going into the merits and contentions raised by the
petitioner in respect of the merits of the appeal preferred before the first
respondent, I only direct the first respondent to consider the appeal filed
by the petitioner and pass orders on the same on merits and in accordance
with law, after hearing the respondents 3 and 4 as well.  Such exercise shall
be done by the first respondent within a period of twelve weeks from the date
of receipt of a copy of this order.
</p><p id="p_5">        5. With the above directions, the Writ Petition is disposed of. No
costs.
</p>
<p id="p_6">To
</p><p id="p_7">1.The Revenue Divisional Officer,
   Usilampatti, Madurai District.
</p><p id="p_8">2.The Tahsildar,
   Usilampatti, Madurai District..
