                             IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA
                                          AT CHANDIGARH


                                       Criminal Misc.No.M-21629 of 2015
                                       Date of Decision: September 03, 2015

           Rajinder Kumar
                                                                ...Petitioner
                                  Versus
           State of Punjab
                                                                ...Respondent</pre>

<p id="p_1">           CORAM: HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE NARESH KUMAR SANGHI

           Present: Mr.Sudhir Sharma, Advocate,
                    for the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                             Mr.P.S.Ghuman, Addl.AG, Punjab.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                             Mr.Vivek Singla, Advocate,
                             for the informant.
</p>
<p id="p_4">           Naresh Kumar Sanghi, J.(Oral)
                   Prayer in this petition, filed under Section 438,

           <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_1">Cr.P.C</a>., is for grant of anticipatory bail to the petitioner,

           Rajinder Kumar, who has been booked for having committed

           the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_2">Sections 120-B</a>, <a href="/doc/1270615/" id="a_3">380</a> and <a href="/doc/664789/" id="a_4">406</a>, <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_5">IPC</a>,

           in a case arising out of FIR No.34, dated 27.05.2015, registered

           at Police Station, Nayagaon, S.A.S.Nagar (Mohali).

</p><p id="p_5">                             Learned counsel for the State, on instructions from SI

           Harbhinder Kumar, Police Station, Nayagaon, S.A.S.Nagar

           (Mohali) submits that in compliance of the order dated

           15.07.2015 passed by this Court, the petitioner did join the

           investigation however, the documents could not be recovered

           from the petitioner.            He fairly concedes that silver articles

           recovered from the kidnappers of the nephew of Seema
SEEMA RANI
2015.09.04 10:53
I attest the accuracy and
authencity of the document
 Criminal Misc.No.M-21629 of 2015                  [2]

Sharma, co-accused of the petitioner, are in possession of the

Chandigarh police. He also concedes that the custodial

interrogation of the petitioner is not required. He further

concedes that the case for forging the documents has not been

registered against the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_6">            Learned counsel for the informant/complainant has

vehemently opposed the             grant of anticipatory bail to the

petitioner.    He contends that not only the documents were

forged but the petitioner has also filed the present petition on

wrong averments.           On the query by this Court, he fairly

concedes that in the present case, the petitioner is not required

for forging the documents as per police case.

</p><p id="p_7">            I have heard learned counsel for the parties and with

their able assistance gone through the material available on

record.
</p>
<p id="p_8">            The     main     allegations   were   with   regard    to

misappropriation of the silver ornaments which were allegedly

recovered by the Chandigarh police in FIR No.33 dated

18.01.2014 registered at Police Station, Sector 39, Chandigarh.

The joining of the investigation by the petitioner has not been

denied by learned counsel for the State.

</p><p id="p_9">            In view of the totality of the facts and circumstances of

the case, the present petition is allowed.        Interim directions

issued by this Court vide order dated 15.07.2015 are made
 Criminal Misc.No.M-21629 of 2015                [3]

absolute.
</p>
<p id="p_10">            The petitioner shall continue to join the investigation

as and when required to do so and abide by all the terms and

conditions as laid down under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_6">Section 438(2)</a>, <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_7">Cr.P.C</a>.</p><pre id="pre_2">



September 03, 2015                      (Naresh Kumar Sanghi)
seema                                           Judge
</pre><p id="p_11"> Criminal Misc.No.M-21629 of 2015   [4]
