<div class="doc_title">C.S. Construction Ganges [Jv vs Union Of India &amp; Ors on 3 September, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                           ORDER SHEET
                           AP 540/2017
                  IN THE HIGH COURT AT CALCUTTA
              Ordinary Original Civil Jurisdiction
                          ORIGINAL SIDE



                   C.S. CONSTRUCTION GANGES [JV]
                               Versus
                       UNION OF INDIA &amp; ORS.


   BEFORE:
   The Hon'ble JUSTICE ASHIS KUMAR CHAKRABORTY

   Date : 3rd September, 2018.

                                    Mr.Anirban Ray,
                                    Mr.Subhasis Pyne, Advs.,
                                    for the petitioner.

                                    Ms.Aparna Banerjee, Adv.,
                                    for the respondent.</pre>


<p id="p_1">    The    Court   :   In    this    application   the   petitioner   has

prayed for an order of injunction restraining the respondent

railway from encashing the bank guarantee furnished by it

towards    performance      guarantee    or   forfeiting   the   security

deposit.
</p>
<p id="p_2">    It is submitted by Ms. Banerjee, learned Advocate for

the respondent that the construction agreement under which

the petitioner furnished the performance bank guarantee and

the security deposit contains an arbitration agreement for
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                             2</span>


adjudication        of    all    disputes       between   the    parties    through

arbitration. The respondent railway has encashed the bank

guarantee and forfeited the security amount provided by the

petitioner, inter alia, on the ground that the petitioner

failed to perform the said contract and the said contract has

been    terminated.            According    to     the    respondent,      all   the

disputes between the parties with regard to the validity of

the termination of the contract as well as the validity of

the encashment of the performance guarantee and forfeiture of

the security deposit are to be decided by the arbitrator in

the arbitration proceeding.
</p>
<p id="p_3">       In view of the settled principle of law, the present

application, as filed by the petitioner by impleading the

bank which is not a party either to the contract or the

arbitration agreement is not maintainable. However, Mr. Ray

appearing     for        the    petitioner       submitted     that   a   direction

should be passed against the respondents railway to continue

to furnish quarterly statement of account with regard to the

security deposit in terms of the order dated July 21, 2017

passed by a learned Single Judge of this Court.

</p><p id="p_4">       When   the    application       itself      is    not   maintainable,     the

petitioner cannot obtain any order in this application.
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                      3</span>


</p><p id="p_5">         Accordingly, the application, AP No.540 of 2017, stands

dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_6">         The   interim   order   passed   on   July   21,   2017   stands

vacated.
</p>
<p id="p_7">         There shall, however, be no order as to costs.




</p><p id="p_8">                                     (ASHIS KUMAR CHAKRABORTY, J.)

S.Das
AR[CR]
 </p>
