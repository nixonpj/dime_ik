<div class="doc_title">Syndicate Bank vs Sh. Vijay Singh Malik on 6 September, 2007</div><div class="doc_author">Author: Sh. N.K. Sharma</div>

<pre id="pre_1">                          1

       IN THE COURT OF SH. N. K. SHARMA,
      ADDITIONAL DISTRICT JUDGE, DELHI

SUIT NO. :- 16/2007

Syndicate Bank,
Having its Head Office at Manipal,
Karnataka and a Branch amongst
others at Palam Village, New Delhi
through its Chief Manager.      ..... Plaintiff

Versus

Sh. Vijay Singh Malik
S/o Sh. Jawala Singh Malik
Prop. Of M/s V. V. Constructions,
R/o :- 7, MBK Apartments,
Plot No. 6, Sector-13, Dwarka,
New Delhi.

Also available at :-

Sh. Vijay Singh Malik
S/o Sh. Jawala Singh Malik
House No. 725/20, Jail Road,
Rohtak, Haryana.                  ...... Defendant

DATE OF INSTITUTION OF SUIT :- 07/02/2007
RESERVED FOR JUDGMENT ON :- 01/09/2007
DATE OF DECISION            :- 06/09/2007

     SUIT FOR RECOVERY OF RS. 6,19,028.10.

EX-PARTE JUDGMENT :-
           The brief facts, as stated in the plaint are
that plaintiff, namely, Syndicate Bank, is a body
corporate constituted under the Banking Companies
(Acquisition &amp; <a href="/doc/984780/" id="a_1">Transfer of Undertaking) Act</a>, 1970,
                             2

having its Office at Manipal - 576119, Udupi District
Karnataka and having branches among others Branch
at Palam Village, New Delhi through its Chief
Manager(hereinafter as "Plaintiff").
2.          It is stated that Sh. S. H. Rao is posted as
Chief Manager at the aforesaid branch of the Bank and
is holding a Power of Attorney, executed by Directors
of the Bank in his favour by virtue of which he is
competent to sign, verify and institute suits on behalf
of the Bank and he is also fully conversant with the
facts, as such in a position to depose to the facts stated
therein and institute the present suit.
3.          It is further stated that on the request and
application of the defendant on 09/02/2004 and
16/03/2004</pre><p id="p_1">, as Proprietor of M/s V. V. Constructions,
a Temporary Overdraft Facility/Loan of Rs. 3,00,000/-
was sanctioned and granted by the plaintiff under CA
No. 268 and he assured and agreed to obey the terms
and conditions in this regard and to pay the same by
04/03/2004 and       22/03/2004        alongwith interest
@ 16 %.
</p><p id="p_2">4.          It is further stated that defendant availed
and   withdrawn     the   aforesaid     TOD   Facility   of
Rs. 3,00,000/- and on 18/05/2004 he had also
confirmed the outstanding dues of Rs,. 3,06,005 as on
30/04/2004. It is stated that apart from the TOD
Facility, on 27/02/2004 he also approached the Bank
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                3</span>

for its Global Credit Card and after completing the
necessary formalities and acceptance of the terms and
conditions for issuance of Global Credit Card, the same
was     granted     to   him       by     the   plaintiff     vide
No. 4090310000195780 and he used the same, but
had not paid the outstanding dues of the plaintiff.
</p><p id="p_3">5.          It is further stated that on 30/06/2004
defendant acknowledged the debt/outstanding of the
TPD Facility given in the Current Account No. 268 and
Credit Card No. 4090310000195780 and he promised
to clear the outstanding dues latest by 31/07/2004
and 15/07/2004 respectively, but had failed to pay the
outstanding dues as promised vide letters dated
30/06/2004 to the plaintiff and despite several written
requests of the plaintiff.
</p><p id="p_4">6.          It is further stated that plaintiff finally
issued a legal notice dated 30/01/2006 to the
defendant to pay the total outstanding amount and the
same was served           by hand/personally at House
No. 725/20, Jail Road, Rohtak, Haryana to the father
of the defendant.
</p><p id="p_5">7.          It is further stated that as per the books of
accounts, maintained by the plaintiff in its normal and
usual   course    of     banking        business,   a   sum     of
Rs. 4,76,460/- as on 17/01/2007 is due and
outstanding in the TOD/Loan Account No. NPL
01/2005 of the defendant. It is further stated that he is
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                            4</span>

also liable to pay interest @ 16.5 % per annum,
compounded monthly w.e.f. 18/01/2007 till the date
of realisation and also apart from the above, he is
liable to pay the amount of Rs. 1,42,568.10 alongwith
interest @ 24 % per annum alongwith service tax
@ 12.24 % on service charges, compounded monthly
w.e.f. 18/01/2007 till the date of realisation in regard
to     the      Global     Credit      Card      bearing
No. 4090310000195780 and further that rate of
interest is variable and payable as per Reserve Bank of
India Rate/Banks directions from time to time and the
variation in the rate of interest was duly affixed on the
Notice Board of the plaintiff bank/Branch.
</p><p id="p_6">8.           Hence it is prayed that a decree for a sum
of Rs. 4,76,460/- as on 17/01/2007 alongwith
pendente lite and future interest @ 16.5 % per annum,
compounded monthly w.e.f. 18/01/2007 till the date
of realisation be passed in favour of the plaintiff and
against the defendant and also it is prayed that a
decree against the defendant be passed to pay
Rs. 1,42,568.10 alongwith interest @ 24 % per annum
alongwith service tax @ 12.24 % on service charges,
compounded monthly w.e.f. 18/01/2007 till the date
of realisation in regard to the Global Credit Card
bearing No. 4090310000195780.
</p><p id="p_7">9.           Defendant could not be served by ordinary
way and the summons were returned with the report
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                            5</span>

that "he is not residing at the given address".
Thereafter on plaintiff's application under Order 5 Rule
20 CPC he was served by way of publication in the
newspaper "Statesman" dated 07th June, 2007. Despite
service by way of publication, none appeared on his
behalf and he was proceeded ex-parte vide order dated
23/07/2007.
</p><p id="p_8">10.        In the ex-parte evidence plaintiff has
examined its Chief Manager Sh. Chander Shekher
Ganeja as PW-1.
</p><p id="p_9">11.        I have heard ld. Counsel for the plaintiff
and perused the record.
</p><p id="p_10">12.        PW-1 Sh. Chander Shekher Gagneja has
stated that he has been authorised to sign, verify and
institute the present suit by virtue of General Power of
Attorney dated     10/04/1979,    copy   of   which is
Ex. PW1/1. He has stated that on the request and
application dated 09/02/2004 and 16/03/2004 of the
defendant, a loan of Rs. 3,00,000/- was sanctioned
and granted by the plaintiff under CA No. 268 and
defendant accepted and agreed to obey the terms and
conditions in this regard and to repay the same by
04/03/2004 and 22/03/2004 with interest @ 16 %
per annum. The original letter dated 09/02/2004 and
16/03/2004, signed by the defendant are Ex. PW1/2
and Ex. PW1/3.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                            6</span>

<p id="p_11">13.          He further stated that defendant had
availed and withdrawn the aforesaid TOD Facility of
Rs. 3,00,000/- and on 18/05/2004 the defendant had
also confirmed the outstanding dues of Rs. 3,06,005/-
as on 30/04/2004 and the said confirmation letter
dated 18/05/2004, which is signed by the defendant
in bank format is Ex. PW1/4 and the same letter in
defendant's letterhead is Ex. PW1/5.
</p><p id="p_12">14.          He further stated that on 30/06/2004 the
defendant also acknowledged the debt/outstanding of
the TOD Facility given in the Current Account No. 268
and the Credit Card No. 4090310000195780. The
original letter of acknowledgment dated 30/06/2004
signed by the defendant towards Current Account
No. 268 is Ex. PW1/6 and towards Credit Card is
Ex. PW1/7.
</p><p id="p_13">15.          He further stated that defendant failed to
pay the outstanding dues despite several letters and
reminders and plaintiff sent legal notice dated
30/01/2006, copy of which is Mark-A. He has proved
the statement of account dated 17/01/2007 as
Ex. PW1/8 vide which there is an outstanding in the
TOD/loan account of the defendant. He further stated
that plaintiff is entitled for a decree, as prayed in the
suit.
</p><p id="p_14">16.          The testimony of PW-1 Sh. Chander
Shekher Gagneja is un-controverted and unchallenged.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_5">                             7</span>

<p id="p_15">The testimony of this witness is corroborated and
supported by the documentary evidence as proved by
him during his testimony, under these circumstances I
do not find any reason to disbelieve the testimony of
this witness.
</p><p id="p_16">17.         In view of above, the suit of the plaintiff is
decreed for a sum of Rs. 4,76,460/- as on 17/01/2007
alongwith pendente lite and future interest @ 16.5 %
per annum, compounded monthly w.e.f. 18/01/2007
till the date of realisation is passed in favour of the
plaintiff and against the defendant and further a
decree against the defendant is passed to pay
Rs. 1,42,568.10 alongwith interest @ 24 % per annum
alongwith service tax @ 12.24 % on service charges,
compounded monthly w.e.f. 18/01/2007 till the date
of realisation in regard to the Global Credit Card
bearing No. 4090310000195780. The plaintiff is also
awarded the cost of the suit.
</p><p id="p_17">            Decree Sheet be prepared accordingly. File
be consigned to Record Room.
</p>



<pre id="pre_2">ANNOUNCED IN OPEN              (N. K. SHARMA)
COURT ON 06/09/2007        ADDL. DISTT. JUDGE : DELHI
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_6">                             8</span>

SUIT NO. :- 16/2007
06/09/2007
</pre><p id="p_18">Present :- Proxy counsel for the plaintiff.
</p><p id="p_19">             Defendant is ex-parte.
</p><p id="p_20">             Vide    separate     ex-parte    judgment
announced today, the suit of the plaintiff is decreed
with cost.
</p><p id="p_21">             Decree Sheet be prepared accordingly. File
be consigned to Record Room.
</p>

<p id="p_22">                             (N. K. SHARMA)
                      ADDL. DISTT. JUDGE : DELHI
                               06/09/2007
