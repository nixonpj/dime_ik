                                    Criminal Appeal (DB) No.174 of 2015
                          Arising Out of PS.Case No. -34 Year- 2014 Thana -CHOUTARWA District-
                                             WESTCHAMPARAN(BETTIAH)
                 ======================================================
                 1. Santosh Yadav
                 2. Raju Yadav
                 3. Govind Yadav All three sons of Satyadeo Yadav
                 4. Raghunath Yadav Son of Briksha Yadav
                  All are resident of Village - Nadava Pipara, P.S. Chautarwa, District - West
                 Champaran.

                                                                                .... ....   Appellants
                                                       Versus
                 The State of Bihar.

                                                                .... .... Respondent
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Appellant/s  : Mr. Kanhaiya Prasad Singh
                 For the Respondent/s : Mr. A.K.Sinha (App)
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE KISHORE KUMAR
                 MANDAL
                           and
                           HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE SANJAY KUMAR
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>
<p id="p_1">                 (Per: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE KISHORE KUMAR MANDAL)

3   23-03-2017               Ref.:-I.A. No. 2417 of 2016

                             We have heard Mr. Kanhaiya Prasad Singh, learned

                  Senior Counsel in support of the interlocutory application for

                  suspension of sentence and grant of bail to all the four (04)

                  appellants during the pendency of the appeal as well as the

                  learned APP for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                             The appellants have been held guilty and convicted

                  under <a href="/doc/1258372/" id="a_1">Sections 147</a>, <a href="/doc/763672/" id="a_2">148</a>, <a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_3">149</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_4">323</a>, <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_5">324</a>, <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_6">302</a>, <a href="/doc/162506/" id="a_7">447</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_8">504</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_9">506</a> of

                  the Indian Penal Code and sentenced to undergo R.I. for life

                  under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_10">Sections 302</a>/<a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_11">149</a> of the Indian Penal Code, besides

                  imposition of fine with default clause.
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">             Patna High Court CR. APP (DB) No.174 of 2015 (3) dt.23-03-2017</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                      2/2</span>




</p><p id="p_3">                                      Leaned counsel for the appellants submits that the

                           allegation of having assaulted the son of the informant is

                           general and omnibus. In this connection, he has drawn our

                           attention to the evidence of P.W 3 where he has alleged that the

                           deceased was only fisted and slapped by the accused persons.

</p><p id="p_4">                                       Be that as it may, considering the allegation and other

                           materials available on record(s), we are inclined to direct their

                           release on bail. During the pendency of the appeal, the sentence

                           imposed on the appellant(s) be suspended and they be released

                           on bail on furnishing bail bonds of Rs. 10,000/- (Ten thousand)

                           each with two sureties of the like amount to the satisfaction of

                           learned Adhoc-1st Additional District and Sessions Judge,

                           Bagaha, West Champaran in connection with Sessions Trial

                           No. 442 of 2014 arising out of Chautarwa P.S. Case No. 34 of

                           2014, subject to condition that:
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                       (i) One of the bailors shall be own/close family

                           members of each appellant.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                       I.A. No. 2417 of 2016 stands disposed of.



</p><p id="p_7">                                                 (Kishore Kumar Mandal, J)



                                                       (Sanjay Kumar, J)
ajaypd./-
