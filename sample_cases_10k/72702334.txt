<p id="p_1">      The   petitioner herein     is  the  sole   accused    in  Crime

No.1149/2014 of the Perinthalmanna Police Station, registered

under Section 3 and 7 r/w 17 of the Kerala Money Lenders Act and

Sec. 4 of the Prohibition of charging Exorbitant Interest Act 2012,

on the complaint of one Aneesh.          The petitioner seeks orders

quashing the proceeding on the ground that he and Aneesh have

come to terms. Aneesh is the second respondent herein. He has

filed affidavit to the effect that the has settled the dispute with the

accused, and that he has no grievance or complaint. On a perusal

of the first information statement I find that the dispute is purely

personal in nature. The police has now reported that no material

could be collected on search in the premises of the petitioner to

indicate his involvement in any money lending business. No doubt,

a prosecution cannot be initiated under the Kerala Money Lenders

act on the basis of a solitary loan transaction.       It appears that

without and before getting any material for a prosecution the police

rushed to register FIR against the petitioner.        I find that the

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">Crl. M.C No. 5456 of 2014         2</span>

personal dispute between the petitioner and the de facto

complainant stands well settled. In such a situation, continuance of

further proceedings in the crime will be abuse of legal process, and

it will cause harm and hardship to both the parties.

</p><p id="p_2">       In the result, this petition is allowed. The FIR and further

proceedings in crime No. 1149/2014 of the Perinthalmanna Police

Station will stand quashed under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C.




</p><p id="p_3">                                             P.UBAID,
                                              JUDGE
