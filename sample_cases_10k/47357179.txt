                           ORDER

<p id="p_1">     Although these matters are listed for preliminary

hearing,   with   the   consent    of   the   learned   Counsel

appearing for the petitioners and the learned High Court

Government Pleader appearing for the State and its

authorities, matters are disposed of finally as the same are

covered by several judgments of this Court and of the

Apex Court passed in such and similar cases.
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                               :4:</span>




</p><p id="p_2">      2.      The petitioners in these petitions who belong to

teaching      and    non-teaching    staff   of   Educational

Institutions run by Private Managements have preferred

these writ petitions seeking a Writ of Certiorari to quash

the impugned orders passed by the 1st respondent dt.

20.07.2006 as per at Annexure-B only in so far as it

relates to denial of the benefit of past service of the

petitioners for the purpose of fixation of pay with revisions

from time to time along with pensionary benefits with

arrears and to issue a Writ of Mandamus directing the

respondents to reckon and count the services rendered by

them from the date of their initial appointment upto the

date of approval of their appointment with grant-in-aid

facility for the purpose of fixation of initial pay, seniority,

promotion, increment and pensionary benefit and to

consider their representation for extending the aforesaid

benefits to them in the light of the earlier Judgments of

this Court.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                               :5:</span><p id="p_3">




</p><p id="p_4">     3.       The learned Counsel for the petitioners

submits, the issue involved in these petitions is fully

covered by several judgments of this Court and a few of

such judgments are cited hereunder :
</p>
<p id="p_5">
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">     (i)     W.A.Nos.848/2008        and    connected    cases
             disposed   of   on     3-11-2009    -   PRINCIPAL
             SECRETARY         TO       GOVERNMENT         OF
             KARNATAKA AND OTHERS vs. NAGEGOWDA
             AND OTHERS.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_2">     (ii)    W.P.Nos. 37250-37254/2010 disposed of on 2-
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">             12-2010 - K.C.PRAKASH AND OTHERS vs.
             STATE OF KARNATAKA AND OTHERS.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_4">     (iii)   W.P.Nos. 66892-66910/2011 disposed of on
             19-<a href="/doc/1668503/" id="a_1">10-11   -    A.ANANDA      AND   OTHERS    vs.
             STATE OF KARNATAKA AND OTHERS</a>.</blockquote><p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">     4.       Learned High Court Government Pleader for

the State does not dispute the said fact.


</p><p id="p_8">     5.      The submissions made by the learned Counsel

for the parties are placed on record.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                                   :6:</span><p id="p_9">




</p><p id="p_10">      Accordingly, the writ petitions are allowed          with the

following directions :
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_5">      (i)     Impugned order dt.20.07.2006 at Annexure -B
              is quashed only in so far as it relates to denial
              of the benefit of past service of the petitioners
              for the purpose of fixation of pay with revisions
              from time to time along with pensionary
              benefits with arrears.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_6">      (ii)    Writ of Mandamus is issued directing the State
              and     its      authorities    to    consider    the
              representations made by the petitioners in
              terms of the decisions referred to supra within
              four months from the date of receipt of a copy
              of this order.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_7">      (iii)   If   representations      are   not   made   by   the
              petitioners, they are given liberty to make
              detailed representations in this regard within
              one month from the date of receipt of a copy of
              this order and        if such representations are
              made, the State and its authorities shall
              consider and dispose of the same within three
              months from the date of receipt of such
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                              :7:</span>




          representations in the light of the decisions of
          this Court rendered in the aforesaid cases.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_8">     No order as to costs.
</blockquote>


<blockquote id="blockquote_9">                                    SD/-</blockquote><p id="p_11">
</p><p id="p_12">                                   JUDGE
