<div class="doc_title">K.P.Sinha@ Kalika Prasad Sinha vs State Of Bihar Thru.Vig. on 25 July, 2009</div><pre id="pre_1">                     IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                      Cr.Misc. No.22446 of 2009
                              K.P.SINHA@ KALIKA PRASAD SINHA
             S/o Late Ram Bhajan Sinha residing at Road no. 23, S.K. Nagar,
              P.S. Budha Colony, Dist. Patna                  Petitioner
                                                 Versus
               THE STATE OF BIHAR through the Cabinet (Vigilance) Department
                      Investigation Bureau, Circular Road, Patna
                                                               Opposite party
                                                 -----------
</pre><p id="p_1">02   25.07.2009               The petitioner's application was rejected on 19.5.2009 vide

                   order dated passed in Cr. Misc. no. 16575 of 2009 with observation

                   that once charges are framed, the petitioner may renew his prayer for

                   bail before the trial court. It is not disputed by learned counsel for the

                   State Vigilance that the charges have already been framed. This is

                   case of disproportionate assets.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                             Mr Giri submits that all the evidence are matter of record.

                   Charges having been framed. Petitioner being an old man with various

                   ailments. No useful purpose will be served by keeping him in jail as

                   he is ready to cooperate in the trial for its expeditious disposal.

</p><p id="p_3">                             Considering all the matters, the petitioner is already in

                   custody for exactly three months and charges having been framed in

                   view of the observation made above, let the petitioner (K.P.Sinha @

                   Kalika Prasad Sinha ) be released on bail on furnishing Rs 50,000

                   (rupees fifty thousand) with two sureties of the like amount each to the

                   satisfaction of the Special Judge, Vigilance I, Patna in Special Case

                   no. 22 of 2000 arising out of Vigilance P.S. Case no. 16 of 2000.

</p><p id="p_4">                             It is expected that the petitioner and other accused persons

                   would fully cooperate in the trial and if at any point, there is non-

                   cooperation in regard, the court below would be free to take steps for
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                 -2-</span>




         cancellation of the privilege of bail.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                   Application stands disposed of.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><pre id="pre_2">shahid                                        (Navaniti Prasad Singh, J)
 </pre>
