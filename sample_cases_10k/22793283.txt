<div class="doc_title">Balchand Yadav Alias Balchan ... vs The State Of Jharkhand And Anr on 7 July, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                           Cr.M.P. No. 225 of 2015
                                       ...
                1. Balchand Yadav @ Bal Chandra Yadav @ Balchan Yadav
                2. Sakli Devi
                3. Dharmendra Yadav
                4. Indu Devi
                5. Mahesh Yadav                         ...     Petitioners
                                 -V e r s u s-
                1. The State of Jharkhand
                2. Sima Kumari                          ... Opposite Parties
                                       ...
                CORAM: HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE PRASHANT KUMAR.
                                       ...
                For the Petitioners     : - Mr. Sanjeev Thakur, Advocate.
                For the State           : - Mr. Anjani Kr. Toppo, A.P.P.
                                       ...

02/07.07.2015</pre><p id="p_1">        This application has been filed for quashing the order
                dated 28.02.2013 passed by learned Judicial Magistrate,
                Bokaro in connection with Mahila P.S. Case NO. 03 of 2012,
                G.R. No. 723 of 2012, whereby and whereunder, the learned
                court below took cognizance under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_1">Section 498A</a>, <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_2">120B</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_3">34</a>
                of the I.P.C. &amp; <a href="/doc/751411/" id="a_4">Section 3</a> / <a href="/doc/1023340/" id="a_5">4</a> of the Dowry Prohibition Act.
</p><p id="p_2">                     It is submitted by Sri Sanjeev Thakur, learned counsel
                for the petitioners that no occurrence took place in the
                territorial jurisdiction of Bokaro court. Under the said
                circumstance, Bokaro court has no power to try the case.
                Hence, the impugned order is without jurisdiction.
</p><p id="p_3">                     Having heard the submission, I have gone through the
                record of the case. From perusal of F.I.R., I find that on
                24.03.2012, petitioner nos. 1 &amp; 5 came to Bokaro and there
                they have demanded dowry and when the informant and her
                parents refused to meet the demand, they falsely made
                allegation against the chastity of informant. This shows that
                they have tortured the informant mentally for demand of
                dowry. Thus, in my view, in Bokaro part of the occurrence
                took place. Hence, I find no illegality in the impugned order.
</p><p id="p_4">                     In view of the discussions made above, this application
                is dismissed.
</p>

<p id="p_5">                                                   ( Prashant Kumar, J.)
