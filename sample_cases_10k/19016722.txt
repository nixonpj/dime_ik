<div class="doc_title">Nitish Kumar vs The State Of Bihar on 18 November, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                              CRIMINAL MISCELLANEOUS No.68326 of 2019
                        Arising Out of PS. Case No.-656 Year-2018 Thana- HILSA District- Nalanda
                 ======================================================
                 NITISH KUMAR Son of Mahesh Prasad Resident of Village - Sinduara, P.S.-
                 Chandi, District- Nalanda

                                                                                    ... ... Petitioner/s
                                                        Versus
                 The State of Bihar

                                                        ... ... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :       Mr. Rajeev Kumar
                 For the Opposite Party/s :       Mr. Abhay Kumar
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE ARVIND SRIVASTAVA
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   18-11-2019                  Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and the

                   learned A.P.P. for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                Petitioner seeks bail in a case registered for the

                   offence punishable under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_1">sections 302</a>, <a href="/doc/1128948/" id="a_2">120(B)</a>/ <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_3">34</a> of the Indian

                   Penal Code and under <a href="/doc/244673/" id="a_4">section 27</a> of the Arms Act.

</p><p id="p_3">                                Learned counsel appearing for the petitioner submits

                   that petitioner has been falsely implicated in this case. Nothing

                   has been recovered from the possession of this petitioner. It

                   appears from the F.I.R. that cause of death is use of firearm.

                   From the post mortem report, the cause of death is Hemorrhagic

                   shock due piercing from sharp object. Petitioner is in custody

                   since 07.12.2018.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                Considering the facts and circumstances of the case

                   and the submissions advanced on behalf of the parties, let the
             Patna High Court CR. MISC. No.68326 of 2019(2) dt.18-11-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                        2/2</span>




                      petitioner, above named, be released on bail on furnishing bail

                      bonds of Rs. 10,000/- (ten thousand) with two sureties of the

                      like amount each to the satisfaction of Additional Session

                      Judge-IInd, Hilsa, Nalanda in connection with S.T. No.

                      218/2019, arising out of Hilsa P.S. Case No. 656 / 2018.


</p><p id="p_5">                                               (Arvind Srivastava, J)

Ranjeet/-
</p>
