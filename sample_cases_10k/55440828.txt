<pre id="pre_1">                                       1


 Serial No.36.
February 16, 2015.
      SG
                                   WP 4230 (W) of 2015



                                   Tapan Kumar Banerjee
                                         -versus-
                               State of West Bengal and others


                             Mr Manas Kumar Ghosh
                             Ms Susmita Dey (Basu)
                             Mr Paresh Nath Samanta
                                           ... for the petitioner.

                             Sm Samim Ullah
                                              ... for the State.



                 The respondent corporation is not represented despite service</pre>
<p id="p_1">         but the State is represented.
</p>

<p id="p_2">                 The grievance of the petitioner is that the retiral dues of the
         petitioner have not been released to him despite his superannuation
         on June 30, 2013.
</p>

<p id="p_3">                 The general refrain of the respondent corporation in similar
         matters in this court is that it does not have adequate funds to pay
         its retired employees and depends wholly on the State for the
         release of funds.
</p>

<p id="p_4">                 WP 4230 (W) of 2015 is disposed of by directing the managing
         director of the respondent corporation to look into the propriety of
         the petitioner's claim and make an appropriate recommendation to
         the State Government, if the petitioner is found entitled to any
         payment, within eight weeks of the receipt of a copy of this order.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                             2</span>


<p id="p_5">The recommendation, if any, should be made through the
Secretary, Department of Finance.
</p>

<p id="p_6">     If the petitioner is found entitled to any payment, the
respondents should ensure that the payment due to the petitioner
together with interest at the rate of 7% per annum calculated from a
month after the petitioner's retirement till the date of payment is
paid off to the petitioner within three months of the State receiving
the managing director's recommendation. In the event the payment
is delayed beyond the period of three months as indicated, the
petitioner will be entitled to interest at the rate of 10% per annum
on the principal sum due for the delayed period beyond three
months of the date of receipt of the recommendation by the State.
</p>

<p id="p_7">     There will be no order as to costs.
</p>

<p id="p_8">     Certified website copies of this order, if applied for, be urgently
made available to the parties upon compliance with the requisite
formalities.
</p>



<p id="p_9">                                 (Sanjib Banerjee, J.)
