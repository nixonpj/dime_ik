<div class="doc_title">State Of Rajasthan &amp; Ors vs Shri Roshan Lal on 12 December, 2013</div><pre id="pre_1">                            1



IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR RAJASTHAN AT
                    JODHPUR.

                           O R D E R

    S.B. CIVIL WRIT PETITION NO.14229/2013
State of Rajasthan &amp; Ors. Vs. Roshan Lal &amp; Ors.

Date of order                   :            12.12.2013

                           PRESENT

         HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE VIJAY BISHNOI

Mr. Vimal Mathur for petitioner.


BY THE COURT:</pre>

<p id="p_1">    The      petitioners-State         of   Rajasthan     and

others     have    filed    this    writ    petition    while

challenging the judgment and award dated 17th

August, 2012 passed by the Industrial Tribunal,

Udaipur in case No.1/2006 whereby the learned

Tribunal     has     given      a    direction     to     the

petitioners to grant all consequential benefits

to the respondent-workman from the date he was

declared semi-permanent.
</p>
<p id="p_2">    Brief facts of the case are that in a

dispute raised by the respondent-workman, the

State Government has made a reference to the

Industrial Tribunal, Udaipur to the effect that

whether the non-grant of semi-permanent status

to the petitioner on completion of two years of
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                 2</span>



services by the petitioners is valid and proper

and   if    not,       then     the       respondent-workman         is

entitled for what relief?
</p>
<p id="p_3">      It is not in dispute that at the time of

making     reference          i.e.         on     5.11.2005,        the

respondent-workman              was       not     granted         semi-

permanent status by the petitioners, however,

during the pendency of the reference before the

Industrial       Tribunal,          Udaipur,      the    petitioners

granted semi-permanent status to the respondent-

workman         from     4.3.1989          vide     order         dated

29.1.2008.
</p>
<p id="p_4">      The Industrial Tribunal after taking into

consideration this fact has held that when the

petitioners have granted semi-permanent status

to the respondent-workman from 4.3.1989, he is

entitled        for    all    consequential             benefits     in

pursuance of that.
</p>
<p id="p_5">      Learned         counsel       for    the    petitioners       has

failed     to    satisfy      this        Court    that    when     the

petitioners           themselves          have     granted        semi-

permanent status to the respondent-workman from

4.3.1989,        how    the     respondent-workman           is     not

entitled for the consequential benefits.

</p><p id="p_6">      In view of the fact that the respondent-
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                               3</span>



workman was granted semi-permanent status from

4.3.1989,        this     Court   does     not   find    any

illegality        in    the   directions    given   by   the

learned labour Court to grant the consequential

benefits to the respondent-workmen from the date

when semi-permanent status was granted to him.

</p><p id="p_7">           In such circumstances, this Court does not

find any merit in this writ petition and the

same is hereby dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                                         [VIJAY BISHNOI],J.
</p><p id="p_9">Babulal/
