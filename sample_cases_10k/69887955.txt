<div class="doc_title">Rameshchandra vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 30 October, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1"> HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH : BENCH AT INDORE

                   Cri.A. No.7990/2018
             (<a href="/doc/78532972/" id="a_1">Rameshchandra vs. State of M.P</a>)


Indore dated :30.10.2018
      Shri Yogesh Kumar Gupta, learned counsel for the
appellant.
      Shri   Vaibhav     Jain,   learned     counsel    for   the
respondent/SPE, Lokayukta, Indore.</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Heard on the question of admission.
</p><p id="p_2">      Appeal is admitted for final hearing
      Record of the trial Court be called for.
      Also heard learned counsel for the appellant on I.A.
No.7534/2018 that is, an application for suspension of jail
sentence of appellant - Rameshchandra, who has been
convicted under <a href="/doc/1326844/" id="a_2">Section 409</a> of IPC and sentenced to 3 year
RI with fine of Rs.2,000; under <a href="/doc/1128948/" id="a_3">Section 120(B)</a> of IPC
sentenced to 1 year RI with fine of Rs.1,000/- and under
Sections 13(1) (D) &amp; 13(2) of <a href="/doc/1331755/" id="a_4">P.C. Act</a>, 1988 sentenced to 3
years RI with fine of Rs.2,000/- with default stipulation.
</p><p id="p_3">      Learned counsel for the appellant has submitted that
the learned trial Court has not properly appreciated the
evidence on record and has recorded the conviction without
considering the serious anomalies, contradictions and
omissions present in the testimony of various witnesses. The
appellant was released on regular bail during trial and
previously custodial sentence has already been suspended
by the trial Curt itself upto 28/10/2018 and there is no
possibility of the early hearing of this criminal appeal before
this Court, hence, it is prayed that custodial sentence of the
                 HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH : BENCH AT INDORE

                                   Cri.A. No.7990/2018
                             (<a href="/doc/78532972/" id="a_5">Rameshchandra vs. State of M.P</a>)

               appellant be suspended during the pendency of this criminal
               appeal.
</p><p id="p_4">                      Though prayer for suspension is opposed by the leaned
               counsel for the respondent/State, however, looking to the
               aforesaid, without expressing any opinion on the merits of the
               case, I.A. No.7534/2018, an application for suspension of jail
               sentence of appellant - Rameshchandra is allowed and it
               is directed that on depositing the fine amount awarded by the
               trial Court, the substantive jail sentence of appellant shall
               remain suspended and he be released on bail subject to his
               furnishing a personal bond in the sum of Rs.50,000/-(Rupees
               fifty thousand) with one solvent surety in the like amount to
               the satisfaction of the trial Court and he shall appear before
               the Registry of this Court on 12/02/2019 and on all other
               subsequent dates as may be given in this behalf.
</p><p id="p_5">                      List this appeal for final hearing in due course.
                      Cc as per rules.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                                     (Ms. Vandana Kasrekar)
                                                               Judge

Preetha Nair
2018.10.30 17:48:57
