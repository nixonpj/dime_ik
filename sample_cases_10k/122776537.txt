<div class="doc_title">Kamaljit Singh @ Kamal vs State Of Punjab on 14 January, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                     IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA AT CHANDIGARH

                                            Criminal Misc.No.M-222 of 2015 (O&amp;M)
                                            Date of decision: 14th January, 2015

            Kamaljit Singh alias Kamal
                                                                               ...Petitioner
                                                    Versus
            State of Punjab
                                                                             ...Respondent

            CORAM: HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE INDERJIT SINGH

            Present:            Mr. Mohinder Kumar, Advocate,
                                for the petitioner.

                                     ****

            INDERJIT SINGH, J.
</pre><p id="p_1">                                Petitioner Kamaljit Singh alias Kamal has preferred this
            petition under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> Cr.P.C., seeking anticipatory bail in case
            FIR No.151 dated 21.09.2014, registered at Police Station Division
            No.3, Jalandhar, District Jalandhar, under <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_2">Sections 307</a>, <a href="/doc/1540253/" id="a_3">326</a>, <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_4">341</a>,
            <a href="/doc/763672/" id="a_5">148</a>, <a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_6">149</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_7">506</a> IPC.
</p><p id="p_2">                                I have heard learned counsel for the petitioner and have
            gone through the record.
</p><p id="p_3">                                The petitioner is named in the FIR. He is stated to be
            armed with sword. 'Lalkara' is also attributed to him. As per FIR,
            though no injury has been attributed to the petitioner but as per the
            allegations in the FIR, after the blow inflicted with 'dattar' by co-
            accused Puneet, the hand of complainant was cut and it started
            hanging. Since the sword is to be recovered, therefore, the petitioner
            is required for custodial interrogation.
</p><p id="p_4">                                Keeping in view the facts and circumstances of the
            present case and gravity of the offence, I do not find it a fit case
            where the petitioner is entitled to the benefit of anticipatory bail.
</p><p id="p_5">                                Accordingly, the instant petition stands dismissed.
</p>
