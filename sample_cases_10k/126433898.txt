<div class="doc_title">Deepak Kumar @ Deepak Sharma vs The State Of Bihar on 12 October, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                   Criminal Miscellaneous No.62900 of 2018
                       Arising Out of PS. Case No.-265 Year-2018 Thana- AMARPUR District- Banka
                 ======================================================
                 Deepak Kumar @ Deepak Sharma, Son of Sri Janardhan Sharma, Resident
                 of Village- Makdumma, Police Station- Amarpur, District- Banka.
                                                                             ... ... Petitioner/s
                                                   Versus
                 The State of Bihar.
                                                                     ... ... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :       Mr. Ajay Mukherjee
                 For the Opposite Party/s :       Mr. Sri Mritunjay Kumar Nirala
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE S. KUMAR
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   12-10-2018                   Heard learned counsel for petitioner and learned

                  counsel for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                  Petitioner, who is in custody, seeks bail in

                  connection with Amarpur P.S. Case No. 265 of 2018 registered

                  for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1258372/" id="a_1">Sections 147</a>, <a href="/doc/763672/" id="a_2">148</a>, <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_3">341</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_4">323</a>,

                  <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_5">307</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_6">504</a> and <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_7">506</a> of the Indian Penal Code.

</p><p id="p_3">                                   Informant has alleged that when he was going to

                  Civil Court, Banka, petitioner along with other co-accused

                  persons stopped him and started abusing him and on protest

                  assaulted him by lathi, Danda and rod as a result of which he

                  sustained fracture, injuries on his head and body.

</p><p id="p_4">                                   It has been submitted on behalf of the petitioner

                  that he is innocent and has committed no offence. He has been

                  falsely implicated in this case due to dirty village politics and

                  previous enmity. There is no allegation against petitioner.

                  Injuries are simple            in nature. He is in custody since
            Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.62900 of 2018(2) dt.12-10-2018
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                        2/2</span>




                    31.07.2018.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                     Considering the aforesaid fact and circumstances of

                    the case, let the petitioner named above be released on bail upon

                    furnishing bail bond of Rs. 10,000/- (ten thousand) with two

                    sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of learned

                    Chief Judicial Magistrate, Banka in connection with Amarpur

                    P.S. Case No. 265 of 2018. subject to conditions:

</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">                                 (1) Bailors should be local having sufficient

                                 immovable property within the jurisdiction of the

                                 court concerned.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_2">                                 (2) Petitioner shall co-operate in the trial and shall be

                                 present on each and every date fixed by the court and

                                 his absence on two consecutive dates without proper

                                 and valid reason trial court shall have liberty to cancel

                                 his bail bond.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_3">                                 (3) If the petitioner tampers with the evidence or the

                                 witnesses of the case, in that case case diary,

                                 prosecution will be at liberty to move for cancellation

                                 of bail of the petitioner.</blockquote><p id="p_6">



</p><p id="p_7">                                                     (S. Kumar, J)
Rajiv/Manoj-
</p>
