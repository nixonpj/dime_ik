<div class="doc_title">Shankar Saw @ Hari Shankar &amp; Anr vs The State Of Bihar on 27 January, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                   IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.51768 of 2014
                    Arising Out of PS.Case No. -282 Year- 2014 Thana -LAKHISARAI District- LAKHISARAI
                 ======================================================
                 1. Shankar Saw @ Hari Shankar.
                 2. Md. Rafiq
                                                                   .... .... Petitioners.
                                                Versus
                 The State of Bihar
                                                               .... .... Opposite Party.
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioners    :  Mr. Navin Kumar, Advocate.
                 For the Opposite Party :  Mr. S.N.Shukla(App)
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE DINESH KUMAR
                 SINGH
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">3   27-01-2015                Heard learned counsels for the petitioners

                 and the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                              The petitioners are languishing in custody

                 since      16.09.2014           in     a    case       registered          for         the

                 offences punishable under <a href="/doc/764237/" id="a_1">Section 394</a> of the Indian

                 Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                              The accusation is of robbing the tractor of

                 the informant by two miscreants. The name of the

                 petitioners sprang up during investigation when it

                 is alleged that they confessed their guilt.

</p><p id="p_4">                              It is submitted by learned counsel for the

                 petitioners that no recovery has been made from the

                 petitioners nor the petitioners were put on T. I.

                 Parade and a supplementary affidavit to that effect

                 has been filed before this Court.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                              Considering the fact that the petitioners
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> 2        Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.51768 of 2014 (3) dt.27-01-2015</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                  2/2</span>




                  were     not     put     on    T.I.      Parade       till    date,    let   the

                  petitioners, above named, be released on bail on

                  furnishing bail bond of Rs.10,000/-(Ten Thousand)

                  each with two sureties of the like amount each to

                  the satisfaction of the learned C.J.M. Lakhisarai,

                  in connection with Lakhisarai P.S. Case No. 282 of

                  2014, G. R. No. 758 of 2014.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                               Since       the     petitioners          are    accused    in    two

                  other cases of similar nature, let the learned court

                  below cancel the bail bonds of the petitioners if

                  the       petitioners            default          for       two   consecutive

                  occasions         or     get     substantially          involved       in    some

                  serious nature of offence.
</p>
<p id="p_7">


</p><pre id="pre_2">U.K./-                                                          (Dinesh Kumar Singh, J)

    U    T
