<div class="doc_title">Biswanath Manjhi vs The State Of Jharkhand on 22 March, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">        IN     THE       HIGH    COURT        OF     JHARKHAND        AT    RANCHI
                           B. A. No. 1434 of 2014
        Biswanath Manjhi                               ..... ... Petitioner
                                    Versus
        The State of Jharkhand                           ..... ...    Opposite Party
                                 --------
               CORAM       :   HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE H. C. MISHRA
                                 ------
        For the Petitioner       :        Mr. Mohit Prakash, Advocate
        For the State            :        A.P.P.
                                 --------

 2/ 22.03.2014</pre><p id="p_1">         Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned A.P.P. for the
        Prosecution.
</p><p id="p_2">                       The petitioner has been made accused for the offences under
        <a href="/doc/329571/" id="a_1">Sections 392</a>, <a href="/doc/1119707/" id="a_2">395</a> and <a href="/doc/844968/" id="a_3">412</a> of the Indian Penal Code, in connection with Bano
        P.S. Case No. 44 of 2013 corresponding to G.R. No. 412 of 2013.
</p><p id="p_3">                       The case relates to dacoity and the case was instituted against
        unknown.
</p><p id="p_4">                       The impugned order shows that the petitioner has been made
        accused in this case only on the basis of confessional statements.
</p><p id="p_5">                       In the facts of this case, I am inclined to enlarge the petitioner,
        Biswanath Manjhi, on bail. Accordingly, the petitioner, named above, is directed
        to be released on bail, on furnishing bail bond of Rs. 10,000/- (ten thousand),
        with two sureties of the like amount each, to the satisfaction of learned Chief
        Judicial Magistrate, Simdega, in connection with Bano P.S. Case No. 44 of 2013
        corresponding to G.R. No. 412 of 2013.
</p>

<p id="p_6">                                                                      ( H. C. Mishra, J.)
R.Kr.
</p>
