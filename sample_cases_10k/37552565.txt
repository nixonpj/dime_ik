<div class="doc_title">Union Of India vs Jai Singh &amp; Anr on 24 February, 2015</div><div class="doc_author">Author: G.P. Mittal</div>

<pre id="pre_1">$-12
*    IN THE HIGH COURT OF DELHI AT NEW DELHI

                                        Decided on: 24th February, 2015
+       MAC.APP. 145/2013

        UNION OF INDIA
                                                      ..... Appellant
                             Through:   Mr. Aakash D. Pratap,
                                        Advocate

                    versus

        JAI SINGH &amp; ANR                             ..... Respondents
                      Through:          Mr. Nitin Yadav, Advocate for
                                        Respondent no.1.


        CORAM:
        HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE G.P.MITTAL

                             JUDGMENT
</pre>
<p id="p_1">G. P. MITTAL, J. (ORAL)

</p><p id="p_2">1.      The appeal is for reduction of compensation of Rs.6,06,809/-

</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">        awarded by the Motor Accident Claims Tribunal(the Claims

        Tribunal) in favour of Respondent no.1 for having suffered

        injuries in a motor vehicular accident which occurred on

        20.08.2010 at about 6:15 p.m.


</blockquote><p id="p_3">2.      It is urged by the learned counsel for the Appellant that




<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">MAC APP 145/2013                                               Page 1 of 3</span>
         Respondent no.1 ought to have been granted compensation

        under the <a href="/doc/1806623/" id="a_1">Workmen Compensation Act</a>, 1923. It is urged that

        the compensation awarded is exorbitant and excessive.


</p><p id="p_4">3.      Admittedly, Respondent no.1 was not an employee of the

        Appellant having suffered injuries during the course of his

        employment. Thus, Respondent no.1 was not obliged to seek

        any compensation under the <a href="/doc/1806623/" id="a_2">Workmen Compensation Act</a>,

        1923. In fact, it was not permissible for Respondent no.1 to

        seek compensation under the <a href="/doc/1806623/" id="a_3">Workmen Compensation Act</a>,

        1923.
</p>
<p id="p_5">
</p><p id="p_6">4.      The learned counsel for the Appellant has failed to point out

        under what head the compensation awarded is unjust or

        exorbitant and excessive.
</p>
<p id="p_7">
</p><p id="p_8">5.      The impugned judgment does not call for any interference.


</p><p id="p_9">6.      The appeal is accordingly dismissed.


</p><p id="p_10">7.      The compensation amount deposited shall be kept in Fixed

        Deposit/released in terms of the orders passed by the Claims

        Tribunal.
</p>
<p id="p_11">

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">MAC APP 145/2013                                                Page 2 of 3</span>
</p><p id="p_12"> 8.      Pending applications also stand disposed of.


</p><p id="p_13">9.      Statutory amount, if any, deposited shall be refunded to the

        Appellant.
</p>
<p id="p_14">


</p><p id="p_15">                                                       (G.P. MITTAL)
                                                          JUDGE
FEBRUARY 24, 2015
pst
