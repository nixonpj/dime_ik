<p id="p_1">                                          DATED : 13th JUNE, 2019.
</p><p id="p_2"> PER COURT:-
</p>
<p id="p_3"> 1.               We       have     heard     Mr.        Kasliwal,           learned
 counsel for petitioners, Mrs. Deshpande, learned
 A.G.P..
</p>

<p id="p_4"> 2.               The affidavits are filed in some of the
 cases.          The licences are renewed up to year 2020
 and in some up to the end of 2019.
</p>

<p id="p_5"> 3.               It      is      not     disputed       that       the        liquor
 licences are issued to petitioners prior to the
 notification dated 04.03.2015.
</p>

<p id="p_6"> 4.               In      light      of     the   above,         the       judgment
 delivered              on     23.11.2016           in       Writ          Petition
 No.3390/2015 with connected writ petitions of this
 Court at Nagpur Bench would squarely apply.
</p>
<p id="p_7"> 5.               In      light      of    the    above,         we      pass       the
 following order:
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">      "For       the      reasons        recorded     in     the      judgment
      dated        November        23,     2016     in     Writ       Petition
      No.3390/2015             with       connected      writ        petitions
      (supra), we direct the respondents to consider
      the application of the petitioners for renewal
      of their licences without insisting upon the
      resolution of the Gram Sabha No Objection as
      required under Rule 4 of the Rules of 2015."
</blockquote>
