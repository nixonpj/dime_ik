  IN THE HIGH COURT OF KERALA AT ERNAKULAM

CRL.A.No. 679 of 2008()


1. K.C. SURESH KUMAR
                      ...  Petitioner

                        Vs



1. S. GOPAKUMAR, CASHIER
                       ...       Respondent

2. STATE OF KERALA, REP. BY

                For Petitioner  :SRI.P.PUSHPARAJAN

                For Respondent  :SRI.VINOY VARGHESE KALLUMOOTTILL

The Hon'ble MR. Justice R.BASANT

 Dated :09/06/2008

 O R D E R
                            R.BASANT, J
                         ----------------------
                       Crl.A.No.679 of 2008
                   ----------------------------------------
               Dated this the 9th day of June 2008


                         J U D G M E N T
</pre>
<p id="p_1">      The appellant is the complainant in a prosecution under

<a href="/doc/1823824/" id="a_1">Section 138</a> of the Negotiable Instruments Act. When the matter

came    up   for   recording         evidence         on    28/4/2006, the

appellant/accused was absent and accordingly powers under

<a href="/doc/466785/" id="a_2">Section 256</a> Cr.P.C were invoked. The complaint was dismissed

and the accused was acquitted under <a href="/doc/1988204/" id="a_3">Section 256(1)</a> Cr.P.C.

</p><p id="p_2">      2.   The learned counsel for the appellant submits that the

matter was posted for evidence for the first time on 28/4/2006.

On that day, the complainant was represented. Adjournment

was sought on valid grounds. The same was turned down and

the complaint was dismissed. I have perused the order sheet. I

find merit in the submission of the learned counsel for the

petitioner.     The    respondent           has      entered    appearance.

Notwithstanding the opposition of the learned counsel for the

respondent, I am satisfied, on a perusal of the order sheet on

the dates 06/02/2006, 17/3/2006 and 28/4/2006 that the order

dated 28/4/2006 deserves to be set aside.</p><pre id="pre_2">

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">Crl.M.C.No.679/08                2</span>




      3.    In the result,

      a)    This appeal is allowed.

      b)    Impugned order is set aside.

      c)    The learned Magistrate is directed to dispose of the

</pre><p id="p_3">case afresh and in accordance with law.

</p><p id="p_4">      4.    Parties shall appear before the learned Magistrate on

7/7/2008 without waiting for any further instructions.       The

complainant shall file the chief affidavit also before that date

with notice to the learned counsel for the accused.



</p><p id="p_5">                                            (R.BASANT, JUDGE)
