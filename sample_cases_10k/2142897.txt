<div class="doc_title">M/S Imperial Agro Pvt. Ltd vs The State Of Bihar on 15 May, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">         IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                    Civil Writ Jurisdiction Case No.6741 of 2019
     ======================================================
</pre><p id="p_1">     M/s Imperial Agro Pvt. Ltd. through Managing Director Uday Shankar Ojha,
     aged about 59 years (Male), S/o late Shiv Layak Ojha, Resident of Village-
     Beur, Police Station- Beur, District- Patna.

</p><p id="p_2">                                                                 ... ... Petitioner/s
                                       Versus
</p><p id="p_3">1.   The State of Bihar through the Chief Secretary, State of Bihar, Patna.
</p><p id="p_4">2.   The District Magistrate, at Patna.
</p><p id="p_5">3.   The Superintendent of Excise Department, Patna.
</p><p id="p_6">4.   The Officer-in-Charge, Fatuha Police Station- Patna.

</p><p id="p_7">                                               ... ... Respondent/s
     ======================================================
     Appearance :
</p><p id="p_8">     For the Petitioner/s   :       Mr.Ranjan Kumar Sharma, Advocate
     For the Respondent/s   :       Mr.Kumar Manish ( SC5 )
     ======================================================
     CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE JYOTI SARAN
             and
             HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE ANJANI KUMAR SHARAN
                         ORAL JUDGMENT
     (Per: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE JYOTI SARAN)

      Date : 15-05-2019

                Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned

      counsel appearing on behalf of the state.

</p><p id="p_9">                The petitioner prays for provisional release of the truck

      (D.Van) bearing Registration No. BR-1GD-4097, Chassis No.

      MB1AA22E3DRP60455, Engine No. PDH037198P, which has

      been seized in connection with Fatuha P.S. Case No. 19 of 2019

      for the offences punishable under Sections 30(a), 37(c) of the

      Bihar Prohibition and <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_1">Excise Act</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_10">                It is stated by learned counsel for the petitioner that

      confiscation proceeding is yet to be initiated and the vehicle is
 Patna High Court CWJC No.6741 of 2019 dt.15-05-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                           2/3</span>




         lying under the open sky in the police station. The seizure list

         reflects the seizure of 400 ml. of country liquor (Mahua).

</p><p id="p_11">                   Having heard learned counsel for the parties and taking

         note of the nature of seizure made as well as the fact that the

         confiscation proceeding is yet to be initiated, we direct that the

         vehicle in question be released provisionally in favour of the

         petitioner on production of ownership and registration with

         respect to vehicle in question in his name before the designated

         Court below with two sureties to the extent of the value of the

         vehicle as indicated in the insurance document. The petitioner

         while submitting the sureties shall also furnish the following

         affidavits/undertakings:
</p>
<p id="p_12">                   (i) That the vehicle in question has never been involved

         in any offence of similar nature in past and shall not indulge in

         similar offence in future.
</p>
<p id="p_13">                   (ii) That the petitioner shall not indulge in creating any

         third party right or interest in respect of the vehicle during the

         pendency of the confiscation proceeding, if any, and shall not

         alienate the vehicle during this period.

</p><p id="p_14">                   (iii) The petitioner shall furnish an undertaking to

         produce the vehicle before the confiscating authority as and

         when required.
</p><p id="p_15">              Patna High Court CWJC No.6741 of 2019 dt.15-05-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                        3/3</span>




</p><p id="p_16">                                (iv) Prior to release of the vehicle, a Panchanama would

                      be got prepared by the designated Court below, wherein the

                      photograph of the vehicle shall be taken and will be certified by

                      the petitioner and the same shall be kept on record so that in

                      future, if so required, it may be used as a secondary evidence.

                      The petitioner shall furnish an undertaking not to challenge the

                      said Panchanama in course of trial.
</p>
<p id="p_17">                                The release shall be allowed within a period of 14 days

                      from the date of submission of the sureties and the undertakings

                      as stated above. This release of the vehicle would, however, be

                      subject to initiation and finalization of the confiscation

                      proceeding.
</p>
<p id="p_18">                                With the observations/directions above, this writ

                      petition is allowed.
</p>
<p id="p_19">


</p><p id="p_20">                                                    (Jyoti Saran, J)


                                             ( Anjani Kumar Sharan, J)
Nasimul/-
