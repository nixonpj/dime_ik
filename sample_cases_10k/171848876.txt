<div class="doc_title">Suraj Bhuia Alias Suraj Bhuiyan vs The State Of Jharkhand on 19 February, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">                IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                           B.A. No. 11726 of 2013
                                      ...
                Suraj Bhuia @ Suraj Bhuiyan            ...  Petitioner
                                -V e r s u s-
                The State of Jharkhand                 ...    Opposite Party
                                      ...
                CORAM: HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE PRASHANT KUMAR.
                                      ...
                For the Petitioner    : Mr. Manoj Kr. No. 3, Advocate
                For the State         : Mr. Dilip Kr. Chakraverty, A.P.P.
                                      ...

03/19.02.2014</pre><p id="p_1">        Bail application filed by Suraj Bhuia @ Suraj Bhuiyan,
                is moved by Sri Manoj Kumar No. 3, learned counsel for
                the petitioner and opposed by Sri Dilip Kumar Chakraverty,
                learned Additional P.P. for the State.
</p><p id="p_2">                     This is a case under <a href="/doc/1119707/" id="a_1">Sections 395</a>/<a href="/doc/844968/" id="a_2">412</a> of the I.P.C.
                Earlier the bail prayer of petitioner rejected because some
                looted articles recovered from his possession, but it
                appears that the seizure list witnesses namely Manoranjan
                Dubey and Surendra Kumar Malakar have already been
                examined as P.W.-1 &amp; P.W.-2, but they have not supported
                the case of prosecution. It further appears that other
                witnesses including informant have also been examined,
                but they were declared hostile by the prosecution.
</p><p id="p_3">                     Considering the aforesaid facts and circumstances, I
                allow this application and direct court below to enlarge the
                petitioner on bail on his furnishing bail bond of Rs.
                10,000/-(Ten Thousand) with two sureties of the like
                amount each to the satisfaction of learned ADJ-VII,
                Dhanbad, in connection with Kenduadih P.S. Case No. 36 of
                2012 corresponding to G.R. No. 1121 of 2012 (S.T. No. 398
                of 2012).
</p>


<p id="p_4">                                                  (Prashant Kumar, J.)
