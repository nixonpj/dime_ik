<p id="p_1">59   24-06-2015                     Heard the learned counsel for the parties on I.A.

</p><p id="p_2">                    No.8992 of 2014.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                    It appears that subsequently separate applications have

                    been filed under Order 22 Rule 9 and under <a href="/doc/100581/" id="a_1">Section 5</a> of the

                    Limitation Act which are I.A. No.1728 of 2015 and 1729 of 2015.

</p><p id="p_4">                                    It appears that notices were issued to the legal

                    representatives of the deceased respondent No.4.                In spite of

                    service of notice, they have not appeared.

</p><p id="p_5">                                    It further appears that the only contesting respondent
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">             Patna High Court FA No.322 of 1983 (59) dt.24-06-2015</span>
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                       2</span>




                         is plaintiff-respondent No.1.              The respondent No.4 was the

                         defendant in the court below.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                         In view of the submission of the learned counsel, the

                         delay in filing the substitution application is condoned, abatement

                         if any, is set aside and the legal representatives of the deceased

                         respondent No.4 are substituted in place of the deceased. The

                         I.As. thus stand allowed.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><p id="p_8">                                                    (Mungeshwar Sahoo, J)
