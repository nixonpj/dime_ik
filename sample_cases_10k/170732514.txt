RAMESHWAR SINGH MALIK, J. (Oral)
</pre>
<p id="p_1">             Learned counsel for the petitioner, at the very outset, fairly

states that since during the pendency of this writ petition, State of Punjab

has issued notification dated 28.1.2013 restoring the powers of Financial

Commissioner for entertaining the appropriate petition against the

impugned orders, he will not be in a position to substantiate his

arguments, at this stage.    Thus, he seeks permission of the Court to

withdraw the present writ petition with liberty to the petitioner to

approach the Financial Commissioner, at the first instance. However, he

submits that since the petitioner was pursuing the bona fide litigation

before this Court, the issue of limitation may not be permitted to be raised

by the respondents, in case the petitioner approaches the Financial

Commissioner within a period of 30 days from today.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> CWP No.15440 of 2011                                          2</span><p id="p_2">

</p><p id="p_3">             In view of the above statement made by learned counsel for

the petitioner, the instant writ petition is ordered to be dismissed as

withdrawn with liberty to the petitioner to approach the Financial

Commissioner, at the first instance. It is also made clear that if the

petitioner files an appropriate petition before the Financial Commissioner

within a period of 30 days from today, the respondents shall not raise the

issue of limitation because the petitioner had been pursuing the bona fide

litigation before this Court.
</p>
<p id="p_4">

</p><pre id="pre_2">11.03.2013                      (RAMESHWAR SINGH MALIK)
