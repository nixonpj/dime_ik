<div class="doc_title">Chellathai vs ) The Superintendent Of Police on 8 September, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">       

  

  

 
 
 BEFORE THE MADURAI BENCH OF MADRAS HIGH COURT

DATED : 08.09.2014

CORAM
THE HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE S.MANIKUMAR
AND
THE HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE V.S.RAVI

HABEAS CORPUS PETITION(MD)No.692 of 2014

Chellathai					... Petitioner

Vs.

1)	The Superintendent of Police,
	Virudhunagar District,
	Virudhunagar.

2)	The Inspector of Police,
	Sivakasi East Police Station,
	Sivakasi,
	Virudhunagar District.			... Respondents

Prayer
	Petition filed under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the Constitution of India,
directing the respondents to produce the petitioner's minor daughter by name
Pandiselvi (ages 16 years) D/o.Mahalingam and handover her to the
petitioner's custody.

!For Petitioner		: Mr.A.Haja Mohideen
For Respondents	: Mr.C.Mayil Vahana Rajendran
			Additional Public Prosecutor

:ORDER
</pre><p id="p_1">(Order of the Court was made by V.S.RAVI, J.)
	Today, the matter is called in open court.  Counsel for the petitioner
along with the petitioner and detenue are present.  The petitioner identified
the detenue.  The detenue asserted that there is no illegal detention,
confinement or harassment caused to her, by any one and also asserted that
she is willing to go along with her mother/petitioner herein.  The petitioner
also asserted that she would take care of the detenue, by providing food,
shelter, clothing and other basic amenities.  In such circumstances, the
Habeas Corpus Petition becomes infructuous.  Hence, the Habeas Corpus
Petition is dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_2">To

</p><p id="p_3">1)	The Superintendent of Police,
	Virudhunagar District,
	Virudhunagar.
</p><p id="p_4">2)	The Inspector of Police,
	Sivakasi East Police Station,
	Sivakasi, Virudhunagar District.</p>
