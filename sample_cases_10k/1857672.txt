<div class="doc_title">P.A.Haridas vs Superintendent Of Police on 24 February, 2009</div><pre id="pre_1">       

  

  

 
 
  IN THE HIGH COURT OF KERALA AT ERNAKULAM

Crl.Rev.Pet.No. 276 of 2009()


1. P.A.HARIDAS, S/O.M.T.AYYAPAN,
                      ...  Petitioner

                        Vs



1. SUPERINTENDENT OF POLICE, VACB,THRISSUR.
                       ...       Respondent

2. STATE OF KERALA,

                For Petitioner  :SRI.T.G.RAJENDRAN

                For Respondent  :PUBLIC PROSECUTOR

The Hon'ble MR. Justice V.RAMKUMAR

 Dated :24/02/2009

 O R D E R
                       V. RAMKUMAR, J.
                   = = = = = = = = = = = = =
                      Crl.R.P.No.276 of 2009
                  = = = = = = = = = = = = = =
             Dated this the 24th day of February, 2009

                              ORDER
</pre>

<p id="p_1">      The revision petitioner, who is the sole accused in

C.C.No.20 of 2006 on the file of the Enquiry Commissioner &amp;

Special Judge, Thrissur for offences punishable under <a href="/doc/324254/" id="a_1">Section 7</a>

and <a href="/doc/1259316/" id="a_2">13(2)</a> of the Prevention of Corruption Act for the

contravention of <a href="/doc/1101716/" id="a_3">Section 13(1)(d)</a>         of the Prevention of

Corruption Act, challenges the order dated 12.1.09 passed by the

Special Judge dismissing the petitioner's application filed as

C.M.P.No.2867 of 2008 for discharge.
</p>
<p id="p_2">      2.   The learned counsel appearing for the revision

petitioner made the following submissions before me in support

of the revision:-
</p>
<p id="p_3">      This is a case where it was after registering the FIR that

the trap was laid and the statements of the accused have been

incorporated in the pre-trap and post trap mahazars. In the light

of the decision <a href="/doc/540986/" id="a_4">Maha Singh v. State (Delhi Administration</a>)

1976 SCC(Crl.) 135, those statements are hit by <a href="/doc/523607/" id="a_5">Section 162</a>

Cr.P.C and cannot be looked into. The learned special judge has

Crl.R.P.No.276 of 2008
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                  2</span>


overlooked the above vital aspects of the matter.

</p><p id="p_4">      3. Contradicting the above submissions the learned Public

Prosecutor argued that whatever is recorded in the pre-trap and

post trap mahazars do not amount to statements within the

meaning of <a href="/doc/1110422/" id="a_6">Section 161(2)</a> Cr.P.C so as to attract the interdict

under <a href="/doc/523607/" id="a_7">section 162</a> Cr.P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_5">      4.   Since the special judge has only dismissed the

application for discharge after finding that there is a prima facie

case for framing a charge against the revision petitioner, I am

not going into the rival contentions made at the Bar. It shall be

open to both sides to make their submissions regarding the legal

positions at the time of the trial.
</p>
<p id="p_6">      5. It is needless to mention that the petitioner can raise all

objections during the trial of the case and the same shall be

considered by the Special Judge at the appropriate stage.

</p><p id="p_7">      Reserving this right of the revision petitioner, this Crl.R.P.

is dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_8">
Dated this the 24th day of February, 2009.



</p><p id="p_9">                                  V. RAMKUMAR, JUDGE

</p>
