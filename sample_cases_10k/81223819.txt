<div class="doc_title">Amar Nath vs Shri Ashok Kumar Gupta And Others on 21 August, 2012</div><pre id="pre_1">IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA AT
              CHANDIGARH


                                 C.O.C.P.No.2404 of 2012 (O&amp;M)

                                 Date of decision : 21.8.2012


Amar Nath
                                                ....Petitioner
              Versus

Shri Ashok Kumar Gupta and others
                                                ...Respondents

CORAM : HON'BLE MR.JUSTICE MAHESH GROVER
                  ....

Present:    Mr.A.K.Khunger, Advocate
            for the petitioner.

                         .....

MAHESH GROVER, J.
</pre>
<p id="p_1">             The petitioner alleges violation of the order of this

Court dated 28.11.2011 vide which the competent authority that

is respondent No.2 (arrayed in the writ petition) was directed to

pass orders on the validity of the auction proceedings and if the

same found approval with him, the possession of the shop was

directed to be given to respondent No.7 (in the writ petition), who

incidentally already was enjoying the possession of the same.

</p><p id="p_2">            It may be noticed that the petitioner had filed a PIL

agitating the grant of lease in favour of said respondent (Arpit

Sharma arrayed as respondent No.7 in the writ petition). Pursuant

to the directions given by this Court the respondents have passed

an order on 9.12.2011 indicating that the matter was investigated
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> C.O.C.P.No.2404 of 2012 (O&amp;M)              -2-</span>

into by the Director, Local Government Punjab and thereafter the

possession was legitimised in favour of said respondent.

</p><p id="p_3">            Learned counsel for the petitioner contends that the

order of this Court has not been complied with in letter and spirit

as there is no comment or mention of the auction proceedings in

the order dated 9.12.2011 passed purportedly as compliance of

the order of this Court.
</p>
<p id="p_4">            On due consideration, I am of the opinion that the

petitioner has invoked the contempt jurisdiction of this Court

under a mistaken belief. The order concededly has been passed

pursuant to the directions of this Court. Whether it measures upto

the expectation of the petitioner is not the issue and whether it is

legitimate or not can be tested only in appropriate proceedings.

But it cannot be said that the respondents have willfully flouted

the directions of the Court so as to invite action under the

<a href="/doc/1396751/" id="a_1">Contempt of Courts Act</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_5">            No ground to intervene.
</p>
<p id="p_6">            However, the petitioner is at liberty to avail himself of

any remedy that may be available to him under the law.

</p><p id="p_7">            Petition stands disposed of.
</p>
<p id="p_8">
</p><pre id="pre_2">21.8.2012                                  (MAHESH GROVER)
                                               JUDGE
