<p id="p_1">K.M.Joseph, J.
</p>
<p id="p_2">             Petitioner alleges harassment by police. Briefly

put, the case of the petitioner is as follows:--Petitioner's wife

is the absolute owner in possession of 20 cents of land. The

5th respondent is the owner in possession of the remaining

property which was originally the family property of the

petitioner's wife. There is a demand by the 5th respondent to

the petitioner and his wife to sell their property in his favour.

They refused the request.         The complaint is that the 5th

respondent sought the assistance of the Ist respondent to

compel the petitioner to effect sale of the property in favour of

the 5th respondent.
</p>
<p id="p_3">            2. We heard the learned counsel for the petitioner

and the learned Government Pleader.              Certainly the Ist

respondent cannot interfere in the civil dispute between the

petitioner and the 5th respondent.           Learned Government

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">WPC 14026/2010                              -2-</span>

Pleader submits that the Ist respondent will not intervene on

behalf of the 5th respondent to compel the petitioner to sell the

property in favour of    the 5th respondent.    We record the

above submission and close the writ petition. It is open to the

petitioner to prosecute Ext.P4 before the competent authority.




</p><p id="p_4">                                         (K.M.JOSEPH)
                                            JUDGE.
</p>
