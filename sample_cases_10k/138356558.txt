     gd                              W.P. 19179 (W) of 1992

                                  Chapal Kumar Biswas
                                               Vs.
                             The State of West Bengal &amp; Ors.

                        Mr. Debabrata Roy
                                ..for the Petitioner


                                             The   respondents   are   not   represented</pre>
<p id="p_1">                                     despite the matter having appeared in the warning
                                     list.
</p><p id="p_2">                           The petitioner was given an appointment as a secondary

                 teacher in a government-aided school in Nadia.        According to the

                 petitioner, after obtaining the appointment in 1992, the petitioner

                 was informed that the appointment stood annulled by reason of the

                 relevant panel having been recast and the petitioner's name not

                 figuring therein.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                           The petitioner says that his enquiries revealed that an

                 unsuccessful candidate had filed a petition in this court without

                 impleading this petitioner wherein an order was passed for the

                 relevant authorities to consider the matter afresh. It was apparently

                 on such basis and without any information being furnished to the

                 petitioner, that the panel was sought to be recast and the

                 petitioner's rights affected without the petitioner's knowledge.

</p><p id="p_4">                           On this petition being received on November 11, 1992, an

                 interim order was passed restraining the respondents from giving

                 any or further effect to the memorandum of September 9, 1992

                 issued by the Director of School Education, West Bengal.

</p><p id="p_5">                           The petitioner says that pursuant to such order, the

                 petitioner has continued in service and is on the verge of retirement.

</p><p id="p_6">                           Since the respondents show no interest despite the matter

                 appearing in the warning list and in view of the long-standing

                 interim order on this petition, W.P. 19179 (W) of 1992 is allowed by

                 confirming the interim order passed on November 11, 1992.
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">            2</span>



         The long pendency of this petition should not

 prejudice the petitioner in the matter of the benefits

 due to him in accordance with law.
</p>
<p id="p_7">         There will be no order as to costs.
</p>

be made available to the parties upon compliance with the requisite

formalities.
</p>
