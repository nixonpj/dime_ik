<p id="p_1">3   12-07-2016            Heard learned counsel for the parties.

</p><p id="p_2">                          Both the applications for grant of anticipatory bail arise out

                 of Tikapatti P.S. Case No. 10 of 2016, disclosing offences under
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                         2</span>




<a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_1">Sections 302</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_2">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">       For an occurrence alleged to have taken place on

03.02.2016, the First Information Report was lodged on

15.02.2016, on the basis of a written report of the informant.

</p><p id="p_4">        It is alleged that because of land dispute between the

accused persons and the informant's side, these petitioners have

been falsely implicated. It appears that father of the informant was

treated by a local doctor.        On 14.02.2016, the father of the

informant died in course of treatment. In order to explain the delay

in lodging the First Information Report, it has been mentioned that

because of land dispute and treatment of the injured, the First

Information Report could not be lodged earlier.

</p><p id="p_5">       Mr. Ramakant Sharma learned Senior counsel appearing

on behalf of the petitioners has submitted that there is no cogent

explanation for delay in lodging of the First Information Report.

He has, secondly, submitted that the post mortem report does not

suggest any external injury.
</p>
<p id="p_6">       Learned Additional Public Prosecutor appearing on behalf

of the State of Bihar has opposed the prayer for bail and has

submitted that the post mortem report does indicate some

occurrence of physical assault.
</p>
<p id="p_7">       Be that as it may, considering the genesis of the occurrence
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                       3</span>




              and the inordinate delay in lodging of the First Information

              Report, both the applications are allowed.

</p><p id="p_8">                     Let the petitioners above-named in the event of their

              arrest/surrender within four weeks from today in the Court below,

              be released on bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs. 10,000/-(ten

              thousand) each with two sureties of the like amount each to the

              satisfaction of the learned ACJM- V, Purnea in Tikapatti P. S.

              Case No. 10 of 2016, subject to the conditions as laid down under

              <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_3">Section 438(2)</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure.

</p><p id="p_9">                      This is subject to the condition that the petitioner shall

              present themselves before the police/court, as the case may be, as

              and when required and in the event of failure on their part to

              appear before the Court on two consecutive occasions, their bail

              bonds shall be liable to be cancelled.
</p>
<p id="p_10">

</p><p id="p_11">                               (Chakradhari Sharan Singh, J)
ArunKumar/-
