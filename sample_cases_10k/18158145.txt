<div class="doc_title">State vs Swarang Ved Singh on 11 September, 2008</div><div class="doc_author">Author: Sh. Sanjay Sharma</div>

<p id="p_1">     IN THE COURT OF SHRI SANJAY SHARMA : ADDL. SESSIONS JUDGE
                  KARKARDOOMA COURTS : DELHI.
</p>
<p id="p_2">           State         Versus Swarang Ved Singh

                                 FIR No. 81/1998
                                 Under Section 25, <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_1">Arms Act</a>
                                 PS Shahdra

                                 Swarang Ved Singh S/o Shiv Charan
                                 R/o 103, Gali No. 9, Balbir Nagar Extn
                                 Delhi


ORDER ON THE QUANTUM OF SENTENCE :
</p>
<p id="p_3">           The convict named above has been held guilty for the offence

punishable under <a href="/doc/73862/" id="a_2">Section 25</a> Arms Act, vide judgment dated 15.7.2008.



</p><p id="p_4">2.         I have heard the Ld. Addl. PP for the State and Shri SK Gill - Ld.

Counsel for convict. It has been argued by Ld. Counsel for the convict that

the convict is a person aged about 59 years and is having seven daughters

who are all school/college going and he also has a 90 years old mother. He

is the only bread earner of his family. Hence, he has prayed for a lenient

view.
</p>
<p id="p_5">

</p><p id="p_6">3.         On the contrary, it was argued on behalf of the State that the

convict was found in possession of a country made pistol and could have

done any serious offence.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><p id="p_8">4.         I have considered the aggravating &amp; mitigating circumstances.



</p><p id="p_9">5.         No previous criminal antecedents of the convict could be

placed on record by the State, nor any other case has been reported

against him after lodging of the present FIR. The present case pertains to


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                     1</span>
 the year 1998 and thus, the trial has already dragged for 10 years during

which the convict has suffered enough . He remained in JC during

investigation for 19 days.
</p>
<p id="p_10">

</p><p id="p_11">6.         Considering the totality of the circumstances including the age

of the convict and his family, he is hereby sentenced to Imprisonment for

the period already undergone by him and is further sentenced to pay a

fine of Rs.500/- failing which he shall undergo SI for 15 days, for the

offence punishable under <a href="/doc/73862/" id="a_3">Section 25</a>, <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_4">Arms Act</a>, 1959. He is set at liberty.

His bail bond stands discharged.
</p>
<p id="p_12">           Copy of the judgment and Order on sentence be given to the

convict free of cost. File be consigned to record room .

ANNOUNCED IN OPEN COURT
ON 11th day of September 2008


                                        (SANJAY SHARMA)
                                         ACMM cum ASJ
                                 KARKARDOOMA COURTS : DELHI.
</p>
