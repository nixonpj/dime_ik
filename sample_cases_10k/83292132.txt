 IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT MADRAS
DATED : 20.04.2015
CORAM
THE HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE P.N.PRAKASH

CRL.OP.No.9894 of 2015


Prasath			      ..    Petitioner
Vs

1.Inspector of Police,
   J-1, Saidapet Ps.,
   Chennai-15.

2.The Deputy Commissioner of Police,
   Adayar Police District,
   Adayar, Chennai.

3.The Commissioner of Police,
   Greater Chennai,
   Vepery, Chennai-7.	 		 ..    Respondents


	Criminal Original Petition filed under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C., praying to direct the respondents Police to register a criminal case against the culprits based on the complaint of this petitioner dated 19.12.2014 and 31.01.2015.

	 For Petitioner     :   Mr.T.Jaishankar
	 For Respondent  :   Mr.C.Emalias, Addl. Public Prosecutor		 

  O R D E R
</pre>
<p id="p_1">	This Criminal Original Petition has been filed praying to direct the respondents Police to register a criminal case against the culprits based on the complaint of this petitioner dated 19.12.2014 and 31.01.2015.
</p>
<p id="p_2">	2. Heard Mr.T.Jaishankar, learned counsel for the petitioner and Mr.C.Emalias, learned Additional Public Prosecutor appearing for the respondents. 
</p>
<p id="p_3">	3. It appears that this petitioner is the tenant of the opposite party.  Even after he vacated the premises, the landlord is not returning the advance amount.  Therefore, it is purely a civil case.
</p> 
<p id="p_4">	4. Hence, this petition is closed with a liberty to the petitioner, to workout his remedy in the manner known to law.
</p>
<p id="p_5">20.04.2015
gya



P.N.PRAKASH, J
gya
To

</p><p id="p_6">1.Inspector of Police,
   J-1, Saidapet Ps.,
   Chennai-15.
</p>
<p id="p_7">2.The Deputy Commissioner of Police,
   Adayar Police District,
   Adayar, Chennai.
</p>
<p id="p_8">3.The Commissioner of Police,
   Greater Chennai,
   Vepery, Chennai-7.
</p>
<p id="p_9">4. The Public Prosecutor,
    High Court, Maras.
</p><p id="p_10">		CRL.OP.No.9894 of 2015





				         





                                                                      




20.04.2015




Crl.O.P.No.9894 of 2015 

P.N.PRAKASH, J,

	The matter is listed under the caption 'for being mentioned' at  the instance of the learned counsel appearing for the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_11">	2. On 20.04.2015, this Court passed an order dismissing the case as civil in nature on the premises that it is a landlord and tenant dispute.  The matter was mentioned before this Court and posted today.  Today, the learned counsel for the petitioner brought  to the notice of this Court that the complaint dated 19.12.2014, from which, it is clear that the petitioner's father was a tenant and after the death of the petitioner's father, the landlord is refusing to return the lease advance amount to the petitioner.  Even CSR No.824 of 2014 states that it is an allegation of cheating. 
</p>
<p id="p_12">	3. Under these circumstances, the respondent police is directed to conduct enquiry on the petitioner's complaint dated 19.12.2014 in CSR No.824 of 2015 and take action in accordance with law, following the decision laid down by the Hon'ble Supreme Court in Lalita Kumari v. Government of Uttarpradesh and Others reported in (2014) 2 SCC 1.  
</p>
<p id="p_13">sms/ds
10.06.2015
Note: Issue order copy on 11.06.2015

P.N.PRAKASH, J,

sms/ds





















Crl.O.P.No.9894 of 2015
