<div class="doc_title">Manish Parihar vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 3 January, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                         1


                                      M.Cr.C.No.14820/2016
               (Manish Parihar Vs. State of M.P.)
03.01.2017
      Mr. Amit Rawat, learned counsel for the applicant.
      Mr.    R.S.   Yadav,   learned   Public   Prosecutor   for   the
respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Heard.
</p><p id="p_2">      This is first bail application under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of Cr.P.C. The
applicant has been arrested in Crime No.150/2016 registered at
Police Station Sewda, District Datia (M.P), for the offence
punishable under <a href="/doc/329571/" id="a_2">Sections 392</a> of IPC and Section 11/113 of
MPDVPK Act.
</p><p id="p_3">      As per prosecution case, the complainant was coming to his
house after collecting dues from various customers. He was having
Rs.13,500/-, at about 7:30 PM when he was on the way at that
time Ajju Tomar and Manish met and stopped him and asked to
hand over the cash when he resisted Ajju caught hold his hand
and Manish slapped him and snatched cash and ATM kept in his
pocket.
</p><p id="p_4">      It is submitted by learned counsel for the applicant that the
applicant has not committed any offence. He has falsely been
implicated in this case. Report of the incident has been lodged on
the next day. During investigation, in pursuance of memorandum
of the applicant cash of Rs.2,500/- has been recovered. The
applicant is under custody since 05.11.2016. Trial will take
sometime. On these grounds learned counsel for the applicant
prays for grant of bail.
</p><p id="p_5">      Prayer is opposed by learned Public Prosecutor.
      Case diary perused.
</p><p id="p_6">      Considering the facts and circumstances of the case coupled
with the fact that report has been lodged after incorporate delay
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                                 2</span>

       and the applicant is under custody since 05.11.2016, without
       commenting on the merits of the case, the application is allowed.
       It is directed that the applicant shall be released on bail on his
       furnishing personal bond in the sum of Rs.50,000/- (Rupees
       Fifty Thousand Only) with one solvent surety in the like amount
       to the satisfaction of Trial Court/Committal Court.
</p><p id="p_7">             This order will remain operative subject to compliance of the
       following conditions by the applicant :-
</p><p id="p_8">          1. The applicant will comply with all the terms and conditions
             of the bond executed by him;
</p><p id="p_9">          2. The applicant will cooperate in the investigation/trial, as the
             case may be;
</p><p id="p_10">          3. The applicant will not indulge himself in extending
             inducement, threat or promise to any person acquainted
             with the facts of the case so as to dissuade him/her from
             disclosing such facts to the Court or to the Police Officer, as
             the case may be;
</p><p id="p_11">          4. The applicant shall not commit an offence similar to the
             offence of which he is accused;
</p><p id="p_12">          5. The applicant will not seek unnecessary adjournments
             during the trial; and
</p><p id="p_13">          6. The applicant will not leave India without previous
             permission of the trial Court/Investigating Officer, as the
             case may be.
</p><p id="p_14">             A copy of this order be sent to the Court concerned for
       compliance.
</p><p id="p_15">             C.C. as per rules.
</p>

<p id="p_16">                                                  (D.K. Paliwal)
                                                      Judge
bj/-
</p>
