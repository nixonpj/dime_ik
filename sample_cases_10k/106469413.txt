<p id="p_1">       The petitioner has approached this Court aggrieved by the

rejection by the respondents of its request in Ext.P2 application for

permission to revise a return under the KVAT Act. It is the case of

the petitioner in the writ petition that it had approached the

respondents for revising the return for the year 2014-2015 since

there was a technical mistakes that crept into the return, while

uploading the details of branch transfers.                    It is stated that the

mistakes in the return have affected the petitioner prejudicially

since they are not in a position to issue F Forms against branch

transfers.
</p>
<p id="p_2">


</p><p id="p_3">      2.  I have heard the learned counsel appearing for the

petitioner as also the learned Government Pleader appearing for

the respondents.
</p>
<p id="p_4"><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                 -2-</span>
W.P.(C).No.20706 Of 2017-(K )




      The     learned    Government   Pleader   would   submit    on

instructions, that since the audited reports have already been

submitted by the petitioner, so long as the revision of returns does

not result in any alteration in the turnover, the returns can be

revised for the limited purposes of enabling the petitioner to issue

F Forms to cover the branch transfers effected. Taking note of the

said submission, I dispose the writ petition with a direction to the

respondents to permit the petitioner to revise the returns for the

assessment year 2014-2015 under the Kerala Value Added Tax Act

in the presence of the Assessing Officer. The respondents shall do

the needful in enabling the KVATIS system to accept the revision

of the returns by the petitioner within a period to three weeks

from the date of receipt of a copy of this judgment.




</p><p id="p_5">      The writ petition disposed as above.
</p>
