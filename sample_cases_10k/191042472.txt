                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.56362 of 2017

                 1. Md. Shamim, S/o Late Amiruddin, Resident of village Simaria (Naya
                 tola), P.S. Karha District- Katihar.

                                                                                  .... ....   Petitioner/s
                                                         Versus
                 1. The State of Bihar.

                                                                 .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     : Mr. Hansraj
                 For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr. Sri Chandrasen Prasad Singh
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE SANJAY PRIYA
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   01-12-2017                Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and the State.

</p><p id="p_2">                              The petitioner apprehends arrest in Mahila P.S. Case

                 No. 52 of 2017 instituted for the offence under <a href="/doc/1279834/" id="a_1">Sections 376</a>, <a href="/doc/140515/" id="a_2">313</a>

                 and <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_3">506</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                              It is alleged in the written report that the informant is 30

                 years old married lady having three children. Her husband is living

                 out of village for livelihood. This petitioner started giving pressure

                 to her for physical relation on seeing her alone and when she

                 protested, he threatened to kill her and her other family. The

                 petitioner established physical relation with her at the point of

                 three nut about three years ago. She became pregnant then he got

                 her pregnancy terminated by giving medicine. She again became

                 pregnant and he wanted to terminate her pregnancy, but she
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Patna High Court Cr.M isc. No.56362 of 2017 (2) dt.01-12-2017</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                            2/3</span>




             opposed and told the story to her husband. Thereafter, the present

             case has been lodged.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                            Counsel for the petitioner has submitted that the

             informant is 30 years old married lady having three children. It is a

             case of consented sex even if allegation is taken to be true.

             Counsel for the petitioner further stated that no such occurrence

             took place. He has falsely been implicated by the informant on

             account of land dispute.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                            Be that as it may from the narration made in the written

             report, it appears that even if sexual act was committed by the

             petitioner, it was with consent of informant who was 30 years old

             married lady.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                            In such circumstances, prayer for anticipatory bail is

             allowed and it is ordered that the petitioner named above in the

             event of his arrest or surrender in the court below within four

             weeks from the date of receipt/production of copy of this order,

             shall be released on bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs. 10,000/-

             (ten thousand) with two sureties of the like amount each in

             connection with Mahila P.S. Case No. 52 of 2017 to the

             satisfaction of learned S.D.J.M., Katihar, subject to condition as

             laid down u/S 438(2) of the <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_4">Cr.P.C</a>. with further conditions (1)

             bailors should be local having sufficient immovable property
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">           Patna High Court Cr.M isc. No.56362 of 2017 (2) dt.01-12-2017</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                                                      3/3</span>




                       within the jurisdiction of the court concerned, (2) petitioner shall

                       cooperate in the trial and shall be present on each and every date

                       fixed by the court and absence on two consecutive dates without

                       proper and reasonable reason, will automatically cancel bail bond

                       of the petitioner and (3) if petitioner tampers with the evidence or

                       the witnesses of the case, in that case, prosecution will be at

                       liberty to move for cancellation of bail of the petitioner.
