<div class="doc_title">Dr.Anup Singh Baghel vs The State Of M.P. on 8 December, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">                W.P. No. 18184/2014
08/12/2014
     Shri S.P. Mishra, learned counsel for the
petitioner.
     Shri Rahul Rawat, learned Panel Lawyer for the
respondents/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">     With the consent of learned counsel for the
parties, the matter is heard finally.
</p><p id="p_2">     In this petition, the petitioner, inter alia, has
prayed for a direction to the respondents to
consider the representations and further for a
direction to post the petitioner in Government
Adarsh Science College, Rewa, by issuing fresh list.
</p><p id="p_3">     Learned counsel for the petitioner submitted
that with regard to his grievance, the petitioner has
filed representations to the respondent No.3 and the
instant petition may be disposed of with the
direction to the said authority to consider and
decide the same. On the other hand, learned Panel
Lawyer fairly submitted that the representations
filed by the petitioner shall be dealt with in
accordance with law.
</p><p id="p_4">     Taking into account the submissions made by
learned counsel for the parties and as agreed to by
them, the writ petition is disposed of with the
direction to the respondent No.3 the Additional
     Director, Higher Education, Government of M.P.
    Rewa, o consider and decide the representation
    filed by the petitioner expeditiously preferebaly
    within a period of two months from the date of
    production of certified copy of this order. While
    deciding the representations filed by the petitioner,
    the respondent No.3 shall afford an opportunity of
    hearing to all necessary parties. It is made clear
    that this Court has not expressed any opinion on the
    merits of the case.
</p><p id="p_5">         C.C. as per rules.
</p>

<p id="p_6">                                           (K.K. Trivedi)
                                              Judge
