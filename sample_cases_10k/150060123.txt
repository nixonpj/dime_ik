<div class="doc_title">Lieutenant Colonel Horipal Singh vs M.K.Anand on 6 November, 2012</div><pre id="pre_1">                         W.P.No. 15787 / 2007
        (Lieutenant Colonel Horipal Singh..Vs..M.K.Anand &amp; others)

      06-11-2012

              Heard Shri V. Yadav, learned counsel for the petitioner on
      the question of admission and interim relief.

              The petitioner has filed this petition alleging inaction on
      the part of the police authorities in respect of the complaint filed
      by the petitioner regarding fraud being played by the respondent</pre>
<p id="p_1">      No.1.
</p>
<p id="p_2">              The appropriate remedy of the petitioner, as has been
      held by the Supreme Court in the cases of <a href="/doc/1836621/" id="a_1">Sakiri Vasu v. State
      of Uttar Pradesh and others</a>, (2008) 2 SCC 409 and <a href="/doc/336701/" id="a_2">Divine
      Retreat Centre v. State of Kerala and others</a>, (2008) 3 SCC
      542, is to take up appropriate proceedings as prescribed and
      provided in<a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_3"> the Criminal Procedure Code</a> before the competent
      Court in accordance with law for redressal of his grievance.
</p>
<p id="p_3">              In view of the aforesaid, the petition filed by the petitioner
      is disposed of with liberty to the petitioner to take up appropriate
      proceedings before the competent Criminal Court or the Civil
      Court in accordance with law.
</p>
<p id="p_4">              With the aforesaid liberty, the petition filed by the
      petitioner stands disposed of accordingly.
</p>
<p id="p_5">              C.C. as per rules.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                                             ( R.S.Jha )
                                                               Judge
mct
