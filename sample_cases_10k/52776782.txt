<div class="doc_title">Mantu Rai vs State Of Bihar on 13 August, 2008</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                 Cr.Misc. No.33376 of 2008
                                          MANTU RAI
                                             Versus
                                      STATE OF BIHAR
                                           -----------
</pre>
<p id="p_1">2.   13.8.2008.                 Heard the learned counsel for the petitioner and counsel

                   appearing on behalf of the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                The petitioner is an accused in a case under <a href="/doc/1119707/" id="a_1">Sections 395</a>

                   and <a href="/doc/1865117/" id="a_2">397</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that one of the

                   co-accused has been granted bail by this Court vide Cr.Misc.No.55070

                   of 2007 disposed of on 27.3.2008. The petitioner has remained in

                   custody for about 2 years.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                Considering the period of custody, the petitioner Mantu Rai

                   is directed to be released on bail on furnishing bail bond of

                   Rs.10,000/-(Ten Thousand) with two sureties of the like amount each

                   to the satisfaction of the C.J.M., Patna in connection with Budha

                   Colony P. S. Case No. 163 of 2005 with the condition that one of the

                   bailors should be closed family member of the petitioner and the

                   petitioner should appear in the trial of this case on each and every date

                   as and when required by the court below and if he absents even on a

                   single date without any proper cause his bail bond shall stand

                   cancelled.
</p>
<p id="p_5">


</p><pre id="pre_2">            U.K.                                                     (Sheema Ali Khan,J)
 </pre>
