<div class="doc_title">Binod Mahto @ Binod Singh And Anr vs The State Of Bihar on 27 February, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                              CRIMINAL MISCELLANEOUS No.12131 of 2019
                  Arising Out of PS. Case No.-207 Year-2018 Thana- SASARAM MUFFSIL District- Rohtas
                 ======================================================
           1.     Binod Mahto @ Binod Singh Son of Kedar Mahto R/o Village- Dahiyar,
                  P.S- Sasaram(M) , District- Rohtas
           2.    Saryu Mahto @ Saryu Singh Son of Dukhi Mahto @ Dukhi Singh R/o
                 Village- Dahiyar, P.S- Sasaram (M), District- Rohtas

                                                                                 ... ... Petitioner/s
                                                       Versus
                 The State Of Bihar

                                                        ... ... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :      Mr.Rajani Kant Singh
                 For the Opposite Party/s :      Mr.Gauri Shankar Gupta
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE PRABHAT KUMAR JHA
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   27-02-2019                  Heard both sides.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                The petitioners apprehend their arrest in Sasaram (M)

                   P.S. Case No.207 of 2018 registered under Section 30(a) of the

                   Bihar Prohibition and <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_1">Excise Act</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                The informant got information from reliable source

                   that Binod Mahto, Saryu (petitioners) and Durgesh were running

                   Bhatti of illicit liquor. On such information, raid was conducted

                   and three bags containing 10 litres of country made liquor each

                   bag are said to have been recovered.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                Learned counsel for the petitioners submits that the

                   petitioners have no manner of concern with the country made

                   liquor. There is no material to show that the petitioners ever
            Patna High Court CR. MISC. No.12131 of 2019(2) dt.27-02-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                       2/2</span>




                     indulged in illicit trade of liquor.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                  Having considered the fact that nothing has been

                     recovered from possession of the petitioners and the only fact

                     that the informant got reliable information about involvement of

                     the petitioners, the above named petitioners, in the event of their

                     arrest or surrender before the learned court below within a

                     period of four weeks from the date of receipt/production of a

                     copy of this order, are directed to be enlarged on bail on

                     furnishing bail bonds of Rs.10,000/- (Ten thousand) each with

                     two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of

                     learned Additional Sessions Judge II-cum-Special Judge,Excise,

                     Rohtas at Sasaram in connection with Sasaram (M) P.S. Case

                     No.207 of 2018, subject to the conditions as laid down under

                     <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_2">Section 438</a> (2) of the Code of Criminal Procedure.



</p><p id="p_6">                                             (Prabhat Kumar Jha, J)
Harish/-
</p>
