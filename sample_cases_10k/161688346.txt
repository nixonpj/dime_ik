              CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE AJAY KUMAR TRIPATHI
              ORAL ORDER</pre>
<p id="p_1">                                          ***************

</p><p id="p_2">03.   21.03.2013           Stand of the State is that a departmental

                    proceeding has now been initiated against the petitioner

                    for disappearing since 1995. This decision is dated 20th

                    of December, 2010, Annexure - E to the counter

                    affidavit.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                           The stand of the petitioner does merit some

                    consideration that if a departmental proceeding has

                    been initiated, it can only be done against a regular

                    employee. Secondly, if the joining of the petitioner has

                    not been taken or shown to have been taken, how can a

                    departmental proceeding be initiated or continued

                    against an employee. If the above two aspects is also

                    ignored, then petitioner is entitled to some kind of
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Patna High Court CWJC No.2963 of 2010 (3) dt.21-03-2013</span>
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                          2/2</span>




                    subsistence allowance as well, if he is treated to be

                    under suspension or payment of salary, if he is treated to

                    be in employment. All these aspects have significance to

                    the issue in this regard.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                            But the Court will not express any opinion on the

                    issue at this stage. Let the respondents do what is

                    required to be done. But it should be done within a

                    reasonable time-frame of four months, as Annexure - E

                    is of the year 2010 and already more than two and a half

                    years have gone past since then. If the petitioner would

                    like to cooperate, then let him do so or else the law will

                    take its course.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                            Writ Application is disposed off with observation

                    as above.
</p>
<p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">                                                (Ajay Kumar Tripathi, J.)
SKM/-
</p>
