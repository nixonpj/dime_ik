<div class="doc_title">Dr. Meenakshi Chatterjee vs The President on 26 June, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">ORDER SHEET                      WP No.682 of 2017
                     IN THE HIGH COURT AT CALCUTTA
                          Constitutional Writ Jurisdiction
                                    ORIGINAL SIDE




                              DR. MEENAKSHI CHATTERJEE
                                       Versus
              THE PRESIDENT, GOVERNING BODY, BASANTIDEVI COLLEGE &amp; ORS


     BEFORE:
    The Hon'ble JUSTICE ARINDAM SINHA
    Date : 26th June, 2018.

                                         For Petitioner: Mr. Sanjib kr. Mal with
                                         Mr. Atanu Roychoudhuri, Mrs. Reshmi Ghosh
                                                      &amp; Mr. Pushan Majumdar, Advs.
                                         For Respondent nos.1 &amp; 2 : Mr. Ashok</pre>
<p id="p_1">                                         Sarkar, Adv. with Mr. Subrata Banerjee, Advs.


          The Court:-Petitioner complains her salary is not being

paid in full since April, 2017 and issues regarding adjustment of

leave.
</p>
<p id="p_2">         Mr.Mal, learned Advocate appears on behalf of petitioner

and refers to letter dated 20th April, 2017 from the College to his

client enclosing minutes of meeting on 17th April, 2017 of the

Governing Body. It appears from the said minutes that there was

earlier   Governing      Body    resolution    for     deduction     of   salary   of

petitioner effective from April, 2017 (not exceeding 50% of her

total    salary).    This   earlier     Governing      Body   resolution     is    not

before Court. Mr. Mal submits, leave related issues arise because

his client was entrusted work by Central Government and grant

received,     50%   of   which   was   paid   to     the   College   since   Central
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                       2</span>


Government was using her services, an Associate Professor in the

College.
</p>
<p id="p_3">    On behalf of the College, it is submitted that Director of

Public Instruction (DPI) is a necessary party since that is the

office, which sanctions and pays the salaries.

    The      College     will    produce      Governing   Body    resolution,

containing decision to deduct salary, referred to in minutes of

meeting held on 17th April, 2017.          College will also inform Court

as to how it has dealt with deducted amount. College will also

provide    instruction   for    submissions    being   made   regarding   money

received from grants for work done by petitioner.

    Mr. Ashok Sarkar, learned Advocate appearing for respondent

nos. 1 &amp; 2, files Vakalatnama upon obtaining leave.

</p><p id="p_4">     List on 10th July, 2018.
</p>
<p id="p_5">


</p><p id="p_6">                                                    (ARINDAM SINHA, J.)
