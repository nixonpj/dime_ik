                                PRESENT:

      AGAINST THE AWARD IN OPMV 768/2003 of M.A.C.T., THALASSERY
                            DATED 03-11-2009
APPELLANT(S)/PETITIONER:
----------------------------------------------

       UNNI KALLEN
       S/O.NARAYANAN, KAKKADANCHAL, MATTOOL NORTH
       KANNUR DISTRICT.

       BY ADV. SRI.M.V.AMARESAN

RESPONDENT(S)/RESPONDENT NO.3:
------------------------------------------------------------

       UNITED INDIA INSURANCE CO.LTD.
       PAYYANNUR, KANNUR.

       BY SMT. R. REMA

       THIS MOTOR ACCIDENT CLAIMS APPEAL  HAVING COME UP FOR ADMISSION
ON  20-02-2015, THE COURT ON THE SAME DAY DELIVERED THE FOLLOWING:



                       T.R. RAMACHANDRAN NAIR &amp;
                                   P.V. ASHA, JJ.
                     - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -
                           M.A.C.A.No.1687 of 2010
                     - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -
                     Dated this the 20th February, 2015

                           JUDGMENT
</pre>
<p id="p_1">Ramachandran Nair, J.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">      The claimant before the Tribunal is the appellant herein. He was

injured in a motor vehicle accident on 17.7.2001. Before the Tribunal, an

amount of Rs.1,10,000/- was claimed as compensation and the tribunal

granted compensation in the following manner:

</p><pre id="pre_2">                          Head of claim                       Amount awarded by
                                                               the Tribunal (Rs)

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">          Transport to hospital                                                500</span>

          Medical expenses                                                    2900

          Pain and suffering                                                  3000

          Compensation for loss of one tooth                                  3000

          Total                                                               9400


</pre><p id="p_4">      2. There is loss of tooth and the appellant had to undergo root

canalization as evident from Ext.A6 certificate. The Doctor has charged

Rs.1,512/- as seen from Exts.A5 and A6.                   The Tribunal has granted a

sum of Rs.2,900/- towards medical expenses and Rs.3,000/- for pain and

suffering. Towards loss of one tooth, a sum of Rs.3,000/- is granted. No

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">MACA 1687/2010                      2</span>

amount is granted for extra nourishment. Root canalization was done

on 20.8.2001. It is clear that the appellant would have suffered much

pain. Therefore, we grant a further amount of Rs.5,000/- towards

compensation for pain and suffering and as compensation for loss of

tooth, another amount of Rs.7,000/- is granted. Towards compensation

for loss of amenities, we grant a sum of Rs.10000/-. Accordingly, the

total compensation will be as follows:
</p>
<p id="p_5">


</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">            Head of claim     Amount awarded by    Modified award
                               the Tribunal (Rs) passed by this Court
                                                        (Rs)

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">         Transport to hospital                500                 500</span>

         Medical expenses                    2900                2900

         Pain and suffering                  3000                8000

         Compensation      for               3000               10000
         loss of one tooth

         Loss of amenities                                      10000

         Total                                                  31400
        (Rupees Thirtyone thousand and four hundred only)


The enhanced amount of compensation will carry interest at the rate of

9% per annum and the insurance company is directed to deposit the

entire amount of compensation less the amount already deposited

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">MACA 1687/2010                    3</span>

before the Tribunal, within a period of three months.</blockquote><p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">     The appeal is allowed as above. The parties shall bear their

respective costs in the appeal.
</p>
<p id="p_8">
</p><p id="p_9">                      (T.R. RAMACHANDRAN NAIR, JUDGE.)




                                  (P.V. ASHA, JUDGE.)
