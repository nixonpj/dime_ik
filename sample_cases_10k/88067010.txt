<div class="doc_title">Dayashankar Turi ( Basod ) @ Taya ... vs State Of Chhattisgarh on 10 August, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                                       1

                                                                    NAFR

          HIGH COURT OF CHHATTISGARH, BILASPUR

                        MCRC No. 3843 of 2015

      • Dayashankar Turi (Basod) @ Taya Turi, S/o Sukan Basod,
        aged about 55 years, r/o village Godasoth, PS Kusmi, Distt.
        Balrampur-Ramanujganj (C.G.)

                                                           ---- Applicant

                                 Versus

      • State Of Chhattisgarh Through the Station House Officer,
        Police Station Samripath, Distt. Balrampur-Ramanujganj
        (C.G.)

                                                      ---- Non-applicant



For Applicant:        Mr. Jitendra Shrivastava, Advocate.
For Respondent/State: Mr. Qamrul Aziz, Panel Lawyer</pre>



<p id="p_1">              Hon'ble Shri Justice Sanjay K. Agrawal


                             Order On Board

10/08/2015

Heard.
</p>
<p id="p_2">(1)     This is an application filed under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of the Cr.P.C. for

grant of regular bail to the applicant, who has been arrested in

connection with crime No.18/2015 registered at Police Station

Samripath, for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/619940/" id="a_2">Sections 363</a>, <a href="/doc/1153041/" id="a_3">370(1)(5)</a>

of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">
(2)     Case of the prosecution, in brief, is that applicant is said to have

abducted the minor girl for the purpose of trafficking on 3.5.2015 and,

thereby, committed the aforesaid offences.
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                2</span>



</p><p id="p_4">      (3)    Counsel for the applicant submits that applicant is aged about

      55 years and he has falsely been implicated in the offence as he has

      not committed any offence. He further submits that minor girl has come

      to the applicant's house voluntarily and he is not abducted her as she

      has been recovered on the same day in the village itself; the applicant

      is in jail since 4.5.2015; and the charge sheet has already been filed

      and, therefore, the applicant may be released on bail.


      (4)    On the other hand, counsel for the State opposes the bail

      application.
</p>
<p id="p_5">
      (5)   Taking into consideration the facts &amp; circumstances of the case;

      and the role of the present applicant in the offence in question;

      considering the facts that the minor girl has been recovered on the

      same day in the village itself; applicant is in jail since 4.5.2015; and the

      charge sheet has already been filed; this court is of the view that it is a

      fit case to release the applicant on bail. Accordingly, the bail

      application is allowed.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
      (6)    Accused/applicant is directed to be released on bail on his

      executing a personal bond in the sum of Rs.25,000/- with one surety in

      the like sum to the satisfaction of the trial Court. He is directed to

      appear before the trial Court on each and every date given to him by

      the said Court till disposal of the trial.

</p><p id="p_7">             Certified copy, as per rules.
</p>
<p id="p_8">
</p><p id="p_9">                                                                      Sd/-
</p><p id="p_10">                                                             (Sanjay K. Agrawal)
                                                                   Judge
