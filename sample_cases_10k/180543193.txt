        IN THE COURT OF SH. ASHUTOSH KUMAR :
   ADDL. SESSIONS JUDGE­3 : DWARKA COURTS : DELHI.



In the matter of: ­

CA No. 63/2014.



Ankur Bhanot,
S/o Sh. Ravinder Bhanot,
R/o RZ­D­1/136, Gali No. 5,
Mahavir Enclave, Delhi.                                  ... Appellant.

           Vs.

Government of NCT of Delhi.                              ... Respondent.</pre>



<pre id="pre_2">Date of Institution.              :   9.10.2014.
Date of Arguments.                :   9.10.2014.
Date of Order.                    :   9.10.2014.



9.10.2014.

</pre><p id="p_1">                 Fresh   criminal   appeal   u/s   374   <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_1">CrPC</a>   has   been 

received by way of assignment, vide order of the ld. District &amp; 

Sessions Judge, South West District, Dwarka Courts, Delhi.  It 

be checked and registered.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
Present:         Appellant­convict with ld. counsel Sh. Kanwar S.N.
</p><p id="p_3">                 <a href="/doc/734813/" id="a_2">Sh. V.K. Swami</a>, ld. Addl. PP for State/respondent.

Page No. 1.                                                   Contd... ... ...
</p><p id="p_4"> CA No. 63/14.                                            Ankur Bhanot Vs. State.
</p>
<p id="p_5">


</p><p id="p_6">                Heard.  Perused.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                At   the   request   of   ld.   counsel   for   appellant,   it   is 

ordered   that   the   robkar   be   issued   calling   the   TCR   for   today 

itself.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                At this stage, TCR has been received.
</p>
<p id="p_9">                Arguments on the present criminal appeal heard.

                Perused the entire record including TCR carefully.

                The present appeal u/s 374 <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_3">CrPC</a> has been filed by 

the   appellant­convict   against   the   impugned   judgment   of 

conviction   dated   6.9.2014,   passed   by   Ms.   Ruchika   Singla,   ld. 

MM,   Dwarka   Courts,   Delhi,   in   the   case   titled   as   "State   Vs. 

Ankur   Bhanot",   FIR   No.   25/10   of   PS   Dwarka   South,   u/s 

454/380/34 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_4">IPC</a>, vide which the appellant herein was convicted 

for   the   offence   u/s   454   <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_5">IPC</a>   and   order   on   sentence   dated 

10.9.2014, whereby the appellant was sentenced to undergo SI 

for a period of one year with fine of Rs. 5,000/­ for the offence 

u/s 454 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_6">IPC</a> and in default of payment of fine within 30 days 

from the order of sentence, the appellant was to undergo further 

SI for a period of three months and benefit of <a href="/doc/914361/" id="a_7">Section 428</a> CrPC 

was also extended to him.
</p>
<p id="p_10">


Page No. 2.                                                          Contd... ... ...
</p><p id="p_11"> CA No. 63/14.                                           Ankur Bhanot Vs. State.
</p>
<p id="p_12">
</p><p id="p_13">                               ­ :: ORDER :: ­



</p><p id="p_14">1.        Ld.  counsel   for   appellant   has   stated   that   he   is   not 

challenging the judgment of conviction dated 6.9.2014 of the ld. 

Trial   Court,   whereby   the   appellant   was   held   guilty   for   the 

offence u/s 454 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_8">IPC</a>, and the order on sentence dated 10.9.2014 

to the extent of imposition of fine of Rs. 5,000/­ in default of 

payment of fine, the appellant was to undergo further SI for a 

period   of   three   months,   but   is   challenging   the   order   of 

substantive   sentence   dated   10.9.2014   whereby   he   was 

sentenced to undergo SI for a period of one year for the offence 

u/s 454 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_9">IPC</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_15">

</p><p id="p_16">2.        On the point of sentence, ld. counsel for appellant has 

stated at bar that appellant is 22 years old and at the time of 

offence in question, he was only 18 years old, is having family to 

support,   is   a   student,   is   first   time   offender   and   no   useful 

purpose   would   be   served   by   sending   him   for   further 

incarceration,   as   he   has   already   spent   27   days   in   JC   in   this 

case.  He has also submitted that after the aforesaid offence, the 

appellant   has not  committed  any  further   offence  till date and 

the  appellant is feeling guilty for his conduct, is remorseful for 

the   same   and   shall   not   repeat   the  same   and   may   be   given   a 



Page No. 3.                                                         Contd... ... ...
</p><p id="p_17"> CA No. 63/14.                                           Ankur Bhanot Vs. State.
</p>
<p id="p_18">
chance to reform himself.
</p>
<p id="p_19">

</p><p id="p_20">3.        On   the   other   hand,   ld.   APP   for   respondent/State   has 

submitted that there is no infirmity or illegality in the impugned 

judgment   of   conviction   and   order   on   sentence   and   that   the 

same may be upheld.
</p>
<p id="p_21">

</p><p id="p_22">4.        Since the ld. counsel for appellant has not challenged 

the judgment of conviction u/s 454 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_10">IPC</a> dated 6.9.2014 and the 

order on sentence dated 10.9.2014 to the extent of imposition of 

fine   of   Rs.   5,000/­   upon   the  appellant,  of   the   ld.   Trial   Court 

and, hence the same stand upheld.
</p>
<p id="p_23">

</p><p id="p_24">5.        However on the point of order on sentence, considering 

the totality of facts and circumstances of the case, in view of the 

reasons mentioned by the ld. counsel for appellant, in view of 

the fact that the appellant is of young age and is guilty of the 

offence u/s 454 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_11">IPC</a>, is first time offender, has family to support 

and has already spent 27 days in JC and also in view of the fact 

that there is no previous conviction of the appellant, I am of the 

considered opinion that on the point of quantum of sentence, 

ends of justice would be met, if the appellant is sentenced to the 

period   already   undergone   by   him.     Ordered   accordingly.     The 



Page No. 4.                                                         Contd... ... ...
</p><p id="p_25"> CA No. 63/14.                                       Ankur Bhanot Vs. State.
</p>
<p id="p_26">
order   on   sentence   dated   10.9.2014,   passed   by   the   ld.   Trial 

Court, is accordingly modified.
</p>
<p id="p_27">

</p><p id="p_28">6.       Ld. counsel for appellant undertakes that the appellant 

shall deposit the aforesaid fine amount before the ld. Trial Court 

before 4.00 pm today itself.
</p>
<p id="p_29">

</p><p id="p_30">7.       The appeal is disposed of accordingly.



</p><p id="p_31">8.       A copy of this order alongwith TCR be sent back to the 

ld. Trial Court, for information and compliance.



</p><p id="p_32">9.       Appeal file be consigned to record room.



Announced in the open Court on 9.10.2014.
