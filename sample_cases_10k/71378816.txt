                     JUDGMENT
</pre>
<p id="p_1">     Heard the learned counsel for the appellant-

Insurer and the learned counsel for the respondents.


</p><p id="p_2">     2.    In respect of the injuries sustained by the

claimant-respondent No.1, Tribunal has awarded total

compensation of Rs.96,797/- by assessing the disability

at 20% to the whole body and taking income of the

claimant as Rs.2,400/- per month.         The accident

occurred on 03.05.2008, when the claimant was driving

the vehicle bearing No.KA-03/AA-1994 and he has

suffered disability. Based on the injuries recorded and

also discharge summary of the claimant, it is seen
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                 3</span>




appellant has sustained fracture of head of femur and

also dislocation of hip joint. The Tribunal taking his

income at Rs.2,400/- after deducting 60%, applying the

appropriate    factor     has   awarded    compensation    of

Rs.96,797/-.    The only question that is raised by the

appellant - insurer is that although the disability has

not   been     properly    calculated     awarding   of   the

compensation taking 20% disability into consideration

is unreasonable.
</p>
<p id="p_3">
</p><p id="p_4">      3.     What is not in dispute is that the fracture of

femur and dislocation of the hip joint due to accident

will come in the way of discharging his duties. I do not

see any error or infirmity in the compensation awarded

by the Tribunal.
</p>
<p id="p_5">
</p><p id="p_6">      Accordingly, appeal is dismissed.            Amount in

deposit be transferred to the Workmen's Compensation

Commissioner, Bangalore for disbursement.


</p><p id="p_7">                                            Sd/-
</p><p id="p_8">                                          JUDGE
