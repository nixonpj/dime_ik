<p id="p_1">      The petitioner has approached this Court aggrieved by the stand

of the 4th respondent that while accepting /3 of the disputed amount
                                               1  rd



of tax, as directed to be furnished by the Appellate Tribunal in Ext.P7

order, the credit of tax amounts paid by the petitioner before the

lower authority will not be granted to the petitioner. I find from a

perusal of Ext.P7 order, that is produced along with the writ petition,

that the Appellate Tribunal, while considering a stay petition

preferred by the petitioner in an appeal against the order of the lower

authority, clearly found that a stay could be granted on condition that

the petitioner deposited /3 of the disputed amount, and made it clear
                         1  rd



that the amounts already paid by the petitioner should be given credit

to. In view of the directions in Ext.P7 order, it would follow that the

petitioner is now required to make only a payment of approximately

Rs.40,000/-. Accordingly, I dispose the writ petition, with a direction

that if the petitioner pays an amount of Rs.40,000/- to the 4th

respondent, and furnishes a simple bond for the balance amount of tax

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">W.P.(C).No.35064/2017             2</span>




found to be due from the petitioner, then the same shall be treated as

in compliance with the directions in Ext.P7 order, and the Tribunal

will proceed to hear the appeal preferred by the petitioner in due

course, and Exts.P4 and P5 notices will not be enforced against the

petitioner during the pendency of the appeal before the Tribunal. The

petitioner shall produce a copy of the writ petition together with a

copy of this judgment, before the 4th respondent, for further action.
