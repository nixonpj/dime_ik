  IN THE HIGH COURT OF KERALA AT ERNAKULAM

WP(C) No. 32386 of 2007(E)


1. P.M.PADMANABHAN, S/O.M.K.MADHAVAN NAIR,
                      ...  Petitioner

                        Vs



1. THE DISTRICT COLLECTOR,
                       ...       Respondent

2. THE DEPUTY TAHSILDAR (RR)

3. THE MANAGER SYNDICATE BANK,

4. B.JAGADESH, C/O.JAYA SURESH,

                For Petitioner  :SRI.C.R.SIVAKUMAR

                For Respondent  : No Appearance

The Hon'ble MR. Justice V.GIRI

 Dated :05/<a href="/doc/1390448/" id="a_1">11/2007

 O R D E R
                         V.GIRI, J
                       -------------------
                    W.P.(C</a>). 32386/2007
                       --------------------
        Dated this the 5th day of November, 2007

                       JUDGMENT
</pre>

<p id="p_1">      Petitioner stood as a guarantor for the loan availed

by the fourth respondent from the            third respondent,

Bank. The fourth respondent committed default in re-

payment. Revenue recovery steps were taken by the Bank.

Petitioner is aggrieved by the steps that are being taken

against him. Hence this writ petition for a direction to the

third respondent to initiate revenue recovery proceedings

against the principal debtor.
</p>
<p id="p_2">

</p><p id="p_3">2.    Learned Standing Counsel for the third respondent

submits that the revenue recovery steps have been taken

against the fourth respondent also. It is also submitted

that the steps are being taken against the sureties as

such. Submission made in this regard is recorded.



</p><p id="p_4">3.    Having regard to the facts and circumstances of the

case, this writ petition is disposed of directing the third

respondent to issue a notice to the petitioner and fourth

respondent requiring both of them to come to the office of

W.P.(C).32386/2007
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                              2</span>

the third respondent to work out the modalities         for

recovering the balance amount due to the Bank from the

fourth respondent. Such notice may be issued within one

month from the date of receipt of a copy of this judgment

which will be produced by the petitioner before third

respondent. On receipt of notice and on appearance of

the petitioner and the fourth respondent, it is open to the

third respondent to fix a re-payment schedule and

modalities for recovery of the amount from the fourth

respondent.       Subject to this right, writ petition is

disposed of.
</p>
<p id="p_5">


</p><p id="p_6">                                 V.GIRI
                                 Judge
