                           IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB &amp; HARYANA
                                       AT CHANDIGARH


                                                                 CRM-M No.37405 of 2015 (O&amp;M)
                                                                 Date of Decision: 05.01.2016

       Amit
                                                                                    .......... Petitioner
                                                          Vs.

       State of Haryana

                                                                                 .......... Respondent


       CORAM:                   HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE JASWANT SINGH


       Present:                 Mr. Mohammad Arshad, Advocate for the petitioner.

                                Mr. M. S. Sidhu, Additional Advocate General, Haryana
                                for the respondent/State assisted by ASI Manoj Kumar.

                                                  ****

       JASWANT SINGH, J. (Oral)
</pre>
<p id="p_1">                                The prayer is for grant of regular bail to the petitioner in Case FIR

       No.441 dated 14.12.2014, under <a href="/doc/764237/" id="a_1">Section 394</a> of the Indian Penal Code,

       registered with Police Station Udyog Vihar, Gurgaon.

</p><p id="p_2">                                The complainant-Yogesh Kumar had taken a journey on 14.12.2014

       at 2:00 AM by boarding a passenger Innova Car. The petitioner along with two

       other co-accused were fellow travellers, who gave beatings to the complainant

       and robbed him of cash and ATM Cards including the passwords. Thereafter,

       the petitioner along with his co-accused have withdrawn money by using the

       said ATM Card. The footing of the petitioner in the CCTV at the ATM Centre has

       come in evidence.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                It is contended that the offence is triable by the Magistrate and the

       petitioner has already undergone more than one year and the evidence of the

       prosecution is yet to conclude.
</p>
<p id="p_4">

GUGNANI GAGANDEEP
2016.01.07 10:37
I attest to the accuracy and
authenticity of this document
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">        CRM-M No.37405 of 2015 (O&amp;M)                                                        -2-</span>


                                Learned State Counsel, assisted by ASI Manoj Kumar, submits that

       04 out of 17 prosecution witnesses have already been examined. He further

       assures the Court that all steps would be taken to conclude the prosecution

       evidence at the earliest.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                 Keeping in view the nature and gravity of the offence, no case for

       grant of regular bail is made out.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                 Accordingly, the present petition is dismissed.

                                 It is hoped that the prosecution would expedite the adducing of its

       evidence.
</p>
