<div class="doc_title">Awadhesh Jha Alias Awadhesh Kumar ... vs The State Of Jharkhand And Anr on 8 December, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                 IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                              A. B. A. No. 2112 of 2016
           1. Awadhesh Jha @ Awadhesh Kumar Jha
           2. Bharti Jha                                     ..... Petitioners
                                           Versus
           The State of Jharkhand &amp; Another                  ..... Opposite Parties
                                             -----
                                          CORAM
                     HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE ANANT BIJAY SINGH
                                             -----
           For the Petitioners:        Mr. K.P.Deo
           For the State:              Mr. S.K.Keshri, A.P.P
           For O.P.No.2:               Mr. Rakesh Kumar
                                             -----

03/08.12.2016</pre><p id="p_1">      Heard learned counsel for the parties.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                   The petitioners apprehending their arrest in connection with the case

           registered under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_1">Section 498A</a> IPC have prayed for grant of anticipatory

           bail.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                   Learned counsel for the petitioners submitted that the petitioners

           have been falsely implicated in this case. The petitioner No.1 is the father-

           in-law and the petitioner No.2 is the mother-in-law of the deceased. There

           are general and omnibus allegations against them regarding demand of

           dowry and torture.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                   Learned A.P.P opposed the petitioners' prayer for bail. However, he

           has not disputed the said factual contentions of the petitioners.

</p><p id="p_5">                   Having considered the facts and circumstances of the case, the

           above named two petitioners are directed to surrender before the

           concerned Court below within a period of two weeks. If they surrender

           before the Court below within the aforesaid period, they shall be released

           on bail on furnishing bail-bond of Rs.10,000/- (ten thousand only) each with

           two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of the A.C.J.M,

           Bermo at Tenughat in connection with Complaint Case No. 257/2015,

           subject to the conditions as laid down under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_2">Section 438(2)</a> Cr.P.C.



</p><pre id="pre_2">Satish/-                                                    (ANANT BIJAY SINGH, J)
