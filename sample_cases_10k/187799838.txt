<div class="doc_title">Bhota @ Ubay vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 27 August, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                                  1
                        M.Cr.C. No.8738/2015 &amp; M.Cr.C No. 8722/2015

                   (M.Cr.C. No. 8738/<a href="/doc/1775095/" id="a_1">2015
                Bhonta @ Abhay v. State of MP</a>)
                   (M.Cr.C. No. 8722/<a href="/doc/144026017/" id="a_2">2015
                Udaybhan Singh v. State of MP</a>)
27.08.2015
     Shri Jitendra Tyagi, Advocate for applicant.
        Shri B.K. Sharma, Govt. Advocate for Respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">        Case Diary is perused.
</p><p id="p_2">        Learned counsel for the rival parties are heard.
        These applications are being decided together as they
arise out of same crime number.
</p><p id="p_3">        The applicants have filed this third application u/S 439,
<a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_3">Cr.P.C</a>. for grant of bail. First applications were dismissed on
21.10.2010 passed in M.Cr.C No. 6452/2010, M.Cr.C. 5693/2010
respectively,    The applicants have been arrested         by Police
Station Kelaras District Morena in connection with Crime
No.194/2010 registered in relation to the offences punishable
u/Ss.    147, 148, 149, 302, 307, 341 and 506-B of <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_4">IPC</a> and
<a href="/doc/73862/" id="a_5">Section 25</a>/<a href="/doc/244673/" id="a_6">27</a> Arms Act.
</p><p id="p_4">        Present bail applications have been filed by the petitioners
seeking bail inter-alia on the ground enlargement on bail of co-
accused Sughar Singh.
</p><p id="p_5">        Earlier bail was granted to co-accused Sughar Singh vide
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                     2</span>
                          M.Cr.C. No.8738/2015 &amp; M.Cr.C No. 8722/2015

dated 22.11.2010 in M.Cr.C No. 7304/2010. Subsequently, it
was canceled. Thereafter, he was given the benefit of regular
bail by this Court on 23.01.2014 passed in M.Cr.C, No.
8492/2013.
</p><p id="p_6">      Close scrutiny of order dated 23.01.2014 passed in M.Cr.C
8492/2013 reflects that the bail bonds of co-accused Sughar
Singh were cancelled on account of the said co-accused having
suppressed information regarding rejection of bail of co-accused
Udaybhan, whereas the present applicant was involved in
subsequent offence thereby violating the conditions under which
bail was granted.
</p><p id="p_7">      In view of the above the cause of cancellation of bail of
Sughar   Singh      and    present   applicants   is   distinguishable.
Moreover, the other aspect which deserves consideration is the
fact that the petitioners bail was cancelled by           order dated
16.07.2015 passed in M.Cr.C No. 9322/2013 pursuant to which
he surrendered on 11.08.2015 since then the applicants are in
custody. The period of custody suffered by the applicants is
about 15 days. Whereas, the period of custody suffered by
Sughar Singh was more than four months after surrendering on
16.09.2013 before being granted bail on 23.01.2014.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                         3</span>
<p id="p_8">                              M.Cr.C. No.8738/2015 &amp; M.Cr.C No. 8722/2015

              In view of the above, no parity can be claimed by citing
        the case of co-accused Sughar Singh.
</p><p id="p_9">              It is further informed by the learned counsel for the State
        and victim that the trial is at the fag end as the case is listed
        for pronouncing judgment on 07.09.2015.
</p><p id="p_10">              In view of the above, no case for bail is made out.
             Accordingly, these bail applications deserve to be and is
        hereby rejected.
</p>

<p id="p_11">                                                    (Sheel Nagu)
                                                      Judge
LJ*/-
</p>
