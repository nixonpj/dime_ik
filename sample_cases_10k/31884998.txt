This petition coming on for preliminary hearing this day, the

                              ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Petitioner gave a representation on 27.07.2011 to the

respondent Town Municipal Council to take action against the

third respondent for putting up illegal construction. Since the

respondent - Town Municipal Council failed to react to the

request of the petitioner, he filed a writ petition before this court

in W.P.No.38189/2011. Before this court learned counsel for the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                3</span>




respondent - Town Municipal Council made a submission that

they will consider the petitioner's representation within a

reasonable time and pass appropriate orders in accordance with

law. Accordingly, this court disposed W.P.No.38189/2011 vide

order dated 1.10.2012. Since the respondent Municipal Council

failed to take any action against the third respondent, petitioner

filed a contempt petition before this court in CCC No.347/2013.

In the contempt proceedings the counsel for respondents Town

Municipal Council filed a copy of the notice dated 5.11.2012

stating that they have initiated action against the third

respondent. In view of this development the contempt court vide

order dated 21.03.2013 dropped the contempt proceedings. The

respondent Municipal issued a notice on 05.11.2012 to the third

respondent to remove the illegal encroachment. Thereafter the

Municipal Council has not taken any further action in the matter.

It is the specific case of the petitioner that the third respondent is

proceeding with the illegal construction despite service of notice
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                            4</span>




issued by the Municipal Council on 5.11.2012.         In the

circumstances, the respondent Municipal Council is hereby

directed to take further steps pursuant to the notice dated

5.11.2012 forthwith, without further loss of time. Accordingly

the writ petition is hereby disposed of without reference to

respondents.
</p>
