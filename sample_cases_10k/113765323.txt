             Suit for Recovery of Rs.54,678.65/- (Under Order XXXVII CPC)


JUDGMENT
</pre>
<p id="p_1"> 1.                 Vide this judgment, I shall decide the suit for recovery of Rs.

</p><p id="p_2">        54,678.65/- filed by the plaintiff against the defendant.



</p><p id="p_3">Suit No.­ 27/2014                                                                                                                               Page..... 1/4
</p><p id="p_4">  2.                 In brief, the facts of the case are that the plaintiff is a Nationalized

        Bank, a body corporate constituted under the Banking Companies

        (Acquisition and <a href="/doc/1232997/" id="a_2">Transfer of Undertakings) Act</a>, 1980 (Act No.3 of 1980)

        having its Head Office at Mangalore (South Kanara, Karnataka State) and

        having a branch at Corporation Bank, LIC Card Centre, 13 &amp; 14, First Floor,

        Old Market, Tilak Nagar, New Delhi-110018. The plaint is signed, verified

        and instituted by Mrs. Anuja Mehta, Manager, who is authorized to do so.



</p><p id="p_5"> 3.                 The defendant approached the plaintiff bank for issuing a LIC Credit

        Card and submitted dully filled up and signed application no.1347/015 dated

        23.09.2010 along self attested copies of PAN Card, BSNL Telephone Bill,

        ITR, Form No.16A etc. for the proof of photo identity and residence of the

        defendant. The plaintiff bank sanctioned a limit of Rs.15,000/- on 05.01.2011

        and issued a LIC Credit Card no.4628460014644009 to the defendant. The

        defendant purchased goods/things from the stores/shops by using the LIC

        Credit Card from 04.02.2011 to 16.04.2011 and the amount of the

        goods/things have been claimed by the sellers/member establishments through

        VISA and the same has been paid by the plaintiff by debiting the LIC Card

        account no. 4628460014644009 of the defendant which was duly reflected in

        the periodical statements/bills of the defendant.                                                         As per the terms and

        conditions of LIC Credit Card user guide, the defendant is liable to make the

        payments of all the outstanding of the said credit card and other charges as

Suit No.­ 27/2014                                                                                                                               Page..... 2/4
         applicable. The defendant has been regularly billed by means of periodically

        monthly statements/bills through courier, email and SMS etc. The defendant

        failed to make the payment of the outstanding dues. The plaintiff bank issued

        a recall notice dated 11.12.2013 through registered post on 04.01.2014 calling

        upon the defendant to pay a sum of Rs.51,814.02/- which was due in

        November, 2013 against the defendant. No reply of the recall notice or any

        payment has been made by the defendant. As per the terms and conditions of

        LIC Credit Card facility, the defendant was liable to pay interest on the

        overdue amounts @ 2.5% per month along with finance charges, late

        payment, over limit and other charges thereon. As per the plaintiff, on

        21.12.2013 a sum of Rs.54,678.65/- (Rs.10,076.00/- as principal amount and

        Rs.44,602.65/- as finance charges, late payment, over limit, other charges and

        interest) was due and payable by the defendant with further interest @ 2.5%

        per month along with finance charges, late payment, over limit and other

        charges thereon. On the basis of these averments, the plaintiff has filed the

        present suit under the provisions of Order XXXVII CPC.
</p>
<p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7"> 4.                 Summons u/o XXXVII CPC were sent to the defendant. The defendant

        was served by way of registered AD card on 09.04.2014. Defendant failed to

        file the appearance within 10 days from the receiving of the summons u/o

        XXXVII CPC. As per provisions of Order XXXVII CPC, if the defendant

        failed to file the appearance within 10 days from receiving of the summons

Suit No.­ 27/2014                                                                                                                               Page..... 3/4
         u/o XXXVII CPC, then averments made in the plaint are deemed to be

        admitted. Thus, the suit of the plaintiff is decreed and a decree for a sum of

        Rs.54,678.65/- is passed in favour of the plaintiff and against the defendant.

        The plaintiff is also entitled to interest @ 10 % per annum from the date of

        the filing of the suit till the order of the date. The plaintiff is further entitled to

        interest @ 6% per annum from the order of the date till realization along

        with costs of the suit. Decree sheet be prepared accordingly.

</p><p id="p_8">                    File be consigned to Record Room after necessary compliance.</p><pre id="pre_2">



Announced in the open court                                                      (RACHNA TIWARI LAKHANPAL)
on 08.05.2014                                                                         SCJ/RC(WEST) / DELHI
