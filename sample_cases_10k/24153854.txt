<div class="doc_title">G.Balasubramanian vs The District Collector on 20 June, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">       

  

  

 
 
 BEFORE THE MADURAI BENCH OF MADRAS HIGH COURT

DATED: 20.06.2014

CORAM

THE HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE K.RAVICHANDRABAABU

Writ Petition(MD)No.8823 of 2014
and
M.P(MD)No.1 of 2014

G.Balasubramanian		... Petitioner

Vs.

1.The District Collector,
   Tirunelveli District,
   Tirunelveli.

2.The Assistant Director,
   Department of Geology and Mining,
   GF  201, Tirunelveli District,
   Tirunelveli.

3.The Superintendent of Police,
   Tirunelveli District,
   Tirunelveli.		... Respondents

Prayer
	Writ Petition is filed under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the Constitution of India
praying for the issuance of a Writ of Mandamus, directing the respondents to
allow the petitioner to transport M-sand from Tamil Nadu to Kerala through
Puliyarai Check Post at Shencottah Taluk, Tirunelveli District in accordance
with license granted in Registration No.TN-TVN/21313/2013, dated 29.07.2013.

!For Petitioner	: Mr.S.Chellapandian

For Respondents	: Mr.K.Mahesh Raja
			Government Advocate	

:ORDER
</pre><p id="p_1">	The petitioner seeks for a Mandamus, directing the respondents to allow
the petitioner to transport M-sand from Tamil Nadu to Kerala through
Puliyarai Check Post at Shencottah Taluk, Tirunelveli District in accordance
with license granted in Registration No.TN-TVN/21313/2013, dated 29.07.2013.
</p>
<p id="p_2">	2. Mr.K.Mahesh Raja, learned Government Advocate takes notice for the
respondents.
</p>
<p id="p_3">	3. The case of the petitioner is as follows:-
</p>
<p id="p_4">	The petitioner is an authorized licensee to carry out mining activity
in his patta land situated at Survey Nos.369/1A and 369/2A of Palamadai
Village, Tirunelveli Taluk. The petitioner was authorized to
store/process/consume M-sand, crusher dust and jelly. The stored minerals
were often transported to nearby area and also to nearby State. M-sand is a
product derived by crushing jelly. There is no express prohibition either in
the Mines and <a href="/doc/25127/" id="a_2">Minerals Act</a> or in the Rules made thereunder against the
transportation of M-sand from one place to another either within the State or
outside the State. When the vehicles of the petitioner carrying M-sand for
transporting the same to outside the State to reach the State border, they
are stopped in the check post and the officials are preventing the vehicles
from transporting M-sand from Tamil Nadu to Kerala. The said action of the
authorities are without any power conferred under the statute.
</p><p id="p_5">	4. The learned counsel appearing for the petitioner submits that in an
identical circumstances, this Court granted direction to the respondents to
permit the vehicles carrying M-sand to outside the State, if the petitioner
therein produce valid transit passes in Form-F. The said order was made in
W.P(MD)No.8060 of 2014, dated 07.05.2014.
</p>
<p id="p_6">	5. The learned Government Advocate appearing for the respondents
submitted that there is no prohibition for transporting M-sand outside the
State. However, he has submitted that the petitioner should produce valid
transit passes in Form-F issued by the second respondent.
</p>
<p id="p_7">	6. Considering the above stated facts and circumstances and in view of
the submission made by the learned Government Advocate and also in view of
the order passed by this Court in W.P(MD)No.8060 of 2014, dated 07.05.2014
under similar circumstances, this Writ Petition is disposed of with a
direction to the respondents to permit the petitioner to transport M-sand
from Tamil Nadu to Kerala through Puliyarai check post at Shencottah Taluk,
Tirunelveli District, subject to production of valid transit passes in Form-F
issued by the second respondent herein. No costs. Consequently, connected
Miscellaneous Petition is closed.
</p>	
<p id="p_8">To
</p><p id="p_9">1.The District Collector,
   Tirunelveli District,
   Tirunelveli.
</p>

   Department of Geology and Mining,
   GF  201, Tirunelveli District,
   Tirunelveli.
</p>
