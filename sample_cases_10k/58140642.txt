<div class="doc_title">Sunil Kumar @ Sunil Singh &amp; Ors. vs The State Of Bihar on 6 March, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">       IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                       Criminal Appeal (SJ) No.320 of 2015
         Arising Out of PS.Case No. -70 Year- 1999 Thana -KASHICHAK District- NAWADA
======================================================
1. Sunil Kumar @ Sunil Singh
2. Akhilesh Kumar @ Akhilesh Singh,
3. Anil Kumar @ Anil Singh
  All sons of Late Deen Dayal Singh,
4. Pawan Kumar
5. Marut Kumar.
   Both sons of Akhilesh Kumar,
6. Anjani Kumar Son of Anil Kumar.
   All resident of village - Shahpur, P.S. - Kashichak, District Nawadah.
                                                                 .... .... Appellant/s
                                        Versus
The State of Bihar                                    .... .... Respondent/s
======================================================
Appearance :
For the Appellant/s        :    Mr. S.N.P. Sinha, Sr. Adv.
                                Mr. Devendra Prasad Singh, Adv.
For the State               :   Mr. Sujit Kumar Singh, APP
For the informant           :   Mr. Pawan Kumar, Adv.
======================================================
CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE ADITYA KUMAR TRIVEDI
ORAL JUDGMENT</pre>
<p id="p_1">Date: 06-03-2018


                  Heard learned counsel for the appellants as well as

 learned Additional Public Prosecutor.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">        2.        Gone through the order dated 16.01.2018 passed by

 First Additional Sessions Judge, Nawada in Sessions Trial No.46 of

 2001 whereby and whereunder appellants Pawan Kumar, Marut

 Kumar have been declared juvenile in pursuance of order dated

 02.11.2017 passed by this Court wherein learned lower court was

 directed to conduct an inquiry relating to status of the aforesaid two

 appellants whether they were juvenile on the date of occurrence or

 not, on a prayer having been raised on their behalf under I.A.

 no.2197/2017. After going through the order impugned, it is evident
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Patna High Court CR. APP (SJ) No.320 of 2015 (13) dt.06-03-2018                    2</span>




      that learned lower court had not appreciated the evidence having

      been collected during course of inquiry nor properly adhered to the

      legal requirement as prescribed under <a href="/doc/148942/" id="a_1">Section 94(2)</a> of the Juvenile

      Justice (Care and <a href="/doc/1973522/" id="a_2">Protection of Children) Act</a>. For better appreciation

      the same is quoted below:
</p>
<p id="p_4">
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">                          "Section 94

                          (2) In case, the Committee or the Board has
                              reasonable grounds for doubt regarding whether
                              the person brought before it is a child or not, the
                              Committee or the Board, as the case may be, shall
                              undertake the process of age determination, by
                              seeking evidence by obtaining --
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_2">                                  I. the date of birth certificate from the school,
                                     or the matriculation or equivalent certificate
                                     from the concerned examination Board, if
                                     available; and in the absence thereof;
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_3">                                  II. the birth certificate given by a corporation or
                                      a municipal authority or a panchayat;
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_4">                                 III. and only in the absence of (i) and (ii) above,
                                      age shall be determined by an ossification
                                      test or any other latest medical age
                                      determination test conducted on the orders
                                      of the Committee or the Board:
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_5">                          Provided such age determination test conducted on
                          the order of the Committee or the Board shall be
                          completed within fifteen days from the date of such
                          order."</blockquote><p id="p_5">
</p><p id="p_6">              3.         There should have been sincere effort at the end of the

      learned lower court to procure the relevant documents that means to

      say the date of birth having disclosed in the school first attended. In

      likewise manner, in spite of observation having made by the learned

      lower court that EW-3 Anish Anand was directed to be present with

      admission register which he failed should have been taken into
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">            Patna High Court CR. APP (SJ) No.320 of 2015 (13) dt.06-03-2018                3</span>




                  account in view of paragraph 11 of PW.1 who happens to be father

                  and in likewise manner PW.2, para-4 who happens to be mother.

                  The learned lower court should have considered the event as,

                  whatever documents have been considered at his end that happens

                  to be subsequent to the date of occurrence. Accordingly, the finding

                  having been recorded by the learned lower court is hereby scumbled,

                  remitting the matter to the learned lower court with a direction to

                  proceed afresh and conclude the inquiry strictly in terms of criteria

                  having affixed under Section 94 of the Juvenile Justice Act failing

                  which, will be viewed seriously.
</p>
<p id="p_7">
</p><p id="p_8">                         4.         The aforesaid exercise must be completed within two

                  months. Office is directed to return back the relevant file (inquire file)

                  and after concluding the inquiry, the learned lower court will

                  transmit its finding along with relevant record as well as documents.


</p><p id="p_9">                         5.         Office to list accordingly.</p><pre id="pre_2">



                                                             (Aditya Kumar Trivedi, J.)

Prakash Narayan


AFR/NAFR       A.F.R.
