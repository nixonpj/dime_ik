<div class="doc_title">Rohit Malakar vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 12 July, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                       1                           MCRC-28129-2019
                             The High Court Of Madhya Pradesh
                                       MCRC-28129-2019
                                      (ROHIT MALAKAR Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)


                     Jabalpur, Dated : 12-07-2019
                            Shri Anil Kumar Tiwari, learned counsel for the applicant.

                            Shri Pradeep Dwivedi, learned PL. for the respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">                            This is first bail application filed on behalf of the applicant under
                    <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of the Cr.P.C. The applicant is in custody since 18.06.2019 in
                    connection with Crime No.392/2019 registered at Police Station City Kotwali,

                    District Khandwa (MP), for the offence punishable under <a href="/doc/619940/" id="a_2">Sections 363</a>, <a href="/doc/796352/" id="a_3">366</a>,
                    <a href="/doc/1279834/" id="a_4">376</a> of IPC r/w Section 3 &amp; 4 of POCSO Act.
</p><p id="p_2">                            As per case of prosecution story, the applicant misguided the
                    prosecutrix on the assurance of marriage and took her to another place,
                    whereas committed sexual intercourse with her, later on the prosecutrix was
                    recovered on 17/06/2019 and on her statement the police arrested the
                    applicant and sent him to judicial custody.
</p><p id="p_3">                            Learned counsel for the applicant submits that the prosecutrix have not
                    supported the case of prosecution. In her statement under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_5">Section 164</a> of

                    Cr.P.C. recorded by the trial court she have stated that she went with
                    applicant on her own and applicant did not commit any rape upon her they
                    have solemnized marriage and she is
                    legally wedded wife of applicant and they were living as husband and wife
                    after marriage. The prosecutrix is above 18 year. He also submits that the
                    applicant is innocent person and has been falsely implicated in this case and
                    his age about 24 years. There is no criminal antecedent against the applicant
                    and trial will take long time to conclude. So he prays for grant of bail to the
                    applicant.
</p><p id="p_4">                            Learned PL for the respondent/State on the other hand opposes the
                    application.
</p><p id="p_5">                            Considering the contention of both the parties, it appears that this is a
                    case of love affairs,    trial will take long time to conclude , it would be

Digitally signed by ARVIND KUMAR

                    appropriate to allow the application of the applicant. Consequently, without
                    commenting on the merits of the case, the first application for bail under
                    <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_6">Section 439</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure filed on behalf of applicant is
                    allowed.
</p><p id="p_6">                            It is directed that the applicant- Rohit Malakar be released on bail

                    on furnishing a personal bond in the sum of Rs. 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty
                    Thousand Only) with one solvent surety in the like amount to the satisfaction
                    of the trial Court for his appearance before that Court on all dates fixed in the
                    case and for complying with the conditions enumerated under <a href="/doc/1682361/" id="a_7">Section 437</a> (3)<a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_8">
                    of the Code</a> of Criminal Procedure.
</p><p id="p_7">                            C.C. as per rules.
