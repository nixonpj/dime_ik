<p id="p_1">	Mr.
</p><p id="p_2">S.V. Raju, learned senior counsel appearing for the applicant submits
that  the impugned FIR is based on the order dated 7.12.2011 passed
by the learned Chief Judicial Magistrate, Surat, by which, a report
was called for from the Commissioner of Police, Surat, and the power
also came to be exercised under <a href="/doc/788422/" id="a_1">Section 210</a> 
of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 of staying the proceedings.
In view of the above, it is submitted that, having taken cognizance,
the
learned Chief Judicial Magistrate, Surat, could not have switched
over to pre-cognizance stage and, subsequently, directed the
Investigating Agency to register FIR in exercise of power under
<a href="/doc/99487/" id="a_2">Section 156(3)</a> of the Code as per the order dated 31.3.2012. Even on
merit, it is submitted that the applicant is a bona-fide purchaser
and the dispute is of civil nature.
</p> 

<p id="p_3">	Issue
notice returnable on  5.7.2012.
</p> 

<p id="p_4">	Ad-interim
relief in terms of paragraph 8(c).
</p> 

<p id="p_5">	Learned
APP waives service on behalf of the State.
</p> 

<p id="p_6">	Direct
service is permitted qua the private respondent.
</p> 

<p id="p_7">(ANANT
S. DAVE, J.)
