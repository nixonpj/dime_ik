<div class="doc_title">Jatinder Singh Chaudhary vs State Of Punjab And Others on 16 January, 2013</div><pre id="pre_1">            IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA
                         AT CHANDIGARH

                                              CWP No. 828 of 2013
                                              Date of Decision : 16.1.2013


Jatinder Singh Chaudhary                                  ..... Petitioner(s)

                                     Versus

State of Punjab and others                                ..... Respondent(s)


CORAM:       HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE AUGUSTINE GEORGE MASIH


Present:-    Mr. Amrik Singh, Advocate, for the petitioner.

AUGUSTINE GEORGE MASIH, J. (ORAL)
</pre>
<p id="p_1">             Petitioner, who retired on 31.8.2008, has till date not been

released the final pension and only provisional pension is being paid to him.

Apart from that GPF has till date not been released to him. He is, through the

present writ petition, praying for release of the same alongwith interest on

delayed payment. Claiming the said benefit, petitioner had served a legal

notice dated 15.11.2012 (Annexure-P-7) upon the respondents.

</p><p id="p_2">             Counsel states that the petitioner, at this stage, would be satisfied

if a direction is issued to the Chief Engineer, Drainage Irrigation Works,

Punjab-respondent No. 2 to consider and decide the aforesaid legal notice of

the petitioner within some specified time.
</p>
<p id="p_3">             Without going into the merits of the case or commenting thereon,

the present petition is disposed of with a direction to the Chief Engineer,

Drainage Irrigation Works, Punjab-respondent No. 2 to consider and decide

the legal notice of the petitioner dated 15.11.2012 (Annexure-P-7) within a

period of four months from the date of receipt of certified copy of this order.

The decision, so taken, be conveyed to the petitioner forthwith.
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> CWP No. 828 of 2013                                                       -2-</span>

</p><p id="p_4">             In case, the petitioner is held entitled to the claim as has been

made by him in his aforesaid legal notice, the consequential benefits, if any,

be released to him, in accordance with law, within a further period of two

months. In case, the claim as has been made by the petitioner in his aforesaid

legal notice is not to be accepted, then a speaking and well reasoned order be

passed by respondent No. 2 and the same be conveyed to the petitioner

forthwith.
</p>
<p id="p_5">

</p><p id="p_6">                                     (AUGUSTINE GEORGE MASIH)
                                             JUDGE
16.1.2013
