                            BEFORE THE MADURAI BENCH OF MADRAS HIGH COURT

                                                 DATED : 23.04.2019

                                                        CORAM:

                                 THE HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE R.MAHADEVAN

                                               W.P(MD)No.9617 of 2019

                     V. Arasumani                                                   ... Petitioner
                                                            Vs.

                     The District Collector,
                     Thanjavur District,
                     Thanjavur.                                                     ... Respondent



                     PRAYER: Writ Petition filed under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the Constitution of
                     India praying for issuance of a Writ of Mandamus, directing the
                     respondent to revoke the suspension of the petitioner and reinstate
                     the petitioner in the post of Block Development Officer (Village
                     Panchayat), Peravurani Block, Thanjavur District in the light of the
                     order passed by the Hon'ble Supreme Court in Ajaykumar Choudhary
                     Vs. Union of India reported in 2015(7) SCC 291 within the time
                     period stipulated by this Court.


                                 For Petitioner      : Mr.K.A.S. Prabu

                                 For Respondents : Mrs.C.M. Mari Chellaiah Prabhu
                                                   Additional Government Pleader

                                                           ORDER
</pre>

<p id="p_1">                                 This   writ    petition    is    filed   seeking     to   direct    the

                     the respondent to revoke the suspension of the petitioner and

                     reinstate the petitioner in the post of Block Development Officer

                     (Village Panchayat), Peravurani Block, Thanjavur District in the light

                     of the order passed by the Hon'ble Supreme Court in Ajaykumar

                     Choudhary Vs. Union of India reported in 2015(7) SCC 291, within

                     the time period stipulated by this Court.

<p id="p_2">

</p><p id="p_3">                                    2.      Mr. C.M. Marichellaiah Prabhu, learned Additional

                     Government Pleader, takes notice for the respondent. By consent, the

                     writ petition itself is taken up for disposal at the admission stage

                     itself.
</p>
<p id="p_4">

</p><p id="p_5">                                    3. The case of the petitioner is that on account of false

                     criminal case registered against him, he was placed under suspension

                     by proceeding dated 12.06.2017 and seeking appropriate direction to

                     the       respondent    for   revocation       of   suspension   order   and   for

                     reinstatement, the petitioner into service, in line with the Judgment of

                     the Hon'ble Supreme Court in the case of Ajaykumar Choudhary Vs.

                     Union of India and another reported in 2015(7) SCC 291, he is before

                     this Court.
</p>
<p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">                                    4. The learned Additional Government Pleader appearing

                     for the respondent submitted that the claim of the petitioner would be

                     considered accordingly.
</p>
<p id="p_8">

</p><p id="p_9">                                 5. Under such facts and circumstances of the case, this

                     Court, without going into the merits of the case, directs the

                     respondent to consider the case of the petitioner based on his

                     representation dated 12.06.2017, on merits and in accordance with

                     law and also in the light of the decision in Ajykumar Choudhary's

                     case, cited supra and pass appropriate orders, after affording

                     opportunity of hearing to the petitioner, within a period of eight weeks

                     from the date of receipt of a copy of this order.



</p><p id="p_10">                                 6. This Writ Petition is disposed of, on the above terms.
