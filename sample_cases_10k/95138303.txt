<div class="doc_title">Upendra Sao vs The State Of Jharkhand on 18 July, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                 IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                                  A.B.A. No. 3989 of 2017

            Upendra Sao @ Upendra Prasad                     ...        Petitioner
                                       Versus
            The State of Jharkhand                          ...       Opp. Party

        Coram:      HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE ANIL KUMAR CHOUDHARY


        For the Petitioner(s)          : Mr. Abhay Kr. Chaturvedy, Adv.
        For the State                  : Addl. P.P.

04 /18.07.2017</pre><p id="p_1">      Apprehending his arrest, the petitioner has moved this Court
            for grant of privilege of anticipatory bail in connection with Latehar
            P.S. Case No. 27 of 2017 (G.R. No. 96 of 2017) registered under
            <a href="/doc/1258372/" id="a_1">sections 147</a>, <a href="/doc/763672/" id="a_2">148</a>, <a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_3">149</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_4">323</a>, <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_5">324</a>, <a href="/doc/1101188/" id="a_6">379</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_7">504</a> and <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_8">307</a> of the Indian Penal
            Code.
</p><p id="p_2">                    Heard learned counsel appearing for the petitioner and the
            learned Addl. P.P. for the State.
</p><p id="p_3">                    The learned counsel for the petitioner submits that the
            allegation against the petitioner is that the petitioner along with
            other co-accused persons assaulted the informant causing injuries to
            him. There is specific allegation against the petitioner that at the time
            of occurrence, the petitioner was armed with lathi. The learned
            counsel further submits that the allegations against the petitioner are
            all false. It is also submitted on behalf of the petitioner that the
            matter has been settled by the parties, as is evident from the order
            dated 15.06.2017 passed in A.B.P. No. 104 of 2017. The learned
            counsel for the petitioner, further, submits that the petitioner is
            ready and willing to pay the ad interim victim compensation to the
            informant-victim. Hence, the petitioner be given the privilege of
            anticipatory bail.
</p><p id="p_4">                    The learned Addl. P.P. opposes the prayer for bail.
                    Considering the submissions of the counsels and the fact as
            discussed above, I am inclined to grant privileges of anticipatory bail
            to the petitioner Upendra Sao @ Upendra Prasad. Hence, in the
            event of his arrest or surrender within a period of four weeks from
            the date of this order, he shall be released on bail on furnishing bail
            bond of Rs. 10,000/- (Rupees Ten Thousand) with two sureties of
            the like amount each to the satisfaction of learned Chief Judicial
         Magistrate, Latehar, in connection with Latehar P.S. Case No. 27 of
        2017 (G.R. No. 96 of 2017) and depositing Rs. 5,000/- as ad interim
        victim compensation to be paid to the to the informant-victim,
        subject to the conditions laid down under <a href="/doc/1144664/" id="a_9">section 438</a> (2) Cr. P.C.
</p><p id="p_5">              In case, the petitioner deposits the ad interim victim
        compensation amount in the court of learned Chief Judicial
        Magistrate, Latehar, the court is directed to issue notice to the
        victim-informant and release the said amount his favour, after
        proper verification.
</p>

<p id="p_6">                                               (Anil Kumar Choudhary, J.)
Sonu-
</p>
