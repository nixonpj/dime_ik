<p id="p_1">                                                     Sukhan Bhagat
</p><p id="p_2">                                                           - vs.-
</p><p id="p_3">                                              The State of West Bengal &amp; Ors.
</p>

<p id="p_4">                     Mr. Biswadeb Ray Chaudhuri
                     Mr. Sourav Chaudhuri.
</p><p id="p_5">                                                      ....For the Petitioner.
</p><p id="p_6">                     Mr. Asim Hati
                                                      ....For the State.
</p><p id="p_7">                     Mr. Niladri Bhattacharjee
                                                      ....For the Respondents
                                                                  No. 2 to 6.
</p>

<p id="p_8">                     The petitioner was an employee of the Clacutta Tramways
               Company (1978) Ltd. (hereafter the Company). He has retired
               from service on attaining the age of superannuation on
               23.09.2012.    He is aggrieved because gratuity and leave salary
               have not been paid to him by the Company.
</p>

<p id="p_9">                     It   appears    that   the   petitioner   has   addressed   a
               representation to the Managing Director of the Company dated
               12.08.2013 claiming gratuity and leave salary, which is yet to be
               decided. I am of the view that the Managing Director must look
               into such representation and decide the question of payment of
               admissible unpaid dues to the petitioner upon granting him
               opportunity of hearing. It is ordered accordingly.
</p>
<p id="p_10">                     Let this exercise be completed as early as possible, but
               not later than three months from date of receipt of a copy of this
               order together with a copy of the writ petition.
</p>

<p id="p_11">                     In the event the petitioner is not entitled to any amount
               on account of gratuity and leave salary, a reasoned order shall
               be passed and communicated to him.
</p>

<p id="p_12">                     On the contrary, if any amount is found to be due and
               payable on account of gratuity and leave salary, the same shall
               be paid to the petitioner together with interest @ 10% per
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                       2</span>



annum from the date following his retirement till the date of
actual payment.
</p>

<p id="p_13">      It is made clear that the Company shall be free to move
the Government for sanction of requisite funds for meeting the
dues of the petitioner and if such an approach is made, I hope
and trust that the Government shall pay due heed to the
request of the Company and sanction the same.
</p>

<p id="p_14">      The writ petition stands disposed of, without costs.
</p>
<p id="p_15">      Urgent photostat certified copy of this order, if applied for, be
furnished to the parties as early as possible.
</p>


<p id="p_16">                                          (Dipankar Datta,J.)
