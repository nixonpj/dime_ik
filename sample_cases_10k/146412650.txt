<div class="doc_title">Vijay Das vs The State Of Bihar on 14 July, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.16507 of 2014
                   Arising Out of PS.Case No. -398 Year- 2013 Thana -MAHUA District- VAISHALI(HAJIPUR)
                 ======================================================
                 Vijay Das sosn of Sudhir Das, Resident of Village -Narayanpur, P.S.-
                 Mahua, District -Vaishali.

                                                                                .... ....   Petitioner/s
                                                        Versus
                 The State of Bihar

                                                            .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s : Mr. Sabal Kumar Jha
                 For the State        : Mr. A.P.P.
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE JITENDRA MOHAN
                 SHARMA
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">3   14-07-2014               Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned

                 A.P.P. for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                             The petitioner seeks bail in a case registered for the

                 offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1258372/" id="a_1">sections 147</a>, <a href="/doc/763672/" id="a_2">148</a>, <a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_3">149</a> and <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_4">302</a> of the

                 Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                             The informant and his father has cut down the branches

                 of palm tree which fell in the field of the informant and when

                 father of the informant went to pick up those branches of the palm

                 tree, the petitioner and other co-accused arrived there and brutally

                 assaulted the father of the informant with Lathi Sota, brickbats,

                 fists and slaps resulting the father of the informant succumbed to

                 the injuries.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                             Submission at the Bar for release of the petitioner is that
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.16507 of 2014 (3) dt.14-07-2014</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                           2/3</span>




             in this case all the ladies accused have already been allowed pre-

             arrest bail vide Cr. Misc. No.18521 of 2014 by order dated

             25.6.2014, postmortem report does not support the prosecution

             case, as alleged no accused was armed with sharp cutting weapon,

             200 ml. wine has been found in the urinary bladder of the

             deceased that goes to suggest that father of the informant was in

             drunken state and fell down from the tractor and received injuries

             which proved fatal.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                          Learned A.P.P. opposes the prayer for bail by submitting

             that the informant and other witnesses have supported regarding

             the assault.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                          In the facts and circumstances, as stated above, noticing

             that no specific allegation has been attributed against the

             petitioner, the occurrence took place due to land dispute and, as

             such, considering the detention of the petitioner since 5.11.2013,

             the petitioner, namely, Vijay Das is hereby directed to be released

             on bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs.10,000/- (ten thousand) with

             two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of learned

             Chief Judicial Magistrate, Vaishali at Hajipur in connection with

             Mahua P.S. Case No.398 of 2013 subject to the conditions that

             one of the bailors must be a near relative and another having

             sufficient immovable property within the territorial jurisdiction of
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">           Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.16507 of 2014 (3) dt.14-07-2014</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                                                     3/3</span>




                       the court concerned and the petitioner shall remain present on each

                       and every date during trial and the default on two consecutive

                       dates on his part without any reason shall disentitle the petitioner

                       from privilege of bail.
</p>
<p id="p_7">
</p><p id="p_8">                                               (Jitendra Mohan Sharma, J)
Md.S./-
</p>
