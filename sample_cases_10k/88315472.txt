                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No. 21713 of 2013

                  Rajendra Yadav S/O Late Balmiki Yadav Resident of Village Sarsal, P.S.
                  Goh, District Aurangabad.
                                                                        .... .... Petitioner
                                                   Versus
                  The State of Bihar
                                                                   .... .... Opposite Party
                  ======================================================
                  Appearance :
                  For the Petitioner        : Mr.
                  For the Opposite Party    :   Mr.
                  ======================================================
                  CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE AHSANUDDIN
                              AMANULLAH
                                              ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">5.   24-07-2013                    Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and

                  learned A.P.P. for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                   The petitioner apprehends arrest in connection

                  with Daudnagar P.S. Case No. 328 of 2012 registered for

                  offence punishable under <a href="/doc/94452/" id="a_1">Sections 414</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_2">34</a> of the Indian

                  Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                   The allegation against the petitioner is that he

                  assisted Vinay Yadav in selling stolen iron plates to the

                  traders.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                    Learned counsel for the petitioner submits

                  that he is innocent and neither was he caught at the spot nor

                  any recovery has been made from his possession.                     It is

                  submitted that at best it is only by way of confessional

                  statement of another accused that the petitioner's name has

                  come in the F.I.R.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                   Learned A.P.P. for the State submits that there
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> 2           Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.21713 of 2013 (5) dt.24-07-2013</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                     2/2</span>




                     is no explanation as to why the petitioner's name would be

                     taken or he would be falsely implicated.                  It is further

                     submitted that from the F.I.R. it transpires that the name of

                     the petitioner had been taken as the person along with whom

                     the stolen iron plates were sold and later on recovered and

                     identified.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                         Considering the facts and circumstances of the

                     case, this Court is not inclined to grant anticipatory bail to

                     the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                                         The application is accordingly, dismissed.

                                         However, in the event the petitioner surrenders

                     and seeks regular bail, the Court below shall not be

                     prejudiced by the present order and shall decide the

                     application on its own merits.
</p>
<p id="p_8">

</p><p id="p_9">                                                                 (Ahsanuddin Amanullah, J.)
Anand Kr.
</p>
