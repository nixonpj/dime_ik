<div class="doc_title">Chandra Bhushan @ Chandra Bhushan ... vs State Of Bihar And Anr on 18 September, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                               CRIMINAL MISCELLANEOUS No.24914 of 2017
                        Arising Out of PS. Case No.-1 Year-2015 Thana- C.B.I CASE District- Patna
                  ======================================================
                  Chandra Bhushan @ Chandra Bhushan Prasad, S/o- Late Bhagwan Singh,
                  Resident of Village- Raipura, P.S.- Fatuha, District- Patna.

                                                                                      ... ... Petitioner
                                                        Versus

            1.    The State of Bihar
            2.    The Director of C.B.I., SCB, Patna.

                                                          ... ... Opposite Parties
                  ======================================================
                  Appearance :
                  For the Petitioner/s  :          Mr. Nafisuzzoha, Advocate
                  For the State         :          Mr. Dilip Kumar Sinha, APP
                  For the C.B.I.        :          Mr. Bipin Kumar Sinha, S.C.
                  For Umesh Chandra Singh :        Mr. Harish Kumar, Advocate
                                                   Mr. Raj Krishna Jha, Advocate
                                                   Mr. Arvind Kumar Mouar, Advocate
                  ======================================================
                  CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE SHIVAJI PANDEY
                                        ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">18   18-09-2019                    With consent of the parties, this case is being heard

                    at the admission stage.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                   Heard learned counsel appearing for the respective

                    parties.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                   In this case, the petitioner is seeking anticipatory

                    bail in connection with Patna C.B.I. / S.C.B. / Patna / 2015 RC

                    0922015S0001, registered for the offence under <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_1">Sections 120B</a>,

                    <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_2">420</a> and <a href="/doc/556166/" id="a_3">468</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_4">
</p><p id="p_5">                                   From the record, it appears that the petitioner was

                    made an accused in Fatuha P.S. Case No.37 of 2013 for the

                    offence under <a href="/doc/653797/" id="a_4">Section 304B</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_5">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code,
               Patna High Court CR. MISC. No.24914 of 2017(18) dt.18-09-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                          2/2</span>




                        wherein two bail applications were filed vide Criminal

                        Miscellaneous Nos. 38932 of 2014 and 3984 of 2015, in which

                        different stands have been taken with regard to relationship of

                        the deceased, that generated the present case.


</p><p id="p_6">                                        Learned counsel for the C.B.I. submits that

                        chargsheet      has     already      been     filed   on   30.09.2016   in

                        RC0922015S0001.
</p>
<p id="p_7">
</p><p id="p_8">                                        In such view of the matter, this Court is not

                        inclined to grant anticipatory bail to the petitioner. Accordingly,

                        the prayer for bail is rejected.
</p>
<p id="p_9">
</p><p id="p_10">                                        This order has been passed in presence of Mr.

                        Arvind Kumar Mouar, Mr. Harish Kumar and Mr. Raj Krishna

                        Jha, learned counsel for Mr. Umesh Chandra Singh and Mr.

                        Bipin Kumar Sinha, learned counsel for the C.B.I.




</p><p id="p_11">                                                                (Shivaji Pandey, J)

pawan/-
</p>
