<div class="doc_title">Sunil Kumar vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 14 July, 2015</div><p id="p_1">                                       M.Cr.C. No. 10357/15
      14.07.15.
</p><p id="p_2">               Shri Shakti Prakash Pandey, Advocate for the applicants.
               Shri Ajay Shukla, Govt. Advocate for the State.
               Heard finally. Case diary perused.
</p><p id="p_3">               This is the first bail application filed by the applicants under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of
      the Cr.P.C. for grant of bail.
</p><p id="p_4">               The applicants are in custody since 30.05.15 in connection with Crime No.
      218/15 registered at P.S. Kotwali, District-Mandla for the offence punishable under
      <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_2">Section 420</a> of the IPC and <a href="/doc/711469/" id="a_3">Section 66</a> of the I.T.Act.
</p><p id="p_5">               Learned counsel for the applicants submits that the applicants have been
      falsely implicated in this case. He further submits that the complainant has lodged
      the complaint after about 9 days from the date of incident. The applicants are
      having no past criminal antecedents. They are in custody since 30.05.15, and trial
      would take considerable time to conclude, therefore, they may be released on bail.
</p><p id="p_6">               Learned counsel for State has opposed the application.
               On due consideration of the contentions raised by the learned counsel for
      the parties, nature of allegation against the applicants, and overall facts and
      circumstances of the case, I am of the considered view that it is a fit case to
      release the applicants on bail, therefore, without expressing any view on the merits
      of the case, the application is allowed and it is directed that applicants Sunil
      Kumar and Gautam Sidharth shall be released on bail on their each furnishing a
      personal bond in a sum of Rs. 30,000/- (Rs. Thirty Thousand only) with one surety
      each in the like amount to the satisfaction of the committal Court/trial Court for
      securing their presence before the said Court on all the dates of hearing fixed in
      this regard during trial.
</p><p id="p_7">               Certified copy as per rules.
</p><p id="p_8">                                                           (Subhash Kakade)
                                                               Judge.
</p>
