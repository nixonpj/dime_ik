<div class="doc_title">Md.Sadique &amp;Amp; Anr vs The State Of Bihar on 10 August, 2010</div><pre id="pre_1">                   IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                Cr.Misc. No.29019 of 2010
                                   MD.SADIQUE &amp; ANR
                                            Versus
                                  THE STATE OF BIHAR
                                          -----------
</pre>
<p id="p_1">2.   10.08.2010.              Heard        learned       counsel         for      the

                   petitioners and the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                              The petitioners are apprehending their

                   arrest in a case registered under <a href="/doc/1258372/" id="a_1">Sections 147</a>,

                   <a href="/doc/763672/" id="a_2">148</a>, <a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_3">149</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_4">323</a>, <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_5">324</a>,<a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_6">307</a>, <a href="/doc/1255223/" id="a_7">507</a> and <a href="/doc/839778/" id="a_8">380</a> of the

                   I.P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                              The     F.I.R.     was    lodged        specifically

                   naming eight persons who committed the alleged

                   offence.    There       was   a   specific        allegation    of

                   assault     against      Vijay      Sah     who    subsequently

                   filed complaint case no. 338 of 2010 against

                   the informant's side in which the petitioner

                   no.1     became        witness      which     resulted       into

                   subsequently       implicating       the     petitioners       and

                   their sympathizers in the present case.

</p><p id="p_4">                              Considering the fact that the informant

                   canvassed        the    specific     case     against       eight

                   persons in the F.I.R. and the background in

                   which the petitioners have been roped in the

                   present case, let the petitioners, Md. Sadique

                   and      Sanjay Sah be released on bail in the

                   event of their arrest or surrender before the

                   learned court below within a period of twelve
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                        2</span>




       weeks   from         today       in      connection         with

       Runnisaidpur    P.    S.     Case     no.   87   of   2010    on

       furnishing     bail     bond        of   Rs.     10,000/-(Ten

       Thousand)each    with      two   sureties        of   the   like

       amount each to the satisfaction of the learned

       C.J.M., Sitamarhi, subject to the conditions as

       laid down under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_9">Section 438(2)</a> of Cr.P.C.




</p><pre id="pre_2">U.K.                                 ( Dinesh Kumar Singh, J.)
 </pre>
