<div class="doc_title">Prasanth @ Abhilash vs State Of Kerala on 19 January, 2009</div><pre id="pre_1">       

  

  

 
 
  IN THE HIGH COURT OF KERALA AT ERNAKULAM

Bail Appl..No. 7926 of 2008()


1. PRASANTH @ ABHILASH, S/O.APPICHERUKKAN,
                      ...  Petitioner
2. SAROJAM, W/O.APPICHERUKKAN,

                        Vs



1. STATE OF KERALA, REP. BY PUBLIC
                       ...       Respondent

                For Petitioner  :SRI.S.MOHAMMED AL RAFI

                For Respondent  :PUBLIC PROSECUTOR

The Hon'ble MRS. Justice K.HEMA

 Dated :19/01/2009

 O R D E R
                            K.HEMA, J.
                     ------------------------------
                     B.A. No.7926 OF 2008
                     ------------------------------
            Dated this the 19th day of January, 2009


                             O R D E R
</pre>

<p id="p_1">      This petition is for anticipatory bail.

</p><p id="p_2">      2. The alleged offences are under Sections 323, 324, 308

read with 34 of <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_1">I.P.C</a>. According to prosecution, petitioners

(accused nos.1 and 2), in furtherance of common intention,

assaulted defacto complainant, who is the father of first

petitioner and the husband of second petitioner. Chopper was

used for the assault. Second petitioner pelted stones also at

the defacto complainant. This was done, since the defacto

complainant did not agree to defacto complainant's decision to

assign the property by the second petitioner in favour of first

petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_3">      3. Learned counsel for petitioners submitted that

petitioners were deserted by the defacto complainant and

thereafter, second petitioner was looking after her children.

But defacto complainant came home drunk and assaulted

second petitioner and first petitioner intervened and some

incident happened. No serious injuries are sustained by

B.A.No.7926 of 2008
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                  2</span>


defacto complainant and hence, petitioners may be granted

anticipatory bail, it is submitted.
</p>
<p id="p_4">       4. Learned Public Prosecutor submitted that a chopper

was used and it is to be recovered. It is also submitted that

defacto complainant sustained injuries by cutting the chopper.

</p><p id="p_5">       On hearing both sides, I find that it is not a fit case to

grant anticipatory bail. However, on going through the wound

certificate and other records, I find that offence under <a href="/doc/4266/" id="a_2">Section

308</a> of I.P.C. can be treated as one falling under <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_3">Section 324</a> of

I.P.C. for the purpose of bail.
</p>
<p id="p_6">            Petitioners are directed to surrender before

            the Investigating Officer and co-operate with

            the investigation, in which event, the offence

            under <a href="/doc/4266/" id="a_4">Section 308</a> of I.P.C. will be treated as

            one under <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_5">Section 324</a> of I.P.C. for the

            purpose of bail.
</p>
<p id="p_7">       With this observation, this petition is disposed of.




</p><p id="p_8">                                         K.HEMA, JUDGE
pac

</p>
