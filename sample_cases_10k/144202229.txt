<div class="doc_title">Amrita Das vs State Of West Bengal And Others on 11 July, 2014</div><div class="doc_author">Author: Soumitra Pal</div>

<pre id="pre_1">                                                         1

                     W.P. 38136(W) of 2013


11.7. 2014

                          Amrita Das
                             Versus
                    State of West Bengal and others


                    Mr. Kallol Basu,
                    Mr. Bratin Kumar Dey,
                                     ..for the petitioner.

                    Mr. Subhabrata Datta,
                    Mr. Benazir Ahmed,
                               ..for the State.

                    Let affidavit of service filed today be kept on record.

                    In the writ petition the petitioner, - daughter of late Amulya Kumar Das, an

             employee of the West Bengal Legislative Assembly, who had expired on 25th July, 2011

             in harness, - has prayed for a direction upon the respondents to consider her prayer for</pre>

<p id="p_1">             appointment on compassionate ground since she is duly qualified.

</p><p id="p_2">                    Learned advocate appearing on behalf of the State submits, on instruction, that

             in view of the notification dated 4th January, 2011 the petitioner is not entitled to the

             relief as prayed for.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                    In reply learned advocate for the petitioner submits that as the said notification,

             an executive instruction, is not a law, appropriate order may be passed for

             consideration of the representations dated 2nd January, 2012, 23rd April, 2012 and 25th

             June, 2013.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                    Having heard the learned advocates for the parties and considering the facts and

             circumstances of the case, the writ petition is disposed of by directing the Secretary,
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                           2</span>

West Bengal Legislative Assembly, Kolkata, the respondent no.3 to consider the case

of the petitioner for appointment on compassionate ground after dealing with the

representations dated 2nd January, 2012, 23rd April, 2012 and 25th June, 2013 and by

passing a reasoned order after giving an opportunity of hearing to the petitioner within

four weeks from the date of presentation of a copy of the order down-loaded from the

official web-site of the High Court. It is, however, made clear that I have not gone into

the merits of the matter and all points are left open to be dealt with by the respondent

no.3.
</p>
<p id="p_5">        No order as to costs.
</p>
<p id="p_6">        Urgent photostat certified copy of this order, if applied for, be furnished on

  priority basis.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><p id="p_8">                                                           (Soumitra Pal, J. )
 </p>
