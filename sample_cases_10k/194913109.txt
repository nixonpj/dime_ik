                                 ... RESPONDENT

     THIS W.P. FILED PRAYING TO DIRECT THE
RESPONDENT    TO     CONSIDER  THE   PETITIONER'S
REPRESENTATION DTD.5.5.2014 AT ANNEX-N FOR GRANT
OF ALTERNATIVE SITE.

    THIS PETITION COMING ON FOR PRELIMINARY
HEARING THIS DAY, THE COURT PASSED THE
FOLLOWING:
                                2


                            ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">             The petitioner in this writ petition has sought for

a direction to the respondent to consider the request of the

petitioner dated 5.5.2014 at Annexure N and representation

dated 25.4.2014 at Annexure P for grant of alternative site

</p><p id="p_2">      2. I have heard the learned counsel for both sides. The

petitioner is allottee of BDA site by the respondent in the

year 2006. In view of the dispute between the owners of the

land and BDA, The owner approached this Court in W.P.

No.38733/2010(BDA). In view of the pendency of the case,

the petitioner has not been handed over the possession of

the allotted site.
</p>
<p id="p_3">      3. The learned counsel for petitioner submitted since

where the site is allotted to the petitioner is located in

disputed land, he has given representation to the respondent

to allot alternate site . The said representation has not been

considered and allotted alternative site. The petitioner has

complied with the formalities of paying entire site value and

stamp duty and even lease cum sale agreement has been
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                 3</span>


executed but possession of site has not been handed over to

the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_4">       4. The learned counsel for the BDA submits that the

case of the petitioner will be considered on top priority basis

after locating the land and similar persons are in the queue

and after identifying the land for the purpose of allotment ,

the process of reallotment of the site will take place and then

the case of the petitioner will be considered. In view of the

submission of the respondent, the respondent - BDA is

directed to consider the case of the petitioner Under Rule

11-A of the BDA Allotment Rules, 1984. The re-allotment

process to be completed at the earliest and in any event not

later than eight weeks from date of this order. Accordingly,;

this writ petition is disposed of.
</p>
