<div class="doc_title">Mukesh Kumar Manjhi vs The State Of Bihar on 12 July, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                              CRIMINAL MISCELLANEOUS No.42796 of 2019
                      Arising Out of PS. Case No.-134 Year-2019 Thana- BASANTPUR District- Siwan
                 ======================================================
                 MUKESH KUMAR MANJHI Son of Dudun Manjhi Resident of Village-
                 Sendurkha, P.S.- Basantpur, District- Siwan.

                                                                                  ... ... Petitioner/s
                                                       Versus
                 The State of Bihar

                                                        ... ... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :       Mr.Ajay Kumar Pandey
                 For the Opposite Party/s :       Mr.Uday Chand Prasad
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE ANIL KUMAR SINHA
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   12-07-2019                   Heard learned Counsel for the petitioner and learned

                   Additional Public Prosecutor for the State.

</p><p id="p_2">                                 This application, for grant of anticipatory bail, arises

                   out of Basantpur Police Station Case No. 134 of 2019,

                   disclosing offences under <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_1">Sections 341</a>/<a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_2">323</a>/<a href="/doc/203036/" id="a_3">354</a>/<a href="/doc/203036/" id="a_4">354(c)</a>/<a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_5">504</a> of

                   the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                 The allegation against the petitioner is that on the

                   alleged date of occurrence, while the informant was going to

                   attend the call of nature, she was pulled by the petitioner in the

                   bushes and tried to outrage her modesty. Upon raising alarm, the

                   villagers came and saved the informant from the clutches of the

                   petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                 Learned Counsel for the petitioner submits that the

                   allegation levelled in the First Information Report is totally
 Patna High Court CR. MISC. No.42796 of 2019(2) dt.12-07-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                            2/3</span>




          concocted and no offence in the manner alleged has been

          committed by the petitioner. He further submits that the

          petitioner was assaulted by the family members of the

          informant, including the informant, on 22.04.2019, for which

          father of this petitioner has lodged Basantpur Police Station

          Case No. 135 of 2019, under <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_6">Sections 341</a>/<a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_7">323</a>/<a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_8">324</a>/<a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_9">307</a>/<a href="/doc/1101188/" id="a_10">379</a>/

          <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_11">504</a>/<a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_12">506</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_13">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code and <a href="/doc/1507082/" id="a_14">Sections 3</a> (i) (r) (s)/3

          (2) (va) of the Scheduled Castes and <a href="/doc/25085007/" id="a_15">Scheduled Tribes

          (Prevention of Atrocities) Act</a>, 1989. He submits that in order to

          save themselves from the case lodged by the father of the

          petitioner, the present false First Information Report has been

          lodged and from perusal of the formal First Information Report,

          it would be evident that the date and time of the occurrence are

          not mentioned therein. He, accordingly, submits that due to

          previous dispute, this case has been lodged against the

          petitioner, who is pursuing his engineering course outside the

          village and had come in the village due to the vacation.

</p><p id="p_5">                       After having heard learned Counsel for the parties and

          taking into consideration the fact that there is case and counter

          case and that the petitioner is pursuing his engineering course

          and has no criminal antecedent, this application is allowed.

</p><p id="p_6">                       Let the petitioner, Mukesh Kumar Manjhi, in the
             Patna High Court CR. MISC. No.42796 of 2019(2) dt.12-07-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                        3/3</span>




                      event of his arrest or surrender before the Court below within

                      four weeks, be released on bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs.

                      10,000/- (ten thousand) with two sureties of the like amount

                      each to the satisfaction of learned Chief Judicial Magistrate,

                      Siwan, in connection with Basantpur Police Station Case No.

                      134 of 2019, subject to the condition laid down under <a href="/doc/25085007/" id="a_16">Section

                      438</a> (2)<a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_17"> of the Code</a> of Criminal Procedure.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><p id="p_8">                                                                           (Anil Kumar Sinha, J.)
Prabhakar Anand/-
</p>
