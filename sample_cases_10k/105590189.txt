BEFORE THE MADURAI BENCH OF MADRAS HIGH COURT               

DATED: 30.01.2017  

CORAM   

THE HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE S.VAIDYANATHAN             

W.P(MD)No.1334 of 2017   

T.Chellappa                                                             ... Petitioner

vs.

1.The Managing Director,
Tamil Nadu State Express Transport Corporation,
Thiruvalluvar Illam, Pallavan  Salai, Chennai ? 600 002.
2.The General Manager, 
Tamil Nadu State Express Transport Corporation,
Thiruvalluvar Illam, Pallavan Salai, Chennai ? 600 002.
3. The Administrator,
Tamil Nadu State Express Transport Corporation,
Thiruvalluvar Illam, Pallavan Salai, Chennai ? 600 002.         ...
Respondents  

        Petition filed under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the Constitution of India, praying
for the issuance of a Writ of Mandamus, directing the respondents to pay the
retirement benefits including Gratuity, leave salary and SSS amount (Social
Security Scheme), IRT (Institute of Road Transport) with interest at the rate
of 18% per annum within the time limit that may be fixed by this Hon'ble
Court.

!For Petitioner         : Ms.J.Balameenakshi  

For Respondents : Mr.K.Sathiya Singh for R1 &amp; R2        
                                          Mr.A.P.Muthupandian for R3 

:ORDER  
</pre><p id="p_1">        The petitioner was employed as Checking Inspector in the respondents
Corporation and he retired from service on 30.06.2016 on attaining the age of
superannuation.
</p><p id="p_2">        2.He has filed this writ petition seeking for a direction to the
respondents to pay the retirement benefits.
</p><p id="p_3">        3.The First Bench of this Court in similar matter has passed an order
dated 12.06.2015 in W.A.(MD) Nos.383 to 457 of 2015, issuing direction to the
transport Corporations to settle the terminal benefits of its employees in
equal monthly instalments and to pay 6% interest on the terminal benefits
payable to the workman. 
</p><p id="p_4">The Bench has also held that workman is entitled to 18% interest for the
defaulted period of instalment.
</p><p id="p_5">        4.In these circumstances, this writ petition is disposed of with the
following directions:-
</p><p id="p_6">        i)A direction is issued to the transport corporation to settle the
terminal benefits of the petitioner that are yet to be settled, in twelve
equal monthly instalments;
</p><p id="p_7">        ii)The first instalment shall commence by making payment on or before
the 10th March 2017 and the amount in each of the remaining instalments shall
be paid on or before 10th of every succeeding month;
</p><p id="p_8">        iii) The said terminal amount shall carry interest @ 6% per annum, as
per the Division Bench judgment referred to above. In case of delay in making
instalments, the interest payable could be 18% for the delayed period;
</p><p id="p_9">        iv) The aforesaid direction to settle the terminal benefits would not
preclude the workman to question the computation of any of the terminal
benefits, if the same is paid lesser than the amount to which, he is entitled
to receive. Likewise, if the petitioner has any grievance that he is entitled
to interest for the amount already settled, he can agitate the same as per
law, if he is entitled. No costs.
</p>
<p id="p_10">To

</p><p id="p_11">1.The Managing Director, Tamil Nadu State Express Transport Corporation,
Thiruvalluvar Illam, Pallavan  Salai, Chennai ? 600 002.
</p>
<p id="p_12">2.The General Manager, Tamil Nadu State Express Transport Corporation, 
Thiruvalluvar Illam, Pallavan Salai, Chennai ? 600 002.
</p>

Tamil Nadu State Express Transport Corporation,
Thiruvalluvar Illam, Pallavan Salai, Chennai ? 600 002.
