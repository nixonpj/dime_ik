<div class="doc_title">Md. Nizam @ Pappu vs The State Of Bihar on 21 July, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                 IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA

                                 Criminal Miscellaneous No.20764 of 2016
                          Arising out of PS.Case No. -210 Year- 2015 Thana -AMAS District- GAYA
                 ======================================================
                 Md. Nizam @ Pappu, son of Aminat resident of village- Babupur, P.S-
                 Pirpainti, District Bhagalpur.

                                                                                   .... ....   Petitioner
                                                        Versus
                 The State of Bihar

                                                              .... .... Opposite Party
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner     : Mr. Dhirendra Kumar, Advocate.
                 For the Opposite Party : Mr. Iftekhar Mahmood(App)
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE SUDHIR SINGH
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">3   21-07-2016              Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned

                 counsel for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                           The petitioner is languishing in custody since 31.12.2015

                 in connection with Amas P.S. Case No. 210 of 2015 for the

                 offences instituted under <a href="/doc/329571/" id="a_1">Section 392</a> of the IPC.

</p><p id="p_3">                           The prosecution story, in brief, is that the truck of the

                 informant was stolen away by the accused persons.

</p><p id="p_4">                           It has been submitted on behalf of the petitioner that the

                 petitioner has got no criminal antecedent. The petitioner is in

                 custody since 31.12.2015 and the charge sheet has been submitted

                 in the present case. There is no allegation of tampering with the

                 evidence against the petitioner. He is not named in the F.I.R. He

                 has not been put on T.I.P. to ascertain the involvement of the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">          Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.20764 of 2016 (3) dt.21-07-2016</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                    2/2</span>




                      petitioner in the present case. As per the confession, the petitioner

                      wanted to purchase a truck from one Bharat and for the said

                      reason the truck was parked by the side of the house. There is no

                      material to suggest that the petitioner committed an offence under

                      <a href="/doc/329571/" id="a_2">Section 392</a> of the IPC.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                  On behalf of the State it has been submitted that the

                      petitioner is not named in the F.I.R.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                  Considering the aforesaid facts and circumstances, let the

                      petitioner above named, be released on bail on furnishing bail

                      bond of Rs. 10,000/-(Ten Thousand) with two sureties of the like

                      amount each to the satisfaction of the learned J.M.Ist Class,

                      Sherghati, Gaya, in connection with Amas P.S. Case No.

                      210/2015.
</p>
<p id="p_7">
</p><pre id="pre_2">U.K./-                                                (Sudhir Singh, J)
  U         T
