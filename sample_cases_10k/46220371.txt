<div class="doc_title">Hindusingh vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 3 May, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                      M.Cr.C. No. 4044 Of 2016

03.05.2016
      Shri Anupam Chouhan, learned counsel for the applicant.
      Shri Milind Phadke, learned GA for the respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">      This is second application under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of Cr.P.C. First
bail application has been dismissed as not pressed vide order
dated 07/04/16 passed in M.Cr.C. No.2326/16.
</p><p id="p_2">      As per prosecution case, complainant was going to Mundla
from Jamunia, four persons on two motorcycle came, stopped
their motorcycle infront his vehicle, asked the complainant to
handover the money. When he refused, then two of them caught
hold him and another two gave beating by means of piece of
stone causing injuries. Hindu Singh snatched the cash of
Rs.20,000/- from his pocket along with golden chain and mobile.
Thereafter, they flew away.
</p><p id="p_3">      Learned counsel for the applicant submits that applicant has
not committed any offence. He has falsely been implicated.
Complainant Dr. Jitesh Singh (PW/1) and Krishna Pal Singh
(PW/2) have not supported the prosecution case. Applicant is
under custody since 31/12/15. Conclusion of trial likely to take
time, hence prayed for bail.
</p><p id="p_4">      The    prayer    is   opposed    by    learned   GA     for   the
respondent/State.
</p><p id="p_5">      Case-diary perused.
</p><p id="p_6">      As per the memorandum of applicant/Hindu Singh, cash of
Rs.5,000/- has been recovered. Though the applicant is not
named in the FIR, but in the statement complainant Dr. Jitesh and
other witnesses have not made any allegation against the
applicant.
</p><p id="p_7">      Taking into consideration the statement of the witnesses
including the complainant and the fact that applicant is under
custody since 31/12/15, but without commenting anything on the
merits of the case, the application is allowed. It is directed that the
 applicant shall be released on bail on his furnishing personal bond
in the sum of Rs.50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand Only) with one
solvent surety in the like amount to the satisfaction of Trial Court.
</p><p id="p_8">        This order will remain operative subject to compliance of the
following conditions by the applicant :-
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">     1. The applicant will comply with all the terms and conditions of the bond
        executed by him;
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">     2. The applicant will cooperate in the investigation/trial, as the case may
        be;
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">     3. The applicant will not indulge himself in extending inducement, threat
        or promise to any person acquainted with the facts of the case so as to
        dissuade him/her from disclosing such facts to the Court or to the Police
        Officer, as the case may be;
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_4">     4. The applicant shall not commit an offence similar to the offence of
        which he is accused;
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_5">     5. The applicant will not seek unnecessary adjournments during the trial;
        and
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_6">     6. The applicant will not leave India without previous permission of the
        trial Court/Investigating Officer, as the case may be.</blockquote>


<p id="p_9">        A copy of this order be sent to the Court concerned for
compliance.
</p><p id="p_10">        C.c. as per rules.
</p><p id="p_11">                                                       (D.K.Paliwal)
                                                          Judge
sk
