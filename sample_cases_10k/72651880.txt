                                  JUDGMENT
</pre>
<p id="p_1">Ramachandran Nair, J.
</p>
<p id="p_2">      The petitioner challenges Ext.P6 order passed in I.A.No.154 of 2013 in

R.C.P.No.3 of 2011. Therein, the prayer is to accept the replication filed along

with the petition.
</p>
<p id="p_3">      2. We heard the learned counsel on both sides.

</p><p id="p_4">      3. The learned counsel for the petitioner submitted that the petitioner

wants only to place the replication on record and as far as the new pleas

under Section 11(4)(i) is concerned, the petitioner is not desirous to project

that issue in the petition, since various formalities also have to be complied

with. Therefore, it is submitted that with the above clarification, this Court

may direct the court below to accept the replication so that it will form part of

the records.
</p>
<p id="p_5">      4. The learned counsel for the respondent also submitted that if the

petitioner is not pressing for the ground pleaded under Section 11(4)(i), there

may not any objection for the same.
</p>
<p id="p_6"><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">OP(RC).900/13                         2</span>




</p><p id="p_7">      5. In that view of the matter, we set aside Ext.P6 order. There will be

a direction to the learned Munsiff to accept the replication and we clarify that

the petitioner will not pursue with the plea under Section 11(4)(i) in the

present proceedings and the issue is left open.

</p><p id="p_8">      Original petition is disposed of accordingly.
