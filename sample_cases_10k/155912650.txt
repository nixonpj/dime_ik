<div class="doc_title">The Amritpal Coop L&amp;C; Society Ltd vs Anirudh Tewari Ias And Ors on 14 March, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">    IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA
                 AT CHANDIGARH
                                                          COCP 2954 of 2018
                                             Date of Decision : March 14, 2019

The Amritpal Co-op L&amp;C Society Ltd.
                                                                 .....Petitioner
                                         VERSUS
Anirudh Tiwari and others                                     .....Respondents

CORAM: HON'BLE MS. JUSTICE NIRMALJIT KAUR
Present :    Mr. R.K.Girdhar, Advocate for the petitioner.

             Ms. Sudeepti Sharma, Addl. Advocate General, Punjab.
             Mr. S.S.Bedi, Advocate for respondents No.2 and 3.

NIRMALJIT KAUR, J. (Oral)
</pre>
<p id="p_1">             Ms. Sudeepti Sharma, Addl. Advocate General, Punjab on

instructions from Shri Anirudh Tewari, Principal Secretary to Government

of Punjab, Finance Department, who is present in the Court, states that the

amount shall be released to the respondent-Department by 31.3.2019.

Learned counsel for respondents No.2 and 3-Department states that the bills

have already been forwarded to the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">             In view of the above, the contempt petition is disposed of with

a direction that as soon as the amount is released by respondent No.1-State,

respondents No.2 and 3 shall further disburse the same to the petitioner

within four weeks from the receipt of the Department.

</p><p id="p_3">             In case the said amount is not deposited within the stipulated

period after release by respondent No.1, the petitioner is at liberty to revive

the contempt petition and in case the petitioner is forced to revive the

contempt petition, serious note will be taken.
</p>
