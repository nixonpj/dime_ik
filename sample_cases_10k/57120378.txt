<p id="p_1">Date of institution of claim petition:                                            23.04.2013
Date on which the Award/judgment reserved:                                        19.12.2013
Date on which the Award/judgment  pronounced:                                     19.12.2013


Suit No: 116/13                                                                                Pate 1 of 4
 A W A R D:
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">1.                This  petition under <a href="/doc/136948773/" id="a_1">Section 166</a> of the Motor Vehicle Act, 1988 

was filed by the petitioner, Mohd Zulfakar Khan S/o Dildar Khan claiming 

compensation   of   Rs.2,50,000/­   from   the   respondents   for   the   injuries 

sustained   by   him   in   a   road   vehicular   accident   which   took   place   on 

30.09.2012 near Holy Family Hospital, New Delhi involving vehicle bearing 

registration No. UP­17­T­2984 (Indica Car) driven by respondent No. 1, Shri 

Shiv Kumar.  A Detailed Accident Report (DAR) has also been filed by the 

police in respect of this accident (FIR No. 23/12, PS New Friends Colony) 

and the said DAR was also clubbed with the present claim petition vide 

proceedings dated 23.04.2013.
</p>
<p id="p_4">2.                The respondents No. 1, 2 and 3 are respectively the driver, 

owner   and   insurer   of   the   offending   vehicle.  During   the   course   of   the 

proceedings, the petitioner, Mohd Zulfakar Khan as well as his counsel and 

the learned counsel R­3/Insurance company submitted that the matter can 

be   settled   amicably.   Efforts   of   reconciliation   have   been   made   and   after 

judicial intervention, petitioner, Mohd Zulfakar Khan as well as his counsel 

and   the   learned   counsel   for   R­3/Insurance   Company   submitted   that   the 

claim for the compensation for the injuries suffered by the petitioner, Mohd 

Zulfakar Khan has been settled amicably for a full and final amount of Rs.

50,000/­.  Statements of petitioner, Mohd Zulfakar Khan as well as Shri B K 

Suit No: 116/13                                                                      Pate 2 of 4
 Sharma,   learned   counsel   for   R­3/Insurance   Company   have   been 

separately recorded today. The settlement, being voluntary and the amount 

of compensation being just and reasonable, is accepted.

</p><p id="p_5">3.                In view of the statements of the parties, I pass an award in the 

sum   of   Rs.50,000/­   (Rupees   Fifty  Thousand   Only)   in   full   and   final 

settlement   in   favour   of   the   petitioner   and   against   the   respondents   on 

account of their liability being joint and several.

APPORTIONMENT OF LIABILITY:
</p>
<p id="p_6">4.                The   respondent   No.   3,  being     the   insurer   of   the   offending 

vehicle at the time of accident, is jointly and severally liable with the driver 

and owner of the offending vehicle.  Accordingly, the respondent No. 3 i.e. 

M/s United India Insurance Co. Ltd is directed to deposit the award amount 

within a period of 30 days. In case of any delay, it shall be liable to pay 

interest at a rate of 12% per annum for the period of delay.

</p><p id="p_7">5.                In terms of the directions passed by the Hon'ble High Court 

of Delhi  in its judgment titled as "Amod Kumar Ray &amp; Ors Vs Raj Kumar 

Chauhan   &amp;   Ors"   {CM(M)   649/2011   decided   on   25.05.2011},   the 

Insurance company is directed to deposit the award amount in the State 

Bank   of   India,   District   Courts   Saket,   New   Delhi   in   the   name   of   the 

petitioner in terms of the award. It is made clear that at the time of the 

deposit of the award amount with the bank, the Insurance company shall 

Suit No: 116/13                                                                        Pate 3 of 4
 specifically mention the suit number of the case,  title of the case as well 

as   date   of   decision   with   the   name   of   court   on   the   back   side   of   the 

cheque.  The insurance company shall also file the attested  copy of the 

award attested by its own officer to the bank at the time of deposit of the 

amount with the bank.
</p>
<p id="p_8">6.                The copy of this award be given to the respondents as well 

as to the petitioner free of cost.  The petitioner shall approach the State 

Bank of India, District Court Saket, New Delhi for opening the account. 

</p><p id="p_9">7.                The Manager of the Bank   is directed to comply with the 

award. The Bank Manager is directed to release the award amount to 

the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_10">8.                The petition stands allowed and disposed off. Accordingly, 

the   file   be   consigned   to   record   room   after   completion   of   necessary 

formalities.
</p>
