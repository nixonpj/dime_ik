<div class="doc_title">Raj Kumar Panwar vs State Of Uttarakhand on 10 July, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">BA1 No. 559 of 2017
Hon'ble V.K. Bist, J.
</pre><p id="p_1">     Mr. Arvind Vashistha, Senior
Advocate with Mr. Karan Anand &amp;
Mr. K.R. Gazi, Advocates for the
applicant.
</p><p id="p_2">     Mr. Raman Kumar Sah, Deputy
Advocate General with Mr. V.S. Pal,
A.G.A. for the State of Uttarakhand.
</p><p id="p_3">     This is the first bail application
moved by the applicant.
</p><p id="p_4">     The     applicant-    Raj    Kumar
Panwar is seeking regular bail in
connection with Crime No. 162 of
2013, offences punishable under
<a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_1">Section 420</a>, <a href="/doc/1985627/" id="a_2">467</a>, <a href="/doc/556166/" id="a_3">468</a>, <a href="/doc/1466184/" id="a_4">471</a>, <a href="/doc/308396/" id="a_5">193</a>, <a href="/doc/1128948/" id="a_6">120-</a>
B of I.P.C., registered at Police Station
Doiwala, District Dehradun.
</p><p id="p_5">     Learned Senior Counsel for the
applicant submitted that the applicant
is only the purchaser of the land. At
the time of purchase of land, he had
no knowledge about the attachment
order passed by the Metropolitan
Magistrate,      Delhi.    He     further
submitted       that    the     applicant
bonafidely purchased the property. He
also submitted the fact that the
property attached was not recorded in
the Khatauni, therefore, there was no
occasion     to    know     about     the
attachment order.
</p><p id="p_6">     It is the contention of the learned
Senior Counsel for the applicant that
if applicant had the knowledge about
the attachment order, he would not
have invested a huge amount.
</p><p id="p_7">       Learned       Deputy       Advocate
strongly opposed the bail application
of the present applicant. He submitted
that the property in dispute was an
attached property and it was the duty
of the purchaser to enquire about it
before purchasing the same. It was
also the duty of the applicant to
inform the court concerned about the
malafide transaction of the said
property, as the said transaction is
void in law.
</p><p id="p_8">      Considering the submission of
learned counsel for the parties, and
without expressing any opinion as to
final merits of the case, this Court is
of the view that the applicant deserves
bail at this stage.
</p><p id="p_9">      The bail application is allowed.
      Let the applicant be released on
bail, on executing personal bond and
furnishing two reliable sureties, each
of the like amount to the satisfaction
of the Magistrate concerned.
</p>


<p id="p_10">                         (V.K. Bist, J.)
                          10.07.2017
Navin
