<div class="doc_title">Srei Equipment Finance Limited vs Vyankati Ashruba Kande &amp; Anr on 11 March, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">ORDER SHEET                EC No.50 of 2014
                     IN THE HIGH COURT AT CALCUTTA
                   Ordinary Original Civil Jurisdiction
                           ORIGINAL SIDE




                           SREI EQUIPMENT FINANCE LIMITED
                                       Versus
                           VYANKATI ASHRUBA KANDE &amp; ANR.

         BEFORE:
       The Hon'ble JUSTICE HARISH TANDON</pre>
<p id="p_1">       Date : 11th March, 2016.
</p><p id="p_2">                                                                    Appearance:
</p><p id="p_3">                                                                Mr. R. Ghosh, Adv.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                                                Ms. S. Sharma, Adv.
</p>
<p id="p_5">         The Court:-The receiver appointed in this case was directed

to sell the vehicle in question by public auction and/or private

treaty upon intimation to the Judgement-debtors. Pursuant to the

said order dated 17th July, 2015, receiver made publications of

sale     notice   once    in    "Lokmat    Times"   and    the    other    in   "Daily

Deshannoti" on 14th October, 2015. The receiver's report reveals

that he received only one offer from one Mohammad Younus Safi, son

of Shafi Inayatullah Patel Mohammad, who offered to purchase the

vehicle in question at Rs.15 lakhs. Pursuant to the sale notice,

he   deposited     with   the    receiver    demand     draft    bearing    no.040339

dated 30th November, 2015 drawn on ICICI Bank Ltd. payable at

Kolkata for a sum of Rs.3 lakhs, towards earnest money for the

vehicle.
</p>
<p id="p_6">       The    decree-holder       says     that   the     Judgement-debtors          were

intimated of such offer being received by the receiver and filed

affidavit of service to that effect today.

</p><p id="p_7">       Despite service, the Judgement-debtors are not represented.

As there is no other offer received by the receiver, this Court

permits the receiver to effect the sale to the said intending
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                  2</span>
purchaser     at       Rs.15       lakhs.   The intending purchaser              shall    issue

a fresh demand draft of Rs.3 lakhs within one week from date. The

receiver shall return the demand draft received from him on the

basis of the sale notice immediately. The balance amount shall be

paid by the intending purchaser within three weeks from date to

the receiver. The receiver is directed to encash the demand draft

as and when submitted by the intending purchaser within the time

indicated         hereinabove        and    thereafter        shall     issue    the     cheque

covering     the       entire      amount   to     the    decree-holder.        The    receiver

shall      also     issue      a    certificate          in   favour    of    the     intending

purchaser. In default of any of the payments, as indicated above,

within the stipulated time, the offer of the intending purchaser

shall be deemed to have been rejected.
</p>
<p id="p_8">     The receiver shall open an account in such capacity in any

nationalised bank of his choice and after encashing the demand

draft shall pay the entire amount to the decree-holder therefrom.

Possession        of    the    vehicle      in   question       shall    be   given     to   the

intending purchaser upon payment of the entire amount either by

receiver himself or his agent.
</p>
<p id="p_9">     The    decree-holder           shall    pay     a    further    remuneration       to   the

receiver assessed at Rs.400 GMs.
</p>
<p id="p_10">     Liberty to mention.
</p>
<p id="p_11">


</p><p id="p_12">                                                                    (HARISH TANDON, J.)
