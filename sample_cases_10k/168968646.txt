                              The High Court Of Madhya Pradesh
                                        MCRC-46907-2019
                               (KRISHNA PAL SINGH @ NANHERAJA Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)

                     2
                     Jabalpur, Dated : 30-11-2019
                            Shri Arvind Kumar Soni, learned counsel for the applicant.
                            Shri Vaibhav Saxena, learnd Government Advocate for the
                     respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">                            Heard. Perused the case diary.
</p><p id="p_2">                            This is first application filed on behalf of the applicant under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section

                     439</a> of Cr.P.C. for grant of bail.
</p><p id="p_3">                            T he applicant has been arrested on 14.10.2019 in connection with
                     Crime No.71/2019 registered at Police Station Dehat, District Tikamgarh
                     (M.P.) for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/619940/" id="a_2">Sections 363</a>, <a href="/doc/796352/" id="a_3">366</a>, <a href="/doc/1279834/" id="a_4">376</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_5">34</a> of Indian
                     Penal Code and Section 5/6 of POCSO Act.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                            Learned counsel for the applicant submits that the applicant is
                     innocent and has been falsely implicated. The applicant has not criminal
                     antecedents. The applicant is in custody since 14.10.2019 and the trial
                     will take considerable time to conclude. The applicant is a permanent

                     resident of District Tikamgarh and there is no likelihood of absconding
                     or tampering with the prosecution evidence by the applicant. On these
                     grounds, prayer is made to enlarge the applicant on bail.
</p><p id="p_5">                            Per contra, learned Government Advocate for the State opposes the
                     bail application and prays for its rejection.
</p><p id="p_6">                            Heard rival contentions of both the parties and perused the entire
                     material available on record including the case diary.
</p><p id="p_7">                            Looking to the facts and circumstances of the case, this Court is of
                     the view that the applicant may be enlarged on bail, hence, without
                     commenting on the merits of the case, the application is allowed. The
                     applicant-Krishna Pal Singh @ Nanheraja is directed to be released on


Digitally signed by NITESH PANDEY
Date: 03/12/2019 14:58:22
                                                        2                         MCRC-46907-2019
                     bail upon his furnishing personal bond in the sum of Rs.50,000/-
                     (Rupees Fifty Thousand Only) with one solvent surety, in the like
                     amount to the satisfaction of the concerned Court for his appearance
                     before the trial Court on all such dates as may be fixed in this behalf by
                     the trial Court during the pendency of trial.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                            This order will remain operative subject to compliance of the
                     following conditions by the applicant :
</p><p id="p_9">                            (1) The applicant will comply with all the terms and conditions of
                     the bond executed by him;
</p><p id="p_10">                            (2) The applicant will cooperate in the trial;
                            (3) The applicant will not indulge himself in extending inducement,
                     threat or promise to any person acquainted with the fact of the case so as
                     to dissuade him from disclosing such facts to the Court or to the Police
                     Officer;
</p><p id="p_11">                            (4) The applicant shall not commit any offence during the entire
                     period of bail;
</p><p id="p_12">                            (5) The applicant will not seek unnecessary adjournments during
                     the trial;
</p><p id="p_13">                            (6) The applicant will not leave India without previous permission
                     of the trial Court;
</p><p id="p_14">                            (7) The applicant shall inform the Investigating Officer/Court about
                     hi s address and residence in case the applicant moves out from his
                     permanent address for any point of time; and
                            (8) The applicant shall not contact any of the other accused persons
                     in this case in any manner whatsoever.
</p><p id="p_15">                            This order shall remain effective till the end of the trial but in case
                     o f bail jump and breach of any of the pre-condition of bail, it shall
                     become ineffective and cancelled without reference to this Bench.
