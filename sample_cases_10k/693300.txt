  IN THE HIGH COURT OF KERALA AT ERNAKULAM

WP(C).No. 13836 of 2009(Y)


1. HAMEED V.C., S/O KUNHAYIN,
                      ...  Petitioner

                        Vs



1. DISTRICT COLLECTOR,
                       ...       Respondent

2. TAHSILDAR,

                For Petitioner  :SRI.C.A.MAJEED

                For Respondent  : No Appearance

The Hon'ble MR. Justice V.GIRI

 Dated :14/<a href="/doc/1390448/" id="a_1">07/2009

 O R D E R
                            V.GIRI, J.

       - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -
              W.P. (C) No</a>. 13836 OF 2009

           Dated this the 14th day of July, 2009

                        J U D G M E N T
</pre>

<p id="p_1">          A mini lorry bearing registration no. KL 11C

7722 belonging to the petitioner was seized by the second

respondent on 18.01.2009, on the allegation that it was

unauthorisedly transporting sand. The matter was then

placed before the District Collector who commenced

proceedings under Section 23 of the sand Act.                 A final

order passed by the District Collector on 31.03.09 finding

that the vehicle is liable to be confiscated is under

challenge in this writ petition. I have called for the files

leading to the impugned order and I have perused the

files. The registered notice proposing a hearing in the

matter of seizure and confiscation of the vehicle was in

the first instance despatched from the Collectorate on

5.2.2009.    A hearing was proposed on 9.2.2009.                The

petitioner submitted that he received a notice of hearing

only on 10.2.2009. He requested for a hearing on some

WPC.No. 13836 OF 2009
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                             : 2 :</span>


other date. In fact he did not take any contention that the

consignment was supported by a pass. The petitioner

submitted a representation on 19.2.2009 with a request that

the vehicle be released after levying a nominal fine. A fresh

hearing was conducted on 10.3.2009 and another registered

notice dated 26.2.2009 was despatched on 27.6.2009.

There was no response by the petitioner.

</p><p id="p_2">     2.    District Collector considered the materials on

record and rightly came to the conclusion that the

transportation of sand is unauthorized. There was no pass

and there was no explanation for the absence of the same.

District Collector issued a direction to remit Rs.62,500/- to

the River Management Fund failing which the vehicle will be

confiscated.     I do not find any grounds to interfere with

Ext.P2.   The finding that the vehicle was used for illegal

transportation of sand is perfectly justified. There was not

even an attempt by the petitioner to explain the legitimacy

of the consignment or the transportation. The value of the

vehicle has been fixed on a reasonable basis.

WPC.No. 13836 OF 2009
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                             : 3 :</span>


</p><p id="p_3">     3.    I find no reason to interfere with Ext.P3.

</p><p id="p_4">           The writ petition is dismissed. If the petitioner

pays the amount       fixed under Ext.P3    within six months

from today, the vehicle in question shall be released to the

petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_5">


</p><p id="p_6">                                            (V.GIRI, JUDGE)
