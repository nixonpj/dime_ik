<div class="doc_title">Naresh Kumar Mishra vs The State Of Bihar &amp; Anr on 29 September, 2010</div><pre id="pre_1">               IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                              Cr Misc No 31323 of 2010
                 Naresh Kumar Mishra, son of late Baleshwar Mishra, resident of
                 Village - Indpaygarh, P S - Jamui, District - Jamui -     Petitioner
                                        Versus
              1 The State of Bihar
              2 Sri Deonath Das, son of Sri Rameshwar Das, resident of village -
                 Premadih, P S - Halsi, District - Lakhisarai -      Opposite Parties
                                       -----------
</pre>
<p id="p_1">2      29.09.2010                 The petitioner is in custody in relation to Jamui Police

                    Station Case No 330 of 2009 instituted under <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_1">Sections 420</a>, <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_2">120B</a> of

                    Indian Penal Code read with <a href="/doc/1507082/" id="a_3">Section 3</a> (x) of the Scheduled Castes and

                    <a href="/doc/25085007/" id="a_4">Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act</a>. This Court had earlier

                    rejected the anticipatory bail application of the petitioner vide order dated

                    12.05.2010 passed in Cr Misc No 6406 of 2010. Petitioner has since

                    surrendered and his prayer for bail has been rejected and, as such, is

                    before this Court. It appears that this Court had tried to see that the matter

                    was settled but having failed to reach a settlement, the anticipatory bail

                    application was dismissed.      Learned counsel for the informant Shri

                    Praveen Kumar Gupta submits that the matter has been fully

                    compromised as between the parties.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                  In that view of the matter, let the petitioner abovenamed be

                    released on bail on his furnishing bonds of Rs 10,000/- (Rupees Ten

                    Thousand) with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of

                    Chief Judicial Magistrate, Jamui in Jamui Police Station Case No 330 of

                    2009.
</p>
<p id="p_3">
</p><pre id="pre_2">    M.E.H./                                                 (Navaniti Prasad Singh)
