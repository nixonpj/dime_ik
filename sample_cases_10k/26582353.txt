<div class="doc_title">Lalua Kumhar vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 25 February, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">       HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH
             Criminal Appeal No.1274/2019
Jabalpur,
25.02.2019
     Mr. Bhupendra Shukla, learned counsel for the
appellant.

     Mr. Ashish Tiwari, learned Government Advocate
for the respondent/State</pre>

<p id="p_1">     Record of the Court below has been received.
</p>
<p id="p_2">     Also heard on the question of admission.
</p>
<p id="p_3">     Perused the statement of witnesses recorded
before the trial Court.
</p>
<p id="p_4">     The appeal seems to be arguable, hence the
appeal is admitted for hearing.
</p>
<p id="p_5">     Also heard on I.A. 2595/2019, an application
under Section 389 (1) of the Code of Criminal
Procedure, 1973 for suspension of sentence and grant
of bail to the appellant.
</p>
<p id="p_6">     The appellant-accused has been convicted for
the offence punishable under <a href="/doc/203036/" id="a_1">sections 354</a>, <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_2">354-D</a> of
the IPC and sentenced to undergo R.I. for three years
and fine of Rs.5,000/- each with default stipulation
and under sections 7/8 and 11/12 of the POCSO Act
and sentenced to undergo R.I. for 3 years and fine of
Rs.5,000/- each with default stipulation.
</p>
<p id="p_7">     Learned counsel for the appellant submits that
the finding of the trial Court is contrary to law. It is
submitted that the appellant is on bail till 02.03.2019.
It is further submitted that the disposal of the appeal
will take considerable time, therefore, prays for
suspension of sentence and grant of bail to the
  appellant.
</p>
<p id="p_8">      Learned                                      Government   Advocate   for   the
 respondent/State opposes the bail application.
</p>
<p id="p_9">      Considering the statement of witnesses and the
 fact the appellant is on bail till 02.03.2019, without
 expressing any opinion on the merits of the case, the
 aforesaid I.A. is allowed.
</p>
<p id="p_10">      It is directed that on furnishing a personal bond
 of Rs.40,000/- (Rupees Forty thousand) along with
 one solvent surety of like amount to the satisfaction of
 trial Court, the remaining jail sentence of the
 appellant is hereby suspended subject to depositing
 the fine amount, if not already deposited, the
 appellant- Lalua Kumhar be released on bail with a
 condition that the appellant will not indulge in any
 criminal activities with a further direction to mark his
 presence before the trial Court on 08.04.2019 and
 also on such other dates as may be notified by that
 Court in this regard till disposal of this appeal. If any
 condition is violated by the appellant, this order shall
 be treated canceled.
</p>
<p id="p_11">      List for final hearing in due course.
</p>
<p id="p_12">      Certified copy as per rules.
</p>
