</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">      " The petitioners above named humbly submits as follows:

</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">             1. That the petitioner has              perfected the above writ petition             challenging
             the   order     dated      05.07.2011     passed     by the           Land Tribunal, Kolar.        The
             petition     may        kindly    be       read     as part and      parcel of this application.
</blockquote>

<blockquote id="blockquote_3">             2.     That the         petitioner      and        respondent        NO.3    and         respondents
                    No.8 to 14 on the advice of the elders and well wishers                                  have
             compromised as per the terms and conditions below:
</blockquote>

<blockquote id="blockquote_4">                    a)        The grant made by the land tribunal in favour of the
                              petitioner       husband          dated      25.06.1984              may          be
                              confirmed
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_5">                    b)        The       respondent       No.3     proposed           respondents No.8            to
                              13 and also on behalf of              their children           and             minor
                              children also agreed the same.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_6">                    c)        The petitioners         have      agreed     that    to    confirm       the     sale
                              made        by   her      in     favour      of   A. Shivakumar, S/o Adappa,
                              the respondent          No.14      and       the petitioner,      her      children
                              have no right over the land in question.
</blockquote><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                             5</span>




<blockquote id="blockquote_7">              3.        The petitioner          and the respondents agreed for the                 above said
                        terms and hence                the petition may kindly dispose off as per the
                        terms of this compromise petition.
</blockquote>

<blockquote id="blockquote_8">              4.        The petitioner and respondents will be put to great hardship and
                        injury, if this compromise petition is not allowed."
</blockquote>
<p id="p_3">        3.    In view      of    the      joint   memo filed, the grant made             in   favour    of    the

husband of    the       petitioner       by     order       dated    25.06.1984      by the Land Tribunal is

confirmed.         In     view       of respondent Nos. 8 to 13 and respondent No.3                      having

agreed for the compromise                 and      also for         further     transaction   that has taken

place    in favour of       the respondent No.14 since                    the impleading applicants          have

no objections, writ      petition is      disposed of in terms of                  the compromise petition.



</p><p id="p_4">        4.    The       petitioner has not sought for any claim                          against     respondent

Nos. 4, 5, 6 and 7. The writ           petition        is       dismissed      against   respondent     Nos. 4,

5, 6    and   7     and          their        rights        would    be       considered by the Land Tribunal

independently.
