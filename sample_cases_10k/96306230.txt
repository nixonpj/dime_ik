<div class="doc_title">Gaurav Alias Gaur Bauri Alias ... vs The State Of Jharkhand on 25 April, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">                     IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                               B. A. No. 742 of 2014

        Gaurav @ Gaur Bauri @ Gaurabh Bauri                ...... Petitioner
                                Versus
        The State of Jharkhand                             ...... Opposite Party
                               ---------
</pre><p id="p_1">        CORAM: HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE AMITAV K. GUPTA
</p><p id="p_2">                               ---------
</p><pre id="pre_2">        For the Petitioner     :       Mr. Nilesh Kumar, Advocate
        For the State          :       A.P.P.
        For the Informant      -       Mr. Sidhartha Roy, Advocate
                                       ---------

        06/Dated: 25th April, 2014
</pre><p id="p_3">               Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned counsel for the State.
               Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that the earlier bail application was
        rejected by this Bench in B.A. No. 9067 of 2013 vide order dated 25.11.2013 and now
        he has renewed his prayer on the fresh ground that petitioner has remained in custody
        since 9 months. Learned counsel also relied on a decision of the Apex Court and
        submitted that in similar circumstance the Apex Court had granted bail and the case of
        the present petitioner is at par. Learned counsel further submitted that as per the
        provision of Section 437 clause 6 of the Cr. P.C, if the trial is not concluded within
        sixty days from the date of the custody of the petitioner he shall be enlarged on bail
        which is the mandate of law.
</p><p id="p_4">               Learned counsel for the A.P.P assisted by the learned counsel for the
        Complainant has submitted that the decision relied on by the learned counsel for the
        petitioner is not applicable to the facts of the case as the same relates to the pretrial
        stage, whereas in the present case the trial is going on and the petitioner has been
        adopting a delay dallying tactics in not cross examining the witness produced in the
        Court and after imposing cost by the Court the witness was discharged.
</p><p id="p_5">               Having heard learned counsel for the petitioner and the complainant, it is
        evident that the trial is going and the delay, if any, has occurred due to non-
        examination of the witnesses by the petitioner as such at this stage, there is no fresh
        ground to enlarge the petitioner on bail. It is however made clear that if the trial is not
        concluded within three months from the date of this order and if there is no laches on
        the part of the petitioner, he is at liberty to renew his prayer for bail with the said
        direction the prayer for bail is hereby rejected.
</p>



<p id="p_6">                                                                  (Amitav K. Gupta, J.)
Amardeep/-
</p>
