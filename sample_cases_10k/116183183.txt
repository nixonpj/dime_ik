<div class="doc_title">95A/447/427/504/506/34 Of The ... vs In Re: Bapi @ Somenath Naskar on 3 December, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                      1

03.12.2019</pre>
<p id="p_1">Sl No.134
AP
                                               CRM 11355 of 2019

             In Re: An application for anticipatory bail under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> of the Code of
             Criminal Procedure filed on 02.12.2019 in connection with Sonarpur Police
             Station Case No.1591 of 2018 dated 20.09.2018 under <a href="/doc/76352360/" id="a_2">Sections
             195A</a>/<a href="/doc/162506/" id="a_3">447</a>/<a href="/doc/222396/" id="a_4">427</a>/<a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_5">504</a>/<a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_6">506</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_7">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code.

</p><p id="p_2">                                                     And

                   In Re: Bapi @ Somenath Naskar
                                                                                    ... ... Petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                   Mr. Sabyasachi Mukherjee,
                   Ms. Arunima Das Sharma
                                                                               ... for the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_4">
                   Mr. Iqbal Kabir
                                                                                 ... ... for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_5">
                   It is submitted on behalf of the petitioner that he has been falsely

             implicated in a series of cases at the behest of the de facto complainant.

</p><p id="p_6">                   Learned lawyer for the State opposes the prayer for anticipatory bail and

             submits that the petitioner is misusing his liberty and threatening witnesses and

             has caused mischief.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                   Having considered the materials on record and bearing in mind the nature

             of allegations, we are of the opinion though custodial interrogation of the

             petitioner may not be necessary, movement of the petitioner requires to be

             restricted in order to prevent commission of similar offences.

</p><p id="p_8">                   Accordingly, we direct that in the event of arrest, the petitioner be released

             on bail upon furnishing a bond of Rs.10,000/- (Rupees Ten Thousand Only),

             with two sureties of like amount each, to the satisfaction of the arresting officer

             and also be subject to the conditions as laid down under <a href="/doc/1144664/" id="a_8">Section 438(2)</a> of the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                          2</span>


Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 and on condition that the petitioner shall

appear before the court below and pray for regular bail within a period of four

weeks from date and on further condition that the petitioner shall not enter the

jurisdiction of Sonarpur Police Station until further orders except for the

purposes of investigating and/or for attending the court proceedings and shall

provide the address where the petitioner shall presently reside to the

Investigating Agency as well as the Court below and shall report to the Officer-in-

Charge of the concerned police station within whose jurisdiction the petitioner

shall presently reside once in a week until further orders.

</p><p id="p_9">      The application for anticipatory bail is, thus, disposed of.



</p><pre id="pre_2">      (Suvra Ghosh, J.)              (Joymalya Bagchi, J.)
