<pre id="pre_1">                    HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH
 1                  M.Cr.C. No.32689/2019
                   (Omveer Vs. State of M.P.)

Gwalior Bench:
Dated:04/09/2019
     Shri Jitendra Singh Kushwaha, learned counsel for the

applicant.

         Shri Ankit Saxena, learned Panel Lawyer for the respondent-</pre>

<p id="p_1">State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">         This is second bail application under Section 439 of Cr.P.C. on

behalf of the applicant, who is in custody since 05/01/2019 in

connection with Crime No.06/2019 registered at Police Station

Chanchoda, District-Guna for the offence punishable under Section

34(2) of Excise Act and Sections 379 and 420 of IPC and Section

41(1) and 102 of Cr.P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_3">         It is submitted by learned counsel for the applicant that today

status report has been filed by the SHO, Police Station, Chanchoda in

which he has referred the fact that investigation is going on in respect

of other co-accused Mukesh Sihar who happens to be the owner of the

vehicle and as soon as investigation is completed, action would be

taken against him. It is the submission of learned counsel for the

applicant that a false case has been registered against the applicant.

Charge-sheet has already been filed and confinement since

05/01/2019 amounts to pre trial detention. He undertakes to cooperate

in the trial and would make himself available as and when required.

He prayed for grant of bail.
</p>
<p id="p_4">         Learned Panel Lawyer for the respondent-state opposed the
                    HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> 2                  M.Cr.C. No.32689/2019</span>
                   (Omveer Vs. State of M.P.)

prayer and prayed for its dismissal.
</p>
<p id="p_5">         Heard the learned counsel for the parties and perused the case

diary.
</p>
<p id="p_6">         Considering the submissions made by learned counsel for the

parties as well as the fact that confinement amounts to pretrial

detention, without expressing any opinion on merits of the case, the

application filed under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of Cr.P.C. is allowed.          It is

directed that applicant shall be released on bail on furnishing a

personal bond in the sum of Rs.1,00,000/- (Rs. One Lac Only) with

one solvent surety of the like amount to the satisfaction of the trial

Court concerned for his regular appearance before the trial Court

concerned on the dates fixed by the Court during the trial.

</p><p id="p_7">         This order will remain operative subject to compliance of the

following conditions by the applicant:-
</p>
<p id="p_8">       1. The applicant will comply with all the terms and conditions
of the bond executed by him;
</p><p id="p_9">       2. The applicant will cooperate in the investigation/trial, as the
case may be;
</p><p id="p_10">       3. The applicant will not indulge himself in extending
inducement, threat or promise to any person acquainted with the facts
of the case so as to dissuade him from disclosing such facts to the
Court or to the Police Officer, as the case may be.
</p><p id="p_11">       4. The applicant will not commit an offence similar to the
offence of which he is accused;
</p><p id="p_12">       5. The applicant will not seek unnecessary adjournments during
the trial; and
</p><p id="p_13">       6. The applicant will not leave India without previous
permission of the trial Court/Investigating Officer, as the case may be.

</p><p id="p_14">         Investigating Officer is directed to ensure appropriate

investigation and consequential action in accordance with law against
                          HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">       3                 M.Cr.C. No.32689/2019</span>
                        (Omveer Vs. State of M.P.)

     co-accused Mukesh Sihar.
</p>
<p id="p_15">              A copy of this order be sent to the Court concerned for

     compliance.
</p>
