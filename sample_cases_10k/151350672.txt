<div class="doc_title">Vanga Rajitha vs Bank Of India on 18 February, 2019</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: V Ramasubramanian, Shameem Akther</div>

<pre id="pre_1">        THE HON'BLE SRI JUSTICE V. RAMASUBRAMANIAN
                             &amp;
          THE HON'BLE DR. JUSTICE SHAMEEM AKTHER

                WRIT PETITION No.43427 of 2018

ORDER: (Per Hon'ble Sri Justice V. Ramasubramanian)


       Aggrieved by a condition imposed by the DRT for the grant of

stay of an auction, the borrowers have come up with the above writ

petition.


2.     Heard Mr. P Nagender, learned counsel for the petitioners and

Smt. V. Dyumani, learned standing counsel for the respondents.</pre>


<p id="p_1">3.     As per the sale notice, the amount outstanding is stated to be

around Rs.42,00,000/-. When the authorized officer issued an auction

sale notice, the petitioners moved an appeal under Section 17 of the

Securitization and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and <a href="/doc/198257891/" id="a_1">Enforcement

of Security Interest Act</a>, 2002 (for short 'the Act') in SA.No.461 of

2018 before the DRT-II, Hyderabad and sought interim stay in

IA.No.5464 of 2018. The tribunal, by order dated 13.11.2018, granted

stay on condition that the petitioners deposited 15% of the amount

due within a week and another 15% within two weeks thereafter.


</p><p id="p_2">4.     Aggrieved by the said condition, the petitioners came up with

the above writ petition.
</p>
<p id="p_3">
</p><p id="p_4">5.     While ordering notice in the writ petition on 29.11.2018,

we directed the sale not to be confirmed. Subsequently, by order

dated 29.01.2019, we granted two weeks time to the petitioners to

deposit the first instalment of 15% of the amount due as directed by

the tribunal.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                     2</span><p id="p_5">



</p><p id="p_6">6.     Today, the learned counsel, on both sides, agreed that the said

order has been complied with. Therefore, the first portion of the order

passed by the tribunal, which is impugned in the above writ petition,

has now been complied with.
</p>
<p id="p_7">
</p><p id="p_8">7.     In such circumstances, the writ petition is disposed of granting

four weeks time to the petitioners, from the date of receipt of a copy

of this order, to pay another 15% of the amount due. Once another

15% of the amount due is paid by the petitioners, the stay granted by

the tribunal shall continue to be in force till the disposal of the appeal

by the tribunal.
</p>
<p id="p_9">
</p><p id="p_10">       Consequently,   pending    miscellaneous   applications,   if   any,

shall stand closed. There shall be no order as to costs.



</p><p id="p_11">                                              _____________________
                                              V. RAMASUBRAMANIAN, J



                                               ____________________
                                               Dr. SHAMEEM AKTHER, J
February 18, 2019
DSK
