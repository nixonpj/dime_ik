<div class="doc_title">Nagendra Puri @ Nagendra Kumar ... vs The State Of Bihar on 9 July, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                 IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA

                                 Criminal Miscellaneous No.37125 of 2018
                          Arising Out of PS.Case No. -71 Year- 2018 Thana -PIPRAKOTHI District-
                                             EASTCHAM PARAN(MOTIHARI)
                 ======================================================
                 1. Nagendra Puri @ Nagendra Kumar Puri, S/o Late Parsuram Puri,
                 2. Ravindra Puri S/o Late Parsuram Puri,
                 3. Vijay Puri @ Vinay Kumar Puri, S/o Late Chandrika Puri, All are R/o
                    Vill.- Kishanpur, P.S.- Pipra Kothi, District- East Champaran, Motihari.


                                                                                  .... ....   Petitioners
                                                        Versus
                 The State of Bihar
                                                               .... .... Opposite Party
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioners    : Mr. Vipin Kumar Singh, Advocate.
                 For the Opposite Party : Mr. Lalan Kumar, A.P.P.
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE SUDHIR SINGH
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">3   09-07-2018                  Heard learned counsel for the petitioners and learned

                 counsel for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                The petitioners are apprehending their arrest in a case

                 for the offence registered under Section 30(a) of the Bihar

                 Prohibition and <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_1">Excise Act</a>, 2016.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                The prosecution story, in brief, is that total 1319.76

                 liters wine is said to have been recovered.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                It has been submitted by learned counsel for the

                 petitioners that the petitioners have got no criminal antecedent.

                 There is no allegation of tampering with the witnesses alleged

                 against the petitioners. The petitioners have falsely been
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Patna High Court Cr.M isc. No.37125 of 2018 (3) dt.09-07-2018</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                            2/3</span>




             implicated in the present case. It is alleged that total 1319.76 liters

             wine is recovered from the Mango Orchard. The Mango Orchard

             does not belong to the petitioners. The name of the petitioners has

             come on the basis of secret information as per F.I.R. The source

             and genuineness of secret information has not been disclosed by

             the prosecution. Except for this, there is no other substantive

             evidence to suggest the implication of the petitioners in this case.

             Nothing incriminating has been recovered from the conscious

             possession of the petitioners. The petitioners had no knowledge

             regarding the alleged incident. There is no compliance of <a href="/doc/1137396/" id="a_2">Section

             100</a> Cr. P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                On behalf of the State, it is submitted that the

             petitioners are named in the F.I.R/ Complaint Case.

</p><p id="p_6">                            Considering the aforesaid facts and circumstances, let

             the petitioners above named, in the event of arrest or surrender

             before the learned court below within a period of six weeks from

             today, be released on anticipatory bail on furnishing bail bonds of

             Rs.10,000/- (Ten Thousand) each with two sureties of the like

             amount each to the satisfaction of the learned Additional District

             and Sessions Judge, IVth-cum-Special Judge, Excise, East

             Champaran, Motihari, in connection with Pipra Kothi P.S. Case

             No. 71 of 2018, subject to the conditions as laid down under
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">          Patna High Court Cr.M isc. No.37125 of 2018 (3) dt.09-07-2018</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                                                     3/3</span>




                      <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_3">Section 438(2)</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure.




</p><pre id="pre_2">U.K./-                                                 (Sudhir Singh, J)
  U         T
