<div class="doc_title">Hoda Sk vs Unknown on 22 April, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                                   1




22.04.2016</pre>
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">129</span>
<p id="p_1">pk
                        CRA No. 321 of 2015

In Re:- An application for bail under <a href="/doc/985477/" id="a_1">Section 389</a> of the
Code of Criminal Procedure being CRAN No. 1840 of 2015
filed on 16.06.15 in connection with ST No.05(June/2014).
</p><p id="p_2">                       And
In the matter of:- Hoda Sk.</p><pre id="pre_2">
                                       Appellant
Mr. Sekhar Basu,
Mr. Tapas Kumar Ghosh,
Mrs. Somsubhra Ganguly         For the appellant


Mr. Neguive Ahmed              for the State


</pre><p id="p_3">      Heard the Learned Counsel appearing on behalf of the
appellant as well as the learned Counsel appearing on
behalf of the State. Perused the impugned judgement and
certified copy of the depositions of the witnesses.
</p>

<p id="p_4">      The   appellant     having       convicted   under   <a href="/doc/1926477/" id="a_2">Sections
489B</a>/<a href="/doc/257920/" id="a_3">489C</a> of the Indian Penal Code and having sentenced
to suffer rigorous imprisonment for ten years and seven
years respectively and to pay fine with default clause
preferred an appeal before this court after the appeal
being admitted, now he has approached this court for
suspension of sentence.
</p>

<p id="p_5">      Going through the impugned judgement we find this is
a case where from the possession of the appellant and
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                         2</span>




another who was later found to be juvenile, a total 400
pieces of fake Indian Currency note each of denomination
of   Rs.     1000/-    and    197    pieces   of    similar    fake    Indian
Currency note each of denomination of Rs. 500/- were
recovered in present of independent witnesses, while the
same were being trafficked.
</p>

<p id="p_6">        Having regard to the facts as above and considering
the findings on which the order of conviction is based,
in our opinion, this is not a fit case for suspension of
sentence.
</p>

<p id="p_7">     Accordingly,           the     application     for    suspension         of
sentence stands rejected.
</p>

<p id="p_8">        So   far     as   the     punishment       imposed     against       the
conviction      of    the    appellant      under    <a href="/doc/1926477/" id="a_4">Section         489B</a>    IPC
considering the huge quantity of counterfeit                         currency
note was recovered and that too from a place which is
near to the Indo- Bangladesh Border and certainly the
same being theft to the national economy of the country.
</p><p id="p_9">        In our opinion, the sentence imposed is not at all
adequate.
</p>

<p id="p_10">        We, therefore, in the light of the ratio laid down
by the Hon'ble Supreme Court in the case of Jaspal Singh,
Deputy Superintendent of Police -vs-                      State of Pubjan
reported in (2012) 1 SCC (Cri) 1 and in exercise of our
power      conferred      under     <a href="/doc/1736516/" id="a_5">Section   386(e)</a>      of   the    Code    of
Criminal Procedure directed the notice be issued upon the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                               3</span>




appellant to show cause why sentence imposed upon him
should not be enhanced.
</p>

<p id="p_11">    Office   is   directed   to   issue    notice   upon    the
appellant through the Learned Chief Judicial Magistrate,
Murshidabad and the same be made returnable four weeks
hence and the appeal shall be heard on the question of
sentence at the time of final hearing of the appeal.
</p>

<p id="p_12">    We find from the records that preparation of paper
book is complete and appeal is made ready for hearing.
</p>

<p id="p_13">    Office is directed to place the matter before the
appropriate Bench.
</p>

<p id="p_14">                                          (Ashim Kumar Roy, J.)



                                          (C. S. Karnan, J.)
