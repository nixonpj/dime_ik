<div class="doc_title">Rakesh Kumar Mishra vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh Thr on 12 August, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                    WP 4295/2015 (PIL)
          ( Rakesh Kumar Mishra Vs. State of MP)
12/08/2015
      Shri Abhishek Parashar, learned counsel for the
petitioner.
      Shrimati Sangita Pachori, learned GA for the
respondents 1,4 and 5.</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Shri     JD   Suryavanshi,      learned     counsel   for
respondents 2 and 6.
</p><p id="p_2">      Shri    Sushil   Chaturvedi,    learned     counsel   for
respondent 3.
</p><p id="p_3">      Presence of respondent No.7 is not required at this
stage.
</p><p id="p_4">      Considering      the   prayer    of   the   counsel   for
respondents - Lokayukt as well as EOW, petitioner's
counsel is directed to supply them copy of the petition
along with its annexures during the course of the day
enabling them to go through the same.
</p><p id="p_5">      Heard on the question of admission.
</p><p id="p_6">      Petitioner by stating himself to be a social worker
of Guna has filed this Public Interest Litigation petition
under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the Constitution of India for
issuance of appropriate writ against the authorities of
the respondents for the following reliefs :
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">         1.     That, the respondents may kindly be
         directed to hold an enquiry into the
         allegations of corruption levelled by the
         petitioner in relation to the works done by
                           2                  WP 4295/2015 (PIL)

        the respondent No.7.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_2">        2.     That, the respondents may further be
        directed to register the criminal case against
        the real culprits who may be found indulged
        into such corrupt practices on the basis of
        outcome of the aforesaid enquiry and they
        may further be directed to recover the money
        illegally spent by the respondent No.7 and
        personal accountability of the concerned office
        bearers may also be fixed.</blockquote>

<p id="p_7">        Having heard counsel, keeping in view the arguments,
after   perusing   the   annexed   papers,    in   the   available
circumstances, instead of admitting or issuing any notice to
the other side, so also without expressing any opinion on
the merits of the matter, this petition is disposed of with a
direction to the Lokayukt Organisation, State of MP, through
its Secretary, Bhopal to consider and decide the pending
representation of the petitioner ( Annexure P/3) filed in the
office of such authority with respect to the dispute raised in
this petition, keeping in view the concerning provisions,
rules, regulations so also in accordance with the procedure
prescribed under the law, within sixty days from the date of
filing certified copy of this order along with the petition and
annexures in the office of such authority, under intimation to
the petitioner. On such consideration, if any of the authority
or person is found to be involved in the alleged corruption,
                                3               WP 4295/2015 (PIL)

      then such authority ( Lokayukt Organisation ) shall proceed
      against them in accordance with the procedure prescribed
      under the law.
</p><p id="p_8">           Petitioner is directed through his counsel to submit
      aforesaid papers in the office of the authority of respondents
      Lokayukt Organisation within fifteen days from today.
</p><p id="p_9">           There shall be no order as to costs.
</p><p id="p_10">           Certified copy.
</p>

<pre id="pre_2">      (U.C.Maheshwari)                        (Sheel Nagu)
          Judge                                 Judge
