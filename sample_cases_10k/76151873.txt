                              *****
      These Writ Petitions filed under Articles 226 and 227 of
the Constitution of India praying to quash both the preliminary
and final notifications, preliminary notification dated 21.5.2008
and also final notification dated 18.2.2010 issued by the
respondent no.1 as per Annexure- P and R in so far as the lands
of these petitioners viz., Sy.No.14 and 15, Beemanakuppe
Dakale, Bettanapalya Village, Kengeri Hobli, Bangalore South
Taluk.


       These Writ Petitions coming on for Preliminary Hearing
in 'B' Group this day, the court made the following:

                           ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">      The Bangalore Development Authority has filed a memo

which reads as follows:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">                                "MEMO
                   The Bangalore Development Authority
            in its Board meeting dated 18.12.2010 vide
            No.440/2010 has resolved to give up the
            revenue sites situated in 'Nadaprabhu Kempe
            Gowda Layout' in favour of the respective
            owners who have purchased the same prior to
            preliminary notification and thereafter resolved
            to request the Government to take necessary
            action under <a href="/doc/460525/" id="a_1">Section 48</a> of the Land Acquisition
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                          6</span>




Act. In pursuance of the said resolution, the
Bangalore         Development      Authority    has
requested the Government of Karnataka through
a        letter       dated     17.8.2011       vide
No.BDA/ALAO/1580/2011-12 and thereafter
through a reminder dated 3.8.2013 to withdraw
from the acquisition of revenue sites.</blockquote>


<p id="p_2">         In the instant case, the petitioners No.1
to 5 have produced copies of the sale deeds to
show that they have purchased the revenue sites
prior     to      preliminary   notification   dated
21.5.2008. Therefore, in this regard necessary
order may be passed by this honourable Court
subject to production of original documents
before         this    respondent/Authority      for
verifications.
</p>

<p id="p_3">         The petitioners No.6 to 9 and 11 have
not produced any documents of title deeds to
show that they are the owners of revenue sites
and hence they may be directed to produce
documents, if any, for consideration and liberty
may be given to this respondent/Authority to
pass necessary orders.
</p>

<p id="p_4">         The Petitioner No.10 has produced the
sale deed pertaining to one Smt. R. Yashoda and
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                      7</span>




                claiming that he is her son.           If necessary
                documents     are    produced      before      this
                respondent/Authority,    his    case     may    be
                considered in accordance with law.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                       Therefore, the matter could be disposed
                of with such observations subject to decision of
                the Government."
</p>

<p id="p_6">      In view of the above, it is for the State Government to

take appropriate action pursuant to the recommendation, which

it is hoped, would be done expeditiously.                 In any event, the

State Government shall respond and pass appropriate orders

within three months from the date of receipt of a copy of this

order and the same shall be communicated to the petitioners

individually.
</p>
