                                J U D G M E N T

<p id="p_1">1.     The brief facts as necessary for the adjudication are that on 22.06.2005 

ASI Arvind and Ct. Mohan Singh were on patrolling duty and met Ct. Umesh 

who produce the accused along with a recovered car stereo.   Statement of 

complainant Jafruddin was recorded.  On the basis of his statement the present 

FIR was registered.  After completion of investigation charge sheet was filed 

in the court for trial. 
</p>
<p id="p_2">2.     On 04.07.2005 production warrants were issued against the accused as 

he  was  in  J/C.   Vide   order  dated  04.05.2006   charge  u/s   379/411   <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_4">IPC</a>   was 

framed against the accused to which he pleaded not guilty and claimed trial. 
</p><p id="p_3"> Page no. 3 of 4                                                      FIR No. 278/2005

</p><p id="p_4">3.    In order to prove its case prosecution examined as many as 4 witnesses. 

PW1   is   the   complainant   who   proved   on   record   his   complaint   which   is 

Ex.PW1/A.     Seizure   memo   of   the   car   stereo   which   is   Ex.PW1/B   and   the 

accused was arrested in his presence vide arrest and personal search memo 

Ex.PW1/D&amp;E.   PW2 is the Duty Officer who proved on record the present 

FIR which is Ex.PW2/A.  PW3 is HC Mukesh Kumar who stated that he was 

patrolling duty on that day and while patrolling he met ASI Arvind and Ct. 

Mohan   Singh   and   handed   over   the   accused   along   with   a   recovered   case 

property i.e. stereo. IO recorded the statement of complainant and prepared 

rukka and handed over the same to Ct. Mohan Singh.  He prepared site plan 

which is Ex.PW1/C.   PW4 is Ct. Mohan Singh who was with the IO. 

</p><p id="p_5">4.    Thereafter prosecution evidence was closed on  08.11.2013 as sufficient 

opportunities were granted to the prosecution.  Statement of accused  u/s 313 

<a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_5">Cr.PC</a> was recorded on 03.12.2013.   
</p>
<p id="p_6">5.    File perused.  From the evidence available on record accused had chosen 
 Page no. 4 of 4                                                          FIR No. 278/2005

not engage any counsel and none of the witness were cross­examined by him. 

In   view   of   unrebutted   testimony   of   the   witnesses   accused   is   liable   to   be 

convicted as there is nothing which could discredit the testimony of any of the 

witnesses.  PW1 has categorically stated that while sleeping he heard noise as 

a result he woke up and he saw the accused was taking away the stereo of his 

car and apprehended him at the spot.   Police came at the spot and he handed 

over the accused and the recovered case property to the police.  His testimony 

is corroborated with the testimony of two police witness.  There is no reason to 

disbelieve the testimony of these three witnesses.   In view of the unrebutted 

and   unchallenged   testimony   of   the   witnesses   accused   is   convicted   for   the 

offence u/s 379 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_6">IPC</a>. 
</p>
<p id="p_7">6.     Put up for arguments on sentence today itself at 12 o'clock.




</p><pre id="pre_2">ANNOUNCED IN THE OPEN                           ANKUR JAIN
