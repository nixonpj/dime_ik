<div class="doc_title">Mohnish Ahluwalia vs Nirmal Singh on 19 October, 2012</div><pre id="pre_1">Crl. Misc. No. M-33642 of 2012 (O&amp;M)                                -1-

   In the High Court of Punjab and Haryana at Chandigarh

                         Crl. Misc. No. M-33642 of 2012 (O&amp;M)
                         Date of Decision: 19.10.2012.

Mohnish Ahluwalia                                    .......Petitioner

                                   Versus

Nirmal Singh                                       .......Respondent


CORAM: HON'BLE MRS. JUSTICE SABINA

Present:    Mr. Sandeep Arora, Advocate
            for the petitioner.
                  ****

SABINA, J.
</pre>
<p id="p_1">            Petitioner has filed this petition under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> of

the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 seeking quashing of the

criminal complaint case No. 548/2 dated 20.7.2004 titled as

'Nirmal Singh Vs. Ram Saran Dass Ahluwalia and others' under

<a href="/doc/1823824/" id="a_2">Section 138</a> of the Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 (Annexure P-1)

and all subsequent proceedings arising therefrom including the

summoning order dated 21.1.2006 (Annexure P-2) and order dated

3.1.2011 (Annexure P-3).
</p>
<p id="p_2">            Learned counsel for the petitioner has submitted that

now with the intervention of relatives and friends, parties have

arrived at a compromise.         FIR No. 234 dated 12.12.2003 under

<a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_3">Section 406</a>, <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_4">420</a>, <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_5">120-B</a> of the Indian Penal Code, registered at

Police Station Division No.1, Pathankot has since been quashed by

this Court on the basis of compromise in Criminal Miscellaneous

No. M-23087 of 2012. The petitioners may be permitted to

withdraw this petition to enable her to move an appropriate

application for compounding the offence before the trial court.

</p><p id="p_3">            Dismissed as withdrawn.
</p><p id="p_4"> Crl. Misc. No. M-33642 of 2012 (O&amp;M)                         -2-

</p><p id="p_5">            However, in case the petitioner surrenders before the

trial court within two weeks from today and moves an application

for regular bail, the same be disposed of on the same day, in

accordance with law.
</p>
<p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">                                           (SABINA)
                                            JUDGE
October 19, 2012
Gurpreet
 </p>
