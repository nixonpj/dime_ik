<div class="doc_title">Commissioner Of Income-Tax vs N.G.E.F. Ltd. (No. 2) on 9 January, 1991</div><div class="doc_citations">Equivalent citations: 1992 195 ITR 360 KAR, 1992 195 ITR 360 Karn</div>

<div class="doc_author">Author: K S Bhat</div>

<p id="p_1">JUDGMENT 
 

K. Shivashankar Bhat,  J.  
</p> 

<p id="p_2">1.  The following two questions are referred for our answer under the provision of the <a href="/doc/789969/" id="a_1">Income-tax Act</a>, 1961 :  
</p>  

<p id="p_3"> "1. Whether, on the facts and in the circumstances of the case, the Appellate Tribunal is right in law in allowing depreciation on roads and culverts ?  
</p> 

<p id="p_4">2. Whether, on the facts and in the circumstances of the case, the Appellate Tribunal is right in law in upholding the order of the Commissioner of Income-tax (Appeals) who directed the Income-tax Officer to treat the technical know-how payment as a revenue expenditure ?"   
</p> 

<p id="p_5">3. The first question is covered, against the Revenue, in the decision of this court in <a href="/doc/739996/" id="a_2">CIT v. Bangalore Turf Club Ltd</a>. [1984] 150 ITR 23. The second question is covered, also against the Revenue, in the decision of this court reported in <a href="/doc/94072/" id="a_3">Mysore Kirloskar Ltd. v. CIT</a> [1978] 114 ITR 443 [FB]. Consequently, both the questions are answered in the affirmative and against the Revenue.  
</p>
