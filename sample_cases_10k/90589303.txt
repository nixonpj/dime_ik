<div class="doc_title">Shambu Bhusan Singh vs The State Of Bihar on 16 October, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                              CRIMINAL MISCELLANEOUS No.62551 of 2019
                      Arising Out of PS. Case No.-144 Year-2019 Thana- PARBATTA District- Khagaria
                 ======================================================
           1.     SHAMBU BHUSAN SINGH S/o Late Chakrapani Singh Resident of
                  Agmanipuari Tola, P.S.- Parbatta, District- Khagaria
           2.    Om Prakash Singh @ Gore Singh S/o Late Ram Naresh Singh Resident of
                 Agmanipuari Tola, P.S.- Parbatta, District- Khagaria

                                                                                   ... ... Petitioner/s
                                                        Versus
                 THE STATE OF BIHAR

                                                        ... ... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :        Mr.Manish Kumar No2
                 For the State            :        Mr.Amit Kumar Rakesh, APP
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE VIKASH JAIN
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   16-10-2019                     Learned counsel for the petitioners Mr. Manish

                   Kumar No.2 states that the petitioner no.2 has already been

                   arrested and seeks permission to withdraw the anticipatory bail

                   petition as against him, as the same has become infructous.

</p><p id="p_2">                                 2. Permission is accorded. Anticipatory bail petition

                   as against petitioner no.2 is permitted to be withdrawn and is

                   dismissed as such.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                 3. Heard learned counsel for the petitioner no.1 and

                   learned APP for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                 4. The petitioner no.1 apprehends his arrest for the

                   offences alleged under <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_1">Sections 341</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_2">323</a>, <a href="/doc/1944660/" id="a_3">384</a>, <a href="/doc/1183069/" id="a_4">386</a>, <a href="/doc/1552733/" id="a_5">435</a>, <a href="/doc/222396/" id="a_6">427</a>,

                   <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_7">504</a> and <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_8">506</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_9">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code, registered in
 Patna High Court CR. MISC. No.62551 of 2019(2) dt.16-10-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                            2/3</span>




          connection with Parbatta P.S.Case No. 144 of 2019.

</p><p id="p_5">                       5. It is submitted that petitioner no.1 has been falsely

          implicated in the backdrop of admitted land dispute, in respect

          of which petitioner no.1 had filed a complaint petition before

          the Officer-in-charge of Parbatta Police Station. A complaint

          petition was also filed on 13.03.2018 before the S.D.M., stating

          that the informant and his family members were threatening to

          capture the property. The property in question comprising 26

          bigha of land was the subject matter of a proceeding under

          <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_10">Section 144</a> Cr. P.C. in Case No.627(M) of 2019, which was

          heard and concluded by the S.D.O., vide order dated

          22.07.2019, observing that there is title dispute between the

          parties. It is, therefore, submitted that the present FIR has been

          lodged merely to put pressure upon petitioner no.1, who claims

          clean antecedents.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                       6. Be that as it may, in the event of arrest of petitioner

          no.1 or surrender before court below within six weeks from the

          date of communication of this order, let petitioner no.1 be

          released on bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs. 10,000/-(ten

          thousand) with two sureties of the like amount each to the

          satisfaction of learned Additional Chief Judicial Magistrate, 1 st,

          Khagaria in connection with Parbatta P.S.Case No. 144 of 2019,
            Patna High Court CR. MISC. No.62551 of 2019(2) dt.16-10-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                       3/3</span>




                     subject to the conditions as laid down under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_11">Section 438(2)</a> Cr.

                     P.C. and also subject to the following further conditions:

</p><p id="p_7">                                  (i) That one of the bailors shall be a close relative of

                     the petitioner no.1.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                                  (ii) That the petitioner no.1 shall not indulge in any

                     similar offence till conclusion of the trial.

</p><p id="p_9">                                  (iii) That the petitioner no.1 shall co-operate with the

                     investigation, if not already concluded, and make himself

                     available as and when so required and in case of failure, the

                     State shall be at liberty to move for cancellation of bail.

</p><p id="p_10">                                  (iv) That the petitioner no.1 shall remain physically

                     present in Court on each and every date during trial and in the

                     event of failure on two consecutive dates without sufficient

                     reason, his bail bond shall be liable to be cancelled by the

                     learned Court concerned.
</p>
<p id="p_11">

</p><p id="p_12">                                                  (Vikash Jain, J)
HR/-
</p>
