<div class="doc_title">Loona Ram vs State Of Punjab on 27 March, 2009</div><pre id="pre_1">Criminal Misc. No. M-6896 of 2009                                    1




     In the High Court of Punjab and Haryana, at Chandigarh.


                  Criminal Misc. No. M-6896 of 2009

                     Date of Decision: 27.3.2009


Loona Ram
                                                            ...Petitioner
                                Versus
State of Punjab
                                                         ...Respondent


CORAM: HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE KANWALJIT SINGH AHLUWALIA.


Present: Mr. Amrik Singh Kalra, Advocate
         for the petitioner.

         Mr. Anter Singh Brar, Deputy Advocate
         General, Punjab, for the State.

         Mr. Lajwant Singh Virk, Advocate
         for the complainant.


Kanwaljit Singh Ahluwalia, J. (Oral)
</pre>
<p id="p_1">         The present petition has been filed under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> Cr.P.C.

for grant of pre-arrest bail to the petitioner in case FIR No. 427 dated

16.12.2008 registered at Police Station Abohar, District Ferozepur,

under <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_2">Sections 420</a>, <a href="/doc/1317063/" id="a_3">465</a>, <a href="/doc/1985627/" id="a_4">467</a>, <a href="/doc/556166/" id="a_5">468</a>, <a href="/doc/1466184/" id="a_6">471</a> &amp; <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_7">120-B</a> IPC.

</p><p id="p_2">         On 10.3.2009, this Court had passed the following order:-

</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">                             "Counsel for the petitioner has submitted

                   that he had purchased the combine vide agreement

                   (Annexure P1). It is submitted that Rs.4,00,000/-

                   were to be paid by the petitioner to the complainant.

                   Rs.2,50,000/- were paid and Rs.1,50,000/- were the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Criminal Misc. No. M-6896 of 2009                                          2</span>




                     balance. Allegation against the petitioner is that the

                     petitioner     had   obtained    duplicate    registration

                     certificate of the combine by forging the signatures

                     of the complainant. Counsel for the petitioner further

                     stated that it is to be proved during the course of

                     trial. Further stated that offence, if any, is based on

                     the   documents and custodial interrogation is not

                     necessary.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_2">                                  Issue notice of motion to learned Advocate

                     General, Punjab, for 27.3.2009.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_3">                                  Meanwhile,   in    the   event   of   arrest,

                     petitioner shall be released on interim bail to the

                     satisfaction of the Arresting Officer. Petitioner shall

                     join investigation as and when called for. Petitioner

                     shall also abide by the conditions specified under

                     <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_8">Section 438(2)</a> Cr.P.C."
</blockquote>
<p id="p_3">          Counsel for the State, on instructions from Amar Nath, Sub

Inspector, submits that petitioner has joined investigation and has given

his specimen handwriting in the Court on the asking of the Investigating

Officer. Mr. Brar further submits that as per his instructions, custodial

interrogation of the petitioner is not required.

</p><p id="p_4">          At this stage, Mr. Lajwant Singh Virk, Advocate, appearing for

the complainant, submits that duplicate Registration Certificate of the

Combine has not been recovered.
</p>
<p id="p_5">          Non-recovery of the duplicate Registration Certificate can be

explained by the accused at the time of trial. If it is not explained, it will
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2"> Criminal Misc. No. M-6896 of 2009                                        3</span>




be open for the Court to draw adverse inference.

</p><p id="p_6">          In view of the statement made by counsel for the State and

order dated 10.3.2009 passed by this Court, interim pre-arrest bail

granted to the petitioner is affirmed till filing of the report under <a href="/doc/1412034/" id="a_9">Section

173</a> Cr.P.C. On submission of report under <a href="/doc/1412034/" id="a_10">Section 173</a> Cr.P.C.

petitioner shall furnish regular bail-bonds to the Court concerned.

</p><p id="p_7">          With these observations, present petition is disposed off.


</p><p id="p_8">                                             (Kanwaljit Singh Ahluwalia)
                                                                  Judge
March 27, 2009
"DK"
</p>
