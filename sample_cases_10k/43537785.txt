<div class="doc_title">Pramod vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 6 March, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">              THE HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH
                             1
                        MCRC No. 7344/2019
                         ( PRAMOD VS THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)


Gwalior, Dated : 06/03/2019
       Shri Anshu Gupta, learned counsel for the applicant.

       Shri   Purushottam Pandey, learned Public Prosecutor for the

respondent-State.</pre>

<p id="p_1">       Case Diary is perused.
</p>
<p id="p_2">       Learned counsel for the rival parties are heard.

       The applicant has filed this first application under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">section 439</a> of

the Cr.P.C. for grant of bail.
</p>
<p id="p_3">       The applicant has been arrested by Police Station Shamshabad

District Vidisha (M.P.) in connection with Crime No.44/2019 registered

in relation to the offence punishable under section 34(2) of the M.P.

Excise Act.
</p>
<p id="p_4">       Allegations against the applicant, in short, are that 135 quarter of

country liquor was recovered from possession of             the applicant. On

the aforesaid basis, crime has been registered against the applicant.

</p><p id="p_5">       Learned counsel for the applicant submits that the applicant has

been falsely implicated in the matter. He is in custody since 03/02/2019.

Conclusion of trial is likely to take time. The applicant is a permanent

resident of Daamkeda Tehsil Huzur District Bhopal (M.P.) and there is
               THE HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                             2</span>
                        MCRC No. 7344/2019
                            ( PRAMOD VS THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)


no likelihood of his absconsion if released on bail. With the aforesaid

submissions, prayer for grant of bail is made.

</p><p id="p_6">       Learned Public Prosecutor has opposed the application and

prayed for its rejection.
</p>
<p id="p_7">       Considering the fact that prolonged pre-trial detention is an

anathema to the concept of liberty, this Court is inclined to extend the

benefit of bail to the applicant.
</p>
<p id="p_8">       Accordingly, without expressing any opinion on merits of the

case, this application is allowed and it is directed that the applicant

namely Pramod be released on bail on furnishing a personal bond in

the sum of Rs. 1,00,000/- (Rupees One Lac only) with two solvent and

local sureties of Rs.50,000/- (Rupees fifty thousand Only) each to the

satisfaction of the concerned trial Court/Committal Court for his

appearance during trial on the dates fixed by the concerned Court.

</p><p id="p_9">       This order will remain operative subject to compliance of the

following conditions by the applicant :-

</p><p id="p_10">1. The applicant will comply with all the terms and conditions of the
bond executed by him;
</p><p id="p_11">2. The applicant will cooperate in the investigation/trial, as the case may
be;
</p><p id="p_12">3. The applicant will not indulge himself in extending inducement, threat
or promise to any person acquainted with the facts of the case so as to
                           THE HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                         3</span>
                                    MCRC No. 7344/2019
                                  ( PRAMOD VS THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)


         dissuade him/her from disclosing such facts to the Court or to the Police
         Officer, as the case may be;
</p><p id="p_13">         4. The applicant shall not commit an offence similar to the offence of
         which he is accused;
</p><p id="p_14">         5. The applicant will not seek unnecessary adjournments during the trial;
         and
</p><p id="p_15">         6.      The applicant will not leave India without previous permission of
         the trial Court/Investigating Officer, asthe case may be.
</p><p id="p_16">               A copy of this order be sent to the Court concerned for
         compliance.
</p><p id="p_17">                 C.c. as per rules.
