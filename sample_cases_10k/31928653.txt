<p id="p_1">RAM CHAND GUPTA, J.(Oral)

Crl.M.No.50045 of 2012

             Application is allowed subject to all just exceptions.

Crl.M.No.M-25961 of 2012

             The present petition filed under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> Cr.P.C. is for grant

of anticipatory bail to the petitioner in case FIR No.267, dated 18.8.2011,

under <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_2">Sections 307</a>, <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_3">120-B</a>, <a href="/doc/386021/" id="a_4">201</a>, <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_5">34</a> IPC and under <a href="/doc/73862/" id="a_6">Section 25</a> of the Arms

Act, registered at Police Station Naraingarh, District Ambala.

</p><p id="p_2">             I have heard learned counsel for the petitioner and have gone

through the whole record carefully, including the impugned order passed by

learned Additional Sessions Judge, Ambala, vide which application filed on

behalf of the present petitioner for anticipatory bail was dismissed.

</p><p id="p_3">             Briefly stated, petitioner was on bail in this case and, however,

he absented from the Court and jumped bail and hence, his bail bonds were

cancelled.
</p>
<p id="p_4">             It has been contended by learned counsel for the petitioner-
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Crl.M.No.M-25961 of 2012(O&amp;M)                                          -2-</span>

accused that he had done mistake in noting down the date of hearing as he

noted down the date of hearing as 26.3.2012 instead of 6.3.2012 and that

challan has already been filed on 6.3.2012.
</p>
<p id="p_5">             However, even if that is so, petitioner should have appeared

before learned trial Court on 26.3.2012 and should have explained reasons

for his absence. Moreover, he did not approach the Court of Sessions

immediately thereafter and even anticipatory bail          before the Court of

Sessions was filed on 2.5.2012, which was dismissed on 7.5.2012. He again

waited for about more than three months to approach this Court and due to

this conduct of petitioner, trial could not proceed.

</p><p id="p_6">             Hence, in view of these facts, it is not such a case in which

extraordinary relief of anticipatory bail should be granted to the petitioner-

accused.
</p>
<p id="p_7">             Without expressing any opinion on the merits of the case, the

present petition filed by petitioner-Mohit for grant of anticipatory bail is,

hereby, dismissed being devoid of any merit.
</p>
<p id="p_8">
</p><pre id="pre_2">27.8.2012                                              (Ram Chand Gupta)
meenu                                                       Judge
