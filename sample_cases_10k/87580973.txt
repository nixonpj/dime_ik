THE HONOURABLE Mr.JUSTICE P.N.PRAKASH

1.T.Pachiyappan
2.P.Jeya	                                                                            ...   Petitioners
Vs
1.The Superintendent of Police,
Thiruvannamalai District.

2.The Inspector of Police,
Desur Police Station,
Vandavasi Taluk,
Thiruvannamalai District.			...   Respondents
	Criminal Original Petition filed under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> of Cr.P.C., praying to direct the respondents to register the petitioners complaint dated 07.04.2016, as against the accused referred above, to investigate the same.
	                For Petitioners      :  Mr.P.Karl Marx
 For Respondents  :  Mr.C.Emalias, APP

   O R D E R
</pre><p id="p_1">	This petition has been filed to direct the respondents to register the petitioners' complaint dated 07.04.2016.
</p>
<p id="p_2">	2. In State of Punjab Vs. Davinder Pal Singh Bhullar and others [(2011) 14 SCC 770], the Supreme Court has held as under:
</p><p id="p_3">	63. Application under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_2">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C. lies before the High Court against an order passed by the court subordinate to it in a pending case/proceedings. Generally, such powers are used for quashing criminal proceedings in appropriate cases. Such an application does not lie to initiate criminal proceedings or set the criminal law in motion. Inherent jurisdiction can be exercised if the order of the Subordinate Court results in the abuse of the "process" of the court and/or calls for interference to secure the ends of justice. The use of word process implies that the proceedings are pending before  the  Subordinate Court.  When reference is made to 
P.N.PRAKASH, J.
</p><p id="p_4">gya
the  phrase "to  secure  the ends of justice", it is in fact in relation to 
the order passed by the Subordinate Court and it cannot be understood in a general connotation of the phrase. More so, while entertaining such application the proceedings should be pending in the Subordinate Court. In case it attained finality, the inherent powers cannot be exercised. The party aggrieved may approach the appellate/revisional forum. Inherent jurisdiction can be exercised if injustice is done to a party, e.g., a clear mandatory provision of law is overlooked or where different accused in the same case are being treated differently by the Subordinate Court. (emphasis supplied).
</p><p id="p_5">	64. An inherent power is not an omnibus for opening a Pandora's box, that too for issues that are foreign to the main context. The invoking of the power has to be for a purpose that is connected to a proceeding and not for sprouting an altogether new issue. A power cannot exceed its own authority beyond its own creation. . . . . .

</p><p id="p_6">	3. Following the said judgment, this Court dismissed Crl.O.P. Nos.19197, 19198, 19343 and 19359 to 19363 of 2016 by a detailed order dated 27.09.2016.
</p>
<p id="p_7">	4. In view of the above, this Criminal Original Petition is disposed of with liberty to the petitioner to follow the procedure laid down in the order dated 27.09.2016 passed by this Court in Crl.O.P. Nos. 19197, 19198, 19343 and 19359 to 19363 of 2016 and file a fresh petition, if necessary.
</p>
<p id="p_8">28.09.2016
gya
To
</p><p id="p_9">1.The Superintendent of Police,
Thiruvannamalai District.
</p><p id="p_10">2.The Inspector of Police,
Desur Police Station, Vandavasi Taluk,
Thiruvannamalai District.</p><pre id="pre_2">
