<div class="doc_title">Alka Devi vs The State Of Bihar on 4 October, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                 Criminal Miscellaneous No.47283 of 2017
                           Arising Out of PS.Case No. -51 Year- 2017 Thana -HARSIDHI District-
                                            EASTCHAMPARAN(MOTIHARI)
                 ======================================================
                 Alka Devi W/o Phulena Sahni, R/o Village- Raneeta Kathaiya, P.S.-
                 Haraidhi, District- East Champaran.
                                                                       .... .... Petitioner
                                                   Versus
                 The State of Bihar
                                                                  .... .... Opposite Party
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s      :  Mr. Raj Bansh Dubey
                 For the Opposite Party/s: Mr. Tapeshwar Sharma
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE RAJENDRA KUMAR
                 MISHRA
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   04-10-2017                 Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and the learned

                 A.P.P. for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                               The petitioner apprehends her arrest in connection with

                 Harsidhi P.S. Case no. 51 of 2017, registered under <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_1">Sections 341</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_2">323</a>,

                 <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_3">324</a>, <a href="/doc/1133601/" id="a_4">325</a> and <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_5">307</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_6">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                               The accusation is that while informant Harinandan Sahai

                 was returning to his house from the Police Station after giving

                 application regarding committing Marpit by Fulena Sahani and others

                 then in the way this petitioner caught hold him from the back and

                 Nilam Devi caused injury at his head through Farsa in which he

                 sustained injury.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                               Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that, in fact,

                 occurrence of Marpit took place due to land dispute in which Vikas

                 Sahni, son of the petitioner, sustained injury regarding which Harsidhi
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">            Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.47283 of 2017 (2) dt.04-10-2017</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                      2/2</span>




                        P.S. Case No. 41 of 2017, under <a href="/doc/1258372/" id="a_7">Sections 147</a>, <a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_8">149</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_9">323</a>, <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_10">324</a>, <a href="/doc/1324577/" id="a_11">367</a>,

                        <a href="/doc/1517318/" id="a_12">436</a>, <a href="/doc/1101188/" id="a_13">379</a> and <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_14">504</a> of the Indian Penal Code is instituted against

                        informant and his family members of the present case and only to give

                        undue pressure, the informant lodged the present case after six days of

                        the occurrence on 12.02.2017. Moreover, the allegation of causing

                        injury to the informant is against co-accused Nilam Devi.

</p><p id="p_5">                                        Having regard to the facts and circumstances of the case,

                        let the above named petitioner, be released on bail, in the event of her

                        arrest or surrender before the learned Court below within a period of

                        four weeks from today, on furnishing bail bond of Rs. 10,000/- (ten

                        thousand) with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction

                        of the Chief Judicial Magistrate, Motihari, East Champaran in

                        connection with Harsidhi P.S. Case No. 51 of 2017, subject to the

                        condition as laid down under <a href="/doc/1144664/" id="a_15">Section 438</a> (2) of the <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_16">Cr.P.C</a>.


</p><p id="p_6">                                                (Rajendra Kumar Mishra, J)
manish/-
</p>
