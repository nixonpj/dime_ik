<div class="doc_title">Narayan Lal Tailor vs Public Works Department on 26 February, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">    THE HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH
                        W.P.No.4522/2018
 (Narayan Lal Tailor and others Vs. State of M.P. and
                            another)
Indore; dated 26.02.2018
        Shri Dinesh Singh Tomar, learned counsel for the
petitioners.
        Ms.    Bharti   Lakkad,     learned   counsel   for   the
respondent.</pre>
<p id="p_1">        The petitioners have filed the present petition,
seeking direction to the respondents to grant the benefit of
Kramonnati in light of the order dated 25.07.2014 passed in
the case of <a href="/doc/32310366/" id="a_1">Indrajeet Singh vs. State of M.P. and others
(W.P.No</a>.11199/2012 (s).
</p><p id="p_2">        According to the petitioners, they were appointed on
07.08.1974,       01.12.1973,       08.06.1989,     10.08.1974,
30.05.1989, 02.05.1967 and 17.09.1973 respectively.
Thereafter, they are retired from the services on
30.04.2012,       30.09.2012,       31.01.2014,     30.08.2008,
30.06.2016, 31.03.2002 and 30.11.2007 respectively.
According to them, they have not been given the benefit of
Kramonnati during the entire service period.
</p><p id="p_3">        Today, learned counsel for the petitioners have filed a
photocopy of the order dated 25.08.2017, by which the be-
nefit    of    Kramonnati      as    been     granted   to    one
Inderjeetsingh, who filed the Writ petition before this
Court. According to the petitioners, their case is identical to
the case of Inderjeetsingh.
</p><p id="p_4">        In view of the aforesaid, the petitioners are directed to
make a representation before respondent No.2/ The
                             Executive Engineer, Public Works Department Jhabua
                            (M.P.) and if such a representation is filed by the
                            petitioners, the respondent No.2 shall consider and decide
                            the representation keeping in mind the order dated
                            25.08.2017 passed in the case of Inderjeetsingh. The entire
                            exercise be completed within a period of 45 days from the
                            production of the certified copy of this order.
</p><p id="p_5">                                          With the aforesaid, the writ petition stands disposed
                            of.
</p><p id="p_6">                                          Certified copy as per Rules.
</p>



<p id="p_7">                                                                                (Vivek Rusia)
                                                                                   Judge
Praveen

Date: 2018.03.17 15:07:40 +05'30'
