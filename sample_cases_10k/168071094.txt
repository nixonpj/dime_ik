SUIT   FOR   RECOVERY   OF   Rs.5,76,570/­   (Rupees   Five 
Lacs Seventy Six Thousand Five Hundred Seventy only) 
BEING   THE   AMOUNT   FUND   DUE,   PAYABLE   AND 
LEGALLY   RECOVERABLE   FROM   THE   DEFENDANTS 
JOINTLY   AND   SEVERALLY   AS   ON   05.07.2013   ALONG 
WITH   PENDENTALITE   AND   FUTURE   INTEREST   WITH 
AGREED   QUARTERLY   RESTS   FROM   THE   DATE   OF 
FILING   OF   THE   SUIT   TILL   ACTUAL   AND   FINAL 
REALIZATION   ON   THE   BASIS   OF   ORAL   AND 
DOCUMENTARY EVIDENCE  BY  PASSING  A JOINT  AND 
SEVERAL DECREE AGAINST THE DEFENDANTS.

EX­PARTE JUDGMENT

</p><p id="p_4">1.               Case of the plaintiff is that the plaintiff bank has 

filed the present suit for recovery of Rs.5,76,570/­ (Rupees 

Five   Lacs   Seventy   Six   Thousand   Five   Hundred   Seventy 

only)     being   the   amount   fund   due,   payable   and   legally 

recoverable from the defendants jointly and severally as on 

05.07.2013 along with pendentalite and future interest with 

agreed quarterly rests from the date of filing of the suit till 

Suit No.574/14                        Punjab &amp; Sind Bank Vs Anil Rana &amp; Anr.                          Page No.2
 actual   and   final   realization   on   the   basis   of   oral   and 

documentary evidence by passing a joint and several decree 

against the defendants, stated therein that the plaintiff is a 

a body corporate constituted under the Banking Companies 

(Acquisition   &amp;   <a href="/doc/1232997/" id="a_2">Transfer   of   Undertakings)   Act</a>,   40  of   1980, 

having its head office at Bank House, 21, Rajendra Place, 

New Delhi.   And a Branch Amongst Branches at 1/20, Asaf 

Ali   Road,   New   Delhi­110002,   through   Shri   Pankaj   Kumar 

Haldar, Sr. Manager of the Branch and Sh. Arvind Singh, 

Sr. Manager of the Chawri Bazari Branch duly constituted 

attorneys and Principal officers 

</p><p id="p_5">2.                  It is stated that the defendant No.1 approached 

the   plaintiff   bank   and   requested   the   plaintiff   bank   for 

consumer loan for purchase of Honda City and defendant 

No.2 agreed to stood as Guarantor.  That on the request of 

the defendant, the plaintiff bank sanctioned a loan of Rs.

6,00,000/­ and the same was disbursed to the defendant 

against hypothecation of New Honda City IV Tech and the 

defendant agreed to repay the same along with interest in 

equal monthly installments.     The defendant No.1 execute 

various loan documents in favour of the plaintiff bank, the 


Suit No.574/14                        Punjab &amp; Sind Bank Vs Anil Rana &amp; Anr.                          Page No.3
 documents are as follows:­

</p><p id="p_6">a)                  Form No.103

</p><p id="p_7">b)                  Form No.106

</p><p id="p_8">c)                  Form No.291

</p><p id="p_9">d)                  Form No.412

</p><p id="p_10">e)                  Demand Promissory Note for a sum of Rs.6 lacs.

</p><p id="p_11">f)                  Letter of Undertaking executed by the defendant 

No.1 and 2.
</p>
<pre id="pre_2">g)                  Form No.256

h)                  Form No.192

</pre><p id="p_12">                    That   the   defendant   had   purchased   the   vehicle 

namely  "NEW   HONDA   CITY   IV   TECH".     That   the 

defendant No.1 is the borrower and defendant No.2 is the 

guarantor   

</p><p id="p_13">3.                  That pursuant to the said receipt of the loan the 

defendants failed to repay the loan amount despite several 

repeated requests/demands.
</p>
<p id="p_14">4.                  That the plaintiff left with no option and issued a 

legal   notice   dtd.07.09.2012   but   neither   the   same   was 

replied by the defendant nor any payment was made.  And 

as per the accounts maintained by the plaintiff  bank the 


Suit No.574/14                        Punjab &amp; Sind Bank Vs Anil Rana &amp; Anr.                          Page No.4
 defendant   is   liable   to   pay   a   sum   of   Rs.5,76,570/­   as   on 

04.07.2013   @   12.55%   (BR   +   2.30%)   along   with   future 

interest   compounded   with   monthly   rest   plus   2%   penal 

interest simple to be charged from the date of the filing of 

the suit till its realization.
</p>
<p id="p_15">5.                         By way of the present suit, plaintiff bank has 

prayed that:­

</p><p id="p_16">i)                  To  pass   a   joint   and   several   decree   in  favour   of 

the plaintiff bank and against the defendants for a sum of 

Rs.5,76,570/­ (Rupees Five Lacs Seventy Six Thousand Five 

Hundred   Seventy   only)   along   with   future   interest 

compounded   with   monthly   rest   plus   2%   penal   interest 

simple to be charged from the date of the filing of the suit 

till its realization of the decreetal amount in full.

</p><p id="p_17">ii)                 To   pass   an   order   for   the   sale   of   Hypothecated 

Honda  City IV Tech Car and the  money so realized to be 

paid   to   the   plaintiff   bank   towards   the   satisfaction   of   the 

decretal amount.
</p>
<p id="p_18">Iii)                To   pass   a   personal   decree   for   a   sum   of   Rs.

5,76,570/­   (Rupees   Five   Lacs   Seventy   Six   Thousand   Five 

Hundred   Seventy   only)   along   with   future   interest 


Suit No.574/14                        Punjab &amp; Sind Bank Vs Anil Rana &amp; Anr.                          Page No.5
 compounded   with   monthly   rest   plus   2%   penal   interest 

simple to be charged from the date of the filing of the suit 

till its realization of the decreetal amount in full in favour of 

the plaintiff bank and against the defendants.

</p><p id="p_19">iv)                 Award   the   cost   of   the   suit   in   favour   of   the 

plaintiff and against the defendants.

</p><p id="p_20">6.                 That upon summons of the suit the defendants 

have tendered their appearance.   Thereafter, several times 

the summons have been issued against both the defendants 

but   the   same   could   not   be   served   upon   the   defendants. 

Thereafter,   the   defendants   were   served   by   way   of 

substituted   service   in   the   Newspaper   "Veer   Arjun"   dated 

09.09.14  but   despite  service  by  way  of   publication   in   the 

Newspaper "Veer Arjun" dated 09.09.14 the defendants have 

not   appeared   before   the   court.     Hence,   vide   order   dtd.

22.09.14 both the defendants were proceeded ex­parte and 

case was fixed for Ex­parte PE.   
</p>
<p id="p_21">7.                  That   in   order   to   prove   his   case   plaintiff   has 

examined   one   witness   Sh.   Kunwar   Kehri   Singh   who   has 

proved  his affidavit  as Ex. PW1/X.  He has also proved the 

documents Ex.PW­1/A (OSR) i.e. copy of power of attorney, 


Suit No.574/14                        Punjab &amp; Sind Bank Vs Anil Rana &amp; Anr.                          Page No.6
 Ex.PW­1/1 i.e. loan application, Ex.PW­1/2 i.e. sanction of 

loan proposal, Ex.PW­1/3 i.e. form No.103, Ex.PW­1/4 form 

No.106, Ex.PW­1/5 i.e, Form No.291, Ex.PW­1/6 i.e. Form 

No.412, Ex.PW­1/7 i.e. Form No.14, Ex.PW­1/8 and 9 letter 

of undertaking from borrower and guarantor, Ex.PW­1/10 

i.e.   Form   No.256,   Ex.PW­1/11   i.e.   Form   No.192, 

Ex.PW­1/12   &amp;   13   i.e.   performa   invoice,   credit   note, 

Ex.PW­1/14 i.e. Legal notice, Ex.PW­1/15 i.e. postal receipt, 

Ex.PW­1/16 i.e. Statement of account and Ex.PW­1/17 i.e. 

Certificate from manager.
</p>
<p id="p_22">8.      After leading the ex­parte plaintiff evidence case was 

fixed   for   ex­parte   final   arguments.     Having   heard 

submissions made by ld counsel for plaintiff and after going 

through   the   documents   placed   on   record   by   PW­1,   this 

Court is of the considered view that the documents placed 

on record are presumed to be genuine and proved by the 

PW­1.     Since   PW­1   has   proved   by   way   of   affidavit   Ex. 

PW1/X     and   defendant   did   not   opt   to   appear   before   this 

Court   to   controvert   the   contents   of   plaint   as   well   as 

PW­1/X   and   the   same   remained   uncontroverted   and 

unrebutted.     Accordingly,   decree   for   a   sum     of   Rs.


Suit No.574/14                        Punjab &amp; Sind Bank Vs Anil Rana &amp; Anr.                          Page No.7
 5,76,570/­   (Rupees   Five   Lacs   Seventy   Six   Thousand   Five 

Hundred   Seventy   only)   along   with   future   interest 

compounded   with   monthly   rest   plus   2%   penal   interest 

simple to be charged from the date of the filing of the suit 

till its realization of the decretal amount in full along with 

cost of the suit is hereby passed in favour of the plaintiff 

bank and against both the defendants.

</p><p id="p_23">9.                 Decree   sheet   be   prepared   accordingly.     File   be 

consigned to record room.
