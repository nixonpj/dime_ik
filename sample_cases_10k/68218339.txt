<div class="doc_title">Urmila Devi vs The State Of Bihar on 3 November, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                 IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA

                                 Criminal Miscellaneous No.41983 of 2015
                          Arising out of PS.Case No. -79 Year- 2015 Thana -SONO District- JAMUI
                 ======================================================
                 Urmila Devi, Wife of late Shankar Mandal, resident of Village- Tetaria,
                 P.S-Sono, District Jamui.

                                                                                   .... ....      Petitioner
                                                       Versus
                 The State of Bihar

                                                                .... .... Opposite Party
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner      : Mr. Bharat Lal, Advocate.
                 For the Opposite Party :  Mr. Rajendra Nath Jha(App)
                 ======================================================

                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE SUDHIR SINGH
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">3   03-11-2015              Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned

                 counsel for the State

                           The petitioner is languishing in custody since 18.07.2015

                 in connection with Sono P.S. Case No. 79 of 2015 (G.R. No. 790

                 of 2015) for the offences instituted under <a href="/doc/1868826/" id="a_1">Sections 498(A)</a> and

                 <a href="/doc/409589/" id="a_2">304(B)</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_3">34</a> of the IPC.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                           The prosecution story, in brief, is that the informant's

                 daughter, namely, Fulwanti Devi, was married with Kamlesh

                 Mandal of Village-Tetariya two years ago according to Hindu

                 Rites and Customs. It is stated that after marriage, the daughter of

                 the informant remained in her Sasural in well manner. Thereafter

                 the accused persons started to abuse and torture her. They have
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.41983 of 2015 (3) dt.03-11-2015</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                           2/3</span>




             also assaulted her and demanded one Motorcycle. The informant

             in presence of local police wanted to pacify the matter but of no

             avail. On 11.05.2015 at about 5.00 P.M., in the early morning, the

             accused persons after closing the door of the house and set her on

             fire by sprinkling petrol on her and Fulwanti Devi started crying.

             Thereafter, the villagers on hearing the sound of Fulwanti Devi

             went there and opened the door. The villagers brought her in Sono

             P.S. and the police sent her to Jhajha for her treatment and on the

             way she died. Accordingly, the case was lodged.

</p><p id="p_3">                         It has been submitted on behalf of the petitioner that the

             petitioner has got no criminal antecedent. The petitioner is in

             custody since 18.07.2015 and the charge sheet has been submitted

             in the case. There is no allegation of tampering of the evidence

             against the petitioner. The petitioner is the mother-in-law of the

             deceased and she is separate in mess and property from the

             husband of the deceased. She has falsely been implicated in the

             present case.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                         On behalf of the State it has been submitted that the

             petitioner is named in the F.I.R.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                         Considering the aforesaid facts and circumstances, let the

             petitioner above named, be released on bail on furnishing bail

             bond of Rs. 10,000/-(Ten Thousand) with two sureties of the like
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">          Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.41983 of 2015 (3) dt.03-11-2015</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                                                    3/3</span>




                      amount each to the satisfaction of the learned A.C.J.M., Jamui, in

                      connection with Sono P.S. Case No. 79/2015(G.R. No. 790/2015).


</p><pre id="pre_2">U.K./-                                                (Sudhir Singh, J)
  U         T
