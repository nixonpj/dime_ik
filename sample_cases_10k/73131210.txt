<p id="p_3">LPA No.1100 of 2017

           This appeal is directed against the judgment of the

learned Single Judge dated 21.02.2017 declining interference in

the writ proceedings on the ground of delay and laches. The

appellant wanted consideration to a selection process relating

back to the year 2007 in which regard she has filed a writ petition

in the year 2016 resulting in the impugned order. Aggrieved

against the same, learned counsel for the appellant contends that

similarly situated persons were granted consideration under the

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2"> LPA No.1100 of 2017 (O&amp;M) -2-</span>

orders of this Court and there would be no reason to deny him

similar consideration. We, however, do not agree with the

contention as a person aggrieved of an action or inaction should

be vigilant enough to approach the court within a reasonable time

which in the matter of service has been described by the Hon'ble

Supreme Court as one year. We thus do not find any reason to

interfere with the impugned order. If the respondents have an

inclination to consider the claim of the appellant, they would be

at liberty to do so without being hindered by the dismissal of the

writ petition.
</p>
