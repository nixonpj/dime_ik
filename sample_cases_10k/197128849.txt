           W.P. 18975 (W) of 2018

         Mr. K. Guha Thakurata...For the Petitioner.

         Mr. S. Nayak,
         Ms. Rituparna Mitra...For WBSEDCL.

         Mr. M. Karim...For Respondent No. 4.</pre>

<p id="p_1">         Learned Advocate for the respondent No. 4 submits that he has not

received any copy of the writ petition. This submission is vehemently opposed by

the learned Advocate for the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_2">         Learned Advocate for the petitioner submits that it is evident from the affidavit of service that long back the

respondent No. 4 has received the copy of the writ petition.

</p><p id="p_3">         Be that as it may, I direct the learned Advocate for the petitioner to serve copy upon the respondent No. 4 here

and now.
</p>
<p id="p_4">         Accordingly, learned Advocate for the petitioner has served copy of the writ petition upon the learned

Advocate for the respondent No. 4. Therefore, learned Advocate for the respondent No. 4 prays for some time to take

instruction.
</p>
<p id="p_5">         That being the scenario, let the matter appear in the Monthly List of January, 2019 under the heading " Listed

Motion".
</p>
<p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">                        ( Samapti Chatterjee, J )
