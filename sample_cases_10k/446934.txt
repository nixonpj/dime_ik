<div class="doc_title">Sugathan vs State Of Kerala on 24 August, 2007</div><pre id="pre_1">       

  

  

 
 
  IN THE HIGH COURT OF KERALA AT ERNAKULAM

Bail Appl No. 5188 of 2007()


1. SUGATHAN, AGED 37,
                      ...  Petitioner

                        Vs



1. STATE OF KERALA, REPRESENTED BY THE
                       ...       Respondent

                For Petitioner  :SRI.C.P.UDAYABHANU

                For Respondent  : No Appearance

The Hon'ble MR. Justice R.BASANT

 Dated :24/08/2007

 O R D E R
                                R.BASANT, J
                         ------------------------------------
                          B.A.No.5188 of 2007
                        -------------------------------------
               Dated this the 24th day of August, 2007

                                     ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Application for regular bail. The petitioner is the 1st accused.

Altogether there are 3 accused persons. Accused 2 and 3 are the wife

and brother of the petitioner. The crux of the allegations is that the

accused persons entertained ill will and animosity against the

deceased. The deceased had filed a complaint against them before the

police. On account of such animosity, the petitioner along 2 other

accused allegedly caused injuries to the victim by stabbing him with a

knife. The other two accused did not use any dangerous weapons.

They allegedly threw stone and hit him with hands. The petitioner

using a dangerous weapon - a knife allegedly planted a stab on the

victim.   The victim who suffered the injuries on 21.07.07 had

succumbed to those injuries later on 09.08.07.                 Crime was initially

registered under <a href="/doc/4266/" id="a_1">Section 308</a> I.P.C and <a href="/doc/42436223/" id="a_2">Section 3(1)(x)</a> of the

Scheduled Castes/<a href="/doc/25085007/" id="a_3">Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act</a>.

Later the Section was altered to <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_4">Section 307</a> I.P.C. Later consequent

to the death of the victim, the offence under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_5">Section 302</a> I.P.C has also

been alleged.
</p>
<p id="p_2"><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">B.A.No.5188 of 2007                 2</span>

</p><p id="p_3">      2.    The petitioner was arrested on 01.08.07. He continues in

custody from that date.      The learned counsel for the petitioner

submits that the petitioner is absolutely innocent. The learned counsel

further submits that even if the entire allegations were accepted, the

petitioner cannot reasonably be found guilty of the capital offence of

murder. In these circumstances, it is prayed that a lenient view may

be taken and the petitioner may be enlarged on bail.

</p><p id="p_4">      3.    The learned Public Prosecutor      opposes the application.

The learned Public Prosecutor        submits that the allegations are

serious. Accused 3, it is submitted, has been recently arrested. The

2nd accused, a woman, has not so far been arrested. Investigation is

not complete. This is too early to consider the petitioner's application

for bail favourably. Investigator in a serious crime like this may be

granted reasonable time to complete the investigation. There is no

merit in the allegation that the allegation even if accepted in toto,

cannot reveal the offence under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_6">Section 302</a> I.P.C. The nature of the

weapon used, the nature of the injury inflicted and the consequence of

the injury must definitely convey that there is prima facie acceptable

material to conclude that the offence under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_7">Section 302</a> I.P.C has been

committed, submits the learned Public Prosecutor.

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">B.A.No.5188 of 2007                  3</span>

</p><p id="p_5">      4.     I have considered all the relevant inputs. I shall carefully

avoid any detailed discussions on the acceptability of the allegations or

the credibility of the data collected. Suffice it to say that I am satisfied

that there is merit in the opposition raised by the learned Public

Prosecutor. In a crime like this, the investigator is certainly entitled

for further time to complete the investigation. I find no merit in the

contention that the allegation under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_8">Section 302</a> I.P.C is totally

unjustified.
</p>
<p id="p_6">      5.     This application for regular bail is, in these circumstances,

dismissed, but with the observation that the petitioner shall be at

liberty to move the learned Sessions Judge or this Court for bail again

at a later stage of the investigation - not, at any rate, prior to

07.09.07.     The investigator shall in the meantime make every

endeavour to complete the investigation.
</p>
<p id="p_7">


</p><p id="p_8">                                                     (R.BASANT, JUDGE)
rtr/-
</p>
