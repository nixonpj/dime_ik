<p id="p_4">
                   Mr. Dibyendu Chatterjee
                   Ms. Madhuparna Kanrar
                              ......For the Petitioner
                   Mr. K. K. Bandyopadhyay
                                .....For the S. S. C.
</p><p id="p_5">                   Mr. Tulshidas Ray
                                ....For the State



                         Learned counsel for the writ petitioner submits that

                   the writ petitioner appeared in the Regional Level

                   Selection Test, 2012 for the post of assistant teacher but

                   despite her name appearing in the combined merit list,

                   the petitioner was not appointed to the post sought for.

                   Counsel submits that candidates who have been placed

                   lower than the petitioner have been appointed. Counsel is

                   however unable to show any document from which such

                   contention would be evident.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                         The State and the Commission are represented.

                         Since there is considerable delay in filing the writ

                   petition, the concerned test being of 2012 and the

                   averment made in paragraph 10 being that the petitioner

                   came to know of the alleged irregularity in January, 2019,
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                            2</span>


list this matter after four weeks.

</p><p id="p_7">      Both the State and the Commission are directed to

file Reports in the form of affidavits responding to the

averments within the returnable date.       Copies of such

Reports should be given to counsel appearing for the

petitioner well before the next date of hearing.




</p><p id="p_8">                           (Moushumi Bhattacharya, J.)
