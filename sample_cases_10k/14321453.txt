<div class="doc_title">Riesh Kumar vs State Of Bihar on 5 May, 2009</div><pre id="pre_1">                IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                              Cr. Misc. No.7149 of 2009
             Dharamveer Singh, S/o Lakshmi Narayan Singh, R/o Village- Shirshi Ghangh,
             P.S.-Bakhtiyarpur, District-Patna.                      . . . . . . Petitioner.
                                         Versus
                                  STATE OF BIHAR
                                         WITH
                              Cr. Misc. No.8505 of 2009
               Ritesh Kumar, s/o Dinesh Singh, R/o-Village Ram Nagar, Tinkhutti, P.S.-
                 Athmalgola, District- Patna.                               . . . . . Petitioner.
                                         Versus
                                  STATE OF BIHAR
                                        -----------
</pre><p id="p_1">2/    05.05.2009              Since both  the applications have arisen out of one and the same

                      police case, they have been heard together and are being disposed of by this

                      common order.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                 Petitioners seek bail in a case which has been registered for the

                      offences under <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_1">Sections 324</a>, <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_2">341</a>, <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_3">307</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_4">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code and 27 of

                      the <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_5">Arms Act</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                 Allegation is that the informant was caught by the petitioners and

                      fire was made by Guddu Singh.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                 Submission is that the occurrence took place on National

                      Highway in another district and it is not explained as to why no information

                      was given there. The fardbeyan has been given for the first time at P.M.C.H.,

                      Patna. It has further been submitted that the injuries appears to be

                      superficial.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                 Considering the facts and circumstances of the case, petitioners,

                      named above, are directed to be released on bail on furnishing bail bonds of

                      Rs. 10,000/- (Ten thousand), each, with two sureties of the like amount each

                      to the satisfaction of Chief Judicial Magistrate, Nalanda at Bihar Sharif

                      /concerned court in connection with Harnaut P.S. case no. 270 of 2008.


</p><p id="p_6">                                                       (Shyam Kishore Sharma, J.)
     Uday/
 </p>
