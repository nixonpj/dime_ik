<div class="doc_title">Rajesh Yadav vs State Of Jharkhand on 8 April, 2011</div><pre id="pre_1">                     IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                               B. A. No. 363 of 2011
       Rajesh Yadav                                  ..... Petitioner
                                         Versus
       The State of Jharkhand                        ..... Opposite Party
                                          -----
                CORAM: HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE NARENDRA NATH TIWARI
                                          -----
       For the Petitioner        - Mr. J.S.Singh
       For the State             - Mr. A.P.P
                                          -----

3/8.4.2011</pre><p id="p_1">   The petitioner is in custody in connection with the case registered under

       <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_1">Sections 341</a>/<a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_2">302</a>/<a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_3">307</a>/<a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_4">324</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_5">34</a> IPC, <a href="/doc/244673/" id="a_6">Section 27</a> of the Arms Act and <a href="/doc/258943/" id="a_7">Section 17</a> of the

       Criminal Law (Amendment) Act.
</p>
<p id="p_2">             Learned counsel for the petitioner submitted that the petitioner has been falsely

       implicated in this case; he is not named in the F.I.R; subsequently, the petitioner's

       name transpired on the basis of the statements of some interested persons; nothing

       incriminating has been recovered from the petitioner's possession; the petitioner is in

       custody since August 2010, but till date no T. I. Parade has been conducted; co-

       accused with almost similar allegations have been granted bail by this Court, as has

       been stated in Paragraph 11 of the bail application; the petitioner is a local permanent

       resident and there is no chance of his absconding.
</p>
<p id="p_3">             Learned A.P.P opposed the petitioner's prayer for bail. However, after going

       through the Case Diary, he has not controverted the said factual contentions

       submitted by learned counsel for the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_4">             Considering the facts and circumstances of the case, the above named

       petitioner is directed to be enlarged on bail on furnishing bail-bond of Rs. 10,000/-

       (Ten thousand only) with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of the

       C.J.M, Garhwa in connection with Ranka P. S. Case No. 76/07, corresponding to

       G. R. No. 693/07.
</p>
<p id="p_5">

</p><pre id="pre_2">    S.K                                                      (NARENDRA NATH TIWARI, J)
