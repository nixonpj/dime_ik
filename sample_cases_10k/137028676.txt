<div class="doc_title">Baleshwar Sah vs State Of Bihar &amp; Anr on 7 August, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">    Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.20901 of 2014 (2) dt.07-08-2014




                          IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                Criminal Miscellaneous No.20901 of 2014

                  Arising Out of PS.Case No. -4 Year- 2014 Thana -SAKRA District-
                 MUZAFFARPUR
                 ======================================================
                 1. Baleshwar Sah Son of Late Makhan Sah R/o Village-Muriyari, P.S.-
                 Sakra (Bariyarpur O.P.), District-Muzaffarpur

                                                                                .... ....   Petitioner/s
                                                        Versus
                 1. The State of Bihar
                 2. North Bihar Power Distribution Company Limited through Bihar State
                 Electricity Board.

                                                                .... .... Opposite Party
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner        :   Mr. Sanjay Kumar @ S.K. Advocate
                 For the North Bihar Power
                 Distribution Company Ltd.   : Mr. Padma Deo Narayan, Advocate
                 For the State              :  Mr. T.P. Mandal, APP
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE AKHILESH CHANDRA
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2       07-08-2014                        Heard learned counsel for the petitioner, learned

                         counsel for the North Bihar Power Distribution Company

                         Limited through Bihar State Electricity Board and learned

                         Additional Public Prosecutor for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                          The    petitioner,       apprehends   his    arrest   in

                         connection with a case registered for the offence punishable

                         under <a href="/doc/132967048/" id="a_1">Section 135</a>, <a href="/doc/132967048/" id="a_2">140</a> of Electricity Act, is named accused in

                         this case with allegation of power theft causing loss of Rs.

                         1,18,460/- by running oil mill after connecting with loose wire.

</p><p id="p_3">                                          Submission is that petitioner after applying for

                         valid connection and deposit of money on the assurances of

                         formalities given started consuming energy and ready to pay the

                         amount, subject to adjustment of the amount so defalcated by
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">        Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.20901 of 2014 (2) dt.07-08-2014</span>




                            filing application, and of course, to pay on installment basis.

</p><p id="p_4">                                            Having regard to the facts and circumstances of

                            the case, in the event of filing duly verified application

                            supported with personal affidavit stating all such submission

                            and deposit of the amount in court, in the event of his

                            arrest/surrender before the court below within six weeks, let the

                            above named petitioner be enlarged on bail on furnishing bail

                            bond of Rs. 10,000/- (ten thousand only) with two sureties of the

                            like amount each to the satisfaction of Special Judge, Bihar

                            State Electricity Board, Tirhut Division, Muzaffarpur, in

                            connection with Sakra (Bariyarpur O.P.) P.S. Case No. 04 of

                            2014, subject to condition laid down under <a href="/doc/132967048/" id="a_3">Section 438</a> (2) of

                            the Code of Criminal Procedure with additional condition to

                            remain physically present before the court below till disposal of

                            the case and in case of failure on two consecutive dates without

                            giving any reasonable explanation, the liberty granted shall be

                            deemed to be cancelled.
</p>
<p id="p_5">

</p><pre id="pre_2">    Rajeev/-                                                   (Akhilesh Chandra, J.)

U         T
