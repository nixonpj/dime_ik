<div class="doc_title">Krishna Kumar Somkuwar vs Smt. Sharmila on 24 June, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                           CRR-499-2016
              (KRISHNA KUMAR SOMKUWAR Vs SMT. SHARMILA)


24-06-2016

None for the applicant.
       The applicant/husband has filed this criminal revision
under <a href="/doc/1411868/" id="a_1">section 19(4)</a> of the Family Courts Act being aggrieved by
the order dated 15.07.2015 passed by the Principal Judge, Family
court Chhindwara in M.Cr.C.No.923/2014 whereby the
application under <a href="/doc/1056396/" id="a_2">section 125</a> Cr.P.C. has been allowed and
maintenance to the tune of Rs.3,000/- per month has been
awarded in favour of the respondent/wife.</pre>
<p id="p_1">      This revision is barred by 116 days.
</p><p id="p_2">      I.A.No.3725/2016 has been filed under <a href="/doc/100581/" id="a_3">section 5</a> of the
Limitation Act for condonation of delay in filing the revision
petition stating that the applicant has no knowledge about the
limitation period, hence the delay be condoned.
</p><p id="p_3">      Looking to the non-appearance of the applicant on last five
dates of listing and the fact that no sufficient cause has been
shown for the condonation of delay, I.A.No.3725/2016 is
rejected. Consequently, revision petition also stands dismissed
for want of prosecution.
</p>

<p id="p_4">                                                     (J. P. GUPTA)
                                                         JUDGE
