<div class="doc_title">Rajender Parshad vs Vikram Lamba on 17 July, 2012</div><pre id="pre_1">Civil Revision No. 4113 of 2011 (O&amp;M)                     1


      IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANAAT
                   CHANDIGARH

                          Civil Revision No. 4113 of 2011 (O&amp;M)
                          Date of Decision: 17.07.2012


Rajender Parshad                                   ........Petitioner


            versus


Vikram Lamba                                       ......... Respondent

CORAM: Hon'ble Mr. Justice Ajay Tewari

Present:      Mr.Mukesh Kumar Verma , Advocate
              for the petitioner.
                   ****
</pre>
<p id="p_1">1. Whether Reporters of local papers may be allowed to see the judgment?
</p><p id="p_2">2. To be referred to the Reporters or not?
</p><p id="p_3">3. Whether the judgment should be reported in the Digest?

Ajay Tewari, J. (Oral):
</p>
<p id="p_4">            This petition has been filed against the concurrent orders of the

learned Rent Controller and the Appellate Authority in regard to fixation of

fair rent. The petitioner claimed rent at the rate of Rs. 1700/- per month,

while the respondent claimed rent of Rs. 250/- per month.

</p><p id="p_5">            Both the Courts on an examination of the entire evidence led by

both the parties have concurrently held that the rate of rent was Rs. 250/-

per month. The only argument raised by learned counsel for the petitioner is

that the respondent led evidence which was beyond his pleadings. In this

connection it must be noticed that originally the petitioner had not led any

evidence with respect to the rate of rent in affirmative, however, in rebuttal

he led affirmative evidence. The respondent came to this Court by way of
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Civil Revision No. 4113 of 2011 (O&amp;M)                     2</span>


Civil Revision No. 919 of 2009 which was disposed of on 29.04.2009 in the

following terms:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">            "While granting such permission since it is not in the nature of
            rebuttal, the defendant shall have again a right to explain the
            income tax returns if he so wishes to do and the Court below
            shall give such opportunity after the plaintiff tenders his
            evidence and closes his evidence, to the defendant to give
            evidence before inviting parties to address arguments. Subject
            to this liberty, the Civil Revision is dismissed."</blockquote><p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">            Learned counsel has fairly accepted that it was in terms of this

direction that the respondent led evidence of RW6 and RW7 to explain the

income tax returns. In view of this order it does not now lie in the mouth of

the petitioner to argue that it was incumbent upon the respondent to have

amended his written statement before taking this plea.

</p><p id="p_8">            No other point has been raised.
</p>
<p id="p_9">            Consequently the petition is dismissed. No costs.

            Since the main case has been decided, the Civil Misc.

Applications, if any, also stand disposed of.


</p><p id="p_10">                                                (AJAY TEWARI)
                                                    JUDGE

July 17, 2012
sunita
