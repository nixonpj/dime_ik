              IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA
                         AT CHANDIGARH.

                                     Crl. Misc. No.M-15865 of 2016
                                     Date of Decision: 09.05.2016


Rupinder Kaur @ Raj Kaur                               ....Petitioner

              Versus

State of Punjab                                      ....Respondent


BEFORE :- HON'BLE MRS. JUSTICE DAYA CHAUDHARY

      1. Whether reporters of local newspapers may be allowed
          to see the judgment ? (Yes/No)
      2. To be referred to reporters or not ? (Yes/No)
      3. Whether the judgment should be reported in the Digest ?
           (Yes/No)

Present:-     Mr. M.S. Basra, Advocate
              for the petitioner.

                          *****

DAYA CHAUDHARY, J. (ORAL)
</pre>

<p id="p_1">              This petition has been filed under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> Cr.P.C for grant

of anticipatory bail to the petitioner, in case, FIR No.46 dated 22.09.2015

registered under <a href="/doc/653797/" id="a_2">Sections 304-B</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_3">34</a> IPC at Police Station Tibbar, District

Gurdaspur.
</p>
<p id="p_2">              Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that the petitioner

was found innocent during investigation. He further submits that the

petitioner is still ready to join investigation and no custodial interrogation is

required. Learned counsel also submits that the trial Court has taken

cognizance against the petitioner under <a href="/doc/1145599/" id="a_4">Section 193</a> Cr.P.C, whereas, there

are only general allegations and no specific allegation of demand of dowry

or harassment thereof before death has been attributed to the present

petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                      1 of 2


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                   ::: Downloaded on - 13-05-2016 00:02:52 :::</span>
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2"> Crl. Misc. No.M-15865 of 2016                                              2</span>

             Admittedly, the petitioner has not approached before the trial

Court for anticipatory bail and has straightway filed the present petition

before this Court. Even the summoning order has not been challenged by

her so far. Only on the ground that the petitioner is ready to join

investigation, the petitioner is not entitled for grant of anticipatory bail.

</p><p id="p_4">             Keeping in view the submissions made by learned counsel for

the petitioner, in case, the petitioner surrenders before the trial Court within

a period of one week from the date of receipt of certified copy of this order

and moves an application for bail, the trial Court is directed to decide the

same within a period of one week thereafter.
</p>
<p id="p_5">             The petition is accordingly disposed of.</p><pre id="pre_2">


                                                      (DAYA CHAUDHARY)
09.05.2016                                                  JUDGE
