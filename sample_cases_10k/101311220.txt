                                   Before

       THE HON'BLE MR JUSTICE HULUVADI G RAMESH

                   Writ Petition   15719 / 2005 (LR)
Between:
Meena Moolya S/o late Venkappa Moolya
80 yrs, R/a Kolambe Village &amp; Post
MangaloreTaluk, D K                                Petitioner

(By Sri B V Krishna, Adv.)

And:

1      Smt Sumathi S Rai W/o Sankappa Rai
       R/a Panchalamaru House, Kolambe Village
       Mangalore Taluk, D K District

2      Smt Sarvani S Hegde - since dead by Lrs

a      K Rajarama Hege S/o Palli Srinivasa Hegde

b      K Nataraj Hegde S/o Palli Srinivasa Hegde

c      K Nivedita Hegde W/oDhiraj Shetty

       All are r/a Hiriadka Post, Hiriadka House
       Udupi Taluk &amp; District

3      Land Tribunal, Mangalore Taluk, D K
                                                                         2

4     State of Karnataka - by its Secretary
      Revenue Department, M S Building
      Dr Ambedkar Veedhi, Bangalore 1                Respondents

(By Sri Shashidhar S Karmadi,GP for R3-4;
Sri H Mujtaba, Adv. For R1, R2(a-c)


      Writ Petition is filed under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Art.226</a><a href="/doc/1331149/" id="a_2">/227</a> of the Constitution praying
to quash the order dated 18.1.1989 - annexure A by the Land
Tribunal,Mangalore.

      The Petition    coming on for hearing    this day, Court made the
following:
                                ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Petitioner has sought for quashing the order of the Land Tribunal,

granting occupancy rights in respect of Sy.No.234/1B measuring 1.45 acres

situate at Kolambe Village.
</p>
<p id="p_2">

</p><p id="p_3">      On the application filed by the petitioner before the Land Tribunal

claiming five properties, after having conducted spot inspection and

inquiry, Land Tribunal proceeded to grant occupancy rights in respect of

four properties and rejected the claim in respect of the above said survey

number. Hence, this petition.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                                             3</span><p id="p_4">

</p><p id="p_5">       Heard the counsel representing the parties.



       It is the contention of the petitioner that the property in question is

required in assistance to cultivate the other agricultural lands to the extent

of 3.00 acres and therefore, prayed for allowing the petition.



</p><p id="p_6">       The order of the Land Tribunal is dated 18.1.1999.        Although the

order in question is not a speaking order with regard to the property claimed

and rejected and though it is very much required for cultivation of other

properties by way of assistance as it was getting sufficient manure, etc., the

same has not been considered.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><p id="p_8">       However, the majority of the members including the Chairman have

opined that the land is a hilly land i.e., punja land and not cultivable. As

such, the claim of the petitioner has been rejected. More over, after lapse

of twenty years, petitioner approaches this Court. The impugned order does

not call for interference.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                       4</span><p id="p_9">

</p><p id="p_10">     Petition is dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_11">                               Sd/-
</p><p id="p_12">                              Judge
An
