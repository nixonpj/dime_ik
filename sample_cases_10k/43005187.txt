<div class="doc_title">Kailashchandra vs Nanuram on 10 July, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                               SA-232-2014
                        (KAILASHCHANDRA Vs NANURAM)


10-07-2017
       Parties through their counsel.
       Heard on I.A. no. 5219/2014 filed under Order XXXIX Rule1 and 2
of C.P.C.
       Present appeal has been filed against the judgment and decree dated
16/05/2014 passed by 3rd A.D.J. Dhar in Civil Appeal no. 38-A/2013,
whereby the lower Appellate Court has allowed the appeal preferred by the
respondent no. 1 and set aside the judgment passed by the trial Court.</pre>
<p id="p_1">       Vide order dated 21/07/2016, appeal has been admitted for final
hearing on the substantial questions of law. By way of application under
Order 39 Rule 1 and 2 of C.PC, the appellant is praying that the
respondents be restrained from alienating the said property during
pendency of this appeal. If the property is sold, then it would create
complications and unnecessary third party right would be created.
</p><p id="p_2">       Counsel for the respondent has filed reply opposing the stay.
       Since the appeal has been admitted, hence the respondents are
restrained from alienating the suit property during pendency of this appeal.
</p><p id="p_3">       That, the respondent are at liberty to seek permission from this Court
for alienation of the suit property, if circumstances so arise in future.
</p><p id="p_4">       With the aforesaid liberty, I.A. no. 5219/2014 stands disposed of. C
c as per rules.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                                         (VIVEK RUSIA)
                                                             JUDGE
