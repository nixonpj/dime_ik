                              ORDER
</pre>

<p id="p_1">          The petitioners seek orders quashing the F.I.R and further

proceedings in Crime No.400 of 2019 of the Parippally Police Station,

registered under Sections 294(b), 323, 324, 341 read with <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_1">Section

34</a> IPC on the complaint of one Rahul Prasad. Orders are sought on

the ground of amicable settlement of the whole dispute between the

accused and the defacto complainant out of court.          The defacto

complainant is the 3rd respondent in this proceeding brought under

<a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_2">Section 482</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure. He has filed affidavit

to the effect that he has settled the whole dispute with the accused,

and he has no grievance or complaint. The person who sustained

injuries in the alleged incident is the 4 th respondent. He has filed

affidavit to the effect that he has settled the dispute with the

accused, and he has no grievance or complaint now.

</p><p id="p_2">          2.    In so many decisions, the Hon'ble Supreme Court

has held that even in cases involving non-compoundable offences,

the High Court can quash the prosecution; be it at the crime stage

or at the trial stage or even at the appellate or revision stage; if the

parties have really settled the whole dispute, or if continuance of

the prosecution will not serve any purpose. Here, I find a real case
 Crl.M.C.No.3399 of 2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                   3</span>

of settlement between the parties, and I also find that continuance

of the prosecution in such a situation will not serve any purpose

other than wasting the precious time of the court, when the case

ultimately comes before the court. A counter case has also been

settled between the parties.
</p>
<p id="p_3">          In the result, this petition is allowed. The F.I.R and further

proceedings in Crime No.400 of 2019 of the Parippally Police Station

will stand quashed under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_3">Section 482</a> of the Code of Criminal

Procedure.
</p>
