<div class="doc_title">Surendra Prasad @ Surendra Rai vs The State Of Bihar on 3 September, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                               CRIMINAL MISCELLANEOUS No.22996 of 2019
                          Arising Out of PS. Case No.-548 Year-2018 Thana- MANER District- Patna
                 ======================================================
           1.     SURENDRA PRASAD @ SURENDRA RAI Son of Late Ram Gulam
                  Yadav Resident of Village - Nagwan, P.S.- Maner, Distt - Patna.
           2.    Ramesh Kumar SURENDRA PRASAD @ SURENDRA RAI Resident of
                 Village - Nagwan, P.S.- Maner, Distt - Patna.
           3.    Niranjan Kumar @ Niranjan Rai SURENDRA PRASAD @ SURENDRA
                 RAI Resident of Village - Nagwan, P.S.- Maner, Distt - Patna.
           4.     Sulekha Devi Wife of Niranjan Kumar @ Niranjan Rai Resident of Village -
                  Nagwan, P.S.- Maner, Distt - Patna.
                                                                        ... ... Petitioner/s
                                                    Versus
                 The State of Bihar.......................................... ... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :         Mr.Nilesh Kumar
                 For the Opposite Party/s :         Mr.Atul Chandra
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE VINOD KUMAR SINHA
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>
<p id="p_1">5   03-09-2019                      Petitioners seek bail in anticipation of their arrest in

                   connection with Maner P.S. Case No. 548 of 2018, registered for

                   the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_1">Sections 341</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_2">323</a>,<a href="/doc/1540253/" id="a_3">326</a>, <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_4">307</a>,

                   <a href="/doc/140515/" id="a_5">313</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_6">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                 Allegation against the petitioners is that they set

                   ablaze the daughter of the informant by fire causing injury to

                   her.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                  Submission of the learned counsel for the petitioners

                   that there is general and omnibus allegation against the

                   petitioners and statement of victim girl was not recorded by the

                   police. Petitioner no. 4 is a lady.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                 Learned A.P.P. for the State has opposed the prayer of
             Patna High Court CR. MISC. No.22996 of 2019(5) dt.03-09-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                        2/2</span>




                      bail on the ground that injury report disclosed that victim has

                      received 45 per cent burn injury.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                   Having heard both sides. So far as petitioners no.1 to3

                      are concerned, in view of the allegation, I am not inclined to

                      grant privilege of anticipatory bail to them, however, they are

                      directed to surrender before the learned Court below and make

                      prayer for regular bail and Court below will consider the matter

                      on its own merit without being prejudiced by order of this court.

</p><p id="p_6">                                   So far as petitioner No.4 is concerned, considering the

                      fact that she is lady, let the petitioner No.4 above named, in the

                      event of her arrest or surrender before the Court below within

                      six weeks, be released on bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs.

                      25,000/- (Rupees Twenty Five Thousand) with two sureties of

                      the like amount to the satisfaction of the learned Judicial

                      Magistrate, 1st class, Danapur in connection with Maner P.S.

                      Case No. 548 of 2018, subject to the condition as laid down

                      under <a href="/doc/1144664/" id="a_7">Section 438</a> (2) of the <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_8">Cr.P.C</a>. and further condition is

                      that one of the bailors of the petitioner shall be a local person

                      having sufficient immovable property within the jurisdiction of

                      the Court concerned.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                                                        (Vinod Kumar Sinha, J)
T.Kr./-
</p><pre id="pre_2">U       T
