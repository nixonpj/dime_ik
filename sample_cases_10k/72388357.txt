<div class="doc_title">Wasim Akram And Anr vs The State Of Jharkhand And Anr on 2 August, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                 IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                                A.B. A. No. 3488 of 2017

                    1. Wasim Akram
                    2. Sheikh Yasin                            ...         Petitioners
                                                  Versus
                   1. The State of Jharkhand
                   2. Saba Parveen                                 ... Opp. Parties

           Coram: HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE ANIL KUMAR CHOUDHARY


          For the Petitioner                       : Mr. A. K. Choudhary, Adv.
          For the State                            : Addl. P.P.
          For the Opposite party no. 2             : Mr. Pran Pranay, Adv.

04 / 02.08.17</pre><p id="p_1">            Apprehending their arrest, the two petitioners have moved this
                Court for grant of privilege of anticipatory bail in connection with PCR
                Case No. 724 of 2016 registered under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_1">Sections 498A</a> of the Indian Penal
                Code and under Section 3/4 D.P. Act.
</p><p id="p_2">                     Heard learned counsel appearing for the petitioners, learned
                counsel for opposite party no. 2 as well as learned Addl. PP for the
                State.
</p><p id="p_3">                          Both the parties- the petitioners and the opposite party no. 2 are
                present in the Court today.
</p><p id="p_4">                            The learned counsel appearing for the petitioners submits
                that it is agreed between the parties that petitioner no. 2 will execute a
                deed of gift or suitable deed of transfer of the house property situated
                near Mazar at Sarath, where the opposite party no. 2 is now residing,
                in the name of his grand son- Faizal Seikh. The allegations against the
                petitioners are false. Hence, the petitioners be given the privilege of
                anticipatory bail.
</p><p id="p_5">                         The learned Addl. PP opposed the prayer for anticipatory bail.
                         Considering the submissions of the counsels and the fact as
                discussed above, I am inclined to grant the privilege of anticipatory
                bail to the petitioners. Hence, in the event of their arrest by the police
                or surrender within a period of four weeks from the date of this order,
                they shall be released on bail on producing the proof of transfer of the
                house as indicated above in favour of grand son of petitioner no. 2
                namely Faizal Seikh, who is the son of the petitioner no.1 and
                furnishing bail bond of Rs. 10,000/- (Rupees Ten Thousand) each with
                two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of learned
 SDJM, Deoghar in connection with PCR Case No. 724 of 2016 subject to
the condition that if the opposite party no. 2 joins the society of the
petitioner no. 1, the petitioners will keep and maintain her with full
dignity and honour and with other conditions laid down under <a href="/doc/1144664/" id="a_2">section
438</a> (2) Cr. P.C.
</p>

<p id="p_6">                                  (ANIL KUMAR CHOUDHARY, J.)
Smita/-
</p>
