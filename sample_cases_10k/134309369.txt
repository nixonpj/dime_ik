                            JUDGMENT

<p id="p_1">     Petitioner    is   a   student   pursuing    Bachelor   of

Business      Administration   with    Aviation   Management

Course in Christian Institution of Management, Banglore,

which is affiliated to the Banglore University. Petitioner

has approached the first respondent bank seeking

education loan.         The grievance highlighted by the

petitioner is that the application, namely Ext.P7 is not

being processed by the bank and thus seeks appropriate

directions.
</p>
<p id="p_2">     2.    I have heard learned counsel for the petitioner

and learned Standing Counsel appearing for respondents

1 and 2 and perused the pleadings and the documents on

record.
</p>
<p id="p_3">     3.    Learned counsel for the petitioner reiterated

the contentions raised in the writ petition and sought for

disposal of Ext.P7, within a time frame.               Learned
W.P.(C). No. 4576 OF 2018

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                               2</span>

Standing Counsel appearing for the bank submitted

already the application is forwarded to the sanctioning

authority and it is under process.

</p><p id="p_4">      4.    Having regard to the facts and circumstances of

the case, there will be a direction to the first and second

respondents to process the application submitted by the

petitioner, in accordance with law, and attain finality, at

the earliest possible time, and at any rate, within one

month from the date of receipt of a copy of this judgment,

taking into account Exts. P8 to P10 guidelines issued by

the Indian Banks' Association approved by the Reserve

Bank of India.
</p>
<p id="p_5">      The writ petition is disposed of, accordingly.


</p><p id="p_6">                                        Sd/-
