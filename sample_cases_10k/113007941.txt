            IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

           R/SPECIAL CIVIL APPLICATION NO. 6754 of 2018
                              With
           R/SPECIAL CIVIL APPLICATION NO. 6755 of 2018
                              With
           R/SPECIAL CIVIL APPLICATION NO. 6756 of 2018
                              With
           R/SPECIAL CIVIL APPLICATION NO. 6758 of 2018
                              With
           R/SPECIAL CIVIL APPLICATION NO. 6759 of 2018
                              With
           R/SPECIAL CIVIL APPLICATION NO. 6760 of 2018

                      KUVERBEN MAFATLAL MAKWANA
                                Versus
                           STATE OF GUJARAT
==========================================================
Appearance:
MR SHITAL R PATEL(2166) for the PETITIONER(s) No. 1
for the RESPONDENT(s) No. 10,11,12,13,14,15,2,3,4,5,6,7,8,9
MS DIVYANGNA JHALA, ASST.GOVERNMENT PLEADER for
RESPONDENT No.1
==========================================================
 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE N.V.ANJARIA

                             Date : 27/04/2018
                           COMMON ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">      The   amendment   tendered   in   Special   Civil 

Application Nos.6755 of 2018, 6756 of 2018 and 6759 

of   2018   is   granted.   The   same   may   be   carried   out 

immediately.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
1.1   These   six   petitions   were   cognate,   which   were 

taken up together and are being disposed of by this 

common order.
</p>
<p id="p_3">

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                 Page 1 of 9</span>
</p><p id="p_4">         C/SCA/6754/2018                             ORDER



1.2   Heard learned advocate Mr.Shital Patel for the 

petitioners and learned Assistant Government Pleader 

Ms.Divyangna Jhala, appearing upon service of copies 

of the petitions in advance.
</p>
<p id="p_5">
</p><p id="p_6">2.   As far as Special Civil Application Nos.6754 of 

2018,  6758   of  2018   and   6760  of   2018  are   concerned, 

the   petitioners   have   sought   to   raise   challenge   to 

common   order   dated   06.10.2017,   passed   by   the 

Secretary,   Revenue   Department   (Appeals),   Ahmedabad, 

which   is   a   common   order   passed   in   Revision 

Application   Nos.63   of   2011,   64   of   2011   and   65   of 

2011, which were preferred by the petitioners herein. 

Thereby   the   revisional   authority   dismissed   the 

Revision   Applications   and   confirmed   order   dated 

06.04.2011 of the Collector, Ahmedabad.



2.1 In the rest of the petitions what is challenged 

is   common   order   dated   17.10.2017   in   Revision 

Application Nos.171 of 2015, 172 of 2015 and 173 of 

2015, passed by the revisional authority.


</p><p id="p_7">3.   The   dispute   dealt   with   by   the   revenue 

authorities was in respect of Mutation Entry No.10235 



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                             Page 2 of 9</span>
         C/SCA/6754/2018                              ORDER



which   was   entered   in   the   revenue   records   on 

13.05.2011. The entry was in relation to land bearing 

Block   Nos.506/4,   505/2   and   506/2   at   village 

Chandkheda,   Taluka   Dascroi.   The   controversy   was 

whether   in   respect   of   the   said   land,   names   of   the 

petitioners were required to be mutated or that the 

entry was to continue in the name of Railways. 



3.1      It   appears   that   upon   three   separate 

applications   made   by   the   predecessors   of   the 

petitioners,   the   Deputy   Collector,   Viramgam, 

concluded that the name of the Railways was wrongly 

figuring   in   the   extract   of   Village   Form   No.7/12   in 

connection   with   the   aforementioned   lands.   According 

to   the   Deputy   Collector,   the   Railway   authorities 

which appeared before him were in dearth of evidence 

to   satisfy   that   the  name   of  the   Railways   which   was 

already there in the revenue records, was required to 

be   continued   by   mutating   entry.   By   order   dated 

20.02.2009, the Deputy Collector removed the name of 

the Railways from the revenue records. 


3.2 It   appears   that   on   07.07.2008,   the   petitioners 

purchased   the   land   in   question   getting   a   Sale   Deed 


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                             Page 3 of 9</span>
         C/SCA/6754/2018                             ORDER



executed, for which Entry No.8936 was certified. The 

petitioners claim that they are the exclusive owners 

on the basis of the said sale and  that the entry was 

rightly mutated. The Railways were aggrieved by the 

order   of   the   Deputy   Collector   dated   20.02.2009, 

therefore,   preferred   three   separate   Revision 

Application Nos.226, 227 and 228 of 2009 before the 

Collector,   Ahmedabad.   The   Collector   by   his   order 

dated   26.04.2011   set   aside   the   order   of   the   Deputy 

Collector   and   directed   the   Railway   authorities   to 

produce the requisite details showing the acquisition 

of the land and make an application to the Mamlatdar 

within one month.
</p>
<p id="p_8">
3.3   It   is   the   case   of   the   petitioners   that   they 

being   the   purchasers   of   the   land   were   vitally 

affected   and   were   necessary  parties.   They   contended 

that   the   Collector   erroneously   allowed   the   Revision 

Applications as per his order dated 26.04.2011. It is 

this   order   dated   26.04.2011   which   was   challenged 

before   the   revisional   authority   by   filing   three 

aforementioned   Revision  Applications   to  end  up   with 

the impugned order.
</p>
<p id="p_9">


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                             Page 4 of 9</span>
</p><p id="p_10">         C/SCA/6754/2018                             ORDER



</p><p id="p_11">4.   It   may   be   noted   that   pursuant   to   the 

aforementioned   order   dated   26.04.2011   of   the 

Collector,   Mutation   Entry   No.10235   was   entered   and 

the   name   of   the   Railways   was   shown.   The   attendant 

proceedings   were   initiated   after   26.04.2011   whereby 

the   Collector   required   the   Railways   to   produce   the 

evidence   relating   to   acquisition   of   the   land.   It 

appears   that   some   documents   were   produced   by   the 

Railways. Entry No.10235 was certified on 01.06.2011. 

Against   the   said   Entry   No.10235,   the   petitioners 

preferred   appeal   before   the   Deputy   Collector   which 

was   rejected   on   30.06.2014.   Further   revisions   were 

preferred before the Collector, Ahmedabad, which were 

also dismissed by common order dated 27.07.2015. 


4.1   Now   the   aforesaid   order   dated   27.07.2015   was 

upheld   by   the   revisional   authority.   Revision 

Application Nos.171, 172 and 173 of 2015 came to be 

dismissed   on   17.10.2017.   It   is   stated   that   against 

the   said   order   of   the   revisional   authority   dated 

17.10.2017, separate Special Civil Applications have 

been   filed.   They   are   Special   Civil   Application 

Nos.6755,  6756   and   6759  of   2018,   which   are  part   of 

this very group.
</p>
<p id="p_12">
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_5">                            Page 5 of 9</span>
</p><p id="p_13">         C/SCA/6754/2018                              ORDER



</p><p id="p_14">5.   Having   carefully   considered   the   facts, 

attentively   gone   through   the   impugned   orders   and 

further   considering   the   submissions   of   both   the 

sides, the challenge in neither of the petitions was 

found   meritorious.   The   facts   show   that   the   case   of 

the   Railways   that   the   lands   were   acquired   for   its 

public purpose deserve  prima facie  acceptability for 

the   purpose   of   maintaining   the   Mutation   Entry   in 

favour   of   the   Railways.   It   was   given   out   that   the 

most part of the lands in question have been utilized 

and   Railway   Colonies   have   been   constructed.   The 

revisional   authority   in   its   impugned   order   dated 

06.10.2017   observed   that   Notification   under   Section 

4(1) of the Land Acquisition Act, 1894, was issued on 

22.08.1961.   This   was   pursuant   to   a   communication 

dated 19.08.1961 by the Land Acquisition Officer. A 

copy of this communication was on record before the 

revenue  authorities  and  that   the   Notification   under 

Section 4(1) was published in the Government Gazette 

on 23.11.1961 at Page No.3060. 
</p>
<p id="p_15">
5.1 The   revisional   authority   has   noticed   from   the 

said   record   that   it     contained   the   details   of 

different lands at Chandkheda which were acquired and 


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_6">                             Page 6 of 9</span>
         C/SCA/6754/2018                             ORDER



the land at Sabarmati Township was also acquired and 

further that there was a joint measurement undertaken 

for   the   said   land   to   be   acquired.   The   Notification 

under   Section   6   was   also   published   and   award   was 

made. The objection against the award was raised by 

the Railways, but subsequently came to be withdrawn. 

The Railways deposited the amount of compensation as 

per award No.123 declared on 10.10.1962.


5.2   It   is   a   clear   finding   recorded   by   the 

revisional authority that in this award the lands in 

question were included. It is further found that the 

land   owners   -   the   petitioners   had   handed   over   the 

possession   for   which   the   Railways   had   addressed   a 

letter dated 26.10.1962. The possession was taken and 

handed over on 30.11.1962 and that the acquired lands 

are   in   possession   of   the   Railways.   Even   the   Land 

Acquisition Case No.3 of 1963 and others were filed 

and   by   a   judgment   dated   20.06.1963,   additional 

compensation was awarded. 
</p>
<p id="p_16">

5.3 All   the   above   attendant   facts   go   to   clearly 

support   the  case   of  the   Railways   that  the   land   was 

acquired for its purpose long back and that since the 


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_7">                             Page 7 of 9</span>
          C/SCA/6754/2018                            ORDER



year 1963­64, its name stood in the revenue records. 

The   claim   purchase   of   land   by   the   petitioners   was 

subsequent   to   the   acquisition.   In   this   view,   the 

challenge   to   the   order   of   the   revenue     authorities 

culminating   into   the   two   orders   of   the   revisional 

authority in Revision Application Nos.63, 64 and 65 

of 2011 as well as in Revision Application Nos. 171, 

172 and 173 of 2015 are not sustainable. 


</p><p id="p_17">6.   Learned   advocate   for   the   petitioners   still 

persisted   that   there   was   no   sufficient   evidence 

showing   the   factum   of   acquisition   of   land.   It   was 

submitted   that   the   petitioners   would   like   to 

challenge the acquisition proceedings in appropriate 

proceedings.   The   petitioners   are   not   precluded   for 

that. 
</p>
<p id="p_18">
6.1 The scope  of this petitions is confined to the 

orders passed by the revenue authorities which are in 

all   respects   eminently   just,   proper   and   legal. 

However,   the   petitioners   are   free   to   pursue   the 

remedy as may be and if available in law to challenge 

the   acquisition   proceedings   and   to   raise   all 

available contentions. 
</p>
<p id="p_19">

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_8">                            Page 8 of 9</span>
</p><p id="p_20">           C/SCA/6754/2018                         ORDER



</p><p id="p_21">7.   In   the   aforesaid   view,   no   case   is   made   out 

whatsoever   warranting   interference   by   this   Court   in 

the   common   order   dated   06.10.2017,   passed   by   the 

revisional   authority   in   Revision   Application   Nos.63 

of 2011, 64 of 2011 and 65 of 2011 as well as common 

order   dated   17.10.2017,   passed   by   the   revisional 

authority in Revision Application Nos. 171 of 2015, 

172 of 2015 and 173 of 2015. 
</p>
<p id="p_22">
</p><p id="p_23">     The petitions are thoroughly meritless. They all 

accordingly stand dismissed.       
</p>
<p id="p_24">
</p><p id="p_25">                                              (N.V.ANJARIA, J.)
