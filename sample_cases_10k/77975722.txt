<div class="doc_title">Sanjay Yadav vs The State Of Bihar on 21 October, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">                  IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                   Criminal Miscellaneous No. 27011 of 2014
                          Arising Out of PS.Case No. -145 Year- 2005 Thana -BELHAR District- BANKA
                  ======================================================
                  Sanjay Yadav Son of Late Mahadeo Yadav Resident of Village- Hardia,
                  Police Station- Belhar, District- Banka.
                                                                    .... .... Petitioner/s
                                                     Versus
                  The State of Bihar
                                                               .... .... Opposite Party/s
                  ======================================================
                  Appearance :
                  For the Petitioner/s            : Mr.
                  For the Opposite Party/s        : Mr.
                  =====================================================
                  CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE AHSANUDDIN
                             AMANULLAH
                                                ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">4.   21.10.2014                         Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and

                  learned A.P.P. for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                       The petitioner seeks bail in Belhar P.S. Case

                  No. 145 of 2005 dated 12.11.2005 instituted under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_1">Sections

                  302</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_2">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code and 27 of <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_3">The Arms Act</a>,

                  1959.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                        A supplementary affidavit has been filed on

                  behalf of the petitioner in which copy of the deposition of the

                  informant during trial has been enclosed.

</p><p id="p_4">                                         Learned counsel for the petitioner submits

                  that though the allegation against the petitioner and others is

                  that they had taken the father of the petitioner with them

                  towards the river front and later his body was found but the

                  allegation against the petitioner is false. It is submitted that

                  during trial, the informant has deposed before the Court and
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">       Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.27011 of 2014 (4) dt.21-10-2014</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                 2/2</span>




                   has specifically stated that the petitioner was not one of the

                   persons who had taken his father to the river front and that he

                   was not involved in the incident and only because of being

                   misguided by certain persons, the petitioner has been named

                   in the F.I.R. Learned counsel submits that the petitioner is in

                   custody since 26.11.2013.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                           Learned A.P.P., does not dispute the aforesaid

                   position.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                           Considering the facts and circumstances of

                   the case and submissions of learned counsel for the parties, let

                   the petitioner be released on bail on furnishing bail bonds of

                   Rs. 10,000/- (ten thousand) with two sureties of the like

                   amount each to the satisfaction of the learned Adhoc A.D.J.-I,

                   Banka in Belhar P.S. Case No. 145 of 2005.                 The petitioner

                   shall cooperate in the trial and be present before the Court on

                   each and every date. Failure to do so on two consecutive dates,

                   without sufficient cause, shall lead to cancellation of his bail

                   bonds.</p><pre id="pre_2">


                                                                (Ahsanuddin Amanullah, J.)

P. Kumar
