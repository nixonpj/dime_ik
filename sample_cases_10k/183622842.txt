BEFORE THE MADURAI BENCH OF MADRAS HIGH COURT               
DATED: 15.02.2018  
CORAM   
THE HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE V.BHARATHIDASAN             

Writ Petition (MD) No.3019 of 2018

M.Moorthy Selvan                                                ...     Petitioner
Vs.
1.The District Collector,
   Pudukottai District,
   Pudukottai.

2.The District Revenue Officer,
   Pudukottai District, Pudukottai.

3.The Revenue Divisional Officer,
   Pudukottai.

4.The Tahsildar,
   Alangudi Taluka,
   Pudukottai District.

5.The Tahsildar,
   Karampakudi Taluka,
   Pudukottai District.                                         ...   Respondents
         Petition filed under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the Constitution of India praying
for issuance of a Writ of Mandamus, to direct the 2nd respondent to initiate
proper enquiry and consequently issue patta to the land property in Survey
No.136/4 Acres 4 Cents 72 and Survey No.159/1 Acres 0 Cents 74 of  
Sulamangalam South and North Village of Alangudi Taluka  Pudukottai District
based on petitioner representation dated 01.08.2017 according to law.

!For Petitioner                 : Mr.S.Muthalraj
For Respondents         :Mrs.J.Padmavathidevi,        
                                                Special Government Pleader. 


:ORDER  
</pre>
<p id="p_1">                This writ petition has been filed seeking direction to the
second respondent to initiate proper enquiry and consequently issue patta to
the property in Survey No.136/4 Acres 4 Cents 72 and Survey No.159/1 Acres 0 
Cents 74 of Sulamangalam South and North Village, Alangudi Taluk, Pudukottai 
District by considering the  representation of the petitioner dated
01.08.2017.
</p><p id="p_2">                2.According to the petitioner, the petitioner has filed an
application before the District Revenue Officer on 01.08.2017 on the ground
that during UDR scheme, patta has been wrongly issued in the name of the
third parties. Subsequently, the District Revenue Officer directed the
concerned Tahsildar to conduct enquiry and file a report but so far no order
has been passed. Hence, the petitioner has filed the present writ petition.
</p><p id="p_3">                3.Considering the facts and circumstances of the case and
considering the fact that the application of the petitioner is pending from
2017, this Court, without going into the merits of the case, directs the
second respondent to consider the representation of the petitioner dated
01.08.2017 and pass appropriate orders, on merits and in accordance with law,
within a period of twelve weeks from the date of receipt  of a copy of this
order, after issuing notice to the petitioner as well as to the interested
parties, if any.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                4.With the above direction, the writ petition is disposed of. No
costs.
</p>
