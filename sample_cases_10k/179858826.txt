<span class="hidden_text" id="span_20">                                                11</span>




2   29-11-2016             The order under challenge dated 14.11.2015 refers to a

                   judgment and order of this Court passed in C.W.J.C. No. 8942 of

                   2001 and C.W.J.C.No.10212 of 2001 preferred by Shri Kashi Nath

                   Singh and Shri Bhagwan Singh respectively questioning their

                   termination/dismissal from the post of Assistant Teachers,

                   Government Basic Schools and while doing so charging the

                   authorities with indulging him in wholesale irregularities in the

                   matter of appointment. The judgment and order passed by the

                   Bench has led to institution of a Vigilance Case as well as the

                   departmental proceeding and it is following which that the

                   petitioners in the batch of writ petitions which is being heard

                   analogous to the present case have been terminated/dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                   Unfortunately neither the writ petitioners whose complaint has led

                   to institution of vigilance case as well as the disciplinary

                   proceeding have been added as a party nor the Investigation

                   Bureau in the Cabinet Vigilance Department who held the enquiry

                   and which is the foundation for the disciplinary proceedings, have

                   been added as a party. Obviously, since the termination order

                   makes reference to the bench decision of this Court in a matter

                   preferred by the two complainants and also rests upon the

                   vigilance enquiry, in my opinion, they are necessary parties to the

                   contest and required a notice. It is next informed by the counsel
