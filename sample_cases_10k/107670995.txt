<p id="p_1">      By   this   order,   I   shall   dispose   of   a   revision   petition   directed 
against  the  order dated 21.3.2014 passed by Sh. Vipin  Kharb, Ld. 
Metropolitan Magistrate.  
</p>


<pre id="pre_2">Criminal Revision No. 19/2014                                              Page 1 / 3
 Dheeraj Kapoor Vs M/s Bharat Ispat       2              Order dt. 6.6.2014

</pre><p id="p_2">       The   impugned  order  was passed  by  trial  court  in  a  criminal 
complaint under Section 138 of Negotiable Instruction Act filed by 
the respondent herein namely M/s Bharat Ispat.   In this complaint, 
the   present   petitioner   Dheeraj   Kapoor   was   mentioned   as   accused 
number 2.   In the impugned order, Ld. Metropolitan Magistrate has 
mentioned   that  "complainant   is   not   pursuing   the   complaint 
against accused no.2 Dheeraj Kapoor".  However, in the statement 
of Kartik Bansla, the proprietor of M/s Bharat Ispat, it is written that 
he was pursuing the case against accused no.2 Dheeraj Kapoor.
</p><p id="p_3">       In   the   impugned   order,   Ld.   Metropolitan   Magistrate   has 
ordered to issue summons to "accused persons for 14.8.2014".
</p><p id="p_4">       It   is   submitted   by   Ld.   Counsel   for   petitioner   that   summons 
were   served   upon   accused   no.2   Dheeraj   Kapoor   i.e.   the   present 
petitioner/revisionist.     Hence,   he   has   preferred   revision   petition 
against the impugned order.
</p><p id="p_5">       I have perused the impugned order.   I am of the opinion that 
clearly   there   is   some   typographical   mistake   in   the   order   and   the 
statement   of   Kartik   Bansal   dated   21.3.2014.     Although   criminal 
courts   cannot   review   their   own   orders,   however,   they   are   fully 
empowered to remove clerical, typographical, inadvertent mistakes 
or defects under <a href="/doc/789330/" id="a_1">Section 362</a> CrPC. 
</p><p id="p_6">       Accordingly,   the   present  revision  petition  is disposed  of  with 
the   direction   to   Ld.   Metropolitan   Magistrate   that   he   shall   specify, 


Criminal Revision No. 19/2014                                            Page 2 / 3
 Dheeraj Kapoor Vs M/s Bharat Ispat     3             Order dt. 6.6.2014

after   the   necessary  correction  in  the  order, as to whether  accused 
no.2   Dheeraj   Kapoor   has   been   summoned   by   him   or   not.     The 
revisionist   herein   shall   moved   appropriate   application   praying   for 
correction   in   the   order   dated   21.3.2014   before   Ld.   Metropolitan 
Magistrate   and   Ld.   Metropolitan   Magistrate   after   giving   opposite 
party a chance to be heard, shall pass a specific order mentioning if 
accused   no.2   Dheeraj   Kapoor   was   summoned   vide   order   dated 
21.3.2014 or was not summoned by the impugned order.  
</p><p id="p_7">      The revision petition is disposed of accordingly.  Copy of order 
along with the TCR be returned to the trial court.   Revision file be 
consigned to record room.
</p><p id="p_8">Announced in the open
court on 6.6.2014.</p><pre id="pre_3">
