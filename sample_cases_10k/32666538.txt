<div class="doc_title">Smt. Basanti @ Champa Kashyapi vs Sri Nemai Chandra Mondal And Ors on 8 August, 2017</div><p id="p_1"><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                             1</span>


    Sl. AD 24
    08-08-2017
    S.d.                             C O 2544 of 2016



                                Smt. Basanti @ Champa Kashyapi
</p><p id="p_2">                                             -versus-
</p><p id="p_3">.                               Sri Nemai Chandra Mondal and ors.
</p>
<p id="p_4">


</p><p id="p_5">                             Mr. Subrata Roy Karmakar
                                            ....for the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_6">


                          In view of the order proposed to be made, no previous

                 service is required to be effected on the opposite parties. The

                 petitioner will, however, remain obliged to immediately forward

                 copies of the petition and this order to the opposite parties.

</p><p id="p_7">                          The petitioner complains of an amended written statement

                 being taken on record by the trial court despite it being filed beyond

                 the time period permitted by this court.

</p><p id="p_8">                          The second grievance of the petitioner is that the amended

                 written statement is not verified by an affidavit since the affidavit

                 portion is unsigned. The third grievance of the petitioner is that

                 despite a previous order of this court directing the appeal to be

                 disposed of within a particular period by the order dated September

                 29, 2016, the appeal has not yet been disposed of.
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                2</span>


            As far as the first ground urged, the trial court has due

authority to enlarge the time unless the order by which this court

directed a thing to be done specifically precludes the authority of

the trial court to be exercised in such regard. As to the second

grievance, it will be open to the petitioner to urge whatever grounds

may be available to the petitioner including that the contents of the

amended written statement should not be taken into account since

it may not be supported by any affidavit.

</p><p id="p_9">            Apropos the third grievance of the petitioner, the District

Judge, Hooghly is requested to look into the matter and prevail on

the Additional District Judge, 1st Court at Hooghly          to dispose of

Title Appeal No. 266 of 2010 within two months of the receipt of a

copy of this order. The District Judge will ensure that directions

issued by this court for the expeditious disposal of any matter are

adhered to.
</p>
<p id="p_10">            CO 2544 of 2017 is disposed of, but without any order as

to costs.
</p>
<p id="p_11">            Urgent certified website copies of this order, if applied for,

be made available to the parties upon compliance with the requisite

formalities.
</p>
<p id="p_12">

</p><p id="p_13">                                     (Sanjib Banerjee, J.)
