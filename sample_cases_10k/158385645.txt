<p id="p_1">H.G.RAMESH, J. (Oral):
</p>
<p id="p_2">1.    Learned Counsel appearing for the petitioners in both

the writ petitions submit that the petitioners will not press

their respective writ petitions. Their submission is accepted.

</p><p id="p_3">2.    However, learned Counsel on both sides pray for a

direction to the trial Court not to disburse the amount lying

in deposit with it to any of the parties till the disposal of the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                               -4-</span>
                                    WP No.49412/2012 C/W
                                        WP No.48261/2012

suit. Accordingly, the trial Court is directed not to disburse

the amount to any of the parties till the disposal of the suit.


</p><p id="p_4">3.    At this stage, the learned Counsel pray for a direction

to the trial Court to dispose of the suit expeditiously

preferably within nine months. They submit that the suit is

of the year 2011 and the parties will fully cooperate with

the trial Court for expeditious disposal of the suit. As the

suit is of the year 2011, the trial Court is directed to

dispose of the suit expeditiously and in any event within

nine months from the date of receipt of a copy of this order.

The trial Court shall avoid seeking for any extension of time

for disposal of the suit.
</p>
<p id="p_5">      The writ petitions are disposed of in the above terms.
