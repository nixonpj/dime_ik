<div class="doc_title">Shikalchand vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 9 June, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                        MCRC-9622-2016
             (SHIKALCHAND Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)


09-06-2016

      Shri Manish Tiwari, learned counsel for the
applicant.
      Shri Pradeep Gupta, learned Panel Lawyer for the
respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Heard.
</p><p id="p_2">      This is the first bail application filed on behalf of the
applicant/accused under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> of the Cr.P.C. for
grant of anticipatory bail, as he is under apprehension of
his arrest, in connection with Crime No.208/2015
registered at Police Station Roopjhar District Balaghat
for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_2">Section 420</a> of the IPC
and Section 9(1), 9(3) of M.P. Adim Janjatio (Vriksho Ka
Hit) Sanrakshan Adhiniyam, 1999.
</p><p id="p_3">      The applicant was working as Additional Collector
District Balaghat. The allegation against the applicant is
that he had illegally granted permission to cut trees. In
other case of similar nature, applicant has been enlarged
on anticipatory bail. This Court passed passed following
order in M.Cr.C. No.7465/2016 :
</p>

<blockquote id="blockquote_1">             "Shri Manish Tiwari, learned
         counsel for applicant.</blockquote>
<p id="p_4">             Smt. Shobhna Sharma, learned
         Panel Lawyer for the non-applicant/
 State.
</p><p id="p_5">      Heard.
</p><p id="p_6">      Perused the case diary.
</p><p id="p_7">      This is first application filed by the
applicant under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_3">Section 438</a> of the
Cr.P.C. for grant of anticipatory bail as
he apprehends his arrest in connection
with crime No. 190/2015, registered at
Police Station-Baihar District- Balaghat,
against him for the offence punishable
under <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_4">Sections 420</a> of IPC and <a href="/doc/811293/" id="a_5">Section
9</a> (1) and 9 (3) of M.P. Adim Janjatio
(Vraksho Ka Hit) Sanrakshan
Adhiniyam, 1999.
</p><p id="p_8">      The applicant is working as
Additional Collector, Baihar, district
Balaghat.
</p><p id="p_9">      The allegation against the
applicant is that he had granted
permission to cut the trees in regard to
Scheduled Tribe persons. However he
was not authorized to do so.
</p><p id="p_10">      Learned counsel for the applicant
submits that the Collector had
authorized the applicant to grant
permission. Apart from this, the
applicant had passed the order in
official capacity and for the purpose of
 prosecution of the applicant, the
sanction from the Government to
prosecute him under <a href="/doc/12704/" id="a_6">Section 197</a> of
Cr.P.C. has not been obtained. Other
persons have been granted anticipatory
bail, vide order dated 26.04.2016 in
M.Cr.C. No. 6582 of 2016. The court
passed following order :-
</p><p id="p_11">      "Shri Manish Tiwari, learned
counsel for applicant.
</p><p id="p_12">      Shri A.N. Gupta, learned Panel
Lawyer for the non-applicant/State.
</p><p id="p_13">      Heard.
</p><p id="p_14">      This is first application filed by the
applicant under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_7">Section 438</a> of the
Cr.P.C. for grant of anticipatory bail as
he apprehends his arrest in connection
with crime No. 138/2015, registered at
Police Station- Birsa, District- Balaghat,
against him for the offence punishable
under <a href="/doc/1128948/" id="a_8">Sections 120(B)</a>, <a href="/doc/1243353/" id="a_9">342</a>, <a href="/doc/886598/" id="a_10">365</a>,
<a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_11">420</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_12">34</a> of the IPC Section 3(1)(10) of
SC/ST (Prevention of Atrocities) Act
and 9(3) of M.P. Adim Janjatio (Vraksho
Ka Hit) Sanrakshan Adhiniyam, 1999.
</p><p id="p_15">      The applicant was working as joint
Collector at the relevant time. The
allegation against the applicant is that
 he had imposed nominal fine against
the persons who were involved in
illegal cutting of trees. The applicant
has already retired from service. In
another case registered vide crime no.
135/2015 in regard to same act, this
court has granted anticipatory bail to
the applicant. The court passed the
following order:-
</p><p id="p_16">      "Shri Manish Tiwari, learned
counsel for the applicant.
</p><p id="p_17">      Shri A.N.Gupta, learned Panel
Lawyer for the respondent/State.
</p><p id="p_18">      This is first application filed by the
applicants under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_13">Section 438</a> of the
Cr.P.C. for grant of anticipatory bail as
they apprehend their arrest in
connection with crime No. 135/2015,
registered at Police Station- Birsa,
DistrictBalaghat against them for the
offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1128948/" id="a_14">Sections
120(B)</a>, <a href="/doc/1243353/" id="a_15">342</a>, <a href="/doc/886598/" id="a_16">365</a>, <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_17">420</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_18">34</a> of IPC,
3(1)(10) of SC/SC (PA) Act and 9(3) of
MP Adim Janjatio (Vraksho ka hit)
Sanrakshan Adhiniyam, 1999.
</p><p id="p_19">      The applicant is a retired person.
At the relevant time, he was working as
Joint Collector. The allegation against
 him is that he had imposed nominal
amount of penalty for illegal act of
cutting trees and thereafter the trees
were sold. Looking to the age of the
applicant and nature of the case, the
application is allowed. It is ordered that
in the event of arrest of the applicant in
connection with the aforesaid crime
number and the offences, applicant be
released on bail on his furnishing a
personal bond in the sum of Rs.
50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand only)
by the applicant with one solvent surety
of the like amount to the satisfaction of
the Investigating Officer/ Arresting
Authority.
</p><p id="p_20">      The applicant is directed to join
the investigation immediately and to
cooperate with the investigating
agency. He will further abide by the
conditions enumerated in sub Section 2
of Section 438 of the <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_19">Cr.P.C</a>.
</p><p id="p_21">      Certified copy as per rules."
      Learned Panel Lawyer opposes
the prayer.
</p><p id="p_22">Looking to the aforesaid facts and
nature of the case, but without
expressing any opinion on the merits of
 the case, the application is allowed. It is
ordered that in the event of arrest of
the applicant in connection with the
aforesaid crime number and the
offences, applicant be released on bail
on his furnishing a personal bond in the
sum of Rs. 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty
Thosuand only) with one solvent surety
of the like amount to the satisfaction of
the Investigating Officer/ Arresting
Authority.
</p><p id="p_23">      The applicant is directed to join
the investigation immediately and to
cooperate with the investigating
agency. He will further abide by the
conditions enumerated in sub-Section 2
of Section 438 of the <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_20">Cr.P.C</a>.
</p><p id="p_24">      Certified copy as per rules."
      Looking to the aforesaid facts of
the case, without expressing any
opinion on merits of the case, the
application is allowed. It is ordered that
in the event of arrest of the applicant in
connection with the aforesaid crime
number and the offences, applicant,
Sikalchand Paraste be released on bail
on his furnishing a personal bond in the
sum of Rs. 50,000/-(Rupees Fifty
         Thousand only) by the applicant with
        one solvent surety of the like amount to
        the satisfaction of the Investigating
        Officer/ Arresting Authority.
</p><p id="p_25">             The applicant is directed to join
        the investigation immediately and to
        cooperate with the investigating
        agency. He will further abide by the
        conditions enumerated in sub Section 2
        of Section 438 of the <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_21">Cr.P.C</a>.
</p><p id="p_26">             Certified copy as per rules."
</p>

<p id="p_27">     The facts of the present case is similar to that of
M.Cr.C. No. 7465/2016. Hence, the application is
allowed. It is ordered that in the event of arrest of the
applicant in connection with the aforesaid crime number
and the offences, applicant Shikalchand be released on
bail on his furnishing a personal bond in the sum of
Rs.50,000/- (Rs. fifty thousands) with one solvent
surety of the like amount to the satisfaction of the
Investigating Officer/Arresting Authority.
    The applicant is directed to join the investigation
immediately and to co-operate with the investigating
agency. He will further abide by the condition
enumerated in sub-section 2 of Section 438 of the <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_22">Cr.P.C</a>.
    Certified copy as per rules.
</p><p id="p_28">              (S.K. GANGELE)
               V. JUDGE
vkt/Rashid
