<div class="doc_title">Bhupendra vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 29 August, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                 M.Cr.C.No.14050/2017
29.08.2017
     Mr.Sourabh       Singh,   learned         counsel   for   the
applicant.
     Mr.Arvind Singh, learned Government Advocate
for the respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">     This application under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of the Code of
Criminal Procedure, 1973 has been filed for the grant of
bail to the applicant who has been arrested in connection
with Crime No.569/2016 for offences under <a href="/doc/1086076/" id="a_2">sections 294</a>,
<a href="/doc/647828/" id="a_3">307</a>, <a href="/doc/1983271/" id="a_4">302</a>, <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_5">304-B</a> read with <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_6">section 34</a> IPC registered at
Police Station-Banda, District-Sagar.
</p><p id="p_2">     According   to    the   case   of   the    prosecution,   the
applicant herein is the brother-in-law of the deceased
who is in judicial custody since 03.11.2016. Learned
counsel for the State has argued that there is a dying
declaration of the deceased, in which, the active role of
burning her is attributed to the applicant herein along
with the co-accused Gajendra. Per contra, learned counsel
for the applicant has drawn the attention of this Court to
the statements of the parents recorded before the trial
Court. PW-1 is the real sister of the deceased who has
turned hostile and has stated that her sister, set herself
on fire after a quarrel with her younger sister-in-law and
it was her husband who tried to save her by approaching
from the roof of the room. She is also stated that her
sister had a very short temper similarly PW-2 is the
mother of the deceased who has stated that her daughter
was a person with a very short temper and that she had
      committed suicide by setting herself on fire. Likewise, the
     father of the deceased has also turned hostile and has
     stated that his daughter set herself on fire. In all, four
     prosecution    witnesses      have   been   examined   from   the
     family of the deceased and each of them have turned
     hostile.
</p><p id="p_3">            Looking at the facts and circumstances of the case
     and the fact that the principal witnesses in this case have
     all turned hostile and absolved the applicant herein, the
     application is allowed and it is directed that the applicant
     herein be enlarged on bail upon his furnishing a personal
     bond    in   the   sum   of    Rs.50,000/-     ( Rupees   Fifty
     Thousand Only ) with one solvent surety in the like
     amount to the satisfaction of the trial Court.
</p><p id="p_4">            C.C. as per rules.
</p>

<p id="p_5">                                             (ATUL SREEDHARAN)
                                                   JUDGE
