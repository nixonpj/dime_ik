<div class="doc_title">Abhilash vs Ali on 2 March, 2010</div><pre id="pre_1">       

  

  

 
 
  IN THE HIGH COURT OF KERALA AT ERNAKULAM

Crl.MC.No. 717 of 2010()


1. ABHILASH,S/O.MOHANAN,KURIKULAM HOUSE,
                      ...  Petitioner

                        Vs



1. ALI,S/O.PAREKKUTTY,AGED 57 YEARS,
                       ...       Respondent

2. STATE OF KERALA,THROUGH THE S.I.OF

                For Petitioner  :DR.V.N.SANKARJEE

                For Respondent  :SRI.SAKIR.K.H.

The Hon'ble MR. Justice M.SASIDHARAN NAMBIAR

 Dated :02/03/2010

 O R D E R
               M.Sasidharan Nambiar, J.
              --------------------------
                Crl.M.C.No.717 of 2010
              --------------------------

                         ORDER
</pre>


<p id="p_1">    Petitioner is the accused and first respondent,

the defacto complainant in C.C.No.1233/2004 on the file

of  Judicial   First   Class   Magistrate's   Court-II,

Ernakulam,  taken  cognizance for   the  offence  under

Section 511 of 379 of Indian Peal Code. Prosecution

case  is  that  on  24.4.2004  at  about   12.45  p.m.,

petitioner attempted to commit theft of a bicycle

belonging to the first respondent and thereby committed

the offence. This petition is filed under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a>

of Code of Criminal Procedure to quash the proceedings

contending  that   entire  disputes   with  the   first

respondent  were   settled   amicably   and   in   such

circumstances, it is not in the interest of justice to

continue the prosecution.
</p>
<p id="p_2">    2. First respondent appeared through a counsel and

filed an affidavit stating that entire disputes with

the  petitioner  were  settled  amicably  due  to   the

intervention of mediators and he has no subsisting

grievance against the petitioner and therefore, he has

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">CRMC 717/10                 2</span>


no objection for quashing the proceedings.

</p><p id="p_3">     3. Learned counsel appearing for the petitioner,

first respondent and learned Public Prosecutor were

heard.
</p>
<p id="p_4">     4. As is clear from Annexure-A1 final report, the

offence alleged against the petitioner is that he

attempted to commit theft of a bicycle belonging to the

first   respondent.  The   offence  alleged  is  purely

personal   in  nature.  Affidavit  filed by  the  first

respondent   establishes that   there  was an  amicable

settlement of the disputes between the parties. As held

by the Apex Court in <a href="/doc/1442974/" id="a_2">Madan Mohan Abbot v. State of

Punjab</a> (2008 (3) KLT 19), when there has been a

settlement of the disputes, it is not in the interest

of justice to continue the prosecution.

</p><p id="p_5">     Petition is allowed. C.C.No.1233/2004 on the file

of   Judicial   First   Class   Magistrate's  Court-II,

Ernakulam is quashed.
</p>
<p id="p_6">

2nd March, 2010            (M.Sasidharan Nambiar, Judge)

</p>
