<div class="doc_title">Dhikonda Anjaneyulu vs The State Of Andhra Pradesh on 31 October, 2018</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: A.Rajasheker Reddy</div>

<pre id="pre_1">      HON'BLE SRI JUSTICE A.RAJASHEKER REDDY
             WRIT PETITON No.39151 of 2018
ORDER:
</pre>
<p id="p_1">      It is the case of the petitioner that he            made a

representation    on   20-06-2018     to    the   respondents     for

cancellation of registered sale deed bearing document No.1699

of 2007, dated 04-05-2007 as the same was executed illegally

without having any       right   over the    subject   property   by

respondents 3 to 5 in favour of 6th respondent. As no action

has been taken, the present writ petition is filed.

</p><p id="p_2">       Learned counsel for the petitioner has not shown any

provision of law empowering the 2nd respondent to cancel the

registered sale deed. Learned Assistant Government Pleader for

Revenue also submits that once registration of document is

done, the same cannot be cancelled by them unilaterally and

parties have to approach the civil Court.

</p><p id="p_3">      In view of the same, I do not see any reason to entertain

the writ petition and accordingly the same is dismissed.

However, it is open for the petitioner to avail alternative

remedies available to him under law. There shall be no order as

to costs. Consequently, miscellaneous petitions, if any, pending

in this Writ Petition shall stand closed.



</p><p id="p_4">                                       _________________________
                                      A.RAJASHEKER REDDY,J
