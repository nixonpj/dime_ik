<div class="doc_title">Mahendra Yadav vs State Of Bihar on 12 January, 2010</div><pre id="pre_1">                 IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                        Cr.Misc. No.28578 of 2009
                 DWARIKA YADAV SON OF INDER YADAV
                 RESIDENT OF VILLAGE FIROJI,P.S.KAKO,DISTT.JEHANABAD
                                  Versus
                              STATE OF BIHAR
                                   with
                        Cr.Misc. No.45835 of 2009
                 MAHENDRA YADAV SON OF LATE KHIRAHUN YADAV
                 RESIDENT OF VILLAGE FIROJI,P.S.KAKO,DISTT.JEHANABAD
                                  Versus
                              STATE OF BIHAR
                               -----------
</pre>
<p id="p_1">4/   12.1.2010                 Both the above applications have been

                    heard together and are being disposed of by

                    this common order because they have arisen

                    out of one occurrence.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                               The petitioners are accused of a case

                    which has been registered for offence under

                    <a href="/doc/94452/" id="a_1">Section 414</a> of the Indian Penal Code.

</p><p id="p_3">                               Submission    is    that    the    petitioners

                    have been falsely implicated and nothing has

                    been     recovered       from         their      conscious

                    possession.      The    articles       seized    are     not

                    stolen articles and the petitioners                   are in

                    custody since 28.4.2009.
</p>
<pre id="pre_2">                               Considering         the        facts          and

                    circumstances     of    the    case,    let     the    above

</pre><p id="p_4">                    named petitioners of both the applications be

                    released on bail on furnishing bail bonds of

                    Rs.10,000/-(ten        thousand)       each     with     two

                    sureties    of   the    like    amount    each    to     the

                    satisfaction      of     learned       Chief      Judicial
           Magistrate, Jehanabad in     in Pali P.S.Case

          No. 07 of 2009.
</p>
<p id="p_5">


</p><p id="p_6">                            ( Shyam Kishore Sharma, J.)
Tahir/-
</p>
