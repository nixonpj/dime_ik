<div class="doc_title">Munni Devi W/O Shri Shiv Singh B/C ... vs State Of Rajasthan on 3 April, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">       HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR RAJASTHAN
                   BENCH AT JAIPUR

      S.B. Criminal Miscellaneous Fourth Bail No. 3237/2019

Munni Devi W/o Shri Shiv Singh B/c Thakur, R/o Bhava Sahab Ka
Bada, Police Station Nihalganj, Distt. Dholpur Raj. (At Present
Confined In Distt. Jail Dholpur)
                                                                  ----Petitioner
                                   Versus
State Of Rajasthan, through P.P.
                                                                ----Respondent</pre>


<p id="p_1">For Petitioner(s)         :    Mr. Govind Prasad Rawat.
</p><pre id="pre_2">For State(s)              :    Mr. Arvind Bhadu, PP.



          HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE PANKAJ BHANDARI

                          Judgment / Order

03/04/2019

</pre><p id="p_2">1.   Petitioner has filed this fourth bail application under Section

439 of Cr.P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_3">
</p><p id="p_4">2.   F.I.R. No.31/2012 was registered at Police Station Mahila

Thana, Dholpur, for offence under Sections 498A, 304B in

alternate 302 of I.P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_5">
</p><p id="p_6">3.   It is contended by counsel for the petitioner that petitioner

has remained in custody for a period of more than one and half

years. The allegations which are leveled against the petitioner

were also leveled against husband and one of the sons of the

petitioner. It is contended that brother-in-law and father-in-law

have not been made accused by the police. Out of list of thirty one

witnesses, twenty one have been examined. Conclusion of trial will

take time.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                    (Downloaded on 27/06/2019 at 10:15:03 PM)</span>
</p><p id="p_8">                                                                         (2 of 2)                   [CRLMB-3237/2019]


</p><p id="p_9">                                   4.    Learned Public Prosecutor has opposed the fourth bail

                                   application.
</p>
<p id="p_10">
</p><p id="p_11">                                   5.    I have considered the contentions.
</p>
<p id="p_12">
</p><p id="p_13">                                   6.    Considering the contentions put forth by counsel for the

                                   petitioner, I deem it proper to allow the fourth bail application.


</p><p id="p_14">                                   7.    This fourth bail application is accordingly allowed and it is

                                   directed that accused petitioner shall be released on bail provided

                                   she furnishes a personal bond in the sum of Rs.50,000/- (Rupees

                                   Fifty Thousand only) together with two sureties in the sum of

                                   Rs.25,000/- (Rupees Twenty Five Thousand only) each                      to the

                                   satisfaction of the learned trial court with the stipulation that she

                                   shall appear before that Court and any court to which the matter

                                   is transferred, on all subsequent dates of hearing and as and when

                                   called upon to do so.
</p>
<p id="p_15">
</p><p id="p_16">                                   8.    Copy of this order be sent to the concerned Court by

                                   fax/email today itself with a direction to serve a photostat copy of

                                   the same to the petitioner and an additional copy of this order be

                                   also sent to the concerned Court by ordinary process.




</p><p id="p_17">                                                                                          (PANKAJ BHANDARI),J.
</p>
