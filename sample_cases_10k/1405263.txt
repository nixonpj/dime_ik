<p id="p_1">	Rule.
</p><p id="p_2">Mr. Divyesh Sejpal, learned APP waives service of rule on behalf of
the opponent no.1 State and Mr. Y.N. Ravani, learned  counsel waives
service of Rule on behalf of opponent no.2. Having regard to the
facts and circumstances of the case, this application is taken up for
hearing today. 
</p>
 

 


 

<p id="p_3">	This
is an application preferred by the applicant under Section 389[1] of
the Code of Criminal Procedure seeking bail against the judgment and
order of conviction and sentence passed by the learned Special Judge,
C.B.I. Court No.3, Mirzapur, Ahmedabad in  Special Case No. 39 of
1994 dated  30.4.2010 by which the learned Judge has convicted the
applicant for a period of two years' RI and to pay  fine of Rs.
5000/- for each of the offences punishable under Sections 120B and
under sections 420, 467, 468 and 471 read with <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_1">section 120B</a> of Indian
Penal Code and  under <a href="/doc/86648814/" id="a_2">section 13</a>[2] read with <a href="/doc/86648814/" id="a_3">section 13</a>[1][d] of the
<a href="/doc/1331755/" id="a_4">Prevention of Corruption Act</a>, 1988. The aggregate amount of fine
imposed by the learned Judge is to the tune of Rs. 30,000/-, in
default of payment of fine, further RI for one month. 
</p>
 

 


 

	
<p id="p_4">Considering the fact that the amount of fine is paid  and since the
appeal is arising from the short sentence imposed by the learned
Judge and considering the pendency of large number of matters, I am
of the view that the applicant deserves to be enlarged on bail on
same bail and fresh bond with usual terms and conditions.
</p> 

 


 

<p id="p_5">	In
the meanwhile, substantive sentence imposed by the learned Special
Judge shall remain suspended during pendency and final disposal of
the appeal. 
</p>
 

 


 

<p id="p_6">	Rule
is made absolute to the aforesaid extent. 
</p>
 

<p id="p_7">	Direct
service is permitted.
</p> 

 


 

<p id="p_8">[H.B.
</p><p id="p_9">ANTANI, J.]
 

pirzada/-
</p>
