<div class="doc_title">Lallaram vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 10 January, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1"> 1 Lallaram V. State of MP.
                                             M.Cr.C. No.14283/2016

10.1.2017
       Shri FA Shah, Advocate for the petitioner.
       Shri Arun Barua Panel Lawyer for the respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">       Case Diary is not available. Case is being decided on the
basis of the chargesheet filed by the petitioner.
</p><p id="p_2">       Learned counsel for the rival parties are heard.
       The applicant has filed this first application u/S 439, <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_1">Cr.P.C</a>.
for grant of bail. The applicant has been arrested by Police Station
Kolaras, District Shivpuri in connection with Crime No.62/2015
registered in relation to the offences punishable u/Ss. 394 of
<a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_2">IPC</a>, 11/13 of MPDVPK Adhiniyam and Sec. 25/27 <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_3">Arms Act</a>.
</p><p id="p_3">       Learned Public Prosecutor for the State opposed the
application and prayed for its rejection by contending that on the
basis of the allegations and the material available on record no
case for grant of bail is made out.
</p><p id="p_4">       The     applicant is   in custody since 17.10.2016. Subject
matter of robbery which is Bolero Geep, two mobile phones and
two Shawls have been recovered from the other co-accused.
Further custodial interrogation may not be not necessary. The
applicant has no criminal antecedents.
</p><p id="p_5">       In view of the above and that prolonged pre-trial detention
being an anathema to the concept of liberty, this Court is inclined
to extend the benefit of bail to the applicant but with certain
stringent conditions in view of criminal antecedents being
presumed to be on the safer side in the absence of the case diary.
</p><p id="p_6">       Accordingly, without expressing any opinion on merits of the
case, this application is allowed and it is directed that the applicant
be released on bail on furnishing a personal bond in the sum of
Rs. 100,000/- (Rs. One Lac only) with two solvent sureties of
      2 <a href="/doc/192617195/" id="a_4">Lallaram V. State of MP</a>.
</p><p id="p_7">                                                 M.Cr.C. No.14283/2016

     the like amount to the satisfaction of the concerned trial Court.
</p><p id="p_8">           This order will remain operative subject to compliance of the
     following conditions by the applicant :-
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">           1.        The applicant will comply with all the terms and
           conditions of the bond executed by him;
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">           2.        The      applicant     will  cooperate     in     the
           investigation/trial, as the case may be;
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">           3.        The applicant will not indulge himself in extending
           inducement, threat or promise to any person acquainted
           with the facts of the case so as to dissuade him/her from
           disclosing such facts to the Court or to the Police Officer, as
           the case may be;
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_4">           4.        The applicant shall not commit an offence similar
           to the offence of which he is accused;
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_5">           5.        The applicant will not seek unnecessary
           adjournments during the trial; and
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_6">           6.        The applicant will not leave India without previous
           permission of the trial Court/Investigating Officer, as the
           case may be.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_7">           7.        The applicant shall mark his presence before the
           concerned trial court once every fortnight.</blockquote>
<p id="p_9">           A copy of this order be sent to the Court concerned for
     compliance.
</p><p id="p_10">           C.c. as per rules.</p><pre id="pre_2">


                                                       (Sheel Nagu)
ar                                                       Judge
