                                    Mrinal Kanti Ghosh

<p id="p_4">                     Mr. Shibaji Kumar Das
                                         ... For the Petitioner
                     Mr. Saibalendu Bhowmick,
                     Mr. Biplab Guha
                                     ... For the WBMC


                     An order passed by the Appellate Authority exercising
               jurisdiction under the provisions of the Bengal Medical Act,
               1914 is under challenge.
</p><p id="p_5">                     Learned Advocate appearing for the West Bengal
               Medical Council submits that the writ petition is not
               maintainable in view of <a href="/doc/2634963/" id="a_1">Section 24</a> of the Indian Medical
               Council Act, 1956.
</p><p id="p_6">                     Without prejudice to the point of maintainability, let
               affidavits be filed.
</p><p id="p_7">                     Let the affidavit in opposition be filed within two weeks
               after reopening of the Court. Reply, if any thereto be filed
               within one week. The writ petition will be treated as ready
               for   hearing   immediately     on   completion   of   the   time
               stipulated for filing affidavits.
</p><p id="p_8">                     Liberty is given to the parties to mention the matter
               for early hearing.
</p>


<p id="p_9">                                          (Debangsu Basak, J.)
