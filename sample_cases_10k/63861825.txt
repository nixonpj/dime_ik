<div class="doc_title">Seeta Singh vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 18 July, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                               WP-10018-2017
                 (SEETA SINGH Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)


18-07-2017
        Shri P.N. Dubey, counsel for the petitioner.
        Shri Swapnil Gangully, Government Advocate for the State.
        Heard.
        Petitioner is aggrieved by her transfer to Lodha which as Shri Dubey is
about 100 kms. away. Petitioner contends that she has been transferred to a
remote place during rainy season. Petitioner's husband is working in SAF at
Rewa. It is prayed that petitioner's transfer may be set aside.
        In the peculiar factual matrix of the case, I deem it proper to dispose of
this petition with the following directions:
        (i) Petitioner may file a detailed representation taking all possible grounds
against his transfer and submit it before respondent No.3 within seven working</pre>
<p id="p_1">days from today, failing which interim protection will not be available to him;
</p><p id="p_2">        (ii) If representation is preferred by the petitioner within aforesaid time,
the respondent No.3 shall consider and decide it in accordance with law;
</p><p id="p_3">        (iii) Till decision of representation, the impugned order Annexure P/1
shall remain stayed.
</p><p id="p_4">     Petition is disposed of without expressing any opinion on merits.
</p>

<p id="p_5">                                                              (SUJOY PAUL)
                                                                 JUDGE
