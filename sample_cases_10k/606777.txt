<div class="doc_title">Robin Gupta, S/O-Late Rakesh ... vs State Of U.P.,Thru. Secretary, ... on 2 April, 2010</div><pre id="pre_1">Court No. - 25

Case :- MISC. BENCH No. - 2934 of 2010

Petitioner :- Robin Gupta, S/O-Late Rakesh Kumar Gupta,
Respondent :- State Of U.P.,Thru. Secretary, Home &amp; Others
Petitioner Counsel :- Kapil Gupta,Alok Singh
Respondent Counsel :- G.A.

Hon'ble Abdul Mateen,J.
</pre><p id="p_1">Hon'ble Vedpal,J.
</p><p id="p_2">Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned Additional
Government Advocate.
</p><p id="p_3">Under challenge in the instant writ petition is FIR relating to Case
Crime No. 84 of 2010, under <a href="/doc/959623/" id="a_1">Sections 458</a>, <a href="/doc/329571/" id="a_2">392</a>, <a href="/doc/812083/" id="a_3">411</a>, <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_4">307</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_5">34</a> IPC, police
station Mahanagar, district Lucknow.
We have gone through the FIR.
</p><p id="p_4">It has been argued by learned counsel for the petitioner that the
impugned FIR has been registered against two persons, who were
arrested by the police personnel, only and there is nothing said about
any unknown person and in-spite of that the petitioner is being harassed
unnecessarily by the police personnel.
Since the FIR discloses commission of cognizable offence, the same
cannot be quashed.
</p><p id="p_5">However, it is provided that the petitioner shall not be arrested in the
aforesaid case crime number unless and until some cogent and reliable
evidence comes forward against him, provided he cooperates with the
investigation, which shall go on.
</p><p id="p_6">With the aforesaid observations/directions, the writ petition is disposed
off finally.
</p><p id="p_7">Order Date :- 2.4.2010
Pradeep/-
</p>
