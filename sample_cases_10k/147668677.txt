<div class="doc_title">Shiv Raj Singh Sisodiya vs Union Of India Thru Central Bureau ... on 9 January, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                      W.P. No.8492/2016

9.01.2017

      Shri A.S. Parihar, learned counsel for the petitioner.
      Shri Vikas Jain, learned counsel for the respondents.</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Heard on the question of admission.
</p><p id="p_2">      By this writ petiton under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the Constitution of
India, the petioner is praying for setting aside of order dated
17/10/2006 passed by the authority and 27/09/2007 by which
the appeal has been dismissed.
</p><p id="p_3">      It is not in dispute that licence was granted in favour of
Nahar Singh Rajput for the year 2005-2006, who happens to be
the grandfather of petitoner for cultivbation of Opium.
</p><p id="p_4">      Learned counsel for the petitioner has submitted that
during the life time of grandfather     of the petitioner, he has
executed a will in favour of the petitioner and on the basis of the
aforesaid will name of the petitioner has been mutated in the
revenue record on 8/02/2006 and, therefore, the learned
authority has committed an error in rejecting the application for
issuing licence of the petitioner 17/10/2006 and 27/09/2007.
</p><p id="p_5">      As per order passed by the Appellate Authority, Shri
Vikram Singh Sisodiya, son of Late Nahar Singh is alive and,
therefore,   the petitioner can not be considered as legal
representatives of Late Nahar Singh.
</p><p id="p_6">      Considering the aforesaid, no case to interfere with the
impugned orders, as prayed is made out.
</p><p id="p_7">      The writ petition is accordingly dismissed.
</p>

<p id="p_8">                                                (P.K. Jaiswal)
                                                   Judge
