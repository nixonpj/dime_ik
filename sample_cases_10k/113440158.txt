<div class="doc_title">Manoj Soni vs State Of Chhattisgarh on 26 October, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                                        Page No.1


                                                                      NAFR
                HIGH COURT OF CHHATTISGARH, BILASPUR
                              M.CR.C. No. 5685 of 2015
     1. Manoj Soni, S/o. Ram Dular Soni, aged about 36 years, Caste-Sonar,
        Occupation - Labour, R/o. Village - Shantipara, Bhatgaon, Tahsil-
        Bhaiyathan, District Surajpur (C.G.)

                                                                   ----Applicant
                                    Versus

     1. State Of Chhattisgarh, Through : Station House Officer, Police Station
        Bhatgaon, District Surajpur, Chhattisgarh.

                                                               ---- Respondent</pre>


<p id="p_1">For Applicant                      : Mr. Dhirendra Mishra, Advocate
For Respondent/State               : Mr. Arvind Shukla, Panel Lawyer



                    Hon'ble Shri Justice Goutam Bhaduri

                               Order On Board

26/10/2015

</p><p id="p_2">1.      This is the first bail application filed under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of the Code of

        Criminal Procedure, 1973 for grant of regular bail to the applicant who

        has been arrested in connection with Crime No.171/2015, registered at

        Police Station - Bhatgaon, District Surajpur (C.G.) for the offence

        punishable under <a href="/doc/387648/" id="a_2">Section 34</a> (2) of C.G. Excise Act.


</p><p id="p_3">2.      Case of the prosecution, in brief, is that on 20.09.2015, when the

        raid/inspection was conducted by the police, 06 bulk liters of illicit

        liquor was seized from the possession of the applicant.


</p><p id="p_4">3.      Learned counsel for the applicant would submit that the applicant is

        innocent person and has been falsely implicated in the case. He would

        further submit that the applicant has no previous criminal antecedents.

        Therefore, he prays that applicant may be enlarged on bail.
</p><p id="p_5">                                                                                Page No.2

</p><p id="p_6">         4.   On the other hand, learned counsel for the State opposes the bail

              application.
</p>
<p id="p_7">
</p><p id="p_8">         5.   I have heard learned counsel appearing for the parties and perused

              the case diary.
</p>
<p id="p_9">
</p><p id="p_10">         6.   Having regard to the fact that the charge sheet has been filed and the

              quantum of the liquor seized, this Court is of the opinion that present is

              a fit case, in which, the applicant should be enlarged on regular bail.


</p><p id="p_11">         7.   Accordingly, the bail application filed under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_3">Section 439</a> of the Cr.P.C.

</p><p id="p_12">              is allowed.
</p>
<p id="p_13">
</p><p id="p_14">         8.   It is directed that applicant shall be released on bail on his furnishing a

              personal bond in the sum of Rs.25,000/- with one surety in the like

              sum to the satisfaction of the concerned trial Court, for his appearance

              as and when directed.
</p>
<p id="p_15">
</p><p id="p_16">         9.   Certified copy as per rules.
</p>
<p id="p_17">                                                             Sd/-
</p><p id="p_18">                                                       (Goutam Bhaduri)
                                                            Judge
Balram
 </p>
