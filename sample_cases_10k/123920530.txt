<p id="p_1">         1.   Heard   Mr.   Pravin   Panchal,   learned   advocate 

         for petitioner and Mr. Manan Mehta, learned AGP.


</p><p id="p_2">         2.   In   present   petition,   the   petitioner   has 

         prayed, inter alia, that:
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">                     "18(A)         That   this   Honourable   Court   be   pleased   to 
                     issue   a   writ   of   mandamus   or   a   writ   in   the   nature   of  
                     mandamus   or  a   writ  in   the   nature   of  mandamus   and  /   or  
                     any other appropriate writ, order or direction, quashing  
                     and setting aside the order dated 23rd  May, 2007, passed  
                     by the Joint Secretary  (Appeals),  Revenue Department,  a  
                     copy whereof figures at Annexure "G" to the petition;
                     (B)    That   this   Honourable   Court   be   pleased   to   issue   a  
                     writ   of   mandamus   or   a   writ   in   the   nature   of   mandamus  
                     and/ or any other appropriate writ, order or direction,  
                     quashing   and   setting   aside   the   proceedings   pending  
                     before   respondent   no.1,   initiated   by   respondent   No.3  
                     herein,   by   way   of   revision   application,   being   not  
                     maintainable."
</blockquote>


<p id="p_3">         3.   The   petitioner   is   aggrieved   by   order   dated 

         23.05.2007   which   is   passed   at   interim/ 

         interlocutory   stage   by   Secretary   (Appeals)   in 

         Revision Application No.29 of 2006.



</p><p id="p_4">         4.   By  the  said  impugned   order  dated   23.5.2007, 

         the   Secretary   (Appeals)   considered   the 

         application   for   interim   relief   and   directed   the 

         parties   to   maintain   status­quo   as   regards   order

         dated 30.01.2006 passed by the Collector. 



</p><p id="p_5">         5.   Feeling  aggrieved  by  the  said  interim  order 

         passed   at   interlocutory   stage   of   revision 

         application,   the   petitioner   filed   present 

         petition.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><p id="p_7">         6.   According   to   the   allegations   by   present 

         petitioner,   the   hearing   with   reference   to 

         revision   application   had   taken   place   on 

         19.01.2007   and   the   revision   application   was 

         admitted   on   14.3.2007   and   the   authority   passed 

         impugned   order   on   23.5.2007.   According   to   the 

         petitioner,proceedings were conducted irregularly 

         and   as   a   result   of   irregular   conduct   of   the 

         proceedings,   the   impugned   order   came   to   be 

         passed. While admitting the petition, this Court 

         in   the   order   dated   27.06.2007   observed,   inter 

         alia, that:
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_2">                     "Heard   learned   advocate   Mr.Prakash   K.   Jani   for  
                     petitioner, who pointed out the sequence of events which  
                     led   to   passing   of   order   under   challenge   dated  
                     23.05.2007.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_3">                     It   is   shocking   that   the   Revision   Application   filed   in  
                     December   2006,   hearing   had   taken   place   on   19.01.2007.  </blockquote><p id="p_8">
</p><p id="p_9">                     Revision was admitted on 14.03.2007. All of a sudden the

                     any observations with regard to the order.

                     service is permitted."

         order   dated   27.6.2007   came   be   passed.   The 

         proceedings   before   the   authority   have   remained 

         stayed.
</p>
<p id="p_14">

</p><p id="p_15">         8.   Having   regard   to   the   fact   that   ordinarily, 

         this Court would not entertain a petition against 

         interim order passed at interlocutory stage while 

         the   revision   application   remains   pending   before 

         the   authority,   this   petition   deserves   to   be 

         disposed of.
</p>
<p id="p_16">

</p><p id="p_17">         9.   Further, in view of the pendency of present 

         petition, in all probability, the proceedings of 

         revision   application   no.29   of   2006   must   have 

         remained   pending   before   the   concerned   authority 

         i.e.   Secretary   (Appeals)   and   even   the   said 

         proceedings have become old and that, therefore,

         with appropriate direction, so that the revision

</p><p id="p_18">         10. Having   regard   to   above   mentioned   facts   and 

         circumstances of the case, this Court is of the 

         view that if present petition is disposed of with 

         direction   to   the   concerned   authority   i.e. 

         Secretary   (Appeals)   to   expeditiously   decide 

         Revision  Application   No.29  of 2006,  if same  has 

         still   remained   pending,   then   interest   of   both 

         sides   will   be   protected.   Therefore,   following 

         order is passed:
