<p id="p_1">(Per
: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE D.A.MEHTA)
 


 


 
	  


	The
	appellant-revenue has proposed the following two questions:
</p>	 



 
	  


<p id="p_2">	?SWhether
	the Appellate Tribunal is right in law and on facts in confirming
	the order of the CIT(A)
	in disallowance of Rs.47,75,768/- on account of depreciation and
	Rs.7,92,808/- on account of interest expenses ???
</p>	 


	
	  


<p id="p_3">	?SWhether
	the Appellate Tribunal is right in law and on facts in confirming
	the order of the CIT(A)
	in disallowance of Rs.3,57,737/- on account of transportation ?
</p>	 


	

 
	  

<p id="p_4">The
	appeal relates to Assessment Year 2004-2005. Heard the learned
	advocate for the appellant.
</p>	 


	  

<p id="p_5">As
	can be seen from the impugned order of the Tribunal, the Tribunal
	has followed its own order in assessee's own case for Assessment
	Years 2001-2002 and 2003-2004. Against the said order of the
	Tribunal the revenue had preferred Tax Appeal No.1335 of 2007 which
	came to be dismissed vide order dated 22.04.2008.
</p>	 


	  

<p id="p_6">In
	the circumstances, the present appeal is also required to be
	dismissed in absence of any question of law, much less a substantial
	question of law, for the year under consideration. The appeal is
	accordingly dismissed.
</p>
