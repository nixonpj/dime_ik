<p id="p_1">1.               Vide this judgment, I shall dispose off the petition filed under 

<a href="/doc/105862/" id="a_2">section   372</a>   of   the   Indian   Succession   Act,   1925   for   obtaining   succession 

certificate in respect of debts/securities etc of Late Sh. Harbans Lal Bhandari 

s/o. Sh. Mukand Lal Bhandari (deceased).  
</p>
<p id="p_2">

</p><p id="p_3">2.               The relevant facts for the disposal of the present petition are that 

Late   Sh.   Harbans   Lal   Bhandari   the   father   of   the   petitioner   expired   on 

30.10.2011   who   was   ordinarily   residing   at   House   no.   2,   Tagore   Park, 

Delhi­110   009.  It   is   stated   that   the   deceased   had   left   behind 

SCC No. 58/<a href="/doc/89009079/" id="a_3">12                    Dr. Mala Bhandari vs. State &amp; Ors</a>.                                     2
 petitioner/daughter,   respondent   no.   2/son,   respondent   no.   3/daughter, 

respondent   no.4/daughter,   respondent   no.   5/widowed   daughter­in­law   and 

respondent no. 6/grand son as his legal heirs. 
</p>
<p id="p_4">                 The present petition has been filed for the grant of succession 

certificate in respect of the amount lying in the name of the deceased in the 

Demat Account with HDFC Bank and two bank accounts in Canara Bank. 

</p><p id="p_5">                 It  is  submitted  that  petitioner is also a beneficiary along with 

respondent no. 3 &amp; 4 under the will and testament dt. 20.08.2011 executed by 

the deceased Sh. Harbans Lal Bhandari. Further it is submitted that there is no 

impediment in grant of succession certificate in favour of the petitioner in 

respect of above said accounts.
</p>
<p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">3.               General   public   had   been   served   through   publication   in   daily 

newspaper 'Rashtriya Sahara' dated 02.06.2012 but none had appeared from 

general public to oppose or contest the present petition.  



</p><p id="p_8">4.               During   the   stage   of   PE   a   joint   Statement     was   made   by   the 

petitioner and respondent no. 5 Smt. Praveen Bhandari w/o. Late Sh. Surinder 

Kumar Bhandari to the effect that they have settled their disputes amicably. It 

was agreed that the respondent no. 5 Smt. Praveen Bhandari shall receive a 

sum of Rs. 33,60,000/­ from the estate of late  Sh. Harbans Lal Bhandari from 


SCC No. 58/<a href="/doc/89009079/" id="a_4">12                    Dr. Mala Bhandari vs. State &amp; Ors</a>.                                     3
 the bank account with HDFC Bank, Gujrawalan Town Branch as full and 

final claim. It has also agreed that after receipt of said sum the respondent no. 

5 shall have no claim left in respect of schedule A.



</p><p id="p_9">5.               In support of her case, petitioner examined herself and deposed 

on the lines of the petition and tendered her affidavit Ex.PW1/1A and relied 

upon   the   documents   Ex.PW1/1   to   Ex.PW1/9   i.e.  death   certificate   of   the 

deceased   Ex.PW1/1,   voter   identity   card   of   petitioner   Ex.PW1/2,   copy   of 

passport Ex.PW1/3, copy of bill dt. 16.03.2012 Ex.PW1/4, voter identity card 

of wife of deceased Ex.PW1/5, copy of bill dt. 08.12.2011 Ex.PW1/6, copy of 

statement of account from demat account with HDFC Ex.PW1/7, statement of 

account in Canara Bank Ex.PW1/8 &amp; Ex.PW1/9.
</p>
<p id="p_10">                 Thereafter, petitioner examined Sh. Om Prakash who tender his 

affidavit Ex.PW2/A who proved the will executed and signed by deceased 

Ex.PW2/1 being the attesting witness.
</p>
<p id="p_11">                 Petitioner  also   examined RW1, RW2 &amp; RW3 who gave their 

NOC through their affidavits submitting that they have no objection if the last 

wish of their father late Sh. Harbans Lal Bhandari as declared in his last will 

dt.   20.08.2011  is  implemented   and succession  certificate in respect  of  the 

debts   and   securities   of   the   deceased   is   issued   jointly   in   the   name   of   the 

petitioner, Dr. Sushma Mahajan and Smt. Anita Verma. 


SCC No. 58/<a href="/doc/89009079/" id="a_5">12                    Dr. Mala Bhandari vs. State &amp; Ors</a>.                                     4
</p><p id="p_12"> 6.               Heard.
</p>
<p id="p_13">

</p><p id="p_14">7.               Considering the above stated facts, this Court is of the opinion 

that there is no impediment in grant of Succession Certificate in favour of 

petitioner,   respondent   no.   3   Dr.   Sushma   Mahajan   and   respondent   no.4 

Smt. Anita Verma. The other legal heir respondent no. 5 has also entered into 

a compromise and it was agreed that she would be entitled to Rs. 33,60,000/­ 

as total claim from the estate of the deceased. The other legal heir respondent 

no. 6 did not appear before the court as he was stated to be untraceable for the 

last ten years. As per the order of Ld. Predecessor dt. 11.10.2013 he was 

deemed to have been served through publication. 

</p><p id="p_15">                 In view of the same the succession certificate be issued in respect 

of the following amount :</p><pre id="pre_2">

favour of the petitioner, respondent no. 3 Dr. Sushma Mahajan, respondent 

no. 4 Smt. Anita Verma for the above mentioned amount after deducting the 

amount of Rs. 33,60,000/­ lying in HDFC Bank Account which is awarded in 

favour   of   respondent   no.   5   as   per   the   settlement,   on   furnishing   of   an 

Indemnity Bond of the same value with one surety of like amount within one 

month   from   today.   The   HDFC   Bank,   Gujrawalan   Branch   is   directed   to 

released Rs. 33,60,000/­ in favour of respondent no. 5 from the account no.
