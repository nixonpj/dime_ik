<p id="p_1">                                   The present petition under <a href="/doc/903398/" id="a_1">Section 482</a>, <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_2">Cr.P.C</a>., has

                 FIR No. 316, dated 4.12.2013 (Annexure P-1), for the offences

                 punishable under <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_3">Sections 323</a>, <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_4">324</a>, <a href="/doc/1101188/" id="a_5">379</a> and <a href="/doc/513777/" id="a_6">451</a> read with

                 and all the consequential proceedings arising therefrom, on the

                 basis of compromise dated 15.7.2014 (Annexure P-2).

                 consequential proceedings emanating therefrom would be sheer

                 abuse of the process of law. In support of his contention, learned

                 have resolved their dispute and effected a compromise. He

                 further submits that he has no objection if the impugned FIR and

                 all the consequential proceedings are quashed on the basis of

                 the compromise.

                 petitioner are bleak in view of the compromise so effected

                 between the private parties.
