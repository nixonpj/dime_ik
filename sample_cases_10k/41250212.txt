<div class="doc_title">Amarjit Bansal vs State Bank Of India And Others on 4 October, 2011</div><pre id="pre_1">      IN THE HIGH COURT FOR THE STATES OF PUNJAB AND
                  HARYANA AT CHANDIGARH


                                 CWP No.18721 of 2011
                                 Date of Decision: 04.10.2011

Amarjit Bansal                                             ...Petitioner


                                      Versus
State Bank of India and others                             ...Respondents</pre>

<p id="p_1">CORAM: HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE HEMANT GUPTA
       HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE G.S.SANDHAWALIA

Present:     Mr. Pawan Kumar, Sr. Advocate, with
             Mr. Vishal Sharma, Advocate, for the respondents.


HEMANT GUPTA, J.(Oral)


             Challenge in the present writ petition is to an order passed by the

Debt Recovery Tribunal, Chandigarh on 26.08.2011 in an appeal against an

order of the Recovery Officer.
</p>
<p id="p_2">             We find that the order passed by the Debt Recovery Tribunal is an

appealable order before the Debt Recovery Appellate Tribunal in terms of

<a href="/doc/1393354/" id="a_1">Section 20</a> of the Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions

Act, 1993.
</p>
<p id="p_3">             In view of the said fact, the present writ petition is dismissed as not

maintainable. However, the petitioner is at liberty to avail the remedy of appeal

under the aforesaid Act.</p><pre id="pre_2">


                                                        (HEMANT GUPTA)
                                                            JUDGE



04.10.2011                                            (G.S.SANDHAWALIA)
Vimal                                                        JUDGE
