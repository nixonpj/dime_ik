                                                 ORDER

<p id="p_1">                           This Criminal Original Petition has been filed by the petitioner

                 against the order dated 10.09.2018 in Crl.M.P.No.5246 of 2018 in

                 C.C.No.58 of 2003 passed by the VIII Additional Principal Special Judge

                 for CBI Cases, Chennai, wherein the learned trial Judge had rejected the

                 petition filed under Section 311 of Cr.P.C.



</p><p id="p_2">                           2. The learned counsel appearing for the petitioner would submit

                 that though the petitioner has filed a petition in Crl.M.P.No.5246 of 2018

                 in C.C.No.58 of 2003 under <a href="/doc/1780550/" id="a_2">Section 311</a> of Cr.P.C, requesting to recall 12

                 prosecution witnesses viz., Pws.7, 11, 15, 28, 31, 41, 42, 44, 45, 47, 50

                 and 60 for the purpose of cross examination, the said request was

                 rejected on the ground that the petition has been filed after more than

                 three months from the date of completion of questioning under <a href="/doc/767287/" id="a_3">Section

                 313</a> of Cr.P.C. The lower Court observed that to drag on the proceedings,

                 the petitioner has come forward with the petition under <a href="/doc/1780550/" id="a_4">Section 311</a>

                 Cr.P.C.    and dismissed the said petition, against which the present

                 petition has been filed. Though the petitioner has filed a petition under

                 <a href="/doc/1780550/" id="a_5">Section 311</a> of Cr.P.C to recall 12 prosecution witnesses for the purpose

                 of cross examination, however, he restricted to only Pws. 42, 47 and 60




http://www.judis.nic.in
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                       3</span>

                 within a reasonable time.       This Court permit the petitioner to cross

                 examine only three witnesses who have spoken against the accused, for

                 which it is necessary to cross examine the witnesses.



</p><p id="p_3">                             3. The learned Special Public Prosecutor for CBI Cases conceded

                 the      request made by the learned counsel appearing for the petitioner.

                 In view of the above, I am inclined to set aside the order passed by the

                 lower court. Accordingly, the impugned order passed by the Court below

                 is set aside to the limited extent and the petitioner is permitted to recall

                 the witnesses, viz. Pws.42, 47, 60.       Accordingly, the learned Special

                 Public Prosecutor for CBI Cases undertakes to produce the said three

                 witnesses 15.11.2018.       On the same day, the petitioner has to cross

                 examine all the three witnesses viz., Pws.42, 47 and 60 and no further

                 time will be granted. After completing the cross examination, the lower

                 Court shall proceed with the matter further in accordance with law.



</p><p id="p_4">                             4. With the above direction, this Criminal Original Petition is

                 allowed.
