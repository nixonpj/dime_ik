<div class="doc_title">Ashokbhai Nanubhai Godani vs State Of Gujarat on 7 January, 2019</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: A.Y. Kogje</div>

<pre id="pre_1">      R/CR.MA/22541/2018                                      ORDER




    IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

   R/CRIMINAL MISC.APPLICATION NO. 22541 of 2018

=============================================
                  ASHOKBHAI NANUBHAI GODANI
                            Versus
                       STATE OF GUJARAT
=============================================
Appearance:
MR HB CHAMPAVAT(6149) for the PETITIONER(s) No. 1
PUBLIC PROSECUTOR(2) for the RESPONDENT(s) No. 1
=============================================

CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE A.Y. KOGJE

                           Date : 07/01/2019

                            ORAL ORDER</pre>



<p id="p_1">  1. This application is filed by the applicant under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a>

     of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 for regular bail in

     connection with FIR registered as C.R. No.I-24 of 2018

     with Kadodara GIDC Police Station, District-Surat

     (Rural) for the offence punishable under <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_2">Sections 406</a>,

     <a href="/doc/1667388/" id="a_3">419</a>, <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_4">420</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_2">

</p><p id="p_3">  2. Learned advocate appearing on behalf of the applicant

     submits that considering the nature of offence, the

     applicant may be enlarged on regular bail by imposing

     suitable conditions.</p><pre id="pre_2">



  3. On    the    other    hand,   the        learned   Additional    Public



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                Page 1 of 5</span>
     R/CR.MA/22541/2018                                         ORDER



  Prosecutor         appearing      for        the   respondent-State   has

</pre><p id="p_4">  opposed grant of regular bail looking to the nature and

  gravity of the offence.
</p>
<p id="p_5">

</p><p id="p_6">4. Learned Advocates appearing on behalf of the respective

  parties do not press for a further reasoned order.



</p><p id="p_7">5. I have heard the learned advocates appearing on behalf of

  the respective parties and perused the papers. Following

  aspects are considered :-
</p>
<p id="p_8">  I) The F.I.R. is registered on 04-03-2018 for the offence
  which is alleged to have taken place on 28-02-2018;

  II) The applicant is in custody since 14-07-2018;

  III) Investigation qua the applicant is over as the remand
  period is over;
</p>
<p id="p_9">  IV) Submission of the learned Advocate for the applicant that
  offence is under <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_5">Sections 406</a>, <a href="/doc/1667388/" id="a_6">419</a>, <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_7">420</a> of the Indian
  Penal Code and is triable by the Magistrate and
  considering the maximum sentence;
</p>
<p id="p_10">  V) Submission of the learned Advocate for the applicant
  under instruction that he is ready and willing to deposit an
  amount of Rs.9,70,000/- (Rupees Nine Lakhs Seventy
  Thousand only) within the period of three months in
  installments before the trial Court and file an undertaking
  to that effect within the period of one week before this
  Court from his release;
</p>
<p id="p_11">  VI) Learned Additional Public Prosecutor under the
  instructions of the Investigating Officer is unable to bring
  on record any special circumstances against the applicant.


  This Court has taken into consideration the law laid down



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                 Page 2 of 5</span>
     R/CR.MA/22541/2018                             ORDER



  by the Hon'ble Apex Court in the case of <a href="/doc/1563495/" id="a_8">Sanjay Chandra

  v. Central Bureau of Investigation</a> reported in [2012] 1

  SCC 40.
</p>
<p id="p_12">

</p><p id="p_13">6. In the facts and circumstances of the case and considering

  the nature of the allegations made against the applicant in

  the First Information Report, without discussing the

  evidence in detail, prima facie, this Court is of the opinion

  that this is a fit case to exercise the discretion and enlarge

  the applicant on regular bail.
</p>
<p id="p_14">
</p><p id="p_15">7. Hence, the present application is allowed. The applicant is

  ordered to be released on regular bail in connection with

  C.R. No.I-24 of 2018 with Kadodara GIDC Police

  Station, District-Surat (Rural) on executing a personal

  bond of Rs.10,000/= (Rupees Ten Thousand Only) with one

  surety of the like amount to the satisfaction of the trial

  Court and subject to the conditions that he shall;

</p><p id="p_16">  (a) not take undue advantage of liberty or misuse liberty;

</p><p id="p_17">  (b) not act in a manner injurious to the interest of the
  prosecution;
</p>
<p id="p_18">  (c) surrender passport, if any, to the lower Court within a
  week;
</p>
<p id="p_19">  (d) not leave the State of Gujarat without prior permission
  of the Sessions Judge concerned;
</p>
<p id="p_20">


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                          Page 3 of 5</span>
</p><p id="p_21">       R/CR.MA/22541/2018                               ORDER



</p><p id="p_22">  (e) mark presence before the concerned Police Station on
  alternate Monday of every English calendar month for a
  period of six months between 11.00 a.m. and 2.00 p.m.;

</p><p id="p_23">  (f) furnish the present address of his residence to the
  Investigating Officer and also to the Court at the time of
  execution of the bond and shall not change the residence
  without prior permission of this Court;

</p><p id="p_24">  (g) deposit an amount of Rs.9,70,000/- (Rupees Nine Lakhs
  Seventy Thousand only) within the period of three months
  in installments before the trial Court and file an
  undertaking to that effect within the period of one week
  before this Court from his release.
</p>
<p id="p_25">
</p><p id="p_26">8. The authorities will release the applicant only if he is not

  required in connection with any other offence for the time

  being.         If breach of any of the above conditions is

  committed, the Sessions Judge concerned will be free to

  issue warrant or take appropriate action in the matter.



</p><p id="p_27">9. Bail bond to be executed before the lower Court having

  jurisdiction to try the case.            It will be open for the

  concerned Court to delete, modify and/or relax any of the

  above conditions, in accordance with law.



</p><p id="p_28">10.        At the trial, the trial Court shall not be influenced by

  the observations of preliminary nature qua the evidence at

  this stage made by this Court while enlarging the applicant

  on bail.
</p>
<p id="p_29">


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                             Page 4 of 5</span>
</p><p id="p_30">          R/CR.MA/22541/2018                          ORDER



</p><p id="p_31">   11.        The application is allowed in the aforesaid terms.

      Rule is made absolute to the aforesaid extent.         Direct

      Service is permitted.
</p>
<p id="p_32">


</p><p id="p_33">                                                (A.Y. KOGJE, J)
PARESH J SOMPURA




<span class="hidden_text" id="span_5">                              Page 5 of 5</span>
