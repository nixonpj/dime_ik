EX. PARTE JUDGMENT

   1.</pre><p id="p_1"> This is a suit filed for the recovery of Rs. 1,97,367/­as against the 

      defendant.
</p>
<p id="p_2">   2. It is the case of the plaintiff that the plaintiff is a body corporate 

      constituted   under   <a href="/doc/444965/" id="a_1">Banking  Companies   Act</a>,    1956  to  carry  on   the 

      banking   business   having   its   Head   Office   at   World   Trade   Centre 

      Complex,   Cuffe   Parade,   Mumbai­400005   and   having   its   branch 

      office at 65 M. M. Road, Jhandewalan, New Delhi­55.

</p><p id="p_3">   3. The   defendant   is   borrower   and   approached   the   plaintiff   bank   for 

      grant   of   loan   of   Rs.5,00,000/­   for   personal   expenditure   and   other 

      allied   activities   vide   the   prescribed   loan   application   form   dated 

      18.12.05   and   after   execution   of   the   document,   the   plaintiff   bank 

      disbursed   the   loan   amount   which   was   to   be   repaid   in   forty   eight 

      equated installment of Rs.11,032/­ each.

</p><p id="p_4">   4. Defendant failed to adhere the financial discipline of repayment of 

      loan   either   towards   principal   or   interest   or   charges   thereon   and 

      ultimately   plaintiff   got   issued   demand   notice   dated   22.02.11   but 

      despite that when defendant failed to make the payment, hence, the 

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">CS No. 431/11            IDBI Vs. MANJYOT SANDHU                              Page 2 of 4</span>
        present suit has been filed.
</p>
<p id="p_5">   5. The  defendant was duly  served  by way of publication  but despite 

       service none has appeared on behalf of the defendant, the defendant 

       was proceeded ex parte vide order dated 11.05.12.

</p><p id="p_6">   6. In support of its claim plaintiff examined Sh. Rohit Mehra, as PW1 

       who led his evidence by way of affidavit in which he supported the 

       averment made in the plaint.   PW1 has relied upon the document 

       namely:
</p>
<p id="p_7">       copy   of   power   of   attorney   Ex   PW1/1,   loan   application   form   Ex 

       PW1/2,   sanction   letter   Ex   PW1/3,   personal   loan   agreement   Ex 

       PW1/4, statement of account of earlier loan availed by the plaintiff 

       and its adjustment against the present loan Ex PW1/5, office copy of 

       notice   alongwith   postal   receipts   Ex   PW1/6   colly,   statement   of 

       account as per bankers books of evidence act Ex PW1/7 colly. 

</p><p id="p_8">   7. After recording the statement, ex parte final argument heard.

</p><p id="p_9">   8. The suit of the plaintiff plaintiff is within limitation and jurisdiction. 

       Keeping   in   view   the   unrebutted   testimony   of   witness   and   various 

       documents placed and proved on record, the suit of the plaintiff is 

       decreed.  
</p>
<p id="p_10">   9. Relief:   In   view   of   the   above   facts,   a     decree   for   a   sum   of   Rs.

       1,97,367/­ together with pendente­lite and future interest @ 14.50% 
       per annum up to the date of the realization of the decree is passed in 


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">CS No. 431/11              IDBI Vs. MANJYOT SANDHU                               Page 3 of 4</span>
       favour of plaintiff and against defendant.

</p><p id="p_11">   10. Cost of the suit is also awarded in favour of plaintiff and against the 

      defendant.
</p>
<p id="p_12">   11. Decree sheet be prepared accordingly. 

</p><p id="p_13">   12. File be consigned to record room. 
</p>
<p id="p_14">(ANNOUNCED IN
