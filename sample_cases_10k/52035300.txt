<pre id="pre_1">                                 WP No. 1243 of 2009

                            IN THE HIGH COURT AT CALCUTTA

                        Constitutional Writ Jurisdiction

                                    ORIGINAL SIDE




    SREI EQUIPMENT FINANCE PVT LTD &amp; ANR        Plaintiff/Petitioner/Applicant

        Versus

    JOINT COMMISSIONER OF SERVICE TAX,KOL &amp; ORS     Defendant/Respondent</pre>



<p id="p_1">      For Plaintiff/Petitioner : DR.D.P.PAL,SR.ADVOCATE WITH MR.S.MOOKHERJEE,ADV

      For Respondent : MR.FAROOK M.RAZACK,ADDL.SOLICITOR GENERAL WITH
      MR.R.BHARADWAJ,ADVOCATE

    BEFORE:
</p>
<p id="p_2">    The Hon'ble JUSTICE SOUMITRA PAL

    Date : 5th February, 2010.
</p>
<p id="p_3">
</p><p id="p_4">                         The Court : Affidavit-of-service filed in court today

be kept with the records.
</p>
<p id="p_5">      Let affidavit-in-opposition to the writ petition be filed by 26th March,

2010. Reply thereto, if any, by 9th April, 2010.

</p><p id="p_6">      Having perused the notice to Show cause-cum-demand dated 20th October,

2009 read with Corrigendum dated 28th October, 2009, if the petitioner furnishes

a bank guarantee of Rs.7 lakhs of any nationalized bank to the satisfaction of

the Commissioner of Service Tax, respondent no.2, within four weeks from the

date of communication of the order, the respondents authorities shall not take

any coercive action against the petitioners.

</p><p id="p_7">      However, it is made clear that since the proceedings are continuing and

the petitioners have filed their reply, the respondent no.2 is at liberty to
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                        2</span>


pass final order after giving the petitioners opportunity of hearing but it

shall be communicated and/or given effect to without leave of the court.

</p><p id="p_8">       Let there be an unconditional stay of Demand-cum-notice for a period of

four weeks from the date of communication of the order.

</p><p id="p_9">       This order is passed without prejudice to the rights and contentions of

the parties and subject to further order that may be passed.

</p><p id="p_10">       All parties concerned are to act on a xerox signed copy of this order on

the usual undertakings.
</p>
<p id="p_11">

</p><p id="p_12">                                   (SOUMITRA PAL, J.)

ssaha
AR(C.R.)
