<div class="doc_title">Manjit Kaur vs Sub Divisional Officer on 13 September, 2011</div><pre id="pre_1">           IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA
                         AT CHANDIGARH

                  Civil Revision No.5556 of 2011 (O&amp;M)
                  Date of decision: 13th September, 2011

Manjit Kaur
                                                              ... Petitioner
                                  Versus
Sub Divisional Officer, Sirhind and another
                                                           ... Respondents

CORAM:        HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE KANWALJIT SINGH AHLUWALIA

Present:      Mr. Berjeshwar S. Jaswal, Advocate for the petitioner.


KANWALJIT SINGH AHLUWALIA, J. (ORAL)
</pre>
<p id="p_1">              In the present revision petition, a prayer has been made that

order dated 8th June, 2011 (Annexure P-3) be set aside, vide which

evidence of the plaintiff was closed.
</p>
<p id="p_2">              I have perused the impugned order (Annexure P-3). The trial

Court, considering the fact that numerous opportunities were availed but

plaintiff's evidence was not being concluded, had by its order closed the

evidence of the plaintiff.
</p>
<p id="p_3">              Counsel for the petitioner states that only one witness

namely Manjit Kaur is to be examined. He further states that this witness

has already furnished her affidavit in examination in-chief and only her

cross-examination is to be carried. Counsel submits that in case one

opportunity is granted, Manjit Kaur will be present in the Court at the risk

and responsibility of the plaintiff. It is stated that next date of hearing

fixed before the trial Court is 14th September, 2011 and that till today,

defendant's evidence has not commenced.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Civil Revision No.5556 of 2011 (O&amp;M)                                     2</span><p id="p_4">



</p><p id="p_5">              For the reasons stated in this petition and to ensure fair play

and balance of equities, this Court is of the view that one opportunity is

to be granted to the plaintiff to conclude his evidence. Therefore, on the

undertaking that the plaintiff, at his own risk and responsibility, shall

produce Manjit Kaur in the Court on 14th September, 2011, the trial

Court is directed to conclude cross-examination of Manjit Kaur on that

day, subject to payment of cost of Rs.3,000/-.

</p><p id="p_6">              With the observations made above, present petition is

disposed of.
</p>
<p id="p_7">
</p><p id="p_8">                                       [KANWALJIT SINGH AHLUWALIA]
                                                  JUDGE
September 13, 2011
