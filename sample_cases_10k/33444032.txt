<div class="doc_title">Jayesh Sah @ Jaiyas Sah vs The State Of Bihar on 22 September, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                 Criminal Miscellaneous No.37793 of 2016
                  Arising Out of PS.Case No. -181 Year- 2016 Thana -LAHERIASARAI District- DARBHANGA
                 ======================================================
                 Jayesh Sah @ Jaiyas Sah S/o Sambhu Prasad Sah Resident of Mohalla -
                 Sundarpur Chhatthi Pokhar, P.S. L.N.M.U., Distt. Darbhanga

                                                                                .... ....   Petitioner
                                                      Versus
                 The State of Bihar

                                                              .... .... Opposite Party
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner     : Mr. Kedar Jha, Advocate
                 For the Opposite Party : Mr.
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE JITENDRA MOHAN SHARMA
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   22-09-2016                   Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and the learned

                 counsel representing the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                 The petitioner seeks bail in connection with Laheriasarai

                 P.S Case No. 181 of 2016 registered for the offences punishable under

                 <a href="/doc/1258372/" id="a_1">Sections 147</a>, <a href="/doc/763672/" id="a_2">148</a>, <a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_3">149</a>, <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_4">341</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_5">323</a>, <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_6">324</a>, <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_7">307</a>, <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_8">302</a>, <a href="/doc/1128948/" id="a_9">120(B)</a> of the Indian

                 Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                 Allegedly, the son of the informant was stabbed by F.I.R

                 named accused persons due to old enmity and he died during course of

                 treatment and the petitioner is also named as one of the accused.

</p><p id="p_4">                                 Submission is of false implication and that the petitioner

                 is in custody since 02.05.2016, chargesheet has already been submitted

                 against the petitioner and there is no chance of tampering with the

                 prosecution evidence against the petitioner, there is no specific

                 allegation rather the allegation are general and omnibus in nature. In
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">           Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.37793 of 2016 (2) dt.22-09-2016</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                     2/2</span>




                       this case similarly situated Gautam Kumar Singh @ Gautam Singh @

                       Jonty has been allowed bail by another co-ordinate Bench of this Court,

                       and as such the petitioner deserves sympathetic consideration.

</p><p id="p_5">                                        Learned A.P.P. opposes the prayer of bail by submitting

                       that petitioner is named in the F.I.R.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                        In the facts and circumstances stated above, the petitioner

                       is directed to be released on bail on execution of bail bond of Rs.

                       10,000/- (ten thousand) with two sureties of like amount each to the

                       satisfaction of learned Chief Judicial Magistrate, Darbhanga, in

                       connection with Laheriasarai P.S. Case No. 181 of 2016, subject to the

                       conditions that one of the bailors must be near relative and another

                       having sufficient immovable property within the territorial jurisdiction

                       of the Court concerned and the petitioner shall remain present on each

                       and every date during trial and the default on two consecutive dates on

                       his part without any reason shall disentitle the petitioner from privilege

                       of bail.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                                                                 (Jitendra Mohan Sharma, J.)
Rajiv/-
</p>
