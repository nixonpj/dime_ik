<div class="doc_title">Kirit Chandrakant Vyas vs State Of Gujarat &amp; 13 on 17 June, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">     R/CR.MA/9817/2015                                   ORDER




          IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

 CRIMINAL MISC.APPLICATION (FOR LEAVE TO APPEAL) NO. 9817 of
                                2015
==========================================================
             KIRIT CHANDRAKANT VYAS....Applicant(s)
                            Versus
             STATE OF GUJARAT &amp; 13....Respondent(s)
==========================================================
Appearance:
MR P P MAJMUDAR, ADVOCATE for the Applicant(s) No. 1
MS MOXA THAKKER ADDITIONAL PUBLIC PROSECUTOR for the
RESPONDENT(s) No. 1
==========================================================

         CORAM: HONOURABLE MS.JUSTICE HARSHA DEVANI

                          Date : 17/06/2015
                            ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">1.    Mr. P. P. Majmudar, learned advocate for the applicant
has drawn the attention of the court to the purshis submitted
by the accused No. 2, 3 and 4 wherein they have admitted that
they had borrowed a sum of Rs.31 lakhs from the complainant.
It was submitted that in the face of the admission made by the
accused persons, the trial court was not justified in holding
that the applicant had failed to establish his case against the
respondents. Reliance was placed upon the decision of this
court in the case of <a href="/doc/1068500/" id="a_1">Girishbhai Natvarbhai Patel v. State of
Gujarat</a>, 2006 (1) G.L.H. 530.
</p>
<p id="p_2">2.    Having regard to the submissions advanced by the learned
advocate for the applicant, the court is of the view that the matter
requires consideration. Hence, leave to appeal is granted. The
application stands disposed of accordingly.
</p>
