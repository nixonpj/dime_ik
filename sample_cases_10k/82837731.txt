<div class="doc_title">Sh. Chander Pal vs The State (Govt Of Nct Of Delhi) on 24 March, 2015</div><div class="doc_author">Author: Ms.Hemani Malhotra</div>

<pre id="pre_1">                                                         1

    IN THE COURT OF MS. HEMANI MALHOTRA, ASJ-05 (CENTRAL),
                       TIS HAZARI, DELHI

CA No. 16/15
Chander Pal
Vs.
State

Sh. Chander Pal
S/o Sh. Randheera
R /o Village Baral Garhi Daulat
Post Gangerv, PS Kandhla
Distt. Muzaffar Nagar, New Shamli
UP                                                                 ....... Appellant / Convict

VERSUS

The State (Govt of NCT of Delhi)                                   ..........Respondent

          Date of institution                                  :         05.02.2015
          Date of reservation of Judgment                      :         24.03.2015
          Date of Judgment                                     :         24.03.2015


          JUDGMENT:
</pre>
<p id="p_1">     1. Present criminal appeal has been preferred by the convict Chander
          Pal against the impugned judgment of conviction dated 09.07.2014
          and impugned order on sentence dated 19.01.2015 passed by the
          Learned Trial Court in case FIR No. 249/2005 , PS RMD, U/s
          356/379/511 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_1">IPC</a> whereby the appellant/convict has been convicted
          under <a href="/doc/1760174/" id="a_2">section 356</a>/<a href="/doc/1101188/" id="a_3">379</a>/<a href="/doc/1185693/" id="a_4">511</a> IPC and has been directed to undergo RI
          for one month and fine of Rs.2000/- .
</p>

<p id="p_2">     3. I have heard the learned counsel for the appellant/convict and the
          learned Addl.PP for the state and have gone through the trial court
          record with utmost care.
</p><p id="p_3">     4. It was argued by the learned counsel for appellant/convict that
          learned Trial Court after passing the impugned judgment of

Judgment on appeal No.16/15 titled <a href="/doc/1217204/" id="a_5">Chander Pal vs. The State</a>                              1 of 2
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                          2</span>

          conviction, called the probation report at the request of counsel.
          However, inadvertently the correct report could not be furnished
          and thereafter the then learned MM was transferred and the learned
          Successor of the then Learned MM passed the impugned order on
          sentence without considering the application for grant of probation.
          Probation Report was again called on the request of learned
          counsel for appellant/convict. The Probation Report reveals that the
          accused/convict is native of Village Garidaulat, PS Kandhla, District
          Shamli, UP and he has agricultural land in his village. Report also
          reveals that he is a farmer by profession. Probation Report has
          been verified by the Sarpanch of the village as well. It is further
          prayed          by       learned          counsel    for   convict/appellant        that
          appellant/convict be given benefit of <a href="/doc/167447/" id="a_6">Probation Offenders Act</a> and
          that he be released on probation.
</p>

<p id="p_4">     5. Considering the Probation Report of the appellant/convict and
          nature of offence coupled with the fact that convict/appellant has
          been facing trial for the last about 10 years, convict is given benefit
          of <a href="/doc/167447/" id="a_7">Probation Offenders Act</a>. Convict is ordered to be released on
          furnishing personal bond for the sum of Rs.10,000/- for a period of
          six months with direction to maintain peace in the society and to
          appear before the Court as and when summoned by the Court to
          receive the sentence in the present case.
</p>

<p id="p_5">     6. Appeal stands disposed of accordingly. File be consigned to record
          room. Trial Court Record be sent back with copy of this order.
</p>

<p id="p_6">          Dictated and announced today                                 (Hemani Malhotra)
          i.e. on: 24.3.2015                                   ASJ-05 (Central)/THC/Delhi




Judgment on appeal No.16/15 titled <a href="/doc/1217204/" id="a_8">Chander Pal vs. The State</a>                         2 of 2
