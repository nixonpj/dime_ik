<div class="doc_title">Rajesh M Vala &amp; 6 vs State Of Gujarat &amp; 4 on 5 February, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">        C/SCA/1982/2014                                 ORDER




         IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

            SPECIAL CIVIL APPLICATION NO. 1982 of 2014
================================================================
                  RAJESH M VALA &amp; 6....Petitioner(s)
                              Versus
                STATE OF GUJARAT &amp; 4....Respondent(s)
================================================================
Appearance:
MR SANJAY D SUTHAR FOR MR NK MAJMUDAR, ADVOCATE for
Petitioners No. 1 - 7
MR DM DEVNANI,ASST.GOVERNMENT PLEADER for Respondent No.1,2,5
================================================================
        CORAM: HONOURABLE SMT. JUSTICE ABHILASHA
               KUMARI

                          Date : 05/02/2014
                            ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">1.   This   petition   under   Article­226   of   the 

Constitution of India, has been preferred with several 

prayers, which pertain to the main grievance voiced by 

the petitioners that is regarding upper age limit of 

30 years in the advertisement No.05/2013­14, issued by 

respondent   No.5­Gujarat   Panchayat   Service   Selection 

Board.   The   petitioners   are   desirous   of   applying   for 

the   posts   of   Pharmacist/Compounder,   pursuant   to   the 

said advertisement, but they have crossed the age bar 

of 30 years.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">2.   Heard   Mr.Sanjay   D.   Suthar,   learned   advocate   for 

Mr.N.K.Majmudar, learned advocate for the petitioners 


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                              Page 1 of 3</span>
           C/SCA/1982/2014                          ORDER



and   Mr.D.M.Devnani,   learned   Assistant   Government 

Pleader for respondents Nos.1, 2 and 5, appearing on 

an advance copy of the petition.
</p>
<p id="p_4">
</p><p id="p_5">3.   It is submitted by the learned advocate for the 

petitioner that the interest of justice would be met, 

if   the   petitioners   are   permitted   to   approach 

respondent No.5 by way of a representation regarding 

relaxation   of   upper   age   limit   in   the   advertisement 

No.05/2013­14 and respondent No.5 may be directed to 

consider   and   decide   the   same,   within   a   time­bound 

period.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><p id="p_7">4.   Mr.D.M.Devnani,   learned   Assistant   Government 

Pleader   for   respondents   Nos.1,   2   and   5   have   no 

objection if such an order is passed.


</p><p id="p_8">5.   In   view   of   the   above   statement,   the   following 

order is passed :
</p>
<p id="p_9">
</p><p id="p_10">     It   is   open   to   the   petitioners   to   make   a 

representation regarding relaxation of upper age limit 

in the advertisement No.05/2013­14 to respondent No.5. 

If the representation is made within a period of one 

week   from   today,   respondent   No.5   shall   consider   and 



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                            Page 2 of 3</span>
           C/SCA/1982/2014                               ORDER



decide   the   same,   in   accordance  with   law,   within   a 

period   of   15   days   from   the   receipt   of   the 

representation.
</p>
<p id="p_11">
</p><p id="p_12">      The petition is disposed of, in the above terms, 

without entering into the merits of the case. 


</p><p id="p_13">      Direct   service   of   this   order,   today,   is 

permitted. 
</p>
<p id="p_14">
</p><p id="p_15">                                          (SMT. ABHILASHA KUMARI, J.)
Gaurav+
