<div class="doc_title">Sardar Bishan Singh, vs State Of Telagana, on 1 May, 2018</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: T.Sunil Chowdary</div>

<pre id="pre_1">       THE HON'BLE SRI JUSTICE T. SUNIL CHOWDARY

                WRIT PETITION No.41790 of 2016

ORDER:
</pre>
<p id="p_1">      This Writ Petition is filed under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the

Constitution of India seeking a Writ of Mandamus declaring the

action of the third respondent in preventing the petitioner from

construction of compound wall around his site admeasuring 696

square yards in Survey No.362/D of Algunoor Revenue Village,

Timmapur Mandal, Karimnagar District, as illegal and arbitrary.


</p><p id="p_2">2.    Heard the learned counsel for the petitioner and the learned

Standing Counsel for Gram Panchayat (TG) representing the third

respondent.
</p>
<p id="p_3">
</p><p id="p_4">3.    The case of the petitioner is that he purchased an extent of

696 square yards of land in Survey No.362/D of Algunoor Revenue

Village, Timmapur Mandal, Karimnagar District, from one Irukulla

Trilokanadh under a registered sale deed dated 13.04.2007. The

petitioner with an intention to construct a compound wall around

his site submitted an application on 08.09.2016 in the prescribed

form to the third respondent-Gram Panchayat seeking permission.

For one reason or other, the third respondent did not pass any

order on the application of the petitioner.


</p><p id="p_5">4.    At the time of hearing, the only relief sought by the learned

counsel for the petitioner is to direct the third respondent to

dispose of the application of the petitioner dated 08.09.2016 as

expeditiously as possible.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                    2</span><p id="p_6">


</p><p id="p_7">5.       In view of the submission made by the learned counsel for

the petitioner, this Court is not inclined to express any opinion

touching the merits of the main case.
</p>
<p id="p_8">
</p><p id="p_9">6.       Having regard to the facts and circumstances of the case, the

third respondent is hereby directed to pass appropriate orders on

the application of the petitioner dated 08.09.2016 in the light of

the provisions of the Telangana Gram Panchayat Act and Rules

made thereunder, as expeditiously as possible, preferably within a

period of two (2) months from the date of receipt of a copy of this

order.
</p>
<p id="p_10">
</p><p id="p_11">7.       With the above direction, the Writ Petition is disposed of.

There shall be no order as to costs.    Consequently, Miscellaneous

Petitions, if any, pending in this Writ Petition shall stand closed.


</p><p id="p_12">                                            _________________________
                                            T. SUNIL CHOWDARY, J
Date: 01.05.2018
