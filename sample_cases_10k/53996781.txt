<div class="doc_title">Ridhima Gupta D/O Rajesh Gupta vs University Of Rajasthan on 14 October, 2019</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: Inderjeet Singh</div>

<pre id="pre_1">       HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR RAJASTHAN
                   BENCH AT JAIPUR

               S.B. Civil Writ Petition No. 7603/2019

Ridhima Gupta D/o Rajesh Gupta, Aged About 21 Years, By
Caste Gupta, Resident Of 46, Jobner Bag, Near Chandpol Metro
Station, Station Road, Jaipur, Rajasthan.
                                                                      ----Petitioner
                                    Versus
1.     University Of Rajasthan, Through Its Registrar, J.l.n.
       Marg, Jaipur Rajasthan.
2.     Contorller Of Examination, University Of Rajasthan, J.l.n.
       Marg, Jaipur, Rajasthan.
3.     Principal, University Maharani College, Jaipur, Rajasthan.
                                                                   ----Respondents</pre>


<p id="p_1">For Petitioner(s)         :     Mr. Vijay Singh Yadav
For Respondent(s)         :     Mr. Ajit Maloo



            HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE INDERJEET SINGH

                                     Order

14/10/2019

     This writ petition has been filed by the petitioner with the

following prayer:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">        It is, most humbly and respectfully prayed that

        the present writ petition may kindly be accepted

        and allowed and by an appropriate writ, order or

        a   direction   the     respondents          may         kindly   be

        directed to permit/allow to the humble petitioner

        for submitting her offline examination form of

        BBA Part III-2019 and further may kindly be

        directed to the respondents to issue the admit

        card/permission letter in favour of the humble



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                     (Downloaded on 18/10/2019 at 09:04:20 PM)</span>
                                             (2 of 4)                       [CW-7603/2019]



        petitioner for participating the BBA Part III

        examination 2019 in the interest of justice.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_2">        Any other Hon'ble Court may kindly make such

        other order or direction in the nature thereof

        which it may deem expedient in the facts and

        circumstances of the present case and such other

        additional relief to the petitioner as may be

        deemed just and proper.</blockquote><p id="p_2">

</p><p id="p_3">     While issuing the notices, this Court on 25.04.2019 passed

the following order:-
</p>
<p id="p_4">
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_3">     "Counsel for the petitioner submits that the petitioner
     is a regular student of BBA and has also cleared BBA
     Part-I and II Examination and has also deposited
     complete fees including examination fees however
     the examination form could not be filled as she was
     suffering from illness for which documents has been
     placed before this Court. The Examination for BBA
     Part-III   are     to    commence            from      tomorrow         i.e.
     26.04.2019. She has already made a representation
     to the Controller of Examination on 20.04.2019 to
     allow her to appear in the examination and accept
     her examination form manually which has remained
     unanswered. Issue notice of the writ petition as also
     of stay application, returnable within two weeks.
     In the meanwhile, the petitioner's Examination Form
     shall be accepted manually today by the University,
     Controller of Examination and provisional admit card
     shall be issued to her today so that she would be
     allowed    provisionally         for     her      BBA         Part-      III
     Examination 2019 from the University Maharani
     College.    The     result      of     the     petitioner        and    her
     appearing    in      the     examination           shall       be     purely
     provisionally."
</blockquote>
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                       (Downloaded on 18/10/2019 at 09:04:20 PM)</span>
<blockquote id="blockquote_4">                                           (3 of 4)               [CW-7603/2019]




     Again on application submitted by the petitioner, this Court

on 30.09.2019 passed the following order:-
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_5">       "The matter comes up on an application filed by the

       petitioner for calling result of the petitioner of BBA

       Third Year.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_6">       For the reasons assigned in the application, it is

       allowed. Learned counsel for the respondents is

       directed to bring result of the petitioner in a sealed

       cover on the next date of hearing. Let this case be

       listed on 14th October, 2019."</blockquote>

<blockquote id="blockquote_7">     Today, in compliance of the order dated 30.09.2019, counsel

for the University has brought result of the petitioner.</blockquote><p id="p_5">

</p><p id="p_6">     I have perused the result of the petitioner for BBA Part-III,

Examination 2019, according to which, the petitioner has been

declared pass with First Division. Counsel for the petitioner

submitted that due to illness the petitioner failed to submit the

examination form, however, she has deposited the exam fee

within time.
</p>
<p id="p_7">     Counsel for the respondent-University submits that ample

opportunities were given to the petitioner for submitting the

application form and she herself was at fault for not submitting

the examination form within time.
</p>
<p id="p_8">     Heard counsel for the parties and perused the material

available on record.
</p>
<p id="p_9">     The writ petition filed by the petitioner deserves to be

allowed for the reasons; firstly this Court vide interim order dated

25.04.2019 granted permission to the petitioner to appear in the

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                     (Downloaded on 18/10/2019 at 09:04:20 PM)</span>
                                                                            (4 of 4)                [CW-7603/2019]



                                   aforesaid examination which she has successfully passed and

                                   secondly, this Court while granting the interim order has already

                                   considered the fact of illness of the petitioner due to which she

                                   failed to submit the examination form within time.

</p><p id="p_10">                                         In that view of the matter, the writ petition is allowed and

                                   the respondents are directed to declare result of the petitioner

                                   forthwith and issue mark-sheet to her.



</p><p id="p_11">                                                                                           (INDERJEET SINGH),J

                                   Upendra/119
