       THIS CRIMINAL PETITION COMING ON FOR ADMISSION THIS DAY,
THE COURT MADE THE FOLLOWING:-
                                   2

                                                CRL.P. NO.2600/2019




                               ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Heard Shri D.S. Shivananda, learned advocate for

petitioner     and    Shri   Nasrulla Khan, learned    HCGP    for

respondent-State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">2.    Petitioner has challenged proceedings initiated against

him for violation of Section 78(3) of Karnataka Police Act,

1963, alleging that he was involved in playing Matka.


</p><p id="p_4">3.    Learned advocate for the petitioner submits that the

said offence is non-cognizable and the learned Magistrate has

granted permission without applying his mind.


</p><p id="p_5">4.    Learned advocate for the petitioner has made available

a copy of requisition submitted by the Station House Officer,

Malebennur Police Station. A perusal of the said requisition

shows   that    the    learned   Magistrate   has   endorsed    as

'permitted'. No reasons are recorded for granting permission.


</p><p id="p_6">5.    This Court has taken a consistent view that a mere

endorsement made by the learned Magistrate as 'permitted'

without recording reasons is not a speaking order. [See The

Padubidri Members Lounge and others Vs. Director
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                3</span>

                                                CRL.P. NO.2600/2019




General and Inspector General of Police and others

(W.Ps.No.42073-42075/2018 D.D. 3.10.2018)].


</p><p id="p_7">6.    In the circumstances, this petition merits consideration

and it is accordingly allowed. Order dated 09.03.2017 in

C.C.No.326/2017 (Crime No.2/2017) passed by Principal Civil

Judge &amp; JMFC, Harihara, taking cognizance of the offence

punishable under Section 78(3) of Karnataka Police Act, 1963,

is quashed so far as petitioner is concerned.


</p><p id="p_8">7.    In view of disposal of this petition, I.A.No.1/2019 does

not survive of consideration and the same stands disposed of.


</p><p id="p_9">      No costs.
</p>
