<div class="doc_title">Cwp No.12378 Of 2013 (O &amp; M) vs Union Of India And Others on 30 May, 2013</div><pre id="pre_1">CWP No.12378 of 2013 (O &amp; M)                                           ::1::

 IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA AT CHANDIGARH

                    CWP No.12378 of 2013 (O &amp; M)
                    Date of Decision: May 30, 2013
Tarsem Singh
                                                                ....Petitioner
                              Versus

Union of India and others
                                                            .... Respondents

CORAM:         HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE AJAY KUMAR MITTAL
               HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE G.S. SANDHAWALIA

Present:       Mr.Naresh Kaushal, Advocate,
               for the petitioner.
         ****
AJAY KUMAR MITTAL, J.
</pre>
<p id="p_1">               The petitioner has approached this Court under Articles

226/227 of the Constitution of India seeking directions to the respondents

to pay solatium and other statutory benefits under Sections 23(1-A), 23(2),

28 and 34 of the <a href="/doc/7832/" id="a_1">Land Acquisition Act</a>, 1894 (for short 'the Act') along with

upto date interest to him.
</p>
<p id="p_2">2.             It has been prayed that the petitioner is entitled to the same

in view of the judgment of this     Court in M/s Golden Iron and Steel

Foregings versus Union of India and others, 2011(4)R.C.R. (Civil) 375.

</p><p id="p_3">3.             Learned counsel for the petitioner submitted that a demand

notice dated 16.05.2013 (Annexure P-3) had been sent to the respondents

whereupon no decision has been taken by them so far.

</p><p id="p_4">4.             After hearing learned counsel for the petitioner, perusing the

present petition and without expressing any opinion on the merits of the

case, the writ petition is disposed of by directing respondent No.2 to take a

decision on Annexure P-3 by passing a speaking order after affording an

opportunity of hearing to the petitioner in accordance with law within a

period of three months from the date of receipt of certified copy of the

order.
</p><p id="p_5"> CWP No.12378 of 2013 (O &amp; M)                                         ::2::
</p>
<p id="p_6">5.             Needless to say that, in the event, the petitioner is found

entitled to release of any payment, the same shall be released to him within

a period of next one month.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                                                  (AJAY KUMAR MITTAL)
                                                        JUDGE


                                                    (G.S. SANDHAWALIA)
                                                        JUDGE
May 30, 2013
sukhpreet
