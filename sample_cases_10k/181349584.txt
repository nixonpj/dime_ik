<div class="doc_title">Crl.O.P.No.6547 Of 2018 vs The Inspector Of Police on 5 March, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">        

 
IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT MADRAS

DATED: 05.03.2018

CORAM:

THE HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE M.S.RAMESH

Crl.O.P.No.6547 of 2018
Nisha Robert					                 ...  Petitioner
         Versus
The Inspector of Police,
Annur Police station,
Coimabore District.   					     ...  Respondent    					

	Criminal Original Petition filed under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C. praying to direct the respondent to register the FIR based on the petitioner complainant dated 18.01.2018 and proceeding in accordance with law.

		For Petitioner  	: 	M/s.Thenmozhi Shiva Perumal

	    	For Respondent 	: 	Mr.C.Raghavan
						Government Advocate (Crl. side)
				
					ORDER	
</pre><p id="p_1">	This petition is filed seeking a direction to the respondent to register the complaint of the petitioner dated 18.01.2018.
</p>
<p id="p_2">	2. By consent of both sides, this Criminal Original Petition is taken up for final disposal.
</p>
<p id="p_3">	3. The grievance of the petitioner is that inspite of a complaint given by her on 18.01.2018 to the respondent, the same has been kept in abeyance without any action. It is well settled in the judgment of the Hon'ble Supreme Court in Lalita Kumari Vs. Government of Uttar Pradesh and others [2013 (6) CTC 353], that registration of an FIR  is mandatory under <a href="/doc/1980578/" id="a_2">Section 154</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure if an information furnished to the police officer disclose commission of a cognizable offence and in cases where the information does not disclose a cognizable offence, a preliminary enquiry has to be conducted.
</p>

<p id="p_4">	4. The respondent is not justified in having received the complaint and keeping it unattended without any further action.  In view of the same, the petitioner has made out a case for this Court to invoke its inherent powers under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_3">Section 482</a> of the Criminal Procedure Code.
</p>  

<p id="p_5">	5. In the result, the petitioner is directed to give a copy of the complaint dated 18.01.2018 to the Station House Officer having territorial jurisdiction  over the issue. On receipt of the said copy of the complaint, the concerned Station House Officer shall adhere to the following directions.
</p><p id="p_6">	1) If the information received by the respondent discloses  commission of a cognizable offence, then, the same shall be forthwith registered.
</p><p id="p_7">	2) If an information received does not disclose a cognizable offence, the respondent shall conduct a preliminary inquiry to ascertain whether cognizable offence is disclosed therein or not and such inquiry shall be completed within a period of fifteen days from the date of information.
</p><p id="p_8">	3) If the inquiry discloses the commission of a cognizable offence, the FIR must be registered, if not already registered or closed.
</p><p id="p_9">	4) If the preliminary inquiry ends in closing the complaint, the disclosure report must be recorded along with the reasonings and a copy of the same shall be furnished to the complainant within one week.
</p><p id="p_10">	5) All information relating to cognizable offences whether resulting in registration of FIR or leading an inquiry must be reflected in the general diary/station diary/daily diary of the  respondent's police station.
</p>
<p id="p_11">	6. In the result, the Criminal Original Petition is allowed with the above directions.
</p>
