                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA AT
                                   CHANDIGARH


                                               CRM NO. M-28195 OF 2014 (O&amp;M)
                                               DECIDED ON : 31.10.2014


           Vipan Sharma and another
                                                                     ...Petitioners
                                     versus

           State of Punjab and another
                                                                     ...Respondents


           CORAM : HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE K. C. PURI


           Present : Mr. Vikas Gupta, Advocate,
                     for the petitioners.

                                Mr. Yogesh Gupta, AAG, Punjab.

                                Mr. V. K. Kaushal, Advocate,
                                for respondent No.2.


           K. C. PURI, J. (ORAL)
</pre>

<p id="p_1">                                This is a petition under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> of the Code of

           Criminal Procedure for quashing FIR No.212 dated 03.10.2013

           under <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_2">Sections 406</a>/<a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_3">498-A</a> of the Indian Penal Code registered at

           Police Station Cantonment, District Amritsar, on the basis of

           compromise, along with all subsequent proceedings arising from

           that FIR.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                Report from the trial Court has been received in which

           it is mentioned that parties have arrived at a compromise. This is

           a matrimonial dispute which has been amicably settled.


SHALINI BHATIA
2014.11.03 16:24
I attest to the accuracy and
authenticity of this document
High Court Chandigarh
            CRM NO. M-28195 OF 2014 (O&amp;M)                                       -2-
</p>
<p id="p_3">

</p><p id="p_4">                                Learned counsel for the complainant has also stated at

           the bar that the matter has been compromised.

</p><p id="p_5">                                So, in view of the above circumstances and in view of

           authority reported as, "<a href="/doc/168691516/" id="a_4">Kulwinder Singh and others vs. State

           of Punjab and others</a>" 2007 (3) RCR (Criminal) 1052, the

           above said FIR stands quashed. Further proceedings in pursuant

           to that FIR also stands quashed.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                Disposed of.
</p>
