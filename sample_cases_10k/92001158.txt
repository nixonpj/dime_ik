<div class="doc_title">Prayag Shiksha Samiti vs Union Of India And Others on 17 May, 2012</div><pre id="pre_1">   IN THE HIGH COURT OF UTTARAKHAND AT NAINITAL

                       Writ Petition (M/S) No. 944 of 2012.

Prayag Shiksha Samiti, Khatima, Udham Singh Nagar.
                                                                                ...Petitioner.
                                              Vs.
Union of India and others.
                                                                           ...Respondents.
Mr. S.S.Yadav, Advocate learned counsel for the petitioner.
Mr. B.P.S.Mer, Advocate, learned counsel for the U.O.I.-respondent no.1.
Mr. T.A.Khan, Senior Advocate, with Mr. Rajesh Joshi, Advocate, learned counsel for the N.C.T.E.-
respondent nos. 2 to 4.
Mr. B.D.Upadhyay, Senior Advocate, with Mr. Navin Tiwari, Advocate, learned counsel for the
Kumaun University.-respondent nos. 5 and 6.
Mr. N.P.Sah, learned Standing Counsel for the respondent No. 7.
Date May 17, 2012.
Hon'ble B.S.Verma, J.
</pre><p id="p_1">(Stay Application No.4732 of 2012)
                 Learned counsel for the petitioner seeks permission to
implead the State of Uttarakhand through Secretary Higher Education as
respondent no.7. Permitted to incorporate necessary amendments in the
array of parties during the course of the day.
</p><p id="p_2">                 The grievance of the petitioner is that recognition was
granted to the petitioner-institute by the National Council for Teacher
Education, Northern Regional Committee, for the Session 2012-2013,
while the application of the petitioner was pending since August, 2009.
</p><p id="p_3">                 Learned counsel for the petitioner has contended that in
view of the directions issued by the N.C.T.E. to the Kumaun University
by its letter dated 9-5-2012 (Annexure-8 to the writ petition), the
counselling ought to have been made for the session 2012-2013 instead
of academic session 2011-12. A reference was made to the order of the
Apex Court passed in Civil Appeal No. 1125-1128/2011 in the matter of
Bhawar Kanwar Singh Mahavidyalaya and others, therefore, as per time
schedule, it is a time for counselding for academic session 2012-2013.
Learned counsel for the petitioner further contended that the petitioner
be also permitted to participate in the counseling, which is being held by
the examining body for the session 2011-2012.
</p><p id="p_4">                 So far as the interim order sought by the petitioner is
concerned, there is no recognition in favour of the petitioner for the
academic session 2011-12, therefore, I am not inclined to grant any
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                     2</span>




interim order. (Stay application is rejected.) However, the University
and the N.C.T.E. shall file their counter affidavits and shall also explain
therein as to what schedule has been given by the Apex Court in the
aforesaid Civil Appeal No. 1125-1128 of 2011 in the matter of Bhawar
Kanwar Singh Mahavidyalaya and others.
</p><p id="p_5">             The respondents may file their counter affidavit in four
weeks.
</p><p id="p_6">             List this petition on 18-6-2012.
</p>



<p id="p_7">                                                      (B.S.Verma,J.)
RCP
