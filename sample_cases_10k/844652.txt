			: 
			
		
		 
			 

HONOURABLE
			MR.JUSTICE RAJESH H.SHUKLA
		
	

 

 
 


 

Date
: 01/09/2010 

 

ORAL
ORDER</pre> 

 

 


 


<p id="p_1">1.	Present
application has been filed by the applicant for grant of regular bail
under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of Cr.P.C. 
</p>
 

 


 


<p id="p_2">2.	The
applicant accused is charged with having committed offences under
<a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_2">Sections 406</a>, <a href="/doc/1326844/" id="a_3">409</a>, <a href="/doc/112749/" id="a_4">114</a>  of Indian Penal Code for which F.I.R. being
CR No.I-3/2010 has been registered with Javaharnagar Police Station,
Vadodara. 
</p>
 

 


 


<p id="p_3">3.	Learned
Advocate Ms. Kruti shah referred to the papers and submitted that on
the basis of the audit the inquiry was made and the applicant has
also been involved subsequently. She has further submitted that most
of the investigation is over and other co-accused have been released
and therefore the present application may be allowed.  
</p>
 


 


 


<p id="p_4">4.	Learned
A.P.P. resisted the application. He has stated that the charge-sheet
is yet to be filed but most of the investigation is over and 
therefore the present application may not be entertained.   
</p>
 


 


 


<p id="p_5">5.	Having
heard the learned Advocate Ms. Kruti Shah and learned A.P.P. and
having perused the papers and considering the fact that the
investigation is about to over and the other co-accused have been
released by way of an order passed in Criminal Misc. Application
No.6546 of 2010 dated 25.06.2010, the present application deserves to
be allowed subject to the conditions hereinafter. 
</p>
 
	 


	 


<p id="p_6">	6.	Accordingly,
	present application stands allowed. The applicant is ordered to be
	released on regular bail in connection with the F.I.R. being CR
	No.I-3/2010 which has been registered with Javaharnagar Police
	Station, Vadodara, on his executing a bond of Rs.5,000/- (Rupees
	Five Thousand) with one solvent surety of the like amount to the
	satisfaction of the lower Court and subject to the conditions that
	he shall:
</p>
 


<p id="p_7">(a)	not
take undue advantage of his liberty or abuse his liberty.
</p> 


<p id="p_8">(b)	not
to try to tamper or pressurize the prosecution witnesses or
complainant in any manner.
</p> 


<p id="p_9">(c)	not
act in any manner injurious to the interest of the prosecution.
</p> 


<p id="p_10">(d)	maintain
law and order and should cooperate with the investigating officers.
</p> 


<p id="p_11">(e)	mark
his presence before concerned Police Station on 1st Monday
of every month of English calender between 11:00 AM to 2:00 PM till
the trial commences;
</p> 


<p id="p_12">(f)	furnish
the address of his residence to the Investigating Officer and also to
the Court at the time of execution of the bond and shall not change
his residence without prior permission of the Court.
</p> 


<p id="p_13">(g)	surrender
his passport, if any, to the lower Court, within a week.
</p> 


 


 
	 

<p id="p_14">7.	If
	breach of any of the above conditions is committed, the concerned
	Sessions Judge will be  free to issue warrant or take appropriate
	action in the matter.
</p>	 


	 

<p id="p_15">8.	Bail
	before the lower Court having jurisdiction to try the case. It would
	be open to the trial Court concerned to give time to furnish the
	solvency certificate if prayed for.
</p>
 

 


 

 


 
	 

<p id="p_16">9.	Rule
	is made absolute to the aforesaid   extent. Direct service is
	permitted. 
</p>	

 


 


 


 


 


<p id="p_17">(RAJESH
H.SHUKLA, J.)
