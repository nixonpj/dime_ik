<div class="doc_title">Raj Kumar Mehtar vs State Of Bihar on 2 November, 2010</div><pre id="pre_1">                 IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                             Cr.Misc. No.33186 of 2010
                                RAJ KUMAR MEHTAR
                                          Versus
                                   STATE OF BIHAR
                                        -----------
</pre>


<p id="p_1">2   02.11.2010              Heard learned counsel for the petitioner as well

                 as learned counsel for the state.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                            Petitioner is husband of the informant and his

                 marriage   has   taken    place     in   the   year,   2004   but

                 subsequently, he used to torture the informant and

                 solemnized the second marriage with another girl.

</p><p id="p_3">                            Though the stand of petitioner is that he wants to

                 keep the informant with full honour and dignity but it appears

                 to me that after solemnization of the second marriage, there

                 is no chance of reconciliation. But in spite of that, I am

                 directing the learned Chief Judicial Magistrate, Madhepura

                 to take up the matter of reconciliation and if the informant

                 expresses her wishes to lead her conjugal life with

                 petitioner, in that event, the petitioner (Raj Kumar Mehtar)

                 shall be released on bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs

                 10,000/- (ten thousand) with two sureties of the like amount

                 each in connection with Bihariganj P.S. Case No. 38 of 2010

                 to the satisfaction of court below (Chief Judicial Magistrate,

                 Madhepura) u/S 438(2) of the <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_1">Cr.P.C</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                            Accordingly, the petitioner is directed to surrender

                 before the learned court below within 30 days from the date
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                             2</span>




        of receipt of the order before the concerned court, the

        learned court below shall enlarge him on provisional bail in

        the manner as stated above for the period of two months

        and also issue notices to the informant fixing a date for

        reconciliation and if proceeding of reconciliation is not

        concluded in positive manner, then the learned court below

        shall stop the proceeding of the reconciliation and remand

        the petitioner in the instant case.
</p>
<pre id="pre_2">AKV/-                           ( Hemant Kumar Srivastava,J.)
 </pre>
