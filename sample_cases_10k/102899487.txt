<div class="doc_title">Dwivedi Amar Kumar @ K.A.Dwivedi vs State Of Bihar Thru.C.B.I. on 18 June, 2009</div><pre id="pre_1">                          IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                 Cr.Misc. No.17999 of 2009
                              DWIVEDI AMAR KUMAR @ K.A.DWIVEDI
                                           Versus
                                 STATE OF BIHAR THRU.C.B.I.
                                        -----------
</pre>
<p id="p_1">2.   18.06.2009.                 Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned

                   counsel for the CBI.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                 This petition for bail has been filed by the petitioner,

                   who is an accused for offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1128948/" id="a_1">Section 120(B)</a> of

                   the Indian Penal Code read with <a href="/doc/1281473/" id="a_2">Section 13(1)</a>, <a href="/doc/1259316/" id="a_3">13(2)</a> of the

                   Prevention of the Corruption Act and Section 136 of the Custom

                   Act.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                 Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that the

                   petitioner is one of the inspectors and was a member of the team for

                   checking the passengers and their goods. Learned counsel for the

                   petitioner further states that the allegation is that in conspiracy with

                   each other the members of the team instead of making proper

                   checking and collecting custom duties from the passengers, chose to

                   collect less custom duties and/or no custom duties from them which

                   caused wrongful loss to the Government to the extent of Rs.1.25

                   lacs. Learned counsel for the petitioner also avers that assessment

                   of loss in the custom duty chargeable from the goods had been

                   prepared prior to arrival of the special team and hence the said

                   assessment is imaginary and cannot be relied upon. Learned counsel

                   for the petitioner further avers that the main accused and the leader

                   of the team was Mr. S. S. Sahay who was the Superintendent of

                   Custom (Headquarter) Patna and he has been enlarged on bail on the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                 -2-</span>




          orders of this Court dated 11.1.2008 passed in Cr. Misc. No. 54358

          of 2007. Learned counsel for the petitioner also submits that no

          specific overtact has been alleged against the petitioner.

</p><p id="p_4">                         Considering the facts and circumstances of this case

          as well as the arguments of the parties, this petition is allowed. Let

          the petitioner, namely Dwivedi Amar Kumar @ K. A. Dwivedi be

          released on bail on furnishing bail bonds of Rs.10,000/-(Rupees ten

          thousand) with two sureties of the like amount each to the

          satisfaction of learned Special Judge South Patna in connection with

          CBI Case No. 31/2004 ( R.C. No. 28(A)2004).
</p>
<p id="p_5">
</p><pre id="pre_2">kanchan                                            (S. N. Hussain, J.)
