<div class="doc_title">Harish Bari vs The State Of Jharkhand on 7 August, 2017</div><p id="p_1"> IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
          Criminal Revision No. 903 of 2017
 Harish Bari                                ... Petitioner
                 Versus
 The State of Jharkhand                     ... Opposite Party
 CORAM:           HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE RONGON MUKHOPADHYAY
 For the Petitioner              : Mr. Pravin Kumar, Advocate
 For the State                   : A.P.P.
</p><p id="p_2"> Order No. 04                                        Dated 07th August, 2017
           Learned counsel for the petitioner is permitted to remove the
 defect(s), as pointed out by the office in curse of the day.
     I.A. No. 6051 of 2017

           This application has been preferred by the petitioner for condonation
     the delay of 116 days in filing the criminal revision.
</p><p id="p_3">           In view of the fact that sufficient reasons have been shown with
     respect to delay of 116 days which had occurred in filing this criminal
     revision, the same is condoned and this I.A. is allowed and disposed of.
     Criminal Revision No. 903 of 2017
           It has been submitted by the learned counsel for the petitioner that
     neither the trial court nor the appellate court has correctly appreciated the
     material available on record while convicting the petitioner for the offence
     punishable u/s 307/448<a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_1"> of the Indian Penal Code</a> and <a href="/doc/1961417/" id="a_2">Section 25</a>(1-B)a of
     the <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_3">Arms Act</a> and the maximum sentence awarded to him is to undergo
     R.I. six years. It has also been submitted that the petitioner was acquitted
     for the offence u/s 26 of the <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_4">Arms Act</a> by the learned appellate court and
     that the petitioner is in custody since 13.11.2013.
</p><p id="p_4">           Call for the L.C.R.
</p><p id="p_5">           List this case under the heading 'For Admission' after six months.
           Considering the fact that the petitioner has remained in custody for
     more than half of the sentence awarded to him, during pendency of this
     application, the petitioner, named above, is directed to be released on bail
     on furnishing bail bond of Rs.10,000/- (Rupees Ten Thousand) with two
     sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of learned Trial Court
     [Assistant Sessions Judge-I, West Singhbhum at Chaibasa] in connection
     with Sessions Trial Case No. 171 of 2007(S).
</p><p id="p_6">           Let this order be communicated through fax at the cost of petitioner.
</p>

<p id="p_7">                                                     (Rongon Mukhopadhyay, J)
MK
