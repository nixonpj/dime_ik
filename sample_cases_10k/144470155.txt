<div class="doc_title">Rajendra Kumar Maheshwari vs State Of Uttarakhand And Others on 14 January, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">     IN THE HIGH COURT OF UTTARAKHAND AT NAINITAL
              Criminal Writ Petition No. 58 of 2016


Rajendra Kumar Maheshwari                       .......Petitioner
                           Versus
State of Uttarakhand &amp; others                 .......Respondents

Hon'ble Sudhanshu Dhulia, J. (Oral)
</pre><p id="p_1">1.            Mr. P.C. Pethshali, Advocate present for the
petitioner.
</p><p id="p_2">2.            Mr. K.S. Rautela, Government Advocate, present
for the State/respondent nos.1 &amp; 2.
</p><p id="p_3">3.            This Court finds that the matter is urgent, hence
heard on admission.
</p><p id="p_4">4.            The First Information Report has been lodged by
respondent no. 3, which has been registered as Crime/FIR No.
06 of 2016, under <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_1">Sections 420</a>/<a href="/doc/1985627/" id="a_2">467</a>/<a href="/doc/556166/" id="a_3">468</a> &amp; <a href="/doc/1466184/" id="a_4">471</a> of IPC, at
Police Station ITI, District-Udham Singh Nagar against the
present petitioner.
</p><p id="p_5">5.            The petitioner works in a Post Office. There are
allegations   of   cheating   against   the   present   petitioner.
Primarily, the allegation against the petitioner is that in
collision with one Smt. Janki Upreti and other co-accused
committed embezzlement of amount which he has to deposit
in the Post Office concerned.
</p><p id="p_6">6.            This is also prima facie opinion of this Court that
though the First Information Report has also been registered
under <a href="/doc/1985627/" id="a_5">Sections 467</a>/<a href="/doc/556166/" id="a_6">468</a> &amp; <a href="/doc/1466184/" id="a_7">471</a> of IPC as well, presently from
the averments of the First Information Report, the offences
against the above provisions are not made out against the
present petitioner. This, however, only a prima facie opinion of
this Court and it will all depend on further investigation.
</p><p id="p_7">7.             Considering the nature of offence and since the
maximum punishment for offence under <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_8">Section 420</a> of IPC is
seven years, a limited interference is called for in the matter.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                  2</span>




<p id="p_8">Another petition of the other co-accused has also been
disposed of by this Court today itself.
</p><p id="p_9">8.            The writ petition stands disposed with the
direction to the police authorities to proceed with the
investigation in accordance with law but as far as the arrest of
the petitioner is concerned the same may be done only under
the parameters as framed under <a href="/doc/1899251/" id="a_9">Section 41</a> of Cr.P.C. read
with the directions given by the Hon'ble Apex Court in the
case of Arnesh Kumar Vs. State of Bihar &amp; another,
reported in (2014) 8 SCC 273, are complied with, inter alia, by
giving due notice and reasons if the arrest is required.
</p><p id="p_10">9.            It is made clear that this order shall remain
operative till the filing of charge sheet.
</p><p id="p_11">10.           Urgency application (IA No.229 of 2016) stands
disposed.
</p><p id="p_12">11.           Stay application (CLMA No.512 of 2016) stands
disposed.
</p>

<p id="p_13">                                     (Sudhanshu Dhulia, J.)
                                        Vacation Judge
14.01.2016
Nitesh
 </p>
