<div class="doc_title">An Application For Anticipatory ... vs Chandana Mahaldar on 7 September, 2016</div><p id="p_1"><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                         1</span>


Sl.180
Sept 07,
2016                                    C.R.M. 6046 of 2016
s.d.
</p><p id="p_2">  rejected

              In the matter of : An application for anticipatory bail under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_1">Section
              438</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure filed on July 28, 2016 in
              connection with Pukhuria P. S Police Station Case no. 293 of 2015
              dated 23.11.2015 under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_2">Sections 498A</a>/<a href="/doc/653797/" id="a_3">304B</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_4">34</a> of the Indian Penal
              Code.
</p><p id="p_3">                                     And

              In the matter of : Chandana Mahaldar
                                                               ...petitioners.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                Mr. Musharraf Alam Sk.
</p><p id="p_5">                                                 ...... for the petitioners.

                                Mr. Prasun Kr. Dutta
                                Mr. Subrata Roy
                                                             ...for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_6">

                          The petitioner, apprehending arrest in connection with Pukhuria

              Police Station Case no. 293 of 2015 dated 23.11.2015 under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_5">Sections

              498A</a>/<a href="/doc/653797/" id="a_6">304B</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_7">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code, has come to this Court for anticipatory

              bail.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                          Heard the learned counsel appearing on behalf of the

              parties. Perused the case diary.

</p><p id="p_8">                         The    petitioner   is   the   mother-in-law     of   the   victim

             housewife, who committed suicide.          Both the husband and father-

             in-law are on bail.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                             2</span><p id="p_9">


              Having regard to the same, our attention has been

drawn to the statement of the neighbouring people at pages 10, 11

and 12 of the case diary and now considering the same, more

particularly what transpires therefrom, in our opinion, this is not a

fit case for anticipatory bail.

</p><p id="p_10">       Accordingly, the application for anticipatory bail stands

rejected.
</p>
<p id="p_11">

</p><p id="p_12">                                  (Ashim Kumar Roy, J.)




                                (Malay Marut Banerjee, J.)
