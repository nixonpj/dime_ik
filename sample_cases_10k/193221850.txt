<div class="doc_title">An Application For Anticipatory ... vs In Re:- Nazrul Islam Sk on 28 August, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                                        1

   92
28.08.2015</pre>
<p id="p_1">  sm
                                  CRM No.7527 of 2015

             In the matter of an application for anticipatory bail under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> of the Code of Criminal
             Procedure filed on 12.08.2015 in connection with Murarai P.S. Case No.33 of 2015 dated
             11.02.2015 under <a href="/doc/132967048/" id="a_2">sections 135</a> of the Electricity Act, 2003.</p><pre id="pre_2">

                                           And

             In Re:- Nazrul Islam Sk.    .. Petitioner


                     Mr.Mahaboob Ahmed .. for the petitioner

                     Mr.Dipankar Paramanik.         .. for the State.

                     Ms.Sreyashee Biswas.         ..for the WBSEDCL.


</pre><p id="p_2">                     The petitioner, apprehending arrest in connection with Murarai P.S. Case No.33 of 2015

             dated 11.02.2015 under <a href="/doc/132967048/" id="a_3">sections 135</a> of the Electricity Act, 2003, has approached this Court for

             anticipatory bail.
</p>
<p id="p_3">
</p><p id="p_4">                     Heard the learned advocates appearing on behalf of the parties. Perused

             the case diary.
</p>
<p id="p_5">
</p><p id="p_6">                     Acting on a source information, the officers of the West Bengal State

             Electricity Distribution Co. Ltd. together with the police personnel held a raid at the

             agricultural field of the petitioner and found that he was running a submersible

             pump by hooking electricity from overhead low tension line. At the said raid,

             various hooking items were seized from the spot and the local people, who were

             examined, supported the prosecution case.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                     We have been informed that already final assessment has been done, but

             against the same no step has been taken from the side of the petitioner and the

             actual loss caused to the Electricity Company is around Rs.60,000/-
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                                      2</span>



</p><p id="p_8">                     Having regard to the nature and seriousness of the allegation and the

             materials collected in support of the same, in our opinion, this is not a fit case for

             anticipatory bail. Accordingly, this application for anticipatory bail of the petitioner

             is rejected.
</p>
<p id="p_9">

</p><p id="p_10">                                                                                    (Ashim Kumar Roy, J.)



                                                                               (Malay Marut Banerjee, J.)




<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">   94</span>
28.08.2015
  sm
                              CRM No.7730 of 2015

             In the matter of an application for anticipatory bail under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_4">Section 438</a> of the Code of Criminal
             Procedure filed on 19.08.2015 in connection with Khanakul P.S. Case No.253 of 2015 dated
             16.06.2015 under <a href="/doc/132967048/" id="a_5">sections 135</a> (1)(a) of the <a href="/doc/132967048/" id="a_6">Electricity Act</a>, 2003 read with <a href="/doc/409589/" id="a_7">section 304</a> of the Indian
             Penal Code

                                            And

             In Re:- Arun Ghanta     .. Petitioner


                     Mr.Tanmay Chowdhury.. for the petitioner

                     Mr.Asit Nayek      .. for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_11">                     Ms.Sreyashee Biswas.            ..for the WBSEDCL.
</p>
<p id="p_12">
                     The petitioner, apprehending arrest in connection with Khanakul P.S. Case No.253 of 2015

             dated 16.06.2015 under <a href="/doc/132967048/" id="a_8">sections 135</a> (1)(a) of the <a href="/doc/132967048/" id="a_9">Electricity Act</a>, 2003 read with <a href="/doc/409589/" id="a_10">section 304</a> of the

             Indian Penal Code, has approached this Court for anticipatory bail.

</p><p id="p_13">                     Heard the learned advocates appearing on behalf of the parties. Perused

             the case diary.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                                                    3</span><p id="p_14">



       A little girl, aged about 7 years, died being electrocuted.


       It is submitted by the learned counsel for the petitioner that the girl died on

the land of one Soumen Hazra, who by joining the electric wire electrified his own

wire fencing and when the girl went there, she came in contact with the wire and

being electrocuted died.
</p>
<p id="p_15">       It is contended by the learned counsel of the petitioner that the present

petitioner is not involved in any way and he should be granted anticipatory bail.


</p><p id="p_16">       The learned counsel appearing on behalf of the State, on the other-hand,

opposing the prayer for anticipatory bail, submits that it is the petitioner, who was

drawing electricity illegally by hooking from overhead low tension line and from

that illegal connection, Soumen Hazra also electrified his wire fencing and the

present petitioner cannot escape his liability.


</p><p id="p_17">       After carefully going through the materials in the case diary, we are in

agreement with the learned counsel of the State. Accordingly, the prayer for

anticipatory bail of the petitioner is rejected.



</p><p id="p_18">                                                             (Ashim Kumar Roy, J.)



                                                         (Malay Marut Banerjee, J.)
