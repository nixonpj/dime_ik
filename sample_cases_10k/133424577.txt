<div class="doc_title">Sanjay Kumar &amp; Anr vs The State Of Bihar on 9 March, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                 IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA

                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.13829 of 2018
                          Arising Out of PS.Case No. -32 Year- 2018 Thana -BIHTA District- PATNA
                 ======================================================
                 1. Sanjay Kumar, Son of Vinay Yadav,
                 2. Vidya Yadav, Son of Late Subalal Yadav, Both are Resident of Village-
                    Raghopur, P.S.- Bihta, District- Patna.

                                                                                  .... ....   Petitioners
                                                        Versus
                 The State of Bihar.

                                                                .... .... Opposite Party
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioners    : Mr. Niraj Kumar, Advocate.
                 For the Opposite Party : Mr. Md. Sufiyan, A.P.P.
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE SUDHIR SINGH
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   09-03-2018                  Heard learned counsel for the petitioners and learned

                 counsel for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                The petitioners are languishing in custody since

                 12.01.2018 in a case for the offence registered under Sections

                 30(a) and 34 of the Bihar Prohibition and <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_1">Excise Act</a>, 2016.

</p><p id="p_3">                                 The prosecution story, in brief, is that total 30 liters

                 wine is said to have been recovered.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                 It has been submitted by learned counsel for the

                 petitioners that the petitioners have got no criminal antecedent.

                 There is no allegation of tampering with the witnesses alleged

                 against the petitioners. The petitioners have falsely been

                 implicated in the present case. It is alleged that total 30 liters wine
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">          Patna High Court Cr.M isc. No.13829 of 2018 (2) dt.09-03-2018</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                     2/2</span>




                      is recovered from the Tractor in question. The name of the

                      petitioners has come on the basis of alleged recovery made from

                      the Tractor. There is no compliance of <a href="/doc/1137396/" id="a_2">Section 100</a> Cr. P.C.

</p><p id="p_5">                                         On behalf of the State, it is submitted that the

                      petitioners are named in the F.I.R/ Complaint Case.

</p><p id="p_6">                                     Considering the aforesaid facts and circumstances, let

                      the petitioners above named, be released on bail on furnishing bail

                      bonds of Rs.10,000/- (Ten Thousand) each with two sureties of the

                      like amount each to the satisfaction of the learned Special Judge,

                      Excise, Patna, in connection with Special Case No. 331 of 2018,

                      arising out of Bihta P.S. Case No. 32 of 2018.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><pre id="pre_2">U.K./-                                                 (Sudhir Singh, J)
  U         T
