        IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB &amp; HARYANA
                    AT CHANDIGARH


                                               Date of decision:03.03.2017

Jassu and another

                                              ..............Petitioners

                                v.

Vikas Gupta and another
                                              ..............Respondents

Coram:      Hon'ble Mr.Justice Jaswant Singh


Present:-   Mr.MS Rana,Advocate for the petitioners

Jaswant Singh,J.
</pre>
<p id="p_1">            Land of petitioners, situated in revenue estate of Badkhal Tehsil

District Faridabad, was acquired for a public purpose namely for the

development and utilization of land as Institutional Sector 48,Faridabad.

Petitioners moved representations dated 28.7.2004 and 2.8.2009 to

respondent-HUDA for allotment of plot under the oustee policy. Since no

action was taken on their representations, they filed CWP 23907/2012

which was disposed of by this Court vide order dated 4.12.2012(P-1) with a

direction to respondents to look into the representations dated 28.7.2004

and 2.8.2009 of the petitioners and to take a decision thereon as per ratio of

law laid down by a DB of this Court in LPA No.2096/2011 decided on

25.4.2012(P-2).
</p>
<p id="p_2">            Now the instant contempt has been filed alleging disobedience

of orders dated 4.12.2012(P-1) and 25.4.2012(P-2) passed by this Court.



</p><p id="p_3">                                     1 of 2
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                  ::: Downloaded on - 11-03-2017 14:08:32 :::</span>
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2"> COCP 2413/2016                          2                                 105</span>

             Having heard counsel for the petitioners, no case for

proceedings under the <a href="/doc/1396751/" id="a_1">Contempt of Courts Act</a> is made out as the issue

involved herein has already been considered in COCP 623/2015 (Ashish

Kumar and others v Brijendra Singh and another) decided on 12.1.2017.

</p><p id="p_4">             It was noticed therein that in the aforesaid orders 4.12.2012 (P-

</p><p id="p_5">1) and 25.4.2012 (P-2) there is no positive direction mandating issuance of

advertisement, either periodically or annually or at any given point of time.

However, as and      when any allotment is made for general public on

acquisition of land, the oustees like the petitioners of District Faridabad

would be given proper opportunity in terms of the directions of the

aforesaid order dated 25.4.2012(P-2).
</p>
<p id="p_6">             Even today, at the time of hearing, learned counsel for the

petitioners is unable to show any directions contained in the order dated

25.4.2012(P-2) which requires the respondent-HUDA to issue advertisement

to allot plots to allottees without there being any acquisition. Further, it is

not the case of the petitioners that any advertisement for general allotment

of plots has been issued wherein plots for the oustees like the petitioners

have not been reserved.
</p>
<p id="p_7">             Dismissed.
</p>
