<div class="doc_title">Vijay Tiwari vs Babli on 16 April, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                    <a href="/doc/35709282/" id="a_1">Vijay Tiwari v. Smt. Babli and Ors</a>.
                                    1
                                                      WP. No.1144/2015

16.04.2015
      Shri H.K. Shukla, learned counsel for the petitioner.
      Shri R.P. Rathi, learned Deputy Govt. Advocate for the
respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">      This petition under <a href="/doc/1331149/" id="a_2">Article 227</a> of the Constitution of India
prays for the following reliefs:-
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">       (1)     That, the trial court ACJM chachoda Guna be
       directed to conclude the trial of miscellaneous case
       no. 1/12 Babli and other v. Vijay Tiwari within a
       stipulated time bound program within four months as
       the proceeding u/s 125 as a summary in nature.
       (2)     That, trial court further be directed not to allow
       the adjournment of the case without any sufficient
       cause.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">       (3)     That, the trial court further be directed to decide
       the petitioner's application u/s 91 CRPC immediately
       and also to decide the case on merit without allowing
       the unnecessary adjournment either of the parties.
       (4)     That, Cost of this litigation may also be
       awarded.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">       (5)     Any other relief which this Hon'ble Court deem
       fit in the facts and circumstances of the case may
       kindly be granted to the petitioner.
</blockquote><p id="p_2">2.    The principle grievances of the petitioner is that an
application filed under <a href="/doc/788840/" id="a_3">Section 91</a> of the Cr.P.C by the non-
applicant/petitioner in miscellaneous case no. 1/2012 pending
before the Court of JMFC, Chachoda is pending adjudication
since July 2014, as a result of which the main proceedings under
<a href="/doc/1056396/" id="a_4">Section 125</a> Cr.P.C (miscellaneous case no. 1/2012 Bubbly and
other v. Vijay Tiwari), ar getting delayed .
</p><p id="p_3">                          <a href="/doc/35709282/" id="a_5">Vijay Tiwari v. Smt. Babli and Ors</a>.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                         2</span>
<p id="p_4">                                                           WP. No.1144/2015

</p><p id="p_5">     3.    Though, the order sheets have been filed of the hearings
     that have taken place in the main case but during the course of
     arguments the learned counsel of the petitioner has restricted
     his prayer for a direction to the trial court to decide the
     application under <a href="/doc/788840/" id="a_6">Section 91</a> Cr.P.C within a time.
</p><p id="p_6">     4.      In view of the above, innocuous prayer and without
     commenting upon the fact as to whether the main case under
     <a href="/doc/1056396/" id="a_7">Section 125</a> Cr.P.C is protracted or not, this Court disposes of
     this petition with the following directions:-
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_4">           1.     The application filed on 15/7/2014 u/S 91 of
           <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_8">Cr.P.C</a>, if pending, be decided by learned Trial
           Judge, as expeditiously as possible, preferably
           within a period of two months from the date of
           receipt of copy of this order.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_5">           2.     No order as to costs.</blockquote>


<p id="p_7">                                                     (Sheel Nagu)
                                                        Judge
ar
