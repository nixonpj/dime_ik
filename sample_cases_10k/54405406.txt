<div class="doc_title">Siya Ram Singh &amp; Anr vs The State Of Bihar on 14 July, 2010</div><pre id="pre_1">                  IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                         Cr.Misc. No.21369 of 2010
                          1. SIYA RAM SINGH
                          2. Pawan Pratap Singh
                                   Versus
                             THE STATE OF BIHAR
                                   ------
</pre>
<p id="p_1">2/   14.07.2010               Heard learned counsel for the petitioners, learned

                  counsel for the State and Mr. Awadhesh Kumar, learned counsel for

                  the informant.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                              Petitioners along with others are alleged for making

                  demand of ransom, snatching of cash and chain as informant's

                  brother was a contractor. A lease was given to him for taking away

                  stone from Pachar Pahar. Allegation is doubted on the ground that

                  undoubtedly, lease was granted to the informant's brother but

                  explosion was not allowed even then violating the terms of lease

                  explosion was being made and in that continuation one day earlier

                  to the incident while there was explosion villagers injured. One of

                  the accused persons namely Santosh filed an F.I.R. to the above

                  effect. Later on petitioner no.1 received injury admitted to hospital

                  and made a statement to the effect that due to explosion several

                  villagers were injured and to save the skin, this false allegation is

                  leveled and petitioners are apprehending their arrest which never can

                  be justified at the moment.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                              In the event of arrest or surrender within one month

                  from the date of receipt/production of a copy of this order in

                  connection with Rafiganj P.S. Case No. 42 of 2010 above named

                  petitioners shall be released on bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs.

                  10,000/- (ten thousand) each with two sureties of the like amount
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                               2</span>




        each to the satisfaction of C.J.M., Aurangabad subject to the

        conditions as laid down under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_1">Section 438(2)</a> of Cr. P.C.


</p><pre id="pre_2">shail                             (Mandhata Singh, J.)
