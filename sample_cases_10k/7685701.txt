                    AT CHANDIGARH

                                         CWP No.2012 of 2014
                                         Decided on : 23.05.2016


Gaganpreet Kaur and others
                                                             ... Petitioners

                                   Versus

State of Punjab and others
                                                           ... Respondents

CORAM : HON'BLE MR.JUSTICE G.S. SANDHAWALIA

Present :   Mr. Maninder Arora, Advocate
            for the petitioners.

            Mr. Avinit Avasthi, AAG, Punjab.

G.S. Sandhawalia, J. (Oral)
</pre>
<p id="p_1">             The present writ petition has been filed for restraining the

respondents from relieving the petitioners from service and directing the

respondents to re-induct the petitioners as Guest Faculty Lecturers for the

Academic Year 2014-15 and allow them to continue in service till the

posts are filled up with regular incumbents.
</p>
<p id="p_2">            It is the case of the petitioners that they are working with

respondent No.5. Petitioners No.1 to 5 were appointed in July, 2013

against the advertisement published in June, 2013. Petitioners No.6 and

7 had also been in service for the last three years as Guest Faculty

Lecturers. They were being threatened that they would be relieved by the

Principal of the respondent-college to accommodate other similarly

situated persons. Resultantly, this Court was approached.

</p><p id="p_3">            Interim protection was granted to the petitioners vide order

dated 30.10.2014.
</p>
<p id="p_4">

</p><p id="p_5">                                1 of 2


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">             ::: Downloaded on - 25-05-2016 00:09:01 :::</span>
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2"> CWP No.2012 of 2014                                                    -2-</span>




             State in its reply through the Principal of the College has

taken the plea that they were engaged by the Parent Teacher Association

of Government Mohindra College, Patiala on account of additional work

load, due to the influx of students in 2013-2014. Reference is made to

CWP No.15482 of 2012 'Prem Lata Vs. State of Punjab and others'

decided on 14.08.2012 and that the case of the petitioner would be dealt

accordingly provided work was available with the department, and

subject to the satisfaction of the authorities.

</p><p id="p_6">             Accordingly, keeping in view the written statement filed, the

present writ petition is disposed of in the same terms as Prem Lata

(supra) and the respondent No.5 would be bound by the same.</p><pre id="pre_2">
