<div class="doc_title">Jitendar Rai &amp; Anr. vs The State Of Bihar on 12 August, 2013</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.22580 of 2013
                 ======================================================
                 1. Jitendar Rai Son Of Ram Kripal Rai
                 2. Arun Rai Son Of Satyadeo Rai
                 All Residents Of Village- Banjaria, P.S.- Madhuban, District- East
                 Champaran
                                                                 .... ....   Petitioner/s
                                                   Versus
                 The State Of Bihar
                                                      .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s : Mr.
                 For the Opposite Party/s   :   Mr.
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE AHSANUDDIN AMANULLAH
                                                ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">3   12-08-2013                   Heard learned counsel for the petitioners and

                 learned A.P.P. for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                 The petitioners apprehend arrest in connection with

                 Madhuban P.S. Case No. 160 of 2012 registered for offence

                 punishable under <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_1">Sections 307</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_2">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code and 27

                 of the <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_3">Arms Act</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                 The allegation against the petitioners is of firing

                 upon the brother of the informant, who somehow managed to save

                 himself.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                 Learned counsel for the petitioners submits that

                 there has been no recovery from the place of occurrence and that

                 even as per the allegation, there was no injury on the brother of
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">            Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.22580 of 2013 (3) dt.12-08-2013</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                      2/2</span>




                        the informant. Learned counsel submits that the petitioner no. 1

                        has one criminal case under the <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_4">Arms Act</a> whereas the petitioner

                        no. 2 has no criminal antecedent. The same has been stated in the

                        supplementary affidavit filed today.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                           Learned A.P.P. for the State opposes the prayer for

                        anticipatory bail and submits that there is specific allegation of

                        possessing and using of firearms by the petitioners. It is further

                        submitted that as per the finding in the order dated 22.04.2013 of

                        the Sessions Judge, Motihari, by which the prayer for anticipatory

                        bail of the petitioners was rejected, various witnesses have

                        supported the occurrence of firing.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                           Considering the facts and circumstances of the case,

                        this Court is not inclined to enlarge the petitioners on anticipatory

                        bail.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                                           Accordingly, the same stands dismissed.

                                           However, in the event the petitioners surrender and

                        seek regular bail, the Court concerned shall decide the same on its

                        own merits without being prejudiced by the present order.


</p><p id="p_8">                                                         (Ahsanuddin Amanullah, J.)
Anjani/-
</p>
