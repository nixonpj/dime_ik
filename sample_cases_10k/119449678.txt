<div class="doc_title">Snandy vs Saraswati Bala &amp; Ors on 26 March, 2014</div><div class="doc_author">Author: Harish Tandon</div>

<p id="p_1"><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">1</span>



                                              CO 903 of 2014
    26.03.2014
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">    Court No. 22</span>
                                                Sufia Bibi
    Item No. CL-70
    snandy                                         Vs.
                                           Saraswati Bala &amp; Ors.
</p><p id="p_2">    (DISPOSED OF)


                     Mr. Partha Pratim Roy, Advocate
                     Mr. S. Sanyal, Advocate
                                                .....for the Defendant/Petitioner


                            This revisional application is directed against order no.

                     86 dated 07.01.2014 passed by the learned Civil Judge,

                     Junior Division, Tehatta, Nadia, in Title Suit No. 76 of 2008 by

                     which an application under Order 39 Rule 7 of the Code of

                     Civil Procedure is rejected.
</p>
<p id="p_3">
</p><p id="p_4">                            The plaintiff/opposite party filed a suit for declaration of

                     title and permanent injunction. It is specifically averred in the

                     plaint that the subject property is an agricultural land and the

                     defendants/petitioners has no semblance of right, title and

                     interest thereupon as they have been simply allowed to

                     cultivate.   In the written statement, the petitioner took a

                     specific defence that they have acquired right, title and

                     interest in a portion of the suit property on the strength of a

                     deed executed by the admitted owner. It is further averred

                     that they are enjoying the suit property as a dwelling house

                     and, therefore, the assertions of the plaintiff/opposite party

                     that the suit property is an agricultural land, is incorrect.



</p><p id="p_5">                            The suit reached at the peremptory stage when the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3"> 2</span>



    third witness of the plaintiff was on the dock. During cross-

    examination, a suggestion was made to the said witness

    whether there exists a dwelling house on the suit premises,

    the said witness denied the same. Because of such denial,

    the present application is taken out by the petitioner for

    appointment of an Advocate Commissioner to inspect and

    submit a report whether there exists any dwelling house on

    the suit plot and the number of dwelling houses of the

    respective families residing there.


</p><p id="p_6">          Order 39 Rule 7 of the Code of Civil Procedure cannot

    be invoked to fish out and/or gather evidence for the parties.

    The inspection is necessary when the Court is satisfied that it

    would elucidate the matter in dispute.      Whether the suit

    property is an agricultural land or is a land purchased by the

    defendant/petitioner and have made construction thereon can

    be proved by other evidences for which the Commissioner

    should not be appointed. The Trial Court, therefore, have not

    committed any illegality and/or infirmity in passing the

    impugned order.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><p id="p_8">          With these observations, the revision application is

    disposed of. No order as to costs.
</p>
