<div class="doc_title">Shrimati Prema Devi vs State Of U.P. And 2 Others on 16 November, 2019</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: Pankaj Naqvi, Suresh Kumar Gupta</div>

<pre id="pre_1">




HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT ALLAHABAD
 
 

?Court No. - 43
 

 
Case :- CRIMINAL MISC. WRIT PETITION No. - 23758 of 2019
 

 
Petitioner :- Shrimati Prema Devi
 
Respondent :- State Of U.P. And 2 Others
 
Counsel for Petitioner :- Rakesh Kumar Yadav
 
Counsel for Respondent :- G.A.
 

 
Hon'ble Pankaj Naqvi,J.
</pre> 
<p id="p_1">Hon'ble Suresh Kumar Gupta,J.
</p> 
<p id="p_2">Heard Sri Rakesh Kumar Yadav learned counsel for the petitioner and Sri Amar Nath Shukla learned AGA. 
</p> 
<p id="p_3">This writ petition has been filed for seeking a writ of mandamus commanding the respondent concerned to conduct fair investigation in pursuance of FIR dated 13.8.2018 in Case Crime No. 0125/2018, under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_1">Sections 302</a>, <a href="/doc/386021/" id="a_2">201</a> IPC, P.S. Sujanganj, District Jaunpur. 
</p> 
<p id="p_4">It is submitted that petitioner is an informant in the above case and despite an application to the authorities concerned for fair investigation, no action whatsoever has been taken, an appropriate direction be issued for fair and expeditious investigation. 
</p> 
<p id="p_5">It is well settled in view of the decision of the Apex Court in Sakiri Vasu v. State of U.P., (2008) 2 SCC 409 as reiterated in Sudhir Bhaskarrao Tambe v. Hemant, Yashwant Dhage and others, (2016) 6 SCC 277 that in the event of unsatisfactory investigation, remedy of the aggrieved person is not to approach the High Court under Article 226 of the Constitution of India but to approach the Magistrate concerned under Section 156(3) Cr.P.C. 
</p> 
<p id="p_6">Paragraphs 2 and 3 of Tambe (supra) are quoted hereunder: 
</p> 
<p id="p_7">"2. This Court has held in <a href="/doc/1836621/" id="a_3">Sakiri Vasu v. State of U.P</a>., that if a person has a grievance that his FIR has not been registered by the police, or having been registered, proper investigation is not being done, then the remedy of the aggrieved person is not to go to the High Court under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_4">Article 226</a> of the Constitution of India, but to approach the Magistrate concerned under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_5">Section 156(3)</a> Cr.P.C. If such an application under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_6">Section 156(3)</a> Cr.P.C. is made and the Magistrate is, prima facie, satisfied, he can direct the FIR to be registered, or if it has already been registered, he can direct proper investigation to be done which includes in his discretion, if he deems it necessary, recommending change of the investigating officer, so that a proper investigation is done in the matter. We have said this in Sakiri Vasu case because what we have found in this country that the High Courts have been flooded with writ petitions praying for registration of the first information report or praying for a proper investigation. 
</p> 
<p id="p_8">3. We are of the opinion that if the High Courts entertain such writ petitions, then they will be flooded with such writ petitions and will not able to do any other work except dealing with such writ petitions. Hence, we have held that the complainant must avail of his alternate remedy to approach the Magistrate concerned under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_7">Section 156(3)</a> Cr.P.C. and if he does so, the Magistrate will ensure, if prima facie he is satisfied, registration of the first information report and also ensure a proper investigation in the matter, and he can also monitor the investigation." 
</p> 
<p id="p_9">Thus, in view of the above, remedy, if any, for the petitioner is to approach the competent Magistrate in respect of his grievance.
</p> 
<p id="p_10">With the aforesaid observation, writ petition is disposed of. 
</p> 
<p id="p_11">Order Date :- 16.11.2019
 
Ankita
