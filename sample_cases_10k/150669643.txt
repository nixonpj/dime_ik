     THIS MFA FILED U/S.173(1) OF MV ACT, AGAINST THE
JUDGMENT AND AWARD DATED 03.02.2016 PASSED IN MVC

    THESE MFAs ARE COMING ON FOR CONCILIATION

ORDER IS PASSED.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                 CONCILIATION ORDER

     MFA No.101370/2016 is filed by the Insurance

Company seeking reduction and MFA No.100686/2016

is filed by the claimant seeking enhancement of

compensation made in MVC No.516/2015 on the file of

the Senior Civil Judge cum Member, Motor Accident

Claims Tribunal-VI, Kudligi.


</p><p id="p_9">2.   The   learned     counsel   for   the   claimant   and

representative of the Insurance Company along with its

counsel are present.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                     :4:</span><p id="p_10">

</p><p id="p_11">3.    After prolonged negotiations, the matter is settled.

The claimant has agreed to receive and the Insurance

Company      has       agreed   to        pay   a   compensation     of

Rs.2,00,000/- (Rupees Two Lakhs only) globally, instead

of   Rs.3,49,800/-       (Rupees          Three     Lakhs    Forty-nine

Thousand      Eight      Hundred          Only)     awarded    by   the

Tribunal. A joint Memo is filed on behalf of the parties

to this effect.
</p>
<p id="p_12">
</p><p id="p_13">4.    Out         of    the     compensation             amount      of

Rs.2,00,000/-,         Rs.50,000/-(Rupees            Fifty   Thousand

only) is ordered to be kept in Fixed Deposit in the

nationalized bank for a period of three year with liberty

to the claimant to withdraw the periodical interest

accrued      thereon          and         remaining      amount      of

Rs.1,50,000/- (Rupees One lakh Fifty Thousand Only) is

ordered to be released in favour of the claimant.


</p><p id="p_14">5.    The Insurance Company has agreed to deposit the

said amount before the Tribunal within six weeks from
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                              :5:</span>

the date of preparation of Award, failing which the said

amount shall carry interest at the rate of 9% p.a. from

the date of default, till the date of deposit.


</p><p id="p_15">6.    In view of the above, both the appeals stands

disposed of in terms of the Joint Memo. The award of

the Tribunal shall stand modified accordingly. Draw up

the award accordingly.
</p>
<p id="p_16">
</p><p id="p_17">      The amount deposited by the Insurance Company

before   this   Court    shall     be   transmitted   to   the

jurisdictional Tribunal.
</p>
