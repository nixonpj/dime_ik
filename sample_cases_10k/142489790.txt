                           JUDGMENT
</pre>
<p id="p_1">ANTONY DOMINIC,J.
</p>
<p id="p_2">      The petitioner and the 1st respondent are wife and husband.

They have three children in their marital life. It is stated that,

after the separation of the couple, leaving the children in the care

and custody of the petitioner's parents, she left to Sharjah for an

employment. While so, on 2.10.2013, the 1st respondent took

the children from the care and custody of her parents by force.

She thereupon filed O.P.(G&amp;W)No.1042/2013 before the Family

Court, Malappuram. In that O.P., an order was passed for the

production of the children before the Family Court on

11.11.2013. She says that this order was not complied with by

the 1st respondent and that thereupon the Family Court passed

Ext.P7 order directing the production of the children on

13.11.2013.     That also was not complied with by the 1st

respondent.     Therefore, contending that the custody of the

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">W.P.(Crl)No.537/13                 2</span>

children with the 1st respondent amounts to an illegal

detention, she has filed this writ petition seeking production of

the children before this Court and to set them at liberty.

</p><p id="p_3">      2.     Evidently therefore, the case of the petitioner is one

of non-compliance of the order passed by the Family Court,

Malappuram requiring the 1st respondent to produce the

children.     In our view, if that is the grievance, the remedy

available to the aggrieved person is to approach the Family

Court itself requesting to take appropriate action for enforcing

its own order.
</p>
<p id="p_4">      Therefore, with that liberty, we close this writ petition.




</p><p id="p_5">                                 ANTONY DOMINIC, JUDGE




                                       P.D. RAJAN, JUDGE.
