  ap
                           W. P. 4866 (W) of 2014

                              Adhar Chandra Dey
                                           Vs.
                          The State of West Bengal &amp; Ors.

                             Mr. Amit Kumar Ghosh
                                  ... For the petitioner.

                            Mr. Subhabrata Datta
                            Mr. Benazir Ahmed
                                         ... For the State.

              Affidavit-of-service filed in Court today be taken on record.
                         Having heard the learned advocates for the parties
                  and upon perusing the instant writ petition, it appears that
                  the petitioner is essentially seeking to be considered
                  favourably for the purpose of being recruited as a Gram
                  Panchayat Karmee on the basis of his earlier engagement as
                  a Tax Collecting Sarkar. This is not a matter for the writ
                  Court to adjudicate and issue a writ in the nature of
                  mandamus commanding the respondent authorities to</pre>
<p id="p_1">                  earmark 33 per cent of the vacant posts for the Tax
                  Collectors/ Collecting Sarkars in respect of the selection
                  process for the post of Gram Panchayat Karmees. It will be
                  open to the concerned respondent authorities to take
                  necessary steps in accordance with law and if the petitioner
                  is found otherwise eligible and comes within the zone of
                  consideration, his candidature will be suitably considered in
                  accordance with law.
</p><p id="p_2">                         The     writ    petition,     thus,     stands       disposed   of
                  accordingly.
</p><p id="p_3">                         Urgent photostat certified copy of this order, if applied
                  for, be given to the learned advocates for the parties.
</p>



<p id="p_4">                                        (Biswanath Somadder, J.)
