<div class="doc_title">Rajkiran Jaiswal @ Rajkiran Kumar vs The State Of Bihar on 14 December, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                        IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                            Criminal Miscellaneous No.66489 of 2018
                   Arising Out of PS. Case No.-35 Year-2018 Thana- MAHILA P.S.
                                          District- Bhagalpur
                 ==========================================
                 Rajkiran Jaiswal @ Rajkiran Kumar, Son of Mahendra Bhagat,
                 Resident of Village- Dholbajja, P.S.- Dholbajja, District- Bhagalpur.

                                                                   ... ... Petitioner/s
                                                Versus
                 The State of Bihar.

                                                           ... ... Opposite Party/s
                 ==========================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :      Mr. Manoj Kumar Jha
                 For the Opposite Party/s :      Mr. Sri Chaubey Jawahar
                 ==========================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE ASHUTOSH KUMAR
                                            ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   14-12-2018              Heard the learned counsel for the petitioner, the

                  informant and the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                            The petitioner seeks bail in anticipation of his

                  arrest in connection with Naugachia Mahila P.S. Case No. 35

                  of 2018 dated 02.07.2018 instituted for the offences under

                  <a href="/doc/1868826/" id="a_1">Sections 498(A)</a>, <a href="/doc/508426/" id="a_2">494</a>, <a href="/doc/140515/" id="a_3">313</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_4">323</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_5">504</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_6">506</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_7">34</a> of the Indian

                  Penal Code and <a href="/doc/751411/" id="a_8">Section 3</a>/ <a href="/doc/1023340/" id="a_9">4</a> of the Dowry Prohibition Act.

</p><p id="p_3">                            The petitioner is husband of the informant who is

                  alleged to have tortured the informant for non-fulfillment of

                  dowry demand, got her pregnancy aborted and solemnized a

                  second marriage.
</p><p id="p_4"> Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.66489 of 2018(2) dt.14-12-2018
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                             2/3</span>




                      The      learned      counsel       for   the   petitioner   has

         submitted that prior to lodging of the present F.I.R, the

         petitioner has filed a Sanha against the informant and as

         soon as the informant received notice in the Matrimonial

         Case No. 112 of 2017 which was filed by the petitioner for

         the dissolution of marriage, the present F.I.R has been

         lodged.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                      The learned counsel appearing for the informant,

         however has submitted/suggested that the matter be sent to

         the Mediation Centre of the Patna High Court for the

         needful.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                      This Court has an opinion that the matter would be

         best settled before the Mediation Centre of the High Court.

</p><p id="p_7">                      With consent of both the parties, the matter is

         transferred to the Mediation Centre of the Patna High Court

         and the Secretary of the Mediation Centre shall assign this

         matter to a Seasoned Mediator.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                      For the purposes of commencing the act of

         mediation, the petitioner as well as the informant shall

         appear before the Mediation Centre on 17th of January,
             Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.66489 of 2018(2) dt.14-12-2018
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                         3/3</span>




                     2019 at 2:30 P.M with all relevant documents along with this

                     order of the Court. The process of mediation shall be

                     concluded within a period of three months from the date of

                     first presentation of the parties.
</p>
<p id="p_9">                                  Re-notify on 24th of April, 2019 to report on the

                     result of the mediation.
</p>
<p id="p_10">                                  In the meantime, no coercive steps shall be taken

                     against the petitioner in connection with Naugachia Mahila

                     P.S. Case No. 35 of 2018.
</p>
<p id="p_11">


</p><p id="p_12">                                                                    (Ashutosh Kumar, J)
Shageer/-
</p>
