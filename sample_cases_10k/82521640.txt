          KARKARDOOMA COURTS : DELHI

Title                                    State Vs. Tek Chand &amp; Ors.

Name of accused                          1. Tek Chand S/o Mawasi Ram
                                         2. Lilu @ Lalit S/o Tek Chand

Final order                              Acquitted

</pre><p id="p_2">BRIEF REASONS FOR THE DECISION OF THE CASE:­


                The case was registered on the basis of a tehriri bayaan of
one Jyoti. Police investigated the matter and filed the present charge­
sheet indicting the aforesaid four accused persons who are relatives of
said complainant Jyoti. The case appears to have been a family dispute.
Following notice was framed in this case:­


                "That   on   09.01.2015   in   night,   you   all   in
                furtherance   of   your   common   intention,   beaten
                the complainant, resulting into simple hurt and 

FIR No. 44/15                     State Vs. Tek Chand &amp; Ors.             PS Gazipur
                                               ­2­
                  also wrongfully restrained her in such a manner
                  so as to prevent her from going anywhere out of
                  your house and as such you committed offences
                  punishable under Section 323/342/34 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_3">IPC</a> and
                  within my cognizance."
</p>
<p id="p_3">2.                The prosecution examined PW1 ASI Vinod Kumar, PW2 Ct.
Shiv Kumari, PW3 Ct. Vinita, PW4 ASI Naresh, PW5 Dr. Ruby, PW6 SI
Kiran Pal, PW7 ASI Raj Kumar.     However, the prosecution has failed to
produce complainant Jyoti as a witness. Since no incriminating circumstances
have  been found, examination of accused u/s. 313 <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_4">Cr.PC</a> has been dispensed
with.   Both   the   sides   have   been   heard.   I   proceed   to   dispose   off   this   case
through this Judgment. 
</p>

<p id="p_4">3.                The IO ASI Raj Kumar deposed in following manner:­

                  "On 10.01.2015, I was posted as HC at PS
                  Gazipur.    On   that   day   investigation   of   the
                  present   case   was   marked   to   me   after
                  registration of FIR. At that time, complainant
                  was present in the PS, therefore, I alongwith
                  complainant and W/Ct. Shiv Kumari went to
                  the spot i.e. C­648, Mulla Colony, Gazipur.
                  At   the   spot,   I   prepared   site   plan   at   the
                  instance of complainant, which is Ex. PW7/A
                  bearing my signature at point A. I searched
                  for the accused persons, but could not find
                  them.   On   21.01.2015,   complainant   Jyoti
                  came   to   PS   and   informed   me   that   accused
                  persons who were her in­laws were present
                  at   her   matrimonial   home.   Thereafter,   I
                  alongwith complainant and W/Ct. Anita went
                  to the house of accused persons i.e.   C­648,
                  Mulla   Colony,   Gazipur.   I   interrogated
                  accused persons Tek Chand (father in law),
                  Nirmla (Jaithani), Pramod (Jeth) and Leelu
                  @ Lalit (Devar). Thereafter, I arrested the

                accused   persons   vide   arrest   memo   already
                Ex.   PW3/A   to   Ex.   PW3/D   all   bearing   my
                signature   at   points   B.   I   conducted   the
                personal search of the accused persons Tek

                prepared   the   charge­sheet   and   filed   before
                the   Court.  All   the   accused   persons   are
                present   in   the   Court   today   (correctly
                identified by the witness)." 
</p>

<p id="p_5">4.              The   aforesaid   though   shows   what   happened   after   the
incident,   but   the   same   does   not   establish   as   to   whether   the   alleged
incident ever occurred or if occurred, in what manner and who were the
culprits. Only the complainant can be said to be in the knowledge of
actual   event   and   no   one   else   can   establish   the   same   except   her.
Ofcourse,   we   cannot   ask   the   accused   persons   to   give   testimony
regarding   the   incident   as   it   would   be   violetive   of   their   rights.   The
prosecution   having   not   produced   the   complainant   in   evidene,   cannot
claim   to   have   established   the   incident   by   the   deposition   of   police
officials

</p><p id="p_6">5.              PW5 Dr. Rubi deposed in follwing manner:­


FIR No. 44/15                      State Vs. Tek Chand &amp; Ors.                   PS Gazipur
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                             -4-</span>
                "On 09.01.2015, I was posted as CMO at LBS
                hospital and was working in Casualty.  On that
                day   at   about   11.30   p.m.,   I   examined   injured
                namely Jyoti with alleged history of assault.   I
                prepared   the   MCL   Ex.   PW5/A   bearing   my
                signature at point A. The nature of injury was
                simple   fresh   since   the   patient   had   sustained
                traumatic   swelling   on   right   parietal   region
                about 1x1 cm."
</p>

<p id="p_7">6.               The aforesaid statement of doctor even if taken as true, can
only   establish   that   some   injury   was   there   in   respect   of   complainant.
However,   this   doctor   cannot   establish   if   the   injury   was   due   to   any
assault made by the accused persons or not. In such circumstances, the
State cannot take any benefit from this doctor.
</p>
<p id="p_8">7.               Other police witnesses can also not establish the incident
in the absence of testmony of complainant.
</p>
<p id="p_9">8.               The   above   analysis   shows   that   the   prosecution   has   not
been able to establish the incident or the guilt of accused persons and as
such   accused   persons   deserve   an   acquittal.  Ordered   accordingly.
Accused persons to furnish bail bond and surety bond to the tune of
Rs. 10,000/­ for the purpose of Section 437­A <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_6">Cr.PC</a>.</p><pre id="pre_2">
