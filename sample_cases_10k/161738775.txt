<p id="p_1">            TEJINDER SINGH DHINDSA.J

                                The instant petition has been filed under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C

            praying for issuance of directions to official respondents no.1 to 4 to

            proceed further in the matter in the light of an inquiry report dated

            16.5.2011 at Annexure P-1.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                Learned counsel for the petitioner has been heard.

                                Brief facts, as would emerge from the pleadings on record are

            that a complaint was filed by the present petitioner as regards negligence at

            the hands of Dr. Parminder Kaur Johal, respondent no.5 and Dr. Harjinder

            Singh Sidhu, respondent no.6 while dealing with the delivery case of his

            daughter-in-law namely Amandeep Kaur and which finally had resulted in

            the unfortunate incident of a still born child on 28.4.2011.

</p><p id="p_3">                                Apparently, acting on the complaint lodged by the present

            petitioner, the Deputy Commissioner, Moga had constituted a Committee

            consisting of S.D.M., Dy.S.P., City Moga and C.M.O, Moga to go into the

            allegations of medical negligence at the hands of the afore-noticed two

            doctors. The report dated 16.5.2011 of such Committee has been placed on

            record at Annexure P-1. The conclusion and findings recorded by the

</p>
<p id="p_4">                                The petitioner has approached this Court by filing the present

            petition being aggrieved of the inaction of the official respondents in not

            having proceeded further in the matter inspite of the report having been

            submitted by the duty constituted Committee.

</p><p id="p_5">                                Without making any comments and observations on merits, I

            deem it appropriate to dispose of the present petition with a direction to the

            Deputy Commissioner, Moga, respondent no.3 to look into the matter and to

            take all such steps as he may deem fit and as would be warranted strictly in

            accordance with law as a follow-up to the inquiry report dated 16.5.2011 at

            Annexure P-1 furnished by the duly constituted Committee.
