<div class="doc_title">Lallan Sharma vs The State Of Bihar on 9 September, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                              CRIMINAL MISCELLANEOUS No.38691 of 2019
                   Arising Out of PS. Case No.-213 Year-2018 Thana- KUCHAIKOTE District- Gopalganj
                 ======================================================
           1.     Lallan Sharma Son of Late Bhagwat Sharma Resident of Village- Pakari,
                  P.S. - Kuchaikote, District- Gopalganj.
           2.    Vishal Sharma Son of Lallan Sharma Resident of Village- Pakari, P.S. -
                 Kuchaikote, District- Gopalganj.
           3.    Dhiraj Kumar Sharma @ Dhiraj Sharma Son of Lalu Sharma Resident of
                 Village- Pakari, P.S. - Kuchaikote, District- Gopalganj.

                                                                                 ... ... Petitioner/s
                                                      Versus
                 The State of Bihar

                                                        ... ... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :      Mr. Mukesh Kumar Singh, Advocate
                 For the Opposite Party/s :      Mr. Satya Nand Shukla, APP
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE PRABHAT KUMAR SINGH
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">4   09-09-2019                  Heard learned counsel for the petitioners and learned

                   counsel for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                The petitioners apprehend their arrest in Kuchaikote

                   P.S. Case No. 213 of 2018, registered for the offences

                   punishable under <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_1">Sections 341</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_2">323</a>, <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_3">324</a>, <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_4">307</a>, <a href="/doc/203036/" id="a_5">354</a>, <a href="/doc/1947545/" id="a_6">448</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_7">504</a>,

                   <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_8">506</a> and <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_9">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                Allegation against petitioner no. 1 and petitioner no. 2

                   is that both the petitioners ordered the accused persons to assault

                   the informant and her family members and it is also alleged that

                   they torn the apparel of informant, whereas, against petitioner

                   no. 3 allegation is that he dragged the informant by catching her
              Patna High Court CR. MISC. No.38691 of 2019(4) dt.09-09-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                         2/2</span>




                       hair and pressed her neck by his legs.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                    It is submitted on behalf of petitioners that petitioners

                       have falsely been implicated in this case due to land dispute.

                       There is case and counter case between the parties. Informant

                       and petitioners are agnates. Petitioners have got no criminal

                       antecedent. Similarly, situated co-accused have been granted

                       anticipatory bail by a coordinate bench of this court vide order

                       dated 06.10.2018 passed in Cr. Misc. No. 61858 of 2018.

</p><p id="p_5">                                    Considering the facts aforesaid, the petitioners above-

                       named, in the event of their arrest/surrender before the court

                       below within a period of six weeks from the date of

                       receipt/production of a copy of this order, are directed to be

                       enlarged on bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs. 10,000/- (Ten

                       thousand) each with two sureties of the like amount each to the

                       satisfaction of the learned 7th Additional Chief Judicial

                       Magistrate, Gopalganj in connection with Kuchaikote P.S. Case

                       No. 213 of 2018, subject to the conditions as laid down under

                       <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_10">Section 438(2)</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure.


</p><p id="p_6">                                             (Prabhat Kumar Singh, J)


vinita/-
</p><pre id="pre_2">U        T
