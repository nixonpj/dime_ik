<div class="doc_title">C482/459/2019 on 27 March, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">C482 No. 459 of 2019
Hon'ble Manoj K. Tiwari, J.
</pre><p id="p_1">     Mr. Tanuj Semwal, Advocate for the
applicants.
</p><p id="p_2">     Ms. Preeta Bhatt, Brief Holder for the
State of Uttarakhand.
</p><p id="p_3">     This C482 application has been filed by
applicants for a direction to the learned
Additional District &amp; Sessions Judge,
Kotdwar to decide the Criminal Appeal No.
38 of 2018 under <a href="/doc/1823824/" id="a_1">Section 138</a> of N.I. Act on
the basis of compromise between the parties.
</p><p id="p_4">     I have considered the submission
advanced by the learned counsel for the
parties.
</p><p id="p_5">     In my considered opinion, this is not a
fit case in which inherent powers under
<a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_2">Section 482</a> of Cr.P.C. can be exercised.
Hon'ble Supreme Court in catena of
judgments has held that the High Court
should interfere in rarest of rare cases. This
Court does not find that this case comes
under that category.
</p><p id="p_6">     Consequently, the criminal misc.
application is dismissed.</p><pre id="pre_2">


                         (Manoj K. Tiwari, J.)
Arpan                         27.03.2019
 </pre>
