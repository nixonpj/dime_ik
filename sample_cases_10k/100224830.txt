<div class="doc_title">Asu Das vs The State Of Bihar on 9 October, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                 IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA

                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.38070 of 2015
                 Arising out of PS.Case No. -73 Year- 2015 Thana -MOHIUDDIN NAGAR District- SAMASTIPUR
                 ======================================================
                 Asu Das, S/o- Babu Das, resident of village- Pukho Gor, P.S.- Kurai, Distt.-
                 Jaipur (Jaipur Road), State- Orissa.

                                                                                .... ....   Petitioner
                                                       Versus
                 The State of Bihar

                                                              .... .... Opposite Party
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner     : Mr. Mukesh Kumar No-1, Advocate.
                 For the Opposite Party : Mr. Yogendra Kumar Singh (App)
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE SUDHIR SINGH
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">3   09-10-2015                Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned

                 counsel for the State

                            The petitioner is languishing in custody since 11.05.2015

                 in connection with Mohiuddin Nagar P.S. Case No. 73 of 2015 for

                 the offences instituted under <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_1">Sections 406</a> and <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_2">420</a> of the IPC.

</p><p id="p_2">                            The prosecution story, in brief, is that the informant's

                 son, Rakesh Kumar Singh, is working in Indian Army and a bank

                 account bearing No. 30650604747 stood in his name in the State

                 Bank of India, S.B.S. Road, Lukhiyana, for the purpose of

                 debiting the two ATM Card has been issued. One ATM is in

                 possession of his son another ATM in possession of him and used

                 to send money for the maintenance of his family members. On

                 29.04.2015, he went at ATM, SBI, situated in Bus Stand
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.38070 of 2015 (3) dt.09-10-2015</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                           2/3</span>




             Mohiuddinagar at 4.00 P.M. where 40 years aged person was

             standing wearing pant shirt who asked him to deliver ATM to

             debit the amount. Accordingly, he handed over the ATM and

             password and he debited Rs. 4500/- and handed over ATM as well

             as money. He returned back. On the next day, he again went to

             ATM for debiting the money but in spite of several affords taken

             by him, money could not be debited. He made telephonic contact

             with his son who locked his account and it was revealed that Rs.

             80,000/- has been debited from the said account and his son

             disclosed that after changing the ATM and delivered fake ATM

             and some one debited Rs. 80,000/- from the said account. During

             the course of investigation, witnesses examined in para no. 2 of

             the case diary have supported the prosecution version in their

             respective statements before the I.O. From perusal of para 12 of

             the case diary , it appears that the complainant came at the police

             station and informed that the person who exchanged his ATM on

             29.04.2015 is still near the ATM. I.O. and informant rushed there

             and this petitioner was apprehended who disclosed his name as

             Ashu Das. During the course of search, from his possession iron

             rod work for breaking the dickey of the motorcycle was recovered.

             The informant identified him and stated that he is the person who

             exchanged his ATM.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">          Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.38070 of 2015 (3) dt.09-10-2015</span><p id="p_3">

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                                                    3/3</span>




</p><p id="p_4">                                  It has been submitted on behalf of the petitioner that the

                      petitioner has got no criminal antecedent. The petitioner is in

                      custody since 11.05.2015 and the charge sheet has been submitted

                      in the case. There is no allegation of tampering with the evidence

                      against the petitioner. The petitioner has been made accused due to

                      mistake of fact. There is no substantive evidence which show the

                      implication of this petitioner in the present case. It has further

                      been submitted that the petitioner is ready to deposit an amount of

                      Rs. 20,000/- in the court below which shall be subject to final

                      disposal of the case.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                  On behalf of the State it has been submitted that the

                      petitioner is named in the F.I.R.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                  Considering the aforesaid facts and circumstances, it is

                      directed that the petitioner shall deposit an amount of Rs. 20,000/-

                      in the court below which shall be subject to final disposal of the

                      case and on doing so, let the petitioner above named, be released

                      on bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs. 10,000/-(Ten Thousand)

                      with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of the

                      learned      J.M. Ist        Class,     Samastipur,   in connection   with

                      Mohiuddinnagar P.S. Case No. 73/2015.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><pre id="pre_2">U.K./-                                                (Sudhir Singh, J)
  U         T
