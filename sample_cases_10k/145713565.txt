<div class="doc_title">Sanjay Sahu Alias Sanjay Sao vs The State Of Jharkhand on 2 February, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">           IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                                           B.A. No. 86 of 2017
          Sanjay Sahu @ Sanjay Sao................                  Petitioner   
                                  Versus
          State of Jharkhand   ............                                 Opp. Party  
                                  ......
          Coram:  Hon'ble Mr. Justice Ananda Sen
                                  ......
          For the Petitioner               : Mr. Brajesh Kr. Singh,  Advocate
          For the State                    : Mr. APP 
                                  ......

04./02.02.2017</pre><p id="p_1">      Heard   learned   counsel   appearing   for   the   petitioner   and   the 

          learned counsel for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                    The petitioner is one of the accused in connection with G. (Excise 

          Act)   Case   No.   370   of   2015,   registered   for   the   offence   punishable   under 

          Section 47 (A) of the Excise Act.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                    It   is   alleged   that   from   the   vehicle   of   the   petitioner,   which   was 

          running on hire, huge quantity of liquor was recovered. 

</p><p id="p_4">                    Learned   counsel   for   the   petitioner   submits   that   the   petitioner, 

          being   the   owner   of   the   vehicle,   had   no   knowledge   about   the   materials, 

          which   were   being   transported.   He   further   submits   that   two   of   the   co­

          accused   have   already   been   granted   bail   by   a   Coordinate   Bench   of   this 

          Court on 26.11.2015 in B.A. No. 9106 of 2015.

</p><p id="p_5">                    Learned APP opposed the prayer for bail but did not dispute the 

          fact that two co­accused persons have been granted bail by this Court.

</p><p id="p_6">                    In   view   of   the   aforesaid   fact,   the   petitioner,   named   above,   is 

          directed to be released on bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs. 10,000/­ (ten 

          thousand) with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of 

          the Chief Judicial Magistrate, Ramgarh,   in connection with    G. (Excise 

          Act) Case No. 370 of 2015. 
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><p id="p_8">                                                                                  (Ananda Sen, J)
                                                                         
Mukund­/cp. 3
 </p>
