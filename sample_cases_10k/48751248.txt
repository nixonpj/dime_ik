<div class="doc_title">Randhir Mandal &amp; Anr vs The State Of Bihar on 27 March, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                     IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                   Criminal Miscellaneous No. 38146 of 2014
                        Arising Out of PS.Case No. -212 Year- 2014 Thana -DUMRA District- SITAMARHI
                  ======================================================
                  1. Randhir Mandal Son of Mohan Mandal
                  2. Uday Mandal Son of Hitnarayan Mandal
                     Both are residents of village- Methaura, P.S.- Dumra, District- Sitamarhi

                                                                                  .... ....   Petitioners
                                                         Versus
                  The State of Bihar

                                                                   .... .... Opposite Party
                  ======================================================
                  Appearance :
                  For the Petitioner/s     :  Mr. Anil Kumar
                  For the Opposite Party/s   : Smt. Anusaiya Jaiswal(App)
                  ======================================================
                  CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE RAKESH KUMAR
                  ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2.   27-03-2015                  Heard learned counsel for petitioners and Smt.

                  Anusaiya Jaiswal, learned Addl. Public Prosecutor.

</p><p id="p_2">                                 Two petitioners, apprehending their arrest in Dumra

                  P.S. Case No. 212 of 2014 registered for the offence under

                  <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_1">Sections 341</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_2">323</a>, <a href="/doc/838469/" id="a_3">452</a>, <a href="/doc/839778/" id="a_4">380</a>, <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_5">307</a>, <a href="/doc/203036/" id="a_6">354</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_7">504</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_8">506</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_9">34</a> of the Indian

                  Penal Code, have prayed for grant of anticipatory bail.

</p><p id="p_3">                                 It was submitted by learned counsel for petitioners

                  that petitioners are bataidar of complainant and due to family

                  dispute, a complaint was filed in the court of Chief Judicial

                  Magistrate, Sitamarhi, which was referred to police and thereafter,

                  an F.I.R. under <a href="/doc/99487/" id="a_10">Section 156(3)</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure

                  has been instituted.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                 In view of the facts and circumstances, particularly
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">          Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.38146 of 2014 (2) dt.27-03-2015</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                    2/2</span>




                      the fact that for alleged offence instead of lodging F.I.R. initially

                      the compliant was filed, the Court is of the opinion that it is a fit

                      case for extending the privilege of anticipatory bail.

</p><p id="p_5">                                      In the event of arrest or surrender within a period of

                      eight weeks from today, both the petitioners, namely; Randhir

                      Mandal and Uday Mandal shall be enlarged on bail on furnishing

                      bail-bonds of Rs. 10,000/- (ten thousand) each with two sureties of

                      the like amount each to the satisfaction of learned Chief Judicial

                      Magistrate, Sitamarhi in connection with Dumra P.S. Case No.

                      212 of 2014 under conditions as contemplated under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_11">Section

                      438(2)</a> of the Cr.P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">                                                                        (Rakesh Kumar, J.)
Anay/-
</p>
