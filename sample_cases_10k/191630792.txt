<div class="doc_title">Sachin @ Sachchu vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 18 February, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">                                       Cr.A. No. 347 / 2 0 1 5
18.02.2015

                   Shri Anil Kumar Soni, Advocate for the appellant.
                   Shri     Umesh Pandey, Government             Advocate for the
             respondent /State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">                   Heard on the point of admission.
</p><p id="p_2">                   Appeal seems to be arguable, hence admitted for final
             hearing.
</p><p id="p_3">                   Record of the Court below be called for.
                   Learned Government Advocate for the State has taken
             notice of this appeal, hence no notice is required.
</p><p id="p_4">                   Also heard on I.A. No. 2264 /2015, an application for
             suspension of sentence and grant of bail to appellant Sachin
             @ Sachchu.
</p><p id="p_5">                   The appellant has been convicted under <a href="/doc/1439698/" id="a_1">Sections 25(1)</a>
             (B)<a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_2">(A) of Arms     Act</a> and     sentenced to undergo rigorous
             imprisonment for one year and fine of Rs.1,000 /- , with default
             stipulation.
</p><p id="p_6">                   Learned counsel for the appellant submits that the
             appellant is innocent and has been falsely implicated in the
             matter. It is further submitted by learned counsel for the
             appellant that the appellant was on bail during trial and he
             never misused the liberty granted to him. There is no
             likelihood of coming up of this appeal for final hearing in near
             future, hence, the sentence of the appellant be suspended and
             he be released on bail.
</p><p id="p_7">                   Learned counsel for the State opposed the prayer for
             suspension of sentence and grant of bail.
</p><p id="p_8">                   On due consideration of the facts and circumstances of
             the case and the nature of allegation, this application is
       allowed. It is directed that the execution of Jail sentence of
      appellant Sachin @ Sachchu shall remain suspended. He is
      directed to be enlarged on bail on his furnishing a personal
      bond in the sum of Rs.25,000 / - (Twenty Five Thousand
      Only) and a surety bond in the like amount to the satisfaction
      of trial Court for his appearance before the Registry of this
      Court    on 09.09.2015       and on such other dates, which
      normally shall not be less than the period of 8 months as may
      be fixed by the Registry in this regard during the pendency of
      this appeal.
</p><p id="p_9">              It is made clear that the sentence of fine amount is not
      suspended.
</p><p id="p_10">              List the case for final hsearing in due course.
              C.C. as per rules.
</p>

<p id="p_11">                                              (SUBHASH KAKADE)
                                                   JUDGE

RJ/
 </p>
