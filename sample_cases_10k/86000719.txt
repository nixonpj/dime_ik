                               The High Court Of Madhya Pradesh
                                          WP-14188-2019
                                    (SMT. SUKHMANTI JAISAWAL Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)

                    1
                    Jabalpur, Dated : 06-08-2019
                             Shri Sandeep Kumar Sen, learned counsel for the petitioner.
                             Shri   S.P.    Mishra,    learned    Government      Advocate     for   the
                    respondents/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">                             The petitioner has filed the present writ petition challenging the order
                    dated 25.04.2019 (Annexure P-1) passed by respondent no.2.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                             The petitioner is aggrieved by the order as the vehicle of the petitioner
                    is confiscated.
</p><p id="p_3">                             There is provision of revision under Section 47(c) of the M.P. Excise
                    Act. Under the said provision, the petitioner can challenge the illegality of the
                    order.
</p><p id="p_4">                             As the petitioner is having alternate remedy, therefore, writ petition is
                    disposed of with liberty to the petitioner to approach the Authorities
                    mentioned under Section 47(c) of the M.P. Excise Act.
</p><p id="p_5">                             Certified copy of the order dated 25.04.2019 be returned to the

                    petitioner on filing photocopy of the order.
</p><p id="p_6">                             The petitioner will be entitled to benefit of limitation as per the
                    <a href="/doc/1317393/" id="a_1">Limitation Act</a>.
</p><p id="p_7">                             Accordingly, this writ petition is disposed of.
                             Certified copy as per rules.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                                                                                (VISHAL DHAGAT)
                                                                                     JUDGE


                    Tabish
