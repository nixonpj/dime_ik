                 Ajay Kumar, S/o Late S.P. Srivastava, R/o Dwarika Nath Lane, Gali No. 3,

                                                             .... .... Opposite Parties

                 For the Petitioner : Mr. Anuj Kumar, Advocate.

<p id="p_1">2   04-09-2018                  Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned

                 A.P.P. for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                 The petitioner is apprehending his arrest in a case for

                 the offence registered under <a href="/doc/203036/" id="a_1">Sections 354</a>, <a href="/doc/1868826/" id="a_2">498(A)</a>, <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_3">420</a>, <a href="/doc/1101188/" id="a_4">379</a> of the

                 IPC and Â¾ of the D. P. Act in which cognizance has been taken

                 under <a href="/doc/1868826/" id="a_5">Section 498(A)</a> of the IPC.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                 The prosecution story, in brief, is that the accused

                 persons including the petitioner tortured the victim due to non-

                 fulfilment of demand of dowry.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                 It has been submitted by learned counsel for the

                 petitioner that the petitioner has got no criminal antecedent. There
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">          Patna High Court Cr.M isc. No.52291 of 2018 (2) dt.04-09-2018</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                     2/2</span>




                      is no allegation of tampering with the witnesses alleged against the

                      petitioner. The petitioner has falsely been implicated in the present

                      case due to petty family dispute. The case is triable by the

                      Magistrate. The petitioner has further relied upon the judgment of

                      this Court in the case of Md. Naimul Haque Ansari @ Naimul

                      Haque Ansari &amp; Ors. Vs. The State of Bihar, reported in 2006(3)

                      PLJR 182.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                        On behalf of the learned counsel for the State, it has

                      been submitted that the petitioner is named in the F.I.R/ complaint

                      case.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                        Considering the aforesaid facts and circumstances, let

                      the petitioner above named, in the event of arrest or surrender

                      before the learned court below within a period of six weeks from

                      today, be released on anticipatory bail on furnishing bail bonds of

                      Rs.10,000/- (Ten Thousand) with two sureties of the like amount

                      each to the satisfaction of the learned S.D.J.M. Patna, in

                      connection with Complaint Case No. 472 (C) of 2017, subject to

                      the conditions as laid down under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_6">Section 438(2)</a> of the Code of

                      Criminal Procedure.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><pre id="pre_2">U.K./-                                                 (Sudhir Singh, J)
