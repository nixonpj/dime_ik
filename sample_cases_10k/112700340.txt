                             The High Court Of Madhya Pradesh

                                     (RAMPRASAD PATEL Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)

                      2
                      Jabalpur, Dated : 23-04-2019

                            Shri Rahul Diwakar, learned counsel for the applicant.
                            Shri Abhijeet Awasthi, learned counsel for the respondent/SPE</pre>
<p id="p_1">                      Lokayukta.
</p><p id="p_2">                            Heard.
</p><p id="p_3">                            This is first application filed under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> of Cr.P.C. for

                      grant of anticipatory bail, as the applicant apprehends his arrest in
                      connection with Crime No.159/2018 registered at Police Station SPE
                      Lokayukt Office Sagar, (M.P.) for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/324254/" id="a_2">Sections
                      7</a> of the Prevention of Corruption Act.
</p><p id="p_4">                            A s per the prosecution case, applicant, who was Patwari, on
                      25/06/2018 demanded bribe of Rs.14,000/- from complainant for
                      demarcation of the land. The complainant has made a complaint to the
                      Superintendent of Lokayukt, Sagar. Accordingly the Lokayukt police

                      prepared a trap and caught the applicant red-handed and also seized
                      Rs.5000/- from his possession.
</p><p id="p_5">                            Learned counsel for the applicant submits that the applicant was
                      released on bail by the Investigating Officer on the scene of alleged
                      crime after taking his bail-bonds. He further submits that the investigation
                      of the case is completed and the charge sheet is yet to be filed. He further
                      submits that in a similar case of Anil Gupta Vs. State of M. P. (M. Cr.
                      C. No.9432/2019), the co-ordinate Bench of this Court has granted
                      anticipatory bail. He apprehends that the Court concerned may take him
                      into custody at the time of filing of the charge-sheet against him. Upon
                      these submissions, he prays for grant of anticipatory bail to the applicant.
</p><p id="p_6">                            Learned counsel for the respondent/Lokayukt opposed the prayer

                      and submitted that the charge-sheet against the applicant will be filed in
                      near future. He confirms that the applicant has been released on bail at
                      scene of the alleged crime. Ever since, he is on bail.
</p><p id="p_7">                            Looking to the facts and circumstances of the case and as to the fact
                      that the investigation is over and custodial interrogation of the applicant

                      is not required and there is no likelihood of his absconding or tempering
                      with the prosecution evidence and conclusion of trial will take time, so
                      without commenting on merits of the case, the application is allowed.
</p><p id="p_8">                            It is directed that if the Court concerned takes the applicant into
                      custody at the time of filing of the charge-sheet in the aforesaid crime
                      number against him, in that event the Court concerned will release him on
                      bail upon his furnishing a personal bond in the sum of Rs. 50,000/-(Rs.
                      Fifty Thousand only) with a solvent surety in the like amount to the
                      satisfaction of the Court concerned.
</p><p id="p_9">                            This order will remain operative subject to compliance of the
                      following conditions by the applicant:
</p><p id="p_10">                            1. The applicant will comply with all the terms and conditions of
                      the bond executed by him;
</p><p id="p_11">                            2. The applicant will cooperate in the investigation /trial as the
                      case may be;
</p><p id="p_12">                            3. The applicant will not indulge himself in extending inducement,
                      threat or promise to any person acquainted with the facts of the case so as
                      to dissuade him from disclosing such facts to the Court or to the Police
                      Officer, as the case may be;
</p><p id="p_13">                            4. The applicant shall not commit any offence similar to the offence
                      of which he is accused;
</p><p id="p_14">                            5. The applicant will not seek unnecessary adjournments during the
                      trial; and
</p><p id="p_15">                            6. The applicant will not leave India without previous permission
