<div class="doc_title">Manoj Kumar @ Manoj Kumar Mahto vs The State Of Bihar on 28 August, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.52903 of 2018
                   Arising Out of PS.Case No. -176 Year- 2018 Thana -WARISNAGAR District- SAM ASTIPUR
                 ======================================================
                 Manoj Kumar @ Manoj Kumar Mahto, S/o. Late Ram Sunder Mahto, R/o.
                 Village- Paroriya Ward No. 10, P.S.- Warisnagar, District- Samastipur.
                                                                        .... .... Petitioner/s
                                                   Versus
                 The State of Bihar
                                                                   .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s       : Mr. Dilip Kumar Roy
                 For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr. Mukeshwar Dayal
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE PRAKASH CHANDRA
                 JAISWAL
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   28-08-2018                    Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned

                 APP for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                  The petitioner seeks bail in a case registered for the

                 offence punishable under <a href="/doc/285163/" id="a_1">Sections 273</a> and <a href="/doc/1099867/" id="a_2">290</a> of the Indian Penal

                 Code and <a href="/doc/1320984/" id="a_3">Sections 30(a)</a>, <a href="/doc/222372/" id="a_4">37(b)</a> (d), 38 and 41 of the Bihar

                 Prohibition and <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_5">Excise Act</a>, 2016.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                  On the disclosure of the petitioner 120 litres of

                 foreign liquor is said to have been recovered from his hut and he

                 was apprehended in inebriated condition.

</p><p id="p_4">                                  It is submitted by learned counsel for the petitioner

                 that no incriminating article has been recovered from the

                 conscious physical possession of the petitioner. He has no concern

                 either with the seized liquor or with the said hut in question. He

                 had not made any disclosure. Nothing has been recorded on the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">          Patna High Court Cr.M isc. No.52903 of 2018 (2) dt.28-08-2018</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                     2/2</span>




                      basis of disclosure of the petitioner. He happens to be teetotaler

                      and never consumed liquor. He has been falsely implicated in the

                      case at the instance of his enemy and due to dirty village politics.

                      There is violation of <a href="/doc/1137396/" id="a_6">Section 100</a> Cr.P.C. He has no criminal

                      antecedent. He has been languishing in custody since 10.07.2018.

</p><p id="p_5">                                        Considering the facts aforesaid, the above named

                      petitioner is directed to be enlarged on bail on furnishing bail bond

                      of Rs. 10,000/- (Ten thousand) with two sureties of the like

                      amount each to the satisfaction of the learned Additional Sessions

                      Judge-VI cum Special Judge, <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_7">Excise Act</a>,                Samastipur in

                      connection with Warisnagar P.S. Case No. 176 of 2018.



</p><p id="p_6">                                              (Prakash Chandra Jaiswal, J)
Kr. Uday/-
</p>
