1.The District Collector and Sole Arbitrator
   Salem Collectorate, 
   Salem  636 001.	


2.The Special DRO (Land Acquisition) 
   National Highways No.68
   Ram Nagar, Swarnapuri II Gate
   Five Roads
   Salem  636 004.				... Respondents

Prayer :- Writ Petition filed under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the Constitution of India, praying to issue a Writ of Mandamus, directing the respondents herein to conduct the arbitration proceedings in respect of the acquisition of the petitioners' lands in National Highways 7 &amp; 68 as per the representation made by the writ petitioners dated 23.07.2017, 26.07.2017, 18.09.2017, 19.09.2017 and 20.09.2017.

	For Petitioners 	: Mr.P.B.Sampath Kumar 
	For Respondents	: Mr.Akhil Akbar Ali
   			  Government Advocate 


ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">The only grievance of the petitioners in this case is that they were dissatisfied with the  quantum of compensation awarded to them by the  Competent Authority under the provisions of the <a href="/doc/1222415/" id="a_2">National Highways Act</a>, 1956, that they have moved the Statutory Arbitrator separately under <a href="/doc/1163443/" id="a_3">Section 3(G)(5)</a> of the Act.   However, the District Collector/Statutory Arbitrator has not concluded the arbitral proceedings pertaining to these 43 petitioners.  The petitioners submitted that on an earlier occasion in a similar batch of cases, another set of owners of land who had moved the arbitration have approached this Court and have obtained necessary directions from this Court, based on which, the statutory arbitrator had passed his award.   The petitioners seek that their case also may be decided as expeditiously as possible.  
</p>
<p id="p_2">2. Mr.Akhil Akbar Ali, learned Government Advocate enters appearance for the respondents.
</p>
<p id="p_3">3. Heard both sides.  The limited prayer of the petitioners is that their arbitration proceedings initiated separately, are pending with the first respondent since 2010 may be disposed of at the very earliest.   This Court, therefore directs the first respondent to dispose of the arbitration proceedings pertaining to all the 43 petitioners herein, within three months from the date of receipt of a copy of this order after affording effective and meaningful hearing to the petitioners.
</p>
<p id="p_4">4. With the above direction, this petition is disposed of.  No costs.  Consequently, connected miscellaneous petition is closed.
</p>
