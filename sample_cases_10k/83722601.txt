<div class="doc_title">Md. Ajam vs The State Of Bihar on 29 November, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                              CRIMINAL MISCELLANEOUS No.76722 of 2019
                    Arising Out of PS. Case No.-23 Year-2019 Thana- MAHESI District- East Champaran
                 ======================================================
                 Md. Ajam Son of Islam Ansari Resident of Village-Surajpur, Police Station-
                 Piprakothi, District-East Champaran.

                                                                                   ... ... Petitioner/s
                                                       Versus
                 The State Of Bihar

                                                        ... ... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :       Mr.Anil Kumar, Advocate
                 For the Opposite Party/s :       Mr.Narsingh Tanti, APP
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE BIRENDRA KUMAR
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   29-11-2019                  Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned

                   Additional Public Prosecutor for the State.

</p><p id="p_2">                                The petitioner is languishing in custody for the

                   offences punishable under <a href="/doc/844968/" id="a_1">Sections 412</a>/<a href="/doc/1976294/" id="a_2">413</a>/<a href="/doc/94452/" id="a_3">414</a> of the Indian

                   Penal Code and <a href="/doc/1961417/" id="a_4">Sections 25</a>(1-b)/26/35 of the <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_5">Arms Act</a>.

</p><p id="p_3">                                 Submission is that allegation is of recovery of fire-

                   arm from a truck. Petitioner and others were also sitting on the

                   same truck. Hence, on suspicion he was arrested. Co-accused

                   Chandan Kumar has already been allowed bail by a coordinate

                   Bench of this Court.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                Considering the facts aforesaid, let the petitioner,

                   above named, be released on bail on furnishing bail bond of

                   Rs.20,000/- (Twenty Thousand) with two sureties of the like

                   amount each to the satisfaction of the learned Court-below
           Patna High Court CR. MISC. No.76722 of 2019(2) dt.29-11-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                      2/2</span>




                    where the case is pending in connection with Mahesi P.S. Case

                    No.23 of 2019, with following conditions:

</p><p id="p_5">                                 (a) Both the bailors shall be the resident of territorial

                    jurisdiction of the learned Court-below.

</p><p id="p_6">                                 (b) The petitioner shall fully cooperate with the

                    investigation/trial of the case, failing which the court below

                    shall be at liberty to cancel the bail bond of the petitioner.



</p><p id="p_7">                                                                         (Birendra Kumar, J)
    Mkr./-
</p>
