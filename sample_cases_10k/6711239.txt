<div class="doc_title">Suresh vs State Of U.P. on 18 December, 2019</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: Ajit Singh</div>

<pre id="pre_1">




HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT ALLAHABAD
 
 

?Court No. - 79
 

 
Case :- CRIMINAL MISC. BAIL APPLICATION No. - 54898 of 2019
 

 
Applicant :- Suresh
 
Opposite Party :- State of U.P.
 
Counsel for Applicant :- Vijay Singh Sengar
 
Counsel for Opposite Party :- G.A.
 

 
Hon'ble Ajit Singh,J.
</pre> 
<p id="p_1">Heard learned counsel for the applicant, learned A.G.A. for the State and perused the entire record.
</p> 
<p id="p_2">The present bail application has been filed by the applicant in Case Crime No. 84 of 2019, under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_1">Sections 302</a>, <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_2">120-B</a> of IPC, Police Station Kadaura, District Jalaun with the prayer to enlarge the applicant on bail.
</p> 
<p id="p_3">The report of the incident which taken place on 20.06.2019 at about 10:00 P.M. in night was got lodged on 22.06.2019 by the complainant against accused Kailash, Mansa Ram, Suresh and unknown persons on the allegation that the deceased Pushpendra, son of the complainant, was having love affairs with Sangeeta daughter of accused Kailash who happens to be brother-in-law (Sarhu) of the complainant. On month earlier to the incident the son of complainant namely Pushpendra had come with Sangeeta and due to relationship between both the families Pushpendra and Sangeeta were scolded and Sangeeta was sent back to village Ikauna. A mention is further made in the FIR that on the date of incident i.e. 20.06.2019 the complainant's son Pushpendra was called by Sangeeta and he was done to death by Kailash with the help of his nephew Mansa Ram and after committing the murder his murder the dead body was thrown in the forest of Ikauna. On the basis of the aforesaid allegations the FIR was lodged against the accused persons. 
</p> 
<p id="p_4">The learned counsel for the applicant submits that the present accused is son-in-law of the co-accused Kailash and that is why he has been falsely implicated in the matter. He had nothing to do with the alleged crime. He has further submitted that as per the statements of the witnesses which were recorded after about one month of the incident i.e. on 27.07.2019 the role assigned to the present accused Suresh is that he carried the accused Kailash on motorcycle towards the place of occurrence and after that he went back and Pushpendra was beaten by Mansha Ram and Kailash. Nothing incriminating has been recovered from the possession of the present accused and alleged recovery of Danda has been shown from the possession of accused Kailash. The role of assaulting on deceased was assigned to accused Kailash and Mansha Rama only. 
</p> 
<p id="p_5">Learned A.G.A. as well as learned counsel appearing on behalf of the complainant has opposed the prayer for bail. 
</p> 
<p id="p_6">After considering the rival submissions of learned counsel for the parties, considering the facts and circumstances of the case, considering the role assigned to the present accused, and also considering the period of detention of the applicant and all the attending facts and circumstances of the case, without commenting on the merits of the case, at this stage, prima facie, this Court deems it a fit case to release the applicant on bail, with conditions given below as well as with condition that he will cooperate during trial. 
</p> 
<p id="p_7">The prayer for bail is granted. The application is allowed. 
</p> 
<p id="p_8">Let applicant Suresh involved in the aforesaid crime be released on bail on furnishing a personal bond and two local sureties to the satisfaction of court concerned subject to the following conditions: 
</p> 
<p id="p_9">(1). The applicant shall file an undertaking to the effect that he shall not seek any adjournment on the dates fixed for evidence and the witnesses are present in Court. In case of default of this condition, it shall be open for the trial court to treat it as abuse of liberty of bail and pass orders in accordance with law; 
</p> 
<p id="p_10">(2). The applicant shall remain present before the trial court on each date fixed, either personally or through his counsel. In case of his absence, without sufficient cause, the trial court may proceed against him under <a href="/doc/77781517/" id="a_3">Section 229-A</a> of the Indian Penal Code; 
</p> 
<p id="p_11">(3). In case, the applicant misuses the liberty of bail and in order to secure his presence proclamation under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_4">Section 82</a> Cr.P.C. is issued and the applicant fails to appear before the Court on the date fixed in such proclamation, then, the trial court may initiate proceedings against him, in accordance with law, under <a href="/doc/119436650/" id="a_5">Section 174-A</a> of the Indian Penal Code. 
</p> 
<p id="p_12">(4). The applicant shall remain present, in person, before the trial court on the dates fixed for (i) opening of the case, (ii) framing of charge and (iii) recording of statement under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_6">Section 313</a> Cr.P.C. If in the opinion of the trial court default of this condition is deliberate or without sufficient cause, then it shall be open for the trial court to treat such default as abuse of liberty of his bail and proceed against him in accordance with law. 
</p> 
<p id="p_13">Order Date :- 18.12.2019
 
LBY
