<div class="doc_title">Harendra Singh Gurjar vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 30 November, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                        MCRC-13561-2016
         (HARENDRA SINGH GURJAR Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)


30-11-2016
Shri D.S. Rana, learned counsel for the applicant.
Shri R.S. Yadav,P.P. for the respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">This is first application under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of Cr.P.C. The applicant
has been arrested in Crime No.134/2016 registered at Police
Station, Antri, District Gwalior, for the offence punishable under
<a href="/doc/399026/" id="a_2">Section 399</a>, <a href="/doc/468585/" id="a_3">400</a>, <a href="/doc/1784492/" id="a_4">402</a> of IPC, Section 11/13 of MPDVPK Act and
<a href="/doc/73862/" id="a_5">Section 25</a>/<a href="/doc/244673/" id="a_6">27</a> of Arms Act.
</p><p id="p_2">According to prosecution case, on receipt of information that some
dacoits have been collected and they are planning to commit
dacoity, police party reached near temple and they found that
dacoits were planning to commit dacoity in the house of Punjab
Singh and apprehended Deepu alias Deepak and Vishnu, Chota
Gurjar, Harendra and Ram Lakhan. An axe has been seized from
the possession of the applicant.
</p><p id="p_3">It is submitted that applicant has not committed any offence. He
has falsely been implicated. There is no criminal antecedents
against the applicant. He is under custody since 25.10.2016. Hence
prayed for bail.
</p><p id="p_4">The prayer is opposed by learned PP for the respondent/State.
Case-diary perused.
</p><p id="p_5">Considering the allegation against the applicant and the facts and
circumstances of the case, but without commenting anything on
the merits of the case, the application is allowed. It is directed that
the applicant shall be released on bail on his furnishing personal
bond in the sum of Rs.50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand Only) with
 one solvent surety in the like amount to the satisfaction of Trial
Court.
</p><p id="p_6">This order will remain operative subject to compliance of the
following conditions by the applicant :-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">     The applicant will comply with all the terms and
     conditions of the bond executed by them;
     The applicant will cooperate in the
     investigation/trial, as the case may be;
     The applicant will not indulge himself in extending
     inducement, threat or promise to any person
     acquainted with the facts of the case so as to
     dissuade him from disclosing such facts to the Court
     or to the Police Officer, as the case may be;
     The applicant shall not commit an offence similar to
     the offence of which he is accused;
     The applicant will not seek unnecessary
     adjournments during the trial; and
     The applicant will not leave India without previous
     permission of the trial Court/Investigating Officer, as
     the case may be.
</blockquote>
<p id="p_7">A copy of this order be sent to the Court concerned for compliance.
C.c. as per rules.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                                               (D.K. PALIWAL)
                                                    JUDGE
 Van
