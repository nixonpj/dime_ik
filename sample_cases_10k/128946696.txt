<div class="doc_title">Navin Kumar @ Navin Kumar Niraj vs State Of Bihar And Anr on 1 August, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                              CRIMINAL MISCELLANEOUS No.71410 of 2018
                       Arising Out of PS. Case No.-75 Year-2018 Thana- SURYAPUR District- Rohtas
                 ======================================================
                 Navin Kumar @ Navin Kumar Niraj S/o Late Ram Pratap Singh, Resident of
                 Village and P.O.-Nonsari, P.S.-Nokha, District-Rohtas. Presently residing at
                 Saguna More, Bailey Road, Danapur, District-patna.

                                                                                   ... ... Petitioner/s
                                                       Versus
           1.    The State Of Bihar.
           2.    Puja Devi, W/o Navin Kumar @ Navin Kumar Niraj, D/o Nagenda Singh,
                 Village-Audhar, P.S.-Suryapura, District-Rohtas.

                                                        ... ... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :       Mr.Randhir Kumar No-1
                 For the Opposite Party/s :       Mr.Rana Randhir Singh
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE RAJENDRA KUMAR
                 MISHRA
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">8   01-08-2019                  Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and the

                   learned A.P.P. for the State. No one appears on behalf of the

                   opposite party no.2.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                The petitioner apprehends his arrest in connection

                   with Surajpura P.S. Case No.75 of 2018 registered under

                   <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_1">Sections 341</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_2">323</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_3">504</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_4">506</a> and <a href="/doc/1868826/" id="a_5">498(A)</a> of the Indian Penal

                   Code besides <a href="/doc/751411/" id="a_6">Sections 3</a>/<a href="/doc/1023340/" id="a_7">4</a> of the Dowry Prohibition Act.

</p><p id="p_3">                                The accusation is of torturing the opposite party no.2

                   by her husband (petitioner) and other in-laws for non-fulfillment

                   of the dowry demand of cash Rs.2,00,000/- and removing the

                   opposite party no.2 from the matrimonial house.

</p><p id="p_4">                                Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that,
              Patna High Court CR. MISC. No.71410 of 2018(8) dt.01-08-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                         2/2</span>




                       admittedly, the petitioner is the husband of the opposite party

                       no.2. Earlier, on the joint prayer of the parties, the matter was

                       referred to the Conciliation and Mediation Centre, Patna High

                       Court, Patna, for settlement of the dispute in between the

                       petitioner and the opposite party no.2 but due to non-appearance

                       of the opposite party no.2, the matter could not be settled in

                       between the petitioner and the opposite party no.2 there, which

                       would appear from the report dated 12.07.2019 of the Mediator,

                       Conciliation and Mediation Centre, Patna High Court, Patna.

</p><p id="p_5">                                    Having considered the facts and the circumstances of

                       the case, let the petitioner, above named, in the event of his

                       arrest or surrender by him within six weeks from today, be

                       enlarged on bail on his furnishing bail bond of Rs.10,000/-

                       (Rupees Ten Thousand) with two sureties of the like amount

                       each to the satisfaction of the Sub Divisional Judicial

                       Magistrate, Rohtas at Sasaram, in connection with Surajpura

                       P.S. Case No.75 of 2018, subject to the conditions laid down

                       under Section 438(2) Cr.P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><p id="p_7">                                                          (Rajendra Kumar Mishra, J)

P.S./-
</p>
