                     Criminal Misc. No.M-16234 of 2017

                         Surender Kumar and others

Present:     Mr. Amit Singla, Advocate for the petitioners.

             Mr. Brijesh Sharma, Assistant Advocate General, Haryana for
             the respondent-State.

             Ms. Harpreet Kaur, Advocate for complainant-respondent No.2.

Inderjit Singh, J.
</pre>
<p id="p_1">             This petition has been filed under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C. praying

for quashing of FIR No.43 dated 11.3.2017 (Annexure-P.1) registered for

the offences under <a href="/doc/763672/" id="a_2">Sections 148</a>, <a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_3">149</a>, <a href="/doc/222396/" id="a_4">427</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_5">323</a>, <a href="/doc/838469/" id="a_6">452</a> and <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_7">506</a> IPC at Police

Station Sadar Sirsa, District Sirsa and all subsequent proceedings arising

therefrom in view of the compromise (Annexure-P.2).

</p><p id="p_2">             The FIR has been registered on the statement of complainant-

Sudesh Kumar on the allegations that the accused-petitioners attacked him

and inflicted injuries. Now with the intervention of respectable persons, the

matter has been amicably compromised between the parties and they have

resolved their disputes and differences.
</p>
<p id="p_3">
</p><p id="p_4">                                  1 of 3
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">               ::: Downloaded on - 02-09-2017 18:09:33 :::</span>
                                                  Cr. Misc. No.M-16234 of 2017
                                           [2]

            Keeping in view the fact that the parties have entered into a

compromise, they were directed to appear before learned trial Court for

getting their statements recorded in support of the compromise. After doing

the needful, learned Additional Chief Judicial Magistrate, Sirsa has sent

report dated 19.5.2017 submitting that the compromise arrived at between

the parties is without any pressure or coercion from any one and the same is

genuine one.
</p>
<p id="p_5">            Learned Assistant Advocate General, Haryana, on instructions

from the Investigating Officer and learned counsel for the complainant-

respondent No.2 admit the factum of compromise and submit that in case

the parties have indeed settled their dispute, the State would have no

objection to the quashing of the FIR in view of the law laid down by the

Hon'ble Supreme Court.
</p>
<p id="p_6">            I have heard learned counsel for the parties as well as learned

State counsel and have gone through the record.

</p><p id="p_7">            In a decision, based on compromise, none of the parties is a

loser. Rather, compromise not only brings peace and harmony between the

parties to a dispute, but also restores tranquility in the society. After

considering the nature of offences allegedly committed and the fact that

both the parties have amicably settled their dispute, continuance of criminal

prosecution would be an exercise in futility, as the chances of ultimate

conviction are bleak.
</p>
<p id="p_8">            Therefore, keeping in view the fact that the matter has been

amicably settled and in view of the law laid by the Hon'ble Supreme Court

petition is allowed and FIR No.43 dated 11.3.2017 (Annexure-P.1)

registered for the offences under <a href="/doc/763672/" id="a_9">Sections 148</a>, <a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_10">149</a>, <a href="/doc/222396/" id="a_11">427</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_12">323</a>, <a href="/doc/838469/" id="a_13">452</a> and <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_14">506</a>

IPC at Police Station Sadar Sirsa, District Sirsa and all subsequent

proceedings arising out of the same are hereby quashed qua the petitioners.</p><pre id="pre_2">
