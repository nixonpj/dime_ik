<div class="doc_title">Jadda Pulla Reddy, vs State Of Andhra Pradesh, on 7 June, 2018</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: S.V.Bhatt</div>

<pre id="pre_1">                        HON'BLE SRI JUSTICE S.V. BHATT

                             W.P.No.9561_OF 2018
ORDER:
</pre>
<p id="p_1">         Heard     Mr.K.S.Murthy   for    petitioners   and   the   Assistant

Government Pleader (Revenue) for respondents.


</p><p id="p_2">         The petitioners challenge the arbitrary action of respondents

in changing the entries in the revenue records and cancelling the

pattadar passbooks of petitioners in collusion with respondent No.6.

The petitioners further pray for taking action against the appeal

filed by them before the 3rd respondent-Joint Collector.


</p><p id="p_3">         The Assistant Government Pleader (Revenue) submits that the

petitioners have to work out the grievance either in appeal or

revision under the A.P. Rights in Land and Pattadar Pass Books Act,

1971 (for short 'the Act'), as held by this Court in RATNAMMA v. RDO1,

DHARMAVARAM            and   KARUVA      HANUMANTHAMMA        v.    PRINCIPAL

SECRETARY, REVENUE DEPARTMENT, HYDERABAD AND ANOTHER2.


</p><p id="p_4">         After perusing the prayer and the averments in the affidavit,

this Court is of the view that pattadar pass book if is issued, pursuant

to proceedings issued by the Tahsildar, the remedy of appeal is

available. On the other hand, if the pattadar pass book is merely

issued without mutation order or correction of 1-B Register, the

remedy of revision is available under Section 9 of the Act.


</p><p id="p_5">         Since    the    arguments       are   already    heard     by   the

3rd respondent/Joint Collector in the appeal filed by the petitioners,


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">1</span>
    2015 (6) ALD 609
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">2</span>
    2018(1) ALD 290
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                                    2</span>



this Court does not prefer to pass orders in the writ petition at this

stage. The ends of justice are met by directing respondents 2 and 3

to pass orders pending in case Rc.No.368/2018-D5 before the

3rd respondent/Joint Collector.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><p id="p_7">       The writ petition is, accordingly, disposed of. There shall be no

order as to costs.
</p>
<p id="p_8">
</p><p id="p_9">       Miscellaneous petitions, if any, pending stand closed.



</p><p id="p_10">                                                          ____________
                                                          S.V.BHATT, J
07th June, 2018
