 Date of filing of Petition                                              :      31.05.2012

Present:        Sh. Udai Raj Singh, Ld. counsel for the petitioner along with 
                Petitioner no. 1 and 5.

                Shri Rajesh Ranjan, Administrative Officer 

                                                AWARD

         Order disposes off Petition under Section 166 of th <a href="/doc/785258/" id="a_1">Motor Vehicles Act</a>, filed by 
the legal heirs of Shri Ashok Kumar, who died in an accident dated 08.11.2011, on 
account  of  rash  and negligent driving of Tanker no. UP­80T 9434, by  Respondent 
no. 1.
</p>

<p id="p_3">2.       Issues   were   framed   on   04.12.2012   and   the   evidence   is   over.   Parties   have 
however, shown interest to settle amicably.
</p>

<p id="p_4">3.       Upon Judicial intervention and conciliatory efforts, the Parties have settled and 
the Insurance Company has agreed to pay a sum of Rs. 9.25 lacs, including the interim 
award of Rs. 50,000/­, as full and final settlement of all claims of the Petitioners.
</p>

<p id="p_5">4.       Petitioners no. 1 and 5, after due deliberations, have agreed to accept the same 
as fair and just compensation for all of the petitioners, while submitting that they have 
instructions from other petitioners to this effect.
</p>

<p id="p_6">5.       Separate statements of the Petitioners and the Authorised Representative of the 
Insurance Company have been recorded. Petitioners have also agreed that the said 
amount is just and fair compensation on account of the death of Shri Ashok Kumar.
</p>


<p id="p_7">FIR No.108/2011                             Smt. Sarvesh Devi &amp; Ors Vs. Shyam Kishore &amp; Ors.
</p><p id="p_8"> 6.      In view of the above, the claims of the Petitioners are disposed off as settled, 
with direction to the Respondent no. 3 to deposit the award amount of Rs. 8.75 lacs, 
i.e. after adjusting Rs. 50,000, already paid as interim compensation,  within 15 days 
from today, with SBI Saket Branch, wherein, the accounts of the Petitioners are stated 
to have already opened and maintained in the name of petitioners, ever since the 
grant of interim award.
</p>

<p id="p_9">7.      It   has   been   impressed   that   the   Petitioner   no.   2­   Ms   Varsha   Kumari     and 
Petitioner no. 4­ Nitin Gupta, have already attained majority, during the pendency of 
the Petition.  The said petitioners are directed to furnish proof of their majority to the 
Bank, at the time of deposits of FDR. 
</p>
