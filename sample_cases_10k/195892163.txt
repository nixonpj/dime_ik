<div class="doc_title">Ranjana Devi vs The State Of Bihar on 20 September, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.30936 of 2016
                     Arising Out of PS.Case No. -88 Year- 2016 Thana -AHIAPUR District- MUZAFFARPUR
                 ======================================================
                 Ranjana Devi W/o Umesh Prasad Shrivastava R/o Mohalla - Saraswati
                 Nagar Road No.-10, P.O.- M.I.T., P.S.- Ahiyapur, District-Muzaffarpur
                                                                         .... .... Petitioner
                                                  Versus
                 The State of Bihar
                                                                    .... .... Opposite Party
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner          : Mr. Manoj Kumar Manoj, Advocate.
                 For the Opposite Party      : Mr. Rajendra Prasad Nat(App)
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE PRABHAT KUMAR JHA
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">3   20-09-2016                Heard learned counsel for the petitioner, learned counsel for

                 the informant as well as learned APP for the State.

</p><p id="p_2">                              The petitioner apprehends her arrest in Ahiyapur P.S. Case

                 No. 88 of 2016 registered for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/653797/" id="a_1">Sections

                 304B</a> and <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_2">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                              The informant alleged that he got information from Ajay

                 Kumar, the elder brother of his brother-in-law Rakesh Kumar and when

                 the informant went to the residence of his sister he came to know that

                 the body of his sister lying inside the room. The informant further

                 alleged that the petitioner and other accused persons were torturing his

                 sister and due to non-fulfillment of demand of dowry they all killed his

                 sister.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                              Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that the petitioner

                 is married sister-in-law of the deceased. The petitioner was married 23

                 years ago and she was living in her Sasural. The petitioner has no
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">        Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.30936 of 2016 (3) dt.20-09-2016</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                  2/2</span>




                    manner of concern with the family affairs of the deceased and her

                    husband. The deceased was found hanging from the roof and the room

                    was bolted from inside. The deceased never made any complaint during

                    her lifetime about demand of dowry and torture, although the deceased

                    was married 15 months ago.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                  Learned counsel for the informant however opposed the

                    prayer for anticipatory bail and submitted that the witnesses during

                    course of investigation have stated that the petitioner was also torturing

                    the deceased.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                  Considering the facts aforesaid and the fact that the

                    petitioner is married sister-in-law of the deceased and she was married

                    about 23 years ago, living in her Sasural and no specific allegation is

                    made against her, the petitioner above named, in the event of her arrest

                    or surrender before the court below within a period of four weeks from

                    the date of receipt/production of a copy of this order, is directed to be

                    enlarged on bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs. 10,000/- (Ten

                    Thousand) with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction

                    of the Chief Judicial Magistrate, Muzaffarpur in Ahiyapur P.S. Case

                    No. 88 of 2016, subject to the conditions as laid down under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_3">Section

                    438(2)</a> of the Cr.P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_7">
</p><p id="p_8">                                               (Prabhat Kumar Jha, J.)
KKSINHA/-
</p>
