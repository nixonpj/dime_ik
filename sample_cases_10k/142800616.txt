<div class="doc_title">Luhar Vijaybhai Amratbhai vs State Of Gujarat on 15 March, 2019</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: Vipul M. Pancholi</div>

<pre id="pre_1">        R/CR.MA/4948/2019                                      ORDER




            IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

        R/CRIMINAL MISC.APPLICATION NO.                 4948 of 2019

==========================================================
                       LUHAR VIJAYBHAI AMRATBHAI
                                  Versus
                            STATE OF GUJARAT
==========================================================
Appearance:
MR M.NISAR VAIDHYA(3386) for the Applicant(s) No. 1
MR SADIK A ANSARI(5388) for the Applicant(s) No. 1
MR MITESH AMIN, PUBLIC PROSECUTOR for Respondent(s) No. 1
==========================================================

 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE VIPUL M. PANCHOLI

                            Date : 15/03/2019

                                  ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">1.     Rule.        MR      Mitesh        Amin,     learned         Public
Prosecutor waives service of notice of Rule on
behalf of respondent-State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">2.     By     way     of    the     present       application          under
<a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure,
1973, the applicant accused has prayed to release
him on anticipatory bail in case of his arrest in
connection with the FIR being C.R.NO.III-73 of
2019        registered       with       Aslali      Police        Station,
Ahmedabad Rural for offence under Sections 65-AE,
116-B, 81 and 98(2) of the Gujarat Prohibition
Act.
</p>
<p id="p_3">3.     Learned advocate for the applicant submits
that the nature of allegations are such for which
custodial        interrogation            at     this     stage    is    not


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                   Page 1 of 5</span>
         R/CR.MA/4948/2019                                          ORDER



necessary.         Besides,       the     applicant          is    available
during the course of investigation and will not
flee    from      justice.       In     view      of    the       above,    the
applicant may be granted anticipatory bail.
</p>

<p id="p_4">       Learned         advocate        for       the     applicant,          on
instructions, states that the applicant is ready
and    willing          to     abide    by       all     the      conditions
including imposition of conditions with regard to
powers       of      Investigating              Agency       to     file    an
application before the competent Court for his
remand. He would further submit that upon filing
of such application by the Investigating Agency,
the right of applicant accused to oppose such
application on merits may be kept open.
</p>

<p id="p_5">4.     Learned Public Prosecutor appearing on behalf
of the respondent - State has opposed grant of
anticipatory            bail    looking          to    the     nature       and
gravity of the offence.
</p>

<p id="p_6">5.     Having heard the learned advocates for the
parties and on perusing the material placed on
record and taking into consideration the facts of
the    case,        nature       of    allegations,            gravity       of
offences, role attributed to the accused, without
discussing the evidence in detail, at this stage,
I am inclined to grant anticipatory bail to the
applicant.          This       Court      has         also     taken       into



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                  Page 2 of 5</span>
         R/CR.MA/4948/2019                                     ORDER



consideration the law laid down by the Hon'ble
Apex Court in the case of Siddharam Satlingappa
Mhetre      vs.      State     of      Maharashtra      and    Ors.   as
reported at [2011] 1 SCC 694, wherein the Hon'ble
Apex Court reiterated the law laid down by the
Constitution Bench in the case of Shri Gurubaksh
Singh Sibbia &amp; Ors., as reported at (1980) 2 SCC
</p><p id="p_7">665.

</p><p id="p_8">6.     In   the      result,    the         present    application     is
allowed       by      directing          that     in   the    event   of
applicant herein being arrested pursuant to FIR
registered at C.R.No. III-73 of 2019 registered
with Aslali Police Station, Ahmedabad Rural, the
applicant shall be released on bail on furnishing
a    personal         bond     of      Rs.10,000/-       (Rupees      Ten
Thousand only) with one surety of like amount on
the following conditions that he:
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">       (a) shall cooperate with the investigation
           and    make   himself    available for
           interrogation whenever required;
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_2">       (b) shall remain present at the concerned
           Police Station on 25.03.2019 between
           11.00 a.m. and 2.00 p.m.;
</blockquote>

<blockquote id="blockquote_3">       (c) shall not directly or indirectly make
           any inducement, threat or promise to any
           person acquainted with the fact of the
           case   so  as   to  dissuade   him  from
           disclosing such facts to the court or to
           any police officer;
</blockquote>


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                                    Page 3 of 5</span>
<blockquote id="blockquote_4">        R/CR.MA/4948/2019                                         ORDER



</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_5">      (d) shall not obstruct or hamper the police
          investigation and not to play mischief
          with the evidence collected or yet to be
          collected by the police;
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_6">      (e) shall at the time of execution of bond,
          furnish the address to the investigating
          officer and the court concerned and
          shall not change his residence till the
          final disposal of the case till further
          orders;
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_7">      (f) shall   not  leave   India  without the
          permission of the Court and if having
          passport shall deposit the same before
          the Trial Court within a week; and

</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_8">      (g) it would be open to the Investigating
          Officer to file an application for
          remand if he considers it proper and
          just and the learned Magistrate would
          decide it on merits;
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_9">      (h) shall            not   enter       Ahmedabad         city    for   a
             period of 3 months.
</blockquote>
<p id="p_9">7.    Despite this order, it would be open for the
Investigating Agency to apply to the competent
Magistrate, for police remand of the applicant.
The    applicant           shall   remain          present      before   the
learned Magistrate on the first date of hearing
of    such      application            and        on     all    subsequent
occasions,        as       may   be    directed          by    the    learned
Magistrate. This would be sufficient to treat the
accused in the judicial custody for the purpose
of    entertaining          application            of    the   prosecution
for    police       remand.        This          is,    however,      without



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                                   Page 4 of 5</span>
         R/CR.MA/4948/2019                                    ORDER



prejudice to the right of the accused to seek
stay against an order of remand, if, ultimately,
granted, and the power of the learned Magistrate
to consider such a request in accordance with
law. It is clarified that the applicant, even if,
remanded to the police custody, upon completion
of such period of police remand, shall be set
free immediately, subject to other conditions of
this anticipatory bail order.
</p>
<p id="p_10">8.     At the trial, the Trial Court shall not be
influenced by the prima facie observations made
by this Court in this order while enlarging the
applicant on bail.
</p>
<p id="p_11">9.     Rule      is         made   absolute       to   the   aforesaid
extent. Direct service is permitted.
</p>

<p id="p_12">                                            (VIPUL M. PANCHOLI, J)
Jani




<span class="hidden_text" id="span_5">                                    Page 5 of 5</span>
