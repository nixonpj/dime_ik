                      services of the respondent Corporation as on 19.09.1991 or
                      25.09.1991 when the employees appointed on contract basis
                      along with the petitioner, are appointed in the regular time scale
                      of pay in the services of the respondent Corporation for the
                      purpose of pensionary benefits under the old pension scheme or
                      to treat the petitioner as regular employee as found in the order
                      in Na.Ka.No.1380, 1381, 1382, 1383, 1384, 1385, 1386, 1387
                      and 1389 of 2000, dated 09.04.2001 passed under Conferment
                      of Permanent Status on <a href="/doc/88376/" id="a_2">Employees Act</a> and consequently, direct
                      the respondent Corporation not to deduct 10% of basis salary
                      from the petitioner towards the new pension Scheme and to bring
                      the petitioner under the old pension Scheme and grant benefits
                      available under the old pension Scheme.


                           For Petitioner        :     Mr.R. Saseetharan

                           For Respondents       :     Mr.P.R. Dillip Kumar


                                                     ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">                           It is the case of the petitioner that on 19.09.1980, the

                      petitioner was appointed as Contract Labour at Dayanur, South

                      Mettupalayam Division, under the Authority of Tamil Nadu



http://www.judis.nic.in
                                                                      W.P.No.13800 of 2019

                      Electricity Board. On 08.09.2007, he was appointed as Mazdoor

                      in regular time scale of pay in the services of Tamil Nadu

                      Electricity Board on the ground that he was working from

                      19.09.1990 as Contract Labour.      But, the petitioner was not

                      granted consequential absorption in the post of Helper.         The

                      contract labours, who were appointed in the year 1980, were

                      brought under the old pension Scheme on the ground that they

                      were regularly appointed in the service of the Board in the year

                      1991. The name of the petitioner has been shown in the list at

                      Sl.No.23 and his date of appointment is given as 9th month of

                      1980. Hence, he made a representation to the respondent Board

                      for absorption from 1991 for the purpose of pensionary benefits

                      and to bring him under the old pension Scheme, but till date no

                      orders have been passed. Hence, this petition has been filed.



</p><p id="p_2">                           2.The learned counsel for the petitioner would submit that

                      the petitioner has made a representation on 04.05.2018 to treat

                      him on par with other employees who were appointed in the year

                      1980. No orders have been passed till date.




http://www.judis.nic.in
                                                                     W.P.No.13800 of 2019




</p><p id="p_3">                           3.Mr.P.R. Dillip Kumar, learned Standing Counsel appearing

                      for the respondents conceded to the limited request made by the

                      learned counsel for the petitioner.



</p><p id="p_4">                           4.Without expressing any opinion on merits of the case and

                      considering the limited request made by the learned counsel for

                      the petitioner, I am inclined to issue direction to the 1st

                      respondent to consider the representation dated 04.05.2018

                      submitted by the petitioner and pass orders within a period of

                      twelve (12) weeks from the date of receipt of a copy of this

                      order.
</p>
<p id="p_5">

</p><p id="p_6">                           With the above directions, the Writ Petition is disposed of.

                      No costs.</p><pre id="pre_2">
