<div class="doc_title">Jhaman Ray And Ors vs The State Of Bihar on 1 March, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">         IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                      CRIMINAL APPEAL (SJ) No.708 of 2019
           Arising Out of PS. Case No.-50 Year-2018 Thana- SC/ST District- Samastipur
     ======================================================
</pre><p id="p_1">1.    Jhaman Ray, S/o Late Heerde Ray
</p><p id="p_2">2.   Ram Babu Ray S/o Late Heerde Ray
</p><p id="p_3">3.   Ashok Ray S/o Late Mahendar Ray

     All Resident of Mohanpur Sahpur, P.S.- Paroti, District- Samastipur (Bihar)

                                                                        ... ... Appellant/s
                                            Versus
     The State of Bihar

                                               ... ... Respondent/s
     ======================================================
     Appearance :
</p><p id="p_4">     For the Appellant/s     :        Mr.Subodh Kumar
     For the Respondent/s    :        Mr.Sadanand Paswan
     ======================================================
     CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE VINOD KUMAR SINHA
     ORAL JUDGMENT
      Date : 01-03-2019

              This is an appeal under <a href="/doc/50707649/" id="a_1">Section 14A(2)</a> of the Scheduled

      Castes and <a href="/doc/25085007/" id="a_2">Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act</a>,

      1989 against the refusal of prayer of anticipatory bail dated

      21.01.2019 passed by the learned ADJ-1 st Samastipur in ABP

      No. 179 of 2019 arising out of Samastipur SC/ST P.S.Case No.

      50 of 2018 registered under <a href="/doc/261195/" id="a_3">Sections 323</a>, <a href="/doc/261195/" id="a_4">341</a>, <a href="/doc/261195/" id="a_5">354</a>, <a href="/doc/261195/" id="a_6">379</a>, <a href="/doc/261195/" id="a_7">504</a>

      and <a href="/doc/261195/" id="a_8">506</a> of the Indian penal Code and <a href="/doc/42436223/" id="a_9">Sections 3(i)(r)(s)</a> of the

      Scheduled Castes and <a href="/doc/25085007/" id="a_10">Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of

      Atrocities) Act</a>, 1989.
</p>
<p id="p_5">               Allegation against the appellants is that they tried to

      dispossess the informant from the land and abused as well as
              Patna High Court CR. APP (SJ) No.708 of 2019 dt.01-03-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                          2/2</span>




                      also tried to disrobe the informant.

</p><p id="p_6">                              Submission of learned counsel for the appellants is that

                      purchased land is of the appellants and they have falsely been

                      implicated in this case making ornamental allegations.

</p><p id="p_7">                              Heard learned Spl. P.P also who has opposed the prayer

                      for anticipatory bail stating that land is of Government land and

                      informant has got Parcha for that land.

</p><p id="p_8">                              Having heard both sides and in view of the facts and

                      circumstances, as stated above, I am not inclined to grant

                      privilege of anticipatory bail to them. However, if appellants

                      surrender within a period of six weeks from the date of receipt

                      of a copy of this order and make prayer for regular bail, the

                      same shall be considered and disposed of on its own merit

                      without being prejudiced by this order, if possible, on the same

                      day.
</p>
<p id="p_9">
</p><p id="p_10">                                               (Vinod Kumar Sinha, J)
sujit/-
