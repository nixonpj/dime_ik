  IN THE HIGH COURT OF KERALA AT ERNAKULAM

CRL.A.No. 1169 of 2009()


1. VELLIYATH AGRO INDUSTRIES (P) LTD.,
                      ...  Petitioner

                        Vs



1. M/S.P.K.ABU SONS, MATTANCHERY,
                       ...       Respondent

2. RAFFEEK, PARTNER,

3. RIYAS,

4. STATE OF KERALA REPRESENTED BY

                For Petitioner  :SRI.P.K.SAJEEV

                For Respondent  :SRI.C.A.CHACKO

The Hon'ble MRS. Justice K.HEMA

 Dated :27/01/2011

 O R D E R
                             K.HEMA, J.
             -----------------------------------------------
              Criminal Appeal No.1169 of 2009
             -----------------------------------------------
                   Dated 27th January, 2011.

                          J U D G M E N T
</pre>

<p id="p_1">           This appeal arises from an order of acquittal passed

under <a href="/doc/1988204/" id="a_1">Section 256(1)</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure ('the

Code', for short).
</p>
<p id="p_2">           2.   The appellant is the complainant.           He filed a

complaint against first respondent before the Magistrate Court,

alleging  offence under <a href="/doc/1823824/" id="a_2">Section            138</a>    of   the Negotiable

Instruments Act. The accused did not appear before the court

and warrants were issued against him.              On 21.2.2006, the

complainant was absent and hence, accused was acquitted.

</p><p id="p_3">           3. Heard learned counsel for appellant. There is no

representation for respondents 1 to 3. On hearing appellant's

counsel and on going through the proceeding sheet produced

along with the appeal memorandum, it is clear that the case

was posted for "production" of accused to 21.2.2006, in

execution of the non-bailable warrant issued against accused

through the Station House Officer.           An accused cannot be

acquitted, under <a href="/doc/1988204/" id="a_3">Section 256(1)</a> of the Code on the day to

which the case is posted for production of accused as per a

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">Crl.Appeal No.1169/09                2</span>




warrant issued. Learned counsel for appellant submitted that in

the light of the decision reported in <a href="/doc/54948533/" id="a_4">Joy Abraham v. Jiju

Thomas</a> (2010(2) KLT 735), the order of acquittal under

<a href="/doc/1988204/" id="a_5">Section 256(1)</a> of the Code is unsustainable. I am satisfied of

the submissions made and hence the order under challenge is

to be set aside.
</p>
<p id="p_4">             In the result, the following order is passed :

</p><p id="p_5">        (i) The impugned order is set aside.
</p>
<p id="p_6">        (ii) The court below shall take the case on file and

              dispose of the same in accordance with law.

</p><p id="p_7">        (iii) The parties shall appear before the trial court on

              1.3.2011.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                   The appeal is allowed.
</p>
<p id="p_9">


</p><p id="p_10">                                                   K.HEMA, JUDGE.
</p>
