<div class="doc_title">Present : Mr. D. Mohanty vs Unknown on 27 January, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">                                           W.P.(C) No.766 of 2014




                       Present :     Mr. D. Mohanty, Advocate
                                     for the petitioner.

                                    Mr. T. Sahoo, Advocate
                                    for the Bank.




02.       27.01.2014                This petition seeks direction against initiation of coercive
                            measures by the Bank for recovery of loan under the provisions of the
                            <a href="/doc/52229129/" id="a_1">SARFAESI Act</a>, 2002.
                                    Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that the petitioner is
                            willing to repay the loan subject to decision of his application to the
                            Bank for grant of time and extending benefit under the One Time
                            Settlement Scheme, if applicable. Learned counsel further states that the
                            petitioner is willing to pay a sum of Rs.6,00,000/- (Rupees Six Lakhs)
                            within two months from today along with the proposal for repayment for
                            consideration of the Bank.
                                    Without expressing any opinion on merits, we direct that if the
                            petitioner makes deposits as aforesaid and files an application for
                            consideration of the Bank, the Bank may take decision thereon in
                            accordance with law within one month and, till such decision is taken,
                            the Bank may not take coercive measures.
                                    The petition is disposed of.
                                    It is made clear that if the petitioner defaults in making the
                            deposit, this petition will be treated to have been dismissed. It is further
                            made clear that if the Bank is aggrieved by this order, it will be at
                            liberty to move this Court.


                                                                ..............................
                                                                 A.K.Goel, C.J.


                                                                .............................
                                                                 Dr A.K.Rath, J.
</pre>
