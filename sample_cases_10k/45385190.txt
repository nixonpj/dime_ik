<div class="doc_title">Amiya Ranjan @ Amiya Ranjan Kumar vs The State Of Bihar on 19 February, 2013</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                   Criminal Miscellaneous No.4384 of 2013
                 ======================================================
                 Amiya Ranjan @ Amiya Ranjan Kumar, son of Sri Ramesh Kumar, resident
                 of village Farda, P.S. Naya Ram Nagar, Distt. Munger.
                                                                        .... .... Petitioner/s
                                                   Versus
                 The State of Bihar
                                                                   .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :   Mr. P. Kr. Lal, Adv.
                 For the State            :   Mr. C. Jawahar, A.P.P.
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE JUSTICE SMT. ANJANA PRAKASH
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   19-02-2013                 Heard learned counsel for the Petitioner and the State.

</p><p id="p_2">                               The Petitioner seeks bail in a case instituted for the

                 offence under <a href="/doc/619940/" id="a_1">Sections 363</a>, <a href="/doc/796352/" id="a_2">366(A)</a>, <a href="/doc/1128948/" id="a_3">120(B)</a> and <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_4">506</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_5">34</a> Indian

                 Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                               Considering that the Petitioner is in custody since

                 28.9.2012 and there is a general allegation against him as also he

                 has fair antecedent, let the Petitioner above named, be released on

                 bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs. 5,000/- (Five thousand) with

                 two sureties of the like amount each or any other surety to be fixed

                 by the Court below to the satisfaction of                    Chief Judicial

                 Magistrate, Gaya, in connection with Delha P.S. Case No. 97 of

                 2012 subject to the following conditions: (i) That one of the

                 bailors will be a close relative of the Petitioner who will give an

                 affidavit giving genealogy as to how he is related with the

                 Petitioner. The bailor will also undertake to inform the Court if
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">     Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.4384 of 2013 (2) dt.19-02-2013</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                               2/2</span>




                 there is any change in the address of the Petitioner. (ii) That the

                 affidavit shall clearly state that the Petitioner is not an accused in

                 any other case and if he is, he shall not be released on bail. (iii)

                 That the bailor shall also state on affidavit that he will inform the

                 court concerned if the Petitioner is implicated in any other case of

                 similar nature after his release in the present case and thereafter

                 the court below will be at liberty to initiate the proceeding for

                 cancellation of bail on the ground of misuse. (iv) That the

                 Petitioner will give an undertaking that he will receive the police

                 papers on the given date and be present on date fixed for charge

                 and if he fails to do so on two given dates and delays the trial in

                 any manner, his bail will be liable to be cancelled for reasons of

                 misuse. (v) That the Petitioner will be well represented on each

                 date and if he fails to do so on two consecutive dates, his bail will

                 be liable to be cancelled.
</p>
<p id="p_4">

</p><p id="p_5">                                                                  (Anjana Prakash, J)
S.Ali
