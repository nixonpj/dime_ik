Sukhjinder Singh @ Bittu

State of Punjab
                                                                ...Respondent

            for the petitioner.

INDERJIT SINGH, J.

<p id="p_1">            Petitioner has filed this third petition under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> Cr.P.C.

for grant of regular bail in case FIR No.103 dated 13.05.2017 under

<a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_2">Sections 307</a>, <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_3">34</a> IPC and <a href="/doc/73862/" id="a_4">Sections 25</a> and <a href="/doc/244673/" id="a_5">27</a> of the Arms Act, registered at

Police Station Beas, District Amritsar.
</p>
<p id="p_2">            Notice of motion.
</p>
<p id="p_3">            Mr.Dhruv Dayal, Sr.DAG, Punjab, has put in appearance on

behalf of the respondent-State and contested the petition.

</p><p id="p_4">            I have heard learned counsel for the petitioner as well as

learned State counsel and have gone through the record.

</p><p id="p_5">            From the record, I find that present petitioner is named in the

FIR. He is the main accused. He was armed with pistol and fired 4-5 shots,

which hit on the right side of chest of the complainant, second hit on right

side of hip-bone, third hit on the upper right side of shoulder and fourth shot

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2"> CRM No.M-11221 of 2019                                                 -2-</span>


hit near the kidney on the left side.
</p>
<p id="p_6">             Keeping in view the facts and circumstances of the present

case, nature and gravity of the offence and in view of the fact that petitioner

is the main accused, I do not find it a fit case where petitioner is entitled to

benefit of regular bail.
</p>
<p id="p_7">             Therefore, finding no merit in the present petition, the same is

dismissed.
