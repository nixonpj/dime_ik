<div class="doc_title">Vikas S/O Shankarbhai Babunand ... vs State Of Gujarat on 11 February, 2019</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: A.Y. Kogje</div>

<pre id="pre_1">       R/CR.MA/1221/2019                                                  ORDER




           IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

           R/CRIMINAL MISC.APPLICATION NO. 1221 of 2019

==========================================================
           VIKAS S/O SHANKARBHAI BABUNAND UPADHYAY
                             Versus
                        STATE OF GUJARAT
==========================================================
Appearance:
MR CHETAN B RAVAL(2090) for the PETITIONER(s) No. 1
MR LR PUJARI, APP for the RESPONDENT(s) No. 1
==========================================================

 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE A.Y. KOGJE

                                 Date : 11/02/2019

                                    ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">  1. This application is filed by the applicant under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of the

     Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 for regular bail in connection with

     FIR    registered      as   C.R.    No.I-115       of   2016   registered       with

     Chandkheda            Police   Station,          Ahmedabad     for    the    offence

     punishable under <a href="/doc/1667388/" id="a_2">Sections 419</a>, <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_3">406</a>, <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_4">420</a>, <a href="/doc/1128948/" id="a_5">120(B)</a>, <a href="/doc/386021/" id="a_6">201</a>, <a href="/doc/1185693/" id="a_7">511</a> of the

     Indian Penal Code and under <a href="/doc/273571/" id="a_8">Sections 65</a>, <a href="/doc/326206/" id="a_9">66(A)(B)(C)(D)</a> Of the

     Information Technology Act.
</p>
<p id="p_2">

</p><p id="p_3">  2. Learned advocate appearing on behalf of the applicant submits that

     considering the nature of offence, the applicant may be enlarged on

     regular bail by imposing suitable conditions.



</p><p id="p_4">  3. On the other hand, the learned Additional Public Prosecutor

     appearing for the respondent-State has opposed grant of regular

     bail looking to the nature and gravity of the offence.




<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                        Page 1 of 4</span>
</p><p id="p_5">       R/CR.MA/1221/2019                                          ORDER




</p><p id="p_6">4. Learned Advocates appearing on behalf of the respective parties do

   not press for a further reasoned order.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><p id="p_8">5. I have heard the learned advocates appearing on behalf of the

   respective parties and perused the papers. Following aspects are

   considered :-
</p>
<p id="p_9">

   I) The FIR is registered on 19.11.2016 for the offence which is

   alleged to have taken place on 19.11.2016.

   II) The applicant is in custody since 03.01.2019;

   III)   The   investigation   qua   the     applicant   is   concluded   and

   chargesheet is filed;
</p>
<p id="p_10">   IV) The allegations against the applicant is that he had supplied the

   data to the main accused, who was running the call center and had

   utilized the data for the activity of asking the consumer to deposit

   their installment, which was ultimately going into the account of the

   company headed by the accused;
</p>
<p id="p_11">   V) The accused running call-center has already been enlarged on

   regular bail, hence applying the principle of parity;

   VI) Learned Additional Public Prosecutor under the instructions of

   the Investigating Officer is unable to bring on record any special

   circumstances against the applicant.
</p>
<p id="p_12">

   This Court has taken into consideration the law laid down by the

   Hon'ble Apex Court in the case of <a href="/doc/1563495/" id="a_10">Sanjay Chandra v. Central

   Bureau of Investigation</a> reported in [2012] 1 SCC 40.




<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                Page 2 of 4</span>
</p><p id="p_13">      R/CR.MA/1221/2019                                     ORDER




</p><p id="p_14">6. In the facts and circumstances of the case and considering the

   nature of the allegations made against the applicant in the First

   Information Report, without discussing the evidence in detail, prima

   facie, this Court is of the opinion that this is a fit case to exercise

   the discretion and enlarge the applicant on regular bail.



</p><p id="p_15">7. Hence, the present application is allowed. The applicant is ordered

   to be released on regular bail in connection with C.R. No.I-115 of

   2016 registered with Chandkheda Police Station, Ahmedabad

   on executing a personal bond of Rs.10,000/= (Rupees Ten

   Thousand Only) with one surety of the like amount to the

   satisfaction of the trial Court and subject to the conditions that he

   shall;
</p>
<p id="p_16">

</p><p id="p_17">   (a) not take undue advantage of liberty or misuse liberty;

</p><p id="p_18">   (b) not act in a manner injurious to the interest of the prosecution;

</p><p id="p_19">   (c) surrender passport, if any, to the lower Court within a week;

</p><p id="p_20">   (d) not leave the State of Gujarat without prior permission of the

   Sessions Judge concerned;
</p>
<p id="p_21">   (e) mark presence before the concerned Police Station once of

   every English calendar month for a period of six months between

   11.00 a.m. and 2.00 p.m.;
</p>
<p id="p_22">   (f) furnish the present address of his residence to the Investigating

   Officer and also to the Court at the time of execution of the bond

   and shall not change the residence without prior permission of this

   Court;
</p>
<p id="p_23">


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                               Page 3 of 4</span>
</p><p id="p_24">          R/CR.MA/1221/2019                                     ORDER




</p><p id="p_25">   8. The authorities will release the applicant only if he is not required in

       connection with any other offence for the time being. If breach of

       any of the above conditions is committed, the Sessions Judge

       concerned will be free to issue warrant or take appropriate action in

       the matter.
</p>
<p id="p_26">

</p><p id="p_27">   9. Bail bond to be executed before the lower Court having jurisdiction

       to try the case. It will be open for the concerned Court to delete,

       modify and/or relax any of the above conditions, in accordance with

       law.
</p>
<p id="p_28">

</p><p id="p_29">   10.At the trial, the trial Court shall not be influenced by the

       observations of preliminary nature qua the evidence at this stage

       made by this Court while enlarging the applicant on bail.



</p><p id="p_30">   11.The application is allowed in the aforesaid terms.       Rule is made

       absolute to the aforesaid extent. Direct Service is permitted.



</p><p id="p_31">                                                            (A.Y. KOGJE, J)
URIL




<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                                  Page 4 of 4</span>
