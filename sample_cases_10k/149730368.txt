<div class="doc_title">Mahaveer Gupta &amp; Anr vs Rent Tribunal Sgnr., &amp; Anr on 24 October, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                                   1

     IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR RAJASTHAN
                             AT JODHPUR
      ------------------------------------------------------------

          CIVIL WRIT (CW) No. 7716 of 2015
PETITIONER :

1.     Mahaveer Gupta S/o Shri Jai Narain Gupta, b/c

Agarwal, aged 33 years, Resident of 24-A, Public Park, Sri

Ganganagar.

2.     Bal Gopal S/o Sh. Om Prakash, b/c Agarwal, aged

around 26 years, Resident of Vishwakarma Building Material

and Cement Store, 32-33, Shanti Nagar, Vridh Aashram

Road, Sri Ganganagar.

                         VERSUS

RESPONDENTS :

1.     Rent Tribunal, Sri Ganganaggar.

2.     Shiv Ratan, S/o Sh. Birbal Singh, b/c Rajput Address

of Business Place : Bajrang Handloom, 24-A, Public Park,

Sri Ganganagar.


       Date of Order : 24.10.2016

                        HON'BLE MR. SANDEEP MEHTA,J.

       Mr. Pankaj Sharma, for the petitioners.


                         ORDER
</pre><p id="p_1">                          -----
</p><p id="p_2">       By way of this writ petition, the petitioners have

approached this Court praying for the following relief:-

</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">             "It is, therefore, most respectfully prayed that
        this writ petition may kindly be allowed and by issuing
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                 2</span>

     appropriate writ, order or direction, learned Rent
     Tribunal, Sri Ganganagar may kindly be directed to
     decide Case No. 3/2014 arising out of the application
     preferred by the petitioner under Section; 9(a) of the
     Act     Rajasthan   Rent       Control    Act,     2001,    most
     expeditiously and preferably within a period of three
     months from the date of decision of this writ petition."</blockquote><p id="p_3">




</p><p id="p_4">     Counsel    for   the   petitioners       submits    that    after

presentation of the writ petition Rent Tribunal has recorded

the petitioners' evidence    without      complying       with    the

procedure provided under Section 15(5) of the Rent Control

Act, 2001.
</p>
<p id="p_5">     Having regard to the facts and circumstances of the

case and after perusing the material available on record and

considering the fact that recording of evidence has already

commenced, it is expected that as far as possible the

Tribunal shall adhere to the procedure provided under

Section 15 of the Rent Control Act, 2001 and shall try and

decide the application expeditiously.

</p><p id="p_6">     The writ petition is disposed of with the above

observations.
</p>
<p id="p_7">


</p><p id="p_8">                                          ( SANDEEP MEHTA ),J.
</p>
<p id="p_9">/sushil/
