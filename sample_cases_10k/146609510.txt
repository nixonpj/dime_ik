<div class="doc_title">Murari Yadav vs The State Of Bihar &amp; Ors on 5 February, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                   IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                Civil Writ Jurisdiction Case No.1528 of 2018
                 ======================================================
                 1. Murari Yadav, son of Late Mahendra Singh, resident of Village- Kukuri,
                 P.S.- Garhani, District- Bhojpur at Arrah            .... Petitioner
                                                     Versus
                 1. The State of Bihar through the Principal Secretary, Department of
                 Education, Govt. of Bihar, Patna.
                 2. The District Education Officer, Bhojpur at Arrah.
                 3. The District Programme Officer (Establishment), Bhojpur at Arrah.
                 4. The Block Education Officer, Agiaon, Bhojpur at Arrah.
                                                                      .... Respondents
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner       : Mr. Anil Kumar Verma, Adv.
                 For the Respondents       : Mr. Narendra Kumar, AC to GP XX
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE ANIL KUMAR
                 UPADHYAY
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   05-02-2018                 Heard the learned counsel for the petitioner and the

                 learned counsel appearing for the State.

</p><p id="p_2">                               The petitioner was put under suspension vide Memo

                 No. 2695, dated 17.08.2016.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                               The learned counsel for the petitioner has drawn the

                 attention of the Court to the order contained in Annexure 5, dated

                 15.09.2017, whereby a coordinate Bench of this Court has

                 examined the validity of the order, contained in Memo No. 2695,

                 dated 17.08.2016, and on consideration of the various aspects the

                 coordinate Bench quashed the order, contained in Memo No.

                 2695, dated 17.08.2016.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                               In view of the above, the Court is left with no option

                 to follow the principle of parity. The Court is constrained to quash
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">        Patna High Court CWJC No.1528 of 2018 (2) dt.05-02-2018</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                 2/2</span>




                    the order, contained in Memo No. 2695, dated 17.08.2016.

</p><p id="p_5">                                    In view of the litigation policy, it is expected that the

                    respondents will not only comply this order for petitioner, but, the

                    order is applicable with regard to all the similarly circumstanced

                    mentioned in Memo No. 2695, dated 17.08.2016, and will be

                    extended to all the similarly situated persons irrespective of the

                    fact that whether those put under suspension along with the

                    petitioner has moved before this Court or not.

</p><p id="p_6">                                    With the aforesaid observation, this writ application

                    is disposed of.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                                    However, quashing of the order of suspension will

                    not come in the way of the respondents in taking final decision in

                    accordance with law in the departmental proceeding.



</p><p id="p_8">                                            (Anil Kumar Upadhyay, J)
SA/-
</p>
