CR.MA/2944/2010	 1/ 4	ORDER 
 
 

	

 

IN
THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD
 

 


 

CRIMINAL
MISC.APPLICATION No. 2944 of 2010
 

=========================================================

 

DINESHBHAI
AUDHABHAI VAGHANI &amp; 1 - Applicant(s)
 

Versus
 

STATE
OF GUJARAT - Respondent(s)
 

=========================================================
 
Appearance
: 
MR
APURVA R KAPADIA for
Applicant(s) : 1 - 2. 
MR
DIVYESH SEJPAL APP
for Respondent(s) :
1, 
=========================================================


 
	  
	 
	  
		 
			 

CORAM
			: 
			
		
		 
			 

HONOURABLE
			MR.JUSTICE H.B.ANTANI
		
	

 

Date
: 13/04/2010 

 

ORAL
ORDER</pre> 

 

 


 


<p id="p_1">1.	This application is
preferred under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973
seeking regular bail by the applicants, who have been arrested in
connection with F.I.R. registered as C.R.No.I-28 of 2010 with
Dediyapada Police Station for the offence punishable under <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_2">Sections
406</a>, <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_3">420</a> and <a href="/doc/112749/" id="a_4">114</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_2">2.	Learned advocate
Mr.A.R. Kapadia for the applicants submitted
that the applicants are innocent persons and they have not committed
the offence as alleged in the F.I.R. Considering  the role attributed
to the applicants and since the goods in question is already
recovered, they deserve to be enlarged on bail.
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_3">3.	Learned
A.P.P. Mr.Divyesh Sejpal, representing the respondent-State, while
opposing the bail application,
submitted that the applicants are involved in the offence punishable
under <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_5">Sections 406</a>, <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_6">420</a> and <a href="/doc/112749/" id="a_7">114</a> of the Indian Penal Code. Considering
the nature of offence and manner in which the applicants have
committed the said offence, they are not entitled to claim
discretionary relief, as prayed for in the application and the
application be dismissed.
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_4">4.	I
have heard learned advocate Mr.A.R. Kapadia for the applicants and
learned A.P.P. Mr.Divyesh Sejpal for the respondent-State at length
and in great detail. I have considered the role attributed to the
applicants which is reflected in the F.I.R. at Annexure A to the
application, provisions of <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_8">Sections 406</a>, <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_9">420</a> and <a href="/doc/112749/" id="a_10">114</a> of the Indian
Penal Code, police papers, quantum of punishment and gravity of
offence etc. The goods in question is already recovered from the
applicants and that aspect is required to be taken into account.
Thus, considering the aforesaid aspect, I am of the view that the
applicants deserve to be enlarged on bail.
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_5">5.	The
application is allowed and the applicants are ordered to be enlarged
on bail in connection with C.R.No.I-28 of 2010 with Dediyapada Police
Station on executing a bond of Rs.10,000/- each [Rupees ten thousand
only] with one surety each of the like amount to the satisfaction of
the Trial Court and subject to the conditions that they shall:
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_6">[a]	not take undue
advantage of their liberty or abuse their liberty;
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_7">[b]	not act in a manner
injurious to the interest of the prosecution;
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_8">[c]	surrender their
passport, if any, to the lower Court within a week;
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_9">[d]	not leave the State
of Gujarat without prior permission of the Sessions Court concerned;
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_10">[e]	mark their presence
at the concerned police station on any day of first week of every
English calendar month between 9.00 AM and 2.00 PM. till the trial is
over;
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_11">[f]	furnish the present
address of their residence to the I.O. and also to the Court at the
time of execution of the bond and shall not change their residence
without prior permission of this Court;
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_12">[g]	maintain law and
order.
</p> 


 


 


 


 


<p id="p_13">6.	If breach of any of
the above conditions is committed, the Sessions Judge concerned will
be free to issue warrant or to take appropriate action in the matter.
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_14">7.	Bail bond to be
executed before the lower Court having jurisdiction to try the case.
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_15">8.	At the trial, the
Trial Court shall not be influenced by the observations of
preliminary nature, qua the evidence at this stage, made by this
Court while enlarging the applicants on bail.
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_16">9.	Rule is made absolute
to the aforesaid extent. Direct service is permitted.
</p> 


						
 


<p id="p_17">						(H.B.
</p><p id="p_18">Antani, J.)
 

Hitesh
