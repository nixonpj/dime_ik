<div class="doc_title">Pradeep Sah @ Pradeep Kumar &amp; Anr vs The State Of Bihar on 27 June, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.18136 of 2016
                    Arising Out of PS.Case No. -130 Year- 2015 Thana -NATHNAGAR District- BHAGALPUR
                 ======================================================
                 1. Pradeep Sah @ Pradeep Kumar Son of Prakash Sah
                 2. Bina Devi W/o Pradeep Sah All resident of village - Nurpur, P.S.
                 Nathnagar, District - Bhagalpur

                                                                              .... ....   Petitioner/s
                                                       Versus
                 1. The State of Bihar

                                                                  .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :  Mr. Rajive Ranjan Singh
                 For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr. Anish Chandra (App)
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE JUSTICE SMT. ANJANA MISHRA
                                             ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">3   27-06-2016           Heard learned counsel for the petitioners and learned

                 Additional Public Prosecutor for the State and learned counsel for

                 the Informant.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                         The petitioners being Bhaisur and Gotni of the deceased

                 lady apprehend their arrest in connection with a case registered for

                 the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/653797/" id="a_1">Sections 304B</a> and <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_2">34</a> of the Indian

                 Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                         Learned counsel for the petitioners submits that the

                 petitioner and his wife are living separately from entire family and

                 no particular role has been attributed to them in the First

                 Information Report. It is further submitted that there is general

                 and omnibus allegations against the all family members including

                 these petitioners.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">          Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.18136 of 2016 (3) dt.27-06-2016</span><p id="p_4">
</p><p id="p_5">                                                   P2/2




                               Diary in this case was called for earlier which has since

                      been received.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                               Learned counsel for the State after perusal of the case diary

                      submits that there is no further material in the case diary, save and

                      except, the allegations as made out in the First Information Report

                      which are indeed general and omnibus in nature.

</p><p id="p_7">                               Considering the aforementioned facts and circumstances,

                      let the petitioners, in the event of their arrest / surrender within a

                      period of four weeks from the date of receipt/production of a copy

                      of this order, be enlarged on bail on furnishing bail bonds of Rs.

                      10,000/- (ten thousand) each with two sureties of the like amount

                      each to the satisfaction of the learned Chief Judicial Magistrate,

                      Bhagalpur in connection with Nath Nagar (Madhusudanpur) P.S.

                      Case No. 130/2015, subject to the conditions as laid down under

                      <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_3">Section 438(2)</a> of the Cr. P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                                                                 (Anjana Mishra, J)
Saif/-
</p>
