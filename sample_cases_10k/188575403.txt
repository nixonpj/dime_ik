<div class="doc_title">Sampathkumar vs Palaniyammal on 11 April, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">        

 

BEFORE THE MADURAI BENCH OF MADRAS HIGH COURT               

DATED: 11.04.2017  

CORAM   

THE HONOURABLE DR.JUSTICE G.JAYACHANDRAN             

C.R.P.(MD).No.755 of 2017 

1.Sampathkumar   
2.Srinivasan                    ...Petitioners/Petitioners/Plaintiffs

Vs.

Palaniyammal                    ...Respondent/Respondent/Defendant  

PRAYER: Civil Revision Petition filed under <a href="/doc/1331149/" id="a_1">Article 227</a> of Constitution  
of India,praying to order for the speedy disposal of the Execution Petition
filed in E.P.No.155 of 2009 in O.S.No.155 of 2007 on the file of Sub Court,
Pudukkottai and to allow this Civil Revision Petition.

!For Petitioner : Mr.G.Sridharan        

^For Respondent :                               
                

:ORDER  
</pre>
<p id="p_1">        The revision petitioner seeks indulgence of this court for  disposal of
the E.P.No.155 of 2009 pending on the file of Sub Court,  Pudukkottai,
</p><p id="p_2">        2. The Dairy Extract indicates that the counter in Execution Petition
was filed on dated 29.09.2011.  Thereafter pursuant tofilling of certain
Interlocutory Application and due to pendency of revision petition,  the
matter has been adjourned periodically for more than 2 years.  The latest
adjudication indicates that due to pendency of unnumbered civil revision
petition, the case was adjourned, without any progress.
</p><p id="p_3">        2. The learned counsel for the revision petitioner submits that a memo
was filed by the respondent stating that C.R.P.(SR) No.39275 of 2015, is
pending and therefore, the matter has been adjourned since 09.10.2015 and  on
verification, it is submitted by the counsel  the revision petition is not
yet numbered and there is no impediment for the Execution Court to proceed
with the Execution Petition.
</p><p id="p_4">        3. It is unfortunate that the decree which was passed on 01.04.2008 of
April is not yet able to be executed even after the lapse of 9 years and
atleast for the past 2 years the revision petitioner produced unnumbered
revision petition, the Execution Court has kept simply adjourning the matter
without any progress and without ascertaining from the judgment debtor about
the fate of the unnumbered revision petition. Therefore, the Execution Court
is directed to dispose of Execution Petition within 3 months from the date of
receipt of a copy of this order.
</p><p id="p_5">        4. Hence, the Civil Revision Petition is disposed of. No Costs.
</p>


<p id="p_6">To
The Sub Court, 
Pudukottai.
