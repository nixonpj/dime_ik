                   BENCH AT JAIPUR

              S.B. Civil Writ Petition No. 11969/2010

Jagdish Meena And Anr
                                                                  ----Petitioner
                                   Versus
Addi District And Session Jud Anr
                                                                ----Respondent</pre>


<p id="p_1">For Petitioner(s)         :    Mr. Shashi Shekhar
For Respondent(s)         :    Mr. Rahul Sharma on behalf of
                               Mr. S.K.Gupta



     HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE SANJEEV PRAKASH SHARMA

                                    Order

17/05/2019
     The writ petition is directed against the order dated 2.8.2010

passed in the appeal which was preferred by the petitioner

wherein he moved an application under Order 41 Rule 27 CPC for

placing on record the copy of the final report submitted by police

in relation to FIR which was relevant for the purpose of the case.

The Appellate Court has rejected the application while the appeal

is pending.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">     It is settled law that Appellate Court ought to have decided

the application alongwith appeal and examine the contents thereof

in reference to the arguments raised in the appeal. Coordinate

Bench of this Court at Principal Seat, Jodhpur in Judgment

reported in 2017(4)DNJ(Raj.)1812" Bikaner Road Transport

Service(M/s.) Thro' Proprietor Vs. Additional District Judge

No.6, Jodhpur Metropolitan, Jodhpur &amp; Anr. has taken the




<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                    (Downloaded on 29/06/2019 at 02:11:01 AM)</span>
                                                            (2 of 2)                                [CW-11969/2010]


                                   similar view, based on 2012(8)SCC148 in the case of "Union of

                                   India Vs. Ibrahim Uddin".
</p>
<p id="p_4">
</p><p id="p_5">                                         Learned appearing for the respondent does not seriously

                                   contest the legal issues.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><p id="p_7">                                         In view thereof, the order dated 2.8.2010 is set aside with

                                   direction to the Appellate Court to examine the contents of

                                   application under Order 41 Rule 27 CPC as well as the documents

                                   thereof at the time of deciding the appeal.




</p><p id="p_8">                                                     (SANJEEV PRAKASH SHARMA),J

                                   Anu /397
