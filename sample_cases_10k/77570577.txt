<p id="p_1">                             (ORDER OF THE COURT WAS MADE BY M.DURAISWAMY, J.)



                             The petitioner has filed the above Writ Petition to issue a Writ

                      of Mandamus,       forbearing the respondents from laying any road or

                      fixing lampposts and burial ground name board amidst the residential

                      plots at Plot Nos.201 and 225 and the other plots in the approved

                      layout at VGP Golden Beach,             Uthandi,      East       Coast Road,

                      Kancheepuram District, contrary to the scheme of approved lay out and

                      to remove the lamppost and name board already fixed.



                      _________
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                      Page 3 of 7</span>
http://www.judis.nic.in
                                                                              W.P. No.4208 of 2006



</p><p id="p_2">                             2. The learned counsel appearing for the petitioner submitted that

                      the only prayer that has to be granted to the petitioner is with regard to

                      the removal of the burial ground name board.



</p><p id="p_3">                             3.     The learned counsel appearing for the 10th respondent, the

                      Commissioner of Corporation, Chennai, submitted that the             burial

                      ground name board was removed about four years ago and therefore,

                      the said relief cannot be granted.



</p><p id="p_4">                             4. However, the learned counsel appearing for       the petitioner

                      disputed the submission made by the learned counsel appearing for the

                      10th respondent and submitted that the burial ground name board is

                      remaining in the same place till today.



</p><p id="p_5">                             5.      The learned counsel appearing for the 10th respondent

                      submitted that in the event of the burial ground name board not being

                      removed, the 10th respondent undertakes to remove the same, within a

                      period of two weeks.
</p>
<p id="p_6">

                      _________
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                      Page 4 of 7</span>
http://www.judis.nic.in
                                                                                 W.P. No.4208 of 2006



</p><p id="p_7">                             6. In view of the submissions made by the learned counsel on

                      either side and also recording the submission made by the learned

                      counsel appearing for the 10th respondent, we make it clear that in the

                      event of the burial ground name board not yet been removed, the 10th

                      respondent shall remove the same, within a period of two weeks, from

                      the date of receipt of a copy of this order.



</p><p id="p_8">                               With these observations, the Writ Petition is disposed of. No

                      costs.
</p>
