<div class="doc_title">Ravneet Walia And Others vs State Of Haryana And Another on 5 October, 2012</div><pre id="pre_1">CRM M-25901 of 2012 and
CRM M-27925 of 2012                                                      1

      IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANAAT
                     CHANDIGARH


                                       Date of Decision: October 05, 2012

(1)   CRM M-25901 of 2012

Ravneet Walia and others                               ... Petitioners

                                   Versus

State of Haryana and another.                          ... Respondents

(2)   CRM M-27925 of 2012

Pritika Dutta Walia                                    .... Petitioner

                                   Versus

State of Haryana and another.                          ... Respondents


CORAM: HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE PARAMJEET SINGH


      1) Whether Reporters of the local papers may be allowed to see the
      judgment ?.

      2) To be referred to the Reporters or not ?.

      3) Whether the judgment should be reported in the Digest ?

Present:    Mr. Devinder Singh, Advocate,
            for the petitioners (In CRM M-25901 of 2012) and
            for respondent No.2 (In CRM M-27925 of 2012)

            Mr. P.R. Yadav, Advocate,
            for the petitioner (In CRM M-27925 of 2012) and
            for respondent No.2 (In CRM M-25901 of 2012)

            Mr. Anupam Sharma, AAG, Haryana.


Paramjeet Singh, J. (Oral)
</pre>
<p id="p_1">            By this common order, Crl. Misc. Petitions viz. CRM M-
 CRM M-25901 of 2012 and
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">CRM M-27925 of 2012                                                       2</span>

25901 of 2012 and CRM M-27925 of 2012 are being decided together as

the same arise out of FIR No. 225 dated 17.08.2009 and FIR No.484 dated

26.10.2009.
</p>
<p id="p_2">              CRM M-25901 of 2012 has been filed by Ravneet Walia and

two others under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C. for quashing of FIR No. 484 dated

26.10.2009, under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_2">Sections 498-A</a>, <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_3">406</a> IPC, registered at Police Station

Civil Lines, District Gurgaon, and all subsequent proceedings arising

therefrom, on the basis of compromise dated 24.09.2011 (Annexure P/2).

</p><p id="p_3">              CRM M-27925 of 2012 has been filed by Pritika Dutta Walia

under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_4">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C. for quashing of FIR No. 225 dated 17.08.2009,

under <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_5">Sections 323</a>, <a href="/doc/1243353/" id="a_6">342</a>, <a href="/doc/1985627/" id="a_7">467</a>, <a href="/doc/556166/" id="a_8">468</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_9">506</a> / <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_10">34</a> IPC, registered at Police

Station Palam Vihar, Gurgaon and all subsequent proceedings arising

therefrom, qua petitioner on the basis of compromise dated 24.09.2011

(Annexure P/2).
</p>
<p id="p_4">              Learned counsel for the petitioners has relied upon a judgment

of this Court in the matter of <a href="/doc/1618121/" id="a_11">Bhupinder Kaur vs. State of Punjab and

another</a>, 2004(2) RCR (Criminal) 443           to contend that there is no

reasonable likelihood of the accused being convicted for the offence for

the reason that the complainant has compromised the matter with the

accused and he is not likely to support the prosecution and from other facts

and circumstances available on the record, therefore, it would not be in the

interest of justice to decline the prayer for quashing of the FIR on the

ground that it would amount to be permitting the parties to compound non-
 CRM M-25901 of 2012 and
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">CRM M-27925 of 2012                                                        3</span>

compoundable offence.
</p>
<p id="p_5">             Learned counsel for Pritika Walia and Ravneet Walia-

complainants, who are present in Court today, state that the complainants

would have no objection, if the present FIRs along with consequential

proceedings, arising out of it, are quashed.
</p>
<p id="p_6">             Two affidavits dated 05.10.2012 of Pritika Dutta Walia and

Ravneet Walia - complainants in the two cases affirming the factum of

compromise have been filed in Court today, which are taken on record.

</p><p id="p_7">             Consequently, in view of compromise between the parties and

keeping in view the law laid down by the Hon'ble Apex Court in the case

of <a href="/doc/1442974/" id="a_12">Madan Mohan Abbot vs. State of Punjab</a>, 2008(2) RCR (Criminal)

429, which has been affirmed by a Larger Bench of the Hon'ble Supreme

Court in Gian Singh vs. State of Punjab and another, S.L.P. (Crl.) No.

8989 of 2010, decided on 24.09.2012, by the Full Bench judgment of this

Court in the case of <a href="/doc/811682/" id="a_13">Kulwinder Singh and others vs. State of Punjab and

another</a>, 2007(3) RCR (Crl.) 1052, and judgment of this Court in

Bhupinder Kaur's case (supra) no useful purpose would be served in

prolonging the litigation, especially when this case does not fall within the

category of exceptional cases where this Court should not exercise its

inherent jurisdictional powers to quash the criminal proceedings, as held in

Gian Singh's case (supra). In the facts and circumstances of this case, it

would be unfair or contrary to the interest of justice to continue with the

criminal proceedings or continuation of the criminal proceedings would
 CRM M-25901 of 2012 and
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">CRM M-27925 of 2012                                                        4</span>

amount to abuse of process of law despite settlement and compromise

between the victim and wrongdoer and to secure the ends of justice,

therefore, it is appropriate that criminal case is put to an end.

</p><p id="p_8">             Both petitions are allowed.      FIR No. 484 dated 26.10.2009,

under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_14">Sections 498-A</a>, <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_15">406</a> IPC, registered at Police Station Civil Lines,

District Gurgaon, is quashed along with all consequential proceedings. FIR

No. 225 dated 17.08.2009, under <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_16">Sections 323</a>, <a href="/doc/1243353/" id="a_17">342</a>, <a href="/doc/1985627/" id="a_18">467</a>, <a href="/doc/556166/" id="a_19">468</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_20">506</a> / <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_21">34</a>

IPC, registered at Police Station Palam Vihar, Gurgaon and consequential

proceedings is hereby quashed qua petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_9">

</p><pre id="pre_2">October 05, 2012                                     [ Paramjeet Singh ]
vkd                                                        Judge
