            CORAM: HON'BLE MRS.JUSTICE JAYA ROY

            For the Petitioners : Mr. Bhaiya Vishwajeet Kumar, Advocate
            For the State       : Mr. D.K. Prasad, A.P.P.
                                    ----
06 / 02.12.2011</pre><p id="p_1"> Heard the counsel for the petitioner and counsel for State.

</p><p id="p_2">                  The petitioners are apprehending their arrest in this case which

            has been registered for the offence committed under <a href="/doc/1944660/" id="a_1">Sections 384</a> of

            the Indian Penal Code, in connection with Kanke P.S.Case No.42 of

            2009 (G.R.No.2177 of 2009), pending in the court of C.J.M., Ranchi.

</p><p id="p_3">                  The counsel for the petitioner submits that R.S.Khewat no.6

            Khata No.72 of the village Chandwa originally belongs to Khewatdar

            Sanicharwa Pahan who was great grand father of the petitioner no.21

            Binod Munda and petitioner No.22 Ram Munda and the petitioner

            No.20 Sudhni Devi is wife of Khandu Pahan who was grandson of

            Sanicharwa Pahan.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                  The counsel for the petitioners has further submitted that the

            petitioner No.20 has already filed a case under Scheduled Area

            Regulation Act against the informant (Ram Pravesh Singh) being
       S.A.R. Case No.46 of 2009-10, which is still pending. Therefore, the

      land in question belongs to the petitioners Nos.20, 21 and 22 and the

      land dispute is still pending between those petitioners and the

      informant. To support their contention, the petitioners have annexed

      the order passed in S.A.R.Case No.46 of 2009-10 as Annexure-3 and

      the Khewat of the land in question as Annexure-2 which clearly support

      the submission of the petitioners.
</p>
<p id="p_5">            The counsel for the State opposed but not disputed the

      aforesaid submissions made by the counsel for the petitioners.

</p><p id="p_6">            Considering the facts and circumstances of the case, the

      petitioners, named above, are directed to surrender before the trial

      court within a period of one month from the date of this order i.e.

      02.12.2011. If the petitioners surrender before the trial court within the

      said period, the trial court is directed to release the petitioners, named

      above, namely 1.Gansa Munda, 2.Jatru Pahan @ Jatru Munda,

</p><p id="p_7">      3.Sudhuwa Pahan, 4.Sukra Pahan, 5.Sulchand Pahan, 6.Phulchand

      Pahan, 7.Lilchand Pahan, 8.Furku Munda, 9.Phuleshwar Munda,

</p><p id="p_8">      10.Jagarnath Munda, 11.Manju Devi, 12.Manju Pahain, 13.Shivani

      Devi, 14.Jhubo Devi, 15.Bidhani Pahan, 16.Jharia Munda, 17.Naresh

      Munda, 18.Jatri Devi, 19.Poklo Devi, 20.Sudhni Devi, 21.Binod Munda

      and 22.Ram Munda on bail, on furnishing bail bond of Rs.10,000/-

      (Ten Thousand) each, with two sureties of the like amount each, to the

      satisfaction of the trial court/ C.J.M., Ranchi, in connection with Kanke

      P.S.Case No.42 of 2009 (G.R.No. 2177 of 2009), subject to the

      conditions that   one of the bailors will be their close relatives and

      another will be of local resident having immovable property within the

      jurisdiction of the trial court concerned and also subject to the

      conditions laid down under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_2">Section 438(2)</a> Cr.P.C.



</p><p id="p_9">                                                          ( Jaya Roy, J. )
SI/
