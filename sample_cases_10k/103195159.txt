<div class="doc_title">Suraj Bind vs The State Of Bihar on 3 July, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                     IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                 CRIMINAL APPEAL (SJ) No.1658 of 2019
                     Arising Out of PS. Case No.-18 Year-2019 Thana- ARIYARI District- Sheikhpura
                 ======================================================
                 Suraj Bind S/o Radhe Shyam Bind R/o village- Hussainabad, P.S.- Ariyari,
                 Distt.- Sheikhpura

                                                                                   ... ... Appellant/s
                                                       Versus
                 The State of Bihar

                                                           ... ... Respondent/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Appellant/s     :        Dr. Anjani Pd. Singh
                 For the Respondent/s    :        Mr. Binay Krishna
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE PRAKASH CHANDRA
                 JAISWAL
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">4   03-07-2019                   Heard learned counsel for the appellant and learned

                   Spl. P.P. for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                  The appellant seeks bail in connection with

                   Ariyari P.S. Case No. 18 of 2019 registered for the offence

                   punishable under <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_1">Sections 341</a>, <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_2">324</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_3">504</a>, <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_4">307</a> of the Indian

                   Penal Code, Section 37(c) of Bihar Prohibition &amp; <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_5">Excise Act</a>-

                   2016 and <a href="/doc/42436223/" id="a_6">Section 3(1)(r)(s)</a> of the Scheduled Castes and

                   <a href="/doc/25085007/" id="a_7">Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act</a>.

</p><p id="p_3">                                  On protest by the informant over abusing his father

                   by the appellant, the appellant gave knife blow on his neck in

                   inebriated condition inflicting injury to him.

</p><p id="p_4">                                  It is submitted by learned counsel for the appellant

                   that no such occurrence as alleged ever took place. Appellant
               Patna High Court CR. APP (SJ) No.1658 of 2019(4) dt.03-07-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                           2/2</span>




                        has been falsely implicated in this case due to dirty village

                        politics. Appellant happens to be poor labourer. He happens to

                        be tee-totaller and had never consumed any liquor. Injury

                        sustained by the victim is simple in nature and there is no

                        repetition of blow, hence, no offence under <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_8">Section 307</a> IPC is

                        made out against the appellant. Appellant has been languishing

                        in custody since 07.03.2019.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                        On the other hand, learned Spl. PP for the State

                        opposed the bail prayer of the appellant.

</p><p id="p_6">                                        In the facts and circumstances of the case, the

                        above named appellant is directed to be enlarged on bail on

                        furnishing bail bond of Rs. 10,000/- (Ten thousand) with two

                        sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of the

                        learned 2nd A.D.J. Sheikhpura in connection with Ariyari P.S.

                        Case No. 18 of 2019.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                                     Accordingly, the impugned order is set aside and

                        appeal is allowed.
</p>
<p id="p_8">

</p><p id="p_9">                                            (Prakash Chandra Jaiswal, J)
rohit/-
</p>
