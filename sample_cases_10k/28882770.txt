                       IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                      Criminal Revision No.1655 of 2010
                    =====================================================
                    Shani Kumar, son of Late Tribhuman Prasad Singh, resident of
                    Mohalla-Durga Asthan, Madhubani, Police Station-K. Hat, District-
                    Purnea.
                                                                        .... .... Petitioner.
                                                   Versus
                    1. The State of Bihar.
                    2. Sushil Kumar Gupta, son of Late Kusumlal Sah, resident of
                       Mohalla-Durga Asthan Madhubani, Police Station-K. Hat,
                       District-Purnea.
                                                                 .... .... Opposite parties.
                    =====================================================
                    CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE RAJENDRA KUMAR
                    MISHRA
                    ORAL ORDER</pre>
<p id="p_1">                                                 -------------
</p>
<p id="p_2">2   07-05-2013                 Heard.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                               This Criminal Revision is directed against the order dated

                  7.10.2010 passed in Criminal Revision No.129 of 2009 by the 2 nd

                  Additional Sessions Judge, Purnea, whereby the learned 2 nd

                  Additional Sessions Judge, Purnea, set aside the order dated

                  23.5.2009 passed in Case No.152M of 2009 by the Sub Divisional

                  Magistrate, Sadar, Purnea, initiating a proceeding under <a href="/doc/983382/" id="a_1">Section 133</a>

                  of the Code of Criminal Procedure against the opposite party no.2.

</p><p id="p_4">                               From perusal of the impugned order, apparently, I find no

                  illegality for interference with the same in revisional jurisdiction.

                  Accordingly, this Criminal Revision stands dismissed.


</p><p id="p_5">                                                        (Rajendra Kumar Mishra, J)
      P.S./-
</p>
