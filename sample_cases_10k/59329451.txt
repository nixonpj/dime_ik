<div class="doc_title">Syed Iqbal Tahir Geelani vs . State Of J&amp;K And Ors. on 23 April, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">Serial No. 22
  Suppl.
                          HIGH COURT OF JAMMU AND KASHMIR
                                    AT SRINAGAR

        IA No. 92/2018 in
        PIL No. 159/2002
                                                               Date of order:- 23.04.2019

        Syed Iqbal Tahir Geelani                 Vs.          State of J&amp;K and Ors.

        Coram:
              Hon'ble Mr Justice Ali Mohammad Magrey, Judge
              Hon'ble Mr Justice Tashi Rabstan, Judge
        Appearance:
        For the Petitioner(s)   :    Ms. Moksha Kazmi, Advocate
        For the Respondent(s)    :   Ms. Asifa Padroo, AAG</pre>



<p id="p_1">                Despite notice respondents have failed to file the objections.
                Applicant Secretary J&amp;K State Sports Council, is seeking permission for
        up-gradation/renovation of existing water sports Centre at Nehru Park, Dal
        Lake, Srinagar, on the grounds detailed out in the application with particular
        reference that the State Sports Council has got the water sports Centre,
        approved from the Government of India under PMDP at a cost of Rs. 3.00
        crores. In paragraph 3 of the application, it is stated that the Lakes and
        Waterways Development Authority, Srinagar, made a communication dated
        08.08.2018, intimated the Secretary as under:-
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">                      " Since the proposed site falls within 200 meters
                      from Dal Lake, this office cannot consider your
                      permission for up-gradation/renovation of existing
                      water sports Centre in the said area. You are
                      requested to approach Hon'ble Court in this
                      behalf."</blockquote>
<p id="p_2">                It is stated that the sports structure at Nehru Park, Dal Lake, Srinagar, got
        damaged in the devastating floods of September, 2014, for which the funds have
        been placed at the disposal of the concerned authorities, but the applicants are
            not in a position to carry out the up-gradation/renovation of existing water
           sports Centre in view of restraint orders passed by this Court. It is stated that
           renovation is required to carry out the sports activities smoothly and to provide
           better sports facilities to the players involved in water sports games. It is further
           undertaken that sports council shall not carry out any construction in the water
           body within the meaning and contemplation of the order of this Hon'ble Court
           dated 19.07.2002, but only upgrade/renovate the existing water sports.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                     Considering the nature of the relief prayed for in the application, having
           reference to seeking permission for renovation of existing water sports Centre at
           Nehru Park, Dal Lake, Srinagar on account of damages suffered due to
           devastating floods of September, 2014. We are of the considered view that the
           Jammu and Kashmir Lakes and Waterways Development Authority shall
           consider the grant of such permission, by allowing up-gradation/renovation of
           sports Centre at Nehru Park, Dal Lake, Srinagar, for the betterment of sports
           activities in the State.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                     MP No. 92/2018 is disposed of.
</p>
