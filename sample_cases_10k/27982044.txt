<div class="doc_title">Dhiraj Kumar vs The State Of Bihar on 11 May, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">      Patna High Court Cr.M isc. No.28227 of 2018 (2) dt.11-05-2018




                       IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                     Criminal Miscellaneous No.28227 of 2018
                          Arising Out of PS.Case No. -391 Year- 2018 Thana -SAHARSA District- SAHARSA
                   ======================================================
                   1. Dhiraj Kumar, Son of Late Rambilash Chaudhary, Resident of Village-
                   Bengha, Ward No. 4, P.S.- Saharsa, District- Saharsa.
                                                                            .... .... Petitioner/s
                                                    Versus
                   1. The State of Bihar.                          .... .... Opposite Party/s
                   ======================================================
                   Appearance :
                   For the Petitioner/s     : Mr. Ashok Kumar
                   For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr. Sri Ashok Kumar Singh 1
                   ======================================================
                   CORAM: HONOURABLE JUSTICE SMT. NILU AGRAWAL
                   ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   11-05-2018                     Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned

                   APP for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                   Petitioner seeks bail in connection with Saharsa Sadar

                   P.S. Case No. 391 of 2018, Special (Excise) Case No. 173 of 2018

                   for offences punishable under Section 37 (b) of the Bihar

                   Prohibition and <a href="/doc/110162683/" id="a_1">Excise Act</a>, 2016.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                  The prosecution case, as lodged by the police personnel

                   is that during patrolling duty, they apprehended two persons

                   including the petitioner in a drunken condition and with the help

                   of breath analyzer which confirmed that petitioner along with

                   another person had consumed liquor.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                  It has been submitted by the learned counsel for the

                   petitioner that he is innocent and has been falsely implicated in the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">            Patna High Court Cr.M isc. No.28227 of 2018 (2) dt.11-05-2018</span>




                        aforesaid case and is languishing in judicial custody since

                        01.04.2018.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                                       However, learned APP for the State opposes the prayer

                        for bail stating therein that the petitioner does not have a clean

                        antecedent and is involved in one more case of similar nature.

</p><p id="p_6">                                          Considering the facts and circumstances and the

                        materials on record, let petitioner, above named, be enlarged on

                        bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs.10,000/-(Ten Thousand) with

                        two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of learned

                        Special Judge Excise Act, Saharsa, in connection with Saharsa

                        Sadar P.S. Case No. 391 of 2018, Special (Excise) Case No. 173

                        of 2018.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><p id="p_8">                                                         (Nilu Agrawal, J)
sushma/-
</p><pre id="pre_2">  U           T
