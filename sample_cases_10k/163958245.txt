<div class="doc_title">Kranti Mandal &amp; Anr vs The State Of Bihar on 12 July, 2010</div><pre id="pre_1">                   IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                   Cr.Misc. No.21490 of 2010
                  1.</pre><p id="p_1"> KRANTI MANDAL, S/o Upendra Mandal
</p><p id="p_2">                  2. Santosh Mandal, S/o Late Rampravesh Mandal
                     Both residents of Village - Dakra Satkhajuria, P.S.-Naya Ram
                     Nagar(Safiabad), District- Munger.
</p><p id="p_3">                                                                        ........ Petitioners.
</p><p id="p_4">                                                 Versus
                     THE STATE OF BIHAR                       ............. Opposite Party.

</p><p id="p_5">2.   12-07-2010               Both the petitioners are apprehending their arrest in a case

                  registered under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_1">sections 302</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_2">34</a> IPC.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                              It is alleged against the petitioners and others that they

                  assaulted the victim as well as his brother by Lathi.

</p><p id="p_7">                              From the impugned order, it appears that the witnesses

                  have deposed that petitioners were not present at the place of

                  occurrence. Further, post-mortem as well as inquest reports suggest

                  that there was no external injury on the person of the victim as it is

                  apparent from the impugned order itself.           Subsequently, police

                  submitted final form treating the case against petitioners as false and a

                  statement to that effect has been made in the petition, but subsequently

                  learned Chief Judicial Magistrate, Munger vide order dated 6.11.2009

                  took cognizance under <a href="/doc/409589/" id="a_3">section 304</a> IPC against the petitioners also.

                  The said order is brought on record as Annexure-2, but it is not a

                  complete order, later on certified copy of the order has been filed by

                  the learned counsel for the petitioners. Let it be kept on record.

</p><p id="p_8">                              Learned counsel for the state submits that petitioners were

                  named in the F.I.R. and now the cognizance has been taken against

                  them.
</p>
<p id="p_9">                              Considering the fact that no injury has been found either
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                               -2-</span>




        in the post-mortem or in the inquest reports and the police submitted

        final form against the petitioners, let petitioners above named are

        directed to be released on anticipatory bail in the event of their arrest

        and/or surrender before the court below within a period of 12 weeks

        from today, on furnishing bail bonds of Rs. 10,000/-(ten thousand)

        with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of the

        learned Chief Judicial Magistrate, Munger in connection with Naya

        Ram Nagar P.S. Case No. 23/2009.
</p>
<p id="p_10">


</p><pre id="pre_2">Sujit                                               (Dinesh Kumar Singh, J.)
 </pre>
