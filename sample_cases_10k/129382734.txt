                                                          ORDER

<p id="p_1">                            This Writ Petition has been filed to issue a Writ of Mandamus, directing

                      the respondents to reinstate the petitioner in the post of Hospital Worker with all

                      service and monetary benefits in the light of the order passed by this Court in

                      W.A(MD) No.743 of 2015 dated 22.04.2016 complied by the 1 st respondent in

                      G.O (Ms) No.325 Health and Family Welfare (C1) Department dated 11.09.2017

                      within the time limit that may be stipulated by this Court by considering the

                      representation dated 25.07.2019.
</p>
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                      2/6</span>


<p id="p_2">http://www.judis.nic.in
                                                                                    W.P.No.22544 of 2019

</p><p id="p_3">                                 2. Heard the learned counsel for the petitioner and the learned

                      Government Advocate appearing for R1, R3 to R6 and the learned Special

                      Government Pleader for R2. By consent of both sides, the writ petition is taken

                      up for final disposal at the admission stage itself.
</p>



<p id="p_4">                               3.The petitioner was appointed in the post of Hospital Worker and the

                      wages for the post of Hospital Worker was fixed as Rs.990/- per month which

                      was sanctioned by the respondents and the same was encashed and was paid

                      through the society till 2006 and the petitioner continued in the outsource

                      employment till 2013, and his service was not regularised. Hence the

                      petitioner made representation for regularisation of service on 25.07.2019.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                      Till date, the petitioner's' representation was not considered. Hence the

                      present writ petition is filed.
</p>


<p id="p_6">                               4.The learned counsel for the petitioner submitted that it would suffice

                      if a direction is passed to the respondents to consider the representation of

                      the petitioner dated 25.07.2019, within a reasonable time as fixed by this

                      Court.
</p>



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                      3/6</span>


<p id="p_7">http://www.judis.nic.in
                                                                                     W.P.No.22544 of 2019

</p><p id="p_8">                               5.The learned counsel appearing for the respondents accede to the

                      submission made by the learned counsel for the petitioner and submitted that

                      if any such representation was received by the respondents, the same will be

                      considered on merits and in accordance with law.
</p>



<p id="p_9">                               6.Thus, without going into the merits of the case, this Court is inclined

                      to direct the respondents to consider the representation of the         petitioner

                      dated 25.07.2019 and pass orders on merits and in accordance with law within

                      a period of twelve weeks from the date of receipt of copy of this order.
</p>


<p id="p_10">                               7.With the above direction, the writ petition stands disposed of. No

                      costs.
</p>
