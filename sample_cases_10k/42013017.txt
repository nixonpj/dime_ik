THIS WRIT PETITION FILED UNDER ARTICLES 226 &amp; 227 OF

ENDORSEMENT DT.9.10.12 ISSUED BY THE ASST. REVENUE
OFFICER, DOMLUR, BBMP PRODUCED AT ANN-A &amp;
FURTHER DIRECT THE R1 TO CHANGE KHATHA IN
RESPECT OF THE PROPERTY NO.124 DOMLUR SECOND
STAGE, SECOND PHASE, BANGALORE IN FAVOUR OF THE
PETITIONER.

This petition coming on for preliminary hearing 'B' group this
day, the court made the following:

                            ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">      In this writ petition the petitioner has prayed for a writ in

the nature of certiorari to quash the endorsement dated

09.10.2012, Annexure-A issued by the first respondent.

</p><p id="p_2">      2.    Petitioner contends that one Smt.N.Pushpavathi was

the owner of property bearing No.124, Domlur Second Stage,
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                              3</span>




Second Phase, Bangalore, Ward No.72/112 having acquired the

same under a registered sale deed dated 05.07.2011 from the

Bangalore Development Authority. The owner Smt.Pushpavathi

in turn sold the property in question in favuor of the petitioner

under a registered sale deed dated 23.07.2012. On the strength of

this sale deed the petitioner approached the first respondent for

transfer of khatha to his name. Under the impugned endorsement

at Annexure-A dated 09.10.2012, first respondent stated that they

will consider the claim of petitioner after disposal of RFA

No.1791/2010 pending on the file of this court. Aggrieved by the

impugned endorsement the petitioner is before this court.

</p><p id="p_3">      3.      Heard arguments on both the side and perused the

entire writ papers.
</p>
<p id="p_4">      4.     In the normal course, on the strength of registered

sale deed the petitioner is entitled for transfer of khatha to his

name. But in this case, the second respondent filed a civil suit

against the original owner Pushpavathy in O.S.No.9165/2001 for
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                               4</span>




partition and the same came to be dismissed. Now the second

respondent is before this court in RFA No.1791/2010 and the

property in question is the subject matter of the appeal. This

court recorded the undertaking given by the petitioner that he

will not alienate the property in question till the disposal of the

appeal. In the circumstances, in the interest of both the parties, it

will be just and appropriate to wait till the disposal of RFA

No.1791/2010. After disposal of RFA No.1791/2010 the petitioner

is entitled to approach the first respondent for necessary orders.

In the circumstances, I find no justifiable ground to interfere with

the impugned endorsement.         Accordingly the writ petition is

hereby disposed of.
</p>
<p id="p_5">      5.     Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that in

the property in question there is a building and there is no power

supply and water supply. In the absence of khatha, petitioner is

not able to secure the essential amenities like water and power to

the building in question. If that is so, liberty is reserved to move
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                          5</span>




necessary application in RFA No.1791/2010 for appropriate

orders.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                      Sd/-
</p><p id="p_7">                                    JUDGE.
</p>
