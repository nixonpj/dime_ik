IN THE HIGH COURT OF KARNATAKA AT BANGALORE

     DATED THIS THE 13TH DAY OF AUGUST 2012

                        BEFORE

     THE HON'BLE MR.JUSTICE V.JAGANNATHAN

          CRIMINAL PETITION NO.1813/2012

BETWEEN:

1.     Sri. Manjunatha,
       Son of M.N. Nagaiah,
       Aged about 27 years,
       Residing at no.28,
       1st Main Road, Near Bajanemanne,
       Nelagadaranahalli, Nagasandra Post,
       Bangalore-560073.

2.     Sri. G. Muniraju,
       Son of Gangalakshmaiah,
       Aged about 26 years,
       Residing at Ramapalya Village,
       Dasanapura Hobli,
       Bangalore North Taluk.

3.     Sri. Murali R.
       Son of Rangegowda,
       Aged about 27 years,
       Residing at Nagarur Village,
       Dasanapura Hobli,
       Bangalore North Taluk.
                                          ...PETITIONERS
(By Sri. Mylaraiah, Adv.)

AND:

State of Karnataka
By Madayakanahalli Police.              ...RESPONDENT

(By Sri. Satish R Giriji, HCGP)
                             2




      This Crl.P. is filed under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> of Cr.P.C.
praying to enlarge the petitioners on bail in the event
of   their   arrest     in  Crime    Mo.150/2012     of
Madayakanahalli Police Station, for the offence
p/u/Ss. 420, 468, 403, 419, 423, 471, r/w 34 of <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_2">IPC</a>.

     This Crl.P. coming on for orders this day, the
Court made the following:-

                         ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">       Heard learned counsel for the petitioners and

learned HCGP for the respondent-State in respect of

anticipatory bail sought by the petitioners.


</p><p id="p_2">       2. Having regard to the nature of the complaint

allegations and offences alleged being under <a href="/doc/1099980/" id="a_3">Sections

420</a>, <a href="/doc/1599077/" id="a_4">468</a>, <a href="/doc/803870/" id="a_5">403</a>, <a href="/doc/1849163/" id="a_6">419</a>, <a href="/doc/1487146/" id="a_7">423</a>, <a href="/doc/1590825/" id="a_8">471</a> r/w 34 of <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_9">IPC</a>, petitioners

are directed to surrender before the trial court and

seek regular bail and application if filed in that regard,

the same shall be considered by the trial court within

four days.
</p>
<p id="p_3">
</p><p id="p_4">       3. Petition stands disposed of in the above terms.



</p><p id="p_5">                                     Sd/-
</p><p id="p_6">                                    JUDGE.
</p>
<p id="p_7">Srl.
