kc. 162                        W.P.13603(W) of 2010

             Mr. Amal Baran Chatterjee
             Mr. S. K. Mukherjee ....for the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_2">             Mr. Saikat Banerjee      ...for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                     Leave is granted to the petitioner to correct the cause title of the petition.
                     Having regard to the nature of controversy raised herein, I find no reason
             to keep the writ petition pending.
</p><p id="p_4">                     The dispute relates to non-recognition of a Madrasah as Minority
             Educational Institution.     It appears that the Director and Ex-officio Joint
             Secretary of the Minority Affairs and Madrasah Education Department, being the
             competent authority, has sought for particulars from the District Magistrate,
             Nadia by memo dated 17.6.2009. The petitioner is aggrieved because particulars
             as sought for have not been furnished.
</p><p id="p_5">                     Accordingly, this writ petition stands disposed of with a direction upon
             the District Magistrate, Nadia to furnish to the said competent authority the
             particulars as sought for by him as early as possible but not later than four weeks
             from date of receipt of a copy of this order. Upon receipt of the particulars, the
             competent authority shall proceed to consider the claim for grant of recognition
             within a further period of eight weeks.
</p><p id="p_6">                     If recognition, as prayed for, is not granted, a reasoned order shall be
             passed and communicated to the petitioner.
</p><p id="p_7">                     There shall be no order for costs.
</p><p id="p_8">                     Urgent xerox certified copy of this order, if applied for, be supplied to the
             parties, as early as possible.
</p>

<p id="p_9">                                                          (DIPANKAR DATTA,J.)
