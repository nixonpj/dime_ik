<div class="doc_title">Smt. Sika Bharti vs The State (Nct Of Delhi) &amp; Another on 8 April, 2013</div><div class="doc_author">Author: Mr. Sumit Dass</div>

<pre id="pre_1">   IN THE COURT OF SH. SUMIT DASS,  ADMINISTRATIVE CIVIL 
        JUDGE (NORTH WEST)  ROHINI COURTS,  DELHI 

Petition No.                : SCC 32/12.

Date of Institution         : 05.03.2012.

Date of arguments           : 05.03.2013.

Date of order               : 08.04.2013.


In the matter of:­ 

   1. Smt. Sika Bharti

      W/o Late Ashok Kumar

   2. Master Mayank

      S/o Late Ashok Kumar

      (Petitioner no. 2 is minor and 

      is represented through his 

      mother, the Natural Guardian)

      Both R/o A­10, Hanuman Road

      Adarsh Nagar, Delhi­110033                                   ....Petitioners

                            Versus

   1. The State (NCT of Delhi) &amp; Another                           ....Respondents



J U D G M E N T:
</pre>

<p id="p_1">               Vide this judgment, I shall dispose off the petition filed on behalf of 

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">SCC No. 32/12.                           1</span>
 the   petitioners   under   <a href="/doc/105862/" id="a_1">section   372</a>   of   the   Indian   Succession   Act,   1925   for 

obtaining  succession  certificate  in respect  of debts, securities  etc of Late Sh. 

Ashok Kumar.
</p>
<p id="p_2">               The relevant facts for the disposal of the present petition are that 

Late   Sh.   Ashok   Kumar,   the   husband   of   the   petitioner   no.   1   and   father   of 

petitioner no. 2 expired on 19.01.2012.  It is further stated that the deceased had 

left behind  only petitioners  as their legal  heirs.   It is further stated that Smt. 

Geeta Rani who is the sister in law of the petitioner by taking undue advantage 

of mental/ medical problems of deceased had got herself nominated in respect of 

debts/   securities   of   the   deceased.     It   is   further   alleged   that   there   is   no 

impediment in the grant of succession certificate in favour of the petitioners in 

respect   of   in   FDR   account   no.   64039311891  maintained   with  State   Bank  of 

Mysore   Pitampura   Delhi,   FDR   account   no.   1590   maintained   with   Karnataka 

Bank Ltd R K Puram New Delhi, LIC policy no. 121905887 with Life Insurance 

Corporation   of   India   Limited   and   dues   lying   with   Northern   Zone   Railway 

Employees CTC Society Ltd. vide reg. no. HDQ37898.



</p><p id="p_3">2.             General   public   had   been   served   through   publication   in   daily 

newspaper   'Rashtriya   Sahara'   dated   21.03.2012   but   none   had   appeared   from 

general public to oppose or contest the present petition.  



</p><p id="p_4">3.             Petitioner had examined herself as PW1 deposing on the lines of 

facts as alleged in the petition and in support of his oral testimony relied upon 

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">SCC No. 32/12.                                2</span>
 documents i.e. death certificate Ex. PW1/A, affidavit in respect of her date of 

birth  Ex. PW1/B,  affidavit  in respect  of date of birth of Master  Mayank Ex. 

PW1/C, copy of her identity card Ex. PW1/D, copy of her ESI Card Ex. PW1/E, 

date of birth  record  of Master Mayank Ex. PW/F,  Legal  notice sent to State 

Bank   of   Mysore   Ex.   PW1/G,   legal   notice   sent   to   Karnataka   Bank   Ltd.   Ex. 

PW1/H,  legal notice  sent  to LIC Ex. PW1/I,  legal  notice  sent  to NZRECTC 

Society Ltd. Ex. PW1/J, postal receipts of all the four legal notices Ex. PW1/K, 

publication report Ex. PW1/L, photograph showing the last rites Ex. PW1/M, 

LIC policy no. 121905887 Ex. PW1/N, copy of FIR no. 399/2006 Ex. PW1/O, 

statement of petitioner and deceased Ex. PW1/P and order of Hon'ble court of 

Ms. Seema Maini, Ld. ADJ Ex. PW1/Q.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                      Objections   were   filed   by   the   respondent   Smt.   Geeta   and 

thereafter, she had settled with the petitioner and had given her no objections for 

the issuance of succession certificate in favour of the petitioner.

</p><p id="p_6">              Witness, Sh. Pushkal Kumar Pandey, Deputy Manager from State 

Bank of Mysore, Pitampura, Delhi was examined who proved on record that an 

amount of Rs 28,176/­ is lying in SB Account no. 64039311891, an amount of 

Rs 18,769/­ is lying against deposit no. 64039634153 and a locker no 6 is also 

lying with them in the name of the deceased Ashok Kumar.

</p><p id="p_7">              Witness, Sh. Poorna Prajna M, Assistant Manager, Karnataka Bank 

Ltd., R K Puram, Delhi was examined who proved on record that an amount of 

Rs 58,703/­ is lying against deposit receipt no. 5501500200349501 dt 29.05.10 

and an amount of Rs 1,506.50p is lying in account no. 5502500100159001 with 

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">SCC No. 32/12.                             3</span>
 them in the name of the deceased Ashok Kumar.
</p>
<p id="p_8">               Witness,   Sh.   Ravi   Kumar,   Assistant   from   Life   Insurance 

Corporation of India Limited, Wazirpur, Delhi was examined who proved on 

record that an amount of Rs 1,00,000/­ is due against LIC policy no. 121905887 

in the name of Sh. Ashok Kumar.
</p>
<p id="p_9">               Witness,   Sh.   D   D   Gaur,   Branch   Manager   from   Northern   Zone 

Railway Co­operative Thrift and Credit Society Limited, Delhi was examined 

who proved on record that an amount of Rs 76,187/­ is lying with the society in 

the shape of Money &amp; Compulsory deposit including LIC claim of the deceased 

Ashok Kumar having Membership name Ashok Kumar­100.

</p><p id="p_10">               Upon   the   request   of   the   petitioner,   a   Local   Commissioner   was 

appointed in the matter to ascertain the list the articles lying in the locker no. 6 

with   State Bank of Mysore, Pitampura, Delhi in the name of deceased Ashok 

Kumar but no other movable property of the deceased was found lying therein 

and only certain papers were found.
</p>
<p id="p_11">

</p><p id="p_12">4.             Heard.
</p>
<p id="p_13">

</p><p id="p_14">5.             During the course of the submissions, the petitioner stated that ½ 

share be kept as a FDR in the name of Master Mayank which would be maturing 

once he attains the age of majority and the same shall also preserve his interests.

</p><p id="p_15">               Accordingly,   in   my   opinion,   in   view   of   the   submission   at   bar 

Succession   Certificate   being   issued   to   the   petitioner   is   conditioned   with   the 

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">SCC No. 32/12.                               4</span>
 undertaking of the petitioner herself that she shall get an FDR prepared of the ½ 

amount in the name of Master Mayank which would be maturing once he attains 

the age of majority.  The petitioner is directed to comply with the same within 

one month of once she gets the money from the bank.  

</p><p id="p_16">             Considering the above stated facts, this Court is of the opinion that 

prima facie there is no impediment in grant of Succession Certificate in favour 

of petitioner Smt. Sika Bharti in respect of the following:

</p><pre id="pre_2">         S. No.                 Particulars                      Amount
            1        SB Account no. 64039311891               Rs 28,176/­

                   and

</pre><p id="p_17">                   Deposit   receipt   no.   64039634153  Rs 18,769/­

                   with   State   Bank   of   Mysore, 

                   Pitampura, Delhi 
            2      Deposit           receipt          no.  Rs 58,703/­

                   5501500200349501   dt   29.05.10 

                   and                                        Rs 1,506.50p

                   Account   no.   5502500100159001 

                   with   Karnataka   Bank   Ltd.,   R   K 

                   Puram, Delhi
            3      LIC   policy   no.   121905887   with  Rs   1,00,000/­ 

                   Life   Insurance   Corporation   of  along   with 

                   India Limited, Wazirpur, Delhi             vested bonus
            4      Northern   Zone   Railway   Co­  Rs 76,187/­


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_5">SCC No. 32/12.                         5</span>
                     operative Thrift and Credit Society 

                    Limited, Delhi against membership 

                    name Ashok kumar­100


             Accordingly,   Succession   Certificate   is   ordered   to   be   issued   in 

favour of petitioner Smt. Sika Bharti, on furnishing of an Indemnity Bond of the 

same value with one surety of like amount within one month.  File be consigned 

to Record Room.
</p>

</pre><p id="p_19">              Witness,   Sh.   D   D   Gaur,   Branch   Manager   from   Northern   Zone 

Railway  Co­operative Thrift  and Credit  Society ltd. is present.   He has been 

examined and is discharged.
</p>
<p id="p_20">              Witness, Sh. Ravi Kumar, Assistant from LLIC, Wazirpur, Delhi is 

present.  He has been examined and is discharged.

</p><p id="p_21">              Petitioner has also been examined and is discharged.

              Final arguments have been heard.
</p>
<p id="p_22">              Vide separate judgment of even date, petition filed u/s 372 of the 

<a href="/doc/1450343/" id="a_2">Indian Succession Act</a>, 1925 on behalf of the petitioner is allowed and disposed 

off.
</p>
<p id="p_23">              File be consigned to Record Room.
</p>
