<div class="doc_title">State Of Haryana vs Narender on 19 March, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">       IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA
                     AT CHANDIGARH

                                CRA D-1022-DBA of 2004
                                Date of Decision: March 19, 2018

State of Haryana                                            ..... Appellant
                                   Versus
Narender                                                    ...... Respondent

CORAM : HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE T.P.S.MANN
        HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE ANIL KSHETARPAL

                        ***
Present :    Mr. Kapil Aggarwal, Addl. A.G., Haryana.

                          ***
T.P.S.MANN, J. (Oral)
</pre>
<p id="p_1">             The State had filed the present appeal for challenging
the judgment dated 26.5.2004 passed by the Additional Sessions
Judge (Adhoc) Jhajjar, whereby, the sole accused/respondent,
namely, Narender, stood acquitted of the charges under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_1">Sections
302</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_2">34</a> IPC and <a href="/doc/73862/" id="a_3">Section 25</a> of the Arms Act.
</p><p id="p_2">             As     per     the          Office   report,      the    acquitted
accused/respondent expired on 12.11.2005. In this regard, death
certificate has been placed on record.
</p><p id="p_3">             It   being   an      appeal      against   acquittal     and     the
accused/respondent has died during its pendency, the appeal
abates and, accordingly, disposed of in terms of <a href="/doc/1493299/" id="a_4">Section 394</a>
Cr.P.C.</p><pre id="pre_2">


                                                  ( T.P.S. MANN )
                                                        JUDGE


March 19, 2018                                    (ANIL KSHETARPAL)
amit rana                                              JUDGE
