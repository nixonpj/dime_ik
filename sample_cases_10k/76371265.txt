<div class="doc_title">Ram Sagar Sahu &amp; Ors vs State Of Bihar on 25 August, 2009</div><pre id="pre_1">                   IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                Cr.Misc. No.13158 of 2009
                                RAM SAGAR SAHU &amp; ORS
                                             Versus
                                      STATE OF BIHAR
                                           -----------
</pre>
<p id="p_1">3.   25.08.2009            Heard the counsel for the petitioners and the counsel

                  appearing for the state.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                           Petitioner Nos. 1 and 3 are; father-in-law and mother-

                  in-law of the complainant respectively. Petitioner No. 2 is

                  brother of petitioner No. 1, petitioner No. 4 is daughter,

                  petitioner No. 5 is married daughter, petitioner No. 6 is husband

                  of petitioner No. 5 and petitioner Nos. 7, 8 &amp; 9 are relatives of

                  petitioner No. 6.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                           Allegation against all these petitioners is that they tried

                  to persuade husband of the complainant for solemnizing

                  second marriage. They also assaulted the complainant for

                  obstructing that marriage. However, finally, the second marriage

                  was solemnized with help of the petitioners.

</p><p id="p_4">                           Counsel for the petitioners submits that petitioner Nos.

                  2, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8 &amp; 9 cannot be held directly responsible for the

                  second marriage or for assault and torture.

</p><p id="p_5">                           Considering the fact, prayer for grant of anticipatory

                  bail on behalf of petitioners, Ram Sagar Sahu and Jilebiya Devi

                  is rejected. So far petitioners, namely, Raja Ram Sahu @ Raja

                  Ram Sah, Sudha Kumari, Kanchan Devi, Dharmendra Sahu,

                  Pankaj Sahu,        Neelam Devi and Jai Narayan Sahu are

                  concerned, their prayer for grant of anticipatory bail is allowed.
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                           -2-</span>




      In the event of arrest or surrender within four weeks from today,

      they will be released on bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs

      10,000/- (ten thousand) each with two sureties of the like

      amount each in connection with Complaint Case No. 405C of

      2008 to the satisfaction of Sub Divisional Judicial Magistrate,

      Khagaria.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><pre id="pre_2">akv                         (Mridula Mishra, J.)
