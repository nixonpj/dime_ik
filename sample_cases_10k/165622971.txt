<div class="doc_title">Bscpl Infrastructure Limited vs State Of Gujarat &amp; 4 on 28 March, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">           C/SCA/13170/2013                                  ORDER




           IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

              SPECIAL CIVIL APPLICATION NO. 13170 of 2013

================================================================
              BSCPL INFRASTRUCTURE LIMITED....Petitioner(s)
                               Versus
                 STATE OF GUJARAT &amp; 4....Respondent(s)
================================================================
Appearance:
MR SN SOPARKAR, SR COUNSEL WITH MR AKSHAY A VAKIL, ADVOCATE
for the Petitioner(s) No. 1
MR JAIMIN GANDHI, AGP for the Respondent(s) No. 1 - 5
================================================================

          CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE AKIL KURESHI
                 and
                 HONOURABLE MS JUSTICE SONIA GOKANI

                              Date : 28/03/2014


                               ORAL ORDER</pre>
<p id="p_1">    (PER : HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE AKIL KURESHI)


        The   petitioner   has   challenged   an   order   dated   10.9.2013 
passed   by   the   Commercial   Tax   Officer,   Dahod,   cancelling   the 
permission   for   composition   of   tax   on   a   works   contract 
undertaken  by the petitioner  for and on behalf  of the National 
Highway authority. This order was passed in terms of Rule 28(8) 
of the Gujarat  Value  Added Tax Rules,  2006  read with  section 
14A of Gujarat Value Added Tax Act, 2003. 
</p>

<p id="p_2">        The   petitioner   had   undertaken   works   contract   for 
constructing   four­lane   highway   for   which   the   permission   for 
composition of tax was granted. It appears that in execution of 
such  works  contract,  the petitioner  used  certain  raw materials 


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                  Page 1 of 3</span>
           C/SCA/13170/2013                                      ORDER




from its own sources namely, mines leased by the petitioner. On 
such raw materials, petitioner was not required to pay any tax. 
The State authorities believed that in  terms of rule 28(8)(a)(vi­a)
(2),   since   the   dealer   had   used   taxable   goods   in   execution   of 
works contract, but such goods had not borne the tax payable 
under the Act, one of the vital conditions for composition of tax 
was breached. The said provision reads as under :
</p>

<blockquote id="blockquote_1">       "(2) If   such   dealer   uses   any   taxable   goods   in   the 
       execution of works contract covered under the permission 
       to pay the lump sum tax, such goods ought to have borne 
       the tax payable under the Act."
</blockquote>

<p id="p_3">      Case   of   the   petitioner   is   that   the   requirement   of   the 
provision   is   that   the   goods   which   are   otherwise   taxable   must 
have borne  the tax payable.  Since  the goods  were procured  by 
the petitioner from its own leased mines, there was no question 
of payment of any tax and in that sense such transaction cannot 
be said to be taxable transaction and the goods were therefore, 
not taxable. The alternative contention of the petitioner is that  if 
such   interpretation   is   not   accepted,   said   provision   will   be 
vulnerable to challenge under <a href="/doc/367586/" id="a_1">Article 14</a> read with <a href="/doc/935769/" id="a_2">article 19(1)(g)</a> 
of the  Constitution. The petitioner has therefore, challenged the 
vires of the said rule also. The issue requires consideration.
</p>

<p id="p_4">      Hence,   Rule.     Learned   AGP   Shri   Jaimin   Gandhi   waived 
service of rule.
</p>

<p id="p_5">      We   notice   that   by   virtue   of   impugned   order,   sizeable   tax 
demands have arisen against the petitioner for the period before 
and   after   passing   of   the   order.   Considering   the   facts   and 
circumstances  of the  case,  we require  the petitioner  to deposit 


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                   Page 2 of 3</span>
           C/SCA/13170/2013                                   ORDER




25%   of   the   total   tax   dues   including   interest   but   excluding 
penalty latest by 30.6.2014 and also give immovable security of 
another   25%.   On   such   condition,   there   shall   be   stay   against 
recovery of further dues.
</p>

<p id="p_6">         To be placed for hearing on 11.7.2014



                                                        (AKIL KURESHI, J.)




                                                   (MS SONIA GOKANI, J.)
