                  IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

                     SPECIAL CIVIL APPLICATION NO. 9489 of 2017


                                           With
                     SPECIAL CIVIL APPLICATION NO. 9512 of 2017
         ==========================================================
                         RAJESH UMEDBHAI MACHHI....Petitioner(s)
                                       Versus
                         STATE OF GUJARAT &amp; 2....Respondent(s)
         ==========================================================
         Appearance:
         MS SUBHADRA G PATEL, ADVOCATE for the Petitioner(s) No. 1
         ADVANCE COPY SERVED TO GP/PP for the Respondent(s) No. 1
         ==========================================================

          CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE S.H.VORA

                                    Date : 04/05/2017


                                     ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">         1.   By way of the present petitions under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the
         Constitution of India, the respective petitioners apprehend
         their detention under the provisions of Gujarat Prevention of
         Anti Social Activities Act, 1985 (for short, the PASA Act) on
         account of registration of FIR being C.R. No. III 282/2017
         registered with Bapod Police Station, Vadodara.
</p>

<p id="p_2">         2.   According to the petitioners, except registration of the
         aforementioned FIR, no other material is available with the
         competent authority to detain the petitioners under the
         provisions of the PASA Act.
</p>

<p id="p_3">         3.   Having considered the allegations made in the FIR, it

HC-NIC                                Page 1 of 2      Created On Mon Aug 21 06:43:06 IST 2017
                       C/SCA/9489/2017                                          ORDER




         appears that the petitioners are not named in the FIR and
         therefore, the apprehension of the respective petitioners is
         well-founded. Therefore, liberty of the respective petitioners
         is required to be protected.
</p>

<p id="p_4">         4.        Hence, rule to the respondents, returnable on 31.7.2017.
         Learned A.G.P. Mr. Janak Raval waives service of rule for the
         respondent State. Meanwhile, the detaining authority is
         directed not to detain the respective petitioners on account of
         registration of the offence/s as mentioned herein above till
         returnable date. The learned A.G.P. is also directed to place
         on record the detention order, if any, passed against the
         respective petitioners for Courts perusal on returnable date.
</p>

<p id="p_5">         5.        It is made clear that the petitioners are protected in
         respect of the only FIR as mentioned herein above and the
         competent authority is at liberty to take suitable action
         against the petitioners in respect of other offences, if any,
         registered and incriminating materials.
</p>

<p id="p_6">                   Direct service is permitted.
</p>
