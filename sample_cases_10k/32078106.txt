<div class="doc_title">Shyam Narayan Dubey And Anr vs Water Resources Departement on 13 November, 2013</div><pre id="pre_1">                 IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                                 W.P.(S). No.93 of 2013
              Shyam Narayan Dubey &amp; Anr.                .......... Petitioners.
                                         ­Versus­
              The State of Jharkhand &amp; Ors.             ......... Respondents.
                                           ­­­­­­
            CORAM : HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE NARENDRA NATH TIWARI
                                           ­­­­­­
              For the Petitioner :         M/s. Rajiv Ranjan &amp; 
                                           Amit Kumar Tiwari, Advocates.
              For the State        :       Mr. A. Allam, Sr. S.C.II
                                           ­­­­­­
4/13.11.2013</pre><p id="p_1">:        The   petitioners   are   aggrieved   by   reduction   of   their   pay 
              scale   by   the   office   order   issued   by   Memo   No.1376   dated   2nd 
              March,   2013   under   the   signature   of   Deputy   Secretary,   Water 
              Resources   Department,   Government   of   Jharkhand 
              (Annexure­10). 
</p><p id="p_2">                      The petitioners are Research Assistants in Water Resources 
              Department. On completion of 12/24 years of service, they were 
              given the benefits of first and second ACP under Assured Career 
              Progression Scheme, by office order contained in Memo No.4787 
              dated 31st December, 2005 (Annexure­3). Accordingly, they were 
              placed in the pay scale of Rs.6500­200­10500/­ after giving the 
              benefit of first ACP and in the pay scale of Rs.8000­275­13500/­ 
              after   second   ACP.   The   petitioners'   pay   was   fixed   accordingly 
              and they had been getting pay on the said pay scale. Suddenly, 
              by Memo   No.1376  dated  2nd  March, 2013, their  pay scales  are 
              sought to be reduced from from Rs.6500­10500/­ to Rs.5500­9000/­ 
              and from Rs.8000­13500/­ to 6500­10500/­ as benefits of first and 
              second ACP, respectively.
</p><p id="p_3">                      The   grievance   of   the   petitioners   is   that   the   order   of 
              reduction of pay has been passed without giving any notice and 
              opportunity of hearing or representation to them and the same is 
              wholly arbitrary, illegal and violative of principle of natural justice. 
</p><p id="p_4">                      The   respondents   have   contested   the   petitioners'   claim, 
              stating,  inter  alia, that though the petitioners were rightly given 
              the   benefits   of   first   and   second   ACP,   their   pay   scale   was 
              inadvertently   fixed   in   much   higher   pay   band   that   what   were 
              admissible   to   them.   By   the   impugned   order   dated   2nd  March, 
              2013, the order dated 31st December, 2005 (Annexure­3) has 
                                         ­2­
          been  modified, giving them the proper pay scales admissible on 
          the basis of first and second ACPs.
</p><p id="p_5">                 Mr. A. Allam, learned Senior S.C.II, appearing on behalf of 
          the respondents, submitted that the order dated 2nd March, 2013 
          has been issued by the Department after detection of the said 
          error   while   considering   the   matter   of   ACP   of   one   Ajay   Kumar 
          Mukherjee, who is similarly situated. He further submitted that the 
          order does not reduce the petitioners' pay scale rather the order 
          prescribes   the   proportionate   pay   scale   admissible   to   the 
          petitioners. There is no illegality or arbitrariness in the order. 
</p><p id="p_6">                 I   have   heard   learned   counsel   for   the   parties   and 
          considered the facts and materials on record. It is an admitted 
          fact that by order dated 31st  December, 2005 (Annexure­3), the 
          petitioners were given the benefits of first and second ACP and 
          their   pay   scales   were   fixed   in   the   next   higher   scales   of 
          Rs.6500­10500/­   and   Rs.8000­13500/­,   respectively.   By   the 
          impugned order dated 2nd  March, 2013 (Annexure­10) the pay 
          scales   of   the   petitioners   have   been   substantially   reduced   to 
          Rs.5500­9000/­   and   Rs.6500­10500/­,   respectively,   without   giving 
          any   notice   or   opportunity   of   hearing   or   representation   to   the 
          petitioners before such reduction of pay for whatever reason. 
</p><p id="p_7">                 Reduction of pay substantially certainly affects the person 
          adversely   and   any   such   order   cannot   be   passed   without 
          complying with the basic requirements of the principle of natural 
          justice.               
</p><p id="p_8">                 For the reasons aforesaid, the impugned order issued by 
          Memo   No.1376   dated   2nd  March,   2013   (Annexure­10)   cannot 
          sustain and is hereby quashed. 
</p><p id="p_9">                 This writ petition is allowed. 
</p> 


<p id="p_10">                                                (Narendra Nath Tiwari, J.)

Sanjay/
