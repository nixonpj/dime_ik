Date

ORAL
ORDER</pre> 

 


 


 


<p id="p_1">1.	The
present Special Criminal Application under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the
Constitution of India has been preferred by the petitioner to quash
and set aside the impugned order dated 06/06/2007 passed by
respondent no. 1 in Externment Appeal  No. 41/2007 as well as the
order dated 23/03/2007 passed by respondent no. 2.
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_2">2.	Having
heard the learned advocates appearing on behalf of the respective
parties, it appears that initially respondent no. 2 passed an order
dated 23/03/2007 under Section 56 of the Bombay Police Act to extern
the petitioner for a period of two years, which came to be modified
by the learned appellate authority by impugned order dated 06/06/2007
and instead of two years the period of externment has been reduced to
six months. 
</p>
 


 


 


<p id="p_3">3.	This
petition is of the year 2007 and there was no interim relief granted
at the time of admission and, therefore, the period of externment
pursuant to the impugned order passed by the learned appellate
authority is over and, therefore, the present petition has become
infructuous.  Under the circumstances, the present petition is
dismissed as having become infructuous.  Rule is discharged.  
</p>
 


 


 


 


 


<p id="p_4">								(M.R.
</p><p id="p_5">SHAH, J.)
