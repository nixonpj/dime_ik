<div class="doc_title">Kanchan Bai vs State Of Rajasthan And Ors on 17 January, 2013</div><pre id="pre_1">    

 
 
 

 IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR RAJASTHAN 
 JAIPUR BENCH, JAIPUR
ORDER 
SB Criminal Revision Petition No. 1303/2011
Kanchan Bai versus State of Rajasthan &amp; ors 
17.1.2013
HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE MN BHANDARI
Mr Govind Choudhary  for petitioner 
Mr Laxman Meena, PP  for the State 
BY THE COURT: </pre>
		
<p id="p_1">		Heard on the application under <a href="/doc/100581/" id="a_1">section 5</a> of the Limitation Act for condonation of delay in filing the revision petition. 
</p>
<p id="p_2">		While hearing the application, case was taken up even  for consideration on merit. 
</p>
<p id="p_3">		It is a case where on conviction, accused respondents were given benefit of probation. The order of probation has been challenged on the ground that offence took place within the  family thus benefit of probation should not have been extended to the accused respondents. 
</p>
<p id="p_4">		I find that the court below has taken note of the fact that matter is old by ten years and case involves offence under <a href="/doc/1258372/" id="a_2">sections 147</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_3">323</a> and <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_4">341</a> IPC. While maintaining conviction, accused  were extended benefit of probation in view of <a href="/doc/1828439/" id="a_5">section 4(1)</a> of the Probation of Offenders Act, 1958. The order aforesaid has been challenged mainly on the ground that accused and complainant are family members.  I do not find aforesaid to be a  ground to interfere with the order. The court below has to grant benefit of probation, denial thereof should be for the reasons to be recorded. 
</p>
<p id="p_5">		Looking to the facts aforesaid, grant of probation to the accused respondents cannot be said to be illegal thus I am  not inclined to interfere in the impugned order. Thus even if the delay in filing the revision petition is condoned, I do not find any merit in the revision petition. 
</p>
<p id="p_6">		In view of aforesaid, application under <a href="/doc/100581/" id="a_6">section 5</a> of the Limitation Act so as the revision petition are dismissed. 
</p> 
<p id="p_7">		(MN BHANDARI), J.

</p>
