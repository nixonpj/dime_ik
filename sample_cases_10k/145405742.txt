<div class="doc_title">Meena W vs Hargobind Kessumal Kriplani on 27 March, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                  C/CA/2731/2017                                                ORDER




                  IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

         CIVIL APPLICATION (FOR CONDONATION OF DELAY) NO. 2731 of 2017

                 In FIRST APPEAL (STAMP NUMBER) NO. 225 of 2017

         ==========================================================
                 MEENA WD/O LATE ANANT KRIPLANI &amp; 1....Applicant(s)
                                    Versus
                  HARGOBIND KESSUMAL KRIPLANI....Respondent(s)
         ==========================================================
         Appearance:
         MR NL RAMNANI, ADVOCATE for the Applicant(s) No. 1 - 2
         YUVRAJ G THAKORE, CAVEATOR for the Respondent(s) No. 1
         ==========================================================

          CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE RAJESH H.SHUKLA

                                    Date : 27/03/2017


                                     ORAL ORDER</pre>




<p id="p_1">         1.    Rule. Learned advocate Shri Ramnani for the applicants
         and learned advocate Shri Yuvraj Thakore for the respondent.
</p>

<p id="p_2">         2.    Present Civil Application is filed by the applicants for
         condonation of delay of 188 days in filing the appeal on the
         ground stated in the application, inter alia, that the applicants
         had   moved       application      before        the     District        Court,        and
         thereafter, he has withdrawn and preferred appeal which has
         consumed time resulting in delay.
</p>

<p id="p_3">         3.    Heard learned advocate Shri Ramnani for the applicants
         and learned advocate Shri Yuvraj Thakore for the respondent.</p><pre id="pre_2">



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                            Page 1 of 2</span>

HC-NIC                                   Page 1 of 2      Created On Tue Mar 28 00:18:25 IST 2017
                      C/CA/2731/2017                                          ORDER




</pre><p id="p_4">         4.       As the applicant pursued their remedy, he was not
         negligent in pursuing the remedy, however, after verification of
         the jurisdiction, he was required to file an appeal before this
         Court, and therefore, withdrawn an application from the
         District Court.
</p>

<p id="p_5">         5.       Learned advocate Shri Yuvraj Thakor strongly resisted
         the application contending that it could have been filed and
         this itself suggests negligence.
</p>

<p id="p_6">         6.       In view of the grounds mentioned in the application, the
         same would constitute sufficient cause for delay. The Hon'ble
         Apex Court in catena of judicial pronouncements has laid down
         the guidelines with regard to the approach in such matter of
         condonation of delay. It is in this background and having
         regard to the guidelines with regard to the approach in such
         matter including observation made by Hon'ble Apex Court in
         the case of         Collector, Land Acquisition, Anantnag Vs.
         Mst.Katiji, reported in AIR 1987 SC 1353, the present civil
         application is allowed. The delay is condoned. Prayer in terms
         of 9(B) is granted. Rule is made absolute.
</p>


<p id="p_7">                                                              (RAJESH H.SHUKLA, J.)
         Girish
