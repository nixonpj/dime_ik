<a href="/doc/162506/" id="a_17">Sections 447</a>, <a href="/doc/222396/" id="a_18">427</a> and <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_19">506</a> of the Indian Penal Code, 1860

      These Criminal Petitions coming on for admission this
day, the Court made the following:

                         ORDER
</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Heard the learned senior Advocate, Shri Tomy

Sebastian appearing for the petitioner/s in both the

petitions.
</p>
<p id="p_2">      2.     These petitions are heard and disposed of by

this common order.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                             -4-</span><p id="p_3">




</p><p id="p_4">     3.    It transpires that there were civil disputes

between the petitioners and respondent No.2 and during

the course of those proceedings, a document, namely, an

email is said to have originated from respondent No.2 and

was sought to be produced by the petitioners, to which

respondent No.2 took a stand that it was a forged, bogus

document and obviously, fabricated and on that ground,

had lodged a criminal complaint.          It is that criminal

proceeding, which is under challenge in these petitions.


</p><p id="p_5">     4.    Incidentally, it now transpires that the civil

proceedings between petitioners and respondent No.2

have been amicably settled and at the settlement, it was

further agreed that all the criminal proceedings including

the present petitions could be dropped.


</p><p id="p_6">     5.    Accordingly,   the     petitions   are   summarily

allowed in the light of the law laid down by the Hon`ble

Supreme Court in the case of <a href="/doc/69949024/" id="a_20">Gian Singh vs. State of

Punjab and another</a> ((2012) 10 SCC 303) and the

proceedings in Crime No.40/2014 of Cyber Police Station,
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                             -5-</span>




Bangalore,    pending   before    the   I   Additional   Chief

Metropolitan Magistrate, Bangalore, in PCR No.14829/2014

as also the proceedings in C.C. No.56224/2014 pending on

the file of XI Additional Chief Metropolitan Magistrate,

Bangalore, are hereby quashed.
</p>
<p id="p_7">
</p><p id="p_8">      6.     In view of disposal of these petitions, I.A.

Nos.1, 2 and 3 of 2015 in Crl.P. No.93/2015 and I.A. Nos.

2 and 3 of 2015 in Crl.P. No.2409/2015 do not survive for

consideration and the same stand disposed of.



</p><p id="p_9">                                             Sd/-
