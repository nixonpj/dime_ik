Date
: 16/07/2012 

 

 
					ORAL
ORDER</pre>
 

 
 


 

<p id="p_1">1.	This
Application has been preferred by the applicant-convict no.13914 to
allow him to enjoy the furlough leaves as he has been denied the same
on the ground that untoward incidence may occur if that liberty is
granted. 
</p>
 

<p id="p_2">2.	Having
heard learned APP Mr. R.C. Kodekar and on perusal of the record, it
emerges that the complainant side has apprehended threat to his life
if the applicant is allowed to enjoy the furlough leave. 
</p>
 

 
 


 

<p id="p_3">3.	As
can be noted from the record that the accused has already been
granted temporary bail by virtue of the order of this Court and it is
further conveyed that no untoward incidence had occurred while he was
given such bail. There is no reason to deny him his entitlement of
furlough leave. Some stringent conditions can be imposed to ensure
the safety. Accordingly,
this challenge to the order succeed.
</p> 

 
 


 

<p id="p_4">4.	Applicant
is permitted to be enlarged on furlough leave for a period of 14 days
with the following conditions:  
</p>
 

 
 


 


<p id="p_5">a)	Applicant-convict
shall be permitted his 	fourteen days of furlough leave and such
	period shall begin on the date on which he 	is released  from the
jail;
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_6">b)	Applicant
shall be permitted to enjoy furlough 	on his furnishing personal bond
of Rs. 20,000/- 	and 	one surety of like amount.
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_7">c)	While
on furlough, he shall not indulge in any 	activity which is contrary
to the law;
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_8">d)	Upon
completion of the said furlough leave 	period, the applicant shall
surrender before 	the jail 	authorities.
</p> 


 


 


<p id="p_9">Special
Criminal Application stands disposed of. Rule is made absolute
accordingly.
</p> 


 


 


 


 


<p id="p_10">	Registry
shall forthwith communicate this order to the prisoner in
jail.
</p> 


 


 

 
 


 

<p id="p_11">						(MS
SONIA GOKANI, J.)
