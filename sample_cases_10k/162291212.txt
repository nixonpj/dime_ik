<div class="doc_title">Shiv Nath Mandal vs The State Of Bihar on 2 December, 2010</div><pre id="pre_1">             IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                     Cr.Misc. No.9127 of 2010
SHIV NATH MANDAL S/O SRI DEVENDRA MANDAL, R/O VILLAGE BARHAD P.S.
HATHAURI, DISTRICT MUZAFFARPUR,
                               Versus
THE STATE OF BIHAR
                            -----------
</pre>
<p id="p_1">/3/   02.12.2010             Heard Mr. Rakesh Kumar Verma, learned

                   counsel for the petitioner and Smt. Indu Bala

                   Pandey, Additional Public Prosecutor for the

                   State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                             The petitioner, who is in custody in

                   connection with Hathauri P.S. Case No. 10 of

                   2009,    for   the    offences    under      Sections        366

                   (A)/376/34<a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_1">      of    the     Indian    Penal     Code</a>       has

                   prayed for grant of bail. It was alleged that

                   petitioner      had     kidnapped       13      years        old

                   daughter of the informant and she was kept in

                   the house of the petitioner. Subsequently she

                   was recovered by the informant from the house

                   of the petitioner. It was alleged that during

                   the kidnapping period the petitioner had also

                   committed      rape    with     the    daughter       of    the

                   informant.      On    that     allegation       the        First

                   Information was lodged against the petitioner

                   and other.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                             It was submitted by learned counsel

                   for the petitioner that it was out and out

                   false case and no occurrence has taken place.

                   It      was     submitted        that     even         during
 investigation the victim girl was examined by

medical expert, however, no sign of rape was

found. It was further submitted                   that after

alleged recovery of the victim the informant

has lodged the present case. Despite the fact

that     it    was    alleged       that    the    girl    was

recovered        from       the     possession      of      the

petitioner she was not produced before the

Magistrate for her examination under <a href="/doc/497457/" id="a_2">Section

164</a> of the Criminal Procedure Code. Only on

the basis of statement of the victim recorded

under <a href="/doc/447673/" id="a_3">Section 161</a> of the Criminal Procedure

Code, the petitioner has been made accused.

It   was      also   submitted      that     subsequent      to

filing of the present case the victim girl

was murdered by the informant and his family

members. Thereafter on the basis of complaint

filed by elder brother of the petitioner a

First Information Report vide Hathauri P.S.

Case No. 132/09 was registered, in which the

informant       of    the    present       case   and     other

persons have made accused.
</p>
<p id="p_4">           Smt.      Indu    Bala    Pandey,      Additional

Public        Prosecutor      for    the     State,       after

examining the case diary does not dispute the

fact that no sign of rape was found on the

person of the victim girl.
</p><p id="p_5">                    In    view        of      the       facts        and

        circumstances, let the petitioner above named

        be released on bail on furnishing bail bond

        of   Rs.    10,000/-     (ten      thousand)        with     two

        sureties    of   the    like      amount    each       to    the

        satisfaction     of    the     learned      Chief    Judicial

        Magistrate,      Muzaffarpur        in     connection       with

        Hathauri P.S. Case No. 10 of 2009 and G.R.

        No. 390 of 2009.
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><p id="p_7">                                          ( Rakesh Kumar, J.)
Safik
