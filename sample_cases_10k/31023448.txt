<div class="doc_title">Deepak vs State Of Haryana on 25 May, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">      IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA AT
                      CHANDIGARH

210                                 CRM-M-22007-2018
                                DATE OF DECISION:25.05.2018


DEEPAK                                              ... Petitioner(s)

                         Versus

STATE OF HARYANA                                    ... Respondent(s)

CORAM: HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE ANUPINDER SINGH GREWAL

Present:   Mr. G.S. Simble, Advocate
           for the petitioner.

           Mr. D.R. Singla, DAG, Haryana.

                  ****

ANUPINDER SINGH GREWAL, J. (ORAL)
</pre>
<p id="p_1">           The petitioner is seeking regular bail in FIR No.267, dated

29.10.2017, under <a href="/doc/764237/" id="a_1">Sections 394</a>, <a href="/doc/1865117/" id="a_2">397</a>, <a href="/doc/814524/" id="a_3">196</a>, <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_4">120-B</a> IPC, registered at

Police Station Narwana Sadar, District Jind.

</p><p id="p_2">           Learned counsel for the petitioner contends that the

petitioner is not named in the FIR. He has been arrested as an

accused on the disclosure statement of a co-accused Paramjit @

Jaiky. The alleged recovery of `50,000/- and a mobile phone without

sim card has been effected from the petitioner. He further states

that the petitioner is 27 years of age and is not involved in any other

case. He also states that the petitioner is in custody for over 04

months.
</p>
<p id="p_3">           Learned State counsel, upon instructions from ASI Azad

Singh, states that the investigation has been completed and the

challan is being filed shortly.
</p>
<p id="p_4">           Heard.
</p>
<p id="p_5">

</p><p id="p_6">                                  1 of 2
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">               ::: Downloaded on - 09-07-2018 03:09:11 :::</span>
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2"> CRM-M-22007-2018                                                      -2-</span>


            In view of the submissions of learned counsel for the

petitioner especially when the petitioner is in custody for over 04

months and is not involved in any other case, I deem it a fit case to

grant the concession of regular bail to the petitioner.

</p><p id="p_7">            Therefore, without expressing any opinion on the merits

of the case, the instant petition is allowed. The petitioner, namely,

Deepak is ordered to be released on regular bail on his furnishing

requisite bonds to the satisfaction of the trial Court/Duty Magistrate.
