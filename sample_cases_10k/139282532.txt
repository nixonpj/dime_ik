<div class="doc_title">Balwinder Singh And Ors vs State Of Punjab And Ors on 29 January, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">      IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA
                 AT CHANDIGARH

                                        CRM-M No. 32206 of 2017

                                        Date of decision: 29.01.2018

Balwinder Singh and others
                                                          ...Petitioners

                          VERSUS

State of Punjab and others                                ...Respondents


CORAM: HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE RAJ SHEKHAR ATTRI

Present:    Mr. Harinder Singh, Advocate
            for the petitioners.

            Mr. V.G. Jauhar, Sr. DAG, Punjab..

            Mr. A.S. Gill, Advocate
            for respondents No. 2 and 3.

                          *****
</pre>
<p id="p_1">RAJ SHEKHAR ATTRI, J.(Oral)
            By invoking <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C., the petitioners have prayed
for quashing of FIR No. 138 dated 02.12.2011 for offence punishable under
<a href="/doc/1540253/" id="a_2">Sections 326</a>/<a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_3">323</a>/<a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_4">324</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_5">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code registered in Police
Station Dharamkot, District Moga and proceedings emanating therefrom on
the basis of affidavits with regard to compromise (Annexures P-2) arrived at
between the parties.
</p><p id="p_2">            In the present case, the FIR was registered on the statement of
Balkar Singh son of Lehna Singh. Now, dispute between the parties has
been resolved by way of compromise Annexures P-2.
</p><p id="p_3">            Vide order dated 31.08.2017, the parties were directed to
appear before the trial Court to get their statements recorded with regard to
genuineness of compromise.
</p><p id="p_4">            Pursuant thereto, a report has been submitted by the Judicial
Magistrate, 1st Class, Moga wherein it has been reported that the
compromise between the L.Rs of complainant Balkar Singh(since deceased)
i.e Gian Kaur and Surjit Singh as well as alleged eye-witness namely

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                                           2</span>
CRM-M No. 32206 of 2017

Sukhjinder Singh and accused Balwinder Singh, Mukhtiar Singh and
Nirmal Singh seems to be genuine and without any pressure or corecion.
</p><p id="p_5">               Counsel for the State and respondents No. 2 and 3 have not
disputed that the parties i.e. petitioners and respondents No.2 and 3
(complainants) have arrived at a settlement with an intent to give burial to
their differences.
</p><p id="p_6">               Perusal of allegations in the FIR reveals that the present case
squarely falls in the category of cases that can be quashed by the High
Court, in exercise of its inherent power under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_6">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C. Keeping
in view authoritative enunciation of law laid down by Hon'ble the Supreme
Court in '<a href="/doc/69949024/" id="a_7">Gian Singh v. State of Punjab and another</a>', 2012 (4) R.C.R.
(Criminal) 543 and in the light of facts and circumstances discussed
hereinbefore, this Court is of the considered opinion that continuation of
criminal proceedings would amount to abuse of process of law and it is
expedient in the interest of justice that the same are put to an end.
</p><p id="p_7">               For the foregoing reasons, the petition is allowed, FIR No. 138
dated 02.12.2011 for offence punishable under <a href="/doc/1540253/" id="a_8">Sections 326</a>/<a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_9">323</a>/<a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_10">324</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_11">34</a> of
the Indian Penal Code registered in Police Station Dharamkot, District
Moga and proceedings emanating therefrom stand quashed qua the
petitioners.
</p>
