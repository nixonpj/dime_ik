  IN THE HIGH COURT OF KERALA AT ERNAKULAM

1. N.V.MOHAMMED ALI, AGED 55, S/O.

                        Vs

                  W.P.(C)No.16389 of 2011
               - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -
            Dated this the 20th day of June, 2011

                         J U D G M E N T
</pre>

<p id="p_1">     The petitioner retired on 31.03.2011 as Deputy

Secretary of the Corporation of Kozhikode while under

suspension.    A criminal case has also been registered

against the petitioner in respect of the allegations of

misconduct raised against the petitioner. The petitioner is

now aggrieved by non-disbursal of retirement benefits due

to the petitioner. In regard to the same, the petitioner has

filed Ext.P5 representation before the 1st respondent. The

petitioner seeks a direction to the 1st respondent to consider

and pass orders on Ext.P5 as expeditiously as possible and

also to direct the respondents to disburse the retirement

benefits due to the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_2">     I have heard the learned Government Pleader also. In

the facts and circumstances of the case, I dispose of this

writ petition with a direction to the 1st respondent to

W.P.(C)No.16389 of 2011
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                              -2-</span>

consider and pass orders on Ext.P5 as expeditiously as

possible, at any rate, within a period of three months from

the date of receipt of a certified copy of this judgment,

after affording an opportunity of being heard to the

petitioner. In the meanwhile, the petitioner shall be paid

provisional pension with arrears as expeditiously as possible

at any rate within a period of one month from the date of

receipt of a copy of this judgment. I also make it clear that

the pendency of the disciplinary proceedings or the criminal

prosecution shall not be a bar for disbursing provident fund

amounts due to the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                                      Sd/-
</p>

                                                S. SIRI JAGAN
                                                     JUDGE
