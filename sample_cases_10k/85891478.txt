<div class="doc_title">Smt.Sanjukta Devi vs Bihar State Transport Corporat on 31 July, 2012</div><pre id="pre_1">              IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                           W.P. (C) No. 23 of 2007
                                 ...
              Smt. Sanjukta Devi                            ...     ...    Petitioner
                                 -V e r s u s-
              Bihar State Road Transport Corporation, Patna
              and Others                              ...     ...     Respondents.
                                         ...
              CORAM: - HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE APARESH KUMAR SINGH.
                                        ...
              For the Petitioner : - Mr. Jyoti Prasad Sinha, Advocate
              For the Respondents       : - JC to SC (L&amp;C)
                                        ...
04/31.07.2012</pre><p id="p_1">       Heard learned counsel for the parties.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                     Petitioner has prayed for direction upon the respondent nos.

               1 and 2     to pay the amount of award passed in Motor Vehicle

               Accident Claim Case 23/92-93 wherein the final Award and Decree

               was passed by this Court in Miscellaneous Appeal No. 192/1992

               vide judgment dated 09.01.1998 with interest @ 18% from the date

               of institutions of claim.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                     From     the   arguments   of   parties,   it   appears   that   the

               respondent-Corporation claim to have paid the amount of Rs.

               2,33,774/- towards satisfaction of awarded amount in the certificate

               proceeding itself. Petitioner further disputes the calculation and

               being dissatisfied about the aforesaid amount, has preferred this

               writ application.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                     From the provisions of the Jharkhand and Orissa Public

               Demand Recovery Act, 1914, the remedy of the parties lies before

               Certificate Officer itself, who can either in exercise of power of

               review or in Appeal by the Appellate Forum under Section 60 of the

               aforesaid Act, 1914 can adjudicate the matter.

</p><p id="p_5">                     In view of the above, parties are allowed to approach the

               concerned authority under the Act, 1914 seeking for redressal of

               grievance relating to the quantum of total decretal amount.

</p><p id="p_6">                     With the aforesaid liberty, this writ petition stands disposed

               of.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><p id="p_8">                                                       (Aparesh Kumar Singh, J.)
  Kamlesh/
 </p>
