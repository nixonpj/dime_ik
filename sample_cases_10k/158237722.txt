                             WP(S) No. 71 of 2012
                                      ----
</pre><pre id="pre_2">          Balram Pandey                           ...  ...    Petitioner
                                   -Versus-
</pre><p id="p_1">          Mineral Area Development Authority and ors.. ...  Respondents
</p><p id="p_2">                                      ----
</p><p id="p_3">          CORAM : HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE APARESH KUMAR SINGH
</p><p id="p_4">                                      ----
</p><pre id="pre_3">          For the Petitioner     :Mr. K. Shankar
          For the Respondents    :M/s. Bhawesh Kumar, Ravi Kumar &amp;
                                        Rahul Kamlesh
                                      ----

</pre><p id="p_5">3- 27.11.2014   Heard learned counsel for the parties.

</p><p id="p_6">                The petitioner had earlier approached this Court after his

          retirement on 31.1.2008 from the post of Head Assistant under

          respondent-MADA, Dhanbad by preferring a writ petition being WP(S)

          No.1479/2010 and certain payments are said to have been made for

          post retirement benefits. However, according to the petitioner, claim

          for senior selection grade and ACP as also the interest on the amount

          of GPF, which was deducted from his salary and not deposited in the

          bank account of the petitioner by the          respondent-Authority are

          pending.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                Representations have been made by the petitioner, which is

          contained in Annexure 4 series for redressal of his aforesaid

          grievances and      payment of interest for delay in making the

          admissible payment.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                No counter affidavit has been filed in this writ petition, which is

          pending since 2012.
</p>
<p id="p_9">                Having regard to the aforesaid facts and circumstances, in stead

          of keeping the writ petition pending for the limited grievances, the

          petitioner is allowed to pursue his representation before the

          respondent-Managing Director, Mineral Area Development Authority,

          Dhanbad, duly supported with all necessary facts and documents.

          Thereafter, the Managing Director, MADA shall consider the same in

          accordance with law after due verification of the       relevant service

          records of the petitioner and take a decision thereupon within 12

          weeks thereafter. Needless to say, dependent upon such decision if
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                 2.</span>

         any claim of the petitioner is found genuine and legally admissible,

         the same shall be disbursed to the petitioner along with statutory

         interest, if any, as per the   scheme framed for retired employee of

         MADA, within a reasonable time thereafter.

</p><p id="p_10">              This writ petition stands disposed of accordingly.



</p><p id="p_11">                                        (Aparesh Kumar Singh, J. )

Pandey
