<div class="doc_title">Govind Kumar @ Govind Madeshiya vs The State Of Bihar on 11 January, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                Criminal Miscellaneous No.37961 of 2015
                              Arising Out of PS.Case No. -165 Year- 2014 Thana -DHANAHA
                                       District- WEST CHAMPARAN(BETTIAH)
                 ======================================================
                 Govind Kumar @ Govind Madeshiya, Son of Sri Bhola Nath, resident of
                 village - Tekuatad, P.S. Ramkola, District - Kushinagar ( U.P. )

                                                                              .... ....   Petitioner/s
                                                      Versus
                 The State of Bihar

                                                              .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     : Mr. Bimlesh Kumar Pandey, Advocate
                 For the Opposite Party/s : Mr. Navin Kr. Panday (APP)
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE JUSTICE SMT. ANJANA MISHRA
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">3   11-01-2016              Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and the

                 learned counsel appearing on behalf of the State.

</p><p id="p_2">                            The petitioner is apprehending his arrest in

                 connection with Dhanaha P.S. Case No.165 of 2014 for

                 allegedly having committed the offence under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_1">Sections 302</a>

                 and <a href="/doc/386021/" id="a_2">201</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_3">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                            Case diary in the present case was called for, which

                 has since been received.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                            Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that the

                 husband of the informant went missing on 8.11.2014 and

                 thereafter a dead body was recovered on 29.12.2014, which

                 was identified as that of the informant's husband. Learned

                 counsel for the petitioner further submits that there was some
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.37961 of 2015 (3) dt.11-01-2016</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                           2/4</span>




             illicit relationship of the deceased's Bhabhi with one

             Amarnath Madhesiya, who was the Principal of the School.

             The informant's husband was also working as a Driver in the

             said School. It is submitted that since the deceased came to

             know of the illicit relation between the said Amarnath

             Madhesiya and his Bhabi Sandhaya Devi, who is also a

             School Teacher in the said School, the Principal delinked the

             deceased from working as a Driver in the aforementioned

             School. It is submitted that thereafter the Bhabhi of the

             deceased used to go to the School in the vehicle belonging to

             one Amarnath Madhesiya. Learned counsel for the petitioner

             further submits that between 9.11.2014 and 29.12.2014, the

             informant did not file any Sanha or any information before

             any authority regarding the disappearance of her husband, but

             subsequently when the dead body was recovered and

             identified, she made statement against the aforementioned

             persons, which included one Yogi Madhesiya and also this

             petitioner; saying that he is the Gram Pradhan, who had also

             participated in the alleged conspiracy giving Supari for killing

             of the informant's husband. Learned counsel submits that

             name of this petitioner has surfaced only on the basis of

             suspicion and on account of village politics and the other co-
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3"> Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.37961 of 2015 (3) dt.11-01-2016</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                                           3/4</span>




             accused persons, who had made confessional statements, have

             been extended the privilege of regular bail. So far as this

             petitioner is concerned, he has having no criminal antecedents

             and there is no further material in the case diary so as to

             implicate him.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                           Learned counsel appearing on behalf of the State

             submits that save and except the statement made by the

             informant and that too after lapse of almost one and a half

             months of the disappearance of her husband, there is no

             cogent material available in the case diary so as to implicate

             the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                          In view of the above facts and circumstances and

             also because three other co-accused persons have been

             extended the privilege of regular bail,           let the petitioner,

             above named, in the event of his arrest or surrender before the

             court below within a period of four weeks from the date of

             receipt/production of a copy of this order, be released on bail

             on furnishing bail bond of Rs.10,000/- (Ten thousand) with

             two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of the

             learned Additional Chief Judicial Magistrate, Bagaha, West

             Champaran, in connection with Dhanaha P.S. Case No.165 of

             2014, subject to the conditions as laid down under Section
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_5">     Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.37961 of 2015 (3) dt.11-01-2016</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_6">                                               4/4</span>




                 438(2)<a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_4"> of the Code</a> of Criminal Procedure.</p><pre id="pre_2">



                                                (Anjana Mishra, J)
    PNM
