<div class="doc_title">P.Ayyakannu vs The Government Of Tamilnadu on 16 March, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">        

 

BEFORE THE MADURAI BENCH OF MADRAS HIGH COURT               

DATED: 16.03.2016  

CORAM   
THE HON'BLE MR.JUSTICE P.N.PRAKASH         
                                                                        
W.P.(MD) No.5281 of 2016  


P.Ayyakannu                                             ... Petitioner

                                        -vs-
1.The Government of Tamilnadu, 
   by its Secretary,
   Department of Home, 
  Chennai.

2.The Director General of Police,
   Chennai.

3.The City Police Commissioner, 
   Tiruchirappalli City,
   Tiruchirappalli.

4.The Superintendent of Police,
   Trichirappalli.                                              .. Respondents

Prayer: Writ Petition filed under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the Constitution of India
for issuance of a writ of Mandamus, forbearing the respondents from
interfering with the function and activities of the petitioner's association
illegally.

!For Petitioner:        Mr.S.Muthukrishnan

For Respondents: Mr.D.Muruganandam,    
                        Addl. Govt. Pleader

:O R D E R 
</pre><p id="p_1">        It is the case of the petitioner that he is the president of an
association which campaigning cause of interlinking of rivers. The petitioner
and his association members are intending to travel New Delhi to conduct a
demonstration on 23.03.2016, for which they are leaving Tiruchirappalli on
19.03.2016. They have also booked valid train ticket to go to New Delhi by
train. It is the apprehension of the petitioner that police are likely to
thwart their movements by detaining the petitioner and his members illegally.
</p>
<p id="p_2">        2. Earlier this petitioner filed W.P.(MD).No.16079 of 2015 for a
Mandamus forbearing the respondents therein from interfering with the
function and activities of his association and this Court vide order, dated
07.10.20105, disposed of the writ petition and the relevant portion of the
order is reproduced hereunder:-
</p>
<p id="p_3">        ?7. Considering the same, I am of the view that the respondent police
is directed not to prevent any person from his free movement which has been
guaranteed under <a href="/doc/1218090/" id="a_2">Article 19</a> of the Constitution of India. If the petitioner
has sought for police protection, then the respondent police is directed to
consider the representation of the petitioner in accordance with law. Without
any authority, the respondent police have no locus-standi to prevent the
petitioner and the office bearers of the Sangam to conduct any meeting or
agitation in respect of National South Indian River Interlinking
Agriculturist Sangam, Trichy and curtail the free movement and personal
liberty.?
</p><p id="p_4">        3. Heard the learned counsel for the petitioner and Mr.D.Muruganandam,
learned Additional Government Pleader for the respondents.
</p>
<p id="p_5">        4. It is the fundamental right of every Indian citizen to travel freely
anywhere throughout India and that can be prevented only in a manner known to
law and not illegally. Agitation and protest are integral part of a sound
Democracy.  
</p>
<p id="p_6">        5. Learned Additional Government Pleader submitted that the police have
no plan of preventing the petitioner and its member from going anywhere,
because the State is also aware that every citizen has a right to move about
freely within India, guaranteed under <a href="/doc/1218090/" id="a_3">Article 19</a> of the Constitution of
India. Recording the submissions of the learned Additional Government
Pleader, this writ petition is closed. Connected miscellaneous petition is
also closed.
</p>

</p><p id="p_8">1.The Government of Tamilnadu, 
   by its Secretary,
