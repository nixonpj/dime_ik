<div class="doc_title">Nimai Rawani vs The State Of Jharkhand on 18 May, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">          IN THE HIGH COURT OF                  JHARKHAND AT              RANCHI
                        Criminal Appeal (DB) No. 445 of 2017
          Nimai Rawani
          @ Nimai Chandra Rawani                         ..... .... Appellant
                                       Versus
          The State of Jharkhand                         ..... ...      Respondent
                                   --------
</pre><p id="p_1">                CORAM         :   HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE H. C. MISHRA
                                   HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE ANANDA SEN
</p><p id="p_2">                                   ------</p><pre id="pre_2">
          For the Appellant        :    Mr. Kumar Sundram, Advocate
          For the State            :     A.P.P.
                                   --------
          I.A. No. 8247 of 2018

</pre><p id="p_3">06/ 18.05.2019.        Heard learned counsel for the appellant and learned counsel for
          the State on the interlocutory application for granting bail, during the
          pendency of this appeal.
</p><p id="p_4">                       The appellant has been convicted and sentenced for the
          offences under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_1">Sections 302</a> and <a href="/doc/140515/" id="a_2">313</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p><p id="p_5">                       There is allegation against the appellant, who is a quack, to
          have surgically operated the deceased lady for abortion. The other doctor,
          who had treated the deceased, had found several raptures within the
          intestine and uterus, which ultimately led to her death.
</p><p id="p_6">                       Earlier prayer for bail of this appellant was rejected by this
          Court on merit.
</p><p id="p_7">                       Learned counsel for the appellant has renewed the prayer for
          bail.
</p><p id="p_8">                       In the facts of this case, we are not inclined to release the
          appellant, Nimai Rawani @ Nimai Chandra Rawani on bail. Accordingly,
          his prayer for bail is rejected.
</p><p id="p_9">                       This interlocutory application stands dismissed.
</p>


<p id="p_10">                                                                     ( H. C. Mishra, J.)



                                                                     (Ananda Sen, J.)
  Anu/-
</p>
