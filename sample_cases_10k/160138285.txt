<div class="doc_title">Bhajan Sarkar vs Unknown on 24 February, 2011</div><p id="p_1">.02.2011
                                       CRA No. 593 of 2010

           In Re:- An application for suspension of sentence under <a href="/doc/985477/" id="a_1">Section
           389</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure filed on 18th January, 2011
           being CRAN No. 176 of 2011 in connection with S.T.No. 07(3)/2007.
</p><p id="p_2">                                    And
           In the matter of:- Bhajan Sarkar
                                                        Appellant

           Mr. Joymalya Bagchi,
           Mr. Sukdeb Chatterjee
                                                       for the appellant

           Mr. Debobrata Roy                           for the State


                Heard the learned Counsel appearing on behalf of the parties.

                The   appellant    has      been   convicted     for   having   committed

           offences under <a href="/doc/1279834/" id="a_2">Sections 376</a> of the I.P.C. and sentenced to suffer

           rigorous imprisonment for eight years and pay fine of Rs. 25,000/-

           , in default, to suffer further simple imprisonment for one year.

</p><p id="p_3">                It is the prosecution case that on 6.8.2006 at about 4-30

           p.m. the prosecutrix aged about 13 years was on her way to her

           uncle place, the appellant accosted her and dragged to a jute

           field and committed rape.
</p>
<p id="p_4">               The learned Counsel for the appellant submitted that the age

           of the prosecutrix was more than sixteen years on the date of the

           incident which can be easily ascertained on the cross-examination

           of the father of the prosecutrix and that they were having a love

           affairs, the police has       falsely implicated in this case.

               We have gone the evidence, particularly the evidence of the

           prosecution   in    order   to    prove   the   age    of   the   prosecutrix.

           Prosecution has placed the document from the school to show the

           correct date of birth of the prosecutrix. The evidence of the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                       2</span>




prosecutrix    does   not   show   that   she   has   consented   for   sexual

intercourse.
</p>
<p id="p_5">     In the facts and circumstances of the case, we do not think

that this is a fit case where the substantive sentence of the

applicant can be suspended.
</p>
<p id="p_6">     The    application     for    suspension    of   sentence    is,    thus,

rejected.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><p id="p_8">                                                (J.N. Patel, Chief Justice)



                                                      (Ashim Kumar Roy, J.)
 </p>
