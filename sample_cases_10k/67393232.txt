<div class="doc_title">Ram Niwas vs Jodhpur Vidhyut Vitran Nigam, ... on 13 September, 2018</div><div class="doc_bench">Bench: Sandeep Mehta, Sandeep Mehta</div>

<p id="p_1">      HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR RAJASTHAN AT
                       JODHPUR
                    S.B. Civil Writ No. 10078/2012

Ram Niwas
</p><p id="p_2">                                                         ----Petitioner
                                Versus
Jodhpur Vidhyut Vitran Nigam, Ltd. &amp; Ors.
</p><p id="p_3">                                                      ----Respondents


For Petitioner(s)        :   Mr. Vikas Bijarinia.
For Respondent(s)        :   Mr. Dinesh Kumar Joshi.



</p><p id="p_4">            HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE SANDEEP MEHTA

                                 Order

13/09/2018

     The    interlocutory    application    seeking   exemption   from

personal presence of the MD, JVVNL has already been disposed of

on the previous date.
</p>
<p id="p_5">     The issue which the petitioner has raised through this writ

petition filed under <a href="/doc/1712542/" id="a_1">Article 226</a> of the Constitution of India is

regarding the demand of excess charges calculated by the

respondents pursuant to the extra load allegedly utilised by the

petitioner in his electricity connection.

</p><p id="p_6">     Learned counsel Shri Bijarnia submits that the petitioner may

be allowed to submit a representation to the competent authority

of the Jodhpur Discom who, in turn, may be directed to objectively

consider such representation.
</p>
<p id="p_7">     Shri Joshi appearing on behalf of the respondents does not

object to the said submission subject t the condition that that the

petitioner deposits the demand amount.
</p><p id="p_8">                                                                      (2 of 2)          [CW-10078/2012]



                                         Having regard to the facts and circumstances as emerging

                                   from record, the petitioner is directed to deposit 50% of the

                                   demand amount within a period of two weeks from today and

                                   thereafter, filed a detailed representation to the competent

                                   authority of the Jodhpur Discom alongwith a copy of this order for

                                   ventilating his grievances. Upon receiving such representation

                                   alongwith the receipt of deposit and copy of this order, the

                                   competent authority of the Jodhpur Discom shall objectively

                                   consider the prayer of the petitioner and pass a reasoned order

                                   thereupon within a period of four weeks thereafter. In case, the

                                   petitioner fails to comply with any of the above directions, the

                                   respondents shall be free to continue the recovery proceedings.

</p><p id="p_9">                                         With these observations and directions, the writ petition as

                                   well as stay application are disposed of.



</p><p id="p_10">                                                                                (SANDEEP MEHTA),J

                                   Tikam/15
