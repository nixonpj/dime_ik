<div class="doc_title">Smt.Naini Bai vs Hitesh Kumar on 2 March, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">              1 Miscellaneous Appeal No.1108/2008
           (Smt. Naini Bai vs. Hitesh Kumar and others)

02/03/2015
      Shri N.K. Gupta, Senior Advocate, assisted by Shri Ravi
Gupta, Advocate for appellant.
      Shri K.S. Tomar, Senior Advocate, assisted by Shri J.S.
Kaurav, Advocate for respondents.</pre>
<p id="p_1">      During the course of hearing as both parties agreed to,
this appeal is disposed of by setting aside the judgment and
decree passed by the first appellate court dated 28/8/2008 in
Civil Appeal No.10-A/2008 remanding the case back to the
first appellate court on the following terms:-
</p><p id="p_2">1.    Let record of the first appellate court (civil appeal No.10-
A/2008) as well as of trial court (civil suit No.42-A/2003) be
made over to the first appellate court within ten days.
</p><p id="p_3">2.    Both parties shall appear before the first appellate court
on 30th March, 2015.
</p><p id="p_4">3.    Plaintiff/appellant shall move an application to add State
as a party within a week from the date of appearance before
the first appellate court. Notice shall be issued to the State for
a date to be fixed by the first appellate court.
</p><p id="p_5">4.    Both parties shall have right to file objections to the
Commissioner's report obtained by the trial court under Order
XXVI Rule 10 CPC as regards suit property.
</p><p id="p_6">5.    After objections, so filed, first appellate court shall
proceed to decide the appeal on merits.
</p><p id="p_7">6.    Parties, as prayed before this Court, may file application
                       2 Miscellaneous Appeal No.1108/2008
                   (Smt. Naini Bai vs. Hitesh Kumar and others)

        under Order XLI Rule 27 CPC, the same shall be decided by
        the first appellate court on its own merit.
</p><p id="p_8">        7.    Since the suit is of the year 2001, the first appellate
        court shall endeavour to decide the appeal on priority
        preferably within six months from the date of appearance of
        the parties before the first appellate court.
</p>

<p id="p_9">                                                      (Rohit Arya)
                                                         Judge
Arun*
