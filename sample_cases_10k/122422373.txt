<div class="doc_title">Lakhan Sao And Others vs The State Of Jharkhand on 17 September, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">            IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                          B. A. No. 5700 of 2016
                                        ---
            1. Lakhan Sao
            2. Pappu Sao
            3. Pradeep Sao                    ...     ...      Petitioners
                                       Versus
            The State of Jharkhand            ...     ...      Opp. Party
                                        ---
      CORAM       : HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE RONGON MUKHOPADHYAY
                                       ---
            For the Petitioners        : Mr. A. K. Sahani, Advocate
            For the State              : Mr. Vijay Kumar Roy, A.P.P.
            For the Informant          : Mr. Munna Lal Yadav, Advocate
                                       ---
03/17.09.2016</pre><p id="p_1">     Heard Mr. A. K. Sahani, learned counsel for the petitioners and
            Mr. Vijay Kumar Roy, learned A.P.P. for the State assisted by
            Mr. Munna Lal Yadav, learned counsel appearing for the informant.
</p><p id="p_2">                  Petitioners have been made accused in connection with Simariya
            P.S. Case No. 141 of 2015 corresponding to G. R. No. 1080 of 2015
            registered for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_1">Sections 302</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_2">34</a> of the
            Indian Penal Code.
</p><p id="p_3">                  It has been submitted by the learned counsel for the petitioners
            that none of the petitioners were armed with any weapons and the
            allegations levelled against them are general and omnibus in nature. It
            has also been submitted that the petitioners are in custody since
            24.05.2016.
</p><p id="p_4">                  Learned A.P.P. has opposed the prayer for bail of the petitioners.
                  It appears that the petitioners had assaulted the brother of the
            informant and so severe was the assault that the second and third ribs
            were fractured on the left side chest and the left side lung was found
            ruptured and congested and the small intestine was also found
            ruptured.
</p><p id="p_5">                  In view of the nature of injuries suffered by the deceased and the
            allegations levelled against the petitioners, I am not inclined to grant
            bail to the petitioners which is, accordingly, rejected at this stage.
</p>

<p id="p_6">                                                           (R. Mukhopadhyay, J.)
Umesh/-
</p>
