<div class="doc_title">BLAPL/4161/2018 on 10 July, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                                       BLAPL No.4161 of 2018




02.     10.07.2018                Heard the learned counsel for the petitioner and
                     learned counsel for the State.
                                  This is an application under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> Cr.P.C. in
                     connection with 2(a)C.C. No.11 of 2018 <a href="/doc/1727139/" id="a_2">(N.D.P.S. Act</a>) pending
                     in the Court of learned Addl. Sessions Judge, Bhanjanagar for
                     offence punishable under <a href="/doc/170878130/" id="a_3">section 20(b)(ii)(B)</a> of N.D.P.S. Act.
                                  The petitioner moved an application for bail before
                     the learned Addl. Sessions Judge, Bhanjanagar which was
                     rejected on 08.06.2018.
                                  Considering the submissions made by the learned
                     counsel for the petitioner that the petitioner is in custody since
                     30.05.2018 and it is the prosecution report that the contraband
                     'Ganja' which is stated to have been seized from the possession
                     of the petitioner is 5 kgs. 20 grams which is lesser than
                     commercial quantity and therefore, <a href="/doc/496325/" id="a_4">section 37</a> of N.D.P.S. Act is
                     not a bar for grant of bail and after hearing the learned counsel
                     for the State, I am inclined to release the petitioner on bail.
                                  Let the petitioner be released on bail in the
                     aforesaid case on furnishing bail bond of Rs.20,000.00 (rupees
                     twenty thousand) with two solvent sureties each for the like
                     amount to the satisfaction of the Court in seisin over the matter
                     with further terms and conditions as the learned Court may
                     deem just and proper.
                                  Accordingly, the BLAPL is disposed of.
                                  Urgent certified copy of this order be granted on
                     proper application.
sisir                                                         .......................
                                                              S. K. Sahoo, J.
