                                   J U D G M E N T

<p id="p_1">        Petitioner   is   aggrieved   by   the   disciplinary   proceedings   initiated


against him culminating in the impugned orders.  In view of the intervening


developments and in view of the order, I propose to pass in this case it is


not necessary to go into the merit of the case since the petitioner has an


effective   remedy   of   adjudication   under   Section   69   of   the   Kerala   Co-


operative   <a href="/doc/1700055/" id="a_1">Societies   Act</a>   before   the   Co-operative   Arbitration   Court.   In   the


nature   of   the   dispute   raised   in   this   case   it   is   only   appropriate   that   the


petitioner   pursues   his   statutory   remedy.     Since   the   petitioner   has   been


reinstated   in   service,   petitioner   will   return   Ext.P10   cheque   to   the   1st


respondent.  The writ petition is disposed of as follows.


</p><p id="p_2">        In the event of the petitioner invoking the remedy within a period of


two months from today, the same shall be treated to have been initiated in


time.     In   the   event   of   the   petitioner   thus   approaching     the   Co-operative


Arbitration Court, the case will be disposed of, within another six months.





</p><p id="p_3">                                                   KURIAN JOSEPH, JUDGE
