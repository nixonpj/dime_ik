                       In the High Court at Calcutta
                         Constitutional Writ Jurisdiction
                              Appellate Side
                         W.P.No.2746(W) of 2014
                            Rajendra Rabi Das
                                   Vs.
                      The State of West Bengal &amp; Ors.

            Mr.Lakshman Chandra Halder. .. for the petitioner.

            Mr.Shyamal Kumar Dey. .. for the State.


                   Affidavit-of-service filed today be kept with the record.


                   The Certificate Officer is directed to ensure that the Certificate Case, being
            No.30(M)12-13, is concluded and the amount is recovered and paid to the
            employee/petitioner concerned within a period of four weeks from the date of
            communication of this order.

                   This order is passed having regard to the fact that the employer has suffered orders under
            <a href="/doc/1556477/" id="a_1">section 7(4)</a> of the Payment of Gratuity Act and has been unsuccessful in resisting the certificate
            proceedings. The said order has attained finality and it is the duty of the Certificate Officer to
            ensure that such amount is recovered and paid to the employee/ petitioner within a period of four
            weeks from the date of communication of this order.

                   The respondent no.3 is directed to comply with the order passed by the Certificate Officer.</pre>

<p id="p_1">                   This writ petitioner stands disposed of with the aforesaid directions.

</p><p id="p_2">                                                                                            (SOUMEN SEN, J.)
