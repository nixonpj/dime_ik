           IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA
                       AT CHANDIGARH

                                         CRM-M-26053-2019
                                         Date of Decision: 20.11.2019

Kulwant Singh and another
                                                                .. Petitioners
             Vs.

State of Haryana
                                                              ..Respondent

CORAM: HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE MANOJ BAJAJ

Present:     Mr. Amit Choudhary, Advocate for the petitioners.

             Mr. Sukhdeep Parmar, DAG, Haryana.

             Mr. Arun Singh, Advocate for the complainant.
                              ...
Manoj Bajaj, J. (Oral)
</pre>
<p id="p_1">             This petition has been filed by the petitioners under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section

438</a> Code of Criminal Procedure for grant of anticipatory bail in a case FIR

No.109 dated 27.04.2019 under <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_2">Sections 420</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_3">506</a> and <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_4">120-B</a> IPC, 1860

registered at Police Station City Ratia, District Fatehabad. The petitioners

apprehended their arrest at the hands of Police.
</p>
<p id="p_2">             Learned counsel for the petitioners has invited the attention of

the Court to the order dated 03.06.2019 whereby while issuing notice of

motion to the respondent-State, the interim protection was extended to the

petitioners. The said order reads as under:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">                           "Counsel for the petitioners states that the matter
                           has been compromised between the parties. He
                           refers to compromise dated 27.05.2019 (Annexure
                           P-3).
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">                           Notice of motion.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">                           At this stage, Mr. Arun Singh, Advocate, has put in
                           appearance on behalf of complainant and filed his

                             vakalatnama, which is taken on record.
                             List on 24.07.2019.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_4">                             Meanwhile, in the event of arrest of the
                             petitioners, they shall be released on ad-interim
                             bail to the satisfaction of the arresting officer.
                             However,      the   petitioners   shall   join   the
                             investigation as and when directed by the
                             investigating agency and shall abide by the terms
                             and conditions laid down under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_5">Section 438(2)</a>
                             Cr.P.C."</blockquote><p id="p_3">
</p><p id="p_4">               Learned counsel for the petitioners further contends that in

deference to the said order, the petitioners submitted themselves before the

Police and joined the investigation. According to him, the petitioners

cooperated with the Police Authorities during the investigation and

furnished requisite bonds to the satisfaction of the Investigating

Officer/Arresting Officer.
</p>
<p id="p_5">               Learned State counsel who is assisted by SI Ramesh Kumar

does not dispute this fact that the petitioners have joined the investigation.

Learned counsel on instructions from SI Ramesh Kumar further states that

the petitioners are not required for custodial interrogation for the time being.

</p><p id="p_6">               Considering above, the petition is allowed and the interim bail

granted by this Court vide order dated 03.06.2019 is made absolute.</p><pre id="pre_2">
