<div class="doc_title">Bikash Paul vs Unknown on 9 September, 2014</div><div class="doc_author">Author: Pranab Kumar Chattopadhyay</div>

<p id="p_1">09.09.14
700/akd
 (Allowed)



                                   C. R. M. No. 9599 of 2014


               In re: An application for anticipatory bail under
               <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure filed on
               25.07.2014 in connection with Ashoke Nagar Police
               Station   Case   No.   179   of   2014   under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_2">Sections
               498A</a>/<a href="/doc/140515/" id="a_3">313</a>/<a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_4">406</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_5">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                             And

               In the matter of: Bikash Paul
                                                         ... Petitioner


                      Ms. Mitra Banerjee,
                      Ms. Kabita Mukherjee
                                             ... for the Petitioner.
</p>

<p id="p_3">                      Mr. Debabrata Das Gupta
                                           ... for the State


                      Heard the learned Advocate of both the parties.
</p>

<p id="p_4">                      Having considered the materials in the case diary,
             we are of the view that custodial interrogation of the
             petitioner in this case is not necessary. There is also no
             apprehension of abscondence of the petitioner.
</p>

<p id="p_5">                      We are, therefore, inclined to allow the prayer of
             the petitioner for anticipatory bail under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_6">Section 438</a> of
             the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973.
</p>

<p id="p_6">                      Accordingly, we direct that in the event of arrest,
             the petitioner shall be released on bail upon furnishing a
             bond of Rs. 3,000/- (Rupees Three thousand only), with one
             surety    of   like   amount,   to    the   satisfaction   of   the
             arresting officer and subject to conditions as laid down
             in sub-section (2) of <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_7">Section 438</a> of the Code of Criminal
             Procedure, 1973.
</p><p id="p_7">        The   application   for   anticipatory   bail   is,   thus,
disposed of.
</p>

<p id="p_8">                    (Pranab Kumar Chattopadhyay, J.)


                             (Sudip Ahluwalia, J.)
 </p>
