<div class="doc_title">Sharad Kapoor vs Sharan Pal Singh on 30 August, 2011</div><pre id="pre_1">CRR No.1970 of 2011 (O&amp;M)                                          -1-



     IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA
                   AT CHANDIGARH

                                CRR No.1970 of 2011 (O&amp;M)
                                Date of Decision : 30th August, 2011

Sharad Kapoor
                                                                   .... Petitioner
                                    Versus
Sharan Pal Singh
                                                                 .... Respondent

CORAM : HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE L. N. MITTAL.

Present:     Mr. Naveen Mahajan, Advocate for the petitioner.

                                    ****
L.N.MITTAL, J. (ORAL)
</pre>
<p id="p_1">CRM No.46349 of 2011

             For   reasons   mentioned       in   the   application,     which   is

accompanied by affidavit, and adopting liberal approach, delay of 96 days

in filing the revision petition is condoned.

CRM No.46350 of 2011

             Allowed as prayed for.
</p>
<p id="p_2">Main Case

             Petitioner filed criminal complaint (Annexure P-1) at Amritsar,

against respondent (resident of Gurgaon) and two others (residents of New

Delhi) under Sections 323, 504, and 506 read with <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_1">Section 34</a> IPC.

Learned trial Magistrate vide order dated 11.08.2009 Annexure P-2 ordered

summoning of all the three accused for the aforesaid offences. Accused

No.1 respondent challenged the said summoning order by filing revision

petition before Sessions Court. Learned Additional Sessions Judge (Ad

hoc), Amritsar vide impugned judgment dated 01.03.2011 Annexure P-3
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> CRR No.1970 of 2011 (O&amp;M)                                       -2-</span>


has partly allowed the revision petition and after setting aside the

summoning order, the case has been sent back to the Court of Judicial

Magistrate Ist Class, Amritsar, to pass order in accordance with law and

after complying with the mandatory provisions of <a href="/doc/1149595/" id="a_2">Section 202</a> of the Code

of Criminal Procedure (in short 'Cr. P. C'). Feeling aggrieved, complainant

has filed the instant revision petition.
</p>
<p id="p_3">              I have heard learned counsel for the petitioner and perused the

case file.
</p>
<p id="p_4">              Learned counsel for the petitioner vehemently contended that

summoning order Annexure P-2 was passed by the trial Magistrate after

complying with provisions of <a href="/doc/1149595/" id="a_3">Section 202</a> Cr. P.C. i.e. after holding

enquiry.     The contention cannot be accepted.      Accused named in the

complaint are residing at places beyond the territorial jurisdiction of the

trial Magistrate. Consequently, in view of mandatory provision of <a href="/doc/1149595/" id="a_4">Section

202</a> Cr. P.C., it was mandatory for the trial Magistrate to either enquire into

the case himself or to direct an investigation to be made by a police officer

or by such other person as the Magistrate deems fit, to find out whether or

not there is sufficient ground for proceeding against the accused where he

resides outside his area of jurisdiction.   The said enquiry is to be made

after the complainant and his witnesses have been examined on oath under

<a href="/doc/444619/" id="a_5">Section 200</a> Cr. P. C. In the instant case, however, the Magistrate passed

the summoning order Annexure P-2 just after recording statements of

complainant and his witnesses in preliminary evidence, but without holding

any further enquiry as per aforesaid mandatory provision of <a href="/doc/1149595/" id="a_6">Section 202</a> Cr.

P.C.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_2"> CRR No.1970 of 2011 (O&amp;M)                                            -3-</span><p id="p_5">


</p><p id="p_6">             For the reasons aforesaid, I find no illegality in the impugned

judgment of the learned Additional Sessions Judge so as to warrant

interference by this court in exercise of revisional jurisdiction.

</p><p id="p_7">             Instant revision petition lacks any merit and is accordingly

dismissed in limine.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                                                     ( L. N. MITTAL )
                                                           JUDGE
30th August, 2011
    'raj'
