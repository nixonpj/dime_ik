<div class="doc_title">Bati Bai vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 18 July, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                                           1                             MCRC-22118-2018
          The High Court Of Madhya Pradesh
                    MCRC-22118-2018
                             (BATI BAI Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)


Jabalpur, Dated : 18-07-2018
       Shri Sharad Verma, learned counsel for the applicant.
       Shri Devendra Shukla, learned Panel Lawyer for Respondent-State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">       Arguments heard. Case diary perused.
</p><p id="p_2">       This is first application under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> of Criminal Procedure Code,
1973 filed by the applicant for grant of anticipatory bail.
</p><p id="p_3">       The applicant is apprehending her arrest in connection with Crime
No.301/2018 registered at Police Station Pathariya, District Damoh (MP) for
the offence punishable under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_2">Sections 498-A</a>, <a href="/doc/92983/" id="a_3">306</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_4">34</a> of IPC.
</p><p id="p_4">       As per the prosecution story, the applicant is mother-in-law of
deceased Meena @ Devki. Marriage between deceased Meena and her
husband Devendra Singh was solemnized 9 years ago and after their marriage,
applicant was residing separately with the deceased. It is further contended
that on the same ground other co-accused has been released from this
Court. The applicant is ready to cooperate in the investigation and trial. She is
also not having any criminal antecedent. In the event of arrest, reputation will
be tarnished, therefore, on the ground of parity, applicant is entitled to be
released on anticipatory bail.
</p><p id="p_5">       Learned Panel Lawyer for the respondent/State has vehemently
opposed the application.
</p><p id="p_6">       On due consideration of the facts and circumstances of the case,
without commenting upon the merits of the case, this Court deems it
appropriate to grant anticipatory bail to the applicant. On the similar ground
co-accused has been released on bail vide order dated 31.05.2018
passed in M.Cr.C. No.19529/2018. Consequently, it is hereby allowed
on the ground of parity. In the event of arrest, applicant-Bati Bai shall be
released on bail on her furnishing personal bond in the sum of Rs. 30,000/-
(Rs. Thirty Thousand only) with solvent surety of like amount to the
satisfaction of the Arresting Officer. She is directed to join the investigation
immediately and fully cooperate with the investigating agency and the trial.
</p><p id="p_7">       Conditions of <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_5">Section 438(2)</a> of Cr.P.C. shall apply on the applicant
during the currency of bail.
</p><p id="p_8">       CC as per rules.
</p>
