<div class="doc_title">Yadu Nandan Mishra vs State Of Bihar &amp; Anr on 29 September, 2010</div><pre id="pre_1">                  IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                              Cr.Misc. No.24136 of 2009
                                YADU NANDAN MISHRA
                                           Versus
                               STATE OF BIHAR &amp; ANR
                                         -----------
</pre>

<p id="p_1">2.    29.9.2010             It has been submitted that the petitioner as a Field

                   Officer took action against the opposite party no.2, who had

                   defaulted as a loanee against whom a certificate of

                   proceeding was also initiated, where after the present

                   complaint was filed on trumped up charges.

</p><p id="p_2">                            Issue notice to the opposite party no.2 to show

                   cause as to why the application be not admitted/disposed of

                   at the stage of admission itself, for which requisites etc.

                   under registered cover with A/D as well as ordinary process

                   must be filed within 10 days, failing which this application

                   shall stand rejected without further reference to a Bench.

</p><p id="p_3">                            Rule returnable three months.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                            In the mean while, the further proceeding in

                   Complaint case No. 6C of 2008 pending in the court of Sri

                   Sakal Deo Roy, Judicial Magistrate, Ist class, Hilsa shall

                   remain stayed.
</p>
<p id="p_5">


</p><pre id="pre_2">Narendra/                             ( Anjana Prakash, J. )
