<div class="doc_title">South Eastern Coalfields Limited vs Birhulia on 24 August, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                                              NAFR
                      HIGH COURT OF CHHATTISGARH, BILASPUR

                                Writ Appeal No.225 of 2016

            1. South Eastern Coalfields Limited through its Chief Managing Director
               (S.E.C.L. Head Office), Seepat Road, Bilaspur, Post &amp; P.S. Sarkanda,
               Tahsil Bilaspur, Revenue and Civil District Bilaspur, Chhattisgarh
               495006
            2. South Eastern Coalfields Limited through its Chief General Manager
               (Bishrampur Area), Post &amp; P.S. Bishrampur, Tahsil Surajpur, Revenue
               &amp; Civil District Surajpur, Chhattisgarh
            3. Personnel Manager (Rehar, Gayatri, Jai Nagar Sub Area) Tahsil
               Surajpur, Revenue and Civil District Surajpur, Chhattisgarh
            4. Sub Area Manager, Jai Nagar Sub Area, Tahsil Surajpur, Revenue and
               Civil District Surajpur, Chhattisgarh
                                                                      ---- Appellants
                                          versus
            1. Birhulia, W/o Late Shri Sujaan Ram, aged about 60 years,
            2. Parmeshwar, S/o Late Sujaan Ram, aged about 35 years,
               Both resident of Village Pachira, P.S. and P.O. Surajpur, Tahsil
               Surajpur, Revenue and Civil District Surajpur, Chhattisgarh
                                                                   ---- Respondents</pre>

<p id="p_1">       For Appellants                      :          Shri Vivek Chopda, Advocate
       For Respondents                     :          Shri Surfaraz Khan, Advocate


                        Hon'ble Shri Deepak Gupta, Chief Justice
                          Hon'ble Shri Justice P. Sam Koshy

                                       Order on Board

       Per Deepak Gupta, Chief Justice
24.8.2016
</p><p id="p_2">            1. By means of this writ appeal the Appellants have challenged the

               judgment of the Learned Single Judge dated 19.2.2016 passed in Writ

               Petition (S) No.585 of 2015, whereby the writ petition was allowed and

               the Petitioners/Respondents herein were held entitled to monetary

               compensation.
</p>
<p id="p_3">
</p><p id="p_4">            2. We had asked the parties to produce the scheme under which the

               monetary compensation is payable. A photo copy of the scheme is

               now placed before us.
</p>
<p id="p_5">
</p><p id="p_6">            3. The only point raised before us is that the monetary compensation,

               wherever applicable, is payable till the female dependant attains the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                            2</span>



           age of 60 years.
</p>
<p id="p_7">
</p><p id="p_8">        4. Paragraph 10 of the impugned judgment clearly says that the payment

           of monetary compensation shall be continued as permissible under

           NCWA-VI.
</p>
<p id="p_9">
</p><p id="p_10">        5. We fail to understand why the South Eastern Coalfields Limited

           thought it fit to file the instant appeal in the present manner. This is

           the third case which has come to the notice of this Court where the

           South Eastern Coalfields Limited has filed totally frivolous case.


</p><p id="p_11">        6. Accordingly, we dismiss the instant writ appeal. Cost of Rs.10,000/-

           (Rupees Ten Thousand) is imposed upon the Appellants/South

           Eastern Coalfields Limited for filing a frivolous case, i.e., the instant

           writ appeal. The cost is payable to the High Court Legal Services

           Committee within one month from today.
</p>
<p id="p_12">
</p><p id="p_13">        7. A copy of this order be sent to the Chief Managing Director, South

           Eastern Coalfields Limited, Head Office, Seepat Road, Bilaspur with a

           direction to ensure that cases are not filed by the South Eastern

           Coalfields Limited on false and frivolous grounds.




</p><pre id="pre_2">                   Sd/-                                               Sd/-
              (Deepak Gupta)                                    (P. Sam Koshy)
              CHIEF JUSTICE                                          JUDGE

Gopal
 </pre>
