<div class="doc_title">Ahar Sk vs Unknown on 4 April, 2011</div><p id="p_1">4.04.2011

                                    CRM No. 2300 of 2011

            In Re:- An application for bail under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of the Code
            of Criminal Procedure filed on 10th March, 2011 in connection
            with Krishnagar Kotwali    P.S. Case No. 327/07 dated 9.8.07
            under <a href="/doc/1279834/" id="a_2">Sections      376(g)</a> of the I.P.C.
</p><p id="p_2">                                And
            In the matter of:- Ahar Sk.</p><pre id="pre_1">
                                                    Petitioner
            Mr.Prabir Majumder
                                           For the petitioner

            Mr. Asimesh Goswami,
            Ms. Rita Dutta                     for the State

                 Heard    the   learned   Counsel   appearing   on   behalf   of   the

</pre><p id="p_3">            parties. Perused the case papers.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                 The petitioner is seeking bail in connection with a case

            relating to the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1279834/" id="a_3">Section 376(g)</a> of

            the I.P.C. which was registered vide Krishnagar Kotwali P.S.

            Case No. 327/07.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                 This is a case where the petitioner is in custody for

            about three years and already the recording of evidence has

            been commenced.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                 The learned Counsel for the petitioner made over to us

            the deposition of the complainant victim girl and pointed out

            that during the trial the petitioner has not been identified

            and she was declared hostile. It is also submitted that one of

            the accused persons against whom similar allegation was made

            during investigation has been granted bail by this court.

</p><p id="p_7">                 This position has not been disputed by the learned Public

            Prosecutor.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                      2</span><p id="p_8">




     In view of above, in our opinion, this is a case where

petitioner's   prayer   for   bail   can    be   considered   favourably.

Accordingly, prayer for bail is allowed.

</p><p id="p_9">     Let the petitioner be released on bail upon furnishing

P.R. Bond of Rs.10,000/- with one local             surety of the like

amount and shall not commit any offence               while on bail or

tamper with the prosecution case.
</p>
<p id="p_10">     The bail application is, thus, disposed of.



</p><p id="p_11">                                           (J. N. Patel, Chief Justice)



                                                  (Ashim Kumar Roy, J.)
