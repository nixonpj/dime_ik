<div class="doc_title">Wpcrl No. 473 Of 2013 vs Mr. M.A.Khan on 27 April, 2013</div><pre id="pre_1">WPCRL No. 473 of 2013
Hon'ble U.C.Dhyani, J.
</pre><p id="p_1">        Mr. Deepak Sharma, Advocate,
present for the writ petitioner.
</p><p id="p_2">       Mr. M.A.Khan, learned AGA, present
for the State/ respondents no. 1 &amp; 2.
</p><p id="p_3">        Issue notice to respondent no. 3. Steps
be taken within a week.
</p><p id="p_4">        List after the notice is served upon the
said respondent.
</p><p id="p_5">       When the criminal writ petition of
Satnam Singh and Meenakshi Arora was
taken up for hearing on 16.04.2013, allegedly
abducted girl Meenakshi Arora was present in
person along with co-petitioner Satnam.
Meenakshi said that she married with Satnam
Singh on 16th March, 2013 on her own
volition. She said that according to high
school certificate, her date of birth is 18.
05.1993 and said certificate is in the custody
of her parents. The certificate was of not
much importance, in as much as, the first
information report itself indicated that
Meenakshi Arora was aged about 18 years
when the incident took place. Present
petitioner is shown as wife of Satnam Singh
in the FIR. Paramjeet Kaur (present
petitioner) should also be granted interim
protection.
</p><p id="p_6">        It is provided as an interim measure
that no coercive steps shall be adopted to
enforce the attendance of the petitioner before
the police or the I.O., provided she cooperates
with the Investigating Agency, till the next
date of listing.
</p><p id="p_7">                             (U.C.Dhyani, J.)
                               27.04.2013
Kaushal
