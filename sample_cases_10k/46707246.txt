<div class="doc_title">Rupinder Kaur And Others vs Jasjit Singh Sandhu on 28 January, 2013</div><pre id="pre_1">CRM-M No. 407 of 2013(O&amp;M)                        1

      IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANAAT
                   CHANDIGARH

                          CRM-M No. 407 of 2013(O&amp;M)
                          Date of Decision: 28.01.2013


Rupinder Kaur and others                                 ........Petitioners

            versus

Jasjit Singh Sandhu                                      ......... Respondent


CORAM: Hon'ble Mr. Justice Ajay Tewari

Present: Mr. Sanjeev Sharma, Advocate
         for the petitioners.
             ****
</pre>
<p id="p_1">1. Whether Reporters of local papers may be allowed to see the judgment?
</p><p id="p_2">2. To be referred to the Reporters or not?
</p><p id="p_3">3. Whether the judgment should be reported in the Digest?

Ajay Tewari, J. (Oral):
</p>
<p id="p_4">            This is a petition against an order declining permission to lead

additional evidence.
</p>
<p id="p_5">            The petitioner had filed the instant application under <a href="/doc/1056396/" id="a_1">Section

125</a> Cr.P.C. in the year 2006. After taking numerous opportunities she had

closed her evidence in 2011. After the respondent-husband could lead his

evidence,the instant application was moved praying for permission to

summon the following witnesses:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">            (A) Concerned official of Jones Lang LaSalle(JLL),304, 3rd
            Floor, SCO No. 120-122, Sector 17-C, Chandigarh along with
            complete record of the respondent.
</blockquote>

<blockquote id="blockquote_2">            (B) Concerned official of Government Medical College,
            Sector 32 Chandigarh along with complete record of petitioner
            Rupinder Kaur.
</blockquote><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> CRM-M No. 407 of 2013(O&amp;M)                        2</span>

<blockquote id="blockquote_3">             ( C ) Secretary of Army Welfare Society with complete record
             pertaining to Flat No. 123, Army Welfare Society, Sector 4,
             Mansa Devi Complex, Panchkuala.
</blockquote>

<blockquote id="blockquote_4">             (D) Mrs. Asha Aggarwal r/o Flat No. 141, Army Welfare
             Society, Sector 4, Mansa Devi Complex, Panchkula.</blockquote><p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">              This application has been rejected by the trial court on the

ground that it was for the petitioner to have alleged or proved the source of

income of the husband but neither any pleadings were made nor any

evidence was lead at that relevant time. Learned counsel for the petitioner

has very fairly accepted that necessary pleadings with regard to the evidence

now sought to be lead were not made in the petition.

</p><p id="p_8">             In this view of the matter no fault can be found in the order of

the trial Court.
</p>
<p id="p_9">             Petition is dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_10">             Since the main case has been decided, the pending Civil Misc.

Application, if any, also stands disposed of.



</p><p id="p_11">                                                  (AJAY TEWARI)
                                                      JUDGE

January 28 , 2013
sunita
