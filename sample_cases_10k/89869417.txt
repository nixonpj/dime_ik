<p id="p_1">(Ct. no. 11)
  ARDR

                                          CRM 3582 of 2014
                                                  +
                                          CRAN 2890 of 2014
               In Re: An application for anticipatory bail under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> of the Code
               of Criminal Procedure filed on 27th February, 2014 in connection with
               Ratua P.S. Case no. 256/2013 dated 04/5/2013 under <a href="/doc/1868826/" id="a_2">Sections
               498(A)</a>/<a href="/doc/140515/" id="a_3">313</a>/<a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_4">307</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_5">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code read with Section 4 of D.P.
               Act.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                                   And
               In the matter of: Emam Hossain &amp; Anr.
</p><p id="p_3">                                                               ...Petitioners.
</p><p id="p_4">               Mr. Musharraf Alam Sk.,
                                                         ...for the Petitioners.
</p>
<p id="p_5">               Mr. Tapandeb Nandi,
               Ms. Manasi Roy,
                                                         ...for the State.
</p><p id="p_6">ALLOWED

                                           Re : CRAN 2890 of 2014

                     This is an application for recalling of the order dated 01/7/2014

               passed by a Division Bench of this Court dismissing the application for

               anticipatory bail for non-appearance of the petitioners.

</p><p id="p_7">                     After hearing the learned Advocates of both the parties, we are

               satisfied that the learned Advocate for the petitioners was prevented by

               sufficient cause from not appearing in Court on 01/7/2014 when the

               application for anticipatory bail stood dismissed for non-appearance of the

               petitioners.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                     We, therefore, recall the order dismissing the application for

               anticipatory bail for non-appearance of the petitioners and restore the

               application to its original file and number.

</p><p id="p_9">                     The application being CRAN 2890 of 2014, thus stands allowed.

</p><p id="p_10">                                            Re : CRM 3582 of 2014

                     Heard the learned Advocates of both the parties.
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                             2</span>



      Learned Advocate for the petitioners submits that the petitioners

are the parents-in-law and charge sheet has already been submitted and

the husband of the victim has already been granted bail.

</p><p id="p_11">      Considering the aforesaid submissions and also considering the

materials in the case diary produced before us, we are of the opinion that

detention of the petitioners, at this stage, is not necessary.

</p><p id="p_12">      We are, therefore, inclined to allow the prayer of the petitioners for

granting anticipatory bail under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_6">Section 438</a> of the Code of Criminal

Procedure, 1973.
</p>
<p id="p_13">      Accordingly, we direct that in the event of arrest, the petitioners

shall be released on bail upon furnishing a bond of Rs.3,000/- (Rupees

Three Thousand only) each with one surety of like amount each to the

satisfaction of the arresting officer and subject to the conditions as laid

down under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_7">Section 438(2)</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973.

</p><p id="p_14">      The application for anticipatory bail is, thus disposed of.

      If urgent certified copy of this Order, duly photocopied, is applied for

by the parties, the same should be given expeditiously.



</p><p id="p_15">                             (Pranab Kumar Chattopadhyay, J.)


          &lt;




                                    (Sudip Ahluwalia, J.)
