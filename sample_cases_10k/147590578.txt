<div class="doc_title">Manjit Singh vs State Of Haryana on 16 April, 2009</div><pre id="pre_1">IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB &amp; HARYANA AT CHANDIGARH



                           Criminal Misc. No. 6120 of 2009 (O&amp;M)
                           Date of decision : April 16, 2009


Manjit Singh
                                                ....Petitioner
                           versus

State of Haryana
                                                ....Respondent


Coram:       Hon'ble Mr. Justice Pritam Pal


Present :    Mr. Vikram Sheoran, Advocate for the petitioner
             Mr. SS Pattar, Sr. DAG Haryana


Pritam Pal,J. (Oral)
</pre>


<p id="p_1">             Petitioner has brought this petition under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">section 439</a> of Code

of Criminal Procedure for seeking regular bail in case FIR No. 57 dated

5.4.2008 under <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_2">sections 489-A</a>, B, C, D, E &amp; 420 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_3">IPC</a> registered at Police

Station Siwani District Bhiwani.
</p>
<p id="p_2">             The petitioner is stated to be in custody since 5.4.2008.   The

allegations against him are that he was found to have kept apparatus of

manufacturing the fake currency notes. Not only that, four currency notes

of Rs 1000 each were also recovered from him. The co-accused Sandeep

and Sonu, from whom the recovery of fake currency notes was effected,

have already been granted bail by the court of Sessions itself.          The

prosecution evidence is yet to be concluded in this case. The petitioner is

not facing any other case as fairly admitted by learned State counsel. Thus,

the trial of this case is likely to take a long time.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Criminal Misc. No. 6120 of 2009 (O&amp;M)                             -2-</span><p id="p_3">




</p><p id="p_4">             In the given facts and circumstances and without commenting

on the merits of the case, the petitioner is ordered to be released on bail on

his furnishing bail/surety bonds to the satisfaction of the trial court.

</p><p id="p_5">             Criminal Misc. is disposed of.</p><pre id="pre_2">


                                                         ( Pritam Pal )
April 16, 2009                                                 Judge
  'dalbir'
 </pre>
