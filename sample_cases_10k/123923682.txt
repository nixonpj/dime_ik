<div class="doc_title">Chhathu Sah &amp; Anr. vs The State Of Bihar on 30 November, 2012</div><pre id="pre_1">                   IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                               Criminal Miscellaneous No.33632 of 2012
                 ======================================================
                 1. Chhathu Sah S/O Paltu Sah Resident Of Village- Phulwaria, P.S.-
                 Manjhagarh, District- Gopalganj.
                 2. Dilip Sah S/O Paltu Sah Resident Of Village- Phulwaria, P.S.-
                 Manjhagarh, Distt.- Gopalganj.

                                                                       .... ....    Petitioners.
                                                   Versus
                 The State Of Bihar

                                                      .... .... Opposite Party.
                 ======================================================
                 s
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE DINESH KUMAR
                 SINGH
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">3   30-11-2012              Heard learned counsels for the petitioners

                 and the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                            The       petitioners        being       the    husband          and

                 husband's      brother      of    the      victim    are    apprehending

                 their arrest in a case registered for the offences

                 punishable under <a href="/doc/1868826/" id="a_1">Sections 498(A)</a> and <a href="/doc/409589/" id="a_2">304(B)</a> of the

                 Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                            The accusation is of killing the sister of

                 the informant after two years of marriage for non-

                 fulfillment of dowry demand.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                            It is submitted by learned counsel for the

                 petitioners          that   the    victim       received          accidental

                 burnt injury while she was cooking and subsequently

                 she died.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                            It is submitted by learned A.P.P. that the

                 victim died due to 100% burnt injury and death has
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> 2        Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.33632 of 2012 (3) dt.30-11-2012</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                  2/2</span>




                  occurred within two years of marriage.

</p><p id="p_6">                               Considering the nature of accusation, this

                  Court is not inclined to grant anticipatory bail to

                  petitioner           no.1     Chhathu        Sah       the   husband   of   the

                  victim.         His       anticipatory             bail      application      is

                  rejected in connection with Manjhagarh P.S. Case No.

                  70 of 2012, corresponding to G.R. No. 1437 of 2012

                  pending         in     the      court       of        the    learned   C.J.M.,

                  Gopalganj.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                               Considering            the     fact        that   there   is     no

                  specific        accusation          against           the petitioner    no.   2

                  Dilip Sah, let petitioner no. 2 except petitioner

                  no.1 be released on bail in the event of arrest or

                  surrender before the learned court below within a

                  period of twelve weeks from today in connection with

                  Manjhagarh P.S. Case No. 70/2012, corresponding to

                  G.R. No. 1437 of 2012 on furnishing bail bond of

                  Rs.10,000/-(Ten Thousand) with two sureties of the

                  like amount each to the satisfaction of the learned

                  C.J.M. Gopalganj, subject to the conditions as laid

                  down under <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_3">Section 438(2)</a> of the Code of Criminal

                  Procedure.
</p>
<p id="p_8">


</p><pre id="pre_2">U.K./-                                      (Dinesh Kumar Singh, J)
