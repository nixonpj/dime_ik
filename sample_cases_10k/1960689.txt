<p id="p_1">1.	Rule.
</p><p id="p_2">Learned A.P.P. Mr.J.K. Shah waives service of notice of Rule for the
respondent-State.
</p> 

 


 


<p id="p_3">2.	The
present application has been moved by the petitioner challenging the
order dated 30.04.2011  passed by the Administrative Office,
Inspector General of Police, State of Gujarat, Ahmedabad who had
dismissed the appeal of the petitioner.
</p> 

 


 


<p id="p_4">3.	The
Superintendent, Rajkot jail has submitted a letter dated 12.05.2011
addressed to the Registrar, Gujarat High Court from which, it
transpires that the petitioner is undergoing life imprisonment at the
Rajkot jail. It further transpires that the petitioner was released
on furlough as per the order dated 23.02.2007 passed by the Inspector
General of Police, Gujarat State, Ahmedabad for a period of 14 days,
however, the petitioner did not report to the jail on the due date
and remained absconding for about 933 days and was thereafter
arrested by the police. Under the circumstances, the petitioner was
imposed the jail punishment by the Superintendent, Central Jail,
Ahmedabad forfeiting the furlough deposit and three furlough leaves
as per the provisions contained in the Jail Manual, <a href="/doc/626516/" id="a_1">Prisons Act</a> and
Gujarat Jail Regulations. The said order was challenged by the
petitioner before the Administrative Officer, Inspector General of
Police by filing appeal, however, the same was rejected as per the
order dated 30.04.2011. The said order has been challenged by the
petitioner before this Court by way of the present application.
</p> 

 


 


<p id="p_5">4.	Having
regard to the conduct of the petitioner that he did not report to the
jail authority on due date and remained absconding for a period of
933 days, the jail punishment imposed by the jail authority could not
be said to be harsh or disproportionate which would require
interference of this Court.
</p> 

 


 


 


 


<p id="p_6">5.	There
being no substance in the present application, the same deserves to
be dismissed and is accordingly dismissed. Rule is discharged.
</p>
