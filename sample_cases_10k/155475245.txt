                              JUDGMENT
</pre>
<p id="p_1">       The property was originally mortgaged by the defaulter with the

second respondent Bank and by virtue of the default, the Bank

proceeded with steps under the <a href="/doc/52229129/" id="a_1">SARFAESI Act</a> leading to sale of the

property. Pursuant to the private sale of the property, the petitioner

satisfied the entire sale consideration of  23,11,000/- and Ext.P2 Sale

Certificate was issued by the Bank in this regard. In the course of

further steps to have Ext.P2 registered by the first respondent, the

request was not accepted referring to some attachment ordered by

the Civil Court over the property.        This made the petitioner to

approach this Court by filing this writ petition.

</p><p id="p_2">       2. Heard the learned counsel for the petitioner, learned Standing

Counsel for the 2nd respondent Bank and the learned Government

Pleader for the first respondent.
</p>
<p id="p_3">       3. In the course of the proceedings, the petitioner produced a

copy of the Encumbrance Certificate as Ext.P3 along wit I.A. No. 17456

of 2013 which shows that the attachments were ordered by the Civil

Court only on a subsequent date, which cannot place any bar with

regard to the rights created on the Bank already by virtue of the

mortgage and in turn, the rights conveyed to the petitioner as per

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">W.P.C. No. 30613 of 2013             -2-</span>

Ext.P2. The issue is squarely covered by the verdict rendered by this

Court in <a href="/doc/186619410/" id="a_2">Housing Development Finance Corporation v. Sub

Registry Officer</a> (2011 (3) KLJ 561).
</p>
<p id="p_4">      4. In the above circumstance, the first respondent is directed to

register the original of Ext.P2 as and when the same is produced, upon

satisfying the requirements in accordance with the relevant provisions

of the <a href="/doc/1489134/" id="a_3">Registration Act</a>/Rules. In view of the fact that the petitioner

had approached this Court by filing this writ petition as early as on

10.12.2013 and further since, the prescribed period of 'four months'

will be expiring tomorrow, it shall not be a bar to cause registration of

the document, if the same is presented within a further period of 'one

week' from the date of receipt of a copy of this judgment.           The

petitioner shall produce a copy of the judgment along with a copy of

the writ petition before the first respondent for further steps.

</p><p id="p_5">       The writ petition is disposed of.
</p>
