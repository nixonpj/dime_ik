<div class="doc_title">Jashvantbhai Babulal Patel vs Municipal Commissioner &amp; on 7 October, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">        C/SCA/11735/2014                                  ORDER




         IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

           SPECIAL CIVIL APPLICATION NO. 11735 of 2014

================================================================
            JASHVANTBHAI BABULAL PATEL....Petitioner(s)
                            Versus
           MUNICIPAL COMMISSIONER &amp; 1....Respondent(s)
================================================================
Appearance:
MR GAURAV K MEHTA, ADVOCATE for the Petitioner(s) No. 1
================================================================

        CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE K.M.THAKER

                           Date : 07/10/2014


                            ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">1.   When   the   matter   is   called   out   and   taken   up 

for hearing, learned advocate for the petitioner 

is   not   present.     However,   Mr.   Kaushal   S.   Jani, 

learned advocate for Mr. Mehta, learned advocate 

for the petitioner is present.   
</p>
<p id="p_2">

</p><p id="p_3">2.   The   dispute   raised   in   the   petition   is   with 

regard   to   the   allegedly   illegal   construction 

which   is   said   to   have   been   put   up,   within   the 

precincts of the society, by some of the members 

of a housing society.  
</p>
<p id="p_4">
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                               Page 1 of 9</span>
</p><p id="p_5">         C/SCA/11735/2014                                    ORDER




</p><p id="p_6">3.   It   is   claimed   that   some   of   the   owners   of 

residential houses have put up some constructions 

over the internal road of the society. 



</p><p id="p_7">4.   It   is   further   alleged   that   the   said 

construction   is   unauthorised   and   it   is   put   up 

without   obtaining   permission   from   the   competent 

authority   and   it   is   actually   encroached   over 

internal road.  
</p>
<p id="p_8">

</p><p id="p_9">5.   With   such   allegations   the   petitioner   has 

prayed, inter alia, that:
</p>
<p id="p_10">
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">     "7(B)       Your   Lordships   may   be   pleased   to   issue 
     appropriate writ, order or direction for quashing and 
     setting aside the order dated 11.06.2014 passed by the 
     Deputy   Municipal   Commissioner   and   further   may   be 
     pleased to hold the same as bad in law."
</blockquote>

<p id="p_11">6.   To justify the request made in the petition, 

the petitioner has averred, inter alia, that:


</p><blockquote id="blockquote_2">     "3.1          The  petitioner  is   the  residence  of  Swastik 
     Society,   Opp.   Gujarat   High   Court,   Chandlodia, 
     Ahmedabad.     There   are   in   total   47   units/tenements   in 
     the   said   society.     Originally   there   was   no   compound 
     wall   surrounding   the   tenements   of   the   society. </blockquote><p id="p_12">
</p><p id="p_13">     Subsequently,   without   obtaining   any   valid   permission 
     from   competent   authority   several   residence   of   the 



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                Page 2 of 9</span>
    C/SCA/11735/2014                                    ORDER


society constructed compound wall on the society road 
moreover   residence   of   the   same   society   constructed 
water tanks and toilets in that margin area, therefore, 
the   petitioner  made   application   dated   15.06.2009  into 
the Swagat Online Program and narrated the facts about 
the   unauthorized  construction  of   compound  wall,  water 
tank and toilet made by the residents of the society. 

3.2    It is respectfully stated and submitted that, the 
District Collector, Ahmedabad, relegated the matter to 
the   Ahmedabad   Urban   Development   Authority   to   take 
required   steps.     It   is   submitted   that,   the   Ahmedabad 
Urban   Development   Authority   forwarded   the   said 
application   to   the   Ahmedabad   Municipal   Commission   as 
the   area   of   the   society   is   incorporated   into   the 
territorial   jurisdiction   of   Ahmedabad   Municipal 
Corporation. 
</p>
<p id="p_14">3.3    It is respectfully stated and submitted that, the 
show cause notice under Sec. 260(1) came to be  issued 
by   the   Deputy   Town   Development   Officer   on   22.07.2009 
upon the petitioner who was having tenement No.46.  It 
is   pertinent   to   note   that,   the   Town   Development 
Officer,   society   on   22.07.2009   and   approached   the 
petitioner   and   requested   to   co­operate   in   the   legal 
procedure   as   the   petitioner   also   constructed 
unauthorized compound wall, water tank and toilet.   It 
is   pertinent   to   note   petitioner   being   law   abiding 
citizen   co­operated   the   officers   and   then   the   Sketch 
work   was   prepared   by   the   officer   wherein   the 
unauthorized   construction   was   marked.     Thus,   on 
22.07.2009   the   Town   Development   Officer   prepared   the 
Sketch work of the Bungalow No.41 to 47 and concluded 
that, there are unauthorized constructions made by the 
residents of the society as shown in the Sketch work.  

3.4   It is respectfully stated and submitted that, in 
the show cause notice it was instructed to demolish the 
unauthorized construction but, or to submit appropriate 
evidence   with   regard   to   validity   of   the   said 
construction.   However, this was not complied with the 
residents of the society and therefore, the order dated 
29.07.2009 came to be issued wherein the unauthorized 
construction   made   by   the   society   was   directed   to   be 
demolish   within   period   of   3   days.     Unfortunately, 
inspite of the said order the unauthorized construction 
was not demolished by the said society.

3.5    It is respectfully stated and submitted that, the 
petitioner made an application dated 28.06.2012 to the 
chairman/secretary of the society and requested to take 
appropriate   action   with   regard   to   demolish   the 
unauthorized construction made by the said society.  It 
was   further  requested  to   provide  relevant   information 



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                           Page 3 of 9</span>
         C/SCA/11735/2014                                    ORDER


     about whether the Chairman/Secretary of the society has 
     power   to   permit   the   resident   to   construct   compound 
     wall, water tank and toilet on the road of the society. 

     3.6   It is respectfully stated and submitted that, the 
     Chairman/Secretary   by   a   letter   dated   29.06.2012 
     informed the petitioner informed that, the association 
     is not competent to give any permission for any type of 
     additional construction, which is unauthorizedly put by 
     the members. 
</p>
<p id="p_15">     3.7    It is respectfully stated and submitted that, the 
     petitioner   on   05.11.2012   and   on   19.08.2013   requested 
     the   adjoining   resident   to   demolish   the   unauthorized 
     compound   wall,   water   tank   and   toilet   constructed   by 
     him.  It is most pertinent to note that, initially the 
     resident   of   the   society   were   agreed   to   demolish   the 
     unauthorized   construction,  however   the   same   was   not 
     implemented for reasons best known to them.

     3.8   It is respectfully stated and submitted that, the 
     petitioner   lastly,   made   an   application   before   the 
     District   Collector,   Ahmedabad   on   dated   27.08.2013 
     wherein   the   petitioner   narrated   the   whole   event   and 
     facts   regarding   the   unauthorized   construction   along 
     with necessary and relevant documents. 

     3.9   It is respectfully stated and submitted that, the 
     Deputy   Municipal   Commissioner   passed   the   order   dated 
     11.06.2014   wherein   it   was   stated   that,   said 
     unauthorized construction came to be regularized under 
     the provision of GRUDA.  
</p>
<p id="p_16">     3.10 It is respectfully stated and submitted that, the 
     order dated 29.07.2009 passed by the Town Development 
     Officer,   New   West   Zone,   Ahmedabad   Municipal 
     Corporation,   is   as   on   date   remains   wherein   it   was 
     ordered the demolish the unauthorized construction made 
     by the residents of the society.  However, the same is 
     not   yet   complied   with   and   therefore,   appropriate 
     direction may be issued upon the respondent authorities 
     for compliance order dated 29.07.2009."


</p><p id="p_17">7.   From   the   said   details   it   has   emerged,   as 

mentioned   earlier,   that   the   dispute   is   with 

regard   to the alleged  construction   said to  have 

been   put   up   by   some   of   the   members   of   the 



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4">                                Page 4 of 9</span>
        C/SCA/11735/2014                        ORDER



society.    According  to the  petitioner,  the  said 

construction   is   unauthorized   and   illegal   and   is 

in nature of encroachment over internal roads of 

the society.  
</p>
<p id="p_18">

</p><p id="p_19">8.   Meaning   thereby   the   alleged   construction   is 

not over any public road or public property.  



</p><p id="p_20">9.   Moreover, from the documents on record, more 

particularly  at Annexure­I, pages  40 and  41, it 

has emerged that some of the persons, out of the 

persons   who   have   allegedly   put   up   unauthorized 

construction,   had   submitted   applications   under 

the   provisions   of   the  Gujarat   Regularisation   of 

Unauthorised   Development   Act,   2011   and   by   order 

of the competent authority the said construction 

is regularized on payment of impact fee. 



</p><p id="p_21">10. It is not in dispute that the petitioner has 

not challenged the said decision of the competent 

authority   under   the   provisions   of   the   Gujarat 

Regularisation   of   Unauthorised   Development   Act, 

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_5">                          Page 5 of 9</span>
         C/SCA/11735/2014                                 ORDER



2011.
</p>
<p id="p_22">

</p><p id="p_23">11. In this view of the matter, the allegations 

made by the petitioner raise disputed questions, 

e.g.  whether   the other  / concerned  persons   have 

put up the construction after obtaining requisite 

permission   or whether  the  said  persons  had  also 

made   application   under   the   provisions   of   the 

Gujarat           Regularisation              of       Unauthorised 

Development   Act,   2011   and   got   the   construction 

regularized on payment of impact fee, etc.    



</p><p id="p_24">12. Similarly, the response given by the AUDA to 

the Collector also provide reason to believe that 

several disputed questions of facts are involved 

in present petition.  
</p>
<p id="p_25">

</p><p id="p_26">13. According   to   the   petitioner,   the   respondent 

corporation had, vide intimation dated 29.7.2009, 

directed   owner   of   Tenement   No.46   to   remove   the 

unauthorized   construction,   however,   the   said 

owner   of   Tenement   No.46   has   not   removed   the 

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_6">                            Page 6 of 9</span>
        C/SCA/11735/2014                          ORDER



construction   put   up   by   him,   despite   the   said 

permission by the respondent corporation.  



</p><p id="p_27">14. It is, however, not clear as to whether the 

said   owner   of   Tenement   No.46   had   also   made 

application   under   provisions   of   the   Gujarat 

Regularisation   of   Unauthorised   Development   Act, 

2011   or   not   and   as   to   whether   any   order   with 

reference to the said construction (put up by the 

owner   of   Tenement   No.46)   is   passed   by   the 

competent authority or not. 
</p>
<p id="p_28">

</p><p id="p_29">15. Since   the   dispute   raised   by   the   petitioner 

and   allegations   made   by   the   petitioner   raise 

disputed   questions   of   facts,   the   Court   is   not 

inclined to entertain present petition.



</p><p id="p_30">16. Moreover, dispute raised by the petitioner is 

purely private civil dispute and internal dispute 

of the society. 
</p>
<p id="p_31">

</p><p id="p_32">17. Therefore,   the   petitioner   has   remedy 

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_7">                           Page 7 of 9</span>
        C/SCA/11735/2014                        ORDER



available before the Civil Court.  
</p>
<p id="p_33">

</p><p id="p_34">18. When   the   petitioner   seeks   direction   that 

illegal or unauthorised construction put up over 

society's land / internal road by the members of 

the   society   should   be   removed,   then   the 

petitioner   has   remedy   to   take   out   appropriate 

proceedings before Civil Court and writ petition, 

for such purpose, is not proper remedy and a writ 

petition   for   resolution   of   disputes   amongst   the 

members of the society and/or between the society 

and some of the members, i.e. an internal dispute 

does not deserve to be entertained.  
</p>
<p id="p_35">

</p><p id="p_36">19. Having   regard   to   the   fact   that   the   dispute 

raised in the petition is of civil nature and is 

private  dispute  amongst  the members  of the  said 

society,   it   is   required   to   be   adjudicated   and 

decided   by   the   Civil   Court   and   appropriate 

directions to the members of the society can be 

passed   by the learned  Civil  Court,  the  petition 

does not deserve to be entertained.  
</p>
<p id="p_37"><span class="hidden_text" id="span_8">                          Page 8 of 9</span>
</p><p id="p_38">          C/SCA/11735/2014                      ORDER




</p><p id="p_39">20. In   this   view   of   the   matter   and   for   the 

foregoing   reasons,   the   petition   is   disposed   of. 

Orders accordingly.
</p>
<p id="p_40">
</p><p id="p_41">                                            (K.M.THAKER, J.)
Bharat
