<div class="doc_title">Ahmad vs Addi District Judge And Anr on 4 April, 2011</div><pre id="pre_1">    

 
 
 

 IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE FOR RAJASTHAN
BENCH AT JAIPUR

ORDER

S.B. Civil Writ Petition No. 9440/2010

Ahmad 
Vs. 
Additional District Judge No. 6, Jaipur City, Jaipur &amp; Another.



Date of Order:                     04.04.2011


Hon'ble Mr. Narendra Kumar Jain,J.


Mr. J.P. Goyal, Senior Counsel with Ms. Shikha Parnami, for the petitioner.
Mr. R.P. Garg, for the Respondent No. 2.

BY THE COURT:</pre>
<p id="p_1">		Heard learned counsel for the parties.
</p>
<p id="p_2">2.		Admit.
</p>
<p id="p_3">3.		At the request of learned counsel for the parties, arguments were heard finally and writ petition is being disposed off finally. 
</p>
<p id="p_4">4.		Plaintiff has preferred this writ petition challenging impugned order dated 20.05.2010 passed by trial court rejecting his application under Order 8 Rule 9 read with Section 151 C.P.C. for allowing the petitioner to file rejoinder.
</p>
<p id="p_5">5.		Learned counsel for the petitioner submitted that number of new facts were given by the defendant in his written statement, therefore, filing of rejoinder was necessary in the present case and trial court committed an illegality in rejecting application of the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_6">6.		Learned counsel for the Respondent No. 2. submitted that there were no new facts in the written statement, rather the plaintiff wants to insert those facts, which cannot be allowed to be taken on record even by way of amendment in the plaint, therefore, trial court was right in rejecting the application of the plaintiff.
</p>
<p id="p_7">7.		I have considered submissions of learned counsel for the parties.
</p>
<p id="p_8">8.		Trial court has observed that it does not find any new fact in the written statement, therefore, it is not necessary to file rejoinder and consequently, it has dismissed the application.
</p><p id="p_9">9.		A copy of plaint as well as written statement both have also been placed on record.  Para 14 and 15 of the written statement were referred during course of arguments and after considering all the facts and circumstances of the case, I am of the view that trial court committed an illegality in not allowing the application of the petitioner under Order 8 Rule 9 C.P.C.
</p>
<p id="p_10">10.		Consequently, writ petition is allowed.  Impugned order passed by the trial court is set aside.  Application filed by the petitioner under Order 8 Rule 9 C.P.C. is allowed.  Petitioner is permitted to file his rejoinder within a period of three weeks from today.     
</p><p id="p_11">     (Narendra Kumar Jain),J.
</p>







<p id="p_12">Manoj
S.No.65.
</p>
