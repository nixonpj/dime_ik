<div class="doc_title">Gurlal vs State Of Haryana on 15 March, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">         IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB &amp; HARYANA
                      AT CHANDIGARH

                                              CRM-M No. 7578 of 2019
                                              Date of decision : 15.03.2019

Gurlal
                                                                ......Petitioner
            Versus

State of Haryana
                                                                ...Respondent


CORAM : HON'BLE MRS. JUSTICE DAYA CHAUDHARY

Present :   Mr. Amit Choudhary, Advocate for the petitioner.
            Mr. Rajesh K. Sheoran, Addl. A. G., Haryana.
                   ***

DAYA CHAUDHARY, J. (Oral)
</pre>
<p id="p_1">            This petition has been filed by petitioner Gurlal under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section

439</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 for grant of regular bail to him

in case FIR No.158 dated 01.05.2017 under <a href="/doc/1560742/" id="a_2">Sections 302</a>, <a href="/doc/695990/" id="a_3">364</a>, <a href="/doc/386021/" id="a_4">201</a>, <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_5">120-B</a>

of the Indian Penal Code, 1860 and <a href="/doc/42436223/" id="a_6">Section 3</a> of the Scheduled Castes and

the Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities), Act, 1989, registered at

Police Station - Sadar, Tohana, during pendency of the trial.

</p><p id="p_2">            Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that the petitioner is

in custody since 04.05.2017. There are total 10 accused, out of which 9 have

already been released on regular bail. Learned counsel also submits that out

of total 21 prosecution witnesses, 8 witnesses including the material

witnesses have been examined. The complainant and the eye witness have

not supported the case of the prosecution. Trial may take some time to

conclude and no purpose would be served by keeping the petitioner in

custody.
</p>
<p id="p_3">


</p><p id="p_4">                               1 of 2
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">            ::: Downloaded on - 14-04-2019 18:09:24 :::</span>
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2"> CRM-M No. 7578 of 2019                                           -2-</span>



               Learned State counsel has not disputed the custody period and

release of all co-accused on regular bail. It has also not been disputed that

the complainant and eye witness have not supported the case of the

prosecution.
</p>
<p id="p_5">               Heard arguments of learned counsel for the parties and have

also gone through the contents of the FIR and other documents available on

record.
</p>
<p id="p_6">               By considering that the petitioner is in custody since

04.05.2017 and also the fact that out of total 21 prosecution witnesses, 8

witnesses including the material witnesses have been examined and the

complainant and the eye witness have not supported the case of the

prosecution; all co-accused of the petitioner have already been released on

regular bail; trial may take some time to conclude, no purpose would be

served by keeping the petitioner in custody, the present petition is allowed

and the petitioner is directed to be released on regular bail on furnishing

bail/surety bonds to the satisfaction of the trial Court/Duty Magistrate.



</p><pre id="pre_2">15.03.2019                                       ( DAYA CHAUDHARY )
