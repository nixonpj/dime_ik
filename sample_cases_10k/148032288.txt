<div class="doc_title">Ashok Sao @ Ashok Kumar vs The State Of Bihar on 29 November, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">      Patna High Court Cr.M isc. No.45898 of 2017 (3) dt.29-11-2017




                       IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                      Criminal Miscellaneous No.45898 of 2017
                         Arising Out of PS.Case No. -48 Year- 2016 Thana -M AHILA P.S. District- SEKHPURA
                   ======================================================
                   1. Ashok Sao @ Ashok Kumar Son of Dev Narayan Sao, resident of
                   Village- Chordargah, P.S.- Ariyari (Mahila O.P.), District- Sheikhpura.

                                                                                     .... ....   Petitioner/s
                                                            Versus
                   1. The State of Bihar

                                                                 .... .... Opposite Party/s
                   ======================================================
                   Appearance :
                   For the Petitioner/s      : Mr. Ambika Bhagat, Adv.
                   For the Opposite Party/s : Mr. Sri Chandrasen Prasad Singh, A.P.P
                   ======================================================
                   CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE ARVIND SRIVASTAVA
                   ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">3   29-11-2017                        Heard learned Counsel for the petitioners and

                     learned Additional Public Prosecutor for the State.

</p><p id="p_2">                                      This application, for grant of anticipatory bail, arises

                     out of Mahila Police Station Case No. 48 of 2016, disclosing

                     offences under Sections 376 (D) and 506<a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_1"> of the Indian Penal

                     Code</a> and Section 4 of the POCSO Act.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                      Learned counsel for the petitioner has submitted that

                     the petitioner, who is of clean antecedent, is innocent and has

                     not committed any offence. In fact, the allegation of commission

                     of rape upon the victim girl is attributed against co-accused,

                     Sujeet Sao, which is evident from the F.I.R. itself. The police

                     after competition of investigation has submitted final form

                     finding the case to be false against the petitioner. Moreover,
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">             Patna High Court Cr.M isc. No.45898 of 2017 (3) dt.29-11-2017</span>




                           medical report of the victim does not support the allegation of

                           commission of rape upon her, which is evident from annexure-2.

                           Hence, the petitioner deserves the privilege of anticipatory bail.

</p><p id="p_4">                                           Considering the facts and circumstances of the case,

                           let the petitioner, above named, in the event of his arrest or

                           surrender before the Court below within six weeks, be released

                           on bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs. 10,000/- (ten thousand)

                           with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of

                           learned 1st Additional District &amp; Sessions Judge, Sheikhpura, in

                           connection with Mahila Police Station Case No. 48 of 2016,

                           subject to the condition laid down under <a href="/doc/1144664/" id="a_2">Section 438</a> (2)<a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_3"> of the

                           Code</a> of Criminal Procedure.
</p>
<p id="p_5">


</p><p id="p_6">                                                      (Arvind Srivastava, J)
brajesh/-
</p><pre id="pre_2">  U            T
