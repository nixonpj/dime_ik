</p><p id="p_2">         1.    Petitioner   has   challenged   an   order   dated 

         12.05.2008   passed   by   the   Collector,   Banaskantha,   as 

         confirmed   by   the   learned   Additional   District   and 

         Sessions Judge, Palanpur, in Criminal Appeal No.17 of 

         2008.  Brief facts are as under.
</p>
<p id="p_3">
</p><p id="p_4">         2.    Petitioner   is   a   proprietary   concern   and   is 

         engaged   in   the   business   of   repacking,   labeling   and 

         selling mustered seed oil and related businesses.  On 

         28.02.2008,   the   stock   of   such   edible   oil   valued   at 

         Rs.60,572/­   alongwith   a   loading   rickshaw   valued   at 

         Rs.55,000/­ were seized by the competent officer under 

         the provisions of the Essential Commodities Act, 1955 

         ("the Act" for short) and Rules Regulations and Orders 

         issued   under   the   said   Act.     On   10.04.2008,   the 

         Collector, Banaskantha, issued a show­cause notice to 

         the   petitioner   why   for   alleged   irregularities   in 

         transportation   of   such   edible   oil   and   consequent 

         breach   of   the   provisions   of   the   Gujarat   Essential 

         Commodities   (Transportation   by   the   Dealers)   Order, 

         1977 (hereinafter to be referred to as "the Order of 

         1977"   for   short)   and   the   Edible   Oil   Packaging 

         (Regulation) Order, 1998 (hereinafter to be referred 

         to   as   "the   Order   of   1998"   for   short)   should   not   be 


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                        Page 2 of 8</span>

HC-NIC                               Page 2 of 8      Created On Sun Aug 13 17:17:57 IST 2017
                 R/SCR.A/1199/2011                                          JUDGMENT



         confiscated.     The   petitioner   was   asked   to   remain 

         present for personal hearing on 16.04.2008.   In such 

         show­cause notice, following defects were cited.  

         I.   The   driver   could   not   produce   the   bill   for   the 

         goods being carried.
</p>
<p id="p_5">
         II. The   rickshaw   driver   could   produce   only   some 

         writing on the letter­pad of the petitioner concerned 

         which   did   not   contain   the   bill   number   or   the   VAT 

         number.     It   also   did   not   have   the   quantity   of   the 

         goods, the signature at the bottom was illegible.  It 

         would,   therefore,   appear   that   the   petitioner   was 

         transporting the food stuff which was unaccounted and 

         had the intention of disposing it off illegally.   


         III. Upon verification of the details of the quantity 

         given in the letter­pad with the actual stock in the 

         rickshaw, there were discrepancies.  Thus, it could be 

         presumed that the petitioner was not preparing correct 

         bills.  
</p>
<p id="p_6">
</p><p id="p_7">         3.   The petitioner replied to such show­cause notice 

         under reply dated 17.04.2008, in which, besides taking 

         the general defense of no breach having been committed 

         by the petitioner, it was contended that the stock was 


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                                        Page 3 of 8</span>

HC-NIC                               Page 3 of 8      Created On Sun Aug 13 17:17:57 IST 2017
                R/SCR.A/1199/2011                                         JUDGMENT



         loaded   in   the   rickshaw   on   27.02.2008   for 

         transportation, however, the rickshaw could not start 

         because   of   technical   defect.     The   rickshaw   driver 

         undertook   the   journey   in   the   morning   of   28.02.2008 

         after   repairing   the   rickshaw.     He   therefore   at   the 

         time did not have the bill.   The petitioner however 

         did not have any intention to breach any of the legal 

         provisions.  
</p>
<p id="p_8">
</p><p id="p_9">         4.   The   Collector   however,   did   not   accept   such 

         defense.     By   an   order   dated   30.04.2008,   he   ordered 

         confiscation of entire stock of the edible oil.   He 

         however   released   the  rickshaw  from   the  seizure.     In 

         such order, he referred to a previous breach by the 

         petitioner of similar nature, for which, under order 

         dated   19.09.2007,   penalty   of   confiscation   of   50%   of 

         stock  was   imposed.     Referring  to  this   incident,  the 

         Collector concluded that the petitioner was a habitual 

         offender.   Being a second case in a short span, the 

         petitioner   was   visited   with   confiscation   of   100%   of 

         stock of the goods.  
</p>
<p id="p_10">
</p><p id="p_11">         5.   Against   the   said   order   of   the   Collector,   the 

         petitioner   preferred   the   appeal   before   the   District

                R/SCR.A/1199/2011                                         JUDGMENT



         Court.     Before   the   District   Court,   the   petitioner 

         raised three contentions:
</p>
<p id="p_12">         I.   The petitioner had not committed any breach.  The 

         rickshaw driver did not carry the bill since when the 

         stock was loaded, the rickshaw had developed fault and 

         could   not   start.     The   rickshaw   driver   left   next 

         morning after repairing the rickshaw without carrying 

         the bill.
</p>
<p id="p_13">
         II. That   by   virtue   of   Gujarat   Essential   Articles 

         (Licensing   Control   and   Stock   Declaration)   Amendment 

         Order,   2003,   the   edible   oils   including   mustered   oil 

         was deleted from the definition of "food stuffs". The 

         Collector   therefore   had   no   authority   to   levy   any 

         penalty.  
</p>
<p id="p_14">
         III. It   was   pointed   out   that   after   the   Collector 

         passed   the   impugned   order,   the   petitioner   was 

         exonerated in the earlier default case by the learned 

         Additional   Sessions   Judge   by   a   judgment   dated 

         30.09.2008.
</p>
<p id="p_15">            
</p><p id="p_16">         6.  The learned District Judge however, rejected the 

         appeal.  On facts, it was held that the Collector was 

         correct in concluding that the petitioner had breached 


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_5">                                      Page 5 of 8</span>

HC-NIC                             Page 5 of 8      Created On Sun Aug 13 17:17:57 IST 2017
                 R/SCR.A/1199/2011                                          JUDGMENT



         statutory   requirements.     Regarding   deletion   of 

         mustered seed from the definition from "food stuff", 

         it   was   held   that   the   present   proceedings   were   for 

         breach of the Order of 1977 and the amendment Order of 

         2003   did   not   apply   to   such   order.     Allowing   of   the 

         appeal of the petitioner in the earlier case also did 

         not persuade the learned Judge to interfere with the 

         order of the Collector.  
</p>
<p id="p_17">
</p><p id="p_18">         7.   Having heard learned counsel for the parties and 

         having perused documents on record, I have reached to 

         the following conclusions:
</p>
<p id="p_19">
         I.   Insofar as the factual findings are concerned, I 

         have   no   reason   to   interfere.     The   Collector   has 

         considered the defense of the petitioner, but it was 

         found wonting.   The petitioner merely contended that 

         when   the  stock  was   loaded   the  rickshaw,   developed   a 

         fault   and   could   not   start.     After   repairing   the 

         rickshaw,   the   driver   left   next   morning   without   the 

         bill.     The   Collector   as   well   as   the   District   Court 

         examined   such   defense   but   did   not   found   it   valid. 

         There is no perversity in such consideration.  


         II. Regarding non­applicability of the Order of 1977,

         the   amendment   relied   upon   by   the   petitioner   is   in 

         relation  to  the   definition  of  "food   stuffs"   for  the 

         purpose   of  1981  Order   and   the   1977   Order   would 

         remained untouched.  In this respect also, the learned 

         Judge has committed no error.  
</p>
<p id="p_20">
         III. Regarding the third contention of the petitioner, 

         however,   I   find   that   the   Collector   had   imposed   the 

         penalty   of   100%   confiscation   of   the   goods   on   the 

         premise   that   the   petitioner   is   a   habitual   offender. 

         This   was   on   the   basis   that   for   earlier   breach,   the 

         petitioner   was   visited   with   the   penalty   of 

         confiscation   of   50%   of   the   stock.     This   order,   was 

         subsequently set aside by the District Court. Whatever 

         the   reason   for   setting   aside   such   order,   the   fact 

         remains that the penalty order on which the Collector 

         relied   for   imposing   a   harsher   penalty   of   100% 

         confiscation in the present case, does not survive.  


</p><p id="p_21">         8.   In   that   view   of   the   matter,   the   order   for 

         confiscation   of   the   edible  oil   seized   is   reduced   to 

         50%.  The petition is allowed in part.  Orders of the 

         authorities   below   shall   stand   modified   accordingly. 

         The   respondent  shall  release   50%  value  of  the   goods
