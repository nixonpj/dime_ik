<div class="doc_title">Naveen &amp; Others vs U.T. Chandigarh &amp; Another on 15 January, 2013</div><pre id="pre_1">IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB &amp; HARYANA, CHANDIGARH



     Criminal Miscellaneous No.M-36891 of 2012 (O &amp; M)
                       Date of Decision: January 15, 2013




Naveen &amp; others
                                          .....PETITIONERS

                          VERSUS


U.T. Chandigarh &amp; another
                                          .....RESPONDENT</pre>

<pre id="pre_2">                      .     .      .


CORAM:        HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE NARESH KUMAR SANGHI



PRESENT: -    Mr. Dhawal Bhandari, Advocate, for the
              petitioners.

              Ms.   Ashima     Mor,    Advocate,     for
              respondent No.1.

              None for respondent No.2.


                      .     .      .


Naresh Kumar Sanghi, J (Oral)

</pre><p id="p_1">1.            Prayer in this petition filed under Section

482, <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_1">Cr.P.C</a>., is for quashing of FIR No.63, dated

21.3.2011, under <a href="/doc/1154131/" id="a_2">Sections 147</a>, <a href="/doc/770961/" id="a_3">323</a>, <a href="/doc/1289794/" id="a_4">452</a>, <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_5">506</a> read with

<a href="/doc/777535/" id="a_6">Section 149</a>, <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_7">IPC</a>, registered at Police Station, Industrial

Area, Chandigarh, and the consequential proceedings

arising therefrom, on the basis of compromise.
 Crl. Misc. No.M-36891 of 2012                               [2]



</p><p id="p_2">2.                The above said petition came up for hearing

before this Court on 6.12.2012 and the following order

was passed:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">                      "This is a petition for quashing of FIR No.63
                  dated 21.03.2011, under <a href="/doc/1154131/" id="a_8">Sections 147</a>, <a href="/doc/770961/" id="a_9">323</a>,
                  <a href="/doc/1289794/" id="a_10">452</a>, <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_11">506</a> read with <a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_12">Section 149</a> of the Indian
                  Code, registered at Police Station, Industrial
                  Area,   Chandigarh,      and   all  consequential
                  proceedings arising therefrom, on the basis of
                  compromise.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_2">                        Notice of motion for 15.01.2013.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_3">                       The affected parties are directed to appear
                  on 13.12.2012 before the learned trial court,
                  which shall record their respective statements
                  with regard to the compromise and submit its
                  detailed report in that regard on or before the
                  date fixed by this Court."
</blockquote>

<p id="p_3">3.                In compliance of the above order passed by

this Court, a report has been received from the learned

Chief Judicial Magistrate, Chandigarh, with the following

contents :-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_4">                       "In the above said case, vide order dated
                  6-12-2012 passed        by the Hon'ble High
                  Court, parties were directed to appear on 13-12-
                  2012 before this court and this court was
                  required to record their statements with regard
                  to the compromise and submit detailed report in
                  that regard on or before 15-1-2013. However,
                  on 13-12-2012, all the accused i.e. Balwan,
                  Naveen, Sat Kaur, Rajinder Kumar and Ashok
                  Kumar have appeared before the court, but the
                  complainant Satbir did not turn up. As such, the
                  statements of parties could not be recorded and
                  it could not be determined in the absence of
                  statement of complainant as to whether the
                  parties have compromised the matter or not.
                  Report is submitted accordingly.
</blockquote>

<p id="p_4">4.                From the perusal of the report received
 Crl. Misc. No.M-36891 of 2012                        [3]



from the learned Chief Judicial Magistrate, Chandigarh, it

transpires that respondent No.2 - complainant did not

turn up and as such, there is no compromise between the

parties, therefore, there is no force in the present petition

and the same is hereby dismissed.</p><pre id="pre_3">


                                 (Naresh Kumar Sanghi)
January 15, 2013                         Judge
avin
 </pre>
