<div class="doc_title">An Application Under Section 438 ... vs In Re: Raju Bhattacharya And ... on 11 May, 2016</div><p id="p_1"><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                 1</span>




 11.05.2016
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2"> 37</span>
  m.d.
</p>
<p id="p_2">Allowed



                                       C.R.M. 2375 of 2016

              In the matter of: An application under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> of the Code of
              Criminal Procedure affirmed on 22.03.2016 in connection with
              Chanditala Police Station Case No. 58 of 2016 dated 4th February
              2016 under <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_2">Sections 420</a>/<a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_3">406</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p><p id="p_3">                                   And
                 In Re: Raju Bhattacharya and Another
                                                           ... Petitioners
                 Mr. Sandipan Ganguly,
                 Mr. Sirsanya Bandopadhyay,
                 Mr. A.K. Nag

                                                       ...for the petitioners

                Mr. Jayanta Narayan Chatterjee,
                Mr. Apalak Basu
                                          ... for the de facto complainant

                Mr. Aniket Mitra

                                                             ... for the State

                     Apprehending arrest, in connection with Chanditala Police
              Station Case No. 58 of 2016 dated 4th February 2016 under <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_4">Sections
              420</a>/<a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_5">406</a> of the Indian Penal Code, this application has been filed
              under <a href="/doc/1783708/" id="a_6">Section 438</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure.
</p><p id="p_4">                     Defence counsel submits that the petitioners are in no way
              involved in the offence alleged and they have been falsely implicated.
              The transaction took place in 2008. A demand notice was issued in
              2015 and the complaint has been filed in 2016.
</p><p id="p_5">                     Counsel for the State produces the Case Diary and takes us to
              the agreement at page-14 and the certificate issued by the United
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                                      2</span>


Bank of India at page-19 of the Case Diary. Bricks were to be
supplied by the petitioners to the de facto complainant in May, 2008.
Money in respect of such supply was paid in January, 2008. Out of a
sum of Rs. 3 lakhs,      Rs.2 lakhs was paid in cash and Rs.1 lakh was
paid in Cheque. Statements recorded under <a href="/doc/447673/" id="a_7">Section 161</a> Cr.PC. at
pages 6, 7, 8 and 9 of the Case Diary be looked into. Seizure list at
page-13 thereof be also looked into.
</p><p id="p_6">       Having considered the submissions of the parties and on
scrutiny of the Case Diary, sufficient material exists to warrant grant
of anticipatory bail to the petitioners, namely, Raju Bhattacharya and
Soma Bhattacharya.
</p><p id="p_7">       Accordingly, the petitioners, aforesaid, in the event of their
arrest, be released on bail upon furnishing bond of Rs.5,000/-each,
along with two sureties of Rs.2,500/- each, to the satisfaction of the
arresting authority and subject to the conditions imposed under
<a href="/doc/1144664/" id="a_8">Section 438</a> (2) <a href="/doc/445276/" id="a_9">Cr.PC</a>.
</p><p id="p_8">       The application for bail is, thus, allowed and disposed of.
     Certified copy of this order, if applied for, be given to the parties
 on priority basis upon compliance of all formalities.
</p>

<p id="p_9">                                             (Patherya, J.)


                                          (Debi Prosad Dey,J.)
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_4"> 3</span>
