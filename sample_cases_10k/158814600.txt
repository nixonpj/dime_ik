<div class="doc_title">Hardeep Singh And Others vs State Of Punjab And Another on 19 April, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1"> IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB &amp; HARYANA AT CHANDIGARH

                         Criminal Misc.No.M-5687 of 2018 (O&amp;M)
                         Date of decision : 19.04.2018

Hardeep Singh and others
                                                              ... Petitioners

                         Versus

State of Punjab and another
                                                              ... Respondents

CORAM: HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE RAJ SHEKHAR ATTRI

                         ****
</pre>
<pre id="pre_2">Present:    Mr. K.B.S.Mann, Advocate
            for the petitioners.

            Mr. Karanbir Singh, AAG, Punjab.

            Mr. B.P.Singh Dhindsa, Advocate
            for respondent No.2.

                         ****

RAJ SHEKHAR ATTRI, J.(Oral)

</pre><p id="p_1">            By invoking <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C., the petitioners have prayed

for quashing of cross case DDR No.72 dated 27.07.2017, registered under

<a href="/doc/838469/" id="a_2">Sections 452</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_3">323</a>, <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_4">324</a>, <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_5">34</a> IPC in case FIR No.76 dated 27.07.2017,

registered under <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_6">Sections 324</a>, <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_7">341</a>, <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_8">34</a> IPC at Police Station Gidderbaha,

District Sri Muktsar Sahib and proceedings emanating therefrom on the

basis of affidavits with regard to compromise (Annexures P-2) arrived at

between the parties.
</p>
<p id="p_2">            In the present case, the DDR was registered on the statement

of Mehinga Singh son of Bakhtaur Singh. Now, dispute between the parties

has been resolved by way of compromise Annexures P-2.

</p><p id="p_3">            Vide order dated 12.02.2018, the parties were directed to

appear before the trial Court to get their statements recorded with regard to

genuineness of compromise.
</p>
<p id="p_4">
</p><p id="p_5">                                   1 of 2
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                ::: Downloaded on - 07-05-2018 03:39:43 :::</span>
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2"> Criminal Misc.No.M-5687 of 2018 (O&amp;M)                          -2-</span>

             Pursuant thereto, a report has been submitted by the Sub

Divisional Judicial Magistrate, Gidderbaha wherein it has been reported

that statements of the parties have been recorded and they have voluntarily

compromised the matter.
</p>
<p id="p_6">             Counsel for the State and respondent No. 2 have not disputed

that the parties i.e. petitioner and respondent No.2 (complainant) have

arrived at a settlement with an intent to give burial to their differences.

</p><p id="p_7">             Perusal of allegations in the FIR reveals that the present case

squarely falls in the category of cases that can be quashed by the High

Court, in exercise of its inherent power under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_9">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C. Keeping

in view authoritative enunciation of law laid down by Hon'ble the Supreme

Court in '<a href="/doc/69949024/" id="a_10">Gian Singh v. State of Punjab and another</a>', 2012 (4) R.C.R.

(Criminal) 543 and in the light of facts and circumstances discussed

hereinbefore, this Court is of the considered opinion that continuation of

criminal proceedings would amount to abuse of process of law and it is

expedient in the interest of justice that the same are put to an end.

</p><p id="p_8">             For the foregoing reasons, the petition is allowed, DDR No.72

dated 27.07.2017, registered under <a href="/doc/838469/" id="a_11">Sections 452</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_12">323</a>, <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_13">324</a>, <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_14">34</a> IPC in case

FIR No.76 dated 27.07.2017, registered under <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_15">Sections 324</a>, <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_16">341</a>, <a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_17">34</a> IPC at

Police Station Gidderbaha, District Sri Muktsar Sahib and proceedings

emanating therefrom stand quashed qua the petitioners.</p><pre id="pre_3">



                                              ( RAJ SHEKHAR ATTRI)
19.04.2018                                           JUDGE
