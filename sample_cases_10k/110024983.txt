IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA AT CHANDIGARH


                    Criminal Misc. No. M-3631 of 2012 (O&amp;M)
                    Date of Decision: February 28th, 2012


Ranjit Singh
                                                   .... Petitioner

                                Versus

State of Punjab
                                                  .... Respondent

CORAM : HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE VIJENDER SINGH MALIK

1.Whether Reporters of local papers may be allowed to see the judgment?
2.Whether to be referred to the Reporters or not?
3.Whether the judgment should be reported in the Digest?

Present    Mr. Vivek K. Thakur, Advocate,
          for the petitioner.


VIJENDER SINGH MALIK, J.
</pre>
<p id="p_1">          Ranjit Singh has applied for pre-arrest bail in a case registered

by way of FIR No.224           dated 4.12.2011   at Police Station        City

Kapurthala, District Kapurthala for an offence punishable under <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_1">sections

420</a> and <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_2">120-B</a> of Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_2">          The case against the petitioner is that he mortgaged his

80/5580 share in land measuring 279 kanals with P.C.A.D. Bank to raise

a loan of Rs. 1.00 lac. His co-accused Kulwinder Kaur mortgaged her

share in the said land in favour of the said bank and took a loan of

Rs.5.00 lacs. The complainant claimed to have already purchased the

entire share of these persons in the said land vide sale deed dated

18.12.1989 and after the said sale, these persons are claimed to have
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Crl. Misc. No.M-3631 of 2012                                         2</span>
                     ..
</p><p id="p_3">been left with no share in the said land.
</p>
<p id="p_4">           Learned counsel for the petitioner has submitted that it is a

very old matter. According to him, the bank has not made any complaint

in this case. He has, however, admitted that the loan amount still stands

against the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_5">          After the sale of his share in the land as early as on

18.12.1989, the petitioner can only be said to have mortgaged that land

in favour of the bank with a view to cheat the bank. It is true that the

bank has not lodged a complaint but that makes no difference. The

complainant is also an aggrieved person in this case because, the

petitioner tried to encumber his land while raising loan from the bank. No

effort has been made to repay the loan amount and to clear the loan

even after registration of this case. This circumstance alone shows that

the petitioner is not entitled to anticipatory bail.     The petition is,

consequently, dismissed.
</p>
<p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">                                            (VIJENDER SINGH MALIK)
                                                   JUDGE
February 28th, 2012
