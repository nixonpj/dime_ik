                           JUDGMENT
</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Being aggrieved of Exts.P1 and P2 orders passed by the

Commercial Tax Officer (Luxury Tax) Thrissur, the petitioner has

preferred Exts.P5 and P6 appeals, along with Exts.P7(a) and P7(b)

petitions for condoning the delay and Exts.P8(a) and P8(b) petitions

for stay, which are pending consideration before the first

respondent. The grievance of the petitioner is that, without any

regard to the pendency of the above proceedings, the respondents

are proceeding with coercive steps, as borne by Exts.P9(a) and P9

</p><p id="p_2">(b), which hence are sought to be intercepted by this Court.

</p><p id="p_3">      2. Heard the learned Government Pleader as well.

</p><p id="p_4">      3. Considering the facts and circumstances, the first

respondent is directed to consider and pass appropriate orders on

Exts.P7(a) and P7(b) petitions for condoning the delay and Exts.P8

</p><p id="p_5">(a) and P8(b) petitions for stay, in accordance with law, as

expeditiously as possible, at any rate, within 'one month' from the

date of receipt of a copy of this judgment. Coercive proceedings

W.P.(C)No.10039 of 2014
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                     2</span>

pursuant to Exts.P9(a) and P9(b) shall be kept in abeyance till such

time.
</p>
<p id="p_6">     The petitioner shall produce a copy of this judgment along

with a copy of this writ petition before the concerned respondent for

further steps.
</p>
<p id="p_7">     The writ petition is disposed of as above.




</p><p id="p_8">                                                Sd/-
