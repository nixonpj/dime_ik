<p id="p_1">     The     petitioner    in      Criminal       Petition

No.10334/2013     is   arrayed     as   accused     No.1,

petitioner in Criminal Petition No.10351/2013 is

arrayed as accused No.2 and petitioner in Criminal

Petition   No.10350/2013    is   arrayed   as   accused

No.3, in C.C. No.142/2012 registered for offences

punishable under <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_4">Sections 420</a>, <a href="/doc/1328629/" id="a_5">463</a>, <a href="/doc/1745798/" id="a_6">464</a>, <a href="/doc/1317063/" id="a_7">465</a> and
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                  :5:</span>




468 read with <a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_8">Section 149</a> of the Indian Penal

Code.
</p>
<p id="p_2">
</p><p id="p_3">        2. Heard the learned counsel for parties.


</p><p id="p_4">        3.   As per investigation records annexed to

final    report,         petitioners    are   alleged   to   have

manipulated the entries in RTC relating to land

bearing Sy.Nos.146/2A and 146/2B of Alakanur

Village in Raibag Taluk to the detriment of interest

of complainant (respondent No.2 herein).


</p><p id="p_5">        4.         The     learned     counsel   appearing    for

petitioners would submit that the RTC extracts are

obtained           from     KIOSK.         The    extracts    are

downloaded from the computer.                    There was no

scope        for     human        intervention.         In    the

circumstances, the investigation records accepted

on their      face        value do     not constitute   offences

alleged against petitioners.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                :6:</span><p id="p_6">




</p><p id="p_7">       5.    The learned counsel for respondent No.2

has submitted that copies of RTC alleged to have

been forged by petitioners.             The contents of final

report      accepted      on    their    face   value   would

constitute offences alleged against petitioners.


</p><p id="p_8">       6.    If the petitioners feel that there are no

grounds to presume             that they have     committed

offences alleged against them, they are at liberty

to plead for discharge before the trial court.


</p><p id="p_9">       The    petitions    are    dismissed      with   these

observations.
</p>
