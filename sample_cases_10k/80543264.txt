<div class="doc_title">Chandan Kumar vs The State Of Bihar on 21 February, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                     IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                   Criminal Miscellaneous No.9150 of 2019
                 Arising Out of PS. Case No.-214 Year-2018 Thana- PAKRIDAYAL District- East Champaran
                 ======================================================
                 Chandan Kumar (Male), aged about 24 Years, Son of Bharti Bhushan Paswan,
                 Resident of Village - Dih Pakri, P.S. Pakaridayal, District-East Champaran.

                                                                                    ... ... Petitioner
                                                       Versus
                 The State of Bihar

                                                          ... ... Opposite Party
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner     :         Mr.Anuj Kumar, Advocate.
                 For the Opposite Party :         Mr.Prem Kumar Jha, A.P.P.
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE SUDHIR SINGH
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">2   21-02-2019                 A supplementary affidavit has been filed on behalf of

                   the petitioner. Let it be kept on the record.

</p><p id="p_2">                               Learned counsel for the petitioner is permitted to

                   make necessary corrections in paragraph no. 3 of the main bail

                   application, in light of the supplementary affidavit filed on

                   behalf of the petitioner, in course of the day.

</p><p id="p_3">                               Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned

                   A.P.P. for the State and learned counsel for the informant.

</p><p id="p_4">                                The petitioner is apprehending his arrest in a case for

                   the offence registered under <a href="/doc/1868826/" id="a_1">Sections 498(A)</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_2">323</a> and <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_3">504</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_4">34</a> of

                   the IPC and Â¾ of the D.P. Act.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                               The prosecution story, in brief, is that the accused

                   persons including the petitioner tortured the victim due to non-

                   fulfilment of demand of dowry.
</p><p id="p_6"> Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.9150 of 2019(2) dt.21-02-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                             2/3</span>




                       It has been submitted by learned counsel for the

          petitioner that the petitioner has falsely been implicated in the

          present case due to petty family dispute. There is no allegation

          of tampering with the witnesses alleged against the petitioner.

          The petitioner is the husband of the victim. The case is triable

          by the Magistrate. The petitioner has further relied upon the

          judgment of this Court in the case of Md. Naimul Haque Ansari

          @ Naimul Haque Ansari &amp; Ors. Vs. The State of Bihar, reported

          in 2006(3) PLJR 182.
</p>
<p id="p_7">                       On behalf of the learned counsels for the State and

          informant, it has been submitted that the petitioner is named in

          the F.I.R/ complaint case.
</p>
<p id="p_8">                       Considering the aforesaid facts and circumstances, let

          the petitioner above named, in the event of arrest or surrender

          before the learned court below within a period of six weeks

          from today, be released on anticipatory bail on furnishing bail

          bonds of Rs.10,000/- (Ten Thousand) with two sureties of the

          like amount each to the satisfaction of the learned C.J.M. East

          Champaran at Motihari, in connection with Pakaridayal P.S.

          Case No. 214 of 2018, subject to the conditions as laid down

          under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_5">Section 438(2)</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure.

</p><p id="p_9">                       If so advised, either of the parties will be at liberty to
              Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.9150 of 2019(2) dt.21-02-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                          3/3</span>




                       make an application before the court below for referring the

                       matter to the District Mediation Centre for the purpose of

                       reconciliation or one time settlement.
</p>
<p id="p_10">

</p><p id="p_11">                                                     (Sudhir Singh, J)
U.K./-
</p>
