<div class="doc_title">Rajendra Raikwar vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 17 July, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">     THE HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH
                      MCRC-28018-2019
       (RAJENDRA RAIKWAR Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)
                                                                     1

Jabalpur, Dated : 17-07-2019
      Shri R. S. Patel, counsel for the applicant.
      Shri Shiva Hazare, learned P.L. for the respondent/State.</pre>
<p id="p_1">      Heard with the aid of case diary.
</p><p id="p_2">      This is a second application filed under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> Cr.P.C.
Applicant Rajendra Raikwar was arrested on 13.11.2018 in Crime
No. 335/2018 registered at Police Station Jatara, District
Tikamgarh for the offence punishable under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_2">Section 363</a>, <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_3">366</a>, <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_4">376</a>
(2)(n) of the <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_5">IPC</a> and Sections 5/6 of the POCSO Act.
</p><p id="p_3">      Earlier bail application filed by the applicant was dismissed
as withdrawn by this Court vide order dated 23/04/2019 passed in
M.Cr.C.No.13168/2019 with liberty to file afresh after recording
the statement of the prosecutrix.
</p><p id="p_4">      As per the prosecution case, on 19.10.2018, applicant
abducted the prosecutrix, who was minor and took her to Delhi,
where he kept her for 15 days in a hut and sexually exploited her
on the pretext of marriage.
</p><p id="p_5">      Learned counsel for the applicant submits that applicant has
not committed any offence and has falsely been implicated in the
offence. Statements of the prosecutrix and her parents have been
recorded by the trial Court. They did not supported the
prosecution story. Charge-sheet has been filed. The applicant is in
custody since 13.11.2018 and the conclusion of trial is likely to
take long time, hence prayed for release of the applicant on bail.
</p><p id="p_6">      Learned counsel for the State opposed the prayer and
submitted that applicant abducted a minor girl and committed rape
with her, so he should not be released on bail.
</p><p id="p_7">          THE HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH
                         MCRC-28018-2019
         (RAJENDRA RAIKWAR Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                                      2</span>

         Looking to the facts and circumstances of the case and as to
the fact that statements of the prosecutrix and her parents have
been recorded by the trial Court, the applicant is in custody since
13.11.2018, charge-sheet has been filed and conclusion of trial is
likely to take long time, without commenting on the merits of the
case, the application is allowed and it is directed that the applicant
be released on bail upon his furnishing personal bond in the sum
of Rs.50,000/- (Rs. Fifty Thousand Only) with one surety in the
like amount to the satisfaction of the concerned C.J.M/trial Court
for his appearance before the trial Court on all such dates as may
be fixed in this behalf by the trial Court during the pendency of
trial.
</p><p id="p_8">         This order will remain operative subject to compliance of
the following conditions by the applicant :-
</p><p id="p_9">1.       The applicant will comply with all the terms and conditions
of the bond executed by him;
</p><p id="p_10">2.       The applicant will cooperate in the investigation/trial, as the
case may be;
</p><p id="p_11">3.       The applicant will not indulge himself in extending
inducement, threat or promise to any person acquainted with the
fact of the case so as to dissuade him from disclosing such facts to
the Court or to the Police Officer, as the case may be;
</p><p id="p_12">4.       The applicant shall not commit an offence similar to the
offence of which he is accused;
</p><p id="p_13">5.       The applicant will not seek unnecessary adjournments
during the trial; and
       THE HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH
                                       MCRC-28018-2019
        (RAJENDRA RAIKWAR Vs THE STATE OF MADHYA PRADESH)
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                                      3</span>

</p><p id="p_14">6.      The applicant will not leave India without previous
permission of the trial Court/Investigating Officer, as the case may
be.
</p><p id="p_15">        C.C. on payment of usual charges.
</p>
