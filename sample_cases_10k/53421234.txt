<div class="doc_title">Ravi @ Ravindra Surajbali Yadav vs State Of Gujarat &amp; Anr on 18 April, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">     R/CR.MA/7440/2015                                            ORDER




          IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

       CRIMINAL MISC.APPLICATION (FOR TEMPORARY BAIL)
                              NO. 7440 of 2015

===============================================================
     RAVI @ RAVINDRA SURAJBALI YADAV                         ....Applicant
                Versus
     STATE OF GUJARAT &amp; ANR                                  ....Respondents
===============================================================
Appearance:
MR VIRAL J VYAS, ADVOCATE for the Applicant
MR JK SHAH APP for the Respondent State
==========================================================

        CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE PARESH
               UPADHYAY

                           Date : 18/04/2015

                             ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">1.   This is an application for temporary bail.
</p>
<p id="p_2">2.   At the outset, it is recorded that today is a holiday but
considering     the      urgency,      the    matter   is   taken    up      for
consideration and orders. It is also noted that, this application
is already notified for hearing on Monday i.e. 20.04.2015, but it
is pre-poned.
</p>

<p id="p_3">3.   Rule. Learned Additional Public Prosecutor waives service
of notice of Rule on behalf of the State.
</p>

<p id="p_4">4.   The applicant accused is in judicial custody in connection
with the offence being CR- I No.29 of 2014 registered with the
DCB Police Station, Ahmedabad for the offences punishable


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                    Page 1 of 2</span>
        R/CR.MA/7440/2015                                  ORDER


under <a href="/doc/988620/" id="a_1">Sections 406</a>, <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_2">420</a>, <a href="/doc/1317063/" id="a_3">465</a>, <a href="/doc/1985627/" id="a_4">467</a>, <a href="/doc/556166/" id="a_5">468</a>, <a href="/doc/1466184/" id="a_6">471</a> and <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_7">120B</a> of the
Indian Penal Code and <a href="/doc/273571/" id="a_8">Sections 65</a> and <a href="/doc/326206/" id="a_9">66</a> of the Information
Technology Act.
</p>

<p id="p_5">5.      Learned advocate for the applicant states that the
mother of the applicant has died today morning at Mumbai.
</p>

<p id="p_6">6.      Considering the totality, the following order is passed.
</p>

<pre id="pre_2">6.1     This application is allowed.


6.2     The applicant / accused is ordered to be released on
</pre><p id="p_7">temporary bail for a period of two weeks on his executing
personal bond today itself. There is no bar even to leave the
territory of Gujarat.
</p>

<p id="p_8">6.3     The accused shall surrender before the jail authority on
expiry of the stipulated period.
</p>

<p id="p_9">6.4     Rule is made absolute to the aforesaid extent.
</p>


<p id="p_10">6.5     It is stated that the applicant is in Sabarmati Central Jail.
Yadi be sent by the Registry to the concerned Jail through FAX.
Over and above that direct service today is also permitted.
</p>


<p id="p_11">                                             (PARESH UPADHYAY, J.)
M O Bhati
