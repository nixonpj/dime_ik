<div class="doc_title">Mahmood Alam @ Md. Mahmood @ ... vs The State Of Bihar on 3 April, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                              CRIMINAL MISCELLANEOUS No.20523 of 2019
                        Arising Out of PS. Case No.-851 Year-2018 Thana- ARARIA District- Araria
                 ======================================================
                 Mahmood Alam @ Md. Mahmood @ Mahmood, Son of Sawood Alam
                 Resident of Village - Sangora, P.S.- Palasi, Distt.- Araria.
                                                                                ... ... Petitioner/s
                                                      Versus
                 The State of Bihar                                    ... ... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :        Mr.Ramesh Kumar Singh
                 For the Opposite Party/s :        Mr.Khurshid Anwar
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE DINESH KUMAR SINGH
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>
<p id="p_1">2   03-04-2019                   Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned

                   APP for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                                The petitioner is languishing in custody since 23.12.2018

                   in a case registered for the offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1101188/" id="a_1">Sections

                   379</a>/<a href="/doc/812083/" id="a_2">411</a> of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                         The prosecution case is that the informant,

                   Dinesh        Thakur          parked       his      motorcycle          in      the

                   premises of Primary Health Centre, Araria at 11.00 A.M. and at

                   12.30 P.M., he found his motorcycle missing, leading to

                   registration of FIR against unknown. During investigation, the

                   petitioner     was         apprehended     and     made       confession        and

                   consequently the informant's motorcycle was recovered from

                   the possession of the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                  It is submitted by learned counsel for the petitioner

                   that the petitioner is bonafide purchaser of the said motorcycle

                   and he had no knowledge about the motorcycle being stolen
             Patna High Court CR. MISC. No.20523 of 2019(2) dt.03-04-2019
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                        2/2</span>




                      one. A statement has been made in paragraph 3 of the petition

                      that the petitioner is not having any criminal antecedent.

</p><p id="p_5">                                   Learned APP submits that the recovery has been made

                      from the possession of the petitioner and he has made

                      confession.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                   Considering the fact that the investigation has already

                      been concluded and statement being made in paragraph 3 of the

                      petition that the petitioner is not having any criminal antecedent,

                      let the above named petitioner be released on bail, on furnishing

                      bail bond of Rs.10,000/- (Ten thousand) with two sureties of the

                      like amount each to the satisfaction of learned Chief Judicial

                      Magistrate, Araria in connection with Araria (R.S.) P.S. Case

                      No. 851 of 2018.
</p>
<p id="p_7">

</p><p id="p_8">                                             (Dinesh Kumar Singh, J)
DKS/-
</p>
