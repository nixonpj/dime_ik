<div class="doc_title">Sps Metal Cast &amp; Alloys Limited &amp; ... vs State Of West Bengal &amp; Ors on 15 September, 2010</div><div class="doc_author">Author: Jayanta Kumar Biswas</div>

<pre id="pre_1">                                                 1




                          In the High Court at Calcutta
                        Constitutional Writ Jurisdiction
15-09-2010                       Appellate Side
 sb-9
.                             W.P.No.19433(W) of 2010
                        SPS Metal Cast &amp; Alloys Limited &amp; Anr.
                                         -vs-
                             State of West Bengal &amp; Ors.

                    Mr. S.K. Kapoor
                    Mr. Meghajit Mukherjee           .... for the petitioners

                    Mr. S.S. Koley                           ....for DPL


                    The petitioners are questioning the validity of the
             demand made in the bill (at p.105) raised by the Durgapur
             Projects Limited, a licensee under the <a href="/doc/132967048/" id="a_1">Electricity Act</a>, 2003,
             and the decision dated July 6, 2010 (at p.96) on the basis
             whereof the bill has been raised.

                    According to the petitioners, under an incentive
             scheme the petitioners were entitled to several benefits and
             the licensee was under an obligation to give the benefits.

                    The petitioners claimed the benefits and the licensee
             gave the benefits. These all happened in 2001. The licensee
             was told that the petitioners were not entitled to certain
             benefits and acting on such advice it issued the bill
             demanding around Rs.17 crore with interest.

                    Mr Kapoor, counsel for the petitioners, has argued
             that the licensee had no authority to raise the bill. Mr Koley,
             counsel for the licensee, submits that since the competent
             authorities pointed out the mistakes committed by the
             licensee, it became necessary to raise the bill demanding the
             amount.

                    In my opinion, the petitioners have made out a strong
             prima facie case for admission and interim relief. There is
                                     2




substantial force in the submission that the licensee did not
have any authority to demand the amount connected with
the concession benefit given sometime in 2001. Even if the
benefit was given by mistake, it is to be examined whether
the licensee can saddle the petitioners with the liability. In
my opinion, the balance of convenience and inconvenience is
entirely in favour of making a restraining order.

          For these reasons, I admit the petition and stay the
operation of the intimation dated September 6, 2010 (at
p.105).     The respondents shall file opposition within five</pre>
<p id="p_1">weeks; reply, if any, shall be filed by a week thereafter.
</p>
<p id="p_2">          This order shall be communicated to the non-
appearing respondents within a week and affidavit of service
showing compliance shall be filed at the time of final hearing.
Liberty to mention the petition for final hearing.       Certified
xerox.
</p>


<p id="p_3">                                        (Jayanta Kumar Biswas, J.)
