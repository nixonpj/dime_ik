      IN THE COURT OF JITENDRA KUMAR MISHRA ADDITIONAL
                DISTRICT JUDGE-Central-9, DELHI.
</p>

<p id="p_2">Civil Suit No.: 707/08
Unique Case ID No.02401C0322352008.
</p>
<p id="p_3">M/s Kotak Mahindra Bank Ltd.
</p><p id="p_4">Having its registered office at
36-38A, Nariman Bhawan,
227, Nariman Point, Mumbai - 400021.
</p><p id="p_5">Having its Regional Office at:
</p><p id="p_6">G-9, Vikas Puri, New Delhi-110018

Mr. Ashutosh Kumar.                                     ...........Plaintiff

Ajeet Kumar.

              Date of institution of the suit : 22.04.2008.
              Reserved for judgment on : 17.05.2013.
              Date of pronouncement of judgment : 18.05.2013.
</p>
<p id="p_8">SUIT FOR RECOVERY OF RS.3,25,335/- (RS. THREE LACS TWENTY
FIVE THOUSAND THREE HUNDRED THIRTY FIVE ONLY).
</p>

<p id="p_9">EXPARTE-JUDGMENT


</p><p id="p_10">01.           The present suit is of recovery for a sum of Rs. 3,25,335/-
together with cost, pendentelite and future interest filed on behalf of plaintiff
against the defendant.
</p>


<p id="p_11">M/s. Kotak Mahindra Bank Ltd. Vs. Ajeet Kumar                                  Page  1
                                        CS No.707/06

</p><p id="p_12">02.              Brief facts of the case are :-
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">      (a) Plaintiff is a bank and is a body corporate incorporated and registered
          under the <a href="/doc/257409/" id="a_1">Indian Companies Act</a> 1956.              Mr. Anil Panchal, is a
          constituted attorney of the plaintiff who is fully conversant with the
          facts of the case and is authorized by power of attorney               dated
          28.01.2008 passed in his favour to depose the facts on behalf of the
          plaintiff and to sign, verify and file the present suit.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">      (b) Defendant is the borrower, who approached the plaintiff for financial
          assistance to purchase a vehicle namely INDICA under the loan and
          hypothecation scheme of the plaintiff.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">      (c) The request of the defendant was duly considered by the plaintiff and
          a loan of Rs.308000/- on 23.12.2006 was sanctioned.                The loan
          amount along with interest and other charges was repayable by the
          defendant in 59 monthly installments of Rs.7090/- each.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_4">      (d) Defendant in order to secure the said loan, executed Credit Facility
          Application form dated 23.12.2006 in favour of the plaintiff, which was
          accepted vide loan agreement no.LADEL00008969502. Defendant
          also hypothecated the said vehicle and also executed an unattested
          deed of hypothecation and irrevocable power of Attorney.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_5">      (e) The vehicle of the defendant is registered with the registration
          authority vide registration number DL8CM3558.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_6">      (f) After availing the loan from the plaintiff, the defendant failed to adhere
          to the terms and conditions of the loan agreement regarding
          repayment amount either towards principal or interest or charges
          thereon. Several ECS instructions issued by the defendant for the
          repayment were dishonoured or returned unpaid. Several reminders
          were issued to the defendant to pay the amount on account of above
          said dishonour but the defendant failed to adhere to the financial
          discipline and failed to honor his commitments.</blockquote>
<p id="p_13">      (g) Plaintiff   specifically   issued   demand     notice      dated 22.01.2008,


M/s. Kotak Mahindra Bank Ltd. Vs. Ajeet Kumar                                  Page  2
                                    CS No.707/06

         terminating the loan agreement and called upon the defendant to
         repay the entire outstanding amount and return the vehicle which is
         hypothecated to the plaintiff under the agreement.
</p><p id="p_14">      (h) Defendant is neither clearing the outstanding dues of the plaintiff nor
         is replying to the notice, nor has returned the vehicle.
</p><p id="p_15">      (i) As per the accounts maintained by the plaintiff, the defendant is liable
         to pay a sum of Rs.3,25,335/- towards principal amount, interest and
         other charges as per the loan agreement dated 23.12.2006.
</p>

<p id="p_16">03.             By order dated 08.07.2011, application under Order 22 Rule 10
CPC for substitution of the applicant/ M/s. Kotak Mahindra Bank Ltd as
plaintiff was allowed and M/s. Kotak Mahindra Bank Ltd. in place of M/s.
ICICI Bank Limited was substituted as plaintiff.
</p>

<p id="p_17">04.             By order dated 17.04.2013 it was observed that publication
report was received.       Summons issued by the court were published in
"Rashtriya Sahara" dated 29.03.2013. Copy of publication had also been
sent to the last known address of the defendant. Defendant was deemed to
be served through publication. Defendant was proceeded ex-parte by order
dated 17.04.2013.
</p>

<p id="p_18">05.             On 10.05.2013, an application under Section 151 of CPC
seeking substitution of AR as moved by plaintiff was allowed and Sh. Pankaj
Wasan, AR of the plaintiff, was allowed to represent the plaintiff in place of
Sh. Ashutosh Sharma.
</p>

<p id="p_19">06.             Plaintiff led ex-parte evidence of Sh. Pankaj Wasan,
Authorized Representative of the plaintiff on 10.05.2013. He was examined
as PW1 and his evidence by way of affidavit as Ex.PW1/A. In support of the
claim, he also tendered documents in evidence as Ex.PW1/1 to Ex.PW1/3


M/s. Kotak Mahindra Bank Ltd. Vs. Ajeet Kumar                             Page  3
                                   CS No.707/06

(OSR) and Ex.PW1/4 to Ex.PW1/9.
</p>

<p id="p_20">07.          Ex.PW1/1 is copy of the board of Resolution; Ex.PW1/2 (OSR)
and Ex.1/3(OSR) are copies of Deed of Assignments; Ex. PW1/4 is original
Credit Facility Application Form; Ex.PW1/5 is original Unattested Deed of
Hypothecation; Ex.PW1/6 is original Irrevocable ower of Attorney; Ex.PW1/7
is copy of notice dated 05.02.2008; Ex.PW18 is original postal receipt;
Ex.PW1/9 is the statement of account.
</p>

<p id="p_21">08.          I have heard the arguments as advanced by learned counsel
for the plaintiff and have carefully gone through the record of the case.
</p>

<p id="p_22">09.          By order dated 16.08.2011 application under Order 39 Rule 6
CPC for seeking permission of interim sale of vehicle was allowed and
vehicle bearing no.DL-8CM-3558 was permitted to be sold in public auction
after issuing Auction Notice in "Veer Arjun".
</p>

<p id="p_23">10.          Perusal of affidavit dated 04.04.2012 submitted by Ms. Sheetu
Bhat, the then AR of plaintiff, reveals that the plaintiff had sold the vehicle
namely Indica car bearing registration no.DL-8CN-3558 for a sum of Rs.
85,000/- which was the highest bid offered by Sh. Sandeep and accordingly
the said vehicle was sold to him in public auction.
</p>

<p id="p_24">11.          In para 15 of affidavit Ex.PW1/A it was stated by Sh. Atul
Wasan, AR of the plaintiff as under :
</p><blockquote id="blockquote_7">             "In the pendency of the suit the vehicle bearing registration
             number    DL-8CM-3558     financed   to   the   defendant   was
             surrendered and by the permission of the Hon'ble court the
             said vehicle was sold for the sum of Rs.85,000/- which was
             best price available in the market at that time. After adjusting
             the said amount of Rs.85,000/- received from sale of the said


M/s. Kotak Mahindra Bank Ltd. Vs. Ajeet Kumar                                   Page  4
                                      CS No.707/06

                vehicle against the total amount due and payable i.e.Rs.
                3,25,335/- by the defendant to the bank in respect of loan taken
                by him, the amount of Rs.2,40,000/- is still due and payable by
                the defendant."
</blockquote>

<p id="p_25">12.             Thus, in view of the unrebutted and unchallenged evidence of
PW1 which finds support with the documentary evidence and in view of the
facts mentioned above, a principal outstanding of Rs.2,40,000/- was due
and payable by the defendant to the plaintiff as on the date of filing of the
suit.
</p>

<p id="p_26">13.            The plaintiff in the present case has prayed for interest
@ 24 % p.a. However, perusal of Credit facility application form mentions
about the rate of interest @ 7.18 % per annum. Thus, the plaintiff is entitled
for interest of decree amount @ 7.18 % per annum from the date of
institution of the suit till realisation.
</p>

<p id="p_27">14.            Thus, in view of the observations made herein, the present suit
is decreed in favour of the plaintiff and against the defendant for a sum of
Rs.2,40,000/- along with interest @ 7.18 % per annum from the date of
institution of the suit till realization along with proportionate costs.
</p>

<p id="p_28">15.            Decree Sheet be prepared accordingly. File be consigned to
Record Room.

today on 18.05.2013
