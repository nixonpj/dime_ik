<div class="doc_title">Anand Kishore Singh @ Mitthu vs The State Of Bihar on 9 October, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                  Criminal Miscellaneous No.45623 of 2017
                     Arising Out of PS.Case No. -583 Year- 2017 Thana -FORBESGANJ District- ARRARIA
                 ======================================================
                 Anand Kishore Singh @ Mitthu son of Vinod Kumar Singh, Resident of
                 village Majhuwa, Ward No.5, P.s. Forbesganj, Dist.- Araria.
                                                                        .... .... Petitioner/s
                                                  Versus
                 The State of Bihar
                                                                   .... .... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :  Mr. Ramesh Kumar Singh
                 For the Opposite Party/s   : Mr. Sri Prem Kumar Jha
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE VINOD KUMAR SINHA
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">3   09-10-2017          The case has been listed today under the heading "to be

                 mentioned" at the instance of the learned counsel for the

                 petitioner and from perusal of the order dated 19.09.2017 passed

                 in this case, it appears that in place of recovery of 72 ltrs. of

                 liquor, it has wrongly been typed as 720 ltrs. of liquor in the

                 order-sheet, as such the prayer for bail of the petitioner has been

                 rejected with some observations.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                          Perused the record, it appears that the allegation is about

                 recovery of 72 ltrs. of foreign liquor from the co-accused and not

                 from the petitioner and the other co-accused persons have already

                 been granted bail.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                          In such view of the matter, order dated 19.09.2017 passed

                 in this case is recalled and the petitioner, above named, is directed

                 to be enlarged on bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs.25,000/-
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">         Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.45623 of 2017 (3) dt.09-10-2017</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                   2/2</span>




</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">                     (Twenty Five Thousand) with two sureties of the like amount each

                     to the satisfaction of 2nd Addl. Session-cum-Special Judge, Araria

                     in connection with Forbesganj P.S.Case No.583 of 2017.
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_2">                              With following conditions :
</blockquote>
<blockquote id="blockquote_3">                              (i)    One of the bailors of the petitioner shall be a local
                                     person having sufficient immovable property within
                                     the jurisdiction of the concerned court.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_4">                              (ii) The petitioner will not induce any witness or tamper
                                     with the evidence.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_5">                              (iii) The petitioner shall co-operate in the disposal of trial
                                     and make himself available as and when required by
                                     the court and on the event of failure on his part to
                                     appear before the court below on two consecutive
                                     dates without showing any genuine reasons, the
                                     prosecution is free to move for cancellation of his bail
                                     bond.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_6">                               Let the aforesaid order be communicated to the court below

                     concerned.</blockquote><p id="p_4">




</p><p id="p_5">                                                (Vinod Kumar Sinha, J)
chn/-
</p>
