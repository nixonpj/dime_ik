      This Criminal Petition is filed under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_1">Section 439</a> of
Cr.P.C. praying to enlarge the petitioner on bail in Crime
No.198/2016 (SPL.C.C.No.367/2016) of Yeshwanthapura
of Police Station, Bangalore for the offences punishable
                               2


under <a href="/doc/619940/" id="a_2">Sections 363</a> and <a href="/doc/1279834/" id="a_3">376</a> of IPC and Sections 3,4,7 and
8 of POCSO Act.


      This Criminal Petition coming on for orders this day,
the court made the following:


                          ORDER
</pre>

<p id="p_1">      This is a petition under <a href="/doc/1290514/" id="a_4">Section 439</a> Cr.P.C.        The

petitioner is facing trial in Spl.C.C.No.367/2016 on the file

of Addl. City Civil and Sessions Judge, Bengaluru. He was

released on bail on earlier occasion.      Since he did not

appear before the court continuously, the trial court issued

warrant to secure his presence and then took him to

custody.   His application for bail was rejected by the

Sessions Court.    The learned counsel for the petitioner

submits that case of ill health of the petitioner he could not

appear before the trial court. But on perusal of the order

passed by the trial court, it becomes clear that there was

no explanation offered by the petitioner as to why he did

not appear before the court continuously.          When the

accused is on bail, it is his duty to appear before the court
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                              3</span>


to see that trial is completed as early as possible.   If he

remains absent without any justifiable cause, the trial

court was justified in taking him to custody. There is no

reason to grant bail. Hence petition is dismissed.




</p><p id="p_2">                                      Sd/-
