<div class="doc_title">Pankaj Kumar Das vs State Of West Bengal &amp; Ors on 19 November, 2018</div><p id="p_1"><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                             1</span>

         19.11.2018                       W.P. No. 21494 (W) of 2018
sd
                             <a href="/doc/133820468/" id="a_1">Pankaj Kumar Das vs. State of West Bengal &amp; Ors.
Ct</a> 24.
</p>
<p id="p_2">


</p><p id="p_3">                      Mrs. R. Mukherjee...For the petitioner.

                      Mr. S. Chakraborti ..For the State

                                    Affidavit of service filed in Court today be taken on

                        record.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                    The petitioner was a School Teacher. He retired

                        from service on 31.07.1993. The pension payment order was

                        issued on 30.06.2000 and the gratuity amount and the

                        arrear pension was disbursed on 05.09.2000. The petitioner

                        claims interest on delayed payment of pension.

</p><p id="p_5">                                    I have heard learned counsel for the parties and

                        considered the orders passed by this court in similar facts.

</p><p id="p_6">                                    It is settled law that the right of a retired employee

                        to   get    his   retiral      dues     on   the   date    of   attaining

                        superannuation is a valuable right which accrues in his

                        favour on the date of his attaining superannuation. Further,

                        gratuity and pension are no more considered to be a bounty

                        to be handed out by the State at its whim. An employee has a

                        statutory    right       to   receive   gratuity   and    pension   upon

                        retirement. If payment of such gratuity and pension is

                        delayed the retired employee is surely entitled to get some

                        interest for such delayed payment. The delay in approaching
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                  2</span>


the writ Court per se may not be a ground for dismissal of the

writ petition.
</p>
<p id="p_7">           In the present case, it was the bounden duty of the

State to disburse the gratuity and pension amount on the

due date. If it has failed to do so and has released such

amount after unexplained delay, it is obliged to pay interest

to the retired employee. Pension and gratuity are welfare

provisions aimed at maintaining the life of a retired employee

and his/her dependents. This is compensatory in nature.

</p><p id="p_8">          In view of the aforesaid, I direct the Director of

Pension, Provident Fund and Group Insurance, Government

of West Bengal as also the concerned Treasury Officer to pay

interest to the writ petitioner at the rate of 9% per annum on

pension calculated on and from the date of retirement till

date of actual payment.
</p>
<p id="p_9">          Such payment is to be made within eight weeks

from the date of communication of the certified copy of this

order to the concerned authorities.

</p><p id="p_10">          Since no affidavit in opposition has been invited,

the allegations contained in the writ petition are deemed not

be admitted.
</p>
<p id="p_11">          WP 21494 (W) of 2018 is disposed of.
</p>
<p id="p_12">          There will be no order as to costs.
</p>
<p id="p_13">          Urgent Photostat copy of this order, if applied for,
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                  3</span>


be made available to the parties upon compliance of the

requisite formalities.
</p>
<p id="p_14">


</p><p id="p_15">                                ( Amrita Sinha, J. )
