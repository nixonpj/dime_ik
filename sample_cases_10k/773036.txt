  IN THE HIGH COURT OF KERALA AT ERNAKULAM

WP(C).No. 26983 of 2010(W)


1. M/S.OIL AND GAS PLANT ENGINEERS (INDIA)
                      ...  Petitioner

                        Vs



1. COMMERCIAL TAX OFFICER (WC),
                       ...       Respondent

2. DEPUTY COMMISSIONER (APPEALS),

3. INSPECTING ASSISTANT COMMISSIONER,

                For Petitioner  :SRI.N.MURALEEDHARAN NAIR

                For Respondent  : No Appearance

The Hon'ble MR. Justice C.K.ABDUL REHIM

 Dated :30/08/2010

 O R D E R
                  C.K.ABDUL REHIM, J.

              --------------------------------------
               W.P.(C).No.26983 of 2010
              --------------------------------------

        Dated this the 30th day of August, 2010


                      J U D G M E N T
</pre><p id="p_1">                      ----------------------
</p>
<p id="p_2">     Aggrieved by Ext.P1 order of assessment issued

under the provisions of the Kerala Value Added Tax Act

(the Act for short), pertaining to the year 2008-09, the

petitioner  had    preferred       appeal      before  the  2nd

respondent as evidenced from Ext.P2. The appeal was

filed along with Ext.P3 petition seeking condonation of

delay. Petitioner has also filed Ext.P4 stay petition. It is

submitted   that    the    appellate       authority   has  not

considered    the     appeal       or     the      accompanying

applications.    Grievance of the petitioner is that

coercive steps of recovery are now being threatened

pursuant to Ext.P6 notice.
</p>
<p id="p_3">     2.   Heard,       learned        Government        Pleader

appearing on behalf of the respondents. Considering

circumstances attendant on this case, I am of the

opinion that the appellate authority can be directed to

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">W.P.(C).26983 of 2010           -2-</span>



deal with the matter on an early basis.

</p><p id="p_4">      3.    In the result, the writ petition is disposed of

directing the 2nd respondent to consider and pass orders on

Ext.P3 application filed seeking condonation of delay in

filing the appeal, after affording an opportunity of hearing

to the petitioner, as early as possible, at any rate, within a

period of one month from the date of receipt of a copy of

this judgment.
</p>
<p id="p_5">      4.    If the delay in filing the appeal is condoned, the

2nd respondent shall consider and pass orders on stay

applications (Ext.P4), simultaneously. Till such time orders

are passed as directed above all further steps of recovery

initiated pursuant to Ext.P6 notice shall be kept in

abeyance.
</p>
<p id="p_6">      The petitioner shall produce a copy of this judgment

before the 2nd respondent.
</p>
<p id="p_7">


</p><p id="p_8">                           C.K.ABDUL REHIM, JUDGE.
</p>
