                           JUDGMENT
</pre>

<p id="p_1">       This writ petition has been filed when the Advocate

Commissioner     had    approached     the    petitioner to take

possession of the secured asset as per the direction issued by

the Chief Judicial Magistrate, Kalpatta.

</p><p id="p_2">      2.   It is now submitted by the learned counsel for the

petitioner that the loan account had been regularised insofar

as the entire outstanding amount had been paid by the

petitioner. This fact has been accepted by the learned counsel

for the respondent.
</p>
<p id="p_3">      3.   Having regard to the fact that the petitioner had

already remitted the outstanding amount, it shall be open for

the respondent Bank to regularise the account, so that the

petitioner can pay the regular instalments in time.

</p><p id="p_4">      4.   Learned counsel for the respondent Bank submits

W.P.(C) No. 30541 of 2015

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                              -2-</span>



that they do not intend to proceed with the direction issued by

the Chief Judicial Magistrate.
</p>
<p id="p_5">        5.    The respondent Bank shall submit necessary

application before the Chief Judicial Magistrate indicating that

the account have been regularised and they do not intend to

proceed further in the matter.    This shall be done within a

period of two weeks from today.
</p>
<p id="p_6">        I take on record the above submission of the learned

counsel appearing for respondent Bank. Accordingly, this writ

petition is closed.
</p>
