<div class="doc_title">The State Of Madhya Pradesh vs Ajay Pal Singh on 10 July, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">                       W.P.No.1681/2016

10.07.2017
      Shri   A.P.    Singh,   Government      Advocate   for the
petitioners/State.
      Shri Akash Choudhury, Advocate for respondent No.1.</pre>


<p id="p_1">      The challenge in the present writ petition is to an order
passed by the Central Administrative Tribunal, Jabalpur Bench,
Jabalpur on 15.05.2015, whereby, the order dated 22.08.2013
of recovery of Rs.1,48,493/- was set aside.
</p><p id="p_2">      Such recovery was ordered to be made from the applicant
(respondent No.1 herein) for the reason that one Shri N.S.
Bundela was permitted to join duty though he has been
compulsorily retired and the charge has been taken by other
employee on 30.05.2004.
</p><p id="p_3">      An order of compulsory retirement was passed against
Shri Bundela on 24.02.2004 with three month notice period.
The period of three months notice was to expire on 03.06.2004.
Shri Bundela filed a writ petition in which there was an order of
status quo granted on 31.05.2004. On the strength of status quo
order, Shri Bundela was permitted to rejoin and continue in
service. The recovery is of the salary paid to Shri Bundela for
the period, he discharged duties in terms of order passed by the
applicant.
</p><p id="p_4">      We do not find any illegality in the order passed by the
          Tribunal. Since there was an order of the Court, an Officer has
         taken a view that it is incumbent upon him to permit Shri
         Bundela to join the duties. This was in purported compliance of
         the order of the Court. The applicant has not drawn any benefit
         to himself as the salary has been paid to Shri Bundela. For such
         salary paid to Shri Bundela, the recovery cannot be affected
         from the applicant.
</p><p id="p_5">               Accordingly, we do not find any illegality in the order
         passed by the Tribunal which may warrant interference in the
         present writ petition.
</p><p id="p_6">               Writ petition stands dismissed.
</p>

<pre id="pre_2">                (Hemant Gupta)                   (Vijay Kumar Shukla)
                  Chief Justice                          Judge
