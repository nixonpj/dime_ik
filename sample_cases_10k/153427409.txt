<div class="doc_title">Pintu Kumar @ Pintu Ravidas vs State Of Bihar &amp; Anr on 9 July, 2018</div><pre id="pre_1">                      IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                   Criminal Miscellaneous No.36085 of 2018
                      Arising Out of PS. Case No.-58 Year-2017 Thana- MAHILA P.S. District- Nalanda
                 ======================================================
                 Pintu Kumar @ Pintu Ravidas Son of Suresh Ravidas Resident of Village-
                 Chandapur, P.S. Telhara, District- Nalanda.

                                                                                    ... ... Petitioner/s
                                              Versus
            1. The State of Bihar.
            2. Rubi Devi Daughter of Nathun Ravidas Resident of Village- Chandapur, P.S.
               Telhara, District- Nalanda.

                                                        ... ... Opposite Party/s
                 ======================================================
                 Appearance :
                 For the Petitioner/s     :        Mr. Pramod Kumar Sinha
                 For the Opposite Party/s :        Mr. Ram Bilash Roy Raman
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE SUDHIR SINGH
                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">3   09-07-2018                  Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned

                  APP for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                         The petitioner is apprehending his arrest in a case

                  registered under <a href="/doc/538436/" id="a_1">Sections 498A</a>, <a href="/doc/508426/" id="a_2">494</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_3">323</a>, <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_4">341</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_5">504</a> and <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_6">506</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_7">34</a>

                  of the Indian Penal Code.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                         Allegation against the petitioner is of committing torture

                  upon the victim due to non-fulfilment of demand of dowry.

</p><p id="p_4">                         It has been submitted on behalf of the petitioner that the

                  petitioner has got no criminal antecedent. There is no allegation

                  of tampering of witnesses alleged against the petitioner. The

                  petitioner has falsely been implicated in the present case due to

                  petty family dispute. The case is triable by the Magistrate. The

                  petitioner has relied upon the judgment of this Court in the case
              Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.36085 of 2018(3) dt.09-07-2018
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                          2/2</span>




                      of Md. Naimul Haque Ansari @ Naimul Haque Ansari &amp; Ors.

                      Vs. The State of Bihar, reported in 2006(3) PLJR 182.

</p><p id="p_5">                             On behalf of the State, it is submitted that the petitioner is

                      named in the complaint case/F.I.R.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                   Considering the aforesaid facts and circumstances, let

                      the petitioner, above named, in the event of arrest/surrender

                      before the learned court below within a period of six weeks

                      from today, be released on anticipatory bail on furnishing bail

                      bonds of Rs.10,000/- (Ten thousand) with two sureties of the

                      like amount each to the satisfaction of learned S.D.J.M.,

                      Nalanda at Bihar Sharif in connection with Mahila P.S. case

                      No.58 of 2017, subject to the conditions as laid down under

                      <a href="/doc/1692057/" id="a_8">Section 438(2)</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure.



</p><p id="p_7">                                                     (Sudhir Singh, J)
Narendra/-
</p>
