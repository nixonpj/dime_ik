                   CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE CHAKRADHARI
                   SHARAN SINGH
                   ORAL ORDER


21   26-07-2017                     Mr. Pradeep Kumar Yadav, the Regional Director,

                     Easter Regional Committee, National Council for Teachers

                     Education, Bhuwaneshwar, was personally present in the Court

                     on 18.05.2017 and he had made statement before this Court that

                     no effective inspection could be carried out of the institutions,

                     during the summer vacation. He had assured the Court that once

                     the institutions opened after summer vacation, the National

                     Council for Teachers Education (hereinafter referred to as the

                     'NCTE'), in association with other functionaries, shall expedite

                     inspection of the colleges.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                                    Learned Counsel, appearing on behalf of the NCTE,

                     has sought adjournment till tomorrow for filing an affidavit. He

                     states that he does not have any instruction whether any college

                     has been inspected by the team after 18.05.2017, after the
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_13">          Patna High Court CWJC No.9230 of 2016 (21) dt.26-07-2017</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_14">                                                   8/8</span>




                       institutions were reopened after summer vacation.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                      There is no application filed on behalf of the NCTE,

                       seeking extension of time or modification of the order, dated

                       18.05.2017, or any other order, passed in the present proceeding.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                       The Court is of the, prima facie, view that the NCTE is acting in

                       defiance of this Court's orders.
</p>
<p id="p_6">                                      List this case, under the same heading, on

                       27.07.2017 at 10.30 A.M, as requested by learned Counsel for

                       the NCTE

                                      Considering the past conduct of the officials of the

                       NCTE, coupled with the present situation, the Court may

                       consider initiating contempt proceeding against the officials of

                       the NCTE, if not satisfied with their conduct.
</p>


<p id="p_7">                                                              (Chakradhari Sharan Singh, J)
Praveen-II/-
