     JUDGE: KARKARDOOMA COURTS: DELHI.

Assigned to Sessions on              15.05.2008.

                  Versus

<p id="p_1">JUDGMENT: (Oral)



</p><p id="p_2">1. This is an appeal against the judgment dated 15.4.08        and order on

  sentence dated 21.4.08 passed by Ld. M.M. Karkardooma Courts, Delhi

  vide which appellant was convicted and sentenced to undergo R I for a

  period of six months with a fine of Rs.2000/- for committing offence u/s

  411 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_1">IPC</a>. In default of payment of fine, appellant was ordered to further
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                       2</span>

  undergo R I for a period of one month.
</p>
<p id="p_3">

</p><p id="p_4">2. In brief, facts relevant for the disposal of the present appeal are that on

  21.06.2006 at about 2.30 p.m, accused Asif snatched gold chain of the

  complainant    Anita    near   Water    Tank,   Tahirpur,   Delhi.   During

  investigation, accused Asif made a disclosure statement pursuant to which

  appellant was arrested and gold piece (melted gold chain) was recovered.

  Appellant was charged for committing offence under <a href="/doc/812083/" id="a_2">Section 411</a> IPC.



</p><p id="p_5">3. I have heard Sh. Kapil, Ld counsel for the appellant and Sh R K Pandey,

  Ld.APP for the State. Ld. Counsel for the appellant states that he is not

  challenging the conviction of the appellant on merits. However, he prays

  that a lenient view be taken against the appellant and he be released on

  probation of good conduct.
</p>
<p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">4. I have gone through the trial court record. PW5 Anita is the star witness in

  this case who has fully supported the prosecution case. She was not cross-

  examined by the appellant. The recovery of the gold piece was effected
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                      3</span>

  from the appellant. From the perusal of evidence on record, I am of the

  opinion that appellant was rightly convicted by the trial court for

  committing offence u/s 411 <a href="/doc/1569253/" id="a_3">IPC</a>. Hence, the conviction of the appellant is

  upheld.
</p>
<p id="p_8">

</p><p id="p_9">5. Considering the fact that appellant Dalip is a young man aged about 34

  years and has a family to support. Appellant is not a previous convict and

  he remained in custody for ten/twelve days. He is not a previous convict

  and is facing trial since 2001, appellant is released on probation of good

  conduct on furnishing personal bond in the sum of Rs.10,000/- with one

  surety in the like amount for a period of one year to the satisfaction of

  Ld.MM/Link MM/Duty MM. If the appellant contravenes conditions of

  the probation bond, he shall appear and receive the sentence when called

  upon during the said period as the Court may direct and in the meantime

  he shall keep peace and be of good behaviour. Probation bond be

  furnished before the trial court within a week from today. The appeal

  stands disposed of. A copy of the judgment be supplied to the appellant

  free of cost. Trial court record be sent back with a copy of the judgment
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                                    4</span>

  and appeal file be consigned to Record Room.
</p>
<p id="p_10">

Court on May 15, 2008.           Addl. Sessions Judge, KKD/Delhi.
