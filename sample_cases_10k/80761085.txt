<p id="p_1">Item No.45
   SB

                                W.P. 33537 (W) of 2013


                Mr. M. K. Ghosh,
                Ms. S. Dey (Basu)........for the petitioner.

                Ms. S. Majumder..........for the CSTC.


                None appears for the State despite service. Affidavit of service

             filed in Court today shall be retained with the records.

                The petitioner was an employee of Calcutta State Transport

             Corporation (hereafter the Corporation).      He has retired from

             service on attaining the age of superannuation on 31.01.2013. He

             is aggrieved because certain retiral dues are yet to be paid to him

             by the Corporation.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                It appears that the petitioner has addressed a representation

             to the Managing Director of the Corporation dated 23.10.2013

             claiming the unpaid benefits, which is yet to be decided. I am of

             the view that the Managing Director must look into such

             representation and decide the question of payment of admissible

             unpaid dues to the petitioner upon granting him opportunity of

             hearing. It is ordered accordingly.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                            2</span><p id="p_3">


    Let this exercise be completed as early as possible, but not

later than three months from date of receipt of this order together

with a copy of the writ petition.
</p>
<p id="p_4">    In the event the petitioner is not entitled to any further retiral

benefits, a reasoned order shall be passed and communicated to

him.
</p>
<p id="p_5">    On the contrary, if any amount is found to be due and payable

on account of retiral dues, the same shall be paid to the petitioner

together with interest @ 10% per annum from the date following

his retirement till the date of actual payment.

    It is made clear that the Corporation shall be free to move the

Government for sanction of requisite funds for meeting the dues of

the petitioner and if such an approach is made, I hope and trust

that the Government shall pay due heed to the request of the

Corporation and sanction the same.
</p>
<p id="p_6">    The writ petition stands disposed of, without costs.

    Urgent photostat certified copy of this order, if applied for, be

furnished to the parties as early as possible.



</p><p id="p_7">                                    (DIPANKAR DATTA, J.)
