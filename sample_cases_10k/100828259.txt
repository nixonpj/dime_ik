<div class="doc_title">Satrughan Singh &amp; Ors vs Arun Prasad &amp; Ors on 15 September, 2010</div><pre id="pre_1">              IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                    W. P. (C) No. 6156 of 2009
                                ---
              1. Satrughan Singh
              2. Pushpa Devi
              3. Abhilasha Devi                ...   ...                     Petitioners
                                       Versus
              1. Arun Prasad
              2. Ramesh Prasad
              3. Ahilya Devi                                 ...     ...     Respondents
                                 ---
              CORAM        : HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE R. K. MERATHIA
                                  ---
              For the Petitioners     : Mr. Satish Kumar Keshri, Advocate

                               ---
5. 15.09.2010</pre><p id="p_1">:      This writ petition has been filed against the order dated
              23.11.2009 passed by the learned Sub-Judge III, Ranchi in Title Suit
              No. 225 of 2003 rejecting the prayer of the petitioner to bring on record
              certain documents.
</p><p id="p_2">                    This suit was filed by the plaintiff for specific performance of
              contract against the defendants-respondents on the ground that during
              the tenancy, there was an oral agreement with respect to the suit
              property against which Rs. 19.90 lacs was paid out of Rs. 22 lacs.
</p><p id="p_3">                    It is submitted that the documents sought to be brought on the
              record are necessary for proper adjudication of the case.
</p><p id="p_4">                    It appears that the suit was filed in the year 2003 in which issues
              were framed on 5.9.2006.          Petitioner prayed for bringing on record
              certain documents on two occasions earlier which prayer were allowed.
              This was the third time when the prayer was made for bringing on
              record some more documents. The learned court below after hearing
              the parties and considering their respective cases rightly held that such
              process cannot be allowed to continue.
</p><p id="p_5">                    In the circumstances, no grounds are made out for interference
              with the impugned order in exercise of supervisory power under <a href="/doc/1331149/" id="a_1">Article
              227</a> of the Constitution of India.
</p><p id="p_6">                    Accordingly, this writ petition is dismissed. However, no costs.
</p>

<p id="p_7">                                                                   (R. K. Merathia, J.)

R. Shekhar Cp 2
