<div class="doc_title">Ujjwal Kumar vs State Of Jharkhand on 4 September, 2015</div><pre id="pre_1">     IN       THE     HIGH     COURT          OF   JHARKHAND        AT      RANCHI
                        Cr. M.P. No. 349 of 2015
                                       with
                         I. A. No. 4674 of 2015
     Ujjwal Kumar                                     ..... ... Petitioner
                                   Versus
     The State of Jharkhand                             ..... ...     Opposite Party
                                --------
            CORAM : HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE H. C. MISHRA
                                ------
     For the Petitioner         :        M/s Chandrajit Mukherjee
                                         &amp; Puja Kumari, Advocates
     For the State              :        A.P.P.
                                --------

3/ 04.09.2015</pre><p id="p_1">       Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned A.P.P. for the
     State.
</p><p id="p_2">                    The petitioner has filed this petition for quashing the FIR in Bundu
     P.S. Case No. 44 of 2014 corresponding to G.R. No. 2141 of 2014, for the
     offences under <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_1">Sections 323</a>, <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_2">341</a>, <a href="/doc/555306/" id="a_3">504</a>, <a href="/doc/180217/" id="a_4">506</a>, <a href="/doc/1208971/" id="a_5">353</a> of the Indian Penal Code and
     <a href="/doc/42436223/" id="a_6">Section 3(1)(x)</a> of the Scheduled Castes and <a href="/doc/25085007/" id="a_7">Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of
     Atrocities) Act</a>.
</p><p id="p_3">                    In the FIR, it is alleged that the petitioner had gone to the office of
     informant, who is Block Development Officer-cum-Circle Officer, Bundu, and he
     tried to assault the informant and also abused the office staff in the name of their
     castes. On the basis of the FIR lodged by the informant, the police case was
     instituted.
</p><p id="p_4">                    Learned counsel for the petitioner has submitted that the petitioner
     has been falsely implicated in this case, who had gone to take the certified copies
     of some documents in the office. It is also submitted that though there is
     allegation of abusing the informant and the office staff in the name of their castes,
     but according to FIR, the said occurrence had taken place in the chamber of the
     Block Development Officer-cum-Circle Officer. Accordingly, the offence cannot
     be said to be made out against the petitioner under <a href="/doc/42436223/" id="a_8">Section 3(1)(x)</a> of the
     Scheduled Castes and <a href="/doc/25085007/" id="a_9">Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act</a>. Learned
     counsel has accordingly, submitted that it is a fit case for quashing the FIR, as the
     same has been lodged with false allegations.
</p><p id="p_5">                    Learned counsel for the State has opposed the prayer submitting that
     there is specific allegation against the petitioner in the FIR, which makes out the
     offence.
</p><span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                                    -2-</span>
<p id="p_6">                      The FIR clearly shows there is allegation against the petitioner of
        abusing the Block Development Officer-cum-Circle Officer and his office staff in
        the name of their castes and also to have attempted to assault him. The point,
        taken by learned counsel for the petitioner that the occurrence had not taken
        place in public view, cannot be looked into at this stage, in view of the fact that
        normally the Block Office remains crowded by general public.
</p><p id="p_7">                      In the facts of this case, I am not inclined to interfere with the FIR at
        this stage, as the offence is clearly made out against the petitioner on the basis of
        the allegations made therein. There is no merit in this criminal miscellaneous
        petition and the same stands dismissed.
</p><p id="p_8">                      The aforesaid interlocutory application filed for interim protection
        also stands dismissed.
</p>

<p id="p_9">                                                                         ( H. C. Mishra, J.)
R.Kr.
</p>
