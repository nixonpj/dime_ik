<div class="doc_title">Sanjay Kumar vs State Of Haryana on 21 November, 2012</div><pre id="pre_1">Crl. Revision No. 2380 of 2012 (O&amp;M)                                -1-

   In the High Court of Punjab and Haryana at Chandigarh

                                Crl. Revision No. 2380 of 2012 (O&amp;M)
                                Date of Decision: 21.11.2012.


Sanjay Kumar                                        .......Petitioners

                                Versus

State of Haryana                                    .......Respondent


CORAM: HON'BLE MRS. JUSTICE SABINA

Present:    Mr. Namit Khurana, Advocate
            for the petitioner.

            Mr. Satyavir Singh Yadav, Addl. A.G., Haryana.
                 ****

SABINA, J.
</pre>

<p id="p_1">            By way of the present petition, petitioner has challenged

the order dated 24.7.2012 whereby application for release of his

car on Sapurdari was declined.
</p>
<p id="p_2">            Learned counsel for the petitioner has submitted that

petitioner Sanjay Kumar was the owner of the car bearing

registration No. HR-02G-4222. The said car was liable to be

released on Sapurdari. Learned counsel has further submitted that

the petitioner will produce the car in the Court during trial as and

when it is directed to be produced. In support of his argument,

learned counsel has placed reliance on 'Nirmal Singh versus

State of Punjab', 2007(1) R.C.R. (Criminal) 986.

</p><p id="p_3">            Learned State counsel, on the other hand, has opposed

the petition.
</p>
<p id="p_4">            In the present case, contraband was recovered from the

car in question. Learned counsel for the petitioner has submitted

that the petitioner will produce the car in question during trial as

and when it is directed by the Court. In these circumstances, it
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> Crl. Revision No. 2380 of 2012 (O&amp;M)                              -2-</span>

would be in the interest of justice if the car in question is ordered

to be released on sapurdari to the petitioner on his furnishing

requisite undertaking in this regard.      In case, the car is not

released to the petitioner on sapurdari, the same would become

unfit for the road worthiness. The trial of the case may take time.

</p><p id="p_5">            Accordingly, this petition is allowed.    The impugned

order dated 24.7.2012 whereby request of the petitioner for release

of his car on sapurdari was declined, is set aside. The trial Court is

directed to release the car in question to the petitioner on

sapurdari subject to its satisfaction.
</p>
<p id="p_6">


</p><p id="p_7">                                              (SABINA)
                                               JUDGE
November 21, 2012
Gurpreet
