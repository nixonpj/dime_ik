<div class="doc_title">Jagmal vs State Of Haryana on 17 December, 2019</div><pre id="pre_1">                                                                                   213

    IN THE HIGH COURT OF PUNJAB AND HARYANA
                 AT CHANDIGARH

                                               CRM-M No.38224 of 2019 (O&amp;M)
                                               Date of Decision: 17.12.2019
Jagmal
                                                                   ......... Petitioner
                                          Versus
State of Haryana
                                                                ......... Respondent


CORAM: HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE MAHABIR SINGH SINDHU

Present:-     Mr. Naveen S. Bhardwaj, Advocate for the petitioner.

              Mr. Arun Beniwal, DAG, Haryana
              for the respondent/State.

                                 ****
MAHABIR SINGH SINDHU, J. (Oral)
</pre>
<p id="p_1">            Present petition has been filed under <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_1">Section 438</a> Cr.P.C. for

grant of pre-arrest bail to the petitioner in case bearing FIR No.0046 dated

15.03.2019, under <a href="/doc/1436241/" id="a_2">Sections 420</a>, <a href="/doc/1985627/" id="a_3">467</a>, <a href="/doc/556166/" id="a_4">468</a>, <a href="/doc/1466184/" id="a_5">471</a> and <a href="/doc/1897847/" id="a_6">120-B</a> of the Indian

Penal Code, 1860, registered at Police Station Loharu, District Bhiwani.

</p><p id="p_2">            This Court, while issuing notice of motion on 09.09.2019, passed

the following order:-
</p>
<blockquote id="blockquote_1">               "         Contends that as per the allegations in the FIR,
              Udmi Ram died on 22.04.2016 at Jhunjhunu (Rajasthan), but
              as a matter of fact he expired only on 03.06.2017 and thus, on
              the date of obtaining insurance policy he was very much alive
              and a reference is made to the death certificate annexure P-2.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">                         Notice of motion for 21.10.2019.
</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_3">                         In the meanwhile, the petitioner is directed to join
              the investigation before the Investigating Officer. In the event
              of his arrest, the Arresting Officer would admit him to interim
              bail till the next date of hearing on his furnishing adequate bail

             and surety bonds to his satisfaction. The petitioner is also
             directed to abide by all the conditions as envisaged under
             <a href="/doc/1233094/" id="a_7">Section 438(2)</a> Cr.P.C.          "</blockquote><p id="p_3">

</p><p id="p_4">          Contends that in terms of the above order, petitioner has joined

the investigation.
</p>
<p id="p_5">          Learned State Counsel, on instructions from H.C. Ravinder, has

acknowledged that in terms of order dated 09.09.2019, petitioner has joined

the investigation and his custodial interrogation is not required.

</p><p id="p_6">          In view of above, interim order dated 09.09.2019 is made absolute

subject to the conditions as envisaged under Section 438(2) Cr.P.C.

</p><p id="p_7">          It is also made clear that petitioner shall fully co-operate with the

Investigating Officer as and when called for further investigation.

</p><p id="p_8">          Disposed off accordingly.</p><pre id="pre_2">



December 17, 2019                                ( MAHABIR SINGH SINDHU )
