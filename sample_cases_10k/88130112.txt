                      JODHPUR


          S.B. CRIMINAL MISC. PETITION NO. 3372 / 2015
Naresh S/o Shri Sumati Lal Lodha, age 43 years, by caste Jain,
R/o Janakpura, Mandsore (M.P.).
                                                   ...........Petitioner
                             VERSUS

1.   Smt. Nilima @ Vidhya W/o Shri Naresh Lodha, D/o Shri Babu
     Lal Barodiya, age 39 years, R/o Mandsore, Presently R/o
     Pratapgarh.
2.   Kumari Nivisha D/o Shri Naresh Lodha, age 17 years, R/o
     Mandsore, Presently R/o Pratapgarh.
3.   Kumari Avisha D/o Shri Naresh Lodha, age 15 years,

     Respondents No.2 and 3 are minor through natural guardian
     mother Smt.Nilima Lodha, R/o Pratapgarh. (Respondent
     No.1)
4.   The State of Rajasthan

                                     .............Respondents
____________________________________________________
Counsel For Petitioner(s)   : Mr. Rakesh Arora
Counsel For Respondent(s) : Mr. V.S. Rajpurohit, PP
_____________________________________________________

           HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE VIJAY BISHNOI
                        Judgment / Order
13/02/2017

     This criminal misc. petition under <a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_1">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C. has

been filed on behalf of the petitioner being aggrieved with the

order dated 8.6.2015 passed by the Addl. Sessions Judge &amp;

Special   Judge,   SC/ST (Prevention of Atrocities) Act Cases,

Pratapgarh (hereinafter referred to as the revisional court),

whereby the Criminal Revision Petition No.16/2014 filed on behalf

of the petitioner has been dismissed.

     The said revision petition was filed by the petitioner against

the order dated 23.7.2012 passed by the Addl. Judicial Magistrate,
                                 (2 of 2)


Pratapgarh (hereinafter referred to as the trial court) in Criminal

Case No.313/2008 in the proceedings under <a href="/doc/1056396/" id="a_2">Section 125</a> Cr.P.C.,

whereby the trial court has directed the petitioner to pay

maintenance to the tune of Rs.5,000/- (Rs.2,000/- to the

respondent No.1 and Rs.1,500/- to the respondent Nos.2 and 3

each).

      This criminal misc. petition was presented before this Court

on 30.10.2015 and the office has pointed out as many as three

defects, however, the same have been cured, but later on office

has pointed out three more defects, out of which, one defect has

been cured, however, two defects have not been cured till date.

      After going through the impugned orders passed by the

courts below, this Court does not find any illegality in the same

because the petitioner has failed to prove before the courts below

that the respondent Nos.1 to 3, wife and children of the petitioner,

are having sufficient source to maintain themselves or are having

no sufficient reason to live separately.

      Moreover, this criminal misc. petition has been filed under

<a href="/doc/1679850/" id="a_3">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C. but in fact it is a second revision petition,

which is clearly barred under sub-section (3) of <a href="/doc/1457888/" id="a_4">Section 397</a>

Cr.P.C.

      Hence, this criminal misc. petition is dismissed for non

removal of defects as well as on merits. Stay petition also stands

dismissed.

                                             (VIJAY BISHNOI), J.
