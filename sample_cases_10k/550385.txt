  IN THE HIGH COURT OF KERALA AT ERNAKULAM

WP(C).No. 9986 of 2008(L)


1. BEN-TEK ELECTRICAL PVT. LTD.,
                      ...  Petitioner

                        Vs



1. THE STATE CO-OPERATIVE BANK LTD.,
                       ...       Respondent

2. THE REGIONAL MANAGER,

3. THE BRANCH MANAGER,

4. THE DISTRICT COLLECTOR,

                For Petitioner  :SRI.K.P.DANDAPANI (SR.)

                For Respondent  :SRI.NAGARAJ NARAYANAN,SC,K.S.CO-OP BANK

The Hon'ble MR. Justice THOTTATHIL B.RADHAKRISHNAN

 Dated :28/08/2008

 O R D E R
           THOTTATHIL B.RADHAKRISHNAN, J.
                   -------------------------------------------
                     W.P(C).No.9986 OF 2008
                  -------------------------------------------
             Dated this the 28th day of August, 2008


                              JUDGMENT
</pre>


<p id="p_1">     This writ petition is filed for a direction to the first

respondent to consider Ext.P5 representation, before invoking

the provisions of the <a href="/doc/52229129/" id="a_1">SARFAESI Act</a> and to allow the petitioner to

clear the entire liability as per Ext.P5 proposal. On 25.3.2008,

the following interim order was issued:



</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">           "The learned standing counsel takes notice for

     respondents 1 to 3. The learned senior Government

     Pleader takes notice for the 4th respondent.

</blockquote><blockquote id="blockquote_2">           There will be an interim direction that if the

     petitioner appears before the third respondent on

     27.3.2008, the decision of the bank on the request of

     the petitioner for one time settlement scheme shall be

     given. If such facility is not availed by the petitioner,

     any distress action against it will stand suspended for

     a period of four months on condition that the

     petitioner deposits at the rate of Rs.25 lakhs per

     month, payable on or before the last working day of

     every month commencing from March, 2008".

</blockquote><p id="p_2">WPC.9986/08

                            Page numbers


However, the petitioner neither appeared before the third

respondent nor paid any amount in terms of the order.



</p><p id="p_3">      In the aforesaid circumstances, I do not find any ground to

issue any direction in this writ petition in favour of the

petitioner. The petitioner having obtained an interim order on

25.3.2008 and having enjoyed it for five months, this writ

petition is dismissed with an order of costs to respondent No.1,

fixed at Rs.25,000/-. It is made clear that this judgment will not

stand in the way of the petitioner enjoying any benefit as stated

by the respondent in the counter affidavit.

</p><p id="p_4">                                           Sd/-
</p>
<p id="p_5">                          THOTTATHIL B.RADHAKRISHNAN,
                                           Judge
kkb.
