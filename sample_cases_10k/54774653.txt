<div class="doc_title">Jallaluddin Khan &amp; Anr. vs The State Of Bihar on 3 June, 2014</div><pre id="pre_1">                    IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                                Criminal Miscellaneous No.14759 of 2014
                 ======================================================
                 1. Jallaluddin Khan
                 2. Mirhassan Khan
                   Both are sons of Late Yusuf Khan, Resident of Village- Mathia, P.S.-
                 Ramnagar, District -West Champaran.
                                                                    .... ....   Petitioners
                                                Versus
                 The State of Bihar                           .... .... Opposite Party
                 ======================================================
                 CORAM: HONOURABLE MR. JUSTICE ASHWANI KUMAR SINGH
                 ORAL ORDER</pre>


<p id="p_1">2   03-06-2014                Heard learned counsel for the petitioners and learned

                 counsel for the State.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                              The petitioners seek bail in a case registered for the

                 offences punishable under <a href="/doc/1258372/" id="a_1">Sections 147</a>, <a href="/doc/763672/" id="a_2">148</a>, <a href="/doc/999134/" id="a_3">149</a>, <a href="/doc/1599401/" id="a_4">341</a>, <a href="/doc/1011035/" id="a_5">323</a>,

                 <a href="/doc/724142/" id="a_6">324</a>, <a href="/doc/455468/" id="a_7">307</a> and <a href="/doc/1101188/" id="a_8">379</a> of the Indian Penal Code as well as 27 of the

                 <a href="/doc/1934415/" id="a_9">Arms Act</a>.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                              It is contended that there is a case and counter case in

                 which both side sustained injuries. The genesis of the occurrence

                 is land dispute. The specific allegation of firing is against co-

                 accused Samsudin Khan. So far as the petitioners are concerned,

                 there is general and omnibus allegation and similarly

                 circumstanced two other co-accused namely, Raju Khan and

                 Kaju Khan have already been granted bail vide order dated 21st
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1"> 2           Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.14759 of 2014 (2) dt.03-06-2014</span>

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                                     2/2</span>




                     May, 2014 passed in Cr. Misc. No. 14237 of 2014 by another

                     Bench of this Court.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                                    Considering the facts and circumstances of the case,

                     the petitioners, who are in custody since 2.2.2014 and 6.2.2014

                     respectively, are directed to be released on bail on furnishing

                     bail bond of Rs. 10,000/- (Ten Thousand) with two sureties of

                     the like amount each to the satisfaction of the learned A.C.J.M.

                     Bagaha, West Champaran in connection with Ram Nagar P.S.

                     Case No. 18 of 2014.
</p>
<p id="p_5">


</p><p id="p_6">                                              (Ashwani Kumar Singh, J)
    Sanjeet/-
</p>
