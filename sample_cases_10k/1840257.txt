<div class="doc_title">M/S. Icico Bank vs P. Sabeena on 6 January, 2009</div><pre id="pre_1">       

  

  

 
 
  IN THE HIGH COURT OF KERALA AT ERNAKULAM

WP(C).No. 395 of 2009(V)


1. M/S. ICICO BANK, RAPG DIVISION,
                      ...  Petitioner

                        Vs



1. P. SABEENA, AGED 28 YEARS,
                       ...       Respondent

2. C.K. ASHRAF, AGED 34,

                For Petitioner  :SRI.KKM.SHERIF

                For Respondent  : No Appearance

The Hon'ble MR. Justice K.P.BALACHANDRAN

 Dated :06/01/2009

 O R D E R
               K.P. Balachandran, J.
            --------------------------
              W.P.(C)No.395 of 2009 V
            --------------------------

                     JUDGMENT
</pre>


<p id="p_1">    Heard counsel for the petitioner.
</p>
<p id="p_2">    2.   Petitioner/plaintiff assails Exhibit P4

order, dismissing the cheque application filed by

them in O.S.No.55/08 for disbursement of the sale

proceeds  of  the  vehicle  sold  after  attachment

before judgment in the suit.
</p>
<p id="p_3">    3.  It is submitted that the suit is not yet

decreed.  In the normal course, as observed by the

court below, it is true that without obtaining a

decree in the suit, petitioner/plaintiff is not

entitled to file a cheque application for the

amount.  But, in the instant case, it appears that

liability is there for the respondents/defendants

to  repay  the  sum  and  if  the  amount  is   not

disbursed, naturally, interest will accrue also on

the amount brought to court and the respondents/

defendants will be burdened to pay that interest as

well. Further, counsel for the petitioner/plaintiff

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">WPC 395/09                 2</span>


submits that petitioner is prepared to give bank

guarantee for the amount in the event of cheque

being issued, so that no loss or damage will be

caused to the respondents/ defendants as well.

</p><p id="p_4">     4.    In view of the above submissions, I am of

the view that Exhibit P4 order passed by the court

below     deserves  to  be  set   aside  and   Cheque

Application    No.388/08 filed  by  the   petitioner/

plaintiff in O.S.No.55/08 is to be directed to be

considered afresh by the court below.

</p><p id="p_5">     5.   In the result, I set aside Exhibit P4 order

and direct the court below to consider the cheque

application     filed  by  the   petitioner/plaintiff

afresh hearing the counsel for the respondents/

defendants as well.       In case the respondents/

defendants have no objection in issuing the cheque

to the petitioner/plaintiff for the amount brought

to court, cheque can be issued on such consent and

without insisting for bank guarantee.     If that is

not possible, the court below may consider the

<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">WPC 395/09               3</span>


feasibility of disbursing the amount accepting the

bank guarantee.
</p>
<p id="p_6">     With the above directions, I dispose of this

writ petition.   Issue copy of this judgment to the

counsel for the petitioner urgently so as to enable

them to produce it before the court below and get

appropriate orders.
</p>
<p id="p_7">


6th January, 2009        (K.P.Balachandran, Judge)
tkv

</p>
