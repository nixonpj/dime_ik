For Petitioner        :   Mr. Partha Sarathi Basu
                          Mr. A. Sinha

                          Mr. Debdip Mukherjee

<p id="p_4">
</p><p id="p_5">Judgment On :             03-03-2010.
</p>
<p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">      This is an application for quashing of a case relating to an offence

punishable under <a href="/doc/1823824/" id="a_1">Section 138</a> of the Negotiable Instruments Act, now pending

before the Learned Metropolitan Magistrate, 9th Court, Calcutta involving the

dishonour of a cheque for Rs. 2,638/-.         During the pendency of this criminal

revision a joint compromise petition has been filed. It appears from the said joint
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                           2</span>


compromise petition that the parties have settled their dispute amicably out of

Court and the cheque amount has already been repaid to the defacto-

complainant and the defacto-complainant has no further grievances against the

petitioner and no more intended to proceed against him. This fact has not been

disputed    by    the   Learned     Counsel     appearing     on    behalf   of   the

complainant/opposite party.
</p>
<p id="p_8">      2.    In view of the facts that the cheque amount to the tune of Rs.

2,638/- has been repaid to the complainant and the complainant is no longer

desirous to proceed against the petitioner. In my opinion, no useful purpose will

be served by keeping the impugned proceeding survived inasmuch as after the

settlement, there would be no possibilities of the criminal case in question to

reach to its logical conclusion.
</p>
<p id="p_9">            In view of the above, the impugned proceeding stands quashed.

            In view of the disposal of main criminal revisional application, an

application for passing appropriate orders being CRAN No. 586 of 2010 also

stands disposed of.
</p>
<p id="p_10">            Criminal Section is directed to deliver urgent Photostat certified copy

of this Judgement to the parties, if applied for, as early as possible.



</p><p id="p_11">                                                   ( Ashim Kumar Roy, J. )
