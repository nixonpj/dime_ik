<pre id="pre_2">   mb          In the High Court at Calcutta
                      Civil Revisional Jurisdiction
                             Appellate Side
                              C.O. No. 3367 of 2018

                            Susanta Kumar Dass &amp; Anr.
                                      -Vs.-
                              Rabin Banerjee &amp; Ors.


                Mr. Souradipta Banerjee
                                 ...for the petitioners


</pre><p id="p_1">                  By virtue of the impugned order, the trial court refused a

             formal prayer of the plaintiffs in an eviction suit, for recording the

             correct date of receipt of the relevant eviction notice.    Although

             initially the plaint contained the correct date, on a subsequent

             amended plaint being filed on the death of one of the parties, the

             said date was printed erroneously in such amended plaint due to

             alleged bona fide inadvertence of the plaintiffs.

</p><p id="p_2">                  It is argued on behalf of the plaintiffs/petitioners that the

             correction prayed for by the plaintiffs was formal in nature, but it

             was refused by the trial court without adverting to the attending

             circumstances.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                  This revisional application is required to be heard on such

             question.
</p>
<p id="p_4">                  Accordingly, the petitioners will serve copies of C.O. No.

             3367 of 2018 on the opposite parties indicating that the matter
<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                            2</span>


will appear as a "listed motion" in the monthly list of November,

2018 and file an affidavit of service on the next date of hearing.

</p><p id="p_5">     There will be an order of stay of all further proceedings in

Ejectment Suit No. 356 of 2007, pending before the Third Bench,

Small Causes Court at Calcutta till November 30, 2018 or until

further order, whichever is earlier.
</p>
<p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">                                       (Sabyasachi Bhattacharyya, J.)
