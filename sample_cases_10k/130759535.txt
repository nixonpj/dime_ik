<div class="doc_title">Nirmal Oraon vs The State Of Jharkhand on 26 May, 2017</div><pre id="pre_1">           IN THE HIGH COURT OF JHARKHAND AT RANCHI
                                 B.A.No. 4239 of 2017
           Nirmal Oraon                               ...  ...      ...Petitioner
                                     -Versus-
           The State of Jharkhand.                ...   ...  ...Opp. Party
                                     -----------
           CORAM:        THE HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE DR. S.N.PATHAK

           For the Petitioner:    Mr. Abhay Kr. Chaturvedy, Advocate.
           For the State:         A.P.P.
                                         -----------

02/ 26.05.2017</pre><p id="p_1">       Heard the parties.
</p>
<p id="p_2">                     The petitioner is an accused in a case registered under <a href="/doc/619940/" id="a_1">Sections 363</a>,

            <a href="/doc/886598/" id="a_2">365</a>, <a href="/doc/1324577/" id="a_3">367</a> &amp; <a href="/doc/1153041/" id="a_4">370</a>/<a href="/doc/37788/" id="a_5">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code, Section 23/26 of J.J. Act, Section

            14/16 of Labour Bounded Act and Section 25/26 of Inter State Migrant Labour

            Act.
</p>
<p id="p_3">                     Learned Counsel for the petitioner has submitted that the petitioner has

            been falsely implicated in the present case and has not committed any offence

            as alleged in the F.I.R. It is further submitted that the victim girl has been

            recovered during investigation of the case and her statement has also been

            recorded before the Magistrate. It appears from her statement that she had

            gone Delhi with her own will and consent. It is also submitted by the learned

            counsel for the petitioner that during investigation of the case no cogent

            evidence material and evidence has been found against the petitioner to

            establish his complicity with the instant case. Moreover, the petitioner is in

            custody since 20.03.2017 and hence, he may be granted the privilege of bail.

</p><p id="p_4">                     Learned A.P.P. opposes the prayer for bail.
</p>
<p id="p_5">                     In the facts and circumstances of the case, the petitioner is directed to

            be released on bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs.10,000/-(Ten thousand) with

            two sureties of like amount each to the satisfaction of the Chief Judicial

            Magistrate, Gumla in connection with AHTU Dumri P.S. Case No. 56 of 2015,

            corresponding to G.R.No.1046 of 2015.
</p>
<p id="p_6">

</p><p id="p_7">                                                             [Dr.S.N.Pathak,J.]
        Pankaj/R.Sinha
