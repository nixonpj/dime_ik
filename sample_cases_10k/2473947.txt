<div class="doc_title">Swetaben Ashishbhai Patel vs State Of Gujarat &amp; 4 on 22 April, 2016</div><pre id="pre_1">                 R/SCR.A/2612/2016                                              ORDER




                  IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD

              SPECIAL CRIMINAL APPLICATION (DIRECTION - TO LODGE
                              FIR/COMPLAINT) NO. 2612 of 2016

         ==========================================================
                       SWETABEN ASHISHBHAI PATEL....Applicant(s)
                                      Versus
                        STATE OF GUJARAT &amp; 4....Respondent(s)
         ==========================================================
         Appearance:
         MR BHAVESH J PATEL, ADVOCATE for the Applicant(s) No. 1
         MR RADHESH Y VYAS, ADVOCATE for the Applicant(s) No. 1
         MS MONALI BHATT, ADDL.PUBLIC PROSECUTOR for Respondent(s) No. 1
         ==========================================================

         CORAM HONOURABLE MS JUSTICE SONIA GOKANI
             :

                                     Date : 22/04/2016


                                       ORAL ORDER</pre>

<p id="p_1">           1. The   petitioner   is   aggrieved   by   non­registration 

              of the  first information report  on the basis of 

              the   complaint   dated   May   23,   2015,   given   in 

              writing by the petitioner to the respondent No.2. 
</p>
<p id="p_2">              It is her say that though cognizable offence is 

              made out, her complaint is not being registered 

              as the first information report.
</p>

<p id="p_3">           2. Both   the   sides   have   been   heard.   This   Court   has 

              also taken into consideration the judgment of the 



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_1">                                          Page 1 of 5</span>

            R/SCR.A/2612/2016                                               ORDER



         Apex Court in the case of <a href="/doc/10239019/" id="a_1">Lalita Kumari v. State  

         of Uttar Pradesh and others</a>, reported in  (2014)  

         2   SCC   1.   It   would   be   beneficial   to   regurgitate 

         the   relevant   paragraph   of   the   said   decision, 

         which reads as under :
</p>

<pre id="pre_2">               "120.           In   view   of   the   aforesaid 
               discussion, we hold: 

               (i)      Registration  of  FIR  is  mandatory under 
</pre><p id="p_4">               Section 154 of the Code, if the information 
               discloses commission of a cognizable offence  
               and no preliminary inquiry is permissible in 
               such a situation.
</p>
<p id="p_5">               (ii) If   the   information   received   does   not 
               disclose a cognizable offence but indicates 
               the necessity for an inquiry, a preliminary 
               inquiry   may   be   conducted   only   to   ascertain 
               whether   cognizable   offence   is   disclosed   or 
               not. 
</p>
<p id="p_6">               (iii)           If   the   inquiry   discloses   the 
               commission of a cognizable offence, the FIR 
               must   be   registered.   In   cases   where 
               preliminary   inquiry   ends   in   closing   the 
               complaint,   a   copy   of   the   entry   of   such  
               closure   must   be   supplied   to   the   first 
               informant   forthwith   and   not   later   than   one 
               week. It must disclose reasons in brief for 



<span class="hidden_text" id="span_2">                                     Page 2 of 5</span>

HC-NIC                            Page 2 of 5      Created On Sat Apr 23 02:56:19 IST 2016
          R/SCR.A/2612/2016                                             ORDER



             closing   the   complaint   and   not   proceeding 
             further. 
</p>
<p id="p_7">             (iv) The   police   officer   cannot   avoid   his 
             duty   of   registering   offence   if   cognizable  
             offence   is   disclosed.   Action   must   be   taken 
             against erring officers who do not register 
             the   FIR   if   information   received   by   him 
             discloses a cognizable offence. 
</p>
<p id="p_8">             (v)      The scope of preliminary inquiry is not  
             to   verify   the   veracity   or   otherwise   of   the 
             information   received   but   only   to   ascertain 
             whether   the   information   reveals   any 
             cognizable offence. 
</p>
<p id="p_9">             (vi) As   to   what   type   and   in   which   cases  
             preliminary inquiry is to be conducted will 
             depend   on   the   facts   and   circumstances   of 
             each   case.   The   category   of   cases   in   which 
             preliminary   inquiry   may   be   made   are   as  
             under:
</p>
<p id="p_10">             (a) Matrimonial disputes/ family disputes 
</p><p id="p_11">             (b) Commercial offences 
</p><p id="p_12">             (c) Medical negligence cases 
</p><p id="p_13">             (d) Corruption cases 
</p><p id="p_14">             (e)   Cases   where   there   is   abnormal 
             delay/laches   in   initiating   criminal 
             prosecution,   for   example,   over   3   months 
             delay   in   reporting   the   matter   without 
             satisfactorily   explaining   the   reasons   for 


<span class="hidden_text" id="span_3">                                 Page 3 of 5</span>

HC-NIC                        Page 3 of 5      Created On Sat Apr 23 02:56:19 IST 2016
               R/SCR.A/2612/2016                                               ORDER



                  delay. 
</p>
<p id="p_15">                  The aforesaid are only illustrations and not 
                  exhaustive   of   all   conditions   which   may  
                  warrant preliminary inquiry.
</p>
<p id="p_16">                  (vii)             While ensuring and protecting the 
                  rights of the accused and the complainant, a 
                  preliminary   inquiry   should   be   made   time 
                  bound and in any case it should not exceed 7 
                  days. The fact of such delay and the causes 
                  of it must be reflected in the General Diary 
                  entry. 
</p>
<p id="p_17">                  (viii)          Since   the   General   Diary/Station 
                  Diary/Daily   Diary   is   the   record   of   all 
                  information received in a police station, we 
                  direct   that   all   information   relating   to 
                  cognizable   offences,   whether   resulting   in 
                  registration   of   FIR   or   leading   to   an  
                  inquiry,   must   be   mandatorily   and 
                  meticulously reflected in the said Diary and 
                  the   decision   to   conduct   a   preliminary 
                  inquiry must also be reflected, as mentioned 
                  above."
</p>
<p id="p_18">         3. Bearing   in   mind   the   aforementioned   ratio   laid 

           down   by   the   Apex   Court,   the   respondent   No.4­

           Police Inspector shall look into the said written 

           complaint of the petitioner and lodge the  first 

           information   report  if   any   cognizable   offence   is

HC-NIC                               Page 4 of 5      Created On Sat Apr 23 02:56:19 IST 2016
                      R/SCR.A/2612/2016                                             ORDER



                 made   out;   if   not,   for   the   limited   purpose   of 

                 knowing   as   to   whether   cognizable  offence   is 

                 revealed,   the   preliminary   inquiry   shall   be 

                 conducted.   The   petitioner   shall   be   forwarded   a 

                 copy   of   the  first   information   report,   if   any 

                 registered,  at   her   residence   forthwith.   In   the 

                 event,   the   respondent   No.4   choses   not   to   lodge 

                 the  first   information   report,  the   petitioner 

                 shall   be   communicated   in   writing   the   brief 

                 reasons   accordingly.   Such   exercise   shall   be 

                 completed by the respondent No.4 at the earliest, 

                 but   not   later   than  one   week  from   the   date   of 

                 receipt   of   a   copy   of   this   order.   The   entire 

                 exercise   shall   be   done   at   the   earliest   without 

                 further loss of time.
</p>
<p id="p_19">                                  The   petition   stands   disposed   of 

                 accordingly. Direct Service is permitted.
</p>
