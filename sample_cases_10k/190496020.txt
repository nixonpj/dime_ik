<p id="p_1">For Petitioner(s)        :    Mr. Lokesh Kumar Verma
For Respondent(s)        :    Ms. Charu Jain



     HON'BLE MR. JUSTICE SANJEEV PRAKASH SHARMA

                         Judgment / Order

02/04/2018

     Both the counsel agree that the controversy stands covered

by judgment passed by this Court as well as other Coordinate

Benches relating to grant of caste certificate       on the basis of

having been married in State of Rajasthan. Of course, the same is

required to be issued after verification from the concerned State

relating to the status of the petitioner.
</p><p id="p_2">     In S.B.Civil Writ Petition No.4551/2018 relying on        "Manju

Yadav Vs. State of Rajasthan" decided on 27 th February, 2018
                                     (2 of 2)             [CW-6452/2018]


wherein following directions issued:


</p><blockquote id="blockquote_1">          "Taking into consideration aforesaid directions, this
     Court finds that present cases to the petitioner has
     prayed for issuance of the certificate on the basis of their
     residence after marriage in State of Rajasthan and in
     view thereof and in terms of the aforesaid order passed
     in the judgment(supra), the present writ petitions are
     allowed. In this writ petition, the directions, as quoted
     above shall be applied mutatis mutandis equally and the
     State is now directed to immediately take up steps.
     Concerned SDM in relation to each petitioner is directed
     to take steps for issuing OBC certificate in line of the
     aforesaid directions as early as possible."</blockquote><p id="p_3">



</p><p id="p_4">      Taking into consideration aforesaid directions, this Court

finds that present cases to the petitioner has prayed for issuance

of the certificate on the basis of their residence after marriage in

State of Rajasthan and in view thereof and in terms of the

aforesaid order passed in the judgment(supra), the present writ

petitions are allowed. In this writ petition, the directions, as

quoted above shall be applied mutatis mutandis equally and the

State is now directed to immediately take up steps. Concerned

SDM in relation to each petitioner is directed to take steps for

issuing OBC certificate in line of the aforesaid directions as early

as possible.
</p>
<p id="p_5">
</p><p id="p_6">      With aforesaid directions, the writ petition is allowed.


</p><p id="p_7">                                  (SANJEEV PRAKASH SHARMA),J

Anu/112
